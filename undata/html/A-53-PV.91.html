Mr. Gerus (Belarus)
It is my honour to introduce the reports of the Second Committee to the General Assembly. I take pleasure first in introducing the report of the Second Committee on agenda item 12, entitled "Report of the Economic and Social Council", which the Assembly has before it in document A/53/605.
Paragraph 18 of that document contains three draft resolutions, and paragraph 19 contains two draft decisions recommended by the Committee for adoption by the Assembly.
The Assembly also has before it the report of the Second Committee on item 91, entitled "Macroeconomic policy questions", in document A/53/606 and addenda 1 to 5. Document A/53/606 contains the introduction to the item.

In paragraph 7 of document A/53/606/Add.3, the Second Committee recommends to the General Assembly the adoption of one draft resolution under sub-item (c), "Commodities".

In paragraph 18 of document A/53/609/Add.6, the Second Committee recommends to the General Assembly the adoption of three draft resolutions which are not related to a specific sub-item. In paragraph 19 of the same document, the Second Committee recommends to the General Assembly the adoption of one draft decision.
Under agenda item 95, entitled "Operational activities for development", the Assembly has before it the report of the Second Committee contained in document A/53/610. I should like to bring to the attention of the General Assembly three corrections to be made to this document. First, in the third line of the first preambular paragraph of draft resolution I, the word "and" should be deleted. In the eighth line of paragraph 16 of draft resolution I, "board" should read "boards". In the second line of paragraph 22 of draft resolution I, "Nations" should read "Nations'".
With those corrections, the Second Committee recommends to the General Assembly the adoption of two draft resolutions contained in paragraph 13 and one draft decision contained in paragraph 14 of document A/53/610.
The Assembly also has before it the report of the Second Committee under agenda item 96, entitled "Training and research", which is contained in document A/53/611. Paragraph 14 of that document contains one draft resolution under sub-item (a), "United Nations University"; and one draft resolution under sub-item (b), "United Nations Institute for Training and Research", which are recommended for adoption by the General Assembly.

Paragraph 11 contains one draft resolution, which was adopted by the Second Committee by a recorded vote and is recommended to the Assembly for adoption.
Under agenda item 98, entitled "Implementation of the first United Nations Decade for the Eradication of Poverty (1997-2006)", the Assembly has before it the report of the Second Committee contained in document A/53/613. The Second Committee recommends to the General Assembly the adoption of two draft resolutions contained in paragraph 14 of that document and one draft decision, which is contained in paragraph 15.
I should like to inform the General Assembly that, during this session, the Second Committee adopted 36 draft substantive proposals. These proposals should be considered to be significant contributions of the Second Committee to the process of revitalization of the United Nations activities in the economic and environmental fields. The Committee tried to promote the establishment of favourable conditions for the sustainable development of all nations of the world, developed and developing, economies in transition, small island States and landlocked countries. We have come to agreement on 32 draft resolutions.
Now I should like to take this opportunity to thank all the members of the Second Committee for their contribution to the work of the Committee and to thank the Chairman, Mr. Bagher Asadi, and the three Vice-Chairmen, Mr. David Prendergast of Jamaica, Mr. Burak Özügergin of Turkey and Mr. Odyek Agona of Uganda, for their efforts in successfully completing the work of the Committee. My thanks also go to the members of the Secretariat for their assistance and endurance.
I have the honour to submit the reports of the Second Committee to the General Assembly for consideration.
The Acting President: The positions of delegations regarding the recommendations of the Second Committee have been made clear in the Committee and are reflected in the relevant official records.
May I remind Members States that under paragraph 7 of decision 34/401, the General Assembly agreed that
"When the same draft resolution is considered in a Main Committee and in plenary meeting, a delegation should, as far as possible, explain its vote only once, i.e., either in the Committee or in plenary meeting unless that delegation's vote in plenary meeting is different from its vote in the Committee."
May I also remind delegations that, also in accordance with General Assembly decision 34/401, explanations of vote are limited to 10 minutes and should be made by delegations from their seats.
Before we begin to take action on the recommendations contained in the reports of the Second Committee, I should like to advise representatives that we are going to proceed to take decisions in the same manner as was done in the Second Committee. This means that where a recorded vote was taken, we will do the same, unless the Secretariat has been notified otherwise in advance.
I should also hope that we may proceed to adopt without a vote the recommendations that were adopted without a vote in the Second Committee.

Mr. Rønneberg (Marshall Islands)
At the outset, I wish to thank the Rapporteur of the Second Committee for his hard work and for his reports to the General Assembly.
The convening next year of a special session to review the implementation of the Barbados Conference is of extreme importance to the Republic of the Marshall Islands, and indeed to all small island developing States. For this reason, my delegation is extremely disappointed that the Second Committee could not find an equitable solution to the issue of the representation of those States which are not Members of the United Nations.
As the draft resolution is currently drafted, these States will be able to be present only as observers, even though they are independent and sovereign States and did participate as full members in the Barbados Conference itself. My delegation sees this as further evidence of the increasing marginalization of small island developing States, and we deeply regret that the General Assembly is about to endorse this state of affairs.
My delegation has regretfully decided to accept the consensus which has been forced on us by a minority in the Committee for reasons that we believe were unrelated to the particular draft resolution. We have been forced to accept this consensus for the sake of retaining other important aspects of the draft resolution.
My delegation will work closely with the delegations of the small island developing States affected by the situation so that their participation in the special session and in its preparatory process will be more meaningful than that which has been envisaged by the small minority.
Ms. Hawkins (Australia)
Australia is pleased to have been a sponsor of the draft resolution on the implementation of the outcome of the Global Conference on the Sustainable Development of Small Island Developing States.
We wish, however, to register our disappointment that the draft resolution was not able to ensure the full participation of small island developing States that are not Members of the United Nations in the special session review of the Barbados Programme of Action, despite great efforts by several fellow sponsors to find a solution, which we greatly appreciate.
We would nonetheless request that all will be done to facilitate the participation of small island developing States that are not United Nations Members to the fullest extent possible to ensure a successful review.

Agenda item 98
Implementation of the first United Nations Decade for the Eradication of Poverty (1997-2006)
Report of the Second Committee (A/53/613)
The Acting President
The Assembly will now take a decision on the two draft resolutions recommended by the Second Committee in paragraph 14 of its report (A/53/613) and on the draft decision recommended by the Second Committee in paragraph 15 of the same report.
I call on the representative of Bangladesh, who wishes to speak in explanation of position before action is taken on these texts.
Mr. Sorcar (Bangladesh)
At the 31st meeting of the Second Committee, held on 2 November 1998, the Under-Secretary-General for Economic and Social Affairs, Mr. Nitin Desai, made a statement to introduce and explain the report of the Secretary-General on the role of microcredit in the eradication of poverty, documents A/53/223 and A/53/223/Add.1. The text of that statement was subsequently issued on 15 November 1998 annexed to a note by the Secretariat, document A/C.2/53/7. That document elaborates some of the points in the report of the Secretary-General; it should be viewed as an integral part of that report, and the two should be considered together.
The preference of the Bangladesh delegation would have been to appropriately adjust the text of the draft decision recommended in paragraph 15 of document A/53/613 to take this into account. However, owing to time constraints, we went along with the decision to recommend the draft decision to the Assembly. At the same time, however, we would like the General Assembly to take note both of documents A/53/223 and A/53/223/Add.1 and of document A/C.2/53/7.
The Acting President: We turn first to draft resolution I, entitled "International Year of Microcredit, 2005".
Draft resolution I was adopted by the Second Committee without a vote. May I take it that the Assembly too wishes to adopt the draft resolution?
Draft resolution I was adopted (resolution 53/197).
The Acting President
The Second Committee adopted draft resolution II, entitled "Implementation of the first United Nations Decade for the Eradication of Poverty", without a vote. May I take it that the Assembly wishes to do likewise?
Draft resolution II was adopted (resolution 53/198).
The Acting President
We turn now to the draft decision, entitled "Report of the Secretary-General on the role of microcredit in the eradication of poverty".
May I take it that the General Assembly wishes to adopt the draft decision as recommended by the Second Committee?
The draft decision was adopted.
The Acting President
May I take it that it is the wish of the General Assembly to conclude its consideration of agenda item 98?
It was so decided.
