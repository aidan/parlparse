The situation in Cyprus

Report of the Secretary-General on the United Nations Operation in Cyprus (S/1997/437 and Corr.1 and Add.1)
Letter dated 20 June 1997 from the Secretary-General addressed to the President of the Security Council (S/1997/480)

The President Russian

The Security Council will now begin its consideration of the item on its agenda. The Council is meeting in accordance with the understanding reached in its prior consultations.

Members of the Council have before them the report of the Secretary-General on the United Nations Operation in Cyprus, documents S/1997/437 and corrigendum 1 and addendum 1, and the letter dated 20 June 1997 from the Secretary-General addressed to the President of the Security Council, document S/1997/480.

Members of the Council also have before them document S/1997/492, which contains the text of a draft resolution prepared in the course of the Council's prior consultations.

It is my understanding that the Council is ready to proceed to the vote on the draft resolution before it. Unless I hear any objection, I shall put the draft resolution to the vote now.

There being no objection, it is so decided.

favour=15 against=0 abstain=0 absent=0

Chile, China, Costa Rica, Egypt, France, Guinea-Bissau, Japan, Kenya, Poland, Portugal, Republic of Korea, Russia, Sweden, United Kingdom, United States

The President Russian

There were 15 votes in favour. The draft resolution has been adopted unanimously as resolution 1117 (1997).

The Security Council has thus concluded the present stage of its consideration of the item on its agenda.

The meeting rose at 10.25 a.m.
