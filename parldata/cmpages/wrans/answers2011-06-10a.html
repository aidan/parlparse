Armed Conflict: Sexual Offences

Ms Harman: To ask the Secretary of State for International Development whether his Department is providing support to the office of the UN Special Representative for Sexual Violence in Conflict. [59082]

Mr Andrew Mitchell: The Department for International Development (DFID) strongly supports the work of the UN Special Representative on Sexual Violence in Conflict, whom the Minister of State, Department for International Development, the right hon. Member for Rutland and Melton (Mr Duncan), met on 19 January 2011. Ministers meet regularly to discuss the UK's work in this area and the Under-Secretary of State for the Home Department and the Minister for Equalities, my hon. Friend the Member for Hornsey and Wood Green (Lynne Featherstone), raises awareness and builds support lot this as the UK's International Violence Against Women Champion. We are firmly committed to tackling sexual violence in armed conflict as part of our wider work to address violence against women and girls, in support of UN Security Council Resolution 1325 on Women, Peace and Security.

For example, DFID currently supports the United Nations' Humanitarian Pooled Fund for the Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC), which in 2010 funded several projects responding to the needs of victims of sexual violence.
