Mr. Keetch: To ask the Secretary of State for Defence what the expected in-service dates are for (a) the Eurofighter, (b) HMS Albion and Bulwark, (c) the Future Aircraft Carrier, (d) the Bowman communication system and (e) the Trigat anti-tank weapon; and if he will make a statement. [94786]

The in-service date of Medium Range Trigat is 2005.

NATO

Mr. Burnett: To ask the Secretary of State for Defence what steps he is taking to ensure that European partner countries shoulder a proportionate burden of contributions to NATO. [96093]

Mr. Hoon: The achievement of NATO's aims depends on the equitable sharing among all Allies of the roles, risks, and responsibilities, as well as the benefits, of common defence. NATO Defence Planning gives practical effect to this burden sharing. Force goals, which set challenging but realistic targets for each Ally, are designed to ensure that our collective defence arrangements continue to provide for the full range of Alliance missions.

In addition, the development of the European Security and Defence Identity (ESDI) will enable European Allies to make a more coherent and effective contribution to the missions and activities of the Alliance. This is being taken ahead in step with NATO's Defence Capabilities Initiative (DCI), which is designed to ensure that all Allies are able to contribute to NATO's effectiveness. The UK is playing a leading role in both of these initiatives.

Horse Guards Tattoo

Mr. Ottaway: To ask the Secretary of State for Defence if he will make it his policy to continue the tradition of the field gun competition in the proposed Horse Guards Tattoo. [96736]

Mr. Hoon: A Military Tattoo will continue to be held in London from 2001 onwards. Using the spectacular backdrop of Horse Guards Parade, the Tattoo will build on the traditional pageantry that has been such a popular element of the Royal Tournament over the years and a special feature of London in summer.

However we will not continue with the Royal Navy Field Gun competition. In part this is because the event cannot readily be run at Horse Guards Parade due to the surface and the special fixings that are required to meet stringent Health and Safety regulations. But also, the Field Gun competition accounted for more than half of the personnel resources used for the Tournament, some 16,000 personnel-days each year. This could not be justified in the light of operational commitments and the other demands placed on our service personnel.

The Royal Navy is now keen to look to the future and concentrate on supporting events that more accurately reflect the training and operational activities of the Service in the next millennium.
