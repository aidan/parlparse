As hon. Members will appreciate, the green belt has a long and noble history. It was first developed in London 1938, and Birmingham and Sheffield later took up that good idea. In 1955, the then Government urged all towns and cities to create green belts—bands of land around their environs designed specifically to restrict urban growth. Today, our green belts are more than just that; they are our green lungs and open spaces enjoyed by all. Today, however, we are discussing the green belt in England, because of the various concerns that have been raised about Government policy.
