    "There is overcrowding whenever there are so many people in a house that any two or more of those persons, being 10 or more years old, and of opposite sexes, not being persons living together as husband and wife, have to sleep in the same room."

That is a clear definition, and I hope that the Minister can accept it. That would answer the points of the hon. Member for Hertsmere about people living together.

Perhaps there should be a space standard, by which one could calculate the permitted number of people who could live in a dwelling. I hope that the space standard will be a criterion for ruling out overcrowded accommodation. If the floor area of a room is less than 50 sq ft, no one should be in it; if that area is between 50 and, 70 sq ft, half a person could fit in the room; one person could fit in a room with a floor area of between 70 and 90 sq ft; one and a half persons could be allowed to live in a room with a floor area of between 90 and 110sq ft. Two people could be allowed to stay in a room with a floor area of 110sq ft or more. I assume that an updated metric version will be produced soon. It would be helpful in determining whether housing was adequate under the Bill if the Minister decided to rule out overcrowded housing on the basis of the room sharing standards in housing legislation and of the space standard.

The Minister's comments on adequacy and suitability were unsatisfactory.
