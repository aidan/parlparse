The point that I seek to clarify is simple. In earlier clauses that deal with registration of a person running a care home there is a relationship between the registered person and the establishment in respect of which they are registered. I have sought--perhaps imperfectly--to draw out that distinction, because in clause 68 the distinction appears not to be properly made. If a person is registered as a child minder and is looking after children on domestic premises, there appears to be an underlying assumption that the domestic premises are their own home. I know, however, that child minders have other people working for or with them. Therefore, a person can look after children in their own domestic premises and employ other people to look after children in those premises.

We are dealing with two categories of people. The first is the principal child minder, who, under the Bill, must be responsible for the provision of the environment in which children are minded. Provisions of clause 68 deal with the condition, quality and appropriateness of the physical facilities.

The second category is people who are not in control of the premises on which child minding is provided. They must satisfy the appropriate criteria for suitability that they are suitable people to look after children but it does not seem right to impose on them a responsibility for the premises and other structures.

In the earlier parts of the Bill, a distinction is clearly made. A registered person who carries on a care home business is responsible for all kinds of things in relation to that home. Care workers must be registered and various provisions apply to them, but it is not suggested that they are personally responsible for the physical standards of the accommodation in the care home.

By probing the Government's intentions, I seek to suggest an alternative model that creates the concept of a child minder as a person who looks after children in domestic premises, of which they are the owner or over which they have control.
