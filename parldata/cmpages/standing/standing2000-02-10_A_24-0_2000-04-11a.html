The Chairman: With this we may discuss Government new clause 23-Modification of standard conditions of gas licences.

Dr. Howells: The new clauses are closely related to clauses 30 to 32, which we have debated. Indeed, the overall effect is to introduce for gas exactly the regime that the Committee agreed to apply to electricity.

New clause 22(2) provides that the Secretary of State may determine and publish standard conditions of licences for each type of gas licence-that is to say, transportation, shipping and supply. Subsection (3) incorporates those conditions into all existing gas licences when the new clauses come into force. Although standard licence conditions are a new concept in electricity, they have been a feature of gas licences since the Gas Act 1995. None the less, the issues in the new clause are the same as those we considered earlier; there is nothing new about them.

The 1995 Act gave the Secretary of State a single-use power to determine and publish standard conditions for each type of licence. The clause gives the Secretary of State another chance to do the same thing, because the Bill makes some necessary changes to the standard conditions. The modification procedures in legislation should be used to make most changes to licences, but when conditions are changed as a matter of Government policy and in line with changes to primary legislation, a more direct means of modification is appropriate.

The Government are taking a broad power but, in practice, the changes needed to gas licences will be limited because, legally and administratively, by far the most straightforward way of making the changes that we want will be to replace all the existing sets of standard licence conditions with new sets of conditions. Inevitably, much in the new conditions will be unchanged.

Taken at face value, the power gives the Secretary of State carte blanche to make new licence conditions whether or not they are related to the Bill. I therefore offer hon. Members a categorical assurance: the Government intend to use the power only to introduce conditions, or to amend existing conditions, when that is required as a matter of Government policy pursuant to matters dealt with in the Bill. Other proposed new standard conditions will have to be made using the existing or the new licence modification procedures.

I remind hon. Members that the Department is at present consulting on the second draft of the standard conditions to be incorporated into gas and electricity licences, and the deadline for comments is 31 May.

The purpose of subsection (1) of new clause 22 is to give effect to a more flexible licensing structure. It is designed to enable the authority to tailor application of the standard conditions to match the circumstances of a licence holder, thereby avoiding the need for him to be subject to unnecessary or irrelevant obligations. I hope that the Committee will welcome that.

We had a detailed discussion of the parallel electricity provisions in our 14 March sitting, and, as the same considerations apply to the proposal, I shall not repeat them. The same applies to collective licence modification, to which new clause 23 relates, as to electricity licences, which we discussed in detail on clause 32.
