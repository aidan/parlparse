Mr. Flight: The amendments would all exempt from tax income that is received by charities and applied to charitable purposes that would otherwise be chargeable to tax under case VI of schedule D. I hope that the Goverment will respond that that is indeed their intent. Charities are not exempt from tax in relation to income or gains treated as income that falls to be taxed under case VI. Case VI is a miscellaneous category, which includes income from the marketing of a charity's name and logo--a common occurrence these days in sponsorship arrangements--income and gains from investment in land and income from the sale of patent rights. Well-advised charities can avoid any charge to tax under case VI by carrying out the relevant activity in a trading subsidiary and then gift aiding that profit.

The provision is particularly relevant in the case of the marketing of a charity's name and logo, which commonly happens when a charity licenses the use of its name and logo to a commercial entity that will raise money on its behalf and derive some benefit from the association with the charity. Unless the payments by the commercial department are structured as annual payments, which they rarely can in practice, such income is taxable under case VI. That could easily catch out charities, just when they are being encouraged to undertake active fund raising and being given, in the case of land, far wider powers of investment. So our amendments seek to exempt from tax the income of charities under case VI of schedule D, except where that schedule backs up appropriate anti-avoidance provisions elsewhere in tax legislation.

Miss Melanie Johnson: The clause implements one of the ideas floated in the consultation document on charity taxation.
