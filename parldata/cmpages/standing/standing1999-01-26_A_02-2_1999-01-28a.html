The amendment refers not exclusively to the function of mayor, but to everyone who presents himself for election to the GLA.

Furthermore, as I understand the amendment it seeks to define the period during which an expense should be regarded as an election expense for the purpose of the return of such expenses. However, it would have the opposite effect to that which the hon. Gentleman intends. Setting a limit to the date from which expenses need to be declared would allow unscrupulous candidates, by delaying a statement of their intentions, to avoid declaring expenditure that should otherwise be returned. The hon. Gentleman will know that the question of whether an expense is an election expense that must be returned is one of fact which must be determined by reference to the purpose for which the expense was incurred, not to the time at which it was incurred. The courts have noted that the provision does not prescribe a time at which the election begins for the purposes of defining expenses because that would enable the expenses limit to be avoided. Given those circumstances, I ask the hon. Gentleman to withdraw his amendment.
