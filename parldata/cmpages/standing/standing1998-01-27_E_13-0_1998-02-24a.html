What my hon. Friend the Member for Wirral, South (Mr. Chapman) said about English Partnerships is true. He has given me the opportunity to put the purpose of clause 40 on the record. It enables provisions to be made for the transfers of property, rights and liabilities from EP to RDAs. Subsection (1) provides that the Secretary of State may, by direction, require EP to make one or more schemes transferring such property rights and liabilities to an RDA for carrying out transfer activities as appropriate. Subsection (2) allows for specific details on the transfer schemes set out in schedule 9 to have effect for the clause.

I should like to put on record the thinking behind our decisions affecting EP assets. I hope to reassure those people who have been circulating the rumour that the assets will be transferred to the centre for redistribution. The bulk of assets will transfer with the work from EP regional offices to RDAs, so that the RDAs can continue the work which they take over.

We made it clear in the White paper that the RDAs will take on the role of EP regional offices to promote physical regeneration. With a few exceptions, which I shall mention in a moment, they will take on existing projects and take forward the task of identifying and preparing sites for business expansion and investment.

The existing network of EP regional offices is being reorganised to match the nine RDAs to which they will be transferred. The assets associated with those regional offices will also be transferred by virtue of a transfer schedule which will be drawn up initially by EP and approved by the Secretary of State. The transfer schedule will cover development schemes, the rights and liabilities arising from continuing EP projects, property, premises, furniture and equipment, intellectual property, guidance notes and records on the rights and liabilities associated with contracts for the provision of services. I expect the bulk of EP's portfolio of assets to be clearly associated with the work being undertaken by EP regional offices. The projects will transfer on vesting day to the RDAs to enable a seamless continuation of that work.

As we made clear when the White Paper was published, a limited number of projects of national importance or which cross regional boundaries will remain with EP in the interests of ensuring uninterrupted completion. They are: regeneration of the Greenwich peninsula; and coalfield initiatives. In April 2000, EP will merge with the Commission for the New Towns. We intend the role and functions of EP to move to the RDAs. Any assets remaining with EP after vesting date will transfer to the RDAs in due course. We are working closely with EP to identify the assets that will be transferred.

The responsibility for TECs, however, is a function of the DFEE_Ministers would decide whether or not to delegate responsibility to RDAs. We do not consider it appropriate to do so, for the time being at least. We believe that we are giving the RDAs the right package of functions; it provides the right critical mass.

It is important to be realistic about the package of functions to be given to the RDAs. They will be new bodies and will have much to do to establish themselves and to develop their work programmes. As their experience develops, scope will exist to extend their role, and further functions will be delegated to them if that is desirable. The Government consider the package of functions to be given to RDAs at the outset to strike the right balance and to provide the right critical mass. If the Committee reflects on the past 20 years_or as far back as 1975_and considers the activities of the SDA and WDA at that time, it will find that they have similar powers.

The functions of the RDAs will afford them considerable influence at the regional level. Their influence will not be limited to areas and organisations for which the RDAs are directly responsible, or for which they control the budgets.

As we have made clear on a number of occasions, education, skills and employability are critical to economic performance and will be central to the regional strategies of RDAs. RDAs will have a crucial role in developing and planning a regional skills strategy in co-operation with all the partners_including business, industry, trade unions, TECs local authorities and further and higher education.

The way in which the responsibilities for all of these functions have been divided is entirely in keeping with the RDA's role as a provider of a strategic overview and its focus on bringing together the work and views of regional partners.

We also want RDAs to play a big part in monitoring and improving the performance of TECs. The Government want to drive the performance of all TECs up to the standard of the best. The details of the role of RDAs in this respect are still to be worked out, but we expect them to take a hard look at the performance of TECs in their region and to recommend how they might make a greater contribution to regional objectives.

The package of functions to be given to RDAs is challenging but realistic.
