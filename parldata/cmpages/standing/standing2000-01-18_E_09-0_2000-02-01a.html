    ``which the CAA ought reasonably to have made itself aware''.

The Fair Trading Act 1973 contains similar provisions for the excision of sensitive information, such as section 83, on the laying before Parliament of Competition Commission reports; section 124, on the publication by the Office of Fair Trading of information and advice; and section 133, which imposes general restrictions on the disclosure of information gained under the auspices of that Act, thereby underlining the importance of treating commercially sensitive information with care.

The expectation is that the provisions will be rarely used. The regulator and the Secretary of State will start with the strong presumption that, in the interests of transparency and accountability, the register of licences should be comprehensive unless it is clear that the person would suffer commercial prejudice as a result, or that the public interest would be adversely affected. If the licensee is not the parent company, there is a possibility that certain matters in the licence might reveal information about the parent company or a subsidiary of the parent company, which could affect its value or competitiveness. Licensed bodies, by their nature, are monopolies or involve monopolistic elements, and it might be argued that considerations of commercial sensitivity are a different animal where monopolies are concerned.

Some information, if disclosed, can be prejudicial to the interests of customers as well as to the monopoly itself. My right hon. Friend the Member for Edinburgh, East and Musselburgh (Dr. Strang) asked for examples. The regulator and the Secretary of State in exercising these powers have to make a balanced judgment as to the legitimate interests of the licensee in associated businesses and the interests of customers. The ability to excise, where necessary, material from the register of licences which might damage the interest of either or both is an important tool in maintaining that balance. I therefore ask the hon. Gentleman to withdraw the amendment.
