Mr. Davis: I should like to take this opportunity Mr. Butterfill, on behalf of the fourth party in this Room, the PAC, to extend my thanks to you and your colleague, Mr. Hood, for your great patience, forbearance and tolerance of our wide wanderings and particularly, this evening, your tolerance of our occasionally fractious proceedings. I thank you for carrying this Bill through so efficiently and effectively.

Mr. Letwin: I echo the comments of my right hon. Friend, Mr. Butterfill. You and your co-Chairman, Mr. Hood, performed a valuable role. This has been a Committee in which we have explored important terrain. Much has been said. Some comments have been less cogent than others, but that is the nature of Committee discussion. I am sure that you will be relieved that you will not have to take the Chair when the matter receives a prolonged ping-ponging between the two Houses That will presumably fall to the Deputy Speakers. I am sure that I speak for the entire Opposition in thanking the Economic Secretary—she may be surprised to hear me say this—for exhibiting considerable patience in what must have been rather adverse circumstances for her.

Mr. Davey: I add my thanks to you Mr. Butterfill, to your colleague Mr. Hood and to the Economic Secretary. May I also earn some brownie points for future Committees by sitting down now.

Miss Johnson: I join the thanks that have been offered to you, Mr. Butterfill, and to Mr. Hood. I thank you for your patience, good humour, and even-handedness in chairing our proceedings. At this unexpectedly late hour I should also like to take the opportunity to thank the Clerk, the Hansard staff, the police and the messengers who have supported our efforts so well. My thanks also go to my hon. Friend the Financial Secretary, to our parliamentary private secretaries and to my other hon. Friends on the Committee.

On behalf of the Labour Members on the Committee I should also like to thank the Conservative and Liberal Democrat Members and even those belonging to the fourth party, the PAC. I am not quite sure where I should put them. I am grateful to them for their learned and thought-provoking contributions, particularly the right hon. Member for Haltemprice and Howden (Mr. Davis), the Chairman of the PAC, and my right hon. Friend the Member for Swansea, West (Mr. Williams), its most senior member.

I should also thank the National Audit Office for its excellent briefing. It is seldom that the Government are opposed by so well briefed a team. We have had full and interesting debates on this important matter. I hope that we have done justice to the resource accounting aspects of the Bill as we have certainly tried to do justice to the issues relating to the NAO and the PAC. If any members of the Committee thought that things were going to be dull, I am sure that they will have been disabused of that idea by the passion of some of our debates. Of course, my patience has never been tested by our proceedings because it is infinite—as infinite as the speeches of the hon. Member for West Dorset sometimes seemed. I am pleased to say that our work has nevertheless been carried out in a constructive and good-humoured fashion.

The Chairman: I thank the Committee for those words of thanks, which I shall pass on to my co-„Chairman, Mr. Hood. It has been a pleasure to chair this Committee. For the most part, it has been good natured and, in the best traditions, constructive in its deliberations on a complex piece of legislation. Thank you all very much.

Question put and agreed to.

Bill, as amended, to be reported.
