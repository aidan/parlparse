Regional Development Agencies Bill

Clause 25

Agency as Local Planning Authority

4.30 pm

Mrs. Caroline Spelman (Meriden): In the light of this morning's developments, I will not move my amendment.

The Chairman: Thank you, Mrs. Spelman. Amendment not moved.

Question proposed, That the clause stand part of the Bill.

Mr. Tim Yeo (South Suffolk): The Committee knows from the morning sitting of our fundamental objections to the powers that could be transferred to regional development agencies under clause 25. As we are awaiting new and exciting developments, which will be unveiled at least a week before the Bill enters the House of Commons on Report, I will not detain the Committee.

Question put, That the clause stand part of the Bill:--

The Committee divided: Ayes 8, Noes 4.

Question accordingly agreed to.

Clause 25 ordered to stand part of the Bill.

Clause 26

Adoption of Private Streets

Question proposed, That the clause stand part of the Bill.

Mr. Yeo: The same point applies to clause 26 as did to clause 25. However, while I am on my feet, may I clarify beyond doubt the Minister's helpful assurance that he would keep the Committee informed of the evolution of the Government's thinking? Am I right to assume that he intends to do that before the Bill reaches the Report stage in the House of Commons rather than when it returns from the other place? Is it his intention to do that before the Bill goes through any further stage in Parliament?

The Minister for the Regions, Regeneration and Planning (Mr. Richard Caborn): Yes, that is the case.

Question put, That the clause stand part of the Bill:--

The Committee divided: Ayes 11, Noes 4.
