We want local authorities to play an active role as leaders of their communities. Opposition Members asked about the new duty, which will cement the place of sustainable development at the centre of community planning. The principles of sustainable development will underpin authorities' policies on services that they deliver and activities that they undertake in partnership with others. Local authorities are already drawing up strategies under Local Agenda 21 that give effect to sustainable development principles. The new quality of life indicators that we have published can be fashioned for local government.

Our policies promote sustainable development. Authorities that ignore sustainable development cannot claim to meet the best value test of effectiveness. However, the prime focus of best value legislation is on securing real improvements to the services on which local people depend. We do not want to weaken that focus. However, under the best value provisions, we will issue guidance ensuring that local authorities link the implementation of best value with relevant issues such as sustainability.

Amendment No. 93 would require the Secretary of State always to consult best value authority representatives—instead of the authorities themselves—before issuing guidance on best value performance plans. The Bill provides a choice between the best value authority and its representative, which could include the Local Government Association, the Welsh Local Government Association, the police authorities, and so on. The amendment would restrict that choice and would allow consultation only with representative associations, which, in certain cases, may not be appropriate. New clause 7 will be introduced to set out what guidance can cover and how it should be consulted upon. Subsection (2)(c) of the new clause is drafted in the same terms as existing clause 6(5)(c).

I understand the Opposition's concerns about new powers being taken by the Secretary of State, but their amendment seeks to downgrade those powers by substituting guidance for orders in those very areas where the national interest and need for consistency are strongest. Best value needs to have teeth if it is to work properly although I can assure hon. Members that we have no intention of reproducing the sort of detailed prescription and over-complexity which was such an unpopular feature of compulsory competitive tendering.

Typically, the official Opposition want all matters specified in the Bill, which is too inflexible. The Liberal Democrats want everything in guidance, which also lacks real rigour. We therefore seek a sensible middle course, taking advantage of the flexibility that orders can bring. The exercise of this power is subject to the negative procedure and there would be parliamentary scrutiny of any order we bring forward. For those reasons the amendment should not be accepted and I hope it will be withdrawn.

In relation to the two amendments tabled since our last sitting, I understand the Opposition's concern about new powers being taken by the Secretary of State, but amendment No. 144 would remove the provision in the Bill that best value authorities must include a number of key elements specified by the Secretary of State by order within a best value performance plan. Instead, the amendment provides only that the Secretary of State should issue guidance specifying the elements authorities should include in their performance plan.

Amendments Nos. 144 and 145 substitute guidance for orders in connection with setting out the key elements for inclusion in the best value performance plan. An order would list specific matters which authorities need to include. Guidance would offer flexibility but would not have quite the same force. It is essential that all authorities include certain key elements in producing performance plans in order to provide a base line. Only in that way can we properly judge the improvements that are being made by local government in the delivery of services.

Comparison is a key element in the best value regime. People must be able to compare their authority's performance with that of other authorities, and authorities will need to look at other authorities' performances when reviewing their own services. If the key elements that authorities are required to include in their performance plan are not prescribed through statutory instrument on a consistent basis, the whole best value performance management system will be undermined. Guidance is not strong enough in this case to achieve our goal of best value. I therefore hope that hon. Members will not press the amendment or those many others grouped with it.
