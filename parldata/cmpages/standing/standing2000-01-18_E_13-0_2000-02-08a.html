The important point about that Henry VIII clause and previous ones is that they at least genuflected towards the origin of Henry VIII clauses. The name dates from the reign of Henry VIII, when the king took the power to create law by proclamation. Secretaries of State issuing orders to create legislation is the nearest one gets to legislating by proclamation, albeit that in this case a cursory genuflection towards Parliament is required in the form of affirmative resolutions.

If legislation is complicated to introduce, it makes sense for Ministers to take powers to amend other statutes in order to facilitate the introduction of that legislation. The CAA 1996 directions are already public knowledge and are conveyed to the public by gazettes and by the CAA annual report. The 1996 directions require the CAA to provide all air navigation services other than those relating to airspace policy through NATS--in other words, operational air traffic control services and related functions. The directions require the CAA to collaborate with the Secretary of State for Defence to ensure that airspace policy functions are provided on its behalf by a Director of Airspace Policy. In other words, airspace policy functions are delegated to the DAP.
