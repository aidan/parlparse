Powers of recall of the Mayor

    " (1) A referendum of those entitled to vote as electors at ordinary elections shall be held on whether the Mayor should be removed from office if 90 per cent. or more of the Assembly members submit a petition, or if 10 per cent. or more of those entitled to vote in the election of Mayor submit a petition to the Assembly.

    New Clause 9

    Impeachment of the Mayor

    " (1) The Assembly shall have the power to remove the mayor from office.

    (2) A resolution for removal will require the support of at least nine members of the Assembly.

    (3) The Mayor will be removed from office, if the resolution for removal is supported by at least seventeen members.

    (4) The criterion for removal will be that the actions of the Mayor are deemed to be harmful to the interests of the people of London,

The Minister for London and Construction (Mr. Nick Raynsford): I am pleased to have the opportunity to respond to what has been a wide-ranging and detailed debate on the four new clauses.

The new clauses provide for the removal of a mayor from office in certain circumstances. On the surface, that might appear to be a desirable and sensible objective. As I intend clearly to demonstrate, however, the recall mechanisms that are set out in the new clauses contain serious inherent defects, which make then undesirable and impractical.

The Government will not support any of the new clauses. In taking that view, we are not, as the hon. Member for Ruislip-Northwood (Mr. Wilkinson) suggested, setting them aside lightly.
