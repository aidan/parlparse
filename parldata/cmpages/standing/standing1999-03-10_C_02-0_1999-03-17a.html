Subsection (2) deals with two distinct eventualities. First, it covers cases where an individual would have been dismissed. An individual who has engaged in misconduct that satisfies the criteria of referral misconduct that harmed a child or placed a child at risk of harm can be suspended or moved while the employer investigates the matter. As a result of the employer's investigation and possibly a disciplinary hearing, the employer may be satisfied that the individual is guilty of misconduct and should be dismissed or moved to a non-childcare post.
