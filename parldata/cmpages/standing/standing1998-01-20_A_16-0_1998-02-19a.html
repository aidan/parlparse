Clause 75

Code of Practice

The Parliamentary Under-Secretary of State for Wales (Mr. Peter Hain): I beg to move amendment No. 336, in page 54, line 16, at end insert

    "(4A) The Secretary of State may under subsection (1) make different provision for England and Wales (whether or not by means of separate codes of practice); and references in this section to `the code' accordingly apply to any such separate code of practice.".

May I briefly intrude into the Committee's deliberations? I hope that in being brief I shall not disappoint you, Mr. McWilliam, in keeping you going until the late hours.

The amendment allows the Secretaries of State for Education and Employment and for Wales to make different provision for England and Wales, which could be incorporated into a single code of practice on admissions in England and Wales or in separate codes for England and Wales.

There will be no difficulty once the assembly for Wales has been established and functions of the Secretary of State transferred to it, because clause 41 of the Government of Wales Bill expressly provides for such transferred functions to be exercisable separately by the assembly. However, as clause 75 stands, it would not be possible for separate provision to be made by the Secretary of State for Wales before the transfer of the function to the assembly. The amendment is therefore necessary to allow the Secretary of State for Wales to make separate provision before such transfer, if it appears appropriate.

Without the amendment, it will not be possible to make any provision that applies only in relation to Wales until after the assembly is established in May 1999. We do not expect the principles that apply in Wales to differ from those in England as the primary legislation is largely common, but the amendment would allow those principles to be applied specifically to Wales. We shall in due course need to determine whether a single code or separate codes are appropriate. Once the National Assembly for Wales has been established, it will be free to develop the code and to apply it in Wales as it sees fit.

The Bill provides for the draft code to be subject to Parliamentary approval. Associated clause 76 sets out the means for making and approving a code. Prior to devolution, those provisions will apply to any code issued under clause 75, whether the guidance contained applies specifically to Wales or covers England and Wales. After devolution, any code for Wales or amendments to an existing code for Wales will require the approval of the Welsh assembly under procedures that are set out in clause 43 of the Government of Wales Bill.
