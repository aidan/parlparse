Amendments Nos. 180, 182 and 181 make it easier to justify continuing hunting as an activity. For example, amendment No. 180 talks about damage to property. If grass has been chewed on one's land, that could be construed as damage to property and therefore as justification for stag hunting. I argue against such a provision.

Amendment No. 182 seeks to leave out the word ``immediate''. If a rogue fox had caused a farmer a problem four weeks earlier, the farmer would be justified in having the hunt on his land to try to catch the nearest fox, which he could then say was the culprit. So, immediacy is important in any legislation that tries to tackle a pest.

I cannot be convinced that each year, on or about 24 or 25 December farmers suddenly suffer stock losses that justify hunts on 26 December. Immediacy is important in dealing with a pest. No one would want to wait four weeks, during which the fox inflicted damage on the poultry night after night, until the local hunt took its turn to be on one's land.

Amendment No. 181 would insert the phrase

    ``or likely to be attacked''.

It is doubtful that an attack would, of necessity, be likely to happen. This is confirmed by the report on research into the ecological and economic impact of foxes in Britain undertaken by the university of Bristol entitled, ``Is the fox a pest?'' I drew attention to the report briefly on Second Reading, but I make no apologies for doing so again. Part of its conclusion states:

    ``Foxes do not warrant their reputation as major pests of agriculture. Nationally, losses of lambs, piglets and poultry to foxes are insignificant relative to other causes of mortality.''

The argument that foxes are the great pest in the countryside is false and it is used only as an excuse to justify hunting. If this group of amendments were accepted, those who hunt would try to justify their activity and would find it easy to do so. As the promoter of the Bill, I cannot allow that and so I must urge members of the Committee to vote against amendment No. 180.
