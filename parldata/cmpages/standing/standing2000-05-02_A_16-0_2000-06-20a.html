Clause 87

Housing benefit

Question proposed, That the clause stand part of the Bill.

The Minister for Local Government and Housing (Ms Hilary Armstrong): Last week, we began to discuss the implications of the changes from housing benefit to supportive housing grant. The clause facilitates the introduction of the supporting people policy in April 2003. As I have explained, clause 86 introduces a new grant that will be dedicated to funding low-intensity support services for vulnerable people in the community. There will no longer be a jumble of overlapping and confused funding streams. Clause 87 sets out what housing benefit will fund and what services the supporting people grant will fund.

We are aware of people's worries about change, particularly about adjustments to benefits. In the lead-up to the new grant, the transitional housing benefit scheme, which came into effect on 1 April this year, will enable us to establish the amount of housing benefit spent on support charges. It will help to inform the amount to be transferred to the new grant. It will also give us information on the different types of support that are eligible for housing benefit at present and the extent to which housing benefit is funding that support. That will enable us to make adjustments at the margins.

When the Government were faced with the change following a court ruling in July 1997, which had been rumbling along for some considerable time, we were aware of the vulnerability of many of the people to whom the grant and policy relate. They are some of the most vulnerable in our community and that is precisely why we decided that there should be a long period of transition, so that the new grant is introduced in a way and at a level that does not put them at risk. The clause provides the flexibility to enable housing benefit to continue to meet some support charges if, on balance, that is considered appropriate. For example, it could continue to cover service charges for help with cleaning but, more importantly, it provides the flexibility for how the supporting people policy will be implemented by allowing for phased implementation, should that be considered appropriate. Phased implementation is the subject of a consultation paper that has been distributed to local authorities and housing and support providers, whether in the voluntary, the public or the private sector.

For completeness, the calculation of housing costs in income support, income-based jobseeker's allowance and child support is based on the housing benefit service charge provisions. Any changes to the housing benefit rules under clause 87 will affect those provisions, too. Expenditure on support service charges in income support and income-based jobseeker's allowance will therefore similarly transfer to the supporting people grant from April 2003. We will continue to work closely with local authorities and service providers to develop an effective strategy to facilitate the smooth implementation of the policy of supporting people. I therefore propose that clause 87 stand part of the Bill.

We took more than 18 months simply to introduce the long transitional period of three years in which we will sort out the issues that the hon. Gentleman raised. I am sure that hon. Members support such a process of continually identifying, supporting and expecting best practice.

The various subsections provide the mechanisms to achieve the new clause's objectives.
