Clause 127

Gas licence conditions

The Minister for Competition and Consumer Affairs (Dr. Kim Howells): I beg to move amendment No. 795, in page 145, line 6, leave out `25' and insert

    `26'.

The Chairman: With this it will be convenient to take Government amendments Nos. 798 and 804.

Dr. Howells: These amendments, like others to clauses l27 and 128, are technical. Clause 127 will principally amend section 26 of the Gas Act 1986 and insert a new section 26A. Clause 128 will principally amend section 14 of the Electricity Act 1989 and insert a new section 14A. The purpose in each case is to provide a new power for the Competition Commission to veto licence modifications proposed by the regulator following a licence modification report by the commission that has found effects adverse to the public interest.

Following such a report, the authority must tell the Competition Commission what licence modifications it intends to make. The commission may in turn veto those changes within four weeks if it does not consider them requisite to remedy the adverse effects specified in its report, or to prevent them from occurring. It will do so by issuing a direction telling the authority not to make some or all of its proposed modifications. If it vetoes the authority's proposals, the commission will step in and make the licence modification that it considers requisite. Amendments Nos. 795, 798 and 804 will correct references to sections of the Gas Act 1986 and the Electricity Act 1989 respectively.

Mr. Nick Gibb (Bognor Regis and Littlehampton): The amendments are part of a set of amendments to clause 127. I should like the Minister to explain amendment No. 798, which will replace ``25'' in the first line of subsection (3) with ``26'', so that it will state:
