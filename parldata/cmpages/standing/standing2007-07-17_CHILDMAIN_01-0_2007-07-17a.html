Mr. Plaskitt: May I try to help in answering that? It is sensible to approach the regulations in different categories. First, much of the Bill is framework architecture for work that the Child Maintenance and Enforcement Commission will do subsequently. It is important to stress that we are moving from an agency model to a commission model. It is right, in many respects, to allow the commission to devise its own systems. Therefore, it is right and proper that the commission be established first and then for it to think how it wants to proceed in respect of various areas—some of those things will eventually emerge via regulation. Logically, the regulations will not come forward until the commission has done that work. Examples of operational regulations that need to await Royal Assent and the establishment of the commission are those that relate to fees, maintenance assessments, and the transfer of cases.
