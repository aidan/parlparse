The steps that we are taking to modernise local government form an interlocking package of reforms. The power to promote well-being is being conferred on principal councils, alongside the measures to enhance political leadership and accountability, and through best value to secure continuous improvement in service delivery. We do not consider it appropriate to confer such broad powers without those complementary measures, and yet neither parishes nor community councils are subject to the provisions in part II of the Bill and only a handful are subject to best value. Parishes have a different set of checks and balances, both financial and legislative, from principal authorities.
