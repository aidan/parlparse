The clause gives the Secretary of State the power to require RDAs to respond in various ways to demands from regional chambers. The first three of my amendments, Nos. 80, 81 and 82, would give local authorities, which are for the time being, and perhaps for a long time to come, the only elected bodies in those regions, a similar right to information from RDAs.

It is hard to believe that even this Minister wants to prevent regional development agencies from advising or assisting local authorities if asked to do so. I hope, therefore, that my amendments will be accepted.

The Parliamentary Under-Secretary of State for the Environment, Transport and the Regions (Angela Eagle): Clause 18 makes provision for regional scrutiny of RDA activities by chambers and gives the Secretary of State specific powers of direction to set a framework for RDA-chamber relationships. It also sets a framework for RDA relationships with the Secretary of State, which follows, as has been said many times during our proceedings, the structure of non-departmental public bodies in the first instance.

The amendments relate to the obligations of a regional development agency to give account of itself and to make information available. In particular, they relate to the position of local authorities. The amendments seek to put local authorities in the same position as the chambers and the Secretary of State. Local authorities are not the same as the chambers, nor are they the same as the Secretary of State. They have a specific role and place in the relationship with each other envisaged in clause 18. The amendments would confuse that relationship.

Amendments Nos. 80, 81 and 82 relate to clause 18, which is fundamental to establishing the special relationship between an RDA and the chamber. The amendments would require the RDA to deal with all the local authorities in its region on the same basis as the chamber, but chambers are not the same as local authorities in that context. The amendments would require them to be treated in the same way as regards the provision of information, answering of questions and giving an account of themselves as directed by the Secretary of State.

As I have made clear in relation to other amendments proposing similar requirements, we expect RDAs to provide local authorities with such information and to answer such questions as local authorities require. I hope that RDAs and local authorities will together work out arrangements that best suit their needs, without the necessity of putting those arrangements in the Bill in intimate detail, as amendments Nos. 80, 81 and 82 would require. If necessary--for example, if there were a breakdown, which we do not anticipate, in relations between local authorities and regional development agencies--powers under clause 31 enable Ministers to intervene.

I have made it clear that we envisage a special relationship between the chamber and RDAs which will not be the same as that between the RDA and local authorities. Within the framework of non-departmental public bodies we want to provide a measure of accountability at regional level. That is what has been missing until now. To do so, we need a body at regional level and that must be the chamber that represents a coming together of the relevant regional interests to express a regional view. Clearly, individual local authorities cannot express that view. We would expect them to express the views that are prevalent in their own local authority areas, but when they come together in the chamber--my hon. Friend the Minister said that it will have a 60 per cent. or 70 per cent. local authority membership--we would expect them to take a more regional and strategic view. Therefore, the regional view of local authorities will find its expression through the chamber. For those reasons I believe that amendments Nos. 80, 81 and 82 are misguided.

Amendment No. 78 relates to clause 16, which concerns the provision of information by the RDA to the Secretary of State. As I said in relation to an earlier amendment, clause 16 is an important link in the chain of accountability to Ministers and Parliament. The Secretary of State should have access to such advice and information as he thinks necessary to maintain that accountability.

The amendment says that the RDA should provide such information to local authorities as they require. We intend that local authorities should have the information necessary and appropriate to their involvement in the RDA's work. We will expect RDA's to adhere to the best principles of open government and to develop a constructive working relationship with the local authorities in their area. We hope that they--the RDA and local authorities--can work out for themselves how information should be provided. It is not helpful to give local authorities the upper hand in deciding what information should or should not be provided. Nor should we be too prescriptive about what should be provided or how the relationship between the RDA and the local authorities in its area should be conducted. We have the powers under clause 18 to intervene if necessary, but we hope that the local authorities and the RDA can sort out their relationships without interference from the Government.

Gentleman will take at least some comfort from my assurance that we expect RDAs to provide local authorities with a copy of the annual report.
