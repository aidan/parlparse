The Chairman: I remind the Committee that with this we are taking amendment No. 27, in page 2, line 31, at end insert--

    `(4A) The regulations shall provide that, for the purposes of determining a person's hourly rate of remuneration--

    (a) anything which is treated as remuneration for the purposes of income tax shall be treated as forming part of a person's remuneration;

Many dangers spring from that situation. The greatest danger is that which my hon. Friend the Member for Runnymede and Weybridge was kind enough to take from me in an intervention. If we allow a difference to emerge between the treatment of remuneration for tax and national minimum wage purposes, employers will subsequently manipulate pay.
