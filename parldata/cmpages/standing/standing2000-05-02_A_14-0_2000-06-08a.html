The Chairman: With this it will be convenient to take Government amendment No. 396.

Ms Armstrong: The amendments relate to the establishment and functions of standards committees and are intended to clarify the Bill's provisions. Amendment No. 395 clarifies the ability of the standards committee of a district council to delegate its functions in relation to parishes to a sub-committee, should the authority choose to appoint one. In all other circumstances, we have provided that the standards committee cannot delegate any of its functions. The amendment is necessary to allow a parish sub-committee that is set up by an authority to carry out its intended functions. Amendment No. 396 replicates the provision for Wales.

Mr. Tim Loughton (East Worthing and Shoreham): You are obviously in a hurry to get home, Mr. O'Brien, so I shall quickly interject.

The National Association of Local Councils is concerned about the role of parishes in the new arrangements. It wants standards committees for parish or town councils to be established at county rather than district level in order to minimise the risk of a conflict of interest. Irrespective of that, the NALC wants the Secretary of State to issue clear guidance that the sub-committees are to be separate from the principal authority's standards committee so that the separation of functions is clear.

The Bill provides that a district council's standards committee, which is responsible for monitoring standards in parish and town councils, or a sub-committee must include at least one member of any of the parish councils for which it is responsible. The NALC is calling for the membership of standards committees or sub-committees to include town and parish council representatives and independent members, who collectively will form a majority. However, given the Government's track record on that, I am not terribly hopeful.

There is also the smaller matter of allowances. The Bill enables the Secretary of State to authorise principal authorities, but not parish or town councils, to pay their members allowances in respect of such expenses or to arrange for the care of children or dependants—which my local authority has done— and other costs incurred in carrying out their duties as members. Understandably, NALC sees no good reason why parish or town councils should be excluded from those measures.

Ms Armstrong: We stay in close contact with NALC. Indeed, I am a former vice-president of the organisation. We discussed the matter in some detail when the House last debated local government legislation. The problem is the sheer number of parish councils. If the parish was represented on the standards committee at the county tier, and if the NALC agreed that at least one member of each parish or town council should be represented on the sub-committee, the resulting committees would be huge because many counties have dozens of parish councils. That is the problem.

We have discussed the matter with NALC, and it understands what we want to achieve. It wants regulations that safeguard its position on the standards committees of district councils. I assure the hon. Gentleman that we intend to introduce the regulations to provide for that. Indeed, we shall do whatever we can to safeguard the position of parish councils, but we must not make the whole thing unwieldy by including them at county level. It would be unworkable.

I hope that the hon. Gentleman will be satisfied with those assurances.

Question put and agreed to.
