Crown Prosecution Service Inspectorate Bill [Lords]

Standing Committee B

Tuesday 6 June 2000

[Mr. Bill Olner in the Chair]

Crown Prosecution Service Inspectorate Bill [Lords]

4.30 pm

The Solicitor-General (Mr. Ross Cranston): I beg to move,

    That during proceedings on the Crown Prosecution Service Inspectorate Bill [Lords], the Committee do meet on Tuesdays at half-past Ten o'clock and at half-past Four o'clock and on Thursdays at Nine o'clock.

I welcome you to the Chair, Mr. Olner—doubly so, because I think that we are the only two Members in the Committee who come from that vital part of the United Kingdom, the west midlands. I know you well and I know that the proceedings will move expeditiously and efficiently.

Mr. Edward Garnier (Harborough): On behalf of the official Opposition, I, too, welcome you to the Chair, Mr. Olner. I welcome the passage of the Bill, at least this far, and trust that during our deliberations—whether they are lengthy or otherwise—the cross-party fraternity which existed at earlier stages in this place and in another will continue to be evident.

Mr. John Burnett (Torridge and West Devon): I, too, welcome you, Mr. Olner. This important Bill deserves careful scrutiny and I am sure that you will be interested in our proceedings and guide us in your usual fluent way.

Question put and agreed to.

The Chairman: I remind the Committee that there is a financial resolution in connection with the Bill. Copies are available in the Room. I remind hon. Members also that adequate notice should be given of amendments. As a general rule, I do not intend to call starred amendments, including any that may be reached during an afternoon sitting.

Clauses 1 and 2 ordered to stand part of the Bill.

Clause 3

Short title, commencement and extent

Mr. Cranston: I beg to move the Government amendment No. 1, in page 2, line 6, leave out subsection (4).

This is a formal amendment. The Bill originated in the House of Lords and the amendment removes that subsection concerning privilege.

Amendment agreed to.

Clause 3, as amended, ordered to stand part of the Bill.

Question proposed, That the Chairman do report the Bill, as amended, to the House.

Mr. Cranston: I predicted that the Bill would be considered expeditiously and efficiently, and that has happened. I am sure that we are grateful for the way in which you have conducted the business, Mr. Olner.

The Chairman: I am grateful for the way in which members of the Committee have acted.

Question put and agreed to.

Bill, as amended, to be reported.

        Committee rose at twenty-six minutes to Five o'clock.
