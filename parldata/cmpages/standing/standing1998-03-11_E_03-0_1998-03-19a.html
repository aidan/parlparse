The Minister for Transport in London (Ms Glenda Jackson): On a point of order, Mr. Stevenson. I had to be in the Chamber before the Committee concluded at 1 pm yesterday, and I was unable to speak in support of the amendments. Amendments Nos. 28 and 39 have been jointly tabled by the hon. Member for Ceredigion (Mr. Dafis) and myself. Amendment No. 38 appears under my name. The amendments' purpose is to show that the Bill will not set United Kingdom-wide targets, but national targets for England, Scotland and Wales. That is a point that the hon. Gentleman made yesterday, with no small relish.

Amendment No. 28 would oblige my right hon. Friends the Secretary of State for the Environment, Transport and the Regions, the Secretary of State for Scotland, and the Secretary of State for Wales to publish targets for their respective territorial areas of responsibility within the United Kingdom.

The hon. Member for Ceredigion and myself have tabled new clause 9, which clarifies the position of Northern Ireland. The Government wish to see the Bill's provisions extended to Northern Ireland, but road traffic reduction there is a devolved matter.

Some worry has been expressed about the lack of a time limit for the Bill's extension to Northern Ireland. The procedures that will make that extension will be established by an Order in Council. As road traffic reduction is a devolved matter, it is regrettably not possible to give a time limit.

The negative procedure used is intended to shorten the time that would otherwise be taken under an affirmative resolution. The Northern Ireland Office is unaware of any instance of an Order in Council not being made following a negative resolution. That procedure was used for the Disabled Persons Act 1981, for the Housing Act 1996 and for the Housing Grants Construction and Regeneration Act 1996. An Order in Council is normally made within three to four months of a Bill's enactment. The Government have no intention of delaying that process.

On reading the Official Report of yesterday's Committee, I understand that the hon. Member for Worthing, West (Mr. Bottomley) asked how a United Kingdom target could be derived from the four national targets. As I mentioned in a letter to the hon. Member for Ceredigion, the Government want the four reports to be drawn together, and there will be a report for the United Kingdom as a whole. In some instances that would be a simple mathematical exercise. However, such a report would be subject to the different ways in which the exercise is approached in each territory, and would be subject to any devolution of powers.

I trust that what I have said this morning clarifies the matter.
