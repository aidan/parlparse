Mr. Forth: This is the occasion to proceed with our gentle probing about costs and related matters. We considered the operators' licensing regime; we touched briefly on the vehicle licensing regime; and we now turn to the third leg of the stool the drivers' licensing regime. We want as clear an idea as possible of the way in which the tripartite regime will operate. The Minister has been helpful so far in outlining her thoughts that matters will be dealt with initially under the aegis of the Metropolitan police and that the Public Carriage office will be involved. The Minister also hinted that she may hand responsibility to the new Greater London authority or assembly.

I set aside for the moment the exact details of the number of drivers and licensing authority staff and emphasise two key considerations. First, how long will it take for this regime to take effect? How long will the people of London have to wait before they can partake of the full reassurance of the Bill and know that operators, vehicles and drivers are properly licensed?

The Minister has been somewhat helpful on that first question. She alluded to the beginning of next year and she mentioned a consultation process, although we have not yet teased out how long it might take. In a different context, she said that completion, at least relating to operations, could occur within 12 months thereafter.

The key question is whether the parallel process for vehicles and drivers can also be undertaken in that period or whether it will take longer. The people of London would like to be assured that all is well, that the Bill is going to take effect and that there is sufficient clarity over objectives but they cannot be so assured yet. The "not yet" is the key issue for us to understand.

The second question is about enforcement. There seems to have been a difference of emphasis between myself and the Minister, who seems to believe that it will take care of itself, partly because of the licensing regime and partly because of the higher number of people involved in the scheme. We may have to agree to disagree because in my view the numbers that she mentioned cannot even begin to solve the problem of enforcement never mind the possible difficulty of the fee level being set so high by the resource requirements of a bureaucratic operation that many operators and drivers will be driven out of the legitimate licensed regime into illicit operation. In that eventuality, we will not have solved the problem that the Bill was intended to solve. We shall, no doubt, return to that issue later.

Now is the time for us to be given more information about the regime for the regulation and licensing of drivers, as opposed to operators and vehicles. I hope that the Committee will be given an appropriate amount of information at this stage of consideration of the Bill.
