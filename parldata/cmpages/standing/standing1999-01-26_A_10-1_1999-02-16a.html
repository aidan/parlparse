Clause 125

Duties of London borough councils etc.

Mr. Ottaway: I beg to move amendment No. 449, in page 65, line 31 at end insert

    "only insofar as is reasonably practicable, having regard to its functions other than transport and its policies relating to such functions".

The amendment relates to the duties of the London borough councils in respect of the transport strategy. When I served in the Royal Navy, there was a famous cartoon in which an admiral says to a junior admiral, "Do it.", the junior admiral turns round to a captain and says, "Do it." the captain turns round to a lieutenant commander and says, "Do it.", and it ripples all the way down to the end of the line, until the leading seaman turns to an able seaman and says, "Do it.", and the able seaman kicks the cat. Here the Secretary of State issues directions to the mayor, and the mayor issues directions down the line to the London borough councils. The whole thing ripples down, and the poor borough councils must carry the can and implement the strategy.

The amendment would ensure that boroughs had to have regard to the transport strategy
