Mr. Thompson: The amendments deal with district policing partnerships. Those partnerships will consist of elected councillors from the various district councils, and independent members. In a district partnership that has eight councillors, there will be seven independent members; in a partnership with nine councillors, there will be eight independent members, and so on. It is our view that the independent members should be appointed by the Policing Board, rather than by the council. The amendments seek to bring that about.

Amendment No. 170 would amend paragraph 4, removing the requirement of the board to appoint independent members nominated by the council. Instead, they would be appointed in accordance with new paragraph 5(l). Amendment No. 174 sets out the details of that new sub-paragraph, which would place a requirement on the board to appoint independent members. The board would seek applications from each district council area, and appoint from those applicants. The appointments should be representative of the council area in terms of religion, gender, age and cultural background, as far as was practical.

It would be wrong for the council to have full control of the appointment of all the members of the district policing partnership. After the nominations have been made from among the councillors, it should be up to the board to complete the membership by appointing nominees whom it has selected from applicants in the council area. Amendments Nos. 175 and 177 are consequential on amendment No. 174.

These are sensible amendments. They would remove any chance of a council giving the post of independent member to someone whom it might wish to be in the partnership, and would leave that task to the independent body--the Policing Board.
