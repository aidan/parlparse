Mr. Hanson: Amendments Nos.177,178 and 179 are technical amendments to alter the manner by which the general body of local government law as it relates to elected members is applied to the question of directly elected mayors. I must emphasise both to my hon. Friends and to Opposition Members that they represent no change in Government policy or intention. The Government see the mayor as having the same status as a councillor elected by the people, wherever meaningful or relevant. There must be exceptions—provided for in the amendments—to cover, for example, the provisions for election and term of office where specific provision for elected mayors is being made in the Bill.

On reflection, it appears to me and my colleagues that the drafting of subsection (3) will not work as we had initially hoped. As I have mentioned, our intention is for a directly elected mayor to be treated as a councillor for the purposes of local government legislation relating to councillors. However, because of the need to make exceptions to the principle and because of the lack of clarity concerning what would be classified as enactments relating to local government, the Bill's current approach will not work and cannot be said to be appropriate with acceptable certainty. We have concluded that the only way to proceed is to reverse the approach by providing that regulations will specify the legislative provisions that will apply to directly elected mayors. In that way, we can be precise about what does and does not apply to mayors. That is not a change of policy or intention but simply a drafting amendment to improve the intention.

Following on from our intention for an elected mayor to be treated like any other councillor, such mayors will have full membership of the council. It further follows that we cannot accept amendment No. 254, tabled by the hon. Member for Eastbourne (Mr. Waterson). I am happy to listen to his comments on that and other amendments in due course. Gentleman chose to press the amendment, I would be interested to hear what the difference was between a directly elected mayor and a current leader of a council.
