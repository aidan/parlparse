May I begin this sitting by warmly welcoming you, Mr. Cook, to the Chair of the Committee, which is considering this very important Bill? You are well known for your experience in such matters and for the firm but fair way in which you deal with Committee proceedings.

This technical amendment will improve the transparency of the text. It makes it clear from when interest will run, where the contract did not stipulate a price and the Sale of Goods Act 1979 or the Supply of Goods and Services Act 1982 provide a duty to pay a reasonable price. An example of such a contract would be where someone was called out to fix a boiler no price would be agreed before the service was rendered, but there would be a duty on the customer to pay a reasonable price.

The amendment provides that the default credit period should start to run from when the debtor has notice of the amount the creditor claims. That is so that, even if the debtor has good cause to dispute the amount, clause 5 on remission of statutory interest could be used by the debtor to remit any undeserved interest.

As I have said, this is a technical amendment. It is intended to aid both the good passage of the Bill and its use when, as I hope, it is enacted. I commend it to the Committee.
