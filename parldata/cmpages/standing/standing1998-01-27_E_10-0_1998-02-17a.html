The Minister for the Regions, Regeneration and Planning (Mr. Richard Caborn): I beg to move amendment No. 126, in page 10, line 23, leave out subsection (3) and insert—

    ‘(3) A person may only exercise a right under this section to enter any land if at least 28 days’ notice of the intended entry was given to every owner or occupier of the land.

The Chairman: With this, we may take Government amendments Nos. 127, 128 and 129.

Mr. Caborn: I am sure that all members of the Committee will join me in wishing many happy returns to my hon. Friend the Under-Secretary, whose birthday it is today. I am sure that the champagne will be flowing tonight.

Clause 21 relates to regional development agencies’ rights of entry on to land for the purposes of surveying it, or of estimating its value, in connection with the acquisition of land or the payment of compensation in respect of acquisition. The right to survey land also includes the power to search, bore and remove soil samples.

The provision in the Bill was based on the existing English Partnerships legislation. However, we believe that that can be improved. The earlier legislation did not contain provision for notifying the occupier of the reason for seeking entry. In the interests of greater oppenness—to which we are committed, as all members of the Committee will know—we propose in amendment No. 126 that the reason for entry be included in the notice. However, that would not protect the rights of owners as opposed to occupiers of land, so they too have been included in the requirement to notify the reason for entry. New subsection (3A) (b) requires the notification to explain to the owners and occupiers their rights under the clause, which include the right to compensation where land is damaged.

Subsection (5) as drafted requires that notification of the intention to search, bore and take samples of land be given only to the occupiers of land. Amendment No. 127 requires that notification to search, bore and take samples be given to both owners and occupiers in the case of all land. That will make the provision more equitable.

Amendment No. 128 is a technical improvement to the drafting of subsection (5), and amendment No. 129 adds a definition of “owner” to the clause, using the definition contained in the Acquisition of Land Act 1981. Essentially, the owner is considered to be the freeholder or the leaseholder if he has a minimum of three years left on his lease.

Amendment No. 126 will make the rights of entry fairer to owners of land and their purpose more open.
