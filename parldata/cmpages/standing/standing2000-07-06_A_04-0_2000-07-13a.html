Additional functions of local authorities in respect of certain children

Amendment proposed [11 July]: No. 9, in page 5, line 15, at end insert—

    `(7A)(a) The duties of a local authority under this section in respect of a former relevant child shall cease during any period when an effective election made by the former relevant child is in force.

Clause 3

Personal advisers and pathway plans

Mrs. Caroline Spelman (Meriden): I beg to move amendment No. 10, in page , line 34, leave out `children who are'.

Our amendment is simple and technical. It draws attention an issue of concern arising from the clause, which is about personal advisers and pathway plans. Proposed new section 23D(1)(a) of the Children Act 1989 uses the phrase

    children who are relevant children,

which is rather long-winded. It would be simpler to delete the first three words, so that it read ``relevant children''.

The amendment enables me to raise an issue about the meaning of the proposed new subsection. To be clear as to what the Government have in mind, one has to read the Bill in conjunction with the explanatory notes, which state on page 10:

    By subsection (1), regulations may be made to allow other individuals aged between 16 and 21, such as children who have received respite care, to have personal advisers in addition to those children and young people listed in paragraphs (a) to (c) whom this Bill entitles to a personal adviser.

Member for Lancaster and Wyre said in the Chamber last night, when we debating the Care Standards Bill, many children are in care only temporarily and they return to their natural family after that period.
