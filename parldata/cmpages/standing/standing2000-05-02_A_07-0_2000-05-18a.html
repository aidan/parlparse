Clause 15

Discharge of functions:

mayor and council manager executive

Ms Beverley Hughes: I beg to move amendment No. 128, in page 10, line 39, leave out `executive' and insert `council manager'.

The Chairman: With this it will be convenient to take Government amendments Nos. 129 to 135.

Ms Hughes: The amendments simply change the drafting of clause 15. They bring the way in which a council manager framework would work more into line with the policy stated in ``Local Leadership, Local Choice'' and other documents than was achieved by the original drafting. The purpose of that form of executive is a clear separation between policy development, which is for the mayor and the council, and implementation of policy, which is the responsibility of the council manger.

The council as a whole would appoint the council manager. He or she would be expected to be responsible for appointing all the staff, as well as being responsible for delivering the policy framework and the budget that has been agreed by the council. Amendment No. 130 makes it clear that he or she must have regard to the directly elected mayor's political steer. The mayor is the only other member of the executive, and his or her role is to give broad political guidance to the council manager. However, the council manager will be responsible for all the day-to-day decisions to implement that framework. The amendment would achieve that clear separation.

The mayor and the council manager are accountable for discharge of executive functions—the mayor is accountable through the ballot box, and through overview and scrutiny, while the council manager is accountable through overview and scrutiny and directly to the full council. The amendments would also ensure that the council manager could discharge functions himself or herself and delegate executive functions to another officer or, for example, to area committees, as in other forms of executive arrangements. In addition, he or she may choose functions to be discharged collectively by the executive—the mayor and the council manager. However, we would expect that to happen only in relation to the drawing up of the policy framework for submission to the full council, when, clearly, the mayor's political input would be necessary.

Such an arrangement operates internationally, and is a proven way of developing and implementing efficient and accountable delivery of services. The amendment would merely clarify the separation and the way in which the framework would work.
