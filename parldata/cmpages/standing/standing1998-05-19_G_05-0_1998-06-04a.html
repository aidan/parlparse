Competition Bill [Lords]

Clause 3

Excluded agreements

Amendment proposed [2 June]: No. 59, in page 2, line 42, at end insert--

    `(1A) The Chapter I prohibition does not apply--

    (a) to agreements constituting trade associations, or

    (b) to vertical agreements in being prior to the coming into force of this Act.'.--[Mr. Boswell]

The Chairman: I remind the Committee that with this we are taking the following amendment: No. 63, in schedule 1, page 43, line 46, at end insert--

    `Trade Associations

    5A. Agreements constituting trade associations which appear to the Director to represent 50 per cent. or more of a given sector shall be treated as if they were designated professional rules within the meaning of Schedule 4.'.

European Community competition law has existed for more than 35 years, and we are not aware of any successful challenge to an employment-relation agreement. A trade union, in representing employees in negotiation with an employer, is generally viewed for the purposes of EC competition law as not being an undertaking. Therefore, agreements between an employer and a trade union on employment conditions should not be caught under the chapter I prohibition, which applies to agreements between undertakings or associations of undertakings. Similarly, a union, in representing employees, should not be subject to the chapter II prohibition because it is not an undertaking.

Some unions, especially in the media sector, represent individuals who are technically self-employed for tax purposes. We do not believe that that would necessarily make such unions undertakings, or associations of undertakings, for the purposes of the Bill.

EC competition law looks to the substance of the matter, not its technical form. For example, a singer in the chorus of a theatre production might be self-employed for tax purposes, although he could not in substance be described as an independent economic player so as to constitute an undertaking. The proposed subsection (2A)(c) would provide some comfort for certain selective distribution arrangements, but it would be limited by the Bill's overall spirit.
