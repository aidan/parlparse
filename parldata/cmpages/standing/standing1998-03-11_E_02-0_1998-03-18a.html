One of the main elements of this group of amendments in my name is to flush out the promoter’s and the Government’s true response to the implications of the original idea that there should be a 10 per cent. reduction in 1990 road traffic levels by the year 2010.

In 1990, cars and taxis travelled 335.9 billion vehicle km, light goods vehicles travelled 35.7 billion km and heavy goods vehicles travelled 29.1 billion km. I shall not refer to public service vehicles because of the Committee’s majority decision during discussion of clause 1 not to include them in the debate.

Road traffic in 1990 totalled 405.3 billion vehicle km. To achieve 90 per cent. of that total would mean reducing the vehicle kilometres travelled by cars and taxis to 300 billion km, by light goods vehicles to 32 billion km and by heavy goods vehicles to 26 billion km. That would produce a total of 364.8 billion vehicle km.

In 1996, the most recent year for which figures are available, cars and taxis travelled 362.4 billion vehicle km, light goods vehicles 40.4 billion km and heavy goods vehicles 30.7 billion km. That is a total of 438.3 billion vehicle km. In their document “National Road Traffic Forecasts (Great Britain) 1997”, the Government forecast that by 2010 a total of 561 billion vehicle km would be travelled on British roads—460.25 billion km of which would be by cars and taxis, and 57.81 billion km by light goods vehicles.
