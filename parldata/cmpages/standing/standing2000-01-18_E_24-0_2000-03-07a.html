Mr. Hill: Under these amendments, we shall make technical drafting changes to clarify the terminology used for different stages of the development and implementation of charging and licensing schemes. A local authority will decide whether it wants to introduce a charging or a licensing scheme and will include its proposals in its local transport plan. If it decides to proceed, the scheme will be made by order, thus the word ``making'' refers to the process of putting in place the orders for schemes. That is catered for in amendments Nos. 607 and 638.

When the order is in place, the scheme will be established. The word ``established'' refers to the process of setting up the scheme, including the installation and operation of equipment. It is made clear in amendment No. 667 that the word ``operation'' refers to the running of the scheme. The remaining amendments in the group will make the same distinction for London, by amending schedule 12. The purpose of the amendments is to make it absolutely clear what each clause covers, and I am sure that the Committee will agree that they are necessary.

That is why a single statutory catch-all deadline is not the right way to achieve the desired end. Although existing legislation on public inquiries does not impose statutory time limits for reaching decisions, we have, as a matter of good practice, set administrative targets for dealing with cases.
