The Bill is important. It is also a large and complex measure. It has been referred to as the ``Transport 2000'' Bill, and I hasten to add that that refers not to the number of clauses but rather to the year in which we expect it to reach the statute book. It is a complex and important measure and it would honour a range of commitments given by the Government, which were mainly set out in our White Paper on integrated transport. Additionally, it will enable a public-private partnership to be established for the National Air Traffic Services.

The sittings motion provides for the Committee to meet for at least 10 hours a week, which should give more than adequate time to enable us to fulfil our proper task of scrutinising carefully, thoroughly and rigorously all aspects of the Bill. We shall want a thorough and serious debate and I hope that we shall have more than adequate opportunity to cover all the critical issues.

The Government's objectives are to ensure that we improve the country's transport system by integrating it and making it as safe, modern and efficient as the country deserves. The problems that we inherited are many and they cannot be put right overnight, but we have a clear vision of where we are going and of what a modern transport system should be. We have a strategy of how to get there and we are putting the delivery mechanisms, resources and, by virtue of the Bill, the legislative powers in place to make it happen. It will be one of the most important Bills to be passed by the House this year and I am honoured to be here, together with my two colleagues, to show the importance the Government attach to the Bill. Three Ministers are serving on the Committee and we are all here to ensure that this important legislation receives the consideration it requires and progresses towards the statute book.
