I turn to the overall intention of clause 3. I have explained in historical terms the fundamental review and how the Bill reflects the investigation and examination of the tripartite approach and, specifically, its staffing aspect.

Clause 3 sets out the administrative arrangements covering the civilian staff of the Police Authority. The main thrust of the clause centres on the delegation of management functions in respect of civilian staff to the Chief Constable. The code reflects the provisions of section 10, which covers England and Wales, and section 14, which covers Scotland, of the Police and Magistrates' Courts Act 1994 which provides for police authorities to be the legal employer of staff working in support of police responsibility for the direction and control conferred by the legislation on chief constables. The 1994 Act gives chief constables statutory responsibility for the utilisation of all human resources, both police officers and support staff, and thus greater flexibility and freedom to manage personnel. When the new Police Authority for England and Wales became effective in April 1995, the chief constables became responsible for all personnel management, including recruitment and dismissal.

Contrary to what the hon. Member for North-East Cambridgeshire (Mr. Moss) said, the information on which I am working is that the provisions are operating beneficially throughout England, Wales and Scotland, so we are keen to see the same principles applied in Northern Ireland.

I remind the Committee of how we are trying to deal with the matter. We have put forward an implementation strategy with which I am involved at steering group level and senior officials are involved at co-ordination level. At working group level and implementation level we hope to engage the Police Authority, representatives of the RUC and officials in the police division. That will enable us to examine the various areas of possible difficulty which may arise and to try to find ways of dealing with them. I explained earlier that we understand that there will be difficulties. The transfer from one section of employment to another must be handled carefully and sensitively. Other difficulties arise from the management structure and we want to make sure that those are handled fairly and to the best effect for those who will manage resources and for those who are to be managed.

We have tried to take account of some criticisms over practical application of the new arrangements in England and Wales. The changes have been to the overall benefit of police service delivery in England and Wales and in Scotland. We are trying to replicate that in the Bill.

I intend to table amendments on Report to this and other clauses. I made a commitment along those lines during our third sitting to the hon. Mr. Worthington: I was trying to be helpful. The clause as drafted replicates section 4 of the Police Act (Northern Ireland) 1970. We are introducing a technical amendment to give the Police Authority the right to
