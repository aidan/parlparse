The Minister for Competitiveness (Mr. Alan Johnson): I welcome you to the Chair, Mr. Butterfill. The Committee has wisdom and sagacity in great measure, given that you and Mr. Maxton are its co-Chairmen.

Clause 1 establishes the Postal Services Commission, which will be the independent regulator for postal services, and does so in a way suggested by the review of the water and gas utilities. Instead of personalising matters, perhaps unfairly, by placing the duties of the regulator on one individual, we shall introduce a group of people as a corporate body.

Subsection (2) provides that the functions of the commission should be performed on behalf of the Crown. The commission will be a non-ministerial Government Department. Subsection (3) gives effect to schedule 1, which makes further provisions in relation to the commission. Subsection (4) abolishes the existing Postal Services Commission, because we are setting up a commission that will operate from 1 April in an advisory role. The legislation enables us to put the Postal Services Commission on a statutory basis. There will be a seamless transfer from the current commission to the new commission that will be established under the Bill.
