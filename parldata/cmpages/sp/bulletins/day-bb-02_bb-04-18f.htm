Business Bulletin No. 61/2002

Thursday 18 April 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-3004# Marilyn Livingstone: Solvent Abuse-That the Parliament recognises the continued threat posed by solvent abuse; acknowledges the continued need to combat solvent abuse, and believes that the Scottish Executive should investigate and pilot new preventative measures such as the test purchasing scheme, aimed at reducing underage sales of potentially abusive products, which has been successfully applied throughout England and Wales, and actively increase awareness among young people of the devastating effects of solvent abuse.

*S1M-3003 Mr Gil Paterson: Live Motions-That the Parliament believes that, for the purpose of good time management and the ability to keep members fully informed in a cost effective manner, the Monday Business Bulletin should be restored to its former effective condition by containing all live motions with a full list of members who have supported each motion.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie*

*S1M-3002# Alasdair Morgan: Current Situation in the Middle East-That the Parliament expresses its deep concern about the situation in the Middle East; recognises the key role of the United Nations and the rule of international law, and affirms that the immediate cessation of violence, the recognition and protection of human rights and the need for negotiation are essential in order to secure just and long lasting peace in the region.

Supported by: Richard Lochhead*, Mr Kenny MacAskill*, Christine Grahame*, Mr Andrew Welsh*, Michael Matheson*, Michael Russell*

*S1M-3001 Fiona Hyslop: Middle East-That the Parliament condemns all acts of violence in the Middle East, whether they be acts of state violence from Israel or terrorist attacks from Palestine factions; calls for the immediate cessation of all acts of violence; supports the codification of the Saudi initiative as a UN Security Council resolution that would involve Israeli troops withdrawing to pre-1967 boundaries, acceptance of the legitimacy of the Israeli state by the Arab world and the establishment of a viable, self-governing Palestine state; recognises that the UK should freeze all military exports to Israel until it agrees to abide by UN Security Council resolutions; calls for wider economic sanctions to be imposed by the US and EU, preferably under the terms of a UN resolution, and further recognises that the Prime Minister should resolve his contradictory stance by accepting that a new UN Security Council resolution is necessary before embarking on any military action in Iraq.

Supported by: Christine Grahame*, Richard Lochhead*, Michael Matheson*, Michael Russell*

*S1M-3000 Mr Kenneth Gibson: Congratulations to Colin MacIver Campbell MSP-That the Parliament congratulates Colin MacIver Campbell MSP on the birth of Ruairidh Alasdair MacLean Campbell, 7lb 13 oz, his eighth grandchild and fifth grandson, born on 16 April 2002 at 19.45 at Paisley Maternity Hospital to his third son Laurie and daughter-in-law Allison, their second child and first son, and is pleased that mother and baby are doing fine.

Supported by: Michael Russell*

*S1M-2999 Richard Lochhead: European Centre for Fisheries Research and Management-That the Parliament supports the establishment of a European Centre for Fisheries Research and Management in Scotland; notes the prestige that this would bring to Scotland and particularly the fisheries scientific community, and calls upon the Scottish Executive to make representations to the European Union and to Her Majesty's Government in support of a bid for such a centre.

*S1M-2997# Mr Lloyd Quinan: Autistic Spectrum Disorders Needs Assessment Report-That the Parliament acknowledges the recent publication of the Autistic Spectrum Disorders Needs Assessment Report undertaken by the Public Health Institute for Scotland; notes the recommendations calling for greater co-operation in working practice between health, education and social services and the desperate need for training all professionals in diagnosis and appropriate aftercare and through- care packages; further notes the increase in diagnosis, and considers the Scottish Executive should implement these recommendations as soon as possible to ensure that Scotland is seen as a leading provider of care and services to those affected by autistic spectrum disorders.

*S1M-2996 Cathy Jamieson: Scottish Qualifications Authority Bill-That the Parliament agrees that the Scottish Qualifications Authority Bill be passed.

Supported by: Nicol Stephen*

S1M-2995 Carpet Manufacturing in Scotland (lodged on 16 April 2002) Brian Fitzpatrick*, Scott Barrie*, Mr John McAllion*, Angus MacKay*, Marilyn Livingstone*, Trish Godman*, Janis Hughes*

*S1M-2994.1 Irene McGugan: Social Care Workforce Development-As an amendment to motion (S1M-2994) in the name of Cathy Jamieson, leave out from second "the work" to end and insert "the steady decline over the last five years in applications to study social work; further notes that Edinburgh University is poised to scrap its social work degree; further notes that difficulties in recruiting social workers are expressed as a serious concern by the majority of local authorities in Scotland; regrets the absence of a recruitment campaign such as has been launched in other parts of the UK and deplores the lack of strategy which will address the poor public image of the profession, review the pay and conditions of social workers, increase flexibility in job opportunities to aid retention, develop positive initiatives to support front line staff or encourage secondment, and concludes that the Executive is merely posturing on this issue and neither acknowledging the crisis nor addressing the critical issues with real commitment.

Supported by: Michael Russell*

*S1M-2994.2 Alex Johnstone: Social Care Workforce Development-As an amendment to motion (S1M-2994) in the name of Cathy Jamieson, leave out from second "the work" to end and insert "with concern their increasing difficulty in carrying out these tasks owing to poor levels of staffing, increased bureaucracy and additional demands on what was already an over-stretched workforce, and therefore calls upon the Scottish Executive to redouble its efforts to secure an appropriately staffed and trained social care workforce which is capable of carrying out its necessary functions.

Supported by: Ben Wallace*

*S1M-2993.1 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: Prison Estates Review-As an amendment to motion (S1M-2993) in the name of Mr Jim Wallace, insert at end ", however, calls on the Scottish Executive to instigate further investigation into the costings for a new prison at Peterhead, specifically for accommodation for 500 to 700 prisoners, in order to ensure that the best possible outcome is achieved to maintain the excellence that has been delivered to date by HM Prison Peterhead."

Supported by: Bill Aitken*

*S1M-2993.2 Roseanna Cunningham: Prison Estates Review-As an amendment to motion (S1M-2993) in the name of Mr Jim Wallace, leave out from "welcomes" to end and insert "notes the publication of the consultation paper Proposals for the Future of the Scottish Prison Service Estate; believes that to help achieve a safer Scotland the prison estate should facilitate the secure holding of prisoners and the delivery of effective rehabilitation programmes, and that prisons should provide a reasonable standard of accommodation; recognises that these objectives can only be achieved by substantial modernisation of the present prison estate; further notes that private prisons have serious deficiencies, in their management of both inmates and staff, and asserts that, in the interest of public safety, such a key component of the criminal justice system should remain in the public sector."
