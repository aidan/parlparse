Business Bulletin No. 37/2003

Tuesday 25 February 2003

Section E : Written questions lodged on 24 February 2003

  

Questions marked with a triangle () are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S1W-34056 Mr Duncan Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive what its definition is of "value for public money" in respect of the Glasgow to Barra air link, as referred to by the Deputy Minister for Enterprise and Lifelong Learning in the debate on the Glasgow to Barra air link on 15 January 2003 (Official Report, c 17011).

S1W-34243 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether police officers policing the Edinburgh area receive any additional payments.

S1W-34244 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive how many police officers assigned to East Lothian and Midlothian division of Lothian and Borders Police have been deployed in the City of Edinburgh division area in each year since 1999.

S1W-34245 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive how many local authorities have been granted anti-social behaviour orders.

S1W-34246 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive which local authorities have applied to the Better Neighbourhood Services Fund and which such authorities were successful in respect of what projects.

S1W-34247 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive how many local authorities have applied to the Better Neighbourhhod Services Fund for funding for schemes to combat anti-social behaviour.

S1W-34248 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether spending on sports is monitored in line with equalities policies.

S1W-34249 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive how much (a) higher education institutions, (b) further education institutions, (c) secondary schools and (d) primary schools spend on sports-related activities, broken down by gender in each of the last three years.

S1W-34250 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any current plans to host major sporting events include women-only events and events for both men and women

S1W-34251 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive who has responsibility for the provision of sports facilities for people with disabilities.

S1W-34252 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive how much has been spent on sports facilities and activities for students (a) with and (b) without disabilities in higher and further education institutions in each of the last three years.

S1W-34253 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) households and (b) pensioners’ homes in the Midlothian Council area are estimated to be eligible for free central heating under its central heating installation programme.

S1W-34254 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) households and (b) pensioners’ homes in each postcode area that covers the Midlothian Council area (i) benefited in 2001-02 and (ii) are expected to benefit in (1) 2002-03, (2) 2003-04 and (3) 2004-05 under its central heating installation programme.

S1W-34255 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive how many pensioners or households in the postcode areas that cover the Midlothian Council area have benefited from the (a) Warm Deal Scheme and (b) central heating installation programme in each of the last five years and how many are expected to benefit in future years.

S1W-34256 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive how many community safety partnerships there are in the Midlothian area and when each such partnership was established.

S1W-34257 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive how the £4 billion additional spending on public services up to 2005-06 as a result of the UK spending review has been, or will be, allocated and how much has been, or will be, allocated to the Midlothian Council area.

S1W-34258 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive how many of the 350 NHS one-stop clinics are located in the Midlothian Council area; where such clinics are located, and what services they offer.

S1W-34259 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive how many families in the Midlothian Council area have benefited from Sure Start Scotland; in what ways they have benefited, and how much of that organisation’s £42 million funding was allocated to projects in the area.

S1W-34260 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive how the (a) £175 million for 79 projects under the Scottish Public Transport Fund and (b) £20 million to local authorities for road and bridge repairs will be allocated and how much has been, or will be, allocated to the Midlothian Council area.

S1W-34261 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive how much of the £250 million for free personal and nursing care was allocated to Midlothian Council area.

S1W-34262 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what action has been taken to increase the proportion of students in higher education that are from under-represented and disadvantaged groups and areas in the Midlothian Council area in each year since 1997.

S1W-34263 Mr Murray Tosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to change its policy on whole-stock local authority housing transfers and allow local authorities pursuing partial stock transfers to bid for support to deal with overhanging debt in line with the policy recently announced for England by Her Majesty’s Government.

S1W-34264 Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will ensure that children and young people with special needs benefit from the investment in school sport announced as part of the Scottish budget for 2003-06.

S1W-34265 John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what will be funded by the (a) £6.0, (b) £8.3 and (c) £6.8 million for related waste initiatives announced in its spending proposals for 2003-06, with particular regard to its Do a Little, Change a Lot campaign.

S1W-34266 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1O-5727 by Iain Gray on 10 October 2002, what action it is taking to retain quality, high-skill call centre enterprises, in the light of any growing pressure within such enterprises to outsource jobs abroad.

S1W-34267 Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it has made towards achieving its target that no-one need sleep rough.

S1W-34268 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will urge Her Majesty’s Government to arrange to meet members of the Parliament in order to discuss the situation of members of the Scottish Bus Group pension schemes and, in particular, the issue of whether Scottish Bus Group Pensioners should have to pay tax on their ex-gratia payments, given that members of the National Bus Company pension scheme did not.
