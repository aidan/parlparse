Business Bulletin No. 105/2002

Tuesday 25 June 2002

Section E : Written questions lodged on 24 June 2002
  

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S1W-27004 Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what powers it has to introduce legislation affecting any aspect of employee transfers under the Transfer of Undertakings (Protection of Employment) Regulations or the provision and application of the Statement of Practice covering these, and under what section of the Scotland Act 1998 any such powers are conferred.

S1W-27005 Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the level of funding under the Young Student Bursary Scheme is lower for Scottish-domiciled students studying outwith Scotland than for those studying in Scotland and, if so, by how much and why there is any such difference.

S1W-27006 Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any companies who have provided training under the Individual Learning Account scheme are still awaiting payment and, if so, how many companies, what total amount is still to be paid and when these final payments will be made.

S1W-27007 Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last made direct contact with the Individual Learning Account centre in Durham.

S1W-27008 Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether spending on the Individual Learning Account programme ever exceeded its allocated budget in 2000-01 and, if so, by how much.

S1W-27009 Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the cost so far has been of the investigation into fraudulent activity in connection with the Individual Learning Account scheme; how many cases have been checked, and how much money has so far been identified as having been received fraudulently.

S1W-27010 David McLetchie: To ask the Scottish Executive which of the top 40 categories of offence referred to children’s reporters it classifies as serious.

S1W-27011 David McLetchie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the actual number and nature was of the serious crimes referred to by the Deputy First Minister and Minister for Justice in his evidence to the Justice 2 Committee on 18 June 2002 as having been referred to the children’s hearing system last year.

S1W-27012 Mr Keith Raffan: To ask the Scottish Executive what practical facilities it provides to organisations in the Mid Scotland and Fife parliamentary region which (a) support male victims of domestic abuse and (b) assist fathers experiencing problems with hostile ex-partners in gaining appropriate contact with their children, broken down by organisation and parliamentary constituency.

S1W-27014 Bill Aitken: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to ensure that the Glasgow Housing Association devolves the control of the houses being transferred from Glasgow City Council to smaller local housing associations.

S1W-27015Δ Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive what its plans are for tackling deprivation in deprived communities.

S1W-27016 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to increase funding for the procurator fiscal service to meet the demands being placed on the service.

S1W-27017 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the National Galleries of Scotland in regard to the present dispute over staff salaries.

S1W-27018 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact a strike by staff of the National Galleries of Scotland will have on tourism.

S1W-27019 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the terms and conditions of employment for staff of the National Galleries of Scotland adequately reflect their roles and responsibilities.

S1W-27020 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Scottish Committee of the Council of Tribunals has any powers to investigate allegations of fraud in respect of tribunals and, in particular, employment tribunals and whether it will detail such powers.

S1W-27021 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what assistance it has given to broadband development.

S1W-27022 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what analysis it has undertaken of the benefits of broadband for business.

S1W-27023 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has identified target timescales for broadband development in partnership with Scottish Enterprise and whether any such timescales are being met.

S1W-27024 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on the dispersal of civil service jobs.

S1W-27025 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the proposed timetable for relocating the Common Services Agency is still on target.

S1W-27026Δ Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive what the outcome is of its consideration of the bids for financial support for school Public Private Partnership projects which were submitted by local authorities in December 2001.
