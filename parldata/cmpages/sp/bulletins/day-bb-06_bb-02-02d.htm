Business Bulletin No.19/2006: Thursday 2 February 2006
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 9 February 2006

Question Time

General

1. Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the place of history in the school curriculum. (S2O-8937)

2. Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is aware of the importance of play opportunities for improving child mental health and tackling obesity and anti-social behaviour in young people. (S2O-8952)

3. David McLetchie: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions have taken place at a ministerial or official level with representatives of the City of Edinburgh Council in respect of an alternative strategy for social housing, in light of the "no" vote in the housing stock transfer ballot of tenants in December 2005. (S2O-8929)

4. Not Lodged

5. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to tackle fuel poverty among low-income households with dependent children. (S2O-8964)

6. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to encourage greater devolution of budgets and management to schools. (S2O-9003)

7. Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with Ayrshire and Arran NHS Board in respect of the board’s proposal to close the specialist accident and emergency unit at Ayr Hospital. (S2O-8926)

8. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive how much of the £89 million budget in 2005-08 for flood prevention and coastal protection has been allocated and to what projects. (S2O-8927)

9. Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is improving sporting achievement. (S2O-8969)

10. Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive what the up-to-date position is in respect of devolved school management. (S2O-8991)

Enterprise, Lifelong Learning and Transport

1. Not Lodged

2. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps its Enterprise and Lifelong Learning Department is taking to ensure that HGV transport managers and drivers are aware that "no smoking" signs must be displayed inside and outside their vehicles when using Scottish roads from 26 March 2006. (S2O-8924)

3. Jim Mather: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of the recent closures of IT manufacturing plants, what steps are being taken to encourage businesses to generate well-paid jobs and how such steps compare with those being taken in England, Ireland and Norway. (S2O-8996)

4. Bristow Muldoon: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with Network Rail in respect of the dual tracking of the single track section of the Edinburgh to Bathgate rail line. (S2O-8958)

5. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Minister for Transport and Telecommunications last met representatives of First Bus and what issues were discussed. (S2O-8950)

6. Mr Charlie Gordon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider introducing a requirement on all transport operators participating in its free bus scheme to use, within a reasonable period of time, vehicles which are fully accessible by passengers whose mobility is impaired. (S2O-8943)

7. Rosie Kane: To ask the Scottish Executive whether, in light of the report on the environmental impact of the Dalkeith Northern Bypass by WA Fairhurst & Partners, it will cease work on the project. (S2O-8993)

8. Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers safety on trunk roads in Dumfries and Galloway to be adequate. (S2O-8960)

9. Tricia Marwick: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent discussions it has had with relevant stakeholders in respect of increasing international ferry links with Rosyth. (S2O-8998)

10. Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide a progress report on the new bridge crossing the Forth at Kincardine and, in particular, when work on the bridge will commence. (S2O-8962)

Justice and Law Officers

1. Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that the evidence presented to it by Amnesty International in respect of the use of Scottish airports by flights linked to acts of extraordinary rendition is sufficiently specific and credible to justify police investigation and what the reason is for its position on this matter. (S2O-9016)

2. Cathie Craigie: To ask the Scottish Executive what powers police and local authorities have to deal with noise nuisance in residential areas. (S2O-8938)

3. Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Vulnerable Witnesses (Scotland) Act 2004 has had a positive impact in respect of prosecutions. (S2O-8954)

4. Mr Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has held with the Chief Constable of Strathclyde Police in respect of rural policing in the Strathclyde Police area. (S2O-8994)

5. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any concerns about the jury selection process. (S2O-8948)

6. Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Minister for Justice last met the General Secretary of the Scottish Police Federation. (S2O-8968)

7. Euan Robson: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will ensure that there are effective reforms in respect of monitoring sex offenders. (S2O-9009)

8. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is aware of the recent figures on police assault, as reported in The Scotsman on 26 January 2006. (S2O-8949)

9. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to address disorder in or around marches and parades. (S2O-8955)

10. Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance its Justice Department issues to public bodies, including local authorities, in respect of their obligations under the European Convention on Human Rights. (S2O-8997)
