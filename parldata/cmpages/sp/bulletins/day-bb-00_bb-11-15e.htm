BUSINESS BULLETIN 174 / 2000

Wednesday 15 November 2000

Section E - Written questions lodged on 14 November 2000

 

 

S1W-11194 Nick Johnston: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has made to Tayside University NHS Trust regarding the retention of maternity services at Perth Royal Infirmary.

S1W-11195 Mr Alex Salmond: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will ensure that there is adequate provision for new NHS patients to register with dentists in Banff and Buchan.

S1W-11196 Mr Alex Salmond: To ask the Scottish Executive how many NHS dentists there are in Banff and Buchan.

S1W-11197 Mr Alex Salmond: To ask the Scottish Executive how many patients are on a waiting list to register with an NHS dentist in Banff and Buchan.

S1W-11198 Mr Alex Salmond: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average waiting time is in Banff and Buchan to register with an NHS dentist.

S1W-11199 Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to promote a Scottish airport as an international hub airport.

S1W-11200 Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has held with the Scotland Office about the availability of funding to support the Euro ferry proposed for Rosyth and what the outcome was of any such discussions.

S1W-11201 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive what audit criteria will be applied in the forthcoming national audit of museums and, in particular, how will these be applied to industrial museums.

S1W-11202 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive what priority will be given to dealing with industrial museums in the forthcoming annual audit of museums and when it anticipates the audit of the main industrial museums will be completed.

S1W-11203 Nick Johnston: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to maximise the number of visitors to Scotland by emphasising the quality, availability and diversity of the field sports industry.

S1W-11204 Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive when public consultation on the proposed amendments to the Waste Management Licensing Regulations 1994 will commence.

S1W-11205 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to make a site available for the testing and demonstration of wave energy production and engineering and what funding is available for the construction of such a site.

S1W-11206 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether present access to the National Grid power supply in the north and islands of Scotland is adequate and, if not, what plans it has to improve such access.

S1W-11207 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding will be made available for wave energy research on an annual basis for the next five years.

S1W-11208 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding is or will be available to improve the infrastructure at ports and harbours.

S1W-11209 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what the anticipated number of cruise ships visiting Leith was expected to be on completion of Ocean Terminal Complex at Leith at the time planning permission was given.

S1W-11211 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is aware of any research in Scotland with regard to the administering of drugs by concealing them in food or drinks in residential and nursing homes.

S1W-11212 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact the higher business rate poundage in Scotland as compared to England has had on new business formation, growth and survival.

S1W-11213 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to reduce the level of unemployment in Scotland to UK levels or below.

S1W-11214 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to address any shortfall in teachers of English.

S1W-11215 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure there is no shortage of supply teachers in any core teaching subject.

S1W-11216 Mr Keith Harding: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will detail the cost of its Social Justice Annual Report 2000 including its publication, launch and distribution costs.

S1W-11217 Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Implementation Support Group on junior doctors' hours and working conditions last met and what its current schedule of work includes.

S1W-11218 Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Implementation Support Group (ISG) on junior doctors' hours and working conditions will make its next report and whether this report will include an update on the non-compliance rates reported to the ISG by NHS Trusts.

S1W-11219 Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many bonuses have been paid to and fines imposed on NHS Trusts for compliance with the targets and standards of the New Deal for Junior Doctors since 31 March 2000.

S1W-11220 Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to ensure that NHS Trusts have sufficient funds to enable them to comply with the targets and standards of the New Deal for Junior Doctors.

S1W-11221 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive why Scotland accounts for only 3.9% of UK service sector exports, as reported in the Scottish Council for Development and Industry's May/June 2000 Indicator, and what steps it is taking to address this situation.

S1W-11222 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive why the target set in Scottish Enterprise's Business Birth Rate Strategy in 1993 for Scotland to at least equal the UK average in the number of new businesses created each year per head of population by the end of the 1990s has not been achieved and what steps it will take to address this situation.

S1W-11223 Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what increases in neurology consultant posts have been agreed in the last twelve months and what future plans exist.

S1W-11224 Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-2687 by Susan Deacon on 25 January 2000, what recent steps have been taken to improve the diagnosis and management of patients with neurological illnesses.

S1W-11225 Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made of the services available to patients with neurological illnesses.

S1W-11226 Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive how many neurology beds are currently available and how many were available for each of the last 10 years, broken down by health board.

S1W-11227 Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what the current waiting times are for first appointments with neurology consultants and what the equivalent figures were for each of the last ten years, broken down by health board.

S1W-11228 Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent representations it has received concerning waiting times for first appointments with neurology consultants.
