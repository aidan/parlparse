Business Bulletin No. 74/2011: Wednesday 15 June 2011

Section E – Written questions lodged on 14 June 2011

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S4W-00766 Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive in how many arrests for domestic abuse related charges in Strathclyde in each of the last five years both partners were arrested and what the outcomes were.

S4W-00767 Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the outcomes were for women in Strathclyde arrested on domestic abuse related charges in each of the last five years.

S4W-00768 Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the outcomes were for men in Strathclyde arrested on domestic abuse related charges in each of the last five years.

S4W-00769 Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive in how many cases of contact and residency disputes where domestic abuse was alleged in each of the last five years contact or residency was awarded to the alleged abuser and what the gender of each party was.

S4W-00770 Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive in how many cases of contact and residency disputes where child abuse was alleged in each of the last five years contact or residency was awarded to the alleged abuser and what the gender of each party was.

S4W-00771 Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive in how many cases of child contact and residency disputes children’s views were (a) sought and (b) implemented in each of the last five years.

S4W-00772 Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive how many and what planning consents have been issued for (a) horticultural or (b) other commercial peat extraction in the last five years.

S4W-00773 Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive how many and what (a) horticultural and (b) other commercial peat extraction sites are operational.

S4W-00774 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what the budget was for the (a) Energy Assistance Package, (b) Home Insulation Scheme, (c) Universal Home Insulation Scheme in each of the last four years.

S4W-00775 Richard Baker: to ask the Scottish Executive what the expenditure was for the (a) Energy Assistance Package, (b) Home Insulation Scheme, (c) Universal Home Insulation Scheme in each of the last four years.

S4W-00776 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what the expenditure on the (a) Energy Assistance Package, (b) Home Insulation Scheme and (c) Universal Home Insulation Scheme is in the 2011-12.

S4W-00777 John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to introduce the reforms proposed in the Report of Scottish Civil Courts Review and what the timescale is.

S4W-00778 John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to review the administration of criminal cases and the impact that this has on the justice system.

S4W-00779 John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to evaluate the legislation passed by the Parliament since 1999.

S4W-00780 John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to review the costs and funding of civil litigation.

S4W-00781 John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-36912 by Fergus Ewing on 28 October 2010, what progress it has made on the development of an international arbitration centre.

S4W-00782 John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to carry out an audit of the compatibility of Scots criminal law with the European Convention on Human Rights (ECHR).

S4W-00783 John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to expand the witness service to all Justice of the Peace courts.

S4W-00784 John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to allow the views of victims to be taken into account when (a) community sentences and (b) parole board decisions are being considered.

S4W-00785 John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to enforce the payment of compensation to victims of crime.

S4W-00786 John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to carry out a review of criminal injuries compensation.

S4W-00787 John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has for investment in the prison estate.

S4W-00788 John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has completed site acquisition for HMP (a) Inverclyde and (b) Highland.

S4W-00789 John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to review the implementation of The Road to Recovery: A New Approach to Tackling Scotland’s Drug Problem.

S4W-00790 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions regarding consumer energy bills it has had since 5 May 2011 with (a) Ofgem and (b) power companies with customers in Scotland.

S4W-00791 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive from what date the target to generate 100% of Scotland’s electricity demand from renewable resources by 2020 will be a material consideration in the determination of development proposals.

S4W-00792 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration it gave to the potential impact on (a) local communities and (b) the environment when deciding to raise the renewable electricity target to 100% of Scotland’s electricity demand being generated from renewable resources by 2020.

S4W-00793 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it has taken to ensure that its decision to raise the renewable electricity target to 100% of Scotland’s electricity demand being generated from renewable resources by 2020 meets the requirements of the (a) Environmental Assessment (Scotland) Act 2005 and (b) Conservation (Natural Habitats, &c.) Regulations 1994.

S4W-00794 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to ensure that renewable energy developments do not harm the natural environment.

S4W-00795 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what the projected cost is for (a) developers and (b) taxpayers through (i) government subsidies and (ii) energy bills for Scotland to have 100% of its electricity demand generated from renewable resources by 2020.

S4W-00796 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive how much public funding has been provided for each onshore wind development in the last five years.

S4W-00797 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S4W-00306 by Roseanna Cunningham on 10 June 2011, when it expects to make a statement regarding how it will proceed with legislation in relation to high hedges.

S4W-00798 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to protect less well-off families experiencing higher than average increases in their day-to-day living costs.

S4W-00799 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it takes to promote and develop credit unions.

S4W-00800 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it ensures financial inclusion for less well-off families.

S4W-00801 Malcolm Chisholm: To ask the Scottish Executive how it defines "so far as reasonably practicable" in section 88(1) of the Housing (Scotland) Act 2001.

S4W-00802 Malcolm Chisholm: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will carry out a review of the governance of further education colleges.

S4W-00803 Malcolm Chisholm: To ask the Scottish Executive what procedures are required for the appointment of board members of further education colleges.

S4W-00804 Malcolm Chisholm: To ask the Scottish Executive what powers it has to overturn decisions made by the principal or board of a further education college.

S4W-00805 Malcolm Chisholm: To ask the Scottish Executive how it can influence decisions made by the principal or board of a further education college.

S4W-00806 Malcolm Chisholm: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will raise with the Principal of Telford College the concerns expressed in letters to the Cabinet Secretary for Education and Lifelong Learning by (a) staff at the college and (b) the constituency MSP.

S4W-00807 Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive what the (a) average and (b) fatal accident rate is for the (i) A75 and (ii) trunk road network.

S4W-00808 Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive what the (a) average and (b) fatal accident rate was for the A75 in (i) 2007, (ii) 2008, (iii) 2009 and (iv) 2010.

S4W-00809 Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive what the (a) average and (b) fatal accident rate was for the section of the A75 affected by the planned Hardgrove to Kinmount development in (i) 2007, (ii) 2008 (iii) 2009 and (iv) 2010.

S4W-00810 Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-39155 by Keith Brown on 11 February 2011, when work will commence on the (a) Hardgrove to Kinmount and (b) Dunragit Bypass A75 improvements.

S4W-00811 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the BMA’s view that the Scottish Government’s workforce planning projections reveal "stark anticipated medical oversupply in Scotland".

S4W-00812 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to reduce the number of undergraduate places for medicine to match anticipated workforce demand from the NHS.

S4W-00813 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many undergraduate places for medicine were and are available in (a) 2010-11 and (b) 2011-12.

S4W-00816 Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive what its policy is concerning peat extraction for (a) horticultural and (b) other commercial uses.

S4W-00817 Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will revise its policy on the extraction of peat for (a) horticultural and (b) other commercial uses in the light of the UK Government’s recent commitment to reduce the use of peat in horticulture to zero by 2030.

S4W-00819 Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to protect and restore peatlands and how its policies regarding the extraction of peat for (a) horticultural, (b) other commercial and (c) domestic use contribute to this.
