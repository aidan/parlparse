BUSINESS BULLETIN 15 / 2001

Thursday 25 January 2001

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 1 February 2001

 

 

Question Time

1. Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are being taken to encourage local authorities to provide free fruit to pre-school and primary school pupils, in line with the initiative recently introduced to a number of schools by Glasgow City Council. (S1O-2865)

2. Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to reduce violence in schools. (S1O-2893)

3. Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it plans to take following the personal letter issued by East of Scotland Water Authority to every employee regarding reductions in the number of staff over the next five years and what staff reductions there will be over the next three years and five years. (S1O-2869)

4. Mr Keith Raffan: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making in encouraging local authorities to provide three-year funding packages for drug misuse organisations and projects and in promoting the availability of Social Inclusion Partnership money for such purposes where it is available. (S1O-2874)

5. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Ministerial Group on Sustainable Scotland was last convened and what was discussed. (S1O-2895)

6. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will ensure that there are adequate systems in place so that there is an effective response to any emergencies that arise at new wastewater treatment plants once they are operational. (S1O-2886)

7. Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are in place to prevent trespassing and vandalism on rail embankments and lines. (S1O-2900)

8. Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive when ministers last met the Chief Executive of the Scottish Prison Service. (S1O-2868)

9. Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make public immediately the contents of the recent Her Majesty's Inspectorate of Schools report on Abercorn Primary School in West Lothian. (S1O-2880)

10. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to reconsider its decision that the inquiries into the Chhokar case be held in private. (S1O-2902)

11. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has in place for the maintenance of trunk roads from April 2001. (S1O-2876)

12. Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive what average class size in primary schools will be achieved by 2003. (S1O-2871)

13. Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria are considered when establishing people’s juries. (S1O-2890)

14. Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of the Rough Sleepers Initiative budget for 2000 has been spent rather than allocated. (S1O-2879)

15. Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the level of availability of the BCG vaccination for schoolchildren is sufficient. (S1O-2864)

16. Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there is a date after which outstanding appeals to the Scottish Qualifications Authority with reference to the 2000 diet of examinations will no longer be dealt with. (S1O-2867)

17. Fiona McLeod: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is considering establishing a national youth strategy. (S1O-2872)

18. Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to protect those who might be targeted by animal rights groups. (S1O-2873)

19. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what provision is made for smaller areas of deprivation which fall outwith Social Inclusion Partnership status. (S1O-2882)

20. Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met the Secretary of State for Trade and Industry and what matters were discussed. (S1O-2896)

21. Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive how learndirectscotland will contribute to the achievement of social justice. (S1O-2877)

22. Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Water Industry Commissioner issues any guidance or makes any recommendations to the water authorities on appropriate skill levels and knowledge of staff such as chartered engineers. (S1O-2899)

23. Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive what it is doing to ensure that discipline in schools is improved. (S1O-2878)

24. Mr Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive what the funding arrangements are for Veterinary Investigative Centres. (S1O-2894)

25. Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to mark appropriately, possibly through Historic Scotland, the historic site of the Battle of Bannockburn in the light of the findings of Dr Fiona Watson of Stirling University. (S1O-2884)

26. Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive why the financial deficit of Tayside University Hospitals NHS Trust is projected to increase to £10 million in the current financial year. (S1O-2870)

27. Tricia Marwick: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last made representations to the Department of Social Security in connection with compensation for ex-miners in Scotland. (S1O-2897)

28. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive what arrangements are in place to notify MSPs and all other interested and affected parties of the registration process for accessing surplus funds under the Scottish Transport Group Pension Scheme. (S1O-2888)

29. Mr John Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to use the estimated annual £15 million saving through the trunk road maintenance contracts to negotiate with the Bank of America for an early return of the Skye Bridge to public ownership. (S1O-2889)

30. Mr Alex Salmond: To ask the Scottish Executive how many pupils are still awaiting the results of examinations from the Scottish Qualifications Authority’s 1999-2000 exam programme. (S1O-2863)
