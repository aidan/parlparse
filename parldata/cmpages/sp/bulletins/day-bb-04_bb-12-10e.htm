Business Bulletin No. 183/2004

Friday 10 December 2004

Section E : Written questions lodged on 9 December 2004

  

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-12924 Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive what the arrangements will be for rail passengers to buy Eurostar tickets at stations in Scotland after 23 December 2004, in light of the decision of GNER to cease selling such tickets at Waverley Station after this date.

S2W-12925 Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many applications were made for electronically-powered wheelchairs and, of these, how many initial applications were successful and how many applications were successful on appeal in each NHS board area in each of the last five years.

S2W-12926 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average cost is, per prisoner, of reception and induction to prison.

S2W-12927 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average length of time spent in prison is for prisoners sentenced to (a) less than three months, (b) three to six months and (c) six months to one year.

S2W-12928 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what sums are allocated to doctors who accept drug addicts onto their practice list.

S2W-12929 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what sums are allocated to pharmacists who take part in the prescribing of methadone on their premises.

S2W-12930 Colin Fox: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on its programmes to install central heating and insulation in pensioners’ homes.

S2W-12931 Colin Fox: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied that the installation of central heating and insulation in pensioners’ homes is being carried out by accredited companies and to the highest standards of quality.

S2W-12932 Colin Fox: To ask the Scottish Executive how it has monitored customer satisfaction of the work carried out to install central heating and insulation in pensioners’ homes.

S2W-12933 Colin Fox: To ask the Scottish Executive what level of complaints it has received against Eaga Partnership Ltd and its sub-contractors in respect of the programmes to install central heating and insulation in pensioners’ homes.

S2W-12934 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive what the cumulative savings, as a percentage of the Scottish Departmental Expenditure Limit for 2007-08, are as a result of the plans referred to in Building a Better Scotland - Efficient Government, Securing Efficiency, Effectiveness and Productivity.

S2W-12935 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-10335 by Mr Andy Kerr on 21 September 2004 on non-domestic rate income since 1996, whether it will publish comparable non-domestic rate income (NDRI) data for 2003-04 and estimated NDRI for 2004-05 and 2005-06.

S2W-12936 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive what the forecast overall rates income is for 2005-06 and how this estimate compares to the most recent estimated total rates income for 2004-05, given its aim of ensuring that there is no overall increase in rates burden on businesses in 2005-06.

S2W-12937 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive which authorities would be expected to exercise the supervisory and disciplinary functions set out in section 64 (1)(e)(i) of the Charities and Trustee Investment (Scotland) Bill.

S2W-12938 Jim Mather: To ask the Scottish Executive how many economically inactive people have been recorded as wanting to work in each quarter since 1997.

S2W-12939 Jim Mather: To ask the Scottish Executive, with reference to A Smart Successful Scotland – Strategic direction to the Enterprise Networks and an enterprise strategy for Scotland, why statistics on the numbers of economically inactive people are shown in round thousands, including at sub-national levels.

S2W-12940 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to meet First ScotRail regarding the provision of disabled access at new and existing railway stations.

S2W-12941 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what safeguards are in place to prevent pollution of the River Clyde in the event of adverse weather conditions.

S2W-12942 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive when the independent task group reviewing provisions of the Civic Government (Scotland) Act 1982 is expected to report.

S2W-12943 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the independent task group reviewing the provisions of the Civic Government (Scotland) Act 1982 has made specific reference to the licensing of limousines in the course of the review.

S2W-12944 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the independent task group reviewing the provisions of the Civic Government (Scotland) Act 1982 has made specific reference to the regulation of lap dancing or table dancing clubs in the course of the review.

S2W-12945 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-9992 by Nicol Stephen on 10 September 2004, whether it has reached a decision on the introduction of licensing for private car hire booking offices.

S2W-12946 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact the proposed changes at BBC Scotland will have on Gaelic broadcasting.

S2W-12947 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact the proposed changes at BBC Scotland will have on regional programming.

S2W-12948 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact the proposed changes at BBC Scotland will have on educational programming.

S2W-12949 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with BBC Scotland on the outcome of its recent review process.

S2W-12950 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any concerns about the development of internet pharmacies in Scotland.

S2W-12951 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to make the Scottish Medicines Consortium guidance mandatory for all NHS boards.

S2W-12952 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to introduce generic substitution prescriptions to pharmacies.

S2W-12953 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will publish details of any schemes it is considering introducing in order to reduce the NHS drugs bill.

S2W-12954 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will meet any shortfall in Supporting People funding to Aberdeen City Council to allow it to meet the agreements it has entered into with voluntary sector providers under the Supporting People initiative, such as the Broomhill Park housing with support development scheme.

S2W-12955 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it has taken to progress implementation of the EU Environmental Noise Directive.

S2W-12956 Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive what the membership and remit are of the committee examining the provision of neurosurgical services in Scotland; when it is expected to report, and whether implementation of any recommendations will be delayed until after the conclusion, and consideration, of the review of the National Framework for Service Change, chaired by David Kerr.

S2W-12957 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-11675 by Tavish Scott on 8 November 2004, whether it has now reached a conclusion in respect of whether there will be a review of the Game (Scotland) Acts and, if so, what that conclusion is.

S2W-12958 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-11675 by Tavish Scott on 8 November 2004, whether it considers the Game (Scotland) Acts to be consistent with the EU Directive on the Conservation of Birds; whether the European Commission has expressed any view on this matter, and how this issue will be addressed in any review of the Game (Scotland) Acts.

S2W-12959 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-11675 by Tavish Scott on 8 November 2004, what powers are granted to landowners, occupiers, gamekeepers and servants under the Game (Scotland) Act 1832 and whether these powers are consistent with its criminal justice policies and the European Convention on Human Rights.

S2W-12960 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many Antisocial Behaviour Orders have been served for offences committed in the (a) Loch Lomond and the Trossachs National Park and (b) Cairngorms National Park.

S2W-12961 Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what further progress has been made in respect of arrangements for cross-border flows of students.

S2W-12962 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will issue a planning advice note on affordable housing and, if so, what consultation will take place prior to its issue.

S2W-12964 Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive what the (a) last published estimate was prior to the start of each financial year since 1999-2000 of the total amount of non-domestic rates to be collected in that year and (b) total amount of non-domestic rates actually collected was in each year.
