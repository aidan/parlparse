Business Bulletin No. 171/2002

Monday 25 November 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-3632 Bruce Crawford: Pollution of Scotland's Seas—That the Parliament supports the Irish Government, on the occasion of the meeting of the British-Irish Council at New Lanark, in its legal case against the UK Government in regard to nuclear pollution from the British Nuclear Fuels Ltd site at Sellafield, England.

*S1M-3631# Ms Sandra White: Glasgow City Council's Withdrawal of Bulk Refuse Uplift Service—That the Parliament notes with deep concern the decision by Glasgow City Council to withdraw its bulk refuse uplift service from back court areas of property managed by housing associations and housing co-operatives and to only uplift bulk items when housing associations agree to purchase back court cleaning services from the council; further notes that this decision was imposed despite representations that the decision be deferred until the end of the current financial year and that the new arrangement to leave bulk refuse on the pavement on specific days for collection is a recipe for disaster, with the consequence that bulk refuse is now accumulating in back court areas, constituting a nuisance, danger and environmental hazard to residents and property, and considers that the Scottish Executive should make representations to the council that it should, as a matter of urgency, come to an agreement with housing associations to restore the bulk refuse uplift service.

*S1M-3630# Cathy Peattie: Assisting Those with Impaired Communication—That the Parliament notes the Clinical Research and Audit Group report published in 1997 The provision of and support for alternative and augmentative communication (AAC) in Scotland: equipment and services; welcomes the Adults with Incapacity (Scotland) Act 2000 with its emphasis on communication; applauds the augmentative communication programme of the Forth Valley NHS Rehabilitation Service, and considers that the Scottish Executive should fund research into the needs of those with impaired communication and end the underfunding of augmentative communication in Scotland.

*S1M-3629 Brian Fitzpatrick: Bioinformatics and Opportunities for All—That the Parliament applauds the announcement on 20 November 2002 by the Department of trade and Industry on its Beacon Project grant of £940,000, as part of the "Harnessing Genomics" Programme announced in the "Opportunities for All" White Paper of April 2001, to support collaborative inter-disciplinary research work which sees scientists and academics from computing, information and mathematical sciences, the Institute for Biology and Life Sciences and the Beatson Cancer Research UK Institute at the University of Glasgow working together to develop software that will simulate and analyse the chemistry of the body; notes that these projects offer the prospect of developing new techniques to tackle diseases including cancer, arteriosclerosis, stroke, heart disease and chronic inflammatory and degenerative diseases and have the potential to revolutionise the future of healthcare, and affirms that this research work capitalises on the excellent science base available in this country and will help the United Kingdom maintain our global position at the leading edge of bioscience and biotechnology while supporting the development of new technologies that enhance the future competitiveness of UK industry.

*S1M-3628# Mr John Home Robertson: Edinburgh Direct Aid—That the Parliament congratulates the charity group Edinburgh Direct Aid on its many years of providing aid throughout the states of the former Yugoslavia and Albania, and applauds its decision to seek to provide aid to the Palestinian people during this period of heightened tension.

Supported by: Pauline McNeill*, Dorothy-Grace Elder*, Gordon Jackson*, Ms Margo MacDonald*, Mr John McAllion*, Tommy Sheridan*, Andrew Wilson*, Nicola Sturgeon*, Mr Lloyd Quinan*

*S1M-3627 Ms Wendy Alexander: Straight Out Advisory Group—That the Parliament commends the work of the Straight Out Advisory Group (SOAG) in co-ordinating and assisting the delivery of social justice within the Paisley Social Inclusion Partnership (SIP) area; recognises the particular emphasis SOAG places on assisting the most excluded young people resident in the SIP area to return to active citizenship, through, for example, the devising of pre-vocational and personal development support, in the form of counselling aimed at tackling an individual’s offending behaviour such as substance abuse and anger management, for former offenders that require additional assistance to join local training and employment projects; notes that the programme was the product of research by Scottish Enterprise, which stated that 80% of employers would not recruit someone with a known offending background; acknowledges the support given to SOAG by the Job Centre Plus Service which identifies the group as a crucial resource in cutting the number of men that sleep rough, fail to seek employment, or present themselves as destitute within the first week of their release from prison, and understands the difficulty faced by fast-growing organisations such as SOAG in maintaining the momentum of current claims and meeting the needs of new recruits.

*S1M-3626# Des McNulty: Regeneration of the Clyde—That the Parliament recognises the importance to Scotland of efforts to regenerate the Clyde; congratulates The Herald for initiating a public debate on the subject; considers that the Scottish Executive and Scottish Enterprise should establish and resource a development framework for the Clyde which encourages joined-up thinking and action; further considers that the respective councils, enterprise companies, regeneration agencies and private developers should work co-operatively in reclaiming derelict land, ensuring adequate flood protection and improving the transport infrastructure, and believes that ministers should make the regeneration of the Clyde one of the top economic, environmental and social justice priorities for Scotland.

Supported by: Bill Butler*, Gordon Jackson*, Pauline McNeill*, Brian Fitzpatrick*, Mr Duncan McNeil*, Dr Sylvia Jackson*, Johann Lamont*

*S1M-3625 Alex Neil on behalf of the Enterprise and Lifelong Learning Committee: Enterprise and Lifelong Learning Committee, Final Report on Lifelong Learning—That the Parliament welcomes the 9th Report 2002 of the Enterprise and Lifelong Learning Committee, Final Report on Lifelong Learning (SP Paper 679), and invites the Scottish Executive to take the report into account when developing the new strategy.

S1M-3624 Mary Scanlon: Anticoagulation Medication Strips on Prescription—That the Parliament notes that Scotland has a significant and growing number of patients who require oral anticoagulation medication to avoid potentially fatal bleeding or clotting; recognises that diagnostic technology enables these patients to self-monitor their blood-clotting times in order to determine the correct dose of anticoagulation medication, at considerable saving to the NHS and convenience to the patient; notes that anticoagulation monitoring strips are available on prescription in England, Wales and Northern Ireland but not yet in Scotland, and calls on the Scottish Executive to make such strips available on prescription in Scotland without further delay.

Supported by: Fergus Ewing*, Michael Russell*, Mr Murray Tosh*, Christine Grahame*, Dorothy-Grace Elder*, Mr David Davidson*

S1M-3623# Mr Duncan Hamilton: Glasgow to Barra Air Link Public Service Obligation—That the Parliament notes with concern the decision to award a public service obligation order to the Glasgow to Barra air link for one year only; recognises that such orders are normally awarded for three years; further recognises the concern felt in Barra that this constitutes a major threat to the long-term future of the air service which, in turn, could engender more social and economic decline in an area already affected by depopulation, and believes that the Scottish Executive should reinstate the order for three years.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson*, Christine Grahame*, Ms Sandra White*

S1M-3613 John Farquhar Munro: Skye Bridge Tolls—That the Parliament recognises that toll charges on the Skye Bridge of £5.70 for non-ticket book holders are vastly higher than other bridges; notes that the Napier University Transport Research Institute report An Assessment of the Economic Impact of the Skye Bridge Tolls shows that the tolls are having a negative effect on the Skye and Lochalsh economy, and calls for the tolls to be removed entirely or at least reduced in line with the toll regimes on other Scottish bridges.

S1M-3612# Trish Godman: Tackling Under Age Drinking—That the Parliament commends the members of the Renfrewshire Council on Alcohol for their innovative and widely welcomed Young Persons Advisory Project which seeks to guide and educate young people and school children on the growing social, personal and health problems associated with underage drinking; notes that many of the youngsters that have attended the project's counselling programmes have benefited from their participation; further notes that they and their parents now have a greater awareness and a more sensible view of alcohol and its dangers; is pleased to see that there is growing interest in the project's work from as far away as the New Zealand Police, and believes that such initiatives dealing with alcohol and substance misuse amongst children and young people should receive appropriate support from the Scottish Executive.

S1M-3610 Robert Brown: Carers' Rights Day - 3 December 2002—That the Parliament welcomes Carers’ Rights Day on 3 December 2002; pays tribute to the estimated 105,000 people in the Greater Glasgow area that act as carers to people that are sick, frail or disabled; applauds the efforts of Carers UK and local care organisations to support the position of carers, and calls on all levels of government to ensure that carers obtain all the benefits and support available to them.

Supported by: Irene McGugan, Christine Grahame*, Ms Sandra White*

S1M-3609 Brian Fitzpatrick: Vascular Disease in Elderly People and the Findings of PROSPER—That the Parliament welcomes the findings of PROSPER, the prospective study of pravastatin in the elderly at risk, undertaken by a team of leading international scientists from the University of Glasgow, University College Cork, Ireland, and Universiteit Leiden, the Netherlands, and their examination of the hypothesis that pravastatin will reduce heart attack and stroke events in elderly subjects with existing vascular disease or at high risk of developing the condition, presented at the American Heart Association Conference on 18 November 2002 in Chicago, USA; recognises that cardiovascular disease is the leading cause of death and disability in elderly people and is also a major contributor to cognitive decline and dementia, both important public health issues for medical care for older people; notes that, while the benefits of statin therapy in reducing cardiovascular events such as heart attack and stroke in middle age are already recognised, its value for citizens in the over–70s age group had never previously been closely scrutinised, and calls on the Scottish Executive, working together with NHS boards, to assess the potential effects of a statin in reducing the risks of coronary heart disease, stroke and their consequences in patients at high risk aged 70 and older, so that current vascular risk management strategies for the middle-aged might be extended to elderly people who currently represent the largest group of undertreated individuals in our communities.

Supported by: Alex Neil, Bill Butler, Mr Kenneth Gibson, Johann Lamont, Cathie Craigie, Helen Eadie, Mr Murray Tosh, Paul Martin, Mr Lloyd Quinan, Mr Kenneth Macintosh, Pauline McNeill, Rhona Brankin, Scott Barrie, Ms Wendy Alexander, Dorothy-Grace Elder, Marilyn Livingstone*, Ms Sandra White*

S1M-3594 Pauline McNeill: Impact on Human Rights Organisations of Proposed Israeli Legislation—That the Parliament condemns the Bill tabled in the Israeli Parliament, the Knesset, on 29 October 2002 by the chair of the Israeli Government coalition, MK Zeev Boim, to secure impunity for war criminals, outlaw human rights activities, imprison human rights activists for up to ten years and destroy evidence of war crimes; notes that such a law, if passed, would forbid "the holding of investigations and writing down of results" and that any association engaging in any such activity would be liable to be disbanded, effectively closing down any action by human rights organisations to collect and then disseminate testimonies of human rights violations; expresses grave concern that such proposals would sabotage the work of Israeli human rights activists assisting international war crimes tribunals at the Hague and make illegal the courageous and conscientious actions of individual citizens of Israel, and calls on MSPs to protest in the strongest terms to members of the Knesset against these proposed violations of human rights.

Supported by: Michael Matheson, Mr Lloyd Quinan, Dorothy-Grace Elder, Tommy Sheridan, Bill Butler, Christine Grahame*
