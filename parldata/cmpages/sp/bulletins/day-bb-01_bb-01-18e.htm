BUSINESS BULLETIN 10 / 2001

Thursday 18 January 2001

Section E - Written questions lodged on 17 January 2001

 

 

S1W-12444 Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive how many patients were waiting for admission to hospital in (a) Scotland and (b) each health board area in each quarter of (i) 1987 and (ii) 1992 and how many patients were waiting for admission to hospital in each NHS Trust in each quarter of each year from 1995 to date.

S1W-12513 Mr Murray Tosh: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-7986 by Sarah Boyack on 28 June 2000, whether it has now responded to the European Commission White Paper on Environmental Liability and, if so, whether its response accepted the principle of strict liability.

S1W-12542 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what the latest estimate is of (a) the number of motor vehicles in Scotland and (b) the number of these which presently run on liquid petroleum gas.

S1W-12543 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Minister for Enterprise and Lifelong Learning will reply to the letter dated 9 December 2000 from Mr W.H. Gray of Dunipace, Denny.

S1W-12544 Mr Murray Tosh: To ask the Presiding Officer whether the major works packages referred to by the Parliament’s Clerk/Chief Executive during the Audit Committee meeting on 3 October 2000 (Official Report, col. 406) have now been awarded; if so, whether the project management team has been able to review the inflation risks with the cost consultants in the light of these tender results, and what conclusions and recommendations arose from any such review.

S1W-12545 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how much financial assistance was made available in each of the last three years for road improvements in (a) Aberdeen, (b) Dundee, (c) Edinburgh, (d) Glasgow and (e) each Scottish parliamentary region and what percentage of the total transport budget for the relevant year each of these figures represents.

S1W-12546 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how many times Aberdeen City Council have requested meetings with the Minister for Transport since 1 January 2001, specifying the date of each such request.

S1W-12547 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list the communications received from Aberdeen City Council since 1 January 2001 in connection with current traffic problems in Aberdeen, specifying the date each such communication was sent.

S1W-12548 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Minister for Transport intends to visit Aberdeen to observe recent traffic problems; if so, when and with whom she intends to meet while in the city.

S1W-12549 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how many official visits the First Minister has undertaken in (a) Aberdeen and (b) the North East of Scotland parliamentary region since his appointment and when he next intends to visit these locations.

S1W-12550 Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive who will be involved in producing the Neighbourhood Renewal Statement for Scotland and whether this will be used to measure the effectiveness of the ways in which the Better Neighbourhood Services Fund is utilised in different local authority areas.

S1W-12551 Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will detail the formula to be used to distribute the Better Neighbourhood Services Fund.

S1W-12552 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive how many listed buildings were demolished in 1999.

S1W-12553 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive whether present statutory powers for safeguarding listed buildings are adequate and enable the aims of all relevant legislation to be met.

S1W-12554 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive who nominates Scottish buildings for World Heritage Site status and who advises this person or body.

S1W-12555 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what funds were allocated for listed building repairs in 1999.

S1W-12556 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive how many applications for grant aid for listed building repairs were received in 1999; how many grants were approved, and how much was spent on such grants.

S1W-12557 Ms Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people in each health board area are currently diagnosed with hepatitis C and how many of these are currently receiving anti-retroviral treatment.

S1W-12558 Ms Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive which Chinese medicines may be prescribed under the NHS, as part of alternative therapy treatments.

S1W-12559 Ms Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive which alternative therapies are offered as part of NHS provision in Scotland.

S1W-12560 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to assist Glasgow City Council in safeguarding the future of priceless pieces in their museums and galleries collections.

S1W-12561 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any of Glasgow’s museums and galleries can be designated as being of national importance and, if so, whether this would entitle them to receive central funding.

S1W-12562 Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the Scottish Society for Autism regarding autistic spectrum disorders.

S1W-12563 Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the Home Office regarding the development of guidelines for police authorities on dealing with people with autistic spectrum disorders.

S1W-12564 Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will support the Scottish Society for Autism in developing guidelines for police authorities on dealing with people with autistic spectrum disorders.
