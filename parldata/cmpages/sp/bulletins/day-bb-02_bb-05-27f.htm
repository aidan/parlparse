Business Bulletin No. 87/2002

Monday 27 May 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-3150 Mr Andy Kerr: The Modernising Government Fund—That the Parliament notes the Scottish Executive’s commitment to modern, high quality, efficient and responsive public services and supports its approach, through the Modernising Government Fund, to help deliver the changes which will integrate government, exploit the benefits of information and communications technology and put citizens at the centre of public service delivery in a 21st Century Scotland.

Supported by: Peter Peacock*

*S1M-3149 Mr Jim Wallace: Alternatives to Custody—That the Parliament commends the Scottish Executive’s commitment to developing a range of high quality alternatives to custody which fit the changing pattern of offending behaviour across Scotland, and recognises the importance of ensuring the delivery of consistently effective community disposals which have the confidence of the judiciary and the public and also have the potential to reduce re-offending without compromising public safety.

Supported by: Dr Richard Simpson*

*S1M-3148 Christine Grahame: Water Charges Soaking up Small Borders Businesses—That the Parliament notes that for a small business in the Scottish Borders with a rateable value in the order of £20,000-£25,000 per annum the water bill will be some £593.60 as compared to £383.89 for a similar business in Berwick; notes that a substantial increase in charges to small Borders businesses is directly attributable to the 179.1% increase from last year in the daily charge, the 45% increase in the sewerage daily charge and the 71.4% increase in the surface water drainage charge; notes that, additionally, the surface water drainage charge impacts disproportionately on small businesses comprising as it does some 50% of the total billing so that small local Borders businesses, such as post offices, newsagents, other retailers and manufacturing companies and offices employing 50 or less, which are still reeling from foot-and-mouth and the consequential downturn in tourism, are further burdened by punitive water charges; considers that the historic method of calculating commercially metered water rates requires radical overhaul, and therefore calls upon the Scottish Executive to review the entire system of charging commercially metered water without delay.

*S1M-3147 John Scott: Suez Medal—That the Parliament acknowledges the contribution made by British Servicemen during the Suez emergency from 1951 to 1954, notes with concern that, despite many representations over the years, no award has been made of a "Suez Medal" for those who served at that time, and calls on the Scottish Executive to make representations to Her Majesty’s Government to invite the Committee on the Grant of Honours, Decorations and Medals to ensure that due recognition is given to this group of servicemen, many of whom served in Scottish regiments.

*S1M-3146 Patricia Ferguson on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Approval of SSIs—That the Parliament agrees that the following instruments be approved—

the draft Valuation and Rating (Exempted Classes) (Scotland) Order 2002; and

the draft Marriage (Approval of Places) (Scotland) Regulations 2002.

S1M-3145# Nora Radcliffe: Legislation for Increased Penalties for Wildlife Crime—That the Parliament recognises that, while a dedicated, all-encompassing, natural heritage Bill based on the proposals in The Nature of Scotland consultation document would be the preferred option, recent incidents such as the poisoning of a sea eagle and a golden eagle in Ayrshire, poisoning of a golden eagle in Aberdeenshire and the theft of two full clutches of osprey eggs in Perthshire, demonstrate the increasing urgency of delivering legislation that brings the level of sanctions against wildlife criminals into line with those available in England and Wales within this session of the Parliament and before next year's breeding season, and considers that the Executive should (a) progress legislation on wildlife crime through amendments to the Criminal Justice (Scotland) Bill and (b) reiterate its firm commitment to, and give a timetable for, a Bill to enact the remaining proposals in The Nature of Scotland.

S1M-3144 Tommy Sheridan: Glasgow Housing Association and European Commission Rules—That the Parliament expresses its concern that the transfer of Glasgow council housing to the Glasgow Housing Association may breach European Commission rules on state aid; notes the inadequate answers given so far on this matter by the Scottish Executive, and calls for an early ministerial statement on the issue.

S1M-3143# Elaine Smith: Promoting Scotland's Industrial Heritage—That the Parliament recognises that our industrial heritage is of interest to many people from other countries as well as Scots, and believes that the Scottish Executive should look at ways to market ex-industrial areas throughout Scotland such as Coatbridge and give consideration to exploring the idea of state-run hotels, along the line of Spanish paradors, where listed buildings could be restored to provide accommodation for tourists thus allowing itineraries to include non-traditional tourist facilities such as Summerlee Heritage Park in Coatbridge which is home to the only working trams in Scotland.

S1M-3142 Michael Russell: Conservative Support for Creationist Schools—That the Parliament notes with astonishment the remarks made by Mr Murdo Fraser MSP, the Deputy Education Spokesperson for the Tory Party in Scotland, on BBC Scotland Television on 17 May 2002, in which he expressed support for groups promoting "creationist" views on science running state schools in Scotland; notes that members of such groups totally reject evolution and have nothing to do with the great religious faiths in Scotland and the wider world; further notes that creationists have taken control of a state-funded secondary school in Gateshead, where they are undermining the teaching of science; rejects the foolish position of the Tory Party in Scotland, which seems now to be not only against devolution but also evolution, and asserts that misguided creationist groups should not be placed in charge of children’s education in Scotland.

Supported by: Michael Matheson

S1M-3142.1 Murdo Fraser: Conservative Support for Creationist Schools—As an amendment to motion (S1M-3142) in the name of Michael Russell, leave out from "astonishment" to end and insert "disappointment the hypocrisy of the Scottish National Party, which one week proclaimed itself the champion of choice and diversity in education and the next condemns any educators with whom they disagree; derides Michael Russell’s assumption that creationist views have nothing to do with the great religious faiths in Scotland and the wider world when there are different views among Christians in many mainstream denominations on the question of creation and evolution; notes that, under Conservative proposals, any state-funded school in Scotland would be required to teach the curriculum and be subject to inspections by Her Majesty’s Inspectorate of Education; warmly welcomes the findings of the poll commissioned by the Catholic Church in Scotland which showed that the majority of Scots support the right of Catholic parents to send their children to Catholic schools and that the overwhelming majority of Scots believe that moral values should be taught in schools; recognises the importance of diversity in Scottish education, and believes that the best way to deliver this is through greater parental choice."

S1M-3141 Mr Adam Ingram: Larkhall to Milngavie Rail Line—That the Parliament notes the comments of South Lanarkshire councillor David McLachlan, vice-convener of Strathclyde Passenger Transport (SPT), in relation to the Larkhall to Milngavie rail line that "all we need is the Parliament to give us the power to head up the project", and calls on the Scottish Executive to make a decision over whether the British Railways (No.3) Order Confirmation Act 1994 can be transferred to SPT or whether a new Bill needs to be introduced into the Parliament to allow for this link, which is vital to the future economic prosperity of Larkhall, Stonehouse and Leshmahagow, to be progressed.

Supported by: Michael Matheson

S1M-3139 Lewis Macdonald: The Draft Undertaking by the Scottish Ministers with the Consent of Northlink Orkney and Shetland Ferries Ltd —That the Transport and the Environment Committee, in consideration of the draft Undertaking by the Scottish Ministers with the Consent of Northlink Orkney and Shetland Ferries Ltd (SE/2002/97), recommends that the draft Undertaking be approved.

Supported by: Iain Gray

S1M-3138 Fiona Hyslop: Congratulations to Linlithgow Rose—That the Parliament congratulates Linlithgow Rose junior football club on winning the Scottish Junior Cup for the first time since 1965; acknowledges the hard work of the players, the backroom staff and the committee in building this success, and congratulates the club’s supporters on helping to make Sunday a celebration of junior football.

Supported by: Michael Matheson, Stewart Stevenson, Mr Kenny MacAskill

S1M-3137 Irene McGugan: UEFA Champions League Final—That the Parliament congratulates both the City of Glasgow and the Scottish Football Association for ensuring that the UEFA Champions League Final 2002 was an outstanding success; notes the vast amount of work involved in staging such a prestigious event; commends all those involved in the preparation and aftermath; welcomes the potential benefits of showcasing Hampden Park, Glasgow and Scotland across the world to a global audience of one billion people across 200 countries, and hopes that Scottish football, the Scottish people and tourism in Scotland will reap the rewards for years to come.

Supported by: Michael Matheson, Stewart Stevenson, Mr Kenny MacAskill

S1M-3136 Donald Gorrie: Royal Opening of Millennium Wheel, Falkirk—That the Parliament congratulates British Waterways, Falkirk Council and all the organisations and people who contributed to the creation and building of the Falkirk Millennium Wheel, which is a testament to Scotland’s well-renowned and innovative engineering skills. and welcomes Her Majesty the Queen, who will officially open the Wheel on 24 May 2002, and whose presence is an indication of the significance of this imaginative project as a new landmark in central Scotland.

Supported by: Robert Brown

S1M-3135 Donald Gorrie: Volunteers Week 2002—That the Parliament welcomes the efforts of Volunteer Development Scotland to encourage people to be volunteers during this year’s Volunteers Week from 7 to 13 June 2002; congratulates and welcomes those who will be volunteering for the first time during that week; appreciates that very many of those volunteering will already be regular and committed volunteers who support individuals and communities in many indispensable ways, and commends the valuable contribution to Scottish life and communities made by volunteers from all walks of society.

Supported by: Michael Matheson, Mr Mike Rumbles, Robert Brown

S1M-3134 Andrew Wilson: Transfers Under PFI/PPP - Protection of Staff Rights and Conditions—That the Parliament recognises concerns expressed by many employees, trade union members, commentators and concerned citizens about the operation of PFI/PPP contracts initiated by the public sector in Scotland; acknowledges that these concerns are based on a number of factors, including the excessive cost of capital provided, the quality of public service provided and the rights and conditions of staff employed under PFI/PPP contracts; further recognises that many of the alleged efficiency gains in PFI/PPP contracts in comparison to the public sector are made at the expense of staff rights and conditions, which, in turn, can have a significant negative impact on the quality of ongoing service; further recognises that there is growing concern that the rights and conditions of employees transferred out of the public sector in Scotland through PFI/PPP deals are less favourably protected than elsewhere in the UK; resolves that any future PFI/PPP contracts should make the detail of alleged efficiency savings publicly available and specify the impact that such savings have on staff rights and conditions so that public bodies entering into such agreements are fully accountable to the people that they serve, and further resolves that, in respect of employee transfers under the TUPE regulations from public bodies for which the Scottish Executive has any responsibility, transfer practice should adhere to the Cabinet Office Statement of Practice as a minimum standard.

Supported by: Michael Matheson, Ms Sandra White

S1M-3051# Richard Lochhead: Drug Misuse in North East Scotland—That the Parliament notes with concern that drug misuse in the north east of Scotland has increased dramatically in recent years, with an estimated 50% rise in problem drug users in Grampian alone between 1997 and 2000; further notes that Dundee has the second highest and Aberdeen the third highest level of drug misuse in Scotland and that Grampian has the highest percentage of injecting drug users in Scotland, and considers that the Scottish Executive should recognise the scale of the problem and investigate the level and nature of resources that have been applied to addressing this growing social problem in the north east region, paying particular attention to (a) the role and accountability of the various agencies involved, (b) the amount of resources dedicated to drug users, (c) the cost to society in terms of loss of life, associated health problems and distress caused to users' families and (d) the enormous amount of drug-related crime inflicted on the local community, reflected by the fact that 80% of inmates at HM Prison Aberdeen test positive for drug use at reception.

Supported by: Michael Matheson, Ben Wallace, Stewart Stevenson, Tricia Marwick, Colin Campbell, Mary Scanlon, Fiona Hyslop, Irene McGugan, Mr Kenneth Gibson, Shona Robison, Fergus Ewing, Andrew Wilson, Roseanna Cunningham, Robin Harper, Brian Adam, Mr Lloyd Quinan, Mr Adam Ingram, Mr Gil Paterson, Fiona McLeod, Ms Sandra White, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton, Dr Winnie Ewing, Mr Kenny MacAskill
