Business Bulletin No. 95 / 2004

Friday 4th June 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-1415 Ms Rosemary Byrne: Belmont Academy Parliament Success-That the Parliament congratulates the pupils and staff of Belmont Academy in Ayr for winning the National Youth Parliament competition, Holyrood Model, where the pupils took on roles of MSPs and conducted their own parliamentary sessions, bringing to life the process of parliament and devolved government.

*S2M-1414 Mr Stewart Maxwell: Seducing our Children to Smoke-That the Parliament abhors any attempt by tobacco manufacturers to try to seduce people, particularly youngsters, into continuing, or starting, to smoke cigarettes; recognises that in Scotland approximately 12,000 people die each year through tobacco-related illnesses and registers its contempt for British American Tobacco's latest attempt to replace these deceased smokers by new and younger ones; agrees that any attempt to make smoking more attractive should be repelled, and urges members to support any legislation that encourages the people of Scotland to give up smoking and helps persuade non-smokers not to start.

*S2M-1413# Carolyn Leckie: Assault on Lanarkshire NHS-That the Parliament is extremely concerned at the announcement by NHS Lanarkshire that it will make cuts of between 5 and 10% of its budget this year in order to address a projected deficit of £20 million; is alarmed that the Scottish Executive refused to assist in avoiding such draconian measures by insisting that the board reach financial balance this year and by rejecting the board's proposals for a two-year recovery plan; notes concern that legislation and changes to be made by the Executive to consultant contracts, the new deal for junior doctors, the new GP contract and other such initiatives have not been fully funded by the Executive's allocation to NHS boards, thereby putting the service under intolerable strain; believes that the Executive should take immediate steps to avoid cuts in NHS Lanarkshire by making adequate funding available; believes that the two PFI hospitals at Hairmyres and Wishaw have placed unacceptable pressure on the board's revenue budget to the detriment of patient care and have led directly to bed cuts, reduced staffing levels and threats to the level of services provided from the non-PFI hospital at Monklands whilst the PFI consortia rake in huge profits; condemns specifically the consortia responsible for the Hairmyres PFI who are additionally set to benefit to the tune of nearly £1 billion from a re-financing deal, and believes that PFI is a wasteful means of funding public building projects, that it only benefits banks and big business and should be abolished and that all public buildings should be financed via direct public funding and the public works loan board.

*S2M-1412 Mary Scanlon: Scottish First Division Champions-That the Parliament fully condemns the decision taken on 1 June 2004 by the Scottish Premier League (SPL) to deny Scottish First Division champions, Inverness Caledonian Thistle, entry into Scotland's top league next season, meaning that, for the second successive year, the team finishing bottom of the SPL has not been relegated and the First Division winners have not been rewarded for their success by rightfully gaining promotion to the SPL; notes that this is despite the club reaching a suitable ground-sharing agreement with Aberdeen Football Club to play at Pittodrie Stadium for one season, and believes that the decision takes away the basic principle of being rewarded for success.

*S2M-1411 Christine Grahame: Borders Voluntary Community Care Forum-That the Parliament recognises the sterling work of Scotland's countless volunteers and notes the contribution of Borders Voluntary Community Care Forum, with representatives from Alzheimer Scotland-Action on Dementia, Borders Independent Advocacy Service, Borderline, Borders Local Health Council, Borders Carers Centre, the Disabled Persons Housing Service, Borders Visual Impairment Network, the British Red Cross, Encompass, Gala Day Services, New Horizons Borders, Penumbra Youth Project, People First Borders and Scottish Borders Elder Council and also the contribution of individual members; notes its mission statement "to represent the views of all those with an interest in community care to the agencies responsible for the planning and provision of community care services in the Scottish Borders", and congratulates it on another successful year's work on behalf of Borders users and carers.

*S2M-1410# Ms Sandra White: Increasing Gender Pay Gap-That the Parliament notes with concern the result of research by PayFinder.com which indicates that the gender pay gap is getting wider, that it has increased by 5% in the last year and that the gap is now 29% in Scotland and believes that this is an unacceptable situation and that the Scottish Executive has a duty to investigate the matter and bring forward proposals to close the gap.

*S2M-1409# Dr Elaine Murray: Congratulations to Food Train, Dumfries and Galloway - Queen's Award for Voluntary Service 2004-That the Parliament congratulates the Food Train, Dumfries and Galloway, on being one of the 110 organisations in the United Kingdom to be awarded the Queen's Award for Voluntary Service on 1 June 2004; notes the expansion of its operations delivering groceries to elderly and disabled people throughout Dumfries and Galloway over the last year and the training and experience being offered to its increasing numbers of volunteers; further congratulates the Food Train on the introduction of its services in Dalkeith, Midlothian, and looks forward to similar services being established throughout Scotland.

*S2M-1408 Alex Fergusson: Kirkcudbright Academy Junior Debating Champions-That the Parliament congratulates the Kirkcudbright Academy debating team and its coach on winning the National Junior Debating Competition, sponsored by the English Speaking Union, particularly as this was the first time the academy had entered this prestigious competition which receives entries from all over Scotland, and notes that the team was commended for showing "maturity beyond its tender years" and a level of debating skills that completely belied the school's previous experience in debating.
