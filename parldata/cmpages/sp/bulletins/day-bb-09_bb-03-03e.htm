Business Bulletin No. 38/2009: Tuesday 3 March 2009
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 2 March 2009

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-21513 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many freight movements have taken place on the Leven to Thornton railway line in each of the last 15 years, broken down by (a) company, (b) type of freight, (c) date and (d) time.

S3W-21514 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what it would cost to enable freight to be transported on the Leven to Thornton railway line.

S3W-21515 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what would be required to enable freight to be transported on the Leven to Thornton railway line.

S3W-21516 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what health risks are associated with hormone replacement therapies provided by the NHS.

S3W-21517 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people have been admitted to NHS hospitals for weight loss surgery in each year of the last 10 years.

S3W-21518 Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will commit to working with rural industries to find an alternative organisation to the Farming and Wildlife Advisory Group Scotland to ensure the delivery of the Scotland Rural Development Programme (SRDP) and maintain choice for people seeking advice on applications to the SRDP.

S3W-21519 Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive whether people being supplied with central heating under the central heating programme will be automatically transferred to the Energy Assistance Package.

S3W-21520 Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive whether household heating measures undertaken under Stage 4 of the Energy Assistance Package will be delivered, from application to completion, in less time than under the central heating programme.

S3W-21521 Jackson Carlaw: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to ensure that drinking water from coolers at (a) schools, (b) leisure centres, (c) hospitals and care homes and (d) companies from all sectors meet safety and hygiene standards.

S3W-21522 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what financial assistance will be given to podiatrists to assist with investment in the decontamination of podiatry instruments in line with the recommendations in Sterile Services Provision Review Group: First Report - The Glennie Framework.

S3W-21523 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it values the views of parents of schoolchildren.

S3W-21524 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive how it consults parents when proposing changes in policy or legislation on schools.

S3W-21525 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that there is a need for a national body to represent the views of parents of schoolchildren.

S3W-21526 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it gives to the Scottish Parent Councils Association.

S3W-21527 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will publish the results of its recent survey of parent councils.

S3W-21528 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will respond to the results of its recent survey of parent councils.

S3W-21529 Bill Aitken: To ask the Scottish Executive, of the 1,134 alcohol off-sales test purchase operations carried out between February 2008 and January 2009, how many resulted in alcohol being sold to (a) an underage person and (b) an underage person for the second time; what action was taken against the licensees in each of these cases, and how many licences were forfeited.

S3W-21530 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make transitional funding available to the Retired and Senior Volunteer Programme.

S3W-21531 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding has been made available to South Lanarkshire Council to support the Retired and Senior Volunteer Programme after March 2009.

S3W-21532 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reasons it has decided to stop providing ring-fenced national funding for the Retired and Senior Volunteer Programme.

S3W-21533 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact it considers that the removal of services provided by the Retired and Senior Volunteer Programme would have on the people of Clydesdale.

S3W-21534 John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive what the cost to the NHS is of having a CT scan taken of a patient’s lung area, including clinical staff time.

S3W-21535 John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive what the cost to the NHS is of having an X-ray taken of a patient’s lung area, including clinical staff time.
