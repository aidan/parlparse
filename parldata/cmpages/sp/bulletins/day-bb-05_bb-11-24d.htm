Business Bulletin No.170/2005: Thursday 24 November 2005
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 1 December 2005

Question Time

General

1. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what concerns it has in respect of the provision of bus services in the Scottish Borders.  (S2O-8285)

2. Tricia Marwick: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to respond to Her Majesty’s Government’s consultation on ship-to-ship oil transfers on the Firth of Forth.  (S2O-8250)

3. Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it is putting in place to help deliver care for elderly people.  (S2O-8309)

4. Janis Hughes: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will publicise entitlement cards before 1 April 2006 to those eligible for free concessionary travel.  (S2O-8300)

5. Not Lodged

6. Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps have been taken to implement the Kerr Report’s recommendation “that the NHS in Scotland seeks to build on some of the success stories in Scotland (such as the Braveheart Project), and looks at what can be learned from the Department of Health’s Expert Patient Project, with a view to developing a more systematic approach to self-management”.  (S2O-8319)

7. Derek Brownlee: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to increase traffic flow monitoring in the south of Scotland.  (S2O-8248)

8. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is required to upgrade the Haudagain Roundabout in Aberdeen.  (S2O-8272)

9. Mr Charlie Gordon: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2O-8117 by Rhona Brankin on 17 November 2005, whether it will give an assurance that Scottish Water will be able to take full account of other exceptional development costs on constrained sites, for example because of poor ground conditions or contaminated land, to avoid disincentives to the development of brownfield sites.  (S2O-8298)

10. Not Lodged

Finance and Public Services and Communities

1. Euan Robson: To ask the Scottish Executive how it encourages the managers of its departments, agencies and non-departmental public bodies to have regard to environmental impact in delivering public services.  (S2O-8273)

2. Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it has made on digital inclusion.  (S2O-8280)

3. Carolyn Leckie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied that its local government finance settlement meets it obligations under existing equal pay legislation.  (S2O-8243)

4. Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide, if necessary, funding to help local authorities to meet the possible costs of equal pay claims.  (S2O-8246)

5. Bill Aitken: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied with current levels of council tax across Scotland.  (S2O-8252)

6. Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider amending the funding formula for grant aided expenditure.  (S2O-8282)

7. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to reduce the number of non-departmental public bodies.  (S2O-8306)

8. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made by local authorities in meeting efficient government targets.  (S2O-8276)

9. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment is being made of value for money in respect of its expenditure on advertising.  (S2O-8245)

10. Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied that efficiency savings required of local authorities will not be passed on as reductions in frontline services.  (S2O-8317)

Education and Young People, Tourism, Culture and Sport

1. Jim Mather: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to continue to teach history in S1 and S2 as a distinct subject, in light of recent announcements and public concern.  (S2O-8238)

2. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to promote safe play for children in communities.  (S2O-8321)

3. Mrs Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making in reducing class sizes.  (S2O-8316)

4. Kate Maclean: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to issue national guidelines for dealing with allegations of abuse against teachers.  (S2O-8304)

5. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the contents of its leaflet for parents and carers describing their rights to be consulted in advance of sex education being delivered to their child meet the criteria laid out in circular 2/2001 from the Executive to Directors of Education.  (S2O-8237)

6. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria are used to determine whether rural primary schools should be closed.  (S2O-8255)

7. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it will take to promote Scottish history in schools.  (S2O-8270)

8. Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive what preparations are being made to support Glasgow’s bid for the Commonwealth Games in 2014.  (S2O-8310)

9. Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what financial support it and its agencies, and NHS boards, provide for women’s football at a grassroots level, in light of the health and social benefits of participation in the game.  (S2O-8251)

10. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken to encourage community use of school sports facilities.  (S2O-8303)
