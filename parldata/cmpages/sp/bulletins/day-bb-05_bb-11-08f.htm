Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (u ).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-3531u Murdo Fraser: David Livingstone: Explorer, Missionary, Anti-Slave Campaigner and Doctor—That the Parliament notes that 17 November 2005 marks the 150th anniversary of David Livingstone being the first European to discover the Victoria Falls; believes that the anniversary should be used to mark the important work which David Livingstone undertook as an explorer, missionary, anti-slave campaigner and doctor, having created strong ties with African nations and being celebrated in Zambia as a national hero for bringing Christianity to the country; notes that David Livingstone’s strong campaigning against slavery and for African rights is still an inspiration to people today; believes that his work should inspire a new generation of Scots, and believes that the Scottish Executive should use this occasion to celebrate the work and life of David Livingstone.

*S2M-3530 Mr Stewart Maxwell: Holocaust Memorial Day—That the Parliament welcomes the unanimous decision of the United Nations General Assembly to designate 27 January as Holocaust Remembrance Day; notes that the Assembly urged the nations of the world to observe the day so that future generations will be spared acts of genocide; further notes that the resolution rejects Holocaust denial, encourages countries to develop educational programmes about the horrors of genocide and condemns religious intolerance, incitement, harassment or violence based on ethnic origin or religious belief, and urges all members to play their part in encouraging Holocaust Memorial Day activities in their constituencies and regions during January.

*S2M-3529 Brian Adam: Congratulations to Bankhead Academy, Aberdeen—That the Parliament congratulates Bankhead Academy pupils, Andrew Grosvenor, Michael Sinclair and Drew Forbes, for raising £1,544 for charity by abseiling off the Forth Rail Bridge and wishes the school’s fundraising committee all the best for its next venture, a ceilidh on 15 December 2005.

*S2M-3528 Brian Adam: Supermarket Buying Power—That the Parliament expresses concern about the growing buying power of the major supermarkets and the impact this has on the food production and processing industries in Scotland; endorses the growing movement amongst dairy farmers to redress the situation; notes that this concern is shared by other sectors, including fish processors, and calls on the Scottish Executive to make appropriate representations to the Office of Fair Trading.

*S2M-3527 Tommy Sheridan: Condemnation of Attacks on Firefighters—That the Parliament utterly condemns the reprehensible attacks on firefighters in Rutherglen, Hamilton, Castlemilk and elsewhere over the Guy Fawkes weekend; believes that that these workers deserve the support of the whole community so that they can go about their life-saving work without being needlessly endangered, and calls on the Scottish Executive to launch an intensive media and education campaign in support of the vital work carried out by firefighters and condemning the actions of the mindless minority who attack them.

*S2M-3526 Mike Pringle: Look at Me - Moving Forward at the Royal Blind School, Edinburgh—That the Parliament welcomes delegates from Jordanstown School, Belfast, Kaunas School for partially sighted children, Lithuania, St Joseph’s School for the Visually Impaired, Dublin, Schule für Blinde und Sehbehinderte, Hamburg and Resource Centre Vision, Sweden to the "Look at Me - Moving Forward!" European school project at the Royal Blind School, Edinburgh; welcomes the three-year project to promote what young people with severe visual impairment can achieve in education, leisure and independent living, and looks forward to the launching of a web site and DVD in 2008 at the conclusion of the project, which will show young people living with a visual impairment participating fully and actively in the community and at school.

*S2M-3525 Carolyn Leckie: Amnesty for the Women of Cornton Vale—That the Parliament notes with concern the latest statistics from the Scottish Executive, which show that the vast majority of women in HM Prison Cornton Vale are there for non-violent crime and that they are essentially victims of society, imprisoned for minor offences that are rooted in poverty, exclusion, drug and alcohol dependency and abuse and violence against them as women; acknowledges that the criminal justice system is widely believed to be failing these women and wider society, and calls on the Executive to make immediate plans for the amnesty and release of all women imprisoned in Scotland for non-violent crime, review the cases of all women in jail and put the appropriate resources and supports in their communities to help them achieve fulfilling lives outside prison.

Supported by: Frances Curran*, Rosie Kane*, Tommy Sheridan*

*S2M-3524 Paul Martin: British Heart Foundation’s Food4Thought Campaign 2005—That the Parliament acknowledges the steady growth in the number of overweight and obese children and accepts that this is likely to increase the future burden of coronary heart disease in Scotland, especially in our most deprived communities; welcomes the aims of the British Heart Foundation’s Food4Thought Campaign which encourages 11 and 12-year-old children to think more about what they eat and take more responsibility for their healthy food choices; recognises that children will only be able to exercise their right to choose healthy foods in a society where government, schools, industry and parents are working together to support them; calls for the removal of barriers which prevent children from making healthier choices wherever needed, and urges the Scottish Executive to play its part by legislating to restrict the sale of unhealthy foods in schools.

S2M-3415*#* Roseanna Cunningham: TV Licence and Digital Reception—That the Parliament recognises that there are still many areas in Scotland, including parts of Perthshire, where digital television reception is not possible; notes with concern the desire by the BBC to increase the television licence fee by 2.3% above inflation, and believes that, until such time as the BBC’s entire broadcast output is available to all licence payers, a differential should be introduced into the licence fee to ensure that people who are not receiving a full service do not pay the same licence fee as those who do.

Supported by: Mr Stewart Maxwell*, Tommy Sheridan*

S2M-3400*#* Mike Rumbles: Aboyne Maternity Unit—That the Parliament welcomes the excellent work carried out by staff at Aboyne Hospital’s maternity unit; notes that the number of mothers giving birth at Aboyne is increasing, with the number of deliveries rising from 34 in 2003 to 60 in 2004, and notes that the number of bookings has increased by 71 per cent for the coming year; agrees that the unit is an excellent example of health services being delivered locally as advocated by Professor David Kerr in his report, Building a Health Service Fit for the Future; further agrees that expectant mothers should have the option of giving birth locally, at Aberdeen Royal Infirmary or in the home; notes with concern the possibility of the unit being closed, and *considers that* NHS Grampian *should* work with local people to ensure that the unit remains open.
