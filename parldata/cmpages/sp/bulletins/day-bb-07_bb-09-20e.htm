Business Bulletin No. 122/2007: Thursday 20 September 2007
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 19 September 2007

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-4446 Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has received from the Association of Chief Police Officers in Scotland to join the national firearms licensing management scheme’s register of all persons who have applied for or been granted a firearm or shotgun certificate or whose certificate has been renewed and when it estimates that Scotland will be included in the scheme.

S3W-4447 Andy Kerr: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to continue the funding to local government for the enforcement of the smoking ban.

S3W-4452 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answers to questions S3W-980 and S3W-2155 by Fiona Hyslop on 3 July and 6 August 2007 respectively, why the Cabinet Secretary for Education and Lifelong Learning stated on 13 June 2007 "Believe it or not, for the first time since the reformation, there has been a drop in the share of the population studying in higher education in Scotland" (Official Report c. 629), when her source, Prof R D Anderson: Scottish Education since the Reformation, Studies in Scottish Economic and Social History No. 5, Edinburgh 1997, indicates in table 6, page 46 a fall in the population share studying higher education in Scotland between 1800 and 1861 and between 1881 and 1911.

S3W-4453 Alasdair Allan: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to safeguard the future of the Harris tweed industry.

S3W-4454 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what linkages operate between local enterprise companies and VisitScotland.

S3W-4455 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive how the enterprise network supports the tourism industry.

S3W-4456 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to publish its science strategy.

S3W-4457 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on developing a science strategy.

S3W-4458 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to support science in Scotland.

S3W-4459 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive how it intends to help meet the future demand for science graduates identified in its Skills Strategy.

S3W-4460 Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-4011 by Nicola Sturgeon on 12 September 2007, whether its close liaison with the Ministry of Defence (MoD) includes regular meetings of representatives of the Executive, the MoD and Scotland’s veterans’ organisations and, if so, when the last such meeting took place, when the next is scheduled and whether it will publish the names of those attending.

S3W-4461 Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-4007 by Nicola Sturgeon on 12 September 2007, whether it will list the Scottish-based members of the Veterans’ Programme Scotland (VSP) Steering Group, also showing the name of the organisation that each represents; when the VSP Steering Group (a) last met and (b) will next meet, and what information it has about the programme of activities planned by the Ministry of Defence in relation to veterans in Scotland.

S3W-4462 Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-4009 by Nicola Sturgeon on 12 September 2007, what consideration has been given to the setting up and funding of pilot schemes in Scotland in order to develop a range of services to bereaved families of armed services personnel killed in Iraq or Afghanistan.

S3W-4463 Jackson Carlaw: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made of the impact of hospital car parking charges on residents living near hospitals where charges have been introduced.

S3W-4464 Jackson Carlaw: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made of the impact of hospital car parking charges on people attending casualty units at short notice to be with friends or family suffering from life-threatening conditions resulting from acute illness or involvement in serious accidents.

S3W-4465 Jackson Carlaw: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will ensure that cancer patients and others who receive regular treatment in NHS hospitals are exempt from hospital car parking charges.

S3W-4466 Jackson Carlaw: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance it has given to NHS boards on whether it is acceptable to fine outpatients whose hospital car parking tickets have expired as a result of treatment taking longer than anticipated or appointments being delayed.

S3W-4467 Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of the findings of the Equal Opportunities Commission’s report, Valuable Assets, how it plans to tackle the undervaluing of the work of classroom assistants in schools, particularly in respect of low pay.

S3W-4468 Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of the findings of the Equal Opportunities Commission’s report, Valuable Assets, what plans it has to encourage local authorities to revisit the job evaluation of classroom assistants’ roles and to carry out equal pay audits using a non-discriminatory job evaluation system.

S3W-4469 Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to develop a national framework of job roles to ensure proper recognition of the role and responsibilities of classroom assistants.

S3W-4470 Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to establish a Scotland-wide training and qualifications framework to ensure a path of career development and progression for classroom assistants.

S3W-4471 Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to support the development of a new support for learning higher national certificate to develop and promote the role and progression of classroom assistants.

S3W-4472 Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to develop a system of registration for jobs for classroom assistants to raise standards and promote their education and training.

S3W-4473 Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive how it intends to assess the evidence and recommendations of the Equal Opportunities Commission’s report, Valuable Assets, and whether a national action group involving all stakeholders will be established.

S3W-4474 Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it plans to make to BT Scotland and what other action it intends to take in relation to those rural telephone subscribers who cannot achieve broadband connection speeds over their BT landlines.

S3W-4475 Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-4002 by Stewart Stevenson on 13 September 2007, what representations it has made to British Airways and Loganair Ltd and what the response was from the airlines.

S3W-4476 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to extend the national concessionary fares scheme for older and disabled persons to include those using local community transport in rural areas.

S3W-4477 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to announce the introduction of a presumption against the closure of rural schools.

S3W-4478 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive what level of public engagement it expects the independent review of accident and emergency facilities in Lanarkshire to undertake.

S3W-4479 Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to improve the lives of children with autism.

S3W-4480 Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to increase the quality of education received by children with autism.

S3W-4481 Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it gives to existing and new teachers to allow them to teach children with autism more effectively.

S3W-4482 Angela Constance: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-15383 by Cathy Jamieson on 19 April 2005, what expenditure the Scottish Prison Service incurred in connection with its involvement in the International Correction and Prison Association in 2005-06 and 2006-07.

S3W-4483 Angela Constance: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of Scottish Prison Service (SPS) expenditure for financial years 2005-06 and 2006-07 was used to fund the involvement of SPS staff in the administration and organisation of the International Correction and Prison Association.

S3W-4484 Angela Constance: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers membership and the active involvement of the Scottish Prison Service and its staff in the International Correction and Prison Association to be a productive use of public funds.

S3W-4485 Angela Constance: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-30117 by Cathy Jamieson on 14 February 2007, whether it will provide a breakdown of the flight costs incurred by senior staff of the Scottish Prison Service on overseas trips in each year since 1999.

S3W-4486 Angela Constance: To ask the Scottish Executive how many conferences on prison privatisation members of staff of the Scottish Prison Service have attended in the last year.

S3W-4487 Angela Constance: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Scottish Prison Service has any plans to attend or participate in overseas conferences.

S3W-4488 Angela Constance: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list the location and costs of all conferences attended by senior members of Scottish Prison Service staff since 1999.

S3W-4489 Angela Constance: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Scottish Prison Service currently seconds staff to the International Correction and Prison Association and, if so, which organisation funds the related salary costs.

S3W-4490 Angela Constance: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are any plans to review the Building (Procedure) (Scotland) Regulations 2004 and the Local Government (Scotland) Act 1973 in order to permit competition between neighbouring local authorities in relation to the provision of building warrants.

S3W-4491 Angela Constance: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider reviewing the Building (Procedure) (Scotland) Regulations 2004 and the Local Government (Scotland) Act 1973 in order to permit competition between neighbouring local authorities in relation to the provision of building warrants.

S3W-4492 Angela Constance: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Scottish Association of Building Standards Managers is funded by public money.

S3W-4493 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many protection orders have been issued under the Freshwater and Salmon Fisheries (Scotland) Act 1976 and in which locations.

S3W-4494 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive what provisions there are under the Freshwater and Salmon Fisheries (Scotland) Act 1976 to review or repeal orders that have been made.

S3W-4495 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has plans to review the Assynt - Coigach Area Protection Order 2004.

S3W-4496 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will publish any reports made in respect of the Assynt - Coigach Area Protection Order 2004.
