Business Bulletin No. 178/2002

Thursday 5 December 2002

Section E : Written questions lodged on 4 December 2002
  

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S1W-32090 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what expenditure has been incurred by Scottish Natural Heritage (SNH) since 1997 in connection with the protection of (a) predator species, including pine martins, sparrow hawks, merlins and eagles, and (b) smaller prey species such as waders, winchats, wheaters, woodpeckers and finches; whether SNH distinguishes between different species perceived to be under threat, and whether some threatened species are considered by SNH to be more worthy of support than others.

S1W-32192 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive what additional restrictions will be proposed on the sale of fireworks.

S1W-32244 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether Scottish Enterprise has assessed the impact of its telecommunications infrastructure programme Project ATLAS on the Scottish communications industry and, if so, how many job losses it estimates the programme may result in.

S1W-32245 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Scottish telecommunications provider industry was excluded from bidding for Phase II of the Scottish Enterprise telecommunications infrastructure programme Project ATLAS to connect communication networks between Edinburgh and regional business parks and what the reasons are for the position on this matter.

S1W-32246 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions Scottish Enterprise has had with the telecommunications policy division of the Department of Trade and Industry regarding the telecommunications infrastructure programme Project ATLAS and whether the project is an element of the UK policy on telecommunications infrastructure.

S1W-32247 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has made any application for European Commission state aid for Scottish Enterprise’s telecommunications infrastructure programme Project ATLAS and, if so, whether it has received any formal approval from the Commission in response.

S1W-32248 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether ferries used by NorthLink Orkney and Shetland Ferries Ltd comply with all applicable safety regulations and, in particular, whether seating, televisions and gaming machines are securely fixed to prevent the risk of serious injury.

S1W-32249 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what volume of freight traffic travels between the mainland and the northern isles annually.

S1W-32250 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Presiding Officer whether the post of project manager for the Parliament’s opening ceremony in 2003 is not to be proceeded with and, if so, whether any costs have been incurred as a result.

S1W-32251 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Presiding Officer whether the final contracts for the new Parliament building at Holyrood have been let and, if so, when they were let and whether there was any cost variance to the Holyrood project’s budget as a result.

S1W-32252 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what sums have been invested in tackling drug abuse within the Highlands and Islands in each year since 1999; what plans it has to provide enhanced facilities for this purpose; what new facilities will be created for this purpose, and where any such facilities will be located.

S1W-32253 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will relax planning rules and other regulations that govern the development of housing in locations that are sited off trunk roads.

S1W-32254 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will arrange for representatives of bodies representing the fishing industry to be seconded to it for the purpose of maximising its effectiveness in the impending EU negotiations.

S1W-32255 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what target it has set for the number of jobs to be relocated to rural areas in each of the next five years and, if no such target has been set, whether it has any plans to do so.

S1W-32256 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive when the costs in relation to the Skye Bridge will have been paid in full and whether toll charges on the bridge will continue to be levied after that date.

S1W-32257 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-30895 by Mr Jim Wallace on 3 December 2002, why it did not state in the answer what events the (a) First Minister and (b) Minister for Enterprise, Transport and Lifelong Learning have attended in support of the Friends of Scotland initiative and whether it will now provide this information.

S1W-32258 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive what its policy is on the issue of top-up fees for university students and whether it has any plans to introduce a scheme, similar to that under consideration for England and Wales, in Scotland.

S1W-32259 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive what its targets are for the reduction of household poverty.

S1W-32260 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made of the capital requirements of further and higher education institutions.

S1W-32261 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive what the current level of funding is per secondary school pupil in each local authority area.

S1W-32262 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to support employment opportunities for adults with learning disabilities.

S1W-32263 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive how many classroom assistants are currently employed in each local authority area.

S1W-32264 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the purpose of the word "furthering" in the amended section 71(1)(j) of the Land Reform (Scotland) Bill is to introduce a comparative test of sustainable development whereby the proposed operation by the crofting community body is compared with the existing operation and, if not, what the purpose of the word is.

S1W-32265 Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive what land in Dumfries and Galloway has been purchased by it or the former Scottish Office since 1970 for the purpose of improvements to the A75 and what land has not yet been used for that purpose and still remains in its ownership.

S1W-32266 Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive what arrangements exist for local authorities to be compensated for damage caused to non-trunk roads by traffic temporarily diverted from trunk roads due to road blockages.

S1W-32267 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many carers have had an assessment of ability to provide care as defined by the Community Care and Health (Scotland) Act 2002.

S1W-32268Δ Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will report on the outcome of the Agriculture and Fisheries Council held in Brussels on 27, 28 and 29 November 2002.

S1W-32269Δ Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made by the working group on possible legislation to tackle religious hatred.

S1W-32270 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive how much each of its departments has spent on match funding in each year since 1999.

S1W-32271 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has made to the European Commission about establishing a solidarity fund for natural disasters.

S1W-32272 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1F-2273 by the First Minister on 21 November 2002, what meetings have taken place to explore any flexibility in the Common Agricultural Policy and European Structural Funds to assist flood prevention schemes in Moray.

S1W-32273 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive how many specialist clinics there are for dealing with patients suffering from eating disorders; where each such clinic is located, and what the take-up rate is for these clinics as a percentage of those diagnosed with eating disorders in each NHS board area.

S1W-32274 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive how many consultants are employed in the NHS to assist people suffering from eating disorders.

S1W-32275 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people have been diagnosed as suffering from (a) anorexia nervosa or (b) bulimia in each NHS board area in each of the last two years.

S1W-32276 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people have been diagnosed as suffering from clinical obesity in each NHS board area in each of the last two years.

S1W-32277 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what the current budget is for ensuring Scottish interests are represented in Europe and what the budget has been in each year since 1999, broken down by department.

S1W-32278Δ Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will publish the report by Philip Banks on support for school boards.

S1W-32279 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will place in the Scottish Parliament Information Centre all guidance, guidelines and official circulars initiated by the Scottish Office and the Scottish Executive that are still operational in respect of pre-school centres, primary schools, secondary schools, special schools and tertiary education institutions.

S1W-32280Δ Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Scottish Environment Protection Agency is due to publish its annual report and accounts for 2001-02.

S1W-32281 Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive how much of the £1.5 million for implementation of the credit union action plan has now been allocated.

S1W-32282 Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive how many credit unions have been created since the launch of the credit union action plan.

S1W-32283 Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people are members of a credit union.

S1W-32284 Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what the benefits of credit unions are in helping to tackle poverty.

S1W-32285 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment has been made of the impact on the salmon farming industry of salmon dumping on EU markets.

S1W-32286 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps have been taken to introduce anti-dumping measures to protect the salmon industry in relation to farmed salmon imported to the EU.

S1W-32287 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has received about the timescale for any forthcoming decisions by the European Commission on anti-dumping measures in relation to the importation of farmed salmon into the EU.

S1W-32288 Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has made to Her Majesty’s Government about the EU review of the agreement between Norway and the EU on minimum import prices for salmon.

S1W-32289 Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made of the effect on the salmon farming industry if the agreement between the EU and Norway on minimum import prices for salmon is not renewed and strengthened in 2003.

S1W-32290 Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make any representations to Her Majesty’s Government in favour of compulsory registration of oil tankers that use the Minch.
