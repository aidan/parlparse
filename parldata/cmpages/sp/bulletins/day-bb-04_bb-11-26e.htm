Business Bulletin No. 174/2004

Friday 26 November 2004

Section E : Written questions lodged on 25 November 2004

  

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-12574 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will address the issue of whether there are sufficient affordable dentists in Fife.

S2W-12575 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to attract more dentists to the NHS.

S2W-12576 Mr Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive how many assaults involved the use of swords in each of the last five years.

S2W-12577 Mr Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive how it defines a sword.

S2W-12578 Mr Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive how many assaults involved the use of non-domestic knives in each of the last five years.

S2W-12579 Mr Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive how it defines a non-domestic knife.

S2W-12580 Mr Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to prohibit the private import of swords as part of its proposed ban on the sale of swords.

S2W-12581 Mr Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive how many assaults involved the use of combat or military-style knives in each of the last five years.

S2W-12582 Mr Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive how many assaults involved the use of reproduction or original historical swords such as claymores or basket-hilted broadswords in each of the last five years.

S2W-12583 Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the most recent figures are on how many consultant posts are vacant within the NHS, broken down by NHS board area.

S2W-12584 Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many consultant posts are filled by locums within the NHS, broken down by NHS board area.

S2W-12585 Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many nursing posts are vacant within the NHS, broken down by NHS board area.

S2W-12586 Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the level of use of bank or agency nursing staff was in each of the last three years, broken down by NHS board area.

S2W-12587 Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) primary and (b) secondary teaching posts are vacant in state schools, broken down by local authority area.

S2W-12588 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive on which occasions it considered lodging a Sewel motion but decided not to.

S2W-12589 Carolyn Leckie: To ask the Scottish Executive what aspects of health provision it plans to contract out to the private sector; how many contracts will be awarded, and what the timetable is for (a) award and (b) implementation of such contracts.

S2W-12590 Carolyn Leckie: To ask the Scottish Executive under what legislation it plans to contract out health provision to the private sector. 

S2W-12591 Carolyn Leckie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether, before contracting out health provision to the private sector, it will seek to debate the issue in the Parliament.

S2W-12592 Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what consultation it has had, or plans to have, with battle re-enactment, pageant and martial arts groups about the proposed ban on the sale of swords.

S2W-12593 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body what information it has, in respect of each Sewel motion passed by the Parliament since 1999, concerning (a) the length of the debate in the Parliament on the motion, (b) the length of any debate on the motion in committee and (c) whether (i) oral or (ii) written evidence was taken by a committee on the motion.

S2W-12594 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many victims of reported crimes were (a) injured and (b) killed by knives or other bladed weapons in each year since 1999.

S2W-12595 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what the net expenditure on policing has been in each year since 1979, expressed in real terms at today’s prices and broken down by police force area.

S2W-12596 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what its target number is in respect of the number of police officers per head of population in (a) urban and (b) rural areas.

S2W-12597 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many incidents of violence in bus stations have been reported to the police in each of the last four years for which figures are available.

S2W-12598 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of sex offence cases brought to court resulted in a conviction in each of the last five years.

S2W-12599 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what the cost was to each of its departments of (a) ministerial cars and drivers, (b) taxis, (c) rail travel, (d) the use of helicopters, (e) airline tickets and (f) chartered aeroplanes in each year since 1999.

S2W-12600 Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to make an announcement on the outcome of the pilot schemes for the test purchasing of age-restricted products and its subsequent plans for the future of test purchasing.

S2W-12601 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider amending the Council Tax (Administration and Enforcement) (Scotland) Regulations 1992 to give local authorities the discretion to accept up to 12 payments per annum on a monthly basis.

S2W-12602 Dr Jean Turner: To ask the Scottish Executive when Professor James McEwen’s review of the provision of chronic pain services will be published.

S2W-12603 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has received any representations from the City of Edinburgh Council or the Festival Theatre Trust in respect of the future of the Kings Theatre, Edinburgh, and, if so, how it responded.

S2W-12604 John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures have been taken under schedule 1, paragraph 5(2)(n) of the Building (Scotland) Act 2003 to introduce building regulations dealing specifically with the fitting of domestic water pipes and any potential risk resulting from high concentrations of lead.

S2W-12605 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take in response to Professor James McEwen’s review of the provision of chronic pain services.

S2W-12606 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how patients can have access to podiatric services if they are unable to pay the full cost of treatment.

S2W-12607 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive which patient groups are entitled to free chiropody and podiatry care and treatment.

S2W-12608 Chris Ballance: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the average rail journey time from Edinburgh to Glasgow will lengthen (a) during and (b) after construction of the Edinburgh Airport rail link and, if so, by how many minutes in each case.

S2W-12609 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many PFI/PPP contracts for schools allow or require the contractor to operate food or drink vending machines.

S2W-12610 Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure that data in respect of the (a) collation of actual and (b) projection of future costs of free personal care for the elderly is accurate and up-to-date.

S2W-12611 Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there is any legislation that determines the placing of radiators installed under its central heating installation programme and, if so, what the legislation is.

S2W-12612 Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidelines exist in respect of the placing of radiators installed under its central heating installation programme and whether there is any flexibility in their application.

S2W-12614 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it received from the Department for Culture, Media and Sport regarding the still life "Le Paté de Jambon", prior to the publication of the spoliation advisory panel’s recently published report.

S2W-12615 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive, with regard to the relocation of its Central Inquiry Unit to Kinlochleven, whether the unit will move into the premises occupied presently by Database Direct and, if so, when it first considered taking occupancy of these premises.

S2W-12616 Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-9767 by Mr Tom McCabe on 12 August 2004, when it now expects to publish its response to the consultation, Modernising NHS Dental Services in Scotland.

S2W-12617 Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive how many medical students (a) were registered to study in and (b) graduated from Scottish universities in each year since1997.

S2W-12618Δ Elaine Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive which voluntary organisations it has allocated (a) direct grants, indicating the amount awarded in each case and (b) indirect grants, indicating the amount offered in each case, in 2004-05.

S2W-12619 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-11102 by Mr Andy Kerr on 19 November 2004, how many patients died within 28 days of discharge, broken down by age group, in (a) 1999, (b) 2000, (c) 2001, (d) 2002 and (e) 2003.
