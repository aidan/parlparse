Business Bulletin No. 161/2001

Thursday 8 November 2001

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions.

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-2426# Helen Eadie: BT plc Sales Marketing Policy-That the Parliament notes newspaper reports alleging that British Telecommunications plc has issued instructions to employees not to undertake sales marketing in Dundee and Kelty on the grounds that these areas are not "posh" enough; is concerned at the stigma this creates for communities, and condemns any companies conducting their business in a way that causes harm and offence to communities across Scotland.

Supported by: Mr John McAllion*

*S1M-2425 Mr Jim Wallace: The Draft Sheriff Courts (Scotland) Act 1971 (Private Jurisdiction and Summary Cause) Order 2001-That the Justice 2 Committee recommends that the draft Sheriff Courts (Scotland) Act 1971 (Private Jurisdiction and Summary Cause) Order 2001 be approved.

Supported by: Iain Gray*

*S1M-2424 Mr Jim Wallace: The Draft Small Claims (Scotland) Amendment Order 2001-That the Justice 2 Committee recommends that the draft Small Claims (Scotland) Amendment Order 2001 be approved.

Supported by: Iain Gray*

*S1M-2423 Angus MacKay: The Draft Budget (Scotland) Act 2001 (Amendment) Order 2001-That the Finance Committee recommends that the draft Budget (Scotland) Act 2001 (Amendment) Order 2001 be approved.

Supported by: Peter Peacock*

*S1M-2422 Mr Gil Paterson: Scotland's Oil-That the Parliament notes that the Brent oil field in the North Sea is 25 years of age and is conservatively expected to be in production for a further 25 years; further notes the pessimistic predictions made by some unionist politicians during the 1970s that Scotland's oil would not last long and would be finished before the decade was out, and draws comparison between Scotland and Alaska on how oil wealth has been used to benefit the citizens of both countries by highlighting that in Alaska every citizen enjoys an annual dividend of $1,850.88 which they are free to spend as they see fit whereas in Scotland, the oil producer of the European Union, our children are born to fail in higher numbers proportionately than elsewhere in the EU.

Supported by: Mr Adam Ingram*, Mr Lloyd Quinan*, Alex Neil*, Ms Margo MacDonald*, Dorothy-Grace Elder*, Stewart Stevenson*

*S1M-2421 Trish Godman: Trade Union and Employee Negotiations-That the Parliament is deeply concerned at the growing number of job losses in Renfrewshire and elsewhere in the West of Scotland; believes that companies experiencing difficulties in domestic and international markets must take their loyal and hard-working employees into their trust when difficult or harsh decisions have to be taken; urges all companies to engage in honest and forthright negotiations with both employees and their trade unions in order to ensure reasonable settlements, retraining and redeployment for those who regrettably lose their jobs, and condemns the small minority of companies that obstinately refuse to engage in constructive dialogue on such serious matters with employees and trade union representatives.

*S1M-2420 Trish Godman: Disabled Drivers and Supermarkets-That the Parliament commends the campaign by the Baywatch Campaign, British Polio Fellowship, the Disabled Drivers' Association, the Disabled Drivers' Motor Club and Disability Now to give more protection to disabled parking bays in supermarket car parks; deplores the selfishness of other drivers who occupy such spaces with their own cars, and welcomes the initiatives taken by Asda, Safeway, Sainsbury's and Tesco to tackle this problem in a much more effective way than has been the case in the past.

*S1M-2419 Tommy Sheridan: Incompetence of the North Glasgow University Hospitals NHS Trust-That the Parliament notes North Glasgow University Hospitals NHS Trust's incompetent handling of the 16-month-long medical secretaries' dispute, the relocation of the Stobhill Hospital and the mismanagement of the Beatson Oncology Unit; expresses its deepest concern over the conduct of the Trust board, and calls for a full inquiry to be conducted as soon as possible.

*S1M-2418 Irene Oldfather: Bicycle Helmets-That the Parliament notes that the majority of deaths in cycling accidents occur as a result of head injuries; recognises that wearing a correctly fitted helmet has been shown to reduce the risk of severe head injury by 63 to 88%; further recognises that cost is a major barrier, particularly for families on a low income, and therefore calls on the Scottish Executive to promote a campaign of responsible cycling, including dissemination of information on wearing helmets correctly and measures to reduce the cost of bicycle helmets to the public.

*S1M-2412.1 Tommy Sheridan: Conduct of the First Minister-As an amendment to motion (S1M-2412) in the name of David McLetchie, leave out from "and calls upon" and insert "and recommends that the Standards Committee conduct a full and thorough investigation of the First Minister in relation to the Code of Conduct for Members of the Scottish Parliament, paragraph 1.2 of Section 1, which states "The Scottish electorate has a high expectation of members of the Scottish Parliament and the way in which they should act in their relationships with their constituents and in the Parliament. Members must meet those expectations by ensuring that their conduct is above reproach and worthy of the trust of the electorate." and paragraph 1.3, which states "The word 'Integrity' is inscribed on the mace which is a symbol of authority of the Parliament; it is one of a number of principles which must be observed in all that members do." "

*S1M-2412.2 Mr John Swinney: Conduct of the First Minister-As an amendment to motion (S1M-2412) in the name of David McLetchie, leave out from "regrets" to end and insert "believes that the First Minister has failed to respect these principles in his actions during the last few months in relation to the issue of his constituency office expenses; further believes that this has severely damaged the credibility of the First Minister and has broken the bond of trust that must exist between the First Minister and the Scottish public and that this, added to the incompetence which he has exhibited during this affair, renders his position untenable, and calls upon him to resign."

Supported by: Roseanna Cunningham*, Fiona Hyslop*

*S1M-2409.1 Fergus Ewing: Foot-and-Mouth Disease - Public Inquiry-As an amendment to motion (S1M-2409) in the name of Alex Fergusson, leave out from "calls on" to end and insert "welcomes the cross-party approach towards the policy adopted in Scotland to eradicate the foot-and-mouth disease epidemic, but believes that it is essential that all lessons are learned by means of a full, open and independently convened public inquiry in Scotland and that it is important to monitor closely the impact of the crisis upon the whole Scottish economy and, in particular, upon those regions of Scotland most affected by the outbreak."

*S1M-2409.2 Ross Finnie: Foot-and-Mouth Disease-As an amendment to motion (S1M-2409) in the name of Alex Fergusson, leave out from "calls on" to end and insert ", recognising the need for independent scrutiny of the handling of the foot-and-mouth disease outbreak, supports the participation of the Scottish Executive in the Royal Society inquiry, the Anderson "Lessons Learnt" inquiry and the Royal Society of Edinburgh inquiry and believes that these inquiries provide ample opportunities for all interested parties to contribute to the process.

Supported by: Rhona Brankin*
