Business Bulletin No. 4 / 2004

Thursday 8th January 2004

Section D : Oral Questions Selected for Answer on Thursday 15th January 2004
  

Question Time

1. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will make an announcement on proposed removal of the 50% council tax rebate from second homes. (S2O-1062)

2. Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take to deal with the rise in drink driving offences over the Christmas period. (S2O-1064)

3. Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to provide free prescriptions for terminally ill people. (S2O-1047)

4. Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is considering reviewing the system of appeal against High Court judgements. (S2O-1051)

5. Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will now consider allowing local authorities (a) the discretion to remove, or restrict, the 50% reduction which second homes receive from council tax, (b) to set out categories to which such provision would apply and (c) to retain the whole benefit of the additional sum raised. (S2O-1095)

6. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met representatives of ScotRail. (S2O-1067)

7. Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive what the total annual cost is to the public purse of the 50% council tax relief on second homes. (S2O-1082)

8. Chris Ballance: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to fulfil its commitment in A Partnership for a Better Scotland to consider the establishment of an environmental court. (S2O-1088)

9. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans are in place to ensure that all railway passengers will have full access to rail facilities at Haymarket Station for the duration of the temporary suspension of services at Waverley Station from 17 to 19 January 2004. (S2O-1056)

10. Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the Sector Skills Development Agency about the establishment of skills councils covering Scotland. (S2O-1050)

11. Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive what legislation it will introduce to ensure that school teachers will require to have a teaching degree after the repeal of the remaining sections of the schools code. (S2O-1046)

12. Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent contact it has had with East Lothian Council and the Innovate consortium in respect of the PPP project in East Lothian's schools. (S2O-1063)

13. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions have taken place with Glasgow School of Art regarding its estate at Garnethill, Glasgow. (S2O-1076)

14. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to seek a renegotiation of the fisheries agreement concluded in Brussels in December 2003. (S2O-1054)

15. Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to promote eco-tourism in the northern Highlands. (S2O-1069)

16. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is contrary to its policies for an NHS board to have no consultant-led maternity services. (S2O-1084)

17. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will ensure that community amateur sports clubs will receive full benefit from the recent announcement by the Chancellor of the Exchequer about a mandatory 80% relief from business rates for such clubs. (S2O-1066)

18. John Swinburne: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has made any representations to national bus companies regarding the delivery of the concessionary travel scheme across local authority boundaries. (S2O-1071)

19. Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what contingency arrangements are in place to deal with casualties that may result from any terrorist attacks in light of plans to reduce the number of accident and emergency units serving greater Glasgow from five to two. (S2O-1080)

20. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is ensuring access to musical instrument tuition for students after primary 6. (S2O-1081)

21. Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to address the issue of postcode prescribing for access to drugs for people with Alzheimer's disease. (S2O-1059)

22. George Lyon: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with Argyll and Clyde and Highland NHS boards over the future of the west Highland hospitals. (S2O-1083)

23. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to support waste reduction and recycling among businesses following its announcement of funding for kerbside collections of recyclable material from domestic residences in Glasgow. (S2O-1086)

24. Cathie Craigie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will promote healthy eating in a way that captures the imagination of Scottish people, particularly the young. (S2O-1078)

25. Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects the programme to end the use of mixed sex wards to be completed. (S2O-1074)

26. Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had regarding any potential impact on the justice system resulting from the introduction of a European public prosecutor. (S2O-1049)

27. Christine May: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to allow monies collected by Ofgem under the fossil fuel levy to be made available to the Scottish Consolidated Fund and what steps it will take to ensure that any such monies are used for the purpose of promoting renewable energy. (S2O-1048)

28. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the current annual cost of flood damage, caused by climate change, is to property and business. (S2O-1091)

29. Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to respond to fear of crime with information about levels of crime. (S2O-1087)

30. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in discussions between its Police Division and Lothian and Borders Police regarding the funding of a combined Sheriff courthouse and police facility in Peebles. (S2O-1061)
