*S1M-2450 Hugh Henry: World AIDS Day: 1 December 2001—That the Parliament notes World AIDS Day on 1 December 2001 and the "Out of sight, out of mind" campaign; further notes that recent figures published show that 91 people have been diagnosed as HIV positive in the first six months of this year compared with 154 in total for the previous year and that, if this trend continues for the rest of the year, it would represent an 18% increase over last year; further notes that, if the number of people aged 15 to 24 being diagnosed as HIV positive continues at the current rate, there will be a 47% increase by the end of the year, and therefore urges the Scottish Executive to undertake a high profile national campaign on HIV and AIDS awareness.

*S1M-2449 Mr Murray Tosh: Convenerships and vice-convenerships of committees—That the Parliament notes that the convenerships and vice-convenerships of the Parliament's committees were allocated initially on a proportional basis; agrees that these posts should always be allocated proportionally; recognises that the Labour Party, with 42.6% of the seats in the Parliament, now holds 53% of the committee convenerships and 47% of the vice-convenerships, and calls upon the Parliamentary Bureau to take the initiative in restoring proportionality following any personnel changes arising from a reshuffle of Scottish Executive ministers.

*S1M-2448 Dennis Canavan: Election of First Minister—That the Parliament agrees that members be given the opportunity to question those nominated for the post of First Minister before the Parliament votes to decide on the successful nominee.

Supported by: Mr Murray Tosh*, Tommy Sheridan*

*S1M-2447# Mr Kenny MacAskill: Congestion and Pollution caused by Edinburgh "Bus Wars"—That the Parliament notes with concern the escalation of the "bus wars" in the city of Edinburgh, a situation which is detrimental to routes, services and passengers and which results in congestion and pollution in some areas, at the same time as routes and services are lost in others, and urges all responsible parties to take appropriate action to address this situation and to allow Scotland’s capital city to develop an integrated transport network in the 21st Century.

*S1M-2446# Shona Robison: National Mouth Cancer Awareness Week—That the Parliament recognises the importance of National Mouth Cancer Awareness Week in highlighting the need for people to visit the dentist regularly to ensure early detection and treatment of oral cancer; is concerned that the incidence of oral cancer has increased by one third over the last fifteen years; is aware of the research by the British Dental Association which showed that one in two people are more likely to visit a dentist regularly if there is no charge for an NHS dental check up, and believes that the Scottish Executive should consider the restoration of free dental check ups as part of its review of charging policy within the health service.

*S1M-2445 Mr Jim Wallace: Anti-Terrorism, Crime and Security Bill - UK Legislation—That the Parliament recognises the urgency of enhancing anti-terrorist capability and security in the current international situation and agrees that the provisions of the Anti-Terrorism, Crime and Security Bill *specified in the Scottish Executive’s memorandum* that relate to devolved matters should be considered by the UK Parliament.

*S1M-2445.1 Roseanna Cunningham: Anti-Terrorism, Crime and Security Bill - UK Legislation—As an amendment to motion (S1M-2445) in the name of Mr Jim Wallace, leave out from "and agrees" to end and insert "; calls on the Scottish Executive to bring forward legislation dealing with the relevant devolved matters, if necessary under the Parliament’s emergency bill procedures, in order that there can be proper consultation with the relevant authorities, companies, agencies and organisations in Scotland, and furthermore notes with concern the new proposals in regard to detention without trial in clauses 21 to 32 of the Anti-Terrorism, Crime and Security Bill which are a suspension of the normal Scottish criminal justice procedures and which would breach the European Convention on Human Rights.

*S1M-2444.1 Dennis Canavan: The Executive's Vision for the Protection and Promotion of Scotland's Natural Heritage—As an amendment to motion (S1M-2444) in the name of Ross Finnie, insert at end "; and calls on the Executive to recognise that Scotland’s mountains, glens and lochs are not simply the property of the landed gentry but are part of our natural heritage by re-drafting the Land Reform (Scotland) Bill to ensure a genuine right of access to the countryside."

*S1M-2444.2 Bruce Crawford: The Executive's Vision for the Protection and Promotion of Scotland's Natural Heritage—As an amendment to motion (S1M-2444) in the name of Ross Finnie, leave out from ", and agrees" to end and insert "and the many open ended commitments in the policy statement The Nature of Scotland; calls on the Executive to publish a timetable for the introduction of a Wildlife Crime Bill and a modernised system for the protection and management of Sites of Special Scientific Interest; records its concerns over the portfolios of rural development and environment continuing to be the responsibility of the same Minister, and further calls on the Executive to adopt an integrated "stewardship" approach to the development and implementation of its policies in regard to the natural heritage and resources of Scotland."

*S1M-2444.3 John Scott: The Executive's Vision for the Protection and Promotion of Scotland's Natural Heritage—As an amendment to motion (S1M-2444) in the name of Ross Finnie, leave out from "and promote" to end and insert "Scotland’s wildlife and welcomes their proposals to promote and continue to protect Scotland’s natural heritage; recognises the conservation practices of previous generations; supports the need for an overall balance to be struck which recognises the needs of local communities, land owners, occupiers, managers and conservationists alike, and urges Scottish Natural Heritage to take more account of local interests and knowledge and to consult more widely before designating further Sites of Special Scientific Interest."

*S1M-2444.4 Robin Harper: The Executive's Vision for the Protection and Promotion of Scotland's Natural Heritage—As an amendment to motion (S1M-2444) in the name of Ross Finnie, leave out from "notes" to end and insert "calls upon the Executive to publish a draft Bill on this issue before the end of 2001 with a view to legislation being enacted before July 2002; notes the widespread support for this proposed reform and therefore calls upon the Scottish Executive to set up a working group, which includes members of the Transport and the Environment Committee, non-governmental organisations and other experts as a matter of urgency to co-ordinate the drafting and wider consultation on the Bill; agrees that the improved protection of nature requires a combination of both new legislation and integrated land use policies and incentives currently being developed by the Executive; further notes that the protection of Scotland’s natural heritage will require the practice of environmentally sustainable development to be applied across all departments of the Executive, and, to that end, calls for the Ministerial Group on Sustainable Scotland and the Executive’s Sustainable Development Unit to be given a central role within the Executive and for the re-instatement of a dedicated Environment Minister with responsibility for championing sustainable development."

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan*
