Business Bulletin No. 80/2009: Wednesday 13 May 2009
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 12 May 2009

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-23879 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people have (a) applied to become police constables and (b) been accepted into the probationer police constable scheme in each of the last 10 years, broken down by age at time of application.

S3W-23880 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people have (a) applied to become police constables and (b) been accepted into the probationer police constable scheme in each of the last 10 years, broken down by gender.

S3W-23881 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what details on the profile of people who apply to become police constables are stored by the police.

S3W-23882 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what the successful completion rates have been for probationer police constables in each of the last 10 years, broken down by course.

S3W-23883 Shirley-Anne Somerville: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are plans to extend eligibility for the free influenza vaccination programme to pregnant women and breastfeeding mothers.

S3W-23884 Shirley-Anne Somerville: To ask the Scottish Executive what recommendations have been made by the Joint Committee on Vaccination and Immunisation regarding the inclusion of pregnant women and breastfeeding mothers in the free influenza vaccination programme.

S3W-23885 Shirley-Anne Somerville: To ask the Scottish Executive what the estimated cost is to the NHS of extending eligibility for the free influenza vaccination programme to pregnant women and breastfeeding mothers.

S3W-23886 Shirley-Anne Somerville: To ask the Scottish Executive what the estimated cost is to the NHS of influenza-related illness in pregnant women and breastfeeding mothers.

S3W-23887 Shirley-Anne Somerville: To ask the Scottish Executive what research it is aware of on the impacts on the child of influenza-related illness during pregnancy and while the mother is breastfeeding.

S3W-23888 Shirley-Anne Somerville: To ask the Scottish Executive what evidence it has of increased risk from influenza-related illness for pregnant women and breastfeeding mothers.

S3W-23889 Shirley-Anne Somerville: To ask the Scottish Executive what evidence it has of the effectiveness of the influenza vaccination in reducing influenza-related illnesses in pregnant women and breastfeeding mothers.

S3W-23890 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive when the results of the next Scottish Student Income, Expenditure and Debt Survey will be published.

S3W-23891 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will take into account when reaching a decision on the proposed Beauly to Denny power line (a) the Health Protection Agency’s acknowledgement of an association between prolonged exposure to intense power frequency magnetic fields and a raised risk of childhood leukaemia, (b) proposed legislation in Germany and Austria in relation to undergrounding 400kV overhead power lines that pass within 200 metres of single homes and 400 metres of residential areas, (c) that the proposed legislation in Germany and Austria could result in the undergrounding of up to 250 kilometres of 400kV power lines in Lower Saxony and Thuringen in Germany, (d) the decision last year to underground 60 kilometres of the interconnector between France and Spain and (e) the decision to remove 52 pylons, 60 metres high, and underground 130 kilometres of overhead lines in east London for the 2012 Olympic Games.

S3W-23892 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive when the final criteria for and listing of Scottish battlefields will be published and whether such a listing will have an influence on any decision to underground the proposed Beauly to Denny power line.

S3W-23893 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive how many individual student loan accounts were passed to debt collection agencies by the Student Loans Company in each year from 2005-06 to 2008-09.

S3W-23894 John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive how many emergency hospital admissions there have been of patients assaulted with a sharp object in each year since 1998-99, broken down by NHS board.

S3W-23895Δ Bob Doris: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will announce the conclusions from its consultation, 16+ Learning Choices: First Step Activity and Financial Support.

S3W-23896 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-19042 by Nicola Sturgeon on 9 January 2009, whether it still expects NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde to complete the Agenda for Change review for its Health Sciences group by the end of June 2009.

S3W-23897 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive how many NHS boards have completed the Agenda for Change review process for health sciences, broken down by board and date of completion.

S3W-23898 Bill Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is aware that "load reduction" letters announcing proposals to reduce employee numbers have been issued to Rolls-Royce plc workers at Inchinnan in Renfrewshire.

S3W-23899 Bill Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what grants it has provided to Rolls-Royce plc and what information it has on any grants provided by the UK Government in relation to the Rolls-Royce facilities at Hillington or the new base at Inchinnan and what conditions relating to employment were associated with any such grants.
