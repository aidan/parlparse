Business Bulletin No. 152/2004

Wednesday 27 October 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-1899 Lewis Macdonald: Making the Most of Scotland’s Forests—That the Parliament recognises the significant contribution which Scotland’s forestry and primary wood processing sector makes to the economy, tourism and jobs; recognises the environmental benefits of the creation and restoration of more than 8,600 hectares of native woodland in the last two years; believes that Scotland’s forests can contribute to the achievement of the Scottish Executive’s renewable energy targets; welcomes the contribution made to delivering greater benefits to communities through 70 partnership agreements between Forestry Commission Scotland and communities and through the setting up of the regional forestry forums and the Strategic Timber Transport Fund, and believes that all stakeholders from the public, private and voluntary sectors should continue to work together to maximise the value of forestry to the economy, the environment and the people of Scotland.

Supported by: Ross Finnie*

*S2M-1898 Malcolm Chisholm: Improving the Quality and Availability of Scotland’s Housing—That the Parliament endorses the Scottish Executive’s commitment to a 30% increase in affordable housing provision by 2007-08 in order to increase the availability of social rented accommodation for homeless people, reduce pressure on waiting lists and help first-time buyers on to the housing ladder; welcomes the introduction of the Housing Quality Standard and the improvements in housing quality that are resulting from substantial community ownership and fuel poverty programmes; recognises the major achievements flowing from devolution for housing in Scotland, such as a modernised and single tenancy in the social rented sector, progressive homelessness legislation and the development of tenant participation and rights, and looks forward to the forthcoming Housing Bill which will raise standards in the private housing sector and strengthen the rights of private sector tenants.

Supported by: Johann Lamont*

*S2M-1897 Ms Sandra White: First ScotRail Anti-Violence Campaign—That the Parliament notes the poster campaign by First ScotRail and the British Transport Police highlighting the physical and verbal abuse that many rail workers face in the course of their working lives; believes that nobody should have to endure the threat of violence at work and that drawing attention to this problem and educating people about the consequences of their actions is a positive step, and congratulates First Scotrail and the British Transport Police for taking the first step towards tackling this serious problem.

*S2M-1896# Ms Sandra White: Proposals to Deregulate Gambling—That the Parliament notes with concern the recent publication of the Gambling Bill by Her Majesty’s Government; notes that the deregulation of gambling proposed in this Bill will lead to more casinos being built and that it is highly likely that more problem gambling will result; further notes with concern the dismissal of the legitimate concerns of those opposed to the proposals by Tessa Jowell, the Secretary of State for Culture, Media and Sport, as being "a whiff of snobbery", and believes that such comments are unworthy of a government minister and should be retracted.

*S2M-1893# Frances Curran: Equal Pay—That the Parliament welcomes the comments of the Secretary of State for Trade and Industry, Patricia Hewitt, that getting rid of "career sexism" is vital to closing the gender pay gap but regrets that the Scottish Executive utterly failed the nursery nurses on this very issue during their three-and-a-half-month-long strike over pay; notes that this group of workers, 95% of whom are women, have suffered "career sexism" for far too long resulting in pay discrimination; considers that the Executive should be ashamed of itself for sitting on its hands and doing nothing when over 4,000, mainly women, workers took to the streets, and fully supports any nursery nurses who take up the advice of the Equal Opportunities Commission to pursue the issue of pay equality through employment tribunals.

*S2M-1883 Mr John Swinney: Release of the Film "Finding Neverland"—That the Parliament welcomes the release on 29 October 2004 of the film "Finding Neverland" which charts the inspiration of J M Barrie in writing Peter Pan; welcomes the association of this film with Barrie’s birthplace in the town of Kirriemuir, Angus; pays tribute to the work of local historians and the local community to sustain the deep relationship between Barrie and Kirriemuir, and looks forward to the film generating renewed interest in the significance of Kirriemuir in inspiring the work of J M Barrie.

*S2M-1882.1 Stewart Stevenson: Tackling Drugs Misuse: Protecting Scotland’s Communities—As an amendment to motion (S2M-1882) in the name of Cathy Jamieson, leave out from "commends" to end and insert "agrees that an integrated approach to reducing the supply of drugs, protecting communities from drug-related offending and improving education and information about the risks from drugs is required and that this must provide an increased range and effectiveness of local drug treatment and rehabilitation services to help people to become free from drug dependence which is available at the time each service user takes the step of acknowledging the need for help."

*S2M-1882.2 Miss Annabel Goldie: Tackling Drugs Misuse: Protecting Scotland’s Communities—As an amendment to motion (S2M-1882) in the name of Cathy Jamieson, leave out from "commends" to end and insert "deplores the escalating drugs abuse problem in Scotland and the implicit reliance on methadone harm reduction programmes and calls for a radical change in policy to reduce demand for drugs by providing a route to abstinence and taking more effective action to cut the supply of drugs."

*S2M-1882.3 Ms Rosemary Byrne: Tackling Drugs Misuse: Protecting Scotland’s Communities—As an amendment to motion (S2M-1882) in the name of Cathy Jamieson, leave out from "commends" to end and insert "views with concern the growing problem in our communities due to drug misuse; views the current provision for drug treatment and support as entirely inadequate, and urges the Scottish Executive to work with all agencies with a view to integrating services and ensuring full resourcing allowing a range of appropriate services across communities."
