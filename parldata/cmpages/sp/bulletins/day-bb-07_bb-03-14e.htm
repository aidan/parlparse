Business Bulletin No. 48/2007: Wednesday 14 March 2007
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 13 March 2007

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-32351 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to ensure that all doctors applying under the Modernising Medical Careers recruitment and selection process get an interview for a Scottish appointment.

S2W-32352 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to reassure junior doctors that, under the Modernising Medical Careers programme, they will have some choice about the location of their training posts.

S2W-32353 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it satisfied with the Modernising Medical Careers recruitment and selection process.

S2W-32354 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many complaints have been received from junior doctors about the Modernising Medical Careers recruitment and selection process.

S2W-32355 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many junior doctors it expects to be unemployed as a result of Modernising Medical Careers recruitment and selection process.

S2W-32356 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what contact it has had with the UK Steering Group on Less Lethal Weapons and how its Justice Department is represented on this group.

S2W-32357 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has considered, or is considering, the extension of taser use and whether it has been asked by any organisations in Scotland to consider extending the use of tasers.

S2W-32358 Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to consult and involve the communities of South Queensferry and West Lothian in respect of decisions about the type and location of a further Forth crossing.

S2W-32359 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive which NHS boards offer routine 20-week foetal anomaly scans as provided for under NHS Quality Improvement Scotland (NHS QIS) guidelines set in 2004.

S2W-32360 Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what input the Crown Office has had in discussions about the definition of aggression in the Statute of the International Criminal Court.

S2W-32361 Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive on how many occasions in the last 20 years the Crown Office has considered prosecutions for war crimes and on how many of these occasions charges were (a) made and (b) not made.

S2W-32362Δ Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive what decisions have been reached on future recycling rates and infrastructure to treat residual waste.

S2W-32363Δ Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will give details of any changes to the income assessment threshold for loan support to part-time students in higher education that will apply for the academic year 2007-08.

S2W-32364 Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive what the source of funding is for the £500,000 Scottish Music Futures Fund and whether the funding is new money or existing money diverted from (a) the creative industries budget or (b) another budget.

S2W-32365 Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive what proportion of household waste was recycled in each local authority area (a) five years ago and (b) at the most recent date for which figures are available.

S2W-32366 Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration has been given to providing one midwife per mother throughout labour.

S2W-32367 Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive what initiatives it has put in place to improve care during pregnancy and post-natal care.

S2W-32368 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive how the Access for All fund is being used to improve railway stations in Scotland.

S2W-32369 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what survey work has been carried out on railway stations to assess their accessibility.

S2W-32370 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will publish the results of any surveys undertaken to assess the accessibility of railway stations.

S2W-32371 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria were used to determine the order in which railway stations would have works to improve accessibility.

S2W-32372 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive why the Scottish Ambulance Service is not subject to a regular inspection of its functions and performance in the same way as prisons, police forces and schools.

S2W-32373 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive whether ministers received a copy of the findings of the recent staff survey undertaken by the Scottish Ambulance Service.

S2W-32376 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any information from medical and official literature which suggests that the eight-minute response time target for ambulance crews is not evidence-based and may be placing patients and ambulance crews at risk and, if so, what the sources of this information are.

S2W-32377 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to review the current performance indicators used by the Scottish Ambulance Service to take account of the number of stages between a patient’s call for assistance and safe arrival at hospital and to better reflect quality of care for patients.

S2W-32378 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what input frontline ambulance staff had in setting the existing performance indicators for the Scottish Ambulance Service.

S2W-32379 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it expects to publish the findings of its review of NHS prescription charges and exemption arrangements prior to dissolution of the Parliament.
