Business Bulletin No.30/2006: Thursday 23 February 2006
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 2 March 2006

Question Time

General

1. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to announce the results of the Tolled Bridges Review. (S2O-9159)

2. Cathie Craigie: To ask the Scottish Executive when the report of the local public inquiry into the proposals to upgrade the A80 will be presented to ministers and be available to the general public. (S2O-9110)

3. Mr Andrew Arbuckle: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it will give to enforce existing legislation on roadside litter. (S2O-9164)

4. Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to ensure that every young person in Scotland has access to either education or training. (S2O-9115)

5. Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to promote economic development in Ayrshire. (S2O-9137)

6. Susan Deacon: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to reach a decision concerning the relocaton of the head office of Registers of Scotland. (S2O-9111)

7. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in the design of the footbridge at Harthill service station. (S2O-9140)

8. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether residents of north-west Sutherland will be able to access discounted flights from Wick Airport. (S2O-9124)

9. Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to ensure that its relocation decisions offer greater benefit to communities throughout Scotland. (S2O-9121)

10. Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that the current revenue funding to universities adequately reflects the costs of operating multi-campus operations such as the Crichton in Dumfries. (S2O-9104)

Environment and Rural Development

1. Carolyn Leckie: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans its Environment and Rural Affairs Department has to ensure that an environmental impact assessment is undertaken of the proposed Rolls-Royce aero engine test bed site at East Kilbride and to monitor its impact subsequently. (S2O-9155)

2. Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is working with other agencies to ensure that skills and training in the fishing sector are maintained. (S2O-9107)

3. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what advice it has given to poultry farmers about avian flu. (S2O-9146)

4. Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Water Environment (Controlled Activities) (Scotland) Regulations 2005 are intended to help reduce the culverting and covering over of streams. (S2O-9114)

5. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether, in the event of an outbreak of avian flu, it will undertake an emergency vaccination programme to curb the spread of the virus in poultry flocks surrounding the affected area and what preparations it has made to facilitate such a programme. (S2O-9171)

6. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will clarify its funding policy in respect of organic farming. (S2O-9112)

7. Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Minister for Environment and Rural Development last met officials from NFU Scotland and what matters were discussed. (S2O-9150)

8. Mr Mark Ruskell: To ask the Scottish Executive what role it will play in the issue of ship-to-ship oil transfers in the Firth of Forth following any decision by the Maritime and Coastguard Agency to approve Forth Ports Authority’s oil spill contingency plan. (S2O-9172)

9. Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment its Environment and Rural Affairs Department has carried out of the climate change implications of building the Aberdeen Western Peripheral Route. (S2O-9174)

10. Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to cultivate a biomass industry in Scotland. (S2O-9166)

Health and Community Care

1. Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to tackle waiting lists for free personal and nursing care. (S2O-9128)

2. Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive on what criteria the locations of the proposed health care pilots were determined. (S2O-9130)

3. Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will instigate a commission to investigate the impact on health of cannabis and other drugs. (S2O-9153)

4. Jim Mather: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of senior staff in NHS Western Isles are currently suffering from stress-related illnesses, are on long-term sick leave or are involved in grievance procedures and how these figures compare with the average percentages for each category in the other NHS boards. (S2O-9142)

5. Mr Jim Wallace: To ask the Scottish Executive how many petitions it has received from Orkney opposing the proposed withdrawal of the Kirkwall-based BN Islander air ambulance; which particular areas of Orkney the petitions have come from, and how many signatures in total are on the petitions. (S2O-9162)

6. Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied with the provision of NHS dentistry services in rural Scotland. (S2O-9152)

7. Not Lodged

8. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what benefits patients in Edinburgh can expect from the most recent announcement on NHS waiting times. (S2O-9108)

9. Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to meet the targets set to reduce alcohol consumption by adults and children. (S2O-9132)

10. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to address any issues in respect of increased waiting times to see epilepsy specialists resulting from a review of epilepsy patients as recommended in the new General Medical Services contracts. (S2O-9175)
