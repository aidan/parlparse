Business Bulletin No. 69/2002

Tuesday 30 April 2002

Section A : Daily Business List
  

Meetings of Committees

All meetings take place in Committee Chambers, Edinburgh unless otherwise indicated.

Equal Opportunities Committee

9th Meeting, 2002

The Committee will meet at 10.15 am in Committee Room 1

1. Items in Private: The Committee will consider whether to take items 5, 6, 7 and 8 in private.

2. Budget Process 2003/04: The Committee will take evidence from—

Bob Benson, Adam Gaines, Heather Fisken, Disability Rights Commission

Wladyslaw Mejka, Disabled Persons Housing Service

Tim Hopkins, Equality Network

Rozanne Foyer, Tracey White, Scottish Trades Union Congress.

3. Monitoring the Impact of the Housing (Scotland) Act 2001: The Committee will consider the Social Justice Committee’s 3rd Report 2002: Monitoring the Impact of the Housing (Scotland) Act 2002.

4. Reporters: The Committee will hear from its Reporters.

5. Budget Process 2003/04: The Committee will consider a paper on the budget process 2003/04.

6. Equalities Framework: The Committee will consider the Scottish Parliament’s draft Equalities Framework.

7. Work Programme: The Committee will consider a paper on its work programme.

8. Civic Participation in Aberdeen: The Committee will consider a paper on civic participation whilst the Parliament is in Aberdeen.

Justice 2 Committee

15th Meeting, 2002

The Committee will meet at 10.30 am in Langs, Port Dundas Place, Glasgow

1. Inquiry into the Crown Office and Procurator Fiscal Service (in private): The Committee will consider evidence to date and discuss the Committee’s emerging conclusions on the inquiry into the Crown Office and Procurator Fiscal Service.

Subordinate Legislation Committee

14th Meeting, 2002

The Committee will meet at 11.15 am in Committee Room 3

1. Executive Responses: The Committee will consider the responses from the Scottish Executive to points raised on the following—

The Loch Ewe, Isle of Ewe, Wester Ross, Scallops Several Fishery (Variation) Order 2002, (SSI 2002/185)

The Little Loch Broom Scallops Several Fishery Order 2002, (SSI 2002/186)

The Road Traffic (Permitted Parking Area and Special Parking Area) (City of Glasgow) Designation Amendment Order 2002, (SSI 2002/187)

The Road Traffic (Permitted Parking Area and Special Parking Area) (City of Edinburgh) Designation Amendment Order 2002, (SSI 2002/188).

2. Draft instruments subject to approval: The Committee will consider the following—

The Valuation and Rating (Exempted Classes) (Scotland) Order 2002, (draft).

3. Instruments subject to annulment: The Committee will consider the following—

The Home Zones (Scotland) Regulations 2002, (SSI 2002/177)

The Registration of Fish Farming and Shellfish Farming Businesses Amendment (Scotland) Order 2002, (SSI 2002/193)

The Animals and Animal Products (Import and Export) (Scotland) Amendment (No.2) Regulations 2002, (SSI 2002/196).

4. Instruments not subject to Parliamentary control: The Committee will consider the following—

The Food Protection (Emergency Prohibitions) (Amnesic, Paralytic and Diarrhetic Shellfish Poisoning) (Orkney) (Scotland) Revocation Order 2002, (SSI 2002/197)

The Food Protection (Emergency Prohibitions) (Amnesic Shellfish Poisoning) (West Coast) (No.12) (Scotland) Revocation Order 2002, (SSI 2002/198).

Justice 1 Committee

14th Meeting, 2002

The Committee will meet at 1.45 pm in Committee Room 1

1. Prison Estates Review (in private): The Committee will consider lines of questioning for witnesses.

2. Prison Estates Review: The Committee will take oral evidence from—

Phil Hornsby, General Secretary, Prison Services Union,

Bill McKinlay, Governor, HM Prison Barlinnie, and

Ian Bannatyne, Governor, HM Prison Low Moss.

3. Petition: The Committee will consider the following petition—

PE494 by HM Prison Peterhead prisoners.

Audit Committee

7th Meeting, 2002

The Committee will meet at 2.00 pm in Committee Room 3

1. Items in Private: The Committee will consider whether to take in private agenda items 2, 4, 5 and 6. It will also decide whether to consider its draft report on public accounts and receive a briefing from the Auditor General for Scotland on his Further Education Overview Report in private at future meetings.

2. Public Accounts: The Committee will consider lines of questioning for the witnesses at agenda item 3.

3. Public Accounts: The Committee will take evidence from—

Dr Peter Collings, Principal Finance Officer, Scottish Executive

Mr David Reid, Assistant Director of Finance, Scottish Executive

on the 2000/01 accounts of the following bodies:

Scottish Prisons Service

Scottish Natural Heritage

Scottish Homes

Scottish Qualifications Authority.

4. Public Accounts: The Committee will consider the evidence taken at agenda item 3.

5. Community Care in Scotland: The Committee will receive a briefing from Barbara Hurst, Director of Performance Audit, Audit Scotland on Community Care in Scotland.

6. Overview of the National Health Service in Scotland 2000/01: The Committee will consider a revised draft report on its inquiry into the report by the Auditor General for Scotland entitled ‘Overview of the National Health in Scotland 2000/01’ (AGS/2001/10).

2. Budget Process 2003-4: The Committee will take oral evidence on the Scottish Executive's Annual Expenditure Report from—

12th Meeting, 2002

The Committee will meet at 2.00 pm in Committee Room 2

Rural Development Committee

12th Meeting, 2002

The Committee will meet at 2.00 pm in the Hub, Castlehill, Edinburgh

1. Integrated Rural Development: The Committee will take evidence on its inquiry into integrated rural development from the following—

John Kinnaird (National Farmers Union of Scotland)

Jonathan Hall (Scottish Landowners Federation)

Ian Rideout (Scottish Crofting Foundation)

David Henderson Howat (Forestry Commission)

John Mayhew (The National Trust for Scotland)

Fiona Newcombe (Royal Society for the Protection of Birds (Scotland))

John Thomson (Scottish Natural Heritage)

Adam Harrison (WWF Scotland).

2. Subordinate Legislation: The Committee will consider the following instruments under the negative procedure—

The Less Favoured Area Support Scheme (Scotland) Regulations 2002 (SSI 2002/139)

The Plant Health (Great Britain) Amendment (Scotland) Order 2002 (SSI 2002/164)

The Import and Export Restrictions (Foot-and-Mouth Disease) (Scotland) (No.3) Amendment (No.2) Amendment Regulations 2002 (SSI 2002/169)

The Artificial Insemination of Cattle (Animal Health) (Scotland) Amendment Regulations 2002 (SSI 2002/191).

3. Petition PE480: The Committee will consider PE480 from Mr William R Campbell, calling for the Scottish Parliament to urge the Scottish Executive to review the current situation at Scottish Agricultural College Auchincruive as a matter of urgency.

4. Petition PE187: The Committee will consider PE187 from the Scottish Gamekeepers Association, calling for the Scottish Parliament to allow limited licensed culling of raptors under the terms of the 1981 Wildlife and Countryside Act in areas where local populations have increased beyond normal levels.

5. Budget Process 2003-04 (in private): The Committee will consider a draft report.
