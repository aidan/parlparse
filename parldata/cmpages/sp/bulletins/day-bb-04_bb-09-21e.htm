Business Bulletin No. 134/2004

Tuesday 21 September 2004

Section E : Written questions lodged on Monday 20 September 2004

  

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-10787 Mr Bruce McFee: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-10060 by Peter Peacock on 13 September 2004, what educational standards require to be met by institutions providing educational services to local authorities.

S2W-10788 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to ensure that Scottish Executive Education Department Circular 2/2001 is implemented by local education authorities.

S2W-10789 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body what insurance protection is in place for the personal property of those employed to work at Holyrood.

S2W-10790 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to address the issue of recruitment and retention of social care staff in the voluntary sector.

S2W-10791 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will increase the training support given to social care employers in the voluntary sector.

S2W-10792 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to enable the original 30% target for substantial improvements in energy efficiency under the Home Energy Conservation Act 1995 to be met.

S2W-10793 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will introduce mandatory targets for local authorities to improve domestic energy efficiency under the Home Energy Conservation Act 1995.

S2W-10794 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to promote debating in schools as both a curricular and extra-curricular activity.

S2W-10795 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people were employed in agriculture in each of the last five years, giving the percentage change on a year-on-year basis.

S2W-10796 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average student debt in Scotland has been in each of the last five years.

S2W-10797 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how many deaths as a result of cold temperatures there were in each of the last five years, broken down by NHS board area and giving the percentage change on a year-on-year basis.

S2W-10798 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how many teachers were employed in each local authority area in each of the last five years, giving the percentage change on a year-on-year basis.

S2W-10799 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will publish its response to the review of drug rehabilitation services.

S2W-10800 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to change the formula for calculating police force funding.

S2W-10801 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what importance it places on having a fully-functioning health service in areas seeking to attract economic development.

S2W-10802 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has made provision for any expense likely to be incurred when communities seek to establish township boundaries ahead of launching bids for community land.

S2W-10803 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it offers to non-social work qualified social care staff.

S2W-10804 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what its plans are in respect of consulting the voluntary sector collectively on social care education and provision.

S2W-10805 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on registration requirements for social care provision in the voluntary sector.

S2W-10806 Campbell Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive what the reasons are for the main beach at Largs continuing not to reach European bathing water standards, why the reasons were not successfully addressed after previously not reaching the standards; and what steps it will now take to address outstanding issues and ensure that in future the beach reaches and surpasses such standards.

S2W-10807 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people in (a) each local authority area and (b) total were defined as living in poverty in each of the last five years, expressed also as a percentage of the population and giving the percentage change on a year-on-year basis.

S2W-10808 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how many pensioners in (a) each local authority and (b) total were defined as living in poverty in each of the last five years, expressed also as a percentage of the pensioner population and giving the percentage change on a year-on-year basis.

S2W-10809 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how many children in (a) each local authority and (b) total were defined as living in a state of poverty in each of the last five years, expressed also as a percentage of the child population and giving the percentage change on a year-on-year basis.

S2W-10810 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how many children in (a) each NHS board area and (b) total were classified as clinically obese in each of the last five years, expressed also as a percentage of the child population and giving the percentage change on a year-on-year basis.

S2W-10811 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people in (a) each NHS board area and (b) total were classified as clinically obese in each of the last five years, expressed also as a percentage of the population and giving the percentage change on a year-on-year basis.

S2W-10812 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how many hours of exercise the average child undertakes weekly.

S2W-10813 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how many hours of exercise the average adult undertakes weekly.

S2W-10814 Colin Fox: To ask the Scottish Executive which pharmaceutical companies are the largest suppliers to the NHS in respect of (a) volume and (b) annual expenditure.

S2W-10815 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body, further to the answer to question S2W-10103 by George Reid on 20 September 2004, whether there will be a ballot for MSPs’ staff to participate in the Parliament’s opening day as guests on the same basis as for its staff.

S2W-10816 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-9485 by Hugh Henry on 28 July 2004, how many drug-related deaths there were in 2003 (a) broken down by NHS board area and (b) in total.

S2W-10817 Mr Ted Brocklebank: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-9829 by Nicol Stephen on 12 August 2004, what information it has regarding what regular safety checks are made on the structure of the Tay Rail Bridge and how often such checks are made.

S2W-10818 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how many grams of fat are eaten by the average child daily.

S2W-10819 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how many grams of fat are recommended to be eaten by the average child daily.

S2W-10820 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how many grams of fat are eaten by the average adult daily.

S2W-10821 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how many grams of fat are recommended to be eaten by the average adult daily.

S2W-10822 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how many NHS doctors were employed in (a) each NHS board area and (b) total in each of the last five years.

S2W-10823 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how many NHS nurses were employed in (a) each NHS board area and (b) total in each of the last five years.

S2W-10824 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how many NHS dentists were employed in (a) each NHS board area and (b) total in each of the last five years.

S2W-10825 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how many direct air links are available from Scotland to the EU accession states and how many new links to these states are being discussed or developed.

S2W-10826 John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive what the reasons are for delay in implementing the parts of the Agricultural Holdings (Scotland) Act 2003 that allow tenant farmers the right to buy.
