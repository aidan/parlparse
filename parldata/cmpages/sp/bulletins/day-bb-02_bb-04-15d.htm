Business Bulletin No. 58/2002

Monday 15 April 2002

Section D : Oral Questions
  

Question Time

1. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to increase support to the Return to Learn schemes. (S1O-5015)

2. Mr Duncan Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to promote tourism. (S1O-5022)

3. Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many accountancy firms have been employed to carry out work on its behalf; which firms have been so employed; how much has been paid to the firm which was employed most often, and how much has been paid in accountancy fees in total, all since May 1999. (S1O-5024)

4. Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what assistance is currently being provided to chronic smokers aged under 16 years old. (S1O-5018)

5. Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to meet the target for the provision of extra teachers in the classroom envisaged in the McCrone settlement. (S1O-5016)

6. Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive when the latest report by the Chief Inspector of Prisons into HM Prison Kilmarnock is due to be published. (S1O-5013)

7. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Minister for Environment and Rural Development last met the Chief Executive of Scottish Natural Heritage and what matters were discussed. (S1O-5023)

8. Bill Aitken: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to amend the provisions of the rule of Scots law preventing imprisonment before trial for more than 110 days. (S1O-5007)

9. Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive what contribution it is making to Autism Awareness Year 2002. (S1O-5026)

10. Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what opportunities currently exist in Scottish hospitals for newly qualified junior house officers. (S1O-5028)

11. Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to tackle youth disorder issues. (S1O-5037)

12. Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding it is providing to the Scottish Maritime Museum in 2002-03. (S1O-5029)

13. Elaine Thomson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to improve pupil attainment in secondary schools. (S1O-5030)

14. Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prison officers currently working at HM Prison Peterhead have worked there for three years or more. (S1O-5014)

15. Mr Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the hotel and bed and breakfast sector is being robustly marketed. (S1O-5008)

16. Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether expulsion from a specific area is an accepted sentencing option for the courts. (S1O-5025)

17. Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any minister attended the recent global wind power conference in Paris to underline Scotland’s position as the prime wind energy generation site in Europe. (S1O-5019)

18. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on the wind up of the Scottish Transport Group pension funds and the making of ex-gratia payments to pensioners. (S1O-5010)

19. Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has been required to change its strategy on boosting golf-related tourism following the events of 11 September 2001. (S1O-5021)

20. Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to support exporters. (S1O-5038)

21. Cathie Craigie: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of the recent No Speeding Day, what steps are being taken to ensure that messages on safe driving are successfully targeting younger drivers. (S1O-5039)

22. Elaine Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to improve the professional standing of those working in, or entering, all areas of the childcare sector. (S1O-5036)

23. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made at the Beatson Oncology Centre. (S1O-5020)

24. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many men aged over 50 are employed in public libraries. (S1O-5011)

25. Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to prevent unused prescription drugs from being unnecessarily destroyed. (S1O-5000)

26. Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive how many crimes attributed to sectarianism were reported to the police and led to the conviction of the perpetrators in the last 10 years. (S1O-5006)

27. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to make any further information available in support of its recommendation on the future of HM Prison Peterhead. (S1O-5003)

28. Kate Maclean: To ask the Scottish Executive what it is doing to ensure that its domestic abuse campaign messages are accessible to hearing impaired people. (S1O-5001)

29. Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it has made in implementing the National Waste Strategy. (S1O-5027)

30. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive what future improvements in Glasgow’s housing stock can be expected following the vote in favour of the transfer of the stock to the Glasgow Housing Association. (S1O-5032)
