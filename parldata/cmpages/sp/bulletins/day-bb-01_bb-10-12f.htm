Business Bulletin No. 146/2001

Friday 12 October 2001

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

The full text of all outstanding motions and amendments will appear in the Business Bulletin every Monday.

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-2321# Mr Jamie Stone: Winter Roads Maintenance Programme in the Highlands—That the Parliament notes with concern the continuing reduction in the winter roads maintenance programme in remote parts of the Highlands and asks the Scottish Executive to look into this issue as a matter of urgency before the onset of winter.

*S1M-2320 Mr Murray Tosh: Water Charges Relief Scheme—That the Parliament notes the unanimous recommendations of the Transport and the Environment Committee Report on Inquiry into Water and the Water Industry, that "a new more targeted relief scheme" from water charges should be established for defined voluntary organisations and charities, that the existing relief scheme should be continued until a new scheme has been put in place and that the proposed Water Services (Scotland) Bill offers an opportunity to introduce the necessary legislation and urges the Scottish Executive to introduce such legislation, extending the existing scheme as an interim measure in order to ensure a permanent Scotland-wide relief scheme at appropriate and uniform levels for charities and voluntary organisations, including churches.

*S1M-2319# Rhoda Grant: Future of Highland Air Services—That the Parliament welcomes the Scottish Executive’s support for a public service obligation on the Inverness to Gatwick air route; recognises the importance of this route for many businesses and communities in the Highlands, and believes that the Scottish Executive should continue to pursue this issue at both UK and European levels to ensure that a direct link between the Highlands and London is maintained.

*S1M-2318# Colin Campbell: The First Scottish Ecumenical Assembly—That the Parliament congratulates the first Scottish Ecumenical Assembly on the themed statement that it agreed in Edinburgh in September 2001 and endorses their intention to work towards a world based on compassion, fairness and justice.

*S1M-2317# Richard Lochhead: Funding for Grampian Police—That the Parliament recognises the concern held throughout the north east of Scotland in respect of the funding formula for police services that leaves Grampian Police with considerably less than its fair share of the national allocation; believes that the Scottish Executive should make public any comparative studies carried out in relation to police resources, and further believes that appropriate steps should be taken to increase the level of resources available to the force in light of the high crime rate, escalating drug misuse and additional responsibilities that have an impact on policing in the region.

*S1M-2316# Bruce Crawford: Reversal of Her Majesty's Government's Decision on Mixed Oxide Fuel Plant at Sellafield, Cumbria—That the Parliament supports the demand of the Irish Prime Minister, Bertie Ahern, that Her Majesty’s Government abandon its proposals to allow for the operation of a new mixed oxide fuel (MOX) plant at Sellafield in Cumbria; further supports Mr Ahern’s wish to rid the waters surrounding both Ireland and Scotland of nuclear contamination; notes the intention of the Irish Government to fight the decision "on every front" both legally and politically and believes that the Scottish Executive should use every means at its disposal to persuade Her Majesty’s Government to reverse this dangerous and irresponsible decision.

Supported by: Fiona McLeod*, Andrew Wilson*, Mr Lloyd Quinan*, Mr Gil Paterson*, Mr Adam Ingram*, Mr Kenneth Gibson*, Robin Harper*, Stewart Stevenson*, Shona Robison*, Colin Campbell*, Mr Kenny MacAskill*, Fiona Hyslop*, Mrs Margaret Ewing*

*S1M-2315# Bill Butler: First Anniversary of the Death of Donald Dewar—That the Parliament notes the first anniversary of the death of Donald Dewar, Scotland’s first First Minister, on 11 October 2000 and places on record its admiration for his life and work, in particular his central role in the long campaign for the establishment of a devolved Scottish legislature, which assumed its full legislative powers on 1 July 1999; recognises Donald Dewar’s contribution to Scottish public life, not only as one who held high ministerial office, but also as a wholehearted advocate for the people he represented – firstly as MP for Aberdeen South (1966-70), then as MP for Glasgow Garscadden (1978-1997) and finally for Glasgow Anniesland, as both MP (1997-2000) and MSP (1999-2000); acknowledges that Donald Dewar’s central political objective was to champion the cause of those most in need and to do so unequivocally, without fear of favour, and remembers with affection the life of a man who was devoted to the creation not only of the Parliament itself but of a more just and equal Scotland.

Supported by: Patricia Ferguson*, Johann Lamont*, Robin Harper*, Karen Whitefield*, Cathie Craigie*, Kate MacLean*, Dr Elaine Murray*, Cathy Jamieson*, Trish Godman*, Mr Frank McAveety*, Maureen Macmillan*, Rhoda Grant*, Mrs Mary Mulligan*, Brian Fitzpatrick*, Mike Watson*, Malcolm Chisholm*, Iain Gray*, Helen Eadie*, Mr John Home Robertson*, Des McNulty*

*S1M-2314# Dorothy-Grace Elder: Prevention of the Sell Off of Glasgow Council Housing—That the Parliament deplores the fact that Glasgow’s council house tenants have never been offered any costed alternative to a sell-off of their homes to a scheme dependent on private financiers; regrets that all the propaganda from Glasgow City Council has concentrated on only one proposal; demands that the council presents full and truthful projections for the alternative, namely housing redevelopment remaining with the public authority, with all the financial benefits plus protection for tenants and of council jobs, and questions many aspects of the information being presented to councillors and the public, including the production of two documents which, within days of each other, appeared to remove major problems, including funding and job security, which are still outstanding in the housing stock transfer proposal.

*S1M-2313 Michael Matheson: Falkirk 2008 European Football Championship Bid—That the Parliament notes with disappointment the decision of the Scottish Football Association (SFA) to exclude from its consideration the joint application submitted by Falkirk Football Club and Falkirk Council for the SFA’s bid for the European Football Championship in 2008; congratulates both the club and the council for their vision in pursuing this application; further notes their continued determination to provide a community football stadium within Falkirk, and calls upon the Scottish Executive to support the club and the council in securing the necessary funding for a community football stadium.

Supported by: Andrew Wilson*, Mr Gil Paterson*, Alex Neil*, Linda Fabiani*

*S1M-2311 John Farquhar Munro: Freedom of Information Bill - Skye Bridge Contract—That the Parliament welcomes the introduction of the Freedom of Information Bill and believes that in order to restore faith in public administration, the Scottish Executive should give full disclosure of the Skye Bridge contract to demonstrate the spirit of openness which the Bill sets out to achieve.

Supported by: Irene McGugan*, Richard Lochhead*, Robert Brown*, Robin Harper*, Stewart Stevenson*, Nora Radcliffe*, Brian Adam*

*S1M-2310 John Farquhar Munro: Concessionary Tolls on the Skye Bridge—That the Parliament welcomes the proposed introduction of concessionary bus fares for pensioners across Scotland; recognises that the high cost of crossing the Skye Bridge places a heavy burden on pensioners living on limited incomes, and calls upon the Scottish Executive to investigate the introduction of concessionary tolls on the Skye Bridge to run until the tolls are scrapped altogether.

Supported by: Irene McGugan*, Robert Brown*, Robin Harper*, Stewart Stevenson*, Nora Radcliffe*, Brian Adam*

*S1M-2309# Mrs Lyndsay McIntosh: International Rescue Corps—That the Parliament recognises the work of the International Rescue Corps based in Grangemouth and applauds its ability and willingness to assist in rescue efforts following the recent terrorist atrocities in the United States of America.

S1M-2308 New Reactor at Chapelcross, Nuclear Power Station (lodged on 4 October 2001) Mrs Lyndsay McIntosh*

*S1M-2308.1 Bruce Crawford: New Reactor at Chapelcross Nuclear Power Plant—As an amendment to motion (S1M-2308) in the name of John Scott, leave out from "British" to end and insert "the output of Chapelcross Nuclear Power station is insignificant with regard to energy production in Scotland, being 196MW of power which is only 2% of Scotland’s normal capacity of 9654MW and consequently, believes there is no business case for substantial investment in the station in order to support it beyond its current expected lifespan, and further notes with concern that nuclear electricity production at Chapelcross is a mere by-product of the process of the station’s primary role in the production of weapons-grade tritium to support the Trident weapons system, a system of mass destruction which has no place in a civilised nation.

Supported by: Fiona McLeod*, Andrew Wilson*, Mr Lloyd Quinan*, Mr Gil Paterson*, Mr Adam Ingram*, Robin Harper*
