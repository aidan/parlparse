BUSINESS BULLETIN 165 / 2000

Thursday 2 November 2000

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 9 November 2000

 

 

Question Time

1. Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it is drawing up to address the needs of homeless people over the Christmas period. (S1O-2498)

2. Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people in Scotland have found work through the New Deal. (S1O-2510)

3. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to address the needs of young carers. (S1O-2499)

4. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken to ensure that Great North Eastern Rail services north of Edinburgh are restored. (S1O-2485)

5. Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has for working relationships with local access panels which promote access to the built environment and the countryside for disabled people. (S1O-2500)

6. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the current position is with regard to recent applications for the erection of mobile phone masts. (S1O-2486)

7. Mr Keith Harding: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Minister for Local Government will next meet representatives of the Convention of Scottish Local Authorities and what issues are likely to be discussed. (S1O-2501)

8. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive what its current position is regarding the Scottish Qualifications Authority’s handling of this year’s examinations. (S1O-2496)

9. Mr John Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive what is being done to ensure that there are sufficient flu vaccines to meet the current demand among vulnerable groups. (S1O-2476)

10. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made on improving public transport in rural areas. (S1O-2492)

11. Miss Annabel Goldie: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it proposes to take to increase the employment opportunities for people aged 50 and over. (S1O-2474)

12. Elaine Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to assist women who are returning to work after career breaks. (S1O-2488)

13. Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make representations to Her Majesty’s Government to obtain a level of autonomy for the Scottish Parliament equivalent to or greater than that applying in the Manx Parliament or the States of Jersey. (S1O-2490)

14. Elaine Thomson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to ensure energy efficiency in industry. (S1O-2473)

15. Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what financial provision it makes for essential lifeline bridge projects and what funding is available where structures reach the end of their lifespan. (S1O-2502)

16. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to ensure that local authorities adequately fund post-adoption support services for young people and their families. (S1O-2479)

17. Mike Watson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to ensure that adequate health care is provided for rough sleepers. (S1O-2489)

18. David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has made to Railtrack or any other body about facilitating disabled access from the south-bound platform at Lockerbie station. (S1O-2503)

19. Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to review the Route Action Plan for the A75. (S1O-2481)

20. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive when a scheme will be introduced to help low income families pay their water charges. (S1O-2471)

21. Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has made to Grampian University Hospitals NHS Trust regarding the retention of paediatric services at Dr Gray’s Hospital in Elgin and other hospitals serving rural communities. (S1O-2480)

22. Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met the Department of Social Security and what issues were discussed. (S1O-2493)

23. Mr Duncan Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to address any problems currently affecting the tourism industry in Scotland. (S1O-2505)

24. Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Deputy Minister for Sport and Culture next proposes to meet representatives of the Scottish Arts Council and what issues are likely to be discussed. (S1O-2506)

25. Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to ensure that rural communities benefit from the Scottish Rural Partnership Fund. (S1O-2484)

26. John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive, in the light of recent announcements of up to 250 job losses at Fullarton Computer Industries, 78 job losses at GE Caledonian and the transfer of 30 jobs from Troon to Belfast by Sea Containers, what action it plans to take to stem job losses in Ayr constituency. (S1O-2483)

27. Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive how it intends to address present financial difficulties being experienced by the Relatives Association so that it can continue to support the relatives of those within continuing care establishments. (S1O-2504)

28. Tricia Marwick: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it proposes to reconsider or review the Police (Scotland) Act 1967. (S1O-2491)

29. Bill Aitken: To ask the Scottish Executive, in view of the targets outlined in Towards a Healthier Scotland, what strategy is in place to achieve a reduction in smoking amongst young people. (S1O-2472)

30. Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has made to the British Tourist Authority concerning the promotion of Scotland overseas. (S1O-2477)
