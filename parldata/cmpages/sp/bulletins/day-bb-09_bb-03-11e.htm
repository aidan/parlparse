Business Bulletin No. 44/2009: Wednesday 11 March 2009
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 10 March 2009

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-21701 Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list the organisations representing faith groups that received grants from the Scottish Government equalities budget in each financial year between May 2003 and May 2007, also showing the value of grants received.

S3W-21702 Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list the organisations representing faith groups that have received Scottish Government grants since May 2007 and the value of the grants received.

S3W-21703 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what level of funding has been allocated to raising public awareness of thrombosis in each year since 1998-99.

S3W-21704 Christina McKelvie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many new-build schools have been completed in each year since 1992.

S3W-21705 Christina McKelvie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many schools have been substantially refurbished in each year since 1992.

S3W-21706 Christina McKelvie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many schools have had minor refurbishments since May 2007.

S3W-21707 Christina McKelvie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many school building or refurbishment projects that were completed after May 1997 were planned before May 1997.

S3W-21708 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how much the charitable sector spent on research in universities in (a) 2004-05, (b) 2005-06, (c) 2006-07, (d) 2007-08 and (e) 2008-09, broken down by (i) institution, (ii) charity and (iii) research topic, and when new statistics will be available.

S3W-21709 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how much the charitable sector spent on medical research in universities in (a) 2004-05, (b) 2005-06, (c) 2006-07, (d) 2007-08 and (e) 2008-09, broken down by (i) institution and (ii) charity, and when new statistics will be available.

S3W-21710 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive whether funding to support charity funded research will continue to be allocated by the Scottish Funding Council (a) in 2009-10 and (b) beyond and when new statistics will be available.

S3W-21711 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how much the Scottish Funding Council will allocate to support charity funded research in 2009-10 and when new statistics will be available.

S3W-21712 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how much universities received from the allocation based on charity income under the Main Quality Research Grant (a) for 2009-10 and (b) in each of the last five years, broken down by institution, and when new statistics will be available.

S3W-21713 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding will be available to support the play aspects of the Early Years Framework.

S3W-21714 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will support pilot initiatives on play linked to the Early Years Framework.

S3W-21715 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make the provision of play facilities a statutory requirement on local authorities.

S3W-21716 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will monitor the success of the play aspects of the Early Years Framework.

S3W-21717 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what the reasons are for the decision to increase the points threshold required for successful applications from Northern Isles agri-environment applicants for Rural Priorities funding.

S3W-21718 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive when the decision was made to increase the points threshold required for successful applications from Northern Isles agri-environment applicants for Rural Priorities funding.

S3W-21719 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive whether ministers took the decision to increase the points threshold required for successful applications from Northern Isles agri-environment applicants for Rural Priorities funding.

S3W-21720 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment was made of the impact of the decision to increase the points threshold required for successful applications from Northern Isles agri-environment applicants for Rural Priorities funding on agricultural businesses in the islands.

S3W-21721 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment was made of the impact of the decision to increase the points threshold required for successful applications from Northern Isles agri-environment applicants for Rural Priorities funding on the environment of the islands.

S3W-21722 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what the points threshold required for successful applications from agri-environment applicants for Rural Priorities funding is in each of the 11 regions.

S3W-21723 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive, at the time that the points threshold required for successful applications from Northern Isles agri-environment applicants for Rural Priorities funding was increased, whether those in the other 10 regions also increased.

S3W-21724 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive when each of the regional proposal assessment committees in the 11 regions was informed of the change to the points threshold required for successful applications for Rural Priorities funding in advance of their last round of meetings.

S3W-21725 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-20834 by Fergus Ewing on 5 March 2009, how many offences were committed by the 248 repeat offenders.

S3W-21726 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-20834 by Fergus Ewing on 5 March 2009, how many of the 248 repeat offenders committed (a) two crimes, (b) three crimes, (c) four crimes, (d) five to 10 crimes and (e) more than 10 crimes.

S3W-21727 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prosecutions brought under the Misuse of Drugs Act 1971 were dealt with by a direct measure in 2007-08, broken down by (a) police force area and (b) type of offence.

S3W-21728 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body what evidence it took from the bodies concerned or relevant stakeholders before submitting its proposal to the Review of SPCB Supported Bodies Committee to reduce the number of SPCB supported bodies from six to three.

S3W-21729 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body whether it proposes to carry out a detailed cost-benefit analysis of the potential for saving public funds through the merging of the core administration infrastructure functions of the SPCB supported bodies.

S3W-21730 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body what account it took of the Paris Principles in making recommendations for the reorganisation of the SPCB supported bodies.

S3W-21731 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body whether it considers that the Parliament’s decision to establish a standalone office of the Commissioner for Children and Young People in Scotland to advocate for children and work with them is no longer relevant and, if so, on what basis.

S3W-21732 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body what other options for carrying out the functions of the SPCB supported bodies it considered in making recommendations to the Review of SPCB Supported Bodies Committee.

S3W-21733 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Queen Mother’s Hospital will transfer its services to the Southern General Hospital.

S3W-21734 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive when the new children’s hospital will open at the Southern General Hospital.

S3W-21735 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there will be a time gap between the transfer of services from the Queen Mother’s Hospital to the new children’s hospital at the Southern General Hospital and, if so, what this will be.

S3W-21737 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde has to increase the budget for ambulances to transport neonates to the Royal Hospital for Sick Children at Yorkhill in advance of the new children’s hospital opening at the Southern General Hospital.

S3W-21738 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what the predicted birth rate is in the Greater Glasgow area for 2009-10.

S3W-21739 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive how it monitors progress towards implementation in full of its health improvement, efficiency, access and treatment (HEAT) targets.

S3W-21740 Bill Aitken: To ask the Scottish Executive how many cases have resulted in fiscal fines being imposed since the implementation of summary justice reforms and what the total value is of these fines.

S3W-21741 Bill Aitken: To ask the Scottish Executive how much has been collected in fiscal fines imposed since the implementation of summary justice reforms and in how many cases (a) no payment has been made and (b) payments are overdue.
