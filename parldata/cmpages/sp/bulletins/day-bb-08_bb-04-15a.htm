Business Bulletin No. 58/2008: Tuesday 15 April 2008
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section A – Today’s Business

Meetings of Committees

All meetings take place in the Scottish Parliament, unless otherwise specified. Contact details for Committee Clerks are provided at the end of the Bulletin.

European and External Relations Committee
8th Meeting, 2008

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in Committee Room 1

1. Decision on taking business in private: The Committee will decide whether to take item 3 in private.

2. Inquiry into international development: The Committee will take evidence from—

Jack McConnell MSP;

and then from–

Malcolm Bruce MP, Chair, International Development Committee, House of Commons.

3. Inquiry into transposition of EU directives: The Committee will consider its draft report.

Finance Committee
9th Meeting, 2008

The Committee will meet at 2.00 pm in Committee Room 2

1. Decisions on taking business in private: The Committee will decide whether to take item 6 in private and whether to consider the evidence heard on its inquiry into the methods of funding capital investment projects in private and at future meetings.

2. Inquiry into the methods of funding capital investment projects: The Committee will take evidence, in a round-table discussion, from—

Lynn Brown, Executive Director of Financial Services, Glasgow City Council;

Douglas Griffin, Director of Finance, NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde;

Peter Haggarty, Assistant Director, Health Facilities Scotland;

Guy Houston, Director of Finance and Corporate Services, Transport Scotland;

Riona Bell, Director of Funding, Scottish Funding Council;

Donald McGougan, Director of Finance, Edinburgh City Council; and

Douglas Millican, Finance and Regulation Director, Scottish Water.

3. Scottish Register of Tartans Bill: The Committee will consider its approach to the Financial Memorandum of the Scottish Register of Tartans Bill.

4. Subordinate legislation: The Committee will consider the following negative instrument—

the Public Contracts and Utilities Contracts (Scotland) Amendment Regulations 2008 (SSI 2008/94).

5. Judiciary and Courts (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will consider a response from the Scottish Government on written evidence received on the Financial Memorandum of the Judiciary and Courts (Scotland) Bill.

6. Inquiry into the methods of funding capital investment projects: The Committee will consider the main themes arising from the evidence heard to date, in order to inform the drafting of its report.

Public Petitions Committee
6th Meeting, 2008

The Committee will meet at 2.00 pm in Committee Room 6

1. Consideration of new petitions: The Committee will give consideration to the following new petitions—

PE1135 Review of guidelines to allow healthy gay and bisexual men to donate blood

Rob McDowall will make a brief statement to the Committee in support of his petition accompanied by Mr Nick Henderson and Miss Angela Paton

PE1134 Bond of Caution

PE1136 Trial by jury in criminal and civil cases

2. Consideration of current petitions: The Committee will give further consideration to the following current petitions—

PE504 Publication of criminal memoirs

PE535 and PE888 Institutional child abuse

PE867 Vulnerable adults and surreptitious medication

PE899, PE1106 Victim Notification Scheme

PE908, PE909, PE1007 Traffic regulation orders and disabled parking bays

PE931 Skin cancer

PE1023 Residential care for the elderly

PE1032 Standard of care for the housebound elderly

PE1036 and PE1069 Employment for disabled people

PE1044 Physiotherapists guarantee

PE1061 Access rights under the Land Reform (Scotland) Act 2003

PE1099 Provision of ambulance services

PE1100 Provision of motorcycle facilities

PE1109 Diagnosis and treatment of psoriasis and psoriatic arthritis

PE1110 Review of recruitment policies for foreign teachers

PE1086 and PE1091 NHS hospital car parking charges. The Committee will hear oral evidence from—

Mr Tom Divers OBE, Chief Executive, Greater Glasgow and Clyde NHS Board;

Mr Billy Hunter, Greater Glasgow and Clyde NHS Board;

Mr Gerry Marr, Chief Operating Officer, NHS Tayside; and

Mr Brian Main, Head of Support Services/Site Manager, NHS Tayside

3. Notification of new petitions: The Committee will consider a paper indicating new petitions lodged since the last Committee meeting.

4. Scottish Government’s consultation on 2014 Commonwealth Games - Delivering a lasting legacy for Scotland: The Committee will consider a paper from the Clerk.

Transport, Infrastructure and Climate Change Committee
7th Meeting, 2008

The Committee will meet at 2.00 pm in Committee Room 1

1. Inquiry into Ferry Services in Scotland: The Committee will take evidence on its inquiry into ferry services in Scotland from—

Graham Bell, Press and Policy Officer, Scottish Chambers of Commerce;

and then from—

Stewart Wood, Vice President and Lisa Webb, Regional Manager, National Farmers’ Union;

Libby Woodhatch, Chief Executive, Seafood Scotland;

and then from—

David Eaglesham, Policy Adviser, Road Haulage Association; and

Gavin Scott, Head of Policy, Freight Transport Association.

2. Subordinate Legislation: The Committee will consider the following negative instruments—

the Road Works (Scottish Road Works Register, Notices, Directions and Designations) (Scotland) Regulations 2008 (SSI 2008/88); and

the Road Works (Settlement of Disputes and Appeals against Directions) (Scotland) Regulations 2008 (SSI 2008/89).

3. Mainstreaming equal opportunities: The Committee will consider a letter from the Convener of the Standards, Procedures and Public Appointments Committee on whether committees should be obliged to report periodically on how they have built equalities considerations into their work.

Subordinate Legislation Committee
12th Meeting, 2008

The Committee will meet at 2.15 pm in Committee Room 5

1. Scottish Government responses: The Committee will consider the Scottish Government’s responses in relation to the following––

the Bankruptcy and Diligence etc. (Scotland) Act 2007 (Commencement No. 3, Savings and Transitionals) Order 2008 (SSI 2008/115);

the Police (Special Constables) (Scotland) Regulations 2008 (SSI 2008/117);

Act of Sederunt (Sheriff Court Rules Amendment) (Diligence) 2008 (SSI 2008/121); and

Act of Sederunt (Rules of the Court of Session Amendment No. 4) (Miscellaneous) 2008 (SSI 2008/123).

2. Instruments subject to annulment: The Committee will consider the following—

the Plastic Materials and Articles in Contact with Food (Scotland) Regulations 2008 (SSI 2008/127);

the Forestry Challenge Funds (Scotland) Regulations 2008 (SSI 2008/135);

the Sexual Offences Act 2003 (Prescribed Police Stations) (Scotland) Regulations 2008 (SSI 2008/128); and

the Eggs and Chicks (Scotland) Regulations 2008 (SSI 2008/129).

3. Instruments not laid before the Parliament: The Committee will consider the following––

the Adoption and Children (Scotland) Act 2007 (Commencement No.1) Order 2008 (SSI 2008/130).
