Business Bulletin No. 159/2009: Tuesday 3 November 2009
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 2 November 2009

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-28562 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many direct measures were issued for (a) assault and (b) assault to injury in September 2009, broken down by sheriff court.

S3W-28563 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many direct measures were issued for (a) assault and (b) assault to injury in October 2009, broken down by sheriff court.

S3W-28564 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive how many invoices from suppliers were paid by its directorates within 10 days of receipt in June 2009.

S3W-28565 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive how many and what percentage of invoices from suppliers were paid by its directorates within 10 days of receipt in July 2009.

S3W-28566 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive how many invoices from suppliers were paid by its directorates within 10 days of receipt in August 2009.

S3W-28567 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive how many and what percentage of invoices from suppliers were paid by its directorates within 10 days of receipt in September 2009.

S3W-28568 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive how many and what percentage of invoices from suppliers were paid by its directorates within 10 days of receipt in October 2009.

S3W-28569 Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to announce the projects that have been successful in the second round of funding from the Town Centre Regeneration Fund.

S3W-28570 Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to publish examples of best practice by local authorities in respect of the allocation of housing on the basis of priority needs and local needs.

S3W-28571 Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to publish new guidance for local authorities on the allocation of housing on the basis of priority needs and local needs.

S3W-28572 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive, in relation to the finding in the 2008/09 Scottish Crime and Justice Survey: First Findings that 69% of adults perceived the crime rate in their local area to have stayed the same or reduced in the last two years, how many perceived it to have (a) stayed the same and (b) reduced.

S3W-28573 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what asssessment it has made of the finding in the 2008/09 Scottish Crime and Justice Survey: First Findings that in 32% of violent crime where the offender had a weapon the victim reported that it was a knife and what action it will take.

S3W-28574 Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive whether an interim report on progress in implementing the Adult Support and Protection (Scotland) Act 2007 will be produced. 

S3W-28575 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding is available from it or its agencies for the creation of high-occupancy-vehicle lanes on trunk roads.

S3W-28576 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to create high-occupancy-vehicle lanes on trunk roads.

S3W-28577 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with NESTRANS regarding the creation of a high-occupancy-vehicle lane on the A90 between Stonehaven and Cairngorm Road in Aberdeen.

S3W-28578 Jackson Carlaw: To ask the Scottish Executive how many NHS prescriptions for antibiotics have been dispensed in each year since 1999, broken down by (a) antibiotic and (b) NHS board.

S3W-28579 Jackson Carlaw: To ask the Scottish Executive how many complaints the NHS has received in each year since 1999, broken down by NHS board.

S3W-28580 Jackson Carlaw: To ask the Scottish Executive how many NHS complaints have been referred to the Scottish Public Services Ombudsman in each year since 1999, broken down by (a) NHS board and (b) outcome of referral.

S3W-28581 Jackson Carlaw: To ask the Scottish Executive how it monitors satisfaction levels among patients who have made a complaint against the NHS and whether it will publish the results of any statistical analysis of satisfaction levels undertaken in each year since 1999, broken down by NHS board.

S3W-28582 Jackson Carlaw: To ask the Scottish Executive what tailored support is available to people who have experienced (a) fertility problems, (b) miscarriage, (c) ectopic pregnancy and (d) stillbirth.

S3W-28583 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason unpaid carers are not included in its influenza A (H1N1) vaccination programme.

S3W-28584 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will ensure that GPs and practice nurses are supported in identifying and managing depression in older people, including psychological and pharmacological approaches.

S3W-28585 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will ensure that people with dementia have access to psychological intervention where appropriate.

S3W-28586 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will increase the number of posts for psychologists with specialist skills to support older people with depression, anxiety or dementia and help reduce the impact of such patients on other NHS services.

S3W-28587 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many vacancies exist for psychologists in each NHS board.

S3W-28588 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what information is available on (a) the effectiveness of treatment of and (b) recovery from (i) drug and (ii) alcohol addiction.

S3W-28589 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people are on waiting lists for treatment of (a) drug and (b) alcohol addiction in each NHS board area.

S3W-28590 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason an optometrist who is registered to work in one NHS board area is not able to work in any other NHS board area without also registering separately with that board.

S3W-28591 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to change the rules on the registration of optometrists with NHS boards to allow an optometrist registered to work in one NHS board area to work in all other NHS board areas without having to register with each board.

S3W-28592 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding will be given to Keep Scotland Beautiful between 2009 and 2011 to run the eco-schools programme.

S3W-28593 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will continue to support the eco-schools programme until 2011.

S3W-28594 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will continue to fund the secondary school development for eco-schools until 2011.

S3W-28595 Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive how many petroleum licences have been issued in each year since 1999, broken down by local authority.

S3W-28596 Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive how many petroleum licences for retail sites have been issued in each year since 1999, broken down by local authority.

S3W-28597 Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive how many petrol stations that received funding under the Rural Petrol Station Grant Scheme are still in operation, broken down by local authority.

S3W-28598 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people with type 2 diabetes are prescribed insulin in each NHS board area.

S3W-28599 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of the note to Table 10 on page 15 of the Scottish Diabetes Survey 2008 that some NHS boards may be reporting some cases of insulin-treated type 2 diabetes as type 1, whether it will estimate the current number of such cases in each NHS board.
