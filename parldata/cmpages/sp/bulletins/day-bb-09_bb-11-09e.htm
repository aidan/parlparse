Business Bulletin No. 163/2009: Monday 9 November 2009
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 6 November 2009

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-28734 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive, following the flooding in the north east on 1 November 2009, what its position is on the concerns expressed by the Fire Brigades Union in a press release on 2 November 2009 that fire service personnel attended incidents and effected rescues while ill-prepared in terms of training and personal protective equipment; whether it intends to take action, and, if so, what action.

S3W-28735 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive, following the flooding in the north east on 1 November 2009, whether it shares the concerns expressed by the Fire Brigades Union in a press release on 2 November 2009 that it is both inappropriate and dangerous that firefighters working waist deep in water were wearing clothing designed to protect them from flame and heat.

S3W-28736 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive, following the flooding in the north east on 1 November 2009, whether it shares the concerns expressed by the Fire Brigades Union in a press release on 2 November 2009 that firefighters were working waist deep in water without the specialist training required to undertake that task safely.

S3W-28737 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive, following the flooding in the north east on 1 November 2009, whether it considers that fire and rescue services are acting in accordance with their own risk assessments and standard operating procedures in committing personnel to these situations.

S3W-28738 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made of compliance with section 5 of The Fire (Additional Function) (Scotland) Order 2005; whether it intends to take action, and, if so, what action.

S3W-28739 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive by what date all primary school children will receive free fruit, as promised in the 2007 SNP manifesto.

S3W-28740 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on clearer labelling and kite marking of foods with a health benefit, as proposed in the 2007 SNP manifesto.

S3W-28741 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made to increase the availability of fresh, nutritious and locally produced food, particularly in areas of deprivation, as promised in the 2007 SNP manifesto.

S3W-28742 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-28387 by Nicola Sturgeon on 5 November 2009, whether it plans to make available to other healthy living centres in Glasgow the £70,000 handed back to it by NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde.

S3W-28743 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance it has issued in relation to (a) banning gagging or similar clauses in NHS contracts, (b) requiring removal of gagging or similar clauses in NHS contracts agreed prior to the coming into force of the Public Interest Disclosure Act 1998 and (c) banning gagging or similar clauses in respect of clinical or patient safety issues from any settlement or termination of employment.

S3W-28744 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive how many fees for public entertainment licences were waived for free events and how many fees for market-operating licences were waived for non-commercial organisations in the last year.

S3W-28745 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is content with the security of supply of medicines to community pharmacies and patients as a consequence of recent changes in the pharmaceutical wholesale network.

S3W-28746 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is content with the reduction in competition in the supply of medicines to community pharmacies as a consequence of recent changes in the pharmaceutical wholesale network.

S3W-28747Δ Bob Doris: To ask the Scottish Executive what the next steps are in its plans to implement the Protecting Vulnerable Groups Scheme.

S3W-28748 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure that small and medium-sized enterprises are given a level playing field in tendering for public sector work.

S3W-28749 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are taken to ensure that the references of tenderers for public sector work are rigorously checked to ensure that the tenderer can deliver the required quality.

S3W-28750 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what evidence there is that changes to procurement processes redress any previous bias in favour of large organisations.

S3W-28751 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has assessed whether more small and medium-sized enterprises are now on approved lists of public sector contractors as a consequence of changes in policy.

S3W-28752 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has assessed whether more small and medium-sized enterprises are winning public sector contracts as a consequence of changes in policy.

S3W-28753 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive whether Scottish Water and other non-departmental public bodies have altered their procurement procedures and arrangements for subcontracting to ensure that small and medium-sized enterprises are given a level playing field in tendering for public sector work.

S3W-28754 Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive, in relation to future fisheries negotiations, what reciprocal fishing rights it expects to be put in place that would allow Scottish fishing vessels to fish in Icelandic fishing grounds and what steps would be taken to ban whaling by Iceland if its bid for EU membership is successful.

S3W-28755 Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions have taken place with the UK Government anent the proposal to register the Atlantic bluefin tuna on the UN Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species.

S3W-28756 Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the European Commission’s proposal for a temporary ban on the trade of Atlantic bluefin tuna and what steps are being taken to ensure that consumers buy only line-caught tuna.

S3W-28757 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason the software required by GP surgeries to help identify priority patients for the influenza A (H1N1) vaccine was not in place sooner.

S3W-28758 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive when it was decided to use GP surgeries to distribute the influenza A (H1N1) vaccine.

S3W-28759 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive whether GPs have all software in place required to help identify priority patients for the influenza A (H1N1) vaccine.

S3W-28760 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive whether GPs are expected to start vaccinating priority patients against influenza A (H1N1) before their Directed Enhanced Services contract is in place.

S3W-28761 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive how much it projects that it will cost to widen the eligibility for Stage Four of the Energy Assistance Package to include homes with a band E energy performance rating.

S3W-28762 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the budget for the Energy Assistance Package will have to be increased in order to widen the eligibility for Stage Four of the package to include homes with a band E energy performance rating.

S3W-28763 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answers to questions S3W-27550 and S3W-28034 by Jim Mather on 30 September and 26 October 2009 respectively, in relation to how many applications for (a) new wind energy developments and (b) extensions to existing wind energy developments received since May 2007 it has failed to meet its target of a decision within nine months where there is no public inquiry.

S3W-28764Δ Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive, following the publication of the UK Government’s response to its clean coal consultation, what its position is in relation to thermal guidance issues and in particular carbon capture and storage.

S3W-28765 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the compatibility of the serving of foie gras with promoting good animal welfare standards.

S3W-28766 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive whether foie gras has been served at any official engagements in 2009 and, if so, whether it will list the date and occasion.

S3W-28767 Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent research there has been on the effectiveness of community planning partnerships.

S3W-28768 Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent research there has been on the extent to which community planning partnerships ensure community involvement.

S3W-28769 Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average value is of payment to kinship carers, broken down by local authority.

S3W-28770 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-27997 by Jim Mather on 4 November 2009, whether it will list the (a) dates of and (b) signatories to correspondence between its ministers and ministers in the Department of Energy and Climate Change about funds in Ofgem’s Fossil Fuel Levy account.

S3W-28771 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-27997 by Jim Mather on 4 November 2009, whether it will list the (a) dates of and (b) signatories to correspondence between its ministers and ministers in the (i) Department of Trade and Industry, (ii) Department of Business, Enterprise and Regulatory Reform and (iii) Department for Business, Innovation and Skills about funds in Ofgem’s Fossil Fuel Levy account.

S3W-28772 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-27997 by Jim Mather on 4 November 2009, whether it will list the (a) dates of and (b) signatories to correspondence between its ministers and Welsh Assembly Government ministers about funds in Ofgem’s Fossil Fuel Levy account.

S3W-28773 Peter Peacock: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has powers to direct Scottish Natural Heritage.
