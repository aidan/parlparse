Business Bulletin No. 93/2010: Thursday 3 June 2010
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 10 June 2010

Question Time

General

1. Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Midlothian Community Hospital will open and what services it will offer. (S3O-10836)

2. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to help newly qualified teachers into work. (S3O-10835)

3. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to respond to the findings by Professor Richard Watt of University College London that people who rarely brush their teeth are 70% more likely to suffer heart disease than those who brush twice a day and his view that these results confirm the link between oral hygiene and the risk of cardiovascular disease. (S3O-10840)

4. Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is taking action to help school nurses resolve outstanding claims under the Agenda for Change programme. (S3O-10856)

5. David Whitton: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to improve affordable warmth. (S3O-10871)

6. Not Lodged

7. Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has had further discussions with the National Trust for Scotland since September 2009 regarding the future of the Bannockburn Heritage Centre. (S3O-10876)

8. Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met representatives of the Scottish Fishermen’s Federation. (S3O-10918)

9. Angela Constance: To ask the Scottish Executive how many GP practices use 084 premium rate numbers and whether it has any plans to actively discourage the use of such numbers by GP practices. (S3O-10892)

10. Alasdair Allan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to assist crofting communities that are facing large increases in their local rabbit population. (S3O-10889)

Finance and Sustainable Growth

1. Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in releasing the funds that have accumulated under the Fossil Fuel Levy. (S3O-10909)

2. Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will reconsider its decision to centralise the book contract for public and school libraries, given concerns expressed by small publishers, librarians and principal teachers. (S3O-10860)

3. Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met representatives of West Lothian Council to discuss transport issues. (S3O-10861)

4. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive how much was paid to its core departmental staff in bonuses in 2008-09. (S3O-10924)

5. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what consultations it has had with the construction industry and how it plans to incorporate the needs of this sector into its economic recovery plan. (S3O-10851)

6. Christina McKelvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to support the growth of the manufacturing sector. (S3O-10907)

7. Stuart McMillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what financial assistance it provides to improve railway stations in the West of Scotland. (S3O-10908)

8. Charlie Gordon: To ask the Scottish Executive how much funding it will contribute toward the Clyde Fastlink Project, broken down by amount, financial year and location. (S3O-10845)

9. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will take action to promote the use of microgeneration technology in corporate residential properties such as care homes. (S3O-10921)

10. Derek Brownlee: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it has made with the powers given to it under the Public Services Reform (Scotland) Act 2010. (S3O-10878)

11. Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it has and is continuing to put in place to protect the manufacturing sector in the Scottish Borders. (S3O-10917)

12. Peter Peacock: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has made to the Chief Secretary to the Treasury regarding petrol prices in rural areas. (S3O-10865)

13. Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with Transport Scotland regarding the consultation on developing rail freight policy in Scotland, and in particular, the timetable for action to develop the Grangemouth freight hub. (S3O-10866)

14. Mr Frank McAveety: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the use of end-year flexibility. (S3O-10853)

15. Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to mitigate the effects of financial cutbacks on public services. (S3O-10915)

16. Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether free school meals, free prescriptions, NHS distinction awards or any other of its policies are protected under the expenditure analysis of the Independent Budget Review. (S3O-10916)

17. Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in cutting journey times by rail from Inverness to Edinburgh. (S3O-10846)

18. David McLetchie: To ask the Scottish Executive what its latest and best estimate is for the total liability still to be met by local authorities in settling equal pay claims. (S3O-10883)

19. Not Lodged

20. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what estimate it has made of the likely reduction in greenhouse gas emissions as a result of the recession. (S3O-10886)
