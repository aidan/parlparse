Business Bulletin No. 65/2011: Thursday 2 June 2011
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 1 June 2011

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S4W-00398 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how obesity is managed for patients denied bariatric surgery due to NHS board restrictions.

S4W-00399 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many patients have received surgery for weight management on the NHS in each of the last 10 years.

S4W-00400 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many patients in each NHS board are under assessment for management of severe and complex obesity.

S4W-00401 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people it estimates to have a body mass index of over 35.

S4W-00402 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of children (a) under five and (b) between five and 15 are estimated to be (i) obese, (ii) severely obese and (iii) morbidly obese.

S4W-00403 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what (a) minimum and (b) maximum time the NHS takes to assess people with severe and complex obesity for bariatric surgery.

S4W-00404 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on how many patients from Scotland have received weight management surgery in other countries in each of the last five years.

S4W-00405 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what is being done to ensure that obese people have fair and equitable access to weight management interventions in line with evidence-based guidelines.

S4W-00406 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration is being given to a national framework of care from prevention to management for severe and complex obesity.

S4W-00407 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what is being done to develop clinical and cost-effective weight management interventions accessible to people in all parts of Scotland.

S4W-00408 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will ensure that early treatment of obesity is available to help prevent obesity-related chronic conditions.

S4W-00409 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what estimated percentage of obese people has comorbidities.

S4W-00410 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether consideration is being given to GP Quality and Outcomes Framework payments for earlier intervention through weight management programmes.

S4W-00411 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive which NHS boards comply with the Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network (SIGN) guideline on the provision of bariatric surgery.

S4W-00412 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many patients in each NHS board area have been denied access to bariatric surgery following initial referral in each of the last three years.

S4W-00413 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the guidance laid out in Circular 38/1992, Disposal of Surplus Government Land - The Crichel Down Rules, remains in place with regard to the exceptions from the obligation to offer surplus government land back to its previous owner.

S4W-00414 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what account the Scottish Medicines Consortium takes of the budgetary impact of the uptake of ultra-orphan medicines when assessing their cost effectiveness.

S4W-00415 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding requests for treatments for ultra-orphan diseases it regards as being exceptional.

S4W-00416 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will issue guidance to ensure that evidence of clinical benefit or suitability is sufficient for patients with rare diseases to receive treatment through the exceptional funding process.

S4W-00417 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide an update on progress on developing a national strategy for a schools counselling service.

S4W-00418 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what national evaluation it has made of psychological therapy services.

S4W-00419 Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to ensure that patient safety is not jeopardised and that unacceptable variations in practice do not emerge following the cancellation of the Scottish Arthroscopy Project.

S4W-00426 Graeme Pearson: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on reports that pharmacies are experiencing a shortage of drugs in (a) Dumfries and Galloway for cancer treatment and (b) for the treatment of other conditions nationally.

S4W-00427 Graeme Pearson: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will ensure that there is a sufficient supply of drugs to meet the demand for prescriptions in South Scotland.

S4W-00428 Graeme Pearson: To ask the Scottish Executive how it ensures that producers and retailers of prescription drugs do not restrict access to patients in Scotland in order to sell them to European markets.

S4W-00429 Malcolm Chisholm: To ask the Scottish Executive how many routine inspections were carried out of the Elsie Inglis Nursing Home in 2010 and what the result was in each case.

S4W-00430 Malcolm Chisholm: To ask the Scottish Executive whether Social Care and Social Work Improvement Scotland next visited the Elsie Inglis Nursing Home after the last routine inspection of 2010 and, if so, when.

S4W-00431 Malcolm Chisholm: To ask the Scottish Executive whether Social Care and Social Work Improvement Scotland produced any reports on the Elsie Inglis Nursing Home following the report of the last routine inspection of 2010 and, if so, when.

S4W-00432 Malcolm Chisholm: To ask the Scottish Executive at what point after the last routine inspection of 2010 a complaint was first received concerning the Elsie Inglis Nursing Home.

S4W-00434 Malcolm Chisholm: To ask the Scottish Executive what routine inspections of the Elsie Inglis Nursing Home would have taken place in (a) 2011 or (b) 2012 under the recently revised inspection scheme if no complaints had been made following the last routine inspection of 2010.

S4W-00435 Malcolm Chisholm: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will review the procedures for care home inspections, in light of the problems that arose at the Elsie Inglis Nursing Home despite routine inspections in 2010.

S4W-00436 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will publish an updated infrastructure investment plan.

S4W-00437 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive how many of the projects proposed the in 2008 Infrastructure Investment Plan (a) have been completed, (b) are being taken forward or (c) have yet to begin.

S4W-00438 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on those projects proposed in the 2008 Infrastructure Investment Plan that are being taken forward.

S4W-00439 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made toward securing funding for the A8 Baillieston to Newhouse upgrade.

S4W-00440 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made toward securing funding for the Aberdeen Western Peripheral Route.

S4W-00441 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether all NHS boards will review the pathway of care for patients in mental health wards who need to be transferred to general medical care because of physical illness, as recommended by the Mental Welfare Commission in Starved of care - investigation into the care and treatment of "Mrs V".

S4W-00442 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to ensure that all NHS boards ensure that there is a multidisciplinary risk assessment process for decisions on feeding people with swallowing difficulties, as recommended by the Mental Welfare Commission in Starved of care - investigation into the care and treatment of "Mrs V".
