Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally printed the day after they are lodged. If an amendment is lodged to a motion then the original motion will appear alongside the amendment, along with any support lodged that day, in this section.

Each Monday, this section also contains all motions and amendments lodged the previous week that are still live and any motions and amendments that have been lodged for debate in the coming week. A motion or amendment is live if it has not been debated, withdrawn or otherwise deleted from the list.

Where a motion or amendment has been withdrawn, it is indicated in this section the day after it has been withdrawn. Support for motions and amendments received after they are lodged is shown at the end of this section the day after such support is received.

Motions and amendments can be published with various symbols:

  • Asterisks before the motion or amendment number indicate a motion or amendment published for the first time;

  • Asterisks also identify alterations to the text of a motion or amendment made since it was first published;

  • A hash symbol identifies motions eligible for debate at Members’ Business;

  • A diamond symbol identifies motions lodged for Members’ Business that have not yet attracted the required cross-party support;

  • An "R", identifies motions or amendments in which the Member who lodged it has a registrable interest.

The Parliamentary Bureau periodically deletes motions or amendments not scheduled for debate and which are over six weeks old.

A full list of current motions is available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/motions.htm.

A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/index.htm.

Any questions in relation to this section should be directed to the Chamber Desk, for which contact details are provided at the end of the Bulletin.

*S4M-00376 Sarah Boyack: Congratulations to Charity Champion Laura Lee—That the Parliament congratulates Laura Lee, winner of the Charity Champion award at the 2011 Scottish Charity Awards; notes that these awards are run by the Scottish Council for Voluntary Organisations; considers tireless her work in bringing to fruition Maggie Keswick Jenks’s vision of a space where cancer patients and their families can access information and support in order to help them build a life beyond cancer diagnosis, and applauds her part in making Maggie’s Cancer Care into what it believes to be one of the United Kingdom’s best known and most loved charities.

*S4M-00375 Elaine Smith: Monklands Residents against Pyrolysis Plant—That the Parliament is concerned by the decision of the Scottish Government’s reporters to grant the appeal by Shore Energy regarding the development of the proposed pyrolysis plant in Coatbridge; notes that planning permission had previously been refused by North Lanarkshire Council on a number of grounds; recognises the anger and frustration of local residents at the decision to grant the appeal despite almost 6,000 objections to the original application; considers that energy-from-waste plants are one of the least preferred options in the waste hierarchy as they undermine efforts to reuse, reduce and recycle; observes that under the terms of the Town and Country Planning (Scotland) Act 1997 the Scottish Government had the power to refuse the appeal, but chose not to; congratulates Monklands Residents against Pyrolysis Plant (MRAPP) for organising what is considered a fantastic protest on Saturday 18 June 2011, which showed the strength of feeling against this plant from across Coatbridge, and calls on the Scottish Government to investigate the processes involved in this decision and to act to ensure that all citizens have equal and affordable access to justice in order to protect their local environment.

*S4M-00374 Patricia Ferguson: Royston’s Garden of Eden—That the Parliament notes the recent official opening of the Royston Garden of Eden memorial garden in Royston, Glasgow; congratulates the pupils from Royston, St Roch’s, Greenview and St Stephen’s primary schools for their hard work, energy and enthusiasm in creating the garden in partnership with Blochairn, Spireview and Copperworks housing associations, Glasgow City Council and Glasgow Community and Safety Services; welcomes the development of this garden as both community greenspace and a memorial focal point for former school pupils, Mark Cummings and Paul Ross and his brother, Jay, and wishes the schoolchildren and local residents much pleasure from this new community space.

*S4M-00373 Patricia Ferguson: North Glasgow Boxing and Fitness Club’s First Anniversary—That the Parliament notes the first anniversary of the North Glasgow Boxing and Fitness Club based in the Ashgill Recreation Centre in Milton; applauds everyone involved in the club, which has produced three champions in its first 12 months; congratulates 12-year-old Declan Duffy, 18-year-old Christopher Donohue and 22-year-old Ian O’Connor on their recent successes, and wishes the non-profit-making organisation well for the future.

*S4M-00372 Drew Smith: Riverside Museum—That the Parliament notes the public opening of the new Riverside Museum on 21 June 2011; congratulates Glasgow City Council, Glasgow Life and, in particular, the Riverside Appeal on raising £4.6 million toward the cost of the museum; believes that the museum, with its 3,000 exhibits, will add to Glasgow’s already thriving cultural scene; welcomes it as part of the ongoing Clydeside regeneration, and hopes that local people and tourists alike enjoy visiting the new museum.

Supported by: John Pentland*, Neil Bibby*, John Park*

*S4M-00369 Margaret Mitchell: Armed Services Advice Project (ASAP) New Online Learning Tool—That the Parliament acknowledges the ongoing work of the Armed Services Advice Project (ASAP) at Citizens Advice Bureaux (CAB) across Lanarkshire and throughout Scotland; notes that the service provides information and support for serving members of the armed services, their families and veterans on a variety of issues including benefits and money advice; further notes that it is funded by Poppyscotland and other organisations; congratulates ASAP on the launch of its new online learning tool which aims to help civilian staff become more aware of the unique nature of military life and understand the issues faced by members of the armed forces community; considers that young service leavers are faced with particular challenges, and believes that this online tool will enable these veterans and CAB staff to communicate effectively with one another and enable veterans to understand and engage with the variety of civilian services set up to help them integrate into civilian life and avoid isolation.

*S4M-00368 Margaret Mitchell: Stanrigg Sports Centre—That the Parliament welcomes the official opening of the new state-of-the-art Stanrigg Sports Centre in Greengairs; notes that the £1 million centre was built principally with funds from the Landfill Communities Fund, which was established to help improve communities close to landfill operations, and will provide facilities for ball sports such as five-a-side football as well as racquet sports; further notes that the centre is part of the Sustainable Greengairs strategy to rejuvenate the former mining village and neighbouring village of Wattston and that 11 other projects including the Stanrigg Memorial Hall, Greengairs Social Club and Wattston Play Park have received funding through the scheme; acknowledges that the sports centre project was jointly sponsored by Greengairs Community Council, EB Scotland Ltd, Waste Recycling Environmental (WREN), sportscotland and North Lanarkshire Council and considers that the centre will be a great boost for the communities of Greengairs and Wattston, especially for the pupils of Greengairs Primary, and hopes that the centre will encourage the local community to take up more sports and activities.

*S4M-00367 Jenny Marra: Congratulations to the Scottish Deaf Lawn Bowls Team—That the Parliament congratulates the Scottish Deaf Lawn Bowls Team on winning the 6th International Deaf Lawn Bowls Championships in Perth, Australia in April 2011; recognises the success of the team in winning the worldwide competition; congratulates the Scottish winners of the individual events on their success, F McGilp, F Wilson, E Sinclair, E Harvey, C Simpson, T Malley, A Aitken, V Keith, C Keith, M Cavanagh and A McIntosh; wishes the team well for future competitions, and thanks the team for winning for Scotland.

*S4M-00366 Sandra White: Freedom Flotilla II, Stay Human—That the Parliament expresses support for the international Freedom Flotilla II as it attempts to deliver humanitarian aid and break what is widely considered the illegal blockade and collective punishment of Gaza and its citizens; notes that the Israeli attack on the first flotilla last year, in which nine activists lost their lives, has been condemned as illegal in a report to the United Nations by the Human Rights Council fact-finding mission, which states that "the conduct of the Israeli military and other personnel towards the flotilla passengers was not only disproportionate to the occasion, but demonstrated levels of totally unnecessary and incredible violence. It betrayed an unacceptable level of brutality. Such conduct cannot be justified or condoned on security or any other grounds. It constituted a grave violation of human rights law and international humanitarian law", and urges Israel and the international community to ensure that the flotilla is granted safe passage to Gaza.

Supported by: Elaine Smith*, Linda Fabiani*, Rob Gibson*, Kevin Stewart*, Richard Lyle*, Chic Brodie*

Club in helping with verification; considers community-based events such as this vitally important in boosting community morale and participation, and looks forward to viewing the Dundee WestFest entry in the forthcoming Guinness World Records.

*S4M-00364 Siobhan McMahon: Save St Philip’s Secure Unit in Airdrie—That the Parliament is concerned at the decision by the Scottish Government to withdraw preferred bidder status from St Philip’s Secure Unit in Airdrie; considers that the unit affords first class care and support to 18 highly vulnerable young people and understands that, during its most recent inspection, it was judged to be very good in both quality of care and support and quality of management and leadership; believes that the unit’s loss of preferred bidder status will almost certainly lead to the closure of St Philip’s residential and day care units, leaving 259 staff and approximately 50 vulnerable children facing an uncertain future, and calls on the Scottish Government to reconsider its decision and ensure that, at all points, it is acting in the best interests of the children concerned, as outlined in Getting it Right for Every Child.

*S4M-00363 Drew Smith: Partick Thistle FC to Become a Police-free Ground—That the Parliament congratulates Partick Thistle Football Club, which is set to have a police-free ground in season 2011-12; recognises the club’s trouble-free record and its fans’ good behaviour; considers its new, six-figure shirt sponsorship by MacB a welcome development, and wishes the club well in the season ahead.

Supported by: John Pentland*, Patricia Ferguson*, Helen Eadie*, Roderick Campbell*, Jackie Baillie*, Fiona McLeod*, Jamie McGrigor*, Neil Bibby*, Hanzala Malik*, John Park*

*S4M-00362 Patricia Ferguson: Learning Disability Week 2011—That the Parliament marks Learning Disability Week from 20 to 26 June 2011; recognises the contribution that the 120,000 Scots with learning disability can make; calls on the Scottish Government to continue to support people with learning disability in Scotland’s communities to reach their full potential; notes with concern recent research commissioned by the Royal National Institute of Blind People (RNIB) and Seeability and carried out by the Centre for Disabled Research, which has concluded that people with learning disability, Down’s syndrome and cerebral palsy are 10 times more likely to have sight loss, and calls on the Scottish Government to examine ways to increase the detection rate of sight loss among these groups in order to improve their quality of life and to mitigate any financial impact on their health and social care.

Supported by: John Pentland*, Jackie Baillie*, Siobhan McMahon*, Margaret McCulloch*, Mary Fee*, Neil Bibby*, John Park*
