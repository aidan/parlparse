Business Bulletin No. 134/2009: Thursday 17 September 2009
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 24 September 2009

Question Time

General

1. Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it now has an expected date of publication for its independent assessment of the energy options open to Scotland, as recommended in the First Annual Report of the Council of Economic Advisers. (S3O-7902)

2. Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive, as Dounreay continues to decommission, how many replacement jobs the enterprise network has established in the far north in the last 12 months. (S3O-7959)

3. Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has received recommendations from the Scottish Courts Service regarding the future location of the Justice of the Peace courts in the Sheriffdom of South Strathclyde, Dumfries and Galloway and, if so, what those recommendations are. (S3O-7935)

4. Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive what the minimum number of required teaching hours will be per month for each early years establishment serviced by a peripatetic teacher. (S3O-7919)

5. Not Lodged

6. Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive when ministers next plan to meet Strathclyde Police Authority and Glasgow City Council to discuss marches and parades. (S3O-7909)

7. Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide an update on the rules regarding vaccination for bluetongue disease in sheep. (S3O-7892)

8. Derek Brownlee: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in implementing The Government Economic Strategy. (S3O-7896)

9. Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive how the 2010-11 budget will assist local communities. (S3O-7914)

10. Alison McInnes: To ask the Scottish Executive when and how it will report on the conclusions of its review of the local government grant distribution process. (S3O-7951)

Health and Wellbeing

1. Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers it acceptable for an NHS board to dispense with public consultation when considering a reconfiguration of services provided by external providers. (S3O-7948)

2. Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive what organisations it consulted before changing the code of practice in relation to Part 5 of the Adults with Incapacity (Scotland) Act 2000 and whether these are the same organisations that it consulted when the original code of practice was drawn up. (S3O-7930)

3. Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Minister for Housing and Communities last met disability organisations and what issues were discussed. (S3O-7923)

4. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding mechanisms are available to NHS Lothian for the development of new surgical techniques. (S3O-7953)

5. David McLetchie: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent discussions it has had with COSLA regarding the provision and funding of personal care services. (S3O-7894)

6. Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will encourage the Vale of Leven Hospital Inquiry under Lord MacLean to take evidence from all hospitals that have experienced an outbreak of Clostridium difficile in the last two years. (S3O-7950)

7. Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether IVF treatment will be subject to a waiting time guarantee. (S3O-7904)

8. Ted Brocklebank: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent discussions it has had with NHS Fife regarding GP practices. (S3O-7961)

9. Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what contact it has had with the UK Department of Health regarding sharing best practice on steps to reduce the incidence of healthcare associated infections in hospitals. (S3O-7920)

10. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what changes it intends to make to the regulation of private rented housing in its forthcoming Housing Bill. (S3O-7946)

11. Nicol Stephen: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it has taken to support the needs of older and disabled people arriving at hospital and other NHS facilities. (S3O-7958)

12. John Park: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has had any involvement in the plans to develop a national centre for asbestos-related diseases. (S3O-7937)

13. Tom McCabe: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in its Audiology Modernisation Programme. (S3O-7927)

14. John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it has taken to help ensure that fracture patients have access to a high-quality fracture liaison service no matter where they live. (S3O-7895)

15. Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive whether a tenancy deposit scheme is its preferred option for safeguarding tenancy deposits and, if so, what its timescale is for introducing such a scheme. (S3O-7906)

16. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will invest in dementia services in Fife to ensure that quality provision is available at all levels of need and appropriate funding is available. (S3O-7924)

17. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met NHS Fife and what issues were discussed. (S3O-7956)

18. Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many residential alcohol and drug rehabilitation places are available and what action it is taking to support their use to full capacity. (S3O-7947)

19. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are plans to change the boundaries of regional NHS boards. (S3O-7954)

20. Cathie Craigie: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-26597 by Alex Neil on 8 September 2009, which showed that no one had benefited from the mortgage to shared equity scheme by that date, what it considers to be the reason for such a low uptake of the scheme. (S3O-7911)
