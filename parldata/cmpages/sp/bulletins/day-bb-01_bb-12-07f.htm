Business Bulletin No. 181/2001

Friday 7 December 2001

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-2530 Karen Gillon: Railtrack and the Larkhall Project—That the Parliament notes with dismay the decision by Railtrack plc not to support the Scottish Executive funded rail link between Larkhall and Milngavie; recognises that this decision has the potential to have a particular impact on Larkhall and the surrounding communities as it jeopardises the future of a joint project between the Scottish Executive and Strathclyde Passenger Transport; re-iterates its view that this project would have brought substantial economic and social benefits to Clydesdale, and urges Railtrack plc to reconsider urgently their decision in order to ensure that this project proceeds as planned to enable the economic and social regeneration of the Larkhall area.

*S1M-2529# Karen Gillon: New Lanark—That the Parliament recognises the significant contribution that New Lanark has made to the social, cultural and educational fabric of Scotland; congratulates the New Lanark Conservation Trust on the excellent work that it has done in restoring the village to its natural beauty, and calls on UNESCO to grant the village full World Heritage Status.

*S1M-2528# Mr Kenneth Gibson: Young Runaways—That the Parliament welcomes the report Missing Out – Young Runaways in Scotland; commends the University of York, Aberlour Child Care Trust, Extern, Children’s Promise and The Children’s Society for producing this report, the most extensive piece of research yet undertaken into young people under 16 who run away or are forced to leave home; notes that this is the first comprehensive research to provide both an overall picture of the scale and extent of the problem while suggesting workable strategies for responding to the needs of this very vulnerable group of young people, and asks the Executive to look closely at the findings and recommendations of the report and act accordingly.

*S1M-2527 Tommy Sheridan: Sentencing for Breach of the Peace at Faslane Blockade—That the Parliament condemns the disgraceful three month prison sentence handed down to Jenny Gaiawyn on 29 November 2001 by Sheriff Morag Galbraith for a breach of the peace at the blockade in February 2001 of the Faslane nuclear base; considers this sentence to be an outrageous attack on this young woman’s human right to protest peacefully, which is a fundamental human right enshrined in the European Convention on Human Rights and as such should be upheld in law; further considers the sentence to be a scandalous reflection of the arbitrariness of the Scottish legal system, and calls on the Minister for Justice to set up an inquiry into the inconsistencies of sentencing in the courts, which have seen people acquitted, fined or even sentenced to a prison term for the same "crime" of breach of the peace.

*S1M-2526 Pauline McNeill: Breastfeeding Clinic at Yorkhill, Glasgow—That the Parliament congratulates the Queen Mother's Hospital at Yorkhill NHS Trust in establishing Scotland's first breastfeeding clinic where breastfeeding mothers can attend every Monday between 8.00 am and 6.00 pm without an appointment; notes that this is the first open clinic of its kind in Scotland, and supports the encouragement which the clinic will offer to mothers to continue breastfeeding beyond the initial two weeks in order to benefit both their health and that of the baby.
