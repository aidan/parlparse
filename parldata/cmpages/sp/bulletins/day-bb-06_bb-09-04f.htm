Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-4741 Cathie Craigie: Don’t Lose the Music Week—That the Parliament welcomes the first ever Don’t Lose the Music Week, taking place between 4 and 10 September 2006, backed by RNID Scotland; further welcomes the launch of the campaign designed to educate people who enjoy listening to music on their iPod or MP3 player to recognise the dangers associated with listening to music at high volumes for sustained periods of time; expresses concern over the potential hearing damage inflicted on themselves by the 19.7% of 16 to 24-year-olds who listen to their players for over 21 hours each week, and believes that safe listening messages displayed on and within product packaging would allow users to make informed choices.

*S2M-4740 Jim Mather: Concessionary Tickets and Tariff Charges by Caledonian MacBrayne—That the Parliament expresses concern that Caledonian MacBrayne’s recent decision to rigorously apply the current tariff system removing concessionary tickets for commercial vehicles of 5 metres or less in length is now dramatically increasing transport costs for business people and other island residents when, for more than 20 years, Caledonian MacBrayne has recognised that there should be rebates via access to concessionary tickets for such vehicles because of the difficulty of determining on any specific occasion whether or not any such vehicle was carrying goods for hire or reward and, as a result of this practice, Caledonian MacBrayne has over those 20 years effectively encouraged local business people to purchase such vehicles and develop business models that depend on this reasonable pro-enterprise approach and calls on Caledonian MacBrayne to reinstate its former policy to ensure the viability of local businesses and the affordability of goods and services to and from the islands.

*S2M-4739 Dave Petrie: Broadband in Rural Scotland—That the Parliament recognises the completion of the Scottish Executive’s Broadband for Scotland programme and the benefit which it has brought to many rural communities; notes that there are, however, still many isolated communities throughout Scotland, not yet covered by this programme, who have no access to the broadband network; recognises that the most isolated parts of Scotland contain those communities which are disproportionately more reliant on services such as broadband internet connection, and urges the Executive to stick to its commitment to improve access throughout rural Scotland and speed up the ongoing technical assessments into the matter.

*S2M-4738 Chris Ballance: Supporting International Primate Day - 1 September 2006—That the Parliament welcomes International Primate Day, organised by Animal Defenders International, on 1 September 2006; expresses its deep concern that the United Kingdom has become Europe’s largest user of laboratory monkeys; notes that the use of monkeys in military experiments has soared by 76% in the last five years; highlights that the use of non-human primates is an unreliable predictor of likely human effects, as the disastrous TGN1412 trial has shown; is alarmed by the fact that the very existence of many primate species is threatened by the bush meat, laboratory and pet trades as well as the use of primates for entertainment; calls on the Scottish Executive to push for a ban on primates in experiments at EU level during the current revision process of EU Directive 86/609, and asks the Scottish Executive to support the Berlin Declaration calling for an end to primate experiments, which has been signed by over 200 animal protection organisations, scientists, public figures and parliamentarians worldwide.

Supported by: Carolyn Leckie*, Frances Curran*, Robin Harper*, Eleanor Scott*, Mr Adam Ingram*, Shiona Baird*

*S2M-4731 Roseanna Cunningham: Overt Acts of Religious Belief—That the Parliament notes with great concern the damage done to Scotland’s international reputation by the reported decision to caution Artur Boruc for making the sign of the cross during a football match; notes the belated explanation from the Crown Office that the caution related to gestures other than the sign of the cross, and believes that, in view of the damage already done, the Lord Advocate should make an explicit statement to the effect that overt expressions of religious belief are not and will not be regarded as criminal acts in Scotland.

Supported by: Rob Gibson*, Mr Adam Ingram*, Mr David Davidson*, Jim Mather*, Brian Adam*, Mike Rumbles*, Alex Neil*, John Swinburne*, Tommy Sheridan*

*S2M-4730 Brian Adam: Northfield Academy’s 50th Anniversary—That the Parliament congratulates Northfield Academy in Aberdeen on reaching its 50th anniversary; notes that a number of events have been arranged to celebrate this milestone date, and hopes that the staff and pupils of Northfield Academy will continue to make positive contributions in the community for many more years.

Supported by: Dr Jean Turner*, Mr David Davidson*, Robin Harper*, Fergus Ewing*, Ms Maureen Watt*, John Swinburne*, Mrs Nanette Milne*, Bruce Crawford*, Shiona Baird*, Mr Jamie Stone*, Dave Petrie*

*S2M-4729 Mr Jim Wallace: Dennis Head Old Beacon, North Ronaldsay—That the Parliament welcomes the success of the Dennis Head Old Beacon, North Ronaldsay, in winning the Scottish leg of the competition to win the BBC Restoration Village 2006 prize fund; congratulates the North Ronaldsay Trust and the North Ronaldsay community on their success against worthy opposition; recognises the importance of the beacon as only the third lighthouse constructed by the Commissioners of Northern Lighthouses and that it was designed by Thomas Smith with the assistance of his stepson Robert Stevenson, the first of four generations of Stevensons who designed most of Scotland’s lighthouses and won international recognition for their engineering achievements, and calls for widespread support, from Scotland and beyond, for the North Ronaldsay community when the Dennis Head Old Beacon competes against opposition from across the United Kingdom in the BBC Restoration Village 2006 final.

Supported by: John Swinburne*, Robin Harper*, Margaret Smith*, Mike Pringle*, Roseanna Cunningham*, Mr Jamie Stone*, Eleanor Scott*, Ms Maureen Watt*, Rob Gibson*, Nora Radcliffe*, Shiona Baird*, Pauline McNeill*, Dave Petrie*

*S2M-4728 Euan Robson: The Commemoration of Black Friday—That the Parliament remembers with respect the east coast disaster of 14 October 1881 in which 189 east coast fishermen, of whom 129 came from Eyemouth, lost their lives; recognises the efforts of Berwickshire coastal towns, villages and others to commemorate the 125th anniversary of Black Friday, the worst fishing tragedy ever suffered in Scotland, and further recognises both the dangers faced by contemporary fishermen at sea and the continuing importance of the fishing industry to communities around Scotland’s shores.

*S2M-4727 Mr Andrew Arbuckle: Support for Scottish Food Fortnight—That the Parliament fully supports the objectives of Scottish Food Fortnight, which runs from 1 to 17 September 2006, in promoting the use of healthy Scottish produce and in the re-establishment of links between consumers and producers in this country, and accepts, furthermore, that this initiative will help the economic well-being of rural areas in Scotland through the promotion of local food.

*S2M-4726 Donald Gorrie: Burns Monument, Kay Park, Kilmarnock—That the Parliament urges East Ayrshire Council to restore the world-famous Burns Monument in Kay Park, Kilmarnock, which is currently suffering from vandalism and decay, so that it can play its part in the celebrations and tourism related to the memory of Burns, especially in view of the desire of the Scottish Executive and many organisations and individuals in Scotland and throughout the world to celebrate the 250th anniversary of Robert Burns’ birth in 2009.

Supported by: Carolyn Leckie*

S2M-4577# Pauline McNeill: Passengers Before Profits - Greater Accountability in Public Transport—That the Parliament reiterates its strong view that decent local bus services are vital to all Scotland’s communities and its belief that such services are integral to cutting road congestion and safeguarding the environment; welcomes the significant investment in bus infrastructure, including better terminuses, priority bus lanes and fast track traffic management systems which have received Scottish Executive support throughout Scotland; expresses significant concern that withdrawal of services is continuing resulting in, for example, a much poorer after-hours service in Glasgow, undermining investment and attempts to deliver more integrated and accessible health care and other public services, as well as leaving many areas of both urban and rural Scotland without adequate services, and believes that the Executive and Transport Scotland, in return for the increased investment, should ensure greater accountability of the bus industry for services across Scotland, should undertake speedily a review of how quality bus partnerships are operating and should consider seriously all possible options which could prevent the loss or absence of vital services, including new and greater forms of regulation such as franchise agreements or allowing the new Regional Transport Partnerships the power to determine routes and timetables, so as to guarantee all Scottish communities the bus links which they need and deserve.

Supported by: Mr Charlie Gordon, Bill Butler, Maureen Macmillan, Michael McMahon, Mr Kenneth Macintosh, Trish Godman, Christine May, Cathy Peattie, Jackie Baillie, Des McNulty, Ms Sandra White, Patrick Harvie, Dr Elaine Murray, Carolyn Leckie, Frances Curran, Paul Martin, Tommy Sheridan, Robin Harper, Cathie Craigie, Karen Gillon, Mrs Mary Mulligan, Chris Ballance, Donald Gorrie, Janis Hughes, Shiona Baird, Helen Eadie, Dr Sylvia Jackson, Marilyn Livingstone, Mr Mark Ruskell, Nora Radcliffe, Eleanor Scott, Mr Duncan McNeil, Linda Fabiani
