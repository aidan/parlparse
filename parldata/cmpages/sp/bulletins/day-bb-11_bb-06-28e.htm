Business Bulletin No. 83/2011: Tuesday 28 June 2011
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 27 June 2011

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S4W-00953 Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it will take to reduce the number of patients fit for discharge who, because they remain in hospital for less than six weeks, are not classified as delayed discharge cases.

S4W-00954 Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it will take to reduce the median length of stay for patients declared fit for discharge who, because they remain in hospital for less than six weeks, are not classified as delayed discharge cases.

S4W-00955 Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has investigated reports of patients dying in hospital after being declared fit for discharge who, because they remained in hospital for less than six weeks, were not classified as delayed discharge cases.

S4W-00961 Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on whether the Scottish Medicines Consortium (SMC) rejected the application for the use of Eculizumab in November 2010, in part on the basis that a health economic analysis of the treatment of paroxysmal nocturnal haemoglobinuria was not provided, and whether SMC had previously written to the manufacturer in April 2010 stating that no such submission would be required.

S4W-01246 Drew Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive (a) how it monitors progress on and (b) whether it plans to review the framework set out in its policy, Achieving our Potential.

S4W-01248 Drew Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will establish a living wage unit.

S4W-01249 Drew Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making on the provision of a living wage across the public sector.

S4W-01250 Drew Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make the living wage a requirement of its procurement practices in order to expand its use.

S4W-01252 Drew Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive how the Scotland Performs initiative is monitored and what resources it provides to carry out this monitoring.

S4W-01255 Drew Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to review the Scotland Performs initiative.

S4W-01256 Drew Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive how it (a) uses, (b) disseminates and (c) responds to the information collected by the Scotland Performs initiative.

S4W-01257 Drew Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what analysis takes place in monitoring the progress recorded by the Scotland Performs initiative and how this has changed since the initiative was launched.

S4W-01258 Drew Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive how many free eye tests have been carried out in each year since the Smoking, Health and Social Care (Scotland) Act 2005 was introduced.

S4W-01270 Drew Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what its policy is on the recognition of trades unions in the Scottish Government and what percentage of its workforce is union members, broken down by recognised union.

S4W-01271 Drew Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what policies it has in place to promote physical activity among (a) its employees and (b) staff in its agencies.

S4W-01274 Drew Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is considering the role of denominational education in relation to its work on tackling sectarianism and, if so, what consultation has taken place with the denominational education sector.

S4W-01276 Mike MacKenzie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is assisting and supporting the restoration of Castle Tioram.

S4W-01277 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made toward eradicating illiteracy and innumeracy since 2007.

S4W-01278 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what reduction in class sizes it expects there to be in (a) 2012, (b) 2013, (c) 2014, (d) 2015 and (e) 2016.

S4W-01279 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what it expects the pupil-teacher ratio to be in 2012.

S4W-01280 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what support there is for initiatives such as nurture classes.

S4W-01281 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in implementing the recommendations of Teaching Scotland’s Future.

S4W-01282 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what (a) financial and (b) other support it has offered to (i) schools and (ii) local authorities to deliver sufficient teaching posts for all probationers who complete their probation in summer 2011.

S4W-01283 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made toward merging Her Majesty’s Inspectorate of Education and Learning and Teaching Scotland; when the merger will be completed, and how much money will be saved as a result of the merger.

S4W-01286 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive how it ensures that the Educational Maintenance Allowance takes account of the needs of young carers.

S4W-01287 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the Scottish Futures Trust regarding delivering greener schools in terms of (a) construction and (b) design.

S4W-01288 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will introduce an education rights and responsibilities bill and what stakeholders will be consulted.

S4W-01290 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on encouraging pupils and schools to use the public library network.

S4W-01291 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it offers public libraries to help them to encourage their use by (a) pupils and (b) schools.

S4W-01292 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it will take to introduce the European Union 1+2 model in schools in this parliamentary session.

S4W-01293 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made with the development of the Glow Scotland network and whether the next generation will be delivered by September 2012.

S4W-01294 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in establishing a standing commission on literacy.

S4W-01295 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with football supporters’ clubs regarding sectarianism.

S4W-01297 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with football supporters’ clubs regarding new criminal offences relating to sectarian and offensive behaviour at football matches.

S4W-01298 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive what the annual cost will be of maintaining police officer numbers at current levels during this parliamentary session.

S4W-01299 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will bring forward legislative proposals on the structural reform of police forces.

S4W-01300 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive how much funding it will provide for the roll out of its No Knives, Better Lives campaign.

S4W-01302 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to review sexual offences legislation.

S4W-01303 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider rolling out Strathclyde Police’s Domestic Abuse Task Force across Scotland.

S4W-01305 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive what its view is on banning people from public parks through the use of antisocial behaviour notices.

S4W-01306 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on drink banning orders.

S4W-01307 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will ban visitors who are caught smuggling drugs into prison from future visits.

S4W-01308 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to bring forward a law that will permit previous convictions to be used as evidence in court.

S4W-01309 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will commence section 18 of the Criminal Justice and Licensing (Scotland) Act 2010.

S4W-01310 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the view of the Scottish Prisons Commission that prison sentences of six months or less should be discouraged.

S4W-01311 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to abolish prison sentences of six months or less.

S4W-01312 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive how many meetings there have been of the Nations and Regions Group of the 2012 Olympic Games and how many meetings its representative has attended.

S4W-01313 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive how many meetings have been held between the Minister for Commonwealth Games and Sport and (a) her counterpart in the UK Government and (b) representatives of the organising committee of the Olympic Games, in each of the last four years.

S4W-01314 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Minister for Commonwealth Games and Sport has meetings planned with (a) her counterpart in the UK Government and (b) representatives of the organising committee of the Olympic Games.

S4W-01315 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to maximise the legacy in Scotland of the 2012 Olympic Games.

S4W-01316 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to maximise the benefits from Olympic events being held in Scotland.

S4W-01317 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to introduce a school Olympics programme in Scotland.

S4W-01341Δ Clare Adamson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will report on the outcome of the British-Irish Council Summit.
