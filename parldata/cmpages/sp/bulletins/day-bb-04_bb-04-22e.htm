Business Bulletin No. 67 / 2004

Thursday 22nd April 2004

Section E : Written questions lodged on Wednesday 21st April 2004
  

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-7635 Frances Curran: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body, further to the answer to question S1W-26960 by Sir David Steel on 6 August 2002, how many of the parliament's staff have been seconded to it since May 1999, specifying (a) post held, (b) name of organisation seconded from and (c) who pays the salary of each such staff member.

S2W-7650 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what support is being given to complementary medicine.

S2W-7651 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-7018 by Allan Wilson on 8 April 2004, whether it will provide a summary of the extent, location and estimated costs of resolving current and known planned developments throughout Scotland, including the timescale involved.

S2W-7652 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list its achievements in the European sphere, as referred to in paragraph 2 of its European Strategy.

S2W-7653 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether its European Strategy is a framework, as referred to in paragraph 1 of the strategy, or whether it is a proposed framework, as referred to in paragraph 3 of that document and, if it is the latter, when a decision will be made on whether to adopt this framework.

S2W-7654 Mr Bruce McFee: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance has been provided for public bodies on considering ethical issues in procurement policies.

S2W-7655 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it can provide any information in respect of the size of land banks available for development held by private housing developers and, if not, where such information can be obtained.

S2W-7656 Carolyn Leckie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether, in light of the introduction of the Mercury-Free Vaccines Act of 2004 in the US House of Representatives on 2 April 2004 and the conclusions of Geier and Geier's 2003 study Thimerosal in childhood vaccines, neurodevelopment disorders and heart disease in the United States, all vaccines containing mercury will be banned as a precautionary measure and, if so, when such vaccines will be banned.

S2W-7657 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to support the United Nations Decade of Education for Sustainable Development 2005-14.

S2W-7658Δ Christine May: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has come to any conclusions as a result of its consultation on Business Improvement Districts (BIDs) in Scotland and how it intends to establish BIDs in line with A Partnership for a Better Scotland.

S2W-7659 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the revised timetable is for the roll-out of the Reliance Secure Task Management Ltd contract in Dumbarton Sheriff Court.

S2W-7660 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with Her Majesty's Government on stimulating increased exploration and development in the oil and gas industry.

S2W-7661 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what the current average waiting times are for cardiac surgery for (a) NHS and (b) private patients in each NHS board.

S2W-7662 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it has taken to eliminate illicit drug use in prisons.

S2W-7663 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it commissioned research into a new Scottish brand and/or logo for the purposes of promoting Scotland overseas and, if so, on what basis such research was commissioned.

S2W-7664 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will respond to the objectors who have written postcards to it in respect of the proposed extension to landfill sites in the village of Greengairs by Eden Waste Recycling Limited.

S2W-7665 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has issued any guidelines to the Scottish Environment Protection Agency in respect of the acceptability of digital photographs provided by a complainer as supportive evidence of allegations of pollution or on any other environmental issue and, if so, what such guidelines are.

S2W-7666 John Swinburne: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-7353 by Mrs Mary Mulligan on 15 April 2004, what the reasons are for the difference between the original estimate of £350 million in 2001 for the delivery of the free central heating programme and the current estimate of £180 million.

S2W-7667 John Swinburne: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-7353 by Mrs Mary Mulligan on 15 April 2004, whether the allocation of £350 million in 2001 for the delivery of the free central heating programme was ring-fenced and, if not, what the remaining £170 million has been allocated to.

S2W-7668Δ Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will launch its review of the children's hearings system.
