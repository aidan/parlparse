Business Bulletin No. 175/2004

Monday 29 November 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-2082 Mark Ballard: Fairtrade Football Match—That the Parliament notes that the football match between Whitehill Welfare and Kelso United in the East of Scotland League Premier Division at Ferguson Park, Rosewell, Midlothian on 27 November 2004 at 2.00 pm will be played to support Fairtrade footballs; notes that Fairtrade ensures that handmade footballs are made in humane working conditions and that workers are ensured a fair price for their work; believes that Fairtrade has a significant role to play in improving the lives of workers in the global south and supports the use of Fairtrade footballs throughout Scottish football, and congratulates Whitehill Welfare on being the first club in Scotland to use Fairtrade footballs.

Supported by: Chris Ballance*

*S2M-2081 Mr Brian Monteith: Gritting on Stirling’s Roads—That the Parliament notes the Scottish Executive’s decision not to allow the additional capital funding for safety-related school transport projects announced by the Minister for Transport on 4 November 2004 to be used on precautionary winter gritting of school bus routes; believes that the lack of any precautionary gritting on school bus routes is an issue of concern for several communities in the Stirling area, not least because of incidents that occurred last winter at Fintry and the Carron Valley where school transport was disrupted by treacherous road conditions; acknowledges that 300 people in Fintry alone signed a petition on this very issue earlier this year; believes that gritting school bus routes is about providing pupils with safer routes to school, and calls on the Executive to allow local authorities the flexibility to determine how best to spend additional roads funding to reflect local circumstances, such as severe road conditions.

*S2M-2080# Alex Neil: Closure of Craigneuk Development and Support Unit—That the Parliament expresses its deep regret that the Craigneuk Development and Support Unit has been forced to announce its closure and the forced redundancy of its staff with effect from Christmas Eve, as a direct result of the actions taken by North Lanarkshire Council to withdraw funding from this organisation and the services it provides; notes that the withdrawal of the unit’s counselling and support services will have a detrimental effect on the levels of poverty and deprivation in the communities which it serves, and believes that the way in which the council has treated this excellent organisation should be the subject of a detailed examination by Audit Scotland and the auditors of the European Commission and that appropriate steps should be taken to stop local authorities creating third party agencies which deliberately undermine and subvert the work of existing providers who are doing an excellent job, as was the case with the Craigneuk Development and Support Unit.

*S2M-2079 Mr Stewart Maxwell: Feu Duty Compensation—That the Parliament agrees with the abolition of the feudal system; considers that in 2004 no one should be paying feu duty to a "feudal superior"; invites the Church of Scotland to reconsider its reported charging of feudal tenants when the feudal system is abolished on 28 November 2004, which would affect a disproportionate amount of elderly people, and considers that, if the Church of Scotland is not persuaded to do this, due to the considerable drop in income levels it claims this will bring, the Parliament should investigate ways of recompensing either the Church of Scotland or the householder to save them from being charged sums of between approximately £400 and £800 by their "feudal superiors".

*S2M-2078# Brian Adam: Arbuthnott Formula—That the Parliament expresses grave concern that the Arbuthnott formula for health funding is exacerbating already difficult circumstances in NHS Grampian; notes that NHS Grampian’s staff continue to deliver 10% of Scotland’s health activity on 9% of the NHS budget; further notes the board’s projected deficit in excess of £13 million for the current financial year; condemns the consequential freezing of infection control nurse posts as a cost-saving measure, and believes that the Scottish Executive should urgently review the formula so that it reflects actual need rather than relying on secondary factors in the formula which skew the funding.

*S2M-2077 Linda Fabiani: Carers’ Rights Day—That the Parliament praises the valuable and vital role played by the 666,000 carers in Scotland; notes that the fourth annual Carers’ Rights Day is being held on 3 December 2004 and that Carers Scotland will be visiting the Parliament on 2 December; recognises that millions of pounds worth of carers’ benefits go unclaimed each year, and considers that the Scottish Executive should step up its activities to encourage better take-up of the allowances rightly due to carers in Scotland.

S2M-2076# Janis Hughes: Network Rail GSM-R Masts—That the Parliament notes the erection by Network Rail of 29 metre-high Global System for Mobile Telecommunications (GSM-R) masts throughout Scotland, beginning with a pilot programme of 84 masts in the Strathclyde area; acknowledges that, despite giving assurances that communities would be fully informed in advance of any work, Network Rail has erected several masts with little or no prior direct notification to elected representatives or local residents; asks Network Rail to give an absolute assurance that masts erected using permitted development rights will not be used at any point for commercial purposes, and acknowledges that, whilst rail safety must always be paramount, the Scottish Executive should examine Network Rail’s permitted development rights in relation to GSM-R masts and, in particular, the role that both Network Rail and local authorities should play in consulting local residents.

S2M-2073# Donald Gorrie: Tackling the Impact of Misuse of Alcohol—That the Parliament, in the light of the major influence which misuse of alcohol has on many issues of great concern to it, such as violence in the home and in the streets, knife crime, sectarian and racial violence and abuse, misbehaviour at and around football matches, hate crimes and ill-health, believes that the Scottish Executive, while working on bringing forward a comprehensive Licensing Bill, should actively pursue bringing together the various proof-of-age card schemes into an effective voluntary national scheme and should encourage the police and the Crown Office and Procurator Fiscal Service to enforce more vigorously existing legislation on adults not buying alcohol for under-age people and on the offence of serving alcohol to someone who is already drunk.

S2M-2072# Fergus Ewing: Lifting the Over Thirty Months Scheme—That the Parliament notes that the Over Thirty Months Scheme (OTMS) was introduced around eight years ago to protect the public against the potential risk that humans consuming beef which is over 30 months might contract CJD via BSE-infected beef; acknowledges that the Food Standards Agency (FSA), which was set up in order to provide an independent source of scientific advice on food safety, recommended in July 2003 that the scheme be scrapped and the ban lifted and replaced by a two-stage process, involving testing of animals for BSE, advice which was reaffirmed in July 2004; expresses concern that the FSA’s advice has not been acted upon by the UK Government and devolved ministers; understands that the cost of the OTMS exceeds £400 million a year, and therefore considers that the Scottish Executive should lift the ban as soon as practically possible in order that public money can be used directly for the health service or other priorities, and not be wasted on a scheme which is, according to the official food safety watchdog, no longer justified.

S2M-2050 Chris Ballance: Biological and Toxin Weapons Convention and Iraq—That the Parliament notes that the Biological and Toxin Weapons Convention (BTWC) binds signatories not to transfer to any nation any agents, toxins, weapons and equipment of biological and toxin warfare and provides that any nation finding another signatory in breach of this undertaking may lodge a complaint to the Security Council; understands that the Riegle Report to the US Senate has published evidence that the United States of America sold biological materials, including Bacillus Anthracis, Clostridium Botulinum, Histoplasma Capsulatum, Brucella Melitensis and Clostridium Perfringens to various agencies of the Iraqi Government pursuant to export licences issued by the US Department of Commerce, in quantities set out in the report and at the time when the United States of America was fully aware of the Iraqi biological warfare programme and that these exports have been fully documented and are also set out in the 23 September 2002 edition of Newsweek and by Senator Robert Byrd in speeches in the Senate on 20 and 26 September 2002; requests the UK Government to exercise its power to report these sales to the Security Council of the United Nations in the light of its commitment in the April 2002 Green Paper, Strengthening the Biological and Toxin Weapons Convention that those at every level responsible for any breach of international law relating to the use of such weapons will be held personally accountable because compliance with the BTWC is an issue the international community cannot avoid, and urges the Prime Minister either to lodge the necessary complaint with the Security Council of the United Nations or change its stated policy after an appropriate public announcement and discussion.

Supported by: Carolyn Leckie, Frances Curran, Mark Ballard, Campbell Martin, Shiona Baird, Eleanor Scott, Robin Harper, Patrick Harvie, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Rob Gibson, Mr Mark Ruskell*
