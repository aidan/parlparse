followed by Members’ Business

Debate on the subject of—

S3M-7361 Kenneth Gibson: Epilepsy Awareness Training—That the Parliament believes that there would be merit in epilepsy training becoming an integral part of the student teacher training course curriculum so that teaching staff are equipped to detect potential seizures in children and young people and can be proactive in providing appropriate support to pupils with epilepsy and agrees that this could boost teacher confidence in handling as many as 7,000 children in Scotland with active epilepsy, enable early detection of seizures in over 800 children each year, provide more tailored learning and emotional support to normalise the condition and reduce classroom stigma for children in North Ayrshire and across Scotland.

followed by Stage 3 Proceedings: Wildlife and Natural Environment (Scotland) Bill

S3M-8020 Roseanna Cunningham: Wildlife and Natural Environment (Scotland) Bill—That the Parliament agrees that the Wildlife and Natural Environment (Scotland) Bill be passed.

followed by Scottish Government Debate: Fuel Duty

S3M-8032 Keith Brown: Fuel Duty—That the Parliament notes that petrol and diesel prices in Scotland are among the highest in Europe and have reached record levels and that the planned rise in fuel duty by the UK Government in April 2011 could increase prices by a further 4p per litre; recognises that such increases impose an additional burden on households and businesses at a time of rising living costs and could undermine the economic recovery; notes the UK Government’s proposal to introduce a 5p-per-litre fuel discount scheme for island communities, and calls on the UK Government to cancel the rise in fuel duty planned for April and implement a fuel duty regulator that would ensure that some of the additional revenue that the UK Government will receive from increased revenues due to recent increases in oil prices is used to reduce fuel duty to help support Scottish households and businesses.

The Presiding Officer has selected the following amendment

S3M-8032.1 Charlie Gordon: Fuel Duty—As an amendment to motion (S3M-8032) in the name of Keith Brown, leave out from "and implement" to end and insert "and to reverse the recent VAT increase on fuel".
