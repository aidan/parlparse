Business Bulletin No. 10/2007: Friday 19 January 2007
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 18 January 2007

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-31084 Mr Jim Wallace: To ask the Scottish Executive what the revenue return has been from modulation of single farm payments in each year since their inception and what sum is estimated to be raised in the (a) current and (b) next financial year.

S2W-31085 Mr Jim Wallace: To ask the Scottish Executive how financial contributions from modulation of single farm payments were spent in each year since the inception of modulated payments and what proposals there are with regard to planned expenditure of modulation payments in the next financial year.

S2W-31143 Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive how many households in the West Aberdeenshire and Kincardine parliamentary constituency have benefited from installations under the central heating programme since its inception.

S2W-31144 Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive how many households in the West Aberdeenshire and Kincardine parliamentary constituency have benefited from the provision of insulation under the Warm Deal scheme since its inception.

S2W-31145 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many planning enforcement officers there were in each year since 1999, broken down by local authority.

S2W-31146 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many graduates qualified as planners in each year since 1999.

S2W-31147 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many planning graduates entered the (a) private and (b) public sector in each year since 1999.

S2W-31148 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many planners it estimates are required in the public sector as a consequence of the Planning etc. (Scotland) Act 2006.

S2W-31150 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what projected funding is for planning aid at the end of this financial year, broken down into constituent parts.

S2W-31151 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-26217 by Mr Tom McCabe on 12 June 2006, whether it will now publish its Brix note, Fiscal Autonomy, Financial Independence and Full Fiscal Power, in light of recent public focus on this issue.

S2W-31152 Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body, whether it has any indication of what the energy efficiency rating of the Parliament might be under the EU Energy Performance of Buildings Directive (2002/91/EC) now that the Scottish Executive has adopted the use of the asset-based energy rating methodology, Simplified Building Energy Model (SBEM), as the principle non-domestic method for assessment of energy rating for non-domestic buildings.

S2W-31153 Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive when guidance will be issued by the Scottish Building Standards Agency on how Articles 7, 8 and 9 of the EU Energy Performance of Buildings Directive (2002/91/EC) will be implemented in Scotland.

S2W-31154 Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made towards full compliance with the EU Energy Performance of Buildings Directive (2002/91/EC), which came into force on 4 January 2006.

S2W-31155 Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body what preparations have been made regarding compliance with Articles 7, 8 and 9 of the EU Energy Performance of Buildings Directive (92002/91/EC) in respect of the Parliament building.

S2W-31156 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) permanent and (b) temporary exclusions from school there were in each year since 1999 and, of these, how many were as a result of (i) drug taking, (ii) selling drugs, (iii) violence towards other pupils, (iv) violence towards teachers, (v) vandalism of school property, (vi) theft of school property and (vii) possession of a weapon and how many were for other reasons.

S2W-31157 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how much was spent on repairing damage to schools caused by vandalism in each local education authority in each year since 1999.

S2W-31158 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many incidents involving (a) sale and (b) use of illegal drugs on school property have been reported in each local education authority in each year since 2000.

S2W-31159 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many offensive weapons were confiscated in (a) primary and (b) secondary schools in each year since 1999, broken down by local authority.

S2W-31160 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many teachers (a) were recruited and (b) retired in each year from 1997 to 2006, broken down by local authority.

S2W-31161 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the total change was in the number of beds in (a) the NHS and (b) each NHS board between 2004-05 and 2005-06.

S2W-31162 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive to what extent the circumstances in which rainbow trout may be introduced to inland waters are regulated and may be further regulated under the Aquaculture and Fisheries (Scotland) Bill or any other proposed freshwater fisheries legislation.

S2W-31163 Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it has taken to ensure that genetically modified chickens, their eggs or any part thereof do not enter the food chain.

S2W-31164 Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what arrangements are in place for the slaughter of genetically modified chickens and disposal of their carcases.

S2W-31165 Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-20437 by Mr Tom McCabe on 5 December 2005, whether it is now in a position to give a more accurate prediction of when it will pay out the remainder of the £126 million which it agreed to pay out in ex gratia payments from the Scottish Transport Group pension funds surplus.

S2W-31166 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive, in respect of Government Expenditure and Revenues in Scotland (GERS), what estimates have been made of Scotland’s notional share of the UK’s public sector borrowing requirement in each year since GERS was first published.

S2W-31167 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive, in respect of Government Expenditure and Revenues in Scotland (GERS), what estimates have been made of the revenues raised from air passenger duty in each year since GERS was first published.

S2W-31168 Ms Maureen Watt: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people were registered as home carers in each year since 1999, broken down by local authority area and showing year-on-year percentage changes.

S2W-31169 Ms Maureen Watt: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people were cared for by home carers in each year since 1999, broken down by local authority area and showing year-on-year percentage changes.

S2W-31170 Ms Maureen Watt: To ask the Scottish Executive what the (a) total and (b) average payments were to home carers in each year since 1999, broken down by local authority area and showing year-on-year percentage changes.

S2W-31171 Ms Maureen Watt: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people were cared for in residential homes in each year since 1999, broken down by local authority area and showing year-on-year percentage changes.

S2W-31172 Ms Maureen Watt: To ask the Scottish Executive what the (a) total cost and (b) average cost per resident were of providing residential care in each year since 1999, broken down by local authority area and showing year-on-year percentage changes.

S2W-31173 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive how many operations were carried out in hospitals in NHS Tayside in each year since 2000.

S2W-31174 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive how many operations were carried out in each hospital in NHS Tayside in the last year for which the information is available.

S2W-31175 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive how many operations were carried out in hospitals in NHS Grampian in each year since 2000.

S2W-31176 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive what the estimated number of face-to-face contacts with patients by GPs was in NHS Tayside in the last year for which the information is available.

S2W-31177 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive what the estimated number of face-to-face contacts with patients by GPs was in NHS Grampian in the last year for which the information is available.

S2W-31178 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive how many attendances there were at each accident and emergency centre in NHS Tayside in each year since 2000.

S2W-31179 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive how many attendances there were at each accident and emergency centre in NHS Grampian in each year since 2000.

S2W-31180 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive how many parents and children were supported by Sure Start Scotland through group or intensive provision in the (a) Dundee City, (b) Angus and (c) Aberdeenshire local authority areas in the last year for which the information is available.

S2W-31181 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive how many children received resource-based support through Sure Start Scotland in the (a) Dundee City, (b) Angus and (c) Aberdeenshire local authority areas in the last year for which the information is available.

S2W-31182 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive how much (a) Dundee City, (b) Angus and (c) Aberdeenshire councils received under the Sure Start Scotland programme in the last year for which the information is available.

S2W-31183 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive how many inpatient and outpatient appointments and procedures there were in NHS Tayside in each year since 2000.

S2W-31184 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive how many inpatient and outpatient appointments and procedures there were in NHS Grampian in each year since 2000.

S2W-31185 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether rainbow trout is considered to be a native species; whether rainbow trout pose any threat to other native species, and what criteria are applied to determine native species.

S2W-31186 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list all those who have served as policy advisers to the Scottish Arts Council (SAC) or to the Chair of the SAC since 1999, also indicating for each (a) how long they served, (b) how much they were paid and (c) what their outputs were.

S2W-31187 Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is aware of a potential withdrawal of the University of Glasgow from the Crichton Campus in Dumfries and, if so, what action it can take to help the university address any shortfall in the funding of its operations in Dumfries.

S2W-31188 Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the number of student places funded by the Scottish Funding Council at the University of Glasgow’s Crichton Campus in Dumfries has increased in line with actual student numbers and what the reasons are for this position.

S2W-31189 Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive how it supports universities which establish outreach campuses, such as Crichton Campus in Dumfries, to provide higher education in rural areas.

S2W-31190 Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it values the provision made at Crichton Campus in Dumfries and what its attitude would be to any diminution in the number and variety of courses on offer.

S2W-31191 Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive how many officers in each fire and rescue service are specifically trained for emergency water rescue duties and what the nature is of the training they were given.

S2W-31192 Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive how much money was allocated to each fire and rescue service in the most recent year for which figures are available to ensure that sufficient water rescue training is carried out.

S2W-31193 Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there is a specific number of officers that should be trained in emergency water rescue in each fire and rescue service.
