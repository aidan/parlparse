Business Bulletin No. 139/2002

Monday 30 September 2002

Section E : Written questions lodged on 27 September 2002

  

Questions marked with a triangle () are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S1W-30044 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to publish its report on the progress made against the Statistical Plan 2001-02.

S1W-30045 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to ensure that public transport workers, passengers and vehicles are protected from the effects of anti-social behaviour, including violence, vandalism and smoking.

S1W-30046 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any systems are currently in place to monitor the incidence of anti-social behaviour on public transport and, if not, whether it has any plans to introduce any such system.

S1W-30047 Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Education Maintenance Allowance scheme will be extended across the whole of Scotland.

S1W-30048 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-25417 by Malcolm Chisholm on 10 May 2002, when it plans to publish a full report on the convention on hospital-acquired infections.

S1W-30049 Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-28706 by Mr Jim Wallace on 24 September 2002, what assessment it has made of best value in respect of contracting out prisoner escorts.

S1W-30050 Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-28706 by Mr Jim Wallace on 24 September 2002, what annual savings it estimates that it will make by contracting out prisoner escorts.

S1W-30051 Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-28706 by Mr Jim Wallace on 24 September 2002, when it will be able to give the cost to the Scottish Prison Service of providing prisoner escorts in each of the last three years and whether it will place the information in the Scottish Parliament Information Centre once it is available.

S1W-30052 Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-28706 by Mr Jim Wallace on 24 September 2002, whether it has sought any external advice with regard to contracting out prisoner escorts.

S1W-30053 Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive what the costs of education services have been in each Scottish Prison Service prison in each of the last five years.

S1W-30055 Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive why the number of proceedings taken by procurators fiscal in cases of vandalism fell as a proportion of cases cleared up by the police between 1997 and 2000.

S1W-30056 Kate Maclean: To ask the Scottish Executive when the first results of the 2001 Census will be published.

S1W-30057 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) (i) reports to and (ii) proceedings taken by procurators fiscal and (b) convictions there have been in each of the last three years under section 19(1) of the Criminal Law (Consolidation) (Scotland) Act 1995.

S1W-30058 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) (i) reports and (ii) proceedings taken by procurators fiscal and (b) convictions there have been in each of the last three years under section 56(1) of the British Transport Commission Act 1949 as amended.

S1W-30059 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) (i) reports to and (ii) proceedings taken by procurators fiscal and (b) convictions there have been in each of the last three years under section 55(1) of the British Transport Commission Act 1949 as amended.

S1W-30060 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) (i) reports to and (ii) proceedings taken by procurators fiscal and (b) convictions there have been in each of the last three years under section 16 of the Railway Regulation Act 1840 as amended.

S1W-30061 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) (i) reports to and (ii) proceedings taken by procurators fiscal and (b) convictions there have been in each of the last three years under section 23 of the Regulation of Railways Act 1924 as amended.

S1W-30062 Angus MacKay: To ask the Scottish Executive how much has been invested in (a) primary and (b) secondary school education in each year since 1996, expressed in (i) cash and (ii) real terms.

S1W-30063 Angus MacKay: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people have taken up further education in each year since 1996.

S1W-30064 Angus MacKay: To ask the Scottish Executive how many drug-related convictions there have been in each year since 1996.

S1W-30065 Angus MacKay: To ask the Scottish Executive what the annual expenditure on drug-related activities has been in each year since 1996, broken down by expenditure on crime, education, rehabilitation and treatment.

S1W-30069 Angus MacKay: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people were treated in hospital in each year since 1996.

S1W-30070 Angus MacKay: To ask the Scottish Executive how much has been spent on hospital buildings in each year since 1996

S1W-30073 Angus MacKay: To ask the Scottish Executive how many crimes were (a) committed and (b) cleared up in each year since 1996.
