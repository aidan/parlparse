Business Bulletin No. 164/2010: Thursday 11 November 2010
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 10 November 2010

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-37422 Bill Kidd: To ask the Scottish Executive how many respite weeks Glasgow City Council provided in (a) 2007-08, (b) 2008-09 and (c) 2009-10.

S3W-37423 Bill Kidd: To ask the Scottish Executive how much of the additional £4 million Scottish Government funding for respite provision Glasgow City Council received in 2009-10 and has received in 2010-11.

S3W-37424 Bill Kidd: To ask the Scottish Executive how much respite funding Glasgow City Council will receive in its local government baseline for 2011-12.

S3W-37425 Bill Kidd: To ask the Scottish Executive what Glasgow City Council’s contribution would be to the 8,950 additional respite weeks identified in paragraph 2.1 of Respite Care, Scotland 2010 based on the local government funding formula.

S3W-37426 Bill Kidd: To ask the Scottish Executive what Glasgow City Council’s contribution has been to the 8,950 additional respite weeks identified in paragraph 2.1 of Respite Care, Scotland 2010.

S3W-37427 Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive what the cost was of developing the new maternal and early years website and how much will be spent annually on maintenance of the site.

S3W-37428 Joe FitzPatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact it considers the decision of HM Treasury to increase interest rates on new loans from the Public Works Loan Board to an average of 1% above UK Government gilts will have on the ability of Scottish local authorities to borrow.

S3W-37429 Joe FitzPatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive how it considers tax incremental financing projects in Scotland will be affected by the decision of HM Treasury to increase interest rates on new loans from the Public Works Loan Board to an average of 1% above UK Government gilts.

S3W-37430 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of the comment of the Minister for Transport, Infrastructure and Climate Change reported in The Courier of 10 November 2010 that "The A9 is a vital link to north Scotland and the Scottish Government is committed to dualling it, from Perth to Inverness, on a phased basis", when it will set out a timetable for such dualling of the A9.

S3W-37431 Joe FitzPatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact it considers the decision of HM Treasury to increase interest rates on new loans from the Public Works Loan Board to an average of 1% above UK Government gilts will have on council house building in Scotland.

S3W-37432Δ Nigel Don: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will publish its response to Lord Gill’s Report of the Scottish Civil Courts Review.

S3W-37433 Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive in what ways the Scottish Social Housing Charter will differ from the Performance Standards for social landlords and homelessness functions.

S3W-37434 Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consult on the powers of the Scottish Housing Regulator to issue performance targets to housing associations contained in the Housing (Scotland) Bill.

S3W-37435 Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive what the total level of housing debt is of local authorities.

S3W-37436 Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive how much interest is to be paid on the total housing debt of local authorities.

S3W-37437 Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive what the implications are for Scottish local authorities of the change in interest rate payable on loans from the Public Works Loan Board.

S3W-37438 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance it issues to parents or caregivers on sharing a bed with an infant.

S3W-37439 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the cost of the Public Health Service community pharmacy contract has been in each of the last three years, also broken down by the costs for (a) smoking cessation and (b) emergency hormone contraception.

S3W-37440 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider allowing pharmacists to change prescriptions for branded drugs to generic alternatives, given the decision by the Department of Health not to proceed with this policy.

S3W-37441 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the predicted cost will be of retaining the current eligibility and exclusions in the minor ailments scheme should prescription charges be abolished.

S3W-37442 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the cost of the minor ailments scheme was in the latest year for which figures are available.

S3W-37443 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken to ensure that community pharmacists have a sufficient supply of medicines from manufacturers to fill prescriptions and whether it is has worked with the Department of Health on this.

S3W-37444 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many patients were given thrombolysis in (a) 2007, (b) 2008, and (c) 2009, also expressed as a percentage of acute stroke patients.

S3W-37445 Alison McInnes: To ask the Scottish Executive what the duration is of Transport Scotland’s contract with the Performance Audit Group.

S3W-37446 Alison McInnes: To ask the Scottish Executive what the cost (a) is of Transport Scotland’s (TS) contract with the Performance Audit Group (PAG) and (b) was of previous contracts for work for TS currently carried out by PAG.

S3W-37447 Peter Peacock: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking with education authorities to promote and report on performance improvement plans under the provisions in the Standards in Scotland’s Schools etc. Act 2000.
