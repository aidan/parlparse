Business Bulletin No. 50 / 2004

Thursday 18th March 2004

Section D : Oral questions selected for answer on Thursday 25th March 2004
  

Question Time

Environment and Rural Development

1. Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive what the implications for the environment are of an environmental court. (S2O-1781)

2. Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will build upon the findings of its research study, The Economic Impact of Game and Coarse Angling in Scotland. (S2O-1690)

3. Mark Ballard: To ask the Scottish Executive whether Chardon LL maize, when grown under the conditions of the farm scale evaluations, will have value for cultivation and use. (S2O-1795)

4. Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide assistance to local authorities to clear beaches polluted by debris brought in by the tide. (S2O-1748)

5. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to encourage Scottish Water to become more efficient. (S2O-1751)

6. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in developing markets for recycled materials. (S2O-1743)

7. Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will raise awareness of any problems associated with collecting shellfish from polluted beaches. (S2O-1715)

8. Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration has been given to the impact of the extended development of Aucheninnes landfill site near Dalbeattie on the biodiversity of the immediate area. (S2O-1749)

9. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to reduce industrial and domestic waste arisings. (S2O-1746)

10. Chris Ballance: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied that Chardon LL maize has satisfied EU regulations concerning distinctness, uniformity and stability. (S2O-1796)

11. Mr Mark Ruskell: To ask the Scottish Executive what increases in support for organic farming have occurred since the introduction of the Organic Action Plan. (S2O-1747)

12. Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive how it intends to increase the number of recycling points in rural areas. (S2O-1801)

Health and Community Care

1. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to reduce inequalities in health. (S2O-1736)

2. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to address health inequalities shown in the recent statistical survey conducted by NHS Health Scotland. (S2O-1688)

3. Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive what its objectives are for NHS dental services in 2004-05. (S2O-1811)

4. Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to introduce legislation similar to the Human Tissue Bill in Westminster. (S2O-1739)

5. Christine May: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will ensure that the new contracts for GPs and consultants deliver benefits for the quality and provision of health care. (S2O-1728)

6. John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to address the health implications of under-age drinking in the Highlands. (S2O-1731)

7. Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will initiate pilot studies and a cost-benefit analysis of exercise on prescription from NHS doctors. (S2O-1788)

8. Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to ensure co-operation between neighbouring NHS boards. (S2O-1716)

9. Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will meet its target to bring 12,000 nurses and midwives into the NHS by 2007. (S2O-1703)

10. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive how out-of-hours services will be provided in areas where GPs opt out under the new contract provisions. (S2O-1729)

11. David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has for diabetic eye screening. (S2O-1726)

12. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact the introduction of NHS 24 has had upon ambulance call-out rates. (S2O-1757)

General

1. Miss Annabel Goldie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many criminal cases were processed by district courts in the west of Scotland in each of the last three years, broken down by parliamentary constituency. (S2O-1771)

2. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met Aberdeen City Council, whether the council's budget was discussed and what the outcome of the meeting was. (S2O-1686)

3. Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made on increasing the number of secure accommodation places. (S2O-1774)

4. Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the benefits are of funding the traditional music school at Plockton High School. (S2O-1763)

5. Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it will give to the maintenance and development of skilled craft industries in the Highlands and Islands. (S2O-1766)

6. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it would support the creation of an association of Scottish prisoners. (S2O-1794)

7. Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive what its plans are for the modern apprenticeship scheme. (S2O-1721)

8. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to support rural schools. (S2O-1704)

9. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there is any regional variation in the rates by which school rolls will fall. (S2O-1764)

10. Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive which housing associations are to be audited in the coming year. (S2O-1755)

11. Christine May: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in accrediting programmes to tackle offending behaviour. (S2O-1775)

12. John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive what assurances it can give to Caledonian MacBrayne staff that their employment terms will not be undermined by putting services out to tender. (S2O-1756)

13. Chris Ballance: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will support a campaign for improved rail connections between Lanark and Edinburgh. (S2O-1792)

14. Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of the costs of the Waverley Station upgrade will be met by the Strategic Rail Authority. (S2O-1778)

15. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in achieving the goals set out in the European Union's Lisbon Agenda. (S2O-1697)
