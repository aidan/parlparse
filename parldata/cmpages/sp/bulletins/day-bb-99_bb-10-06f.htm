BUSINESS BULLETIN 14/1999

Thursday 10 June 1999

Section F –  Motions and amendments lodged on 9 June 1999

 

(Items marked with an asterisk* are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made)

 

*S1M-49 Maureen Macmillan: That the Parliament note that the work plan drawn up by the Scottish Partnership on Domestic Violence is currently out for consultation with a deadline for responses of 30 June 1999, and call upon Scottish Ministers to lay the work plan before the Parliament for consideration as soon as possible after 1 July 1999.

 

*S1M-48 Donald Gorrie: That the Parliament agree to support the proposal made by the Association of University Teachers that a system of independent pay review for university academic and related staff be established in order to maintain their pay at the level necessary to continue to develop the quality of Scotland’s world-class universities.

 

*S1M-47 Brian Adam: That the Parliament call upon the Scottish Ministers to investigate all available means to expedite the building of a peripheral route around Aberdeen.

Supported by: Richard Lochhead, Mr Andrew Welsh, Irene McGugan, Mr David Davidson, Nora Radcliffe.

 

*S1M-46 Robert Brown: That the Parliament agree to the recommendation of the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body that smoking be prohibited within the Parliamentary complex.

 

Supported by: Andrew Welsh, Des McNulty

 

*S1M-45 Phil Gallie: That the Parliament consider that the combined activities of the 129 members reduce significantly the workload of Scotland’s Westminster MPs and express the view that Scottish Westminster MPs’ levels of remuneration should be reduced to the level prevailing for MSPs.

Supported by: Bill Aitken, Mary Scanlon, Alex Fergusson, David Mundell, Mr Murray Tosh, Mr Nick Johnston
