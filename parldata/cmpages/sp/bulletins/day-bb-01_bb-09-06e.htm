Business Bulletin No. 124/2001

Thursday 6 September 2001

Section E : Written questions lodged on 5 September 2001
  

S1W-17916 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what the cost was of changing the name of visitscotland to VisitScotland and from which expenditure head the costs were met.

S1W-17938 Brian Fitzpatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to make drug treatment and testing orders more widely available to courts.

S1W-17953 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the announcement on 7 August 2001 by the Deputy Minister for Enterprise and Lifelong Learning and Gaelic of a £36 million funding package to improve tourist and visitor facilities in the Highlands and Islands, what the source is of the £24 million funding from the private sector; how much of this is currently available; what funding the private sector will make available in each of the next three years; what criteria individuals or businesses applying for assistance under this package will have to meet; whether funding sought by an individual or business will require to be matched by that individual or business, and whether assistance received under this package will require to be repaid at any time.

S1W-17954 Ben Wallace: To ask the Scottish Executive what its current targets are for reducing the incidence of (a) heart disease, (b) cancers, (c) teenage pregnancies, (d) sexually transmitted diseases and (e) strokes.

S1W-17955 Ben Wallace: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will detail all targets within the NHS that have been set since 1997 including those for waiting lists, waiting times and reducing the incidence of heart disease, cancer, teenage pregnancies, sexually transmitted diseases and strokes.

S1W-17956 Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive what effect the introduction of the VAT recovery scheme will have on renovation work carried out by Historic Scotland on Grade A listed churches.

S1W-17957 Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive why Historic Scotland has taken action to implement the VAT recovery scheme for Grade A listed churches before the guidelines for its commencement were in place.

S1W-17958 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive, in the light of proposals for pilot projects in Manchester and London for night and weekend courts, whether it intends to introduce a similar scheme in Scotland.

S1W-17959 Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive what targets it has set for the Scottish Agricultural Science Agency in 2001-02.

S1W-17960 Mr Keith Harding: To ask the Scottish Executive what the total costs were of the publication, including the design, photocopying, printing, internet connection of text, distribution and launch, of Connecting Scotland: our broadband future.

S1W-17961 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of those graduating from dental courses in Scotland in each of the last three years remained in Scotland after graduation to undertake dental vocational training.

S1W-17962 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of dentists who completed their vocational training in Scotland in each of the last three years remained in Scotland working as dentists afterwards.

S1W-17963 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of non-Scottish students graduating from dental degree courses in Scotland in each of the last three years remained in Scotland after graduation to undertake dental vocational training.

S1W-17964 Ben Wallace: To ask the Scottish Executive what the total cost of each public inquiry established since 1995 has been, including salaries, expenses, accommodation, printing and any other items.

S1W-17965 Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether batches of blood and blood products produced before heat treatment was introduced in 1987 continued to be used to treat patients in the NHS after heat treated blood products were available and, if so, when they ceased being used.

S1W-17966 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has received from Strathclyde Passenger Transport regarding any proposed European legislation which might lead to part privatisation of the Glasgow underground system.

S1W-17967 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will assess the cost implications for the Glasgow underground system of the European draft legislation on tendering for public transport systems.

S1W-17968 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made by the Clyde Shipyards Taskforce in securing the long-term future of and offsetting job losses at the BAe Systems shipyard at Scotstoun.

S1W-17969 Pauline McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive what the outcome was of the consultation last year on the physical punishment of children in Scotland.

S1W-17970 Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive when funding will be made available for the installation of central heating for pensioners in owner-occupied houses.
