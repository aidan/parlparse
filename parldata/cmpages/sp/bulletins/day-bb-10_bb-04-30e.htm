Business Bulletin No. 72/2010: Friday 30 April 2010
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 29 April 2010

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-33432 Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive what means NHS boards have at their disposal to ensure that instructions given by NHS Quality Improvement Scotland and the Chief Medical Officer for Scotland are implemented by GP surgeries, which are independent contractors.

S3W-33433 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what special advisers have resigned to participate in the UK general election.

S3W-33434 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what official engagements were undertaken by the Minister for Parliamentary Business on 27 April 2010.

S3W-33435 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what official engagements were undertaken by the Cabinet Secretary for Health and Wellbeing on 27 April 2010.

S3W-33436 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what the travel arrangements are for the First Minister’s visits to the Maldives and Delhi; how many officials will accompany him, and what the total estimated cost is.

S3W-33437 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body for what reason the Parliament’s vehicle entrance security gates are not working and when it is expected that they will be.

S3W-33438 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body what the total cost was of the Parliament’s security apparatus and allied works for the vehicle entrance.

S3W-33439 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many acute occupied bed days in NHS Ayrshire and Arran were directly connected to a diagnosis of assault by sharp object in each of the last five years for which information is available.

S3W-33440 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive what the percentage MMR1 immunisation rate has been in NHS Tayside for children up to (a) two and (b) five years of age in each year since 1998.

S3W-33441 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive what the percentage MMR2 immunisation rate has been in NHS Tayside for children up to five years of age in each year since 1998.

S3W-33442 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive what the estimated number was of people aged over 12 with diabetes in NHS Tayside eligible for diabetic retinopathy screening in the last year for which information is available.

S3W-33443 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people aged 12 and over with diabetes in NHS Tayside who were eligible for diabetic retinopathy screening were (a) offered an appointment for screening and (b) screened in the last year for which information is available.

S3W-33444 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people aged 12 and over with diabetes in NHS Tayside were referred to ophthalmology as a result of diabetic retinopathy screening in the last year for which information is available.

S3W-33445 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is considering introducing a grant similar to the Home Access Grant available in England.

S3W-33446 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what research it has carried out on the economic benefits of home access grants such as those available in England.

S3W-33447 David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Executive when the proposed charitable legal vehicle, the Scottish Charitable Incorporated Organisation, will be available to third sector organisations.

S3W-33448 David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Executive when the proposed charitable legal vehicle, the Scottish Charitable Incorporated Organisation, will be available to third sector organisations.

S3W-33449 David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met the Scottish Council of Voluntary Organisations and whether the Scottish Charitable Incorporated Organisation was discussed.

S3W-33450 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what proportion of deer management groups have produced a deer management plan up to 2010.

S3W-33451 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what proportion of deer management groups are considered by the Deer Commission for Scotland to be carrying out effective implementation of their deer management plans, including meeting agreed cull targets.

S3W-33452 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects that all deer management groups will have deer management plans in place.

S3W-33453 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive how many deer management groups have delivered designated features in their areas into favourable condition, without the need for statutory intervention under section 7 of the Deer (Scotland) Act 1996.

S3W-33454 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive how many deer died as a result of the 2009-10 winter and how this compares with other European countries with red deer.

S3W-33455 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether all NHS boards offer the full screening programme to pregnant women as outlined in the letter from the Director of Public Health and Wellbeing, CEL 31 (2008), dated July 2008.

S3W-33456 Bill Aitken: To ask the Scottish Executive what the extent is of the involvement of Crown Office and Procurator Fiscal staff in assisting other prosecution authorities outwith Scotland and particularly the extent of this involvement in Afghanistan.
