Business Bulletin No. 92/2002

Thursday 6 June 2002

Section E : Written questions lodged on 5 June 2002
  

Questions marked with a triangle () are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S1W-26438 Mr Duncan Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any consultation has taken place between Caledonian MacBrayne and those responsible for drawing up the draft specifications for the Islay route prior to competitive tendering and whether new Caledonian MacBrayne plans for the Islay route will be incorporated into those draft specifications.

S1W-26439 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive what the current position is in respect of the disposal of each dwelling acquired by it during the construction of the M74 from Douglas to Gretna.

S1W-26440 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to phase in policies over the next six to eight years for French to be the only language taught in primary schools and S1 and S2 and for primary teacher trainees only to be taught the French language in colleges.

S1W-26441 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are any plans to downgrade and phase out the teaching of the German language in schools.

S1W-26442 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it supports the recommendation of the Ministerial Action Group on Languages which called for the consolidation and extension of language learning in primary schools and the further diversification of languages at all levels of secondary education.

S1W-26443 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many staff in each local authority area were employed in youth work in each of the last ten years.

S1W-26444 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of each local authority’s budget was allocated to youth work in each of the last ten years.

S1W-26445 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will develop a national youth strategy.

S1W-26446 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what compensation will be paid to the owners of salmon fishings, sporting rights and mineral rights for any immediate losses in the market value of their holdings as a consequence of the absolute right to buy under Part 3 of the Land Reform (Scotland) Bill.

S1W-26447 Mrs Lyndsay McIntosh: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are any plans to introduce new measures to protect vulnerable pre-school children.

S1W-26448 Mrs Lyndsay McIntosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are in place to support vulnerable pre-school children.

S1W-26449 Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance it has issued on the role of classroom assistants in schools and how their role is defined in any such guidance.

S1W-26450 Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it publishes on the number of pupils excluded from schools and whether it will give a breakdown by education authority area of the number of such exclusions in each of the last three years.

S1W-26451 Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to tackle truancy.

S1W-26452 Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive what further plans it has to improve discipline in schools.

S1W-26453 Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment has been made of the value of breakfast clubs in improving school pupils’ attendance and achievement.

S1W-26454 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to support uptake of the Food Quality Assurance scheme.

S1W-26455 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive what action Scottish Enterprise is taking to create stronger links with companies and institutions in order to internationalise their activities.

S1W-26456 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive when web-based visitor information on the sustainable use of natural heritage sites will be made available.

S1W-26457 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive what the current incidence of road rage is and how it compares with levels in the 1970s.

S1W-26458 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-26044 by Hugh Henry on 30 May 2002, when a decision will be made on whether to call in the planning application for a crematorium to be developed at Greenhall Park, High Blantyre, South Lanarkshire.

S1W-26459 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive which local authorities fund independent living services or jointly fund care packages under the Independent Living Fund and how many such packages have been funded in each local authority area in each of the last three years.

S1W-26460 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-24726 by Cathy Jamieson on 1 May 2002, how many projects were included in the proposals from each local authority in respect of the Changing Children’s Services Fund and how many such projects relate (a) exclusively to statutory sector provision, (b) exclusively to voluntary sector provision and (c) to partnership projects.

S1W-26461 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-24726 by Cathy Jamieson on 1 May 2002, what the total percentage share of funding is in each local authority proposal to the Changing Children’s Services Fund for (a) statutory, (b) voluntary and (c) partnership projects.

S1W-26462 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-24726 by Cathy Jamieson on 1 May 2002, how each bid to the Changing Children’s Services Fund will be monitored for adherence to set criteria.

S1W-26463 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-24726 by Cathy Jamieson on 1 May 2002, whether it is intended that projects within the Changing Children’s Services programme will be (a) linked to or (b) part of Children’s Services Plans.

S1W-26464 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-21284 by Iain Gray on 16 January 2002, how many local champions are now operating.

S1W-26465 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-21273 by Iain Gray on 16 January 2002, whether the study into how ICT and web access can be improved for disabled people has been completed.
