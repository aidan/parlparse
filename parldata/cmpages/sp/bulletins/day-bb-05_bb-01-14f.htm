Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-2258 Peter Peacock: Further and Higher Education (Scotland) Bill—That the Parliament agrees to the general principles of the Further and Higher Education (Scotland) Bill.

Supported by: Euan Robson*

*S2M-2257 Alex Neil: Local Authority Public Appointments—That the Parliament calls on local authorities in Scotland to apply the principles of the Public Appointments and Public Bodies etc. (Scotland) Act 2003 for nominations to bodies for which they have responsibility.

*S2M-2256 Patrick Harvie: Release of Guantanamo Bay Detainees—That the Parliament welcomes the announcement of the release of the four Britons still held in Guantanamo Bay and calls on the UK Government to release immediately the 11 detainees held without charge or trial in Belmarsh Prison under the Anti-Terrorism, Crime and Security Act 2001, an action which was condemned by the highest court in the land on 16 December 2004.

*S2M-2255 Patricia Ferguson: National Lottery Bill - UK Legislation—That the Parliament agrees that the provisions in the National Lottery Bill that relate to the executive competence of the Scottish Ministers should be considered by the UK Parliament.

Supported by: Tavish Scott*

*S2M-2254 Alex Neil: War Crimes in Iraq—That the Parliament calls for the prosecution of President George W Bush and the Prime Minister for war crimes, given that the US administration and Her Majesty’s Government have now effectively admitted that there were no weapons of mass destruction in Iraq, which was the basis upon which they justified what many consider to be their illegal war against Iraq.

*S2M-2253 Alex Neil: Vulnerable Adults Bill—That the Parliament calls on the Scottish Executive to introduce its proposed Vulnerable Adults Bill as soon as possible.

*S2M-2252 Pauline McNeill: Reinstatement of Jim O’Donovan—That the Parliament notes the employment tribunal decision in the case of O’Donovan v Central College of Commerce to order the reinstatement of Jim O’Donovan by 31 January 2005 and believes that this ruling should be adhered to by the Central College of Commerce immediately and that it is in the public interest for this dispute to end as soon as possible so that all parties involved can rebuild relationships between management and staff in the interests of further education generally.

Supported by: Marlyn Glen*, Dr Elaine Murray*, Mr Frank McAveety*, Janis Hughes*, Bill Butler*, Elaine Smith*, Gordon Jackson*, Mike Watson*, Marilyn Livingstone*, Mr Kenneth Macintosh*, Christine May*

*S2M-2251 Pauline McNeill: Congratulations to President Abbas—That the Parliament congratulates Mahmoud Abbas on his election to the office of President of the Palestinian people; supports the reports of international observers that the elections for the leader of the Palestinian Authority were free and fair; believes that President Abbas has a strong mandate for peace in the Middle East; urges the Israeli Government to negotiate peace with, and justice for, the Palestinians and withdrawal from the occupied territories, and further calls on the international community to do all that it can to support a peaceful settlement, the return of humanitarian rights to the Palestinian people and the establishment of a viable Palestinian state.

*S2M-2250 Elaine Smith: National No School Uniform Day, Friday 21 January 2005—That the Parliament commends the efforts of the Scottish Youth Parliament in promoting a National No School Uniform Day across Scotland on Friday 21 January 2005 in aid of the Disasters Emergency Committee Tsunami Earthquake Appeal; considers that such an event, held simultaneously across the United Kingdom, has the potential to raise considerable sums of money for the aid effort in Asia; welcomes the opportunity it presents for young people to get involved in this cause; congratulates those young people who have been instrumental in the organisation of this event; wishes the initiative every success, and encourages all Scottish schools to consider getting involved.
