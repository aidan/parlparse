BUSINESS BULLETIN 13 / 2001

Tuesday 23 January 2001

Section F - Motions and Amendments

 

 

 

The full text of all outstanding motions and amendments will appear in the Business Bulletin every Monday.

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-1576 Mr Lloyd Quinan: MMR Triple Vaccine—That the Parliament acknowledges the research carried out by Dr Andrew Wakefield and the subsequent report in the medical journal, Adverse Drug Reactions; believes that this report and the fall in the current level of vaccines being administered requires immediate attention, and further believes that, until a full study is carried out into Autistic Spectrum Disorders and any possible link with the MMR vaccine, single vaccines should be made available as an alternative for parents and carers.

*S1M-1575# Roseanna Cunningham: Tayside Acute Services Review Consultation—That the Parliament expresses its lack of faith in the consultation process associated with the Tayside Acute Services Review and the way in which public opinion is being ignored; is concerned that Tayside Health Board are intent on imposing a centralising agenda which will remove services from Angus and Perthshire and overload the service in Dundee, and believes that the Health and Community Care Committee should undertake an inquiry into the evidence base for the proposals and the effectiveness of the consultation process undertaken.
