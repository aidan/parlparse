Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally printed the day after they are lodged. If an amendment is lodged to a motion then the original motion will appear alongside the amendment, along with any support lodged that day, in this section.

Each Monday, this section also contains all motions and amendments lodged the previous week that are still live and any motions and amendments that have been lodged for debate in the coming week. A motion or amendment is live if it has not been debated, withdrawn or otherwise deleted from the list.

Where a motion or amendment has been withdrawn, it is indicated in this section the day after it has been withdrawn. Support for motions and amendments received after they are lodged is shown at the end of this section the day after such support is received.

Motions and amendments can be published with various symbols:

  • Asterisks before the motion or amendment number indicate a motion or amendment published for the first time;
  • Asterisks also identify alterations to the text of a motion or amendment made since it was first published;
  • A hash symbol identifies motions eligible for debate at Members’ Business;
  • A diamond symbol identifies motions lodged for Members’ Business that have not yet attracted the required cross-party support;
  • An "R", identifies motions or amendments in which the Member who lodged it has a registrable interest.

The Parliamentary Bureau periodically deletes motions or amendments not scheduled for debate and which are over six weeks old.

A full list of current motions is available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/motions.htm.

A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/index.htm.

Any questions in relation to this section should be directed to the Chamber Desk, for which contact details are provided at the end of the Bulletin.

New and altered motions and amendments

*S3M-8137 Margo MacDonald: Count Me In Campaign—That the Parliament endorses the five principles adopted by the Alliance of Sector Skills Councils in Scotland for its Count Me In Campaign for the Scottish Parliament election; commends the principles on which the campaign is based - to deliver a skilled workforce, to put employers at the heart of the skills system, to ensure that high quality advice and information is available to deliver a skilled workforce and to widen access to apprenticeships and vocational qualifications, and acknowledges that these constitute a means whereby skills will be developed and prospects for employment improved.

*S3M-8136 Rhoda Grant: Domestic Abuse (Scotland) Bill—That the Parliament agrees that the Domestic Abuse (Scotland) Bill be passed.

*S3M-8135 Robert Brown: Handling of Asylum Claims in Scotland—That the Parliament welcomes 21 Months Later, a report by the British Red Cross (BRC) and the Refugee Survival Trust (RST), which highlights the plight of people who have to travel from Scotland to Croydon to register claims for asylum without receiving financial help for this from the UK Government; notes that the BRC and RST pay for food and overnight bus travel for the claimants; further notes that the BRC and RST are calling on the UK Border Agency to meet the travel costs for people making these trips of approximately 400 miles to register their claims, and considers that asylum seekers housed in Scotland should have the right to lodge and have their claims dealt with in Scotland.

*S3M-8134 Bob Doris: Strictly for Charity—That the Parliament notes that Scottish Enterprise is hosting its annual (A Little Less) Strictly Come Dancing fundraiser on Saturday 2 April 2011 with all proceeds going to the Prince & Princess of Wales Hospice in Glasgow, which it considers provides a first class, free service for patients and families, supporting them with dignity during distressing times; notes that six couples from the worlds of celebrity and business will compete with their Scottish professional partners; notes that contestants will include the actress, Kathleen McDermott Carberry, Clyde 1’s Romeo, Scottish footballer Gordon Smith, Scottish event rider Nicola Malcolm and Chief Executive of Scottish Enterprise Lena Wilson; believes this to be a worthwhile cause, and urges the public to support it by donating one pound by texting the name of the dancer whom they wish to support to 70100 on 2 April.

Supported by: Christina McKelvie*, Anne McLaughlin*, Bill Kidd*, Aileen Campbell*

*S3M-8133 Anne McLaughlin: Zimbabweans Facing the Death Penalty for Discussing the Egyptian Revolution—That the Parliament condemns the arrest of 45 Zimbabweans for watching and discussing news coverage of the Egyptian revolution; understands that 39 of the 45 have been released and urges the Zimbabwean Government to release the remaining six who face charges of treason and the possibility of the death penalty; is concerned about the special relationship between the Zimbabwean president, Robert Mugabe, and Libya’s Muammar Gaddafi; urges Zimbabwe to join the rest of the international community and publicly denounce the atrocities of the Libyan Government, and, lastly, demands that justice should be faced by the Libyan dictatorship and that Mugabe and his administration do not interfere with this.

Supported by: Bill Wilson*, Hugh O’Donnell*, Bill Kidd*, Kenneth Gibson*, Shirley-Anne Somerville*, Robin Harper*

*S3M-8132 Gil Paterson: 70th Anniversary of the Clydebank Blitz—That the Parliament recognises the 70th anniversary of the Clydebank Blitz and joins with the community of Clydebank in remembering all those who perished or who were adversely affected by the events of 13 and 14 March 1941; further recognises what it considers to have been the heroic efforts of the public services and volunteer organisations that responded from across Scotland and beyond to offer assistance, and puts on record its sincere thanks to the officers and crew of the Polish destroyer, ORP Piorun, who put themselves in harm’s way during the bombardment by drawing the fire of the enemy aircraft to them instead of the town and its people, an action that will never be forgotten by the community of Clydebank.

*S3M-8131 Sandra White: Welcome Modernisation of Glasgow Subway System—That the Parliament welcomes the announcement by the Scottish Government that it is to make a substantial capital contribution to support Strathclyde Partnership for Transport’s (SPT) modernisation proposals for Glasgow’s subway; believes that, while the proposed modernisation will bring about material benefits in the form of new trains, new automated signals, refurbished stations with improved accessibility and smartcard ticketing, any funding must be made on the condition of improved opening hours to reflect the needs of passengers, and calls on SPT to set out in detail how this will be achieved.

Supported by: Rob Gibson*, Stuart McMillan*, Stewart Maxwell*, Bill Kidd*, Christina McKelvie*

*S3M-8124 Bob Doris: Green Light for Subway Modernisation—That the Parliament welcomes the recent announcement by the Scottish Government that it is to support Strathclyde Partnership for Transport’s business case for subway modernisation in Glasgow; believes that this reaffirms the SNP government’s strong commitment to investing in Glasgow, which it considers is already demonstrated by its investment in projects such as the 2014 Commonwealth Games, the M74 extension, the new Southern General Hospital and many others; looks forward to the upgrading of Glasgow’s clockwork orange, and congratulates all those who have helped campaign for subway modernisation.

*S3M-8123 John Wilson: North Lanarkshire’s Gaelic Initiative Awards 2011—That the Parliament congratulates North Lanarkshire Council on recognising the excellent work being done throughout North Lanarkshire in relation to the Gaelic medium by holding the Gaelic Initiative Awards on Friday 4 March 2011; congratulates those groups that were honoured at the awards ceremony, which included Orain Luaidh, Foram Lannraig A’ Tuath, Language Classes, Sradagan, Lorg, Feis, Playsceme, Cumbernauld Gaelic Choir, Camanachd Cearcall Lannraig A’ Tuath, Cearcall Family Learning Group, Condorrat Primary School’s Gaelic Unit, Ulpan Classes, Croilegean Bogha Frois and Croilegean Isrannan; further congratulates those groups that provided the entertainment of music and singing on the night, and joins in wishing all North Lanarkshire-based Gaelic initiatives every future success.

*S3M-8122 Dr Richard Simpson: Tayside Wild Beavers—That the Parliament notes that the group of beavers that have established themselves in Tayside and Angus may be the subject of proposals for capture and extermination and calls on the Scottish Government to make it clear that this action will not happen and that consideration should be given to incorporating them into an extended study of beavers to determine the effect on the environment.

*S3M-8121 Alasdair Allan: 100 Years of the Harris Tweed Orb—That the Parliament notes that on Saturday 12 March 2011 a fashion show will take place in the An Lanntair Arts Centre in Stornoway that will showcase works by some of the UK’s leading designers to mark the 100th anniversary of the famous Harris Tweed Orb; recognises that, for a century now, this mark, which by law has to be stamped on every piece of genuine Harris Tweed, has symbolised the quality of what it considers this extraordinary fabric, which is produced and prepared entirely in the Outer Hebrides; is encouraged to note that both operational tweed mills in the islands are finding successful new markets and that Harris Tweed Textiles, based in Carloway, Isle of Lewis, has recently marked the quadrupling of its production in the last year; believes that Harris Tweed is a unique part of the heritage of the Western Isles and that it has an outstanding role to play in promoting the islands around the world, and looks forward to many more successful years in the future for Harris Tweed.

Supported by: Anne McLaughlin*, Joe FitzPatrick*, Rob Gibson*, Mary Scanlon*, John Wilson*, Des McNulty*, Jamie McGrigor*, Bill Kidd*, Stuart McMillan*, Aileen Campbell*, Stewart Maxwell*, Kenneth Gibson*, Mike Pringle*, Brian Adam*, Robin Harper*

*S3M-8120.1 Michael McMahon: Local Services—As an amendment to motion (S3M-8120) in the name of Robert Brown, leave out from "is concerned" to end and insert "believes that the case for reform and innovation in Scotland’s public services is unanswerable and that meeting the needs of the public is more important than preserving structures; recognises that the need to protect frontline services in the face of UK Government cuts requires the status quo in public services to be reassessed; believes that moving to a single fire and rescue service, a single police service and the creation of a national care service offers the best opportunity for improving delivery of these services in Scotland, and believes that these new structures will allow the reinvestment of savings to maintain frontline staff while ensuring that there are improved mechanisms for local democratic accountability and delivery on local priorities."

Supported by: Richard Baker*

*S3M-8120.2 Kenny MacAskill: Local Services—As an amendment to motion (S3M-8120) in the name of Robert Brown, leave out from "is concerned" to end and insert "notes that Scotland and policing have changed significantly since the existing structure of policing was introduced over 35 years ago; notes the Scottish Government’s consultation papers on police and fire services reform and agrees that, given the significant financial challenges, such reform is necessary to protect and improve local services and to strengthen and improve local accountability and engagement; agrees that reform can only happen if it gives local communities and local elected members a greater say on local priorities and services, and notes that the expectations and requirements of health and social care have similarly changed significantly since existing structures were introduced and that reform is needed to deliver integrated services that are sustainable and appropriate and that make best use of resources focussed on the needs of local populations."

Supported by: Shona Robison*

*S3M-8114.1 Fiona Hyslop: Scotland Bill - UK Legislation—As an amendment to motion (S3M-8114) in the name of Iain Gray, leave out from first "the Bill" to end and insert "Scotland is best served by a Scottish Parliament that has the full range of powers and responsibilities necessary to improve Scotland’s economic performance and promote sustainable economic growth; agrees that the current provisions of the Scotland Bill do not provide those powers and responsibilities; recognises the improvements to the Bill suggested by the Scotland Bill Committee but is of the view that further improvements are needed to provide the financial responsibility that Scotland needs and to address other flaws in the Bill; nevertheless agrees that the UK Parliament should consider the Bill, apart from the provisions that reserve insolvency and the regulation of health professions (clauses 12 and 13) and allow UK ministers to implement international obligations in devolved areas (clause 23) and partial suspension of Acts subject to scrutiny by the Supreme Court (clause 7), and further agrees that, given the amendments requested by the Scottish Parliament, the incoming Scottish Parliament should consider the Bill as amended by the UK Parliament in a further legislative consent motion before the Bill is passed for Royal Assent."

Supported by: Bruce Crawford*

*S3M-8114.2 Margaret Mitchell: Scotland Bill - UK Legislation—As an amendment to motion (S3M-8114) in the name of Iain Gray, after "Committee" insert "together with the proposal that a nationwide referendum be held in Scotland on the fiscal powers of the Scottish Parliament on the grounds that the public has a democratic right to decide whether to transfer powers such as income tax from MPs to MSPs".
