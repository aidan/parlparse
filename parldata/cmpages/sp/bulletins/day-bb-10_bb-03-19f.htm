Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (♦).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S3M-6001 Rhona Brankin: Fertility Postcode Lottery—That the Parliament welcomes the news that 12 out of 14 NHS boards are compliant with the national criteria for Level III Assisted Conception Treatment of 39 years inclusive; regrets that NHS Fife and Lothian have yet to comply, and calls on them to do so.

*S3M-6000 Ken Macintosh: Williamwood High School—That the Parliament congratulates the staff, parents and pupils of Williamwood High School on becoming the best performing state school in Scotland; applauds Williamwood on achieving the highest percentage of S5 pupils gaining five or more Highers in Scotland; commends the high standards of education across the whole of East Renfrewshire, and considers that this illustrates that state schools under local authority control can and do achieve excellence.

*S3M-5999 Margo MacDonald: National School Sport Week—That the Parliament congratulates the Bank of Scotland, sportscotland and the Youth Sport Trust on their joint initiative to bring the National School Sport Week to Scotland from 7 to 11 June 2010; notes that, last year, this event attracted almost three million children to take part in sport and physical activity across England and Wales; welcomes its aim of expanding the numbers of Scots pupils taking part in sport, and urges Scottish education authorities to embrace this initiative.

*S3M-5998 James Kelly: Congratulations to the Oakwood Nursery Council in Securing an Awards for All Scotland Grant—That the Parliament notes the announcement that the Oakwood Nursery Council has been successful in securing a funding grant of £8,000 from the Big Lottery Fund Awards for All Scotland to create an eco-garden for the creative use of the children attending Oakwood House Nursery, children from other local nurseries and elderly residents of a local care home and congratulates all members of the Oakwood Nursery Council for their excellent contribution to the local community.

*S3M-5997 James Kelly: Congratulations to the Toryglen Community Council in Securing an Awards for All Scotland Grant—That the Parliament notes the announcement that the Toryglen Community Council has been successful in securing a funding grant of £664 from the Big Lottery Fund Awards for All Scotland that will enable the group to purchase IT equipment, increasing its capacity to better represent the local community’s views, allowing its community to be safer, stronger and more able to work together to tackle inequalities, and congratulates all members of the Toryglen Community Council for their excellent contribution to the local community.

*S3M-5996 Willie Coffey: Kilmaurs Wild Writers Are Winners—That the Parliament congratulates the pupils of Kilmaurs Primary School on their success in the John Muir Trust’s Wild Writing Competition; notes that the winner of the primary school section was Kilmaurs’ pupil Ailsa McAllan, with fellow pupil Bobby Dunn taking second prize and four other pupils, Lewis Creed, Michael McManus-Hutcheson, Abbie Morton and Emma Shanks, being highly commended, and agrees that their excellent work is a credit to the individual pupils and to the whole school.

*S3M-5995 Jamie Stone: Continuing Loss of Life at Railway Level Crossings—That the Parliament regrets the continuing loss of life at railway level crossings, most recently at Halkirk in Caithness and, in January 2009, at Gatehead in Kilmarnock and Loudoun; notes that there are 23 crossings in Scotland that are automatic open crossings, of which 21 are in the Highlands and two in Ardrossan; believes that crossings without barriers are intrinsically dangerous and inadequate for the task at hand; further believes that there can be no justification for not taking urgent action to stop further fatalities and injuries at rail crossings, and calls on the Scottish Government to work in conjunction with the many organisations and agencies involved in rail safety and direct Network Rail to install barriers at all 23 open crossings in Scotland.

*S3M-5994 Angela Constance: Prostate Cancer Awareness Month—That the Parliament acknowledges March as prostate cancer awareness month; notes with great concern the rising number of men being diagnosed with prostate cancer as a result of an ageing population, and praises the efforts of organisations such as the West Lothian Group for Prostate Cancer Support in the Livingston constituency, which has made great strides in spreading awareness about prostate cancer as well as providing quality support to community members with prostate cancer and their loved ones.

*S3M-5992 Elaine Smith: Celebrating St Patrick’s Day in Coatbridge—That the Parliament congratulates the St Patrick’s Day Festival Committee in Coatbridge on its work in organising and promoting the annual festival, which is now the 8th biggest St Patrick’s Day celebration in the world; notes that Saturday 20 March 2010 will be the 8th annual event in Coatbridge and is expected to attract in excess of 15,000 people from Scotland, the rest of the United Kingdom and abroad; further notes that this year’s festival will be opened by the TV presenter, singer and former pupil of St Ambrose High School, Michelle McManus; recognises the Irish heritage of the Iron Burgh and the contribution made by Irish immigrants in the development of the area; acknowledges the hard work of the organisers of the festival, who have involved the whole community in fundraising all year round in order to have over a week of festivities to celebrate the Irish heritage of many of the Coatbridge populace, and believes that the Scottish Government should look at ways to assist groups of committed citizens such as this in undertaking events that significantly boost tourism and visitor numbers to Scotland’s smaller towns thus benefiting local economies and educating people in the industrial and cultural heritage of areas such as Coatbridge.

*S3M-5991 Angela Constance: Focus on Mental Health Issues—That the Parliament recognises the efforts of Ms Elizabeth Mackay and Ms Nicola Richardson in working to publish Focus magazine for the users of the charity, Penumbra, in the Livingston constituency, which supports West Lothian community members with mental health issues; commends the inclusive nature of Focus, which actively engages and involves community members and service users of Penumbra with informational newsletters, facts and events; strongly supports the efforts of Ms Richardson and Ms Mackay to spread awareness about mental health issues in the West Lothian community, and wishes Focus a long and successful run.

Supported by: Bob Doris*, Robin Harper*, Stuart McMillan*, Jackie Baillie*, Anne McLaughlin*, Nigel Don*, Sandra White*, Pauline McNeill*, Ken Macintosh*, Bill Kidd*, Stewart Maxwell*

*S3M-5990 Angela Constance: 10th Anniversary of the West Lothian Community Rehabilitation and Brain Injury Service—That the Parliament congratulates the West Lothian Community Rehabilitation and Brain Injury Service (CRABIS) on its recently celebrated 10th anniversary; acknowledges the array of multidisciplinary rehabilitation services that CRABIS has offered since its beginning in January 2000; applauds the organisation for aligning itself within the West Lothian Community Health and Care Partnership, and commends the CRABIS staff for their diligence in providing for those whose lives have been affected by brain injuries.

Supported by: Bob Doris*, Kenneth Gibson*, Robin Harper*, Stuart McMillan*, Jackie Baillie*, Jim Hume*, Hugh O’Donnell*, Sandra White*, Karen Whitefield*, Bill Kidd*, Mary Mulligan*

*S3M-5989 Elizabeth Smith: Morrison’s Academy, Crieff, 150th Anniversary Year—That the Parliament congratulates Morrison’s Academy, Crieff, on the 150th anniversary year of the school; notes that Morrison’s Academy was opened on 1 October 1860 following the death of Thomas Morrison, a native of the nearby village of Muthill and noted builder of a substantial part of Edinburgh’s Georgian New Town; notes the year-round programme of events for parents, former pupils and friends of the school to celebrate 150 years of education; considers that the school brings important cultural and economic benefit to the local Strathearn economy by supporting local businesses through the trade that is received from its staff and pupils; recognises the work and achievements of many Morrisonians; understands that the school encourages pupils to be socially aware and play their part in the local community by using their talents and skills to contribute to the colourful array of local life, whether it is the Pipe Band playing at the Crieff market, the Chamber Choir singing at BLESMA or pupils completing their Duke of Edinburgh Award who undertake their service in the local area, and wishes Morrison’s Academy staff and pupils every success in taking the school forward in the future, in the words of Thomas Morrison, to "promote the interests of mankind, having a particular regard to the Education of youth and the diffusion of knowledge".

S3M-5636# Ian McKee: Family Nurse Partnership Programme in Lothian—That the Parliament congratulates NHS Lothian on being selected by the Scottish Government as a test site, fully funded for three years, for a Family Nurse Partnership programme, an intensive preventive programme for vulnerable first-time young parents that has a 30-year evidence base showing the potential for improvements in women’s ante-natal health, reductions in children’s injuries, greater intervals between births, increases in fathers’ involvement, increases in employment and earnings, reductions in the need for benefits, improvements in school readiness, reduced arrests and criminal behaviour in 15-year-olds and mothers and that eventually produces savings five times the amount of an initial investment; realises that one measure alone is not the entire answer to challenges in this field but a welcome start, and wishes the project every success.

*S3M-5636.1# Dr Richard Simpson: Family Nurse Partnership Programme in Lothian—As an amendment to motion (S3M-5636) in the name of Ian McKee, after "Parliament" insert "notes the work done by the Prime Minister’s team in 10 Downing Street in analysing and proposing measures to improve parenting and".
