Business Bulletin No. 9/2002

Thursday 17 January 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-2617 Robin Harper: Japan and Commercial Whaling—That the Parliament calls on the Scottish Executive to request that Her Majesty’s Government convey to the Japanese Government in the strongest terms its opposition to a return to full-scale commercial whaling and to request that the Japanese Government answers the allegation, made in a report by Greenpeace, that it is using development aid to buy votes in favour of a return to full-scale commercial whaling from small nation states within the International Whaling Commission. R

*S1M-2616 Christine Grahame: South of Scotland Alliance—That the Parliament welcomes the launch of the South of Scotland Alliance, comprised of Dumfries and Galloway Council, Scottish Borders Council, Scottish Enterprise Dumfries and Galloway and Scottish Enterprise Borders; notes its manifesto message "Moving the South of Scotland Forward"; further notes its five key targets of raising the profile and image of the south of Scotland, examining and rectifying the causes of economic market failure, promoting inclusion and equal opportunities, working for increased funding for the area and building international links; recognises that this area in particular has suffered from the consequences of foot-and-mouth disease and has in the past been overlooked, and congratulates the South of Scotland Alliance on its endeavours to rectify these matters.

Supported by: Colin Campbell*

*S1M-2615# Cathy Peattie: International Women's Memorial Day - 28 April 2002—That the Parliament notes that 28 April 2002 is International Workers’ Memorial Day, a commemoration of those who have died or suffered accidents or ill health as a result of work, and calls for this day to be widely observed in Scotland through action to improve safety and provide a healthier working environment.

*S1M-2614 Trish Godman: Oxfam Scotland—That the Parliament commends the campaign by Oxfam Scotland to encourage people to give foreign currency notes and coins made worthless by the euro to any of Oxfam Scotland’s 68 shops; believes that Oxfam Scotland will put such currency to very good use, once exchanged in the developing world, and urges all those with soon-to-be useless coins and small denomination notes, of any of the twelve EU member states which have signed up to the euro, left over from holidays and visits to respond to this excellent initiative and by doing so helping poor people to live more tolerable lives.

Supported by: Colin Campbell*

*S1M-2613 Mr David Davidson: Archbishop Mario Conti of Glasgow—That the Parliament congratulates Bishop Mario Conti of Aberdeen on his appointment as the Archbishop of Glasgow; recognises his contribution to inter-faith co-operation in the community in Northern Scotland for over the last two decades, and wishes him success in his new role.

Supported by: Mr Keith Harding*, Colin Campbell*
