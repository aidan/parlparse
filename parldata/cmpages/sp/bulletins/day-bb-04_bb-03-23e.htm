Business Bulletin No. 53 / 2004

Tuesday 23rd March 2004

Section E : Written questions lodged on Monday 22nd March 2004
  

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-7104 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to address the training needs of school science technicians.

S2W-7105 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to enhance the role of school science technicians in the primary and secondary sectors.

S2W-7106 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what the implications for school science technicians are of implementation of the report by the Scottish Science Advisory Committee, Science Matters: making the right connections for Scotland.

S2W-7107 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to review the role of school science technicians and their pay and conditions within a national framework.

S2W-7108 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there will be public consultation regarding a voluntary coexistence scheme for farmers growing GM and non-GM crops.

S2W-7109 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding will be made available for a voluntary coexistence scheme for farmers growing GM and non-GM crops and from where such funding will be allocated.

S2W-7110 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive how a voluntary coexistence scheme for farmers growing GM and non-GM crops will work at the Scottish-English border.

S2W-7111 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive who would regulate a voluntary coexistence scheme for farmers growing GM and non-GM crops.

S2W-7112 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive how a voluntary coexistence scheme for farmers growing GM and non-GM crops would be administered.

S2W-7113 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive who would monitor a voluntary coexistence scheme for farmers growing GM and non-GM crops in respect of (a) gene flow, (b) wildlife contamination and (c) soil contamination.

S2W-7114 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive what compensation scheme will be established for farmers affected by any voluntary coexistence scheme for farmers growing GM and non-GM crops.

S2W-7115 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive who will pay compensation due under any compensation scheme for farmers affected by a voluntary coexistence scheme for farmers growing GM and non-GM crops.

S2W-7116 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what the projected costs are of out-of-hours medical cover and what any projected shortfalls in funding of the service are that each NHS board will be required to meet.

S2W-7117 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what the current financial position is of each NHS trust and board in respect of (a) primary and (b) secondary health care.

S2W-7118 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in, and what the cost has been of, implementing the (a) new consultant contracts and (b) Agenda for Change, broken down by NHS board.

S2W-7119 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what action has been taken, or what plans there are, to utilise the presence of a consular corps and other overseas representatives based in Scotland with a view to promoting Scotland and building on existing mutually-beneficial links with their respective countries; when the most recent survey was conducted to seek their views; what the results of any such survey was, and what action was taken as the result of the survey.

S2W-7120 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what (a) submission it made and (b) its response is to the Prime Minister's strategy unit's inquiry into the UK fishing industry.

S2W-7121 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list the members of the consular corps and overseas representatives that it recognises for the purposes of building overseas links.

S2W-7122 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the statement issued by its spokesperson and reported in the Sunday Herald on 21 March 2004 that "The First Minister and the permanent secretary are fully committed to freedom of information. Openness and transparency have been hallmarks of the organisation since devolution. The new act will ensure that this continues to be the case" and whether this statement means that information and material currently deemed confidential and private as part of the Concordat on Co-ordination of European Union Policy Issues will now be made available.

S2W-7123 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration has been given to providing assistance to NHS boards, following the introduction of the general medical services contract, to relocate GP premises to the areas that they serve as a means of addressing health inequalities.

S2W-7124 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider health pathfinder areas to address health inequalities caused by any inappropriate location of GPs and health services.

S2W-7125 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it seeks from NHS boards to assess the impact of the location of GPs on the health needs of the population.

S2W-7126 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what priority it attaches to the provision of services in rural areas.

S2W-7127 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether parents in rural areas should have the same right to choose schools as parents in urban areas.

S2W-7128 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive how many times it has intervened to prevent the closure of rural schools since 1999.

S2W-7129 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether pupils in rural schools suffer educational disadvantage compared to pupils in urban schools.

S2W-7130 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what policies it has to encourage the setting up of rural businesses.

S2W-7131 Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will publish the review of the livestock improvement scheme and what plans it has for the continuation and improvement of the scheme.

S2W-7132 John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive how funding gained from the sale of Crofters Commission land at Beechwood Farm, Inverness, has been allocated.

S2W-7133 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is encouraging major events in the international brass band calendar to come to Scotland.

S2W-7134 John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive whether free-range pigs have a livestock unit value and, if so, whether such pigs are considered livestock units in the determination of stocking densities in conjunction with other livestock.

S2W-7135 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding, and other support, it has provided to the Scottish Civic Forum in each year of its operation and what funding, and other support, it will provide to the forum in 2004-05.

S2W-7136 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what proportion of pupils attending rural schools go on to higher education compared to the proportion of those attending urban schools.

S2W-7137 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there is any research evidence that urban schools perform better than rural schools.

S2W-7138 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there is any research evidence that pupils from rural schools perform less well at secondary school level than their urban counterparts.

S2W-7139 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there is any research evidence to suggest that the fabric of school buildings alone accounts for differences in educational attainment.

S2W-7140 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what advice is given to local education authorities on minimising travel distances to schools.

S2W-7141 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether pupils attending rural schools have the same entitlement to information technology facilities as those pupils attending urban schools.

S2W-7142 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there is any evidence that pupils from rural schools are less fit than their urban counterparts.

S2W-7143 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what importance it places on parents being able to send their children to the school of their choice.

S2W-7144 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what advice it gives to local authorities on the importance of maintaining sustainable rural communities.

S2W-7145 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what it considers to be good practice by local authorities in consulting local communities on the closure of rural schools.

S2W-7146 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive how its policy on sustainable rural communities is implemented by departments other than its Environment and Rural Affairs Department.

S2W-7147 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive how it defines "rural" when categorising rural and urban schools.

S2W-7148 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken to encourage local authorities to make use of rural schools for the benefit of the local community.

S2W-7149 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what value it places on educational attainment in rural schools.
