Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (u).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-3718 Carolyn Leckie: Councils in Crisis—That the Parliament is concerned that COSLA and individual local authorities have declared that the local government finance settlement is insufficient to secure public services, jobs, terms and conditions or to achieve long-overdue settlement of equal pay; notes that Glasgow City Council has already announced cuts in services to vulnerable members of the community and job losses, whilst offering inadequate compensation for years of pay discrimination against women; further notes that Falkirk Council has indicated that it will be facing a shortfall of at least £2 million in the next financial year and has warned about cuts to services, such as emergency home care, even before any payments are made in relation to equal pay; believes that councils up and down the country may well follow suit, leading to disruption as trade unionists rightly mobilise to defend their members and services; believes that in no way should underpaid women be forced to choose between equal pay and the jobs and terms and conditions of their colleagues; affirms the responsibility of government and employers to implement equal pay by making adequate resources available, and believes that the Scottish Executive should convene a meeting, or meetings, between COSLA, individual local authorities and the trade unions to achieve a solution to this brewing crisis with the intention of delivering on equal pay, protecting public services and securing the jobs and terms and conditions of all local authority employees.

*S2M-3717 Lewis Macdonald: The Food Protection (Emergency Prohibitions) (Amnesic Shellfish Poisoning) (West Coast) (No. 18) (Scotland) Order 2005—That the Health Committee recommends that the Food Protection (Emergency Prohibitions) (Amnesic Shellfish Poisoning) (West Coast) (No. 18) (Scotland) Order 2005 (SSI 2005/626) be approved.

Supported by: Mr Andy Kerr*

*S2M-3716 Brian Adam: Broadcasting Companies’ Income Generation from Telephone Polling, Competitions and Quizzes—That the Parliament calls on the Scottish Executive to urge Her Majesty’s Government to undertake an inquiry into the amounts of money being generated by broadcasting companies from telephone polling, competitions and quizzes.

*S2M-3715# Mike Rumbles: Centre for Ecology and Hydrology, Hill of Brathens, Banchory—That the Parliament notes with concern the proposal by the Natural Environment Research Council to close the Centre for Ecology and Hydrology (CEH) at Hill of Brathens, Banchory; notes that the research carried out at the facility is consistently of the highest quality; considers that, at a time when climate change is of huge concern in Scotland, research operations such as CEH should be expanded rather than rationalised, and further considers that the Scottish Executive should make representations to the Natural Environment Research Council opposing the proposal.

Supported by: Mr Jamie Stone*, Brian Adam*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*

*S2M-3714 Tommy Sheridan: Strike Action by Irish Trade Unions Against Irish Ferries—That the Parliament notes that over 100,000 workers marched in Ireland on Friday 9 December 2005, with 60,000 in Dublin alone, to protest against attempts by the Irish Ferries company to re-flag their ships to Cyprus in an attempt to slash the conditions of the ferry workers and undermine the basic minimum wage; congratulates the Irish Congress of Trade Unions for organising the protest which resulted in the widest industrial action seen in Ireland for 20 years; believes that this action exposes the real agenda of the "Celtic Tiger" economy and proves that working class solidarity can take on big business and at the same time cut across racist sentiments against immigrant workers, and gives its full support to workers on Scottish ferries who may face similar attacks on their wages and conditions in future.

*S2M-3648.1 Mike Pringle: Happy Birthday Sweet 16?—As an amendment to motion (S2M-3648) in the name of Carolyn Leckie, leave out from "charitable work" to end and insert "work providing grants to families with disabled or seriously ill children up to the age of 16 for items that are not the responsibility of a statutory agency; agrees that the stresses and strains placed on families caring for a disabled or ill child do not disappear on the young person’s 16th birthday and that the loss of this grant has a severe impact on the quality of life for these children and their families, and calls for the funding of the Family Fund to be increased in proportion to needs in Scotland to allow the organisation to extend its remit to help families with children over 16, recognising that the state should be providing far more support and resources for these vulnerable families."
