Business Bulletin No. 154/2006: Thursday 2 November 2006
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 1 November 2006

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-29410 Murray Tosh: To ask the Scottish Executive, following the announcement by the Minister for Transport, during the debate on the Elgin Bypass on 21 September 2006, that a liaison officer with specific responsibility for Elgin and Moray has been appointed by Transport Scotland (Official Report, c. 27876), whether it will publish the names and contact details of all trunk road liaison officers in Scotland.

S2W-29411 Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether its International Development Fund is currently closed and, if so, when it will re-open.

S2W-29412 Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many cases have been referred to the appeal court on the grounds of an alleged misdirection of a jury by a judge when summing-up in each of the last five years.

S2W-29413 John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidelines it issues to local authorities on publicising the availability of council tax discounts.

S2W-29414 John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of people eligible for the single person’s council tax discount currently receive it, broken down by local authority.

S2W-29415 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive what the total cost is of the biometric scanner system recently installed in Todholm Primary School in Paisley.

S2W-29416 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive what the total cost would be of supplying a biometric scanner system in every school.

S2W-29417 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive who is paying for the biometric scanner system recently installed in Todholm Primary School in Paisley.

S2W-29418 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive how the cost of implementing any proposal to supply a biometric scanner system in every school would be met and from which budget this funding would come.

S2W-29419 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive who is supplying the biometric scanner system recently installed in Todholm Primary School in Paisley.

S2W-29420 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive how the contract for supply of a biometric scanner system in Todholm Primary School in Paisley was awarded.

S2W-29421 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive who stores the personal data from the biometric scanner system recently installed in Todholm Primary School in Paisley.

S2W-29422 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive what safeguards are in place in respect of the storage of personal data from the biometric scanner system recently installed in Todholm Primary School in Paisley.

S2W-29423 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive whether parents and pupils have the right to opt out of using the biometric scanner system recently installed in Todholm Primary School in Paisley.

S2W-29424 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive whether parents and pupils would have the right to opt out of using biometric scanner systems installed in any schools.

S2W-29425 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-28879 by Lewis Macdonald on 1 November 2006, whether it will publish a list of the professions involved in preparation of the risk management plan in relation to the care of restricted patient, Mark Biggley.

S2W-29426 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-28880 by Lewis Macdonald on 1 November 2006, why Strathclyde Police was not involved in determining the risk in granting restricted patient, Mark Biggley, unsupervised leave within the grounds of Ailsa Hospital, given that he had previously absconded.

S2W-29427 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive, when determining unescorted leave by restricted patients, how risk to the public is measured by multi-disciplinary teams that do not include the police.

S2W-29428 Ms Maureen Watt: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to create additional junior posts for physiotherapists in the NHS.

S2W-29429 Ms Maureen Watt: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to alleviate unemployment among physiotherapy graduates.

S2W-29430 Ms Maureen Watt: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will give a commitment not to reduce places for physiotherapy students in response to unemployment levels among physiotherapy graduates.

S2W-29431 Ms Maureen Watt: To ask the Scottish Executive how many physiotherapists working in the NHS are aged (a) 21 to 30, (b) 31 to 40, (c) 41 to 50, (d) 51 to 60 and (e) over 60, also broken down by NHS board and expressed as a percentage of all NHS physiotherapists.

S2W-29432 Ms Maureen Watt: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that there are age and experience imbalances within NHS physiotherapy services and, if so, how it intends to address such imbalances.

S2W-29433 Ms Maureen Watt: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that there are gaps within the NHS physiotherapy workforce and, if so, how it intends to address such gaps.

S2W-29434 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many children aged between eight and 15 years were admitted to hospital because of alcohol-related problems in each year since 1997, broken down by NHS hospital.

S2W-29435 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of people in young offender institutions have special educational needs.

S2W-29436 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has about how many people have been in receipt of (a) jobseekers allowance, (b) incapacity benefit and (c) disability living allowance in each year since 1997, broken down by parliamentary constituency.

S2W-29438 Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive when its response to the consultation on Review of Access Criteria for Infertility Services in Scotland is expected to be published.

S2W-29439 Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive whether draft road orders with specific reference to the finalised, rather than indicative, route will be published in respect of the Aberdeen Western Peripheral Route before full public consultation has been carried out.

S2W-29440 Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Aberdeen Western Peripheral Route will cross the Old Deeside Railway line and, if so, what action will be taken to ensure that the line will remain capable of supporting the future railway service to and from a re-opened Banchory station.

S2W-29441 Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in relation to the proposed re-opening of Laurencekirk station and when it anticipates that construction work will begin.
