Business Bulletin No. 55/2002

Tuesday 2 April 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-2961 Dennis Canavan: Scotland United—That the Parliament notes the tenth anniversary of the founding of Scotland United which falls on 11 April 2002; recalls the despair in Scotland at a fourth Tory election victory in England which deepened the democratic deficit; commends those who were prepared to work across parties to demand an opportunity for Scotland to choose its own constitutional future; recognises that such work injected a new urgency into mobilising a mass campaign for change which assisted in the process of establishing this Parliament, and expresses the hope that the principle of co-operation between politicians and the citizens of Scotland will assist in the process of developing this Parliament and creating an effective institution capable of winning Scotland’s respect.

Supported by: Michael Russell*, Mr Frank McAveety*

*S1M-2959 Murdo Fraser: "Other Side of Scotland" Tourism Campaign—That the Parliament welcomes the "Other Side of Scotland" campaign developed by the area tourist boards in Aberdeen and Grampian, Angus and Dundee and the Kingdom of Fife; recognises the importance of the communities along Scotland’s spectacular east coast and the need to encourage more tourists to visit this area, and commends the partnership approach taken by the area tourist boards involved in the campaign in an effort to achieve this.

*S1M-2958 Murdo Fraser: Fire Safety at the Victoria Hospital, Kirkcaldy—That the Parliament, in light of the recent decision by the Fife NHS Board to centralise acute services in the Victoria Hospital, Kirkcaldy, and fears expressed by members of the public in relation to fire safety at the hospital, calls upon the Scottish Executive to instigate an investigation by the Health and Safety Authority and the Fife Fire Service of the fire safety precautions and other risks at the Victoria Hospital to ensure that, in the event of any outbreak of fire, there is no risk to patients, visitors and staff, and further calls for any report from such an investigation be made public through the Parliament.

Supported by: Alex Johnstone*, Mr David Davidson*, Ben Wallace*, Mr Keith Harding*

*S1M-2957 Gordon Jackson: Glasgow City Council and Renfrewshire Council Boundaries (Braehead) Amendment Order 2002—That the Local Government Committee recommends that nothing further be done under the Glasgow City Council and Renfrewshire Council Boundaries (Braehead) Amendment Order 2002 (SSI 2002/156).

*S1M-2956# Dennis Canavan: Dystonia—That the Parliament congratulates the Dystonia Society for its efforts in raising awareness of this little-known but severely debilitating neurological disorder which can cause involuntary sustained and painful muscle spasms in various parts of the body; is concerned that, according to the Dystonia Society, there are at least 38,000 sufferers in the UK who sometimes have to wait years for a correct diagnosis due to a lack of awareness on the part of General Practitioners (GPs); is further concerned about the lack of appropriate genetic testing and neurosurgical treatment options in Scotland; calls on the Scottish Executive to ensure that adequate information is given to GPs so that a correct and timeous diagnosis can be made and to provide resources for research into the causes of Dystonia, and calls for the provision of specialist nurses, physiotherapists, counsellors and a regional co-ordinator for Scotland.

Supported by: Mr Kenneth Gibson*, Shona Robison*, Colin Campbell*

*S1M-2955 Tommy Sheridan: Deportation of Eduardo Sanchez—That the Parliament notes with grave concern that Julio Eduardo Hoyos Sanchez, a 36-year-old Peruvian freelance artist, is being held in Dungavel detention centre after having been detained on the morning of Monday 25 March 2002 and that an order for his deportation was granted on 19 March; notes that Eduardo Sanchez has lived in Scotland for 11 years and has a 9-year-old son, Yan Shearer, who lives with his mother, Carol Shearer (Eduardo’s former partner) in Orkney; further notes that Yan is seeking action on behalf of his own human rights to stop his father being deported, that the entire Shearer family is opposed to the deportation of Eduardo and that he himself has lodged an appeal against this deportation order, and urges the Deputy First Minister and Minister for Justice to make immediate representations to the Home Office on behalf of Eduardo with a view to it revoking the deportation order and allowing him to remain in Scotland with his son.

S1M-2953# Alex Fergusson: Timber Industry—That the Parliament acknowledges the value of timber as a primary product within rural Scotland and the 11,000 jobs which are dependent on that industry; recognises the very real difficulties faced by some rural local authorities in maintaining and improving minor roads for timber extraction, and believes that the Scottish Executive should meet COSLA’s Rural Affairs Committee and representatives of the forestry industry to determine a satisfactory way forward, recognising that the placing of weight restriction orders, which effectively landlock large areas of mature timber, is not a realistic solution. Supported by: David Mundell, John Scott, Mr Jamie McGrigor, Mr David Davidson, Mary Scanlon*

S1M-2952 Mr Jim Wallace: Criminal Justice (Scotland) Bill—That the Parliament agrees to the general principles of the Criminal Justice (Scotland) Bill.

Supported by: Dr Richard Simpson

S1M-2951 Mr Brian Monteith: Eigg's Ferry Terminal and Pier—That the Parliament notes with concern that the cost of the ferry terminal and pier at Kildonnan in Eigg, approved by the First Minister, will be £260,000 of public money for each of the 30 residents’ cars on Eigg; further notes that only the cars of Eigg residents may use the car ferry due to the traffic order that prevents non-residents taking cars to Eigg, and believes that mainland taxpayers, who have contributed so generously to the ferry terminal and pier, should be granted the use of it and that the traffic order which prevents them driving their own vehicles in Eigg should be withdrawn.

Supported by: Mr Jamie McGrigor*, Murdo Fraser*, Alex Johnstone*, Mr Keith Harding*

S1M-2950# Fiona McLeod: UN Children's Summit 8 - 10 May 2002—That the Parliament welcomes the United Nation’s Special Session on Children 2002 which will take place in New York on 8 - 10 May 2002; commends the recognition accorded by the summit to the need for adults to listen to the views of young people; considers that the Scottish Executive should regularly monitor and report on the implementation of commitments by Her Majesty’s Government that arise from the Special Session and relate to Scotland’s children; congratulates Save the Children for organising its "world fit for children" event in Edinburgh to coincide with this summit, and believes that a "Children and Young People’s Summit" should be held annually by the Parliament.

Supported by: Michael Russell*

S1M-2944# Karen Gillon: Register of Pensioners' Groups—That the Parliament commends the work undertaken by pensioners to create local elderly forums as a means of increasing their democratic participation; recognises that this is inhibited by the inadequate and sparse detail currently held on pensioners’ groups in Scotland, and encourages the Scottish Executive to give further consideration to the establishment of a standard local authority register of pensioner groups in order to facilitate a crucial basis of communication with such groups and promote empowerment through easy access to information, which will allow pensioners’ issues to be pursued on a much broader basis.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan*, Trish Godman*

S1M-2943 Pauline McNeill: Welcome to Professor Karen Vousden at the Beatson Institute—That the Parliament welcomes the announcement by Cancer Research UK on 15 March 2002 of a £10 million grant for the Beatson Institute in Glasgow; notes that this support will enable the west of Scotland cancer centre to become a world centre for cancer research; further notes that this commitment to the Beatson Institute is particularly welcome as a clear indication of confidence in the institute, and calls on MSPs of all parties to welcome Professor Karen Vousden as the new director of the Beatson Institute and wish her every success in advancing gene therapy to the benefit of cancer patients throughout Scotland and the world.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan*, Trish Godman*

S1M-2942 Pauline McNeill: World's Biggest Burns Supper—That the Parliament congratulates the Lord Provost of Glasgow for successfully hosting the world’s biggest Burns Supper on Friday 25 January 2002 and raising £34,500 to be donated to the Royal Hospital for Sick Children at Yorkhill, Marie Curie Cancer Care and The Prince and Princess of Wales Hospice.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan*, Trish Godman*

S1M-2939 John Farquhar Munro: Inverness and the Highlands' Bid to be the European Capital of Culture 2008—That the Parliament wholeheartedly endorses Inverness and the Highlands’ bid to become the European Capital of Culture 2008 as Scotland’s only contender for the event; recognises the significant economic benefits that a successful bid could bring to the city, the rest of the Highlands and Scotland as a whole, including its potential to create a major increase in tourist spending and new temporary and permanent jobs; notes Inverness’s recently awarded city status; urges organisations and individuals throughout the Highlands to submit ideas and suggestions to Invernesshighland2008 to support and strengthen the bid; welcomes the £100,000 contributions each from the Scottish Executive, Highlands and Island Enterprise and Highland Council to the campaign, and urges the Scottish Executive to continue its active support for the bid.

Supported by: Brian Fitzpatrick, Fergus Ewing, Rhoda Grant, Dr Winnie Ewing*, Mr Mike Rumbles*, Tavish Scott*, Robert Brown*, Donald Gorrie*, Mr Keith Raffan*, Iain Smith*, Nora Radcliffe*, Mr Brian Monteith*

S1M-2938 Jackie Baillie: Naval Defence Review and Faslane and Coulport Naval Dockyards—That the Parliament notes with regret the announcement by Geoff Hoon MP, Secretary of State for Defence, of the outcome of the naval defence review and his failure to give adequate consideration to representations from trade unions and elected parliamentarians; recognises that the trade unions, whose agreement to changes in conditions at the Faslane and Coulport naval dockyards in previous years has been hailed as a model in industrial relations, proposed further in-house efficiencies and proposed "through life" maintenance plans which would team the repair yards with the companies that build Royal Navy ships in long term maintenance deals but would appear now to have been rejected; further notes that in addition to the privatisation of cleaning, catering and maintenance staff, there will be the possibility of redundancies across the UK with a significant proportion of these anticipated at the Faslane and Coulport dockyards; further recognises that this level of job loss will have a critical effect on the local economy as well as a significant negative impact on the individual employees and their families, the majority of which reside in the Dumbarton parliamentary constituency where employment at the dockyards accounts for approximately one quarter of the full-time employment in the area; is concerned that the area already has one of the highest levels of unemployment in Scotland and that the scale of the job loss will be devastating, and therefore calls on the Scottish Executive to take action to mitigate these job losses by providing significant additional financial support for the re-training and re-skilling of those affected, to work closely with the Ministry of Defence to maximise redeployment and to establish a job creation programme for the area and to consider extending the Clyde Corridor initiative to include all areas from Bowling, through Dumbarton and up to Garelochhead in order to assist in the economic regeneration of the area.

Supported by: Pauline McNeill, Mr John McAllion, Elaine Smith, Des McNulty, Margaret Jamieson, Mr Frank McAveety, Brian Fitzpatrick, Bill Butler, Mr Michael McMahon, Karen Whitefield, Susan Deacon, Mr Kenneth Macintosh, Paul Martin

S1M-2936 Kay Ullrich: Dungavel Detention Centre—That the Parliament notes with concern reports on the conditions in which asylum seekers are being held at the Dungavel Detention Centre, including references made to a lack of privacy and freedom of movement, effectively creating a prison environment for the people, including young children, who are guilty of no offence; further notes with concern the reported suicide attempt at Dungavel of the Nigerian asylum seeker, Mr Dotun Adeosun, and calls on the Scottish Executive to liase with the Home Office and to encourage Her Majesty’s Government to accede to requests by human rights groups, such as the Friends of Refugees in Ayrshire, to have wider access to visit and meet asylum seekers at the centre and to provide them with support and to inspect the conditions in which detainees there are held.

Supported by: Michael Matheson, Linda Fabiani, Michael Russell, Ms Sandra White, Tommy Sheridan*, Dr Winnie Ewing*, Dennis Canavan*, Shona Robison*

S1M-2935 Pauline McNeill: Colourfest 2002—That the Parliament welcomes the announcement that Scotland’s biggest dance music festival, Colourfest 2002, is to be held in Glasgow on 2 June 2002 with Radio One, Clyde 1 and world class DJs and performers from around the UK and Europe bringing 10,000 young people to the city venue.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan*
