*S1M-3474 Fiona McLeod: European Health and Safety at Work Week, 14-20 October 2002—That the Parliament welcomes the focus of this year’s European Health and Safety at Work Week on the prevention of psychosocial risks and the stress that they cause, given that nearly one in three of Europe’s workers report that they are affected by stress at work and that one in five workers in Scotland, an estimated half a million people, are exposed to work-related stress.

Supported by: Robin Harper*

*S1M-3473# Fiona McLeod: Report by the United Nations Committee on the Rights of the Child—That the Parliament notes the concluding observations by the UN Committee on the Rights of the Child on the UK’s implementation of the UN Convention on the Rights of the Child published on 4 October 2002 and considers that the Scottish Executive should bring forward measures to address the criticism within its devolved areas of competence and use its influence with Her Majesty’s Government in support of measures to alleviate distress to children in Scotland arising from reserved matters.

Supported by: Robin Harper*

*S1M-3472 Dorothy-Grace Elder: Use of the Holyrood Building as a Full Public Facility—That the Parliament believes that the new Parliament building should be a full public as well as parliamentary facility, giving priority to events held by diverse community groups, especially those unable to afford conference premises; considers that, as the building will have been custom-built for disabled access, it should be the premier meeting place in Scotland for those with disabilities as well as for community groups, including schools and theatre and performing arts clubs; further believes that it would be unacceptable for the building to be used only as the Parliament, especially in the light of cost rises, and that it should provide public facilities on every non-meeting day, including recesses, weekends and at night, and further considers that the cost of doing so would be minimal, given that security staff are present 365 days a year.

*S1M-3471 Malcolm Chisholm: The Food Protection (Emergency Prohibitions) (Amnesic Shellfish Poisoning) (West Coast) (No.12) (Scotland) Order 2002—That the Health and Community Care Committee, in consideration of the Food Protection (Emergency Prohibitions) (Amnesic Shellfish Poisoning) (West Coast) (No.12) (Scotland) Order, recommends that the Order be approved.

Supported by: Mr Frank McAveety*, Mrs Mary Mulligan*

*S1M-3470# Alex Neil: Census 2001: Depopulation—That the Parliament notes the latest Census 2001 figures which show that Scotland’s population continues to fall; recognises that, unless depopulation is addressed now, there will be severe economic consequences, and believes that a Generation Taskforce should be established to assess what can be done now to solve this problem.

*S1M-3469.1 Mr Kenny MacAskill: Future of Air Transport in Scotland—As an amendment to motion (S1M-3469) in the name of Iain Gray, leave out from "consultation" to end and insert "urgent need, in relation to tourism and the economy in Scotland, to increase the limited number of direct international connections and to expand the number of routes operated by low-cost carriers; further notes that Inverness Airport is operated by Highlands and Islands Airport Limited, which is wholly owned by Scottish Ministers; is concerned at studies indicating that Inverness Airport has the highest landing charges in Europe, and therefore calls on the Scottish Executive to set up a route development fund to promote direct international connections from Scotland and to take immediate action to reduce the landing charges imposed at Inverness Airport."
