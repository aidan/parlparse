Business Bulletin No. 61/2002

Thursday 18 April 2002

Section D : Oral Questions
  

Question Time

1. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what response it will make to the Church of Scotland's Church and Nation Committee's report on sectarianism. (S1O-5048)

2. Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the public sector share of the Scottish economy is and whether it wishes this share to change. (S1O-5051)

3. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to update Planning Policy Guidance Note 22 on renewable energy to include guidance on photovoltaics. (S1O-5045)

4. Miss Annabel Goldie: To ask the Scottish Executive why participants in the New Deal for Young People programme in Scotland are more likely to experience two or three periods of unemployment than their counterparts in England and Wales. (S1O-5040)

5. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has identified any benefits from the provision of a grant funded nursery place for all three- and four-year-olds whose parents want one. (S1O-5069)

6. Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to prevent unused prescription drugs from being unnecessarily destroyed. (S1O-5050)

7. Mr Keith Harding: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it proposes to review local authority powers for tackling poor quality private housing. (S1O-5053)

8. Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met representatives of the Scottish School Board Association and what issues were discussed. (S1O-5052)

9. Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will announce the findings of the Mouchet Study on the A75 trunk road. (S1O-5064)

10. Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria govern its use of advertising. (S1O-5044)

11. David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive which first round funding bids to improve school buildings have been successful. (S1O-5042)

12. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many NHS beds are currently blocked. (S1O-5066)

13. Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in improving access to further education. (S1O-5076)

14. Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made on proposals for a railway stop close to Edinburgh Airport. (S1O-5047)

15. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to address pressures currently facing the social care system. (S1O-5073)

16. Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact the removal of acute services from Stracathro Hospital has had on the ambulance service provision for patients in Angus. (S1O-5067)

17. Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria it uses in assessing whether or not to agree to a local authority request to close a primary school when the decision has been referred to it because of the distance to the nearest available alternative school. (S1O-5078)

18. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to encourage exercise among older people. (S1O-5043)

19. John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Scottish air transport consultation will be published. (S1O-5070)

20. Mr Keith Raffan: To ask the Scottish Executive what services are currently provided to help and support the children of parents undergoing treatment for substance abuse. (S1O-5071)

21. Bill Aitken: To ask the Scottish Executive what its current plans are on the establishment of neighbourhood warden schemes. (S1O-5055)

22. Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the introduction of school classroom assistants has led to any identifiable improvement in the quality of children's education. (S1O-5074)

23. Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive how many bed days were taken up by the treatment of hospital-acquired infections in 2001-02. (S1O-5049)

24. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been regarding time out centres and other alternatives to custody for women offenders. (S1O-5080)

25. Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any discussions have been held recently with senior executives at Compaq concerning the retention of production and employment at its plant in Erskine. (S1O-5041)

26. Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive how the proposed boundary revision at Braehead will impact on job opportunities in the area. (S1O-5056)

27. Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive what additional costs Grampian region will incur as a result of the recent UK budget. (S1O-5077)

28. Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to improve the efficiency of administration within the court system. (S1O-5068)

29. Janis Hughes: To ask the Scottish Executive how it aims to improve literacy among adults. (S1O-5063)

30. Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive what tangible benefits Scotland will gain as a result of this year's Tartan Week in the United States of America. (S1O-5072)
