Business Bulletin No. 88/2009: Wednesday 27 May 2009

Section E – Written questions lodged on 26 May 2009

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-24227 Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has sent emails or letters to the american authorities pressing for the restoration of a visa-issuing service in Edinburgh and, if so, on what dates.

S3W-24235 Jam ie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason there is only one special area of conservation (SAC) designated salmon river, the Little Gruinard, from more than 50 rivers between Cape Wrath and the Mull of Kintyre on the Scottish west coast mainland, when there are three SAC rivers on the north coast and nine SAC rivers on the east coast.

S3W-24236 Jam ie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to restore the two salmon and sea trout fisheries in Loch Maree and Loch Stack.

S3W-24237 Jam ie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Fisheries Research Services’ report on restoration of west coast rivers will be published and what the Scottish Government expects the main recommendations of the report to be.

S3W-24238 Jam ie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will confirm that the decision to designate 12 rivers as candidate special areas of conservation on the north and east coasts and only one on the west coast is not connected to any effects of sea lice, disease and genetic dam age to wild fish stocks that may have been caused by the salmon farming industry.

S3W-24239 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is considering introducing regulations on the welfare of greyhounds.

S3W-24240 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-23457 by Roseanna Cunningham on 19 May 2009, whether it considers that breeders of hawks in Scotland will be at a disadvantage compared with breeders in England, who are required to pay only for Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species permits.

S3W-24241 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what regulations apply to falconers from outwith Scotland when taking part in events in Scotland.

S3W-24242 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether a mobility scooter is regarded in law as a mobility aid or a vehicle.

S3W-24243 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the right of pedestrian access to a property includes right of access for a wheelchair user.

S3W-24244 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the right of pedestrian access to a property includes right of access for the user of a mobility scooter.

S3W-24245 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how long the single track section is of the trunk road between Bettyhill and Thurso.

S3W-24246 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason Glasgow has been selected as an appropriate area to pilot new arrangements for public notices.

S3W-24247 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what the duration will be of the pilot scheme for testing new arrangements for public notices.

S3W-24248 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive how the pilot scheme for testing new arrangements for public notices will be assessed.

S3W-24249 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive when fees for legal representatives at Children’s Hearings were last (a) increased and (b) reviewed.

S3W-24250 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to increase the fees for legal representatives at Children’s Hearings.

S3W-24251 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what changes are under consideration for the provision of legal representation in the forthcoming Children’s Hearings (Scotland) Bill.

S3W-24252 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the basis is of the calculation underpinning the Cabinet Secretary for Finance and Sustainable Growth’s comments to the Finance Committee on 19 May 2009 that the total managed expenditure is likely to fall by about 0.1% in real terms from 2011-12 to 2013-14 (Official Report c. 1304).

S3W-24253 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the basis is of the calculation underpinning the Cabinet Secretary for Finance and Sustainable Growth’s comments to the Finance Committee on 19 May 2009 that net debt interest will rise by 8.4%, social security and tax credits by 1.7% and other annual managed expenditure by 1.9% during the period 2011-12 to 2013-14 (Official Report c. 1304).

S3W-24254 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it accepts that the "new definition of a baseline budget" that the Cabinet Secretary for Finance and Sustainable Growth referred to in evidence to the Finance Committee on 19 May 2009 (Official Report c. 1308), refers to the term "baseline" as set out in the UK Treasury’s Funding the Scottish Parliament, National Assembly for Wales and Northern Ireland Assembly: Statement of Funding Policy, which states at page 43 that spending changes are added to or subtracted from each country’s overall baseline.

S3W-24255 Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive on what grounds the decision was taken to discontinue the collection of data for the Scottish School Leavers Survey.

S3W-24256 Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether Glasgow Housing Association (Management) will retain factoring responsibility for domestic properties, previously owned by Glasgow Housing Association, following second stage transfer.

S3W-24272 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many children have been treated for (a) tobacco addiction and (b) tobacco-related illnesses in each of the last five years in each hospital in the Lothians region.

S3W-24273 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many patients confirmed as having (a) Clostridium difficile and (b) MRSA in the Lothians region were transferred from another hospital in the last 12 months.
