Business Bulletin No. 186/2010: Tuesday 14 December 2010

Section A – Today’s Business

Meetings of Committees

All meetings take place in the Scottish Parliament, unless otherwise specified. Contact details for Committee Clerks are provided at the end of the Bulletin.

Equal Opportunities Committee
24th Meeting, 2010

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in Committee Room 1

1. Forced Marriage etc. (Protection and Jurisdiction) (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will take evidence on the Bill at Stage 1 from—

Suzelle Dickson, Joint Head, Forced Marriage Unit, UK Ministry of Justice;

and then from—

Girijamba Polubothu, Manager, Shakti Women’s Aid;

Smina Aktar, Director, AMINA Muslim Women’s Resource Centre;

Rajani Pandher, Chief Support Worker, Hemat Gryffe Women’s Aid;

Laura McCrum, Development Officer, Saheliya;

and then from—

Alex Neil MSP, Minister for Housing and Communities, Lesley Irving, Team Leader, Gender Equality and Violence Against Women, and Eileen Flanagan, Policy Manager, Gender Equality and Violence Against Women, Scottish Government.

Justice Committee
36th Meeting, 2010

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in Committee Room 6

1. Double Jeopardy (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will take evidence on the Bill at Stage 1 from—

The Right Hon Lord Gill, Lord Justice Clerk.

2. Double Jeopardy (Scotland) Bill (in private): The Committee will consider the main themes arising from the oral evidence heard earlier in the meeting.

3. Work programme (in private): The Committee will consider its work programme.

4. Draft Budget Scrutiny 2011-12 (in private): The Committee will consider a draft report to the Finance Committee on the Scottish Government’s Draft Budget 2011-12.

5. Domestic Abuse (Scotland) Bill (in private): The Committee will consider a draft Stage 1 report.

European and External Relations Committee
17th Meeting, 2010

The Committee will meet at 10.30 am in Committee Room 4

1. Decision on taking business in private: The Committee will decide whether to take item 6 in private.

2. Brussels Bulletin: The Committee will consider the Brussels Bulletin.

3. EC Work Programme 2011/12: The Committee will consider its approach.

4. International Engagement inquiry: The Committee will consider a report back on the Brussels study visit.

5. International Engagement inquiry: The Committee will take evidence from—

Fiona Hyslop MSP, Minister for Culture and External Affairs, Lisa Bird, Head of International Development Fund, and Heather Jones, Deputy Director International Division, Scottish Government.

6. Scrutiny of Scottish Government Budget: The Committee will consider its report to the Finance Committee.

7. International Engagement inquiry (in private): The Committee will consider the key points raised during the earlier evidence session.

8. Scottish Parliament’s European Strategy (in private): The Committee will consider an update report.

Scotland Bill Committee
2nd Meeting, 2010

The Committee will meet at 10.30 am in Committee Room 2

1. Scotland Bill (UK Parliament legislation): The Committee will take evidence from—

The Rt Hon Michael Moore MP, Secretary of State for Scotland, David Mundell MP, Parliamentary Under Secretary of State, and The Rt Hon Lord Wallace of Tankerness QC PC, Advocate General for Scotland, Scotland Office;

David Gauke MP, Exchequer Secretary, HM Treasury.

2. Correspondence: The Committee will consider a letter received from the Minister for Culture and External Affairs.

Transport, Infrastructure and Climate Change Committee
26th Meeting, 2010

The Committee will meet at 12.30 pm in Committee Room 5

1. Decision on taking business in private: The Committee will decide whether to consider agenda item 4 in private. The Committee will also decide whether its consideration of a draft report on Low Carbon Scotland: The Draft Report on Proposals and Policies, a draft report on the Scottish Government’s Draft Budget 2011-12 and its approach to the scrutiny of certain road safety issues, should be taken in private at future meetings.

2. Transport issues related to recent severe weather conditions: The Committee will take evidence from—

John Swinney MSP, Cabinet Secretary for Finance and Sustainable Growth, David Middleton, Chief Executive, Transport Scotland, and Jim Barton, Chief Road Engineer and Director of Trunk Road and Bus Operations, Scottish Government;

Chief Constable Kevin Smith, Association of Chief Police Officers in Scotland.

3. Low Carbon Scotland: The Draft Report on Proposals and Policies: The Committee will take evidence from—

John Swinney MSP, Cabinet Secretary for Finance and Sustainable Growth, Bob Irvine, Deputy Director, Scottish Water and Climate Change, and Rosie Telford, Policy Officer, Climate Change Acts: Implementation, Scottish Government.

4. Low Carbon Scotland: The Draft Report on Proposals and Policies: The Committee will consider the evidence heard during the session.

Standards, Procedures and Public Appointments Committee
14th Meeting, 2010

The Committee will meet at 2.15 pm in Committee Room 6

1. Decision on taking business in private: The Committee will decide whether to take item 3 in private.

2. Scottish Parliamentary Standards Commissioner: The Committee will take oral evidence from the Scottish Parliamentary Standards Commissioner.

3. Plenary debates: The Committee will consider a note by the Clerk.

4. Review of SPCB supported bodies (in private): The Committee will consider a note by the Clerk.

5. Minor changes to Standing Orders (in private): The Committee will consider a draft report.

Subordinate Legislation Committee
35th Meeting, 2010

The Committee will meet at 2.15 pm in Committee Room 3

1. Decision on taking business in private: The Committee will decide whether to take item 6 in private.

2. Draft instruments subject to approval: The Committee will consider the following—

the Criminal Justice and Licensing (Scotland) Act 2010 (Consequential and Supplementary Provisions) Order 2011 (SSI2011/draft);

the Advice and Assistance (Assistance By Way of Representation) (Scotland) Amendment Regulations 2011 (SSI2011/draft);

the Crime (International Co-operation) Act 2003 (Designation of Participating Countries) (Scotland) Order 2011 (SSI2011/draft).

3. Instruments subject to annulment: The Committee will consider the following—

the Vegetable Seeds Amendment (Scotland) Regulations 2010 (SSI2010/425);

the Flood Risk Management (Flood Protection Schemes, Potentially Vulnerable Areas and Local Plan Districts) (Scotland) Regulations 2010 (SSI2010/426);

the Registration Services (Fees) Amendment (Scotland) Regulations 2010 (SSI2010/427);

the Registration of Births, Deaths and Marriages (Fees) (Scotland) Order (SSI2010/428);

the Scallops (Luce Bay) (Prohibition of Fishing) Variation Order 2010 (SSI2010/429);

the Planning etc. (Scotland) Act 2006 (Saving and Transitional Provisions) Order 2010 (SSI2010/431);

the Town and Country Planning (Tree Preservation Order and Trees in Conservation Areas) (Scotland) Regulations 2010 (SSI2010/434).

4. Instruments not laid before the Parliament: The Committee will consider the following—

the Planning etc. (Scotland) Act 2006 (Commencement No.11) Order 2010 (SSI2010/430 (C. 29));

the Housing (Scotland) Act 2006 (Commencement No.9) Order 2010 (SSI2010/436 (C. 30));

the Crofting Reform (Scotland) Act 2010 (Commencement, Saving and Transitory Provisions) Order 2010 (SSI2010/437 (C. 31));

the Wild Birds (Special Protection in Severe Weather) (Scotland) (No. 2) Order 2010 (SSI2010/438).

5. Public Records (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will consider the delegated powers provisions in this Bill at Stage 1.

6. Double Jeopardy (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will consider the contents of a draft report.
