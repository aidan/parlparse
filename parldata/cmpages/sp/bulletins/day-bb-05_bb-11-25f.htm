Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday.  That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an “R”.

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm).  A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-3632 Pauline McNeill: Parliament Welcomes the Civil Partnership Act—That the Parliament welcomes the Civil Partnership Act 2004, which comes into force on 5 December 2005; recognises that the Act, which seeks to give social and legal inclusion to couples of the same sex, will be a major step forward for equality, and congratulates everyone at the Scottish Executive, the UK Government and the Scottish and UK Parliaments who contributed to the drafting of the legislation.

*S2M-3631 Shona Robison: Point of Delivery—That the Parliament notes the recent Macmillan Cancer Relief report, Free at the Point of Delivery?, which highlights the financial burden that travelling to and from hospital for regular treatment places on cancer patients; is concerned that there is no record of the percentage of cancer patients in Scotland who are currently eligible for reimbursement of their travel costs under the Hospital Travel Costs Scheme, possibly leaving many cancer patients on low incomes without much-needed financial support; considers that action is needed to increase the number of eligible patients who are claiming reimbursement under the scheme, through improved awareness and advice, and urges the Scottish Executive to look at extending the scheme to cancer patients not receiving benefits, as is done in the non-means-tested Highlands and Islands scheme.

*S2M-3630 Eleanor Scott: Food for Life—That the Parliament commends the Soil Association’s Food for Life project for its tremendously positive work in promoting food education and school food that is 75% fresh and unprocessed, 50% locally produced and 30% organic; is delighted that the project is flourishing with schools across Scotland already taking part and many more due to join in the near future; congratulates Hurlford Primary School in Kilmarnock, East Ayrshire, one of the Food for Life schools, for its success in winning the Soil Association’s Food for Life School of the Year Award and believes that this is an appropriate accolade given the health benefits to children of the project; recognises the economic and social benefits of local food procurement to Scottish food producers, particularly in remote rural areas, and urges the Scottish Executive to promote Food for Life as the logical progression from the Hungry for Success and Health Promoting Schools schemes.

*S2M-3629 Peter Peacock: Joint Inspection of Children’s Services and Inspection of Social Work Services (Scotland) Bill—That the Parliament agrees to the general principles of the Joint Inspection of Children’s Services and Inspection of Social Work Services (Scotland) Bill.

Supported by: Robert Brown*

*S2M-3628 Cathy Jamieson: Family Law (Scotland) Bill—That the Parliament agrees that the Family Law (Scotland) Bill be passed.

Supported by: Hugh Henry*

*S2M-3627 Mr Andy Kerr: Human Tissue (Scotland) Bill—That the Parliament agrees to the general principles of the Human Tissue (Scotland) Bill.

Supported by: Lewis Macdonald*

*S2M-3626<> Cathy Peattie: 16 Days of Activism Against Gender Violence—That the Parliament congratulates all those involved in the 16 Days of Activism Against Gender Violence Campaign; notes that, since 1991, approximately 1,700 organizations in 130 countries have participated in the 16 days campaign; notes that the theme of this year’s campaign is “For the Health of Women, For the Health of the World: No More Violence”, and believes that, while in recent years much has been done in Scotland to increase awareness and improve and extend services, including broadening the focus of the Scottish Executive’s strategy to encompass all forms of violence against women, there is clearly still much to do in all these respects, both within Scotland and as part of the international movement for the elimination of violence against women.

Supported by: Jackie Baillie*, Marlyn Glen*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Bruce Crawford*, Frances Curran*, Irene Oldfather*, Bill Butler*
