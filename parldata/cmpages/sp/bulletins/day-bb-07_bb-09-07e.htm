Business Bulletin No. 113/2007: Friday 7 September 2007
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 6 September 2007

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-4060 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will increase the capacity for cognitive behaviour therapy and whether programmes of this therapy will be extended to all areas of Scotland.

S3W-4061 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what recognised depression assessment tool is used by GPs and whether this tool is used by other health professionals to identify symptoms of depression.

S3W-4062 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans there are for recruitment and training of staff to take on the responsibilities associated with cognitive behaviour therapy.

S3W-4063 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has the legal power to require every local authority to freeze council tax at 2007-08 levels.

S3W-4064 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has the legal power to stop a local authority from increasing council tax in its area from its 2007-08 level.

S3W-4065 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive who is responsible for setting the level of council tax in each local authority area.

S3W-4066 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive whether its intended council tax freeze in 2008-09 will also apply to water and sewerage charges.

S3W-4067 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to continue the work previously undertaken by the Cabinet Sub-Committee on Sustainable Scotland.

S3W-4068 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will make an announcement on establishing a judicial inquiry into the Shirley McKie case.

S3W-4069 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-32287 by Des McNulty on 20 March 2007, whether it has now given consideration to including NHS boards in any list of statutory consultees whom the planning authority must approach in preparing a development plan.

S3W-4070 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make it a requirement on planning authorities to notify and consult community health partnerships about any housing development of a size that might affect local community health services.

S3W-4071 Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive what the methodology is for determining the success of the pilot scheme for free school meals for primary 1 to 3 pupils in deprived areas.

S3W-4072 Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive who has been appointed to conduct research to determine the success of the pilot scheme for free school meals for primary 1 to 3 pupils in deprived areas.

S3W-4073 Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive what the budget is for the analysis of the success of the pilot scheme for free school meals for primary 1 to 3 pupils in deprived areas.

S3W-4074 Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive what methodology it will use to assess the health benefits of the pilot scheme for free school meals for primary 1 to 3 pupils in deprived areas.

S3W-4075 Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive how much has been spent by it on (a) publicity, (b) special advisers, (c) promotion of cabinet secretaries and ministers and (d) commissioning external consultants’ advice since 17 May 2007, broken down by department and Executive agency.

S3W-4076 Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive how much it has budgeted to spend on (a) publicity, (b) special advisers, (c) promotion of cabinet secretaries and ministers and (d) commissioning external consultants’ advice in the next financial year.

S3W-4077 Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will ensure that the Housing Supply Task Force makes publicly available details of the land with outline planning permission currently owned by major housebuilding firms.

S3W-4078 Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will ensure that the Housing Supply Task Force makes publicly available details of the land for potential housing development currently owned by public agencies and local authorities.

S3W-4079 Hugh O’Donnell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to replace Scotland’s eight fire control rooms with larger control rooms.

S3W-4080 Hugh O’Donnell: To ask the Scottish Executive what meetings it has had since May 2007 with the Fire Brigades Union and other key stakeholders on the future of emergency fire control rooms.

S3W-4081 Derek Brownlee: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the proposed freeze in council tax at 2007-08 levels will be in cash or real terms.

S3W-4082 Jamie Hepburn: To ask the Scottish Executive how many evictions due to non-payment of rent have occurred in each year since 1990, broken down by local authority area.

S3W-4083 Jamie Hepburn: To ask the Scottish Executive how it intends to promote the decontamination of brownfield sites for redevelopment.

S3W-4084 Jamie Hepburn: To ask the Scottish Executive how much Scottish Water has paid in bonuses to its directors since its creation.

S3W-4085 Jamie Hepburn: To ask the Scottish Executive what Scottish Water’s net debt has been in each year since its creation.

S3W-4086 Jamie Hepburn: To ask the Scottish Executive, in relation to the Water Supply (Water Quality) (Scotland) Regulations 2001, how many authorised departures have been (a) applied for by Scottish Water and (b) permitted by the Scottish Ministers in each year, broken down by local authority area.

S3W-4087 Jamie Hepburn: To ask the Scottish Executive how much interest on borrowings Scottish Water has paid in each year since its creation.

S3W-4088 Jamie Hepburn: To ask the Scottish Executive how many medical students have graduated from Scotland’s universities in each year since 1997.

S3W-4089 Jamie Hepburn: To ask the Scottish Executive how many junior doctors have been employed by the NHS in Scotland in each year since 1999.

S3W-4090 Jamie Hepburn: To ask the Scottish Executive how many junior doctors have been unable to find work in Scotland this year and in the two previous years.

S3W-4091 Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive whether additional composite classes in primary 1 to primary 3 will result from its decision to reduce class sizes to 18.

S3W-4092 Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any profiling work has been carried out on the impact of reducing class sizes to 18 in primary 1 to primary 3.

S3W-4093 Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive how many additional composite classes in primary 1 to primary 3 will be created as a result of its decision to reduce class sizes to 18.

S3W-4094 Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive whether class sizes of 18 in primary 1 to primary 3 will be achieved by May 2011, as pledged by the First Minister on 5 September 2007 (Official Report c. 1378).

S3W-4095 Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive what the capital implications are of reducing class sizes to 18 in primary 1 to primary 3.

S3W-4096 Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive how much additional capital will be provided in each year from 2007-08 to 2011-12 to support the reduction in class sizes to 18 in primary 1 to primary 3.

S3W-4097 Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive how many schools will require temporary accommodation to meet its commitment to reduce class sizes to 18 in primary 1 to primary 3.

S3W-4098 Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the capital school estate is sufficient to meet its commitment to reduce class sizes to 18 in primary 1 to primary 3.

S3W-4099 Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive how many additional teachers will be required to meet its commitment to reduce class sizes to 18 in primary 1 to primary 3.

S3W-4100 Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive what additional funding will be provided to local authorities in each year from 2007-08 to 2011-12 to meet its commitment to reduce class sizes to 18 in primary 1 to primary 3.

S3W-4101 Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive how many additional teacher training places will be created in each year to 2011 to meet its commitment to reduce class sizes to 18 in primary 1 to primary 3.

S3W-4102 Cathie Craigie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the prison officer to inmate ratio is in the Scottish Prison Service.

S3W-4103 Cathie Craigie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many fine defaulters are held in prisons.

S3W-4104 Cathie Craigie: To ask the Scottish Executive who are classified as minor offenders.

S3W-4105 Cathie Craigie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many minor offenders are held in prisons.
