Business Bulletin No. 159/2003

Friday 21 November 2003

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-642# Brian Adam: Social Work Review—That the Parliament notes with concern the shortage of social workers throughout Scotland and considers that the Scottish Executive should initiate a McCrone-type review to look at social workers’ pay and conditions in order to attract and retain more social workers.

*S2M-641 Rosie Kane: Female Genital Mutilation and Asylum Seekers—That the Parliament notes the plight of the asylum seeker named as "Margaret" featured on "Newsnight Scotland" on 19 November 2003; notes the continuation of the brutal practice of female genital mutilation in Margaret’s homeland of Sierra Leone and the extreme danger that Margaret’s daughters would face if they were to be returned there; resolves to oppose by all necessary means the deportation of women and children to countries where female genital mutilation is practised; calls upon the Home Office to end the asylum lottery in which legitimate refugees are refused asylum because of poor legal representation and erratic asylum adjudicators, and resolves to oppose any attempt to detain or deport Margaret and her children when they attend the Glasgow office of the Immigration and Nationality Directorate on 29 November 2003.

*S2M-640 Christine Grahame: Boycott—That the Parliament considers that the boycott by the Scottish Executive of the debate on Thursday 20 November 2003 on the subject of world peace demonstrated a considered contempt for the Parliament and democratic debate, particularly given the history of Labour in supporting the war in Iraq without a UN mandate specifically authorising such action, and therefore calls on the First Minister to apologise to the Chamber for this behaviour.

Supported by: Fiona Hyslop*, Michael Matheson*, Mr Stewart Maxwell*, Campbell Martin*, Brian Adam*, Shiona Baird*, Linda Fabiani*, Eleanor Scott*, Nicola Sturgeon*, Carolyn Leckie*, Rob Gibson*

*S2M-639# Alex Neil: Scottish Widows and Lloyds TSB—That the Parliament deplores the decision to transfer Scottish Widows jobs out of Scotland and believes that none of Lloyds TSB’s operations in Scotland should be threatened with either closure or downsizing as a consequence of work being transferred abroad.

*S2M-638# Mark Ballard: Road Safety—That the Parliament notes the success of the Lothian and Borders Safety Camera Partnership in addressing the problems of speeding and serious road crashes and commends the pilot of 20 mph zones utilising flashing road signs outside seven Edinburgh primary schools in making roads safer for children.

*S2M-637# Patrick Harvie: Ministerial Boycott of World Peace Debate—That the Parliament deplores the decision by the Scottish Executive to boycott the debate on world peace and considers that Executive ministers should take part in every Parliamentary debate.

Supported by: Campbell Martin*, Nicola Sturgeon*, Chris Ballance*, Richard Lochhead*, Tommy Sheridan*, Carolyn Leckie*, Christine Grahame*, Rosie Kane*, Frances Curran*, Mr Mark Ruskell*, Mark Ballard*, Shiona Baird*, Rob Gibson*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Linda Fabiani*, Eleanor Scott*, Mr Stewart Maxwell*

*S2M-636# Alex Neil: Pensions—That the Parliament considers that Her Majesty’s Government should re-establish the link between retirement pensions and the annual increase in wages.

*S2M-635# Alex Neil: Transfer of Reserved Powers—That the Parliament believes that it should petition Her Majesty’s Government to introduce the requisite Orders in Council to transfer all reserved powers detailed in Schedule 5 of the Scotland Act 1998 to the Scottish Parliament with immediate effect.

*S2M-634# Alex Neil: Breeding of Animals—That the Parliament expresses concern at the inadequacy of current legislation in respect of the breeding of animals, in particular domestic pets, and believes that urgent action should be taken to deal with this situation.

*S2M-633# Alex Neil: Non-numeric Fingerprinting—That the Parliament expresses concern at the introduction of non-numeric fingerprinting evidence by the Scottish Criminal Record Office, its implications for the success rate of convictions and the potential consequential increase in the legal aid budget and considers that the Scottish Executive should review this policy on a cross-departmental basis prior to its implementation.
