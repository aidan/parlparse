Business Bulletin No. 189/2006: Friday 22 December 2006
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 21 December 2006

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-30605Δ - Withdrawn

S2W-30723 Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive what economic, social and environmental benefits have arisen as a result of the designation and management of the Loch Lomond and the Trossachs National Park.

S2W-30724 Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive why the number of adults registered with NHS dentists in the East Kilbride constituency has fallen between 2005 and 2006.

S2W-30725 Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many NHS dental practices there have been in the East Kilbride constituency in each of the last five years.

S2W-30726 Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive how much has been spent by each NHS board on foreign language interpreters in each of the last five years, broken down by language.

S2W-30727 Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive whether extra resources will be made available to reduce the backlog of audiology appointments in NHS Lothian.

S2W-30728 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration it has given to any possibility that the public information campaign for the new local government electoral system will lead to confusion in respect of the constituency section of the ballot for the Scottish parliamentary elections, given that this will now be the only election involving marking a single "x", and what representations have been made to the (a) Scotland Office and (b) Electoral Commission to ensure that the public remains well-informed for participation in all levels of democracy.

S2W-30729 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive how many registered farmers or agricultural workers have committed suicide in each year since 1999.

S2W-30730 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what the figures have been for delayed discharge in each NHS board since 1999, broken down by hospital.

S2W-30731 Carolyn Leckie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many GP partnerships have been dissolved since 2003.

S2W-30732 Carolyn Leckie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many GP partnerships which have been dissolved since 2003 have then been tendered openly, allowing groups other than existing GPs in the NHS board area to submit a tender.

S2W-30733 Carolyn Leckie: To ask the Scottish Executive why it has stated publicly that NHS Lanarkshire had to tender primary care services at Harthill openly, as reported in The Herald on 19 December 2006.

S2W-30734 Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will monitor the uptake and effectiveness of universal free eye examinations.

S2W-30735 Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will advertise the availability of free eye examinations to vulnerable groups such as elderly people, those in deprived areas or those with medical conditions that can cause eye problems.

S2W-30736 Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will publicise the fact that the free eye examination is concerned with assessing eye health and not merely the need for glasses.

S2W-30737 Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making in increasing the range of services provided by optometrists, in line with the Kerr report principle that care should be provided where possible in the patient’s own locality.

S2W-30738 Derek Brownlee: To ask the Scottish Executive how much was spent by the Executive and its agencies on advertising job vacancies in each year since 1999.

S2W-30739 Derek Brownlee: To ask the Scottish Executive how it assesses the value for money of its spending on recruitment advertising.

S2W-30740 Susan Deacon: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to progress Tram Line 3 to link south-east Edinburgh with the city’s planned tram network.

S2W-30741 Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it supports the recommendation made in A Fairer Way: Report by the Local Government Finance Review Committee that consideration should be given to introducing a discretionary power for local authorities to apply a tourism tax

S2W-30742 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-30418 by Malcolm Chisholm on 19 December 2006, how Glasgow Housing Association has spent the £160,150,000 that it claimed from the Secured Repayable Grant.

S2W-30743 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-30419 by Malcolm Chisholm on 19 December 2006, how Glasgow Housing Association has spent the £50,000,000 that it claimed from the Contingent Efficencies Grant.

S2W-30744 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many contractors have agreements in place with Scottish Gas to supply and install gas central heating under the Executive’s central heating programme.

S2W-30745 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive why the publication of the review of Sport 21 has been delayed and when it now expects the publication to take place.

S2W-30747 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will provide a substantive answer to my question S2W-29639 which was due for answer on 20 November 2006.

S2W-30748 Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it gives to guided busway developments.
