Business Bulletin No. 113/2002

Friday 2 August 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-3295 Pauline McNeill: Glasgow Summer Youth Football League Initiative—That the Parliament congratulates the summer youth football league initiative undertaken by police in the west and centre of Glasgow to provide an alternative to drugs and street crime for 14 to 18 year old boys; applauds the financial support of local businesses and the coaching schemes involving ex-Celtic player Paul McStay and ex-Ranger's junior coach David Preston, and supports such initiatives that promote healthy living through sport, fitness, team work and co-operation, develop interests and skills and encourage friendship between youths from neighbouring communities.

*S1M-3294# Mr Mike Rumbles: Safety on the A90—That the Parliament notes with concern the increase in serious accidents on the A90 between Laurencekirk and Aberdeen; congratulates the Scottish Executive on its commitment to making Scotland’s roads safer; welcomes its promise to introduce an Accident Investigation and Prevention Scheme at the Laurencekirk/Marykirk junction as an important first step, and believes that the Executive should introduce similar safety schemes at other junctions along this stretch of road.

*S1M-3293 Pauline McNeill: Windfall Payment to Glasgow City Council—That the Parliament welcomes the windfall payment of £12.5 million to Glasgow City Council from the Scottish Executive to be spent on children and the environment and applauds the council's decision to provide free drinking water in schools, cheap travel for pupils and free swimming lessons.

*S1M-3292 Irene McGugan: Under 18 Scottish Men's Hockey Team Success—That the Parliament notes that, despite several setbacks and injuries, the Under 18 Scottish Men's Hockey Team competed in the 1st European Nations Cup Under 18 Boys B Division tournament in Olten, Switzerland between 1 and 6 July 2002; congratulates the team on winning its group by beating Wales and drawing with Russia and on subsequently beating both Italy and the host nation to win the gold medal and, with it, promotion to the A division; further congratulates the team on this significant and well-deserved sporting success, and commiserates with the English team on its relegation from the A division to the B division.
