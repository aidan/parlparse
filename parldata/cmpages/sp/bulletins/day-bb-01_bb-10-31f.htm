Business Bulletin No. 155/2001

Wednesday 31 October 2001

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions.

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-2386 Mr Gil Paterson: Wallace Clan Contribution to New York Fire Department Fund—That the Parliament acknowledges the action of the Wallace Clan Trust in supporting the New York Fire Department Fund for emergency services and families of the disaster on 11 September 2001 by providing the Braveheart Claymore 911 for auction, which raised over $4,000, and notes that this gift was given in the name of Scotland along with a humanitarian message of support for the families.

*S1M-2385 Tommy Sheridan: Tibet—That the Parliament notes that the Chinese Vice-President is visiting Scotland this week and calls upon the First Minister to make representations to him on behalf of the people of Tibet who, as a nation, have been illegally occupied by the Chinese for the past 50 years.

*S1M-2384 Mr Tom McCabe on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Suspension of Standing Orders—That the Parliament agrees that Rules 9.5.3B and 9.7.9 of the Standing Orders be suspended for the purposes of the Police and Fire Services (Finance) (Scotland) Bill.

Supported by: Euan Robson*

Supported by: Euan Robson*

*S1M-2381 Ms Margo MacDonald: Holyrood Project—That the Parliament thanks the Holyrood Progress Group for its efforts to date; sympathises with its difficulties in adequately monitoring and reporting on the costs, progress and management of the Holyrood Project, and directs the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body to disband the Progress Group in order to substitute a more satisfactory and professional method of controlling the Project.

Supported by: Alex Neil*, Dorothy-Grace Elder*

*S1M-2380 Alex Neil on behalf of the Enterprise and Lifelong Learning Committee: Inquiry into the Scottish Higher Education Funding Council Reviews of Teaching and Research Funding—That the Parliament welcomes the 12th Report 2001 of the Enterprise and Lifelong Learning Committee on the inquiry into the Scottish Higher Education Funding Council reviews of teaching and research funding (SP Paper 423).

*S1M-2379 Mr Jim Wallace: Police and Fire Services (Finance) (Scotland) Bill—That the Parliament agrees that the Police and Fire Services (Finance) (Scotland) Bill be passed.

Supported by: Iain Gray*, Peter Peacock*

*S1M-2378 Pauline McNeill: Glasgow's Museums—That the Parliament welcomes the initial findings of Scotland’s National Audit of museum and gallery collections, premises and public services - Interim Report, October 2001 by the Scottish Museums Council that a third of the artefacts and priceless works in Glasgow City Council's collection have international importance and that over half of those works are of national or regional importance; believes that these findings confirm that museums and galleries in Glasgow make a significant contribution to the Scottish economy, and calls for new discussions between the Scottish Executive and Glasgow City Council to consider how to address the imbalance in funding between museums in Glasgow and the National Museums of Scotland in Edinburgh.

*S1M-2377.1 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: Asylum Seekers and Refugee Integration—As an amendment to motion (S1M-2377) in the name of Jackie Baillie, leave out from first "welcomes" to end and insert "reaffirms the historic commitment of both Scotland and the United Kingdom as a whole to provide a safe haven for those who have been persecuted or are under threat of persecution by oppressive regimes overseas; notes, however, with concern that our ability to provide refuge for the genuinely oppressed is severely hampered by the problems associated with our asylum system, and welcomes the movement of Her Majesty’s Government towards the policy of the Conservative Party, which will speed up the process of establishing which claims are well-founded and subsequently enable every possible step to be taken to welcome and integrate asylum seekers and refugees to Scotland and grant them the same rights of citizenship as enjoyed by the rest of the community."

*S1M-2377.2 Linda Fabiani: Asylum Seekers and Refugee Integration—As an amendment to motion (S1M-2377) in the name of Jackie Baillie, leave out from "and further welcomes" to end and insert "; congratulates the host communities and Scotland’s civic society on the steps taken so far to enable integration; welcomes the appointment of a Scottish Minister with responsibilities in this area; notes that the statement by the Home Secretary in the Westminster Parliament on 29 October 2001 indicates a move by Her Majesty's Government contrary to the current direction of the Scottish Executive, particularly in the establishment of "accommodation centres" as opposed to sensitive dispersal and the imposition of "Smart Cards" as a form of electronic voucher for asylum seekers, and calls for the Scottish Parliament and the Scottish Executive to welcome asylum seekers and refugees into our communities and to have the power to provide appropriate services to promote true integration."

Supported by: Mr Kenneth Gibson*
