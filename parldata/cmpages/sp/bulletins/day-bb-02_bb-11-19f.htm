Business Bulletin No. 167/2002

Tuesday 19 November 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-3594 Pauline McNeill: Impact on Human Rights Organisations of Proposed Israeli Legislation-That the Parliament condemns the Bill tabled in the Israeli Parliament, the Knesset, on 29 October 2002 by the chair of the Israeli Government coalition, MK Zeev Boim, to secure impunity for war criminals, outlaw human rights activities, imprison human rights activists for up to ten years and destroy evidence of war crimes; notes that such a law, if passed, would forbid "the holding of investigations and writing down of results" and that any association engaging in any such activity would be liable to be disbanded, effectively closing down any action by human rights organisations to collect and then disseminate testimonies of human rights violations; expresses grave concern that such proposals would sabotage the work of Israeli human rights activists assisting international war crimes tribunals at the Hague and make illegal the courageous and conscientious actions of individual citizens of Israel, and calls on MSPs to protest in the strongest terms to members of the Knesset against these proposed violations of human rights.

*S1M-3593# Donald Gorrie: St Andrew's Day-That the Parliament believes that the Scottish Executive and other appropriate bodies should consider making St Andrew's Day a national holiday, either by replacing one of the existing holidays in May or by making it an additional holiday.

*S1M-3592 Ms Margo MacDonald: Comments by Scottish Executive Chief Architect on Enric Miralles-That the Parliament is indebted to the Scottish Executive Chief Architect for revealing, as attributed to him in The Herald magazine on 16 November 2002, that, in respect of Enric Miralles, "We did not employ an architect who was able to describe what he was doing and show in traditional ways what he was building" and "He quite deliberately didn't give you all the info"; expresses regret that the Chief Architect should have kept hidden until the day of the decisive debate in the Parliament on the Holyrood Project the true nature of Enric Miralles' illness which "threatened the future of the whole Parliament", and further regrets deeply that, deprived of full information on Enric Miralles and an independent medical assessment and prognosis, the Parliament was misled as to his fitness to perform in accordance with his contractual obligations.

*S1M-3591 Ms Margo MacDonald: Comments by Holyrood Project Director on the Parliament Building-That the Parliament notes the opinion attributed in The Herald magazine on 16 November 2002 to the Holyrood Project Director that "negotiating" with the MSPs elected in May 1999 over a parliament building designed to meet their requirements would have been likely to result in the site for the new parliament still being undetermined in November 2002; records its confidence in its ability to reach democratic decisions on projects or policies which, constitutionally, are executed by officials of either the Scottish Executive or the Parliament and which, in the course of such a process, may be the subject of private, professional exchanges between elected ministers and MSPs but should never be the subject of public pronouncement on the part of officials whether in relation to the credibility or value of the project or policy or the abilities of those elected MSPs responsible for it, and questions whether the Holyrood Project Director is a fit person to execute Parliament's directives given her publicly expressed lack of trust in the collective judgement of MSPs.

*S1M-3590# Shona Robison: Delivery of Family Planning Services in Dundee-That the Parliament notes with concern the changes being proposed to the delivery of family planning services in Dundee; recognises the important work of well woman clinics like the one in Whitfield which provides a range of family planning and health screening services to women; is further concerned that any closure of that clinic will require women to travel by two buses to the other side of the city in order to access an alternative clinic, and believes that such services are best provided within local communities and that Tayside NHS board should urgently reconsider these proposals.
