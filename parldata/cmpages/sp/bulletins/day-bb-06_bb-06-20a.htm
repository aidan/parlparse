Section A – Today’s Business

Meetings of Committees

All meetings take place in the Scottish Parliament, unless otherwise specified. Contact details for Committee Clerks are provided at the end of the Bulletin.

Equal Opportunities Committee
15th Meeting, 2006

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in Committee Room 5

1. Disability inquiry (in private): The Committee will consider a draft report.

Finance Committee
18th Meeting, 2006

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in Committee Room 1

1. Relocation of Public Sector Jobs: The Committee will take evidence on the relocation of public sector jobs from—

George Lyon MSP, Deputy Minister for Finance and Public Service Reform; David Robb, Head of Public Bodies and Relocation Division, Scottish Executive; and Morris Fraser, Public Bodies and Relocation Division, Scottish Executive.

2. Adult Support and Protection (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will take evidence on the Financial Memorandum from—

Jean MacLellan, Bill Team Leader, Adult Support and Protection Unit, Scottish Executive; and Diane Strachan, Adult Support and Protection Unit, Scottish Executive.

3. Efficient Government: The Committee will consider the updated Scottish Executive Efficiency Technical Notes.

5. Review of Scottish Executive Management of Public Finances (in private): The Committee will consider a paper from its budget adviser.

Procedures Committee
11th Meeting, 2006

The Committee will meet at 10.15 am in Committee Room 4

1. Items in private: The Committee will decide whether to take item 6 in private, and whether to consider, in private at future meetings, draft reports on Parliamentary Time; Members’ Bills and Substitution; and Consolidation Bills.

2. House of Commons Committees: Members who attended a meeting on 14 June with the House of Commons Procedure Committee and a press launch on 19 June with the House of Commons Scottish Affairs Committee will report back to the Committee.

3. Regulatory Framework in Scotland: The Committee will continue its consideration of a letter from the Convener of the Subordinate Legislation Committee inviting comments on its draft report.

4. Review of Parliamentary Time: The Committee will consider issues relating to selection of speakers and speaking times in debates, and a draft model for an interpellation procedure.

5. Members’ Bills and substitution: The Committee will consider whether its proposal to prevent MSPs who are members in charge of a Member’s Bill participating as members of committees scrutinising the Bill should also apply to committees considering draft proposals under Rule 9.14.6, and whether there are implications for Executive and Committee Bills.

6. Review of Parliamentary Time: The Committee will consider a draft interim report.

7. Members’ Bills and substitution (in private): The Committee will consider a draft report.
