Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday.  That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an “R”.

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm).  A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-3636 Mr Stewart Maxwell: Reporting Knife-Related Injuries—That the Parliament acknowledges the cross-party consensus to reduce knife crime in Scotland; recognises that knife crime remains a persistent problem in Scotland and that, according to a study by Glasgow Royal Infirmary, only 53% of knife-related assaults are ever reported to the police; agrees that it is vital that hospitals notify the police when they have knowledge of a knife-related injury, thus giving police the opportunity to investigate every knife crime that is actually committed in Scotland, and calls on the Scottish Executive to require NHS boards to implement a system whereby all knife-related injuries are reported to the police.

*S2M-3635 Frances Curran: Terminate Fizzy Drinks in Schools—That the Parliament is surprised to be congratulating free marketeer and Republican Governor of California, Arnold Schwarzenegger, who has banned fizzy drinks, including Coca-Cola, from all schools in California in an attempt to target childhood obesity; notes that the “Terminator” has terminated the sale of these drinks in schools in the same period that the Scottish Executive has been unable to decide what to do; further notes that Scotland is now lagging behind many countries which have also banned junk food and drink from their schools, and believes that decisive action on banning junk food and drink in schools cannot come soon enough in Scotland, where child obesity levels in Scotland have doubled in the last 10 years.

Supported by: Carolyn Leckie*, Mark Ballard*

*S2M-3634 Stewart Stevenson: Banking in Rural Scotland—That the Parliament deplores the attempt by Clydesdale Bank to prevent the possible takeover of their closure-threatened branches by the Royal Bank of Scotland; notes that for many of the rural communities affected the existence of a bank is a vital part of their infrastructure that anchors local business and personal life, and calls on Clydesdale Bank to abandon its current “dog-in-the-manger” attitude and co-operate with any organisation willing to continue banking services in affected towns and villages.

*S2M-3633 Brian Adam on behalf of the Standards and Public Appointments Committee: Interests of Members of the Scottish Parliament Bill—That the Parliament agrees to the general principles of the Interests of Members of the Scottish Parliament Bill.

Supported by: Bill Butler*

S2M-3632 Pauline McNeill: Parliament Welcomes the Civil Partnership Act—That the Parliament welcomes the Civil Partnership Act 2004, which comes into force on 5 December 2005; recognises that the Act, which seeks to give social and legal inclusion to couples of the same sex, will be a major step forward for equality, and congratulates everyone at the Scottish Executive, the UK Government and the Scottish and UK Parliaments who contributed to the drafting of the legislation.

S2M-3631 Shona Robison: Point of Delivery—That the Parliament notes the recent Macmillan Cancer Relief report, Free at the Point of Delivery?, which highlights the financial burden that travelling to and from hospital for regular treatment places on cancer patients; is concerned that there is no record of the percentage of cancer patients in Scotland who are currently eligible for reimbursement of their travel costs under the Hospital Travel Costs Scheme, possibly leaving many cancer patients on low incomes without much-needed financial support; considers that action is needed to increase the number of eligible patients who are claiming reimbursement under the scheme, through improved awareness and advice, and urges the Scottish Executive to look at extending the scheme to cancer patients not receiving benefits, as is done in the non-means-tested Highlands and Islands scheme.

Supported by: Mr Stewart Maxwell*, Christine Grahame*

S2M-3630 Eleanor Scott: Food for Life—That the Parliament commends the Soil Association’s Food for Life project for its tremendously positive work in promoting food education and school food that is 75% fresh and unprocessed, 50% locally produced and 30% organic; is delighted that the project is flourishing with schools across Scotland already taking part and many more due to join in the near future; congratulates Hurlford Primary School in Kilmarnock, East Ayrshire, one of the Food for Life schools, for its success in winning the Soil Association’s Food for Life School of the Year Award and believes that this is an appropriate accolade given the health benefits to children of the project; recognises the economic and social benefits of local food procurement to Scottish food producers, particularly in remote rural areas, and urges the Scottish Executive to promote Food for Life as the logical progression from the Hungry for Success and Health Promoting Schools schemes.

Supported by: Mark Ballard*, Bruce Crawford*

S2M-3629 Peter Peacock: Joint Inspection of Children’s Services and Inspection of Social Work Services (Scotland) Bill—That the Parliament agrees to the general principles of the Joint Inspection of Children’s Services and Inspection of Social Work Services (Scotland) Bill.

Supported by: Robert Brown

S2M-3628 Cathy Jamieson: Family Law (Scotland) Bill—That the Parliament agrees that the Family Law (Scotland) Bill be passed.

S2M-3626*#* Cathy Peattie: 16 Days of Activism Against Gender Violence—That the Parliament congratulates all those involved in the 16 Days of Activism Against Gender Violence Campaign; notes that, since 1991, approximately 1,700 organizations in 130 countries have participated in the 16 days campaign; notes that the theme of this year’s campaign is “For the Health of Women, For the Health of the World: No More Violence”, and believes that, while in recent years much has been done in Scotland to increase awareness and improve and extend services, including broadening the focus of the Scottish Executive’s strategy to encompass all forms of violence against women, there is clearly still much to do in all these respects, both within Scotland and as part of the international movement for the elimination of violence against women.

Supported by: Jackie Baillie, Marlyn Glen, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Bruce Crawford, Frances Curran, Irene Oldfather, Bill Butler, Helen Eadie*, Robin Harper*, Marilyn Livingstone*, Mr Kenneth Macintosh*, Mrs Mary Mulligan*, Mike Pringle*, Tommy Sheridan*, Sarah Boyack*, Christine Grahame*

S2M-3625 Carolyn Leckie: Stop Violence Against Women - Support Amnesty International—That the Parliament congratulates Amnesty International on its Stop Violence Against Women campaign and its latest initiative, “16 days of Activism to Stop Violence Against Women”; regrets, however, that such campaigns are still necessary and is particularly concerned that recent attitude surveys reflect that an unacceptably large proportion of the population blame women for rapes perpetrated by men; is further concerned that conviction rates for rape have decreased and that those rapes that end up in court are a tiny proportion of rapes actually committed; believes and affirms that rape is a brutal expression of power over, and violence against, women, that it is always the responsibility of the perpetrator and never the victim, that the media contribute to a climate where sexist and misogynist attitudes to women are given credence, and further believes that fundamental attitudinal change must be achieved throughout the criminal justice system for women to have the confidence to report and pursue rape allegations and that the Parliament has the responsibility to lead that attitudinal change in the criminal justice system and wider society.

Supported by: Ms Rosemary Byrne, Frances Curran, Rosie Kane, Linda Fabiani, Ms Sandra White, Tommy Sheridan

S2M-3624 Margo MacDonald: Urgent Request for a Ministerial Statement—That the Parliament requests that the First Minister makes a statement on the discussions that have taken place between himself and Home Office ministers concerning the methods used to remove failed asylum seekers from Scotland, in light of his spokesman’s statement at lunchtime on 23 November 2005 which made it clear that the First Minister had not reflected the Parliament’s opposition to the “dawn raids” in his meetings with Home Office ministers to date and believes that, in advance of his meeting on 24 November 2005 with the Home Office Minister, Tony McNulty, he must re-state the Scottish Executive’s position on this matter.

S2M-3623 Carolyn Leckie: Scottish Executive Failing Women on Equal Pay—That the Parliament is concerned that the Scottish Executive in its local government finance settlement fails to comply with its obligations to do all that it can to achieve equal pay; recognises that these obligations are the encouragement of equal opportunities, in particular the observance of equal opportunities requirements and the imposition of duties on Scottish public authorities and cross-border authorities in relation to their Scottish functions; believes that, given that local authorities in Scotland are resisting full settlement of equal pay for women on the grounds of insufficient funding and the risk to council services and jobs and recommending acceptance of compensation arrangements that fall well below what women would be confident of achieving by pursuing their legal entitlement, the Executive could and should have made the necessary funds available to achieve equal pay, both retrospectively and prospectively, but has not; further believes, therefore, that the Executive has not met the financial, legal, political or moral challenge of achieving equal pay and has stood by whilst the unequal pay of women has subsidised public services, and considers that only urgent direct action by the Executive on this issue, including the allocation of sufficient resources, will deliver results that match the Executive pronouncements on equality and preserve public services and the jobs of local authority workers.

Supported by: Ms Rosemary Byrne, Frances Curran, Rosie Kane, Tommy Sheridan

S2M-3619 Christine Grahame: Scott’s Selkirk—That the Parliament recognises the benefits that Scott’s Selkirk contributes to the local economy, running as it does on Saturday and Sunday, 3 and 4 December 2005; notes that it commemorates the ride of Sir Walter Scott into Selkirk from Clovenfords, and then from Abbotsford, to dispense justice in the courthouse that still dominates Selkirk’s market place; further notes these words from Marmion “Heap on more wood! - the wind is chill; But let it whistle as it will, We’ll keep our Christmas merry still... While music, mirth, and social cheer speed on their wings the passing year.”; further notes that during the weekend there are living history performances including the re-enactment of Scott’s court cases, together with street theatre and stalls, and congratulates the Souters of Selkirk on celebrating this extraordinary Borderer with such panache.

Supported by: Bruce Crawford, Mr Bruce McFee, Michael Matheson, Murdo Fraser, Derek Brownlee*

S2M-3618 Mr Kenny MacAskill: FSB Report - The Future of the High Street—That the Parliament welcomes the Federation of Small Businesses’ (FSB) report, The Future of the High Street; acknowledges its 10 pragmatic recommendations to support small independent retailers, and believes that vibrant and healthy independent retailers are vital to the distinct character of Scotland’s towns and cities.

Supported by: Mike Pringle, Tricia Marwick, Jim Mather, Nora Radcliffe, Christine Grahame, Fiona Hyslop, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Bruce Crawford, Brian Adam, Fergus Ewing, Richard Lochhead, Mr Bruce McFee, Mr Stewart Maxwell, Michael McMahon, Stewart Stevenson, Alex Neil, Phil Gallie, John Scott, Murray Tosh, Michael Matheson, Rob Gibson, Murdo Fraser, Mr John Swinney, Linda Fabiani, Ms Sandra White

S2M-3616 Patrick Harvie: Ministerial Commitments on Asylum Protocol—That the Parliament notes the groundswell of opposition to current asylum removal practices being carried out by the Home Office in Scotland; reasserts its view, as expressed in motion S2M-3323 agreed by the Parliament on 22 September 2005, that an agreement is needed between the Home Office and the Scottish Executive which would protect the welfare of children facing removal; notes the commitment made in the Parliament on the same day by the First Minister to seek a “protocol” for this purpose and to report back to the Parliament on progress; notes the First Minister’s statement on 29 September 2005 that the Home Secretary “has agreed that the establishment of such an agreement in Scotland and, perhaps, elsewhere would be advisable”; notes the Deputy First Minister’s assurances to the Parliament on the 27 October 2005 that “the First Minister and the Home Secretary have reached an agreement in principle on the issue” and that the protocol would change removal practices rather than describe existing practice; notes with dismay that the Home Office has this week briefed journalists to the effect that no such protocol will be agreed with the Executive; considers that Scottish ministers have a responsibility to speak honestly in the Parliament and elsewhere, and considers that the contradiction between the ministers’ statements and the Home Office briefing raises the possibility that the First Minister may have misled the Parliament.

Supported by: Ms Rosemary Byrne, Rob Gibson, Robin Harper, Mr Mark Ruskell, Eleanor Scott, Linda Fabiani, Ms Sandra White, Tommy Sheridan

S2M-3609 Alex Johnstone: Impact of Private Water Supplies (Scotland) Regulations—That the Parliament notes with concern the impact of the Private Water Supplies (Scotland) Regulations 2005 on hundreds of small businesses throughout Scotland, which could be facing bills of up to £10,000 to upgrade their water supply as a consequence, and urges the Scottish Executive to consider increasing the grant support on offer to those businesses affected, which would help to protect vulnerable jobs and services in rural businesses.

Supported by: Murdo Fraser

S2M-3608 Eleanor Scott: Reducing Speed Limits for Road Safety—That the Parliament congratulates Brake, the road safety charity, for its Road Safety Week on the theme of “Watch out! There’s a kid about”; notes with alarm that road traffic is the biggest killer of 12 to 16-year-olds in Britain and that last year the number of road deaths of 12 to 19-year-olds on foot rose by 26%; commends and supports the residents of Lerwick who are campaigning to reduce speed limits in residential areas to 20 miles per hour; believes that 20 miles per hour speed limits around schools should be more tightly enforced and extended to all residential areas, and urges the Scottish Executive, local authorities and the police to take urgent steps to reduce road deaths and injuries in Scotland.

Supported by: Christine Grahame, Ms Sandra White, Fergus Ewing, Patrick Harvie, Robin Harper, Rob Gibson, Shiona Baird, Cathy Peattie, Mr Kenneth Macintosh, Mr Stewart Maxwell, Tommy Sheridan

S2M-3607 Rob Gibson: Congratulations to Glas Cymru—That the Parliament congratulates Glas Cymru, the owners of Dwr Cymru/Welsh Water, for attaining the position of joint top-performing private water and sewerage company in Wales and England with Yorkshire Water this year; particularly notes that Glas Cymru is a not-for-profit trust that has no shareholders and reinvests all its financial surpluses for the benefit of customers; welcomes its dividend strategy which will maintain or increase the £18 level of bonus paid to its one million customers this year; believes that Scottish Water should be transformed into a not-for-profit trust in the public sector that could reduce costs to Scottish customers instead of expecting to charge excessive rates to domestic and, especially, business water users, a practice that will continue with the Scottish Executive’s chosen model, which is due for the next determination by the Water Commission on St Andrew’s Day, and notes that this practice is locking Scottish Water customers into a punitive charge regime that has resulted in capital expenditure being mainly funded by current water charge payers since 2003.

Supported by: Michael Matheson, Christine Grahame, Ms Sandra White, Brian Adam, Donald Gorrie, Jim Mather, Mr Bruce McFee, Mrs Margaret Ewing, Linda Fabiani

S2M-3607.1 Murdo Fraser: Congratulations to Glas Cymru—As an amendment to motion (S2M-3607) in the name of Rob Gibson, leave out from first “Scottish” to end and insert “, if Scottish Water was transformed into a mutual company or not-for-profit trust in the private sector along the lines of Glas Cymru, it could reduce costs to Scottish customers instead of expecting to charge excessive rates to domestic and, especially, business water users, a practice that will continue with the Scottish Executive’s chosen model, and concludes that the benefits to be gained from adopting the Welsh model would be lost if Scottish Water were to be retained in the public sector.”

S2M-3606 Alex Johnstone: Arbroath Abbey World Heritage Site Status—That the Parliament supports a campaign to encourage the UK Government to obtain World Heritage Site status from UNESCO for Arbroath Abbey, not only on the basis of its connection with the Declaration of Arbroath of 1320 but also for its outstanding architecture; recognises that the Declaration of Arbroath more than satisfies UNESCO’s requirement for the site to be directly associated with literary works of outstanding universal significance, and considers that the Scottish Executive should support Arbroath Abbey as an excellent Scottish candidate for the UK Tentative List.

Supported by: Christine Grahame, Mr Ted Brocklebank, Robin Harper, Mr Jamie McGrigor, Margaret Mitchell, Murdo Fraser, Mrs Nanette Milne, Ms Sandra White, Fiona Hyslop, Donald Gorrie, Mr David Davidson, Derek Brownlee, Nora Radcliffe, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton, Shiona Baird, Mr Brian Monteith, Bill Aitken

Supported by: Michael Matheson, Ms Sandra White, Robin Harper, Eleanor Scott, Brian Adam, Patrick Harvie, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Mrs Margaret Ewing, Mr Andrew Arbuckle, Mr Jamie Stone, Fergus Ewing, Richard Lochhead, Mr Stewart Maxwell, Stewart Stevenson, Mr Brian Monteith

S2M-3549# Alex Neil: Council Tax in Very Sheltered Housing—That the Parliament expresses astonishment and concern at the decision of the valuation boards in Renfrewshire and Lanarkshire to designate single rooms occupied by residents of very sheltered housing complexes, such as Abbeyfield Homes in Coatbridge, as being fully liable for council tax; furthermore notes that a review of this matter is currently being undertaken by the Scottish Executive; recognises that this injustice has to be rectified as a matter of urgency by redesignating each very sheltered housing complex as being liable for council tax as a whole rather than individual living units, and believes that any council tax payments made as a result of the current designation should be refunded.

Supported by: Michael Matheson, Donald Gorrie, Rob Gibson, Brian Adam, Mr Bruce McFee, Stewart Stevenson, Colin Fox, Fiona Hyslop, Mr Stewart Maxwell, Tommy Sheridan, Mr Adam Ingram, Frances Curran, Shiona Baird, Carolyn Leckie, Robin Harper, Christine Grahame, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Mrs Margaret Ewing, Ms Sandra White, John Swinburne

S2M-3473# Maureen Macmillan: Outsourcing of Rail Call Centre Jobs from Vertex Call Centre, Dingwall—That the Parliament notes with concern the issuing of the 90-day notices to the 200-plus workforce at the Vertex Call Centre in Dingwall; notes that the loss of these jobs will have a major impact on this small Highland town; further notes that these jobs have been put in jeopardy by the decision of TheTrainline, a company which has Virgin Trains as its major shareholder, to move the contract outwith the European Union, in spite of Virgin Trains being in receipt of substantial amounts of UK taxpayers’ money; supports the campaign of the Transport Salaried Staff’s Association on behalf of the call centre workers who are a highly skilled and loyal workforce, and believes that the Scottish Executive should consider what influence it can exert to reverse TheTrainline’s decision or assist Vertex in finding a new contract.
