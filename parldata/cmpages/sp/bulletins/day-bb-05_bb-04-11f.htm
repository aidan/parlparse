Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-2681 Patrick Harvie: Future of Glasgow Homeopathic Hospital In-patient Ward—That the Parliament congratulates Glasgow Homeopathic Hospital Campaign Team for its tireless efforts to prevent the closure of the in-patient ward at Glasgow Homoeopathic Hospital; commends the team on its dedication and commitment over the past 12 months; notes that NHS Greater Glasgow Heath Board’s corporate management team is meeting on 12 April 2005 to decide if it should abandon the proposals to the close the in-patient ward, further notes that politicians from every party have expressed their support for the retention of the in-patient ward, and urges NHS Greater Glasgow’s corporate management team to drop the proposal to close what is acknowledged to be a world-class facility with a pioneering model of integrative care.

*S2M-2680 Gordon Jackson: Congratulations to Govan Law Centre—That the Parliament congratulates Govan Law Centre as it celebrates its 10th anniversary in April 2005; notes the value of its national policy work in the field of debt, housing, education and employment law generally and, in particular, its work in preventing homelessness by stopping mortgage repossessions and evictions across the west coast of Scotland; acknowledges its role in supporting Scottish pro-health measures by drafting the Breastfeeding etc. (Scotland) Act 2005; recognises that community-based law centres have a key role to play as part of local and national social inclusion strategies, particularly in the provision of free expert legal representation, advice and legal support to those in poverty, and encourages the Scottish Executive to consider extending the community-based law centre model to other parts of Scotland.

Supported by: Mr Kenny MacAskill*, Margaret Jamieson*, Colin Fox*, Nicola Sturgeon*, Mr Jamie Stone*, Karen Gillon*, Elaine Smith*, Mark Ballard*, Chris Ballance*, Christine May*, Sarah Boyack*, Jackie Baillie*, Patrick Harvie*, Mike Watson*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*

*S2M-2679# Colin Fox: How We Should Rule Ourselves—That the Parliament welcomes the publication of How We Should Rule Ourselves by Alasdair Gray and Professor Adam Tomkins of Glasgow University; hopes that the book will further stimulate the debate over the form of government which best serves Scotland, either a modern democratic republic or a monarchy; recognises the tremendous success of the 2004 Declaration of Calton Hill which again drew attention to the case for a republic in Scotland, and further recognises the breadth of interest there is in the subject throughout civic Scotland.

Supported by: Carolyn Leckie*, Frances Curran*, Tommy Sheridan*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*

*S2M-2678 Richard Lochhead: Solar One—That the Parliament notes the recent launch of the Solar One van which is propelled by biodiesel and is capable of supplying all its energy needs, both electricity and heat, from solar power; believes that this is a significant development in the renewable energy field which proves that renewable energy can play a significant role in the Scottish power supply; congratulates Dr Kerr MacGregor for coming up with this exciting and innovative idea, and calls on the Scottish Executive to prioritise research into renewable energy and to ensure that Scotland develops a diverse renewable energy programme which utilises a variety of renewable energy sources and harnesses Scotland’s potential in terms of natural and academic resources to make Scotland a leading nation in renewable energy development.

Supported by: Mr Andrew Welsh*, Brian Adam*, Stewart Stevenson*, Mr Jamie Stone*, Rob Gibson*, Mike Pringle*, Eleanor Scott*, Nora Radcliffe*

*S2M-2677 Chris Ballance: Development Aid for Oil—That the Parliament notes that developing countries with economies dependent on oil extraction display poor developmental outcomes; further notes that oil contributes to climate change which could undo decades of development efforts; is concerned that the Department for International Development (DFID) supports multilateral development aid for oil extraction in developing countries through multilateral development banks and fails to assess the impact on the poor of DFID bilateral aid for the oil industry in these countries; believes that this impedes the achievement of the Millennium Development Goals and is inconsistent with UK Government policy on climate change; calls on the UK Government to phase out all bilateral and multilateral development aid for the oil industry and to assess rigorously the poverty alleviation and sustainable development impacts of overseas development aid for the oil industry until a phase-out is implemented, and further calls on the DFID to assist the UK Government’s goals of reducing greenhouse gas emissions worldwide by proactively supporting sustainable renewable energy strategies through overseas development aid.

*S2M-2676 Mr Stewart Maxwell: World Health Organisation World Health Day—That the Parliament congratulates the World Health Organisation on World Health Day 2005: Make every mother and child count; acknowledges that the world needs to make the process of childbirth safer for both women and babies; recognises the great strides forward in maternal and child health that have taken place in the developed world in the last 50 years; regrets that the Scottish Executive intends to publish just one of the 29 indicators on the World Health Organisation’s European health for all database, and calls on the Executive to publish the Scottish figures for all the indicators to enable everyone to see where Scotland sits in the league table of maternal and infant health.

Supported by: Ms Sandra White*, Mr Kenny MacAskill*, Stewart Stevenson*, Fiona Hyslop*, Tommy Sheridan*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Chris Ballance*, Rob Gibson*, Mike Pringle*, Mr Andrew Welsh*

*S2M-2675 Christine Grahame: Scottish Achievements—That the Parliament welcomes the naming of the Central Office of Diabetes UK as Macleod House in recognition of the achievement of Professor JJR (Jack) Macleod who, with Frederick Banting, was awarded the Nobel Prize for the discovery of insulin; notes that it was this Scottish doctor who in Canada, as Professor of Physiology at the University of Toronto, provided the facilities, direction and advice and support to others which helped turn insulin into a usable treatment, saving millions of lives worldwide; further notes that a Macleod medal has been struck which is awarded to any person who has been insulin-dependent for more than 70 years and that already there are recipients of the medal; is concerned that the achievements of this Scot, who is one among many, have failed to be recognised or publicised, and urges the Scottish Executive to consult the Scottish people on placing within the Parliament a wall of fame for those compatriots, such as Professor Jack Macleod, whose achievements remain unrecognised to this day.

*S2M-2674 Mr Jack McConnell: Motion of Condolence—That the Parliament expresses its deep regret and sadness at the death of His Holiness Pope John Paul II and conveys its sincere condolences to the Vatican City State and the wider Roman Catholic community in Scotland and around the world.

S2M-2619 Margo MacDonald: Meeting the Needs and Aspirations of people in Scotland—That the Parliament agrees that its members’ primary function is to reflect and address the needs and aspirations of people in Scotland.

Supported by: Michael Matheson, Dr Jean Turner, Dennis Canavan, John Swinburne, Campbell Martin

S2M-2544# Phil Gallie: Hannah Research Institute - Loss of Home Based Scientists—That the Parliament views with concern the loss of scientists and support staff as a consequence of uncertainties over research funding currently surrounding the Hannah Research Institute near Ayr; considers that the actions of the Scottish Executive Environment and Rural Affairs Department over recent times have been unhelpful and misleading when considering the research direction that the Institute has followed; believes that the levels of expertise and knowledge available within Hannah are identical to those that the Executive seeks to recruit under its Fresh Talent initiative and retain, if its much-used phrase of "a Smart Successful Scotland" has any meaning, and considers that the Ministers for Enterprise, Health and Rural Affairs should come together to secure the future of this quality Scottish resource.

Supported by: Mr Adam Ingram, John Scott, Mr David Davidson, Murray Tosh, Alex Fergusson, Margaret Mitchell, Bill Aitken, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Mr Brian Monteith, Mrs Nanette Milne, Mr Ted Brocklebank

S2M-2482# Scott Barrie: Quality of Fife’s Train Services—That the Parliament notes the importance of the rail network to an efficient public transport system in east central Scotland; notes previous debates in the Parliament that highlighted the poor service offered to Fifers by train operators in respect of reliability and punctuality; regrets that the improvements made to the rail infrastructure in Fife, including longer platforms and increased capacity, are still not meeting the level of demand; notes that the service has failed to improve under the new franchisee; believes that the current level of train cancellation and late running is unacceptable, and further believes that First ScotRail, in particular, must ensure that it improves its performance so that a viable alternative to car usage is available.

Supported by: Christine May, Marilyn Livingstone, Helen Eadie, Tricia Marwick, Nora Radcliffe, Iain Smith, Alasdair Morgan, Richard Baker, Jackie Baillie, Maureen Macmillan, Karen Gillon, Karen Whitefield, Trish Godman, Mrs Mary Mulligan, Cathy Peattie, Elaine Smith, Bristow Muldoon
