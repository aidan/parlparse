BUSINESS BULLETIN 18/1999

Wednesday 16 June 1999

Section E –  Written questions lodged on 15 June 1999
 

S1W-80 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive when it intends to make available information relating to (a) the annual capital spending on construction of the new trunk road schemes in Scotland over the last five years, (b) all such schemes costing over £10 million, and (c) the proposed expenditure in connection with schemes over the next four years.

 

S1W-81 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to prepare practical arrangements for the implementation of the Agricultural Business Improvement Scheme.

 

S1W-82 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to implement an enhanced payment scheme under the Organic Aid Scheme and what is the timescale for such changes.

 

S1W-83 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive how it intends to progress towards moving the payment of Hill Livestock Compensatory Allowances on to an area basis.

 

S1W-84 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to publish the mission statement and the strategic and operational objectives for departments of the Scottish Administration.

 

S1W-85 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to implement any measures within its areas of responsibility to address the inequality in pay between men and women in Scotland.

 

S1W-86 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to make an estimate of the impact on employment in Scotland resulting from the implementation of the European Employment Pact.

 

S1W-87 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive for a breakdown of its plans for the individual funding allocation over the next 3 years for (a) the new Social Inclusion Partnerships in Drumchapel, Greater Pollock and Glasgow, and (b) the 21 Social Inclusion Partnerships formed from the previous Programme for Partnership Areas and Regeneration Programs.

 

S1W-88 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to be represented at the special meeting of the European Council on Employment, Economic Reform and Social Cohesion to be held in the spring of 2000.
