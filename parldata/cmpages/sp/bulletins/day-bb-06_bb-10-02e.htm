Business Bulletin No. 139/2006: Monday 2 October 2006

Section E – Written questions lodged on 29 September 2006

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-28771 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will review its position on classifying the Inverness link route as a trunk road and what the impact of such a classification would be on the Executive’s transport budget.

S2W-28772 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is aware that British Waterways has confirmed that it wishes to see a tunnel or aquaduct at Tomnahurich, rather than another swing bridge, and whether the Executive considers that a swing bridge is a suitable and appropriate option in this instance.

S2W-28773 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide a list of trunk roads on which there are swing bridges, indicating the location of each bridge.

S2W-28774 Mr John Swinney: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider implementing a fast-track procedure to introduce a ban on alcohol consumption in specific public places.

S2W-28775 Mr John Swinney: To ask the Scottish Executive what the process is for introducing a ban on alcohol consumption in specific public places.

S2W-28776 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take against NHS Ayrshire and Arran following the absconding of a restricted patient from Ailsa Hospital, Ayr, for the second time in four months.

S2W-28777 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will conduct an inquiry into the circumstances of a restricted patient absconding for a second time from Ailsa Hospital, Ayr.

S2W-28778 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether all protocols regarding restricted patients were followed in the decision to transfer Mark Biggley from the State Hospital, Carstairs, to Ailsa Hospital, Ayr.

S2W-28779 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it was advised of the decision to grant restricted patient, Mark Biggley, unescorted leave within the grounds of Ailsa Hospital, Ayr.

S2W-28780 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take to review its procedures following a restricted patient at Ailsa Hospital, Ayr, absconding twice in four months.

S2W-28781 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the impact on police resources has been of each occasion on which Mark Biggley has absconded from Ailsa Hospital, Ayr.

S2W-28782 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list the sections of each Act of the Scottish Parliament which await commencement orders.

S2W-28783Δ Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will publish proposals for the reform of the Scottish NHS and Teachers’ Pension Schemes.

S2W-28784 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the Home Office on developing proposals to prevent convicted criminals profiting from writing about their crimes.

S2W-28785 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making in preventing convicted criminals from profiting from writing books and articles in which they discuss their crimes.

S2W-28786 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there is any scope to extend the provisions of the Proceeds of Crime Act 2002 to prevent convicted criminals from making profits by publishing books about their crimes.

S2W-28787 Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to change the council tax bandings for houses built through affordable housing and shared ownership schemes.

S2W-28788 Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to celebrate the 250th anniversary of the birth in Westerkirk, Langholm, of the Scottish civil engineer, Thomas Telford, on 9 August 1757.

S2W-28789 Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are any potential sources of funding to provide drystane dyking and car parking facilities at sites of historic interest.
