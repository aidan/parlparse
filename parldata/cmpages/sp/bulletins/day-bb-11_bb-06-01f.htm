Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally printed the day after they are lodged. If an amendment is lodged to a motion then the original motion will appear alongside the amendment, along with any support lodged that day, in this section.

Each Monday, this section also contains all motions and amendments lodged the previous week that are still live and any motions and amendments that have been lodged for debate in the coming week. A motion or amendment is live if it has not been debated, withdrawn or otherwise deleted from the list.

Where a motion or amendment has been withdrawn, it is indicated in this section the day after it has been withdrawn. Support for motions and amendments received after they are lodged is shown at the end of this section the day after such support is received.

Motions and amendments can be published with various symbols:

  • Asterisks before the motion or amendment number indicate a motion or amendment published for the first time;

  • Asterisks also identify alterations to the text of a motion or amendment made since it was first published;

  • A hash symbol identifies motions eligible for debate at Members’ Business;

  • A diamond symbol identifies motions lodged for Members’ Business that have not yet attracted the required cross-party support;

  • An "R", identifies motions or amendments in which the Member who lodged it has a registrable interest.

The Parliamentary Bureau periodically deletes motions or amendments not scheduled for debate and which are over six weeks old.

A full list of current motions is available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/motions.htm.

A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/index.htm.

Any questions in relation to this section should be directed to the Chamber Desk, for which contact details are provided at the end of the Bulletin.

*S4M-00170 Kevin Stewart: Aberdeen to Baku Air Link—That the Parliament welcomes the new twice-weekly air service between Aberdeen and Baku and hopes that this new connection will lead to even greater trading links between Scotland and Azerbaijan.

*S4M-00169 Fergus Ewing: Faster and Further to Secure Scotland’s Place as the Green Energy Powerhouse of Europe—That the Parliament supports the Scottish Government’s pledge to move faster and further to secure Scotland’s place as the green energy powerhouse of Europe; agrees that the investment and job opportunities presented by the low-carbon economy represent Scotland’s greatest economic opportunity; welcomes the target to generate the equivalent of 100% of Scotland’s electricity demand from renewable sources by 2020; supports the Scottish Government’s aim to maximise the benefits for communities from renewable energy and to transform the level of opportunity for local ownership; supports demands for the release of Scotland’s Fossil Fuel Levy surplus for investment in renewable energy in Scotland; supports the campaign to locate the Green Investment Bank in Edinburgh; supports the Scottish Government’s plans for Scottish Water to become a renewable energy generator; calls on the UK Government to accept the case for the Scottish Ministers to have a greater say in the design of the Electricity Market Reform mechanisms and to subsequently outline greater powers over energy policy in the UK Energy Bill for the Scottish Ministers and the Scottish Parliament, and supports the devolution of the Crown Estate to the Scottish Parliament to ensure that Scotland’s natural assets are managed in Scotland for the benefit of all of Scotland’s people.

Supported by: John Swinney*

*S4M-00168 Shona Robison: Contribution of Sport to Scotland—That the Parliament recognises the important contribution that sport makes to Scotland’s economy, culture and international standing; welcomes the government’s commitment to increase participation in sport and physical activity, thereby creating a lasting legacy for the 2014 Commonwealth Games; notes the benefits to the physical and mental wellbeing of the Scottish people through participation in sport; acknowledges the priority given to increasing physical education in primary and secondary schools, and notes the progress being made in improving community access to sporting facilities.

Supported by: Nicola Sturgeon*

*S4M-00167 Liam McArthur: James Withers Leaving for Pastures New—That the Parliament notes that, after 12 years working for the National Farmers Union Scotland (NFUS), the last three of them as chief executive, James Withers is shortly to leave the NFUS to take up the post of chief executive at Scotland Food and Drink; recognises the key role that he has played at the NFUS during a period when Scotland’s agricultural industry has faced many difficult challenges but has still maintained its place as one of Scotland’s most important industries with a well-deserved reputation for producing quality food; believes that the NFUS’s loss is Scotland Food and Drink’s gain, and wishes James Withers well in his new post.

*S4M-00166 Hugh Henry: Renfrewshire South, Big Lottery Fund—That the Parliament commends the work of the Big Lottery Fund and Awards for All in Scotland; welcomes the recent investment through this programme to eight organisations in Renfrewshire South; notes that Linwood High School, East Renfrewshire Community Enterprise Trust Community Company, Farrier Court Sheltered Housing Tenants Association, East Renfrewshire Council education department, Paisley Veterans Bowling Association, Voluntary Action East Renfrewshire, 1st Neilston Scout Group and Linwood High School Parent Council will benefit from £37,633 of funding; considers that this money will play a significant part in meeting the Awards for All outcomes that communities are safer, stronger and healthier, and congratulates these local groups on making a successful application.

*S4M-00153 Jackie Baillie: Finding Employment in West Dunbartonshire—That the Parliament is concerned at the recent report by the TUC indicating that West Dunbartonshire has the lowest success rate in the United Kingdom for finding employment; notes that the TUC looked at the proportion of Jobseeker’s Allowance (JSA) claimants to Jobcentre Plus vacancies in every local authority and that West Dunbartonshire came out worst with over 40 JSA claimants chasing every vacancy; understands that, when a similar survey was carried out in 2005, West Dunbartonshire was ranked 38th; considers that thisshows a marked decline in employment opportunities in the area; expresses further concern at the news that the Department of Work and Pensions has announced that it is closing its Clydebank call centre with the loss of 130 jobs; believes that this will further damage employment prospects in West Dunbartonshire; calls on the Department of Work and Pensions to reverse its decision, and urges the Scottish Government to take urgent action to address what it sees as the growing problem of unemployment in West Dunbartonshire.

*S4M-00152 Patrick Harvie: Prison Visiting Committees—That the Parliament notes the recent Scottish Government consultation on independent monitoring of prisons, which raised questions about the continued operation of prison visiting committees; recognises that a review of visiting committees took place as recently as 2005 and that recommendations at that time were for a continued role for visiting committees and a number of changes to improve their impact and consistency; considers that visiting committees have a valuable role in providing independent scrutiny of conditions in prisons and examining complaints and concerns raised about the treatment of prisoners; places particular value on the frequency of unannounced visits to prisons, which are not a feature of the inspection carried out by Her Majesty’s Inspectorate of Prisons; believes that the role of visiting committees should continue in a manner that complements the inspection regime, rather than being incorporated into it, and thanks the hundreds of visiting committee members for their thousands of hours of unpaid effort and for their commitment to their work.

Supported by: Tavish Scott

*S4M-00148 Patrick Harvie: Supporting Jubilee Scotland’s Call for Debt Arbitration—That the Parliament notes with concern what it considers to be the current lack of adequate mechanisms for addressing crisis cases of sovereign debt; supports Jubilee Scotland’s belief that arbitration should be encouraged as an effective method for resolving sovereign debt disputes and that, in particular, it should be promoted as a means of settling cases of unjust or unpayable poor country debt; is of the view that, under the Arbitration (Scotland) Act 2010, Scotland possesses a modern and robust framework in which parties can seek arbitration; believes that this framework can be applied to discussions of sovereign debt just as it can to commercial and consumer disputes, and therefore calls on the Scottish Government and Scotland’s legal community to promote Scotland internationally as a seat of arbitration in such cases.
