Business Bulletin No. 173/2004

Thursday 25 November 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-2069 Chris Ballance: Broadcasting in Scotland—That the Parliament condemns the reported proposals of Ofcom to halve the amount of regional programming on ITV in favour of a greater emphasis on London-based programming and calls for the Scottish Parliament to be given responsibility for broadcasting in Scotland.

*S2M-2068 George Lyon: Over 30 Months Scheme—That the Parliament calls on the Scottish Executive and the Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs to follow the advice of the Food Standards Agency and bring to an end, with the full endorsement of the chief medical officers, the Over Thirty Months Scheme on a UK-wide basis which will lead to rapid progress on quality Scottish beef once again being available to consumers across Europe.

Supported by: Jeremy Purvis*, John Farquhar Munro*, Iain Smith*, Mr Keith Raffan*

*S2M-2067 Donald Gorrie: Timetabling of Sewel Motions—That the Parliament urges the Parliamentary Bureau to ensure that as much parliamentary and committee time is allocated to considering each Sewel motion as would have been allocated to the issue if it was being considered as normal Scottish Parliament legislation.

*S2M-2066 Brian Adam: Aberdeen Regeneration Projects—That the Parliament notes that wholesale housing stock transfer was rejected by 91% of Aberdeen City Council tenants and calls on the Scottish Executive to write off Aberdeen’s council housing debt to allow the city council to invest in regeneration projects in the city’s areas of social exclusion.

*S2M-2065 Ms Wendy Alexander: St Mirren Football Club—That the Parliament congratulates St Mirren Football Club for issuing a lifetime ban to two supporters caught making racial taunts at opposition players during a Scottish Football League (SFL) Division One fixture at St Mirren Park; recognises that St Mirren’s prompt action highlights that the club is serious about implementing the measures contained within UEFA’s 10 point action plan to combat racism in the sport; hopes that the players and staff of other SFL clubs follow in St Mirren’s footsteps for similar incidents, and welcomes remarks from the manager and chairman that St Mirren Football Club is a family-friendly club and this type of behaviour will not be tolerated.

Supported by: Mike Pringle*, Elaine Smith*, Mike Watson*, Jackie Baillie*, Richard Baker*, Mr Kenny MacAskill*, Campbell Martin*, Mr Stewart Maxwell*, Mrs Mary Mulligan*, Michael McMahon*, Mr Alasdair Morrison*, Mr Kenneth Macintosh*, Robin Harper*, Murray Tosh*, Bill Butler*, Margaret Smith*, Brian Adam*, Eleanor Scott*, Helen Eadie*

*S2M-2064 Dr Jean Turner: Doctors’ Responsibility to the Public Sector—That the Parliament acknowledges that NHS Scotland has many problems, including the retention, recruitment and training of staff; notes that these may well be made worse if the private sector is called in to reduce waiting lists; further notes that the private sector does not train doctors and nurses and that the public is often outraged that sometimes patients can see the same doctor much sooner privately than they can in the public sector; considers that this situation is unacceptable in 21st century Scotland, and calls on the Scottish Executive to take immediate steps to address it.

Supported by: Patrick Harvie*, Eleanor Scott*, Mrs Margaret Ewing*, John Swinburne*, Mike Pringle*, Tommy Sheridan*

*S2M-2063 Mr Stewart Maxwell: Scotland’s Decisions Must be Made in Scotland’s Parliament—That the Parliament regrets the continual use of Sewel motions which curtails parliamentary scrutiny on issues of great importance to the people of Scotland; recognises that the Scottish Parliament is the right forum for discussion and scrutiny for all matters devolved to it; notes that Lord Sewel, who proposed this mechanism, admitted that this convention was set up to deal solely with "minor, non-controversial" issues yet, since 1999, there have been 51 Sewel motions involving such serious issues as asylum law, higher education and the law reform bill, and looks forward to Sewel motions no longer being used as a method of avoiding parliamentary scrutiny and to a full debate and vote on issues mentioned in the recent Queen’s Speech that have particular relevance to Scotland.

*S2M-2062# Dr Jean Turner: Sensitivity to Promotion of Breastfeeding—That the Parliament acknowledges that not all mothers who desire to breastfeed their babies are able to and that some may only manage for a few hours or over a period of a few weeks at best after a great deal of pain and discomfort; understands that in no way should these mothers be made to feel guilty or feel that they have not considered what is best for their child; accepts that a few of these mothers may have succeeded in breastfeeding if there had been more time for professionals to assist them through a difficult and stressful period, and considers that sensitivity in addressing all mothers’ needs, as well as promoting breastfeeding, is essential.

Supported by: Mrs Nanette Milne*, Patrick Harvie*, David Mundell*, Mrs Margaret Ewing*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Eleanor Scott*, Mr David Davidson*, Tommy Sheridan*

S2M-2060 Destruction of Burns Monument, Kilmarnock (lodged on 23 November 2004) Robin Harper*, Rob Gibson*, Mr Ted Brocklebank*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Murdo Fraser*, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton*, John Scott*, Mr David Davidson*, Alex Neil*

*S2M-2059.1 Margaret Mitchell: Violence Against Women—As an amendment to motion (S2M-2059) in the name of Malcolm Chisholm, leave out from "affirms" to end and insert "acknowledges the significant funding that has gone into domestic abuse initiatives in the last four years; supports the Scottish Executive’s decision to set up an expert committee to support the development of a strategic approach to tackling this issue in future; welcomes the more recent work of the Executive in setting up the Violence Against Women Fund, and calls on it, in supporting Rape Crisis Scotland and the ten rape crisis centres throughout Scotland, to ensure that male victims of rape have access to these services, as part of its commitment to eradicating all forms of violence against all men, women and children in Scotland."

*S2M-2059.2 Christine Grahame: Violence Against Women—As an amendment to motion (S2M-2059) in the name of Malcolm Chisholm, insert at end "and, in particular to explore the cultural reasons underlying the recent report that one in five young men and one in ten women thought that violence against women was acceptable and to examine the crucial role that the media and entertainments industry has in compounding such attitudes, and, furth of Scotland, abhors the increased use of violence against women as a weapon of war."

Supported by: Linda Fabiani*

S2M-2057 Flying the Flag at Holyrood (lodged on 23 November 2004) Mr Jamie Stone*, Dr Elaine Murray*, Iain Smith*, Mr David Davidson*, Mike Pringle*

*S2M-2056.1 Richard Lochhead: Supermarkets and the Scottish Food Chain—As an amendment to motion (S2M-2056) in the name of Shiona Baird, insert at end "recognises that the behaviour of supermarkets can be influenced by pressure from informed consumers as well as the government; calls for a Scottish Executive investigation into each sector’s share of the profits between the plough and plate and particularly the farmer’s share of every pound spent on groceries; further calls on the Scottish Executive to convene a supermarkets’ summit to discuss ongoing concerns; recognises that the more local produce on supermarket shelves and less "food miles" the better it is for the environment and costs; supports a public procurement policy that ensures where possible food is sourced locally; notes that the beef sector’s profitability can be increased by scrapping the Over Thirty Months scheme in accordance with Food Standards Agency advice, and calls for this to happen."

*S2M-2056.2 Alex Fergusson: Supermarkets and the Scottish Food Chain—As an amendment to motion (S2M-2056) in the name of Shiona Baird, leave out from "negative" to end and insert "impact that the dominance of supermarkets can have on Scottish farmers, producers and communities; notes the Office of Fair Trading’s report that fear among suppliers of being delisted by the major supermarkets is preventing them from complaining under the current Supermarket Code of Practice; calls for an independent overseer of the code of practice who will proactively ensure that it is enforced and who will protect complainants from reprisals, and further calls for the code of practice to apply to the whole food supply chain in order to ensure fair trade for all, with particular emphasis on ensuring that the primary producer receives a fair reward for his or her product."

Supported by: Mr David Davidson*

*S2M-2056.3 Frances Curran: Supermarkets and the Scottish Food Chain—As an amendment to motion (S2M-2056) in the name of Shiona Baird, leave out from "notes the Office of Fair Trading’s report" to end and insert "calls on the Scottish Executive to integrate economic, social, health and environmental policies into food policy with the emphasis on local food and to ensure that food policy implementation is both environmentally-friendly and socially-equitable, and further calls on the Executive to ensure that more local organic suppliers provide locally grown high nutritional value foods for school meals."

*S2M-2056.4 Ross Finnie: Supermarkets and the Scottish Food Chain—As an amendment to motion (S2M-2056) in the name of Shiona Baird, leave out from first "notes" to end and insert ", while recognising that competition matters, including those relating to supermarkets and the food sector, are reserved to the UK Parliament, notes that the Office of Fair Trading is currently carrying out an independent audit of the Supermarket Code of Practice; agrees with the Executive’s objective, as set out in A Partnership for a Better Scotland: A Forward Strategy for Scottish Agriculture and Scottish Food and Drink Strategy, of supporting Scotland’s food and drinks industry to build on its reputation for high quality and its strong export record and to ensure that more Scottish produce is processed in Scotland; supports the Executive’s funding of the Scottish Agricultural Organisation Society which assists farmer co-ops, and welcomes the implementation of the Organic Action Plan and the Eating for Health action plan."

Supported by: Lewis Macdonald*
