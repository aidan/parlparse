Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally printed the day after they are lodged. If an amendment is lodged to a motion then the original motion will appear alongside the amendment, along with any support lodged that day, in this section.

Each Monday, this section also contains all motions and amendments lodged the previous week that are still live and any motions and amendments that have been lodged for debate in the coming week. A motion or amendment is live if it has not been debated, withdrawn or otherwise deleted from the list.

Where a motion or amendment has been withdrawn, it is indicated in this section the day after it has been withdrawn. Support for motions and amendments received after they are lodged is shown at the end of this section the day after such support is received.

Motions and amendments can be published with various symbols:

  • Asterisks before the motion or amendment number indicate a motion or amendment published for the first time;

  • Asterisks also identify alterations to the text of a motion or amendment made since it was first published;

  • A hash symbol identifies motions eligible for debate at Members’ Business;

  • A diamond symbol identifies motions lodged for Members’ Business that have not yet attracted the required cross-party support;

  • An "R", identifies motions or amendments in which the Member who lodged it has a registrable interest.

The Parliamentary Bureau periodically deletes motions or amendments not scheduled for debate and which are over six weeks old.

A full list of current motions is available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/motions.htm.

A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/index.htm.

Any questions in relation to this section should be directed to the Chamber Desk, for which contact details are provided at the end of the Bulletin.

New and altered motions and amendments

*S3M-8154 Bruce Crawford on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Local Electoral Administration (Scotland) Bill—That the Parliament agrees that, during Stage 3 of the Local Electoral Administration (Scotland) Bill, debate on amendment 1 shall, subject to Rule 9.8.4A, be brought to a conclusion no later than five minutes after the Stage begins (excluding any periods when other business is under consideration or when a meeting of the Parliament is suspended or otherwise not in progress).

Supported by: Jim Mather*

*S3M-8153 Ian McKee: North Sighthill Regeneration—That the Parliament welcomes the regeneration project for North Sighthill, part of City of Edinburgh Council’s £150 million 21st Century Homes programme that will see the last of Sighthill’s tower blocks demolished by the summer of 2011; considers that the demolition at North Sighthill will end an era of deprived housing, antisocial behaviour and crime and give way to a modern and vibrant community with a mixture of 320 council and private sector flats and houses; notes that the Scottish Government and City of Edinburgh Council’s investment in new homes will allow the first council houses to be built in Edinburgh after what it considers to be generations of neglect, and looks forward to the Sighthill building work, which is scheduled to commence in the second half of 2012.

Supported by: Gil Paterson*, Shirley-Anne Somerville*

*S3M-8152 Mr Frank McAveety: Architecture Award for Shettleston Housing Association—That the Parliament congratulates the Shettleston Housing Association (SHA) on winning the Civic Trust Award for 2011 for its new office building; notes that this is the second design award that the building has won in recent months, following the Royal Incorporation of Architects in Scotland best building award in November 2010; considers that the recognition that these awards bestow on the building reflects the outstanding contribution that the SHA’s new offices make to the quality and appearance of the surrounding environment, and believes that this latest award is a tribute to the hard work of everyone involved in the design and upkeep of the building.

*S3M-8151 Helen Eadie: Parliament Questions Behaviour of Minister—That the Parliament expresses its disappointment that the Minister for Housing and Communities has made an announcement about funding the Coalfields Regeneration Trust (CRT) for one year only at the current level of expenditure; understands that the minister has called on the CRT to provide three business plans with two predicated on what it considers severe cuts in funding; is concerned to learn of allegations that the minister phoned and berated a worker of a voluntary organisation in the coalmining communities; believes that such behaviour would be inappropriate, and considers that no parliamentarian of any rank should resort to it.

*S3M-8150 Mary Scanlon: Herbal Medicine Regulation—That the Parliament welcomes the announcement by the UK Secretary of State for Health on 16 February 2011 asking the Health Professions Council to establish a statutory register for practitioners supplying unlicensed herbal medicines; notes that this announcement followed discussions with health departments in the three devolved administrations to establish a unified UK-wide approach to the regulation of these practitioners; considers that the UK Government will have to have further discussions with the Scottish Parliament and the devolved institutions as it prepares draft legislation prior to the proposed implementation of this register in April 2012; welcomes that this proposal aims to ensure that the public can have access to a register of properly trained and qualified herbal practitioners who in turn will have access to all the medicines that the public wish to use, and supports further collaboration between the UK Government and the Scottish Parliament to secure a vibrant and healthy herbal sector that has potential, following regulation, to work alongside conventional medicine to provide good quality healthcare for Scottish people.

Supported by: John Lamont*, Jamie McGrigor*, Murdo Fraser*, Mike Pringle*, Nanette Milne*

*S3M-8149 Jackie Baillie: Route 81 Youth Project—That the Parliament congratulates the Route 81 Youth Project on opening Centre 81, a new community centre in Garelochhead; further congratulates the group on the active involvement of young people in the decision and planning process of developing the project; welcomes it as an inspiring example of a grassroots effort providing important amenities to the local community; acknowledges the contributions of the Big Lottery Fund, Scotland Rural Development Programme, Communities Scotland, Church of Scotland Development Fund, Climate Challenge Fund, Tudor Trust and CARES, and considers that the new centre will play a significant role in serving the needs of young people in Garelochhead and Portincaple.
