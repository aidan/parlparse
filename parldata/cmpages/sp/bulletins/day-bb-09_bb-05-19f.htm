Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (♦).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S3M-4172 Stuart McMillan: Construction Plus Partnership for Inverclyde—That the Parliament welcomes the approval by Inverclyde Council to introduce the Construction Plus Partnership, which will guarantee local training and job opportunities in Inverclyde; notes that the Construction Plus Partnership has already seen the company build the Riverside Business Centre and recruit six local groundsmen, a heating engineer and a quantity surveyor, and acknowledges that the scheme will bring much needed job opportunities for Inverclyde that are vital during these difficult economic times.

Supported by: Ross Finnie*, Alasdair Allan*, Kenneth Gibson*

*S3M-4171 David Whitton: New Stobhill Hospital—That the Parliament welcomes the opening of the new Stobhill Hospital, which will be one of the largest hospitals in Scotland, covering over 30,000 square feet and treating around 400,000 patients each year; notes that the new £100 million hospital is equipped with state-of-the-art imaging machines, advanced day surgery theatres and modern consulting rooms, and also notes that the new Stobhill Hospital is a key component of the overall modernisation of Glasgow’s acute hospitals that will see investments totalling over £750 million in the next six years.

Supported by: Ross Finnie*, Kenneth Gibson*, Robin Harper*, Sarah Boyack*, Patricia Ferguson*

*S3M-4170 Willie Coffey: Robert Burns Monument Centre, Kilmarnock—That the Parliament congratulates East Ayrshire Council on the occasion of the opening of the Robert Burns Monument Centre in Kilmarnock; recognises that the genealogy research facilities and local conference facilities and services will play a vital part in attracting visitors to the town, and applauds the addition of the interactive Kilmarnock Edition of Poems, Chiefly in the Scottish Dialect, as an innovative development in bringing Burns into a modern setting.

*S3M-4169♦ James Kelly: Social Accounting Pilot Project—That the Parliament congratulates Co-operative Development Scotland on raising awareness of the practice of social accounting following a flagship pilot project with the Social Audit Network to introduce social accounting to six co-operative and social enterprises in Scotland; commends the contribution to the project played by Highland Home Carers, Highland Wholefoods, Loch Fyne Oysters, Scotwest Credit Union, West Whitlawburn Housing Co-operative and the Wise Group, and believes that organisations that can demonstrate their wider social impact beyond financial performance will gain a competitive edge and ensure that co-operatives, social enterprises and voluntary organisations thriving in Scotland contribute to economic growth.

*S3M-4168 David Whitton: HMP Low Moss—That the Parliament congratulates the Scottish Prison Service on making the correct decision to keep the name of the new prison to be built at Low Moss in Bishopbriggs, HMP Low Moss; notes that it was only after a sustained campaign by Bishopbriggs’ residents that the Scottish Prison Service decided to look again at the working title, and looks forward to construction work beginning on the new facility, which will be a welcome boost to construction work in the area.

Supported by: Patricia Ferguson*, Robin Harper*, Claire Baker*, Dr Richard Simpson*, Bill Wilson*, Jackie Baillie*, Mary Mulligan*, Sarah Boyack*, Sandra White*, Gil Paterson*

*S3M-4167 Stuart McMillan: Royal Scots Dragoon Guards, Classical Winners—That the Parliament congratulates the pipe band of the Royal Scots Dragoon Guards for winning the Classical Brit Album of the Year 2009 with Spirit of the Glen - Journey; notes that the regimental band has the first instrumental album to win the category and that its band members are the first non-musicians to win the award; further notes that the album was recorded during the regiment’s tour of duty in Iraq, making it the first winning album to be recorded in a war-torn country, and also notes the value of regimental pipe bands in boosting the morale of troops and engaging the community both at home and when abroad.

Supported by: Robin Harper*, Christina McKelvie*, Murdo Fraser*, Alasdair Allan*, Shirley-Anne Somerville*, Rob Gibson*, Bill Kidd*, Bob Doris*, Jim Tolson*, Kenneth Gibson*, Sandra White*

*S3M-4166 Alasdair Allan: Quarriers’ Epilepsy Support—That the Parliament congratulates Quarriers for the invaluable support it provides to people with epilepsy across Scotland; supports Quarriers’ work in highlighting the misconceptions surrounding epilepsy during National Epilepsy Week, 17 to 23 May 2009; recognises the important role played by Quarriers’ fieldworkers in ensuring that people with epilepsy are motivated to achieve their full potential in their own communities; commends the work of the Quarriers’ National Epilepsy Centre for Scotland, the only facility of its kind in Scotland, and further praises Quarriers’ commitment to raising awareness of this condition and delivering care of the highest quality across Scotland.

Supported by: Bill Wilson*, Jackie Baillie*, Mary Mulligan*, Robert Brown*, Bob Doris*, Jim Tolson*, Christina McKelvie*, Stuart McMillan*, Dr Richard Simpson*, Kenneth Gibson*, Rob Gibson*, Shirley-Anne Somerville*, Patricia Ferguson*, Robin Harper*, Malcolm Chisholm*, Bill Kidd*, Jamie McGrigor*, Sandra White*, Joe FitzPatrick*, Ian McKee*, Brian Adam*, Mary Scanlon*

*S3M-4165 Liam McArthur: Scottish Renewables Festival 2009—That the Parliament looks forward to the Scottish Renewables Festival, which will run on the weekend of 13-14 June 2009 at venues across the whole of Scotland, giving the public a chance to learn more about renewable energy; notes that the festival will allow people to see wind farms, hydropower plants, marine energy demonstrations and biomass heat and power plants, as well as household and community installations of solar panels, wind turbines, heat pumps and wood fuel; believes that, with the continuing harnessing of Scotland’s rich and diverse renewable energy resources being of vital importance to our environment and economy, it is important that people get to see for themselves what renewable energy is all about, and congratulates Scottish Renewables and all other bodies involved in setting up and running this welcome new addition to Scotland’s festivals.

*S3M-4164 Ross Finnie: Welcome Retention of Low Moss Prison Name—That the Parliament welcomes the Scottish Prison Service’s decision to retain the name of HMP Low Moss in East Dunbartonshire, noting the hard work of many members of the local community who have campaigned for the retention of this name.

*S3M-4163 Stuart McMillan: Prison to be Named Low Moss—That the Parliament welcomes the news from the Scottish Prison Service that the new prison being built in East Dunbartonshire will keep the name, HMP Low Moss; notes the concerted campaign by members of the public who were against the working title, HMP Bishopbriggs, as the name for the new prison from the outset, and acknowledges that common sense has prevailed in the naming of the new prison.

Supported by: Christina McKelvie*, Sandra White*, Bill Kidd*, Robin Harper*, Bob Doris*, Stewart Maxwell*, Kenneth Gibson*, Gil Paterson*, Ian McKee*, Brian Adam*

*S3M-4162 Cathy Jamieson: Robert Burns Humanitarian Award 2009—That the Parliament congratulates Guy Willoughby, director of the Halo Trust, on being chosen to receive the Robert Burns Humanitarian Award 2009; notes that the Halo Trust is a not-for-profit organisation based in south west Scotland, which specialises in the removal of the hazardous debris of war; further notes that it has 8,000 full-time mine clearers in nine countries, with ongoing surveys into new regions; also notes that, since it was founded 21 years ago, it has destroyed over one million landmines, 10 million items of larger calibre ordnance, over 50 million bullets, over 2,800 heavy weapon systems and over 85,000 assault rifles and has cleared over 6,000 minefields, making thousands of hectares of land and roads safe; believes that this is a testament to the dedication of the staff who are working in some of the most dangerous jobs on earth, and thanks everyone who has worked with Guy Willoughby to undertake this immensely valuable work in some of the poorest countries in the world.
