BUSINESS BULLETIN 120 / 2001

Thursday 30 August 2001

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 6 September 2001

 

 

Question Time

1. Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making in analysing the success of the less favoured areas support scheme in directing financial support to the least favoured areas. (S1O-3719)

2. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to support the measures being taken by Glasgow City Council and Rangers and Celtic football clubs to combat sectarianism. (S1O-3694)

3. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is proposed to address the financial deficit at Grampian University Hospitals NHS Trust. (S1O-3687)

4. Mr Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive what contingency plans it has made for the fishing industry in the event of the cod quota being taken up before the end of 2001. (S1O-3688)

5. Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in monitoring the health effects of telecommunication masts already in place. (S1O-3681)

6. Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive how much Scottish Natural Heritage estimates that the establishment of a core path network will cost in each of the next 10 years. R (S1O-3689)

7. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to develop digital inclusion amongst older people. (S1O-3724)

8. Mr Keith Harding: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made on a New Housing Partnership strategy in Fife. (S1O-3725)

9. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1O-3606 by Ross Finnie on 21 June 2001, when data was submitted to the European Commission in relation to the restoration of the 10% cut in the nephrops quota and what response has been received. (S1O-3729)

10. Ben Wallace: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to assist Aberdeenshire Council in supporting rural single teacher primary schools. (S1O-3704)

11. Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to meet representatives of ScotRail to discuss the quality of service provided on the Edinburgh to Aberdeen route. (S1O-3699)

12. Ian Jenkins: To ask the Scottish Executive, in the light of the recent imposition of new form D notices on farms in the Scottish Borders, what biosecurity measures are in place to help open the way for a resumption of exports from Scotland. (S1O-3726)

13. Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to undertake a review of its planning policies. (S1O-3718)

14. Colin Campbell: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to increase the £200 assistance available to teachers to purchase a computer to £500. (S1O-3708)

15. Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what proposals it has to address the cost of travel on lifeline air services between the Scottish mainland and the Northern Isles. R (S1O-3728)

16. David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has received from the Highways Agency about a start date for construction of the M6 Carlisle to Guardsmill extension. (S1O-3700)

17. Mr Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it can supply further details about the arrangements for the review of Scotland’s cities. (S1O-3702)

18. Elaine Thomson: To ask the Scottish Executive how patient care can be safeguarded in Aberdeen in the light of the reported financial difficulties of Grampian University Hospitals NHS Trust. (S1O-3722)

19. Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1O-3640 by Mr Tom McCabe on 28 June 2001, on what date it last discussed the operation of the Scotland Act 1998 with Her Majesty’s Government. (S1O-3730)

20. Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to promote the use of school grounds as an education resource available to both school pupils and the community. (S1O-3707)

21. Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are any circumstances in which NHS patients can be treated abroad and, if so, what these are. (S1O-3692)

22. Mike Watson: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in relation to the provision of nursery education for three-year-olds. (S1O-3712)

23. Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent representations it has received concerning planning applications in Glasgow Kelvin constituency. (S1O-3705)

24. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Ministerial Group on Sustainable Scotland was last convened; what was discussed; when it will next meet, and what will be on the agenda. (S1O-3717)

25. Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will ensure that primary health care services receive the resources they need. (S1O-3710)

26. Mrs Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made on the review of initial teacher education since the publication of the first stage report on 9 July 2001. (S1O-3727)

27. Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to support the Angus economy. (S1O-3703)

28. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what proposals it has in relation to the provision of rehabilitation facilities for drug users. (S1O-3716)

29. Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met the Artistic Director of Scottish Ballet and whether it has any plans for future meetings. (S1O-3690)

30. Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether current provision for the treatment of anorexia nervosa within the NHS is uniformly accessible and appropriate to demand. (S1O-3685)
