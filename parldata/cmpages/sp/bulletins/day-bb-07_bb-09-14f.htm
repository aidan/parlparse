Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (♦).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S3M-475 Johann Lamont: Congratulations to the Scottish Football Team—That the Parliament congratulates the Scottish football team on its magnificent result against France and wishes the team, the manager and all Scottish supporters every success for the remaining matches to secure qualification for the European championships.

*S3M-474 Jim Tolson: Congratulations to the Scottish Team—That the Parliament offers its fullest congratulations to Alex McLeish, all the Scotland players and the backroom staff for their victory against France in the European Championship 2008 qualifier at the Parc des Princes; notes that Scotland had not beaten France in France since 1950; further notes that the goal scored by James McFadden was the first competitive goal France had conceded since Gary Caldwell’s winner against the French team last autumn; further congratulates the team for taking Scotland to top of its qualifying group, and wishes the whole squad success in its remaining European Championship 2008 qualifying games against Ukraine, Georgia and the world champions, Italy.

*S3M-473 Gil Paterson: Fire Service Open Days—That the Parliament congratulates the Fire Service and its entire staff for the continuing initiative in opening up fire stations to the general public; encourages community groups and members of the public to take the opportunity to visit their local fire service when it holds its open days where they can appreciate the varied and wide expertise that the fire service has; further supports the continuing sophisticated training required to enable the fire service to utilise the vast array of modern technology in its use, and particularly acknowledges the professional manner and courtesy shown by Fire Service personnel in its open day initiative.

*S3M-472 Rob Gibson: Removing Barriers to Clean Energy Production—That the Parliament notes with concern the findings of European practices with grid connection, reinforcement, constraint and charging of renewable energy projects, the report commissioned by Highlands and Islands Enterprise which states that energy producers in north Scotland "almost certainly" face the highest charges in Europe to connect to the national grid; believes that it is unacceptable that renewable energy producers pay up to 30 times more in charges here than similar producers in Denmark, and further asserts that if the highlands and islands are to be the renewable power house of Europe then OFGEM must reduce this cost and that Scotland must have a energy policy that removes this barrier to clean energy production immediately.

*S3M-471 Robin Harper: Independent Impartial Mediation within Scottish Systems—That the Parliament notes the visit to the Parliament of world-prominent environmental mediator Dr Peter Adler on 19 September 2007 for the event hosted by Scottish Mediation Network, RESOLVE:ASL Mediation Service and Children in Scotland; recognises that independent impartial mediation is an increasingly important skill that can be applied to a variety of areas including in the health service, the planning system, neighbour and community disputes, family conflict and workplace differences; supports the provisions of section 16 of the Education (Additional Support for Learning) (Scotland) Act 2004 which apply duties on education authorities to provide independent mediation when disputes cannot be solved at a local level, and urges the Scottish Government to recognise and actively support mediation as a means of solving problems at local, national and global level.

*S3M-470 Joe FitzPatrick: Scottish Football Success—That the Parliament congratulates the Scottish national football team on its historic victory against France in the Parc des Princes on Wednesday 12 September 2007; praises the exemplary behaviour of the estimated 20,000 Scottish fans in Paris during the day and throughout the evening, and wishes the team the best of luck for the remaining qualification matches against Ukraine, Georgia and Italy.

*S3M-469 Shirley-Anne Somerville: Waste Wise Armadale—That the Parliament congratulates the people of Armadale on their ownership and participation in the pilot waste-minimisation project, Waste Wise Armadale; notes that with Scotland’s waste increasing by over 2% per year it is encouraging to see a community reducing its waste by an average of 4.4% per fortnight; commends the community of Armadale for further demonstrating its ability to respond as an above-average 3 Rs (reduce, reuse, recycling) community, and encourages other areas in Scotland to adopt similiar local intitiatives to tackle this issue.

*S3M-468 Jackson Carlaw: EU Climb-down on Imperial Measures—That the Parliament notes with great pleasure EU Industry Commissioner, Gunter Verheugen’s, statement that "there is not now, and never will be, any requirement to drop imperial measurements"; welcomes the European Commission’s decision finally to concede defeat in its campaign to make the use of traditional British imperial measurements illegal by accepting that they can continue to be used alongside metric measurements indefinitely, and encourages the commission to go one step further by allowing British businesses that wish to trade in traditional imperial measurements alone the flexibility to do so without fear of criminal prosecution and, in so doing, illustrate the desire to turn back from its present existence as a meddlesome, over-centralised, bureaucratic super state in the making to become a collection of independent sovereign nation states that trade freely with one another and co-operate on issues of mutual interest, which is what the United Kingdom was promised when joining the European Union in the first instance.

*S3M-467 George Foulkes: Scottish Football Success—That the Parliament congratulates Alex McLeish and the rest of the Scottish football team on their historic victory over France; commends Scotland and Everton striker, James McFadden, on an excellent goal, and wishes Scotland every success in qualifying for the finals of the European championship in 2008.

S3M-434*#* Duncan McNeil: Unidentified, Unprotected—That the Parliament notes the publication on 13 August 2007 of the letters of assurance correspondence between the Scottish Executive and local authorities, including Inverclyde Council; notes that this reveals that the authorities are unable to identify, far less provide services for, children in their areas living with parental drug misuse; acknowledges that, if authorities do not know who or where these children are, they cannot provide them with the services they need and which the Executive strategy, Hidden Harm – Next Steps: Supporting Children – Working with Parents, states that they should receive at an early stage, and therefore believes that local authorities and agencies should be given every possible assistance to meet the obligations that they are currently not fulfilling before there is another needless and preventable loss of life.
