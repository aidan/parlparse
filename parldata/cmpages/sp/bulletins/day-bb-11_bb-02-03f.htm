Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally printed the day after they are lodged. If an amendment is lodged to a motion then the original motion will appear alongside the amendment, along with any support lodged that day, in this section.

Each Monday, this section also contains all motions and amendments lodged the previous week that are still live and any motions and amendments that have been lodged for debate in the coming week. A motion or amendment is live if it has not been debated, withdrawn or otherwise deleted from the list.

Where a motion or amendment has been withdrawn, it is indicated in this section the day after it has been withdrawn. Support for motions and amendments received after they are lodged is shown at the end of this section the day after such support is received.

Motions and amendments can be published with various symbols:

  • Asterisks before the motion or amendment number indicate a motion or amendment published for the first time;

  • Asterisks also identify alterations to the text of a motion or amendment made since it was first published;

  • A hash symbol identifies motions eligible for debate at Members’ Business;

  • A diamond symbol identifies motions lodged for Members’ Business that have not yet attracted the required cross-party support;

  • An "R", identifies motions or amendments in which the Member who lodged it has a registrable interest.

The Parliamentary Bureau periodically deletes motions or amendments not scheduled for debate and which are over six weeks old.

A full list of current motions is available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/motions.htm.

A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/index.htm.

Any questions in relation to this section should be directed to the Chamber Desk, for which contact details are provided at the end of the Bulletin.

New and altered motions and amendments

*S3M-7868 Mary Scanlon: The Scottish Football Reminiscence Project—That the Parliament welcomes the progress made by the Scottish Football Reminiscence Project; notes that this project aims to provide reminiscence therapy to people with Alzheimer’s and other forms of dementia; further notes that this arose after Michael White, Falkirk Football Club’s historian, discovered that talking through old football photos and programmes acted as a potent trigger for fans; notes that Aberdeen FC and the Hibernian historical trust assist with this project in north-east Scotland and in Edinburgh; welcomes that these stimuli have opened up discussions about players and games with patients and have greatly reduced levels of anger and frustration in the men and women taking part; further notes that Glasgow Caledonian University provided an academic evaluation of the project’s usefulness and concluded that "This kind of reminiscence work could make a major impact" and that, in each instance, memorabilia are catered very specifically toward the individual with everyone attending these reminiscence meetings having their own memory pack that incorporates old programmes, photos and match reports related to their specific team; considers that any initiative that helps those with degenerative illnesses to be a welcome advance for patients and their friends and families, and recognises the scale of the problem, given that there are 72,000 people with dementia in Scotland and around 7,000 people are diagnosed with the illness every year.

*S3M-7867 Kenneth Gibson: War in Congo—That the Parliament is aware of the ongoing suffering in the Democratic Republic of Congo, a nation ravaged by the effects of war since the mid-1990s; considers that, despite being regarded as a forgotten conflict, the near constant state of war and chaos over the last three decades has claimed over five and a half million lives and, as such, lays claim to the tragic status of the most deadly conflict since the Second World War; considers that the people of Congo have borne witness to a most horrific series of war, rape, torture, murder and genocide since the mass influx of over one million Hutu refugees in 1994 resulted in a spate of conflicts between Congo and Rwanda; further notes that the series of wars in Congo included the armed forces of eight different African nations, Angola, Burundi, Chad, Congo, Namibia, Rwanda, Uganda and Zimbabwe plus innumerable armed militias and rebel groups, leading many to label it Africa’s World War and the Great African War; welcomes the recent UN report on the atrocities committed by both state and non-state forces on both sides; hopes that this report is a step toward bringing the guilty parties to justice and will help Congolese society come to terms with its past and face its contemporary challenges in a manner that prevents the reoccurrence of such atrocities, and calls on the international community to focus its efforts on delivering a lasting peace in Congo.

*S3M-7864 Alasdair Allan: A Dream Comes True for the Community of South Uist—That the Parliament congratulates Sustainable Uist, Oxfam Scotland and the Lochboisdale Community Amenity Trust on their joint award of £100,000 from the People’s Postcode Lottery Dream Fund for their Climate Change Adaption and Developing Livelihoods project; considers that the community of South Uist is living at the sharp end of climate change; is of the view that, if nothing is done, there is a real danger that the island could, over time, become uninhabitable; commends the People’s Postcode Lottery for making a dream come true for the community of South Uist by giving them the resources to help themselves and mitigate the effects of climate change on their island; commends the collaborative and diverse range of approaches being taken in this project to meet the local community’s needs, and looks forward to realising the benefits that it is hoped that this project will deliver to the community of South Uist by preserving cultural heritage and promoting sustainable livelihoods.

*S3M-7863 Bill Kidd: Whiteinch Wonder—That the Parliament congratulates the Whiteinch Centre on being shortlisted for the Scottish Urban Regeneration Forum’s 2010 awards; believes that the centre gives a tremendous service to the Glasgow community in providing classes, addiction support, stress management services and money advice, alongside the healthy eating café, under-5s’ crèche and social care support and that this illustrates the huge benefits of community empowerment; notes that the Whiteinch Community Association raised £1.2 million to build and manage the community facility, is now generating 50% of its own income and will produce a report regarding the added value that the centre creates for the local community; believes that the future of community empowerment lies in the understanding and replication of such initiatives, and wishes the Whiteinch Centre continuing success in the future.

*S3M-7862 Liam McArthur: More Success for Orkney’s Traditional Music—That the Parliament notes the recent hat trick of Orcadian traditional music success with Birsay fiddler, Kristan Harvey, winning the BBC Young Traditional Musician of the Year award, Broken Strings, a seven-piece instrumental group of S3 students from Kirkwall Grammar School, winning a Danny Kyle Open Stage award at Celtic Connections and the band, Cara, which includes Orkney’s Jeana Leslie, winning the Top Group award at the Irish Music Awards; takes particular pleasure at the success of Orkney’s young musicians, and considers that this demonstrates that traditional music is not only alive and well in Orkney but has a great future as the next generation joins Orkney’s thriving traditional music scene.

*S3M-7856 Alasdair Allan: SS Politician—That the Parliament notes that 5 February 2011 will mark the 70th anniversary of the sinking of the SS Politician after it ran aground off Eriskay; notes that Eriskay and the neighbouring island of Barra were propelled into the national spotlight following the subsequent salvaging of some of its 24,000 cases of malt whisky in an incident that would go on to become famous thanks to the film, Whisky Galore!, which was inspired by the novel by Compton Mackenzie; considers that the events surrounding the SS Politician still resonate strongly in the communities of the Sound of Barra and that they have now passed into legend, and believes that the SS Politician is a vital part of the rich cultural heritage of the Western Isles.

*S3M-7855 Cathy Peattie: Silent Movies at the Hippodrome—That the Parliament welcomes the Hippodrome Festival of Silent Cinema, planned to take place at the Hippodrome, Falkirk, from 18 to 20 March 2011; notes that the Hippodrome is Scotland’s oldest purpose-built picture house, having opened on 11 March 1912, and considers that it is thus a most appropriate venue for the festival; further notes that the festival will include live musical performances, local archive films, classic and rare silent films; wishes the Hippodrome success in this and in its future activity, and looks forward to the centenary of the cinema next year.

*S3M-7854 Murdo Fraser: Mobile Alcohol Intervention Team—That the Parliament recognises the positive work that the Fife-based Mobile Alcohol Intervention Team (MAIT) achieved in its work with young people during its pilot in Kirkcaldy in 2009; congratulates the team and notes that there was a 30% reduction in police call-outs for youth disorder and antisocial behaviour during that year; looks forward to seeing similar success arising from MAIT’s recent expansion in October 2010 into south-west Fife, notably Rosyth; further notes that its multiagency partnership, including Fife Constabulary, NHS Fife, Equal Well, Community Wardens and Clued Up has, it considers, brought a wealth of experience and wisdom to the project, which seeks to provide information and an early intervention into the health of Fife’s young people, and wishes it every success in the future.

S3M-7759*#* Jackson Carlaw: Proposed Waste Incineration Plant at Loganswell—That the Parliament expresses its concern in respect of the proposal to establish a waste incineration plant at Loganswell near Newton Mearns in Eastwood covering some 29 hectares and which is anticipated to burn some 1.5 million tonnes of raw waste a year, a proposal which would arguably turn Eastwood into the ashtray of the west of Scotland; further notes the evidence of Duncan McLaren of Friends of the Earth Scotland who advised the Transport Infrastructure and Climate Change Committee on 23 November 2010 that it "would be insane to build Europe’s largest incinerator in East Renfrewshire"; *believes that further consideration of this project is needed, and expresses its support for the wider local community, which it considers is resolutely opposed to this proposed development*.
