Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an “R”.

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

* S2M-2380 Mr Andrew Welsh: The Conservation of Salmon (Esk Salmon Fishery District) Regulations 2005—That the Environment and Rural Development Committee recommends that nothing further be done under the Conservation of Salmon (Esk Salmon Fishery District) Regulations 2005 (SSI 2005/24).

* S2M-2379 Frances Curran: Keep CalMac Public—That the Parliament congratulates the RMT union on its campaign to keep Caledonian MacBrayne Ltd (CalMac) ferries in public ownership, despite the refusal of the First Minister to make a clear statement that he was committed to public ownership of our ferries; condemns the pre-emptive decision to invite tenders for the Gourock to Dunoon route and supports the CalMac workers in their legitimate right to take industrial action to defend jobs and services should they decide to take such action; congratulates Bob Crow, the RMT General Secretary, on his record of defending his members’ jobs and conditions in the face of an onslaught of privatisation of our formerly public transport systems, and condemns statements made on BBC Radio Scotland by Alasdair Morrison MSP unjustly attacking Bob Crow and accusing him of having no concern for the communities served by CalMac when it is the Scottish Executive which is showing its lack of concern for these communities by moving to privatise their vital lifeline services.

Supported by: Carolyn Leckie*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Campbell Martin*

* S2M-2378 Frances Curran: Solidarity with Glasgow Airport Firefighters—That the Parliament extends its solidarity to the striking firefighters at Glasgow Airport whose main concern is to protect the lives of passengers using the terminal building at the airport and opposes the withdrawal of a fire appliance from the terminal building.

Supported by: Carolyn Leckie*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Campbell Martin*

* S2M-2377 Mr Kenneth Macintosh: Reduce the Risk of Cancer—That the Parliament congratulates Cancer Research UK on the launch of its five-year Reduce the Risk campaign to raise awareness of the avoidable risks of cancer and the importance of early detection; recognises that one in three of us will be diagnosed with cancer at some time in our lives but that half of all cancers could be prevented by changes to lifestyle; expresses concern that, of those questioned by the charity in Scotland, 70% did not know that a diet low in fruit and vegetables could increase the chances of getting the disease, only 37% mentioned drinking less alcohol as a way of reducing risk, but almost one-third believed that living near power lines was linked to cancer despite there being no strong evidence to support this, and looks to the Scottish Executive, in the light of these findings, to make cancer prevention a priority in health improvement plans and to continue to support Cancer Research UK’s public communication campaign.

* S2M-2376# Mr John Swinney: Boundaries of the Cairngorms National Park—That the Parliament notes that the current boundaries of the Cairngorm National Park differ from those originally recommended by Scottish Natural Heritage (SNH) following extensive consultation on behalf of the Scottish Executive; notes that, as a result of these boundaries, large parts of Highland Perthshire and East Perthshire are excluded from the national park; recognises the strength of support within these areas for participation and involvement in the national park; considers that the case for designation of the Cairngorms as a World Heritage Site would be strengthened by the inclusion of the Perthshire area in the park, and considers that the Executive should conduct an early review of the boundaries of the national park and extend the current boundary to that originally proposed by SNH.

* S2M-2375 Frances Curran: The Right to Protest—That the Parliament believes that the right to peaceful protest is a fundamental right which underpins our democracy; further believes that there should be absolutely no attempt to use repressive laws to prevent or ban any peaceful demonstrations against George Bush and the G8 leaders when they meet at Gleneagles in July; considers that in a democracy we have the democratic right to challenge the policies and actions of these leaders; hopes that the Scottish Executive has no intention of using any repressive measures to prevent this, and will actively challenge any attempt to deny our civil rights.

* S2M-2374 Colin Fox: A Tribute to Martyn Bennett—That the Parliament pays tribute to Martyn Bennett, one of Scotland’s most talented musicians; recognises that his untimely death from cancer at the age of 33 leaves a vacuum in Scottish music; acknowledges that he was classically trained as a violinist at the Royal Scottish Academy of Music and Drama, was an accomplished piper and fiddler and was raised with folk music and storytelling; regards his innovative contribution to Scottish music as unique; further notes that he was responsible for evolving and progressing Scottish traditional music, fusing it together with contemporary dance music and Scottish storytelling to widen its appeal to a new young audience, and deeply regrets his passing.

S2M-2373 Trish Godman: Ferguson’s Shipyard, Port Glasgow—That the Parliament is of the view that Ferguson’s Shipyard, Port Glasgow, is one of the very best shipyards in Europe, is an important element in the local economy in an area of population decline and cannot be allowed to slip away and believes that it is of the utmost importance that the Scottish Executive does all in its power to ensure that both management and workforce are given a fair share of government contracts which, going by the yard’s excellent record, would be built on time, to cost and to the highest levels of design and construction.

S2M-2372 # Roseanna Cunningham: G8 Summit—That the Parliament recognises that the G8 Summit being held in Gleneagles, Perthshire, from 6 to 8 July 2005, offers an opportunity for Scotland to debate issues related to international aid, trade justice and debt relief; acknowledges that there will be inconvenience and disruption to many communities as a result of hosting this summit, and is of the view that such disruption can only be justified if the representatives of those countries with the greatest economic power arrive at decisions which will allow countries in the developing world to grow and prosper, at their own pace and in their own way, by the provision of appropriately-targeted aid, debt relief and a just framework of trade rules.

Supported by: Linda Fabiani, Rob Gibson

S2M-2371 Ms Wendy Alexander: Firefighters’ Dispute at Glasgow Airport—That the Parliament notes with concern the firefighters’ dispute at Glasgow International Airport which has led to fire cover currently not being provided by the in-house team; regrets that the strikers have been taken to the Court of Session for engaging in peaceful picketing, and urges both sides to get back around the table and secure a resolution to this dispute that does not compromise public safety.

Supported by: Dr Elaine Murray, Maureen Macmillan, Bill Butler, Elaine Smith, Richard Baker, Mr Frank McAveety, Jackie Baillie, Trish Godman, Mr Duncan McNeil, Mike Watson, Mr Alasdair Morrison*, Sarah Boyack*, Helen Eadie*, Kate Maclean*, Margaret Jamieson*, Bristow Muldoon*

S2M-2370 Rosie Kane: Alaska’s Arctic National Wildlife Refuge—That the Parliament recognises the international importance of all national parks; acknowledges that development in any national park can adversely affect the whole planet; agrees that the Sandford Principle should be the founding principle of all national parks and that they should be afforded maximum protection under the law, and calls on the Scottish Executive to urge the US House of Representatives Resources Committee not to vote to revive the energy bill that would allow oil drilling in Alaska’s Arctic National Wildlife Refuge.

S2M-2369 Margaret Mitchell: Recovery, Reuse and Recycling of Mobile Phones and Printer Cartridges—That the Parliament commends the work of Eurosource, a Falkirk-based company, which has become an international leader in the recovery, reuse and recycling of mobile phones and printer cartridges; welcomes its innovative approach and individual customer-tailored collection programmes which have resulted in a significant reduction in environmental damage; applauds the company’s contributions of almost £1.5 million to charities, schools and scout groups since its inception five years ago; recognises the benefits of the company’s corporate social responsibility ethos and its work with schools which helps youngsters to learn about the environment, enterprise and citizenship, and considers that, within the Scottish Executive and Scottish local authorities, everything possible should be done to encourage the recovery, reuse and recycling of mobile phones and printer cartridges.

Supported by: Robert Brown, Richard Lochhead, Rob Gibson, Michael Matheson, Alex Johnstone, Nora Radcliffe, Rosie Kane, Mr David Davidson, Roseanna Cunningham, Alex Neil, Linda Fabiani, Mrs Margaret Ewing, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Mrs Nanette Milne, Eleanor Scott, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton, Shiona Baird, Ms Sandra White, Murdo Fraser*, Patrick Harvie*, Dr Jean Turner*, Chris Ballance*, Mr Brian Monteith*

S2M-2368 Carolyn Leckie: Neo-Nazi at St Andrews—That the Parliament notes that it is barely one week from the commemoration of the 60th anniversary of the liberation of Auschwitz; believes that it is an insult to the memories of the millions who died in the Holocaust that Nick Griffin, a man proud to stand in the tradition of Hitler and the Nazis, has been invited to speak at St Andrews University debating society; condemns the unfeeling contempt for the victims of the Holocaust displayed by those who extended an invitation to this contemptible extremist, and considers that all MSPs should apply as much pressure as possible to halt this visit and obstruct it by all means possible.

S2M-2367 # Mrs Nanette Milne: Call for Action on the A90—That the Parliament notes with concern the number of road accidents on the A90 north of Aberdeen between Tipperty and Balmedie; further notes the significant number of new houses in and around the Ellon area which have added to the high volumes of traffic on this already congested road, and considers that the Scottish Executive should take action to upgrade this stretch of road as a matter of extreme urgency.

Supported by: Richard Lochhead, Ms Sandra White, Alex Johnstone, David Mundell, Mr David Davidson, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton, Margaret Mitchell, Murdo Fraser*, Stewart Stevenson*, Mr Brian Monteith*, Mr Ted Brocklebank*

S2M-2366 # Mark Ballard: Norwegian Smoking Ban Not Bad for Business—That the Parliament notes the forecast in Norway of an increase in the number of pub, bar and restaurant businesses that would face bankruptcies due to the introduction of a smoking ban in public places; notes that these fears have proved to be unfounded; notes that the number of pub, bar and restaurant businesses facing bankruptcy declined during the first seven months of the ban; believes that the introduction of a smoking ban in Scotland would not necessarily lead to increased bankruptcies in the bar, pub, hotel and restaurant industries, and congratulates Norway for achieving a reduction in adult smoking in every year since 1998.

Supported by: Mr Stewart Maxwell*

S2M-2365 Murdo Fraser: Wind Farm on Salisbury Crags—That the Parliament welcomes the proposal by Calton Hill Energy to construct a wind farm on Salisbury Crags in Edinburgh and the contribution that this proposal will make to public attitudes in the city towards onshore wind turbine developments; agrees that it makes sense to construct wind turbines close to areas of population density where most power is consumed rather than in remote and rural areas; looks forward to seeing proposals for the construction of wind turbines on the roofs of prominent Edinburgh buildings such as Edinburgh Castle, St Giles, the Balmoral Hotel and the Palace of Holyroodhouse, and believes that the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body should investigate the possibility of erecting 125-metre-high turbines on the roof of the Holyrood building.

Supported by: Alex Johnstone, Mr David Davidson, David Mundell, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton, Mr Brian Monteith*

S2M-2364 # Linda Fabiani: Civilian Peace Service—That the Parliament welcomes the Scottish Executive’s commitment of funds for international development; recognises that discussions are ongoing with aid agencies in Scotland with regard to these funds; recognises that European funding is available for peacemaking and conflict resolution functions, and expresses support for the work of the Scottish Network for a Civilian Peace Service towards the establishment of a civilian peace service with a key role in conflict prevention and resolution and post-conflict peace-building. S2M-2363 Donald Gorrie: Help the Aged’s New Year’s Resolutions—That the Parliament welcomes Help the Aged’s New Year’s resolution campaign to push to eradicate excess winter deaths in Scotland, support a vulnerable adults bill, increase benefit uptake for older people and provide widely-available chiropody services; further welcomes the resolution to reform the council tax to ensure that it is fair, and believes that this is best achieved by abolishing the council tax and replacing it with a fairer local income tax based on the ability to pay.

Supported by: Margaret Smith, Mike Rumbles, Robert Brown, Nora Radcliffe, Tommy Sheridan

S2M-2362 Tommy Sheridan: No Extradition of the NatWest Three—That the Parliament believes it is a fundamental right of any citizen to be presumed innocent until properly and legally found guilty of any crime; notes that three men, David Bermingham, Giles Darby and Gary Mulgrew, also known as the NatWest Three, have been accused of crimes of fraud within the United States of America (USA) in connection with incidents, or apparent incidents, which allegedly took place in Britain; believes that it is therefore essential that any criminal trial against these three men takes place in Britain and that the UK Government resists any attempt to extradite these men to a country where a fair trial is much less likely to take place, and therefore calls on the Home Office to refuse to extradite David Bermingham, Giles Darby and Gary Mulgrew to the USA and to ensure that any criminal prosecution is heard in a British court, consistent with the comment of Caroline Flint MP on 23 June 2003 when she said “the trial should take place in the country where the crime is alleged to have been committed”.

Supported by: Colin Fox

S2M-2359 Mr Tom McCabe: Budget (Scotland) (No.2) Bill—That the Parliament agrees that the Budget (Scotland) (No.2) Bill be passed.

S2M-2357 # Mr Alasdair Morrison: Responses to Extreme Weather Conditions in the Western Isles—That the Parliament recognises the ferocious storms which caused tragic loss of life in the Western Isles in January 2005; expresses its gratitude to those who took part in the recovery operations; notes that extensive damage was done to the infrastructure which links the islands; further notes that many homes and businesses have been seriously affected and that Comhairle nan Eilean Siar is leading a multi-agency group to establish the extent and value of the damage caused, and believes that the Scottish Executive should consider whether further assistance can be made available to communities in the Western Isles to cope with these exceptional circumstances, while also initiating an assessment of future infrastructural implications of changing weather patterns for the Western Isles.

S2M-2356 Pauline McNeill: Catholic Education Week—That the Parliament believes that the education of children is about much more than preparing them for their careers and is also about preparing them for life generally; believes that parents are the principal educators of their children, but recognises the invaluable role of all those involved in teaching and supporting Scotland’s children; notes that the week of 30 January to 6 February 2005 is Catholic Education Week which has as its theme the concept of playing a full and positive role in society; recognises the contribution of Catholic schools in Scotland’s education system and the contribution of many denominational schools in fostering a strong school ethos and in improving academic achievement; affirms the rights of all parents to choose Catholic schools for their children, and further believes that the work of all Scottish schools in improving the life chances of children is the underpinning principle of education policy.

Supported by: Michael McMahon

S2M-2348 Robin Harper: ‘Second Term, One Year On’ Report on Scottish Executive Environmental Policy—That the Parliament welcomes the independent report, Second Term, One Year On, produced by Citigate Public Affairs for Scottish Environment LINK which states that the gap between rhetoric and reality remains wide in many areas of the Scottish Executive’s approach to sustainable development and the environment; notes that, while some progress has been made in the fields of nature conservation and strategic environmental assessment and that Common Agricultural Policy reforms have produced some steps in the right direction, much remains to be done in key areas such as waste and recycling, energy and climate change, transport, air and in freshwater and marine environments, and calls on the Executive to adopt strategies such as ecological footprinting to complement strategic environmental assessment and develop an economic policy set that does not rely on growth above all else as a measure of success.

S2M-2347 Maureen Macmillan: Year of the Volunteer 2005 - Month of Youth and Children—That the Parliament acknowledges that 2005 is the Year of the Volunteer which promotes the social and personal benefits of people giving their time for others and provides information on how they can become involved with volunteering in their local communities; notes that the Year of the Volunteer 2005 is being run across the whole of the United Kingdom and is supported by the Scottish Executive, working in partnership with a range of voluntary organisations, including CSV; further notes that February is dedicated to the theme of Youth and Children; recognises the valuable role which younger volunteers play in communities across Scotland, and pays tribute to their work for people of all ages.

S2M-2343 Robin Harper: The Royal Incorporation of Architects in Scotland: Sustainable Design Accreditation—That the Parliament welcomes the launch of the RIAS Sustainable Design Accreditation Scheme on 1 February 2005 at Holyrood; notes that this is believed to be the first such scheme in the world and as such offers an opportunity for Scotland to take a global lead in sustainable building design and so reap the rewards for communities, for the environment and for Scottish entrepreneurship; further notes the pivotal role of building design in tackling fuel poverty and reducing overall energy use, thus reducing climate change emissions, and supports setting targets for improving the existing, and future, housing stock in Scotland that are both realistic and ambitious.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie

S2M-2342 Mr Brian Monteith: Failure of Norway’s Ban on Smoking in Public Places—That the Parliament recognises that Norway’s ban on smoking in all public places, introduced in 2004, has failed to reduce the incidence of smoking; recognises that, according to Statistics Norway’s annual survey of smoking practices, the number of Norwegians between the age of 16 and 74 who smoke daily, which had dropped from 29% to 26% in 2003 without a smoking ban, has dropped by only 0.3% in 2004 following the ban; recognises that the number of daily smokers among young Norwegians aged 16 to 24 actually increased, rising to 23.7% from 22.8% in 2003; acknowledges that smoking bans do not necessarily reduce the incidence of tobacco consumption, and calls on the Scottish Executive to reflect on the Norwegian example before pressing ahead with its plans for a similar total ban in Scotland.

S2M-2317 # Nora Radcliffe: Responsible Deer Management—That the Parliament notes the collaborative work on responsible deer management being undertaken in Scotland and the vital role of deer management groups and their use of the Deer Commission for Scotland’s best practice guidance; acknowledges the importance of culling deer using such guidance to avoid poor condition of deer in late winter and unnecessary death by starvation or as a consequence of inadequate habitat and shelter to support them; notes the support of the Scottish Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals in ensuring that culling is carried out professionally and humanely; recognises the contribution that sustainable deer management can make to sustaining rural development, with increased rural employment and public benefits such as habitat regeneration, enhanced biodiversity and reducing risk of road accidents; considers that deer management groups should take the issue forward constructively, implementing deer management planning and setting targets that can be monitored to ensure progress, and concludes that deer management groups should be supported by representation from wider public interests, including local community, recreational and conservation interests, which would be particularly useful during preparation and review of deer management plans.

S2M-2294 # Elaine Smith: A Better Deal for People Dealing with Cancer—That the Parliament congratulates Macmillan Cancer Relief on the success of its “A Better Deal for People Dealing with Cancer” campaign which has so far directly helped 8,000 people across the UK access advice on the benefit entitlements of cancer patients; reasserts its support for the campaign and the recent round of radio promotions targeted at areas in the United Kingdom with high cancer and mortality rates and low uptake of disability benefits, including five areas of Scotland; expresses concern that Scotland has the highest non-claimant rate (64%) amongst patients with a terminal diagnosis; acknowledges that the key aim of the campaign is to secure specialist advice for every cancer patient at diagnosis; recognises that such an objective could be best realised through partnership-working between stakeholders, such as the Benefits Agency, the NHS, local authorities and cancer charities, and considers that co-operation between the Scottish Executive and the UK Government is necessary to facilitate such change.
