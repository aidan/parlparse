Business Bulletin No. 11/2011: Monday 24 January 2011
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 21 January 2011

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-38924 John Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive on how many occasions drivers prosecuted for road traffic offences that would normally result in a ban have not received a ban in each year since 2007.

S3W-38925 Bill Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-38364 by Michael Russell on 19 January 2011, whether it will provide the information that it holds regarding the funding of Scots language education in each year since 1990.

S3W-38926 John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on the reasons for the Office of the Scottish Charity Regulator not considering a request from the Ayrshire branch of the Multiple Sclerosis Society to defer approval of the new proposed UK constitution for the society.

S3W-38927 Hugh O’Donnell: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance and regulations are in place for contractors engaged in public construction projects regarding the disposal and recycling of construction waste after construction is completed.

S3W-38928 Hugh O’Donnell: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are in place regarding the disposal of construction or refurbishment waste from Commonwealth Games venues.

S3W-38929 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make a formal representation to the House of Commons Environment, Food and Rural Affairs Committee inquiry into the common agricultural policy.

S3W-38930 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what the reasons are for its failure, as reported in The Scotsman on 20 January 2011, to make a valid submission to the House of Commons Environment, Food and Rural Affairs Committee inquiry into the common agricultural policy.

S3W-38931 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions have taken place between its officials and representatives of the House of Commons Environment, Food and Rural Affairs Committee regarding its inquiry into the common agricultural policy.

S3W-38932 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive at what point it (a) became aware of the planned inquiry into the common agricultural policy by the House of Commons Environment, Food and Rural Affairs Committee, (b) sent its submission to the inquiry and (c) was told that the submission was invalid.

S3W-38933 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has undertaken, or will undertake, a review of the proceedings that have reportedly resulted in a lack of formal representation by it to the House of Commons Environment, Food and Rural Affairs Committee inquiry into the common agricultural policy.

S3W-38934 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will establish a unit to monitor the implementation of the living wage in the public sector.

S3W-38935 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason local authority employees are excluded from its living wage commitment.

S3W-38936 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to increase the living wage for its employees on an annual basis.

S3W-38937 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it has taken to encourage living wage agreements in the private and voluntary sectors.

S3W-38938 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what private sector employers it has met to discuss the implementation of a living wage policy and when.

S3W-38939 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will set out how it is "maximising income within existing powers", as referred to Scotland’s Spending Plans and Draft Budget 2011-12.

S3W-38940 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has made an assessment of the impact on households in Scotland of the changes made by the UK Government to the housing benefit system.

S3W-38941 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has made an estimate of how many households in Scotland will be affected by the changes made by the UK Government to the housing benefit system and what proportion of households will be worse off.

S3W-38942 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the percentage increase in journeys across the Forth Road Bridge has been since the removal of tolls.

S3W-38943 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive how much additional CO2 has arisen from the increase in journeys across the Forth Road Bridge since the removal of tolls.

S3W-38944 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Universal Home Insulation Scheme will operate in 2011-12 and, if so, how much funding it will provide.

S3W-38945 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many new affordable homes planned to be built through the National Housing Trust initiative will be made available for (a) social rent and (b) owner-occupiers.

S3W-38946 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive how much it has spent on tackling poverty in (a) 2007-08, (b) 2008-09 and (c) 2009-10 and how much it plans to spend on this in its 2011-12 draft budget.

S3W-38947 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding will be made available for the Low-cost Initiative for First-Time Buyers scheme in 2011-12.

S3W-38948 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in establishing a national ecological network outside the area covered by the Central Scotland Green Network and the Glasgow and Clyde Valley Green Network.

S3W-38949 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in identifying candidate large strategic habitat restoration projects, as mentioned in paragraph 96 of the National Planning Framework for Scotland 2.

S3W-38950 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the budget allocation for the Irvine Bay regeneration area will reflect the difficulties faced in the local area.

S3W-38951 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive whether North Ayrshire Council’s allocation to the Irvine Bay regeneration budget is proportionately higher than the allocations of other local authorities to their regeneration budgets.

S3W-38952 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will address regeneration in areas of deprivation in the budget for 2011-12.

S3W-38953 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-34012 by Shona Robison on 14 June 2010, whether it will seek to hold a debate on Scotland’s National Dementia Strategy before the dissolution of the Parliament in March 2011.

S3W-38954 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason Moray Council’s grant allocation has reduced from 1.629% of the Scottish budget in 2010-11 to 1.601% in 2011-12.

S3W-38955 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding commitment it will provide to local authorities for schemes where a flood prevention order is granted.

S3W-38956 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how the funding that it provides to local authorities for flood prevention schemes compares with the 80% of eligible costs provided by the previous administration.

S3W-38957 John Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with NHS Lanarkshire regarding the removal of community X-ray facilities in Coatbridge and Cumbernauld.

S3W-38958 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what improvements were carried out to the A9 trunk road (a) from May 1979 to May 1997, (b) from May 1997 to May 2007 and (c) since May 2007; what the cost of each was expressed in today’s terms, and when each improvement was first announced.

S3W-38959 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will set out a full timetable for the dualling of the A9 between Perth and Inverness also showing construction costs.

S3W-38960 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will upgrade the A9 Moy 2+1 overtaking lane to dual carriageway status following its closure in November 2010 due to safety concerns.

S3W-38961 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive when the dual carriageway on the A9 at Killiecrankie was constructed.

S3W-38962 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the statement by the First Minister’s official spokesman reported on the BBC News website on 30 May 2007 in reference to the Edinburgh trams project that "The government intends all expenditure planned for these projects or any alterations will continue to be used for public transport projects that tackle congestion, connectivity and journey times in Edinburgh and across Scotland" accurately reflected the Scottish Executive position on the matter.

S3W-38963 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that the reported statement of a spokeswoman from Transport Scotland on 20 January 2011 that "if Parliament had thrown out the trams project, ministers would have brought forward alternative budget proposals to target early dualling of the A9" contradicts the reported statement by an official spokesman for the First Minister on 30 May 2007 that "The government intends all expenditure planned for these projects or any alterations will continue to be used for public transport projects that tackle congestion, connectivity and journey times in Edinburgh and across Scotland" and what the reasons are for its position on this matter.

S3W-38964 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the reported statement by a spokeswoman from Transport Scotland on 20 January 2011 that "if Parliament had thrown out the trams project, ministers would have brought forward alternative budget proposals to target early dualling of the A9" accurately reflects the Scottish Executive position on the matter.

S3W-38965 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive, if the reported statement by a spokeswoman from Transport Scotland on 20 January 2011 that "if Parliament had thrown out the trams project, ministers would have brought forward alternative budget proposals to target early dualling of the A9" accurately reflects the Scottish Executive position on the matter, when this position was first announced to the Parliament.

S3W-38966 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive how much has been budgeted for (a) image management, (b) advertising and (c) marketing for (i) it, (ii) its agencies and (iii) other public bodies in each of the last five years.

S3W-38967 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive how much has been spent on (a) image management, (b) advertising and (c) marketing for (i) it, (ii) its agencies and (iii) other public bodies in each of the last five years.

S3W-38968 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive how much has been budgeted for (a) image management, (b) advertising and (c) marketing for (i) it, (ii) its agencies and (iii) other public bodies for the remainder of 2010-11.

S3W-38969 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive how much has been spent on (a) image management, (b) advertising and (c) marketing for (i) it, (ii) its agencies and (iii) other public bodies in 2010-11.

S3W-38970 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) press officers, (b) communications officers, (c) strategy officers and (d) other positions with a similar remit are employed by (i) it, (ii) its agencies and (iii) other public bodies.

S3W-38971 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what the annual cost is to (a) it, (b) its agencies and (c) other public bodies of employing (i) press officers, (ii) communications officers, (iii) strategy officers and (iv) other positions with a similar remit.
