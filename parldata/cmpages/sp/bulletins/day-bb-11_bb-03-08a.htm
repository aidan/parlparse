Section A – Today’s Business

Meetings of Committees

All meetings take place in the Scottish Parliament, unless otherwise specified. Contact details for Committee Clerks are provided at the end of the Bulletin.

Equal Opportunities Committee
5th Meeting, 2011

The Committee will meet at 9.00 am in Committee Room 1

1. Subordinate legislation: The Committee will take evidence on the following draft affirmative instruments—

the Equality Act 2010 (Statutory Duties) (Scotland) Regulations 2011;

the Equality Act 2010 (Specification of Public Authorities) (Scotland) Order 2011;

from—

Jatin Haria, Executive Director, Coalition for Racial Equality and Rights;

Wladyslaw Mejka;

and then from—

Alex Neil MSP, Minister for Housing and Communities, Yvonne Strachan, Head of Equality Unit, Graeme Bryce, Policy Co-ordinator, Equality Unit, and Allison Coull, Deputy Director (Legal), Constitutional and Civil Law Division, Scottish Government.

2. Subordinate legislation: Alex Neil MSP (Minister for Housing and Communities) to move the following motions—

S3M-07858 - That the Equal Opportunities Committee recommends that the Equality Act 2010 (Statutory Duties) (Scotland) Regulations 2011 be approved;

S3M-07857 - That the Equal Opportunities Committee recommends that the Equality Act 2010 (Specification of Public Authorities) (Scotland) Order 2011 be approved.

Justice Committee
8th Meeting, 2011

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in Committee Room 2

1. Decision on taking business in private: The Committee will decide whether its consideration of a draft report on the affirmative instruments being considered later in the meeting should be taken in private at its next meeting.

2. Commissioner for Victims and Witnesses (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will take evidence on the Bill from—

David Stewart MSP;

Ruth McGill, Non-Executive Bills Unit.

3. Criminal Procedure (Legal Assistance, Detention and Appeals) (Scotland) Act 2010: The Committee will take evidence from—

Professor Alan Miller, Chair, Scottish Human Rights Commission;

John McGovern, former President, Glasgow Bar Association;

Raymond McMenamin, Member, and Alan McCreadie, Secretary, Criminal Law Committee, Law Society of Scotland;

Jodie Blackstock, Senior Legal Officer, Justice;

and will consider whether to invite a representative of the Association of Chief Police Officers in Scotland to give oral evidence at its next meeting.

4. Subordinate legislation: The Committee will take evidence on the following affirmative instruments—

the draft Advice and Assistance and Civil Legal Aid (Financial Conditions and Contributions) (Scotland) Regulations 2011;

the draft Advice and Assistance (Assistance by Way of Representation) (Scotland) Amendment (No. 2) Regulations 2011;

the draft Debt Arrangement Scheme (Interest, Fees, Penalties and Other Charges) (Scotland) Regulations 2011;

from—

Fergus Ewing MSP, Minister for Community Safety;

James How, Head of Access to Justice Team, and Fraser Gough, Scottish Government Legal Directorate, Scottish Government;

Sharon Bell, Head of Policy Development, and Claire Keggie, Head of Policy and Compliance, Accountant in Bankruptcy.

5. Subordinate legislation: Fergus Ewing MSP (Minister for Community Safety) to move the following motions—

S3M-7876—That the Justice Committee recommends that the Advice and Assistance and Civil Legal Aid (Financial Conditions and Contributions) (Scotland) Regulations 2011 be approved.

S3M-7877—That the Justice Committee recommends that the Advice and Assistance (Assistance by Way of Representation) (Scotland) Amendment (No. 2) Regulations 2011 be approved.

S3M-7883—That the Justice Committee recommends that the Debt Arrangement Scheme (Interest, Fees, Penalties and Other Charges) (Scotland) Regulations 2011 be approved.

6. Subordinate legislation: The Committee will consider the following negative instruments—

Act of Sederunt (Fees of Solicitors in the Sheriff Court) (Amendment) 2011 (SSI2011/86);

the Sale and Hire of Crossbows, Knives and certain other Articles to Children and Young Persons (Scotland) Order 2011 (SSI2011/129);

the Licensing (Local Licensing Forum) (Scotland) Order 2011 (SSI2011/130);

the Advice and Assistance and Civil Legal Aid (Special Urgency and Property Recovered or Preserved) (Scotland) Regulations 2011 (SSI2011/134);

the Criminal Legal Aid (Scotland) (Fees) Amendment Regulations 2011 (SSI2011/135);

the Bankruptcy Fees (Scotland) Amendment Regulations 2011 (SSI2011/142);

the Disclosure (Persons engaged in the Investigation and Reporting of Crime or Sudden Deaths) (Scotland) Regulations 2011 (SSI2011/146);

and will consider whether to invite oral evidence on the Criminal Legal Aid (Fixed Payments) (Scotland) Amendment Regulations 2011 (SSI2011/162) at its next meeting.

7. Subordinate legislation (in private): The Committee will consider a draft report on the Legal Aid and Advice and Assistance (Solicitors’ Travel Fees) (Scotland) Regulations 2011 (SSI2011/41) and five affirmative instruments, all considered at its meeting on 1 March 2011.

8. Long Leases (Scotland) Bill (in private): The Committee will consider a revised draft Stage 1 report.

9. Legacy paper (in private): The Committee will consider a draft report reflecting on its work during the current session and suggesting priorities for Session 4.
