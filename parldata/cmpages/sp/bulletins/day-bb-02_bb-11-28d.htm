Business Bulletin No. 174/2002

Thursday 28 November 2002

Section D : Oral Questions selected for answer on 5 December 2002

  

Question Time

1. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many children under the age of six have been prescribed the drug ritalin. (S1O-6033)

2. Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make representations to the European Commission regarding the loss of Objective 1 status for the Highlands and Islands as a result of any miscalculation of figures by the Office for National Statistics. (S1O-6037)

3. Elaine Thomson: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on developing a "joint future" approach to the delivery of community care services. (S1O-6050)

4. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made on the roll-out of NHS 24. (S1O-6026)

5. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what non-Executive funding has been received to support the bid for the European Football Championships in 2008. (S1O-6043)

6. Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what its estimate is of how much the Highlands and Islands will receive in regional aid from the EU from 2006 to 2010. (S1O-6018)

7. Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made on improving transport infrastructure in Ayrshire. (S1O-6016)

8. Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are being taken to protect the haddock fishing and Arbroath smokie industries. (S1O-6041)

9. Ms Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has held any discussions with, or issued any guidelines to, NHS trusts regarding the inclusion in public private partnership agreements of hospital ancillary facilities such as parking, television and telephones. (S1O-6013)

10. Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has for the future operation of livestock auction markets in the light of last year's outbreak of foot-and-mouth disease. (S1O-6021)

11. Dorothy-Grace Elder: To ask the Scottish Executive how many convicted sex offenders are receiving treatment and how many such offenders that have been released have had treatment. (S1O-6066)

12. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what concerns it has in respect of the delivery of general dental services in the Scottish Borders. (S1O-6029)

13. Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to meet its stated recycling targets. (S1O-6062)

14. Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is helping asylum seekers integrate into communities. (S1O-6049)

15. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will give an update on its position on the firefighters’ dispute. (S1O-6060)

16. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans there are to meet any need for investment in public services and infrastructure in areas where the population is increasing. (S1O-6032)

17. Mr Tom McCabe: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to alleviate child poverty in social inclusion partnership areas. (S1O-6063)

18. Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what arrangements are in place for monitoring the quality of educational provision in the pre-fives sector. (S1O-6048)

19. Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive what efforts are being made to increase the amount of affordable owner-occupied and rented housing. (S1O-6028)

20. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to take forward its consultation on governance and accountability in the further education sector. (S1O-6031)

21. Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has a target for Scotland's share of the UK economy. (S1O-6064)

22. Elaine Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what arrangements it has to meet ministers from other parts of the UK to discuss tourism issues. (S1O-6044)

23. Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on integrated inspections of early years provision. (S1O-6053)

24. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what initiatives there are to support the educational attainment of looked-after children. (S1O-6051)

25. Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive what level of annual pay it considers reasonable for a firefighter. (S1O-6046)

26. Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to promote literacy and numeracy in primary schools. (S1O-6020)

27. Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many young students’ bursaries have been awarded since they were introduced. (S1O-6025)

28. Bristow Muldoon: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in increasing the amount of rolling stock available for the passenger rail franchise. (S1O-6047)

29. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many responses it received to its consultation on the review of area tourist boards and when it expects to announce its conclusions. (S1O-6042)

30. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on securing a future for fishing communities. (S1O-6027)
