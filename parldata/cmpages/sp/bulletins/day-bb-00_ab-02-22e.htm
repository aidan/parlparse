BUSINESS BULLETIN 32 / 2000

Tuesday 22 February 2000

Section E - Written questions lodged on 21 February 2000

 

 

S1W-4576 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-2316 by Susan Deacon on 25 November 1999, in what way the cost to health boards of care provided under the negotiated reciprocal agreement is recognised within the total resources allocated to each health board each year.

 

S1W-4577 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to encourage the development of a Scottish and international centre of excellence of ECMO treatment at Yorkhill Hospital in Glasgow.

 

S1W-4578 Ms Margo MacDonald: To ask the Presiding Officer, if penalty clauses are invoked by the contractors in the event of the feasibility studies being undertaken on the Holyrood Project indicating the unsuitability of the site for the construction of the amended design and volume of the Parliamentary complex, whether the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body or Her Majesty's Government as the original client would be liable for any costs.

 

S1W-4579 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to provide specialist units, similar to those proposed by the Secretary of State for Health, for elderly patients between general hospital and residential care.

 

S1W-4580 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of beds in the NHS were occupied by people aged over (a) 65 and (b) 75, in each of the last five years.

 

S1W-4581 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what its policy is regarding the appropriate number of NHS beds needed to respond adequately to demand.

 

S1W-4582 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to audit the impact on healthcare provision of variations in the number of NHS beds over the last three years.

 

S1W-4583 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will detail the number of NHS beds by (a) speciality and (b) health board in each of the last three years.

 

S1W-4584 Allan Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are any proposals to review the Local Authorities’ Traffic Orders (Procedure)(Scotland) Regulations 1999 (No.614 S.38) to facilitate local authorities introducing Traffic Regulation Orders timeously.

 

S1W-4585 Mr Murray Tosh: To ask the Scottish Executive why East Lothian Council did not receive a copy of its consultation paper on the Landfill Directive; what checks were made to ensure that all local authorities had received the consultation papers; which local authorities requested a time extension to enable them to respond to the consultation paper, and which local authorities were given such an extension.

 

S1W-4586 Ms Margo MacDonald: To ask the Presiding Officer whether the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body will arrange for an independent survey to be carried out on the state of repair of the structure of Queensberry House, in particular its foundations, by a person recognised as being expert in the preservation and restoration of listed buildings such as Queensberry House.

 

S1W-4587 Mr John Swinney: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list the policy commitments it has made which involve money from the New Opportunities Fund specifying, in each case, the exact amount involved.

 

S1W-4588 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive what was the spending in actual and real terms on the NHS dental budget in 1997-98, 1998-99 and to date in 1999-2000.

 

S1W-4589 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive what proportion of the NHS dental budget was raised by dental charges in 1997-98, 1998-99 and to date in 1999-2000.

 

S1W-4590 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive why the Ombudsman for Housing Associations has retired, what plans have been made for a replacement and what arrangements have been made for public scrutiny of the appointment.
