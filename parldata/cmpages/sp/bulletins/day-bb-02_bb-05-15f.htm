*S1M-3106 Malcolm Chisholm: Investment and Reform in Health and Community Care—That the Parliament welcomes the Scottish Executive’s commitment to sustained investment in health; agrees that investment must be accompanied by reform that is focused on the needs and expectations of patients and service users; acknowledges the progress that has already been made by the National Waiting Times Unit and looks forward to further improvements; supports a collaborative approach to reform which involves patients, staff and the wider public; welcomes the priority attached to dealing with delayed discharge and hospital-acquired infection, and believes that improving health and tackling health inequalities in both urban and rural Scotland should be central features of the reform agenda.

Supported by: Mrs Mary Mulligan*, Mr Frank McAveety*

*S1M-3105 Ross Finnie: National Waste Strategy—That the Parliament supports the Scottish Executive’s commitment to sustainable waste management; commends the progress being made towards the establishment of a National Waste Plan by partnership working between the Executive, local government, the Scottish Environment Protection Agency and other stakeholders; welcomes the preparation of draft area waste plans to establish the best practicable environmental options for municipal waste management, and notes that all have a part to play in the reduction and better management of Scotland’s waste.

Supported by: Allan Wilson*

*S1M-3104# Ms Sandra White: Community Police Officers—That the Parliament recognises the extremely effective role played by community police officers in combating crime, particularly youth crime; notes that such officers are often the most visible and recognisable police presence in their communities and recognises the crucial role that they play in providing high quality policing and fostering links with the communities they serve; regrets that these officers are often removed from their vital community role to perform other police duties, and therefore believes that the Scottish Executive should encourage community policing and provide the necessary resources to ensure the provision of adequate numbers of community police officers.

*S1M-3103 Mr Jack McConnell: The Golden Jubilee—That the Parliament congratulates Her Majesty the Queen on the occasion of Her Golden Jubilee; expresses its gratitude for Her Majesty’s outstanding public service and steadfast dedication to duty over half a century of immense change; affirms the respect that is held for Her Majesty the Queen in Scotland, and looks forward with anticipation to the continuation of that long and close association on the occasion of Her Majesty’s Golden Jubilee tour of Scotland later this month and Her visit to the Parliament in Aberdeen on 28 May.

Supported by: Mr John Swinney*, David McLetchie*, Mr Jim Wallace*

*S1M-3102 Mr Andy Kerr: Debt Arrangement and Attachment (Scotland) Bill: Financial Resolution—That the Parliament, for the purposes of any Act of the Scottish Parliament resulting from the Debt Arrangement and Attachment (Scotland) Bill, agrees to any increase in expenditure payable out of the Scottish Consolidated Fund in Consequence of the Act.

Supported by: Mr Jim Wallace*, Dr Richard Simpson*, Peter Peacock*

*S1M-3101# Bill Butler: Local Authority Funding—That the Parliament commends the initiative of Glasgow City, Argyll and Bute, Dundee City, Inverclyde, Midlothian and West Dunbartonshire councils in commissioning a special report from Professor Arthur Midwinter, Grant Distribution and the Mismatch Effect: An Assessment of its Impact on Council since Reorganisation, which concludes that there is "clear evidence of the erosion of grant income" between 1996-97 and 2001-02 in these local authority areas; acknowledges the report’s view that the flawed reorganisation of local government ordered by the last Conservative government is the root cause of a situation where, in these six local authority areas in the period, government grants fell by 4.8% in real terms compared to an average drop of 1.2% across Scotland’s councils and council tax rose by 20% in real terms compared to an average of 16%, and believes that the Scottish Executive should seriously consider accepting the central recommendation of the report, namely that it should provide a supplementary grant of £50 million to redress this historical mismatch of funding: £32 million to Glasgow, £5 million to Dundee, £3.4 million each to Midlothian and Argyll and Bute and £2.5 million each to West Dunbartonshire and Inverclyde respectively, such funding arising from a more equitable distribution of grant increases in the future.
