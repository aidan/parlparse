Business Bulletin No. 120/2008: Wednesday 3 September 2008
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 2 September 2008

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-15910 Hugh O’Donnell: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will strengthen support for parents and carers in each community, including through universal health visitor provision.

S3W-15911 Hugh O’Donnell: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to design services to meet the needs of the most vulnerable families.

S3W-15912 Hugh O’Donnell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider, in partnership with COSLA, providing services for parents of children aged nine to 18 as a follow-on to the early years framework.

S3W-15913 Hugh O’Donnell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will review best practice around emotional and social support for parents, including strengthening the role of ParentLine Scotland.

S3W-15914 Hugh O’Donnell: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will continue prioritising the policy of ending child poverty by 2020, including seeking from Her Majesty’s Government an increase in child benefit and increasing the provision of high quality and affordable childcare.

S3W-15915 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what is being done to ensure that the levy paid by the legal profession to fund the Scottish Legal Complaints Commission represents value for money and what recourse is available to the profession should complaints arise about the use of these funds.

S3W-15916 Margaret Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive how many nurse consultant posts specialising in neurology there are, broken down by NHS board.

S3W-15917 Margaret Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive how many multiple sclerosis specialist (a) physiotherapist, (b) psychiatrist and (c) occupational therapist posts there are, broken down by NHS board.

S3W-15918 John Park: To ask the Scottish Executive what investment it is making in skills and training in the childcare and adult care sectors.

S3W-15919 John Park: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to improve the quality of training and education available to the childcare and adult care sectors.

S3W-15920 John Park: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is supporting the development of the social care workforce.

S3W-15921 John Park: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has carried out, or intends to carry out, an impact assessment of the effect on the social care sector of the redistribution of Skills Development Scotland funding for adult apprenticeships.

S3W-15922 John Park: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria must be met for independent training providers to access Skills Development Scotland funding.

S3W-15923 John Park: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has plans to review the criteria for training providers accessing ILA 500 funding.

S3W-15924 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list all air ambulance flights undertaken by coastguard or military helicopters involving patients picked up in Orkney, also detailing the (a) helicopter used, (b) patient pick-up point, (c) flight destination and (d) reason for not using a Scottish Air Ambulance Service contracted aircraft, since the start of 2008.

S3W-15925 Alison McInnes: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-14241 by Stewart Stevenson on 2 July 2008, whether Transport Scotland has now delivered its initial findings from the strategic transport projects review to ministers.

S3W-15926 Jim Tolson: To ask the Scottish Executive which local authorities have prudential borrowing capacity to build social housing and how many homes each could deliver through prudential borrowing in the next 10 years.

S3W-15929 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken to ensure that there is a sustainable network of petrol stations in rural areas.

S3W-15930 Bashir Ahmad: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on how many people were stopped under Schedule 7 of the Terrorism Act 2000 in (a) Glasgow, (b) Prestwick and (c) Edinburgh airports in 2007, also broken down by ethnicity.

S3W-15931 Bashir Ahmad: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on how many people have been stopped under Schedule 7 of the Terrorism Act 2000 in (a) Glasgow, (b) Prestwick and (c) Edinburgh airports in 2008, also broken down by ethnicity.

S3W-15932 Jackson Carlaw: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people aged 65 or over have moved from south of the border to settle in Scotland in each year since 1999.

S3W-15933 Jackson Carlaw: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people have left (a) Scotland to live elsewhere in the United Kingdom and (b) another constituent part of the UK to live in Scotland in each year since 1999.
