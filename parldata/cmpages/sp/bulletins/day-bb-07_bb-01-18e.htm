Business Bulletin No. 9/2007: Thursday 18 January 2007
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 17 January 2007

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-31093 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any Scottish Ambulance Service rapid response units attending Category A calls are staffed solely by a paramedic or a technician.

S2W-31094 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any Scottish Ambulance Service rapid response units attending Category B calls are staffed solely by a paramedic or a technician.

S2W-31095 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether a Scottish Ambulance Service rapid response unit would ever be staffed solely by a probationary ambulance technician.

S2W-31096 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether a Scottish Ambulance Service rapid response unit would ever be staffed solely by a probationary paramedic.

S2W-31097 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many back-up ambulances have been called to assist rapid response units attending Category A calls in each of the last five years.

S2W-31098 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many back-up ambulances have been called to assist rapid response units attending Category B calls in each of the last five years.

S2W-31099 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many ambulance staff have reported incidents of assault while carrying out their duties in single-staffed ambulances or rapid response units in each year since 1999.

S2W-31100 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive how long the training period is for an ambulance (a) technician and (b) paramedic.

S2W-31101 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive how long the probationary period is for an ambulance (a) technician and (b) paramedic.

S2W-31102 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there is a separate budget for Scottish Ambulance Service rapid response units and, if so, what that budget has been in each year since 2003.

S2W-31103 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of all ambulances sent out as a result of Category A calls are staffed by one ambulance technician or paramedic.

S2W-31104 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether mechanics employed by the Scottish Ambulance Service to service and repair ambulances and rapid response vehicles also service and repair vehicles of NHS boards or private individuals.

S2W-31105 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Scottish Ambulance Service’s target of reaching 75% of Category A calls within eight minutes is being met.

S2W-31106 Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made on twin tracking the railway line between Annan and Gretna.

S2W-31107 Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations have been received by Transport Scotland regarding the provision of a new rail halt at Eastriggs in Dumfriesshire.

S2W-31108 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether ministers are proposing that renumeration for some leaders of local authorities, including Moray Council, will be lower than that proposed for chairs of joint boards, such as Grampian Valuation Board, that have smaller budgets and numbers of employees and, if so, what the justification is for these proposals.

S2W-31109 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide a breakdown of the modes of transport used by ministers on official business in each year since 1999.

S2W-31110 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what modes of transport were used by the First Minister for his visit to Inverness and the surrounding area on 15 January 2007.

S2W-31111 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive on how many occasions ministers have travelled by rail on official business in each year since 1999.

S2W-31112 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive on how many occasions ministers have travelled in official cars on official business in each year since 1999.

S2W-31113 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive on how many occasions ministers have travelled by air on official business in each year since 1999.

S2W-31114 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list any examples of developers who have applied for, and received, the right to pay a commuted sum, calculated by the district valuer, in lieu of building an agreed percentage of affordable homes in a permitted housing development, broken down by local authority area.

S2W-31115 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the (a) longest, (b) shortest and (c) average waiting times were for persons seeking (i) one, (ii) two, (iii) three and (iv) four-bedroom local authority homes in each of the last five years, broken down by authority, and which local authorities had the (A) longest and (B) shortest waiting times.

S2W-31116 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many vacant local authority houses there were in each of the last five years, broken down by authority.

S2W-31117 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prosecutions there were for antisocial behaviour in each of the last five years, broken down by local authority area.

S2W-31118 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) prosecutions there were and (b) fixed penalty notices were issued for (i) dog fouling and (ii) dropping litter in each of the last three years, broken down by police force area.

S2W-31119 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people have reported being victims of identity fraud in each of the last five years, broken down by police force.

S2W-31120 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prisoners have absconded from custody in each of the last 10 years and, of these, how many have not been caught.

S2W-31121 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive to what extent applications for funding under the INCREASE waste strategy programme have exceeded the funds available, showing the numbers of successful and unsuccessful applications and the amounts involved in each case, broken down by local authority.

S2W-31122 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive to what extent the (a) Strategic Waste Fund and (b) INCREASE programme have been oversubscribed in each year since their introduction, broken down by local authority and also showing year-on-year budgets, percentage changes and national totals.

S2W-31123 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how much has been allocated to each local authority from the (a) Strategic Waste Fund and (b) INCREASE programme in each year since their introduction, also showing year-on-year percentage changes and national totals.

S2W-31124 Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive how the proposed relocation of the Royal Highland and Agricultural Society of Scotland’s Highland Showground from Ingliston to Norton Mains will be paid for and whether there will be any cost to Scottish taxpayers.

S2W-31125 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will place copies of the submissions made to its consultation on coastal and marine national parks in the Scottish Parliament Information Centre and whether it plans to prepare an analysis of these submissions showing the name, address and position of each of the respondents and a brief summary of their views.

S2W-31126 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will publish all advice received in connection with the proposal that a local referendum be held in relation to the proposed designation of any part of Scotland as a coastal and marine national park.

S2W-31127 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2O-11639 by Ross Finnie on 11 January 2007 (Official Report c. 31009), whether it anticipates that a decision on the location of the first coastal and marine national park will be taken before dissolution of the Parliament; whether it will publish all advice received in connection with the timing of any such decision or decisions, and whether it has given consideration to whether such a decision should be taken after the Scottish parliamentary elections.

S2W-31128 Colin Fox: To ask the Scottish Executive how many police officers from each constabulary have so far been sent to cover the year-long "Faslane 365" protest at the Faslane naval base.

S2W-31129 Miss Annabel Goldie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many foreign nationals who are currently held in Scottish prisons are eligible for deportation on release.

S2W-31130 Ms Maureen Watt: To ask the Scottish Executive how many women have been charged with soliciting in each of the last five years, broken down by police board area and showing year-on-year percentage changes.

S2W-31131 Ms Maureen Watt: To ask the Scottish Executive how the value of public procurement by Scottish public sector organisations and its impact on the economy is measured.

S2W-31132 Ms Maureen Watt: To ask the Scottish Executive what the total value of public procurement by Scottish public sector organisations was in each of the last five years, also showing year-on-year percentage changes.

S2W-31133 Ms Maureen Watt: To ask the Scottish Executive what proportion of public sector contracts were awarded to companies based in Scotland in each of the last five years and what the total value of these contracts was in each year, also showing year-on-year percentage changes.

S2W-31134 Ms Maureen Watt: To ask the Scottish Executive how many small and medium-sized enterprises based in Scotland were awarded public sector contracts in each of the last five years.

S2W-31135 Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will replace the £200,000 funding of Operation Pentameter, the joint police operation against human trafficking, which was withdrawn by Reflex, a serious crime law enforcement taskforce funded by the Home Office.

S2W-31136 Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive what advice the Crown Office and Procurator Fiscal Service has given to prosecutors regarding human trafficking cases where women have been forced into prostitution and whether such advice includes charging men who pay for sex with such women with rape.

S2W-31137 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive when the National Licensing Forum last met and what issues were discussed.

S2W-31138 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action has been taken by the National Licensing Forum in the last six months.

S2W-31139 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to allow patients and prescribers access to orphan drugs.

S2W-31140 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive when prescribers will be able to prescribe lenalidomide to patients who suffer from multiple myeloma.

S2W-31141 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any NHS employees are exempt from the pay agreements, structures or pay scales agreed as part of Agenda for Change and, if so, what roles these employees fill.

S2W-31142 Derek Brownlee: To ask the Scottish Executive how many applications for exemption from the notification procedure for marches have been received since the publication of Review of Marches and Parades in Scotland - Guidance for Scottish Local Authorities in December 2006; from which local authorities and on what date each application was (a) received and (b) determined, and what the decision was in each case.
