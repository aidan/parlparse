Business Bulletin No. 168/2004

Thursday 18 November 2004

Section D : Oral questions selected for answer on 25 November 2004
  

Question Time

Enterprise, Lifelong Learning and Transport

1. Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to encourage the transfer of more freight from road to rail. (S2O-4211)

2. George Lyon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide an update on progress being made on the Campbeltown to Ballycastle ferry. (S2O-4185)

3. Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will set a target for economic growth for Scotland. (S2O-4203)

4. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what the value is to the economy of contemporary music. (S2O-4272)

5. Mr Mark Ruskell: To ask the Scottish Executive what response the Enterprise, Transport and Lifelong Learning department has received to the recently refreshed A Smart, Successful Scotland from non-governmental organisations dealing with environmental issues. (S2O-4262)

6. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the City of Edinburgh Council regarding the reopening of the Edinburgh south suburban railway. (S2O-4176)

7. Chris Ballance: To ask the Scottish Executive why priority has been given to the Edinburgh airport railway link over schemes such as the Borders railway. (S2O-4258)

8. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to support community transport schemes that ensure access to health services. (S2O-4261)

9. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to support biofuel production as a renewable energy source. (S2O-4215)

10. Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive when it intends to publish the findings of the public inquiry into the proposals for the A96 Fochabers to Mosstodloch bypass. (S2O-4202)

11. Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with First ScotRail to improve the frequency of services and the amount of rolling stock available on the Prestwick International Airport to Glasgow Central route in order to reduce overcrowding. (S2O-4183)

12. Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to improve road safety on the A82. (S2O-4187)

Justice and Law Officers

1. David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many reports have been made to the Procurator Fiscal under the New Roads and Street Works Act 1991 and how many prosecutions have followed. (S2O-4240)

2. Mark Ballard: To ask the Scottish Executive how the criminal justice system protects lesbian, gay, bisexual and transgender young people from being victims of abuse within domestic settings. (S2O-4291)

3. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied with the performance of Disclosure Scotland. (S2O-4177)

4. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will respond to the consultation on fire control rooms in Scotland. (S2O-4248)

5. Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is monitoring the changes resulting from the Vulnerable Witnesses (Scotland) Act 2004. (S2O-4282)

6. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to tackle drug and alcohol abuse by young people. (S2O-4267)

7. Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive what research is being undertaken into the issue of access by prisoners to mobile phones. (S2O-4239)

8. Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment has been made of the pilot scheme to pay special constables in Grampian and Tayside. (S2O-4226)

9. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many applicants making challenges under the European Convention on Human Rights have been funded through legal aid. (S2O-4212)

10. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will issue a detailed response to Her Majesty’s Chief Inspector of Prison’s report into Aberdeen’s Craiginches Prison. (S2O-4233)

11. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to complete the review of marches and parades. (S2O-4236)

12. Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in establishing a system of police warnings for young offenders. (S2O-4253)

General

1. Mr Frank McAveety: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions have taken place with teachers, headteachers and their trade unions regarding the implementation of Ambitious, Excellent Schools. (S2O-4251)

2. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied with its progress in taking forward education for sustainable development in schools. (S2O-4285)

3. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to reach a conclusion on the implication of the Van de Walle case in relation to contaminated land. (S2O-4286)

4. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive which areas will be selected for the Care Commission pilot studies of the regulation and inspection of foster care. (S2O-4193)

5. Christine May: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will take forward its review of planning guidelines for renewable developments. (S2O-4270)

6. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will accelerate the installation of free central heating systems for the most vulnerable people. (S2O-4268)

7. Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are being taken to increase the availability of affordable housing in rural areas. (S2O-4264)

8. Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive what its policy is with regard to the suitability of septic tanks for new housing developments in cases where restrictions imposed by Scottish Water prevent connection to the mains sewer. (S2O-4238)

9. Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will raise standards of educational attainment in schools. (S2O-4246)

10. George Lyon: To ask the Scottish Executive what action Scottish Water has taken to ensure that waste treatment systems in Campbeltown and Inveraray are functioning and whether it has carried out a review of new waste water treatment proposals in Argyll to ensure that any mistakes in Campbeltown and Inveraray are not repeated. (S2O-4277)

11. Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will support the replacement of the Royal Scottish Agricultural Benevolent Institution scheme for recycling farm plastics. (S2O-4225)

12. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive why it has not allocated funding for the Scottish Centre for Learning Disabilities in the budget for 2006-07. (S2O-4275)

13. Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what it considers to be best practice in the delivery of mental health services. (S2O-4266)

14. Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in reducing class sizes to 20 at S1 and S2 for mathematics and English. (S2O-4219)

15. Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it supports the introduction of any super casinos in Scotland. (S2O-4242)
