Business Bulletin No. 179/2004

Monday 6th December 2004

Section E : Written questions lodged on 2 December 2004

  

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-12760 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it reviews the effectiveness of its whistle-blowing policy.

S2W-12761 Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there is a national database of children in Scotland who are at risk of significant harm, as defined in the Children (Scotland) Act 1995.

S2W-12762 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many deaths have been caused by the (a) consumption of animal products, except those caused by contaminated products, and (b) use of prescription drugs in (i) 2000, (ii) 2001, (iii) 2002 and (iv) 2003.

S2W-12763 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has examined the way in which the National Assembly for Wales deals with the issue of whistle-blowing.

S2W-12764 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many frauds have been discovered within its operations in each year since 1999 and, of these, what percentage was discovered as result of whistle-blowing.

S2W-12765 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will initiate a Scotland-wide assessment of renewable potential, taking into account the need to avoid damage to sensitive environmental areas.

S2W-12766 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will conduct a Scotland-wide assessment of transmission improvements to facilitate the strategic development of renewable energy.

S2W-12767 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has carried out any evaluation of the product, Envirocare, regarding its effectiveness in combating MRSA infections in hospitals and medical premises.

S2W-12768 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether responsibility for the maintenance of Kinloch Castle rests with its owners, Scottish Natural Heritage, (SNH) and, if so, whether SNH has discharged that responsibility and how much has been spent on (a) repairs and maintenance and (b) other work at Kinloch Castle in each year since the inception of SNH.

S2W-12769 Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether English has legal status as an official language of Scotland and, if so, what legislation confers this status.

S2W-12770 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to provide opportunities for wider civic society to identify and articulate priorities and to contribute to the formulation of the future government policy agenda.

S2W-12771 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will respond to requests by Coeliac UK for coeliac patient care to be moved into the primary care sector to make more use of community pharmacists and repeat prescriptions.

S2W-12772 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will ensure that it has a stockpile of vaccinia immune globulin sufficient to conduct a mass vaccination should an outbreak of smallpox occur.

S2W-12773 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to reduce further the number of health based non-departmental public bodies.

S2W-12774 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many board places were lost when NHS trusts were incorporated into NHS boards, broken down by NHS board.

S2W-12775 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list the savings expected in health care following the publication of Building a Better Scotland: Efficient Government – Securing Efficiency, Effectiveness and Productivity.

S2W-12776 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many jobs will be lost within the NHS in (a) administration, (b) estates management, (c) health promotion, (d) non-clinical staffing areas in hospitals such as portering, (e) the ambulance service, (f) NHS 24 and (g) its Health Department following the publication of Building a Better Scotland: Efficient Government – Securing Efficiency, Effectiveness and Productivity.

S2W-12777 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to develop district general hospitals.

S2W-12778 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to support NHS boards in providing in-service continuing professional development for all clinical staff.

S2W-12779 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is aware of the £34 million deficit in relation to the Strathclyde Pension Fund, as indicated in the Fund Actuary's report of 31 March 2004.

S2W-12780 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to address the £34 million deficit in respect of the Strathclyde Pension Fund, as indicated in the Fund Actuary's report of 31 March 2004.

S2W-12781 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive how many workers will be affected by the £34 million deficit in respect of the Strathclyde Pension Fund, as indicated in the Fund Actuary's report of 31 March 2004.

S2W-12782 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will investigate the reasons for the £34 million deficit in respect of the Strathclyde Pension Fund, as indicated in the Fund Actuary's report of 31 March 2004.

S2W-12783 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive how many early retirement packages were granted to chief executives and officers at director level in each of the 12 local authorities which participate in the Strathclyde Pension Fund in each financial year since 1999-2000, showing the cost of such packages in each authority, broken down by the level of seniority of staff receiving each package.

S2W-12784 John Swinburne: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to review the use of deferred payment agreements between care home residents and local authorities.

S2W-12785 Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-11453 by Mr Jim Wallace on 10 November 2004, what the (a) output of all hydro schemes was prior to down-rating, (b) estimated output will be following down-rating and whether it will explain the statement that "down-rating does not necessarily mean that output will also reduce".

S2W-12786 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the Department for Work and Pensions regarding the impact of closure orders made under the Antisocial Behaviour etc. (Scotland) Act 2004 on housing benefit.

S2W-12787 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on whether a person subject to a closure order under the Antisocial Behaviour etc. (Scotland) Act 2004 can claim housing benefit on the property (a) from which they are excluded and (b) to which they have moved to for the duration of the order.

S2W-12788 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether a person subject to a closure order under the Antisocial Behaviour etc. (Scotland) Act 2004 will be considered by the local authority to be intentionally homeless.

S2W-12789 Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-11403 by Rhona Brankin on 29 November 2004, what proportion of the Health Improvement Fund (HIF) was directed to NHS boards; how the remainder of the funds from Scotland’s share of tobacco tax which was used under the HIF was allocated; whether the proportion of the HIF which is being directly incorporated into NHS board main allocations is ring-fenced for any specific purpose, and whether it will ensure that the funding being directly incorporated into NHS board main allocations is not used for salaries.

S2W-12793 Frances Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make representations to the Department for Trade and Industry on extending the Supermarket Code of Practice to cover the whole supply chain, including farmers, retailers, processors and the food service sector.

S2W-12794 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what the latest estimate is of excess winter deaths among people over 65 this winter.

S2W-12798 Frances Curran: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body how much locally-grown produce is used in the restaurant facilities in Holyrood.

S2W-12799 Frances Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive how much locally-grown produce is used in its canteen and restaurant facilities.

S2W-12800 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to request the Auditor General for Scotland to carry out an investigation into the accuracy of the billing database maintained by Scottish Water.

S2W-12803 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact the exclusion of care and nursing homes from the water charges exemptions scheme will have on the pace of closure of care and nursing homes.
