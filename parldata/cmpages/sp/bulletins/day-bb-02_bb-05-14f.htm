Business Bulletin No. 78/2002

Day Month 200y

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-3100 Shona Robison: Scottish Local Government Funding Crisis-That the Parliament notes the publication of the report Grant Distribution and the Mismatch Effect: An Assessment of its Impact on Councils since Reorganisation by Professor Arthur Midwinter; recognises the report's conclusions that Argyll and Bute, Dundee City, Glasgow City, Inverclyde, Midlothian and West Dunbartonshire councils have suffered due to the reorganisation of local government to the tune of £50 million per annum; further recognises that this additional grant would allow an average reduction in council tax of £112, and calls on the Minister for Finance and Public Services to take account of this financial injustice in advance of the local government settlement for 2003-04.

*S1M-3099# Pauline McNeill: Funding Mismatch for Local Authorities-That the Parliament notes the erosion of grant income resulting in a severe funding mismatch for six local authorities, including Glasgow City Council, as identified in a specially commissioned report by Professor Midwinter of Strathclyde University; further notes that the mismatch occurred as a direct result of local government reorganisation in 1996 following which grant income fell 4.6% for these local authorities against a Scottish average drop of 1.2%; expresses concern at the direct affect that these budgetary restraints have had on council tax payers with a 20% increase in the affected authorities against a Scottish average of 16%; recognises that Glasgow City Council and the other five councils have since operated at a financial disadvantage; supports the call of the six local authorities and Professor Midwinter for restoration of £50 million, £32 million going to Glasgow, and considers that the Scottish Executive should rectify this mismatch immediately and ensure equitable distribution of future grant increases.

*S1M-3098 Dr Richard Simpson: Scottish Fire Service of the Future-That the Parliament welcomes the Scottish Executive's commitment to developing a fire service that is modern, effective and efficient and which will also have a specific duty to enhance community fire safety, as set out in the consultation paper The Scottish Fire Service of the Future.

Supported by: Mr Jim Wallace*
