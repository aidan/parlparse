Business Bulletin No. 69 / 2004

Monday 26th April 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-1196 Colin Fox: Amnesty for Poll Tax Debts-That the Parliament condemns the local authorities that have recently issued bills for poll tax debts from 14 years ago; notes that poll tax debts have long been written off as unrecoverable in England and Wales due to the statute of limitation on debt recovery after 10 years; recalls that the poll tax was introduced in Scotland one year before England and Wales, and calls on the Scottish Executive to direct local authorities to write off poll tax debts in Scotland in line with the rest of the United Kingdom.

S2M-1195 Michael Matheson: Private Prisons-That the Parliament notes the important progress that has been made by the Scottish Prison Service (SPS) "Bridging the Gap" project and, in particular, the constructive relationship that has developed between the SPS and trade union partners to bid for a second new prison; regrets the decision by the Scottish Executive to contract out the first new build prison to the private sector, therefore denying the public sector the opportunity to openly compete for an important public sector service, and calls on the Executive to reconsider this decision.

S2M-1194# Scott Barrie: Recognition of Jack Vettriano-That the Parliament notes the increasing popularity, both at home and abroad, of the Fife-born and bred artist Jack Vettriano; notes the sale by auction of his most recognised work, "The Singing Butler", for a record amount for a painting sold in Scotland by a Scottish artist; notes with dismay that not one of his works appears in any of our national galleries, and believes that at least one of his paintings should be obtained by one of our national institutions to recognise his contemporary popularity and national significance.

Supported by: Christine May, Janis Hughes, Rhona Brankin

S2M-1193 Mike Rumbles: St Andrew's Church of Scotland, Gibraltar-That the Parliament notes that May 2004 marks the 150th anniversary of the opening of St Andrew's Church of Scotland, Gibraltar; recognises the valuable contribution that the church makes to the lives of members of the British military population and other expatriates in Gibraltar, and wishes it success with the numerous events that it has planned this year to celebrate the anniversary.

S2M-1192 Michael Matheson: Three New Primary Schools in the Falkirk Area-That the Parliament congratulates Falkirk Council on its decision to build three new primary schools at Stenhousemuir, Larbert and Maddiston; notes that these three new schools will be built without the use of PPP/PFI, and welcomes the fact that the new schools will be owned by the residents of Falkirk District, unlike the five schools built by the previous New Labour administration.

S2M-1191 Robin Harper: Joint Marine Programme - The Tangle of the Clyde Report-That the Parliament welcomes the report by the Joint Marine Programme, The Tangle of the Clyde, revealing the historic mismanagement of the Firth of Clyde and highlighting the tangled web of conflicting and confusing legislation that affects all of Scotland's seas and coasts; notes the unacceptable free-for-all, rather than coherent legal basis, by which the seas are managed; notes the importance of the seas to the Scottish economy; supports the call for a Marine Act for Scotland, and calls on the Scottish Executive to take on board the results of this new report in its current consultation on the marine environment in the interests of protecting and restoring both marine life and the livelihoods of coastal communities.

Supported by: Campbell Martin, Alex Neil, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Patrick Harvie, Ms Sandra White, Eleanor Scott, Mark Ballard, Shiona Baird*, Chris Ballance*

S2M-1190 Jeremy Purvis: Borders Adult Learner and Students Association-That the Parliament welcomes Adult Learners' Week as a recognition of the value of learning opportunities across all age groups for individuals and society; applauds those who overcome personal challenges in accessing learning opportunities in adulthood; is aware that there are many reasons why such opportunities are accessed, for example personal or career development; recognises that there are significant barriers to such access, for example transport, accessing childcare and cost; congratulates Borders Adult Learner and Students Association (BALSA) on raising the profile of adult learning in the Borders, and is pleased to acknowledge BALSA's conference on Saturday 8 May 2004 as the start of the process of breaking down the barriers to accessing adult learning opportunities.

S2M-1189 Scott Barrie: Congratulations to Dunfermline Athletic Football Club-That the Parliament congratulates Dunfermline Athletic Football Club on reaching the 2004 Tennent's Scottish Cup Final; acknowledges that this will be the Pars' first such final since last winning the cup in 1968; notes that the club has qualified for European football for the first time since 1970, and wishes all four of Scotland's representatives in next season's European football competitions every success.

Supported by: Brian Adam, Tommy Sheridan

S2M-1188 Robert Brown: Future of Clyde Boat Building and Marine Facilities-That the Parliament notes the historic boat and ship building tradition on the Clyde and the need for comprehensive development of the waterfront that will fully respect and cater for the river's marine heritage and regrets the loss of any existing boat and marine businesses to residential development, such as through the proposals being pursued by Clydeport to develop the area surrounding the Renfrew Ferry including the Clyde River Boat Yard and the "Old Garvel Yard".

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan

S2M-1187# Rob Gibson: Promoting Timber Buildings-That the Parliament congratulates the Caithness area of Highland Council for promoting the successful seminar "Tomorrow's Houses Today" that explored the possibilities for creating energy-efficient, eco-friendly housing using local materials; notes the participants' conclusions that local timber should be a key component in construction as it offers Scotland the use of a plentiful, renewable resource; further notes the opportunities to create new house styles fit for localised climatic and landscape characteristics, and considers that the Scottish Executive should quickly respond by reforming planning laws and building regulations to encourage diverse architectural expression through the medium of timber construction for affordable and sustainable homes and public buildings.

Supported by: Michael Matheson, Tommy Sheridan, Fergus Ewing*, Mr Kenny MacAskill*, Shiona Baird*, Richard Lochhead*, Robin Harper*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Eleanor Scott*, Chris Ballance*, Murray Tosh*, Jim Mather*, Mr Stewart Maxwell*, Alex Johnstone*, Patrick Harvie*, Alex Neil*

S2M-1186 John Swinburne: Death of Ronnie Simpson-That the Parliament expresses its sadness at the sudden passing of Ronnie Simpson; recognises his contribution to national and international football and to goalkeeping from the beginning of his career at Queen's Park Football Club at the age of 14, as well as periods at Third Lanark Football Club, Hibernian Football Club and Newcastle United Football Club where he won two FA cup winners' medals, his caps for Scotland and, in particular, his role as the last line of defence in Celtic Football Club's greatest-ever victory when they won the European Cup in 1967, and extends its condolences to his family and friends, including his fellow Lisbon Lions.

Supported by: Brian Adam, Tommy Sheridan

S2M-1183# Ms Sandra White: Closure of Queen Mother's Hospital-That the Parliament notes with dismay the decision by Greater Glasgow NHS Board to close the Queen Mother's Hospital; further notes that the pioneering work undertaken at this hospital is world-renowned; agrees with many top specialists in this field that the closure of this facility will lead to the lives of new-born babies from Glasgow and around Scotland being placed in jeopardy; believes that it is the duty of the Minister for Health and Community Care to act in the public interest at all times, and considers that the best way to do this would be to reverse this devastating and unwelcome decision.

Supported by: Frances Curran, Carolyn Leckie, Michael Matheson, Tommy Sheridan, Robin Harper*, John Swinburne*, Nicola Sturgeon*, Rob Gibson*, Eleanor Scott*, Mrs Margaret Ewing*, Mr Stewart Maxwell*, Tricia Marwick*, Shona Robison*, Alex Neil*

S2M-1182 Mr John Home Robertson: Successful Completion of A1 Expressway-That the Parliament notes the opening of the A1 expressway between Haddington and Dunbar by the First Minister on 14 April 2004; welcomes the fact that this new dual carriageway will improve road safety and reduce travelling times for local and long-distance journeys; congratulates the staff of the Scottish Executive, Balfour Beatty, Carl Bro and all the designers and contractors on the quality of the new road and associated environmental works, and urges the Executive to work up plans to dual the remaining single carriageway sections of the A1 in Scotland and to complete this essential part of the trunk road network of the United Kingdom.

S2M-1179 Carolyn Leckie: Nursery Nurses' Just Pay Claim-That the Parliament demands the immediate settlement of the nursery nurses' dispute in light of the news that the UK's richest people have seen their wealth increase by nearly 30%; recognises that Scotland's nursery nurses have been denied a national settlement to their just demand for a decent living wage at the same time as the 100 richest Scots are now worth over £11 billion, and regards it as a disgrace that our nursery nurses are having to battle for a decent living wage while the excessively rich sit back and watch their bank balances increase daily.

S2M-1178 Mr Ted Brocklebank: Congratulations to Fife Constabulary-That the Parliament congratulates Fife Constabulary for the successful pilot scheme of housing satellite police stations in rural post offices; notes how this programme has benefited communities in Fife by providing a police presence in remote areas as well as maintaining postal services at a time when many rural post offices are being closed, and hopes that this scheme will be rolled out to other rural areas throughout Scotland.

Supported by: Mr Mark Ruskell, Murdo Fraser, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton, John Swinburne, Mrs Nanette Milne, Christine Grahame, Mary Scanlon, Phil Gallie, Bill Aitken, Mr David Davidson, Dr Jean Turner, Mr Brian Monteith, Alex Johnstone

S2M-1177 Mr Jamie McGrigor: National Gallery of Modern Art-That the Parliament congratulates the National Gallery of Modern Art for its exhibition of the first ever museum showing of Lucian Freud's complete etchings; recognises that this exhibition provides the public with the opportunity to view a unique collection of works of an outstanding artist, and applauds the gallery for bringing forward exhibitions of this calibre that can only enhance the reputation of Scotland in the field of visual arts.

Supported by: Murdo Fraser, Mr Brian Monteith, Colin Fox

S2M-1176# Colin Fox: Abolish Prescription Charges-That the Parliament believes that charging patients for their medicines breaches the principle of universally free health care that underpins the NHS; notes that Unhealthy Charges, published by the National Association of Citizens Advice Bureaux in July 2001, indicates that as many as 70,000 Scots per year may have gone without vital medicines because they could not afford the £6.30 charge for each item; believes that the logic behind the charging criteria, with one patient exempt and another with a very similar condition paying in full, cannot be sustained, and believes that the Scottish Executive should follow the example of the National Assembly for Wales which voted recently to abolish prescription charges.

S2M-1151# Posthumous Pardon for Suffragette Prisoners (lodged on 2 April 2004) Sarah Boyack*

S2M-1145# Dennis Canavan: Threat to Jobs at TransBus International-That the Parliament is gravely concerned about the fact that the Mayflower Corporation has entered administration with a resultant threat to 1,000 jobs at TransBus International in Falkirk and Larbert and believes that the Scottish Executive should contact the employees' trade union representatives, the administrator and any prospective buyers to offer assistance and advice so that everything possible is done to try to save the jobs of the TransBus workers who make a very important contribution to the Scottish economy.

Supported by: Rob Gibson, Bristow Muldoon, Mr Stewart Maxwell, Jackie Baillie, Tricia Marwick, Margaret Mitchell, Mike Watson, Brian Adam, Mike Pringle, Donald Gorrie, Alex Neil, Michael Matheson, Mr Kenny MacAskill, Fiona Hyslop, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Chris Ballance, Cathy Peattie, Linda Fabiani, Bruce Crawford, Phil Gallie, Trish Godman, John Swinburne, Ms Sandra White, Murray Tosh, Robin Harper, Carolyn Leckie, Mrs Margaret Ewing, Scott Barrie, Frances Curran, Pauline McNeill, Christine Grahame, Karen Gillon, Tommy Sheridan, Cathie Craigie, Dr Jean Turner

S2M-1144# Mary Scanlon: Chiropody Care-That the Parliament recognises the importance of regular chiropody and foot care in keeping Scotland's elderly population mobile, pain free and independent; considers that the Scottish Executive should specify who is eligible for NHS chiropody and podiatry care, noting that local access policies differ throughout Scotland and promote variances in the provision of foot care, and further considers that NHS boards should ensure that access to foot health services are maintained in a fair and equitable manner to all patient groups including the elderly and schoolchildren.

Supported by: Murdo Fraser, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton, Margaret Mitchell, Mrs Nanette Milne, Alex Johnstone, Murray Tosh, Phil Gallie, Frances Curran, John Scott, John Swinburne, Robin Harper, Mr David Davidson, Mr Jamie McGrigor, Mike Rumbles, Stewart Stevenson, Tommy Sheridan, Chris Ballance, Ms Sandra White, Carolyn Leckie, Fergus Ewing, Christine Grahame, Shiona Baird, Dr Jean Turner

S2M-1138 The Kenny Macintyre Media Centre (lodged on 31 March 2004) Mr Jamie McGrigor*

S2M-1100 Cathy Jamieson: Criminal Procedure (Amendment) (Scotland) Bill-That the Parliament agrees that the Criminal Procedure (Amendment) (Scotland) Bill be passed.

Supported by: Hugh Henry
