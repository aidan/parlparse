Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (♦).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S3M-4153 Anne McLaughlin: Saluting Learning Experience of Glasgow School Campaign Children—That the Parliament recognises that some of the most valuable learning experiences gained by children take place by engaging democratically with their local community; notes that children who participated in campaigns to save Glasgow schools and nurseries have learned valuable lessons in citizenship that will serve them well throughout their lives, such as how to write petitions and collect signatures, how to communicate with the media, how to build campaigns and how to work with elected officials; further notes that children and families gained valuable experience working with their counterparts in schools and nurseries from different cultural, ethnic and faith groupings across the city; congratulates Glasgow’s schoolchildren for the way in which they are conducting themselves during this difficult time, and wishes them well for the future.

*S3M-4151 Christine Grahame: Hot Branding—That the Parliament notes that from 1 July 2009 permanent identification by microchipping will be mandatory for all newborn foals and adult horses not already registered in the European Union, on implementation of the revised European rules (Commission Regulation (EC) 504/2008) on equine identification; considers that this measure removes, once and for all, any supposed necessity for the hot branding of horses; notes that the British Equine Veterinary Association has called for hot branding to be outlawed; believes that hot branding is an unnecessary mutilation involving the burning and scarring of sensitive living tissue, which should not be permitted in Scotland, and urges the Scottish Government to amend the Prohibited Procedures on Protected Animals (Exemptions) (Scotland) Regulations 2009 to ban hot branding.

*S3M-4150# Liam McArthur: Electronic Indentification of Sheep—That the Parliament notes with concern that the EU Council of Ministers has voted to press ahead with the introduction of compulsory electronic identification (EID) of sheep across the European Union; recognises that this decision was taken despite outright opposition from some member states and growing unease among others about the cost and practicalities of such a scheme; doubts that a system of EID can be found that is workable on most of Scotland’s sheep farms, particularly those in the Highlands and Islands; further notes that £3 million has been committed to a large-scale pilot to try to find a workable and cost-effective solution, and believes that the European Commission has much to learn from observing the pilot and, should a solution not be identified, that a derogation from a compulsory EID scheme would be in the best interests of Scotland’s sheep farmers.

Supported by: Jeremy Purvis*, Jamie Stone*, Ted Brocklebank*

*S3M-4149 Margaret Mitchell: 70th Anniversary of the Royal Gibraltar Regiment—That the Parliament notes that the Royal Gibraltar Regiment will fire the guns at Edinburgh Castle on Tuesday 2 June 2009 to commemorate the 70th anniversary of the regiment; further notes that this celebration pays tribute to the association that Gibraltar has enjoyed with many Scottish regiments; congratulates the Royal Gibraltar Regiment on its 70th anniversary, and wishes the regiment every success for the next 70 years.

*S3M-4148 Dr Richard Simpson: Congratulations to the Dementia Services Development Centre in Stirling—That the Parliament congratulates the Dementia Services Development Centre in Stirling together with Alzheimer Scotland and the Care Commission for winning the award from the Mental Welfare Commission for Scotland in the carer involvement and support category for the Beyond Barriers project, which aimed to improve quality of life for carers and family of dementia sufferers.

*S3M-4147 Bill Wilson: The Advanced Forming Research Centre—That the Parliament welcomes the announcement of the establishment of the Advanced Forming Research Centre in Inchinnan, Renfrewshire, the first of its kind in the United Kingdom; recognises that the centre will research and develop innovative technology for the aerospace, energy, marine and car industries; commends the collaboration between the University of Strathclyde, Scottish Enterprise and leading engineering firms on creating the centre, and recognises the funding that the Scottish Government has provided to keep Scotland at the forefront of the development of such technology.

Supported by: Kenneth Gibson*, Joe FitzPatrick*, Rob Gibson*, Bill Kidd*

*S3M-4146 Aileen Campbell: Putting Lanarkshire Online—That the Parliament congratulates Carstairs Junction resident Arthur Grainger on his initiative to put the towns of South Lanarkshire, including those in the South of Scotland region, on the internet with a series of social networking sites for each town and village in the area; notes that these sites have already attracted great interest from the local community and support from traditional media outlets including the local newspapers and radio stations; believes that initiatives such as this provide an important opportunity for people to link up and learn more about their local area, and wishes Arthur and the project every success in the future.

Supported by: Alasdair Allan*, Stuart McMillan*, Jamie Hepburn*, Bob Doris*, Christina McKelvie*, Rob Gibson*, Shirley-Anne Somerville*, Sandra White*, Bill Kidd*, Kenneth Gibson*

*S3M-4145 Mr Frank McAveety: DEAFinitely Greener Gains Support of Climate Challenge Fund—That the Parliament congratulates Deaf Connections, which received £16,850 from the Climate Challenge Fund for its project, DEAFinately Greener; endorses plans to ensure that all deaf people across Scotland have equal access to information and advice about climate change in British Sign Language (BSL); highlights the opportunity to overcome communication barriers and empower deaf people to reduce their carbon footprints, and welcomes the continued efforts of the Climate Challenge Fund in encouraging people and communities to take steps to reduce carbon emissions.

*S3M-4144 Brian Adam: Dyce Boys Club Scottish Cup Winners—That the Parliament congratulates Dyce Boys Club under-14s on their 3-2 extra time victory over Leith Athletic in the Scottish Youth Football Association under-14s cup final; notes the recent success of north-east teams in the competition; believes that this demonstrates the strength of coaching and playing abilities in the north east of Scotland, and hopes that such success will continue for the foreseeable future.
