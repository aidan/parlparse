Business Bulletin No. 62 / 2004

Monday 5th April 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-1152 Eleanor Scott: Harris Superquarry-That the Parliament welcomes the decision by the multinational company, Lafarge to finally give up plans for a superquarry on Harris in the Western Isles; congratulates local people and the Scottish LINK Quarry Group for defending the local community against the threat to their economy and environment over more than a decade of campaigning, and considers that the current planning review provides a vital opportunity to improve the planning system to ensure that local communities across Scotland are better protected and less vulnerable to the power of multinational companies pursuing unsustainable and environmentally destructive developments.

Supported by: Robin Harper*

*S2M-1151# Cathy Peattie: Posthumous Pardon for Suffragette Prisoners-That the Parliament supports the call for posthumous pardons to be granted to suffrage supporters who went to prison during the votes for women campaign between 1909 and 1914.

*S2M-1150 Michael Matheson: Respite Care for Young Disabled Persons in Forth Valley-That the Parliament recognises the significant role played by Headway and the Princess Royal Trust for Carers in providing much needed assistance and support to the families of young disabled persons; notes the lack of young persons in patient rehabilitation beds within the Forth Valley area, and calls upon NHS Forth Valley to put in place a strategy to provide more dedicated services for young disabled persons, in particular for respite breaks and in-patient rehabilitation.

*S2M-1149 Rosie Kane: Sellafield Clean Up-That the Parliament condemns the continued refusal of the Westminster Government to allow full safety inspections at Sellafield; further condemns the fact that the Westminster Government has consistently failed to allow EU inspectors to ascertain whether or not the 1.3 tonnes of plutonium, currently stored at Sellafield, has ended up in nuclear weapons; notes the decision of the European Commission to extend the deadline for a clean up plan to 1 June 2004, and calls on the Westminster Government to open up all of its nuclear facilities to EU inspectors.

*S2M-1148 Mr Stewart Maxwell: Fire-Safe Cigarettes-That the Parliament congratulates the Canadian Parliament on its recent Bill requiring all cigarettes sold in Canada to be self-extinguishing; recognises that lit cigarettes are the leading cause of fire fatalities within Scotland, and now looks forward to the Scottish Parliament bringing forward similar legislation introducing fire-safe cigarettes and helping to decrease the large number of avoidable deaths within Scotland from the aftermath of lit cigarettes.

S2M-1147 Michael Matheson: Threat to TransBus Plants at Larbert and Camelon-That the Parliament notes with concern the financial problems that the Mayflower Corporation is facing; acknowledges the work done by Falkirk Council, Scottish Enterprise and Mayflower in their proposal to secure 1,000 jobs at the Glenbervie site, and calls upon the Scottish Executive to assist Falkirk Council in protecting the TransBus operation in Falkirk, in order that these highly skilled jobs can be secured and that the long standing manufacture of buses continues in the Falkirk area.

Supported by: Fiona Hyslop*, Linda Fabiani*

S2M-1146 Ms Sandra White: EOC "It's Time to Get Even" Campaign-That the Parliament notes the "It's Time to Get Even" campaign by the Equal Opportunities Commission (EOC) Scotland to highlight pay inequalities between men and women; further notes that the average pay gap between men and women is £482 per month, and congratulates EOC Scotland for bringing forward this high profile campaign and drawing attention to this extremely serious issue.

Supported by: Michael Matheson*, Fiona Hyslop*, Linda Fabiani*

S2M-1145# Dennis Canavan: Threat to Jobs at TransBus International-That the Parliament is gravely concerned about the fact that the Mayflower Corporation has entered administration with a resultant threat to 1,000 jobs at TransBus International in Falkirk and Larbert and believes that the Scottish Executive should contact the employees' trade union representatives, the administrator and any prospective buyers to offer assistance and advice so that everything possible is done to try to save the jobs of the TransBus workers who make a very important contribution to the Scottish economy.

Supported by: Rob Gibson*, Bristow Muldoon*, Mr Stewart Maxwell*, Jackie Baillie*, Tricia Marwick*, Margaret Mitchell*, Mike Watson*, Brian Adam*, Mike Pringle*, Donald Gorrie*, Alex Neil*, Michael Matheson*, Mr Kenny MacAskill*, Fiona Hyslop*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Chris Ballance*, Cathy Peattie*, Linda Fabiani*, Bruce Crawford*

S2M-1144# Mary Scanlon: Chiropody Care-That the Parliament recognises the importance of regular chiropody and foot care in keeping Scotland's elderly population mobile, pain free and independent; considers that the Scottish Executive should specify who is eligible for NHS chiropody and podiatry care, noting that local access policies differ throughout Scotland and promote variances in the provision of foot care, and further considers that NHS boards should ensure that access to foot health services are maintained in a fair and equitable manner to all patient groups including the elderly and schoolchildren.

Supported by: Murdo Fraser, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton, Margaret Mitchell, Mrs Nanette Milne, Alex Johnstone

S2M-1140 John Swinburne: Swimming for Senior Citizens-That the Parliament deplores the actions of North Lanarkshire Council which has increased the cost of swimming in its baths and leisure centres for pensioners by 200%; believes that the actions of the council are contrary to the policies of the Scottish Executive in promoting the health and well-being of our nation, particularly older people; recognises that swimming is an accessible method of keeping fit for those older people experiencing some form of ill health or as a formal part of their medical care; notes that the price change will adversely affect pensioners who will not be able to afford this increase and who will consequently cease their participation, and therefore calls upon the Scottish Executive to deliver a national policy of free swimming for older people throughout Scotland.

Supported by: Alex Neil, Michael Matheson, Colin Fox*

S2M-1138 Donald Gorrie: The Kenny Macintyre Media Centre-That the Parliament hopes that the name "Kenny Macintyre Media Centre" as the title of the Parliament's current media centre will continue to be used in the new parliament building.

Supported by: Margaret Jamieson*, Jackie Baillie*, Kate Maclean*, George Lyon*, Maureen Macmillan*, Mike Rumbles*, Mike Pringle*

S2M-1137# Colin Fox: Standard Life Job Losses-That the Parliament condemns the decision by Standard Life to sack 1,000 staff by the end of the year; is not convinced that the company will reduce risk to its customers or staff by these measures and notes with concern the long-term impact on staff and policyholders of the move towards demutualisation announced today, and considers it to be imperative that the company should open talks with a recognised trade union to ensure that staff are protected and treated fairly at this time.

Supported by: Christine Grahame

S2M-1136# Mr Andrew Welsh: Scotland's Equine Sports Industry-That the Parliament acknowledges the importance of Scotland's equine sports industry for the development of Scotland's rural economy; commends the work of the Scottish Equestrian Association in seeking to coordinate equine-related policy decisions, and believes that the Scottish Executive should set up a dedicated equine industry team within its Environment and Rural Affairs Department.

Supported by: Ms Rosemary Byrne, John Farquhar Munro, Miss Annabel Goldie, Alex Fergusson, Dr Elaine Murray, John Scott, Alex Johnstone, Irene Oldfather, Christine Grahame, Alex Neil, Shiona Baird*, Mark Ballard*, Chris Ballance*, Eleanor Scott*, Robin Harper*, Dr Jean Turner*, Colin Fox*

S2M-1134 Colin Fox: Remembering Mick McGahey-That the Parliament congratulates Midlothian Trades Union Council for organising a night to commemorate the life of Michael McGahey, the former leader of the National Union of Miners in Scotland; notes the success of last year's inaugural event; further notes that the 2004 event will take place in Loanhead Miners Welfare Society and Social Club on Saturday 1 May 2004, and wishes Michael McGahey's family, friends and comrades a successful night.

Supported by: Christine Grahame, Alex Neil

S2M-1133 Colin Fox: May Day 2004 Edinburgh-That the Parliament wishes every success to the Edinburgh May Day committee and its 2004 celebrations to mark International Workers Day; notes that this year's theme is to commemorate the 20th anniversary of the 1984-85 miners' strike, and further notes that Arthur Scargill, former leader of the National Union of Miners, Tommy Sheridan MSP, Bill Speirs, General Secretary of the Scottish Trades Union Council, Hilary Wainwright, Editor of Red Pepper magazine and a Palestinian representative will lead the parade through the centre of Edinburgh on Saturday 1 May.

S2M-1132 Nora Radcliffe: Chardon LL Maize-That the Parliament welcomes the announcement by Bayer Crop Science that it will not attempt to grow Chardon LL GM maize in the UK commercially; notes that this is the result of the tough, restrictive regime supported by the Scottish Executive; believes that this announcement fully justifies the precautionary approach, based on scientific evidence, that the Executive has taken; further believes that calls for an illegal blanket ban on GM could have been challenged in the courts and led to court approval for commercial planting of a GM crop, and calls on the Executive to continue to deliver more sustainable agriculture, promoting organic farming, consumer choice and quality Scottish produce.

Supported by: George Lyon, Robert Brown, Mr Jamie Stone, Margaret Smith, Iain Smith, Sarah Boyack, Maureen Macmillan*

S2M-1132.1 Mr Mark Ruskell: Chardon LL Maize-As an amendment to motion (S2M-1132) in the name of Nora Radcliffe, leave out from "tough" to "continue" and insert "poor performance of Chardon LL as a crop; further notes how this poor performance could be used by the Scottish Executive as a valid reason to reject the addition of the crop to the UK seed list; believes that the Executive is failing to use its wide legal powers of discretion as exercised by EU states in relation to GM crops; further believes that the Executive is relying on the fortuitous decisions of biotechnology companies to make those decisions it is unwilling to make itself, and calls on the Executive."

Supported by: Eleanor Scott, Chris Ballance, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Patrick Harvie, Linda Fabiani, Shiona Baird, Robin Harper, Mr Stewart Maxwell, Mark Ballard, Carolyn Leckie*

S2M-1132.2 Roseanna Cunningham: Chardon LL Maize-As an amendment to motion (S2M-1132) in the name of Nora Radcliffe, leave out from the first "this" to end and insert "Bayer took the UK government decision, supported by the Scottish Executive, to give agreement in principle to the commercialisation of Chardon LL as confirmation that the product was "both safe and effective"; believes that decisions on the commercialisation of GM crops should be based on issues of public health and environmental safety rather than being left to the vagaries of the market, and regrets the failure of the Scottish Executive to use its legal powers to take a firmer stand against the commercialisation of Chardon LL maize."

S2M-1131 Maureen Macmillan: Congratulations on the Building of the New Dawn-That the Parliament congratulates John MacLennan, John Leckie, Mark Dunley, Ian Gwyn, Thomas Taylor and Gary Hynde, all recovering heroin addicts, who built a Ness Yoal, the New Dawn, and launched it on 27 March 2004 at Invergordon, and further congratulates Colin Beattie who supervised the building of the boat, with the help of the Galgael Trust in Govan and Alness Mothers Against Drugs who set up the project with National Lottery funding and New Futures money from Highlands and Islands Enterprise.

Supported by: Alex Neil

S2M-1130 Pauline McNeill: 218 Time Out Centre, Glasgow-That the Parliament welcomes the establishment of the Scottish Executive funded time out centre, 218, an assessment and referral service to assist in reducing women's offending and the number of women in prison; supports the work undertaken at 218 to tackle the root causes of women's offending by offering programmes of care, support and development designed to stop women offending by tackling substance misuse driven by trauma and poverty; is particularly encouraged by the menu of programmes to meet these needs including SAFE, working towards recovery including learning to work with a key worker, substitute prescribing, getting somewhere to live, reducing offending and claiming benefits, CONNEXIONS, more sustained group work, helping women let go of substance misuse and offending, handling reality and learning to look after others as well as themselves, LOSS, letting go of substances, activating plans for a new life in education, work as carers and therapeutic work to begin to address the underlying issues of abuse, neglect, violence and other traumas; notes that women in Glasgow can access this service from court, prison or by themselves if they have had a period of custody in the previous 12 months, and considers that the emphasis on tackling the root causes of women's offending and developing a person-centred alternative lifestyle is a positive development that should be given greater emphasis in the criminal justice system.

Supported by: Maureen Macmillan*, Colin Fox*

S2M-1128 Mr Adam Ingram: National Depression Week, 19 to 25 April 2004-That the Parliament recognises that one in five people will suffer from depression at some point in their lives and considers that National Depression Week will highlight the problem; notes the sharp increase in prescription of medication for depression with little or no psychological support and the continued use of the anti-depressant Prozac for those under 18; considers this to be an intolerable situation, and calls for the support and resources required to enable a holistic approach to treating depression to be put in place as a matter of urgency.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson, Michael Matheson, Alex Neil, Christine Grahame, Linda Fabiani*

S2M-1127 Donald Gorrie: GATS and Water-That the Parliament welcomes Her Majesty's Government's commitment to the UN's pledge to halve the proportion of people without access to safe drinking water by 2015; notes that access to water is the most basic of human rights; notes concern that "water for human use" is included as a proposal under current negotiations of the General Agreement on Trade in Services (GATS) at the World Trade Organisation and that once a country commits its water sector to GATS, this commitment is effectively irreversible, irrespective of the consequences; further notes that current GATS negotiations could threaten the extension of clean water supply to the poorest communities by undermining pro-poor policies such as the preference for public or not-for-profit providers, and calls on Her Majesty's Government to persuade our EU counterparts to withdraw the proposal to bring "water for human use" into the GATS negotiations.

Supported by: Mark Ballard, Richard Lochhead, Mike Pringle, Nora Radcliffe, Brian Adam, Helen Eadie, Elaine Smith, Chris Ballance, Ms Sandra White, Mr Keith Raffan, Alex Neil, Patrick Harvie, Mr Mark Ruskell, Robin Harper, Robert Brown, Shiona Baird

S2M-1119 Peter Peacock: School Education (Ministerial Powers and Independent Schools) (Scotland) Bill-That the Parliament agrees to the general principles of the School Education (Ministerial Powers and Independent Schools) (Scotland) Bill.

Supported by: Euan Robson

S2M-1118# Fergus Ewing: Massacre of Glenfeshie-That the Parliament notes with concern the mass slaughter of deer at Glenfeshie, instigated by the Deer Commission for Scotland; believes that, whilst culling deer is necessary to control numbers, deer fencing is necessary in order to protect forestry and the removal, or non-provision of, deer fencing has contributed substantially to the present situation; further believes that the Deer Commission may intend to carry out further mass slaughter of deer out of season and at various locations in Scotland; considers that the Scottish Executive should meet representatives of the Scottish Gamekeepers Association to hear its concerns regarding the massacre of Glenfeshie and regarding the measures that should be taken in order to secure proper management of both deer and forestry, and, in the meantime, considers that there should be a moratorium on any further mass slaughter of deer in Scotland.

Supported by: Phil Gallie, Alex Johnstone, Christine Grahame, Dr Jean Turner, Mr David Davidson, John Farquhar Munro, Alex Fergusson, Mrs Nanette Milne, Alex Neil, Ms Rosemary Byrne*

S2M-1115# Rob Gibson: Promoting Regional and Minority Languages of Scotland-That the Parliament welcomes the deliberations of the Shetland dialect conference held recently in Scalloway; notes the bearing on these deliberations of recommendations under the current review of the obligations of the Scottish Executive to meet its pledge to promote Scots under the European Charter for Regional or Minority Languages; further notes that this must include creating conditions for the use of Scots in public life, through the adoption of a language policy and concrete measures, in co-operation with the speakers of the language, and considers that Scottish Ministers and the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body should make a commitment to a thorough and explicit adherence to the spirit and letter of this review.

Supported by: Richard Lochhead, Mr Stewart Maxwell, Brian Adam, Stewart Stevenson, Eleanor Scott, Jim Mather, Tricia Marwick, Robin Harper, Michael Matheson, Alex Neil, Ms Sandra White, Mr Mark Ruskell, Christine Grahame, Chris Ballance*

S2M-1114 Mike Pringle: Keep the Flying Scotsman in the UK-That the Parliament notes that the Flying Scotsman steam locomotive has been put up for sale with the bidding process ending on 2 April 2004; is concerned that it may be sold abroad if £2 million is not raised; congratulates the National Railway Museum in York and Sir Richard Branson for finding a substantial amount of money for a bid; notes that recently the Scottish Executive gave £6.5 million for the John Murray Manuscript Archive; considers the Flying Scotsman also to be of significant national value to Scotland, given that it was the first locomotive to run from London to Edinburgh non-stop; calls on the Executive to make a contribution to the bid, and hopes that this will keep the locomotive in the United Kingdom and allow it to be seen regularly on the Scottish railway network.

S2M-1113# Trish Godman: Amnesty International's Campaign to Stop Violence Against Women-That the Parliament commends the recent decision of Amnesty International to conduct a sustained campaign to stop violence against women; agrees with Amnesty International that violence against women is deeply embedded in all nations, including our own and that it is found in all social classes, irrespective of income, education or religion; notes with serious concern the prevailing incidence of domestic violence and other forms of violence against women in Scotland; reminds the Scottish Executive, local authorities and the police forces of their legal and moral obligations to protect women from violence, and considers that the Executive should do all in its power to eliminate this vile abuse of human rights in Scotland and to assist, where it can, the international campaign to stop violence against women.

Supported by: Jackie Baillie, Michael Matheson, Fiona Hyslop, Scott Barrie, Kate Maclean, Eleanor Scott, Christine May, Mr Bruce McFee, Christine Grahame, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Helen Eadie, Maureen Macmillan, Johann Lamont, Margaret Jamieson, Shiona Baird, Alex Neil, Stewart Stevenson, Nicola Sturgeon, Susan Deacon, Mike Watson, Tricia Marwick, Mike Pringle, Mr Kenneth Macintosh, Karen Gillon, Pauline McNeill, Carolyn Leckie, Bill Butler*, Des McNulty*, Bristow Muldoon*, Mr Stewart Maxwell*, Linda Fabiani*, Marlyn Glen*, Mrs Margaret Ewing*, Dennis Canavan*, Mark Ballard*
