Business Bulletin No. 70/2011: Thursday 9 June 2011
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 16 June 2011

Question Time

General

1. Bob Doris: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact it considers the recently announced increases in domestic energy prices will have on its efforts to eradicate fuel poverty. (S4O-00022)

2. Roderick Campbell: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with First Scotrail regarding the provision of commuter rail services in Fife. (S4O-00023)

3. Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive how much it has spent on improvements to the A77 south of Ayr since 1999; how much it plans to spend in the future, and what it considers the economic benefits of such expenditure to be. (S4O-00024)

4. Nigel Don: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made toward reinstating roads damaged by severe winter weather conditions. (S4O-00025)

5. Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to take forward its Early Years Framework. (S4O-00026)

6. Mike MacKenzie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will ensure that provision is made for extra costs associated with social housing in fragile rural communities. (S4O-00027)

7. John Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what statutory duties local authorities have for the provision of education for those seeking refugee status. (S4O-00028)

8. Stuart McMillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what support is available to members of the public who are unable to obtain legal representation in a civil law case. (S4O-00029)

9. Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason the budget and staffing for the Social Care and Social Work Improvement Service has been reduced. (S4O-00030)

10. Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will carry out a survey of island-based businesses, voluntary sector bodies and the public sector to assess the impact of the changes to the Air Discount Scheme on their operations. (S4O-00031)

Finance, Employment and Sustainable Growth

1. Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to produce new guidelines for local authorities on the siting of large-scale onshore wind farms. (S4O-00032)

2. David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in extending the living wage across the public sector. (S4O-00033)

3. James Dornan: To ask the Scottish Executive how much council taxpayers in Glasgow will save through the ending of paid director positions on arm’s length external organisations. (S4O-00034)

4. Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it takes to ensure the highest level of accountability and transparency in its finance, employment and sustainable growth directorates. (S4O-00035)

5. Jamie Hepburn: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to support tourism in Cumbernauld and Kilsyth. (S4O-00036)

6. Joan McAlpine: To ask the Scottish Executive how many jobs have been created and what other economic benefits have arisen in Dumfries and Galloway since the University of Glasgow’s Crichton campus since 2007. (S4O-00037)

7. Not Lodged

8. John Mason: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with COSLA regarding the implementation of a living wage for staff across local authorities. (S4O-00039)

9. Marco Biagi: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will bring forward details of changes to the Local Government Finance formula. (S4O-00040)

10. Gordon MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to relax the planning regulations relating to land designated as greenbelt. (S4O-00041)

11. Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what estimate it has made of additional costs to local authorities in moving from capital grant to revenue funding for the school building programme. (S4O-00042)

12. Jackson Carlaw: To ask the Scottish Executive what it considers constitutes a mass waste incinerator and what factors it may take into account when considering applications for these. (S4O-00043)

13. Kevin Stewart: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it gives to help small companies bid for public contracts. (S4O-00044)

14. Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Cabinet Secretary for Finance, Employment and Sustainable Growth last met the Chief Executive of Skills Development Scotland and what matters were discussed. (S4O-00045)

15. John Pentland: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average change in charges for council services has been for households since the introduction of the council tax freeze. (S4O-00046)

16. David Torrance: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to support microgeneration. (S4O-00047)

17. Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive how much will be provided to local authorities to sustain a council tax freeze for the next five years. (S4O-00048)

18. Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive what the impact of the changes in the national minimum wage in October 2010 has been for employment in Scotland. (S4O-00049)

19. Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to support small businesses to promote vibrant local communities. (S4O-00050)

20. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to make improvements to the consultation process for local people and communities regarding wind farm applications. (S4O-00051)
