Business Bulletin No. 29 / 2004

Thursday 12th February 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-908 Tommy Sheridan: Support for the Ay Family Threatened with Imminent Deportation to Turkey-That the Parliament notes with grave concern the imminent threat to deport the Ay family from Germany to Turkey and the unusually speedy rejection of their last appeal; further notes the disappearance of Mr Ay on his return to Turkey in 2002 and the fact that he remains missing to this day; cautions the Home Office that failure to note this evidence of danger to the family could lead to their death or similar disappearance and represents a breach of their human rights guaranteed under the United Nations Charter and European Convention on Human Rights; calls upon the Scottish Executive to make urgent representations to Home Office ministers to intercede immediately on behalf of the Ay family and use their discretionary powers to grant the family leave to return to the UK, and further calls upon the Executive to work with the Home Office to develop an impartial asylum assessment system.

Supported by: Rosie Kane*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Carolyn Leckie*, Ms Sandra White*

*S2M-907# Mr Alasdair Morrison: Fish Farming in Scotland-That the Parliament welcomes the results of a study into the presence of chemical residues in seabed sediments near fish farms, published by the Scottish Environment Protection Agency (SEPA); notes that 89% of sample measurements did not detect any residues of chemicals used to treat sea lice; further notes that, in the 11% of analysis where there were measurable concentrations of active ingredients, residues were restricted to areas of seabed below and close to fish farms and that all results from outwith these immediate areas were below SEPA's safe environmental standards; welcomes the continuing commitment of the Scottish aquaculture industry to minimising the environmental impact of fish farming, and commends SEPA on its use of sound science, robust regulation and partnership working to ensure the protection of Scotland's marine resources.

*S2M-905# Brian Adam: Satrosphere Science Centre-That the Parliament welcomes the review of science education in Scotland; supports the role that science centres such as Satrosphere in Aberdeen play, and considers that the Scottish Executive should financially support these centres on a fair and equitable basis.

Supported by: Mr Stewart Maxwell*, Robin Harper*, Richard Lochhead*, Rosie Kane*, Bruce Crawford*, Roseanna Cunningham*, Mrs Margaret Ewing*, Mr Kenny MacAskill*, Rob Gibson*, Fergus Ewing*, Shiona Baird*, Mr Adam Ingram*, Mr David Davidson*, Tricia Marwick*, Ms Sandra White*

*S2M-904 Stewart Stevenson: Rock Challenge-That the Parliament notes the excellent work undertaken by the Be Your Best Foundation in organising the 2004 Rock Challenge for school students, allowing them to use and develop music, dance, drama and production skills with the aim of encouraging young people to maintain a healthy lifestyle by making a commitment not to take drugs and avoid drinking and smoking, and congratulates Peterhead Academy on winning the Scottish heat of the competition with their "Maid in Scotland" production.

Supported by: Rosie Kane*
