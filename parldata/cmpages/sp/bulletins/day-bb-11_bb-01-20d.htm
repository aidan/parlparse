Business Bulletin No. 9/2011: Thursday 20 January 2011
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D – Oral questions selected for answer on 27 January 2011

Question Time

General

1. Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it has made on delivering next generation broadband to rural and remote communities in light of its Speak Up for Rural Scotland consultation. (S3O-12805)

2. Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many school nurses there have been in each year since 2007. (S3O-12785)

3. Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of the Getting it Right for Every Child approach, what plans it has to ensure that children and young people experiencing and recovering from domestic abuse receive appropriate support. (S3O-12784)

4. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what the health impact of a minimum price of 45p per unit of alcohol would be compared with the UK Government’s proposal to ban the sale of alcohol below the rate of duty plus VAT. (S3O-12723)

5. Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met representatives of the European Commission and what issues were discussed. (S3O-12811)

6. Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in tackling raptor poisoning. (S3O-12731)

7. John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met representatives of NHS Borders. (S3O-12813)

8. Jamie Hepburn: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the UK Government’s proposals to reform health services in England will have implications for NHSScotland. (S3O-12733)

9. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure that all schoolchildren experience high-quality outdoor educational opportunities. (S3O-12759)

10. Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Minister for Culture and External Affairs has been involved in discussions about disputed Barnett Consequentials at Joint Ministerial Committee meetings. (S3O-12728)

Finance and Sustainable Growth

1. Not Lodged

2. David McLetchie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will specify the work published by Professors Andrew Hughes-Hallett and Drew Scott that forms the basis of the First Minister’s assertion that "with economic powers we could grow the Scottish economy by an extra 1% a year." (S3O-12810)

3. James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive how the community energy loan fund will be used to promote cooperatives in the energy sector. (S3O-12767)

4. Joe FitzPatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations ministers have made to the UK Government regarding fuel prices. (S3O-12729)

5. Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it continues to support full fiscal autonomy for Scotland. (S3O-12790)

6. Christina McKelvie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many businesses are in receipt of business rates relief under the Small Business Bonus Scheme. (S3O-12740)

7. Cathie Craigie: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions the Cabinet Secretary for Finance and Sustainable Growth has had with the Minister for Housing and Communities regarding financial support to former mining communities in 2011-12. (S3O-12758)

8. Not Lodged

9. Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to support the bus manufacturing industry. (S3O-12736)

10. Not Lodged

11. Jim Tolson: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had to address the reported skills shortage in the renewable energy industry. (S3O-12804)

12. Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on local authorities funding severance schemes from reductions in pay. (S3O-12774)

13. Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to help protect small and medium-sized businesses from the economic downturn. (S3O-12730)

14. Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive whether funds are available to tackle congestion at the Inveramsay Bridge. (S3O-12799)

15. Shirley-Anne Somerville: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will discuss with Network Rail a review of the legislation regarding the redevelopment of railway stations. (S3O-12743)

16. Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is financing to tackle unemployment in North East Scotland. (S3O-12763)

17. Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with First Bus regarding services in West Lothian. (S3O-12780)

18. Anne McLaughlin: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on asking large retailers to pay a small increase in business rates. (S3O-12738)

19. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with Scottish Water regarding the recent severe weather. (S3O-12778)

20. Not Lodged
