Business Bulletin No. 61 / 2004

Friday 2nd April 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-1147 Michael Matheson: Threat to TransBus Plants at Larbert and Camelon-That the Parliament notes with concern the financial problems that the Mayflower Corporation is facing; acknowledges the work done by Falkirk Council, Scottish Enterprise and Mayflower in their proposal to secure 1,000 jobs at the Glenbervie site, and calls upon the Scottish Executive to assist Falkirk Council in protecting the TransBus operation in Falkirk, in order that these highly skilled jobs can be secured and that the long standing manufacture of buses continues in the Falkirk area.

*S2M-1146 Ms Sandra White: EOC "It's Time to Get Even" Campaign-That the Parliament notes the "It's Time to Get Even" campaign by the Equal Opportunities Commission (EOC) Scotland to highlight pay inequalities between men and women; further notes that the average pay gap between men and women is £482 per month, and congratulates EOC Scotland for bringing forward this high profile campaign and drawing attention to this extremely serious issue.

*S2M-1145# Dennis Canavan: Threat to Jobs at TransBus International-That the Parliament is gravely concerned about the fact that the Mayflower Corporation has entered administration with a resultant threat to 1,000 jobs at TransBus International in Falkirk and Larbert and believes that the Scottish Executive should contact the employees' trade union representatives, the administrator and any prospective buyers to offer assistance and advice so that everything possible is done to try to save the jobs of the TransBus workers, who make a very important contribution to the Scottish economy.

*S2M-1144# Mary Scanlon: Chiropody Care-That the Parliament recognises the importance of regular chiropody and foot care in keeping Scotland's elderly population mobile, pain free and independent; considers that the Scottish Executive should specify who is eligible for NHS chiropody and podiatry care, noting that local access policies differ throughout Scotland and promote variances in the provision of foot care, and further considers that NHS boards should ensure that access to foot health services are maintained in a fair and equitable manner to all patient groups, including the elderly and schoolchildren.
