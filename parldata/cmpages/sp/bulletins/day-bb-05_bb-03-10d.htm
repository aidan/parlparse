Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 17 March 2005

Question Time

Education and Young People, Tourism, Culture and Sport

1. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will ensure that the business tourism market benefits the whole of Scotland. (S2O-5846)

2. Mr Ted Brocklebank: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to promote tourism in Fife. (S2O-5804)

3. George Lyon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether Rothesay Academy is under consideration as a potential participant by the panel overseeing the Schools of Ambition programme. (S2O-5820)

4. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will co-ordinate the review of social work with other initiatives. (S2O-5888)

5. Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure that PPP schools make use of renewable energy sources. (S2O-5828)

6. Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what the benefits of reforms to VisitScotland will be for tourism in Perthshire. (S2O-5833)

7. Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has received regarding education funding arrangements for local authorities reporting a higher-than-average incidence of autism. (S2O-5854)

8. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many new schools have been built, and how many schools have been refurbished, since May 1999. (S2O-5859)

9. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are any plans for major improvements to the Scottish National Portrait Gallery. (S2O-5796)

10. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to commemorate the 700 th anniversary of the execution of Sir William Wallace. (S2O-5850)

11. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to improve science education. (S2O-5858)

12. Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to promote archaeological tourism in the Highlands and Islands. (S2O-5849)

Finance and Public Services and Communities

1. Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how the additional funding announced for affordable housing will benefit rural areas. (S2O-5876)

2. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will increase the supply of affordable housing. (S2O-5847)

3. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to take advantage of the UK Presidency of the European Union in 2005. (S2O-5851)

4. Chris Ballance: To ask the Scottish Executive whether planning decisions should take account of the health effects of radiation from telecommunications masts. (S2O-5886)

5. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will increase the number of subsidised low-cost homes. (S2O-5868)

6. Christine May: To ask the Scottish Executive how Scottish cities compare with English and European competitors in respect of levels of Gross Valued Added per capita. (S2O-5878)

7. Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is enabling housing associations to use renewable energy in new and existing developments. (S2O-5829)

8. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make an assessment of the number of affordable rented houses that are required to ensure an adequate supply to meet the needs in each local authority area. (S2O-5797)

9. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive why it plans to make the single survey mandatory, given that there were 74 participants in the pilot study. (S2O-5852)

10. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive how the Warm Deal and Central Heating Programme are being promoted. (S2O-5871)

11. Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive how the Housing ( Scotland) Bill will help improve the upkeep of properties and strengthen communities. (S2O-5869)

12. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations the Minister for Finance and Public Service Reform has received from Scottish Borders Council regarding additional funding to improve the road network. (S2O-5805)

General

1. Chris Ballance: To ask the Scottish Executive what the status of aiding and abetting torture is as a crime in Scots Law. (S2O-5842)

2. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in its review of the operation of the sex offender registration scheme. (S2O-5880)

3. George Lyon: To ask the Scottish Executive what help it can provide to the islanders of Lismore to resolve the petrol situation on the island. (S2O-5827)

4. John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the Ministry of Defence regarding the relocation of Z-berths away from population centres. (S2O-5818)

5. Mr Mark Ruskell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is aware of the changes to the route of nuclear weapons convoys travelling through Scotland en route from Burghfield to Coulport and what assessment it has made of any additional risk to the environment. (S2O-5877)

6. Susan Deacon: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to extend the provision of antenatal screening checks. (S2O-5832)

7. Christine May: To ask the Scottish Executive how effective the Scottish Drug Enforcement Agency has been in tackling drug trafficking. (S2O-5866)

8. Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what estimate it has made of the cost in respect of police resources of implementing the proposed ban on smoking in public places. (S2O-5802)

9. Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the Department of Trade and Industry regarding the proposed cap on charges for renewable energy generation on Scottish islands. (S2O-5822)

10. Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive how many NHS-registered dental patients there are compared with 1999. (S2O-5825)

11. Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied with the operation of the Strategic Waste Fund in respect of community recycling groups, with specific reference to the Highlands and Islands. (S2O-5845)

12. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has received regarding the threshold for small claims procedures for consumer cases. (S2O-5815)

13. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will take a decision on the upgrade or replacement of the footbridge on the M8 at Harthill services station. (S2O-5867)

14. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the Association of Chief Police Officers in Scotland regarding the threat of terrorism activity within and against Scotland. (S2O-5819)

15. Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive whether NHS Greater Glasgow has indicated its intention to proceed to formal consultation in respect of the future of the present in-patient facility at the Glasgow Homeopathic Hospital. (S2O-5795)
