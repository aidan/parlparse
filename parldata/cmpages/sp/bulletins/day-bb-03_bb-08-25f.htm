Business Bulletin No. 107 / 2003

Monday 25th August 2003

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-273 Mr Rob Gibson: GM Free Zone on Shetland Islands—That the Parliament welcomes the recent consultation by Shetland Islands Council environmental forum on the creation of a genetic-modification-free zone on the islands and urges the Scottish Executive to assist the local authority achieve this aim in every way possible.

Supported by: Mr Mark Ruskell*, Rosie Kane*, Eleanor Scott*, Mark Ballard*, Robin Harper*, Michael Matheson*, Jim Mather*, Chris Ballance*, Patrick Harvie*, Shiona Baird*, Carolyn Leckie*, Richard Lochhead*, Ms Sandra White*, Brian Adam*, Mr Stewart Maxwell*, Alex Neil*, Mr Bruce McFee*

*S2M-271# Ms Sandra White: It's Scotland's Oil—That the Parliament welcomes the comments by its former Presiding Officer, Sir David Steel, that the revenues from Scotland’s oil reserves should be placed under the control of the Parliament and believes that the Scottish Executive should support this welcome initiative from a leading Liberal Democrat figure and press Her Majesty’s Government for control of Scotland’s oil revenue to be placed in the hands of the Parliament.

Supported by: Alex Neil*, Stewart Stevenson*, Mr Stewart Maxwell*

*S2M-270# Mike Pringle: Single Transferable Vote and Electoral Reform Society Report—That the Parliament welcomes the Electoral Reform Society’s report, Room for Improvement?, on the 2003 Scottish Parliament elections and, in particular, notes some of the problems it identified with the additional member system of voting; notes the report’s conclusion that the single transferable vote (STV) would overcome many of these problems and overcome the conflict that can arise from having both constituency and list MSPs; agrees that the STV system should be used for the Parliament’s elections, which will enable communities to be better represented; believes that it should have the power to legislate for its own electoral system, and calls on the UK Parliament to transfer that power to this Parliament.

Supported by: Mrs Margaret Smith*, Jeremy Purvis*, Mr Stewart Maxwell*

*S2M-269# Colin Fox: Consultation on Tax Powers—That the Parliament supports the call by its former Presiding Officer, Sir David Steel, for the Parliament to be given increased tax powers and believes that the Scottish Executive should institute a consultation on the tax powers that the public considers would best be transferred to the Parliament from Westminster.

Supported by: Carolyn Leckie*, Frances Curran*, Mr Stewart Maxwell*

*S2M-268 Cathy Peattie: Vlaams Blok Visit—That the Parliament notes with concern the visit of Dominiek Lootens-Stael, chairman of the Vlaams Blok group, as part of a delegation of Flemish MPs to the Parliament; further notes the political activities of the group and the concerns of the Foreign and Commonwealth Office about the visit, and calls on the Presiding Officer to reverse his decision to allow Vlaams Blok to visit the Parliament.

Supported by: Susan Deacon*, Christine May*, Bill Butler*, Bristow Muldoon*, Cathie Craigie*, Mr Stewart Maxwell*, Marlyn Glen*, Mr John Home Robertson*, Dr Elaine Murray*, Kate Maclean*, Johann Lamont*

*S2M-267# Shona Robison: Vlaams Blok Visit—That the Parliament notes with concern the forthcoming delegation to the Parliament of Flemish MPs, which includes a member of the far right Vlaams Blok group; believes that Vlaams Blok's extreme views on immigration are not compatible with the founding principles of the Parliament; is concerned that this visit may lend credibility to Vlaams Blok and give it a platform for its views, and urges the Presiding Officer to review the decision to allow Vlaams Blok to visit the Parliament.

Supported by: Tricia Marwick*, Stewart Stevenson*, Patrick Harvie*, Linda Fabiani*, Campbell Martin*, Nicola Sturgeon*, Carolyn Leckie*, Mr Adam Ingram*, Frances Curran*, Ms Sandra White*, Mr Stewart Maxwell*

*S2M-266 Tommy Sheridan: Visit of Vlaams Blok Chairman to the Parliament—That the Parliament applauds the Welsh Assembly’s decision to ban the visit of a delegation of Flemish MPs, which includes Dominiek Lootens-Stael, chairman of the Vlaams Blok group; recognises that the Vlaams Blok group is well known as a far right, racist organisation; believes that it brings shame on the Scottish people and the Parliament that it should provide a platform to a known fascist, and calls on the Presiding Officer to reverse his decision to extend the Parliament’s hospitality to this delegation and on all MSPs to boycott the visit if it proceeds.

Supported by: Carolyn Leckie*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Frances Curran*, Rosie Kane*, Elaine Smith*, Colin Fox*, Mr Stewart Maxwell*, Alex Neil*

S2M-265 Queen Mother's Hospital and Yorkhill Hospital, Glasgow (lodged on 14 August 2003) Mr Adam Ingram*

*S2M-265.1 Carolyn Leckie: Queen Mother's Hospital and Yorkhill Hospital, Glasgow—As an amendment to motion (S2M-265) in the name of Ms Sandra White, leave out "these hospitals" and insert "the full range of maternity services, including intrapartum and paediatric care, at all maternity facilities in Glasgow".
