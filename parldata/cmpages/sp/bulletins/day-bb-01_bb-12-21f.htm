Business Bulletin No. 191/2001

Friday 21 December 2001

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-2574 Roseanna Cunningham: Imprisonment of Ngawang Sangdrol—That the Parliament recognises the plight of Buddhist nun Ngawang Sangdrol who, along with her fellow nuns from the Garu monastery, was arrested and imprisoned in August 1990 for attending a pro-independence demonstration in Lhasa; notes reports which indicate that Ngawang Sangdrol is currently due to be released in 2013 by which time she will be only 36 years old and yet will have been imprisoned for 21 years, and expresses its support for Amnesty International’s campaign to have Ngawang Sangdrol released from prison in Tibet.

*S1M-2571 Michael Russell: Fèisean nan Gàidheal—That the Parliament congratulates Fèisean nan Gàidheal, the National Association of Gaelic Arts Youth Tuition Festivals, on its work in the last year in involving over 3,600 young people in Gaelic cultural events, and commends the hard work by all in the organisation in order to involve a new generation of young people in Scotland’s Gaelic heritage despite the present range of difficult circumstances which face Gaelic, not least the fact that the present shortage of Gaelic teachers results in the language relying heavily on the goodwill of volunteers, such as those in Fèisean nan Gàidheal.

*S1M-2570 Michael Russell: Act of Settlement—That the Parliament notes the resolution of the House of Commons on 19 December 2001 giving leave for the Introduction of a Bill by Kevin McNamara MP, and to be supported by Alex Salmond MP, which would abolish the deeply offensive anti-Catholic provisions of the Act of Settlement; congratulates the sponsors of this proposed Bill and is pleased that they have paid tribute to the Scottish Parliament for its resolution of 16 December 1999 on this matter and to the Guardian and Scotland on Sunday newspapers for actively promoting the cause for repeal; regrets that Her Majesty's Government has taken no action on this matter despite indicating general support, and renews its call for UK legislation at the earliest possible date.
