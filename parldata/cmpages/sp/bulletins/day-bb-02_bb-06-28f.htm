Business Bulletin No. 108/2002

Friday 28 June 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-3269 Irene Oldfather: Bus Accident in France - Motion of Condolence—That the Parliament notes the tragic bus accident near Dijon, France, in the early morning of 27 June 2002 involving pupils and staff from Largs Academy, North Ayrshire; sends its condolences to the bereaved and its best wishes to the families and friends of the injured, and urges the Scottish Executive to do all it can to assist the families at this difficult and distressing time.

*S1M-3268 Murdo Fraser: Scottish Traditional Dance—That the Parliament recognises the importance of traditional Scottish dance and its contribution to Scottish culture; commends the work of the Scottish Traditions of Dance Trust in representing the interests of all Scottish traditions of dance, Ceilidh, Country, Early, Hebridean, Highland, Military, Old Time, Orkney, Shetland and Step, and in promoting traditional dance, particularly within schools, and calls on the Scottish Executive to encourage schools to make traditional dance available as part of the physical education curriculum.

Supported by: Alex Johnstone*, Phil Gallie*

*S1M-3267 Malcolm Chisholm: The Food Protection (Emergency Prohibitions) (Amnesic Shellfish Poisoning) (West Coast) (No.6) (Scotland) Order 2002 (SSI 2002/307)—That the Health and Community Care Committee, in consideration of the Food Protection (Emergency Prohibitions) (Amnesic Shellfish Poisoning) (West Coast) (No.6) (Scotland) Order 2002 (SSI 2002/307), recommends that the Order be approved.

Supported by: Mrs Mary Mulligan*, Mr Frank McAveety*

*S1M-3266 Malcolm Chisholm: The Food Protection (Emergency Prohibitions) (Amnesic Shellfish Poisoning) (West Coast) (No.5) (Scotland) Order 2002 (SSI 2002/306)—That the Health and Community Care Committee, in consideration of the Food Protection (Emergency Prohibitions) (Amnesic Shellfish Poisoning) (West Coast) (No.5) (Scotland) Order 2002 (SSI 2002/306), recommends that the Order be approved.

Supported by: Mrs Mary Mulligan*, Mr Frank McAveety*

*S1M-3265# Robin Harper: World Summit on Sustainable Development—That the Parliament recognises the importance to global social and environmental justice of the World Summit on Sustainable Development, Rio +10, in Johannesburg in August 2002; welcomes the First Minister’s decision to attend the summit; notes the publication by Scotland’s leading environmental organisations, Friends of the Earth, the Royal Society for the Protection of Birds, the Scottish Wildlife Trust and WWF, of the Must Try Harder report card on Scotland’s progress toward sustainability, and considers that the Scottish Executive should consider adopting the report’s urgent 10 point action plan to demonstrate Scotland’s commitment to achieving sustainability.

*S1M-3264 Iain Smith: Dunino Primary School - Eco-Schools Green Flag Award—That the Parliament congratulates the pupils and staff of Dunino Primary School in North East Fife for their unique achievement in being awarded the Eco-Schools Green Flag Award for their third time.

S1M-3263 Development of RAF Turnhouse Site (lodged on 26 June 2002) Margaret Smith*

*S1M-3262 Stewart Stevenson: Trade Unions—That the Parliament rejects the insulting description of trade unions as "other restrictive practices that exist in our economy" by the Chancellor of the Exchequer on the BBC Radio 4 Today programme on 26 June 2002; believes that this reactionary and right-wing attitude to the trade union movement is more akin to that of Victorian mill owners than 21st century government, and celebrates the contribution of trade unions to society as a vital element in protecting and promoting the interests of working men and women.
