Business Bulletin No. 57 / 2004

Monday 29th March 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-1112 Tommy Sheridan: Scotland-wide Referenda-That the Parliament believes that the provision of nutritional, healthy, free school meals for all children, the replacement of the council tax with an income-based alternative and returning rail and bus services to public ownership along with the gas and electricity industries are extremely popular across Scotland and that each of these vital issues should be subject to an individual Scotland-wide referendum as soon as practically possible.

*S2M-1111# Fiona Hyslop: Presumption Against Closure of Rural Schools - Code of Guidance-That the Parliament notes the continuing anxiety of rural communities across Scotland faced with rural school closures; notes the work of, and reports to, the Education, Culture and Sport Committee in Session 1 on rural school closures and its recommendation of 4 July 2000 that national guidelines be revised; regrets that COSLA abandoned its earlier agreement to draw up such a revised code of practice in 2001 by proposing a review of legislation by the Scottish Executive instead, and is of the view that the Executive, having acknowledged in March 2003 that COSLA was no longer carrying out this work, should itself, as a matter of priority, produce a revised code of guidance on rural school closures which should have as a central tenet the presumption against closure of rural schools.

Supported by: Brian Adam*, Mr Kenny MacAskill*, Bruce Crawford*

S2M-1110# Frances Curran: President of COSLA Should Resign-That the Parliament has no confidence in the president of COSLA, Pat Watters, and considers that his refusal to negotiate a national pay and careers structure with the nursery nurses' union UNISON has provoked a national strike, now in its fourth week; believes that, as the leader of local authorities in Scotland who deliver the age three to five curriculum, Mr Watters has responsibility for ensuring excellent standards in our pre-five sector for both children and staff, and believes that Mr Watters has failed in this responsibility and should tender his resignation.

Supported by: Carolyn Leckie*

S2M-1109 Jackie Baillie: Gynaecology Services in NHS Argyll and Clyde-That the Parliament is dismayed at the action of NHS Argyll and Clyde in failing to sustain gynaecology services at the Vale of Leven and Inverclyde Royal hospitals; notes that contingency arrangements will be in place from 29 March 2004 for all in-patient admissions to be centralised at the Royal Alexandra Hospital (RAH), Paisley; recognises that this will place intolerable pressure on existing services at the RAH and, in turn, will lead to longer waiting lists; further recognises the difficulty for patients north of the river to access services in the RAH due to lack of direct public transport and the cost of that transport; notes that the ambulance service is currently struggling to cope with the additional journeys to the RAH, despite the substantial recent increase in investment; further notes the impact that changes will have on allied services at the Vale of Leven and Inverclyde Royal hospitals, and considers that it is inappropriate to pre-empt the outcome of the clinical strategy review in this way.

Supported by: Mr Duncan McNeil

S2M-1108# Brian Adam: North East Regional Sports Facilities-That the Parliament regrets the absence of high standard indoor and outdoor sports facilities in the north east; supports the joint bid from Aberdeen and Aberdeenshire councils with Aberdeen and Robert Gordon universities, other public sector partners and a wide range of sporting clubs and bodies; encourages sportscotland and the Scottish Executive to look favourably on this application, and wishes the partners well in progressing the bid.

Supported by: Fiona Hyslop*, Mr Stewart Maxwell*, Mrs Margaret Ewing*

S2M-1107 Patrick Harvie: Environmental Justice-That the Parliament notes a new report, Environmental Justice, published by the World Wide Fund For Nature, the Environmental Law Foundation and Leigh, Day and Co, which alarmingly shows that the civil justice system effectively prevents people who are concerned about the environment from going to court and that the criminal justice system fails to provide effective deterrents to environmental and wildlife crimes in England and Wales; further notes that the report surveyed over 50 respondents, including 18 solicitors, 20 barristers and 15 non-governmental organisations, and found that 97% of those interviewed did not think that the civil law system delivered environmental justice, and urges the Scottish Executive to undertake a review of the civil and criminal justice systems in Scotland to ensure that the law properly protects the environment.

Supported by: Carolyn Leckie, Robin Harper, Shiona Baird, Eleanor Scott*, Chris Ballance*, Mr Mark Ruskell*

S2M-1106# Elaine Smith: Planned Extension of Dungavel-That the Parliament notes with concern recently announced plans to increase the detention capacity at Dungavel Immigration Removal Centre in Lanarkshire; recognises that immigration and asylum policy is a matter reserved to the UK Parliament; considers, however, that the existence and operational record of Dungavel poses a serious threat to the Scottish Executive's record of promoting equality of opportunity and social inclusion for refugees in Scotland and is contradictory to current efforts to encourage fresh talent to come to Scotland; agrees that the detention of innocent children and families is both inhumane and unnecessary; believes that the question of planning permission for the project should be considered carefully, and considers that concerns regarding the detention of asylum seekers in Scotland should be raised with the Home Office.

Supported by: Fiona Hyslop*, Pauline McNeill*

S2M-1105 Brian Adam: Congratulations to the Young People of Middlefield for their Bus Shelter Artwork-That the Parliament congratulates the young people of Middlefield in Aberdeen for their high quality and innovative bus shelter artwork; notes the involvement of the Whitespace arts development team and PC John Ritchie in supporting and encouraging the activity; further notes that the costs are in line with current replacement screen costs, and commends the initiative to other local authorities for potential roll out across Scotland.

S2M-1104# Sarah Boyack: Congratulations to the University of Edinburgh for Use of Renewable Energy-That the Parliament congratulates the University of Edinburgh for its progressive use of Combined Heat and Power (CHP) and photovoltaics as part of the university's energy strategy; welcomes the university's success in winning a grant from the UK Government's Major Photovoltaics (PV) Demonstration Programme; acknowledges the work of former student Andrea Connelly who undertook a feasibility study and established that the College of Science and Engineering campus at King Buildings would be suitable for CHP; understands that the project will cut the university's annual estates costs and free up scarce budgetary resources for other priorities such as fire safety and disabled access; applauds the university for commissioning another CHP system at Pollock Halls of Residence and George Square which will cut carbon dioxide emissions by over 2,000 tonnes and reduce running costs by approximately £400,000; further congratulates the university for the use of photovoltaic and passive ventilation which will be incorporated into the construction of the roof and cladding façade in the new campus building that will house the School of Engineering and Electronics, and encourages other higher education establishments and the Scottish Executive to endorse, encourage and employ the win-win outcome that renewable technologies provide in urban settings.

Supported by: Helen Eadie, Dr Elaine Murray, Karen Gillon, Maureen Macmillan, Bristow Muldoon, Christine May, Mike Watson, Mr Kenneth Macintosh, Susan Deacon*, Mr Alasdair Morrison*

S2M-1103 Donald Gorrie: Midnight Football Programme-That the Parliament commends the Midnight Football programme, funded by the Bank of Scotland and provided by the Scottish Football Association and local police, whereby for two hours on Friday evenings young people in 14 towns in Scotland play football at appropriate centres in organised tournaments with qualified coaches and transport provided, as an alternative to wandering the streets, and suggests that the Scottish Executive should work with the existing partners to ensure that these successful projects are carried on beyond their existing nine weeks and that similar projects are started in other areas as one part of the Executive's measures to provide young people with constructive alternatives to antisocial behaviour.

S2M-1102# Johann Lamont: Health Inequalities in Scotland-That the Parliament welcomes the information provided in Health and Well-being Profiles for each Scottish Parliamentary Constituency, 2004; acknowledges the serious and significant health inequalities within Scotland that these reports highlight; recognises the clear correlation between poverty, deprivation and ill-health; believes that the Scottish Executive, in its review of the Arbuthnott formula for health spending, should ensure that health spending more closely matches Scottish deprivation indicators and health need, and further believes that the proposed independent review of local government finance should examine the benefits of an Arbuthnott-like review of the distribution of funding to local authorities to ensure that such funding takes proper account of deprivation.

S2M-1101# Mr Jamie Stone: Sustaining Agriculture and Sustaining Communities-That the Parliament believes that sustaining a viable agricultural sector involves encouraging an increase in employment on Scotland's land and that this would strengthen the contribution of agriculture to the viability of rural communities and contribute to the nutritional health of Scotland. S2M-1099 Chris Ballance: Road Safety and World Health Day, 7 April 2004-That the Parliament notes that the World Health Organisation (WHO) has designated road safety as the theme of World Health Day 2004 on 7 April 2004; further notes that the WHO has indicated that 1.2 million people die from road traffic injuries each year; notes that the WHO predicts that by 2020 road traffic injuries will be a greater killer globally than malaria, tuberculosis and HIV/AIDS and that, despite excellent progress in tackling the problem, Scotland's annual death toll from road traffic remains unacceptably high, and supports World Health Day 2004 and measures to improve road safety in Scotland.

S2M-1097 Alex Fergusson: Man's Inhumanity to Man-That the Parliament welcomes the launch and display of the exhibition "Crying Forever" within the Scottish Parliament as the first step of its tour to parliaments in London, Brussels, Washington and Astana in Kazakhstan; abhors the fact that between 1940 and 1990, 607 nuclear devices were purposefully detonated by the Soviet regime in the Semipalatinsk region of Kazakhstan, equivalent to the size of Wales, intentionally exposing the 1.5 million inhabitants, as guinea pigs, to the equivalent strength of 20,222 Hiroshima bombs with disastrous consequences for both the people and the environment alike; notes with horror that within the Semipalatinsk region cancers run at five times the national average and that birth defects are three times the national average, that many women choose not to have children because of the high risk of physical and mental deformity amongst the new born, that many young men are impotent and that suicides amongst the young are common; offers the Parliament's support to the people of Kazakhstan as they struggle to overcome this prime example of man's inhumanity to man, and affirms the importance of democracy in protecting individuals, communities and the precious environment that we live in.

Supported by: Lord James Douglas-Hamilton, David Mundell, Rob Gibson, Stewart Stevenson, Bruce Crawford, Nora Radcliffe, Mr Kenny MacAskill, Roseanna Cunningham, Murdo Fraser, Michael McMahon, Mr Ted Brocklebank, John Scott, Mr Brian Monteith, Tricia Marwick, Mary Scanlon, Murray Tosh, Bill Aitken, Ms Sandra White, Margaret Mitchell, Richard Lochhead, Alex Johnstone, Rosie Kane, Chris Ballance, Robin Harper, Phil Gallie*, Patrick Harvie*, Fiona Hyslop*, Mr Mark Ruskell*

S2M-1096 Mr Stewart Maxwell: BBC Survey-That the Parliament congratulates the BBC on its recent survey and notes that 77% of people in Scotland support a ban on smoking in public places; agrees that both smoking and second-hand smoke are responsible for widespread ill-health and 13,000 avoidable deaths in Scotland, disproportionately affecting those that live within the poorer constituencies of Scotland, and supports measures that will assist in reducing levels of smoking and exposure to passive smoking, including legislation to regulate smoking in enclosed premises.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson, Alex Neil

S2M-1093 Maureen Macmillan: New Scandinavia/Inverness Air Service-That the Parliament welcomes the new Inverness/Stockholm air service to be run by the Scandinavian airline Snowflake which commences on 29 March 2004; congratulates the staff at Highlands and Islands Airports Ltd on their hard work to negotiate the introduction of this new service using Scottish Executive route development funding; notes that this new service will be an asset to both business and holiday travellers in the Highlands and Sweden, and hopes that this will be the first of many new regular air links between Scandinavia and the Highlands of Scotland.

S2M-977# Elaine Smith: Nursery Nurses' Pay and Conditions-That the Parliament recognises that the job of nursery nurse represents one of the remaining professions characterised by both an almost exclusively female workforce and an overarching legacy of low pay; notes however, that the increased academic and professional demands upon nursery nurses have been consistently recognised by both the Parliament and wider society in recent months; expresses concern that this consensual public recognition has not been reflected in the form of an adequate and timely settlement for those nursery nurses currently jeopardising their own financial security in an attempt to obtain a remuneration package that fairly represents their job and responsibilities; conveys regret that nursery nurses have now voted overwhelmingly for indefinite strike action to begin on 1 March 2004 that will result in widespread disruption to children's education and massive inconvenience for parents; acknowledges that this dispute represents a potential watershed not only for the future of the nursery service and early years education but also for the issue of equal pay in Scotland, and suggests that a possible route to a satisfactory settlement would be a national review of the nursery service followed by a Scotland-wide regrading of the role of nursery nurses.
