Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

*S2M-4540 Rob Gibson: Deportation of Immigrants—That the Parliament deplores the arbitrary nature of the recent Home Office initiative to deport immigrants with a criminal record without any apparent distinction being made based on the severity and circumstances of each case prosecuted; notes heavy-handed practices of the police and immigration officials which it considers could fall foul of the European Convention on Human Rights, and urges the Scottish Executive, in light of its Fresh Talent initiative, to make the case for immigrants caught in the Home Office deportation initiative whose removal would deprive communities on the mainland and islands of Scotland of well-integrated citizens.

*S2M-4539 Richard Lochhead: National Cycle Week - 17 to 25 June 2006—That the Parliament supports National Cycle Week, which takes place from 17 to 25 June 2006 and will encompass 1,500 events in which over 250,000 cyclists are expected to participate; congratulates the many local organisations in Scotland involved in National Cycle Week; further supports the aims of National Cycle Week which are to highlight cycling as a healthy and environmentally friendly form of transport and to encourage the increased use of cycling for travel, and recognises that cycling has an important role to play in meeting the targets for stabilising traffic at 2001 levels by 2021.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie*

*S2M-4538 Ms Margaret Curran on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Approval of SSI—That the Parliament agrees that the draft Student Fees (Specification) (Scotland) Order 2006 be approved.

Supported by: George Lyon*

*S2M-4537<> Jackie Baillie: Learning Disability Week 2006—That the Parliament congratulates ENABLE Scotland on commissioning research undertaken by the Scottish Consortium for Learning Disability; notes that the research on the impact of "The same as you?", the Scottish Executive’s seminal policy framework on services for the people with learning disabilities and family carers, has been published to mark Learning Disability Week which takes place between 18 and 25 June 2006; believes that it demonstrates the tremendous difference made to the lives of people with learning disabilities by the implementation of "The same as you?" including new services, such as local area co-ordinators in West Dunbartonshire; considers that there has been a qualitative transformation in the lives of many people with learning disabilities; acknowledges that the survey recognises the positive impact that "The same as you?" has made on those who have benefited from it; notes that not all people have seen positive changes in their lives; considers that the policy needs to be driven at a local and national level, and encourages the Scottish Executive to ensure that all children and adults with learning disabilities in Scotland can benefit.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie*

*S2M-4536 Cathy Jamieson: The Senior Judiciary (Vacancies and Incapacity) (Scotland) Bill—That the Parliament agrees that the Senior Judiciary (Vacancies and Incapacity) (Scotland) Bill be treated as an Emergency Bill.

Supported by: Hugh Henry*

*S2M-4535 Karen Whitefield: Diabetes and Diet—That the Parliament congratulates the Co-operative (Co-op) Group of food stores and pharmacies on its decision to stop selling so-called "diabetic" products such as jams, marmalade, squash drinks and preserves; recognises that this has been done on the basis that the Co-op Group, in consultation with Diabetes UK, has concluded that healthy eating advice for people with diabetes is the same as for the rest of the population and that there is, therefore, no need to have "diabetic" chocolate, jams and similar products which can serve to confuse healthy eating messages; urges other companies to follow the Co-op Group’s lead; agrees with Diabetes UK and the Co-op Group that people with diabetes should be focussing on a balanced diet that is lower in fat, sugar and salt and contains plenty of fruit and vegetables; recognises that the Scottish Executive’s Healthy Living campaign is key to making this happen, and furthermore commends the launch by Diabetes UK of an interactive section on its website which will help people look at their typical shopping basket and find out what they are doing right and what they should change.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie*
