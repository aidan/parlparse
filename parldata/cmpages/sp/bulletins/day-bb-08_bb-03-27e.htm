Business Bulletin No. 53/2008: Thursday 27 March 2008
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 26 March 2008

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-11312 Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the approval of Warrants for the Interception of Communications by judges instead of Scottish Ministers.

S3W-11313 Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will bring forward legislation to replace the authorisation of Warrants for the Interception of Communications by Scottish Ministers with a system of independent judicial oversight where such warrants are authorised by High Court judges or Sheriffs Principal.

S3W-11314 Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive how the £2.5 million for a Healthy Living Centre Transition Fund, announced by the Cabinet Secretary for Health and Wellbeing on 19 February 2008, will be allocated.

S3W-11315 John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive how many parenting orders have been issued under the Antisocial Behaviour etc. (Scotland) Act 2004, broken down by local authority area.

S3W-11316Δ Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive when it intends to publish its Infrastructure Investment Plan.

S3W-11317Δ Bill Kidd: To ask the Scottish Executive what new arrangements the First Minister has put in place to develop and maintain the relationship between the Scottish Government and the Scottish Parliament following the election of the SNP minority administration in May 2007.

S3W-11318Δ Joe FitzPatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive what goals it expects Registers of Scotland to achieve in 2008-09.

S3W-11319 Margaret Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive what national advice and support is available to assist people to access respite care.

S3W-11320 Margaret Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive what it is doing to meet the respite care needs of younger disabled people.

S3W-11321 Margaret Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is supporting the development of a range of respite care opportunities appropriate for people with multiple sclerosis.

S3W-11322 Margaret Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it has made towards its target of delivering 10,000 additional respite weeks per year.

S3W-11323 Margaret Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has given any guidance to NHS boards on the provision of financial support to voluntary sector respite care providers.

S3W-11324 Margaret Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to introduce a statutory right to respite care.

S3W-11325 Margaret Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive what it is doing to promote a standardised approach to the funding of respite care places by local authorities.

S3W-11326 Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive how many licensees have been prosecuted for selling alcohol to intoxicated customers in each of the last five years, broken down by (a) police force and (b) local authority and showing the fines issued in each case.

S3W-11327 Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive how many retailers have been prosecuted for selling alcohol to underage customers in each of the last five years, broken down by (a) police force and (b) local authority and showing the fines issued in each case.

S3W-11328 John Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether international full-time college students, serving in unpaid internships while living in Scotland, are eligible for council tax exemption.

S3W-11329 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider moderating comments before they are published on the Ministers’ Blog section of the National Conversation website.

S3W-11330 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration is given to comments left on the Ministers’ Blog section of the National Conversation website.

S3W-11331 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the cost is of the upkeep and moderation of the Ministers’ Blog section of the National Conversation website.

S3W-11332 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria were taken into consideration when developing measures to monitor comments left on the Ministers’ Blog section of the National Conversation website.

S3W-11333 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria it would consider before removing comments left on the Ministers’ Blog section of the National Conversation website.

S3W-11334 Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive how it ensures that people from ethnic minorities are made aware of available health screening services.

S3W-11335 Aileen Campbell: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it provides to local authorities seeking to reduce levels of light pollution.

S3W-11336 Aileen Campbell: To ask the Scottish Executive what levels of light pollution have been recorded in Scotland in each year for which records exist.

S3W-11337 Aileen Campbell: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance exists to minimise light pollution from new developments.

S3W-11338 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to establish a forum to bring together trade unions, operators of coal-fired generators, coal producers and suppliers, and other relevant parties in the coal industry to find solutions and secure the long-term future of Scottish coal production.

S3W-11339 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to stimulate investment in clean coal technologies.

S3W-11340 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to optimise the use of Scotland’s economical coal reserves.

S3W-11341 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will bring forward legislation to reverse the House of Lords judgment of 17 October 2007 to ensure that people with pleural plaques can continue to raise an action for damages.

S3W-11342 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether, in relation to criminal prosecutions or appeals arising from them, it believes that, if a relevant document in a trial is available to the prosecution but not to the defence, the defendant would be able to obtain justice as defined in the UN Charter for human rights and that the terms of the Charter would be fully met.

S3W-11343 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has reviewed the commitments made by the UK Government to the United Nations prior to the adoption of Security Council Resolution 1192 (1998); what the implications are of these commitments, and what recourse it or any third party individual or organisation has to the courts to ensure that these commitments are fully adhered to by it and the UK Government.

S3W-11344 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive how many local authorities have made a commitment to build houses for rent and how many houses in total they have committed to build since May 2007.

S3W-11345 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken to consult housing associations and the Parliament on the reorganisation of Communities Scotland.

S3W-11346 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive how many additional teachers are required to meet its P1 to P3 class-size reduction commitments, broken down by local authority.

S3W-11347 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive how much additional expenditure will be required to meet the staffing costs of implementing its P1 to P3 class-size reduction policy, broken down by local authority.

S3W-11348 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive how much additional expenditure will be required to meet the capital costs of implementing its P1 to P3 class-size reduction policy, broken down by local authority.

S3W-11349 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance it has provided to local authorities on the provision of free personal care.

S3W-11350 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive which local authorities have bid for a share of the £15 million matched funding to create sustainable travel demonstration communities.

S3W-11351 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to introduce matched funding for transport projects, in addition to the £15 million for sustainable travel demonstration communities.

S3W-11352 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the Ravenscraig partnership regarding the opportunity to include renewable energy, microgeneration and combined heat and power in the plans for the redevelopment of the Ravenscraig site.

S3W-11353 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding it intends to make available to urban regeneration companies in each year from 2008-09 to 2010-11.

S3W-11354 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive by what criteria it judges applications for funding from urban regeneration companies.

S3W-11355 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what financial support it has given to urban regeneration companies (URCs) in each year from 2002-03 to 2008-09, broken down by URC.

S3W-11356 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what sources of public funding area are available to public/private partnerships involved in urban regeneration projects.

S3W-11357 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made of the contribution that the planned development of the Ravenscraig site will make to the Scottish Government’s stated strategic objectives.
