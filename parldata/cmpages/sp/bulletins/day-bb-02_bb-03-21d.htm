Business Bulletin No. 49/2002

Thursday 21 March 2002

Section D : Oral Questions
  

Question Time

1. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether cancer patients have sufficient opportunities to participate in clinical trials for new drugs and therapies. (S1O-4972)

2. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Eastern Link Road bypassing Kincardine will be completed and when work will commence on the new Kincardine Bridge. (S1O-4963)

3. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has identified any adverse implications for economic development following the freezing of the contract for the building of an en route air traffic control centre in Prestwick. (S1O-4988)

4. Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in establishing an organisation to replace Disability Scotland. (S1O-4992)

5. Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking in order to monitor the implementation of the Transport (Scotland) Act 2001. (S1O-4950)

6. Elaine Thomson: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will support community safety projects in order to make progress on reducing crime and reducing the fear of crime. (S1O-4986)

7. John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is supporting the bid by Inverness and the Highlands to be European Capital of Culture in 2008. (S1O-4966)

8. Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the level of salaries paid to nursery teachers is adequate. (S1O-4974)

9. Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive what economic assessment has been undertaken in order to evaluate the impact of Section 9(2)(a) of the Land Reform (Scotland) Bill. (S1O-4971)

10. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will introduce any changes to legislation on freshwater fisheries in order to improve public access to angling on rivers and lochs. (S1O-4968)

11. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made on electoral reform for local government. (S1O-4994)

12. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking in order to support voluntary organisations and projects. (S1O-4980)

13. Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what facilities there are for dealing with the removal of ozone-depleting substances from domestic refrigerators and freezers as required under EC Regulation 2037/2000. (S1O-4984)

14. Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive when the findings of the report of the expert group on MMR will be made public. (S1O-4956)

15. Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to improve the assessment and provision of education services for young people with special educational needs. (S1O-4969)

16. Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will next have discussions with the Department for Work and Pensions. (S1O-4955)

17. Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking in order to tackle financial exclusion and ensure that everyone has access to basic financial services. (S1O-4979)

18. Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it has taken in order to reduce waiting times for tonsillectomies. (S1O-4996)

19. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making towards reaching its goals on community regeneration. (S1O-4989)

20. Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many community nurses, midwives and health visitors have yet to receive a mobile phone, hands-free kit and emergency software under the initiative announced by the Scottish Office on 22 March 1999. (S1O-4961)

21. Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive what new provisions it is making for respite and palliative care for children. (S1O-4981)

22. Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it aims to ensure that looked after young people receive adequate levels of care and support on leaving the care system. (S1O-4987)

23. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to support Gaelic-speaking teachers and enable them to teach their subject in Gaelic. (S1O-4975)

24. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken in respect of giving the victims of crime a greater role in the criminal justice system. (S1O-4983)

25. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will comment on the report by the Auditor General for Scotland in Appendix 13 of the Scottish Prison Service’s Annual Report and Accounts 2000-01. (S1O-4960)

26. Mr Keith Raffan: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is tackling abuse of volatile substances. (S1O-4962)

27. Janis Hughes: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to increase community engagement in public services in order to ensure that such services are meeting the needs of communities. (S1O-4976)

28. Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action will be taken to secure continued manufacturing at the Rolls Royce plant in Hillington, Glasgow. (S1O-4952)

29. Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is promoting breakfast clubs. (S1O-4973)

30. Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking in order to encourage domestic energy conservation. (S1O-4997)
