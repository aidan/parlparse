Business Bulletin No. 82/2008: Tuesday 20 May 2008
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 19 May 2008

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-13151 Stuart McMillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on findings of recent studies in Germany and the United States showing that children are at least 14% to 21% more likely to have leukaemia if they live near to a nuclear power plant.

S3W-13152Δ Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will announce its intentions for the Scottish Futures Trust initiative.

S3W-13153 John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive what contingency plans it has in place to protect the public from food shortages.

S3W-13154 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive how community nurses will work with condition-specific nurse specialists in the community health nurse pilot in NHS Tayside.

S3W-13155 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects that the findings of the review of specialist nursing roles being undertaken by its Health Directorate General will be published.

S3W-13156 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will commence its review of the Scotland-wide free bus travel scheme for older and disabled people.

S3W-13157 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to publish the report on its review of the Scotland-wide free bus travel scheme for older and disabled people.

S3W-13158 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive what the total cost to local authorities of collecting council tax has been in each of the last three years.

S3W-13159 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive what the total cost to (a) Dundee City, (b) Angus and (c) Aberdeenshire councils for collecting council tax has been in each of the last three years.

S3W-13160 Jack McConnell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many young people have participated in Project Scotland schemes in each of the last four years.

S3W-13161 Jack McConnell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many, and what percentage of, young people who participated in Project Scotland schemes in each of the last four years went on to full-time or part-time employment.

S3W-13162 Jack McConnell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many, and what percentage of, young people who participated in Project Scotland schemes in each of the last four years went on to training or further education.

S3W-13163 Jack McConnell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many young people have applied to participate in Project Scotland schemes in each of the last four years.

S3W-13164 Jack McConnell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many organisations in Scotland have benefited from having a Project Scotland volunteer in each of the last four years.

S3W-13165 Jack McConnell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many, and what percentage of, young people who participated in Project Scotland schemes in each of the last four years have not found full-time or part-time employment or have not gone on to training or further education following their placement.

S3W-13166 Jack McConnell: To ask the Scottish Executive what the estimated benefit to the economy is of Project Scotland and whether ministers consider that benefit to be worth maintaining.

S3W-13167 Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive in which year the highest number of central heating systems was installed under the central heating programme since the programme started, combining figures for both private and public sector accommodation.

S3W-13168 Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the total number of central heating systems installed under the central heating programme in 2005-06 was higher than in 2006-07, combining the figures for both private and public sector accommodation.

S3W-13169 Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers nucleic acid testing of blood donations to be a higher standard of test than a serological test.

S3W-13170 Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is considering adopting nucleic acid testing for all blood donations as its standard test to meet all statutory and regulatory requirements and what the reasons are for its position on the matter.

S3W-13171 Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers it to be important for any nucleic acid blood testing regime to specify both hepatitis C virus and HIV if it is to be used to test blood supplies in Scotland.

S3W-13172 Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on why the current invitation to tender for the provision of nucleic acid testing, which caters for the national blood services in England and Wales and Scotland, only specifies a requirement to test for the presence of hepatitis C.

S3W-13173 Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied that only specifying a requirement to test for hepatitis C in its invitation to tender for its provision of nucleic acid blood testing does not constitute a lowering of standards, given that current nucleic acid testing currently also specifies a requirement to test for HIV.

S3W-13174 Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive what improvements have been made to the blood testing regime in Scotland over the last 10 years and what consequent amendments have been made to blood donation guidelines.

S3W-13175 Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that adopting a nucleic acid testing system as its standard test regime would constitute reasonable grounds for the relevant bodies to reassess the current blanket ban on gay men donating blood.

S3W-13176 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to issue guidance to NHS boards on managing sickness and absence rates.

S3W-13177 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has undertaken any comparisons between sickness and absence rates in NHS boards and in other public sector organisations in the areas covered by boards.

S3W-13178 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has undertaken any analysis of sickness and absence rates in NHS boards and how these compare with local deprivation statistics.

S3W-13179 Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive how much electricity was generated by all generators in 2007.

S3W-13180 Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive how much electricity was generated by coal in 2007.

S3W-13181 Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive how much electricity was generated by oil in 2007.

S3W-13182 Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive how much electricity was generated by gas in 2007.

S3W-13183 Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive how much electricity was nuclear generated in 2007.

S3W-13184 Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive how much electricity was generated by (a) hydro natural flow and (b) hydro pump storage in 2007.

S3W-13185 Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive how much electricity was generated by (a) thermal and (b) non-thermal renewables in 2007.

S3W-13186 Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive how much electricity was generated by waste in 2007.

S3W-13187 Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the total losses for the transmission and distribution of electricity were in 2007.

S3W-13188 Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive how much electricity was used in pump storage in 2007.

S3W-13189 Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive how much electricity was consumed in Scotland in 2007.

S3W-13190 Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive how much electricity was exported in 2007.

S3W-13191 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive what the national concessionary fares budget is in each financial year in the period 2008 to 2011 and what these annual amounts are expressed in real terms.

S3W-13192 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive what the total cost of policing in Tayside has been in each of the last three years for which information is available.

S3W-13193 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive how many primary fires there have been in Tayside in each of the last three years for which information is available.

S3W-13194 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made on the additional facilities that will be required to deliver two hours of PE per week to every pupil in each local authority in the Lothians.

S3W-13195 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many affordable homes for sale will be built in the Lothians in (a) 2009, (b) 2010 and (c) 2011, broken down by local authority.

S3W-13196 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how much it has invested in housing in each parliamentary constituency in the Lothians in each year since 1999.

S3W-13197 Aileen Campbell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many no-fines houses were constructed through Communities Scotland and its predecessor organisations in each year for which records exist.

S3W-13198 Aileen Campbell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many no-fines houses constructed through Communities Scotland and its predecessor organisations have been improved or refurbished in each year for which records exist, broken down by local authority area.

S3W-13199 Aileen Campbell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many no-fines houses originally constructed through Communities Scotland and its predecessor organisations have been transferred to (a) housing association or (b) private ownership in each year for which records exist, broken down by local authority area.

S3W-13200 Aileen Campbell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many operational train stations there are, broken down by parliamentary region.

S3W-13201 Aileen Campbell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many closed or unoperational train stations there are, broken down by parliamentary region.

S3W-13203 Hugh O’Donnell: To ask the Scottish Executive what specific arrangements are in place for local authorities to maintain teacher numbers in the face of falling school rolls, as set out in the concordat with local government.

S3W-13204 Hugh O’Donnell: To ask the Scottish Executive what estimates have been made of school rolls over the period of the current spending review.

S3W-13205 Hugh O’Donnell: To ask the Scottish Executive what future workforce needs assessment of teaching numbers has been undertaken.

S3W-13206 Hugh O’Donnell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many students are undergoing teacher training.

S3W-13207 Hugh O’Donnell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many newly qualified teachers are completing their probationary years.

S3W-13208 Hugh O’Donnell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many permanent teaching posts it expects to be available for the 2008-09 academic year.

S3W-13209 Hugh O’Donnell: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to increase the number of permanent teaching posts in special education institutions.

S3W-13210 Hugh O’Donnell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has undertaken a future workforce needs assessment for special education institutions.

S3W-13211 Malcolm Chisholm: To ask the Scottish Executive when the next meeting of the Scotland-Malawi Joint Commission will be held and who will attend.

S3W-13212 Malcolm Chisholm: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will publish the remit for its current review of the International Development Fund projects in Malawi.

S3W-13213 Malcolm Chisholm: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it will provide to Scottish Language Dictionaries when funding from the Scottish Arts Council is discontinued at the end of 2008-09.

S3W-13214 Malcolm Chisholm: To ask the Scottish Executive what capital investment it will provide to the national industrial museums.

S3W-13215 Malcolm Chisholm: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list any national indicators and targets that relate to support for the arts by local authorities.

S3W-13216 Malcolm Chisholm: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will review the level of funding support for drama courses, in view of funding shortfalls for such courses at Queen Margaret University and the Royal Scottish Academy of Music and Drama.
