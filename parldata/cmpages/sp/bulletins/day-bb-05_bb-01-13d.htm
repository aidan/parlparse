Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 20 January 2005

Question Time

Enterprise, Lifelong Learning and Transport

1. Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the UK Department for Transport concerning direct rail links between Scotland and mainland Europe. (S2O-5029)

2. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied with the proposed arrangements for public consultation about plans for congestion charging for Edinburgh. (S2O-5022)

3. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to improve productivity and efficiency in both the private and public sectors. (S2O-4993)

4. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with stakeholders on a proposed Rosyth to Norway ferry service. (S2O-4950)

5. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive whether universities are within the scope of the Nature Conservation (Scotland) Act 2004. (S2O-5027)

6. Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it and its agencies are taking to prevent Stoddard’s factory in Kilmarnock from closing. (S2O-4959)

7. Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to further reduce carbon emissions and air pollutants from road transport. (S2O-5016)

8. Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive what part the Minister for Enterprise and Lifelong Learning will play in ensuring that Scottish Enterprise delivers environmentally-sustainable development. (S2O-5024)

9. Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what further sections of the A9 it plans to upgrade and whether other sections will be upgraded to dual carriageway status or to "two plus one" on a similar basis to those sections recently completed. (S2O-4914)

10. Christine May: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will help improve training and skills in the transport industry. (S2O-5015)

11. Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has sought legal advice on the judicial review sought by Midlothian, West Lothian and Fife councils in respect of City of Edinburgh Council’s congestion charging scheme. (S2O-4982)

12. Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive how many quality contracts have been agreed with bus operators since the implementation of the Transport (Scotland) Act 2001. (S2O-4990)

Justice and Law Officers

1. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will publish a list of fixed speed camera sites and the detailed safety case for each site. (S2O-4958)

2. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to increase the number of secure accommodation places for young offenders. (S2O-4961)

3. Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what priority it gives to community policing. (S2O-4937)

4. Chris Ballance: To ask the Scottish Executive how many cases are currently awaiting hearings following decisions by the Scottish Criminal Cases Review Commission. (S2O-5005)

5. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it remains concerned about the danger of drinks being spiked in pubs and nightclubs. (S2O-4983)

6. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will now establish an independent police complaints commission. (S2O-5008)

7. Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will take a decision on the introduction of football banning orders. (S2O-4974)

8. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prosecutions there have been over the last year for offences related to football matches. (S2O-4916)

9. Mr John Swinney: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will publish its consultation document on reform of the system regulating the conduct of solicitors. (S2O-4930)

10. Mr Alasdair Morrison: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to make offenders compensate victims of crime. (S2O-4980)

11. Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether funding for drug treatment and rehabilitation services is being used effectively. (S2O-5011)

12. Mark Ballard: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average time is for a case which has been referred to the courts by the Scottish Criminal Cases Review Commission to come before the courts. (S2O-5006)

General

1. Mark Ballard: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it supports the use of fair trade products in sport. (S2O-5030)

2. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of dental graduates remain in Scotland to practise dentistry. (S2O-5018)

3. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is tackling the shortage of affordable housing in North East Fife. (S2O-4949)

4. Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in improving audiology services. (S2O-5014)

5. Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it intends to improve leadership in schools. (S2O-5003)

6. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive what proportion of primary school pupils has received, or is currently receiving, music lessons. (S2O-5031)

7. Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to identify and fill any gaps in the current understanding of the role of Scottish peatlands in sequestering carbon implicated in climate change. (S2O-5034)

8. Colin Fox: To ask the Scottish Executive whether a risk evaluation has been made of any threat to patients’ lives posed by the two-hour power failure on the night of 6 and 7 January 2005 at Edinburgh’s New Royal Infirmary. (S2O-4967)

9. Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it plans to take to increase the number of artificial turf football pitches. (S2O-4935)

10. Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive what feedback it has received in relation to the operation of NHS 24 over the Christmas and New Year period. (S2O-4927)

11. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in providing free dental and eye checks for all. (S2O-4999)

12. Susan Deacon: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is improving the health of children. (S2O-5002)

13. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take to promote learning and education among the most deprived communities. (S2O-4938)

14. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider extending the Fresh Talent Initiative to include non-EU students who are in Scotland on placements or internships as part of their studies. (S2O-4976)

15. Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive what the timescale and terms of reference are for its assessment of the impact of the proposed wind farm on the Lewis Special Protected Area. (S2O-5019)
