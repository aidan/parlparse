Business Bulletin No. 4/2003

Thursday 9 January 2003

Section D : Oral Questions selected for answer on 16 January 2003
  

Question Time

1. Ian Jenkins: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it is providing for the development of sports facilities that are useable over the winter months. (S1O-6231)

2. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive when the next meeting of the Cabinet Sub-committee on Sustainable Scotland will be held and what will be discussed. (S1O-6237)

3. Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are being taken to expand education in sustainable development. (S1O-6249)

4. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had, or is currently having, with the Border Railways Forum. (S1O-6230)

5. Elaine Thomson: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it has made in tackling issues that affect the quality of people's lives such as litter and abandoned cars. (S1O-6254)

6. Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in tackling hospital-acquired infections in the NHS. (S1O-6225)

7. Janis Hughes: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are necessary to promote a healthier Scotland. (S1O-6248)

8. Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to reverse the overall increase in the percentage of school leavers from publicly-funded schools that, since 1998-99, have not entered training, employment or full-time higher or further education. (S1O-6215)

9. Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will extend the free off-peak local bus travel scheme to provide a single scheme enabling older people to travel free throughout Scotland. (S1O-6242)

10. George Lyon: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is addressing any concerns regarding landowner liability under access provisions in the Land Reform (Scotland) Bill. (S1O-6232)

11. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any concerns with regard to the level of take-up of legal aid in respect of employment tribunals. (S1O-6217)

12. Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs in relation to the operation of the British cattle movement service. (S1O-6223)

13. Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take to promote the European Year of People with Disabilities. (S1O-6233)

14. Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it supports the medication of the water supply with fluoride. (S1O-6212)

15. Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Minister for Education and Young People last met representatives of the teaching unions. (S1O-6222)

16. Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met Angus Council to discuss the A92 Arbroath-Dundee dualling project and what issues were raised. (S1O-6218)

17. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will prioritise high levels of investment in public services. (S1O-6253)

18. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to improve standards of care in the NHS. (S1O-6229)

19. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to prevent youth crime. (S1O-6245)

20. Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to increase the number of cycle trips taken in the light of the targets of the National Cycling Strategy not being met. (S1O-6236)

21. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to support young people who run away from home or residential care. (S1O-6244)

22. Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive when it intends to announce specific measures to tackle the falling population. (S1O-6221)

23. Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will undertake a review of the operation of the first four months of NorthLink Orkney and Shetland Ferries Ltd's contract to provide ferry services to Orkney and Shetland. (S1O-6234)

24. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact any use of the tax-varying powers under Part IV of the Scotland Act 1998 would have on the economy and employment and poverty levels. (S1O-6252)

25. Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the Home Office or the relevant local authorities about the proposed accommodation centre for asylum seekers at RAF Turnhouse. (S1O-6214)

26. Bill Aitken: To ask the Scottish Executive what disciplinary action was taken against prisoners involved in the disturbance at HM Prison Shotts in April 2002. (S1O-6226)

27. David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive how the future development of the Crichton campus will be funded. (S1O-6228)

28. Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made by Schlumberger Sema through eTourism Ltd with visitscotland.com. (S1O-6219)

29. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive why the number of hospital-acquired infections has risen as reported by the Scottish Centre for Infection and Environmental Health. (S1O-6216)

30. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will issue a consultation document on minimum sentencing for the possession of firearms. (S1O-6243)
