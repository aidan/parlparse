Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-4352 Eleanor Scott: Community Woodlands—That the Parliament congratulates Culag Community Woodland Trust on the opening of its All-Abilities Path at Little Assynt, Sutherland; commends its vision to provide access for all people to enjoy the woodlands and go angling in the lochs; recognises the hard work by local people, including many volunteers, to raise funds and develop these facilities; welcomes the support of the many funders of the trust; believes that Culag Community Woodland Trust is a great example, demonstrating the social, health and economic as well as environmental benefits of community woodlands; notes that there are now over 100 community woodland organisations throughout Scotland, a national Community Woodlands Association and many thousands of people participating in and gaining from this movement, and urges the Scottish Executive to ensure that community woods are given more support for their important role in rural development.

*S2M-4350 Karen Gillon: Congratulations to Graeme Dott—That the Parliament congratulates Larkhall’s Graeme Dott, the new World Snooker Champion, on his incredible victory in the final of the World Snooker Championship in the Crucible Theatre and hopes that Graeme’s success will inspire young people throughout Larkhall and Scotland to participate in sports and, hopefully, some day become Scottish world champions themselves.

*S2M-4349# Helen Eadie: NHS Fife - UK Example of Best Practice in Rheumatic Diseases—That the Parliament notes that inflammatory arthritis covers a number of chronic and debilitating diseases of which rheumatoid arthritis is the most common and that these conditions turn everyday activities into painful and almost insurmountable challenges; welcomes the National Rheumatoid Arthritis Society’s "Everyday Everests" campaign in Scotland which raises awareness of the issues faced by rheumatoid arthritis patients; recognises that over 52,000 people in Scotland are living with the condition; is encouraged by the development of innovative new medicines which can transform the lives of patients and their families; congratulates NHS Fife, which has been highlighted as having a rheumatology disease unit which is an example of best practice within the United Kingdom as the rheumatology unit has organised its staffing resources well by employing physiotherapists, podiatrists, occupational nurses, specialist nurses and psychologists, providing easy access and sharing of offices with consultants so that patient case conferences are simplified; is gratified that the UK National Rheumatoid Arthritis Society has highlighted the Fife Rheumatology Disease Unit as an example of best practice in the UK, and hopes that the Scottish Executive will take action to ensure that this best practice is shared with other NHS boards and that all patients in Scotland can benefit from the best and most up-to-date treatments.

*S2M-4348 Mr Brian Monteith: Football Sponsorship—That the Parliament recognises that commercial sponsorship of sporting events has a natural lifespan which means that it will evolve, change and end; notes that the Bank of Scotland’s sponsorship of the Scottish Premier League is to end after the 2006-07 season after nine successful years with an investment of £17 million; welcomes the Bank of Scotland’s continued support through major new investment of £1.5 million in grassroots football over the next three years; notes that 140,000 young people are expected to benefit, and wishes the Bank of Scotland and boys’ and girls’ football clubs every success in this new initiative.

*S2M-4347 Frances Curran: Aberdeen University Staff Threatened with Pay Stoppage—That the Parliament is appalled at the threat made by Professor Stephen Logan, Senior Vice Principal of the University of Aberdeen, to stop entirely the pay and pension contributions of all staff taking part in the present industrial action short of a strike; considers that this threat, conveyed by Professor Logan in an email to all staff, would constitute an unlawful deduction of wages if carried out; supports the AUT, NATFHE and EIS members in their campaign for better wages and conditions; considers that the union members were forced to take industrial action by the failure of the employers to respond to their pay claim; commends the university staff for the goodwill displayed with regard to carrying out unpaid overtime to ensure that students’ assessments are marked on time and regards the draconian response to the industrial action as seriously undermining this goodwill; believes that if this pay stoppage goes ahead the unions would be fully justified in stepping up their action to all-out strike, and demands that the Court of the University of Aberdeen refuses to authorise the pay stoppage and forces the employers to engage in constructive and unconditional pay talks.

Supported by: Rosie Kane*, Carolyn Leckie*, Tommy Sheridan*

*S2M-4346 Fiona Hyslop: Breastfeeding Awareness Week and WHO Survey on Ideal Baby Weights—That the Parliament notes that 14 to 20 May 2006 is Breastfeeding Awareness Week; welcomes the publication of the recent World Health Organisation (WHO) survey of thousands of babies in a number of countries, tracking the baby weights of breastfed babies; notes the implication that, historically, breastfed babies’ weights in Scotland have been charted against bottle-fed babies’ ideal weights, which have been heavier; recognises that this overestimate can lead to unnecessary supplements of formula milk for some breastfed babies, which can lead later to obesity, and might cause some mothers to stop breastfeeding because of mistaken worries about perceived low baby weights, and calls on the Scottish Executive to take measures to prioritise replacing the current charts in baby development books with the more appropriate tracking charts for breastfed babies at the earliest opportunity and to promote breastfeeding.

*S2M-4345 Des McNulty: Justice for Mesothelioma Sufferers—That the Parliament expresses grave concern at the House of Lords ruling on 3 May 2006 which means that compensation for mesothelioma sufferers will be cut and supports Lord Rodger’s judgement that, no matter where an individual contracted the disease, they should receive a full payment, reflecting practice in the Scottish courts.

*S2M-4344# Mr Ted Brocklebank: Heritage Site Status for St Andrews—That the Parliament supports a campaign to encourage the UK Government to obtain from UNESCO World Heritage Site status for St Andrews; recognises that St Andrews is the home of golf, the world’s fastest-growing sport; further recognises that it is also the historic ecclesiastical capital of Scotland; believes that its cultural and educational contributions, in particular those of Scotland’s oldest university, should be formally acknowledged; notes that St Andrews is regarded worldwide as unique, not only for its medieval town centre but also for its captivating and award-winning natural features; considers that the Scottish Executive should support St Andrews as an outstanding Scottish candidate for the UK Tentative List, and further notes that a consultation with every household in St Andrews showed 99% support for this move, as well as support from St Andrews Community Council, the University of St Andrews, the Royal and Ancient Golf Club of St Andrews, the St Andrews Links Trust, St Andrews Preservation Trust, Scottish Enterprise Fife, the St Andrews Green Belt Forum, Action of Churches Together in St Andrews and cross-party political support from distinguished St Andrews graduates, including Lord Alton, Lord Forsyth, Mark Lazarowicz MP, Alex Salmond MP and many others.

Supported by: Nora Radcliffe*, Mrs Nanette Milne*, Rob Gibson*, Colin Fox*, Mr Adam Ingram*, Murdo Fraser*, Robin Harper*

*S2M-4343 Linda Fabiani: Heartstart UK Session in Scottish Parliament—That the Parliament welcomes the British Heart Foundation’s Heartstart UK training session in emergency life support (ELS) techniques for cross-party members of the Parliament and their staff; notes that Heartstart UK is a training course offering basic instruction in ELS techniques, including cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR), and that taking part in a two-hour basic course ensures that participants know what to do in a life-threatening emergency; remains concerned that, despite the significant fall in mortality from coronary heart disease in Scotland in the last decade, heart and circulatory disease is still Scotland’s biggest killer; is encouraged that evidence shows that CPR provided by a bystander more than doubles a victim’s chance of survival; agrees with BHF Scotland that every child leaving school in Scotland should know what to do in a life-threatening emergency, and urges the Scottish Executive to work with the Heartstart UK team with a view to expanding the provision of affiliated schemes in schools across the country.

*S2M-4342 Mr Jamie McGrigor: Congratulations to Scotland’s Latest World Champion—That the Parliament notes that the final of the 2006 World Snooker Championship between Scotland’s Graeme Dott and England’s Peter Ebdon was the longest final in Crucible history; congratulates Graeme Dott on his 18-14 victory in the championship, and hopes that the success of Scots players at the world championships in this decade and the last will encourage World Snooker to look at the possibility of holding the world championships in Scotland in the future.
