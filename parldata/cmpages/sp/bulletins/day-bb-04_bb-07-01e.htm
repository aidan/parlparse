Business Bulletin No. 114 / 2004

Thursday 1st July 2004

Section E : Written questions lodged on Wednesday 30th June 2004
  

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-9460 Carolyn Leckie: To ask the Scottish Executive how much the NHS has spent on external consultancy in each of the last five years, broken down by NHS board.

S2W-9461 Carolyn Leckie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the cost of cardiothoractic surgical beds has been in (a) the Golden Jubilee National Hospital and (b) all other hospitals where cardiothoracic surgery is carried out in each year since 2002.

S2W-9462 Carolyn Leckie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the operating costs per patient have been at the Golden Jubilee National Hospital in each year since 2002.

S2W-9463 Carolyn Leckie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the operating costs of each surgical patient have been in each year since 1999, broken down by NHS board.

S2W-9464 Carolyn Leckie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether its national review of the early years and childcare workforce will seek, and include, input from the Equal Opportunities Commission.

S2W-9465 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-8861 by Nicol Stephen on 24 June 2004, when the (a) current and (b) next franchise agreement will be placed in the Scottish Parliament Information Centre.

S2W-9466 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-9063 by Nicol Stephen on 29 June 2004, whether it will publish its evaluation of HITRANS' proposals for a Highlands and Islands air network; what the estimated cost is of the work commissioned from AviaSolutions, and by what date AviaSolutions must report.

S2W-9467 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will review the prescribing of selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRI), in light of research published by the Scottish Association for Mental Health which states that SSRI-type antidepressants were outperformed on all measures by older and cheaper tricyclic and related drugs.

S2W-9468 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will review the prescribing of paroxetine, Seroxat, in light of research by the Scottish Association for Mental Health which states that Seroxat performed badly compared to other selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors.

S2W-9469 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will review the use of depot drugs for psychiatric patients, given the overall negative rating for this drug group referred to in research by the Scottish Association for Mental Health.

S2W-9470 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will review the prescribing of antidepressants, following the report by the Scottish Association for Mental Health, All you need to know? - Scottish Survey of People's Experience of Psychiatric Drugs.

S2W-9471 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to set up detoxification clinics for people seeking to stop taking prescribed psychiatric medication.

S2W-9472 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will ensure that people receiving psychiatric drugs are fully aware of the potential side effects and given appropriate written information.

S2W-9473 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how it can ensure that people (a) stopping and (b) reducing their intake of psychiatric drugs are given adequate and appropriate healthcare support and advice.

S2W-9474Δ Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Scottish Children's Reporter Administration's Corporate Plan for 2004-06 has been approved.

S2W-9475 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-8934 by Mr Andy Kerr on 23 June 2004, what proportion of the assumed council tax collection rates for each local authority consists of arrears from previous years.

S2W-9476 Miss Annabel Goldie: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-7860 by Cathy Jamieson on 20 May 2004, whether staff from HM Prison Kilmarnock were involved in the transportation of prisoners to and from court prior to the implementation of the Reliance contract.
