Business Bulletin No. 85/2002

Thursday 23 May 2002

Section D : Oral Questions
  

Question Time

1. Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to carry out a safety review of the A9 south of Perth. (S1O-5247)

2. Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to work with communities in order to promote social inclusion. (S1O-5259)

3. George Lyon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the new GP contracts currently under negotiation at UK level could allow GPs to opt out of providing out-of-hours cover in Scotland and to what extent it is involved in the negotiations. (S1O-5278)

4. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive what further measures are being taken to improve health and tackle cancer and heart disease. (S1O-5297)

5. Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will conclude its consideration of raising the age of criminal responsibility. (S1O-5255)

6. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it supports the proposal to build a waste incinerator in Aberdeen. (S1O-5250)

7. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made of the impact of deferred entry to schools on local authority education budgets. (S1O-5292)

8. Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to improve the provision of criminal justice social work services. (S1O-5267)

9. Janis Hughes: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to rebuild the infrastructure of the NHS. (S1O-5288)

10. Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on the introduction of the new Teacher Induction Scheme. (S1O-5268)

11. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in implementing an all-age careers guidance service throughout Scotland. (S1O-5275)

12. Elaine Thomson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action will be taken to ensure that payments in respect of insurance claims following damage to listed buildings are used to pay for the restoration work. (S1O-5296)

13. Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will reconsider its policy on the provision of financial support for lifeline air services to islands in the light of evidence set out in the comparative study of public service obligations in Europe by Cranfield University. (S1O-5261)

14. Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to encourage seine net fishing. (S1O-5257)

15. Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are any benefits from direct grant investment in the voluntary sector. (S1O-5298)

16. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to create NHS foundation trusts. (S1O-5285)

17. Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans there are to review the provision of ambulances in Angus. (S1O-5287)

18. Bill Aitken: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it plans to take to reduce the number of people admitted to prisons for non-payment of fines. (S1O-5282)

19. Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will ensure that the Saltire is always flown from buildings in the care of Historic Scotland. (S1O-5249)

20. Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken in order to encourage a wider range of candidates to come forward for public appointments. (S1O-5291)

21. Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking in order to increase physical activity amongst older people. (S1O-5266)

22. Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to review its policy on hospital-acquired infection in the light of the outbreak of winter vomiting at Raigmore Hospital. (S1O-5294)

23. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what consultation it has undertaken with women about equality issues. (S1O-5260)

24. David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive what decision it has reached on which first-round funding bids to improve school buildings have been successful. (S1O-5281)

25. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made with regard to delayed discharge. (S1O-5295)

26. Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive whether, in the light of recent press articles, its position on not reviewing local authority boundaries in the foreseeable future has changed. (S1O-5258)

27. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive when the review of the method by which funds are distributed between police forces will conclude. (S1O-5273)

28. Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive, in the light of the theft of osprey eggs in Perthshire, when it will introduce legislation to strengthen the laws on wildlife crime. (S1O-5252)

29. Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any responsibilities in respect of the location of police force headquarters. (S1O-5262)

30. Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent discussions it has had with Dumfries and Galloway Tourist Board. (S1O-5279)
