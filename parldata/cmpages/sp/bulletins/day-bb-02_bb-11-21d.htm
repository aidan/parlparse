Business Bulletin No. 169/2002

Thursday 21 November 2002

Section D : Oral Questions selected for answer on 28 November 2002

  

Question Time

1. John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans for the removal of the council tax rebate on second homes. (S1O-5973)

2. Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to make any changes to the law on sexual offences as a result of changes announced in the Queen’s Speech. (S1O-5990)

3. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the implications for farmers and fishermen will be of the decision by the European Commission to refrain from further action against France in respect of the verdict of the European Court of Justice that France was guilty of illegal restrictions on the importation of British beef over a three-year period. (S1O-5956)

4. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any concerns regarding the future provision of further education in the Borders. (S1O-5983)

5. Mr Alasdair Morrison: To ask the Scottish Executive how it ensures that all people in the Highlands and Islands have a say in the development of transport services. (S1O-5997)

6. Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to its letter to me of 11 November 2002, whether it is its intention to consult local authorities on the abolition of capital receipts set-aside rules as announced by the Minister for Social Justice in her ministerial statement on housing on 7 November 2002 or to abolish these rules as indicated by the Deputy Minister for Finance and Public Services during the debate on quality of life later that day. (S1O-5959)

7. Mr Duncan Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are sufficient air links to the Highlands and Islands. (S1O-5962)

8. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are being taken to tackle stalking and harassment. (S1O-5995)

9. Cathie Craigie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take to improve services for children at risk of abuse and neglect. (S1O-6001)

10. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it called in the planning application for the development within the native School woodland at Nethy Bridge or referred it back to The Highland Council and what the reasons are for its position on this matter. (S1O-6012)

11. Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to provide a unified approach to flood prevention in the River Clyde basin. (S1O-5987)

12. Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is able to introduce a minimum wage for the NHS in Scotland set at a higher rate than the UK statutory minimum wage and, if so, whether it intends to do so. (S1O-5994)

13. Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made on its creative industry strategy. (S1O-5981)

14. Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will give an up-to-date assessment of the contingency arrangements for the fire services. (S1O-5964)

15. Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance has been issued to local authorities regarding the implementation of free personal care. (S1O-5991)

16. Miss Annabel Goldie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there is now provision of foreign language instruction in every primary school. (S1O-5965)

17. Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to abolish council tenants’ right to buy their properties. (S1O-5988)

18. Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is marking the UN International Day for the Elimination of Violence Against Women. (S1O-6000)

19. Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive what response it is making in respect of the proportion of S2 pupils that did not meet the required grades in reading, writing and mathematics. (S1O-5957)

20. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive to what extent social inclusion is tied to the economic agenda for the regeneration of the River Clyde waterfront and the Glasgow Harbour development. (S1O-6010)

21. Mr Tom McCabe: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions have taken place with local enterprise companies regarding improvements to the provision of child care. (S1O-6011)

22. Elaine Thomson: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in ensuring access to NHS dentistry services. (S1O-5982)

23. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to celebrate St Andrew’s Day. (S1O-5985)

24. Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it provides to single parents. (S1O-5975)

25. Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it plans to take to support the Shetland economy in the light of job losses at the Sullom Voe terminal. (S1O-5974)

26. Ms Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has held any discussions with, or issued any guidelines to, NHS trusts regarding the inclusion in public private partnership agreements of hospital ancillary facilities such as parking, television and telephones. (S1O-5976)

27. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what additional statutory and non-statutory support it intends to put in place to assist vulnerable families. (S1O-5980)

28. Brian Fitzpatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration it is giving to amending the criminal law to permit criminal proceedings to be brought against persons previously acquitted of serious criminal charges where compelling new evidence has become available. (S1O-5967)

29. Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to monitor the effectiveness of victim information and advice services. (S1O-6004)

30. Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to introduce a requirement for oncologists to undertake training and placements in hospices. (S1O-5958)
