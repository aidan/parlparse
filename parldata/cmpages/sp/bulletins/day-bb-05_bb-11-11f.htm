Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (u ).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-3557 Ms Wendy Alexander: Congratulations to St Mirren Football Club—That the Parliament recognises the tremendous achievement of St Mirren Football Club in winning the Scottish Challenge Cup and pays tribute to Gus MacPherson and his team for bringing the cup home to Paisley; further recognises the ambitious plans of St Mirren Football Club as it makes progress towards a new stadium; wishes the team well in the First Division for the remainder of the year, and hopes that its continuing success will allow the team and stadium to grace in due course the highest heights of the Scottish Premier League.

*S2M-3556# Cathy Peattie: Deanburn Primary School—That the Parliament congratulates all those involved in the creation of the eco-friendly Deanburn Primary School in Bo’ness; notes that features include grass roofs, its own wind turbine, a rainwater-collection system and "breathing walls" to reduce the need for artificial ventilation, and believes that the Scottish Executive should encourage others to adopt an innovative and environmentally friendly approach to designing and upgrading buildings.

*S2M-3555 Alex Neil: Compensation for Hearing Loss for Ex-Servicemen and Women—That the Parliament notes that around 90% of those ex-servicemen and women who apply for compensation due to having suffered hearing loss because of their service are refused such compensation; calls on Her Majesty’s Government to reduce the threshold for compensation from its present level of 50 decibels to 35 decibels, in line with other countries such as the United States of America and the Republic of Ireland, and commends the Royal National Institute for Deaf People for its campaign on this issue.

*S2M-3554 Helen Eadie: Congratulations to Remploy—That the Parliament congratulates Remploy on supporting 3,500 disabled people into employment in 2004-05, on its progressive approach to the learning and development of disabled people, on the access to learning provided by Remploy which offers disabled people more choice in their careers, on its work to provide a union learning representative in every factory and on the work of its recruitment division, Interwork, in supporting disabled people into mainstream employment; affirms a commitment to learning, equality of choice in employment and full social inclusion of disabled people, and calls on the Scottish Executive to explore with Remploy opportunities to employ increased numbers of disabled people.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie*

*S2M-3553u Jackie Baillie: The Thistle Travel Card Scheme—That the Parliament congratulates Enable, together with a consortium of Scottish charities including Down’s Syndrome Scotland and Alzheimer Scotland, for its work to introduce the Thistle Travel Card; welcomes the support of the Scottish Executive and transport companies for the implementation of the scheme, including in Dumbarton, Vale of Leven and Helensburgh; acknowledges that many people with learning or physical disabilities, as well as those with dementia and epilepsy, may experience some difficulty in using public transport; considers that the Thistle Travel Card provides them with additional assistance by alerting travel staff to their particular needs; notes that awareness of the scheme is limited, and considers that the Scottish Executive and transport companies should actively promote awareness and uptake of the Thistle Travel Card.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie*

*S2M-3548# Mrs Margaret Ewing: Excess Winter Deaths—That the Parliament notes with growing concern that fuel bills will rise by 15% this year and further notes that an 18% rise occurred last year; believes that the vulnerable will suffer most from these charges and that excess winter deaths will regrettably and unnecessarily continue to rise, as was the case in Grampian where last year 320 excess winter death cases were recorded; believes that new solutions must be sought to prevent the rise of excess winter deaths, and further believes that, as an energy-rich nation, the people of Scotland should expect warm, dry and affordable ways to heat their homes and that the Scottish Executive should conduct a review of the effectiveness of existing schemes.

Supported by: Roseanna Cunningham*, Michael Matheson*, Frances Curran*, Rosie Kane*, Tommy Sheridan*, Donald Gorrie*, Alex Johnstone*, Fiona Hyslop*

*S2M-3491.1# Jackie Baillie: The A82: The Case for Major Improvements and the Removal of the Temporary Traffic Lights at Loch Lomondside—As an amendment to motion (S2M-3491) in the name of Fergus Ewing, leave out from first "believes" to end and insert "believes that the A82 trunk road is an essential transport link for people living and working in the west of Scotland, the islands and the Highlands; further notes that the route is vital for tourism to Scotland’s first national park at Loch Lomond and the Trossachs and as the gateway to the west Highlands; acknowledges the HITRANS report which makes a persuasive case for investment in the A82; notes that improvements on economic and road safety grounds are required to the entirety of the route including the section to the north of Tarbet, and welcomes the development of a Route Action Plan by the Scottish Executive to address these concerns."

S2M-3190*u * Jim Mather: Supply of Affordable Housing in Rural Areas—That the Parliament considers that new pension rules to be introduced in April 2006 allowing funds from self-invested personal pension plans to be invested in residential property, such as buy to let or holiday homes with up to 40% tax relief, and allowing residential property already owned to be moved into pensions schemes will reduce the availability of affordable housing, particularly in rural areas*, such as the Highlands,* where young people are already facing difficulties in finding housing to buy or rent, and considers that the Scottish Executive and UK Government should reconsider the proposals, taking into account the particular issues surrounding rural housing in Scotland, and should exempt housing in Scotland from these new pension provisions.
