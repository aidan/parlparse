Business Bulletin No. 9/2009: Thursday 15 January 2009
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 22 January 2009

Question Time

General

1. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what it is doing to support jobs in the oil and gas industry. (S3O-5626)

2. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive how many businesses have received government support for loans since September 2008. (S3O-5612)

3. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reasons the Elgin bypass and the trunk link road in Inverness were not deemed to be priorities for inclusion in the Strategic Transport Projects Review. (S3O-5602)

4. Derek Brownlee: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it proposes to improve the cash flow to small businesses. (S3O-5621)

5. Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will admit community transport operators and people on the lower rate of Disability Living Allowance to the national concessionary travel scheme. (S3O-5585)

6. Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met representatives of the Care Commission and what was discussed. (S3O-5644)

7. David Whitton: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will respond to concerns raised by the Scottish Federation of Housing Associations that housing associations are "desperately struggling to meet demand". (S3O-5595)

8. George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive how many newly qualified teachers will graduate from Scottish educational institutions this year. (S3O-5567)

9. Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive, following the end of the public inquiry into the Aberdeen Western Peripheral Route, when ministers will report on the final decision. (S3O-5625)

10. Not Lodged

Rural Affairs and the Environment

1. Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what assistance it is giving to local authorities to reduce the amount of waste going to landfill sites. (S3O-5615)

2. Nicol Stephen: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are in place to ensure that Scottish-labelled meat has not been born and bred outside Scotland. (S3O-5616)

3. Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the staff of Forestry Commission Scotlandand their trade unions over the future of Scotland’s forests. (S3O-5561)

4. Not Lodged

5. Malcolm Chisholm: To ask the Scottish Executive how many applications there have been to the Climate Challenge Fund and how many have been successful. (S3O-5562)

6. Not Lodged

7. Alison McInnes: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to ensure that the Rural Aberdeenshire LEADER programme is properly resourced. (S3O-5608)

8. Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what action its Rural Directorate is taking to promote environmental awareness through further development of the eco schools programme. (S3O-5581)

9. Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it plans to publish responses to the recent consultation on agricultural pay and conditions. (S3O-5592)

10. Not Lodged

Justice and Law Officers

1. Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people charged with assault to injury have received a direct measure since 1 April 2008. (S3O-5603)

2. David McLetchie: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps have been taken to ensure that the investigation of serious fraud in Scotland can be carried out effectively. (S3O-5596)

3. Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many reported breaches of residency and contact orders there have been in each year since 2006. (S3O-5598)

4. Aileen Campbell: To ask the Scottish Executive how the seized proceeds of crime are reinvested in local communities. (S3O-5630)

5. Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will oppose the renaming of Low Moss Prison as HMP Bishopbriggs. (S3O-5605)

6. Nigel Don: To ask the Scottish Executive to what extent it believes a statement of prosecution policy would clarify the law on assisted dying. (S3O-5634)

7. Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what efforts it is making to ensure that there is provision of effective justice services throughout Scotland. (S3O-5558)

8. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive to what extent it is cutting the funding of the Scottish Inter Faith Council and for what reasons. (S3O-5576)

9. Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to seek out innovative methods for reducing crime levels locally and nationally. (S3O-5652)

10. Not Lodged
