Business Bulletin No. 103/2003

Monday 28 July 2003

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-235 Frances Curran: Johnstone Hospital-That the Parliament notes that the demographic changes taking place within Scotland mean both an increase in the elderly population and an increase in demand for elderly services, especially within the NHS; notes that, as a result, there will be an increase in demand for elderly care beds within the NHS, but that Argyll and Clyde NHS Board is proposing to close Johnstone Hospital and cut beds for elderly people; recognises that this proposal is despite massive opposition in the local community; demands that the board listens to local people and reverses the decision to cut 163 elderly care beds in the board's area; considers that the real reason for these cuts is the need to pay back £30 million of debt which the board has accumulated; affirms that, after working all their lives and paying for the NHS, elderly people deserve better from both the board and the Scottish Parliament, and therefore calls on the Minister for Health and Community Care to guarantee the future of Johnstone Hospital.

*S2M-234 Carolyn Leckie: United Kingdom Cabinet Should Resign-That the Parliament sends condolences to the family of Dr David Kelly; considers his death to be another waste of human life, in addition to the deaths of over 7000 civilians caused by the unjustified and continuing slaughter of the war and occupation of Iraq; recognises that the declared justification for war by the Blair-led Government has been shown to have no credibility whatsoever; further recognises that the real aims of the war were to achieve domination of Iraq and all its resources and to annex the oilfields for the benefit of the American Government and its multi-billion dollar corporate backers; calls on the Scottish Executive to recognise that it was misled by Her Majesty's Government and declare that it was wrong to support the war; calls on the whole discredited United Kingdom cabinet who championed the drive to war, including the Prime Minister, to resign, and calls for the immediate withdrawal of all British forces from the region and the immediate return of Iraq's resources to the Iraqi people and for reparations to be paid for the desperate loss of life, injury and destruction of Iraq's infrastructure.

Supported by: Frances Curran*, Colin Fox*

*S2M-233 Murdo Fraser: Return of Troops from Black Watch Regiment-That the Parliament welcomes the return of soldiers from the Black Watch Regiment to their base in Germany after their service during the conflict in Iraq; thanks them for their service to this country and the people of Iraq; appreciates their gesture to the people of Tayside and Fife in parading through towns in Angus, Perth and Kinross and Fife, and wishes them a well-deserved period of leave with their families.

*S2M-232 Ross Finnie: Draft Code of Recommendations for the Welfare of Livestock: Cattle (SE/2003/175)-That the Environment and Rural Development Committee recommends that the Draft Code of Recommendations for the Welfare of Livestock: Cattle (SE/2003/175) be approved.

Supported by: Allan Wilson*

*S2M-231 Malcolm Chisholm: The Draft Mental Health (Care and Treatment) (Scotland) Act 2003 (Consequential Modification) Order 2003-That the Health Committee recommends that the draft Mental Health (Care and Treatment) (Scotland) Act 2003 (Consequential Modification) Order 2003 be approved.

Supported by: Mr Tom McCabe*

*S2M-230 Pauline McNeill: Child Injury Prevention-That the Parliament notes the extent of accidents causing injury to children and figures recorded at Yorkhill Children's Hospital in 2001 which show that 70% of injuries happen whilst playing; further notes that many such accidents are preventable; applauds the work of Dr NV Doraiswamy, accident and emergency consultant at Yorkhill Children's Hospital, and the Health Promotion Office of Greater Glasgow NHS Board on the Finger Tips safety leaflet, which highlights fingers as being amongst the most mobile parts of a child's body and, as such, vulnerable to being crushed or jammed, sometimes resulting in amputation; believes that the use of simple devices, including doorstoppers or specially insulated doorframes, can prevent such injuries in the home, in schools and in public buildings, and recognises that the Children's Hospital Injury Reporting and Prevention Programme, known as CHIRPP, can assist in raising awareness about the extent and type of accidents or injuries amongst children so that forms of prevention may be identified.
