Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally printed the day after they are lodged. If an amendment is lodged to a motion then the original motion will appear alongside the amendment, along with any support lodged that day, in this section.

Each Monday, this section also contains all motions and amendments lodged the previous week that are still live and any motions and amendments that have been lodged for debate in the coming week. A motion or amendment is live if it has not been debated, withdrawn or otherwise deleted from the list.

Where a motion or amendment has been withdrawn, it is indicated in this section the day after it has been withdrawn. Support for motions and amendments received after they are lodged is shown at the end of this section the day after such support is received.

Motions and amendments can be published with various symbols:

  • Asterisks before the motion or amendment number indicate a motion or amendment published for the first time;

  • Asterisks also identify alterations to the text of a motion or amendment made since it was first published;

  • A hash symbol identifies motions eligible for debate at Members’ Business;

  • A diamond symbol identifies motions lodged for Members’ Business that have not yet attracted the required cross-party support;

  • An "R", identifies motions or amendments in which the Member who lodged it has a registrable interest.

The Parliamentary Bureau periodically deletes motions or amendments not scheduled for debate and which are over six weeks old.

A full list of current motions is available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/motions.htm.

A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/index.htm.

Any questions in relation to this section should be directed to the Chamber Desk, for which contact details are provided at the end of the Bulletin.

*S3M-8178 Ian McKee: Nuclear Power—That the Parliament expresses deep concern at the radioactive leaks from Japan’s Fukushima Daiichi nuclear plant following the recent earthquakes and tsunami that have devastated the region; believes that Fukushima demonstrates that, even in the world’s most technologically and scientifically advanced nations, it is impossible to guarantee safety in the running of atomic plants; welcomes the news that, following the Fukushima incident, the Swiss Government has suspended plans to build and replace nuclear plants and that German Chancellor Angela Merkel has announced a three-month suspension of a law aimed at prolonging the activity of old nuclear plants; further welcomes the emergency meeting that has been called by the EU energy commissioner to discuss the possible application of EU-wide stress tests for the 143 nuclear reactors currently operating in EU territory and to debate the wider issue of nuclear security in Europe; endorses the SNP government’s position that Scotland, which it considers to be a world leader in green, clean, reliable renewable energy, does not require nuclear power, and challenges the other political parties in Scotland and across the United Kingdom to unite on the issue by adopting a similar stance.

Supported by: Christopher Harvie*, Brian Adam*, Bill Kidd*, Shirley-Anne Somerville*

*S3M-8160 Linda Fabiani: East Kilbride Firm Honoured by World’s Whisky Drinkers—That the Parliament congratulates the East Kilbride-based firm, Glencairn Crystal, on being named Industry Leader of the Year in the annual Malt Advocate Whisky Awards in the United States of America for its specially designed whisky tasting glass; notes that the Glencairn Crystal whisky glass is now used by nearly all the major whisky companies in all the major distilling countries and is also the leading whisky glass at various whisky festivals worldwide; further notes that the whisky glass design is a past winner of the Queen’s Award for Innovation; congratulates the glass’s designer and Managing Director Ray Davidson and all at Glencairn Crystal, and is delighted that East Kilbride is home to another innovative Scottish company that is achieving worldwide success.

*S3M-8159 Rob Gibson: New Life for Trad Music Centre at Plockton—That the Parliament congratulates the Scottish Government on what it considers to be the imaginative package of measures that it has put together to save the Plockton-based National Centre of Excellence in Traditional Music from the threat to its funding by Highland Council; considers that these solutions are innovative and have helped to bail out Highland Council in what it sees as a lack of commitment to excellence in traditional music by the authority; praises the musical protests that were held to save the centre; notes that these protests gathered support locally and nationally; calls on Highland Council to show more sensitivity in its duty of care to the centre’s director, staff, students and students’ families; applauds what it sees as the greater co-operation with partners to place beyond doubt the local authority’s commitment to Scotland’s traditional music and culture that, it believes, will help the centre, alongside the Féisian movement, to flourish in challenging financial times, and wishes all associated with the centre a harmonious and successful future.

*S3M-8158 Robin Harper: National Trust for Scotland—That the Parliament commends the progress made by Sir Kenneth Calman and the management team at the National Trust for Scotland in what it sees as their positive response to the trust’s 2010 strategic review; recognises the charity’s genuine efforts to engage with its 310,000 members as it modernises and streamlines its governance, most notably in conducting a ballot for the election of trustees to serve on its new board; further recognises and appreciates what it considers to be the central and crucial role fulfilled by the trust in safeguarding and ensuring access to Scotland’s natural and cultural treasures; welcomes Sir Kenneth’s decision to attend the Parliament on 16 March 2011, on a visit sponsored by Pauline McNeill MSP, to address and engage with members directly and openly on what has been achieved and is still to be done, and believes that the trust is in a unique position to provide independent, expert advice on a range of policy matters linked to heritage. *S3M-8155 Robin Harper: Scotland: a Safe Place for Child Traffickers?—That the Parliament notes with alarm the conclusions of the report on child trafficking by Scotland’s Commissioner for Children and Young People published on the 14 March 2011, which notes that 83 children may have been trafficked into Scotland in the last year and a half without a single person being convicted of the crimes; considers that there is now an evidence base as a result of this report, and urges the Scottish Government to act as swiftly as possible on the recommendations of the report, especially in light of the evident willingness of the professionals contacted during the research to improve the identification and treatment of children trafficked into and within Scotland and in relation to the traumatic effects of the exploitation that these children experience.
