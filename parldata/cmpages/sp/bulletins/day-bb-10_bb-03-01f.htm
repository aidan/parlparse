Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (♦).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

*S3M-5838 Christine Grahame: No to Fascism in Lockerbie—That the Parliament condemns the decision of what is considered to be the ultra-right-wing fascist group, the so-called Scottish Defence League (SDL), to hold an event in the town of Lockerbie on 27 March 2010 connected to the 1988 bombing of Pan Am flight 103; considers that the SDL and its parent organisation, the English Defence League, have a track record of instigating violence at events that they hold, requiring huge additional police resources and associated costs to the public purse, and condemns as insensitive and highly inappropriate the choice and focus of this event by the SDL, which it believes to be acting in the pursuit of narrow, extremist political objectives that have nothing to do with remembering the 270 victims of the PA flight 103 disaster and which is likely to face overwhelming objection from local Lockerbie residents.

*S3M-5836 Bill Wilson: Royal Secrecy against the Democratic System—That the Parliament considers that in any democratic system of government freedom of access to information is an essential safeguard of that democracy; further considers that freedom of information should apply to all individuals within the system of government, both elected parliamentarians and the head of state; notes that, at present, the UK system of government is a constitutional monarchy with the Queen as head of state; believes that, as such, royal papers should be available for inspection on an equal footing with any other government document, and therefore calls on the UK Government to drop plans to allow greater secrecy for the royal family than is proposed for any other government document.
