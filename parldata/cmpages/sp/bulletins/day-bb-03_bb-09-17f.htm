*S2M-358 Campbell Martin: Dr Henry Faulds - Pioneer of Fingerprinting—That the Parliament acknowledges the pioneering work carried out by Scotsman Dr Henry Faulds in the scientific study of fingerprints; recognises that Dr Faulds, who was born in Beith, Ayrshire, in 1843, first published his findings on the forensic application of fingerprinting in the Nature Magazine in 1880, some years ahead of contemporaries in the field, and therefore should be recognised as the true pioneer of fingerprinting, and acknowledges the work of the Dr Henry Faulds - Beith Commemorative Society in seeking to recognise the work and achievements of Dr Faulds by securing funds to provide a permanent memorial in the town.

*S2M-349 Mike Pringle: Dedicated Bus Service to St. Peter's Primary School, Edinburgh—That the Parliament welcomes the agreement between the City of Edinburgh Council and contractors to allow the refurbishment of St. Peter’s Primary School, Morningside, to start immediately; notes that the lack of a dedicated school bus service for the current school year to take pupils to their temporary school near Tollcross has caused traffic congestion at this site and overcrowding on buses running on the A702 into the city, and calls on the City of Edinburgh Council to introduce one immediately to stop travel problems for teachers, pupils, parents and commuters.

*S2M-348# Mike Pringle: Transport in Southern Edinburgh—That the Parliament notes with concern the continued traffic congestion on main routes into Edinburgh from the south, including the problem of parking by city centre commuters in residential streets in southern Edinburgh; further notes with concern the proposal for an out-of-town football stadium at Straiton that can only exacerbate existing traffic problems; considers that improved transport infrastructure, such as the proposed park-and-ride site at Burdiehouse, is essential to help tackle the problem, and considers that the City of Edinburgh and Midlothian councils and the Scottish Executive should ensure that these transport problems in southern Edinburgh can be resolved with added benefit to the wider Lothian and Borders region.

*S2M-339.2 Nicola Sturgeon: Review of Licensing Laws—As an amendment to motion (S2M-339) in the name of Cathy Jamieson, insert at end "and notes further that the misuse of alcohol has a disproportionate effect on those living in Scotland’s most deprived areas and calls on the Scottish Executive to complement any legislative change resulting from the Nicholson Committee’s proposals with initiatives to tackle the poverty in Scotland that often results in alcohol misuse."

*S2M-339.3 Mr Brian Monteith: Review of Licensing Laws—As an amendment to motion (S2M-339) in the name of Cathy Jamieson, insert at end "and believes that bringing about a change in cultural attitudes to alcohol consumption through individuals taking responsibility for their actions must therefore be a key aim of any legislative change."
