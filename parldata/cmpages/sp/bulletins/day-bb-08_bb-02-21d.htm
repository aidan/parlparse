Business Bulletin No. 30/2008: Thursday 21 February 2008
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 28 February 2008

Question Time

General

1. Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is encouraging partnership working with the voluntary sector to deliver key services to vulnerable people. (S3O-2397)

2. James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive what provision it is making in the 2008-09 budget to support blind and partially sighted people. (S3O-2415)

3. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it remains committed to the Sure Start programme to bring together early education, childcare, health and family support for disadvantaged families. (S3O-2399)

4. Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what estimate it has made of the uptake by passengers of the national concessionary travel scheme in 2008-09 and what the difference is between the capped figure in the 2008-09 budget for reimbursement for concessionary travel and the projected claims for reimbursement if the current estimate for uptake is accurate. (S3O-2427)

5. Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that the Designated Place at Albyn House in Aberdeen provides a good model of how to deal with people who are drunk and incapable which can be copied by other alcohol support services and avoid inappropriate admission to hospital and detention in police cells. (S3O-2422)

6. Shirley-Anne Somerville: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to reduce energy consumption in the domestic housing sector. (S3O-2391)

7. Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide an update on its involvement with proposals for a biodiesel plant at Grangemouth. (S3O-2433)

8. Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive what priority it places on tackling deprivation in Glasgow. (S3O-2406)

9. Tom McCabe: To ask the Scottish Executive what specific funding or programmes are in place to help young people deal with low self-esteem or depression. (S3O-2421)

10. Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how much water Scottish Water lost in leakages in the most recent year for which figures are available and how much this cost. (S3O-2364)

Rural Affairs and the Environment

1. Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive how much of the £24.32 million allocation of European Regional Development Funding prioritised towards rural development between 2007 and 2013 has been allocated to eligible projects. (S3O-2414)

2. Willie Coffey: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure that Scotland’s food supplies are safe, secure, and sustainable. (S3O-2375)

3. Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans there are for agri-environment schemes under the Scottish Rural Development Programme. (S3O-2409)

4. Jackson Carlaw: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is taking to protect the biodiversity of the west of Scotland. (S3O-2359)

5. Andy Kerr: To ask the Scottish Executive how its proposed food policy will address the affordability and accessibility of healthy food. (S3O-2416)

6. Iain Gray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether reports, following its statement to the Parliament on its waste strategy, that energy-from-waste plants will only be permitted if they have a capacity of up to 100,000 tonnes per year are accurate. (S3O-2412)

7. Charlie Gordon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will give details of the criteria and processes under which local authorities may apply for finance from the Zero Waste Fund. (S3O-2410)

8. Bill Aitken: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to preserve sites of environmental value in Glasgow. (S3O-2362)

9. Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure that the Scottish Environment Protection Agency works collaboratively with Scottish Water in the interests of supporting local rural development. (S3O-2442)

10. Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive what action its Environment and Rural Affairs department is taking to promote environmental awareness through further development of the eco schools programme. (S3O-2451)

Justice and Law Officers

1. Margaret Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent discussions it has had with Strathclyde Police. (S3O-2404)

2. Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will fund any additional burdens arising from police pensions in the next financial year. (S3O-2396)

3. Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what practical steps it plans to take to implement the SNP manifesto promise to reduce the number of non-violent offenders committed to serve prison sentences of six months or less. (S3O-2434)

4. Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it has made towards the creation of a national clear up fund to assist the families of murder victims. (S3O-2452)

5. Bill Kidd: To ask the Scottish Executive what advice the Scottish Law Officers have given on the feasibility of mounting a legal challenge to the post office closures consultation and programme in Scotland. (S3O-2383)

6. Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken towards the establishment of a drug-free environment in prisons. (S3O-2393)

7. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what role there is for the police in supporting designated places schemes as an alternative disposal to detaining those who are under the influence of alcohol. (S3O-2371)

8. Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to make people feel safe in the Highlands and Islands. (S3O-2363)

9. Jamie Hepburn: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions the Cabinet Secretary for Justice has had with the Home Secretary on the introduction of ID cards. (S3O-2382)

10. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive what educational provision is available in prisons. (S3O-2444)
