Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-4177 Richard Baker: Health and Safety at Work—That the Parliament notes the concerns of Aberdeen City Council that there is a need for greater engagement by the Parliament in issues relating to health and safety in Scotland; notes that, while health and safety legislation and its enforcement are reserved to Westminster, the Scottish Executive has a role in the promotion of health and safety, and urges the Parliament and Executive to explore ways in which a forum can be established for the discussion of ongoing issues relating to health and safety in the workplace.

*S2M-4176 Mark Ballard: Fairtrade NUS—That the Parliament congratulates the National Union of Students Services Limited (NUSSL), the buying consortium for NUS-affiliated students’ unions, on its decision to become 100% Fairtrade for hot beverages, subject to approval at the forthcoming NUSSL Annual General Meeting; supports moves by the NUS to encourage its entire membership to opt in to Fairtrade status; recognises that Edinburgh University Students’ Association, along with others, has submitted a motion to NUS Annual Conference supporting this move; notes that the motion will allow the NUS to encourage all student unions to embrace Fairtrade and ensure that the millions of students across the United Kingdom will drink Fairtrade as default in their unions; further recognises the importance of Fairtrade products for securing a fair deal for farmers in developing countries, and calls on the UK Government to continue to do all it can to support the continued growth of the fair trade market in the United Kingdom. S2M-4173 Jackie Baillie: Whisky Duty on Ice—That the Parliament warmly welcomes the Budget announcement by Gordon Brown MP, Chancellor of the Exchequer, that duty on whisky and other spirits will be frozen yet again; considers this to be a progressive move which helps support the industry’s competitiveness and productivity; notes that a fairer alcohol duty regime helps support a key British industry in its home market and sends a message of tax fairness overseas, as distillers look to develop new opportunities in emerging markets such as China, and congratulates the Labour Government on maintaining this freeze on duty on whisky since 1997.

S2M-4171 Jim Mather: Ensuring the Independence of Scottish National Statistics—That the Parliament, in light of the proposals from HM Treasury for the establishment of an independent UK Office for National Statistics, notes that no proposals were made for Scotland; further notes that such proposals for Scotland are the responsibility of the Scottish Executive, and considers that, in light of previous statistical issues such as the loss of Objective 1 funding for the Highlands and Islands through statistical error, the Executive should take similar measures to place the production of Scottish statistics on a statutory basis, incorporating the National Statistics Code of Practice, and establish an independent Office of Scottish Statistics with independent governance answerable directly to the Scottish Parliament.

Supported by: Mark Ballard, Brian Adam, Roseanna Cunningham, Mr Andrew Welsh, Mr Stewart Maxwell, Richard Lochhead, Rob Gibson, Mr Adam Ingram, Eleanor Scott, Chris Ballance*, Bruce Crawford*, Alex Neil*, Tommy Sheridan*, Dr Jean Turner*, Ms Sandra White*

S2M-4170 Ms Rosemary Byrne: Redundancies at James Watt College—That the Parliament deplores the behaviour of senior management at James Watt College, Greenock and Kilwinning, in announcing on 15 March 2006 their intention to make 70 full-time equivalent academic posts redundant with the intention of filling these places with staff under new terms and conditions; congratulates the academic staff for their 97% "yes" vote in favour of industrial action in opposition to the management proposals to reduce staff and diminish terms and conditions of employment, and calls on the Scottish Executive to ensure the future viability of the college by sending a clear message that this type of behaviour by college management is unacceptable and detrimental to the education of students in both campuses.

S2M-4169 Rosie Kane: Congratulations to Scotland’s Paralympic Curling Team—That the Parliament congratulates the UK Paralympic curling team which picked up the silver medal on 18 March 2006 in Turin, the members of which all come from Scotland; notes that Angie Malone, Tom Killin, Mick McCreadie, Frank Duffy and Ken Dickson achieved the silver in a heroic match against Canada, which came down to the last stone in the last end; is pleased to further note that the team will bring home Britain’s first Paralympic winter games medal since 1994 and Britain’s first winter games silver medal since 1992, and, finally, recognises that Tom Killin and Mick McCreadie are the first Olympians to win medals in games for both winter and summer games in both disabled and able-bodied competitors.

S2M-4168 Phil Gallie: Taiwan’s Exclusion from the WHO: Avian Flu—That the Parliament expresses regret that Taiwan is currently excluded from the World Health Organisation (WHO) given current concerns over the spread of avian flu; recognises that Taiwan is geographically located in the hot-spot area of avian flu presence and plays host to many species of migratory birds, and urges the Scottish Executive to express a view to relevant bodies that it is essential that the WHO takes regard of the situation in Taiwan whilst, in broader terms, pointing out that, if the WHO is to succeed in co-ordinating and controlling actions in potential global pandemics, there should be no exclusion areas.

S2M-4167 Christine Grahame: Public Information Broadcasts in the South of Scotland—That the Parliament notes the decision by the Scottish government to stop public information broadcasts to the south of Scotland (Border TV) region, covering towns as far apart as Portpatrick in the west and Eyemouth in the east, and residents throughout the Scottish Borders and Dumfries and Galloway; further notes that over 5% of the Scottish population live in this TV region and are not now receiving public information broadcasts on issues such as anti-social behaviour, anti-racism, domestic abuse, the new legislation banning smoking in public places, fire safety and many other important notifications, and believes that the decision to halt broadcasts in 2004 has had a detrimental impact on the health and wellbeing of the people in the south of Scotland and that this decision is yet further evidence of the neglect of this region of Scotland by coalition ministers.

Supported by: Alex Neil

S2M-4166 Ms Sandra White: Early Morning Raids: Mary Semirimu—That the Parliament deplores the early morning raids which took Mary Semirimu and her two-year-old twins, Michelle and Michael, from their home to Dungavel detention centre and then to Yarl’s Wood from where they were due to be flown out to Uganda; recognises that Mary and her husband opposed the present Ugandan Government and campaigned vigorously for the Opposition; observes that in March 2003 their house was raided by Chief Military Intelligence officers where Mary, pregnant at the time, was given a serious beating and her husband taken away; records that Mary has not seen nor heard of him since; further recognises that the President of Uganda has blamed Ugandans abroad for spreading lies against his government; notes that whilst in Scotland Mary was a leading member of Karibu (welcome) in the African community, and suggests that to forcibly return her to Uganda where she will be in danger, not only from the Ugandan state authorities but also from a general lack of human rights, will blacken the good name of this country.

Supported by: Alex Neil, Brian Adam*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Rob Gibson*, Patrick Harvie*, Mr Adam Ingram*, Rosie Kane*, Jim Mather*, Eleanor Scott*, Tommy Sheridan*, Frances Curran*

S2M-4160 Mary Scanlon: Support Your Local Shops Campaign—That the Parliament congratulates the Northern Scot on its "Support Your Local Shops" campaign; notes the success of the campaign in generating support for small retailers across Moray; notes that independent retailers across Scotland make a unique and irreplaceable contribution to the character and quality of our local towns and villages, and further notes that just a few pounds out of an average weekly shopping budget spent locally could make a huge difference to the future success of small businesses and local town centres.

S2M-4159 Mark Ballard: Commercial Activities in Schools—That the Parliament welcomes the publication by the Scottish Consumer Council of Guidance on Commercial Activities in Schools; acknowledges that the guidelines are a step in the right direction but notes that they are legally binding on neither schools nor local education authorities; recognises that highly subjective commercial activities may conflict with the essentially objective nature of education, exposing children to what amounts to advertising and leading to "pester power"; believes that commercial sponsorship or advertising has no place in the education of our children, and calls on the Scottish Executive to tighten up these guidelines and make them legally binding.

S2M-4149 Shiona Baird: Decentralised Energy - A Vision of the Future—That the Parliament congratulates Woking Borough Council on its pioneering town centre decentralised energy network; notes that, by utilising microgeneration and combined heat and power and by implementing imaginative energy efficiency measures, the scheme has managed to reduce carbon emissions by no less than 77%; further notes that Woking’s example is inspiring London and other cities to adopt similar models; believes that there is no reason why Scottish towns and cities should not benefit from decentralised energy networks such as Woking’s, and calls on the Scottish Executive to do everything in its power to help Scottish communities who wish to adopt similar decentralised networks in order to enjoy the environmental, social and economic benefits that will inevitably follow.

S2M-4148 Mr Adam Ingram: In the Footsteps of Robert the Bruce—That the Parliament notes and commends the efforts to establish a heritage trail in memory of Robert the Bruce, King of Scots, whose remarkable and historic victory over a superior English force at Bannockburn reinstated Scottish national independence; notes that Bruce’s struggle against the "chains and slavery" of English domination was a defining mark in the history of Scotland; notes that 700 years after he came to power the desire of Scots to be once again free remains unabated amongst those of us who appreciate the real value of liberty and freedom; notes that the Ayrshire-born Bruce had many challenges and obstacles on the road to delivering independence for Scotland but that his commitment to our nation and his leadership of the Scots lie in stark contrast to the pinstriped political leadership Scotland has today, and calls on the Scottish Executive to back the proposed heritage trail and remember the man whose vision, charisma and military prowess led our nation to independence.

Supported by: Michael Matheson, Christine Grahame

S2M-4147 Chris Ballance: Decentralising UK Energy—That the Parliament welcomes the publication of Decentralising UK Energy by the World Alliance for Decentralised Energy (WADE); notes that the report, which compares models of two possible future scenarios: centralised generation using nuclear power and a decentralised system in which energy is predominantly produced close to its point of use using renewables and combined heat and power, concludes that the United Kingdom can meet its targets for cuts in CO2 emissions without resorting to nuclear power and that overall the decentralised solution is far superior, being cleaner as CO2 emissions are 17% lower than in the nuclear scenario and cheaper as overall costs are over £1 billion lower than in the nuclear scenario, even before the cost of managing nuclear waste is factored in, and believes that the Scottish Executive should give urgent consideration to how best to support and encourage the development of a sustainable energy policy based on renewable and micro-renewable technologies.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie, Patrick Harvie, Mark Ballard, Nora Radcliffe, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Eleanor Scott, Richard Lochhead, Shiona Baird, Mr Mark Ruskell, Frances Curran, Robin Harper, Mr Adam Ingram, Dr Jean Turner*

Supported by: Campbell Martin, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Mr Bruce McFee, Rosie Kane, Ms Sandra White, Frances Curran, Carolyn Leckie, Chris Ballance*
