Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (♦).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S3M-2382 Mary Scanlon: Economic Situation in Unst—That the Parliament welcomes Highlands and Islands Enterprise (HIE)’s appraisal of the economic situation in Unst; notes that in 2005 the island was dealt a severe blow when the Ministry of Defence decided to leave RAF Saxa Vord, an early warning radar base that was the centre of the island’s economy from 1957; further notes that the outlook at the time was bleak for the 720 residents of the island with a report predicting 172 job losses and gross household income falling by £3.245 million; congratulates the residents of Unst, Shetlands Isles Council, HIE and Military Asset Management on their tremendous work to ensure that island life continued and prospered, and further notes that Military Asset Management has converted part of the base into self-catering accommodation and a restaurant while Foords Chocolates currently operate on the site and advanced plans are in place for the island’s Valhalla Brewery to relocate and expand at the site, all of which helps to diversify and strengthen the economy of Scotland’s most northerly island.

*S3M-2381 Jack McConnell: Good Luck to Team GB and the Beijing Olympics—That the Parliament welcomes the opening of the 2008 Olympiad in Beijing and sends good luck to Team GB; congratulates the 31 Scots on Team GB for the Olympics and the 16 Scots on Team GB for the Paralympics and wishes them well; celebrates the achievements of all the athletes who will compete for Team GB; looks forward to the medals and the personal bests which will inspire others in the future, and welcomes the opportunity provided by the Olympic Games to further build links between the people of China and the rest of the world.

*S3M-2380 Stuart McMillan: Police Make Second Largest Drugs Seizure Ever in Scotland—That the Parliament congratulates Strathclyde Police on making the second largest drugs seizure ever in Scotland by recovering cannabis with a reported street value of £1.2 million in Inverclyde; notes that the police estimated that 1,000 plants were being grown but, since the equipment has been dismantled, four times that amount has been recovered; further notes this haul will dent the illegal drugs industry in the short-term, and commends Strathclyde Police for its dedication to removing drugs from our streets and communities.

*S3M-2379 Claire Baker: Congratulations to Third Force News—That the Parliament congratulates Third Force News (TFN), Scotland’s weekly voluntary sector newspaper, on reaching its 500th edition and its 10th anniversary; believes that TFN plays a crucial role in keeping the voluntary sector informed and in keeping members of the Parliament and others informed of news and current issues in the sector; further believes that the voluntary sector is crucial to the full breadth of Scottish life; wishes the TFN staff all the best, and looks forward to its 1,000th edition in another 10 years.

*S3M-2378 Malcolm Chisholm: Anniversary of the "8888" Pro-Democracy Demonstration—That the Parliament recognises the twentieth anniversary of the pro-democracy demonstrations in Burma that began on 8 August 1988 and during which an estimated 3,000 people were brutally slaughtered by the ruling military junta; expresses its deep concern about reports that over 2,000 political prisoners remain incarcerated in that country where they face systematic abuse and are denied proper medical care in contravention of their basic human rights; notes that an estimated 900 people have been imprisoned in the past 10 months, including many involved in the September 2007 "Saffron Revolution", and commends and supports the work of organisations across the United Kingdom, including the Amnesty International Edinburgh St Mark’s Group, in highlighting the plight of these prisoners of conscience and calling for their immediate and unconditional release.

*S3M-2377 Bashir Ahmad: RNIB Tackling Ethnic Minority Sight Loss—That the Parliament congratulates the work of RNIB and, in particular, its newly launched project to tackle sight loss among the ethnic minority community; recognises that people of South Asian origin are six times more likely than Caucasians to develop type 2 diabetes, which may lead to retinopathy and eventual sight loss; notes that diseases such as glaucoma are three times more prevalent among people of Afro-Caribbean and Chinese descent; believes that language can often be a barrier, as well as a lack of knowledge, to services that are available to help; applauds RNIB for taking the challenge head on, and is committed to working with RNIB to make our health service as accessible as possible to all, regardless of ethnicity.

Supported by: Rob Gibson*, Stuart McMillan*, Alasdair Allan*, Joe FitzPatrick*, Bob Doris*, Jackson Carlaw*, Bill Kidd*, Ross Finnie*, Kenneth Gibson*, Nigel Don*, Bill Butler*, Elaine Smith*, Sandra White*, Pauline McNeill*, Robert Brown*

*S3M-2376 Bashir Ahmad: Additional Investment in Scotland’s Dental Services—That the Parliament welcomes the announcement by the Cabinet Secretary for Health and Wellbeing of an additional £75 million in funding for dental services across Scotland; further welcomes the £21.5 million that will be invested in Glasgow over the next two years; notes that many Scots, particularly those in rural areas, struggle to find a place on an NHS dental surgery list, and congratulates the Scottish Government on its effective and thorough response to rising concerns over the lack of NHS dentists in Scotland and its drive to improve standards across Scotland’s dental surgeries.

Supported by: Sandra White*, Alasdair Allan*, Bob Doris*, Kenneth Gibson*, Jamie Hepburn*, Bill Kidd*, Rob Gibson*, Alex Neil*, Joe FitzPatrick*, Stuart McMillan*

*S3M-2375 Mike Pringle: Free Tibet—That the Parliament recognises the courageous actions of Iain Thom, member of the Parliament’s Cross-Party Group on Tibet who, with other supporters, was arrested by Chinese police on 6 August 2008 during their peaceful demonstration in Beijing against injustices that are being inflicted on the people of Tibet, and applauds his bravery at this particular time when the Chinese government is trying rigorously to suppress expressions of freedom.

*S3M-2374# Alasdair Allan: Gaelic Language Development—That the Parliament commends Comhairle nan Eilean Siar, along with other local authorities and public bodies, for the recent publication of their Gaelic language plans; likewise commends the Gaelic plan of the Scottish Parliament and the soon to be published Gaelic language plan of the Scottish Government; notes the important work done by the community and by many agencies and bodies in Scotland to maintain and develop the Gaelic language; recognises that in the coming few years it will become clear whether or not Gaelic is to survive as a community language, and therefore recognises that a sense of urgency in this area of policy is essential, particularly in respect of the expansion of Gaelic medium education, if we are to ensure that the number of speakers has, by the 2021 census, been stabilised at 2001 levels as the prerequisite for long term growth.

*S3M-2370 Liam McArthur: Rev Dr Andrew Ross—That the Parliament notes with great sadness the death of Rev Dr Andrew Ross; applauds Dr Ross’ efforts to forge closer ties between his native Scotland and adopted Malawi; acknowledges his long-standing commitment to Malawi, where he did so much invaluable work as a minister in the Church of Central Africa Presbyterian and from where he was forced to flee by its first president, Hastings Banda; also recognises Dr Ross’ enormous contribution to the Church of Scotland and the University of Edinburgh, notably the Faculty of Divinity; believes that his popularity with colleagues and students stemmed from his genuinely global perspective on religion, history and politics combined with his patient, good-humoured and utterly compassionate treatment of all those with whom he came into contact; celebrates the doc’s other great passion, Edinburgh University Association Football Club, of which he was Honorary President and the most ardent, faithful and exasperated supporter; sends its condolences to Joyce and the rest of his family, and gives thanks for a life well lived and with absolutely no "gesture tackles".

*S3M-2369 John Wilson: Multiple Sclerosis: Scotland’s Hidden Epidemic—That the Parliament congratulates the BBC producer, Emma Snodgrass, and journalist, Elizabeth Quigley, for the superb documentary, Scotland’s Hidden Epidemic - The Truth about MS, which was screened on BBC One Scotland; welcomes the impact that the programme has had on awareness about multiple sclerosis (MS) in Scotland; endorses the ongoing research work on MS being undertaken by Professor Charles ffrench-Constant and his staff at the Centre for Translational Research in Edinburgh, and by many others at institutions across Scotland, and welcomes the MS Society Scotland’s role in funding this research programme; commends the Scottish Government for its recent commitment of £70,000 to support the start-up costs for a national audit of MS in Scotland, and urges it to pursue all possible options to increase the amount of research on MS being done in Scotland in the future.

Supported by: Liam McArthur*

*S3M-2368 Bob Doris: Save Wallace Street Post Office—That the Parliament notes the looming deadline of 1 September 2008 for constituents to have their say on post office closures in the area plan for Ayrshire, Inverclyde, Lanarkshire and Renfrewshire; encourages customers of the post offices in these areas to make their views known by taking part in this consultation process by Post Office Ltd; notes that Postwatch has a number of concerns about the proposed closures, which total 44 in this area alone, in what is the latest round of cuts to the post office network; believes that locals should oppose such closures including opposing the closure of the post office at Wallace Street, Rutherglen, where Postwatch Scotland believes that the proposed closure could have a negative impact on quality of service currently being provided; recognises that the post office in Wallace Street is not only a vital facility for many local elderly customers and others but also an integral part of the local economy in that area, which has a number of other small businesses located beside the post office; welcomes South Lanarkshire Council leader, Eddie McAvoy’s, concerns over these closures but believes that the Labour Party cannot escape the blame for the closure programme which it pushed through the UK Parliament and which the SNP opposed, and further believes that local Labour MP, Tommy McAvoy, should apologise to constituents for these ill-thought out, risky and brutal reforms and join SNP calls for the closure programme to be halted immediately.

Supported by: Angela Constance*, Sandra White*, Bashir Ahmad*, Alasdair Allan*, Bill Kidd*, Rob Gibson*, Christina McKelvie*, Gil Paterson*, Kenneth Gibson*

*S3M-2367 Kenneth Gibson: Aids 2008—That the Parliament congratulates the success, and endorses the aims, of the XVII International Aids Conference 2008, held in Mexico City from 3 to 8 August, and its theme of Universal Action Now; recognises that 33 million people are currently living with HIV worldwide; is aware that three times more people are infected each day than begin antiretroviral treatment; believes that universal access to HIV prevention, treatment, care and support and eliminating the stigma often encountered by those suffering from HIV/AIDS is the key to reducing the spread of a disease that has killed around 25 million people worldwide over the last quarter of a century, including at least 1,600 in Scotland, and acknowledges that only continued global co-operation to eliminate poverty and full resourcing of health services and agencies battling with HIV/AIDS will lead to the eventual elimination of this scourge.

Supported by: Bill Kidd*, Hugh O’Donnell*, Alasdair Allan*, Ross Finnie*, Liam McArthur*, Rob Gibson*, Bashir Ahmad*, Angela Constance*, Joe FitzPatrick*, Christine Grahame*, Robin Harper*, Mike Pringle*, Dr Richard Simpson*, Elaine Smith*, Bob Doris*, Stuart McMillan*, Sandra White*, Christina McKelvie*

*S3M-2366 Shirley-Anne Somerville: East Africa Food Crisis Appeal—That the Parliament has grave concerns about the situation in East Africa where millions of people, in northern Kenya, Ethiopia, Somalia and Eritrea, are facing a serious humanitarian crisis due to widespread drought, conflict, chronic poverty and rising global food prices; notes that as a result of the crisis there is a threat to the livelihoods of landless and internally displaced communities within the region, as well as a huge risk of disease, especially in drought-affected areas with poor hygiene and sanitation; recognises the urgency for humanitarian relief but also for long-term solutions, including governments tackling the root causes of chronic poverty in the region; welcomes the work being done by aid agencies in Scotland to help people affected by this crisis, such as Oxfam through its East Africa Food Appeal, and calls on all Scots to support the appeal.

Supported by: Bill Kidd*, Jamie Hepburn*, Robin Harper*, Liam McArthur*, John Scott*, Alex Neil*, Ken Macintosh*, Joe FitzPatrick*, Alasdair Allan*, Bob Doris*, Sandra White*, Bill Butler*, Kenneth Gibson*, Elaine Smith*, Mike Pringle*, Christine Grahame*, Christina McKelvie*, Rob Gibson*, Gil Paterson*

Supported by: Gil Paterson*
