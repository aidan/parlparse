Business Bulletin No. 71/2002

Thursday 2 May 2002

Section D : Oral Questions
  

Question Time

1. John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to establish a research park in Ayrshire. (S1O-5136)

2. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the current position is on the Ayr United Heathfield Stadium planning application in light of the passing of the 4 April deadline it set for the receipt of responses in line with court requirements. (S1O-5139)

3. Mr Keith Harding: To ask the Scottish Executive what issues the Minister for Education and Young People discussed with staff and board members on her recent visit to Bell Baxter High School, Cupar. (S1O-5161)

4. Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking on the Race Relations (Amendment) Act 2000 as it affects education in schools. (S1O-5180)

5. Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has plans to review the law in relation to crimes involving knives. (S1O-5142)

6. Mr John McAllion: To ask the Scottish Executive when it next intends to review the future of unified NHS boards. (S1O-5173)

7. Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking in order to reduce child poverty. (S1O-5165)

8. Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will be represented at the meeting of Education Ministers from EU countries and applicant states to be held in Bratislava in June. (S1O-5137)

9. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to recover the supervision costs in respect of Steven Beech from the appropriate authorities in England. (S1O-5151)

10. Ian Jenkins: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met VisitScotland and what issues were discussed. (S1O-5163)

11. Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has identified any benefits from awards made by the Special Educational Needs Innovation Grants programme and how it intends to recognise and promote good practice in the education of children and young people with special educational needs. (S1O-5158)

12. Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact any plans by Her Majesty's Government to cut child benefit for the parents of truants will have on child poverty. (S1O-5169)

13. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are any plans to carry out an economic impact assessment on the impact of Her Majesty's Government's 10% tax on oil and gas profits. (S1O-5188)

14. Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the deployment of police resources to detect, arrest and charge individuals involved in both the supply and consumption of cannabis represents a productive use of police time. (S1O-5156)

15. Elaine Thomson: To ask the Scottish Executive how it intends to close any widening of the gap in attainment levels between pupils from advantaged and disadvantaged backgrounds in Aberdeen. (S1O-5186)

16. Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to set guidelines on when a dentist can refuse to treat a patient. (S1O-5152)

17. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations the Crown Office has received about the retention of evidence related to the Lockerbie bombing case and what action it has taken. (S1O-5145)

18. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure that older people are aware of the free personal and nursing care scheme commencing on 1 July 2002. (S1O-5166)

19. Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether timber is an asset to the rural economy. (S1O-5155)

20. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive when a Minister last visited Bell Baxter High School in Cupar and what matters were discussed. (S1O-5153)

21. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in addressing the environmental impact of sea cage fish farming on rivers, lochs and seas. (S1O-5181)

22. Mr Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive what proportion of hospital wards is mixed. (S1O-5185)

23. Bill Aitken: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied with the level of compliance with community service orders imposed by courts as an alternative to custody. (S1O-5143)

24. Mr Keith Raffan: To ask the Scottish Executive what services are currently provided to help and support the children of parents undergoing treatment for substance abuse. (S1O-5141)

25. Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive which of its policies will close the gap in GDP growth between Scotland and the south east of England. (S1O-5146)

26. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to develop health care services in rural communities. (S1O-5167)

27. Brian Fitzpatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive what resources are being made available to improve services for young people with special educational needs. (S1O-5174)

28. Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent discussions it has had with Scottish Water concerning its charging policies. (S1O-5134)

29. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it proposes to support the work of firefighters. (S1O-5171)

30. Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive what advice it gives to householders on the prevention of heat loss. (S1O-5149)
