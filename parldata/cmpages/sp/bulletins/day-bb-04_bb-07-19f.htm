Business Bulletin No. 117 / 2004

Monday 19th July 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-1586 Shiona Baird: Supermarket Developments-That the Parliament notes with concern the growing number of planning applications for supermarket developments which, if approved, will lead to the loss of green spaces, increased car use and the further erosion of local shopping areas, all to the detriment of local communities; recognises the threat posed by large supermarket developments to smaller retailers and the disproportionate impact on elderly and less mobile consumers who rely on local services and supplies, and calls on the Scottish Executive to take action to protect local communities from this threat to their sustainability.

Supported by: Mike Pringle*, Mark Ballard*, Robin Harper*

*S2M-1585 Mr Stewart Maxwell: Dial-a-Bus Service-That the Parliament appreciates the importance of Strathclyde Passenger Transport's (SPT) Dial-a-Bus service to people within the SPT area and that this service is of particular benefit to the old and infirm; notes with concern that SPT is considering the withdrawal of evening and Sunday Dial-a-Bus Services which would affect around 7,300 passengers per year, many of them being elderly or infirm people who use this service to attend religious services within their places of worship in the evenings and at weekends, and urges its members to support any measures that will ensure that SPT keep this valuable service running.

*S2M-1584 Rosie Kane: Support for Falun Gong-That the Parliament condemns the Chinese Government for human rights abuses against Falun Gong practitioners; demands that Her Majesty's Government joins it in condemnation of these abuses and in recognising that practitioners of Falun Gong are persecuted inside and outside China; further demands the immediate release of Falun Gong practitioners currently held in China, and supports the peaceful march which will take place in Edinburgh on 1 August 2004 at 12.00 pm from Market Street in opposition to the Chinese army taking part in the military tattoo.

Supported by: Mike Pringle*

*S2M-1583 Robin Harper: ScottishPower, Salmon and North American Indian Tribal Nations-That the Parliament welcomes to Scotland a delegation of representatives from North American Indian tribal nations and US fishermen's and environmental organisations; recognises their quest to obtain justice from the Scottish-based multi-national, ScottishPower; backs their campaign to oblige ScottishPower to establish the highest environmental standards in its operation in North America; regards the damage done by dams now owned by ScottishPower on the River Klamath in California and Oregon, which have blocked 350 miles of historic salmon spawning grounds, as unacceptable; recognises that this damage has had a serious and detrimental impact on the native peoples' social, cultural and economic situations; regards ScottishPower's failure to include salmon restoration strategies in its future plans as a failure, and calls on ScottishPower to lead the way in taking active measures to reverse the decline in salmon numbers in what was once America's third greatest salmon river.

Supported by: Shiona Baird*, Mark Ballard*

*S2M-1582# Murdo Fraser: Renewable Energy Strategy-That the Parliament supports the recommendations set out in the Enterprise and Culture Committee's 6th Report, 2004 (Session 2), Renewable Energy in Scotland (SP Paper 194); notes that the report criticises the Scottish Executive's current strategy for renewables and also highlights that the Executive's current policy has resulted in the development almost exclusively of onshore wind farms to the exclusion of other renewable technologies; believes that Scotland has the opportunity to lead the world in bringing to the market new renewable technologies such as wave, tidal and biomass, but that this will only happen with a change of strategy; believes, therefore, that the Executive should adopt the recommendations in the report and produce a new national planning strategy for renewables, working with local authorities to develop local plans and targets for onshore wind farms, and considers that there must be a national moratorium on all locally-opposed wind farm planning applications until such guidance has been developed and taken effect.

*S2M-1581 Mr Brian Monteith: No Support for Smoking Ban-That the Parliament notes that a poll of 10,000 people conducted this year by Populus showed that 74% of those surveyed did not support a ban on smoking in pubs and bars and that 98% said that it was better to have a choice of smoking or non-smoking facilities than a complete ban; notes that an Office for National Statistics survey of July 2004 showed that 80% of those surveyed did not express support for a ban on smoking in pubs and bars; further notes that a BMRB poll undertaken in September 2003 showed that less than 17% of people agreed that smoking should be banned in pubs, clubs and restaurants and that 86% felt that the smoking situation in pubs, clubs and bars had improved over recent years; acknowledges that the majority of people are against a complete ban on smoking in pubs; believes that a complete ban on smoking in pubs would have a detrimental effect on freedom of choice in Scotland, and calls on the Scottish Executive to allow landlords to determine smoking policy in their establishments and ensure that freedom of choice for consumers to use an establishment that allows smoking is protected.

*S2M-1580 Mr Stewart Maxwell: More Powers for Scotland-That the Parliament welcomes the findings of the State of the Nation 2004 survey, carried out by the Joseph Rowntree Reform Trust, which found that 66% of people living in Scotland wanted the Parliament to have more powers; recognises that only by having these powers can the Parliament and the country truly begin to fulfil their enormous potential, and urges all members to follow the public mood and campaign for all the normal powers of a sovereign nation to be transferred from Westminster to Holyrood forthwith.

*S2M-1579# Bill Butler: Citizens Advice Week-That the Parliament supports Advice Week from 6 to 13 September 2004; commends the vital work that Scotland's 77 citizens advice bureaux do in providing free, impartial and confidential advice to all; recognises that in the last year they handled over 400,000 issues and secured nearly £26m for clients; further recognises that such good quality advice on issues such as benefits, debt, employment, housing and consumer affairs is increasingly necessary in today's society; acknowledges that the use of client evidence means that citizens advice bureaux speak with authority and relevance on changing social issues from both a local and national perspective; congratulates the 2,193 volunteers and 329 paid members of staff who provide this service, and hopes that parliamentarians across the political spectrum can work together to ensure that the service will continue to inform government and meet the needs of local communities across Scotland in the years to come.

Supported by: John Swinburne*, Robert Brown*, Michael McMahon*, Colin Fox*, Mike Watson*, Murdo Fraser*, Bruce Crawford*, Tricia Marwick*, Mrs Nanette Milne*, Brian Adam*, Cathy Peattie*, Mike Pringle*, Jackie Baillie*, Kate Maclean*, Robin Harper*, Mr Stewart Maxwell*, Mr John Home Robertson*, Shiona Baird*, Ms Wendy Alexander*, Mr Kenny MacAskill*, Dr Sylvia Jackson*

*S2M-1578# Mr Duncan McNeil: International Suicide Prevention Week-That the Parliament acknowledges the importance of International Suicide Prevention Week from 5 to 11 September 2004 for raising awareness of this cause of death; recognises that more people die as a result of suicide than from traffic accidents; commends the work being done by Choose life, Scotland's strategy aimed at reducing suicides by 20% over the next 10 years; welcomes the introduction to Scotland of Applied Suicide Intervention Skills Training, helping develop more effective approaches to both identifying and assisting those most at risk of suicide, and looks forward to this suicide-reduction work continuing at a national and local level.
