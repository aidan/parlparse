Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally printed the day after they are lodged. If an amendment is lodged to a motion then the original motion will appear alongside the amendment, along with any support lodged that day, in this section.

Each Monday, this section also contains all motions and amendments lodged the previous week that are still live and any motions and amendments that have been lodged for debate in the coming week. A motion or amendment is live if it has not been debated, withdrawn or otherwise deleted from the list.

Where a motion or amendment has been withdrawn, it is indicated in this section the day after it has been withdrawn. Support for motions and amendments received after they are lodged is shown at the end of this section the day after such support is received.

Motions and amendments can be published with various symbols:

  • Asterisks before the motion or amendment number indicate a motion or amendment published for the first time;

  • Asterisks also identify alterations to the text of a motion or amendment made since it was first published;

  • A hash symbol identifies motions eligible for debate at Members’ Business;

  • A diamond symbol identifies motions lodged for Members’ Business that have not yet attracted the required cross-party support;

  • An "R", identifies motions or amendments in which the Member who lodged it has a registrable interest.

The Parliamentary Bureau periodically deletes motions or amendments not scheduled for debate and which are over six weeks old.

A full list of current motions is available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/motions.htm.

A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/index.htm.

Any questions in relation to this section should be directed to the Chamber Desk, for which contact details are provided at the end of the Bulletin.

New and altered motions and amendments

*S4M-00004 Sarah Boyack: World Fair Trade Day 2011—That the Parliament welcomes World Fair Trade Day on Saturday 14 May 2011, which is organised by the World Fair Trade Organization; notes that the day is being supported by the One World Shop through its branches in Edinburgh and Glasgow, which will host events and special offers on the day covering a wide range of fairly traded products from around the world; recognises the efforts of communities, schools, faith groups, social clubs and fair trade organisations across Scotland to make Scotland a fair trade nation; supports the World Fair Trade Organization’s mission to enable producers to improve their livelihoods and communities through fair trade, and calls on the Scottish Government to continue to support fair trade initiatives in communities across the Lothians and Scotland.

*S4M-00003♦ Elaine Smith: There Is A Better Way—That the Parliament supports the STUC There is a Better Way campaign; believes that cuts are neither unavoidable nor inevitable and that they would actually threaten economic recovery across Scotland and impact on areas such as Coatbridge and Chryston; considers that a sensible and sustainable response to the current economic crisis is contained in the People’s Charter; understands that public sector cuts are likely to have a disproportionate effect on women, children and disabled people, and would welcome widespread support for the STUC campaign and local campaigns such as those in North Lanarkshire and West Lothian.

Supported by: Neil Findlay*

*S4M-00002 Jackie Baillie: The Rotary Club of Dumbarton—That the Parliament congratulates the Rotary Club of Dumbarton on its 50th anniversary in May 2011; notes that the club received its charter on 3 May 1961 and considers that it has been a key part of the local community ever since; believes that rotarians provide positive role models in the local community and internationally with their principle of service above self; congratulates everyone involved with the club for their service over the last 50 years, and hopes that the club continues to play a significant role in the local area for the next 50 years.

*S4M-00001 Kenneth Gibson: Arran in the Square—That the Parliament congratulates Visit Arran on hosting Arran in the Square, a weekend celebration of all things Arran in Glasgow’s George Square from 10 am to 10 pm on Saturday 14 May and noon to 4 pm on Sunday 15 May 2011 as part of Scotland’s Year of Food and Drink; recognises the huge number of events that will take place to keep the whole family entertained; acknowledges that Radio Clyde will be on hand to keep the party going, island musicians will perform, top chefs will cook with Arran food and drink, culture can be soaked up at the art exhibition, retail therapy by way of Arran products will be available and people can relax with an Arran Aromatics massage; believes that Arran in the Square will be a popular and successful event, encouraging many people to visit Arran who have not done so before, and hopes that this initiative becomes an annual feature.

Supported by: Jamie McGrigor*, Rob Gibson*, Alasdair Allan*, Brian Adam*, John Lamont*, Stewart Maxwell*
