Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (♦).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

*S3M-4206 Shirley-Anne Somerville: Imprisonment of Binayek Sen—That the Parliament notes that 14 May 2009 marked the second anniversary of the arrest and imprisonment, without being brought to trial or allowed bail, of Binayak Sen, a medical doctor and human rights activist based in Chhattisgarh state in India; recognises that Amnesty International considers him to be a prisoner of conscience and his incarceration to be politically motivated and based on trumped-up charges; notes that Binayak Sen has devoted decades of his life to working with poor people in central India, and welcomes the work done by the Free Binayak Sen Campaign to condemn attempts to silence peaceful campaigns for justice and equality.

Supported by: Dr Richard Simpson*

*S3M-4205 Jackie Baillie: Midwives Win Gold Standard—That the Parliament congratulates the midwifery team at the Vale of Leven Hospital on the recent recognition of its gold standard service for all its mums and babies; notes that the specialised team at the Community Maternity Unit scored almost 100% in every category of the UNICEF Baby Friendly Initiative; further notes that this award, which will be delivered later this year, recognises top level care provided to pregnant women and new mums, and applauds all the continued hard work and high standards of everyone involved with the midwifery team at the hospital.

*S3M-4204 Lewis Macdonald: Cinnamon Aberdeen, Scottish Curry Awards Winner—That the Parliament congratulates the Cinnamon restaurant in Aberdeen on its success in being named the top place in Scotland to enjoy Indian cuisine at the Scottish Curry Awards 2009; acknowledges that this is the second year in a row that the restaurant has won the award, adding to its already prestigious British Curry Award for Best Restaurant in Scotland and Northern Ireland for 2008; congratulates all those who took part in the awards ceremony, and wishes Cinnamon and all Aberdeen’s thriving and successful Indian restaurants every success in the future.

*S3M-4192 Aileen Campbell: Clydesdale Scoffs-up Top Awards—That the Parliament welcomes the continuing recognition of Clydesdale food producers and restaurants across Scotland; congratulates Carmichael Estate Farm Meats of Biggar and Border Biscuits of Lanark, who both scooped top awards for their produce at the Scotland Food and Drink Excellence Awards 2009; notes the success of butchers J H Cairns of Law and Ramsay of Carluke in winning gold awards in the first Cured Meat Product Evaluations for Scottish butchers and Biggar butcher J B Hamilton, who became the West of Scotland 2009 Haggis Champion; considers that not only does the Clydesdale area produce some of the finest foods in Scotland, it also cooks it and congratulates Biggar’s Indian restaurant, Taj Mahal, for making the final of the Scottish Curry Awards 2009, and believes that all these awards are just recognition of the world-class producers and restaurants in Clydesdale.

*S3M-4191 Bill Butler: ProjectScotland, Yes We Can—That the Parliament commends the significant achievement of ProjectScotland volunteers, mentors and staff in facilitating over 2.2 million hours of volunteering in communities since May 2005 through a programme of structured placements with over 300 non-profit organisations across the country; notes the positive role that ProjectScotland has played in changing young people’s perceptions of volunteering and, indeed, themselves; acknowledges that, during 2008-09, 40% of ProjectScotland volunteers came from the 20% most deprived areas of Scotland; regrets that the Scottish Government decided to withdraw its financial support to the charity as of 1 April 2009; recognises the serious implications that this decision has for the charity, its partner organisations and the hundreds of young people who have been in touch in recent months about placements; contrasts the decision with the bold and ambitious Serve America Act in the United States of America, recently signed into law by President Obama, which will quadruple the number of AmeriCorps volunteers to 250,000, and encourages the Scottish Government to revisit its decision and do all in its power to ensure that ProjectScotland can continue to change lives and communities across our country.

Supported by: Robin Harper*, Hugh O’Donnell*, Elaine Murray*, David Whitton*, Marlyn Glen*, Michael McMahon*, Patricia Ferguson*, Jackie Baillie*, Cathy Jamieson*, Malcolm Chisholm*, David Stewart*, Richard Baker*, Robert Brown*, Andy Kerr*

*S3M-4190 Stewart Maxwell: Congratulations to Mearns Primary School Pupils—That the Parliament congratulates primary six pupils Rory Robertson and Matthew Wilson of Mearns Primary School in East Renfrewshire on reaching the final of the Ready, Willing and Mobile Competition; applauds the innovative design of their Cool Cube concept, which acts as a sensory device to alert pedestrians to approaching traffic when crossing the road; notes that the young inventors designed the safety device after reading an online case study of a person with Down’s syndrome who was too scared to cross the road on his own; further notes that theirs is the only Scottish entry in the running for the top prize at the Mobility Roadshow competition, and wishes them the best of luck for the final on 6 June 2009.

Supported by: Brian Adam*, Jackson Carlaw*, Bill Wilson*, Maureen Watt*, Stuart McMillan*, Angela Constance*, Sandra White*, Jamie McGrigor*, Alasdair Allan*, Gil Paterson*, Robin Harper*

*S3M-4187.2 Jim Mather: Supporting Employment in Scotland—As an amendment to motion (S3M-4187) in the name of John Park, leave out from "with" to first "Scotland" and insert "to establish whether or not a scheme would benefit Scotland’s economic recovery".

*S3M-4186.1 Robin Harper: Importance of Aquaculture to the Scottish Economy—As an amendment to motion (S3M-4186) in the name of Roseanna Cunningham, insert at end "and further notes that problems remain to be addressed by the aquaculture industry, including the commercial confidentiality clauses that restrict access to the minutes of area management group meetings, the comparative lack of action on re-siting fish farm activities where this could be advisable, the huge problem of sea lice infestation and the industry’s wider impact on wild fish stocks, seals and the marine environment and its relationship with future plans for marine conservation areas and no-take zones for fisheries."

*S3M-4186.2 Elaine Murray: Importance of Aquaculture to the Scottish Economy—As an amendment to motion (S3M-4186) in the name of Roseanna Cunningham, after "environmental standards" insert "and the principles of sustainable development".

*S3M-4186.3 John Scott: Importance of Aquaculture to the Scottish Economy—As an amendment to motion (S3M-4186) in the name of Roseanna Cunningham, insert at end "and notes the importance of the 6,200 Scottish jobs supported by the aquaculture industry in maintaining the environmental, economic and social fabric in communities often located in Scotland’s most remote and fragile areas."

*S3M-4186.4 Liam McArthur: Importance of Aquaculture to the Scottish Economy—As an amendment to motion (S3M-4186) in the name of Roseanna Cunningham, after "economic downturn" insert "; further recognises that, if the industry is to attract, retain and develop people for a long-term career in aquaculture, the Scottish Government must ensure that a range of suitable training opportunities are made available".

S3M-4169 *#* James Kelly: Social Accounting Pilot Project—That the Parliament congratulates Co-operative Development Scotland on raising awareness of the practice of social accounting following a flagship pilot project with the Social Audit Network to introduce social accounting to six co-operative and social enterprises in Scotland; commends the contribution to the project played by Highland Home Carers, Highland Wholefoods, Loch Fyne Oysters, Scotwest Credit Union, West Whitlawburn Housing Co-operative and the Wise Group, and believes that organisations that can demonstrate their wider social impact beyond financial performance will gain a competitive edge and ensure that co-operatives, social enterprises and voluntary organisations thriving in Scotland contribute to economic growth.

S3M-4143 John Wilson: 48 Hour Working Time Directive—That the Parliament notes with concern that the UK Government failed to reach an agreement with the European Union with regard to the decision to opt out of the 48 hour working time directive, which aims to ensure that workers have protection against negative effects on their health and safety that may be caused by working excessively long hours or having inadequate rest or disrupted work patterns and to provide other benefits including increasing the motivation and productivity of workers along with creating employment.

*S3M-4143.1 Liam McArthur: 48 Hour Working Time Directive—As an amendment to motion (S3M-4143) in the name of John Wilson, leave out from "with concern" to end and insert "that the UK Government failed to reach an agreement with the European Union with regard to the decision to opt out of the 48 hour working time directive; believes that the defeat of the plans is good news for Scotland’s retained fire service and the communities that rely on it; is surprised that SNP members should express such inconsistent views on these proposals, which posed such a grave threat to the service provided by retained firefighters across Scotland; recognises the need for sufficient flexibility in the working time directive to allow retained firefighters to provide vital fire and rescue services in Scotland’s most remote areas, and therefore calls on ministers to be vigilant and ensure that they keep up the pressure as the European Commission consults on the way forward."
