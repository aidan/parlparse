A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

*S2M-286 Iain Smith on behalf of the Procedures Committee: First Minister's Question Time and Minor Standing Order Changes—That the Parliament agrees to make the changes to the standing orders set out in Annex A to the Procedures Committee's 1st Report, 2003 (Session 2), First Minister's Question Time and minor standing order changes (SP Paper 9) and that those changes to the standing orders should come into force on 4 September 2003.

*S2M-285 Rosie Kane: Bechtel and Britain's Nuclear Waste—That the Parliament condemns Her Majesty's Government's decision to involve the Bechtel Corporation in the establishment of Britain's nuclear waste Liabilities Management Agency; notes that this corporation has on at least four occasions penalised workers for exposing safety risks in its American nuclear plants; further notes that this corporation aided the construction of a chemical plant in Iraq prior to the first Gulf War; notes that Bechtel was proposed by Donald Rumsfeld to Saddam Hussein for the construction of an Iraqi pipeline, and in light of these points calls on Her Majesty's Government to reconsider its position with regard to the Bechtel Corporation's involvement in the safe handling of Britain's nuclear waste.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan*

*S2M-279 Colin Fox: Voting Reform—That the Parliament recalls that the voting system for elections to the Parliament was settled upon after extensive discussions with Scottish society through the Scottish Constitutional Convention and was endorsed by the Scottish people in the 1997 referendum on the establishment of a Parliament; notes that the composition of the Parliament is vastly more representative of the people of Scotland than either the Westminster Parliament or local authorities throughout Scotland; considers that before there is a change in the voting system for the Parliament there should be a reconvening of the convention and full consultation of the Scottish people at which not only the voting system, but the powers of the Parliament, are considered, and believes that the most urgent matter regarding voting systems is the requirement for reform of the systems for Westminster and local government elections.

Supported by: Rosie Kane*, Tommy Sheridan*

*S2M-278 Margaret Mitchell: Hamilton District Crime Prevention Panel—That the Parliament recognises the many potential dangers facing young people using the internet from paedophiles and adults attempting to "groom" youngsters; notes that it is vital that parents, schools, local communities and the police work together to protect youngsters from such threats, and congratulates the Hamilton District Crime Prevention Panel for publishing and distributing its leaflet A parent’s guide to internet safety, aimed at helping educate parents and children about the risks for children using the internet, which is an excellent example of communities working together to eradicate such crime.

*S2M-277 Mr Brian Monteith: Stirling's Appeal to Secure William Wallace Portrait—That the Parliament commends the efforts of Stirling’s Smith Art Gallery and Museum to secure a rare 17th century portrait of William Wallace originally owned by the Wauchopes of Niddrie Marischal, Edinburgh; notes that the gallery has launched an appeal to raise £20,000 to purchase the picture from a private collection in London, and believes that, if this is successful, the portrait would greatly enhance the Smith’s permanent collection of Wallace artefacts, as well as forming an integral part of its major exhibition to commemorate the 700th anniversary of the death of Wallace, scheduled to be held in summer 2005.

*S2M-276 Mr Stewart Maxwell: Pizza Hut Smoking Ban—That the Parliament welcomes the recent announcement by Pizza Hut of a ban on smoking in all of its UK restaurants; notes that the decision was reached in order to protect staff and customers from the dangers of passive smoking; believes that, by making its restaurants smoke-free, Pizza Hut will have a positive impact on its business, and notes that its decision is in response to customer demand for smoke-free restaurants.

Supported by: Dr Jean Turner*, Mr Rob Gibson*, Rosie Kane*, Patrick Harvie*, Brian Adam*, Alex Neil*, Mike Pringle*, Robert Brown*, Jim Mather*
