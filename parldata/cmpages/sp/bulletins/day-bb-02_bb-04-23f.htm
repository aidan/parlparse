Business Bulletin No. 64/2002

Tuesday 23 April 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-3017 Fiona McLeod: Removal of Dr Robert Watson from the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change-That the Parliament expresses concern at the removal of Dr Robert Watson as Chair of the UN's Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change following last year's publication of its Third Assessment Report (TAR) which states that global temperatures are rising nearly twice as fast as previously thought, predicts that temperatures could rise by as much as 5.8 degrees Celsius by the end of this century and claims that industrial pollution is the main cause; notes that the TAR report has the unqualified backing of more than 3,000 scientists; expresses dismay at President Bush's continued refusal to recognise the link between pollution and global warming; urges the President to reinstate America's support for the Kyoto Protocol, and further notes Scotland's determination to play its full part in tackling the threat of global warming.

*S1M-3016 Trish Godman: The 150th Anniversary of The Lifeboat Magazine-That the Parliament warmly commends the editor and staff of The Lifeboat, the magazine of the Royal National Lifeboat Institution (RNLI), on its 150th anniversary; believes that The Lifeboat plays an important role in informing its many readers of all elements of the lifeboat service, including detailed accounts of the numerous rescues carried out by the gallant lifeboat crews, fleet renewals, the fundraising activities of dedicated RNLI supporters and much more; remembers, too, the efforts of previous editors and staff of the magazine who ensured its survival during difficult years, including the two world wars, and, in particular, offers sincere thanks to the crews of the lifeboats whose bravery is so readily acknowledged in the pages of The Lifeboat and by those who live in our maritime communities.

*S1M-3015 Janis Hughes: Stop AIDS Campaign-That the Parliament supports the Stop AIDS Campaign in its efforts to bring together a wide range of groups in the UK in order to raise awareness about the global HIV/AIDS epidemic and to campaign for increased UK and international action; notes that there are over 36 million people living with HIV/AIDS worldwide, of which 3,400 are in Scotland, and encourages people throughout Scotland to support the Stop AIDS Campaign when activists from Africa and the Caribbean visit Scotland to speak about their experiences on 7 and 8 May 2002.

*S1M-3014 Mr Adam Ingram: Mental Health Expenditure-That the Parliament regrets that no mention was made of mental health services during the parliamentary statement by Jack McConnell on the UK Budget and calls on the Scottish Executive to give substance to the lip-service it pays to mental health as a national clinical priority.

*S1M-3013 David Mundell: Queen of the South: Second Division Champions-That the Parliament congratulates the players, manager, board, staff and supporters of Queen of the South Football Club not only for having achieved promotion to the First Division but also for securing the Second Division championship; recognises that the team's achievements over the past season have served as a tremendous psychological boost for the whole area, particularly in the aftermath of the foot-and-mouth disease crisis, and wishes the team and everyone associated with the club well in their endeavours in the First Division.

*S1M-3012 Fiona McLeod: The Scotland Act 1998 (Agency Arrangements) (Specifications) (No.2) Order 2002-That the Transport and the Environment Committee recommends that nothing further be done under the Scotland Act 1998 (Agency Arrangements) (Specification) (No.2) Order 2002 (SI 2002/800).
