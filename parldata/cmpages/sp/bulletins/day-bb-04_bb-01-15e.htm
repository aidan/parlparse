Business Bulletin No. 9/2004

Thursday 15 January 2004

Section E : Written questions lodged on 14 January 2004
  

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Where a member has declared a registrable interest in relation to a question it is marked "R".

S2W-5429 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on what organisations will be involved in Scotland Week in Estonia in July 2004; what funding will be made available for the proposed event, and what information it has on events planned for the week.*R*

S2W-5434 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Presiding Officer what role the Parliament will have in Scotland Week in Estonia in July 2004.*R*

S2W-5450Δ Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the membership and remit of the Cabinet Delivery Group on Closing the Opportunity Gap will be and whether it will take external advice.

S2W-5468 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive when the target to ensure that rail passengers do not have to stand for more than ten minutes during their journey, announced on 17 December 2002, will be met.

S2W-5475 Campbell Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether, under the Regulation of Care (Scotland) Act 2001 or any other legislation, tenants of residential homes attached by an internal corridor to residential care units are prevented from entering or frequenting such units and, if so, what the reasons are for these provisions.

S2W-5476 Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-2480 by Cathy Jamieson on 22 September 2003, when the statistics on offences of careless driving involving a road accident fatality will be available.

S2W-5477 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what health checks it has made of ingredients in approved food pellets for farmed salmon.

S2W-5478 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what sources and quantities of organic food pellets are available for the farmed salmon industry, shown also as a proportion of total feed used in Scottish salmon farms, in each year since 2000.

S2W-5479 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is in respect of the construction of wind farms on Forestry Commission land.

S2W-5480 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what compensation will be payable to property owners whose properties are adversely affected by the construction of wind farms in their vicinity.

S2W-5481 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Presiding Officer what recent discussions have been held with the Nordic Council on behalf of the Parliament; what plans it has to meet the council in 2004, and whether it has any plans to enter into discussions with the council regarding full or associated membership of the council.

S2W-5482 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will publish its Action Plan for the Social Economy.

S2W-5483 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with Her Majesty's Government regarding the upgrade of the A74 from Gretna to Carlisle.

S2W-5484 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is responsible for any part of the ancillary works in relation to the upgrade of the A74 from Gretna to Carlisle.

S2W-5485 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how many endangered species are held in (a) zoos, (b) wildlife parks and (c) private collections.

S2W-5486 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how many indigenous species are considered to be endangered.

S2W-5487 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what the timescale is for considering whether any lessons can be learned from the experience of community courts in the United States of America.

S2W-5488 Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to examine options in respect of the financial viability of the salmon farming industry.

S2W-5489 John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to ensure the continuation of sustainable salmon farming.

S2W-5490 Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) prosecutions and (b) convictions there have been for accessing and downloading child pornography on the internet in each of the last six years.

S2W-5491 Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) prosecutions and (b) convictions there have been for lewd communication with a child through an internet chatroom in each of the last six years.

S2W-5492 Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) prosecutions and (b) convictions there have been for fraud by adults who pretend to be a child for the purpose of securing a meeting with a child or committing, or attempting to commit, a sexual offence in each of the last six years.

S2W-5493 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people were prescribed (a) donepezil, (b) rivastigmine, (c) galantamine and (d) memantime in each of the last five years, broken down by NHS board.

S2W-5494 Miss Annabel Goldie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has responded to the report by the Office of Fair Trading The regulation of licensed taxi and services in the UK and, if not, when it will respond.

S2W-5495 Miss Annabel Goldie: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has received in relation to its invitation for comments on the report by the Office of Fair Trading The regulation of licensed taxi and private services in the UK.

S2W-5496 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will adopt the Swedish scheme of giving every child membership of a local library and a children's book at birth and how much such a scheme would cost.

S2W-5497 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will support the teaching of English as a foreign language (TEFL); how TEFL has been supported in each of the last four years; what information it has in respect of the organisations that support TEFL; what plans it has to promote TEFL abroad and which organisations will be involved in this process; what financial benefits TEFL has brought in each year since 1997; how many visitors have come to Scotland to learn English as a foreign language since 1997, and what funding will be made available to organisations to promote TEFL in each of the next three years, broken down by organisation.

S2W-5498 Elaine Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding it allocated specifically to support and promote breastfeeding in each year since May 1999, expressed also as a percentage of the total health budget in each year.

S2W-5499 Elaine Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding it allocated specifically to support breastfeeding in each NHS board area in each year since May 1999.

S2W-5500 Elaine Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive how much funding has been allocated to NHS Health Scotland, and its predecessor organisations, for the promotion of breastfeeding in each year since May 1999.

S2W-5501 Elaine Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive how much has been allocated to fund the work of the National Breastfeeding Adviser in each year since May 1999.

S2W-5502 Elaine Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive how much has been allocated to fund the work and operation of the Scottish Breastfeeding Group in each year since May 1999.

S2W-5504 Elaine Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive how much each NHS board has spent on the support and promotion of breastfeeding in each year since May 1999, expressed also as a percentage of the total expenditure of each board.
