Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

*S2M-3920# Linda Fabiani: Avondale Schools and Fairtrade—That the Parliament congratulates St Patrick’s Primary School in Strathaven for becoming the third primary school in Avondale, along with Sandford Primary School and Chapelton Primary School, to achieve fair trade status with recognition and award from the Fairtrade Foundation; notes that Avondale’s further four primary schools and Strathaven Academy are also working towards fair trade status, and congratulates the Strathaven Fairtrade Group on the commitment and dedication it has shown both prior to and since Strathaven became the first Fairtrade Town in Scotland. S2M-3919 Alex Johnstone: Cross-Party Consensus on Climate Change—That the Parliament welcomes the cross-party agreement on climate change signed by the Conservative Party, the Liberal Democrats, the Ulster Democratic Unionist Party, Plaid Cymru and the Scottish National Party at Westminster and welcomes the aim of this Conservative initiative, that an independent body should be established to monitor and enforce year-on-year carbon reductions, with the aim of achieving at least 60% by 2050, ensuring that long-term emissions cuts would be guaranteed.

S2M-3918 Fiona Hyslop: Fire Services in West Lothian—That the Parliament welcomes the Scottish Executive’s announcement of the £8 million funding package for fire and rescue services in Scotland but notes that this funding will not restore cuts agreed at the Lothian and Borders Fire Board Meeting on 27 January 2006, where Labour, Conservative and Liberal Democrat councillors voted by 15 to 3 not to retain the Craigshill Fire Station in West Lothian as a two-pump wholetime facility, thereby cutting services at night and on the weekend.

Supported by: Mr Kenny MacAskill, Christine Grahame

S2M-3917 Dr Jean Turner: Voluntary Sector Provision of Vital High-Quality Essential Services—That the Parliament calls on the Scottish Executive to ensure that the high-quality essential services provided by the voluntary sector to a wide range of children and families, many of them from vulnerable groups, is not jeopardised by any freeze on vital funding for such voluntary sector organisations.

Supported by: Christine Grahame, Chris Ballance*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Ms Sandra White*, Mary Scanlon*

S2M-3916 Donald Gorrie: Children Designing Play Facilities—That the Parliament, while welcoming motion S2M-3890 on the importance of play in the name of Kenneth Macintosh, emphasises the importance of good and imaginative design of children’s play facilities, particularly of involving local children in deciding the facilities to be included in their local playground and their design and management, and encourages councils and other groups planning new or renewed playgrounds to co-operate with local young people, the local community and organisations active in this field.

S2M-3915 Chris Ballance: Newsquest Job Losses—That the Parliament notes the announcement by the Newsquest group of up to 65 planned job cuts at The Herald, Sunday Herald and Evening Times, around a third of which may be journalists; offers sympathies to affected workers and their families; notes that this follows the announcement last year by Trinity Mirror of extensive job cuts in Scotland, despite a 20% rise in operational profits and a 5.8% increase in revenue; expresses concern that newspapers are increasingly under-resourced and under-staffed; believes that a wide range of vibrant newspapers with a plurality of opinion is vital to democracy, and calls on Newsquest to reconsider the proposed cuts.

Supported by: Mike Pringle, Donald Gorrie, Ms Sandra White, Tommy Sheridan, Kate Maclean, Bill Butler, Rosie Kane, Mr Kenny MacAskill, Eleanor Scott, Carolyn Leckie, Mark Ballard, Frances Curran, Campbell Martin, Shiona Baird, Pauline McNeill, Fiona Hyslop, Mr Jamie Stone, Christine Grahame, Robin Harper, Patrick Harvie*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Brian Adam*

S2M-3914 John Farquhar Munro: Ownership of Scotland’s Seabed—That the Parliament recognises the importance of Scotland’s coastal waters and of rights over the seabed to Scotland’s coastal communities; further recognises the importance to Scotland’s marine renewable sector of fair access rights to Scotland’s waters and seabed, and therefore believes that it is important that the extent of Scottish Ministers’ ownership of the seabed be clarified so that, if Scottish Ministers do indeed have ownership, and not the Crown Estate Commissioners, the seabed can be managed for the maximum benefit of Scotland’s coastal communities.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie, Christine Grahame, Mike Rumbles*, Chris Ballance*, Robin Harper*, Mr Jamie Stone*

S2M-3913 Rosie Kane: No to the Dalkeith Bypass, Yes to the Clean-up of the Land—That the Parliament welcomes the findings of the Fairhurst report, commissioned by the Scottish Executive, into the proposed Dalkeith bypass; notes that the report shows evidence of potential contaminants, such as mercury, arsenic and phenol amongst other poisons, on the proposed site, and therefore calls for an immediate cessation of all work and for a full and thorough environmental assessment of the site to be carried out in order to ascertain the extent of the contamination and for a full decontamination programme to then be undertaken to clean up the land and restore it for use as a natural heritage site for the local community.

Supported by: Colin Fox

S2M-3912 Karen Whitefield: ScottishPower Energy People Trust—That the Parliament welcomes the launch of the ScottishPower Energy People Trust which will help fund not-for-profit organisations, agencies and groups that represent vulnerable people living in fuel poverty with a key focus on children; welcomes ScottishPower’s investment of £2 million, which will be administered by a board of independent trustees, and looks forward to the trust supporting the many projects working towards eradicating fuel poverty in Scotland.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie

S2M-3911 Ms Sandra White: Women Still Under-Represented—That the Parliament condemns unreservedly the situation demonstrated by the figures contained in Who Runs Scotland 2006, produced by the Equal Opportunities Commission Scotland; notes that, amidst a welter of damning statistics, women make up only 22% of Scottish councillors, 10% of senior police officers and 18% of secondary school head teachers; suggests that 30 years after the Sex Discrimination Act 1975 came into force the pace of change remains painfully slow, and calls on all bodies, both public and private, to dismantle proactively age-old barriers and move forward to a situation where this valuable national resource is utilised to the full.

Supported by: Christine Grahame, Robin Harper, Patrick Harvie*, Chris Ballance*, Rosie Kane*, Jim Mather*, Rob Gibson*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*

S2M-3911.1 Christine May: Women Still Under-Represented—As an amendment to motion (S2M-3911) in the name of Ms Sandra White, leave out from "condemns" to end and insert "notes that in the 2003 Scottish Parliament elections only one-third of SNP MSPs elected were women and believes that all political parties should take steps to match the record of the Labour Party in Scotland in the proportion of women elected as MSPs."

S2M-3902# Mr Duncan McNeil: Greenock Telegraph Brighter Smiles Campaign—That the Parliament acknowledges the importance of ensuring that children get into the habit of regular visits to the dentist at as early an age as possible; expresses concern that some 5,300 children and young people in Inverclyde are not registered with a dentist; notes that this amounts to approximately one-third of the children in the area; further notes that research by Greenock’s Phoenix Community Health Project found that parents were unsure about where, when and with whom to register a child, and therefore congratulates the Greenock Telegraph for running a very successful, high-profile campaign to get local children signed up with a dentist and on the road to better oral health throughout their lives.

S2M-3898 Alasdair Morgan: The Future of the Saltire—That the Parliament notes with concern recent calls for the Saltire to be replaced as the national flag of Scotland and its misappropriation by far right groups in the United States of America; considers the Saltire to be a powerful symbol of Scotland’s identity; further notes the comments of the Lord Lyon, Sir Thomas Innes of Learney, in 1956 that, "The Cross of St Andrew is the flag which any Scotsman [or woman] is entitled to fly or wear as evidence of his [or her] national identity", and believes that the Saltire is an internationally recognised symbol of Scotland with which all modern Scots, regardless of origin or belief, can associate.

S2M-3897 Mr Mark Ruskell: UK Government Report - Avoiding Dangerous Climate Change—That the Parliament notes the report, Avoiding Dangerous Climate Change, published by the UK Government Meteorological Office and endorsed by the Prime Minister; notes that this report updates the science of climate change since the Third Assessment Report of the UN Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) was published in 2001; notes with grave concern the report’s findings that melting of the Greenland and Antarctic ice sheets and shutting down of the Atlantic Gulf Stream may be triggered by temperature rises within the 1.6 to 5.8C range which are predicted for the next century; further notes the Prime Minister’s foreword which states, "It is clear from the work presented that the risks of climate change may well be greater than we thought. It is now plain that the emission of greenhouse gases, associated with industrialisation and economic growth from a world population that has increased six-fold in 200 years, is causing global warming at a rate that is unsustainable", and calls on the Scottish Executive to ensure that the review of its Climate Change Programme is further strengthened, given the seriousness of these findings, and that it brings forward a programme that will reduce Scotland’s carbon dioxide emissions year-on-year in line with a national target of 60% reductions by 2050.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie

S2M-3896# Christine Grahame: Keeping Healthcare Local—That the Parliament notes the determination of the communities of Coldstream and Jedburgh to keep their cottage hospitals and that many hundreds of people turned out at recent rallies in Coldstream and Jedburgh to save local healthcare; further notes that GPs and the churches, together with MSPs across political parties, support retention; recognises that in both communities there are developed plans to modernise healthcare provision through the cottage hospitals; further recognises that the increasing elderly population in these communities means that there is more, not less, need for these two hospitals; notes that the Borders General Hospital is already unable to accept all of the elderly people who require beds and cannot discharge others for want of support services, and believes that the Scottish Executive, in the spirit of the Kerr report and in the interests of these Borders communities, should refuse any application for closures.

Supported by: Rob Gibson, Mr Adam Ingram, Brian Adam, Michael Matheson, Derek Brownlee, Jim Mather, Alex Neil, Ms Sandra White, Mr Andrew Welsh, Mr David Davidson, Mr Stewart Maxwell*, Phil Gallie*, Robin Harper*, Chris Ballance*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*

S2M-3892 Mr John Swinney: Winter Words Festival at the Pitlochry Festival Theatre—That the Parliament congratulates the Pitlochry Festival Theatre on its second highly successful Winter Words Festival which took place from 26 to 29 January 2006; notes the contribution that the festival makes to education about, and appreciation of, literature; recognises that the event contributes significantly to the local economy of Highland Perthshire; applauds the theatre for its innovation in developing new events to ensure that it is open all year round as a centre for cultural and artistic activity; recognises the assistance given to this initiative by Perth and Kinross Council, the Scottish Arts Council, EventScotland, VisitScotland and the Scottish Book Trust, and acknowledges the importance of developing events of this nature to continue the achievements of the theatre in the future.

S2M-3848# Margaret Mitchell: Cumbernauld Town Centre—That the Parliament notes that, in the Channel 4 programme, "Demolition", broadcast in December 2005, the public nominated and voted for Cumbernauld Town Shopping Centre as the most hated building in Britain; acknowledges that Cumbernauld is in a great location; commends the residents of Cumbernauld for maintaining a sense of pride in their town despite the problems, which have existed for many years, associated with design and the lack of an identifiable town centre; congratulates Channel 4 for having produced this excellent piece of broadcast journalism which, firstly, ensured that these problems have been the subject of a national awareness-raising exercise and, secondly, which provided practical solutions to these problems; recognises that quality of life is the core issue; expresses concern that North Lanarkshire Council intends to proceed with design plans for the town shopping centre which will, in the view of those who took part in the programme, merely compound the problems which already exist; believes that the Scottish Executive should encourage North Lanarkshire Council to take on board the suggestions made in the programme to regenerate Cumbernauld by creating an identifiable town centre starting at St Mungo’s Church and continuing to tree-lined boulevards with shop fronts facing widened pavements where bistro and alfresco activities could be provided, and considers that North Lanarkshire Council should initiate a consultation with the residents of Cumbernauld on these proposals.

Supported by: Alex Johnstone, Murdo Fraser, Mrs Nanette Milne, Robin Harper, Phil Gallie, Mr David Davidson, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton, Mr Jamie McGrigor

S2M-3700# Mrs Mary Mulligan: Linlithgow Primary School Volunteers—That the Parliament congratulates the children of Linlithgow Primary School who were honoured by Historic Scotland this week, on International Volunteer Day, for the tremendous work they do as tour guides around Linlithgow Palace and further commends their volunteering to other young people as a way of developing personal skills, but also of establishing a pride in the history of their own town or community.
