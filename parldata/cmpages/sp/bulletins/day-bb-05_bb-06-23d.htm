Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 30 June 2005

Question Time

General

1. Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to reform Disclosure Scotland. (S2O-7329)

2. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact major events such as T in the Park and the Edinburgh Festival have on the economy. (S2O-7345)

3. Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that the licensing boards proposed in the Licensing (Scotland) Bill will adequately represent community interests. (S2O-7365)

4. Christine May: To ask the Scottish Executive whether all local authorities and police forces are now utilising the powers available to them under the Antisocial Behaviour etc. (Scotland) Act 2004. (S2O-7362)

5. Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the national concessionary travel scheme will be introduced in April 2006, as announced by the Minister for Transport on 22 December 2004, and whether the scheme will include the use of the smart card system . (S2O-7317)

6. Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive what role it envisages for Scottish universities in addressing some of the issues to be discussed at the G8 summit, particularly in relation to Africa. (S2O-7351)

7. Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive how many cases of rape, or serious sexual offences, were reported to the police in the last two years and how many of these led to convictions in the High Court. (S2O-7352)

8. Mr Alasdair Morrison: To ask the Scottish Executive how EventScotland is helping to promote Scotland’s island communities at home and abroad. (S2O-7369)

9. John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will give its full support to Fort William to host the mountain biking events at the Olympic Games in 2012 if the United Kingdom’s bid to host the games is successful. (S2O-7312)

10. Mr John Swinney: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to improve road safety on the A9. (S2O-7333)

Health and Community Care

1. Gordon Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive what initiatives are in place to address health inequalities in Scotland’s most disadvantaged communities. (S2O-7355)

2. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people in East Lothian and Midlothian live in areas where dentists are not providing treatment under the NHS and what action can be taken to make NHS dental services available to these patients. (S2O-7353)

3. Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what assistance it gives to support people who wish to die at home. (S2O-7302)

4. Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to meet NHS Greater Glasgow. (S2O-7335)

5. Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive what aspects of health service delivery should not be covered by any future NHS board savings review. (S2O-7292)

6. Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what its policy is on centralisation of the NHS stores operation . (S2O-7293)

7. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will offer financial assistance to NHS Borders to ensure that Coldstream and Jedburgh cottage hospitals remain open, in light of local and professional opposition to closure of these hospitals and following the publication of the Kerr report and its support for community hospitals . (S2O-7321)

8. George Lyon: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made on the break-up of NHS Argyll and Clyde. (S2O-7304)

9. Chris Ballance: To ask the Scottish Executive whether NHS boards have made an assessment of the risk to public health from the spreading of raw untreated sewage sludge on land. (S2O-7379)

10. Janis Hughes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether withdrawing the use of independent sector facilities from the NHS would impact on the amount of time people wait for treatment. (S2O-7354)

Environment and Rural Development

1. Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive what contribution could be made to its sustainable development policies by the introduction of “Green Mortgages”. (S2O-7308)

2. Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the provisions in section 20 of the draft Animal Health and Welfare (Scotland) Bill will improve the protection afforded to horses and other grazing animals vulnerable to ragwort poisoning. (S2O-7372)

3. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made towards its national target for local authorities to recycle 25% of waste by 2006. (S2O-7380)

4. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the impact on the environment and on rural development will be of planning consent being given for additional wind generation sites and what information its Environment and Rural Affairs Department has on the amount of acreage which has been, or will be, given planning consent. (S2O-7296)

5. Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Minister for Environment and Rural Development last met NFU Scotland and what issues were discussed. (S2O-7297)

6. Not Lodged

7. Mr Bruce McFee: To ask the Scottish Executive what role the Scottish Environment Protection Agency has in dealing with contamination at the site of the former Royal Ordnance Factory in Bishopton, Renfrewshire. (S2O-7326)

8. Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive how it intends to take forward plans for Scotland’s first coastal and marine national park . (S2O-7382)

9. Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact the draft Private Water Supplies (Scotland) Regulations 2005 will have on the environment. (S2O-7386)

10. Mr Andrew Arbuckle: To ask the Scottish Executive how many farmers have signed land management contracts and under which categories. (S2O-7301)
