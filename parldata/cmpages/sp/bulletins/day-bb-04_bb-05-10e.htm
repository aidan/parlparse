Business Bulletin No. 78 / 2004

Monday 10th May 2004

Section E : Written questions lodged on Friday 7th May 2004
  

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-8069 Jim Mather: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the proposed tendering of the Caledonian MacBrayne ferry routes will include provision for an operator of last resort and, if not, what other safeguards will be put in place to protect, and ensure the continuation of, lifeline ferry services and minimise the risk to the public purse.

*The member has provided the following Gaelic translation:

A dh'fhaighneachd de Riaghaltas na h-Alba am bi an tairgseachadh, air a mholadh airson slighean aiseag Chaledonian Mhic a' Bhruthainn, a' gabhail a-staigh ullachadh airson luchd-solair mar "innleachd mu dheireadh"; agus mur a bi, dè a thèid a dhèanamh gus seirbheisean aiseig a tha deatamach a dhìon agus a ghlèidheadh, agus gus lùghdachadh a dhèanamh air cunnartan dhan sporan phoblach.*

S2W-8091 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive what the backlog is of applications to Disclosure Scotland for background checks.

S2W-8092 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average waiting time is for people to receive clearance from Disclosure Scotland.

S2W-8093 Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to ensure that horse racing is included in its tourism strategy.

S2W-8094 Frances Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive by how much grant funding allocated to Inverclyde Council changed from 2002-03 to 2003-04.

S2W-8095 Frances Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive how much grant funding was allocated to Inverclyde Council in 2003-04, broken down by purpose.

S2W-8096 Frances Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive how much of the grant funding allocated to Inverclyde Council in 2003-04 was ring-fenced, broken down by purpose.

S2W-8097 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive how much peat is extracted annually from peat extraction sites.

S2W-8098 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive what the annual economic value is of peat extraction.

S2W-8099 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people are employed in the peat extraction industry.

S2W-8100 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive how many peat extraction sites there are and where each site is located.

S2W-8101 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive how many peat sites are currently protected from extraction.

S2W-8102 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria are used to determine which peat sites are protected from extraction.

S2W-8103 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive under what (a) planning and (b) environmental regulations peat extraction may take place.

S2W-8104 Carolyn Leckie: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-7038 by Malcolm Chisholm on 1 April 2004, whether it will list all cases where thiomersal has been removed from vaccines as a precautionary measure.

S2W-8105 Carolyn Leckie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether £23.64 million, the agreed cost of the Skye Bridge at July 1990 prices, has been collected in tolls and, if not, when it estimates that this figure will be reached.

S2W-8106 Carolyn Leckie: To ask the Scottish Executive by what statutory authority it permits the charging of road tolls on the A87 at the Plock of Kyle and how this is consistent with the prohibition on tolling in Inverness-shire granted by William I, King of Scots, in perpetuity in 1180.

S2W-8107 Carolyn Leckie: To ask the Scottish Executive, given that only the road authority or a concessionaire with lawfully assigned powers might properly charge and collect tolls, on what statutory basis other organisations are permitted to charge tolls on the Skye Bridge.

S2W-8108 Jim Mather: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it can confirm that William I, King of Scots, by royal charter declared a prohibition in perpetuity on tolling in Inverness-shire and Moray in 1180 and that this declaration is protected by the terms of Article 21 of the Act of Union 1707, concerning the rights of royal burghs, and has never been repealed.

S2W-8109 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will publish the results of its review of sports facilities.

S2W-8110 Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made of the effect of the withdrawal of the second post on small businesses.

S2W-8111 Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive how much has been paid out from the route development fund to Duo Airways Limited.

S2W-8112 Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment was made of the financial situation of Duo Airways Limited prior to the allocation of money from the route development fund.

S2W-8113 Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make an assessment in respect of whether any money already paid to Duo Airways Limited from the route development fund could be reclaimed due to the cancellation of its services.

S2W-8114 Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to minimise the use of fluorinated gases such as hydrofluorocarbons that are known to cause global warming.

S2W-8116 Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive what support is available to users of industrial refrigeration systems who wish to convert their machinery to less environmentally-damaging hydrocarbon refrigerants.
