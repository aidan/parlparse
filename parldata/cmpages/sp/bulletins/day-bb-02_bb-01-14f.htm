Business Bulletin No. 6/2002

Monday 14 January 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-2600# Ms Sandra White: Glasgow to Edinburgh Rail Service—That the Parliament notes with concern the current ScotRail dispute and the resulting cut in the Glasgow to Edinburgh route to a half hourly rail service; urges the Scottish Executive to make urgent representations to ScotRail to resolve the dispute and to ensure the earliest possible reintroduction of the 15 minute service, and recognises the necessity for Scotland’s largest two conurbations to be linked by the best possible rail service.

*S1M-2599 Fiona Hyslop: Edinburgh's Bus War—That the Parliament condemns the recent demand from Conservative leader David McLetchie that Lothian Buses, the last publicly owned bus company in Scotland, be privatised noting that such a policy would remove bus services needed by the most vulnerable in our society, particularly pensioners, and services which are needed by the community but which are not profitable, and notes that this new privatisation policy from the Conservatives follows on from their discredited deregulation of transport which has led to diminution of transport services including bus services provision.

*S1M-2598 Dorothy-Grace Elder: Maggie's Centre—That the Parliament warmly congratulates the Evening Times, Glasgow, for a splendid example of positive journalism in the public interest through the editor, staff and readers, including child cancer patients, raising the remarkable sum of £500,000 to create a "Maggie’s Centre" for cancer sufferers; regards such work as a splendid example of the continuing power of Scottish journalism to change lives for the better and of the astonishing generosity of the public of Glasgow, and notes with pleasure that a fine piece of once endangered Glasgow architecture, the Victorian Gatehouse at the Western Infirmary, will be saved through becoming a "Maggie’s Centre".

*S1M-2597# Dorothy-Grace Elder: Plight of Chronic Pain Patients—That the Parliament considers that the Scottish Executive and health boards should move the plight of chronic pain patients up the health agenda, chronic pain being regarded as the most neglected health issue in Scotland and possibly the biggest in terms of numbers as, according to the Pain Association Scotland, some 500,000 people suffer long-term pain through problems such as back pain and arthritic conditions, and agrees with health professionals who have appealed to the Parliament that the wreckage of many lives through lost jobs, and the loss of millions of pounds to the economy, could be relieved by ending the dire shortage of specialised pain clinics and staff in Scotland.

*S1M-2596 Robin Harper: Registration of Civil Partnerships—That the Parliament notes the proposed legislation laid before Westminster that is designed to afford couples in long-term relationships the right to legally register their relationships and calls on the Executive to progress equivalent legislation to establish a register of civil partnerships in Scotland and to afford couples who register their partnerships full legal recognition in areas relating to inheritance, pensions, bereavement, damages and all other relevant areas.

S1M-2595 – Withdrawn

S1M-2594 Mr Andrew Welsh: Nuclear Test Veterans—That the Parliament welcomes last month’s ruling by the Pensions Appeal Tribunal on Tom Duggan, a nuclear test veteran who served in the armed forces during Britain’s first A-bomb programme on Christmas Island in 1958 and contracted lymphatic cancer following exposure to radiation; notes that the tribunal’s decision could finally prove to be the decisive case that overturns decades of claims by Her Majesty’s Government that the Christmas Island explosions could not have harmed British servicemen; further notes the Ministry of Defence’s refusal to grant funding to a test pioneered at Dundee University that would determine the effects of radiation on servicemen, and calls upon the Scottish Executive to demand justice for all test veterans and take action where Her Majesty’s Government has failed by granting the £300,000 necessary to carry out these tests.

Supported by: Michael Matheson, Colin Campbell, Shona Robison, Christine Grahame*

S1M-2593 Ian Jenkins: Chernobyl Children Life Line Link with the Scottish Borders—That the Parliament commends the work of Chernobyl Children Life Line, which offers medical help and holidays to young victims of on-going radioactive contamination in Belarus; welcomes the formation of a link between this national charity and the Scottish Borders, and applauds its efforts to bring groups of youngsters to the Scottish Borders for a restorative and life-enhancing stay in Scotland.

Supported by: Christine Grahame*

S1M-2592# Mr Kenneth Macintosh: Continuing Presence of BSE and new variant CJD —That the Parliament notes the continuing presence of Bovine Spongiform Encephalopathy (BSE) in Scotland and the growing numbers of cases of new variant Creutzfeldt-Jakob Disease (CJD), including at least two cases in the parliamentary constituency of Eastwood; welcomes the setting up of a National Care Co-ordinator and CJD advice network, but acknowledges the devastating effects this disease can have on victims and their families and believes that the Scottish Executive should give its continuing support to those families affected by this terrible disease.

Supported by: Mr Michael McMahon, Brian Adam, Rhoda Grant, Tricia Marwick*, Margaret Jamieson*, Sarah Boyack*, Irene McGugan*, Irene Oldfather*

S1M-2591# Irene McGugan: Draft Guidance for Home Education—That the Parliament is concerned at the Scottish Executive issuing for consultation draft guidance on the circumstances in which parents may choose to educate their children at home, without taking proper account of the views of children and parents affected by this; does not support this action and recognises the considerable alarm its issue has generated within the home education community and the damage which it is likely to cause to relationships between families and education authorities; further recognises the fundamental human rights of parents and children to choose education outwith school and without being obstructed by the state; notes that section 14 of the Standards in Scotland's Schools etc. Act 2000, under which this draft guidance was issued, was introduced to protect home educating families from any harassment or intimidation by education authorities, as evidenced by an independent report from the Scottish Consumer Council; further notes that section 14 was supported by the Parliament on condition that the content of the guidance was fully informed by home educating families; refutes the consultation document's unfounded and insulting inferences that home educated children are in need of extraordinary measures of care and protection due to the exercise of a lawful educational choice which is equally valid to schooling; considers that the guidance exhorts local authorities to act beyond their powers under section 37 of the Education (Scotland) Act 1980 and seeks to condone unlawful breaches of data protection and human rights legislation; believes that the Minister for Education and Young People should apologise to the home education community in Scotland and withdraw the consultation document immediately on the grounds that the Scottish Executive has no authority to approve such breaches, and, in the light of its failure to address the serious issues raised in the Scottish Consumer Council's report and the many concerns of families who will be affected by such guidance, considers that the Scottish Executive should end the discrimination against home educating families in Scotland forthwith by amending the necessary laws to afford them the same legal rights as those families in England, whereby children may be removed from state schools upon a written notification.

S1M-2590 Tommy Sheridan: Motion by Jack McConnell on Student Loans—That the Parliament affirms that "education is a right and not a privilege to be paid for, that a student loans system would seriously affect the numbers of students who come from working class and ethnic minority backgrounds and would decrease the number of mature and women students entering higher education, and that instead of considering a loans system, the Government should be removing the means test and providing a full grant for all"; and notes that the above is the text of a motion moved by Jack McConnell at a General Meeting of the Students’ Association on 29 September 1980 when a student at Stirling University and congratulates him on his sentiments then.

S1M-2588# Mr Alasdair Morrison: Renewable Energy in the Western Isles—That the Parliament notes the development of the proposed renewable energy projects on the Isle of Lewis; recognises the substantial potential socio-economic and environmental benefits that they offer; congratulates the Stornoway Trust Estate for its professional handling of developments to date, and believes that ministers and officials of the Scottish Executive and Her Majesty’s Government should continue their excellent partnership working with all relevant agencies.

Supported by: Sarah Boyack, Trish Godman, Dr Sylvia Jackson, Mr Duncan McNeil, Maureen Macmillan, Rhoda Grant

S1M-2586 Brian Adam: Winter Maintenance of North East Roads—That the Parliament calls on the Minister for Enterprise, Transport and Lifelong Learning to investigate the performance of BEAR Scotland Ltd and councils in the north east in maintaining roads and pavements in a safe condition during the recent spell of well forecasted adverse weather.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson, Michael Matheson, Nora Radcliffe

S1M-2585# Angus MacKay: The Colin O'Riordan Trust —That the Parliament recognises the outstanding contribution made by the late Dr Colin O'Riordan to the provision of access to music for all children in Edinburgh, and particularly Sciennes Primary School, and throughout Scotland, in founding the City of Edinburgh Music School and in the many youth orchestra activities with which he was associated, and congratulates his family on the foundation of the Colin O'Riordan Trust, which aims to assist aspiring young musicians by promoting musical excellence and improving access to musical instruments for those children who might otherwise not have the opportunity to pursue music.

Supported by: Rhoda Grant, Irene McGugan, Trish Godman, Brian Fitzpatrick, Mr Brian Monteith, Sarah Boyack, Margaret Smith, Nora Radcliffe, Mr Keith Harding, Ms Margo MacDonald

S1M-2584 Tommy Sheridan: Scottish Power Industrial Dispute—That the Parliament supports the strike action of Scottish Power workers in pursuit of their agreement of 31 May 2000 with Scottish Power management which stated that no job losses and no fundamental changes to conditions would result from the trade unions’ agreement to work in partnership alongside the company; regrets that Scottish Power management is now reneging on this agreement and is intent on fundamentally changing working conditions, which includes transferring staff against their will to a new joint venture company; notes that this has resulted in a democratic ballot of union members within Scottish Power; further notes union members’ decision to take part in a series of two-day strike actions, and therefore calls on the management of Scottish Power to end its confrontational stance and honour the agreement it signed in good faith with the unions in order to avoid any further inconvenience to Scottish citizens, particularly in relation to power cuts and consequential safety issues.

S1M-2581 Roseanna Cunningham: Perth Royal Infirmary Laboratory Services—That the Parliament notes the discussion document produced by Tayside University Hospitals NHS Trust on the future of laboratory services at Perth Royal Infirmary (PRI); recognises that upheaval and uncertainty have been the hallmark of health service provision in Perthshire in recent years and that patients, staff and the general public require a period of stability; welcomes the recognition within the document that, as an Acute Hospital with the full range of District General Hospital Services, PRI will require on-site laboratory services for biochemical medicine, haematology and blood transfusion "at the very least"; acknowledges the strong arguments put forward for relocating laboratory facilities closer to the wards, and rejects suggestions that pathology and microbiology services be relocated to Ninewells Hospital.

Supported by: Mr John Swinney, Bruce Crawford, Michael Matheson

S1M-2580 Mr Andy Kerr: Education (Disability Strategies and Pupils' Records) (Scotland) Bill: Financial Resolution—That the Parliament for the purposes of any Act of the Scottish Parliament resulting from the Education (Disability Strategies and Pupils’ Records) (Scotland) Bill, agrees to any increase in expenditure payable out of the Scottish Consolidated Fund in consequence of the Act.

Supported by: Cathy Jamieson, Nicol Stephen, Peter Peacock

S1M-2579# Mr Jamie Stone: Non-Trunk Roads—That the Parliament notes that the first serious winter weather has resulted in non-trunk roads in the Highlands being in an extremely dangerous condition; recognises that this has come about as a result of the Highland Council downsizing its winter roads maintenance service as a result of the trunk roads maintenance contract being awarded to BEAR Scotland Ltd, and accordingly considers that the Scottish Executive should undertake an urgent review of all roads maintenance in the Highland Council area in order to avoid any future repetition of the recent dangerous problems.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan

S1M-2576 Irene McGugan: Instrumental Teaching in Schools and Support for Music—That the Parliament notes with concern the application of the criterion of "ability to pay" in relation to access to instrumental teaching, which is a crucial element of the music curriculum at Standard and Higher Still grades in schools; believes that such teaching should be available to all children, free at the point of delivery, and calls upon the Scottish Executive to examine the structural underfunding of music which exists and adopt all necessary measures to ensure that every element and genre of musical activity is properly supported and developed in order that future generations of Scottish musicians can continue to achieve international recognition in traditional, classical, acoustic, jazz, folk, rock and popular music.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan, Mr Kenneth Gibson, Linda Fabiani, Alex Neil

  • MSPs, but not other members of Cross-Party Groups, may make reasonable use of the Parliament’s telephone, fax, photocopying, IT facilities and Parliamentary stationary in pursuit of Cross-Party business or where expressly permitted by these rules e.g. use of the Cross-Party Group Bulletin. Groups may not otherwise use the Parliament’s telephone, fax, photocopying, IT facilities and Parliamentary stationery other than where these are available for public use.

  • Groups may not make use of free postage facilities provided by the Parliament.

(b) leave out the sixth bullet point.
