Business Bulletin No. 34/2002

Thursday 28 February 2002

Section D : Oral Questions selected for answer on 7 March 2002
  

Question Time

1. Mr Keith Raffan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether Historic Scotland's powers to protect the built heritage are adequate. (S1O-4801)

2. Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive what transport projects would go ahead if the additional notional allocation of £84 million in the transport budget, outlined in the report Is Enough Being Spent on Transport in Scotland?, were made available. (S1O-4833)

3. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking in order to ensure that beaches meet European standards. (S1O-4810)

4. Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking in order to increase the number of unroadworthy cars that are dismantled and recycled. (S1O-4804)

5. Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking in order to protect wildlife. (S1O-4827)

6. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken in order to support the manufacturing and food processing sectors in the north-east of Scotland. (S1O-4782)

7. Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding it provides for repairs to school buildings. (S1O-4800)

8. Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will prioritise the purchase of more sleep apnoea machines in view of waiting times for sleep apnoea treatment. (S1O-4787)

9. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking in order to promote social work as a career. (S1O-4829)

10. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will take any action in order to ensure that fair packages for pay and conditions are negotiated with education advisers, educational psychologists and musical instrument instructors and that all those affected have a chance to vote on their package. (S1O-4816)

11. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the trustees of the Scottish Transport Group Pension Funds about the winding up of the pension schemes. (S1O-4821)

12. Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it is giving to less prosperous communities in order to close the opportunity gap. (S1O-4799)

13. Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking in order to improve the competitiveness of universities. (S1O-4828)

14. Colin Campbell: To ask the Scottish Executive how it measures the effectiveness of legislation designed to control anti-social tenants and how many anti-social behaviour orders have been served nationally to date. (S1O-4777)

15. Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will introduce the Public Bodies (Scotland) Bill. (S1O-4793)

16. Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking in order to tackle drug misuse in local communities. (S1O-4776)

17. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are being introduced in order to tackle conditions of the respiratory system following the report by the British Thoracic Society, The Burden of Lung Disease. (S1O-4807)

18. Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it is providing to the police in order to ensure that racist incidents are dealt with appropriately. (S1O-4832)

19. Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made of the impact of the Protection of Wild Mammals (Scotland) Bill on fox control operations in the Highlands. (S1O-4771)

20. Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive why the Record of Needs will be replaced with a Co-ordinated Support Plan. (S1O-4798)

21. Janis Hughes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to review the assessment and provision of education services for young people with special educational needs. (S1O-4812)

22. Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to prevent unused prescribed drugs from being unnecessarily destroyed. (S1O-4772)

23. Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to raise awareness of and promote the learning of modern languages among young people. (S1O-4811)

24. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what consultation there has been on the proposed GM crop trial at Newport-on-Tay. (S1O-4779)

25. Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking in order to assist local authorities to tackle any backlog of repairs on the non-trunk road network. (S1O-4818)

26. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive when it next plans to review the energy efficiency requirements within building regulations. (S1O-4773)

27. Mr Keith Harding: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Minister for Social Justice last met representatives of Fife Council and what issues were discussed. (S1O-4802)

28. Mr Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will give evidence to the European Parliament's recently established temporary committee on foot-and-mouth disease. (S1O-4815)

29. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it uses independent observers in order to determine how many dolphins and other cetaceans are caught accidentally in trawlers' nets. (S1O-4794)

30. Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met representatives of the Loch Lomond and the Trossachs Interim Committee and what issues were discussed. (S1O-4803)
