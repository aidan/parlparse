Business Bulletin No. 129/2009: Thursday 10 September 2009
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 9 September 2009

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-27036 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether its civil servants are able to support the pursuit of independence in the same way as other Scottish Government policies.

S3W-27037 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what it considers to be the statutory basis of the authority of a minority government should it be defeated in a vote in the Parliament.

S3W-27038 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive on what authority its civil servants can be encouraged by a senior civil servant to capitalise on conflict with UK Government departments as referred to in the report in The Scotsman on 7 September 2009.

S3W-27056 Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what evaluations it has made of the effectiveness of mental health programmes for children of primary school age.

S3W-27057 Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what evaluations it has made of the effectiveness of in-school mental health programmes for children of primary school age.

S3W-27058 Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to encourage the use of in-school mental health programmes for children of primary school age where there is none.

S3W-27059Δ Angela Constance: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will recognise the outstanding achievement of the Scottish competitors in the WorldSkills UK team in Calgary last week.

S3W-27060 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive for what purpose the First Minister met representatives of the Qatar Investment Authority on 7 May 2008; who was present at the meeting and for what reasons, and what issues were on the agenda.

S3W-27061 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many meetings have been held between Scottish ministers and officials and representatives of the Qatar Investment Authority since 2007; who was present at each meeting, and what issues were on the agenda.

S3W-27062 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any future meetings are planned between Scottish ministers and officials and representatives of the Qatar Investment Authority.

S3W-27063 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what options are available for it to make available the evidence compiled by the Scottish Criminal Cases Review Commission regarding Abdelbaset Ali Mohmed Al Megrahi’s recently dropped second appeal and have that and other relevant evidence judicially tested.

S3W-27064 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it will take to secure the release of evidence compiled by the Scottish Criminal Cases Review Commission regarding Abdelbaset Ali Mohmed Al Megrahi’s recently dropped second appeal.

S3W-27065 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Cabinet Secretary for Justice has the power to order disclosure of the evidence compiled by the Scottish Criminal Cases Review Commission regarding Abdelbaset Ali Mohmed Al Megrahi’s recently dropped second appeal and, if so, whether he will do so.

S3W-27066 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what powers it has to hold an inquiry into the untested findings of the Scottish Criminal Cases Review Commission report regarding Abdelbaset Ali Mohmed Al Megrahi’s recently dropped second appeal.

S3W-27067 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to hold an inquiry into the untested findings of the Scottish Criminal Cases Review Commission report regarding Abdelbaset Ali Mohmed Al Megrahi’s recently dropped second appeal.

S3W-27068 Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive how many times since May 2007 its officials have held meetings with government ministers from other countries, broken down by country and date of meeting, also showing a summary of the topics of discussions.

S3W-27069 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that the draft guidance on the national eligibility criteria for adult social care and waiting times for personal and nursing care will adversely impact on preventative services for disabled adults.

S3W-27070 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what support is being given to local authorities and NHS boards to continue the funding of preventative services for people with learning disabilities and their carers, given the emphasis on critical and substantial risk in the draft national eligibility criteria for adult social care and waiting times for personal and nursing care.

S3W-27071 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider reconfiguring the draft national eligibility criteria for adult social care and waiting times for personal and nursing care following the consultation period to take into account recent policy directions such as the National Guidance on Self-Directed Support and the recommendations from Changing Lives.

S3W-27072 Alison McInnes: To ask the Scottish Executive, following the Climate Change (Scotland) Act 2009 receiving Royal Assent, whether it plans to undertake an early review of the second National Planning Framework to ensure that it is compatible with the Act, and what its reasons are for its position on this matter.

S3W-27073 Alison McInnes: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration was given to the impact of additional greenhouse gas emissions that may result from the policy and the national developments put forward in the second National Planning Framework.

S3W-27074 Alison McInnes: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will assist local planning authorities to comply with section 3F of the Town and Country Planning (Scotland) Act 1997, as introduced by section 72 of the Climate Change (Scotland) Act 2009, in general and specifically where a national development with significant carbon impacts has been identified in their development plan area.

S3W-27075 Alison McInnes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that carbon impact is a material consideration in determining applications for planning and related consents.

S3W-27076 Alison McInnes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that an application for planning or other consent could be refused on carbon-impact grounds.

S3W-27077 Alison McInnes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that an application for planning or other consent for a proposed development identified as a national development in the second National Planning Framework could be refused on carbon-impact grounds.

S3W-27078 Alison McInnes: To ask the Scottish Executive which statutory body provides advice to Scottish ministers and local planning authorities on the carbon impact of proposed projects or plans.

S3W-27079 Alison McInnes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to undertake a review of permitted development rights where existing permitted development rights could result in significant additional greenhouse gas emissions.

S3W-27080 Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to evaluate the benefit and success of the Town Centre Regeneration Fund before making any decision on continuing the fund in 2010-11.

S3W-27081 Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive what factors will determine whether a Town Centre Regeneration Fund will be provided in 2010-11.

S3W-27082 Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to be in a position to announce whether it will continue to provide a Town Centre Regeneration Fund in 2010-11.

S3W-27083 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive how many charges under the Emergency Workers (Scotland) Act 2005 have been reported to procurators fiscal in each year since 2005.

S3W-27084 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive how many charges under the Emergency Workers (Scotland) Act 2005 reported to procurators fiscal have been the subject of court proceedings in each year since 2005.

S3W-27085 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive how many charges under the Emergency Workers (Scotland) Act 2005 that have been reported to procurators fiscal in each year since 2005 resulted in a successful prosecution, broken down by section of the Act.

S3W-27086 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reasons it considers it reasonable for each local authority to require a different disclosure arrangement for each supply teacher when the relevant teacher has a record of service in other local authority areas where disclosure has taken place.

S3W-27087 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive, given the shortage of teaching positions and the rise in those seeking teaching supply positions, whether it will consider making teaching disclosure arrangements portable so that there are no unnecessary delays in supply teachers securing work.

S3W-27088 Bill Aitken: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-26897 by Kenny MacAskill on 8 September 2009 in relation to the foreign national released on compassionate grounds, whether it will provide the (a) length of sentence served, (b) country to which the person was returned, (c) crime for which the person was convicted, (d) date of release and (v) name of the person.

S3W-27089 Jamie Hepburn: To ask the Scottish Executive whether its Housing Investment Division has a locus in deciding whether changes sought by registered social landlords to their structure should go ahead.

S3W-27090 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many direct measures were issued for (a) assault and (b) assault to injury in July 2009, broken down by sheriff court.

S3W-27091 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many direct measures were issued for (a) assault and (b) assault to injury in August 2009, broken down by sheriff court.

S3W-27092 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answers to questions S1W-26687 by Hugh Henry on 26 June 2002 and S2W-170 by Margaret Curran on 3 June 2003, how many compulsory purchase orders were instigated and successfully completed by each local authority in 2008, broken down by categories where appropriate.

S3W-27093 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the acquiring authority was for each compulsory purchase order issued in 2008 where Scottish ministers were the confirming authority.

S3W-27094 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what proportion of the compulsory purchase orders issued in 2008 was for (a) road schemes, (b) other transport projects, (c) housing, (d) other private developments and (e) other types of project where Scottish Ministers were the confirming authority.

S3W-27106 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it proposes that the proposed Referendum Bill should be subject to pre-legislative scrutiny and, if so, in what form and by whom.
