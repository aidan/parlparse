Business Bulletin No. 36/2002

Monday 4 March 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-2822# Shona Robison: Sale and Use of Fireworks-That the Parliament notes the results of the survey carried out by the Scottish Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals in which almost 98% of people supported tighter restrictions on the availability of fireworks; recognises that there has been a large increase in the problems caused for both people and animals by the irresponsible use of fireworks; is concerned that the voluntary code of practice for the sale of fireworks is frequently disregarded, and considers that the Scottish Executive should do everything in its power to tighten up on the regulations relating to the sale and use of fireworks and, in particular, should review the powers available to local authorities in respect of vendor licensing.

*S1M-2821# John Scott: Strategic Approach to the Development and Protection of Scottish Coastal Waters-That the Parliament recognises the need for the development of an integrated strategic approach to the sustainable development and protection of Scotland's coastal waters and marine environment, with particular regard to fishing, fish farming, wave and tidal power generation and the protection of identifiably unique Scottish marine habitats.

Supported by: Mr Jamie McGrigor*, David Mundell*

*S1M-2820 Dr Sylvia Jackson: Funding for Loch Lomond and the Trossachs National Park-That the Parliament welcomes the Scottish Executive's announcement of £4.8 million funding for the Loch Lomond and the Trossachs National Park; recognises the social, economic and environmental benefits that the park will bring to the Loch Lomond and the Trossachs area, much of which lies in Stirling constituency, and congratulates Scottish Ministers for listening to the concerns of local people and working with all involved to provide a funding package that will allow Scotland's first national park to flourish.

Supported by: Jackie Baillie*, Brian Fitzpatrick*, Des McNulty*, Cathy Peattie*, Margaret Jamieson*, Angus MacKay*, Janis Hughes*, Karen Whitefield*, Cathie Craigie*, Pauline McNeill*, Mr Duncan McNeil*, Mr Tom McCabe*, Mr Kenneth Macintosh*, Irene Oldfather*, Trish Godman*, Johann Lamont*, Bristow Muldoon*, Scott Barrie*, Rhoda Grant*, Paul Martin*

*S1M-2819# Karen Whitefield: North Lanarkshire Council - COSLA Award-That the Parliament recognises the devastating effect that anti-social behaviour can have on communities across Scotland; congratulates North Lanarkshire Council on winning the COSLA award for top performing authority in customer focus for its work in establishing an anti-social behaviour taskforce which has dealt with over 300 cases since its inception in January 2001, and believes that this example of good practice should be extended throughout Scotland.

*S1M-2818# Colin Campbell: Regeneration-That the Parliament recognises that a lack of appropriate jobs is a major problem in Scotland; notes that a main cause is the decline of heavy industry in the past three decades; considers that all local authorities, MSPs and key agencies should work together to implement a regeneration programme, and believes that the Scottish Executive should provide resources for its speedy completion.

Supported by: Andrew Wilson*, Mr Lloyd Quinan*, Mr Gil Paterson*, Mr Adam Ingram*, Mrs Margaret Ewing*, Christine Grahame*, Linda Fabiani*, Richard Lochhead*, Mr Kenny MacAskill*, Mr Duncan Hamilton*, Fiona McLeod*, Michael Russell*

*S1M-2817 Richard Lochhead: Labour Administration of Aberdeen City Council-That the Parliament condemns Aberdeen City Council's Labour administration for its flagrant disregard of the citizens of the city by voting to (a) allow the Lord Provost to continue to take a friend on overseas trips, (b) close the Grandholm Bridge, which will once again bring gridlock to the city and (c) abolish the meals-on-wheels service and replace it with a fortnightly frozen food delivery and, in light of these contemptuous actions, calls on the council's Labour administration to resign immediately.

S1M-2815 Ms Sandra White: Glasgow University Student Accommodation Sell Off-That the Parliament notes with concern the plans of the University of Glasgow to sell off over half of its student accommodation to a private housing association; further notes the concerns of students with regard to resulting rent increases, the lack of consultation with student bodies on campus and their exclusion from key talks on the issue; believes that this can only result in increased hardship for students already burdened with debt from loans and tuition fees, and therefore calls on the University of Glasgow to reconsider the decision to sell off its student accommodation.

S1M-2814 Ms Sandra White: Sun-Tanning Parlours-That the Parliament notes with concern recent reports linking the operation of sun-tanning parlours with criminal activities; further notes the recent proliferation of such parlours particularly in Glasgow; urges the Scottish Executive to instigate an immediate investigation into the operation of sun-tanning parlours, and calls for a moratorium on the operation of sun-tanning parlours until such an investigation has been completed.

S1M-2813 Mr Lloyd Quinan: New Housing Partnership-That the Parliament notes the motion passed by Labour-controlled Renfrewshire Council whereby the council fully supports the retention of its housing stock, supports the tenants' position that debt associated with the council's houses should be written off without the need to transfer ownership of housing stock, endorses the recommendations contained within the Board Report and seeks an early meeting with Ministers in order to develop options for retaining the housing stock within council ownership with affordable rent levels, whilst delivering the repairs and improvements identified within its consultant's report; congratulates Renfrewshire Council for adopting SNP policy, and calls on the Scottish Executive to make representations to Her Majesty's Government in order to ensure that funds are available to write off the housing debt for councils that do not opt for housing stock transfer in order to ensure that they are able to carry out any repairs needed.

Supported by: Ms Sandra White, Michael Matheson, Alex Neil, Ms Margo MacDonald, Mr Gil Paterson, Christine Grahame, Brian Adam, Mr Adam Ingram

S1M-2812 Mr Duncan McNeil: Euro 2008-That the Parliament welcomes the announcement that the Scottish Football Association and the Football Association of Ireland are to launch a joint bid to host the Euro 2008 football championships; notes that every major party in the Parliament has, at least in the past if not at present, supported such a joint bid; further notes the potential of the bid to bring all the benefits for tourism and the economy that would have resulted from a solo bid by Scotland without burdening the country with four expensive and unneeded 30,000-seater stadiums; looks forward to the opportunity of working and renewing ties with our Irish neighbours, and urges the whole country to back the bid.

Supported by: Mr John McAllion, Bristow Muldoon, Johann Lamont, Trish Godman, Rhoda Grant, Dr Sylvia Jackson, Margaret Jamieson, Jackie Baillie*, Pauline McNeill*, Mr Tom McCabe*, Brian Fitzpatrick*, Irene Oldfather*

S1M-2811 Robin Harper: Spike Milligan 1918-2002-That the Parliament notes with sadness the death of Spike Milligan at the age of 83; recognises his extraordinary contribution to the development of satire, the extensive contribution he made in support of a large number of charities and campaigning organisations including Greenpeace, Friends of the Earth and the Society for the Preservation of Victorian Lamposts; further notes that he was fiercely against fox hunting, and conveys its sympathy to all of his family. S1M-2810 Mr Mike Rumbles: Code of Conduct for Members of the Scottish Parliament: Confidentiality-That the Parliament agrees to amend the Code of Conduct for Members of the Scottish Parliament by replacing Section 9.4 with Section 9.4 as set out in Annex D of the Standards Committee's 8th Report 2001, Report on the Investigation of Unauthorised Disclosures.

S1M-2809 Christine Grahame: Sport for Disabled People in the Scottish Borders and the Waterside Fitness Club, Jedburgh-That the Parliament congratulates the representatives from the Scottish Borders who will be taking part in the World Special Olympics in Dublin in 2003, namely badminton players Ronald Young, Graeme Lang and Steven George, swimmer Alan German, Gordon Yule the UK badminton coach and Jed Renilson the Assistant Head of Delegation; notes with concern that Jedburgh swimming pool is currently under threat of closure due to the £5.9 million cuts by Scottish Borders Council's administration at a time when athletes from all over the Borders attend the pool for Special Olympics Regional Squad sessions on a weekly basis and that the pool is essential not only for those athletes, but the entire community, with swimming lessons taking place Monday to Saturday with 16 classes and 10 pupils to a class, disabled lessons with 1 to 1 tuition and waiting lists for all swimming lessons, parent and baby classes, fun sessions for children and crash swimming courses; further notes that the pool is used by over 600 pupils per week from the primary schools Parkside, Howdenburn, Hobkirk, Keilder/Bryness, Glendouglas, Jedburgh Grammar, Earlston High and Howdenburn School House Special Needs with disabled dippers' groups, community education group canoeing, Jedburgh Rugby Football Club and Women's Borders Aid also as user groups, and calls upon the Scottish Executive to ensure that this essential community facility does not close.

S1M-2808 Robert Brown: Ratification of the Optional Protocol to the UN Convention on the Rights of the Child on the Involvement of Children in Armed Conflict-That the Parliament notes that Her Majesty's Government has not yet ratified the Optional Protocol to the UN Convention on the Rights of the Child on the Involvement of Children in Armed Conflict; notes the interest of this Parliament in protecting the rights of children; believes that children under 18 are entitled to the protection of the safeguards in the Protocol, and urges Her Majesty's Government to ratify the Protocol as soon as possible.

S1M-2807 Mr Duncan Hamilton: Lack of General Practitioner in Helmsdale-That the Parliament regrets the recent decision by Highland NHS Board not to seek a replacement general practitioner for Helmsdale; notes the negative impact which this will have on the local community; condemns the Board's consultation process as deeply flawed; deplores the fact that some residents must now have to travel up to fifty miles for the services of a GP, and calls upon the Scottish Executive to do all it can to ensure that a GP is appointed in Helmsdale as soon as possible.

S1M-2805 Mr Brian Monteith: Edinburgh Institute for the Study of the Arab World and Islam-That the Parliament notes the publication of the annual newsletter of the Edinburgh Institute for the Study of the Arab World and Islam and congratulates it on the accomplishment of 250 years of teaching Arabic and Islamic Studies in Scotland; believes that it is a tribute to the University of Edinburgh that the department has been awarded a five star rating in the recent Research Assessment Exercise, and wishes it every success in attracting more students to these studies.

Supported by: Mr David Davidson, Mr Keith Harding, Tommy Sheridan*, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton*, Michael Russell*

S1M-2804 Pauline McNeill: The College of Piping-That the Parliament notes with pleasure the recent £300,000 end of year flexibility grant to the Piping Centre in Glasgow; regrets, however, that once again the College of Piping, which has been in existence since 1944, currently has more than 150 pupils on its register and maintains a teaching network covering the whole of Scotland, has failed to receive similar support, and therefore calls on the Scottish Executive to consider distributing financial support to such bodies in a fairer and more equitable way.

Supported by: Maureen Macmillan, Tommy Sheridan*, Michael Russell*

S1M-2803 Trish Godman: Fair Trade Fortnight-That the Parliament warmly welcomes Fair Trade Fortnight (4-17 March 2002); believes that Fair Trade activity, encouraged as it is by Oxfam Scotland and other non-governmental organisations and agencies, performs a valuable role in assisting with the setting up of women's co-operatives, small businesses and village enterprises throughout the developing world which provide important economic and social assistance to the families and communities of those involved in such productive activities, and commends the shops and stores which sell Fair Trade products and individual shoppers, local authorities, schools and churches who buy such products.

Supported by: Mr Kenneth Macintosh, Maureen Macmillan, Tommy Sheridan*, Michael Russell*

S1M-2791 Mr Duncan Hamilton: Travis Success At Brit Awards-That the Parliament congratulates Travis for its success at the recent Brit Awards in winning the Best British Band category; notes the tremendous achievements of Travis and other Scottish bands in the music industry, and looks forward to more success from Scottish bands in the future.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson, Michael Matheson, Mr Brian Monteith, Michael Russell*

S1M-2790# Mr Jamie McGrigor: Environmentally Designated Areas-That the Parliament notes with alarm concerns expressed by local communities highlighted by recent petitions to the public petitions committee (PE462 to 464) about procedures and scientific data used by Scottish Natural Heritage (SNH) for environmentally designated areas, such as Special Protection Areas, Special Areas of Conservation and Sites of Special Scientific Interest and believes that a programme of comprehensive consultation will result in more effective participation and co-operation between SNH and local people within the proposed areas of designation, which will result in better relations between SNH and local communities.

Supported by: Phil Gallie, Murdo Fraser, Mr David Davidson, Alex Fergusson, John Scott, John Young, Mrs Lyndsay McIntosh, Fiona McLeod, Nora Radcliffe, David Mundell, Bill Aitken, Tavish Scott, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton, Mary Scanlon, Dorothy-Grace Elder, Mr Keith Harding, Mr Brian Monteith, Michael Russell*

S1M-2789 Robin Harper: Protection of Scottish Coral Reefs-That the Parliament notes with concern the recent research from the Marine Biological Station on Cumbrae, published by the Royal Society, which shows that large and diverse coral reefs off the north-west of Scotland which are more than 4,500 years old and the habitat of diverse and unique marine species are suffering wide-scale fishing damage and requests that the Scottish Executive and Her Majesty's Government take urgent action to protect the "Darwin Mounds" and other environmentally significant areas of deep-sea coral, including designating them as marine Special Areas of Conservation.

Supported by: Alex Neil

S1M-2788# Angus MacKay: The Tourism Industry-That the Parliament recognises that the economy of Edinburgh and the whole of Scotland depends greatly on not only attracting tourists but also encouraging them to return; further recognises the importance of tourist accommodation and attractions being of the highest possible quality, and considers that the Scottish Executive should encourage and support the maintenance of high standards in the tourism industry to ensure that visitors to Scotland have a memorable experience and return.

Supported by: Mr Kenny MacAskill, Mr Duncan Hamilton, Trish Godman, Mr David Davidson, Robert Brown, Rhoda Grant, Brian Fitzpatrick, Mr Kenneth Macintosh, Tavish Scott, Sarah Boyack, Alex Neil, Margaret Jamieson, Mr Alasdair Morrison, Elaine Thomson, Brian Adam, Maureen Macmillan, Susan Deacon, Michael Russell*

S1M-2787 Mr Gil Paterson: Stop Indecent Displays in Corner Shops-That the Parliament recognises that the public sale and display of pornographic material in our local stores, newsagents and garages is completely unacceptable; further recognises that pornography eroticises violence against women and children and desensitises the public to the meaning of violence towards women in real life, and calls on the Executive to ensure that the Civic Government (Scotland) Act 1982 is fully and effectively enforced and to review this legislation to ensure that it is adequate.

Supported by: Alex Neil

S1M-2786# Miss Annabel Goldie: Renfrewshire Economy-That the Parliament notes with pleasure the very positive Special Report on the Renfrewshire economy in business a.m. on 25 February 2002; congratulates all agencies involved in creating this economic success, and commends the Renfrewshire model to the rest of Scotland as an example of good practice.

Supported by: David Mundell, Murdo Fraser, Phil Gallie, John Young, Mr Murray Tosh, Mrs Lyndsay McIntosh, Mr Jamie Stone, Fiona McLeod, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton, Mr Brian Monteith, John Scott, Bill Aitken, Alex Neil, Trish Godman, Mr Keith Harding

S1M-2785# Elaine Smith: World Day for Water - 22 March 2002-That the Parliament notes the resolution A/RES/47/193 adopted by the United Nations General Assembly on 22 December 1992, which declared 22 March of each year as World Day for Water; further notes that this year the highly pertinent theme is "Water and Health"; expresses concern that many of our children do not have access to free drinking water whilst at school; commends North Lanarkshire Council and the South Coatbridge Social Inclusion Partnership for initiating pilot schemes in four Coatbridge primary schools to supply free, accessible drinking water to pupils, and considers that the Scottish Executive should recognise and promote the importance for our children's health and well-being of providing free, accessible drinking water in all schools.

Supported by: Mr Kenneth Gibson, Alex Neil, Mary Scanlon*, Dorothy-Grace Elder*, Richard Lochhead*, Fiona McLeod*, Pauline McNeill*, Mr John McAllion*

S1M-2784 Johann Lamont: International Women's Day - 8 March 2002-That the Parliament notes that 8 March 2002 is International Women's Day; congratulates the Women Together group in the Pollok constituency and women across Scotland and around the world who are organising events to celebrate the work of women past, present and future; recognises the central role of women in our communities in meeting needs today and creating change for tomorrow and beyond, and urges the Scottish Executive and the Parliament's committees, in partnership with women's organisations, to examine how women's needs and rights might more effectively be addressed across the range of services, departments and organisations that impact on women's lives.

Supported by: Sarah Boyack, Mr Kenneth Gibson, Brian Fitzpatrick, Marilyn Livingstone, Mr George Reid, Karen Whitefield, Alex Neil, Maureen Macmillan, Michael Russell*

S1M-2783# Marilyn Livingstone: National Strategy for Survivors of Childhood Sexual Abuse-That the Parliament welcomes the work of the Cross-Party Group on Survivors of Childhood Sexual Abuse in co-ordinating and developing an outline national strategy for children and adults who have experienced sexual abuse during childhood and, in light of recommendations made by the independent inquiry commissioned by Fife Council following the conviction of David Logan Murphy, believes that the Scottish Executive should actively develop a national strategy, based on the work of the Cross-Party Group on Survivors of Childhood Sexual Abuse, that considers the wider social, health, mental and legal implications of sexual abuse and its impact on survivors and society as a whole.

Supported by: Mr Kenneth Gibson, Stewart Stevenson, Elaine Thomson, David Mundell, Mr John McAllion, Karen Gillon, Rhoda Grant, Fiona McLeod, Trish Godman, Kate Maclean, Jackie Baillie, Brian Fitzpatrick, Dr Sylvia Jackson, Tavish Scott, Mrs Lyndsay McIntosh, Alex Neil, Mr Keith Harding, Margaret Jamieson, Karen Whitefield, Dorothy-Grace Elder, Maureen Macmillan, Cathie Craigie, Mr Kenneth Macintosh

S1M-2778 Ian Jenkins: Scotland's Medal-Winning Winter Olympians-That the Parliament warmly congratulates Team GB on its success at the 2002 Olympic Winter Games in Salt Lake City; admires in particular the outstanding achievements of Scottish members of the team; applauds the women's curling team, Rhona Martin, Debbie Knox, Fiona MacDonald, Janice Rankin and Margaret Morton, for winning a gold medal for Great Britain for the first time in 18 years in any event; further applauds Alain Baxter, also known as "The Highlander", for winning a bronze medal in the men's slalom, the first ever medal for Great Britain in any alpine event, and celebrates with pride the achievements of the entire Great Britain team, including coaching and support, following our most successful Olympic Winter Games since 1936.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie, Mary Scanlon*

S1M-2777 Trish Godman: Scottish Medallists at the Winter Olympics-That the Parliament warmly congratulates the members of the Great Britain Olympic women's curling team and Alain Baxter for their outstanding achievements at the Winter Olympic Games; acknowledges their dedication and hard work in preparing for the Games and commends all of those clubs and individuals who have given such unstinting support to these Scottish gold and bronze medallists and other Scottish competitors at the Olympics.

Supported by: Mr John McAllion, Johann Lamont, Janis Hughes, Mr John Home Robertson, Bristow Muldoon, Dr Sylvia Jackson, Rhoda Grant, Elaine Thomson, Brian Fitzpatrick, Margaret Jamieson, Mr Kenneth Macintosh, Elaine Smith, Des McNulty, Jackie Baillie, Bruce Crawford, Karen Whitefield, Dennis Canavan, Susan Deacon, Cathie Craigie

S1M-2776 Bruce Crawford: The Scotland Act 1998 (Agency Arrangements) (Specification) Order 2002-That the Transport and the Environment Committee recommends that nothing further be done under the Scotland Act 1998 (Agency Arrangements) (Specification) Order 2002.

Supported by: Fiona McLeod

S1M-2775# Mr Kenneth Gibson: Scotland's Participation in Future Olympic Games-That the Parliament congratulates all those who represented Great Britain in the recent Winter Olympic Games, particularly the all-Scottish women's curling team for their magnificent achievement in winning the gold medal and Alain Baxter for his success in the men's slalom; regrets that while Scotland competes in the cricket, football and rugby world cups and the Commonwealth Games in its own right, it does not do so in the Olympics; recognises that while most Olympic teams are from independent nations, the International Olympic Committee also recognises participants from independent territories, commonwealths, protectorates and others; believes that for the Athens Olympiad in 2004 and subsequent winter and summer Olympic games, Scotland should join the other 199 countries of the Olympic movement in participating in its own right, and considers that the Scottish Executive should work to that end.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson, Mr Andrew Welsh, Michael Matheson

S1M-2774 Michael Russell: Congratulations to Alain Baxter-That the Parliament congratulates Alain Baxter on winning the bronze medal in the men's slalom at the Winter Olympic Games, becoming the first Scottish skier to win a medal in such a competition; expresses support for his wish to declare his Scottishness publicly and visibly at the Games, and demands that the British Olympic Committee withdraws its objection, made in contravention of the Olympic Charter, to displays of the Scottish national flag by Scottish athletes.

Supported by: Mrs Margaret Ewing, Stewart Stevenson, Mr Kenneth Gibson, Donald Gorrie, Alex Neil, Michael Matheson
