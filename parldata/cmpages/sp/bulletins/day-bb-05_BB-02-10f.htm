Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

* S2M-2413 Shiona Baird:Historic Scotland Ranger Service, Holyrood Park—That the Parliament commends the Historic Scotland Ranger Service for its dedicated work in preserving the unique heritage of Holyrood Park; congratulates it on organising walks for MSPs during the Parliament’s Environment Week to inform them of this heritage, and urges more MSPs to engage with the fascinating history and archaeology on their doorstep.

* S2M-2412 Bill Aitken on behalf of the Edinburgh Tram (Line Two) Bill Committee:Edinburgh Tram (Line Two) Bill - Preliminary Stage—That the Parliament agrees to the general principles of the Edinburgh Tram (Line Two) Bill and that the Bill should proceed as a Private Bill.

Supported by: Jeremy Purvis*

* S2M-2411# Bill Butler:Co-operative Education—That the Parliament commends the work being undertaken by the Co-operative Group in Scotland to work in partnership with the Scottish Executive and local authorities to introduce knowledge and experience of co-operative principles into the mainstream curriculum of Scottish education; recognises that co-operatives offer a unique and valuable form of business structure from which Scotland can derive economic and social benefit and recognises the need to make more Scots aware of the value of co-operation; supports the establishment of the Co-operative Education Trust Scotland which will involve the wider co-operative movement in education, and believes that the Scottish Executive, Careers Scotland, Learning and Teaching Scotland and local authorities should continue to work with the co-operative movement to ensure the distribution of co-operative resource materials for use in schools, support for teacher placements in the co-operative business environment and support for the involvement of the young co-operatives in schools.

Supported by: Eleanor Scott*, Mr Kenneth Macintosh*, Rosie Kane*

* S2M-2409 Carolyn Leckie:Justice and Apology for the Conlon and Maguire Families—That the Parliament welcomes the visit to the House of Commons on 9 February 2005 by Gerry Conlon and other members of the Conlon and Maguire families, who were victims of one of Britain’s most appalling miscarriages of justice and were wrongfully imprisoned for terms of up to 15 years for crimes they had no part in; notes that Guiseppe Conlon, father of Gerry Conlon, died in prison while serving a sentence for a crime he did not commit; considers that the public apology hoped for by the families from the UK Government for this terrible injustice is long overdue; notes with grave concern that, despite the overturning of all the sentences on the Conlon and Maguire families in 1989 and 1991, no police officers or other officials involved in this injustice have ever been prosecuted for their part in it, nor has the UK Government officially recognised that they were all innocent, and believes that the UK Government should issue a full apology to all, that agreement should be reached with all the families on appropriate financial compensation and that justice should be seen to be done for the injustice they suffered.

Supported by: Frances Curran*, Rosie Kane*, Chris Ballance*

* S2M-2408# Donald Gorrie:Suicides by Older People in Scotland—That the Parliament is concerned at the statistics about suicides by older people in Scotland, especially older men; believes that more attention needs to be given to treating depression in general and, especially, among older people, persuading older men suffering from depression to discuss their problems with their GP and improving the performance of the primary care system in recognising the symptoms of depression and treating it more rapidly, and considers that the relevant authorities should undertake more research into the causes of depression and suicide and should add older people as a priority group to the other seven priority groups focused on in Choose Life, the national strategy to prevent suicide.

Supported by: Iain Smith*, Mike Pringle*, Robert Brown*, John Farquhar Munro*, Margaret Smith*, Jeremy Purvis*, George Lyon*, Nora Radcliffe*

* S2M-2407 Mark Ballard:Link Between Transport Choices and Income—That the Parliament notes the finding of the Scottish Household Survey that, in households with a net income of £5,000 to £10,000, only 20% of householders drive regularly while 55% use the bus regularly, whereas in households with an income of over £40,000, 74% drive regularly and only 29% regularly use the bus; further notes that the data ranges between these two extremes show that the wealthier the household, the more likely its members are to use a car and the less likely they are to use a bus; believes that this survey reflects the income distribution and transport choices of people who will be subject to Edinburgh’s congestion charge which will, therefore, be levied proportionately more on the rich and not the poor; commends the Yes to Edinburgh campaign and the Get Edinburgh Moving campaign for their brave work towards tackling gridlock in Edinburgh, and condemns the Scottish Socialist Party, the Scottish National Party, the Scottish Liberal Democrats and the Scottish Conservative and Unionist Party for campaigning against action to prevent congestion, respiratory disease and road death, all of which disproportionately affect low income Edinburgh households without cars.

Supported by: Robin Harper*

* S2M-2402.1 Rosie Kane:Anti-Racism Strategy—As an amendment to motion (S2M-2402) in the name of Malcolm Chisholm, leave out from “to ensure” to end and insert “, and believes that current immigration and asylum policy from Westminster undermines the Executive’s attempts to eradicate racism in Scotland.”

* S2M-2402.2 Mary Scanlon:Anti-Racism Strategy—As an amendment to motion (S2M-2402) in the name of Malcolm Chisholm, leave out from “and flagship” to end and insert “; supports the continuing need to raise awareness of, and tackle, racism in Scotland and to celebrate our diversity; supports the range of Executive activities and actions to promote race equality including work in health, the police and education; is committed to eradicating racism in Scotland in whatever form it takes to ensure that we create an inclusive and prosperous Scotland, and believes that a fair system of immigration control is necessary in order to promote good race relations.”

* S2M-2402.3 Ms Sandra White:Anti-Racism Strategy—As an amendment to motion (S2M-2402) in the name of Malcolm Chisholm, insert at end “, and expresses concern that Labour and Conservative immigration proposals do not reflect Scotland’s population requirements or multicultural ethos.”

Supported by: Christine Grahame*
