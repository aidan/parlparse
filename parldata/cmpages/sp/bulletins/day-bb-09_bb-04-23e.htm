Section E – Written questions lodged on 22 April 2009

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-23120 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there has been a recent incident of Clostridium difficile at the Edinburgh Western General Hospital and, if so, how many patients have been affected and what additional measures have been taken to contain the infection.

S3W-23121 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive who is responsible for the removal of dead livestock from agricultural land.

S3W-23122 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive what it considers to be the minimum percentage of Grant Aided Expenditure that a local authority should spend on the non-trunk road network.

S3W-23123 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many of the single outcome agreements with local authorities detail how they will maintain the non-trunk road network.

S3W-23124 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive how much it spends per mile on maintaining the trunk road network.

S3W-23125 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive what the distance is of the trunk road network in miles.

S3W-23126 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive how much funding it provides per mile to local authorities to maintain the non-trunk road network.

S3W-23127 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive what the distance is of the non-trunk road network in miles, broken down by local authority.

S3W-23128 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that South Lanarkshire Council requires additional resources to maintain adequately the non-trunk road network.

S3W-23129 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it believes that additional resources are required by any local authorities to enable them to maintain adequately the non-trunk road network.

S3W-23130 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance it provides to local authorities on the standards it expects to be maintained on the non-trunk road network.

S3W-23131 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive to what standards it expects its contractors, such as BEAR Scotland, to work in maintaining the trunk road network.

S3W-23132 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will review the resources available to local authorities to enable them to maintain the non-trunk road network to a standard comparable to that required for trunk roads.

S3W-23133 Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in establishing mechanisms within the context of its national food policy to make land available to local authorities for use as allotment space where there is a proven need and desire for such provision.

S3W-23134 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-22157 by Fergus Ewing on 1 April 2009, whether it will consider amending houses in multiple occupation legislation as a measure to prevent antisocial behaviour by people using short-term holiday lets.

S3W-23135 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive how it envisages preventing antisocial behaviour by people using short-term holiday lets.

S3W-23136 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive which of the issues in relation to antisocial behaviour and the licensing process for houses in multiple occupation highlighted by Sarah Boyack MSP in her speech on the antisocial behaviour framework on 2 April 2009 (Official Report c. 16538) it considers need to be addressed and what action it envisages taking to address them.

S3W-23137 Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will work with the UK Government and relevant transport agencies to ensure that horse transportation lorries used for the purposes of leisure activities are not classed as commercial HGVs in the context of the EU working time directive.

S3W-23138 Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-18217 by Jim Mather on 3 December 2008, whether it expects to reach its target of meeting 100% of the known demand for broadband access in the Scottish Borders by May 2009.

S3W-23139 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-22158 by Alex Neil on 19 March 2009, what it would define as a genuine holiday let.

S3W-23140 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-22155 by Alex Neil on 9 April 2009, whether it considers that holiday lets are covered under Part 7 of the Antisocial Behaviour etc. (Scotland) Act 2004.

S3W-23143 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-22377 by Kenny MacAskill on 21 April 2009, whether it is now in a position to provide the data for 2007-08.
