Business Bulletin No. 180/2001

Thursday 6 December 2001

Section D : Oral Questions
  

Question Time

1. John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive when it intends to conduct a review of the present payment arrangements of the Highlands and Islands Veterinary Fund with a view to addressing any need for increased animal disease surveillance. (S1O-4306)

2. Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met representatives of the North Channel ferry operators. (S1O-4283)

3. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans there are to address any discrepancy between means tested and non-means tested allowances for disabled students in further and higher education. R (S1O-4281)

4. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is monitoring how many people elect to use Gaelic in the sheriff courts of Portree, Lochmaddy and Stornoway. (S1O-4293)

5. Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive what the annual estimated costs are as a result of domestic abuse to the business sector. (S1O-4300)

6. Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to address the rise in the number of homeless people in Edinburgh. (S1O-4311)

7. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are being taken to ensure that places of worship are safe from racist attacks. (S1O-4313)

8. Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive what response it intends to make to recent comments by the Director of Glasgow City Council’s Education Department regarding the effectiveness of league tables as a measure of educational progress and achievement, as reported on BBC News Online on 29 November 2001. (S1O-4277)

9. Mr Duncan Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider carrying out an inquiry into introducing a Road Equivalent Tariff scheme for Scotland's ferries. (S1O-4282)

10. Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive whether socio-economic factors are taken into account when considering the designation of an area of sea as a marine Special Area of Conservation and, if so, what weight such factors are given when reaching a decision. (S1O-4303)

11. Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to review the law to make it illegal for the online "grooming" of children to take place. (S1O-4309)

12. Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to support local democracy in East Ayrshire. (S1O-4310)

13. Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has made to Railtrack following its decision of 5 December 2001 not to support four Scottish Executive funded rail enhancement projects. (S1O-4290)

14. Mr Keith Raffan: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made on the proposed Clackmannanshire Bridge across the Forth at Kincardine. (S1O-4271)

15. Mr Keith Harding: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will next meet representatives of local authorities to discuss housing and homelessness. (S1O-4296)

16. Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to consult crofters and farmers about the implementation of a new Less Favoured Areas Support Scheme. (S1O-4304)

17. Dorothy-Grace Elder: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) council-owned and (b) private houses occupied by pensioners have had heating installed under its free central heating programme. (S1O-4278)

18. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made towards the implementation of the proposed Modern Transport System for the north east of Scotland. (S1O-4291)

19. Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Minister for Education and Young People will next meet representatives of the Scottish School Board Association. (S1O-4307)

20. Mr Frank McAveety: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions have taken place on addressing the issue of the sex industry in Glasgow. (S1O-4314)

21. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what initiatives are being taken to tackle drink-driving during the festive season. (S1O-4305)

22. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in developing a western peripheral route around Aberdeen. (S1O-4288)

23. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what priorities it has set in respect of recruiting and training nurse specialists in the NHS in Scotland. (S1O-4274)

24. Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is planning to take in order to improve the financial resources available to Glasgow City Council for tackling poverty and deprivation. (S1O-4284)

25. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are being taken in order to make health boards more open and accountable. (S1O-4316)

26. Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive when it next intends to meet representatives of Fife Health Board. (S1O-4273)

27. Bristow Muldoon: To ask the Scottish Executive what support is being offered to Scottish football clubs aiming to establish football academies. (S1O-4317)

28. Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Minister for Enterprise, Transport and Lifelong Learning last met her counterpart in the National Assembly for Wales. (S1O-4270)

29. Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with rail transport bodies concerning the rail transport infrastructure in Glasgow and the West of Scotland. (S1O-4325)

30. Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is holding talks with drug manufacturers in order to find a strategy that would enable beta interferon to be obtained for patients in Scotland on the NHS and what progress has been made in any such talks. (S1O-4299)
