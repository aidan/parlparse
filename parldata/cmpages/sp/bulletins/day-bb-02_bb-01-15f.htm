Business Bulletin No. 7/2002

Tuesday 15 January 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-2606 Mr Kenny MacAskill: Scotland's Railways-That the Parliament notes that the Executive's neglect and lamentable lack of vision and strategy for Scotland's railways has contributed to a deterioration of rail services in Scotland to the point at which they have become amongst the worst in Europe; further notes that the Strategic Rail Authority's new strategic plan offers nothing new for Scotland; believes that Scottish railways must be fully controlled by, and accountable to, the Scottish Parliament, and therefore calls upon the Executive to take responsibility for delivering a strategy for the running and operation of Scotland's rail network.

*S1M-2605 Dorothy-Grace Elder: Retain Clive Fairweather-That the Parliament considers it to be in the interests of justice and human rights that Clive Fairweather remains as Her Majesty's Chief Inspector of Prisons for Scotland, and would be deeply concerned had he wished to remain but any unjust interference were found in whether his contract is renewed when it expires as Mr Fairweather has been an exceptional watchdog in his outspoken honesty and soundness of judgement on the state of Scottish Prisons; believes that vulnerable prisoners, and staff concerned about conditions, depend upon the integrity of Mr Fairweather who has used honest language in condemning matters affecting human life such as the high rate of suicides in prisons and in highlighting slopping out as a breach of human rights, and has worked diligently both to protect the public and to attempt to reform prisons, and calls upon all concerned in this appointment under a Royal Warrant, including Her Majesty The Queen, to ensure fairness prevails in appointing a key guardian in the justice system.

*S1M-2604 Alex Neil: Treatment of al-Qaeda Prisoners of War-That the Parliament, while utterly condemning the terrorist activities of al-Qaeda, deplores the refusal by the United States of America to classify Taliban and al-Qaeda prisoners as Prisoners of War, thus denying their basic human rights as set out under the Geneva Convention, and recognises that this kind of action could lead to further acts of terrorism and is ultimately detrimental to any attempts to reduce the threat of terrorism around the world.

*S1M-2603# Mr Lloyd Quinan: Holyrood Project Goods and Services-That the Parliament considers that goods for the Holyrood Project should not be sourced from states currently failing to comply with any resolutions of the United Nations.

*S1M-2602 Mr Andy Kerr: Freedom of Information (Scotland) Bill: Financial Resolution-That the Parliament, for the purposes of any Act of the Scottish Parliament resulting from the Freedom of Information (Scotland) Bill, agrees to the following expenditure out of the Scottish Consolidated Fund-

(a) expenditure of the Scottish Administration in consequence of the Act; and

(b) increases attributable to the Act in the sums payable out of that Fund under any other enactment.

Supported by: Mr Jim Wallace*, Dr Richard Simpson*, Peter Peacock*

*S1M-2601# Robin Harper: United Nations World Summit on Sustainable Development-That the Parliament supports the attendance of the First Minister at the United Nations World Summit on Sustainable Development (Rio+10) in Johannesburg, South Africa in September 2002 in order to represent the Scottish people, to report on the progress being made in Scotland towards implementing the Rio agreement and to demonstrate continuing commitment to this issue.
