Wednesday 29 September 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-1786 Mr Mark Ruskell: Scottish Food Fortnight—That the Parliament welcomes the second annual celebration of Scotland’s regional produce, Scottish Food Fortnight, taking place between 18 September and 3 October 2004, which aims to highlight the world-class quality of Scottish produce, support farms, small rural businesses and independent shops and reduce "food miles" by strengthening local food networks and creating the shortest chain between producer and consumer and preserve local independent retailing and catering by encouraging Scottish people and visitors to Scotland to buy regional speciality food and drink from small shops, pubs, hotels and restaurants.

*S2M-1785 Rosie Kane: Declaration on the Occasion of the Royal Opening of the Scottish Parliament—That the Parliament calls for an independent Scottish republic built on the principles of liberty, equality, diversity and solidarity; believes that these principles can never be put into practice while Scotland remains subordinate to the hierarchical and anti-democratic institutions of the British state; believes that these principles can be brought about by a freely-elected Scottish government with full control of Scotland’s revenues; believes that the right to self-determination is an inherent right, and not a favour to be granted, whether by the Crown or the British state; believes that sovereignty rests with the people and vows to fight for the right of the people to govern themselves for the benefit of all those living in Scotland today, tomorrow and in future times; further believes that the government of a country is the servant to the people, not master of the people; believes that a written constitution would guarantee, under law, everyone’s right to freely vote, speak and assemble and would guarantee the people’s right to privacy and protection and access to information on all of the government’s actions; vows to fight for the power to refuse to send our sons and daughters to kill and die in unjust wars in foreign lands and to fight for the power to banish nuclear weapons of mass destruction from our land; vows to fight for the power to acquire and restrict the use of property or lands controlled by individuals, corporations or governments beyond Scotland’s borders; vows to fight for the power to build a more equal society, free of poverty, through the redistribution of our vast wealth; vows to fight for the power to protect our soil, seas and rivers for our children and for the generations to come; swears to oppose all forms of national chauvinism, imperialism and racism, to oppose all forms of discrimination on the grounds of gender, ethnic origin, religion, place of birth, age, disability, sexuality or language; aims to fight for an independent Scottish republic in which people may live with dignity and with self-respect, free from exploitation and assuming the responsibilities of free women and men, and which will negotiate freely and as an equal with governments of other lands; affirms that its aim is not to erect walls of separation, but to build an outward-looking Scotland that will extend the hand of friendship to all the peoples of the world, and vows to continue the struggle for a free, democratic Scottish republic for as long as it may take as our fight is for freedom.

*S2M-1784 Mr David Davidson: Centralisation of Health Services—That the Parliament is opposed to the centralisation of health services across Scotland; notes the threat facing many local hospital services including those at Caithness General Hospital, Western Isles Hospital, Lorne and Islands District General Hospital, Belford Hospital, Inverclyde Royal Hospital, the Vale of Leven Hospital, St John’s Hospital, Stobhill Hospital, the Victoria Infirmary, the Western Infirmary, Wishaw General Hospital, Hairmyres Hospital, Glasgow Homeopathic Hospital, Queen Mother’s Hospital, Ayr Hospital, Perth Royal Infirmary, Forth Park Hospital, Queen Margaret Hospital, Stirling Royal Infirmary, Falkirk Royal Infirmary, Monklands Hospital and Balfour Hospital and the additional threat to many community and long-term care hospitals right across Scotland; believes that this is part of a strategy of centralising services at a handful of elite hospitals which has led to uproar from coast to coast over hospital reorganisation plans, and therefore has no confidence in the ability of the Minister for Health and Community Care to devise and implement a strategy for the NHS which serves the needs of local communities throughout Scotland.

*S2M-1779 Robin Harper: Support for Outdoor Education—That the Parliament calls on the Scottish Executive, in light of the publication of Outdoor education: Aspects of good practice, by Ofsted which found that there has been a decline in outdoor education activity in England and Wales partly due to teachers’ fears of risk and litigation, to commission and publish a study of the state of outdoor education in Scotland that identifies trends and, after consultation with the Educational Institute for Scotland and other unions, introduces such strategies, legislation and support as will be necessary to encourage teachers to continue providing outdoor education, to make outdoor education and education in the outdoors core educational activities and to provide assurances that any teacher fulfilling all reasonable safety and risk procedures and assessments will be safe in the face of litigation.

Supported by: Eleanor Scott*, Mr Mark Ruskell*, Murray Tosh*, Fergus Ewing*, Roseanna Cunningham*, Linda Fabiani*, Carolyn Leckie*

*S2M-1778 Rob Gibson: Highland Food and Drink Festival—That the Parliament wishes every success to the 4th Highland Food and Drink Festival which takes place from 30 September to 3 October 2004; welcomes the increasing public interest in high quality local produce that forms the basis of the festival’s ingredients; notes the growing demand for clear labelling of all food and drink products, and congratulates the festival organisers for focusing attention on the enjoyment of quality food and drink in the Highlands.

Supported by: Fergus Ewing*, Patrick Harvie*, Richard Lochhead*, Mr Adam Ingram*, Mr Stewart Maxwell*, Brian Adam*, Eleanor Scott*, Linda Fabiani*, Shiona Baird*, Murray Tosh*, Roseanna Cunningham*, Stewart Stevenson*, John Scott*

*S2M-1777# Shiona Baird: Zero Waste—That the Parliament congratulates the Royal Society of Arts on its 21st century manifesto which seeks to stimulate enterprise, expand education and develop mutually reinforcing policies, products, technologies, behaviours and lifestyles that reduce waste of all kinds, with zero waste as the long-term ideal; recognises that the whole of Scotland would derive huge economic and environmental benefits from pursuing a zero waste approach; acknowledges that zero waste is not simply about managing waste better and recycling more, but is about managing resources throughout their lifecycle and eliminating waste in every area of human activity; and considers that the Scottish Executive should follow the example of New Zealand and adopt, as a matter of priority, a nationwide zero waste strategy that will begin to tackle Scotland’s growing waste mountain.

Supported by: Rosie Kane*, Eleanor Scott*, Frances Curran*, Mr Mark Ruskell*, Patrick Harvie*, Richard Lochhead*, Linda Fabiani*
