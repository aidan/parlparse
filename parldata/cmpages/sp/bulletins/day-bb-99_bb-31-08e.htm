BUSINESS BULLETIN 44/1999

Tuesday 31 August 1999 

Section E - Written questions lodged on 30 August 1999

 

 

S1W-1117 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make representations to Her Majesty’s Government requesting that internet websites which encourage suicide be prohibited, given the increased incidence of suicide among young people in Scotland in recent years.

 

S1W-1179 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive how it intends to respond to the request from Scottish police forces for adequate funds to cope with their Millennium workload.

 

S1W-1195 Bill Aitken: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is aware of concerns in the west end of Glasgow that should the Accident and Emergency unit of the Western Infirmary close there will be no emergency facility in that part of the city.

 

S1W-1205 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list the board members of all the Local Enterprise Companies in Scotland and their political affiliation.

 

S1W-1206 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has reviewed the 1999/2000 Business Plan of the DLO Building and Works Unit of East Ayrshire Council and, if so, what the outcome of this review was and in particular whether it accepts the income, expenditure and surplus forecast contained in the Business Plan.

 

S1W-1207 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied that East Ayrshire Council has successfully implemented the new financial management system for its DLO-related activities as recommended by the Audit Commission.

 

S1W-1208 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide a detailed breakdown of the budgeted and actual costs for establishing Scotland House in Brussels and details of its management arrangements and usage.

 

S1W-1209 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive what the current status is of plans for A71 by-passes in the Irvine valley.

 

S1W-1210 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will publish details of the meetings it has had with Her Majesty’s Government to negotiate Concordats with it, including the Ministers involved and minutes of the meetings.

 

S1W-1211 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-866 by Donald Dewar on 24 August, whether it will now publish the draft Concordats it has received from Her Majesty’s Government.

 

S1W-1212 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to maximise the take-up of free school meals.

 

S1W-1213 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to ensure minimum nutritional standards for school meals.

 

S1W-1214 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to develop nutritional education in schools.

 

S1W-1215 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it endorses the concept of "Home Zones" as a method of improving pedestrian road safety in relation to speed and, if so, whether it intends to hypothecate funds raised from speed camera penalties to the setting up of "Home Zones".
