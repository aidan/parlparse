Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (♦).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

*S3M-3675 Margaret Smith: Minimum Income Guarantee for Students—That the Parliament recognises the importance of the higher and further education sector; notes the outcome of the New Horizons: responding to the challenges of the 21st century report and the need to involve key stakeholders in discussions about the funding of the university sector; believes that Scotland’s students have been let down by the SNP’s government failure to deliver on its manifesto pledge to dump student debt; notes the Supporting a Smarter Scotland consultation on student support and rejects its proposals for not adequately addressing student hardship, and calls on the Scottish Government to deliver a simplified support system, which includes a minimum income guarantee of £7,000 per annum for full-time higher education students made up from a combination of grants, loans and parental contributions.

*S3M-3674 Alison McInnes: Aberdeen Crossrail—That the Parliament affirms its support for the Aberdeen Crossrail project, a vital infrastructure link for the region, which would provide a frequent cross-city rail service with commuter trains leaving Inverurie and Stonehaven for Aberdeen every 15 minutes and the opening of new stations north and south of Aberdeen including at Kintore, Newtonhill and Altens; notes that the project previously enjoyed cross-party support and was hailed as a key transport priority for the north east with the potential to bring significant economic and environmental benefits; deeply regrets that the project has been omitted from the Strategic Transport Projects Review, and calls on the Scottish Government to make a firm commitment to work with Aberdeenshire and Aberdeen City councils through NESTRANS and its rail industry partners to restart the active development of the Aberdeen Crossrail project immediately.

*S3M-3672 Bob Doris: Working in Partnership to Improve Hospital Hygiene—That the Parliament welcomes the contribution of two recent initiatives by the Scottish Government to help improve the cleanliness of hospitals in Scotland; believes that government spending of £400,000 on 250 steam cleaners for NHS boards across Scotland, with 61 steam cleaning kits to be provided in the NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde area alone at a cost of £98,000, will allow specialised cleaning to prevent Clostridium difficile and contribute towards reducing infection rates; further welcomes the announcement by the Cabinet Secretary for Health and Wellbeing, Nicola Sturgeon MSP, of new random hygiene inspections, which will see all acute hospitals receive one planned and one unannounced inspection in the next three years as part of the new Care Environment Inspectorate regime, and hopes that the new inspectorate will complement NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde’s Cleaning Services and Peer Public Involvement Programme, which trains volunteers to cleaning standards in its hospitals and health centres.

*S3M-3671 Bruce Crawford on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Health Boards (Membership and Elections) (Scotland) Bill—That the Parliament agrees that, during Stage 3 of the Health Boards (Membership and Elections) (Scotland) Bill, debate on groups of amendments shall, subject to Rule 9.8.4A, be brought to a conclusion by the time limits indicated, each time limit being calculated from when the Stage begins and excluding any periods when other business is under consideration or when a meeting of the Parliament is suspended (other than a suspension following the first division in the Stage being called) or otherwise not in progress:

Groups 1 and 2: 30 minutes

Groups 3 to 5: 1 hour

Groups 6 to 8: 1 hour 20 minutes.

Supported by: Nicola Sturgeon*

*S3M-3670 Bob Doris: Congratulations to Glasgow Credit Union—That the Parliament congratulates Glasgow Credit Union, which loaned more than £5 million in mortgages last year and supported another 70 new homeowners; believes that the value of credit unions to communities is vital, particularly during the current economic climate with banks becoming increasingly unwilling to lend; notes that the credit union has loaned more than £13 million over the last three years and is preparing to offer more loans for first-time buyers in 2009, and also notes that Glasgow Credit Union helps citizens to save money and offers a range of savings accounts and mortgages.

*S3M-3669 Bruce Crawford on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Designation of Lead Committee—That the Parliament agrees that the Justice Committee be designated as the lead committee in consideration of the Criminal Justice and Licensing (Scotland) Bill at Stage 1.

*S3M-3668 Bruce Crawford on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Business Motion—That the Parliament agrees that consideration of the Education (Additional Support for Learning) (Scotland) Bill at Stage 2 be completed by 3 April 2009.

*S3M-3667 Jackson Carlaw: A Special Representative for St Margaret of Scotland Hospice—That the Parliament notes the decision of NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde to defer until 2012 the transfer of 30 continuing care beds from St Margaret of Scotland Hospice to Blawarthill Hospital; believes that this decision can be tentatively welcomed in the sense that it ends the immediate threat to St Margaret’s; further believes that the three years between now and 2012 must not be wasted but should instead be used as an opportunity to find a sustainable and workable solution to the long-running dispute between St Margaret’s and the NHS board, and, to this end, calls on the Cabinet Secretary for Health and Wellbeing to ensure the appointment of a special representative who enjoys the confidence and goodwill of both parties to act as a broker in the search for such a solution so that agreement is reached in advance of 2012.

*S3M-3663 Mary Mulligan: BSL Recognition Day - 18 March 2009—That the Parliament recognises 18 March 2009 as British Sign Language (BSL) Recognition Day; commends Donaldson’s school in Linlithgow for its role as Scotland’s national school for children who are deaf or have severe speech and language difficulties; recognises the dedication of the staff in teaching BSL, which helps pupils transition into the community; encourages all local authorities to consider the range and quality of resources that Donaldson’s offers and how they could be used to benefit children and young people from their areas, and calls on the Scottish Government to continue to support the school in its vital role in Scotland.

*S3M-3662 Angela Constance: Congratulations to Margaret Manson on Election as Captain of the Binny Golf Club—That the Parliament congratulates Margaret Manson on her notable election as Captain of the mixed-membership Binny Golf Club at the Oatridge College course in West Lothian; acknowledges that this represents a great step towards achieving equality for women involved with mixed-membership golf clubs, and hopes that this will encourage more women throughout Scotland to obtain leadership positions in mixed-membership golf clubs.

Supported by: Alasdair Allan*, Aileen Campbell*

*S3M-3661 Pauline McNeill: Clyde Yard Secures Navy Contract—That the Parliament welcomes BVT’s yard at Scotstoun securing a share of a £150 million order; notes that the contract, which includes training and long-term maintenance support, will help the yard maintain staff levels and balance workloads across BVT sites; notes that BVT offers the largest private sector apprenticeship scheme in Scotland; further notes that these moves demonstrate the long-term sustainability of the maritime industry in the United Kingdom, and calls on the Scottish Government to ensure that both current and new workers in the industry have the opportunity to continue to learn and to develop their skills as this is a vital element in winning orders.

Supported by: Elaine Murray*, Trish Godman*, Bill Butler*, David Whitton*, Jackie Baillie*, James Kelly*, Rhona Brankin*

*S3M-3660 Rob Gibson: Inspiring Women Politicians—That the Parliament notes with concern, following International Women’s Day on Sunday 8 March 2009, the recent report by Girlguiding UK that found that the 19% of UK MPs who are women are failing to inspire young women to become interested in politics; further notes that on average fewer than one-fourth of members of national parliaments are women, that out of 12 European Parliament presidents only two have been women and there has never been a woman president of the European Commission; recognises the world-class example set by the Scottish Parliament in having 45 women MSPs, four women ministers and in being the first to have a woman Deputy First Minister; highlights the precedent set by Elish Angiolini in being Scotland’s first woman Procurator Fiscal, Solicitor General and Lord Advocate; hails Rwanda’s example as the first parliament in the world to have a female majority, and finally calls on all politicians, leaders and business executives, men and women, to recognise the unique perspective that women offer and to work mindfully towards inspiring the next generation of female leaders so as to see a more representative democracy at decision-making tables around the world.

*S3M-3659 Anne McLaughlin: EAL Provision in Glasgow Schools—That the Parliament recognises the importance of English as an Additional Language (EAL) support in schools up and down the country; notes that in Glasgow alone there are 86 languages spoken by pupils in the city’s schools; believes that the needs of new migrant children must be fully met but not at the cost of providing much-needed support to Scots-born bilingual children; recognises that European funding streams exist to give EAL support to migrant European children, and urges local authorities to explore this avenue if they have not already done so.

Supported by: Rob Gibson*, Bob Doris*, Alasdair Allan*, Sandra White*, Bill Kidd*, Christopher Harvie*

*S3M-3658 Anne McLaughlin: Palestinian Health Care Suffering—That the Parliament notes its extreme concern at findings in The Lancet medical journal, which describe Palestinians in the West Bank and Gaza as suffering an "ailing landscape" of health services; notes that Birzeit University studies have shown that death rates among children and expectant mothers have failed to decline; finds it deplorable that 60 women have had to give birth at Israeli checkpoints with 36 of their babies dying as a result; calls on the United Kingdom and Scottish governments to exert influence to pressure the Israeli Government to allow in essential medical aid to help adequately equip Palestinian hospitals, and wishes the Justice4Palestine convoy carrying medicines from Glasgow well and a safe passage to the people of Gaza.

Supported by: Aileen Campbell*, Rob Gibson*, Bob Doris*, Bill Wilson*, Shirley-Anne Somerville*, Alasdair Allan*, Sandra White*, Bill Kidd*, Robin Harper*

*S3M-3657 Anne McLaughlin: Red Cross Accolade for Glasgow Teenagers—That the Parliament recognises the achievements of the members of the Global Glasgow Youth Project who have been awarded a British Red Cross Humanitarian Citizen Award for their groundbreaking work with vulnerable young people in a township in South Africa; congratulates the 14 teenagers involved in the project for raising more than £25,000 to deliver aid and peer education to young people from Khayelitsha township in Cape Town, and believes that these young people from Glasgow are great role models for anyone aspiring to make a positive difference to the lives of others.

Supported by: Bob Doris*, Stuart McMillan*, Aileen Campbell*, Bill Wilson*, Shirley-Anne Somerville*, Alasdair Allan*, Sandra White*, Bill Kidd*, Robin Harper*

*S3M-3656 Jackie Baillie: Congratulations to Aggreko—That the Parliament congratulates Dumbarton-based firm Aggreko on its plans to build a new £20 million plant in the Vale of Leven; notes that the company has been in the town for more than 30 years and currently employs over 200 people; further notes that this new plant will create an additional 120 construction jobs; warmly welcomes Aggreko’s commitment to the local area by choosing to expand its operations in Dumbarton rather than relocating abroad; recognises that, despite the current economic climate, Aggreko has continued to flourish and over 80% of all Aggreko equipment across the world is produced in Dumbarton; commends the efforts of the staff, and wishes the company every success for the future.

*S3M-3655 Helen Eadie: Cemetery Memorabilia—That the Parliament is concerned that Fife Council leaders and officials have enforced strict regulations relating to the size and area that can be used for cemetery memorabilia with little consultation with the community and with great insensitivity towards the feelings of the bereaved; is particularly appalled that these leaders and officials refused to represent or hear the concerns of the community at two public meetings; notes that this is an issue of concern, not just in Fife, but in other areas also; regrets that this enforcement of regulations is based on financial motivations, has no regard for care or compassion and is seen by any reasonable person as wholly heartless; is particularly aghast that one family, whose children are buried in Fife, feels so aggrieved at these regulations that it is seriously considering exhuming the bodies of its loved ones and relocating them to its own property, and calls on the Scottish Government to intervene urgently to stop these callous activities by Fife Council and to ensure that all councils across Scotland are given guidance so that they can be caring and listening councils.
