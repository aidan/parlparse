Business Bulletin No. 153/2006: Wednesday 1 November 2006
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 31 October 2006

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-29382 Jim Mather: To ask the Scottish Executive when the invitation to tender for the Clyde and Hebrides lifeline ferry routes will be issued.

S2W-29383 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive what rail lines in Scotland are used for the transportation of nuclear material.

S2W-29384 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive what road routes in Scotland are used for the transportation of nuclear material.

S2W-29385 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive what marine routes, including harbours and piers, in Scotland are used for the transportation of nuclear material.

S2W-29386 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will collate figures for injuries arising from accidents with fireworks or their misuse in Scotland in the three weeks around Guy Fawkes Day, in light of the decision by the Department of Trade and Industry not to collate figures for the United Kingdom in 2006.

S2W-29387 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many deaths linked to hypothermia there have been in each year since 1997 and, of these, how many were of individuals aged (a) 60 to 64, (b) 65 to 79 and (c) 80 and over, broken down by (i) NHS board area and (ii) parliamentary constituency.

S2W-29388 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many junior doctor training posts there will be in each specialty under the Modernising Medical Careers initiative.

S2W-29389 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) trainee and (b) qualified physiotherapists are not employed by the NHS.

S2W-29390 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many individuals died as a result of being (a) shot and (b) stabbed in each of the last 10 years for which figures are available.

S2W-29391 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many convicted drug offenders are participating in rehabilitation schemes in prison.

S2W-29392 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive when Communities Scotland’s inspection of Glasgow Housing Association will take place.

S2W-29393 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive when the report of Communities Scotland’s inspection of Glasgow Housing Association will be published.

S2W-29394 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive when the scheme of assistance for tenants of Glasgow Housing Association will be implemented.

S2W-29395 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what the timetable will be for implementing the scheme of assistance for tenants of Glasgow Housing Association.

S2W-29396 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the scheme of assistance for tenants of Glasgow Housing Association will be retrospective.

S2W-29397 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide a breakdown of how the £100 million committed over a ten-year period to assist owners who are required to take part in Glasgow Housing Association’s investment programme will be spent.

S2W-29398 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide a detailed explanation of the help available under the scheme of assistance for housing purposes in respect of advice and assistance, practical help, loans and equity access products and grants.

S2W-29399 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive how much land has been made available by large estates to young entrants into farming in each year since 1999.

S2W-29400 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average rent is for agricultural land.

S2W-29401 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive what the percentage increase in agricultural rents has been in each year since 1999.

S2W-29402 Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it will provide to workers at Solectron in Dunfermline to find new employment.

S2W-29403 Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive what strategic initiatives will be deployed to help reinvigorate the electronic sector in Fife, following the closure of Lexmark in 2005 and the announcement by Solectron that it intends to close its operations in Dunfermline.

S2W-29404 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what estimate it has made of the level of graduate unemployment among (a) physiotherapists, (b) nurses, (c) midwives and (d) occupational therapists in each year since 1999.

S2W-29405 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive how many full-time school nurses worked in schools in each year since 1999.

S2W-29406 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive how many children suffering from mental ill-health have been admitted to adult hospital wards in each year since 1999.

S2W-29407 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-28963 by Peter Peacock on 31 October 2006, who it has commissioned to undertake the work to identify good practice in relation to school travel.

S2W-29408 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-28965 by Peter Peacock on 31 October 2006, whether the statement that information is not held centrally in the form requested indicates that the Executive does hold information on expenditure on school buses and, if so, to what such information pertains.

S2W-29409 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list for each year since declaration of political affiliation became mandatory as part of the system of appointments to public bodies in Scotland the (a) total number of appointments made, (b) number of appointees who declared a political affiliation and (c) number of these appointees affiliated to each political party.
