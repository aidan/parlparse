Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally printed the day after they are lodged. If an amendment is lodged to a motion then the original motion will appear alongside the amendment, along with any support lodged that day, in this section.

Each Monday, this section also contains all motions and amendments lodged the previous week that are still live and any motions and amendments that have been lodged for debate in the coming week. A motion or amendment is live if it has not been debated, withdrawn or otherwise deleted from the list.

Where a motion or amendment has been withdrawn, it is indicated in this section the day after it has been withdrawn. Support for motions and amendments received after they are lodged is shown at the end of this section the day after such support is received.

Motions and amendments can be published with various symbols:

  • Asterisks before the motion or amendment number indicate a motion or amendment published for the first time;

  • Asterisks also identify alterations to the text of a motion or amendment made since it was first published;

  • A hash symbol identifies motions eligible for debate at Members’ Business;

  • A diamond symbol identifies motions lodged for Members’ Business that have not yet attracted the required cross-party support;

  • An "R", identifies motions or amendments in which the Member who lodged it has a registrable interest.

The Parliamentary Bureau periodically deletes motions or amendments not scheduled for debate and which are over six weeks old.

A full list of current motions is available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/motions.htm.

A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/index.htm.

Any questions in relation to this section should be directed to the Chamber Desk, for which contact details are provided at the end of the Bulletin.

New and altered motions and amendments

*S4M-00270 Drew Smith: Kids Go Free on Glasgow Subway—That the Parliament welcomes the announcement by Strathclyde Partnership for Transport (SPT) that children are to be given free access to the Glasgow Subway for the period of the school summer holidays; hopes that many Glasgow families as well as those from further afield will make use of the Kids Go Free scheme, which will provide free journeys for up two children under 16 with a paying adult, allowing them to travel between many of Glasgow’s excellent attractions including the new Riverside Museum, which will open later this month; congratulates SPT on its initiative, and hopes that another generation of Glasgow youngsters grow up to love the subway and that they continue to make use of the new modernised system for which the SPT has secured funding.

*S4M-00269 Jim Eadie: Parentline Scotland Extended Service —That the Parliament warmly welcomes the launch of ParentLine Scotland’s weekend service, enabling anyone with a concern about a child to call 0800 028 2233 for support, advice and information on Saturdays and Sundays between 12 noon and 5 pm; notes that the extended service has been made possible through Scottish Government funding for a national kinship care service that allows CHILDREN 1ST and ParentLine Scotland to offer specialist emotional and practical help and advice to family members and friends raising and caring for children when their parents can no longer do so; further notes that CHILDREN 1ST has operated ParentLine Scotland since 1999, during which time 113,000 calls have been made and over 300 volunteers have been trained to listen, help and support callers, and calls on members to support ParentLine Scotland by promoting its service to families all over Scotland.

*S4M-00268 Jenny Marra: Royal Bank of Scotland Local Branch Closures—That the Parliament considers that the Royal Bank of Scotland has heavily publicised its commitment to local branches and Scottish communities; condemns its decision to close its branch in Lochee, Dundee, after 150 years in the community; questions the reasoning provided of reduced footfall in the bank when it understands that it is company policy to reduce footfall in every branch by encouraging customers to take up telephone and digital banking; questions the Royal Bank of Scotland’s customer service for its older customers and those without ready access to telephone and internet facilities and how the bank plans to serve these customers, and queries its planned provisions for over-the-counter services in Dundee now that the Lochee and Ninewells branches will close.

*S4M-00267 Paul Martin: Primary Seven Pupils at St Philomena’s Primary School in Provanmill, Business Breakfast—That the Parliament congratulates the primary seven pupils at St Philomena’s Primary School in Provanmill for successfully hosting Business Breakfast; welcomes the innovative way that the event engaged local businesses and taught useful skills to the participating pupils; further welcomes creative events like this that showcase the entrepreneurial spirit among local youngsters, and believes that skills learnt early will become useful for pupils’ future business pursuits.

*S4M-00266 Roderick Campbell: University of St Andrews, Soaring to the Clouds—That the Parliament recognises the achievements in cloud computing research made by the University of St Andrews’ team at its School of Computer Science; considers the team to be a world leader in this field and welcomes the progress that it has made; believes that cloud computing represents a new way for organisations to transform their IT provision by potentially rendering costly software, servers and air conditioning systems redundant; believes that the use of web-based services as an alternative to traditional software has the potential to save the private and public sector millions of pounds per annum, and acknowledges the fact that the University of St Andrews is home to the first research centre in the United Kingdom dedicated to the cloud computing phenomenon.

Supported by: Sandra White*, Willie Coffey*, Kenneth Gibson*, Richard Lyle*, Clare Adamson*, Chic Brodie*, Jamie McGrigor*, Mike MacKenzie*, Richard Simpson*, Liz Smith*, Mark McDonald*

*S4M-00265 Mark McDonald: Congratulations to Robert Gordon’s College—That the Parliament sends its warmest congratulations to the Robert Gordon’s College team of Maura Buchanan and Morag Forbes on their victory in the final of the English-Speaking Union (ESU) Scotland Juniors Debating Competition 2011, held in the Parliament; notes that the competition is in its 10th year and that it attracted a record entry of 128 teams this year; considers it a magnificent achievement for the Robert Gordon’s College team to emerge from such a large field as champions, and also acknowledges the achievement of the teams from Merchiston Castle, Grange Academy and George Watson’s College, which also made it to the final; further congratulates the ESU on organising and running what it considers an outstanding competition, and commends the outreach work that the ESU does in schools across Scotland to promote the involvement of young people in debating and public speaking.

Supported by: Kenneth Gibson*, Hugh Henry*, Richard Lyle*, Clare Adamson*, Chic Brodie*, Mike MacKenzie*, Jackson Carlaw*, Bob Doris*, Nanette Milne*

*S4M-00264 Johann Lamont: World Credit Union Conference—That the Parliament welcomes the World Credit Union Conference to Glasgow in July 2011; looks forward to welcoming up to 1,700 international credit union delegates from around 60 countries to Scotland for this event; notes that 184 million people are served by 49,000 credit unions in 97 countries; congratulates the World Council of Credit Unions on its development work supporting financial co-operatives across the globe; congratulates the directors, staff and volunteers of Scotland’s 110 credit unions for providing affordable ethical financial services to over 250,000 people; notes the success of credit unions in countries such as the United States, Australia and the Republic of Ireland, where they serve over a quarter of the population, and supports the continued development of Scotland’s credit union movement as outlined in the Credit Union Charter produced by the Association of British Credit Unions.

*S4M-00263 Stuart McMillan: The Tall Ships Races 2011—That Parliament notes the Tall Ships Race returns to Scotland in 2011 with events at Greenock and Lerwick; further notes that the race visits Greenock from 9 to 12 July and Lerwick from 21 to 24 July; welcomes this international event to Scotland, in addition to Ireland, Norway and Sweden, as an opportunity to showcase the very best of Inverclyde and the Shetland Isles to a wider tourism market; recalls that the Tall Ships Race was a tremendous success when it visited Greenock 12 years ago with an estimated 800,000 people visiting Inverclyde to witness the event; encourages as many people as possible to visit either, or both, Inverclyde or Lerwick, to attend what it sees as the tourism event of the year, and commends everyone involved with the organising of both Scottish events for their hard work, dedication and determination to promote tourism in their local areas.

Supported by: Sandra White*, Tavish Scott*, Colin Beattie*, Kenneth Gibson*, Ken Macintosh*, Fiona McLeod*, Richard Lyle*, Jamie McGrigor*, Mike MacKenzie*, Jackson Carlaw*, Stewart Maxwell*

*S4M-00261 Sarah Boyack: Inflating the Innertube—That the Parliament welcomes the launch of the Innertube Map online, which highlights and celebrates Edinburgh’s off-street paths and routes for cyclists; considers the map’s design, which is based on the iconic London tube map, to be striking but simple; notes that the website, which can be accessed from http://innertubemap.com, offers a forum for cyclists across Edinburgh to share stories and videos of their cycling adventures and to highlight defects with routes so that action can be taken to fix them; acknowledges the support of the People’s Postcode Lottery, the Bike Station and the Edinburgh & Lothians Greenspace Trust to promote the Innertube Map and to promote and improve Edinburgh’s cycling network through the Inflating the Innertube project, and calls on the Scottish Government to provide support for cycling to help Scotland toward its target of ensuring that 10% of trips are made by bike by 2020.

Supported by: Patricia Ferguson*, Ken Macintosh*, Hugh Henry*, Neil Findlay*, Mike MacKenzie*, Hanzala Malik*, Anne McTaggart*, Drew Smith*, John Park*

*S4M-00255 Jim Hume: Langlee Jubilee Play Park—That the Parliament supports Waverley Tenants Organisation in its efforts to obtain funding from The Jubilee People’s Millions grants programme to enable it to build the Langlee jubilee play park; understands that this will involve the development of a piece of land in the Beech Avenue area of Langlee for the benefit of toddlers and older children from a large housing estate in order to encourage outdoor play; welcomes the funding delivered by the People’s Millions to causes throughout the country, and wishes the Waverley Tenants Organisation luck with the vote on 29 June 2011.
