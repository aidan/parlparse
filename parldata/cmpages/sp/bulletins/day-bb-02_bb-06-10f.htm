Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

S1M-3176 Robin Harper: Threat of Nuclear War over Kashmir—That the Parliament recognises with grave concern the dispute between India and Pakistan over Kashmir, the military build up of millions of troops along their borders and the potential for escalation to nuclear war including the immediate threat of annihilation of at least 12 million civilians of both countries; strongly urges the UK Prime Minister to visit the region and to use his personal relationship with the President of the United States of America to urge that the President also visits the region, and calls on Her Majesty’s Government to suspend immediately the sale of all arms and munitions to the region and to put a proposal before the United Nations for a peacekeeping force to be introduced along the zone of control as a matter of extreme urgency. S1M-3175 Richard Lochhead: The Parliament in Aberdeen—That the Parliament extends its appreciation to all involved in the smooth running of its visit to Aberdeen from 28 to 30 May 2002, particularly to all at the University of Aberdeen and Aberdeen City Council and to parliamentary staff who were involved in the planning and execution of parliamentary business and associated events throughout the week, and notes that the success of the visit and warm welcome from the north-east community suggests that the parliament should explore the possibilities of repeating the exercise in Aberdeen and elsewhere in Scotland in coming years.

S1M-3174 Christine Grahame: Care Provision for Elderly People in Dunbar—That the Parliament notes with concern the reduction of care provision for elderly people in Dunbar and, in particular, the reduction in the number of beds at Belhaven Hospital which may result in frail elderly people being transferred to facilities in Edinburgh, distant from family, friends and familiar surroundings; further notes the imminent closure of the Church of Scotland respite day care centre at the Old Manse, reducing the provision of respite relief for carers; is concerned by the distress that this will cause frail elderly people and their carers, and therefore urges the Scottish Executive as a matter of urgency to correct the failure to provide locally by providing adequate resources in order to ensure that the elderly in Dunbar and, indeed, elsewhere in East Lothian, can remain in their failing years in their home locality, that they are treated with the dignity and consideration they have earned and that their carers, families and friends are given practical support.

Supported by: Mr Adam Ingram, Mr Kenneth Gibson, Fiona Hyslop, Fergus Ewing, Ms Sandra White, Michael Matheson, Michael Russell

S1M-3173# Trish Godman: Loan Sharks—That the Parliament commends the excellent initiative of the Daily Record to name and shame the "loan sharks" who prey on vulnerable people living in poverty throughout Scotland; agrees with the newspaper that Scotland must not be a country where it is a crime to be poor and where loan sharks obtain huge profits out of other people's precarious financial circumstances, and is of the firm view that the Parliament, in close partnership with the UK Parliament, the police and other appropriate public agencies, must take decisive action to drive these predators out of their sordid "business" in the interests of their victims and victims’ families.

Supported by: Colin Campbell, Phil Gallie, Karen Gillon, Mr Kenneth Macintosh, Mr John McAllion, Johann Lamont, Mr Michael McMahon, Brian Fitzpatrick, Angus MacKay, Dr Sylvia Jackson, Robert Brown, Pauline McNeill, Karen Whitefield, Mr Kenneth Gibson, Bristow Muldoon, Jackie Baillie, Elaine Smith*, Shona Robison*, Mr John Home Robertson*, Helen Eadie*, Mr Tom McCabe*

S1M-3172 Fergus Ewing: Anagach Woods—That the Parliament welcomes the purchase of Anagach Woods by the community of Grantown-on-Spey; congratulates everyone involved in ensuring that the community purchase will be successfully completed; acknowledges support from the Scottish Land Fund and Highland and Islands Enterprise Community Land Fund, and hopes that the safeguarding of these woods will safeguard the environment and also provide opportunities for Grantown-on-Spey in the years ahead.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson, Michael Matheson

S1M-3171# David Mundell: VisitScotland Funding of Dumfries and Galloway Tourist Board Foot-and-Mouth Recovery Plan—That the Parliament notes with extreme concern the failure of VisitScotland to allocate the necessary £280,000 to fund the second year of the foot-and-mouth recovery plan prepared by Dumfries and Galloway Tourist Board; believes that funds allocated nationally to VisitScotland by the Scottish Executive to help the tourist industry recover from the foot-and-mouth disease outbreak should be targeted on those areas most affected by the disease, and considers that the Scottish Executive should enter into urgent dialogue with VisitScotland to review their funding allocation to the board in the expectation that the necessary funding will be made available to support the Board's foot-and-mouth recovery plan which has already had considerable success in its first year.

Supported by: Phil Gallie, Mr Murray Tosh, Alex Fergusson

S1M-2822# Shona Robison: Sale and Use of Fireworks—That the Parliament notes the results of the survey carried out by the Scottish Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals in which almost 98% of people supported tighter restrictions on the availability of fireworks; recognises that there has been a large increase in the problems caused for both people and animals by the irresponsible use of fireworks; is concerned that the voluntary code of practice for the sale of fireworks is frequently disregarded, and considers that the Scottish Executive should do everything in its power to tighten up on the regulations relating to the sale and use of fireworks and, in particular, should review the powers available to local authorities in respect of vendor licensing.
