Business Bulletin No. 60/2002

Wednesday 17 April 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-2995 Margaret Jamieson: Carpet Manufacturing in Scotland-That the Parliament regrets the workforce reductions announced today by Stoddard International affecting employees at Kilmarnock and Elderslie and the subsequent closure and transfer of the Elderslie facility to Kilmarnock and calls upon the Scottish Executive to work with all public agencies, Stoddard International Plc, trade unions and other interested parties to ensure the continuation of the last carpet weaving manufacturer in Scotland.

*S1M-2994 Cathy Jamieson: Social Care Workforce Development-That the Parliament commends the work of the social services workforce in local authorities, in voluntary organisations and in the private sector in providing vital services for older people, for children and their families and for people with disabilities and others, and in contributing to better community safety through work with offenders and in youth justice; notes the work already undertaken by the Executive to support the development of this workforce, and endorses the Executive's strategy and commitment to further work to secure a well trained workforce.

Supported by: Nicol Stephen*

*S1M-2993 Mr Jim Wallace: Prison Estates Review-That the Parliament welcomes the publication of the consultation paper Proposals for the Future of the Scottish Prison Service Estate; believes that to help achieve a safer Scotland the prison estate should facilitate the secure holding of prisoners and the delivery of effective rehabilitation programmes, and that prisons should provide a reasonable standard of accommodation; recognises that these objectives can only be achieved by substantial modernisation of the present prison estate, and encourages all interested individuals and organisations to contribute to the current consultation.

Supported by: Dr Richard Simpson*

*S1M-2989 Mr Gil Paterson: Domestic Abuse Courts-That the Parliament welcomes the call by Lord Carloway for the setting up of specialist courts to deal with domestic abuse; notes the effectiveness of this system in dealing with the root causes as well as the symptoms of domestic abuse along with programmes to tackle abusive behaviour as demonstrated in Canada; further notes the cluster courts set up in Leeds which have helped to speed up the court process for cases involving domestic abuse and have made those abused more willing to come forward due to the support they receive and the clear message that is given that abuse is not acceptable, and calls on the Scottish Executive to make the changes necessary to set up these specialist courts in Scotland which will be able to deal with both criminal and civil matters relating to the family.

Supported by: Mr Lloyd Quinan*, Mr Adam Ingram*

*S1M-2988# Mr Lloyd Quinan: Autism Awareness Week, 12-19 May 2002 -That the Parliament acknowledges Autism Awareness Week 2002 and the forthcoming Autism Awareness Week 2003 by supporting both the Scottish Society for Autism and the National Autistic Society in Scotland in their efforts to promote and care for those affected by the condition and notes the incredible amount of work carried out by the very effective network of smaller support groups.

*S1M-2987# Pauline McNeill: International Workers' Memorial Day 28 April 2002-That the Parliament supports International Workers' Memorial Day on 28 April 2002, a date important to trade unionists the world over; notes that, every year, over 1.3 million workers across the world die from accidents and diseases at work, nearly twice the number of deaths caused by war; further notes that many such deaths could be prevented by better health and safety management; welcomes the theme of occupational health in this year's activities, and believes that Members should actively support this important day by wearing the purple ribbon.

*S1M-2986# Alex Neil: STUC Emergency Motion on Prison Privatisation-That the Parliament supports the Scottish Trade Union Congress's emergency motion on prison privatisation which backs the findings contained in Privatised Prisons and Detention Centres in Scotland: An Independent Report by Phil Taylor and Christine Cooper; believes that the Scottish Executive should consider seriously how this report impacts on the Scottish Prison Service estates review; agrees that it is hard to believe that three new public prisons would cost £700 million more to build and run than three new private prisons, and calls for a full public debate on the philosophy and financial implications of prison privatisation.

Supported by: Ms Margo MacDonald*, Mr Lloyd Quinan*, Mr Adam Ingram*, Mr Gil Paterson*

*S1M-2985 David Mundell: Dumfries and Galloway Managed Clinical Network for Heart Disease-That the Parliament recognises that the Dumfries and Galloway Managed Clinical Network for Heart Disease was the first of its kind in the United Kingdom and is a way of focusing and providing care across the boundaries between general practice and hospital and between different health professions with a focus on patients and their needs; notes that, although the network has only been in existence for nine months, it is having a tremendous impact on the quality of care provided, with patients, doctors and nurses involved actively in decision-making about the future of service and on the use of scarce resources; further notes that the pre-hospital thrombolysis service in the area was also the first of its kind in the United Kingdom and will allow life-saving clot-dissolving drugs to be given to patients suffering from a heart attack before they get to hospital, and congratulates all those who have been involved in Dumfries and Galloway in bringing about these cutting edge developments in health services provision.

*S1M-2982.1 Mr Kenneth Gibson: Housing Improvement Task Force-As an amendment to motion (S1M-2982) in the name of Iain Gray, leave out from "meeting" to end and insert "examining the wide range of issues facing the private housing sector in Scotland; looks forward to debating the Task Force's recommendations for action and timescale for implementation following completion of its second stage report; seeks clarification as to when publication of the second stage report is anticipated, and calls on the Scottish Executive to propose that time is provided in the parliamentary process to ensure that any necessary legislation that may emerge will be progressed prior to the end of this session of the Parliament."

Supported by: Colin Campbell*, Nicola Sturgeon*

*S1M-2982.2 Bill Aitken: Housing Improvement Task Force-As an amendment to motion (S1M-2982) in the name of Iain Gray, leave out from "welcomes" to end and insert "notes the publication of the first report of the Housing Improvement Task Force; regrets that this report contains many vague and often contradictory conclusions, and expresses the hope that future reports will provide more focussed conclusions and firmer recommendations aimed at addressing the significant problems of housing quality in the private sector stock."
