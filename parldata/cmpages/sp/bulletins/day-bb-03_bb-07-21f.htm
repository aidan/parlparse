Business Bulletin No. 102 / 2003

Monday 21st July 2003

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-229 Rosie Kane: Success of Scotland Team in Homeless World Cup 2003-That the Parliament congratulates the Scotland team on its success in the Homeless World Cup 2003 in Graz, Austria, from 7 to 13 July when the team triumphed in the final round of scheme B and claimed the Network of Street Papers Trophy; applauds the efforts of Paul Ballie, Stewart Griffen, John McAnnany, Mark Elliot, Jason Elliot, Archie Murray, Thomas Commrie and Michael Till, all of whom took the Scotland squad forward in this event; praises the Scotland team for its courage, and notes that, given the right kind of support, those marginalised and excluded from society can achieve great things.

*S2M-228 Rosie Kane: Dungavel House Immigration Removal Centre-That the Parliament fully supports the efforts of Bishop Mone, President of the Catholic Church in Scotland's Justice and Peace Commission, to secure the closure of Dungavel House immigration removal centre; demands the restoration of human dignity and freedom for those held at the centre; agrees with Bishop Mone that, because children are being held in prison-like conditions, their rights under the UN Convention on the Human Rights of the Child are being breached, and supports the statement from the Association of Directors of Education in Scotland that giving children locked in Dungavel a mainstream education is "the civic duty of the whole of Scotland".

Supported by: Carolyn Leckie*, Nora Radcliffe*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Colin Fox*, Campbell Martin*, Mr Stewart Maxwell*, Frances Curran*
