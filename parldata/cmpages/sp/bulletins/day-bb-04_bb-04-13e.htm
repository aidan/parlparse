Business Bulletin No. 63 / 2004

Tuesday 13th April 2004

Section E : Written questions lodged on 4th to 8th April 2004
  

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-7443Δ Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will publish its consultation document on the reform of family law.

S2W-7444 Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive what appraisals have been made of the costs absorbed by it as a consequence of implementation of EU legislation and decisions, given the statement in the speech by the First Minister to the Congress of Local and Regional Authorities on 23 March 2004 that regions so often pay the bill for the implementation of EU legislation and decisions.

S2W-7445 Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will oppose proposals by the Chancellor of the Exchequer to repatriate regional structural funding, given the statement in the speech by the First Minister on 23 March 2004 to the Congress of Local and Regional Authorities that structural fund programmes have shown the added value of the regional administration of EU policy and funding.

S2W-7446 Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive what arrangements are in place with Her Majesty's Government so that it is consulted on impending EU legislation and decision-making and whether such arrangements are consistent with the six-week timescale proposed in the new EU constitution for consulting with national parliaments on legislative proposals from the commission.

S2W-7447 Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration has been given to ensuring that an adequate large animal veterinary service is available throughout Scotland.

S2W-7448 Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive what large animal veterinary service resources there are and how these resources have changed since the foot and mouth crisis.

S2W-7449 Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive what policies it has put in place for long-term animal disease surveillance.

S2W-7450 Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to ensure that there is adequate surveillance and laboratory resources for large animal veterinary services, in light of financial pressures within the Scottish Agricultural College.

S2W-7451 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will urge Caledonian MacBrayne to enter into discussions with Highland Council to consider the introduction of an additional service on Friday afternoon and Monday morning for those children resident in the small isles who attend Mallaig High School.

S2W-7452 Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the comments made by the First Minister in his speech to the Congress of Local and Regional Authorities of Europe on 23 March 2004 that, with respect to involvement in EU Commission decision-making, not everything requires treaty change and that it would be wrong to sit back and wait for the IGC to conclude and a new treaty to come into force, negates any benefit of signing the new European constitution with respect to consultation at the lowest devolved level.

S2W-7453 Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive which local access forums have been established to date.

S2W-7455 Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the grant aided expenditure allocated to each appropriate local authority takes account of the necessity to dredge small working harbours.

S2W-7456 Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive what sources of funding are available for the upkeep and maintenance of small working harbours.

S2W-7457 Jim Mather: To ask the Scottish Executive what level of public money was invested in the Scotland the Brand project from 1995 to 2003.

S2W-7458 Jim Mather: To ask the Scottish Executive what the asset value of the Scotland the Brand trademark was when it was privatised.

S2W-7459 Jim Mather: To ask the Scottish Executive what the total sum spent on consultants by it and its enterprise agencies was in respect of the Scotland the Brand project from 1995 to 2003.

S2W-7461 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions have taken place with major supermarket chains to ensure compliance with the Animal By-Products (Scotland) Regulations 2003.

S2W-7462 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive how many jobs it anticipates will be created by the establishment of its proposed transport agency.

S2W-7463 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive what factors will be taken into account when deciding the location of its proposed transport agency.

S2W-7464 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive what the timetable is for the decision on the location of the headquarters of its proposed transport agency.

S2W-7465 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consult with appropriate trade unions when selecting the location of the headquarters of its proposed transport agency.

S2W-7467 Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what powers it has in relation to granting consent to offshore renewable energy generation plants outwith the 12 nautical mile territorial sea limit around Scotland and what powers it will have once the UK Energy Bill is enacted.

S2W-7470 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) children and (b) elderly people are in fuel-poor households in (i) Glasgow, (ii) Edinburgh, (iii) Aberdeen, (iv) Stirling, (v) Inverness and (vi) Scotland as a whole.
