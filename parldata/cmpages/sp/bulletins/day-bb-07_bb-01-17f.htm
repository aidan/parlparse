Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (♦).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-5425♦ Ms Maureen Watt: School Transport Safety—That the Parliament notes that current legislation regarding the provision of seatbelts on school transport does not require all school buses to be fitted with seatbelt facilities; further notes that the mandatory fitting of seatbelts on school buses only applies to those vehicles first used on or after 1 October 2001 or those manufactured six months before that date; recognises that this loophole can result in children’s safety being jeopardised, as was experienced at a recent accident in Aberdeenshire, and considers that the Scottish Executive should make representations to the Department for Transport in order to have legislation amended so that all school buses, regardless of age and size, are fitted with adequate seatbelt facilities.

*S2M-5424 Carolyn Leckie: Stick Up for the PCS Union—That the Parliament notes that 30,000 PCS members in Scotland are being balloted for strike action as part of a UK-wide ballot and that this involves staff working in devolved and non-devolved areas and the Scottish Executive; regrets that PCS has been forced to ballot for strike action because of intransigent employers and in the face of government-driven job cuts, service cuts, persistent low pay, privatisation and closures; recognises that the Executive is among the lowest paying 25% of public and private sector employers, with a starting salary of £12,000 per annum; believes that the Executive has a responsibility to resolve the issues affecting its staff and ensure a negotiated settlement with the PCS, but, in the absence of such a response, sends full support and solidarity to all those members being balloted and commits to standing shoulder to shoulder with them on the picket lines.

*S2M-5423 Ms Margaret Curran on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Criminal Proceedings etc. (Reform) (Scotland) Bill—That the Parliament agrees that, during Stage 3 of the Criminal Proceedings etc. (Reform) (Scotland) Bill, debate on groups of amendments shall, subject to Rule 9.8.4A, be brought to a conclusion by the time limit indicated, that time limit being calculated from when the Stage begins and excluding any periods when other business is under consideration or when the meeting of the Parliament is suspended (other than a suspension following the first division in the Stage in the morning and afternoon being called) or otherwise not in progress:

Groups 1 to 3: 50 minutes

Groups 4 to 8 : 1 hour 45 minutes

Group 9: 2 hours 5 minutes

Groups 10 to 11: 2 hours 55 minutes

Groups 12 to 15: 3 hours 25 minutes.

*S2M-5421 Carolyn Leckie: Rename Queensberry House as Fletcher House—That the Parliament notes that there are few people celebrating the 300th anniversary of the Act of Union; recognises that the dissolution of the Scottish Parliament in 1707 was a defeat for the Scottish nation; notes that the central figure who colluded with the monarchy and the English state in the removal of power from that Parliament against the will of the people of Scotland was James Douglas, the Duke of Queensberry and original owner of the Queensberry House building in the new Holyrood complex; further notes that, in contrast, the person noted for the most honourable and progressive role in that period was Andrew Fletcher of Saltoun, who fought against the union and to maintain a Scottish Parliament, and who also championed democratic reforms such as annual elections to that Parliament, only the Parliament having the power to declare war and the removal of the royal veto which, 300 years later, have still never been achieved; believes that the re-establishment of the Scottish Parliament in the late 20th century testifies to the foresight of those such as Fletcher; therefore believes that it would go some way to acknowledge the wrong and injustice that was done if Queensberry House were renamed Fletcher House, and looks forward to the day when that building will reside within a Parliament with full powers in an independent Scotland.

Supported by: Frances Curran*, Rosie Kane*, Campbell Martin*

*S2M-5420 Ms Margaret Curran on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Membership of Committee—That the Parliament agrees that Peter Peacock be appointed to replace Sarah Boyack on the Environment and Rural Development Committee.

Supported by: George Lyon*

*S2M-5419 Peter Peacock: Visit to Scotland by Al Gore—That the Parliament welcomes the visit of Al Gore, former Vice President of the United States of America, to Scotland and commends his work on raising awareness of the issues associated with climate change and global warming; encourages all Scots to read the publication, or see the film, An Inconvenient Truth, and make a commitment to personal action to reduce their carbon impact and combat global warming, and encourages the Scottish Executive to progressively extend actions to reduce carbon emissions and encourage personal action by individual Scots.

Supported by: Mr Alasdair Morrison*

*S2M-5418 John Home Robertson: Bicentenary of the Abolition of Slave Trade—That the Parliament recognises the immense human and historic significance of the abolition of the slave trade in the British empire in 1807; notes with shame the role of some Scots in the slave trade and the cruel exploitation of enslaved people from Africa before abolition; pays tribute to the memory of those Scots who campaigned for abolition, including William Dickson and Zachary Macaulay; condemns the criminal exploitation of human beings that still exists in some parts of the world, and asserts the need for vigilance by national and international agencies to safeguard the principles initiated by the Abolition of the Slave Trade Act 1807.
