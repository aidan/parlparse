Business Bulletin No. 62/2002

Friday 19 April 2002

Section E : Written questions lodged on 18 April 2002
  

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S1W-25176 Fiona McLeod: To ask the Scottish Executive how many applications were made to the Sustainable Action Fund in 2001-02 and how many such applications were successful.

S1W-25177 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive when it was informed that the facility at Dungavel House had been reclassified from being an asylum seeker detention centre to being an immigration removal centre and what the implications of this change are for the Executive and for the local authority.

S1W-25178 Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive how much public money has been spent on research into the causes of ME in (a) 1997, (b) 1998, (c) 1999, (d) 2000 and (e) 2001.

S1W-25179 Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria are employed in the allocation of funding to ME research.

S1W-25180 Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what areas have been covered by current research into the causes of ME.

S1W-25181 Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to include studies into the potential physical and environmental causes of ME in future research projects into the disease.

S1W-25182 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any smallpox vaccine will be stored in Scotland in order to respond to any cases or outbreaks as they arise.

S1W-25183 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what the shelf-life is of the smallpox vaccine.

S1W-25184 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are plans to upgrade the Balfron ambulance station in West Stirlingshire so that it provides round the clock 24-hour cover, and if so, when any such plans will be implemented.

S1W-25185 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are any plans to upgrade the ambulance stations in Callander and Killin in Perthshire so that they provide 24-hour cover, and if so, when any such plans will be implemented.

S1W-25186 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive, with regard to its news release SEHD012/2002 on 16 April 2002, how the priority based dispatch system will affect the Scottish Ambulance Service in the Forth Valley.

S1W-25187 Mr Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average waiting time is for (a) urgent and (b) non-urgent cases between being referred by a general practitioner to an opthamology clinic and the first consultation at the clinic, broken down by NHS board area.

S1W-25188 Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to raise the capital limit at which care home residents become liable to pay accommodation charges, currently set at £18,500, and what the reasons are for its position on this matter.

S1W-25189 Mr Keith Harding: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is unnecessary for Stirling Council to maintain a marked register of all those who voted in the Teith Ward by-election held on 18 April 2002.

S1W-25190 Mr Keith Harding: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there was any scientific evidence to fully justify the closure of the Sprat fisheries in the Firth of Forth.

S1W-25191 Mr Keith Harding: To ask the Scottish Executive what effect the North Atlantic drift has had on cod stocks in the North Sea.

S1W-25192 Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive how many fires have occurred on vessels within 12 miles of the Scottish coastline in the last three years and whether any such incidents involved requests for assistance from coastal fire brigades.

S1W-25193 Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive which coastal fire brigades have crews and resources trained to deal with vessels on fire at sea and other emergencies offshore.

S1W-25194 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-23859 by Dr Elaine Murray on 20 March 2002, whether the increase in funding to the Scottish Museums Council represents new monies or monies from previously announced support for museums, such as those monies announced as part of the National Cultural Strategy.

S1W-25195 Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the Scottish Executive, in respect of the care of children in cases where the parents are separated, whether there are any guidelines obliging NHS trusts to communicate with both parents; if there are not, whether it will consider introducing such guidelines, and what the reasons are for the position on this matter.

S1W-25196 Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many nurse practitioners currently practise in the NHS and in which areas they practise.

S1W-25197 Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many nurse consultants currently practise in the NHS.

S1W-25198 Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many clinical nurse specialists currently practise in the NHS and in which specialisms they practise.

S1W-25199 Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are any plans to increase the number of (a) nurse practitioners, (b) nurse consultants and (c) clinical nurse specialists.

S1W-25200 Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any NHS trust provides on-site childcare facilities for employees.

S1W-25201 Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether nurses working in the NHS receive payment, vouchers or any other financial support to pay for off-site childcare as part of their pay and conditions.

S1W-25202 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-24252 by Jim Wallace on 21 March 2002, which other recommendations from the Expert Panel on Sex Offending’s report, Reducing the Risk: Improving the response to sex offending, have been implemented and when it will implement any other of the committee’s recommendations that have not been implemented.

S1W-25203 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to develop monitoring mechanisms for the tourism niche market of genealogy.

S1W-25204 Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to receive from the Scottish Transport Group Pension Funds Trustees pension records and any other information necessary to the calculation of ex-gratia payments to individual former members of the pension schemes.

S1W-25205 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many audiology departments are complying with the Good Practice Guidance for Adult Hearing Aid Fittings and Services and what action is being taken to assist those departments who are failing to comply with the guidance.

S1W-25206 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Public Health Institute has a remit on tackling coronary heart disease and, if so, what programmes it has developed.

S1W-25207 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-23297 by Malcolm Chisholm on 20 March 2002, what plans it has to collate information centrally on the use of statins, given that they are the drugs of first choice in the Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network Guideline 40 on Lipids and the Primary Prevention of Coronary Heart Disease and that their prescription is one of the essential criteria in the Clinical Standards Board for Scotland's standards for secondary prevention following an acute myocardial infarction.

S1W-25208 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-23296 by Hugh Henry on 20 March 2002, whether there are adequate numbers of primary care nurses working in the field of coronary heart disease and, if not, what action is being taken to rectify this.

S1W-25209 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive what financial assistance it has given to the Scottish Maritime Museum in Irvine since 1999.

S1W-25210 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive what financial assistance it has given to the Big Idea in Stevenston in each year since 1999.

S1W-25211 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive what proposals it has to ensure that no existing industrial museum with a national designation from the Scottish Museums Council is allowed to either cease trading or shed the majority of its staff.

S1W-25212 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive what requests for assistance it has received from industrial museums in the last 18 months and what its response has been.

S1W-25213 Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what monitoring is undertaken of ambulance response times in Angus.

S1W-25214 Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive whether ambulance response times have increased in Angus since the withdrawal of acute services from Stracathro Hospital.

S1W-25215 Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what changes there have been to the provision of ambulance services for Angus since the withdrawal of acute services from Stracathro Hospital.

S1W-25216 Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of patients in Angus requiring ambulance transfers within one or two hours are not transported within this timeframe.

S1W-25217 Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to increase the provision of ambulance services in Angus.

S1W-25218 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what contribution women’s golf events make to the economy.

S1W-25219 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive which major golfing events will be included in the major events strategy.

S1W-25220 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive which major women’s golfing events will be included in the major events strategy.

S1W-25221 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide a list of works of art from the national collections currently on loan both within and outwith Scotland.

S1W-25222 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Presiding Officer what the planned budget is for the arts strategy for the new Parliament buildings at Holyrood.

S1W-25223 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether plans to introduce national motorways, trunk and ring road tolls in England and Wales as published by the UK Minister for Transport, Local Government and the Regions will be extended to Scotland.

S1W-25224 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the staff establishment has been in the Scottish Prison Service estates department in each of the last 20 years.

S1W-25225 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average number of staff employed in the Scottish Prison estates department has been in each of the last 20 years.

S1W-25226 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many Scottish Prison Service staff have been registered with the Architects Registration Board in each of the last 20 years.

S1W-25227 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many Scottish Prison Service staff have been members of the Institution of Structural Engineers in each of the last 20 years.

S1W-25228 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many Scottish Prison Service staff have been members of the Institution of Civil Engineers in each of the last 20 years.
