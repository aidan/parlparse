Business Bulletin No. 119/2006: Monday 4 September 2006
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 7 September 2006

Question Time

General

1. Susan Deacon: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to tackle prejudice and discrimination on grounds of sexual orientation. (S2O-10426)

2. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has made to the UK Government in respect of the application by Forth Ports plc for consent for ship-to-ship transfers of oil cargoes in the Firth of Forth. (S2O-10431)

3. Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will examine the case for adding the Japanese Akita to the types of dog proscribed under the Dangerous Dogs Act 1991. (S2O-10443)

4. Mr Ted Brocklebank: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to address any increased level of noise, litter and antisocial behaviour outside public houses and other licensed premises directly resulting from the legislation prohibiting smoking in public places. (S2O-10418)

5. Tricia Marwick: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it gives for the provision of sports facilities. (S2O-10406)

6. Mr Andrew Arbuckle: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider introducing the Adopt a Highway scheme used in the United States of America, whereby roadside verges are kept free of litter with the voluntary help of individuals and organisations. (S2O-10466)

7. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive how its plans for youth work in Scotland will enhance the personal, social and educational development of young people and enable them to gain a voice, influence and a place in society. (S2O-10468)

8. Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether Dumfries and Galloway Council’s budget for aids and adaptations has been cut from £1.2 million in 2005-06 to £250,000 in the current financial year. (S2O-10449)

9. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance is available to public authorities on dealing with actual or suspected outbreaks of E. coli O157. (S2O-10421)

10. Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to improve speech and language therapy services across Scotland. (S2O-10441)

Enterprise, Lifelong Learning and Transport

1. Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in relation to major improvements to the A9; what sections of the road will be dualled; whether it considers that the provision of dualling will reduce the number of accidents resulting in death or serious injury, and when the design work in respect of dualling the section from Perth to Ballinluig will be completed. (S2O-10410)

2. Not Lodged

3. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it is giving for starting up new businesses. (S2O-10463)

4. Mark Ballard: To ask the Scottish Executive what effect the introduction of medical top-up fees has had on the number of English, Welsh and Northern Irish domiciled students starting medical degrees at Scottish universities in 2006-07. (S2O-10400)

5. Not Lodged

6. Ms Maureen Watt: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers a reduction in Scottish Enterprise’s Modern Apprenticeships programme for over-25-year-olds to be discriminatory on grounds of age. (S2O-10415)

7. Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive what priority will be given in the Strategic Transport Projects Review to proposals for a Maybole bypass. (S2O-10455)

8. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how the people of Cumbernauld will benefit from the decision to follow the online route for the new M80. (S2O-10458)

9. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive how it intends to maintain the international competitiveness of Scottish universities. (S2O-10459)

10. Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment its Enterprise, Transport and Lifelong Learning Department has made of the economic impact on Scotland of the Edinburgh festivals. (S2O-10460)

Justice and Law Officers

1. John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are any plans to extend bail supervision throughout Scotland. (S2O-10465)

2. Dr Jean Turner: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider introducing legislation to control the use of quad bikes by minors. (S2O-10397)

3. John Swinburne: To ask the Scottish Executive whether procurators fiscal covering the parliamentary Central Scotland region have the capacity to progress every antisocial behaviour order submitted to them by the police. (S2O-10399)

4. Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what monitoring is in place of the amount of time telephone callers wait to be answered, particularly at peak times, following Strathclyde Police’s introduction of call centres. (S2O-10445)

5. Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the number of prosecutions for wildlife crime meets its targets. (S2O-10456)

6. Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive what the implications are of the introduction of the non-numeric fingerprint standard to the Scottish Fingerprint Service. (S2O-10429)

7. Mr Jim Wallace: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will undertake a survey of the availability of criminal legal aid in the Scottish islands to enable any shortcomings to be identified and addressed. (S2O-10462)

8. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to encourage local authorities and other agencies to make better use of the legislation on antisocial behaviour. (S2O-10435)

9. Mr Charlie Gordon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider lodging an amendment to the Legal Profession and Legal Aid (Scotland) Bill to make provision for remedying endowment mis-selling by solicitors. (S2O-10430)

10. Mr John Swinney: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making in tackling antisocial behaviour. (S2O-10413)
