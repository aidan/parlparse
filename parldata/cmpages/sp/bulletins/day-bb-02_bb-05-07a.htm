Business Bulletin No. 73/2002

Tuesday 7 May 2002

Section A : Daily Business List
  

Meetings of Committees

All meetings take place in Committee Chambers, Edinburgh unless otherwise indicated.

Public Petitions Committee

8th Meeting, 2002

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in Committee Room 2

1. New Petitions: The Committee will consider the following new petitions—

PE483: Review of the membership of the Justice 1 Committee. Mr Duncan Shields will give a presentation in support of this petition.

PE491: Access to triple assessment breast examinations. Ms Elaine McNeil will give a presentation in support of this petition.

PE493: Development of wind farms in North Argyll. Ms Marilyn Henderson will give a presentation in support of this petition.

PE499: NHS Fife Health Board Right for Fife business plan. Mr Tom Davison will give a presentation in support of this petition.

PE498: NHS Fife Health Board Right for Fife business plan, health board membership and fire safety issues in hospitals. Ms Letitia Murphy will give a presentation in support of this petition.

PE500: Scottish Transport Group Pension Funds. Mr Alex Anderson will give a presentation in support of this petition.

PE492: Alienation of children by a parent.

PE495: Suburbanisation of rural Scotland.

2. Current Petitions: The Committee will further consider the following current petitions—

PE390: Exclusion within higher education

PE395: Planning and environmental regulations

PE449: Impact of predatory birds on other species

PE456: West of Scotland Water Authority

PE466: Funding of the National Library of Scotland

PE467: Impact of the Scottish Borders Council cuts in services

PE472: Adoption procedures for black and ethnic minority children.

3. Convener's Report.

Finance Committee

9th Meeting, 2002

The Committee will meet at 10.30 am in Committee Room 1

1. Cross-cutting Reviews: The Committee will consider a paper outlining progress made on its cross-cutting reviews.

2. Items in Private: The Committee will decide whether to consider an issues paper in relation to its inquiry into PFI/PPP in private at its next meeting.

3. Budget Process: The Committee will consider a paper from the Clerk on whether to discuss the reports of Subject Committees during the Budget Process in public or in private in future.

4. PFI/PPP Inquiry (in private): The Committee will consider a paper on matters relating to its PFI/PPP Inquiry.

Subordinate Legislation Committee

15th Meeting, 2002

The Committee will meet at 11.15 am in Committee Room 3

1. Executive Responses: The Committee will consider the responses from the Scottish Executive to points raised on the following—

The Valuation and Rating (Exempted Classes) (Scotland) Order 2002, (draft)

The Home Zones (Scotland) Regulations 2002, (SSI 2002/177)

The Registration of Fish Farming and Shellfish Farming Businesses Amendment (Scotland) Order 2002, (SSI 2002/193).

2. Draft instruments subject to approval: The Committee will consider the following—

The Marriage (Approval of Places) (Scotland) Regulations 2002, (draft)

The Scotland Act 1998 (Transfer of Functions to the Scottish Ministers etc.) Order 2002, (draft)

The Scotland Act 1998 (Modifications of Schedule 5) Order 2002, (draft).

3. Instruments subject to annulment: The Committee will consider the following—

The Regulation of Investigatory Powers (Source Records) (Scotland) Regulations 2002, (SSI 2002/205)

The Regulation of Investigatory Powers (Juveniles) (Scotland) Order 2002, (SSI 2002/206)

The Regulation of Investigatory Powers (Cancellation of Authorisations) (Scotland) Regulations 2002, (SSI 2002/207).
