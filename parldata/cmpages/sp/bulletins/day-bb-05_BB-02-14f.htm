Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

* S2M-2428 Mrs Nanette Milne:Congratulations to Prince Charles and Camilla Parker Bowles—That the Parliament sends its congratulations to Prince Charles, Duke of Rothesay, and Camilla Parker Bowles on the news of their recent engagement; hopes that the couple will enjoy many years of happiness together; is delighted that the couple have chosen to honeymoon in the beautiful north east of Scotland, and believes that the vast majority of people in Scotland will wish them well in this important step forward.

* S2M-2427 Mrs Mary Mulligan:Congratulations to Home Aid, Bathgate—That the Parliament congratulates Home Aid in Bathgate, West Lothian, on its contribution to furniture recycling; notes that redistribution of furniture helps both the environment and those who find it difficult to furnish their homes, and congratulates Home Aid on its innovative partnership with Oregon in the United States of America for recycling furniture beyond Scotland.

* S2M-2426# Mr Brian Monteith:Resources to Police Boards—That the Parliament recognises the vital role that the police play within Scottish communities; believes that it is vital that Scotland’s police forces are adequately resourced to ensure maximum protection to the public; acknowledges that Scottish police boards have very little time to brief and consult constituent local authorities before finalising the budget for their police force area; agrees that it is vital that figures provided by the Scottish Executive with regard to local authority grants are as accurate as possible to aid this process; therefore notes with concern that there were errors in the detail of local authority funding announced by the Executive on 8 December 2004 which have resulted in uncertainty facing many police boards across Scotland with regard to grant support for police loan charges and common police services; is concerned that some local authorities have had to source additional funding, such as Scottish Borders which has had to find an additional £650,000 from its already-agreed budget allocations, and therefore considers that the Executive should engage urgently with our police boards to resolve any problems and to ensure that such mistakes are not repeated in the future.

* S2M-2425# Pauline McNeill:Franz Ferdinand Rocks—That the Parliament congratulates Franz Ferdinand on winning the Best British Band and Best Rock Act awards at the 25th Brit Awards; welcomes the First Minister’s view that Scotland should be a future host to the Brit Awards; notes that Scotland has an abundance of talented musicians, demonstrated by the fact that 11 acts are to appear at the international music festival in Texas, South by Southwest; believes that Scotland has a strong contemporary music industry which makes a significant contribution to the Scottish economy, comprising of small, medium and large companies; supports the work of the Cross Party Group on the Scottish Contemporary Music Industry which brings together virtually all strands of the music industry; recognises the commitment of the Scottish Executive to the industry and the funding of research into the feasibility of creating a music industry forum; further recognises initiatives such as Music Works, funded by Scottish Enterprise, and the need for the enterprise agencies to promote a distinct music industry strategy so as to encourage further development of Scottish-based musicians and related business activity, and sends its good wishes to all bands who are participating in South by Southwest.

S2M-2419 Margaret Jamieson:Responsible Development of Onshore Wind Farms—That the Parliament notes with concern the continued scaremongering on the subject of onshore wind farms; regrets the tactics being used by some members of the Scottish Parliament to promote the view that all wind farm proposals are in very remote areas; recognises the proposal to build Whitelee wind farm, which crosses the boundaries of East Ayrshire, East Renfrewshire and South Lanarkshire local authority areas and which, if approved, would be the largest onshore development in Europe, providing almost half of the Scottish renewable target, and would be immediately adjacent to a major centre of power demand; welcomes the potential 300 construction jobs over a phased 30-month construction period, the £250 million of contracts that could be tendered for by Scottish companies and the provision of an easily-accessible visitor centre, and calls on all interested bodies involved in this exciting project to work towards a successful outcome to coincide with the G8 summit’s focus on climate change when the eyes of the world will be on Scotland.

S2M-2416 Irene Oldfather:British Heart Foundation - Real Valentine Appeal—That the Parliament recognises that heart disease is the UK’s biggest killer and that, every two minutes, someone in the United Kingdom has a heart attack; acknowledges that a healthy diet and regular exercise is the best way to avoid heart disease; agrees, however, that sufferers of heart disease should have access to the best levels of care possible; appreciates the work of the British Heart Foundation (BHF) for more than 40 years in leading the fight against heart disease by funding research, education and care; supports the charity’s Real Valentine Appeal which aims to raise £1 million to fund 30 new British Heart Foundation nurses who provide specialist care, reassurance and advice to patients, and encourages as many Scottish people as possible to get involved and post a Valentine note, either online on the BHF website or in a BHF shop window, to their loved one for a small donation to the campaign.

S2M-2415 Ms Rosemary Byrne:Scottish Suffragette Women—That the Parliament pays tribute to the life of Flora Drummond; recognises her contribution to the suffragette movement; acknowledges the personal sacrifice she made while on hunger strike and in prison at nine different times; regrets that the role of Scottish suffragette women is not taught widely in Scottish education, and calls on the Scottish Executive to commemorate the role of Scottish suffragette women by pressurising Her Majesty’s Government for a posthumous pardon for all concerned.

S2M-2409 Carolyn Leckie:Justice and Apology for the Conlon and Maguire Families—That the Parliament welcomes the visit to the House of Commons on 9 February 2005 by Gerry Conlon and other members of the Conlon and Maguire families, who were victims of one of Britain’s most appalling miscarriages of justice and were wrongfully imprisoned for terms of up to 15 years for crimes they had no part in; notes that Guiseppe Conlon, father of Gerry Conlon, died in prison while serving a sentence for a crime he did not commit; considers that the public apology hoped for by the families from the UK Government for this terrible injustice is long overdue; notes with grave concern that, despite the overturning of all the sentences on the Conlon and Maguire families in 1989 and 1991, no police officers or other officials involved in this injustice have ever been prosecuted for their part in it, nor has the UK Government officially recognised that they were all innocent, and believes that the UK Government should issue a full apology to all, that agreement should be reached with all the families on appropriate financial compensation and that justice should be seen to be done for the injustice they suffered.

Supported by: Frances Curran, Rosie Kane, Chris Ballance, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Tommy Sheridan, Colin Fox

S2M-2408 # Donald Gorrie:Suicides by Older People in Scotland—That the Parliament is concerned at the statistics about suicides by older people in Scotland, especially older men; believes that more attention needs to be given to treating depression in general and, especially, among older people, persuading older men suffering from depression to discuss their problems with their GP and improving the performance of the primary care system in recognising the symptoms of depression and treating it more rapidly, and considers that the relevant authorities should undertake more research into the causes of depression and suicide and should add older people as a priority group to the other seven priority groups focused on in Choose Life, the national strategy to prevent suicide.

Supported by: Iain Smith, Mike Pringle, Robert Brown, John Farquhar Munro, Margaret Smith, Jeremy Purvis, George Lyon, Nora Radcliffe, Ms Sandra White*

S2M-2407 Mark Ballard:Link Between Transport Choices and Income—That the Parliament notes the finding of the Scottish Household Survey that, in households with a net income of £5,000 to £10,000, only 20% of householders drive regularly while 55% use the bus regularly, whereas in households with an income of over £40,000, 74% drive regularly and only 29% regularly use the bus; further notes that the data ranges between these two extremes show that the wealthier the household, the more likely its members are to use a car and the less likely they are to use a bus; believes that this survey reflects the income distribution and transport choices of people who will be subject to Edinburgh’s congestion charge which will, therefore, be levied proportionately more on the rich and not the poor; commends the Yes to Edinburgh campaign and the Get Edinburgh Moving campaign for their brave work towards tackling gridlock in Edinburgh, and condemns the Scottish Socialist Party, the Scottish National Party, the Scottish Liberal Democrats and the Scottish Conservative and Unionist Party for campaigning against action to prevent congestion, respiratory disease and road death, all of which disproportionately affect low income Edinburgh households without cars.

Supported by: Robin Harper
