Business Bulletin No. 65/2002

Wednesday 24 April 2002

Section E : Written questions lodged on 23 April 2002
  

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S1W-25280 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has made to (a) the Ministry of Defence and (b) the British Army about withdrawal of guards from Edinburgh Castle.

S1W-25281 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it owns any part of Edinburgh Castle and what role it plays in the operation of the castle.

S1W-25282 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what areas of Edinburgh Castle are occupied or used by (a) the Ministry of Defence and (b) the British Army and whether any rent is paid or payment made by each in respect of such use.

S1W-25283 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many officers in Lothian and Borders Police Force claimed Criminal Injuries Compensation in each year since 1997.

S1W-25284 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many officers left Lothian and Borders Police Force as a result of work-related ill health in each year since 1997.

S1W-25285 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how much each local authority has allocated to the concessionary fares scheme in (a) 2002-03 and (b) 2003-04.

S1W-25286 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many incidents police officers from Lothian and Borders Police Force have been requested to attend by (a) the Vehicle Inspectorate, (b) the Immigration and Nationality Directorate, (c) HM Customs and Excise, (d) the Department for Work and Pensions and (e) local authority social work departments in each year since 1997.

S1W-25287 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many officers in Lothian and Borders Police Force have been the subject of a complaint in each year since 1997.

S1W-25288 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on any plans of Strathclyde Passenger Transport in respect of constructing a rail link to Strathbungo and what consideration has been given to any impact of such a link on the financing and delivery of the M74 northern extension.

S1W-25289 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to set up community warden schemes in the Lothians and the Borders or whether it will rule out the use of such schemes in those areas.

S1W-25290 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-24373 by Mr Jim Wallace on 22 April 2002, what the total number of suicides among (a) men and (b) women prisoners was in each of the last five years.

S1W-25291 Mr Keith Harding: To ask the Scottish Executive what the annual financial impact will be of the increase in employers' National Insurance contributions for (a) Scottish Enterprise, (b) Highlands and Islands Enterprise and (c) each local enterprise company in each of the next three years.

S1W-25292 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answers to questions S1W-18369 and S1W-20351 by Malcolm Chisholm on 23 October 2001 and 7 December 2001, whether the Health Education Board for Scotland has completed its research for the purposes of informing the development of a sexual health strategy and when it expects to publish the strategy.

S1W-25293 Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive what the estimated cost will be to each local authority in 2003-04 of the changes to employers' National Insurance contributions announced in the UK 2002 Budget.

S1W-25294 Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive what the estimated cost will be to each NHS (a) board and (b) trust in 2003-04 of the changes to employers' National Insurance contributions announced in the UK 2002 Budget.

S1W-25295 Mr Keith Harding: To ask the Scottish Executive what the annual financial impact will be of the increase in employers' National Insurance contributions for (a) VisitScotland, (b) each area tourist board, (c) the Scottish Prison Service and (d) Communities Scotland in each of the next three years.

S1W-25296 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to extend its central heating installation programme to private sector houses occupied under rent deposit guarantee terms.

S1W-25297 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to support the development of summer play schemes in the west of Scotland.

S1W-25298 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive when work will begin on the construction of the Maryhill to Anniesland rail link and when the powers to provide this link expire.

S1W-25299 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive when a decision will be made on the route for the Glasgow to Edinburgh fast rail link.

S1W-25300 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Changing Children's Services Fund will be allocated for 2002-03.

S1W-25301 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what evaluation will be made of how money from the Changing Children's Services Fund is allocated for 2002-03.

S1W-25302 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria are used for deciding which projects will receive funding from the Changing Children's Services Fund.

S1W-25303 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what role smaller voluntary agencies have played, or will play, in allocating the funds from the Changing Children's Services Fund for 2002-03.

S1W-25304 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking in order to ensure that proposals for funding from the Changing Children's Services Fund (a) add value to existing services and (b) do not duplicate or substitute work already being carried out.

S1W-25305 Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it can take to ensure that the services provided by voluntary organisations, such as Angus Carers, are offered in appropriate cases by GPs and other NHS staff.

S1W-25306 Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any of the additional health funding announced in the First Minister's ministerial statement on the 2002 Budget will be made available in order to assist in the provision of services by voluntary organisations such as Angus Carers.

S1W-25307 Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people the NHS employs (a) directly and (b) indirectly.

S1W-25308 Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the additional cost to the NHS will be of the increase in National Insurance contributions announced in the UK 2002 Budget.

S1W-25309 Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the additional cost will be to local authorities in respect of directly employed staff of the increase in National Insurance contributions announced in the UK 2002 Budget.

S1W-25310 Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the additional cost will be to local authorities in respect of indirectly employed staff of the increase in National Insurance contributions announced in the UK 2002 Budget.

S1W-25311 Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the additional cost will be to police and fire services of the increase in National Insurance contributions announced in the UK 2002 Budget.

S1W-25312 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration it will give to setting up a mental health task force and what the reasons are for its position on this matter.

S1W-25313 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive how it ensures that NHS boards set their priorities in line with the national clinical priorities.

S1W-25314 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive what current targets it has set for mental health services; how such targets are evaluated, and what improvements in mental health services have been made since 1997.

S1W-25315 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive how much funding it currently provides for counselling services and what projects receive such funding.

S1W-25316 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive how much of the additional funding for health announced in the First Minister's ministerial statement on the UK 2002 budget will be spent on mental health projects.

S1W-25317 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to increase the job prospects of those with mental health problems.

S1W-25318 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1O-3941 by Malcolm Chisholm on 25 October 2001, what actions the National Advisory Group has taken.

S1W-25319 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive what action has been taken to provide primary health care services with details of voluntary organisations in their areas.

S1W-25320 George Lyon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will detail each financial allocation it made to Argyll and Bute Council in 2001-02 and for what purpose the money was allocated.

S1W-25321 George Lyon: To ask the Scottish Executive what restrictions or conditions it has placed on Caledonian MacBrayne Ltd on running ferry services from Gourock to Dunoon.

S1W-25322 George Lyon: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidelines it has issued to Caledonian MacBrayne Ltd regarding any reduction in the level of ferry services on the Gourock to Dunoon route.

S1W-25323 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans exist to extend the monitoring system of annual condition surveys on trunk roads to include non-trunk roads.

S1W-25324 Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Presiding Officer whether he will make a statement on behalf of the SPCB about the weekend reports of Red Oak trees being purchased from Germany at £6,000 each for the landscaping of the Holyrood Parliamentary Building.
