Business Bulletin No. 189/2004

Monday 20 December 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-2187# Ms Sandra White: Poverty Task Force for Glasgow—That the Parliament notes the report by the Joseph Rowntree Foundation (JRF), Strategies against Poverty, published on 13 December 2004, which highlights Glasgow as having the worst levels of deprivation in the United Kingdom; expresses concern that the levels of poverty in Glasgow do not seem to be tackled by the current measures being undertaken by both Glasgow City Council and the Scottish Executive; believes that a special task force for Glasgow should be established to ensure that resources are far more effectively channelled into dealing with poverty in the city, and recognises that eradicating poverty will also help communities in Glasgow remove other related problems such as drug cultures, crime and family breakdown.

*S2M-2186 Richard Lochhead: Right to Read—That the Parliament notes that 96% of books are never made available in large print, audio or Braille formats; further notes that there are 180,000 blind or partially-sighted individuals in Scotland who are being denied the ability to enjoy these books; welcomes the Right to Read campaign which is being organised by the Right to Read Alliance, a group of fourteen charities which campaigns for everyone to be able to read the same book at the same time and at the same price, for a greater number of books to be published in accessible formats and to ensure that no student is denied the right to learn because they cannot read study materials; encourages as many people as possible to sign up to the Right to Read Charter; calls on all local authorities to ensure that public libraries stock books in large print, audio and Braille formats, and further calls on all Scottish publishers to ensure that books are made accessible to those who suffer from partial sight or blindness.

*S2M-2185 Brian Adam: Closure of Aberdeen City Council Workplace Nursery—That the Parliament commends companies and organisations that provide workplace nursery schemes for the benefit of their employees and their children and condemns the Liberal Democrat/Conservative administration of Aberdeen City Council which has voted to close its workplace nursery.

*S2M-2184 Brian Adam: Failure to Mainstream Aberdeen’s Great Northern Partnership SIP Projects—That the Parliament recognises the admirable work carried out by the Great Northern Social Inclusion Partnership’s (SIP) community partners since its inception; commends the SIP projects which have delivered vital front-line services to some of Aberdeen’s socially-excluded communities over the years, and regrets that Aberdeen City Council feels unable to mainstream these projects.

*S2M-2183# John Scott: Inadequacies of Funding for Care—That the Parliament notes the Cost of Care Report which was independently audited by the Scottish Local Authority Management Centre and points out the inadequacy of Scottish Executive funding for care; regrets that the funding shortfall will reduce the number of continuing care beds and the quality of care provision and will increase bed blocking; notes that nursing home fees are running at £73 per person per week below the cost of provision; further notes that residential home fees are running at more than £42 per person per week below the cost of provision, and agrees with COSLA and Scottish Care that the Executive must address this shortfall.

S2M-2182# George Lyon: Campbeltown Hospital—That the Parliament notes with concern that NHS Argyll & Clyde has shut the assessment ward at Campbeltown Hospital with no public consultation and no alternative provision in place; further notes that the board is cutting primary care budgets in Argyll and Bute, despite the fact that the primary care sector in Argyll and Bute has not overspent, and therefore believes that the Scottish Executive should intervene and press the board to suspend the closure until proper public consultation is carried out and alternative provision supported by the community is put in place and that the board should refocus its cost recovery plan on the areas that have been consistently overspending in the past.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan*

S2M-2181 Colin Fox: Forth Rail Bridge—That the Parliament supports the bid for World Heritage Site status for the Forth Rail Bridge; recognises the magnificent feat of engineering involved in the construction of the Forth Rail Bridge; acknowledges the dedication, hard work and skill of the working men who built the Forth Rail Bridge; regrets that in the construction of the bridge 57 men lost their lives; calls on the Scottish Executive to recognise these facts within the context of the symbol of Scotland that the bridge represents, and further calls on the Executive to erect a plaque in memory of those workers who lost their lives in the construction.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan*

S2M-2172 Christine Grahame: Strategies Against Poverty—That the Parliament notes the recent report by the Joseph Rowntree Foundation (JRF), Strategies against poverty, published on 13 December 2004, which makes clear the link between deprivation and related problems such as drug cultures, crime and family breakdown; recognises the need to focus greater resources and energy on eradicating poverty from our communities; notes that the JRF report argues that only a "modest share of economic growth would be needed to raise the poorest people in our society above the poverty threshold"; further notes the positive conclusion of the JRF, that real progress is possible to eradicate poverty "provided the political will exists", and calls on the Minister for Communities to back his commitment to "eradicating child poverty" with a firm action plan which incorporates the key recommendations and conclusions of the JRF report.

S2M-2156# Rob Gibson: Improving Scottish Air Ambulance Services—That the Parliament notes with concern a recent consultation by the Scottish Ambulance Service over the future of air ambulance services in Scotland from March 2006; further notes the intention to opt for a new provider with a radically different approach that uses one fewer aircraft to deliver the required work; welcomes the improved accommodation on the new types of aircraft on offer but highlights the deep concerns of Orkney Islands Council which has little confidence in these proposals without the retention of island-based fixed-wing aircraft in the new contract instead of relying on aircraft based in Aberdeen, Inverness or Glasgow; questions the availability of busy coastguard helicopters as back-up in bad weather; draws attention to Shetland Islands Council’s wish to retain an Islander aircraft from the inter-island air service for emergencies; voices concern over the need for a helicopter refuelling stop at Oban on missions from Barra to Glasgow that could itself be affected by severe weather; reiterates the evidence of records for gales and fog which show that an improved air ambulance service for the remote mainland and islands of Scotland requires a greater investment in appropriate aircraft, more of which should be based in the places most affected by severe weather, and believes that the Scottish Executive should ensure that a best value contract does not place lives in greater jeopardy under the proposed new contract.

Supported by: Christine Grahame, Jim Mather, Eleanor Scott, Mr Adam Ingram, Carolyn Leckie, Mr Kenny MacAskill, Roseanna Cunningham, Brian Adam, Chris Ballance, Robin Harper, Richard Lochhead, Michael Matheson, Rosie Kane, Shiona Baird

S2M-2151 Shona Robison: Time to Care Report and Campaign—That the Parliament supports Breast Cancer Care and the Royal College of Nursing’s Time to Care report and campaign to improve access to breast care nurses for people affected by breast cancer; agrees that the use of breast care nurses represents best practice in cancer care and provides an invaluable service; is concerned at the report’s findings that there is a growing shortage of breast care nurses in the United Kingdom; is further concerned that increasing demands on time and the growth in the number of breast cancer patients are making it progressively more difficult for breast care nurses to give patients the information and support that they need, and calls on the Scottish Executive to review urgently the current number of breast care nurses and establish a recruitment and retention strategy to ensure that there are sufficient breast care nurses for the future.

Supported by: Michael Matheson, Donald Gorrie, Christine Grahame, Mr Stewart Maxwell

S2M-2150 Mr Stewart Maxwell: World Health Organisation Tobacco Treaty—That the Parliament congratulates Peru on being the 40th country to ratify the World Health Organisation’s Framework Convention on Tobacco Control, which means that this tobacco treaty will come into force on 28 February 2005; recognises that the United Kingdom will ratify this treaty on 16 December 2004 and will be bound by the new law and, in particular, Article 8 of that treaty which deals with protection from exposure to tobacco smoke, including in workplaces, and therefore calls on the UK Government to honour its obligation by banning smoking in all workplaces.

Supported by: Michael Matheson, Eleanor Scott, Christine Grahame, Mike Pringle, Alex Neil, Patrick Harvie, Brian Adam, Stewart Stevenson, Shiona Baird, Chris Ballance, Rob Gibson, Robin Harper, Trish Godman
