Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-4387# Mrs Mary Mulligan: C-me West Lothian—That the Parliament congratulates West Lothian Council, NHS Lothian, Lothian and Borders Police and the Scottish Children’s Reporter Administration for launching the C-me information-sharing system which, at a time when there is a great deal of concern about child protection issues, is an example of agencies working together to create a clear picture of a child’s needs through sharing relevant background information and will improve the delivery of services to children throughout West Lothian.

*S2M-4386 Ms Maureen Watt: Congratulations to the Rotary Club of Aberdeen—That the Parliament congratulates the Rotary Club of Aberdeen for raising £6,000 to provide a covered market place and public toilet facilities for Aberdeen, Sierra Leone and wishes it well in its future fundraising endeavours.

*S2M-4385 Donald Gorrie: Regulatory Impact Assessment—That the Parliament calls on the Scottish Executive to co-operate with it in setting up a group to carry out a regulatory impact assessment of the regulations introduced by the European Union or the United Kingdom or Scottish governments, or by local authorities, NHS boards or other public bodies, with a remit to identify excessive, unnecessary and duplicated regulations and to press the initiating body to delete, improve or harmonise them.

*S2M-4384 Ms Sandra White: The Chagos Islanders—That the Parliament applauds the decision taken by the High Court in England in favour of families forcibly exiled from the Chagos Islands in the Indian Ocean; notes that in the mid-1960s the United States of America, worried about possible Soviet expansion in the Indian Ocean, wanted a base in the region, but one without a "population problem"; recalls that at that time the Chagos Islands was a British territory campaigning for independence; abhors the campaign of disinformation that was pursued by British diplomats to pretend that there were no permanent inhabitants; notes again that the USA was willing, in return, to offer the United Kingdom an $11 million subsidy on Polaris nuclear missiles; observes that in 2000 the islanders won a ruling that their eviction was unlawful but the British Government then introduced a new order to allow their continued eviction; further notes that in his ruling Sir Sydney Kentridge QC describes the treatment of the islanders as "outrageous, unlawful and a breach of accepted moral standards", and urges the UK and the USA in the name of humanity, to honour the legal ruling and allow the families to return to their rightful home.

*S2M-4383 Mr Tom McCabe: Bankruptcy and Diligence etc. (Scotland) Bill: Financial Resolution—That the Parliament, for the purposes of any Act of the Scottish Parliament resulting from the Bankruptcy and Diligence etc. (Scotland) Bill, agrees—

(a) to any—

(i) increase in expenditure charged on; and

(ii) expenditure for new purposes or increase in expenditure for existing purposes payable out of,

the Scottish Consolidated Fund in consequence of the Act; and

(b) to any charge imposed, and any payment required to be made, in consequence of the Act.

*S2M-4382 Shona Robison: International Nurses’ Day, 12 May 2006—That the Parliament celebrates 12 May 2006 as International Nurses’ Day and recognises the excellent work that nurses across Scotland do and the vital role that they play in the National Health Service, in health promotion and health care; recognises that the Royal College of Nursing (RCN) supports the call for more school nurses as a proactive way to address poor health among Scotland’s youngsters and to tackle health inequalities; notes the RCN’s School Nurse Study which discovered that 64% of school nurses currently feel they are hampered in their ability to do their jobs because of heavy workloads and that 71% say that there are not enough school nurses in their area, and therefore believes that the number of school nurses should be increased to address these concerns.

Supported by: Bruce Crawford*, Stewart Stevenson*, Brian Adam*, Ms Maureen Watt*, Tricia Marwick*, Jim Mather*, Ms Sandra White*, Mr Stewart Maxwell*, Fergus Ewing*, Mr Andrew Welsh*, Rob Gibson*, Roseanna Cunningham*

*S2M-4381<> Mrs Mary Mulligan: National Epilepsy Week, 14 to 20 May 2006—That the Parliament recognises that 14 to 20 May 2006 is National Epilepsy Week and calls on the Scottish Executive to resource NHS provision of patient information to those affected by epilepsy and to encourage community health partnerships to establish epilepsy specialist nurse posts, linking primary and secondary services to provide local support, given Scotland’s aging population and the prevalence of chronic conditions identified in the Kerr report and the Care 21 report.

*S2M-4380 Donald Gorrie on behalf of the Procedures Committee: Procedures relating to Crown Appointments—That the Parliament notes the Procedures Committee’s 2nd Report 2006 (Session 2), Procedures relating to Crown appointments (SP Paper 515) and agrees that the changes to Standing Orders set out in Annexe A to the report be made with effect from 19 May 2006.

*S2M-4379 Bill Aitken: HBOS/Barnardo’s Tug of War 2006—That the Parliament notes that the 2006 HBOS/Barnardo’s Tug of War Competition will take place on 1 June in Holyrood Park, Edinburgh; further notes that last year’s event helped raise just under £4,000 in total for the charity to use for its excellent work; understands that the Labour Party is considering putting its name in the hat this year, although it has yet to confirm whether the Liberal Democrats will be helping it to make up numbers, and hopes that this year the other parties will have more luck in challenging the defending champions and pre-tournament favourites, the all-conquering Scottish Conservatives.

Supported by: Alex Johnstone*, Mrs Nanette Milne*, Margaret Mitchell*, Dave Petrie*, Murdo Fraser*

*S2M-4378 Patrick Harvie: Renew for Freedom—That the Parliament congratulates the campaign group No2ID for their "Renew for Freedom" campaign which urges members of the public to renew their passports en masse in May 2006; notes that by doing so Scottish citizens who wish to exercise the liberty to travel will be able to enjoy at least 10 years of freedom before being compelled to provide biometric data for the UK Government’s ID Card scheme and having their details entered on the National Identity Register (NIR); encourages MSPs and all Scottish citizens to apply for a new passport this month to ensure the slowest possible implementation of the ID card and NIR system, and sincerely hopes that before these 10 years of freedom have passed the system will have been scrapped by a more enlightened government.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan*, Eleanor Scott*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Rob Gibson*, Robin Harper*, Murdo Fraser*, Shiona Baird*

*S2M-4377 Mr Tom McCabe: Adult Support and Protection (Scotland) Bill: Financial Resolution—That the Parliament, for the purposes of any Act of the Scottish Parliament resulting from the Adult Support and Protection (Scotland) Bill, agrees to any expenditure of a kind referred to in paragraph 3(b)(iii) of Rule 9.12 of the Parliament’s Standing Orders arising in consequence of the Act.
