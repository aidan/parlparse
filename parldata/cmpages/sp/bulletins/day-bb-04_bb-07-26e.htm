Business Bulletin No. 118 / 2004

Monday 26 July 2004

Section E : Written questions lodged between 19 and 23 July 2004
  

S2W-9753 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-21756 by Ross Finnie on 14 January 2002, which areas are designated nitrate vulnerable zones (NVZs); what guidance and grant assistance has been issued to farmers on NVZs, and what the results of the monitoring of river water quality have been in each of the last five years, broken down by river.

S2W-9755Δ Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will announce the performance targets set for the Scottish Fisheries Protection Agency for 2004-05.

S2W-9756 Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive how licenses for the control of sparrow-hawks under the Countryside and Wildlife Act 1981 can be applied for.

S2W-9757 Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what conditions require to be met before a license to control sparrow-hawks under the Countryside and Wildlife Act 1981 can be granted.

S2W-9758 Colin Fox: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to increase the income threshold for paying NHS prescription charges.

S2W-9759 Colin Fox: To ask the Scottish Executive what proportion of cancer patients are treated outside hospital in comparison with (a) 10 and (b) 20 years ago.

S2W-9760 Colin Fox: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is in respect of the statement by Macmillan Cancer Relief that financial worries are the second biggest cause of stress for cancer patients and whether it will consider exempting cancer patients from paying NHS prescription charges as a step towards alleviating any such stress.

S2W-9761 Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive what the life expectancy at birth has been in each year since 1997, broken down by gender.

S2W-9762 Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive what the healthy life expectancy at birth has been in each year since 1997, broken down by gender.

S2W-9763 Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive what the mortality rates per 1,000 of children aged under five years have been in each year since 1997, broken down by gender.

S2W-9764 Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive what the mortality rates per 1,000 of people aged from five to 59 have been in each year since 1997, broken down by gender.

S2W-9765 Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive what the total health expenditure per capita has been in each year since 1997.

S2W-9766 Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive what the total health expenditure as a percentage of GDP has been in each year since 1997.

S2W-9767 Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will publish its response to the consultation Modernising NHS Dental Services in Scotland.

S2W-9768 Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive how many NHS dentists there have been per 1,000 population in each of the last five years, broken down by NHS board area.

S2W-9769 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-9398 by Ms Margaret Curran on 13 July 2004, how many homes were purchased under the low-cost home ownership scheme in each of the last five years.

S2W-9770 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what the ratio of administration staff to the total number of beds was in the NHS in each of the last 10 years, broken down by NHS board area.

S2W-9771 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what the ratio of administration staff to the total number of whole-time equivalent nurses was in the NHS in each of the last 10 years, broken down by NHS board area.

S2W-9772 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what the ratio of administration staff to the total number of whole-time equivalent doctors was in the NHS in each of the last 10 years, broken down by NHS board area.

S2W-9773 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive which sporting bodies received funding from (a) sportscotland and (b) other sources in each of the last five years and how much was allocated in each case.

S2W-9774 Mr Bruce McFee: To ask the Scottish Executive how many convictions for theft of power supply there have been in each of the last three years, broken down by sheriff court district.

S2W-9775 Mr Bruce McFee: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has held with public, private or regulatory organisations regarding the theft of power supplies in the last two years.

S2W-9776 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-994 by Nicol Stephen on 21 July 2003, how many (a) enquiries have been received about and (b) cards have been issued for the Thistle Travel Card Scheme in each year since its launch and what guidelines on the scheme have been provided for transport staff and companies.

S2W-9777 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how much funding it provides to ENABLE to assist with the Thistle Travel Card Scheme.

S2W-9778 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-5251 by Hugh Henry on 15 January 2004, whether Dumbarton Sheriff Court has used restriction of liberty orders as a disposal.

S2W-9779 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the anticipated cost is of policing the G8 summit at Gleneagles.

S2W-9780 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether responsibility for policing the G8 summit at Gleneagles will fall entirely on Scottish police forces and, if not, what percentage of overall responsibility will be borne by other parts of the UK.

S2W-9781 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive who will be responsible for meeting the cost of policing at the G8 summit at Gleneagles.

S2W-9782 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether policing levels in local communities across Scotland will be maintained at their usual levels during the G8 summit at Gleneagles.

S2W-9783 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive which agencies currently provide secure accommodation for young offenders and how many places in such accommodation are provided.

S2W-9784 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in providing 29 additional secure accommodation places for young offenders, announced by the Minister for Education and Young People on 24 March 2003.

S2W-9785 Mr Bruce McFee: To ask the Scottish Executive what the reasons are for the increase in the average time period between plea and summary trial date in Paisley Sheriff Court from 2002-03 to 2003-04.

S2W-9786 Mr Bruce McFee: To ask the Scottish Executive what action has been taken in the last year to address the increase in the average time period between plea and summary trial date in Paisley Sheriff Court from 2002-03 to 2003-04.

S2W-9787 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive how many local authorities are enforcing compliance with the Animal By-Products (Scotland) Regulations 2003, with particular reference to raw waste from shellfish processing.

S2W-9788 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is proposing any changes to the eligibility for NHS-funded IVF treatment.

S2W-9789 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to give patients with mental health problems access to homeopathy treatments.

S2W-9790 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the figures are for public libraries in respect of (a) net expenditure, (b) expenditure per head of population, (c) professional, non-professional and manual staff numbers, (d) expenditure on staff, (e) the number of books and other items issued, (f) the average number of issues per head of population of books and other items, (g) book funds, (h) funds for audio and other non-book items, (i) the number of branch and mobile libraries and (j) the number of branches open for 45 or more hours per week in (i) 1995-96, (ii) 1999-2000 and (iii) the latest year for which figures are available.

S2W-9791 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive how many appeals were received against the (a) primary and (b) secondary school placement of a child and how many, and what percentage of, such appeals were successful in each of the last 10 years, broken down by local authority.

S2W-9792 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether an education authority is required to provide the necessary support outwith school for a child identified as having a year-round additional support need.

S2W-9793 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether an education authority can refuse to meet a child's agreed year-round additional support needs.

S2W-9794 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there is a maximum number of days per annum in which an education authority can provide the support necessary to meet a child's additional support needs.

S2W-9795 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there is a minimum number of days per annum in which an education authority must provide the support necessary to meet a child's additional support needs.

S2W-9796 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there is any legislation that prevents education authorities from meeting a child's year-round additional support needs.

S2W-9797 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what evidence it has on whether there is a link between the lack of an education and development plan for an autistic child and the child's anxiety and stress levels.

S2W-9798 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what evidence it has on whether there is a link between the quality and content of an education and development plan for an autistic child and the child's anxiety and stress levels.

S2W-9799 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what evidence it has on whether there is a link between the effectiveness of the implementation of an education and development plan for an autistic child and the child's anxiety and stress levels.

S2W-9800 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many autistic children have been identified as having additional support needs and what provision is available to support such children outwith school.

S2W-9801 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the provisions in the Children (Scotland) Act 1995 place a duty on local authorities to provide support services for children for the whole year and whether these provisions specifically exclude education.

S2W-9802 Jim Mather: To ask the Scottish Executive how much, and what value of its procurement is channelled through the Office of Government Commerce.

S2W-9803 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to give pensioners access to free NHS dental treatment.

S2W-9804 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to address the anomaly whereby Orkney Islands Council's Aggregate External Finance settlement provides 20% less funding per head of population than either Western Isles or Shetland Islands Councils.

S2W-9805 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether all passengers landing at airports in Scotland found to be in possession of offensive weapons are charged with a criminal offence and what the reasons are for its position on this matter; whether information on the actions of such passengers are given to the police or the Procurator Fiscal and who decides whether to prosecute and whether in cases where no prosecution takes place, these passengers are informed that such behaviour may lead to prosecution in future.

S2W-9806 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prosecutions there have been relating to the possession of offensive weapons at airports in each year since 1999, broken down by airport.
