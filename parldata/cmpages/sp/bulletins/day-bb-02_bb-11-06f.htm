*S1M-3543 Mr Andy Kerr: Quality of Life—That the Parliament supports the work of the Scottish Executive, the all-party group on quality of life, local authorities and other stakeholders to improve the quality of life in Scotland’s communities, notably the allocation to local authorities of £95 million this year and a further £180 million for the next three years to improve the local environment and the quality of people’s daily lives.

Supported by: Peter Peacock*

*S1M-3542 Robin Harper: British Energy Finances—That the Parliament notes the decision at the extraordinary general meeting of British Energy in East Kilbride on 4 November 2002 to approve the borrowing of up to £1.6 billion; further notes that the £650 million loan from Her Majesty’s Government to the company has been declared unlawful by the EU Competition Commissioner Mario Monti; is aware that British Energy has requested that local authorities defer payment of business rates for its nuclear power stations; is further aware that Lancaster City Council has deferred the business rates of Heysham Nuclear Power Station at an estimated cost to the taxpayer of £18,000 and a possible cost of up to £1.7 million if British Energy is eventually called into receivership, and calls on the Scottish Executive to state whether any local authority has deferred payment of business rates of any British Energy installations and, if so, at what actual and possible cost to taxpayers.

*S1M-3541 Allan Wilson: Forestry in Scotland—That the Parliament notes the report of the Interdepartmental Review Group on Forestry; welcomes the proposed changes to the administrative arrangements for forestry in Scotland contained in that report; notes the proposals by an industry-wide steering group for a new Scottish Forestry Grants Scheme, and believes these changes will help achieve the objectives set out in the Scottish Forestry Strategy.

Supported by: Ross Finnie*

*S1M-3540# Ms Sandra White: Exploitation of Women—That the Parliament notes with concern the increasing proliferation of lap-dancing clubs and other establishments of a similar nature throughout Scotland; further notes that the US-based firm Spearmint Rhino, reported to be linked with pornographic websites featuring the drugging, abduction and rape of women, has plans to expand its lap-dancing operations into Scotland; believes that these establishments encourage the exploitation of women and have no place in a modern Scotland seeking to promote equality and eradicate violence against women, and therefore considers that the Scottish Executive should monitor this situation closely and take whatever action is necessary to halt the spread of these establishments.

*S1M-3539 Alex Neil: Immediate Establishment of Public Appointments Committee—That the Parliament calls upon the Parliamentary Bureau to consider and propose the establishment of a Parliamentary Public Appointments Committee initially to oversee the current Office of the Commissioner for Public Appointment as its work relates to Scottish Executive appointments pending the establishment of a Scottish Commissioner for Public Appointments, after which the remit of the committee shall be to carry out interviews and recommend an individual for the post of Scottish Commissioner for Public Appointments once the Public Appointments and Public Bodies etc. (Scotland) Bill has been passed; scrutinise the work of the Scottish Commissioner and, specifically, the Commissioner’s annual report; take appropriate action when the Commissioner reports a breach in the Code of Conduct for making public appointments; ask the commissioner for guidance on particular appointments; and interview all major appointees to non-departmental public bodies on their appointment to discuss the policy and priorities of that body.

*S1M-3538# John Farquhar Munro: Affordable Rural Housing—That the Parliament notes the shortfall in affordable housing in rural areas for rent or for purchase particularly for young families; acknowledges that, in part, the cause of this can be traced to second home ownership; believes that in order to maintain a sustainable future for rural Scotland measures must be taken to encourage young people and families to remain in their communities, and considers that the Parliament should review the provision of socially rented housing as well as the extent of second home ownership throughout rural Scotland.

*S1M-3537 Donald Gorrie: Tourism Information for People with Disabilities—That the Parliament urges VisitScotland to respond to the report by Capability Scotland An Open or Shut Case? - How Scotland meets the needs of disabled tourists by taking action to remedy the defect which the report identifies in the system for informing people with disabilities about the many facilities which are suitable for them.

*S1M-3536.1 Michael Russell: Flexibility and Innovation in Schools—As an amendment to motion (S1M-3536) in the name of Cathy Jamieson, insert at end "but regrets that the Executive seems unable to take on board ideas which will make a real difference in the medium and long term such as the need for a substantial lowering in class sizes, particularly in the early years of primary, the simplification of the Scottish exam system, the review of 5-14 with the aim of creating more flexibility and less pressure, the building and development of a collective and collegiate view of school management as envisaged in the McCrone agreement (which should also involve parents and young people), and other major topics, but instead seeks to apply spin and hype to a First Minister’s vision of education that is lacking in vision and depth and does not constructively address the main issue of providing the best education possible for every child in Scotland."

*S1M-3536.2 Tommy Sheridan: Flexibility and Innovation in Schools—As an amendment to motion (S1M-3536) in the name of Cathy Jamieson, insert at end "and, towards concretely achieving this aim, agrees to deliver a maximum primary class size of 20 within 12 months through intensive training, innovative recruitment and necessary investment taking inspiration from the Republic of Cuba which has delivered such a target in a similar timescale with much scarcer resources, and further agrees to implement a similar maximum class size of 20 in the secondary school sector within 24 months as a central plank of a fundamentally improved package of state school funding and investment."

*S1M-3536.3 Mr Brian Monteith: Flexibility and Innovation in Schools—As an amendment to motion (S1M-3536) in the name of Cathy Jamieson, insert at end "and believes that greater flexibility should include giving new opportunities for children to study a wider range of courses, including those at further education colleges, from the age of 14."
