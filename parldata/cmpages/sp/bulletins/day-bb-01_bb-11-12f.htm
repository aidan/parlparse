Business Bulletin No. 163/2001

Monday 12 November 2001

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions.

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-2434 Christine Grahame: Prioritising the Borders Economy—That the Parliament notes the report of 7 November 2001 by the Director of Lifelong Care at Scottish Borders Council on the impact in the area of the foot-and-mouth disease outbreak and, in particular, paragraph 12.1, Economic Impact, where it is estimated that the losses to the Borders economy over the coming year will be £30 million to tourism, £14 million to agriculture and related industries and £27 million to the electronics industry, representing 7% of Borders GDP; expresses concern that Signum Circuits, Selkirk, which re-employed many former Viasystems workers, has now gone into receivership with the immediate redundancy of some 49 employees and with bleak prospects for the remaining 132 employees, in an area with the lowest average earnings in Scotland and an ageing population, and calls upon the Scottish Executive, in the light of these circumstances, to prioritise the Borders as a matter of urgency for future economic investment and funding, including support for local businesses, in order to modernise the area’s infrastructure and thus halt this economic haemorrhage.

*S1M-2433 Tommy Sheridan: North Glasgow University Hospitals NHS Trust's Handling of Industrial Action—That the Parliament condemns the North Glasgow University Hospitals NHS Trust’s incompetent handling of the month-long medical secretaries’ dispute; believes that the secretaries have been forced into industrial action by low pay, an unfair grading system and an intransigent attitude on the part of the Trust’s management; further believes that the secretaries were forced to resume all-out strike action when the Trust reneged on the agreement negotiated between staff and management on 24 October 2001, and calls on the Scottish Executive to bring pressure to bear on the Trust to honour that agreement.

*S1M-2432 Tommy Sheridan: Benefits Agency Industrial Action—That the Parliament supports the current industrial action being taken by members of the PCS at Benefits Agency offices throughout Scotland in pursuit of their demand for the retention of safety screens in the new Jobcentre Plus offices; finds it unacceptable that Benefits Agency workers should be exposed to increased physical attacks from members of the public without adequate protection; believes that these workers on the front line of government services should be able to expect that their concerns are addressed promptly and in full, and recognises that, until these workers are adequately resourced to deliver realistic benefits and compassionate services, they will require the retention of these screens.

*S1M-2431# Mr David Davidson: Diabetes in Scotland—That the Parliament recognises that diabetes is a serious condition affecting over 220,000 people and their families across Scotland and that complications arising from it can have a devastating effect on individuals, including a loss of sight, chronic heart disease and limb amputation; notes that this figure could double over the next decade and that the number of people with undiagnosed diabetes is estimated to be over 90,000, a figure which is unacceptable, and believes that it is vital that the Scottish Diabetes Framework, once published, should become the vehicle for producing short, medium and long-term solutions to this public health challenge.
