Business Bulletin No. 98 / 2004

Wednesday 9th June 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-1442 Cathy Peattie: Big Fit Walk-That the Parliament congratulates Grangemouth Health Promotion Group and Integrated Learning Community on its organisation of the Big Fit Walk on 11 June 2004; notes that the object is to raise awareness of the health benefits of physical activity by encouraging as many people as possible to take a 30-minute walk and that at least 3,000 people in Grangemouth are expected to do so on 11 June; further notes that this event builds upon other innovative local initiatives such as the GP Exercise Referral Scheme, and hopes that in future years there will be Scotland-wide Big Fit Walks.

*S2M-1441# Mark Ballard: Urgent Action for the Dove Centre, Edinburgh-That the Parliament notes the excellent work of the Dove Centre in Edinburgh in providing a community transport service in West Edinburgh, providing a service forum for older people, promoting the West Edinburgh Canal Society and helping to achieve a City for All Ages; further notes the decision of the Edinburgh Capital City Partnership to award the Dove Centre £32,000 social inclusion funding; further notes the Community Planning Partnership's recommendation that the funding arrangements for the Dove Centre be reconsidered in light of the centre's promotion of social inclusion for older people and especially community transport focused on older people; believes that this reconsideration is a matter of urgency given the imminent departure of the co-ordinator and the end of transport provision for vulnerable older people due to lack of funds, and considers that all concerned agencies should help guarantee the future of this project.

*S2M-1440 Murdo Fraser: 80th Anniversary of Gleneagles Hotel-That the Parliament congratulates Gleneagles Hotel on its 80th anniversary; wishes it continued success as being one of the world's most prestigious hotels, and looks forward to it continuing to welcome royalty, movie legends and sporting greats from throughout the world to Perthshire so that they can appreciate the local hospitality and Perthshire's natural beauty and heritage.

*S2M-1439 Patricia Ferguson on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Suspension of Standing Orders-That the Parliament agrees that Rule 5.6.1(c) of the Standing Orders be suspended for the duration of the Meeting of the Parliament on Thursday 17 June 2004.

Supported by: Tavish Scott*

*S2M-1438 Murdo Fraser: England Euro 2004 Success-That the Parliament wishes England every success in the European Championships 2004 in Portugal; hopes that the England fans follow the example of Scotland's Tartan Army in being the friendliest fans in world football, and wishes Portugal a highly successful tournament.

*S2M-1437 Patricia Ferguson on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Change of Decision Time-That the Parliament agrees under Rule 11.2.4 of the Standing Orders that Decision Time on Thursday 17 June 2004 shall begin at 5.30pm.

Supported by: Tavish Scott*

*S2M-1436 Mr Mark Ruskell: UCI Mountain Bike World Cup-That the Parliament congratulates the organisers and supporters of the Fort William round of the 2004 UCI Mountain Bike World Cup; further congratulates the BBC on providing televised coverage of the event; welcomes the growing popularity of mountain biking with visitors to Scotland and the bid to bring the World Championships here in 2007, and notes the promotional role of cycle sport as part of a wider cycle culture in Scotland that can help to deliver goals of public health improvement and sustainable transport.

*S2M-1435# Frances Curran: Glasgow Central College - Failure to Reinstate-That the Parliament notes with concern the failure of the board of management of the Central College of Commerce in Glasgow to reinstate Mr Jim O'Donovan, the National President of the Further Education Lecturers' Association of the Educational Institute of Scotland, within the requisite timescale laid down by the employment tribunal, which found he had been unfairly dismissed because of his trade union activities, and considers that he should be reinstated immediately.

Supported by: Carolyn Leckie*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*

*S2M-1434 Margaret Mitchell: Trans-bus Consortium Buy-Out-That the Parliament congratulates the consortium of Scottish business leaders for safeguarding 900 jobs in Camelon and Larbert by rescuing the Falkirk bus building firm, Alexander Dennis, from receivership and for securing a new contract worth £7.5 million to deliver 50 new double-decker buses and wishes the firm well in all its future negotiations, whilst noting that the rejuvenation of this firm is especially welcome against the background of a Scottish manufacturing slump.

*S2M-1432# Bill Butler: Blairdardie Boys Football Club-That the Parliament congratulates Blairdardie Boys Football Club on their recent triumph in winning the Summer Cup Final at Glasgow Green; recognises the achievements of the club over the last three years in gaining promotion twice; hopes that the players and staff of Blairdardie Boys Football Club enjoy their trip to Hampden Park later this month to be presented with the cup and their medals, and thanks the manager, Brian Graham, and all of the other volunteers who help run the club and provide football opportunities for young people in the Blairdardie area of Glasgow.

*S2M-1431# Chris Ballance: Education for Peace Across Scotland-That the Parliament commends the work of the World Peace Prayer Society at its European headquarters in Dumfriesshire, particularly in peace education in schools; recognises the valuable contribution that principles of non-violence and co-operation can make to society and supports the development of skills in conflict resolution, and believes that the Scottish Executive should therefore consider a cross-sectoral review of various peace initiatives, not just in education, but in justice, health and other areas, liaising with COSLA, NHS boards, religious groups, voluntary organisations and other relevant bodies in order to bring forward an integrated strategy for encouraging the peaceful resolution of disputes in all fields and promoting education for peace.

Supported by: Mark Ballard*, Patrick Harvie*, Robin Harper*, Eleanor Scott*, Mr Mark Ruskell*, Shiona Baird*, Frances Curran*

*S2M-1430 Mr Stewart Maxwell: Support Scottish Cycling-That the Parliament observes that British Cycling has reinstated the Tour of Britain road stage race, after a five-year hiatus, from 1 to 5 September 2004; notes that 16 teams of the world's elite cyclists are taking part; further notes that this event has the support of the BBC with all the coverage that this entails both within the United Kingdom and throughout the world; sees great advantages for tourism for all areas included in this road race and notes that Richard Caborn, UK Minister of State for Sport and Tourism, said that this event can promote tourism in "the whole country"; notes however that this "Tour of Britain" involves the north-west of England, Yorkshire, the East Midlands and South Wales, finishing on the streets of Westminster, but does not include Scotland, and urges British Cycling to apologise for the omission of Scotland from this event and to ensure that as long as Scotland is still a member of the United Kingdom it is fully represented in any future Tour of Britain events.

*S2M-1425 Mark Ballard: Oil War and Climate Change-That the Parliament welcomes the conference, "Oil, War and Climate Change", which took place in Edinburgh on Saturday 29 May 2004; acknowledges that Tony Blair has said that climate change is the most important environmental issue facing the world today and that Her Majesty's Government's Chief Scientific Adviser, Sir David King, describes climate change as a bigger threat than global terrorism; believes that the crisis in Iraq is symptomatic of a greater battle for control of scarce resources, and calls on the Scottish Executive to step up its measures to tackle climate change by using its influence to promote environmental justice, expressing the will of the Scottish people on reserved issues including defence and energy, setting binding emissions reduction targets for Scotland and taking action to reduce Scotland's greenhouse gas emissions by cutting airport expansion and road building, investing in public transport, supporting environmentally-sustainable renewable energy developments and working towards energy efficient housing in Scotland.

Supported by: Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Eleanor Scott*, Robin Harper*
