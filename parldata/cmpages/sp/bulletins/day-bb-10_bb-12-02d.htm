Business Bulletin No. 179/2010: Thursday 2 December 2010
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D – Oral questions selected for answer on 9 December 2010

Question Time

General

1. John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive what help it will give rural NHS dentists to help them comply with the Glennie report on decontamination. (S3O-12323)

2. Mr Frank McAveety: To ask the Scottish Executive what the reduction in full-time equivalent teachers in schools has been in the last year. (S3O-12273)

3. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with representatives of NHS Lothian regarding waiting lists at the Western General Hospital of Edinburgh. (S3O-12261)

4. Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met representatives of Angus Council and what issues were discussed. (S3O-12244)

5. Ian McKee: To ask the Scottish Executive what support or encouragement it offers to schools or individual school classes to develop links with schools in other countries. (S3O-12309)

6. Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has for investment for trunk roads in the South of Scotland. (S3O-12320)

7. Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration it will give to the A76 five-point action plan that was launched in November 2010. (S3O-12282)

8. Not Lodged

9. Not Lodged

10. Elizabeth Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made to improve road safety on the A977. (S3O-12243)

Justice and Law Officers

1. Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of reported comments by the Chief Constable of Grampian Police that a single national police force "threatens to endanger everything we are doing in the north-east", what plans it has to centralise policing in Scotland. (S3O-12327)

2. Alasdair Allan: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is regarding the proposal for the final court of appeal in both civil and criminal cases to be a Scottish court rather than the UK Supreme Court. (S3O-12293)

3. Christina McKelvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on human trafficking. (S3O-12310)

4. Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment has been made of the total cost to the emergency services from hoax calls received in each year since 2007. (S3O-12242)

5. Tricia Marwick: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken to reduce crime in Fife. (S3O-12305)

6. Not Lodged

7. Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will commit to a comprehensive human rights-based approach to securing effective access to justice, effective remedies and reparation for survivors of childhood abuse, including those victims of historic abuse whose cases are prevented from being brought before the courts by prescription or time bar. (S3O-12280)

8. Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider using Friarton Hall to increase prison capacity in Scotland. (S3O-12241)

9. Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it has made in tackling antisocial behaviour. (S3O-12281)

10. John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people on community service orders have been drafted in to help with snow clearing. (S3O-12247)

Rural Affairs and the Environment

1. Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to promote food and agricultural co-operatives in Scotland. (S3O-12258)

2. Joe FitzPatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made towards making Scotland a zero waste society. (S3O-12299)

3. Not Lodged

4. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive what special assistance to farmers is in place or is planned to reinstate water meadows and plant trees as part of the roll-out of the River Basin Management Plans. (S3O-12334)

5. Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many applications in the Clyde area were received for the last round of funding from the Rural Priorities scheme and how many were successful. (S3O-12264)

6. Maureen Watt: To ask the Scottish Executive what it is doing to encourage greater use of locally grown produce by businesses. (S3O-12314)

7. Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive what it is doing to encourage the promotion and marketing of local food including meat from native breeds and produce from integrated farming systems. (S3O-12250)

8. Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to promote high standards of food production in Scotland. (S3O-12283)

9. Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions the Cabinet Secretary for Rural Affairs and the Environment has had regarding incentive schemes that would enable local authorities to make use of local farmers to alleviate the impact of adverse weather conditions on rural communities. (S3O-12331)

10. Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the recently announced increase in the Climate Challenge Fund, or any future similar funding, will be available to groups who have temporary or short-term leasing. (S3O-12251)
