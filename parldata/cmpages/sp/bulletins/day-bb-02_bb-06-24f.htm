Business Bulletin No. 104/2002

Monday 24 June 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-3243 Mr David Davidson: Scottish Men's Lacrosse Team-That the Parliament wishes every success to the Scottish Men's Lacrosse Team at the World Cup Competition in Perth, Australia; notes that the team has received no support from sportscotland in achieving this, and recommends that men's lacrosse in Scotland has a separate association from women's lacrosse.

*S1M-3242 Michael Russell: Scottish Place Names Survey-That the Parliament commends the excellent work undertaken by the Scottish Place Names Survey and by Ian Fraser who retired in Autumn 2001; notes that the discipline of place name studies is of great benefit to a nation, particularly one that has three indigenous languages and whose place names are influenced by no less than six languages, more than in any other European country; accepts that state funding for the discipline is the norm in most European countries and that place name studies contribute valuable assistance to national and local government and to individuals in matters such as sourcing derivations of place names, suggesting appropriate names for new developments, and preserving our cultural and linguistic heritage; agrees with the Principal of the University of Edinburgh that state funding could be expected for the survey while regretting that continued funding by the University is no longer possible, and calls upon the Scottish Executive to provide appropriate financial assistance to the Scottish Place Names Survey to allow it to proceed with the next phase of the Scottish Place Names Database and offer a service to enquirers.

Supported by: Mr George Reid*, Richard Lochhead*

S1M-3241 Mr Keith Harding: Dog Fouling (Scotland) Bill - Stage 1-That the Parliament agrees to the general principles of the Dog Fouling (Scotland) Bill.

S1M-3240# Donald Gorrie: Health in Developing Countries-That the Parliament encourages academic researchers and drug companies in Scotland to give a higher priority to developing medicines which will promote better health and nutrition in developing countries and considers that the Scottish Executive and Her Majesty's Government should introduce inducements to progress along these lines.

S1M-3239 Brian Adam: Aberdeen Council Housing Stock-That the Parliament notes that over the last four years more than £3 million of public money and tenants' rents has been spent by Aberdeen City Council in investigating stock transfer; further notes that after only a few months the tenants' representatives on the Council Housing Options Working Group (CHOWG) rejected wholesale stock transfer and repeated that rejection in the recent survey, and calls on Aberdeen City Council to endorse the views of the tenants without any further cost to the rents such as an expensive ballot.

S1M-3238 David Mundell: Congratulations to Gretna Football Club-That the Parliament congratulates Gretna Football Club on its election to the Third Division of the Scottish Football League; recognises the importance of football in all parts of Scotland; welcomes the fact that the inclusion of Gretna Football Club continues the trend of the football league towards a wider geographic spread of member clubs, and wishes the team, its manager, supporters and everybody else associated with the club well in their endeavours in the new and future seasons.

Supported by: Mr Murray Tosh, Mr Kenneth Gibson, Mr Michael McMahon, Alex Fergusson, Phil Gallie, Mr Keith Harding, Murdo Fraser, David McLetchie, Mr Adam Ingram, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton, Brian Fitzpatrick, John Scott, Mary Scanlon, David Mundell*

S1M-3237 Mr Jim Wallace: Police Reform Bill - UK Legislation-That the Parliament endorses the principle that sex offenders orders made in one jurisdiction within the United Kingdom should be recognised and enforceable throughout the UK with appropriate provisions in place concerning how the orders are applied for, amended and discharged, and agrees that the relevant provisions to achieve these ends should be considered by the UK Parliament in the Police Reform Bill.

Supported by: Dr Richard Simpson

S1M-3236 Ross Finnie: Water Environment and Water Services (Scotland) Bill - Stage 1-That the Parliament agrees to the general principles of the Water Environment and Water Services (Scotland) Bill.

Supported by: Mr Andy Kerr, Iain Gray, Allan Wilson

S1M-3227 Mr Andy Kerr: Local Government in Scotland Bill - Financial Resolution-That the Parliament, for the purposes of any Act of the Scottish Parliament resulting from the Local Government in Scotland Bill, agrees to any increase in expenditure payable out of the Scottish Consolidated Fund in consequence of the Act.

Supported by: Peter Peacock

S1M-3226 Christine Grahame: Legal Aid in Peeblesshire-That the Parliament notes with concern that there is no local access to legal aid in Tweeddale, the last legal aid practitioner having recently left practice in Peebles; is concerned that this, combined with the current closure of Peebles Sheriff Court and uncertainty over its future, must prejudice access to justice by the people of Peeblesshire, and therefore urges the Scottish Executive to address these deficiencies as a matter of urgency.

Supported by: Michael Matheson

S1M-3225 Des McNulty on behalf of the Finance Committee: 3rd Report of the Finance Committee 2002-That the Parliament notes the 3rd Report 2002 of the Finance Committee, Stage 1 of the 2003/04 Budget Process (SP Paper 597) and refers the recommendations to the Scottish Executive for consideration.

S1M-3224 Mr Andy Kerr: Public Appointments and Public Bodies etc. (Scotland) Bill - Stage 1-That the Parliament agrees to the general principles of the Public Appointments and Public Bodies etc. (Scotland) Bill.

Supported by: Peter Peacock

S1M-3222# Richard Lochhead: The Press and Journal's Oil Tax Campaign-That the Parliament welcomes the campaign by The Press and Journal against the 10% tax imposed by Her Majesty's Government on oil and gas profits; agrees that the tax and the way in which it was introduced pose a threat to jobs throughout Scotland, especially in the north east, as well as to the considerable efforts that were underway to prolong the life of the North Sea province, and believes that the campaign deserves cross-party support and the express support of the Scottish Executive.

Supported by: Colin Campbell, Mr Kenny MacAskill, Irene McGugan, Mr Kenneth Gibson, Stewart Stevenson, Roseanna Cunningham, Bruce Crawford, Michael Matheson, Brian Adam, Mr David Davidson, Shona Robison, Phil Gallie, Alex Johnstone

S1M-3217 Dalmellington Area Centre Project Management Team and the 2002 Prize for Public Management Leadership (lodged on 13 June 2002) Angus MacKay*, Ms Wendy Alexander*

S1M-3209 Iain Smith: University of St Andrews (Postgraduate Medical Degrees) Bill - Stage 3-That the Parliament agrees that the University of St Andrews (Postgraduate Medical Degrees) Bill be passed.

S1M-3207# Christine Grahame: Peebles Sheriff Court-*That the Parliament notes with concern the threat of the permanent closure of Peebles Sheriff Court; further notes that the Minister for Justice is currently considering a report into the expenditure required to make the courthouse fully operational and intends to make his conclusion public soon; further notes that the retention of the sheriff court at Peebles is supported by, inter alia, the Sheriff Principal of Lothian and Borders, the two sheriffs who sit there, the Society of Solicitors in the Shires of Selkirk and Peebles and the Faculty of Solicitors in Roxburghshire.*

Supported by: Michael Matheson, Mr Adam Ingram, Mr Lloyd Quinan, Mr Gil Paterson, Mr Murray Tosh, Roseanna Cunningham, Mr Kenneth Gibson, Mr Kenny MacAskill, Ms Sandra White, Brian Adam, Fiona Hyslop

S1M-3185# Scotland's Industrial Museums (lodged on 6 June 2002) Mr John McAllion*, Mr Keith Harding*, Ms Wendy Alexander*

S1M-3173# Trish Godman: Loan Sharks-That the Parliament commends the excellent initiative of the Daily Record to name and shame the "loan sharks" who prey on vulnerable people living in poverty throughout Scotland; agrees with the newspaper that Scotland must not be a country where it is a crime to be poor and where loan sharks obtain huge profits out of other people's precarious financial circumstances, and is of the firm view that the Parliament, in close partnership with the UK Parliament, the police and other appropriate public agencies, must take decisive action to drive these predators out of their sordid "business" in the interests of their victims and victims' families.

Supported by: Colin Campbell, Phil Gallie, Karen Gillon, Mr Kenneth Macintosh, Mr John McAllion, Johann Lamont, Mr Michael McMahon, Brian Fitzpatrick, Angus MacKay, Dr Sylvia Jackson, Robert Brown, Pauline McNeill, Karen Whitefield, Mr Kenneth Gibson, Bristow Muldoon, Jackie Baillie, Elaine Smith, Shona Robison, Mr John Home Robertson, Helen Eadie, Mr Tom McCabe, Margaret Jamieson, Marilyn Livingstone, Elaine Thomson, Paul Martin, Irene Oldfather, Sarah Boyack, Scott Barrie, Nora Radcliffe, Cathie Craigie, Kate Maclean, John Young, Donald Gorrie, Bill Butler
