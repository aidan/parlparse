Business Bulletin No. 98/2010: Thursday 10 June 2010

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 16 and 17 June 2010

16 June

Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body Question Time

1. John Wilson: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body how it ensures that open and transparent processes are utilised when hiring staff. (S3O-11095)

2. David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body how many committee meetings were video conferenced in the last 12 months. (S3O-11094)

3. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body whether it is considering adopting open standards and fair-use licensing in relation to video and audio output from the chamber and committees. (S3O-11096)

4. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body how many of its employees earn more than the First Minister’s combined salary. (S3O-11092)

5. Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body what its position is on reports that the Members’ Restaurant will be closed down. (S3O-11093)

17 June

Question Time

General

1. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures will be included in its carers strategy to ensure that local authority budget cuts do not impact negatively on carers. (S3O-10948)

2. Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what additional support it will provide to children with life-threatening and life-limiting conditions to ensure that their homes are provided with all necessary adaptations and that appropriate care packages are put in place to allow them to remain securely and comfortably at home with their families for as long as possible. (S3O-10973)

3. Alison McInnes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that Scottish businesses have been put at a competitive disadvantage to their English counterparts by the decision not to introduce a business rates transitional relief scheme. (S3O-10989)

4. Not Lodged

5. Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the costs of international flights from Glasgow compared with those from London. (S3O-11014)

6. Maureen Watt: To ask the Scottish Executive how much it estimates has been invested in renewable energy and supporting infrastructure in the north east under the current administration. (S3O-11022)

7. Christina McKelvie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will implement the United Nations Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities. (S3O-11017)

8. Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive when it anticipates that work will commence on improvements at the Haudagain roundabout in Aberdeen. (S3O-10946)

9. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how it ensures that trunk road works, including land use development proposals that impact on trunk roads, give due consideration to the needs of cyclists. (S3O-10942)

10. Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is content that the New Ways waiting times system is working satisfactorily. (S3O-10980)

Europe, External Affairs and Culture

1. Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent discussions it has had with representatives of Scottish publishers. (S3O-10930)

2. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the provision of local news on commercial television. (S3O-10972)

3. Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to encourage collaboration between Scotland and India. (S3O-11008)

4. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to support talented young people who wish to achieve excellence in dance or musical theatre. (S3O-10991)

5. Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the Government of Malawi about the treatment of LGBT citizens and what support it is giving to human rights and equality organisations working to tackle homophobia and transphobia in Malawi. (S3O-10994)

6. Bill Kidd: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to enhance the cultural links between Scotland and the EUA8 nations, from where many of the migrant workers who contribute to the Scottish economy and society come. (S3O-11011)

7. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to develop relations with Taiwan. (S3O-10998)

8. Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what work has been carried out in the last 12 months in improving the delivery of its China Plan. (S3O-10939)

9. Joe FitzPatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive what representation it has made to the Israeli Government following the Israeli boarding of the flotilla delivering aid to Gaza. (S3O-11006)

10. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it will have with the UK Government regarding regional television news. (S3O-10965)

Education and Lifelong Learning

1. Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure pupil safety in schools. (S3O-10947)

2. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take to improve the employment prospects of newly qualified teachers. (S3O-10983)

3. Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to ensure the early identification of children and young people with dyslexia. (S3O-10938)

4. George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will examine ways to make higher education establishments more accountable for their expenditure. (S3O-10955)

5. Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment ministers have made of the Commissioner for Children and Young People in Scotland’s and the Scottish Institute for Residential Childcare’s Go Outdoors guidelines. (S3O-10932)

6. Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the future of Stow College is adversely affected by the development of the new Glasgow Metropolitan College. (S3O-10985)

7. Derek Brownlee: To ask the Scottish Executive how the school building programme aims to maximise the number of schools benefiting from improved facilities. (S3O-10935)

8. David Whitton: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with representatives of East Dunbartonshire Council regarding capital provision to build new primary schools. (S3O-10984)

9. Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will support the provision of resources in the Scots language as part of the Curriculum for Excellence. (S3O-11005)

10. Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive which of the 35 new schools announced on 2 June 2010 will be directly funded by the Scottish Futures Trust. (S3O-10949)
