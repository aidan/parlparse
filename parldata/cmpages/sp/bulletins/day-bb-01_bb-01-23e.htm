BUSINESS BULLETIN 13 / 2001

Tuesday 23 January 2001

Section E - Written questions lodged on 22 January 2001

 

 

 

S1W-12675 Kay Ullrich: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will issue instructions or make representations to all relevant bodies to investigate the source and cause of the low frequency noise around Largs, known locally as the "Largs Hum", and whether it has held any discussions with North Ayrshire Council on this matter.

S1W-12676 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive what response it will make to the statements by Glasgow City Council in a paper to its Policy and Resources Committee on 16 January 2001 and the Convention of Scottish Local Authorities in the December 2000 issue of CoSLA Forward regarding the number of the poorest citizens in Glasgow and of the poorest households in Scotland who will be ineligible for assistance with water charges under its new proposals.

S1W-12677 Mr John Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive why sportscotland has turned down the Camanach Association’s development plan proposals.

S1W-12678 Mr Alex Salmond: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects payments to be made under the 2000-01 Countryside Premium Scheme.

S1W-12691 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to revise Police (CC) Circular No. 3/1990, Investigations of Complaints of Domestic Assault.

S1W-12692 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many cases of domestic assault were prosecuted through Sheriff Courts in (i) 1998-99 and (ii) 1999-2000.

S1W-12693 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many cases of domestic assault were brought before Sheriff Courts between 1992 and 1997.

S1W-12695 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to introduce specialised domestic violence courts.

S1W-12696 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the current average length of time is from charges being brought against an offender of domestic assault to the date of verdict.

S1W-12697 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure that cases of domestic assault are dealt with as quickly as possible through the criminal justice system.

S1W-12698 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many cases of domestic assault resulted in a guilty disposition between February 1998 and February 2000.

S1W-12699 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what types of evidence the police take into account when seeking corroborative evidence in cases of domestic assault.

S1W-12700 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many cases of domestic assault were dropped by the Procurators Fiscal because of a lack of evidence in each year since 1997.

S1W-12701 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many charges of domestic assault there were in each year between 1990 and 2000.

S1W-12702 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many times on average women are assaulted before they contact the police.

S1W-12703 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of all victims of sexual assault were children, in each year since 1993.

S1W-12704 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what estimates exist on what proportion of women with disabilities will be sexually assaulted at some point during their life.

S1W-12705 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what mechanisms are in place to enable national information sharing on domestic violence issues between it, non-governmental organisations, front-line workers and academics.

S1W-12706 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what research it is currently carrying out or planning to carry out on issues surrounding domestic violence.

S1W-12707 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to complete a national survey to measure the extent of domestic violence across Scotland.

S1W-12708 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to fund programmes aimed at reducing violence against people with disabilities and against people from ethnic minorities.

S1W-12709 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive how much funding it is planning to devote to research surrounding domestic violence issues in each of the next five years.

S1W-12710 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many treatment programmes for perpetrators of domestic abuse it is currently funding.

S1W-12711 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many cases of serious assault of women there were in each year from 1993 to 1999 inclusive.

S1W-12712 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many cases of spousal homicide there were from 1978 to 1999 inclusive.

S1W-12713 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what research into public awareness and perceptions of domestic violence it or the Scottish Office have carried out over the past 10 years.

S1W-12714 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to carry out research into attitudes and perceptions of domestic violence before, during and after the development of the National Strategy to Address Domestic Abuse in Scotland.

S1W-12715 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what structures are in place to ensure that its departments co-ordinate efforts to reduce the incidence of domestic violence.

S1W-12716 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the latest estimated annual economic cost is of domestic violence in Scotland.

S1W-12717 David McLetchie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether, following his resignation on 1 December 2000, Mr Ron Smith, the former Chief Executive of the Common Services Agency of the National Health Service in Scotland, has received or is to receive any termination payment relative to the termination of his contract of employment over and above normal entitlements to salary, holiday pay and related matters accrued to that date.

S1W-12718 David McLetchie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any claim for compensation in respect of the termination of the contract of employment for Mr Ron Smith, the former Chief Executive of the Common Services Agency of the National Health Service in Scotland, has been initiated by him or on his behalf.
