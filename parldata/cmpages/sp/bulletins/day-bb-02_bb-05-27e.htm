Business Bulletin No. 87/2002

Monday 27 May 2002

Section E : Written questions lodged on 24 May 2002
  

Questions marked with a triangle () are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S1W-26294 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it or the Scottish Road Safety Campaign has any plans for a publicity campaign on the use of mobile telephones by drivers whilst driving and whether it will make representations to the Department of Transport, Local Government and the Regions in support of change in the law on this matter.

S1W-26295 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what consultations it has had with, and representations it has had from, Glasgow Airport and the appropriate air traffic control authority in regard to the proposed wind farm at Whitelees and what the outcome was of any such consultations and representations.

S1W-26296 Mrs Lyndsay McIntosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what cost-benefit work has been carried out in relation to mortgage rescue schemes.

S1W-26297 Mrs Lyndsay McIntosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what internal appeals procedure, in the event of a disputed assessment by a lender, exists under the mortgage to rent scheme.

S1W-26298 Mrs Lyndsay McIntosh: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any guidance from sheriffs was sought when formulating the policy for its mortgage to rent scheme and, if so, what this guidance was.

S1W-26299 Mrs Lyndsay McIntosh: To ask the Scottish Executive how many mortgage rescue schemes are currently in place.

S1W-26300 Mrs Lyndsay McIntosh: To ask the Scottish Executive whether compliance with the mortgage to rent scheme is compulsory for lenders.

S1W-26301 Mrs Lyndsay McIntosh: To ask the Scottish Executive how the mortgage to rent scheme interacts with sheriff courts.

S1W-26302 Mrs Lyndsay McIntosh: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people are currently assisted by mortgage rescue schemes and how many it is anticipated will be under the mortgage to rent scheme.

S1W-26303 Mrs Lyndsay McIntosh: To ask the Scottish Executive how the mortgage to rent scheme will deal with a defaulting debtor who is party to a shared ownership scheme.

S1W-26304 Mrs Lyndsay McIntosh: To ask the Scottish Executive who will meet the costs of any independent repairs assessment and legal fees incurred by the borrower in relation to the mortgage to rent scheme.

S1W-26305 Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to protect peatlands.

S1W-26306 Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive what proportion of peatlands is used for commercial peat extraction.

S1W-26307 Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to encourage the production and use of alternatives to peat.

S1W-26308 Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in investigating potential fraud in the Individual Learning Accounts scheme.

S1W-26309 Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive what assistance is available to pensioners living in fuel poverty who are permanently resident in a mobile home.

S1W-26310 Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to establish a centre for the care of young chronically sick people in the Lothians.

S1W-26311 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will detail the circumstances when the advice of a special adviser would be sought.

S1W-26312 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether special advisors are able to delay the issuing of answers to parliamentary questions.

S1W-26313 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any guidance has been issued to special advisers on providing advice on answers to parliamentary questions.

S1W-26314 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive when the basis upon which metered water charges are calculated was formulated.

S1W-26315 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to review the basis upon which metered water charges are calculated.

S1W-26316 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will review the surface water drainage charges made to commercial premises with metered water.

S1W-26317 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to harmonise the basis upon which domestic and commercial surface water drainage charges are calculated for metered users.

S1W-26318 John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive whether groundwater contamination could occur as a result of genetically modified crop trials.

S1W-26319 Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) adolescents and (b) adults diagnosed with autism or Asperger’s syndrome have also been diagnosed with depression in each of the last five years.

S1W-26320 Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive what research it has funded in each of the last five years into depression and whether it will place a copy of any such research in the Scottish Parliament Information Centre.

S1W-26321 Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will offer assistance to the parents and carers of those diagnosed with autism or Asperger’s syndrome towards the costs of training packages offered by the Scottish Society for Autism.

S1W-26322 Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive what initiatives are in place regarding supported further education places for adolescents and adults diagnosed with autism or Asperger’s syndrome, broken down by local authority area.

S1W-26323 Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to reduce the risk of mental health illness in adolescents diagnosed with autism and Asperger’s syndrome.

S1W-26324 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive what recommendations regarding eating disorders from the Framework for Mental Health Services have been implemented in each NHS board area since the framework’s publication.

S1W-26325 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people died as a result of anorexia nervosa in each of the last 10 years.

S1W-26326 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive what initiatives are being implemented nationally to address the number of people suffering from anorexia nervosa.

S1W-26327 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive which psychiatric illness currently has the highest death rate.

S1W-26328 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people have been diagnosed with an eating disorder in each year since 1999, broken down by (a) NHS board area, (b) age and (c) gender.

S1W-26329 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive how many in-patient beds are available in each NHS board area for the treatment of anorexia nervosa and other eating disorders.

S1W-26330 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive what community support facilities are available in each NHS board area for those discharged from hospital.

S1W-26331 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive what specialist eating disorder units have closed in each year since 1999 and for what reason.

S1W-26332 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive what specialist training is in place for (a) general practitioners, (b) community nurses, (c) teachers, (d) social workers and (e) psychiatrists in identifying, diagnosing and treating anorexia nervosa.

S1W-26333 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive what differences in the way that Scotland implemented last year’s Programme for International Student Assessment caused the Office for National Statistics to advise that "any comparisons with Scotland should be made with caution".

S1W-26334 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive what schools participated in last year’s Programme for International Student Assessment.

S1W-26335 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive what meetings it has had with relevant organisations to take forward the Larkhall to Milngavie rail extension.

S1W-26336 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made on the Larkhall to Milngavie rail link.

S1W-26337 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects work to begin on the Larkhall to Milngavie rail link.

S1W-26338 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average waiting time is for a routine ophthalmology appointment (a) nationally and (b) in the Lanarkshire NHS Board area.

S1W-26339 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to reduce youth crime in Clydesdale.

S1W-26340 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to improve consultation in the planning process.

S1W-26341 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many children in (a) Clydesdale and (b) South Lanarkshire have benefited from the Sure Start programme.

S1W-26342 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to support the development of new racecourses.

S1W-26343 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to support the development of all weather racecourses.

S1W-26344 Tricia Marwick: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to review the current procedures to require appointees to public bodies to declare any party political membership.

S1W-26345 Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive what weighting is given to poverty and health factors when calculating local authority central funding allocations.

S1W-26346 Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to regenerate deprived areas.

S1W-26347 Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it holds on the number of affordable homes built in the last 12 months.

S1W-26348 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive how the regional planning of services will impact on neurosciences in the Greater Glasgow NHS Board area.

S1W-26349 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many patients from outside the Greater Glasgow NHS Board area are treated within the board area.

S1W-26350 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what neurosurgical services are currently available in the (a) west and (b) east of Scotland and (c) nationally.

S1W-26351 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in the development of a plan for the funding of neurosciences.

S1W-26352 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what additional funding is available to conduct nerve conduction tests in the west of Scotland.

S1W-26353 Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to review the area tourist board network.
