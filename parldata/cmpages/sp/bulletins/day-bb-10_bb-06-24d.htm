Business Bulletin No. 108/2010: Thursday 24 June 2010
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 1 July 2010

Question Time

General

1. Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has identified priorities for bolstering sustainable growth from any funds from the fossil fuel levy that are released. (S3O-11134)

2. Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it supports the introduction of a living wage by local authorities this year. (S3O-11153)

3. Peter Peacock: To ask the Scottish Executive what it considers the impact will be on Scotland of the VAT increase announced by the UK Government. (S3O-11187)

4. John Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met the Scottish Housing Regulator and what issues were discussed. (S3O-11152)

5. Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding and other resources it has allocated for the teaching of Chinese language, culture and history in schools, colleges and universities in the last two years and whether it is taking action to increase educational, economic and other contacts with China. (S3O-11149)

6. Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will assist Dundee City Council and other local authorities comply with their socio-economic duties and other responsibilities when many individuals are facing redundancy. (S3O-11166)

7. Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive how it envisages the regional growth fund announced in the UK Budget operating in Scotland. (S3O-11150)

8. Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met representatives of the Scottish Ambulance Service and what issues were discussed. (S3O-11168)

9. Jamie Hepburn: To ask the Scottish Executive how it expects the privatisation of various public services proposed by the UK Government to impact on Scotland. (S3O-11136)

10. Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive when it estimates work will start on the construction of the Aberdeen Western Peripheral Route and in what year it now expects the project to be completed. (S3O-11106)

Rural Affairs and the Environment

1. Elaine Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken, or are planned, to progress the designation of nature conservation Marine Protected Areas. (S3O-11190)

2. Stuart McMillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what issues were discussed at its most recent meeting with ministers from the Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs. (S3O-11144)

3. Malcolm Chisholm: To ask the Scottish Executive what statements concerning biomass plants have been made recently by the Cabinet Secretary for Rural Affairs and the Environment and the Minister for the Environment. (S3O-11160)

4. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what the outcome was of the discussions between its officials and those of the European Commission on 9 June 2010 regarding proportionality in the penalty system for farmers found to be in breach of EU support scheme rules. (S3O-11119)

5. Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive how its zero waste strategy will be financed. (S3O-11184)

6. Jim Tolson: To ask the Scottish Executive what issues were discussed, and conclusions reached, at the meeting with the ministerial team of the Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs on 23 June 2010. (S3O-11123)

7. Christopher Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what means it has to monitor and question approvals by local authorities for building projects in environmentally sensitive or designated natural scenic areas that threaten rural landscapes. (S3O-11135)

8. David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met representatives of Moray Council to discuss issues regarding rural affairs. (S3O-11191)

9. Not Lodged

10. Not Lodged

Justice and Law Officers

1. John Park: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prosecutions have taken place in Scotland under the Corporate Manslaughter and Corporate Homicide Act 2007. (S3O-11186)

2. Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that the use of Tasers by police officers in the execution of their duties is legal. (S3O-11163)

3. Ian McKee: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will respond to the claim contained in the recent Joseph Rowntree Foundation-sponsored paper by Professor Neil McKeganey of the University of Glasgow that local drug-sellers could have a positive effect on a community by spending cash locally, deterring other crime and making available to the community, by drug users’ theft, a supply of consumer products at reduced prices. (S3O-11142)

4. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many antisocial behaviour orders were issued by courts in 2006-07, 2007-08 and 2008-09. (S3O-11162)

5. Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the report by Professor Neil McKeganey suggesting that markets from drug dealing could be seen as having a positive impact on the surrounding community. (S3O-11133)

6. David Whitton: To ask the Scottish Executive what it is doing to help local authorities tackle antisocial behaviour. (S3O-11193)

7. Not Lodged

8. Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how far it sets policy with regard to the use of Tasers by police forces. (S3O-11110)

9. Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to reduce the incidence of antisocial behaviour in South Lanarkshire. (S3O-11165)

10. Mr Frank McAveety: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to combat antisocial behaviour in Glasgow. (S3O-11175)
