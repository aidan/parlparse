Business Bulletin No. 127 / 2003

Friday 26th September 2003

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-415 Tommy Sheridan: Water Charging Schemes—That the Parliament endorses the recommendation of the Water Customer Consultation Panel’s report Principles of Charging for Water and Wastewater that "the current household charge reduction scheme for low income households should be extended beyond March 2004" and believes that such a scheme should be funded accordingly and extended with a view to expanding its scope to assist many more of those living in poverty.

*S2M-414 Mr Adam Ingram: The Sun Newspaper and Frank Bruno—That the Parliament condemns the ignorant and stigmatising headline by The Sun on Tuesday 23 September 2003, which reported on the ex-boxer Frank Bruno who was sectioned under the Mental Health Act 1983 in England; deplores the damage that this kind of extreme and ill-judged use of words can do both to those suffering from mental illness and to anti-stigma campaigns, and calls upon The Sun to extend its redress beyond the current appeal campaign to provide a platform for long running anti-stigma campaigns such as the "See Me" campaign.

*S2M-413 Mr Andy Kerr: The Draft Local Government in Scotland Act 2003 (Ancillary Provisions) Order 2003—That the Local Government and Transport Committee recommends that the draft Local Government in Scotland Act 2003 (Ancillary Provisions) Order 2003 be approved.

Supported by: Tavish Scott*

*S2M-412 Hugh Henry: The Draft Title Conditions (Scotland) Act 2003 (Consequential Provisions) Order 2003—That the Justice 2 Committee recommends that the draft Title Conditions (Scotland) Act 2003 (Consequential Provisions) Order 2003 be approved.

Supported by: Cathy Jamieson*

*S2M-411 Cathy Peattie on behalf of the Equal Opportunities Committee: Equal Opportunities Committee, Mainstreaming Equal Opportunities in the Work of Committees of the Scottish Parliament—That the Parliament notes the recommendations contained in the Equal Opportunities Committee’s 1st Report 2003: Mainstreaming Equal Opportunities in the Work of Committees of the Scottish Parliament (SP Paper 817).
