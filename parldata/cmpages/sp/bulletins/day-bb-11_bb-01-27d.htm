Business Bulletin No. 14/2011: Thursday 27 January 2011
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 3 February 2011

Question Time

General

1. Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans there are to secure a specialist MS nurse for the Western Isles. (S3O-12872)

2. Andy Kerr: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations have been made to the UK Government regarding the postponement of the minimum broadband speed target of two megabits per second from 2012 to 2015. (S3O-12875)

3. Charlie Gordon: To ask the Scottish Executive what additional costs are consequential on the recent introduction of a new liquid de-icer for use in winter road maintenance. (S3O-12871)

4. Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to reduce levels of domestic violence in light of the 4,784 incidents recorded in December 2010. (S3O-12870)

5. Shirley-Anne Somerville: To ask the Scottish Executive when it plans to review whether large-scale biomass for electricity plants should qualify for support under the renewables obligation scheme. (S3O-12849)

6. Christina McKelvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what benefits it considers will accrue from the completion of the Edinburgh-Glasgow M8 motorway. (S3O-12846)

7. Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the impact on the viability of rural abattoirs of the Food Standards Agency’s proposed new charging regime for meat hygiene and welfare controls. (S3O-12888)

8. Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had regarding the proposal to close the Cowal Hospice at Dunoon Hospital. (S3O-12824)

9. Cathie Craigie: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with Transport Scotland regarding compensation for those impacted by the A80 upgrade. (S3O-12865)

10. Nigel Don: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to reduce levels of underage drinking. (S3O-12832)

Education and Lifelong Learning

1. Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that pupil support assistants make a valued contribution to children’s learning. (S3O-12881)

2. Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is supporting the provision of further education. (S3O-12884)

3. Tom McCabe: To ask the Scottish Executive what reassurance it can give that maintenance of core places in higher and further education will also fully maintain the diversity and volume of courses available to students. (S3O-12880)

4. Mr Frank McAveety: To ask the Scottish Executive what resources it has made available to fund school estate investment in Glasgow since 2007. (S3O-12879)

5. Not Lodged

6. Dave Thompson: To ask the Scottish Executive what capital expenditure provision it has made for education for 2011-12. (S3O-12850)

7. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what its response is to the NUS Scotland Budget for Bursaries campaign. (S3O-12898)

8. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what the planned growth in student numbers in further education is for 2010-11. (S3O-12825)

9. Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive what the legal minimum class contact time with teachers is for primary pupils. (S3O-12873)

10. Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is aware of the French approach to training for trades as represented by the organisation, Les Compagnons Du Devoir. (S3O-12834)

Europe, External Affairs and Culture

1. Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in developing facilities for the Antonine Wall since it was awarded World Heritage Site status. (S3O-12891)

2. David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Executive how links are being strengthened between Scotland and Malawi. (S3O-12894)

3. Not Lodged

4. Not Lodged

5. Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects the first residencies under the artists’ residency programme for young artists to be offered by Creative Scotland. (S3O-12857)

6. Bill Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the economic impact is of traditional cultural events such as Celtic Connections. (S3O-12854)

7. Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions the Minister for Culture and External Affairs has been involved in regarding how its purchasing decisions support fair trade for farmers and workers in the developing world. (S3O-12908)

8. Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has for implementing its China Plan in the next 12 months. (S3O-12826)

9. Not Lodged

10. Not Lodged
