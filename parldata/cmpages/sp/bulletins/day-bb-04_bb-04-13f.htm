Business Bulletin No. 63 / 2004

Tuesday 13th April 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-1159# Johann Lamont: Rosehill Housing Co-operative-That the Parliament notes the excellent Communities Scotland Pathfinder Inspection Report secured by Rosehill Housing Co-operative in the Glasgow Pollok parliamentary constituency; acknowledges the huge efforts of the staff and tenants at Rosehill in creating excellent socially-rented housing in the community and supporting broader community initiatives; welcomes the commitment of the Scottish Executive to the greater involvement of tenants in developing community housing plans, and believes that the Scottish Executive should consider further how it can best support the further development of fully mutual housing co-operatives.

*S2M-1158 Marlyn Glen: Yellow Flag Seaside Award for Broughty Ferry-That the Parliament notes the award of the prestigious Yellow Flag Seaside Award for Broughty Ferry beach for the first time; congratulates the people of Broughty Ferry and the members of the community council on their work towards this achievement; acknowledges the contribution made by Dundee City Council and other local organisations in this award, and recognises the role played by investment in wastewater treatment made possible by the Tay Wastewater project.

*S2M-1157 Scott Barrie: Congratulations to Torryburn Primary School-That the Parliament notes the excellent follow-up report by HM Inspectorate of Education on Torryburn Primary School, published on 21 March 2004; acknowledges the hard work of the staff, pupils, parents and school board since the first inspection in spring 2002; notes the involvement of both Newmills and Torryburn Community Council and High Valleyfield Community Council in this process; pays particular tribute to the work of the new head teacher who took up post at the beginning of this academic year, and congratulates all concerned and wishes the current staff and pupils every success for the future.

*S2M-1156# Bill Butler: Siting of New Transport Agency in Glasgow-That the Parliament supports Glasgow as the location for the headquarters of the proposed Scottish transport agency, Transport Scotland; recognises the employment benefits that locating the agency in Glasgow would bring to the city; notes the significance of Glasgow to Scotland's rail and road transport networks; acknowledges the transport expertise currently within the city, which includes the existing staff in the headquarters of Strathclyde Passenger Transport and in Glasgow City Council, which currently runs the National Driver Information and Control System (NADICS) network on behalf of the Scottish Executive, and believes that the Executive should give serious consideration to Glasgow as an ideal location for Transport Scotland.

*S2M-1155 David Mundell: Launch of the Gretna-Lockerbie-Annan Economic Regeneration Prospectus-That the Parliament welcomes the launch of the prospectus published by the Corridor Regeneration Strategy Steering Group outlining key objectives and a number of themes to stimulate economic growth in Gretna, Lockerbie and Annan to address the impact on the area of the cessation of power production at Chapelcross in March 2005; notes that the regeneration strategy, with its four principle themes of wealth, diversity, inspiration and inclusion, recognises not only the challenges that the Gretna-Lockerbie-Annan corridor now faces, but also the opportunities to be seized; congratulates the steering group on its genuine joined-up and cross-party working to date, and looks to the Scottish Executive to provide all the support it can to the steering group's work and to the regeneration of the Gretna-Lockerbie-Annan corridor.

*S2M-1154 Pauline McNeill: Kenny Richey, a Scot on Death Row-That the Parliament notes that Kenny Richey, a British citizen who grew up in Edinburgh, has spent 16 years on death row in the State of Ohio, despite being described by Amnesty International as a "compelling case of apparent innocence"; notes that following the case having been heard at the sixth Circuit Court of Appeals on 7 May 2003 a final decision is expected imminently on whether he should face execution; notes that important forensic and eye witness evidence has come to light since the original verdict was reached which casts serious doubt on its safety; notes, however, that this new evidence has not been heard by a court of law, and considers that the First Minister should urge the UK Government to make direct and robust representations to the state authorities in Ohio on behalf of Kenny Richey.

Supported by: Colin Fox*

*S2M-1153 Donald Gorrie: Support for the Scots Language-That the Parliament urges the Scottish Executive to set up a board to promote the use and study of the Scots language similar to the Ulster-Scots Agency, set up under the Good Friday Agreement, which advises ministers and co-ordinates activities relating to the promotion of Ulster-Scots as a living language and to respond to international criticism such as "That Scots language in Scotland is very clearly under-funded, under-researched, under-maintained compared to Gaelic, Irish or Ulster Scots" by providing greater support for the Scots language.
