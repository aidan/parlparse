Business Bulletin No. 173/2004

Thursday 25 November 2004

Section D : Oral questions selected for answer on 2 December 2004
  

Question Time

Education and Young People, Tourism, Culture and Sport

1. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent discussions it has had about reducing class sizes. (S2O-4313)

2. Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to support the teaching of foreign languages in primary schools. (S2O-4355)

3. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what further action it will take to make the primary and secondary school modern language programme more successful. (S2O-4318)

4. Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive whether tourism would be boosted by the establishment of statutory marine national parks. (S2O-4407)

5. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what its plans are for VisitScotland’s marketing strategy. (S2O-4334)

6. Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive what place in the curriculum is given to the teaching of works of Scottish poets, such as Robert Burns. (S2O-4346)

7. Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive what specific proposals it has to increase the number of tourists visiting mid-Scotland and Fife. (S2O-4310)

8. Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to support the tourist industry so that it grows by 50% by 2015. (S2O-4339)

9. Chris Ballance: To ask the Scottish Executive who it consulted over the siting of the National Theatre. (S2O-4402)

10. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive how many schools teach Cantonese. (S2O-4354)

11. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on the reorganisation of VisitScotland and area tourist boards. (S2O-4298)

12. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive how tourism will benefit from the Biodiversity Grants Action Scheme. (S2O-4405)

Finance and Public Services and Communities

1. Mark Ballard: To ask the Scottish Executive when the independent body reviewing local government finance will meet to consider the benefits of land value taxation. (S2O-4391)

2. Janis Hughes: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding assistance is offered to organisations involved in the prevention of domestic abuse. (S2O-4397)

3. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it has taken to sustain and invigorate Borders communities. (S2O-4408)

4. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is a good use of public resources for local authorities to compete in the private housing market for homes rather than build new local authority-owned properties to meet their obligations under homelessness legislation. (S2O-4305)

5. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is encouraging the development of one-stop shops for local authority services. (S2O-4356)

6. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to receive a progress report on the work of the independent review into local government finance. (S2O-4296)

7. Christine May: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to improve housing support services. (S2O-4375)

8. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to reduce fuel poverty. (S2O-4369)

9. Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to bring civil service jobs to the Borders. (S2O-4293)

10. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding is made available for public awareness campaigns on the issues of domestic violence and violence against women. (S2O-4396)

11. Mr Mark Ruskell: To ask the Scottish Executive what the main public interest criteria are in respect of planning appeals. (S2O-4393)

12. Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive what flexibility individual local authorities will have in determining how their Supporting People grant allocation will be used. (S2O-4415)

General

1. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to change the funding formula for health services in the Grampian area in the next three years. (S2O-4306)

2. Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to improve road safety. (S2O-4387)

3. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the UK Government regarding plans to alter legislation on possession which would introduce an offence of having drugs in the bloodstream. (S2O-4401)

4. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive when the review of strategic road projects will begin in order to ensure that gaps in the network are closed and that bypasses for those communities seeking them are given consideration. (S2O-4379)

5. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to reduce racially-motivated attacks. (S2O-4317)

6. Mrs Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will release further information on the reopening of the Airdrie to Bathgate rail link. (S2O-4329)

7. George Lyon: To ask the Scottish Executive how much extra funding Argyll and Bute Council will receive in respect of roads. (S2O-4361)

8. Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average waiting time is for access to NHS dental services. (S2O-4341)

9. John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to address the shortage of organs for donation. (S2O-4344)

10. Mr John Swinney: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to raise awareness of MRSA. (S2O-4327)

11. Chris Ballance: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to announce its funding for the Borders rail line. (S2O-4410)

12. Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive when it intends to make a final decision on the future of fire control rooms. (S2O-4312)

13. Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive when enhanced measures to improve the environment through Common Agricultural Policy reform will take effect. (S2O-4385)

14. Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on the Inverurie to Stonehaven Crossrail project. (S2O-4389)

15. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it is providing for small businesses to enable them to compete more effectively with large businesses for contracts, for example local authority contracts. (S2O-4319)
