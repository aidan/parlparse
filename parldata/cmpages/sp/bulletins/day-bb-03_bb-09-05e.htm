Business Bulletin No. 112 / 2003

Friday 5th September 2003

Section E : Written questions lodged on Thursday 4th September 2003
  

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-2365 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has made, or will make, to the Royal Mail regarding the economic impact of its proposed closure of sub-post offices in Glasgow.

S2W-2366 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to establish specific vocational training for care workers in the elderly sector.

S2W-2367 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to increase the number of consultants working at the Golden Jubilee National Hospital, Clydebank and, if so, from where these consultants will be drawn.

S2W-2368 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive from which NHS boards consultants are being drawn to work in the Golden Jubilee National Hospital, Clydebank.

S2W-2369 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) full-time and (b) part-time consultants are working in the Golden Jubilee National Hospital, Clydebank.

S2W-2370 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive how many consultants working in the Golden Jubilee National Hospital, Clydebank were drawn from hospitals in the Glasgow NHS Board Area.

S2W-2371 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to ensure that waiting times do not increase at those hospitals affected by consultants and other health service staff moving to work at the Golden Jubilee National Hospital, Clydebank.

S2W-2372 Miss Annabel Goldie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to launch a nationwide campaign to raise awareness of endometriosis.

S2W-2373 Frances Curran: To ask the Presiding Officer when the website of the Scottish Parliament and Business Exchange will be re-established.

S2W-2374 Frances Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding has been allocated to electricity generating companies for the past year for research and development into renewable energy sources.

S2W-2375 Frances Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive which electricity generating companies have received funding over the last year for research and development into renewable energy.

S2W-2376 Frances Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria are used to determine funding levels allocated to electricity generating companies for research and development into renewable energy.

S2W-2377 Rosie Kane: To ask the Scottish Executive what the total publication costs of its consultation documents have been for each of the last four years.

S2W-2378 John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-21643 by Ross Finnie on 23 January 2002, what method will be used to establish baseline figures in respect of the amount of controlled waste collected by, or for, local authorities for the purposes of meeting its obligations under the EU Landfill Directive (1999/31/EC) and when it will publish the 1995 baseline tonnage figures for each local authority.

S2W-2379 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has made, or is making, any representations to Her Majesty's Government regarding the impact of the aggregates tax in Scotland, on whether Scotland and Northern Ireland are treated in the same way in respect of the tax and exemptions to it, and in support of a level playing field between Scotland and Northern Ireland on this matter.

S2W-2380 Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive how many crawler lanes there are on the trunk road network; what distance each such lane covers, and what the location is of each such lane.

S2W-2381 Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive how many overtaking lanes there are on the trunk road network; what distance each such lane covers, and what the location is of each such lane.

S2W-2383 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding it has provided for credit unions in each financial year since 1997-98 and what further funding it will provide in 2003-04, 2004-05 and 2005-06.

S2W-2384 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether its analysis of the 148 responses to its consultation on the draft Education (Additional Support for Learning) (Scotland) Bill will be published when the bill is introduced.

S2W-2385 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will publish those responses to its consultation on the draft Education (Additional Support for Learning) (Scotland) Bill that indicate that the complete removal of the Record of Needs will be beneficial for children with special educational needs.

S2W-2386 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people were killed by lightning strikes in each of the last 10 years.

S2W-2387 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance it has issued, or plans to issue, on how to avoid lightning strikes and what the reasons are for its position on this matter.

S2W-2388 Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-2058 by Peter Peacock on 3 September 2003, how the statement that the timetable for submission by Her Majesty’s Government of the next periodic report of the UN Convention on the rights of the child is by 14 March 2008 is consistent with the statement in For Scotland’s Children that Her Majesty’s Government is committed to the submission of a report to the UN Committee on the Rights of the Child (UNCRC) every five years, whether it will prepare a Scottish chapter for inclusion in such a report, and whether the chapter will include its views about the detention of children in Dungavel House immigration removal centre in relation to the UNCRC.

S2W-2389 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to reduce the incidence of Japanese knotweed.

S2W-2390 Miss Annabel Goldie: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-1189 by Peter Peacock on 17 July 2003, when it expects to find out how Inverclyde Council intends to address the issues with regard to the school estate in Inverclyde.

S2W-2391 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any television programmes have been sponsored by the General Register Office for Scotland and, if so, which programmes have been sponsored and how much money has been spent on such sponsorship.

S2W-2392 Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to address any problems of access to dental provision encountered by people living in remote areas.

S2W-2393 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive when and where it expects the first regional advisory council proposed under the recent Common Fisheries Policy review to be established; what form it will take, and what its impact will be on the Scottish fishing industry.

S2W-2394 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it was represented at a conference hosted by the Italian presidency of the European Union on the issue of a preventative approach to healthcare and the promotion of healthy lifestyles scheduled for 3 to 4 September 2003 and, if so, by whom.

S2W-2395 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will mark the European Year of Education through Sport in 2004 and, if so, how.

S2W-2396 David McLetchie: To ask the Scottish Executive in what order of priority topics will be considered by the Sentencing Commission to be chaired by Lord MacLean, and over what timescale such topics will be considered.

S2W-2397 Alex Johnstone: To ask the Presiding Officer how much it cost the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body to produce and show the film of Sir Sean Connery’s visit to the new Scottish Parliament building site and on whose authority funding was provided to produce and show the film.

S2W-2398 Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive what revenue it expects to be raised by Scottish Water in Dumfries and Galloway in each of the next five years from (a) domestic customers and (b) business customers and how much it expects to invest in the water infrastructure of Dumfries and Galloway in each of these years.

S2W-2399 Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive what revenue was raised by Scottish Water and the former West of Scotland Water in Dumfries and Galloway for each of the last five years from (a) domestic customers and (b) business customers and how much was invested in the water infrastructure in Dumfries and Galloway in each of these years.

S2W-2401 Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many consultant vacancies there are in each speciality in the NHS.

S2W-2402 Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive what proportion of consultant posts in the NHS are vacant, broken down by speciality.
