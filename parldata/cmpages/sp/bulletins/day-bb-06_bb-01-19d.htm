Business Bulletin No.9/2006: Thursday 19 January 2006
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 26 January 2006

Question Time

General

1. Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has developed a timetable with the Scottish Environment Protection Agency and local authorities for the implementation of the Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment Directive and, if so, what the timetable is.  (S2O-8799)

2. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will change the procedures for flood management schemes in light of future estimates in respect of the incidence of flooding.  (S2O-8793)

3. Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive, following the announcement that waiting times targets are being met, what new measures it will put in place to reduce the longest waiting times for hospital treatment, particularly for out-patient appointments. (S2O-8803)

4. Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether all eligible people, in particular those in North Lanarkshire, have received the new entitlement card which will be required to access free Scotland-wide concessionary travel from 1 April 2006. (S2O-8811)

5. Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive how, in the event of the new payment rates inducing a higher uptake from new entrants to the Organic Aid Scheme, the Executive will decide its priorities within the rural development budget.  (S2O-8833)

6. Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide an update on its review of prescription charge exemptions for chronic conditions such as asthma. (S2O-8820)

7. Tricia Marwick: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there have been any further developments in its plans to publish the Tolled Bridges Review and when it will make a decision on whether to support the Forth Estuary Transport Authority’s proposal for a £4 toll on the Forth Road Bridge. (S2O-8836)

8. Dr Jean Turner: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to introduce a national health programme to educate the public on the risk of spreading infection by spitting and discarding chewing gum in our streets and other public places.  (S2O-8790)

9. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are in place to help reduce the number of people killed or seriously injured on roads. (S2O-8805)

10. Chris Ballance: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will have any input into the contribution of the UK Government to the Council of Europe inquiry into allegations of secret detention and flights and, if so, what that input will be. (S2O-8830)

Finance and Public Services and Communities

1. Derek Brownlee: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making in delivering public service reform.  (S2O-8755)

2. Mark Ruskell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to reduce community rights of appeal through its proposed reform of the planning system. (S2O-8832)

3. Jim Mather: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact the reduction by 98% of the planned spend on the Efficient Government Fund in 2005-06 will have on the achievement of Efficient Government targets and where the residual £200,000 will be allocated. (S2O-8772)

4. Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will make its Regeneration Statement. (S2O-8812)

5. John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive how its proposals for planning reform will ensure that the public interest is protected in respect of maintaining the balance between out-of-town shopping centres and traditional town centre and village shops. (S2O-8779)

6. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration it has given to changing the format of public planning inquiries by giving Reporters an inquisitorial role and excluding paid Advocates. (S2O-8778)

7. Mr Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met the leader of Argyll and Bute Council. (S2O-8766)

8. Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will meet its target of completing all parts of its central heating programme by March 2006.  (S2O-8786)

9. Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with South Ayrshire Council regarding its projected financial deficit for 2006-07 and the implications this has for council tax payers. (S2O-8826)

10. Susan Deacon: To ask the Scottish Executive what proportion of public sector jobs it will relocate outwith Edinburgh.  (S2O-8796)    (S2O-8796)

Education and Young People, Tourism, Culture and Sport

1. Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive whether its strategy to improve Scotland’s sporting achievements is directed towards achieving successful performances at the European Championships rather than the Commonwealth Games.  (S2O-8759)

2. Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with VisitScotland in respect of promoting Ayrshire as a tourist destination. (S2O-8819)

3. Mr Ted Brocklebank: To ask the Scottish Executive what implications the ministerial statement on the Cultural Commission will have for the arts in Fife. (S2O-8765)

4. Bill Aitken: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to ensure that weekly physical education targets for schoolchildren in Glasgow are met.  (S2O-8760)

5. Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether major events in the Scottish artistic calendar such as Celtic Connections can be assured of development funding from the Executive in order to build an innovative, artistic and financially secure future.  (S2O-8787)

6. Mr Alasdair Morrison: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to enhance the status of vocational education. (S2O-8815)

7. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to improve child protection.  (S2O-8806)

8. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take to implement the recommendations of the recent report, Mainstreaming Pupils with Special Educational Needs.  (S2O-8761)

9. Not Lodged

10. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what services are available to young vulnerable children failing to perform in school and achieve their potential.  (S2O-8788)  (S2O-8788)
