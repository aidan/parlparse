Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (♦).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S3M-4303 Kenneth Gibson: Congratulations to Kilbirnie Ladeside FC—That the Parliament congratulates Kilbirnie Ladeside Football Club, manager Tommy Bryce, the players, fans and everyone connected with the club on gaining promotion to the Stagecoach Super League Premier Division after a six-year absence; commends the efforts of the team for its magnificent achievement; wishes The Blasties the very best in future campaigns, and looks forward to the club’s continuing contribution to a strong and successful future for Scottish junior football.

*S3M-4302 Maureen Watt: Cross-party Opposition to UK Government Budget Cuts—That the Parliament notes the concern expressed by Aberdeen City Council in a letter dated 29 May 2009 to Aberdeen MPs and MSPs about the impact of the UK Government’s planned £500 million cut to the Scottish budget; further notes the cross-party opposition that has emerged in Aberdeen to the UK Government’s cuts to make such a letter possible, and calls on all parties to follow this example by joining together to oppose plans to cut £500 million from the Scottish budget at a time when it would do the most harm.

*S3M-4301 Bruce Crawford on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Designation of Lead Committee—That the Parliament agrees that the Finance Committee be designated as lead committee and that the Education, Lifelong Learning and Culture Committee, the Health and Sport Committee and the Rural Affairs and Environment Committee be designated as secondary committees in consideration of the Public Services Reform (Scotland) Bill at Stage 1.

*S3M-4300 Bruce Crawford on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Approval of SSI—That the Parliament agrees that the draft Waste Batteries (Scotland) Regulations 2009 be approved.

*S3M-4299 Jamie Hepburn: Anniversaries of Cumbernauld Old Parish Church and Kildrum Parish Church—That the Parliament congratulates Cumbernauld Old Parish Church on celebrating its 350th anniversary this year; notes that this is a reminder of the long history of Cumbernauld, where there has been a worshipping community since the 13th century; further congratulates Kildrum Parish Church, Cumbernauld, on celebrating its 50th anniversary this year; further notes that this is a reminder of the rejuvenation of Cumbernauld as a new town in the 1950s, and wishes the congregations at both churches well for these significant milestones in the life of their churches.

*S3M-4297 Bruce Crawford on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Business Motion—That the Parliament agrees that, for the purposes of Members’ Business on Thursday 11 June 2009, "at the end of First Minister’s Question Time and at the end of the meeting following Decision Time" be substituted for "at the end of the meeting following Decision Time" in Rule 5.6.1(c) of Standing Orders.

*S3M-4296 Robin Harper: Inspiring Volunteers of the Year 2009—That the Parliament welcomes the announcement by Volunteer Centre Edinburgh of its Inspiring Volunteering Awards 2009 as part of Volunteers’ Week 2009; congratulates Joan Alexander, a citizen advocate for Partners in Advocacy, for being named as Edinburgh’s Inspiring Volunteer of the Year 2009; recognises the hard work and dedication of those people who freely give up their time to contribute to society through volunteering; acknowledges that almost one third of Edinburgh residents have given their time without pay to a charitable or voluntary organisation or club in the last year, and recognises the positive difference that volunteers make to the city of Edinburgh and its people.

*S3M-4295 Jamie Hepburn: Low Bids Undermine Royal Mail Privatisation Plan—That the Parliament notes that only two companies have made offers for a 30% stake in the Royal Mail under the UK Government’s plans to part-privatise the service and that what is understood to be the higher of these bids is of just under £2 billion, far less than the UK Government had hoped to raise; believes that this lack of interest shows that the proposals are unworkable and fatally undermines the UK Government’s plans, and calls for the abandonment of these proposals and for the Royal Mail to remain wholly within the public sector as the Labour Party stated as its ambition in its 2005 manifesto.

Supported by: Joe FitzPatrick*, Bill Kidd*, Kenneth Gibson*, Bob Doris*, Aileen Campbell*, Stuart McMillan*, Bill Wilson*

*S3M-4294 Sarah Boyack: 64 for Aung San Suu Kyi—That the Parliament recognises Nobel Peace Prize winner Aung San Suu Kyi’s role as an important symbol of hope for democracy in Burma; takes the opportunity of her 64th birthday on 19 June 2009 to commend her for her longstanding bravery and inspirational defence of human rights and democratic freedoms against the country’s repressive military regime, and supports the initiative, involving human rights groups such as Amnesty International and Human Rights Watch, calling on people to write 64-word messages of support for Aung San Suu Kyi to mark her 64th birthday.

*S3M-4293 Shirley-Anne Somerville: Edinburgh Diversity Cup—That the Parliament welcomes the Edinburgh Diversity Cup five-a-side football tournament on Saturday 20 June 2009; notes that this will be the third annual cup hosted by the Edinburgh Inter-Faith Association; recognises that the tournament’s aims are to celebrate the diversity of the city’s population and bring more people together through sport; further notes that all the money raised from the event will go to the Hope into Homes charity, which will be building much-needed houses in Malawi in August 2009, and urges teams to visit www.diversitycup.com and enter the competition, have fun and support a worthy cause.

*S3M-4292 Helen Eadie: Scottish Government Fails Fife’s Children at Risk—That the Parliament is gravely concerned that senior Fife Council officials have been judged, in a report published in April 2009 by Her Majesty’s Inspectorate of Education, as leaving some children in Fife in situations of risk or without adequate support; considers that council leaders have failed to put in place systematic scrutiny, at policy and financial management level, of all matters pertaining to child protection policy and management in Fife; expresses profound concern that inspectors were not confident that every child needing help to keep them safe from abuse and neglect had been properly identified, assessed and protected; observes that key developments such as multi-agency screening of children for whom there was concern were not taken forward; recalls the First Minister’s statement that this sort of neglect was unacceptable when senior Aberdeen City Council officials were severely censured for the same reasons last autumn; demands that the Cabinet Secretary for Education and Lifelong Learning, Fiona Hyslop MSP, meets Helen Eadie MSP urgently to discuss the case of a child who has been in need of child protection services; is outraged that Fiona Hyslop has, after almost six weeks, not yet called an emergency meeting with senior officials and politicians in Fife to ensure that urgent and vitally important steps are taken immediately to ensure the protection of all of Fife’s children, and considers that this illustrates complacency on her part.

*S3M-4291 Alison McInnes: Peacock Visual Arts’s 35th Anniversary—That the Parliament congratulates Peacock Visual Arts on reaching its 35th anniversary; notes the outstanding work that Peacock does in the arts in Aberdeen and across the north east, including working to develop the skills of artists, helping young people get involved with new media and film-making and hosting an extensive exhibition programme as well as running community projects; acknowledges the funding and support provided by the Scottish Arts Council, Scottish Screen and Aberdeen City Council, and hopes that all partners will continue to work together to ensure that Peacock can go from strength to strength and continue delivering its outstanding programme for the next 35 years and beyond.

*S3M-4290 Stuart McMillan: RNIB Work Focus Success—That the Parliament congratulates the RNIB on the success of the first year of the Work Focus pilot, started in June 2008; notes that the pilot is running in four areas to evaluate the best methods of practice in supporting blind and visually impaired people to access employment opportunities; further notes that unemployment levels among people with sight loss have remained at a high of 75% to 80% over the last 15 years with no significant reduction; commends the RNIB and Grampian Society for the Blind on their work to date on assisting people with visual impairment to gain employment; appreciates the benefits of gainful employment to an individual’s mental and physical wellbeing, making it even more important for visually impaired individuals to have the support necessary to enhance their employability; also notes the work being done by RNIB in this project to encourage employers to consider employing visually impaired individuals, and wishes the pilot well in the final year and for future success in similar projects.

Supported by: Kenneth Gibson*, Nanette Milne*, Ross Finnie*, Liam McArthur*, Bill Kidd*, Mike Pringle*, Dr Richard Simpson*, Bob Doris*, Bill Wilson*

S3M-3819 *#* Stuart McMillan: Boundary Proposals—That the Parliament notes with concern the proposed boundary change that would relocate the Princes Gate development in Bishopbriggs from the local authority area of East Dunbartonshire to the city of Glasgow; understands that residents were not aware of any such proposal when purchasing their homes; recognises the anxiety and anger of some residents who specifically chose to purchase homes in Bishopbriggs and for whom the proposal would mean the severing of community links, jeopardising their access to amenities and potentially considerable financial loss; notes also that the proposal would see the loss of a much-needed affordable housing site from East Dunbartonshire to the city of Glasgow; wishes the residents every success in working with East Dunbartonshire Council to resist the proposal, and *recognises the importance to the residents of staying within the area and maintaining their community links.*

Supported by: Stewart Maxwell*
