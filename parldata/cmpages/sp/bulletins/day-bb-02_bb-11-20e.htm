Business Bulletin No. 168/2002

Wednesday 20 November 2002

Section E : Written questions lodged on 19 November 2002
  

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S1W-31670 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average income was of people in West Dunbartonshire in (a) 1999-2000, (b) 2000-01 and (c) 2001-02 compared with the average income in (i) Scotland and (ii) Great Britain.

S1W-31671 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average income of couples (a) with and (b) without children was in West Dunbartonshire in (i) 1999-2000, (ii) 2000-01 and (iii) 2001-02 compared with the average income of such couples in (1) Scotland and (2) Great Britain.

S1W-31672 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average income of single households with children was in West Dunbartonshire in (a) 1999-2000, (b) 2000-01 and (c) 2001-02 compared with the average income of such households in (i) Scotland and (ii) Great Britain.

S1W-31673 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive who has been appointed to the Cabinet sub-committee to review issues relating to flooding.

S1W-31674 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what the cost of damage caused by river flooding has been in each year since 1997 and what estimate has been made of the average yearly cost over the next 10 years.

S1W-31675 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has made to the Association of British Insurers regarding householders and businesses that suffered from flood damage in Moray during the period 15 to 18 November 2002 to ensure that full payments are made and prevent any excessive increases in their insurance premiums.

S1W-31676 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive which witnesses the Cabinet sub-committee appointed to review issues relating to flooding will call to give evidence and whether these will include MSPs that represent areas that have experienced flooding.

S1W-31677 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria it uses to define an area as being at high risk of regular flooding.

S1W-31678 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive how many houses are currently at risk from inland or coastal flooding.

S1W-31679 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-11310 by Mr Sam Galbraith on 30 November 2000, how many applications for flood prevention and coast protection schemes have been (a) approved and (b) rejected, broken down by name of applicant, nature of project and amount sought and, where applicable, awarded; whether it will give a progress report on each successful application, detailing the likely completion date for the project, and what the reasons were for any applications being rejected.

S1W-31680 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to increase the number of new houses with solar/photovoltaic tiles.

S1W-31681 Mr Duncan Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are sufficient air links to the Highlands and Islands.

S1W-31689 Mr Keith Harding: To ask the Scottish Executive what the full costs were of publishing, printing, distributing and launching (a) Social Justice.a Scotland where everyone matters - Annual Report 2002 and (b) Social Justice.a Scotland where everyone matters - Indicators of progress: Definitions, data, baseline and trends information - Annex to the Social Justice Annual Report 2002.
