Business Bulletin No. 93 / 2004

Wednesday 2nd June 2004

Section E : Written questions lodged on Tuesday 1st June 2004
  

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-8654 Susan Deacon: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with Her Majesty's Government regarding any concerns over the impact of forthcoming changes in child tax credit arrangements for student parents in Scotland in 2004-05.

S2W-8655 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-7990 by Mr Andy Kerr on 27 May 2004, how much was spent on external consultancy in each department and agency in each of the last five years.

S2W-8656 Miss Annabel Goldie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many applications for its central heating programme have been approved and subsequently cancelled due to the applications not meeting the qualifying criteria.

S2W-8657 Miss Annabel Goldie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average number of home visits has been by (a) an engineer and (b) a surveyor prior to the approval of an application under its central heating programme.

S2W-8658 Miss Annabel Goldie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to change the regulations governing its central heating programme and, if so, whether it has any plans to allow mobile homes to qualify for the programme.

S2W-8659 Miss Annabel Goldie: To ask the Scottish Executive what procedures have been put in place to prevent people being accepted for the central heating programme and subsequently being advised that they do not meet the initial qualifying criteria.

S2W-8660 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-7453 by Mr Tom McCabe on 5 May 2004, which local access forums have representation from deaf and hard of hearing people.

S2W-8661 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance it has given to education authorities on the phasing out of subject principal teacher posts in secondary schools.

S2W-8662 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether free eye tests will be provided by every optometrist and, if so, how much the cost of each test will be to the Executive.

S2W-8663 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many patients have used the in-patient facilities at Glasgow Homeopathic Hospital in each of the last five years.

S2W-8664 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how much NHS Greater Glasgow has received from other NHS boards in each of the last five years for patients using the Centre for Integrative Care at Glasgow Homeopathic Hospital.

S2W-8665 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how much the Centre for Integrative Care at Glasgow Homeopathic Hospital has cost to run in each of the last five years.

S2W-8666 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how much NHS Greater Glasgow expects will be saved (a) in total and (b) by NHS Greater Glasgow through the closure of the Centre for Integrative Care at Glasgow Homeopathic Hospital.

S2W-8667 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what alternative use is planned by NHS Greater Glasgow of the in-patient facilities at Glasgow Homeopathic Hospital in the event of the closure of the in-patient unit and how much it will cost to adapt the facilities for such purposes.

S2W-8668 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what alternative provision it will make for patients who benefit from the Centre for Integrative Care at Glasgow Homeopathic Hospital in the event of its closure and for whom the NHS currently offers no other effective palliation or treatment.

S2W-8670 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-7832 by Cathy Jamieson on 14 May 2004, when it expects these points to be recorded and against which quarter.

S2W-8671 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-7832 by Cathy Jamieson on 14 May 2004, how the penalty sum will be imposed if the recording of these points results in the quarterly baseline total being exceeded.

S2W-8672 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-1869 by Cathy Jamieson on 9 September 2003, why the baseline total for the calculation of payment for services at HM Prison Kilmarnock has been increased.

S2W-8673 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any performance revenue deductions have been applied to payments for services at HM Prison Kilmarnock in each quarter in which the baseline totals were exceeded.

S2W-8674 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive how any HM Treasury guidelines on commercial confidentiality have been applied to Minute of Agreement between the Secretary of State for Scotland and Kilmarnock Prison Services Limited for the Design, Construction, Management and Financing of a Prison at Kilmarnock.

S2W-8675 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether HM Prison Kilmarnock uses methods for controlling incidents which differ from those used in the Scottish Prison Service and, if so, whether it will detail such differences.

S2W-8676 Jim Mather: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-8170 by Mr Andy Kerr on 1 June 2004, whether it will list all invitations received by it to Tartan Day events, showing which events were not accepted and on what grounds.

S2W-8677 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will exercise its power under section 7 of the Housing (Scotland) Act 2001 and publish regulations to establish minimum rights for homeless people living in hostels and other types of short-term accommodation.
