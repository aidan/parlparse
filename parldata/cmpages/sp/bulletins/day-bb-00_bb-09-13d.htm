BUSINESS BULLETIN 135 / 2000

Wednesday 13 September 2000

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 20 September 2000

 

 

Question Time

1. Mr John McAllion: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will raise the issue of fuel poverty at the next meeting of the Joint Ministerial Committee on poverty. (S1O-2283)

2. Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will review the funding arrangements for students of dance, drama or stage management to facilitate them studying at Scottish institutions. (S1O-2259)

3. Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance will be given to hospitals in relation to consent being obtained before the removal of human organs for research purposes. (S1O-2248)

4. Bristow Muldoon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether additional resources will be made available to the NHS for the prescription of Zyban. (S1O-2278)

5. Malcolm Chisholm: To ask the Scottish Executive what preparations the NHS is making for the forthcoming winter. (S1O-2269)

6. Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to offer financial support to owner occupiers affected by the proposed stock transfer in Cumbernauld. (S1O-2258)

7. Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Scottish Qualifications Authority has been able to complete its full programme of moderation of the internal assessment of all educational units for which it has responsibility. (S1O-2276)

8. Mr Duncan Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive how it intends to measure success in its programme of modernisation of the NHS. (S1O-2286)

9. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in implementing the proposals in Aiming for Excellence and how this progress is being communicated to the social services workforce. (S1O-2282)

10. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made of the effects of high road fuel prices on the Scottish economy. (S1O-2256)

11. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive whether Ministers were advised by Her Majesty's Inspectorate of Schools of any difficulties concerning the implementation of Higher Still within the required timescale. (S1O-2263)

12. Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to support family planning and sexual health services for young people. (S1O-2268)

13. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it intends to take to assist retired people. (S1O-2270)

14. Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in implementing the Safer Routes to School scheme. (S1O-2253)

15. Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what response it plans to make to the request from Glasgow City Council that it provide funds for the completion of the M74 Northern Extension. (S1O-2277)

16. Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to ensure that hospitals can no longer remove or retain children’s organs without parental consent. (S1O-2280)

17. Cathie Craigie: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in introducing measures which will require all new ground-based telecommunication masts to be subject to full planning controls. (S1O-2274)

18. Bill Aitken: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to reconsider its position regarding funding of the M74 extension in the light of recent representations. (S1O-2250)

19. Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has held any discussions with Forth Valley Health Board and Tayside Health Board about the future of maternity and paediatric services for people living in the Callander and Dunblane areas. (S1O-2251)

20. Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken by the Scottish Arts Council to promote the creative industries. (S1O-2271)

21. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what new measures it is taking to tackle fuel poverty. (S1O-2266)

22. Tricia Marwick: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will reconsider, in the light of the current fuel situation, the advice it gave to the shadow strategic rail authority about the franchise bids for the east coast mainline between Edinburgh and Aberdeen. (S1O-2254)

23. Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent representations it has made to the Chancellor of the Exchequer regarding the current level of fuel tax and its impact on the Scottish economy. (S1O-2252)

24. Ms Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will ensure that the aim of reducing suicides is not undermined by any shortage of resources in health boards and social work departments. (S1O-2257)

25. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to encourage the take-up of flu vaccinations. (S1O-2273)

26. Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it plans to take to assist pensioners. (S1O-2281)

27. Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it has taken to secure the future of Kvaerner Clydebank. (S1O-2261)

28. Mrs Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive what assistance is being given to organisations which support families affected by separation and divorce. (S1O-2272)

29. Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive when it intends to begin consultation on the regulation of the security industry in Scotland. (S1O-2275)

30. Dorothy-Grace Elder: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact the installation of soft drinks and confectionery vending machines in schools would have on its dental health policy and its Action Plan for Dental Services in Scotland. (S1O-2285)
