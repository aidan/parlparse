Business Bulletin No. 44/2010: Thursday 11 March 2010
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 18 March 2010

Question Time

General

1. Maureen Watt: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to recycle unused stones located on rural land for construction projects. (S3O-9957)

2. David McLetchie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide a briefing to opposition spokespersons on the implications of the judgements in cases C-197/08 Commission v France, C-198/08 Commission v Austria and C-221/08 Commission v Ireland for the legal competence of the Scottish Government’s use of mechanisms referred to in those cases. (S3O-9865)

3. Cathie Craigie: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of efficiency savings made by the public sector in 2008-09 is attributed to the sale of surplus assets and underspends due to staff vacancies. (S3O-9885)

4. Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is committed to reversing the reductions in teacher numbers that have taken place since May 2007. (S3O-9882)

5. Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive how many petrol stations in rural areas have closed in the last 10 years. (S3O-9870)

6. John Park: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide an update on the procurement processes for the Forth Replacement Crossing. (S3O-9912)

7. Aileen Campbell: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the UK Department of Energy and Climate Change about future extraction of oil and gas in Scotland. (S3O-9935)

8. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what importance in the planning system is given to using prime agricultural land for development. (S3O-9876)

9. Not Lodged

10. Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met representatives of HM Revenue and Customs to discuss the impact of the economic downturn on the Falkirk constituency. (S3O-9948)

Health and Wellbeing

1. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in providing respite care places. (S3O-9887)

2. Andy Kerr: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Cabinet Secretary for Health and Wellbeing last met representatives of NHS Lanarkshire. (S3O-9897)

3. Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on recent figures suggesting that many local authorities are reducing respite hours and what actions will be included in the carers and young carers strategy to ensure that reductions do not impact on its stated aim of achieving "rapid, significant and sustainable" improvements in carer support. (S3O-9914)

4. Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to secure employment for recently qualified nurses and midwives. (S3O-9904)

5. Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to recruit and retain junior doctors, particularly in remote and rural areas. (S3O-9893)

6. Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects a decision from NHS Orkney in relation to the application by NorsePharm Ltd to open a community pharmacy in Dounby, Orkney. (S3O-9922)

7. Nicol Stephen: To ask the Scottish Executive what it is doing to address the shortage of dentists in Aberdeen. (S3O-9930)

8. John Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what is being done to address concerns over the rate of suicide among young people in Scotland. (S3O-9873)

9. Joe FitzPatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making toward the 2010 target for 90% of adults to have all or some of their own teeth. (S3O-9941)

10. Alasdair Allan: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it will be making to the relevent agencies, including Scottish and Southern Energy, regarding repeated power failures at the Western Isles Hospital. (S3O-9934)

11. George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent discussions have taken place with local authorities regarding housing provision for asylum seekers. (S3O-9889)

12. Mr Frank McAveety: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met representatives of NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde. (S3O-9901)

13. Not Lodged

14. Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive when ministers last met NHS Lothian and what issues were discussed. (S3O-9909)

15. James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive what provisions are in place to support doctors who take time out to visit schools and teach children about the dangers of knife-related crime. (S3O-9896)

16. Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what it is doing to retain and increase the number of volunteer drivers to provide vital patient transport services in remote parts of Scotland. (S3O-9931)

17. Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussion it has had with the UK Government in relation to minimum pricing for alcohol. (S3O-9940)

18. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether adequate services are in place to assess and meet the mental health needs of asylum seekers and refugees. (S3O-9874)

19. Christina McKelvie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to increase the use of self-directed support by users of health and social care services. (S3O-9952)

20. Jamie Hepburn: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that the findings of the Consumer Focus Wales report, Post Office Closures: Impact of the Network Change Programme, have implications for Scotland’s experience of the Network Change Programme and its impact on social inclusion in Scotland. (S3O-9945)
