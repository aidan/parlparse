Business Bulletin No. 51/2003

Monday 17 March 2003

Section E : Written questions lodged on 14 March 2003
  

Questions marked with a triangle () are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S1W-34665 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Scottish Ambulance Service will end completely the use of on-call working arrangements for staff in part-time stations.

S1W-34666 Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what specialist paediatric services are available for sufferers of Sever’s disease in (a) Scotland and (b) the Cumbernauld and Kilsyth constituency, detailing the level of service each individual unit can provide.

S1W-34667 Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the reasons are for a wheelchair not being provided to a 12-year-old resident of the Cumbernauld and Kilsyth constituency suffering severe debilitation from Sever’s disease in both feet and receiving treatment at Monklands Hospital.

S1W-34668 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what proposals it has to fund managing agents on an equal basis in respect of all students entering modern apprenticeships, regardless of their age.

S1W-34669 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what proposals it has to fund modern apprenticeships to the same level that they are funded in England.

S1W-34670 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to expand illness prevention initiatives, such as flu vaccination schemes, in order to include care providers.

S1W-34671 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-22918 by Nicol Stephen on 22 February 2002, whether needing to realise a financial gain from the sale of a school site in order to fund a public private partnership proposal is a relevant financial consideration in respect of school closure proposals.

S1W-34672 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-22918 by Nicol Stephen on 22 February 2002, what balance should be struck by education authorities between educational, financial and community issues when considering the closure of a school.

S1W-34673 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether education authorities should take into account the level of funding allocated under its schools estate funding package when formulating public private partnership proposals and whether any education authorities that had formulated, but not agreed, public private partnership proposals before the schools estate funding package became available should now revise their proposals in order to take account of it.

S1W-34674 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-33067 on 21 January 2003, what it estimates the likely percentage change will be in school rolls by 2011 in (a) the South Lanarkshire Council area, (b) East Kilbride and (c) Hamilton.

S1W-34675 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has received any representations from (a) community representatives, (b) school boards, (c) church or denominational bodies and (d) parents of pupils in the East Kilbride area of the South Lanarkshire Council area in relation to the council's proposed public private partnership for schools.

S1W-34676 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has received any representations from (a) community representatives, (b) school boards, (c) church or denominational bodies and (d) parents of pupils in the Hamilton area of the South Lanarkshire Council area in relation to the council's proposed public private partnership for schools.

S1W-34677 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many children have been referred to the Children’s Reporter on offence grounds in each of the last four years broken down in respect of (a) one, (b) two to three offences, (c) four to six offences, (d) seven to nine offences and (e) 10 or more offences, broken down by reporter area.

S1W-34678 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive what meetings ministers plan to hold with UK ministers in April 2003; how many such meetings will take place in Scotland; when each meeting will take place, and which Scottish and UK ministers will attend each meeting.

S1W-34679 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many meetings between Scottish and UK ministers have been held in each month since July 1999.

S1W-34680 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive what ministerial announcements are planned for April 2003.

S1W-34681 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has been consulted on any proposal by the Home Secretary to withdraw from individual tenants the automatic right to be granted housing benefit and the impact of such a policy on its social justice policy.

S1W-34682 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the possible withdrawal of housing benefit for anti-social behaviour and the impact of such a policy on its social justice policy.

S1W-34683 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-33813 by Mr Jim Wallace on 12 March 2003, what changes the investigation by the director of HM Prison Kilmarnock has concluded need to be made to improve procedures for suicide prevention at the prison.

S1W-34684 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-33813 by Mr Jim Wallace on 12 March 2003, whether the investigation by the director of HM Prison Kilmarnock has concluded that there were any shortcomings in the procedures for suicide prevention at the prison.

S1W-34685 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-34108 by Des McNulty on 26 February 2003, when a decision will be made on the wind farm public inquiry.

S1W-34686 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question 34109 by Ross Finnie on 24 February 2003, when the new survey on public attitudes towards wind farms will be completed and when the results will be published.

S1W-34687 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-34102 by Ross Finnie on 24 February 2003, what steps are being taken to ensure that the new survey on public attitudes towards wind farms does not include any errors.

S1W-34688 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-33598 by Ms Margaret Curran on 14 February 2003, whether the announcement on neighbourhood wardens will be made before dissolution of the Parliament.

S1W-34689 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-33593 by Mr Jim Wallace on 10 February 2003, who its representation in the UK delegation to the Congress of Local and Regional Authorities of Europe is composed of.

S1W-34690 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-8569 by Nicol Stephen on 14 August 2000, what recent assessments it has made of the impact on its responsibilities of the World Trade Organisation.

S1W-34691 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) children and (b) pensioners it has lifted out of poverty since 1999.

S1W-34692 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what research it has carried out into the ownership history and current tenure of former right-to-buy homes.

S1W-34693 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-34130 by Ms Margaret Curran on 4 March 2003, whether it will publish the results of the review by Communities Scotland of financial procedures and practices in relation to the Social Inclusion Partnership Fund.

S1W-34694 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive who is responsible for any reductions in crew levels of front line appliances attending fire call-outs in the Strathclyde Fire Brigade area.

S1W-34695 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive what its response is to the Fire Brigades Union in Strathclyde’s statement in its letter of 12 March 2003 to all MSPs that a reduction in crew levels would be a danger to the public and front-line firefighters.

S1W-34696 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many fire service staff have (a) been recruited and (b) retired in each of the last four years, broken down by fire brigade, and how many full-time equivalent vacancies there are in each brigade.

S1W-34697 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is aware of any new or existing European funds, previously unallocated or applied for, to help fishing communities and, if so, what progress is being made with any application for such funds and whether it will give details, including the outcome, of any relevant discussions with Her Majesty's Government.

S1W-34698 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will respond to the latest proposals regarding the future of the Scottish Agricultural College's Aberdeen campus at Craigstone and, in particular, whether it plans to allow the proposals to proceed unchallenged.

S1W-34699 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has received about whether the current days-at-sea restrictions and quota allocations will remain in place until the end of 2003 or whether they will be replaced by a different regime on 1 July 2003, as originally agreed by Her Majesty's Government and the European Union.

S1W-34700 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are any representatives from the fish processing sector on the Seafish Industry Authority and what the reasons are for the position on this matter.

S1W-34701 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how many foreign-owned fishing vessels were on the Scottish register (a) in 1997 and (b) on the most recent date for which figures are available and whether it will give the figures for tonnage and power of such vessels if they can be calculated.

S1W-34702 David McLetchie: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-5772 by Donald Dewar on 4 September 2000, what the total cost has been of each Scottish Executive Minister’s private office since July 2000, broken down by year and, where possible, category of expenditure.

S1W-34705) Janis Hughes: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Health Improvement Challenge paper, referred to in Partnership for Care: Scotland’s Health White Paper, will be published.

S1W-34720 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what the cost would be of buying out the public private partnership (PPP) for Inverness airport; whether the terms of the PPP are a disincentive to increasing the number of routes and passengers using the airport; what its estimate is of the total cost of payments due over the life of the PPP contract, and what steps it plans to take in order to increase the number of routes and passengers using Inverness airport.
