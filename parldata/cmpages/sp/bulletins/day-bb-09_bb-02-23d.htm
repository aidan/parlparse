Business Bulletin No. 32/2009: Monday 23 February 2009
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 26 February 2009

Question Time

General

1. Dave Thompson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many projects were removed by ministers from the recommendations of the Strategic Transport Projects Review prior to publication. (S3O-6044)

2. David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in increasing access to insulin pumps for people with type 1 diabetes. (S3O-6019)

3. Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive how many deaths there were at the Royal Alexandra Hospital in 2007 and 2008 where Clostridium difficile was a factor and whether those figures differ from the figures published where Clostridium difficile was listed as the cause of death. (S3O-5997)

4. Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to assist companies in exporting to potential new markets. (S3O-6037)

5. Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason it will not act on the findings of the review of the local authority funding distribution process, which are expected by the end of October 2009, until after the 2011 elections to the Scottish Parliament. (S3O-6057)

6. Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how the new Town Centre Regeneration Fund will benefit the people of Fife. (S3O-5984)

7. Malcolm Chisholm: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made of the ability of individual local authorities to meet the 2012 homelessness target. (S3O-5988)

8. Jim Tolson: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it will give to ports to increase their container capacities. (S3O-6062)

9. Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met Glasgow City Council to discuss education provision. (S3O-5992)

10. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive how the NHS is supporting people with arthritis. (S3O-6056)

Justice and Law Officers

1. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the closure or downgrading of Howdenhall forensic laboratory in Edinburgh will be considered as part of the Scottish Police Services Authority board’s review of the future of forensic services following the completion of new laboratories in Gartcosh and Dundee. (S3O-6055)

2. Not Lodged

3. Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress was made in tackling alcohol-related criminal offences over the last festive period. (S3O-6041)

4. Hugh O’Donnell: To ask the Scottish Executive what legal remedies are available to public authorities when pursuing individuals for debt. (S3O-6054)

5. Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people charged with assault to injury from 1 October 2008 to 31 January 2009 have received a direct measure. (S3O-5970)

6. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to review the effectiveness of the Proceeds of Crime Act 2002. (S3O-6032)

7. Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to support women seeking financial compensation through the legal system from partners convicted of domestic violence. (S3O-6046)

8. Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent discussions it has had with Northern Constabulary on tackling drugs misuse. (S3O-5971)

9. Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive how its policy takes account of the high incidence of communication difficulties among young offenders in terms of both direct provision of speech and language therapy and, more generally, the communication accessibility of criminal justice services. (S3O-5994)

10. Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executivewhat action it is taking to deal with overcrowding at Polmont Young Offenders Institution. (S3O-6048)

Rural Affairs and the Environment

1. David Whitton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will guarantee funding for local authorities to carry out flood prevention work in line with recommendation 36 of the Rural Affairs and Environment Committee’s Stage 1 Report on the Flood Risk Management (Scotland) Bill. (S3O-6021)

2. Not Lodged

3. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what is being done to reverse the decline in livestock numbers on Scotland’s hills. (S3O-5964)

4. Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive what quantity of chlorofluorocarbons and hydrofluorocarbons was contained in buildings demolished in the last year. (S3O-5981)

5. Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will next meet representatives of supermarkets to discuss reducing unnecessary packaging on products. (S3O-6042)

6. Andy Kerr: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will abandon its proposal for leasing out a proportion of Forestry Commission Scotland land. (S3O-6000)

7. Bill Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that Scottish Environment Protection Agency maps of coastal flood risk areas should specify the sea level changes on which the flooding models are based. (S3O-6047)

8. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding it is making available to support and maintain Fife’s coastal paths. (S3O-6002)

9. Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with trade unions on the review of the future of the Scottish Agricultural Wages Board. (S3O-5983)

10. Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive what it is doing to protect and enhance the natural environment. (S3O-6050)
