Business Bulletin No. 123/2004

Monday 6 September 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

* S2M-1640 Elaine Smith:Breastfeeding etc. ( Scotland) Bill—That the Parliament agrees to the general principles of the Breastfeeding etc. ( Scotland) Bill.

* S2M-1639 Frances Curran:Palestinian Hunger Strike—That the Parliament notes that thousands of Palestinian political detainees imprisoned in Israeli jails are currently on hunger strike; notes that the number of detainees is increasing by the day; notes that during the Intifada over 8,000 Palestinians, ranging in age from 10 to 75 years old, have been arrested; notes that many detainees have been denied access to health services, subjected to physical torture, housed in over-crowded and unsanitary conditions and are regularly in solitary confinement and subjected to humiliation, including being forced to stand naked during interrogation; notes that child detainees have been prevented from continuing their education and many detainees are denied all contact with their children and families; expresses its solidarity with the struggle of the Palestinian detainees for basic human rights and condemns the Israeli state’s continuing denial of these same rights, and calls on Her Majesty’s Government to take action, including the use of sanctions, to call Israel to account.

* S2M-1638 Christine Grahame:Hidden Treasure—That the Parliament, in recognition of the historic and political significance of the “Arniston” stone, a 400- year-old lintel that was salvaged by Robert Dundas in the 1800s from the original Scottish Parliament, where it had formed part of the façade, and has been returned to the Parliament, its original home, by the current owner of Arniston House, calls for it to be moved from its current inconspicuous location to a place of significance above the public entrance for all to see.

Supported by: Mr Stewart Maxwell*, Brian Adam*, Michael Matheson*, Roseanna Cunningham*

* S2M-1637# Mr Jamie McGrigor:Olympic Success—That the Parliament congratulates Team GB on its achievement in winning 30 medals at the Games of the 28th Olympiad and Greece and Athens on hosting such an outstanding games; further congratulates Kelly Holmes on winning the historic double of both the 800m and 1500m, Matthew Pinsent for winning his fourth gold medal, Scots Shirley Robertson and Chris Hoy for their golds in the sailing and cycling and Campbell Walsh and Katherine Grainger for their silver medals; believes that the interest generated by the team’s success, and the Olympics in general, must be maximised by both local sports associations and clubs and national sports bodies, and considers that the Scottish Executive should place a higher priority on sports and encourage efforts to ensure that competitive sport is available to all children in Scotland.

* S2M-1636 Mr Brian Monteith:A Tolerance-Free Parliament?—That the Parliament supports the principle of a socially-inclusive Scotland; urges the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body to recognise that smokers and non-smokers work in the Parliament, be they cleaners, security officers, researchers, Scottish Executive ministers or Presiding Officers; notes that the majority of smokers choose to smoke because they enjoy it and that they do not appreciate being marginalised, alienated and made public outcasts, and believes that smokers and non-smokers can coexist by the provision of a designated smoking room so that a tolerant and liberal example is set to the rest of Scotland.

Supported by: Mr Jamie McGrigor*, Murdo Fraser*

* S2M-1635 Patrick Harvie:Sexual Health and Relationships Strategy—That the Parliament notes that the draft Sexual Health and Relationships Strategy recommends full implementation of the McCabe report published in June 2000; condemns the misinformation being circulated about the contents of the draft strategy, in particular the suggestion that the strategy proposes the use of “graphic and intimate sexual instruction” in pre-school settings; believes that the description of “the growing army of sexual health service providers who push a value-free agenda” is another such gross distortion; regrets that a public figure with the prominence of Cardinal Keith O’ Brien should choose to misrepresent the strategy in such a way, and would welcome an apology from Cardinal O’ Brien for his comments in the press.

* S2M-1634# Alex Neil:Rowallan Medical Centre—That the Parliament expresses its concern at the failure of NHS Lanarkshire to involve patients adequately in the selection of a general practitioner for the Rowallan Medical Centre in Blantyre; further expresses its disappointment at the board’s failure to keep its promise of patient involvement at each stage of this process, and considers that the Scottish Executive should urgently review NHS Scotland’s policy and practice in respect of patient involvement in the selection of GPs and wider issues affecting primary health care in local communities.

* S2M-1633 Mr Stewart Maxwell:A Smoke-Free Parliament—That the Parliament supports the principle of smoke-free workplaces and applauds the decision of the Scottish Executive to make all Executive-occupied buildings smoke-free from 30 August 2004 and urges members of the Parliament to lobby the Scottish Parliament Corporate Body to make the new Parliament building at Holyrood a completely smoke-free workplace, protecting the health of workers, including cleaning staff who may have to enter these premises although they may be non-smokers, and also setting a healthy example to the rest of the people of Scotland.

Supported by: Bristow Muldoon*, Rob Gibson*, Shiona Baird*, Mike Pringle*, Michael Matheson*, Eleanor Scott*, Mr Keith Raffan*

* S2M-1632 Patricia Ferguson on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau:Designation of Lead Committee—That the Parliament agrees that the Justice 1 Committee be designated as lead committee in consideration of the Register of Sasines (Application Procedure) Rules 2004 (SSI 2004/318).

Supported by: Tavish Scott*

* S2M-1631 Patricia Ferguson on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau:Designation of Lead Committee—That the Parliament agrees that the Justice 2 Committee be designated as lead committee in consideration of the European Communities (Lawyer’s Practice) ( Scotland) Amendment Regulations 2004 (SSI 2004/302).

Supported by: Tavish Scott*

* S2M-1628 Colin Fox:Congratulations to the Edinburgh People’s Festival 2004—That the Parliament extends its warmest congratulations to all those who participated in the 2004 Edinburgh People’s Festival, making it the most successful yet; notes that, over the week, more than 1,000 people attended events at the Simpsons maternity unit, HM Prison Saughton, Portobello Town Hall, Inch Park, Gilmerton Athletic Boxing Club, community facilities and working men’s clubs across Edinburgh; further notes that, thanks to the generosity of the Edinburgh trades union movement, ticket prices for shows were just £2.00 and, indeed, many attractions were completely free; supports the aim of the People’s Festival of including those who, for various reasons, feel excluded by the world’s greatest arts festival; welcomes the diverse programme of events that was presented, including classical, rock, folk and world music, theatre and ceilidh, film and stand-up comedy, vigorous debate and leisurely concerts in the park, and wishes the Edinburgh People’s Festival continued success for the future.

S2M-1578 # Mr Duncan McNeil:International Suicide Prevention Week—That the Parliament acknowledges the importance of International Suicide Prevention Week from 5 to 11 September 2004 for raising awareness of this cause of death; recognises that more people die as a result of suicide than from traffic accidents; commends the work being done by Choose life, Scotland’s strategy aimed at reducing suicides by 20% over the next 10 years; welcomes the introduction to Scotland of Applied Suicide Intervention Skills Training, helping develop more effective approaches to both identifying and assisting those most at risk of suicide, and looks forward to this suicide-reduction work continuing at a national and local level.
