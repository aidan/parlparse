Business Bulletin No. 81/2009: Thursday 14 May 2009
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 21 May 2009

Question Time

General

1. Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will extend the national concessionary travel scheme to include demand-responsive community transport schemes such as Dial-a-Journey in Forth Valley, which is used by older and disabled people who are eligible for the scheme but cannot use scheduled services. (S3O-7024)

2. Derek Brownlee: To ask the Scottish Executive which areas of the public sector are not covered by its commitment to avoid compulsory redundancies. (S3O-6976)

3. Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive what its views are on the planned waste incinerator and recycling centre at Greengairs in North Lanarkshire in light of the area’s local plan. (S3O-6974)

4. Margaret Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of the Cabinet Secretary for Justice’s decision not to proceed with plans for a pilot community court in the east end of Glasgow and the vote on motion S3M-4065 on 7 May 2009, what plans it has to implement fair, fast and flexible justice for the people of Glasgow. (S3O-6996)

5. James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to review the rules and procedures governing pharmacy applications. (S3O-7006)

6. Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether Transport Scotland plans to quantify baseline noise and air quality indicators at individual residences affected by the proposed Forth Replacement Crossing. (S3O-7040)

7. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to manage registered sex offenders. (S3O-7010)

8. David Whitton: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reasons the Scottish Futures Trust has not put any projects out to tender. (S3O-7028)

9. Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to encourage young people into rural industries. (S3O-7031)

10. David McLetchie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to repeal Part 3 of the Transport (Scotland) Act 2001. (S3O-6977)

Europe, External Affairs and Culture

1. Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has made to Ofcom regarding the local broadcast spectrum to be made available for auction in Scotland. (S3O-7066)

2. John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what its relationship is with the National Trust for Scotland and what meetings the Scottish Government has had with it about its current difficulties. (S3O-6975)

3. George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what meetings it has had with the Chair of the National Trust for Scotland and what was discussed. (S3O-6999)

4. Not Lodged

5. Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it recognises the importance of Scottish Screen in supporting the screen industries. (S3O-6992)

6. Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive, given the recession’s adverse impact on corporate sponsorship and grants to voluntary cultural organisations such as the Paisley Choral Festival, whether it will consider providing short-term core funding to secure the long-term future of these organisations. (S3O-6987)

7. Mr Frank McAveety: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with Slovakian diplomatic officials about Slovakian nationals in Glasgow. (S3O-7011)

8. John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive what is being done to retain the cultural role of newspapers in local communities. (S3O-6973)

9. Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what its three main priorities are in respect of external affairs. (S3O-6980)

10. Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has made to its European counterparts in relation to the Scottish Government’s culture policy, particularly regarding Homecoming Scotland and the promotion of the Year of Homecoming across the European Union. (S3O-7071)

Education and Lifelong Learning

1. Angela Constance: To ask the Scottish Executive what it considers the benefits will be of the recently announced changes to individual learning accounts. (S3O-7048)

2. Alasdair Allan: To ask the Scottish Executive what it considers the benefits of the Schools (Consultation) (Scotland) Bill will be to island communities. (S3O-7045)

3. Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Cabinet Secretary for Education and Lifelong Learning last met COSLA and what issues were discussed. (S3O-7008)

4. Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive how successful Careers Scotland’s Path is Green programme has been. (S3O-7054)

5. Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made on the reform of the children’s hearings system. (S3O-7020)

6. Bill Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there have been any recent Scottish developments from the Bologna process. (S3O-7073)

7. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding mechanisms it has in place to facilitate the refurbishment or reconstruction of schools in urgent need of repair or replacement. (S3O-7036)

8. Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive when it plans to review the membership of the Scottish Centre for Financial Education. (S3O-6993)

9. Dave Thompson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many children are being taught through the medium of Gaelic. (S3O-7069)

10. Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive how many local authority schools in the north east are meeting the Scottish Government’s class size target of 18 pupils or fewer in primaries one to three. (S3O-6986)
