Business Bulletin No. 165 / 2004

Monday 15 November 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-1999 Pauline McNeill: Glasgow - A Fair Trade City—That the Parliament supports the investigation undertaken by Glasgow City Council in order to achieve fair trade status; commends the growing awareness of people in Scotland of the issues surrounding fair trade and the desire to be involved in such a positive way to tackle the injustice of poverty, and recognises the ongoing importance of fair trade as an effective way of empowering disadvantaged producers through receiving a fairer deal.

*S2M-1998# Mr Brian Monteith: Encouraging School Debating—That the Parliament recognises the great benefits that school debating can offer to students; believes that debate develops powers of argument, increases understanding, boosts critical thinking and promotes engagement with society; considers that students who take part in debating show stronger communication skills, raised self-esteem, are better at working in teams, become more articulate and objective and are likely to be better equipped to become active citizens; believes that the opportunity to take part in debate can have a real impact on the life-chances of young people, particularly of those from relatively disadvantaged backgrounds; supports the work of the English-Speaking Union (ESU), with schools throughout Scotland, in promoting and organising debate; notes that its National Juniors Debating Competition has attracted more than eighty entries from schools across the country, and welcomes the innovative new debating outreach programme that the ESU is launching in January 2005 in conjunction with North Lanarkshire Council.

*S2M-1997# Dr Elaine Murray: Recognition of the Achievements of James Clerk Maxwell—That the Parliament notes the 125th anniversary of the death of James Clerk Maxwell, one of Scotland’s greatest scientists, whose work paved the way for television and radio; considers that the Scottish Executive should give consideration to commemorating the work of this outstanding physicist, and believes that Maxwell’s achievements should be promoted in Scotland’s schools to encourage more pupils to study science at school and in further and higher education.

S2M-1996 Pauline McNeill: Yasser Arafat—That the Parliament extends its deepest condolences to the Palestinian people for the very sad loss of their leader, President Yasser Arafat, who retained popular and unified support across Palestinian communities for 30 years; believes that President Arafat should be remembered for making the world aware of the existence and the struggle of the Palestinians as a people displaced from their homeland, and further believes that the most lasting tribute to Yasser Arafat would be international effort to ensure a viable independent Palestinian state in the West Bank and Gaza Strip and to ensure the right of return of Palestinian refugees.

S2M-1995# Jackie Baillie: Employability Programmes for Vulnerable Groups—That the Parliament notes the success of the New Futures Fund Initiative in providing early employability programmes for people who are furthest from the labour market, including those with addiction problems, the homeless and ex-offenders; acknowledges that 21% of participants entered employment, 14% entered training programmes, 12% entered further education and 10% engaged in voluntary work; notes that the funding of the initiative ends in March 2005, and considers that the Scottish Executive should put in place a long-term strategy to provide the most vulnerable adults with effective routes out of the problems they face by providing easy access to employability programmes.

Supported by: Christine May*, Elaine Smith*, Rob Gibson*, Ms Sandra White*, Mr Kenneth Macintosh*, Bruce Crawford*, Robin Harper*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Mr Jamie Stone*, Michael McMahon*

S2M-1994 Christine Grahame: Closure of Hutton and Burnmouth Primary Schools—That the Parliament notes with concern the decision on 11 November 2004 to close Hutton, with a roll of 12, and Burnmouth, with a roll of 18, primary schools in Roxburghshire and Berwickshire in the face of fierce resistance from parents; further notes with concern that these closures will tear the heart out of these communities; believes that these proposed closures must be referred to the Minister for Education and Young People, and urges him to reject these closures which are not in the interests of the children or the community.

S2M-1993# Rob Gibson: Membership of the Convention of the Highlands and Islands—That the Parliament considers that the Scottish Executive should extend invitations to future meetings of the Convention of the Highlands and Islands so as to include all MSPs elected in the area and should afford them full speaking rights.

S2M-1992 Mr Brian Monteith: Hearts Memorial—That the Parliament welcomes the unveiling of a memorial in the French village of Contalmaison to Heart of Midlothian players killed during the Battle of the Somme; recognises the sacrifice that saw seven players, Duncan Currie, John Allan, Thomas Gracie, James Speedie, James Boyd, Ernest Ellis and Harry Wattie, never return and 11 other players wounded or gassed; further recognises that their patriotism and valour inspired many other players and supporters from other clubs to enlist; commends the work of Jack Alexander whose book, McCrae’s Battalion, directly led to the memorial being established, and welcomes the support of the Scottish Executive, the City of Edinburgh Council and other clubs such as Hibernian FC for this fitting tribute.

Supported by: David McLetchie, Alex Johnstone*, Mike Pringle*, Mr Jamie McGrigor*, John Swinburne*, Mr Kenneth Macintosh*, Bruce Crawford*, Mrs Margaret Ewing*, Robin Harper*, Richard Baker*, John Scott*, Margaret Smith*, Mr David Davidson*, Murdo Fraser*

S2M-1991 Mike Rumbles: Ban on Smoking in Enclosed Public Places—That the Parliament notes the intention of the Scottish Executive to introduce a ban on smoking in enclosed public places; recognises that the Executive has already acted to make its buildings smoke-free; believes that the Parliament should lead by example and make its building a smoke-free area, and calls on the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body to reconsider its decision to allow smoking on the premises.

Supported by: Mr Jamie Stone, Mike Pringle, Iain Smith, Jeremy Purvis, Eleanor Scott, Michael McMahon, Mr John Swinney, John Swinburne, Stewart Stevenson, Bristow Muldoon

*S2M-1991.1 Mr Brian Monteith: Ban on Smoking in Enclosed Public Places—As an amendment to motion (S2M-1991) in the name of Mike Rumbles, leave out from "the Executive" to end and insert "the Parliament staff smoking room is just that, a staff smoking room and, as such, is not an enclosed public place; notes that it is ventilated and has double doors, and believes that, as the Parliament does not have jurisdiction over employment legislation and workplace smoking bans, the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body should continue to allow members of the Parliament and all employees the choice of whether or not to use the smoking room."

S2M-1990 Irene Oldfather: Framework Convention on Tobacco Control—That the Parliament notes that the UK Government is a signatory and strong supporter of the World Health Organisation’s Framework Convention on Tobacco Control (FCTC); welcomes the fact that France, Hungary, Malta and Slovenia have now ratified the treaty; regrets that other EU Member States have not yet formally endorsed the principles of the FCTC; urges the Scottish Executive to do all that it can in discussion with Her Majesty’s Government to encourage formal ratification which would act as a catalyst to other member states; notes that the FCTC calls on its signatories to eliminate the illicit trade in tobacco products, end tobacco advertising and ban tobacco sales to and by minors; further notes that the principles of the FCTC are endorsed by the Executive’s policy as set out in the Tobacco Action Plan, and recognises that the global application of the FCTC will result in reducing the number of people who die as a result of smoking-related illnesses each year.

Supported by: Jackie Baillie

S2M-1989 Helen Eadie: Superfast Receives Best Ferry Award—That the Parliament notes the ongoing success story of the passenger and freight Superfast ferry service operating from Fife with its record levels of sea freight transport; congratulates Yannis Criticos, Superfast board member and project director of North Sea and Superfast Ferries on being voted Best Ferry Operator by the Scottish Passenger Agents’ Association (SPAA), which is the oldest travel trade association in the world, established in 1921, and further notes that around 700 travel agents in Scotland associated with the SPAA voted for the award and that Superfast held off competition from Stena Line, SeaCat, DFDS Seaways and P&O Ferries to scoop the top award.

Supported by: Christine May, Marilyn Livingstone, Nora Radcliffe, Tricia Marwick, Sarah Boyack, Murdo Fraser, Roseanna Cunningham, Mr Kenny MacAskill, Mike Pringle, Jackie Baillie, Scott Barrie, Mrs Mary Mulligan, Phil Gallie*, Mr Jamie Stone*

S2M-1987 Bristow Muldoon: National Tree Week—That the Parliament notes that National Tree Week will take place from 24 November to 5 December 2004; welcomes the Tree for All initiative, launched in Livingston on 18 November 2004; recognises that it will see 2 million trees planted across Scotland by children and for children, contributing to a better environment around schools and towns and actively using woods to educate children in maths, English language, science and art and design; welcomes the positive impact that these actions will have, and urges individuals, schools, local authorities and communities to get involved.

S2M-1986# John Scott: Waiting Lists for Access to Free Personal Care—That the Parliament is concerned to note that South Ayrshire Council has been required to operate a waiting list policy for the delivery of free personal care to older people who have been assessed as being eligible for such care; believes that, given the commitment made by the Scottish Executive to fully fund the provision of free personal care, the existence of waiting lists is incompatible with this commitment, and considers that financial allocations should be increased to those local authorities whose existing allocations are insufficient to meet assessed demand for free personal care.

Supported by: Mr Adam Ingram, Margaret Mitchell, Alex Johnstone, Eleanor Scott, John Swinburne, Carolyn Leckie, Campbell Martin, Chris Ballance, Ms Sandra White, Mr David Davidson, Phil Gallie*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Mr Ted Brocklebank*

S2M-1985# Helen Eadie: World COPD Day 2004—That the Parliament welcomes the third annual World Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD) Day on 17 November 2004 which aims to raise awareness of COPD across the world; regrets that COPD is the only major cause of death on the increase in Scotland, with an estimated 33,000 sufferers and more than 4,600 deaths each year, equating to approximately one death every two hours; recognises the importance of effective management of COPD in primary care with services such as early diagnosis with spirometry testing, pulmonary rehabilitation, non-invasive ventilation therapy and access to a multi-disciplinary healthcare team; supports the efforts of the British Lung Foundation Scotland in highlighting the benefits of these services for improving outcomes for patients and reducing the major burden on the NHS; urges members to contact and liaise with their local NHS boards to ensure that these services are available to their constituents, and considers that the Scottish Executive should ensure that COPD and respiratory disease are given appropriate prominence in the NHS in Scotland.

Supported by: Marilyn Livingstone, Mr David Davidson, Sarah Boyack, Marlyn Glen, Eleanor Scott, Ms Sandra White, Dr Elaine Murray, Jackie Baillie, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Mrs Mary Mulligan, Frances Curran*, John Scott*, Mr Jamie Stone*

S2M-1983 Mr Mark Ruskell: National Farmers Union Scotland and Fair Trade—That the Parliament supports the National Farmers Union Scotland in its campaign for fair trade with supermarkets; is alarmed at the power of supermarkets to dictate prices below production costs of milk and other products; agrees that a code of practice for retailers needs to be made compulsory and enforceable and that a lack of contracts between supermarkets, processors and suppliers is a major problem that needs to be rectified; believes that there should be a fair trade inquiry into the practices engaged in by supermarkets in Scotland, and calls on the Scottish Executive to report on the effect such practices have on the rural economy and environment and the livelihoods of people and communities dependent on agriculture.

Supported by: Patrick Harvie, Alex Neil, Robin Harper, Colin Fox, Eleanor Scott, George Lyon, Shiona Baird, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Mr John Swinney, Trish Godman

S2M-1981 Alex Fergusson: National Beef Envelope—That the Parliament regrets the decision to introduce a national beef envelope as part of the reform of Common Agricultural Policy, despite the overwhelming objections to such a measure by the majority of stakeholders; believes that the decision should be reviewed after one year with a view to abolishing the measure and, if the measure is not then abolished, the criteria should be altered to include beef from the dairy herd, and notes that the dairy sector will be contributing to the scheme through modulation whilst deriving no benefit from it, thus further disadvantaging a sector at a time when its financial returns are at an all-time low.

Supported by: John Scott, Phil Gallie, David Mundell, Stewart Stevenson, Alex Johnstone, Margaret Mitchell, Mr David Davidson, Alex Neil*, Mrs Margaret Ewing*

S2M-1978 Donald Gorrie: Co-operation between Parliament and Civil Servants—That the Parliament urges the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body and the Scottish Executive to form a working group composed of representatives of MSPs, ministers and civil servants to examine ways in which the Parliament and Executive ministers and civil servants could work together constructively, possibly in smaller specialist groups, to develop policy, improve public services, promote economic activity and community life and raise the quality of life, without compromising parties’ right to take up different positions and criticise each other, the independence of the Parliament and the Executive or the particular relationship between ministers and civil servants.

S2M-1977 Margaret Smith: Edinburgh Airport Rail Link—That the Parliament supports a comprehensive rail link to Edinburgh Airport; recognises that the consultation period has started and welcomes the opportunity for people to input their views; believes that value for money and impact of construction concerns must be taken into account; nonetheless believes that direct rail links to Edinburgh Airport from Fife, Glasgow and other parts of Scotland have the potential to benefit the local, regional and national economy, increase the number of people travelling by public transport to the airport, reduce congestion, improve integrated transport in west Edinburgh and tackle pollution; therefore condemns the Scottish Green Party’s opposition to the airport rail link; believes that its policy of renationalising the railways is not a current remedy for tackling congestion or air pollution, and further believes that it is the delivery of new and improved public transport links that will improve Scotland’s environment.

S2M-1969 Christine Grahame: "Wear a Hat Day" (and possibly gloves, coat and scarf)—That the Parliament notes and congratulates Age Concern on its initiative, Wear a Hat Day, on 3 December 2004 to raise funds to publicise, and raise the profile of, the devastating impact of cold weather on the health and well-being of older people; recognises that hypothermia is a silent killer and that older people may not realise how cold they have become until it is too late; while applauding this initiative in fighting the freeze, expresses anger that it is necessary in an energy-rich nation such as Scotland, where the predicted figures for admissions to hospital from hypothermia for the current year for people aged 65 and upwards is 115, the figures for excess winter mortality in the year 2002-03 were 2510, 37% of single pension households, comprising 128,000 households, are classed as fuel poor and Scotland falls well behind the Scandinavian nations in ensuring that winter deaths are minimised; notes the substantial impact rising fuel prices have on those on a fixed income such as a pension, and therefore calls on the Scottish Executive to enter into discussions with Her Majesty’s Government with regard to abolishing the means-tested pension credit which 48% of those entitled to claim in Scotland do not claim and which, in any event, is unjust, with a view to providing a pension that will bring an end to pensioner fuel poverty and make "wear a hat" days superfluous.

Supported by: Ms Sandra White, Michael Matheson, Alex Neil
