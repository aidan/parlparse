Business Bulletin No. 23/2010: Thursday 4 February 2010
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section A – Today’s Business

Meeting of the Parliament

9.00 am Parliamentary Bureau Motions—

S3M-5671 Bruce Crawford on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Marine (Scotland) Bill—That the Parliament agrees that, during Stage 3 of the Marine (Scotland) Bill, debate on groups of amendments shall, subject to Rule 9.8.4A, be brought to a conclusion by the time limits indicated, each time limit being calculated from when the Stage begins and excluding any periods when other business is under consideration or when a meeting of the Parliament is suspended (other than a suspension following the first division in the Stage in the morning and afternoon being called) or otherwise not in progress:

Groups 1 and 2: 25 minutes
Groups 3 to 5: 55 minutes
Groups 6 to 9: 1 hour 40 minutes
Groups 10 to 12: 2 hours 10 minutes
Groups 13 and 14: 2 hours 40 minutes
Groups 15 to 17: 3 hours 5 minutes
Groups 18 to 20: 3 hours 40 minutes
Groups 21 and 22: 4 hours.

followed by Stage 3 Proceedings: Marine (Scotland) Bill—

S3M-5559 Richard Lochhead: Marine (Scotland) Bill—That the Parliament agrees that the Marine (Scotland) Bill be passed.

11.40 am General Question Time—

1. Malcolm Chisholm: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-28083 by Shona Robison on 29 October 2009, when it will issue its final guidance on social care procurement. (S3O-9363)

2. Nigel Don: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being considered to increase support available to male victims of domestic abuse and their children. (S3O-9413)

3. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider encouraging users of public service vehicles to share fuel depots and fuel management systems to reduce costs and spills as part of its approach to achieving climate change targets and efficiency savings. (S3O-9408)

4. Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive what issues the Minister for Housing and Communities has discussed recently with local authorities. (S3O-9388)

5. Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what dialogue it has had with Aberdeen City Council on delivery of its single outcome agreements. (S3O-9358)

6. John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to increase access to community-based health services in areas of multiple deprivation. (S3O-9406)

7. Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact the Chancellor of the Exchequer’s plans to increase national insurance contributions by 1% from April 2011 at a cost of £104.3 million to the Scottish Budget will have on jobs in local government, the NHS, the police and fire services. (S3O-9417)

8. Derek Brownlee: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it supports a greater role for the voluntary sector in delivering public services. (S3O-9407)

9. Joe FitzPatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making to increase the accessibility of towns, cities and villages for people with limited mobility and whether it will consider plans to create an exemplar journey as a template for accessibility. (S3O-9416)

10. Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking in respect of the regulation of the property management industry. (S3O-9367)

12 noon First Minister’s Question Time—

1. Iain Gray: To ask the First Minister what engagements he has planned for the rest of the day. (S3F-2182)

2. Annabel Goldie: To ask the First Minister when he will next meet the Secretary of State for Scotland. (S3F-2183)

3. Tavish Scott: To ask the First Minister what issues will be discussed at the next meeting of the Cabinet. (S3F-2184)

4. Jamie Hepburn: To ask the First Minister how the Scottish Government responds to reports that only 17 of 2,806 bids from Scottish-based firms for contracts relating to the 2012 Olympic Games have been successful. (S3F-2185)

5. Michael McMahon: To ask the First Minister what the Scottish Government’s position is on reports that local authorities are considering cutting back education budgets in order to find savings of £270 million in 2010-11. (S3F-2196)

6. David McLetchie: To ask the First Minister whether the Scottish Futures Trust has commissioned consultants KPMG to produce a report as part of its review of the structure and ownership of Scottish Water and whether the terms of reference of the report specifically exclude consideration of models of ownership other than public ownership. (S3F-2195)

2.15 pm Themed Question Time

Europe, External Affairs and Culture—

1. Shirley-Anne Somerville: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that proposals made by the Calman Commission on Scottish Devolution might still be implemented before the next UK general election. (S3O-9431)

2. Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it gives to the Royal Commission on the Ancient and Historical Monuments of Scotland. (S3O-9415)

3. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is aware of the work of the READ International Book project in redistributing used school and university textbooks to support the local curriculum in Tanzania. (S3O-9445)

4. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is committed to the Youth Music Initiative and for what period it will fund the scheme. (S3O-9386)

5. Willie Coffey: To ask the Scottish Executive what implications the Lisbon treaty will have for the Scottish Government’s input into UK Government European policy. (S3O-9411)

6. Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the First Minister’s comments in the chamber on 14 January 2010, what action the Scottish Government has taken to provide support to the emergency relief effort in Haiti. (S3O-9440)

7. Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Minister for Culture and External Affairs has raised or will raise at the Joint Ministerial Committee for Europe the issue of apparent discrepancies between Wales and Scotland regarding applications for Joint European Resources for Micro to medium Enterprises (JEREMIE) funding. (S3O-9435)

8. Mr Frank McAveety: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has held with Home Office ministers or officials regarding the impact of A8 nationals on services in local communities. (S3O-9380)

9. Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to support local newspapers and the cultural contribution that they make to Scotland’s communities. (S3O-9383)

10. Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions have taken place with Sail Inverclyde regarding the Tall Ships Races’ 2011. (S3O-9385)

Education and Lifelong Learning—

1. Bill Kidd: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are being taken to address the lack of community access to schools built under previous private finance initiatives. (S3O-9422)

2. Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive, given that education and lifelong learning for Scotland’s 660,000 unpaid carers are not mentioned in Skills for Scotland, what role agencies such as Skills Development Scotland have in supporting learning and training for and addressing the particular needs of unpaid carers. (S3O-9393)

3. Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it has taken to tackle the number of exclusions in schools. (S3O-9425)

4. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive what the staff headcount was for Skills Development Scotland at 31 March 2009. (S3O-9446)

5. Alison McInnes: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made of the capacity of schools to timetable for pupils to study multiple National Courses at S4 level after their introduction in 2013. (S3O-9442)

6. John Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact the primary school closures proposed by North Lanarkshire Council will have on service delivery for pupils in the areas concerned. (S3O-9401)

7. Angela Constance: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria the Scottish Funding Council uses in allocating funding for strategic growth to colleges. (S3O-9412)

8. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the cancellation by Highland Council of a visit to Craggan Outdoors by pupils at Crown Primary School because of the exclusion of a disabled child. (S3O-9437)

9. James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to tackle alcohol and drug misuse in schools. (S3O-9375)

10. Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive, of the 251 new schools referred to in the SNP press release of 25 January 2010, SNP build more schools than promised, how many were planned or under construction prior to May 2007. (S3O-9372)

2.55 pm Continuation of Stage 3 Proceedings: Marine (Scotland) Bill

followed by Legislative Consent Motion: Flood and Water Management Bill – UK Legislation—

S3M-5638 Richard Lochhead: Flood and Water Management Bill - UK Legislation—That the Parliament agrees that the relevant provisions of the Flood and Water Management Bill, introduced in the House of Commons on 19 November 2009, relating to reservoirs that sit on the border between Scotland and England, so far as these matters fall within the legislative competence of the Scottish Parliament, should be considered by the UK Parliament.

followed by Parliamentary Bureau Motions

5.00 pm Decision Time

followed by Members’ Business:

Debate on the subject of—

S3M-5490 Christopher Harvie: Bo’ness Scottish Railway Museum—That the Parliament pays tribute to the Scottish Railway Museum at Bo’ness, one of Europe’s major transport collections and a leading Scottish tourist attraction, and to its founder, the late John Burnie, and also commends John Burnie’s ambition to see the Scottish Railway Museum play its part in inspiring and training the new generation of engineers needed for infrastructure and manufacturing in the entire Forth basin.
