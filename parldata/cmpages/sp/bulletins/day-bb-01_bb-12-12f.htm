*S1M-2547 Mr Brian Monteith: Physical Education in Primary Schools—That the Parliament welcomes the HM Inspectorate of Education report on improving standards of physical education in primary schools in Scotland; notes that the report states that schools with very good programmes often used flexible curriculum time to ensure a minimum of two physical education (PE) sessions of around 40 minutes weekly for pupils, provide broad and balanced programmes which include games, sports, gymnastics, dance and swimming and that the programmes in these schools often extend beyond the school day; compares this with provision at independent schools where PE is often given more time, regular input from specialist PE teachers and additional extracurricular opportunities are provided, and believes that the provision of more specialist PE teachers providing education in individual and team sports, dance and games would increase the number of schools achieving HM Inspectorate of Education’s ‘very good’ status and lead to improvements in the health and well-being of Scotland’s children.

*S1M-2546 Ross Finnie: Sea Fisheries—That the Parliament calls on the Scottish Executive to seek to negotiate an outcome from the Fisheries Council meeting in December 2001 that reflects the need to preserve stocks for the long term and represents the best achievable deal for the Scottish fishing industry.

Supported by: Allan Wilson*

*S1M-2545 Roseanna Cunningham: Scottish Prison Service—That the Parliament notes the Executive’s failure to bring forward a strategy to address the increasing overcrowding within Scottish prisons and further notes that morale amongst Scottish Prison Service staff is at rock bottom as the result of (a) the deterioration of industrial relations, (b) the continued threat of further privatisation within the Scottish Prison Service, (c) the culture of secrecy within the service and (d) the continued and unacceptable delay in the publication of the Estates Review.

Supported by: Michael Matheson*

*S1M-2541 Nicola Sturgeon: West of Scotland Cancer Services—That the Parliament notes with concern recent developments at the Beatson Oncology Centre and the inevitable impact on cancer services in the West of Scotland; welcomes the removal of the Beatson Oncology Centre from the management of North Glasgow University Hospitals NHS Trust, and calls upon the Scottish Executive to now set out in detail what action will be taken to prevent further staff resignations, attract suitably qualified consultants to fill new and vacant posts, ensure temporary staff cover to avoid a rationalisation of cancer services such as the cancellation of clinics, and significantly shorten the timescale for the construction of the new Beatson Oncology Centre at Gartnavel Hospital.

*S1M-2540 Trish Godman: Career Opportunities in the Scottish Maritime Industry—That the Parliament warmly commends the crew of the Caledonian MacBrayne ferry "Caledonian Isles", their shorebased colleagues and members of the Glasgow and West of Scotland Maritime Careers Group for their active participation in recent promotional events designed to raise schoolchildren’s awareness of career opportunities in the Scottish maritime industry and believes that such recruitment initiatives will be of considerable importance to the industry and to Scottish school leavers who are keen to engage in satisfying and worthwhile careers both afloat and ashore.

*S1M-2539# Mr Duncan McNeil: Research into Cancer Levels in the Semiconductor Industry—That the Parliament notes the publication Cancer Among Current and Previous Workers at National Semiconductor (UK) Ltd, Greenock: Results of an investigation by the Health and Safety Executive; further notes that the study gives cause for concern which can only be addressed through further research across the semiconductor industry; believes that steps should be taken to ensure that GPs and other health professionals are well briefed and prepared for any enquiries from concerned current or former members of the workforce, and further believes that Ministers and officials of the Scottish Executive should work in partnership with all those concerned to ensure that issues arising from this research are addressed as a matter of priority.

*S1M-2538.1 Nicola Sturgeon: 'Our National Health' - Delivering Change—As an amendment to motion (S1M-2538) in the name of Malcolm Chisholm, leave out from "welcomes" to end and insert "regrets the lack of progress by the Executive in delivering improvements in patient care and public health despite the continued efforts of NHS staff, and calls upon the Executive to embark upon an accelerated programme of reform in the NHS that will reduce bureaucracy, enhance local control, remove institutional barriers to better patient care, strengthen the primary care system and ensure that the NHS has sufficient capacity to deliver major reductions in waiting times."

*S1M-2538.2 Tommy Sheridan: 'Our National Health' - Delivering Change—As an amendment to motion (S1M-2538) in the name of Malcolm Chisholm, leave out from "the Executive" to end and insert "NHS staff towards delivering the commitments in Our National Health: A plan for action, a plan for change but recognises that without radical increases in resources to deliver significantly increased wages and improved conditions for the thousands of currently low-paid health workers, delivery on longer-term goals including major reductions in waiting times and significant public health improvement will not be possible; further agrees on the urgent need for a new National Health minimum wage to attract and retain NHS staff currently underpaid and undervalued; agrees that all private hospitals should now be incorporated into the public sector with financial compensation only on terms of proven need and that consultants’ and others’ contracts should be renegotiated to prevent any more moon-lighting in the private sector which is a divisive, corrosive and counter-productive influence in our national health service; reaffirms our central belief in a first-class public health service free at point of use for every citizen regardless of income, paid through progressive general taxation, and rejects the further privatisation of health care through the Trojan horse of poor-value PFI schemes."

*S1M-2538.3 Mary Scanlon: 'Our National Health' - Delivering Change—As an amendment to motion (S1M-2538) in the name of Malcolm Chisholm, leave out from "welcomes" to end and insert "notes the lack of progress made by the Scottish Executive in delivering on its promises for improved health care, in particular the reduction of waiting times, and further notes the rising crisis in staff morale and retention in the health service together with the delays in obtaining consultant assessment and the continuing inability of the Scottish Executive to effect meaningful devolution of funding and decision making to local level."
