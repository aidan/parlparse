Business Bulletin No. 44/2004

Wednesday 10 March 2004

Section E : Written questions lodged on 9 March 2004
  

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-6730 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will review its local government funding allocations to Orkney, Shetland and the Western Isles councils to ensure parity of resources.

S2W-6819 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what input it will have into the BBC Charter Review; whether its evidence to the review will be published, and what the reasons are for its position on the matter.

S2W-6820Δ Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will appoint external members to the Cabinet Sub-Committee on Sustainable Scotland.

S2W-6821 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of the City of Edinburgh Council’s Integrated Transport Initiative will be funded through the £375 million investment in public transport improvements for Edinburgh, announced by the Minister for Enterprise, Transport and Lifelong Learning on 4 March 2003; what information it had prior to the announcement in respect of the (a) implementation and (b) operational costs of the initiative, and whether it expects the fare box revenue to meet the running costs of the initiative.

S2W-6822 Chris Ballance: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken to ensure that furnaces of the kind used by Blue Circle Cement at Dunbar are suitable for the safe incineration of tyres.

S2W-6823 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-5903 by Ms Margaret Curran on 17 February 2004, how many unit approvals are proposed for 2004-05 and 2005-06, broken down by local authority.

S2W-6824 Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what its response is to concerns that have been expressed by butchers in respect of additional costs that they face as a result of the implementation of the Animal By-Products (Scotland) Regulations 2003.

S2W-6825 Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what its response is to concerns expressed by butchers in respect of sectors, for example the catering sector, that are not covered by the Animal By-Products (Scotland) Regulations 2003 and currently dispose of raw meat waste and bones by landfill.

S2W-6826 Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive when it intends to introduce regulations so that all sectors that currently dispose of raw meat waste and bones by landfill are covered by regulations similar to the Animal By-Products (Scotland) Regulations 2003.

S2W-6827 Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to encourage local authorities to develop green solutions, such as composting, for the disposal of all animal waste and former foodstuffs.

S2W-6828 Chris Ballance: To ask the Scottish Executive how many children were injured or killed in accidents while cycling last year.

S2W-6830 Chris Ballance: To ask the Scottish Executive what proposals it has for re-using tyres once the ban on the disposal of whole tyres in landfill sites takes effect on 16 July 2004.

S2W-6831 Chris Ballance: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking in relation to Directive 2002/91/EC of the European Parliament and Council on energy conservation in buildings.

S2W-6832 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in monitoring the implementation of the Building Standards (Scotland) Amendment Regulations 2001 in relation to its sustainable development objectives.

S2W-6833 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in monitoring the implementation of the Building (Scotland) Act 2003 in relation to its sustainable development objectives.

S2W-6834 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it has taken, and will put in place, to promote the National Care Standards.

S2W-6835 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made of public awareness of the National Care Standards.

S2W-6836 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how much money has been (a) allocated to and (b) spent on publicising the National Care Standards in (i) 2002-03 and (ii) 2003-04 to date.

S2W-6837Δ Mr Alasdair Morrison: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects the Council of Europe to publish its report on the application of the European Charter for Regional or Minority Languages in the United Kingdom.

*The member has provided the following Gaelic translation:

A dh’ fhaighneachd de Riaghaltas na h-Alba cuine a tha e an dùil gum foillsich Comhairle na h-Eòrpa a h-aithisg air cuir-an-gnìomh Cairt Eòrpach nam Mion-chànanan anns an Rìoghachd Aonaichte.*
