Business Bulletin No. 68/2003

Tuesday 13 May 2003

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-14 Rosie Kane: M74 Northern Extension—That the Parliament expresses its complete opposition to the M74 Northern Extension; recognises that the process by which this proposal has been pursued, the monies already spent and the estimated cost of the project have failed the people of Glasgow; condemns the Scottish Executive's lack of provision of an alternative public transport system for this part of Glasgow and its apparent disregard for local concerns and needs; calls on the Executive to review its transport policy so that it properly reflects the needs and desires of the Scottish people, and, in the case of the M74 extension, calls for an immediate public inquiry including an environmental impact assessment and proper exploration of alternative options.

*S2M-13# Murdo Fraser: A9—That the Parliament recognises the dangers of the A9 trunk road between Perth and Inverness and, in particular, the confusion to motorists caused by switching between dual and single carriageway, which adds to the accident statistics; notes the support of Tayside Police for the dualling of the A9 in its entirety with improved junctions and adequate rest areas in the interests of road safety; further notes the increase in traffic year-on-year on the A9, which has been assisted by economic growth in the Inverness area, and therefore supports the calls from local campaigners for the Scottish Executive to make a commitment to a programme of dualling the A9 between Perth and Inverness.

*S2M-12 Linda Fabiani: South Lanarkshire Council Secondary School Public Private Partnership Proposal and Hamilton—That the Parliament shares the concerns of parents and pupils in Hamilton regarding the public private partnership secondary school proposal of South Lanarkshire Council which, if implemented, will mean the closure of Earnock High School; notes that local residents feel so strongly with regard to the closure of this school that they have elected two independent "save our schools" councillors to the council; further notes the concern of parents in Hamilton that the council's consultation process is inadequate, and calls on the council and the Minister for Finance and Public Services, in consideration of the local community's deep-felt anxiety on this issue, to reconsider this deeply flawed proposal.

*S2M-11 Linda Fabiani: South Lanarkshire Council Secondary School Public Private Partnership Proposal and East Kilbride—That the Parliament shares the concerns of parents and pupils in East Kilbride regarding the public private partnership secondary school proposal of South Lanarkshire Council which, if implemented, would mean that 75% of resultant school closures will be in East Kilbride, which will also lose 50% of its secondary schools resulting in school rolls becoming amongst the highest in Scotland, and that the locations of the proposed schools will entail considerably longer school journey times for many pupils; notes that the council's financial proposal is underpinned by the closure of St Bride's School, academically the highest performing school in South Lanarkshire, so that its site can be sold for development; further notes the concern of parents in East Kilbride that the consultation process by the council is inadequate, and calls on the council and the Minister for Finance and Public Services, in consideration of the local community's deep-felt anxiety on this issue, to reconsider this deeply flawed proposal.

*S2M-10# Mr Adam Ingram: Scottish Agricultural College—That the Parliament views with concern the plans by the Scottish Agricultural College (SAC) board to move the college's teaching and research services from Auchincruive, Ayrshire and Craibstone, Aberdeen to Edinburgh; believes that there should be a full economic impact assessment that takes into account the effect that this move would have on students, staff and local communities before the proposal is approved by the Scottish Executive; questions the methodology used by the consultants in their review and report, and considers that there should be a halt to the movement of staff from the SAC campuses in Ayrshire and Aberdeen to Edinburgh, which runs contrary to the Scottish Executive's policy of dispersing agency and department jobs and offices outwith the central belt, until proper scrutiny is carried out by the Parliament and its committees.

*S2M-9 Phil Gallie: Burns An' A' That Festival—That the Parliament compliments the Ayrshire "Burns An' A' That" Festival steering committee and supporters for presenting a festival of Burns in a way that not only induces considerable public acclaim and interest but also underlines a real celebration of life and contemporary Scottish culture.

Supported by: Bruce Crawford*, Mr David Davidson*, Margaret Mitchell*, Alex Johnstone*

*S2M-8 Rhona Brankin: Future of the Scottish Agricultural College—That the Parliament welcomes the decision of the Scottish Agricultural College to consolidate its main education and research facilities at King’s Buildings and the Bush Estate; notes that the decision is based on the major options appraisal carried out by Deloitte and Touche to achieve the best configuration of service delivery that is both operationally and financially sustainable for the whole of Scotland; recognises the importance of maintaining the existing advisory and consultancy services throughout Scotland, and recognises the vital strategic importance of the decision to the Scottish Executive's bioscience cluster strategy for the Lothians and for Scotland.

Supported by: Mr John Home Robertson*, Sarah Boyack*
