Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday.  That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an “R”.

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm).  A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-3608 Eleanor Scott: Reducing Speed Limits for Road Safety—That the Parliament congratulates Brake, the road safety charity, for its Road Safety Week on the theme of “Watch out! There’s a kid about”; notes with alarm that road traffic is the biggest killer of 12 to 16-year-olds in Britain and that last year the number of road deaths of 12 to 19-year-olds on foot rose by 26%; commends and supports the residents of Lerwick who are campaigning to reduce speed limits in residential areas to 20 miles per hour; believes that 20 miles per hour speed limits around schools should be more tightly enforced and extended to all residential areas, and urges the Scottish Executive, local authorities and the police to take urgent steps to reduce road deaths and injuries in Scotland.

*S2M-3607 Rob Gibson: Congratulations to Glas Cymru—That the Parliament congratulates Glas Cymru, the owners of Dwr Cymru/Welsh Water, for attaining the position of joint top-performing private water and sewerage company in Wales and England with Yorkshire Water this year; particularly notes that Glas Cymru is a not-for-profit trust that has no shareholders and reinvests all its financial surpluses for the benefit of customers; welcomes its dividend strategy which will maintain or increase the £18 level of bonus paid to its one million customers this year; believes that Scottish Water should be transformed into a not-for-profit trust in the public sector that could reduce costs to Scottish customers instead of expecting to charge excessive rates to domestic and, especially, business water users, a practice that will continue with the Scottish Executive’s chosen model, which is due for the next determination by the Water Commission on St Andrew’s Day, and notes that this practice is locking Scottish Water customers into a punitive charge regime that has resulted in capital expenditure being mainly funded by current water charge payers since 2003.

*S2M-3606 Alex Johnstone: Arbroath Abbey World Heritage Site Status—That the Parliament supports a campaign to encourage the UK Government to obtain World Heritage Site status from UNESCO for Arbroath Abbey, not only on the basis of its connection with the Declaration of Arbroath of 1320 but also for its outstanding architecture; recognises that the Declaration of Arbroath more than satisfies UNESCO’s requirement for the site to be directly associated with literary works of outstanding universal significance, and considers that the Scottish Executive should support Arbroath Abbey as an excellent Scottish candidate for the UK Tentative List.

*S2M-3605 Christine Grahame: Scottish Produce in the Scottish Parliament—That the Parliament notes the success of Scottish Food Fortnight and celebrates the quality food produced Scotland-wide; regrets that the members’ dining room in Holyrood, which plays host to many guests across Scotland and internationally, rarely features home produce, and therefore calls on the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body to take appropriate measures to ensure that the dining room becomes a showcase for Scottish produce featuring regions of Scotland on a rotational basis.
