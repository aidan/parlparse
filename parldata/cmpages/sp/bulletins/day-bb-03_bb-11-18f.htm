Business Bulletin No. 156/2003

Tuesday 18 November 2003

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-612 Roseanna Cunningham: Potential G8 Summit at Gleneagles-That the Parliament notes reports that Gleneagles is being considered as a potential venue for a G8 summit meeting; expresses concern at the disruption this could cause for strategic road and rail links and the negative impact that would have on both the local and national economy; recognises the immense pressures that would be placed on police by the scale of the security operation required; supports the bringing of international events to Scotland but believes that the G8 summit represents a poisoned chalice, and regrets that the decision to consider Gleneagles appears to have been based on a belief amongst the Foreign and Commonwealth Office that Gleneagles is remote.

*S2M-611 Tommy Sheridan: Threat to Teachers' Pensions-That the Parliament notes Her Majesty's Government's Green Paper Simplicity, Security and Choice: Working and Saving for Retirement on teachers' pensions; is opposed to the proposals which would raise the minimum age for taking an occupational pension from 60 to 65 and will have hugely detrimental effects on the future well-being of teachers and their profession; fears that such plans, if implemented, will deter young people from entering the profession with its highly stressful working environment and will force many long-serving teachers to work for five further years beyond their expected retirement date, only serving to disillusion and dishearten them, given that half the secondary teaching workforce is within 12 years of retirement; believes that these proposals will make it extremely difficult to recruit new teachers, and therefore supports the Secondary School Teachers' Association's campaign to have the proposals overturned.

*S2M-610 Alex Fergusson: People, Pensions and Post Offices-That the Parliament notes the House of Commons Trade and Industry Committee report People, Pensions and Post Offices: the impact of 'Direct Payment' on post offices and their customers; commends the recommendations of the report that Her Majesty's Government should revisit a number of areas of policy which could be improved to help to give customer choice as well as protecting post offices; very much regrets, however, the response of Her Majesty's Government which ignores the great majority of the recommendations, and urges the Scottish Executive to encourage Her Majesty's Government to pursue the implementation of the report in order to safeguard the future of Scotland's rural post offices.

Supported by: David Mundell*, John Scott*, Mr Ted Brocklebank*, Mr David Davidson*

*S2M-609 Mr John Home Robertson: Deployment of Royal Scots to Iraq-That the Parliament expresses strong support to all the soldiers of the 1st Battalion, the Royal Scots, on their deployment for peacekeeping duties in Iraq; recalls the fine service of the first regiment of foot in the British Army in the first Gulf War; endorses the objective of restoring security and helping the Iraqi people to achieve a better future after decades of oppressive government, and looks forward to the successful conclusion of this mission and the safe return of Scottish service personnel to their homes and families.

*S2M-608 Ross Finnie: The Mink Keeping (Scotland) Order 2003 (SSI 2003/528)-That the Environment and Rural Development Committee recommends that the Mink Keeping (Scotland) Order 2003 (SSI 2003/528) be approved.

Supported by: Allan Wilson*

*S2M-607 Ross Finnie: The Nitrate Vulnerable Zones (Grants) (Scotland) Amendment Scheme 2003 (SSI 2003/518)-That the Environment and Rural Development Committee recommends that the Nitrate Vulnerable Zones (Grants) (Scotland) Amendment Scheme 2003 (SSI 2003/518) be approved.

Supported by: Allan Wilson*

*S2M-606# Carolyn Leckie: Scotland's Football Victory-That the Parliament congratulates the national Scotland football team for a stunning victory against the Netherlands in the first leg of the UEFA Euro 2004 play off and sends warmest wishes and encouragement for the second leg; regrets that the game was not available on terrestrial TV; is concerned at the monopoly of TV coverage by satellite television; believes that all Scots should be able to access national games where they choose; is concerned that women, who bear the greatest responsibility for childcare, will be less able to go to a public house and therefore less likely to be able to see these games, and believes that the Scottish Executive should intervene to try to secure universal coverage of all future Scotland games so that all of Scotland can be united in cheering the team on.

Supported by: Frances Curran*, Tommy Sheridan*

*S2M-605 John Swinburne: European Championship Play Offs-That the Parliament congratulates the Scotland football squad on the marvellous performance and result in the first leg play off against the Netherlands at Hampden, particularly the goal from James McFadden, and offers its full support to the team for the second leg in Amsterdam.
