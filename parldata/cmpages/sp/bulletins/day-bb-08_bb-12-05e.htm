Business Bulletin No.178/2008: Friday 5 December 2008
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 4 December 2008

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-18509 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list any approved capital projects for which contracts were signed (a) in 2008 and (b) since publication of the outline business case for the Scottish Futures Trust.

S3W-18519 Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends the Homecoming Scotland 2009 advertisement for television and cinema to be broadcast outside Scotland and, if so, in what countries.

S3W-18520 Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive how much it cost to produce the Homecoming Scotland 2009 advertisement for television and cinema, broken down by funding source.

S3W-18521 Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive what estimate it has made of the value of Scotland’s economic, sporting and other cultural links with Argentina and how this compares with the value of such links with England.

S3W-18522 Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive what role it considers that the Scotland football team plays in promoting sports participation rates and whether it has had discussions with the Scottish Football Association about the rules determining entitlement to play for the team and their application.

S3W-18523 Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has had discussions with the football authorities and individual clubs about the way in which Remembrance Day is marked, in the light of any implications for public order.

S3W-18524 John Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what NHS Lanarkshire is doing to increase specialist nursing services for people with multiple sclerosis.

S3W-18525 Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to make sure that all kinship carers receive financial assistance.

S3W-18526 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will announce the funding mechanism for the Aberdeen Western Peripheral Route.

S3W-18527 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will announce a timetable for the construction of improvements at the Haudagain roundabout in Aberdeen.

S3W-18528 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has made an assessment of the impact on black grouse in Dumfries and Galloway of leasing out parts of the national forest estate and whether it will publish any such assessment.

S3W-18529 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has made an assessment of the impact on fisheries of leasing out parts of the national forest estate in Dumfries and Galloway and whether it will publish any such assessment.

S3W-18530 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what the income was to Forest Enterprise Scotland from sales of timber in (a) 2003-04, (b) 2004-05, (c) 2005-06, (d) 2006-07 and (e) 2007-08.

S3W-18531 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what the projected income is to Forest Enterprise Scotland from sales of timber in (a) 2008-09, (b) 2009-10 and (c) 2010-11.

S3W-18532 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to transfer responsibility for forest planning under the lease arrangements proposed in its paper Climate Change and the National Forest Estate - Consultation on forestry provision in the Scottish Climate Change Bill.

S3W-18533 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to transfer responsibility for upkeep and development of forest roads under the lease arrangements proposed in its paper Climate Change and the National Forest Estate - Consultation on forestry provisions in the Scottish Climate Change Bill.

S3W-18534 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to transfer responsibility for forest protection and maintenance under the lease arrangements proposed in its paper Climate Change and the National Forest Estate - Consultation on forestry provisions in the Scottish Climate Change Bill.

S3W-18535 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to transfer responsibility for management and development of the forest estate under the lease arrangements proposed in its paper Climate Change and the National Forest Estate - Consultation on forestry provisions in the Scottish Climate Change Bill.

S3W-18536 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive how many hectares of new woodlands have been created in each of the last five years by the (a) public and (b) private sector.

S3W-18537 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what studies (a) are being planned, (b) are in progress and (c) have been carried out to identify potential sites for new woodlands.

S3W-18538 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of budget cuts by City of Edinburgh Council, what action it proposes to alleviate the pressure on human and financial resources experienced by community centres and similar community services.

S3W-18539 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of budget cuts by City of Edinburgh Council, whether it is aware of the pressure experienced by those delivering frontline services in the voluntary and statutory sector.

S3W-18540 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what provision there is in the single outcome agreement for the delivery of community education facilities in Edinburgh.

S3W-18541 Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answers to questions S3W-17875 and S3W-17871 by Michael Russell on 27 November 2008, whether it will place in the Scottish Parliament Information Centre information supplied by the Crofters Commission on how much it has spent on the Bull Hire Scheme for crofters in each of the last five years.

S3W-18542 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how often sections (a) 17 and (b) 18 of the Housing (Scotland) Act 2001 have been used to date, broken down by local authority.

S3W-18543 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what mechanism exists for tenants to appeal against a local authority determination of abandonment of tenancy.

S3W-18544 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions took place with the (a) Mental Welfare Commission for Scotland (MWCS) and (b) Royal College of Psychiatrists prior to the proposal to merge MWCS, NHS Quality Improvement Scotland and the Care Commission.

S3W-18545 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how the statutory duties of the Mental Welfare Commission for Scotland will be preserved in its merger with NHS Quality Improvement Scotland and the Care Commission.

S3W-18546 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to celebrate Christmas.

S3W-18547 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive how many Scottish ministers have official engagements in Scotland on Hogmanay.

S3W-18548 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list the official engagements of each minister during the 20 December 2008 to 4 January 2009 parliamentary recess.

S3W-18549 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what estimate has been made of the cost of dealing with snow on the roads this winter; what account has been taken of such cost in the local government settlement, and whether there is any contingency provision if the estimate is exceeded.

S3W-18550 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what gifts the First Minister has received since January 2008.

S3W-18551 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive which daily and weekly newspapers and magazines are purchased regularly for ministers.

S3W-18552 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive how many of its employees offered to take a designated St Andrew’s Day holiday as one of their public holidays this year.

S3W-18553 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive how many civil servants will be on official visits during the Christmas period.

S3W-18554 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive how many civil servants visited (a) Iceland, (b) Norway, (c) Ireland and (d) Denmark in 2008.

S3W-18555 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide details of any planned visits by civil servants to (a) Iceland, (b) Norway, (c) Ireland and (d) Denmark in 2009.

S3W-18556 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what change will be made to its procurement practices in light of VAT rising to 21.5% in Ireland.

S3W-18557 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what overseas official visits the First Minister plans to make in the next six months.

S3W-18558 Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to enter into negotiations with the British Dental Association with a view to bringing dentists back into the NHS.

S3W-18559 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will introduce a minimum age of two years for the national surveillance programme for Clostridium difficile associated disease.

S3W-18560 Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects Scottish Enterprise to publish an economic development strategy for broadcasting, as recommended by the Scottish Broadcasting Commission.

S3W-18561 Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive, in the absence of Creative Scotland, who is playing a leadership role in bringing together broadcasters, production companies, economic development bodies, skills agencies and the further and higher education sectors to work collaboratively in delivering what is required to ensure a thriving creative-content sector, in line with the recommendation of the Scottish Broadcasting Commission.

S3W-18562 Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is ensuring that Skills Development Scotland, Skillset and the further and higher education sectors are positioned to anticipate changes in the skills requirement in the fast-moving broadcasting environment and realign their activities accordingly to avoid skills gaps, in line with the recommendation of the Scottish Broadcasting Commission.

S3W-18563 Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding it is making available in 2009-10 to Skillset, either directly or through Scottish Screen or any other agency, to support the development of improved training and continuing professional development in the creative industries.

S3W-18564 Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many landlords have applied to the Landlord Registration Scheme and been refused, broken down by local authority area.

S3W-18565 Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is publicising the Landlord Registration Scheme to prospective tenants.

S3W-18566 Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many landlords have been removed from the register for the Landlord Registration Scheme, broken down by local authority.

S3W-18567 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive how many dwellings there were in each of the last five years.

S3W-18568 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive how many dwellings there were with a single adult discount in each of the last five years.

S3W-18569 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people were convicted of rape in each of the last four years.

S3W-18570 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people were prosecuted for rape in each of the last four years.

S3W-18571 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people were charged with rape in each of the last four years.

S3W-18572 Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to protect jobs in Edinburgh from the current economic downturn given the large number of city residents employed in the banking sector.
