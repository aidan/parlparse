Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

*S2M-4223 Chris Ballance: Takeover of Ottakar’s by Waterstone’s—That the Parliament believes that the provisional decision by the Competition Commission to allow the takeover of Ottakar’s by Waterstone’s is misguided, ignores the distinctive nature of the Scottish book market and disregards the evidence given to the commission by the Cross Party Group on Scottish Writing and Publishing, which made clear its belief that a merger would both limit consumer choice and diminish opportunities for Scottish writers to become established, and urges the Minister for Tourism, Culture and Sport to lobby the Competition Commission to seek to have this decision reversed before it is made final.

Supported by: Shiona Baird*, Mr Kenny MacAskill*, Mike Pringle*

*S2M-4222 Mr Tom McCabe: Local Electoral Administration and Registration Services (Scotland) Bill: Financial Resolution—That the Parliament, for the purposes of any Act of the Scottish Parliament resulting from the Local Electoral Administration and Registration Services (Scotland) Bill, agrees to—

(a)  any increase in expenditure of a kind referred to in paragraph 3(b)(iii) of Rule 9.12 of the Parliament’s Standing Orders; and

(b)  any charges or payments in relation to which paragraph 4 of that Rule applies, arising in consequence of the Act.

S2M-4221 Linda Fabiani on behalf of the European and External Relations Committee: The European Commission’s Work Programme 2006—That the Parliament notes the European Commission’s Legislative and Work Programme for 2006 and its importance for the work of the Parliament and its committees and to Scotland.

S2M-4220 Shona Robison: Caledonia Youth Funding Cuts—That the Parliament notes with concern the funding cuts to Caledonia Youth in Dundee; understands that Caledonia Youth provides sexual health education and advice to Dundee’s youths and vulnerable adults; notes that in 2003-04 Dundee recorded the highest rate of teenage pregnancy at 64.4 per 1,000 against the national average of 42.4 per 1,000; recognises the excellent work carried out by the Corner Project, but believes that Dundee requires more, not fewer, services to tackle its teenage pregnancy problems; further notes that the closure of this facility will not only mean that Tayside will be losing five highly skilled professionals but that the young people who rely on this service will be missing out on much needed support, and calls on NHS Tayside to reconsider this decision.

S2M-4219 Donald Gorrie on behalf of the Procedures Committee: Procedures relating to Crown Appointments—That the Parliament notes the Procedures Committee’s 2nd Report 2006 (Session 2), Procedures relating to Crown appointments (SP Paper 515); endorses its conclusions and recommendations, and agrees that the changes to Standing Orders set out in Annexe A to the report be made with effect from 20 April 2006.

Supported by: Karen Gillon*

S2M-4218 Brian Adam on behalf of the Interests of Members of the Scottish Parliament Bill Committee: Interests of Members of the Scottish Parliament Bill—That the Parliament agrees that the Interests of Members of the Scottish Parliament Bill be passed.

S2M-4216 Richard Lochhead: The Spiralling Cost of the Nuclear Clean-Up—That the Parliament notes with great concern the news that the clean-up cost for existing nuclear facilities is set to rise by £14 billion to £70 billion; believes that this highlights that nuclear power is expensive as well as dangerous and dirty; considers that Scotland has no need for new nuclear power stations which, according to the UK Government-appointed Committee on Radioactive Waste Management, if built, would produce radioactive waste nine times stronger than currently exists and will cost billions of pounds to both build and clean up, and also believes that we should reject Labour’s nuclear ambitions, while promoting clean and safe energy technologies with which Scotland has the potential to lead the world.

Supported by: Bruce Crawford, Frances Curran, Mr Stewart Maxwell*, Jim Mather*, Linda Fabiani*, Shiona Baird*, Mr Adam Ingram*, Brian Adam*, Rob Gibson*, Chris Ballance*, Roseanna Cunningham*, Tommy Sheridan*, Mr Kenny MacAskill*, Patrick Harvie*, Stewart Stevenson*

S2M-4215 Carolyn Leckie: Solidarity with Local Government Workers in Their Fight to Defend Their Pensions—That the Parliament congratulates the trade unions and their members for having the courage and strength to stand up to government attacks on their contractual pension rights and entitlements; admires the show of solidarity displayed by over 200,000 workers in Scotland and over one million more across Britain; regrets that the actions of the Scottish Executive and the Westminster Government have led to strike action being necessary; believes that the Executive should reconsider its stance on the legal position of the Rule of 85, given the conflicting advice from COSLA and UNISON and the contradictory statements of the Westminster Government; further believes that the Executive should reconsider its position in relation to ensuring that local government workers suffer no detriment in terms of their pension rights or entitlements; considers that, as a matter of urgency, the Executive should publish its proposals for resolution of this dispute, whether or not the Westminster Government approves, and believes that the only fair and just way to settle this dispute is for the Executive and employers to offer a solution acceptable to local government trade union members.

Supported by: Campbell Martin, Ms Sandra White, Frances Curran, Rosie Kane, Donald Gorrie, Tommy Sheridan, Ms Rosemary Byrne*

S2M-4214 Donald Gorrie: Caledonia Youth—That the Parliament notes with regret that decisions by NHS Greater Glasgow and NHS Tayside have resulted in the closure of Caledonia Youth clinics and sexual health education and advice services, first in Glasgow and now in Dundee; further notes that these services have been widely praised, including by HM Inspectorate of Education, as providing much-needed individual support to young people facing unplanned pregnancy, relationship problems and issues of sexuality and low self-esteem and proving successful in reducing such problems among Caledonia Youth’s clients, and urges the Scottish Executive and the Parliament to show that they take seriously the Executive’s national sexual health strategy, Respect and Responsibility, by ensuring that Caledonia Youth is sustained financially and allowed to contribute towards the national policy.

S2M-4213 Shiona Baird: DC Thomson Takeover of Aberdeen Journals—That the Parliament notes the takeover by DC Thomson of Aberdeen Journals, publisher of the Press & Journal and the Evening Express; welcomes this development as securing Scottish ownership of Scottish-based newspapers; congratulates DC Thomson on avoiding any major staffing cuts, despite recent drastic cuts in staffing levels at other national and regional newspapers; urges DC Thomson to take all measures possible to secure current levels of employment, and wishes the company and its staff a very prosperous future.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie

S2M-4212 Shiona Baird: More Overseas Success for Scottish Marine Renewables—That the Parliament congratulates Inverness-based company, Wavegen, on the successful development of the Limpet onshore wave energy device; welcomes the news that this pioneering technology could be installed in a harbour development in northern Spain; hopes that there will soon be significant numbers of both onshore and offshore wave energy devices commercially installed in Scottish waters, and calls on the Scottish Executive to commit greater financial resources to support the development of the Scottish marine renewable energy sector that promises so much for the Scottish economy.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie

S2M-4211 Maureen Macmillan: Publication of a Consultation Paper on the Proposed UK Marine Bill—That the Parliament notes the publication of a consultation paper on the proposed UK Marine Bill by Her Majesty’s Government; welcomes this step forward towards comprehensive marine legislation for the United Kingdom including for many reserved issues in Scottish waters; encourages as many stakeholders from Scotland as possible to participate in the consultation exercise; commends the consultation paper’s recognition of the devolution context, in particular the possibility of devolving further or new powers; further commends the parallel work being carried out by the Scottish Executive under the marine and coastal strategy, Seas the Opportunity, and being further developed by the Ministerial Advisory Group; supports this advisory group’s work on identifying any need for parallel legislation in Scotland, and urges those responsible for the legislative programme after May 2007 to accord a high priority to marine legislation in Scotland to complement that being delivered at UK level.

S2M-4178<> Rob Gibson: Unchecked Spiral in Land Prices—That the Parliament notes that prices paid for Scottish estates have outperformed stock market values over the past 25 years; also notes that property prices for farms, houses and flats have risen in consequence of spiralling land prices, with especially negative effects in the Highlands and Islands, so that new entrants to farming and potential house buyers cannot relate the prices being asked to the income and wages which they can realistically hope to earn; regrets the lack of a land value tax in Scotland to correct the inflation of unearned increases in land prices; therefore considers that the Scottish Executive should bring forward a scheme for land value tax; recalls that this was not dismissed but merely postponed in the first range of land reform proposals until adequate research had been conducted, and believes that the Executive should ensure that Scots have a realistic opportunity to access affordable land for both homes and business development in their own country.
