Business Bulletin No. 104/2002

Monday 24 June 2002

Section E : Written questions lodged on 21 June 2002
  

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S1W-26976 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive what the procedure is for electing people to the Older People's Advisory Group.

S1W-26977 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people took part in the most recent election to the Older People's Advisory Group.

S1W-26989 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive what the effect will be on the leadership of the Beatson Oncology Centre when the current secondment of Dr Bryson comes to a close at the end of September 2002.

S1W-26990 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many seizures of each illegal drug category there were in (a) 1997-98, (b) 1998-99, (c) 1999-2000 and (d) 2000-01, broken down by police force area.

S1W-26991 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how much financial assistance was allocated for the rehabilitation of drugs misusers in (a) 1997-98, (b) 1998-99, (c) 1999-2000 and (d) 2000-01, broken down by local authority area.

S1W-26992 Miss Annabel Goldie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the recent resignation of the board of Border Biofuels Ltd will have any impact on the contract awarded to the company under the Scottish Renewables Obligation.

S1W-26993 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Minister for Rural Affairs will reply to my letter of 18 March 2002, which was acknowledged by his office on 25 March 2002.

S1W-26994 Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what the current cost per page is of distributing its press releases; how many press releases, including corrections, recall notices, re-issued releases and invitations for media representatives to interview ministers, it has distributed since July 1999; what the total cost has been of distributing these from July 1999 to the present date, and how many of them received any form of media coverage.

S1W-26995 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking in respect of smoking in enclosed public places that are not covered by the Scottish Voluntary Charter on Smoking in Public Places.

S1W-26996 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take if the Scottish Voluntary Charter on Smoking in Public Places target of 49% of licensed premises having non-smoking provision is not met.

S1W-26997 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any ministers attended the recent European summit in Seville.

S1W-26998 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any analysis has been made of the potential impact on Scottish culture and identity of the Communications Bill and, if so, what conclusions have been drawn.

S1W-26999 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Scottish Prison Service intends to follow the good practice guidelines on working with perpetrators of violence against women developed by RESPECT in April 2001 when developing custodial programmes for men convicted of violence against their partners.

S1W-27000 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any research has been carried out to evaluate the usefulness of hair analysis in detecting drugs in a person's system in order to provide corroborating evidence when a drug-assisted sexual assault has been alleged.

S1W-27001 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive how long it takes voluntary organisations to enrol with the Central Registered Body Scotland.

S1W-27002 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive how long it takes for checks on volunteers in the voluntary sector working with children, young people and vulnerable adults to be processed by Disclosure Scotland.

S1W-27003 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive how information regarding the establishment of the Central Registered Body Scotland was transmitted to voluntary organisations
