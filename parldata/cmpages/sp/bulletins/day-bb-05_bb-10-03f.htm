Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday.  That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (u).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an “R”.

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm).  A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-3371 Bill Butler: The Deportation of Drumchapel High School Pupils—That the Parliament expresses its deep regret at the deportation of Drumchapel High School pupils, Nimet and Saida Vucaj, on Thursday 29 September 2005 to Kosovo from London Stansted Airport following the rejection of their family’s asylum application; deprecates the insensitive manner in which immigration officials apparently handled this part of the process; expresses its sympathy for the pupils and staff of Drumchapel High School whose feelings of anger and dismay at this appalling news regarding what has happened to their friends and fellow pupils can scarcely be imagined; acknowledges the First Minister’s commitment, expressed in the Parliament on 22 September 2005, to initiate discussions with the Home Office to agree a protocol which will cover all removals of children and young people in Scotland, and exhorts the Scottish Executive as a matter of urgency, given this recent distressing case, to expedite the formulation of such a protocol with Westminster and negotiate a moratorium on any further such dawn raids until such a protocol has been agreed.

Supported by: Christine Grahame*, Susan Deacon*, Shiona Baird*, Patrick Harvie*, Dr Elaine Murray*

*S2M-3370 Michael McMahon: Scottish Executive/Home Office Protocol on the Removal of Asylum Seekers from Detention in Scotland—That the Parliament notes that, while asylum is a matter reserved to Her Majesty’s Government, the Scottish Executive has responsibilities in respect of the welfare of children; welcomes the acknowledgement from the First Minister that we must take a responsible approach to these important matters; notes the positive impact children of asylum seekers have in enhancing the cultural diversity of Scotland’s schools, and the effect of removal of those children on classmates left behind; recognises the need to establish a protocol with the Home Office on the way that failed asylum seeker families are removed from detention in Scotland; requests that responsible departments within the Executive discuss with the Home Office an appropriate role in planning for deportations involving children, and further requests that discussions take place between immigration officials and local officials responsible for child welfare, in order to ensure that the dignity of, and respect for, all persons is upheld.

*S2M-3369 Michael McMahon: Hamilton Academical Football Club—That the Parliament congratulates Hamilton Academical Football Club for winning a place in the final of the Bells Challenge Cup against St Mirren, to be held at New Broomfield on Sunday 6 November 2005; notes that this means a great deal to the fans of the club, coming as it does after a long struggle back to the top divisions of Scottish football; encourages the entire town and people of South Lanarkshire to get behind the team, and urges as many people from the town as possible to go along to the match.

S2M-3368 Mr Mark Ruskell: Climate Change Emissions Figures—That the Parliament considers that the apparent reduction in greenhouse gas pollution since 1990, from 5.7% last year to 10.1% this year, as announced by the Scottish Executive during the Environment and Rural Development Committee debate on its inquiry report on climate change, was because of a change in the way the emissions were calculated and not because of any reduction in emissions last year; notes, indeed, that there was no reduction in Scotland’s carbon dioxide emissions and that there was a less than 0.5% reduction in total greenhouse gas emissions in the last year, and calls on the Executive to act on the recommendations of the Environment and Rural Development Committee’s 5th Report, 2005 (Session 2): Report on Inquiry into Climate Change.

S2M-3367 Ms Rosemary Byrne: Pesticide Safety—That the Parliament notes the recent publication of Crop Spraying and the Health of Residents and Bystanders by the Royal Commission on Environmental Pollution, which highlighted the potentially damaging effects of pesticides on our health; recognises the potential conflicts of interest within the current system and the need to revise the way the Pesticides Safety Directorate operates, and calls on the Scottish Executive to institute a fundamental overhaul of the risk assessment of pesticides, a monitoring programme for health and exposure that will determine the real scale of the problem, a mandatory right for the public to be notified of which pesticides are being used, both in advance and with on-site signage, and to introduce buffer no-spray zones as a precautionary measure.

Supported by: Mark Ballard*

S2M-3366 Mr Brian Monteith: Scottish Poetry Library—That the Parliament notes the success of the Scottish Poetry Library’s Poet in Public poetry and architecture competition which saw around 1,000 poems entered from schools across Scotland; congratulates students S1 to S3 category winner, Marcus Kernohan from Skye’s Portree High School, for his poem on the Scott Monument and pupils P3 to P7 winner, Amy Green from Dean Park Primary School, Balerno, for “The Brewery’s Friends”, a poem on the Scottish Parliament; further congratulates the Scottish Poetry Library on its efforts in engaging the public in poetry and in recognising the role which poetry can play in developing creative skills in younger people, and hopes that next year’s Poet in the Past 2006 competition, supported by Edinburgh World Heritage Trust, the Royal Commission on the Ancient and Historical Monuments of Scotland, the Architectural Heritage Society of Scotland and Historic Scotland, will be as great a success in encouraging today’s younger people to become tomorrow’s Scottish poets.

S2M-3365 Dr Jean Turner: Provision of Papers in Alternative Formats to Print—That the Parliament wishes to recognise the work being done by the Scottish Executive in including blind and partially-sighted people in its consultation processes by transcribing print to both Braille and audio formats and commends the role of Cue and Review Recording Services in this process; acknowledges the important role that Executive funding has played in developing Cue and Review’s website which includes an automated quote tool; recognises, however, that provision of material in audio, Braille and large print formats is insufficient unless it publicises that this information is available in these formats, and calls on the Executive to ensure that all future publicity carries this information to encourage blind and partially-sighted people to ask for consultation papers in their preferred format.

Supported by: Mr Mark Ruskell*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Jackie Baillie*, Christine Grahame*, Shiona Baird*, Ms Sandra White*

S2M-3364 Mr Jamie McGrigor: Royal Navy Scottish Schools 7s—That the Parliament notes the success of the inaugural Royal Navy Scottish Schools 7s which took place at Murrayfield on Wednesday 28 September 2005, with 40 teams from schools across Scotland competing; congratulates Aberdeen’s Robert Gordon’s College on winning an exciting final 28-19 against Selkirk High School; further congratulates the players, coaching staff and spectators from all the schools that took part, the Scottish Rugby Union for staging the competition and the Royal Navy for supporting the event, and hopes that rugby will continue to grow in Scotland and that some of the players in this year’s Schools 7s will be part of a Scotland international squad successful in bringing the 2009 IRB Rugby World Cup Sevens title back to the home of sevens rugby.

Supported by: Mary Scanlon, Alex Johnstone, Murdo Fraser, Mr Ted Brocklebank, Bill Aitken, Margaret Mitchell, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton, Phil Gallie, Mr Brian Monteith, Mrs Nanette Milne

S2M-3363 Dr Elaine Murray: Work of the Canonbie Youth Group—That the Parliament congratulates the Canonbie Youth Group on receiving a Standing Up to Antisocial Behaviour award on 5 October 2005; welcomes the participation of two members of the group in the “Protecting Communities: The Antisocial Behaviour etc. (Scotland) Act one year on” conference on 3 October 2005; believes that the work of the group is an excellent example of young people empowering themselves to take a stance against anti-social behaviour by organising events for young people in the Canonbie and Langholm area; wishes the group well in its efforts to raise funds for an all-weather sports pitch for Canonbie, and applauds its involvement with Marie Curie Cancer Care in organising the planting of 26,000 daffodil bulbs to form a “Roadway of Hope” along the B6357 from Canonbie to Rowanburn on Saturday 8 October 2005.

S2M-3362 Robin Harper: NICE Report on Drug Treatments for Children—That the Parliament congratulates the British Psychological Society’s (BPS) Faculty for Children and Young People on its successful conference on child psychology held in Edinburgh on 15 and 16 September 2005; notes, in relation to this conference, the conclusions of the National Institute for Clinical Excellence (NICE) report published on 28 September 2005 in relation to the provision of drug treatments to young children suffering from depression, conclusions that clearly recommend maximising of the benefits of using the skills of clinical psychologists before using drugs, however safe and effective these treatments may be, and calls on the Scottish Executive to make the necessary resources available to the NHS in order to implement such a change in health policy, in the interests of the mental health of all young people, and to take note of other conclusions from the September BPS conference, especially in relation to early recognition of, and pre-emptive treatments for, children who are likely to develop mental health problems, ensuring that the principles outlined in the National Framework for Children and Young People’s Mental Health, due to be launched in October, are consistent with this and that efforts to improve and expand the workforce so as to deliver appropriate psychological interventions early on in the problem cycle are resourced adequately.

S2M-3361 Mr Andrew Welsh: Angus Council and the A92 Road—That the Parliament congratulates SNP-controlled Angus Council, which has just successfully completed the dualling of the A92 road between Dundee and Arbroath; further congratulates Angus Council for undertaking the largest road project by local government in Scotland; commends Angus Council for keeping the road open 24/7 during construction; praises Angus Council and the contractors for completing the project seven months ahead of schedule and within budget, and welcomes the improvements in local infrastructure which will offer massive economic benefits to the local area and replace a road system where injury and accident rates were 50% higher than the national average with a modern road system offering safe and efficient driving conditions suited for the 21st century.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson, Alex Neil

S2M-3360 Dr Sylvia Jackson: Free Use of MECS in Stirling—That the Parliament notes the inaccuracy and misinformation in motion S2M-3251 by Brian Monteith regarding the decision of Stirling Council to introduce an annual charge of £52 for use of the Mobile Emergency Care System (MECS) as community alarms do not fall within the definition of free personal and nursing care as set out in section 2(28) of the Regulation of Care (Scotland) Act 2001 and congratulates Stirling Council on its provision of a free MECS service to all of its most vulnerable residents, while also recognising that the additional provision of this community alarm service makes a valuable contribution to the quality of life for those less vulnerable residents and their families who can use MECS at a nominal cost.

S2M-3359 Mr Stewart Maxwell: Dr Jeffrey Wigand - “The Insider”—That the Parliament welcomes the visit of the internationally renowned anti-smoking campaigner, Dr Jeffrey Wigand, to Scotland; applauds his bravery in deciding to expose the lies and deceit of the tobacco industry despite threats against him; supports his comments on the need to continue the battle against the tobacco industry; thanks him for his support of the Parliament’s decision to ban smoking in public places in Scotland; is concerned by the fact that approximately one-third of fire deaths in Scotland are caused by cigarettes, and agrees with him that Scotland should follow the example of Canada, New York and Vermont by legislating to make all cigarettes sold in Scotland “fire-safe” in order to reduce the fire deaths, injuries and damage to property that are caused by cigarettes.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson, Richard Lochhead, Mr Kenny MacAskill, Brian Adam, Rob Gibson, Alasdair Morgan, Eleanor Scott, Mark Ballard, Shiona Baird, Mike Pringle, Alex Neil, Pauline McNeill*, Chris Ballance*

S2M-3358 Patrick Harvie: Immediate Suspension on Dawn Raids—That the Parliament reiterates its serious concern over the unnecessary and inhumane methods used by the UK Government in the removal and detention of children prior to deportation, which cause fear and distress to the children involved; congratulates all campaigners who oppose this disgraceful treatment of children on their work; recognises the First Minister’s commitment to intervene on this matter and his intention to initiate discussions with the Home Office to establish a protocol which will cover all removals of children and young people in Scotland; supports Positive Action In Housing’s call for an immediate suspension on dawn raids carried out by immigration officers on families with children whilst this protocol is established, and calls on the Scottish Executive to support this plea for a moratorium on dawn raids in order to demonstrate its willingness to safeguard the welfare of the children concerned.

Supported by: Chris Ballance, Mr Kenny MacAskill, Campbell Martin, Mike Rumbles, Linda Fabiani, Robin Harper, Eleanor Scott, Shiona Baird, Mark Ballard, Mr Stewart Maxwell, Mike Pringle, Dr Jean Turner, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Bill Butler, Brian Adam, Margo MacDonald, Mr Mark Ruskell, Alex Neil, Dennis Canavan*, Ms Sandra White*

S2M-3357 Michael Matheson: Campaign for a Scottish Olympic Team—That the Parliament welcomes the launch of the Campaign for a Scottish Olympic Team; notes that an overwhelming majority of Scots, 78%, would like to see Scotland send its own Olympic team to London in 2012 which would act as a catalyst to inspire many young Scottish athletes to represent their nation and pursue sporting excellence; recognises the fact that there is no technical reason why Scotland cannot have its own Olympic team, and calls on politicians from across the political spectrum to get behind the Scottish Olympic Team campaign.

Supported by: Mr John Swinney, Jim Mather, Rob Gibson, Mr Bruce McFee, Linda Fabiani, Alex Neil

S2M-3356 Dennis Canavan: St Andrew’s Day Bank Holiday (Scotland) Bill—That the Parliament agrees to the general principles of the St Andrew’s Day Bank Holiday (Scotland) Bill.

*S2M-3356.1 Murdo Fraser: St Andrew’s Day Bank Holiday (Scotland) Bill—As an amendment to motion (S2M-3356) in the name of Dennis Canavan, insert at end “but, in so doing, believes that a new bank holiday established as result of the Bill should not be so established unless an existing bank holiday is no longer specified as a bank holiday.”

S2M-3355# Maureen Macmillan: Drink Driving in the Highlands—That the Parliament notes with concern the continued high incidence of drink driving offences in the Northern Constabulary area and, in particular, the substantial increase in the statistics for driving while unfit through drink or drugs; notes that in one recent 19-day period there were 19 arrests for drink driving in the Ross, Cromarty and Skye Command area alone; further notes that the profile of drink drivers tends to be middle-aged rather than young, and believes that the Scottish Executive should consider how it can better reinforce the message that drink driving is a criminal offence which can result in severe injury and loss of life.

Supported by: Sarah Boyack, Robin Harper, Mrs Nanette Milne, Jackie Baillie*, Eleanor Scott*, Chris Ballance*

S2M-3354 John Swinburne: Winter-Related Deaths—That the Parliament notes with great concern that experts are forecasting an extremely severe winter; agrees that free central heating is a very laudable flagship policy but notes that, sadly, it is not being delivered at the point of need, as borne out by the fact that over 8,000 senior citizens in Scotland have succumbed to winter-related deaths in the last three winters and, for example in Glasgow, where one-in-36 senior citizens are at risk of dying of cold-related deaths, that, out of 3,297 applications for free central heating, 65% of applications have been rejected and only 1,177 systems installed; notes that this is either due to too high criteria being applied to these applications or perhaps too much red tape, resulting in refusals for the installation of new central heating systems, and further notes that, if this 65% rejection rate is replicated across the country, the deplorable record of deaths due to winter-related cold will inevitably result in a further increase in these statistics which are already at an unacceptable level.

S2M-3353# Rob Gibson: Celebrating the Féisean Movement in the Highlands and Islands—That the Parliament welcomes the findings of a report commissioned by Highlands and Islands Enterprise and the Scottish Arts Council on the Féisean Movement, which was founded on the Isle of Barra in 1981, showing the important contribution of the movement to the tangible economic and demographic benefits associated with our traditional heritage that can build on the renewed sense of pride and of vibrancy amongst young people in the Highlands and Islands and help to strengthen bonds between the people growing up in the area and their sense of place; congratulates all those who have participated in the various Féisean and especially those who helped start off Féisean throughout the Highlands and Islands, and considers that the Scottish Executive should recognise these findings by securing further and greater funding for the movement to safeguard its future for many years to come.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson, Michael Matheson, Jim Mather, Mr Frank McAveety, Richard Lochhead, Robin Harper, Roseanna Cunningham, Mr John Swinney, Eleanor Scott, Shiona Baird, Cathy Peattie, Fergus Ewing, Mrs Margaret Ewing, Dr Jean Turner, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Mr Adam Ingram, Mary Scanlon, Alex Neil*

S2M-3351 Fiona Hyslop: Lothian and Borders Fire Services—That the Parliament expresses concern at the Lothian and Borders Fire and Rescue Services Service Improvement Plan 2005-10 which proposes cuts in night-time and weekend cover for the Livingston area, served by the Craigshill fire station, from two full-time tenders to one full-time tender and one tender served by retained firemen; notes that this will lead to slower response times with implications for safety and rescue opportunities and service generally across the whole of West Lothian; further notes that this service improvement plan is the first of the plans required under the recent Fire (Scotland) Act 2005 for each and every fire and rescue board in Scotland and expresses its fears that, should reduced fire cover at night and dependency on a retained service be approved by the Labour-dominated Lothian and Borders board, other boards across the country may take this as the green light to initiate similar cuts when they come to produce their service improvement plans, and commends local community campaigners in Livingston for highlighting the issue and its implications for the safety of their own and other communities.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson, Michael Matheson

S2M-3350 Michael Matheson: Scottish Opera—That the Parliament notes the comments of Duncan McGhie, former Chairman of Scottish Opera and Scottish Ballet; agrees with his comments that Scotland should be funding its own opera company, and strongly resists any attempt to merge Scottish Opera with another opera company outside Scotland which would threaten the independence of the production of opera in Scotland.

Supported by: Bruce Crawford, Linda Fabiani, Donald Gorrie, Mr Adam Ingram, Richard Lochhead, Mike Rumbles, Mr John Swinney, Mr Stewart Maxwell, Mike Pringle, Dr Jean Turner, Alex Neil*, Ms Sandra White*

S2M-3349 Alex Neil: Congratulations to the Cross-Party Group on the Scottish Economy—That the Parliament congratulates the Cross-Party Group on the Scottish Economy on its second anniversary; recognises the success of the group in improving the levels of debate on the Scottish economy inside and outside the Parliament; thanks all those MSPs, parliamentary staff and individuals and organisations beyond the Parliament who have contributed to the group’s work; notes the in-kind support of HBOS plc through the secondment of the group’s secretary, and encourages all parts of the Parliament to keep engaging with the stakeholders of the Scottish economy to help improve policy and debate.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie, Stewart Stevenson R, Michael Matheson, Mr Brian Monteith

S2M-3345 Mr Brian Monteith: Auld Enemies Cup 2005—That the Parliament notes that the 18th Scotland v England Blind Golf International was held at Hamilton Golf Club from 20 to 24 September 2005 and congratulates all those whose dedicated work behind the scenes made this event possible; further congratulates both the sets of players and their guides on being selected for their respective teams, especially the Scotland team of captain John Miller from Glasgow with guide Ian Sneddon, Cameron McDiarmid from Edinburgh with guide Jim McDiarmid, Alistair Reid from Stonehaven with guide Jock Lawson, Kenneth Freeman from Glasgow with guide Corinne Freeman, John Imrie from Aberdeen with guide Brian Thomson, Stuart Wilkie from Auchterarder with guide Bill Ferguson, Gerry Kelly from Troon with guide Mary Kelly, Jim Eadie from Glasgow with guide Mat Gallagher, Jim Gales from Cupar with guide Paul Shepherd, Myles Clark from Brechin with guide Jill Clarke, Sam Sloan from Edinburgh with guide Scott Sloan, Iain Prime from North Berwick with guide Steve White and Peter Philip from Edinburgh with guide Jimmy Philip, for winning the Auld Enemies Cup 15 and a half to eight and a half in order to take an eight to seven lead in matches won, and believes it is imperative that, as the profile of disabled sports grows, there are events such as the Auld Enemies Cup which allow disabled people the fullest opportunity to play both team and individual sports.

S2M-3343u Ms Sandra White: Sex Discrimination - Ending the Public Sector Pay Gap—That the Parliament notes that November will mark the 30th anniversary of the passing of the Sex Discrimination Act 1975 which, at the time, represented a watershed for equal opportunities campaigners; notes that, despite the Equal Pay Act 1970 and an equal pay strategy agreed by unions and local authorities in 1999, more than 50,000 female local authority workers in Scotland have been paid less than their entitlement and are now threatening to take court action over their claims; notes that the total cost could be as much as £500 million unless an amicable solution can be found by local authorities; notes that the Scottish Executive failed to issue any guidelines to local authorities on the issue of fair pay; notes that it is often women who are among the lowest-paid workers in local authorities working often part-time and that currently their collective bargaining punch is seen as limited by employers; further notes that independent nations, like Norway and Sweden, place a high premium on childcare, removing employment barriers for pregnant women and recognise the need for flexibility among female workers as a means to narrowing the gender gap, and considers that Scottish ministers should engage with local authorities and ensure that resources are in place so that women are fully paid and properly valued.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson R, Michael Matheson, Donald Gorrie, Mr David Davidson, Mr Brian Monteith
