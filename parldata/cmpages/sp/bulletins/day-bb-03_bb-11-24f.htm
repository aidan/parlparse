Business Bulletin No. 160/2003

Monday 24 November 2003

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-645 Nicola Sturgeon: Proceeds of Crime Act 2002-That the Parliament believes that all money recovered in Scotland under the Proceeds of Crime Act 2002 should be retained in Scotland and calls on the Scottish Executive to make urgent representations on this matter to Her Majesty's Government.

*S2M-643 Donald Gorrie: Official Opening of RECAP Recycling Project in Cumbernauld-That the Parliament congratulates the management and staff of the RECAP recycling project in Cumbernauld on its formal opening on 28 November 2003; welcomes the role it will play in providing affordable access to recycled furniture and white goods for disadvantaged sectors of communities across North Lanarkshire; commends the initiative of Cumbernauld Unemployed Workers Centre in establishing the project, and welcomes the positive contribution that RECAP will make to North Lanarkshire's recycling efforts.

S2M-642# Brian Adam: Social Work Review-That the Parliament notes with concern the shortage of social workers throughout Scotland and considers that the Scottish Executive should initiate a McCrone-type review to look at social workers' pay and conditions in order to attract and retain more social workers.

S2M-641 Rosie Kane: Female Genital Mutilation and Asylum Seekers-That the Parliament notes the plight of the asylum seeker named as "Margaret" featured on "Newsnight Scotland" on 19 November 2003; notes the continuation of the brutal practice of female genital mutilation in Margaret's homeland of Sierra Leone and the extreme danger that Margaret's daughters would face if they were to be returned there; resolves to oppose by all necessary means the deportation of women and children to countries where female genital mutilation is practised; calls upon the Home Office to end the asylum lottery in which legitimate refugees are refused asylum because of poor legal representation and erratic asylum adjudicators, and resolves to oppose any attempt to detain or deport Margaret and her children when they attend the Glasgow office of the Immigration and Nationality Directorate on 29 November 2003.

Supported by: Carolyn Leckie*, Frances Curran*, Colin Fox*, Mr Mark Ruskell*

S2M-640 Christine Grahame: Boycott-That the Parliament considers that the boycott by the Scottish Executive of the debate on Thursday 20 November 2003 on the subject of world peace demonstrated a considered contempt for the Parliament and democratic debate, particularly given the history of Labour in supporting the war in Iraq without a UN mandate specifically authorising such action, and therefore calls on the First Minister to apologise to the Chamber for this behaviour.

Supported by: Fiona Hyslop, Michael Matheson, Mr Stewart Maxwell, Campbell Martin, Brian Adam, Shiona Baird, Linda Fabiani, Eleanor Scott, Nicola Sturgeon, Carolyn Leckie, Rob Gibson, Patrick Harvie*, Richard Lochhead*, Chris Ballance*, Margo MacDonald*, Frances Curran*, Mrs Margaret Ewing*, Ms Sandra White*

S2M-639# Alex Neil: Scottish Widows and Lloyds TSB-That the Parliament deplores the decision to transfer Scottish Widows' jobs out of Scotland and believes that none of Lloyds TSB's operations in Scotland should be threatened with either closure or downsizing as a consequence of work being transferred abroad.

Supported by: Margo MacDonald*

S2M-638# Mark Ballard: Road Safety-That the Parliament notes the success of the Lothian and Borders Safety Camera Partnership in addressing the problems of speeding and serious road crashes and commends the pilot of 20 mph zones utilising flashing road signs outside seven Edinburgh primary schools in making roads safer for children.

S2M-637# Patrick Harvie: Ministerial Boycott of World Peace Debate-That the Parliament deplores the decision by the Scottish Executive to boycott the debate on world peace and considers that Executive ministers should take part in every Parliamentary debate.

Supported by: Campbell Martin, Nicola Sturgeon, Chris Ballance, Richard Lochhead, Tommy Sheridan, Carolyn Leckie, Christine Grahame, Rosie Kane, Frances Curran, Mr Mark Ruskell, Mark Ballard, Shiona Baird, Rob Gibson, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Linda Fabiani, Eleanor Scott, Mr Stewart Maxwell, Colin Fox*, Margo MacDonald*, Ms Sandra White*

S2M-636# Alex Neil: Pensions-That the Parliament considers that Her Majesty's Government should re-establish the link between retirement pensions and the annual increase in wages.

Supported by: Carolyn Leckie*, Frances Curran*

S2M-635# Alex Neil: Transfer of Reserved Powers-That the Parliament believes that it should petition Her Majesty's Government to introduce the requisite Orders in Council to transfer all reserved powers detailed in Schedule 5 of the Scotland Act 1998 to the Scottish Parliament with immediate effect.

Supported by: Margo MacDonald*, Carolyn Leckie*, Frances Curran*

S2M-634# Alex Neil: Breeding of Animals-That the Parliament expresses concern at the inadequacy of current legislation in respect of the breeding of animals, in particular domestic pets, and believes that urgent action should be taken to deal with this situation.

Supported by: Carolyn Leckie*, Frances Curran*

S2M-633# Alex Neil: Non-numeric Fingerprinting-That the Parliament expresses concern at the introduction of non-numeric fingerprinting evidence by the Scottish Criminal Record Office, its implications for the success rate of convictions and the potential consequential increase in the legal aid budget and considers that the Scottish Executive should review this policy on a cross-departmental basis prior to its implementation.

S2M-632# Mr Jamie Stone: North and West Scotland Care Alliance-That the Parliament recognises the vital role performed by North and West Sutherland Care Alliance through the provision of a home-based respite care service that brings much-needed relief and assistance to carers and those vulnerable individuals who would otherwise be unable to remain in their own homes and also recognises that equitable and adequate funding of the North and West Sutherland Care Alliance by both NHS Highland and Highland Council is essential.

S2M-631# Nicola Sturgeon: Closure of Post Offices in Glasgow-That the Parliament condemns Royal Mail for its decision to donate £1 million to London's bid for the 2012 Olympic Games at the same time as it is proposing to close 20 local post offices in Glasgow, in addition to the 11 already closed since January 2003, and believes that the watchdog body, Postwatch Scotland, should investigate as a matter of urgency whether this decision represents value for money for Royal Mail's customers in Glasgow.

Supported by: Shona Robison, Ms Sandra White, Michael Matheson, Stewart Stevenson, Patrick Harvie, Fergus Ewing, Mike Pringle, Mr Stewart Maxwell, Carolyn Leckie, Eleanor Scott

S2M-630 Nicola Sturgeon: Scottish Legal Aid Board (Employment of Solicitors to Provide Criminal Legal Assistance) Amendment Regulations 2003-That the Justice 2 Committee recommends that nothing further be done under the Scottish Legal Aid Board (Employment of Solicitors to Provide Criminal Legal Assistance) Amendment Regulations 2003 (SSI 2003/511).

S2M-629# George Lyon: Medical Services in the West Highlands-That the Parliament recognises the deep concern of the communities in Fort William and Oban regarding the threat to future provision of acute medical services in the west Highlands; notes that over 3,500 people, so far, have turned out at public meetings to express their opposition to Argyll and Clyde and Highland NHS boards' plans to downgrade Oban and Fort William hospitals to community status; recognises that, if the proposals go ahead, patients from the West Highlands would be forced to travel hundreds of miles to Glasgow or Inverness to access treatment, and therefore believes that the Scottish Executive should reject these plans and give a commitment that the current service provision will continue to be delivered locally to communities in the West Highlands.

S2M-628 Robert Brown: Small Business, Energy and the Environment-That the Parliament welcomes the Energy and Environment SME Toolkit, produced by the Federation of Small Businesses in association with the Scottish Energy Efficiency Office; notes that it is an example of good practice, demonstrating that addressing environmental concerns saves energy and money and reduces the nation's environmental footprint, and recommends the toolkit to all small businesses in Scotland.

Supported by: Iain Smith

S2M-626 Elaine Smith: State Visit to Britain by the President of the United States of America-That the Parliament notes the inappropriate and insensitive timing of the state visit to Britain by the President of the United States of America, George W Bush; believes, however, that this visit can serve as a reminder of the many military and civilian casualties since the commencement of war in Iraq and the many still suffering in the aftermath; expresses solidarity with the United States citizens who opposed the conflict; notes that the wars in Afghanistan and Iraq have exacerbated instability worldwide, and calls for an end to such pre-emptive attacks on some of the world's most vulnerable peoples and, instead, encourages engagement with the United Nations to promote peace, equality and an end to world poverty.

Supported by: Mark Ballard, Christine Grahame, Robin Harper, Mr Mark Ruskell, Michael Matheson, Rosie Kane, Marlyn Glen, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Tommy Sheridan, Eleanor Scott, Cathy Peattie, Chris Ballance, Ms Sandra White, Pauline McNeill

S2M-619 Mr Ted Brocklebank: St. Andrew's Week Celebrations-That the Parliament congratulates the organisers of the St Andrew's Week celebrations that demonstrate the best of traditional Scottish culture through a host of events and activities showing the rich heritage of Scotland's music, arts, crafts and food and pays tribute to the local St Andrew's Society for its dedication in organising cultural and gastronomic tributes to Scotland's patron saint for over twenty years.

Supported by: Iain Smith

S2M-617 Chris Ballance: Closure of Nuclear Power Stations-That the Parliament celebrates the closure of Stade nuclear power station as the beginning of the end of nuclear power in Germany and looks forward to the impending closure of Chapelcross nuclear power station in Dumfriesshire as the start of the end for nuclear power in Scotland.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan

S2M-616 Mr Andy Kerr: Education (Additional Support for Learning) (Scotland) Bill: Financial Resolution-That the Parliament, for the purposes of any Act of the Scottish Parliament resulting from the Education (Additional Support for Learning) (Scotland) Bill, agrees to the following expenditure payable out of the Scottish Consolidated Fund, namely-

(a) any expenditure of the Scottish Ministers in consequence of the Act; and

(b) any increase attributable to the Act in expenditure payable out of the Fund under any other enactment.

S2M-615# Bill Butler: 20 mph Speed Zones Around Schools-That the Parliament congratulates Glasgow City Council on its decision to introduce mandatory 20 mph speed limits outside every school in Glasgow; hopes that other local authorities will follow Glasgow City Council's example in trying to improve the safety of pupils travelling to and from school, and views this as a significant and welcome step in implementing the Scottish Executive's commitment to improve Scotland's record on road traffic accidents, in particular the objective of cutting the number of young people killed and injured on roads each year.

Supported by: Dr Elaine Murray, Eleanor Scott, Tricia Marwick, Chris Ballance, Jackie Baillie, Mr Kenneth Macintosh, Sarah Boyack, Des McNulty, Elaine Smith, Pauline McNeill, Karen Whitefield, Janis Hughes, Johann Lamont, Scott Barrie, Trish Godman, Robert Brown, Mr Alasdair Morrison, Nora Radcliffe, Frances Curran, Mr Jamie Stone, Robin Harper, Dr Jean Turner, Cathie Craigie, Dr Sylvia Jackson, Iain Smith, Tommy Sheridan, Christine May, Christine Grahame, Ms Sandra White, Cathy Peattie, Mark Ballard, Mr Mark Ruskell*

S2M-614 Mr Andy Kerr: Antisocial Behaviour etc. (Scotland) Bill - Financial Resolution-That the Parliament, for the purposes of any Act of the Scottish Parliament resulting from the Antisocial Behaviour etc. (Scotland) Bill, agrees to any increase in expenditure charged on, and any expenditure payable out of, the Scottish Consolidated Fund in consequence of the Act.

S2M-613 Rob Gibson: Condolences to St-Nazaire-That the Parliament sends its condolences to the people of St-Nazaire whose family members were killed or injured on a pre-launch visit while boarding the new liner Queen Mary 2; notes the direct association of the St-Nazaire shipbuilding industry and that of the Firth of Clyde; recalls the toll of human life in building ships, and urges the community to ensure that a successful launch for the vessel will still be marked by the planned conference on 29 November 2003 to commemorate the strong links between shipbuilding on the Clyde and on the estuary of the Loire.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan, Mr Adam Ingram

S2M-612 Roseanna Cunningham: Potential G8 Summit at Gleneagles-That the Parliament notes reports that Gleneagles is being considered as a potential venue for a G8 summit meeting; expresses concern at the disruption this could cause for strategic road and rail links and the negative impact that would have on both the local and national economy; recognises the immense pressures that would be placed on police by the scale of the security operation required; supports the bringing of international events to Scotland but believes that the G8 summit represents a poisoned chalice, and regrets that the decision to consider Gleneagles appears to have been based on a belief amongst the Foreign and Commonwealth Office that Gleneagles is remote.

Supported by: Michael Matheson, Rob Gibson, Campbell Martin, Richard Lochhead, Brian Adam, Mr Bruce McFee, Christine Grahame, Tommy Sheridan, Linda Fabiani, Mr Adam Ingram, Mr Stewart Maxwell

S2M-612.1 Carolyn Leckie: Potential G8 Summit at Gleneagles-As an amendment to motion (S2M-612) in the name of Roseanna Cunningham, leave out from "expresses" to end and insert "and would welcome the opportunity to help organise mass protests in Scotland against the G8 summit, which has established itself as the forum for the world's dominant powers to further entrench their domination and develop ever more imaginative schemes to undermine and manipulate the developing nations of the world and further widen the gap between rich and poor, currently concentrating 90% of the world's abundant wealth and resources in the hands of only 5% of the world's population."

S2M-611 Tommy Sheridan: Threat to Teachers' Pensions-That the Parliament notes Her Majesty's Government's Green Paper Simplicity, Security and Choice: Working and Saving for Retirement on teachers' pensions; is opposed to the proposals which would raise the minimum age for taking an occupational pension from 60 to 65 and will have hugely detrimental effects on the future well-being of teachers and their profession; fears that such plans, if implemented, will deter young people from entering the profession with its highly stressful working environment and will force many long-serving teachers to work for five further years beyond their expected retirement date, only serving to disillusion and dishearten them, given that half the secondary teaching workforce is within 12 years of retirement; believes that these proposals will make it extremely difficult to recruit new teachers, and therefore supports the Scottish School Teachers' Association's campaign to have the proposals overturned.

Supported by: Frances Curran, Carolyn Leckie, Rosie Kane, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Alex Neil, Chris Ballance, Colin Fox*

S2M-610 Alex Fergusson: People, Pensions and Post Offices-That the Parliament notes the House of Commons Trade and Industry Committee report People, Pensions and Post Offices: the impact of 'Direct Payment' on post offices and their customers; commends the recommendations of the report that Her Majesty's Government should revisit a number of areas of policy which could be improved to help to give customer choice as well as protecting post offices; very much regrets, however, the response of Her Majesty's Government which ignores the great majority of the recommendations, and urges the Scottish Executive to encourage Her Majesty's Government to pursue the implementation of the report in order to safeguard the future of Scotland's rural post offices.

Supported by: David Mundell, John Scott, Mr Ted Brocklebank, Mr David Davidson, Murdo Fraser, Donald Gorrie

S2M-609 Mr John Home Robertson: Deployment of Royal Scots to Iraq-That the Parliament expresses strong support to all the soldiers of the 1st Battalion, the Royal Scots, on their deployment for peacekeeping duties in Iraq; recalls the fine service of the first regiment of foot in the British Army in the first Gulf War; endorses the objective of restoring security and helping the Iraqi people to achieve a better future after decades of oppressive government, and looks forward to the successful conclusion of this mission and the safe return of Scottish service personnel to their homes and families.

Supported by: Rhona Brankin

S2M-608 Ross Finnie: The Mink Keeping (Scotland) Order 2003 (SSI 2003/528)-That the Environment and Rural Development Committee recommends that the Mink Keeping (Scotland) Order 2003 (SSI 2003/528) be approved.

Supported by: Allan Wilson

S2M-607 Ross Finnie: The Nitrate Vulnerable Zones (Grants) (Scotland) Amendment Scheme 2003 (SSI 2003/518)-That the Environment and Rural Development Committee recommends that the Nitrate Vulnerable Zones (Grants) (Scotland) Amendment Scheme 2003 (SSI 2003/518) be approved.

Supported by: Allan Wilson

S2M-606# Carolyn Leckie: Scotland's Football Victory-That the Parliament congratulates the national Scotland football team for a stunning victory against the Netherlands in the first leg of the UEFA Euro 2004 play off and sends warmest wishes and encouragement for the second leg; regrets that the game was not available on terrestrial TV; is concerned at the monopoly of TV coverage by satellite television; believes that all Scots should be able to access national games where they choose; is concerned that women, who bear the greatest responsibility for childcare, will be less able to go to a public house and therefore less likely to be able to see these games, and believes that the Scottish Executive should intervene to try to secure universal coverage of all future Scotland games so that all of Scotland can be united in cheering the team on.

Supported by: Frances Curran, Tommy Sheridan, Eleanor Scott, Rosie Kane, Ms Rosemary Byrne

S2M-605 John Swinburne: European Championship Play Offs-That the Parliament congratulates the Scotland football squad on the marvellous performance and result in the first leg play off against the Netherlands at Hampden, particularly the goal from James McFadden, and offers its full support to the team for the second leg in Amsterdam.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan, Alex Neil

S2M-603 Iain Smith on behalf of the Procedures Committee: The Founding Principles of the Parliament-That the Parliament takes note of the previous Procedures Committee's 3rd Report, 2003 (Session 1), The Founding Principles of the Scottish Parliament (SP Paper 818); continues to endorse the four principles of access and participation, equal opportunities, accountability and power sharing as the guiding principles for the Parliament, and encourages all members to play a part in applying these principles throughout the range of their Parliamentary work.

Supported by: Mr Kenneth Macintosh, Murray Tosh, Karen Gillon*

S2M-564# Des McNulty: World AIDS Day 2003-That the Parliament acknowledges the importance of World AIDS Day, 1 December 2003, both for Scotland and for all those nations and peoples most affected by the spread of HIV/AIDS; notes the need to combat HIV/AIDS-related stigma and discrimination which is highlighted by this year's theme, "Do you have time?"; further notes that there are now over 42 million people living with HIV/AIDS, including 50,000 people in the UK and nearly 4,000 people in Scotland; recognises that women account for half of those infected with HIV/AIDS and there are more than 13.2 million AIDS orphans; notes the importance of practical assistance for the poorer countries devastated by HIV/AIDS; believes that pharmaceutical companies and governments should take the steps necessary to allow access to appropriate antiviral treatments to all those who need them; congratulates and applauds all of the agencies that work with those living with HIV/AIDS in Scotland, the UK and worldwide, and encourages each MSP to raise awareness about the disease and work towards its global eradication.

Supported by: Sarah Boyack, Dr Sylvia Jackson, Roseanna Cunningham, Janis Hughes, Bill Butler, Margaret Jamieson, Mr Stewart Maxwell, Pauline McNeill, Alex Neil, Elaine Smith, Carolyn Leckie, Christine May, Scott Barrie, Cathie Craigie, Karen Whitefield, Karen Gillon, Murray Tosh, Susan Deacon, Jackie Baillie, Dr Elaine Murray, Mr Kenneth Macintosh, Marlyn Glen, Margaret Smith, Cathy Peattie, Robin Harper, Chris Ballance, Patrick Harvie, Dr Jean Turner, Ms Sandra White, Frances Curran, Helen Eadie, Donald Gorrie, Tommy Sheridan, Linda Fabiani, Eleanor Scott, Marilyn Livingstone, Rob Gibson, Mr Richard Baker, Mike Pringle, Mr Adam Ingram, Shiona Baird, Irene Oldfather, Iain Smith, Mark Ballard, Michael McMahon

S2M-553# Nicola Sturgeon: European Parliament Seat Numbers-That the Parliament notes the reduction of UK seats in the European Parliament from 87 to 78 to accommodate enlargement of the EU; welcomes the accession of 10 new countries in 2004; believes, however, that Scotland, with no seat on the Council of Europe, no Commissioners and fewer MEPs than comparably-sized independent member states, has little enough influence in the EU, and therefore believes that the Scottish Executive should resist the reduction in Scotland's MEPs from eight to seven.

Supported by: Bruce Crawford, Linda Fabiani, Mrs Margaret Ewing, Stewart Stevenson, Richard Lochhead, Alex Neil, Michael Matheson, Mr Adam Ingram, Ms Sandra White, Campbell Martin, Rob Gibson, Mr Stewart Maxwell, Christine Grahame, Shona Robison, Fiona Hyslop, Jim Mather, Mark Ballard, Patrick Harvie, Eleanor Scott, Chris Ballance, Shiona Baird, Mr Mark Ruskell, Dennis Canavan
