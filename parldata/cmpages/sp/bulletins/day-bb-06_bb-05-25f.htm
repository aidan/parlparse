Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-4453 John Farquhar Munro: Scottish Water Development Constraints—That the Parliament is becoming frustrated by the increasing number of development constraints imposed by Scottish Water in the Highlands and Islands because of potable water supply limitations; notes that the constraints are blocking the construction of much-needed affordable housing and restricting business developments offering employment opportunities; further notes that Scottish Water’s inability to provide a potable water supply means that the new Dunvegan fire station is sitting unused while the local fire officers remain in their old, sub-standard premises; believes that this situation is becoming untenable, and calls on Scottish Water to investigate the feasibility of reactivating old mains supplies and on the Scottish Executive to consider offering grant aid to those developing alternative private water supplies.

*S2M-4452 John Home Robertson: Nuclear Power and a Balanced Energy Policy—That the Parliament endorses the Scottish Executive’s target of generating 40% of electricity from renewables, its promotion of energy efficiency and its stipulation that the safe storage of radioactive waste must be addressed before decisions can be made about new nuclear development; notes that the Committee on Radioactive Waste Management has reported that permanent geological storage is the appropriate solution for safe storage of such waste; believes that reduction of CO2 emissions must be the main priority for planning for the future; notes that fossil fuel power stations in Scotland are emitting 18 million tonnes of CO2 per year; recognises that older power stations will eventually have to be decommissioned; welcomes the Executive’s stated objective of maintaining Scotland’s share of the British electricity generation industry; concurs with the view of respected environmental scientist, Professor James Lovelock, that nuclear energy is "an essential part of a portfolio of energy sources"; notes that in 2001 a Swiss study of large-scale energy sources around the world found nuclear electricity generation to be the safest of all large-scale energy sources; understands the support for nuclear developments in East Lothian, Ayrshire, Dumfriesshire and Caithness, and looks forward to the development of replacement nuclear power stations in Scotland to meet our needs for baseload electricity while reducing emissions of greenhouse gases.

*S2M-4451 Tricia Marwick on behalf of the Waverley Railway (Scotland) Bill Committee: Waverley Railway (Scotland) Bill—That the Parliament agrees that the Waverley Railway (Scotland) Bill be passed.

*S2M-4450 Tricia Marwick on behalf of the Waverley Railway (Scotland) Bill Committee: 1st Report 2006 (Session 2) of the Waverley Railway (Scotland) Bill Committee, Appropriate Assessment on the River Tweed Special Area of Conservation—That the Parliament notes the 1st Report, 2006 (Session 2) of the Waverley Railway (Scotland) Bill Committee, Appropriate Assessment on the River Tweed Special Area of Conservation (SP Paper 553), and agrees that the works proposed in the Bill will not adversely affect the integrity of the River Tweed Special Area of Conservation.

*S2M-4449 Cathy Jamieson: The Draft Management of Offenders etc. (Scotland) Act 2005 (Supplementary Provisions) Order 2006—That the Justice 2 Committee recommends that the Draft Management of Offenders etc. (Scotland) Act 2005 (Supplementary Provisions) Order 2006 be approved.

Supported by: Hugh Henry*

*S2M-4448 Cathy Jamieson: The Draft Advice and Assistance (Assistance By Way of Representation) (Scotland) Amendment Regulations 2006—That the Justice 1 Committee recommends that the Draft Advice and Assistance (Assistance

By Way of Representation) (Scotland) Amendment Regulations 2006 be approved.

Supported by: Hugh Henry*

*S2M-4447 Ms Margaret Curran on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Police, Public Order and Criminal Justice (Scotland) Bill—That the Parliament agrees that, during Stage 3 of the Police, Public Order and Criminal Justice (Scotland) Bill, debate on groups of amendments shall, subject to Rule 9.8.4A, be brought to a conclusion by the time-limits indicated (each time-limit being calculated from when the Stage begins and excluding any periods when other business is under consideration or when the meeting of the Parliament is suspended, other than a suspension following the first division in the morning or afternoon being called, or otherwise not in progress):

Groups 1 to 6: 40 minutes
Groups 7 to 10: 1 hour and 25 minutes
Group 11: 1 hour and 55 minutes
Group 12: 2 hours and 55 minutes
Groups 13 to 19: 3 hours and 25 minutes.

Supported by: George Lyon*

*S2M-4445<> Cathy Peattie: Carers Week 2006—That the Parliament notes that 12 to 18 June is Carers Week 2006; recognises the valuable work of national and local carers’ organisations, such as Carers Scotland and the Falkirk and Clackmannanshire Carers Centre which this year celebrates its 10th anniversary; recognises the importance of supporting carers in employment and enabling them to fulfil their caring and work responsibilities without detriment to their own health and well-being; notes that the health of the Scottish economy depends on the health and well-being of unpaid carers and their ability to juggle work and care; further notes that recent research has shown that 58% of carers who provide a substantial amount of care give up work because of their caring responsibilities, and believes that the Scottish Executive, local government and employers should consider and support the adoption of flexible alternative care solutions and carer-friendly working arrangements to give more carers the opportunity to remain in, or take up, employment.

*S2M-4444# Mr Jamie McGrigor: UCI Mountain Bike World Cup in Fort William—That the Parliament notes that the UCI Mountain Bike World Cup will be hosted on the Nevis Range on 27 and 28 May 2006 and welcomes the event to Fort William for the fourth consecutive year; further notes that the event attracts the sport’s top competitors from across the world, as well as thousands of supporters, and plays a crucial role in the economy of both the town and the surrounding area; congratulates the organisers of the event and all those involved in securing next year’s World Championships, the blue riband event of mountain biking, which will also be held at the Nevis Range, and, in the light of concerns over future funding for hosting UCI World Cup events after 2007, believes that the Scottish Executive should ensure that financial support is in place to allow Fort William to continue to play its central role in the mountain biking world’s calendar.

*S2M-4443 Alasdair Morgan: Referendum on Independence—That the Parliament welcomes the introduction at Westminster of the Scotland (Petitions for a Referendum on Independence) Bill.
