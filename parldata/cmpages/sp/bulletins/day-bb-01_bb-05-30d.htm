BUSINESS BULLETIN 87 / 2001

Wednesday 30 May 2001

Section D  

First Minister's questions selected for answer on 31 May 2001
Oral questions selected for answer on 6 June 2001

 

 

First Minister’s Question Time

1. Mr John Swinney: To ask the First Minister what issues will be discussed at the next meeting of the Scottish Executive's Cabinet. (S1F-01110)

2. David McLetchie: To ask the First Minister when he will next meet the Prime Minister and what issues he plans to raise. (S1F-01107)

3. Mike Watson: To ask the First Minister what progress is being made towards improving the service delivered by the NHS to patients. (S1F-01113)

4. Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the First Minister what it will cost annually to make personal care free for all elderly people. (S1F-01115)

5. Mary Scanlon: To ask the First Minister how the tourism industry is overcoming the adverse effects of the foot-and-mouth disease outbreak. (S1F-01109)

6. Cathy Jamieson: To ask the First Minister what progress the Scottish Executive is making in ensuring that fewer people in Scotland are excluded from the financial and banking systems. (S1F-01116)

Oral questions selected for answer on 6 June 2001

Question Time

1. Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive what the latest figures are for the percentage of those leaving the New Deal who enter unsubsidised employment. (S1O-3552)

2. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure that there are high levels of visible policing within communities. (S1O-3548)

3. Elaine Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive when its Action Plan on the prevention of violence against women will be published. (S1O-3542)

4. Bristow Muldoon: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to build on Scotland’s science base. (S1O-3549)

5. Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to expand out-of-school learning. (S1O-3553)

6. Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to safeguard the future of cultural and heritage sites in the north east of Scotland. (S1O-3532)

7. Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made by local authorities in improving home energy efficiency and what impact this has had on levels of carbon dioxide emissions. (S1O-3545)

8. Ms Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what the recommended waiting time is for a specialist breast examination at breast cancer units and whether this target is being met. (S1O-3535)

9. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what proposals it has for the recycling of commercially discarded prawn shells. (S1O-3536)

10. Tricia Marwick: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1O-3462 by Susan Deacon on 24 May 2001, whether it will ensure that every patient who is assessed as potentially benefiting from beta interferon is prescribed it. (S1O-3554)

11. Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to develop local democracy. (S1O-3547)

12. Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the total Scottish budget as a proportion of comparable UK spending will rise, fall or stay the same in the next three years. (S1O-3543)

13. Miss Annabel Goldie: To ask the Scottish Executive what provision exists within the primary and secondary curricula for the teaching of technological studies. (S1O-3540)

14. Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met Lothian and Borders Police to discuss Internet pornography. (S1O-3544)

15. Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will seek a meeting with the Prime Minister and the Foreign Secretary to examine any implications for Scotland of the French Prime Minister’s recent proposals regarding reform of the European Union. (S1O-3534)

16. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there has been any improvement in the proportion of groups such as mature persons, women and people from low socio-economic backgrounds entering higher education. (S1O-3546)

17. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will report on its consideration of a priority dispatch system for the Scottish Ambulance Service. (S1O-3531)

18. Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made by local authorities in developing authority-wide improvement plans for education. (S1O-3550)

19. Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive how many residential properties owned by Scottish Homes there currently are in the Central Scotland electoral region and which body or bodies will assume ownership of these properties should the Housing (Scotland) Bill as amended at Stage 2 be enacted. (S1O-3541)

20. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Ministerial Group on Sustainable Scotland was last convened, what was discussed, when it will next meet and what will be on the agenda. (S1O-3538)

21. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive on how many occasions in the past month Ayr hospital has been unable to accept GP referred admissions. (S1O-3530)

22. Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive how it proposes to meet its commitment to cut class sizes to 30 or less in primaries 1, 2 and 3 before 31 August 2001. (S1O-3533)

23. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to review the arrangements between general practitioners and pharmacists regarding repeat prescriptions. (S1O-3539)

24. Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive how it ensures that food served in schools is safe. (S1O-3551)

25. Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive what further discussions have been held with Motorola and what percentage of employees being made redundant from the Bathgate plant have secured other employment. (S1O-3537)
