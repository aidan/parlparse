Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (♦).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S3M-4567 Bob Doris: Summer Sport—That the Parliament recognises the efforts of sporting groups working with children across Scotland as we enter the summer school holidays; notes that the North Glasgow Football Development Group is hosting a four week summer football camp at the north Kelvinside pitches in Maryhill where SFA-qualified coaches will teach valuable skills to youngsters and will hold weekly mini tournaments to encourage their football talents; believes that the summer break is a perfect opportunity for youngsters to enjoy outdoor activities, and expresses the gratitude for the time and commitment so selflessly devoted by the organisers of this programme and also to those contributing to sporting activities for children across the nation, from which the benefit cannot be underestimated.

*S3M-4566 Kenneth Gibson: Inclusion Scotland—That the Parliament welcomes the Homes for Life report from Inclusion Scotland, which highlights issues of significant importance in relation to accessible and affordable housing for disabled people in Scotland and the contribution that it will make to ensuring equality of housing opportunity for all Scotland’s citizens; congratulates Inclusion Scotland for its approach, and wishes Inclusion Scotland every success in its campaign to ensure more accessible and affordable housing for disabled people in Scotland.

*S3M-4565 Brian Adam: Dyce Academy ROV Success—That the Parliament congratulates Dyce Academy in winning the MATE Scotland ROV Challenge, which qualified it for the MATE International ROV competition at the Massachusetts Maritime Academy where it competed alongside schools from across the world in designing the next generation of submarine rescue systems; notes that The Robert Gordon University also took part in this competition where both teams performed admirably, and thanks the sponsors, hosts and staff for giving up their time and money to make these events a success.

*S3M-4564 Rob Gibson: Forres Academy’s 40 Years of Teaching—That the Parliament congratulates Forres Academy on reaching 40 years of teaching excellence; acknowledges the thousands of pupils from the wider community and outlying villages who have passed through its doors over the last 40 years; notes that the commemorative whisky being produced by the Glen Moray distillery is a fitting tribute in the Year of Homecoming 2009 and is pleased that a percentage of the revenue will go to the school’s Enrichment Fund, and finally notes that events will be taking place throughout the year and wishes Forres Academy every success in its next 40 years.

*S3M-4563 Mike Pringle: Boroughmuir High School Wins National Consumer Champions Award Again—That the Parliament congratulates Boroughmuir High School’s team of Scott Duncan, Malcolm Perry, Cameron Roscoe and Ruaridh Beveridge on winning the Young Consumers of the Year 2009 Award in Brighton on 1 July 2009, sponsored by The Trading Standards Institute, in which the team represented not only their school and city but also Scotland, making them national champions, and on regaining the title yet again for Boroughmuir High.

S3M-4561 Willie Coffey: Johnnie Walker Plant in Kilmarnock—That the Parliament condemns the decision by Diageo to close the Johnnie Walker plant in Kilmarnock with the loss of 700 jobs; notes that the Johnnie Walker brand was born in Kilmarnock in 1820 and has contributed to the huge financial success that Diageo now enjoys; recognises that Johnnie Walker Red Label is the world’s number one whisky and that the town of Kilmarnock has played an integral part in this success, and calls on Diageo to reverse its decision and to work with all parties to secure a long-term future for the Kilmarnock workforce, including, if necessary, the sale of the Johnnie Walker brand and plant to a company committed to maintaining operations in Kilmarnock.

S3M-4560 Kenneth Gibson: Arran Outdoor and Walking Festival—That the Parliament welcomes the 2009 Arran Outdoor and Walking Festival, which takes place from 18 to 21 September, and the contribution that it will make to Scotland’s flourishing tourism trade; marvels at the hard work put in by volunteer outdoor enthusiasts to ensure that the festival operates smoothly; commends the festival for raising the profile of Arran on a national and international stage; recognises the importance of such festivals in highlighting Scotland’s natural aesthetic beauty, and wishes the organisers every success this year and in years to come.

S3M-4559 Jamie Hepburn: ID Cards Ruled Out as "Compulsory" for British Citizens—That the Parliament welcomes the announcement by Alan Johnson MP, Home Secretary, that ID cards will not be compulsory for British citizens; considers, however, that Mr Johnson’s announcement obfuscates the intention of the UK Government to make the passport a "designated document", meaning that the details of everyone who applies for a passport will be stored on a national identity register and that the identity scheme will remain, in practice, compulsory; believes that the UK Government’s change of tack is a mere attempt to implement the identity scheme through the back door, and calls on the UK Government to wholly abandon this wasteful and unpopular scheme.

S3M-4558 Margaret Mitchell: Tribute to Scotland’s Justices of the Peace—That the Parliament recognises that justices of the peace have served Scotland for over 400 years; notes that the role has changed considerably in recent years with reforms to the lay justice system; pays tribute to the many men and women who have taken up this important role in Scotland; recognises the valuable contribution that they have made to Scotland’s criminal justice system, and hopes that this will continue to be supported and upheld in years to come.

Supported by: John Lamont*, Bill Kidd*, Jamie McGrigor*, Nanette Milne*, Elizabeth Smith*, Bill Aitken*, David Stewart*, Jackson Carlaw*, Mike Pringle*

S3M-4557 Shirley-Anne Somerville: Shame on Shell—That the Parliament condemns the Shell oil company for its continued refusal to accept any responsibility for actions leading to the hangings in 1995 by Nigeria’s then military government of Ken Saro-Wiwa and eight other Ogoni activists, despite being forced recently to pay £10 million in an out-of-court settlement; remembers that, in his final statement at his trial, Ken Saro-Wiwa predicted that Shell would one day be held accountable for "the crimes of ecological war the company has waged in the Delta"; pays tribute to the courageous, non-violent activism of Ken Saro-Wiwa and his comrades in the Ogoni Nine in their fight for the rights of the people of Ogoniland and to the tenacity of their families in their long pursuit of justice, and joins the ShellGuilty campaign group in demanding an immediate end to Shell’s illegal gas-flaring in Nigeria.

S3M-4556 Rob Gibson: Celebrating Thomas Telford—That the Parliament offers its congratulations to the Wick Paths Project on the organisation of the upcoming three-day event in Caithness from 3 to 5 July celebrating the genius of Thomas Telford, the designer and builder; welcomes the group of engineers making the trip; further recognises the economic benefit to the county and the lasting impact that Thomas Telford made culturally, architecturally and socially on this beautiful part of our country, and wishes it every success with this innovative and important initiative.

S3M-4555 Hugh O’Donnell: Reported Kidnapping of International Humanitarian Activists—That the Parliament condemns the reported kidnapping of 21 international humanitarian activists, including Theresa McDermott from Scotland, and hijacking by the Israeli Defence Force of the peace ship the Spirit of Humanity and its aid cargo in international waters as it travelled to bring some relief to the besieged people of Gaza; believes that this unprovoked attack is in direct contravention of international maritime law and is contrary to all international moral standards of behaviour for a civilised state, and, *notwithstanding the negative activities of Hamas,* believes that the international community, European Union and President Obama should condemn the actions of Israel and bring to bear such pressure on the state of Israel as is necessary to relieve the three-year long suffering of the innocent civilian population of Gaza.

Supported by: Joe FitzPatrick, Rob Gibson, Shirley-Anne Somerville, Bill Kidd, Sandra White, Bill Butler, Bill Wilson*, Mike Pringle*

S3M-4554 Bill Wilson: Brambles as good as Blackberries—That the Parliament believes that Scotland’s cultural and linguistic heritage is something to be proud of, noting the many and varied traditional names given to food grown in Scotland, such as brambles, neeps, tatties and syboes; believes that the Year o Hamecomin is an ideal time to celebrate Scotland’s cultural and linguistic heritage; encourages local and national retailers to consider making wider use of traditional names for food products in labelling and point-of-sale material, and believes that such a move could enhance the quality of service that shops offer both local customers, who would see their own terms being used and respected, and visitors from elsewhere, who would be better aware of the richness and diversity of Scotland’s culture.

Supported by: Bob Doris, Alasdair Allan, Rob Gibson, Christine Grahame, John Wilson

S3M-4553 Charlie Gordon: Congratulations to the Evening Times Community Champions of South East Glasgow—That the Parliament congratulates the organisations and individuals whose voluntary and professional work for the communities of south east Glasgow have been recognised in the Evening Times Community Champions Awards, including the late Charles Boyce of Mount Florida Community Council (Individual Award), Castlemilk Pensioners’ Action Centre (Team Award), Cath Chrisite (Public Service Individual Award), Castlemilk High School teaching and support staff (Public Service Team Award), Alison Drummond (Neighbour Award) and Mount Florida Primary School primary seven (Young Community Champion Award).

S3M-4552 Sandra White: Israel Detains UK Citizens—That the Parliament condemns in the strongest possible terms the illegal boarding and detention of the passengers of the Spirit of Humanity, including, among others, UK citizens, former US Congresswoman Cynthia McKinney and Mairead Maguire, Irish Nobel Peace Laureate, in international waters on route to Gaza; believes that Israel’s continuing flagrant breach of international law and existing UN resolutions must be addressed
in order to demonstrate that no one is above the law, and calls on Prime Minister Gordon Brown to condemn the illegal detention of UK citizens, be it by Iran or Israel, and for the President of the United States of America, Barack Obama, to redouble his efforts to secure meaningful change to Israel’s current position.

Supported by: Bob Doris, Hugh O’Donnell, Anne McLaughlin, Bill Kidd, Shirley-Anne Somerville, Christine Grahame, Bill Wilson*, Joe FitzPatrick*

S3M-4551 Bill Wilson: Israel Must Release Kidnapped Human Rights Observers—That the Parliament notes reports of Israel’s threats towards and subsequent interception of the Spirit of Humanity, an unarmed boat carrying aid to Gaza, and of its apparent kidnapping of the human rights observers aboard, among whom was the Scot, Theresa McDermott; calls on Israel to release all of the detained people immediately, compensate them and allow the aid into Gaza; applauds all of those attempting to break the siege of Gaza in order to deliver such much-needed aid to the people trapped there; condemns Israel’s apparent disregard for human rights and international law; believes that Israel’s ongoing flouting of international norms in this and other ways does nothing to make that country safer, and calls on the international community to take concerted action to put pressure on Israel to conduct itself according to such norms, which conduct it believes would be in the interests of its own people as well as those of Palestinians.

Supported by: Hugh O’Donnell, Bill Kidd, Mike Pringle*

S3M-4550 Stuart McMillan: Station Community Regeneration Fund—That the Parliament welcomes the announcement by ScotRail that it is to invest £1 million to convert disused railway station buildings into community projects under their Station Community Regeneration Fund (SCRF); notes that a list of stations that could receive the funding has been drawn up by ScotRail and includes Fort Matilda, Inverclyde; further notes that the fund will see applications being submitted by community groups to make use of station buildings, and acknowledges the new opportunities that this fund could bring to communities in the way of employment and community involvement.

S3M-4549 Murdo Fraser: Knockhill Racing Circuit’s Teen Drive—That the Parliament commends the work of Scotland’s national motorsport centre, Knockhill Racing Circuit, for its important and proactive work to reduce young-driver accident rates in Scotland; notes that 17 to 21-year-olds make up 4.4% of Scotland’s driving population but comprise 17% of all car drivers killed or seriously injured in road traffic collisions; welcomes the work of Knockhill Racing Circuit to address this issue with its innovative Teen Drive programme, which gives youngsters aged between 13 and 16 the opportunity to drive in a secure environment as well as having racing drivers teaching the youngsters best practice habits at an early age; wishes Knockhill Racing Circuit continued success, and believes that its initiative will save lives in the long term.

S3M-4545 Jamie Hepburn: Condemnation of the Coup d’état in Honduras—That the Parliament condemns the coup undertaken on 28 June 2009 in Honduras against the democratically elected government of President Manuel Zelaya; further condemns the kidnapping of the Cuban, Nicaraguan and Venezuelan ambassadors to Honduras as being totally contrary to the diplomatic norms of cordial relations between countries; notes the condemnation of this coup across the international community, including by the General Secretary of the United Nations, the President of the United States of America, the Organisation of American States, the Association of Caribbean States and many other governments, and believes that President Manuel Zelaya should be allowed to return to Honduras without threat of arrest and detention and to complete his term of office as the democratically elected head of state and head of government of the Republic of Honduras.

Supported by: Bill Kidd, Bob Doris, Alasdair Allan, Anne McLaughlin, Patricia Ferguson, Hugh O’Donnell, Aileen Campbell, Bill Wilson, Bill Butler

S3M-4543 Jamie McGrigor: Reversal of Forfeitures Affecting Descendants of those who Supported the Royal House of Stuart—That the Parliament welcomes the forthcoming petition to permit legislation to be introduced in the Westminster Parliament repealing forfeitures and attainders still in existence affecting those whose ancestors supported the Royal Stuarts; notes that these Jacobites lost civil rights and property and were tainted with the label of "corruption of blood"; further notes that this included all ranks from peers and lairds to clerks in government offices, including such well-known figures as Rob Roy and Flora MacDonald, and believes this to be appropriate as an act of reconciliation in the Year of Homecoming when so many of their descendants are returning to Scotland.

S3M-4535 Sandra White: International Committee of the Red Cross Report on Gaza—That the Parliament notes with extreme concern the International Committee of the Red Cross (ICRC) report, which states that, six months after Israel launched its three-week military operation in Gaza on 27 December 2008, Gazans still cannot rebuild their lives, seriously ill patients face difficulty obtaining the treatment that they need, many children suffer from deep psychological problems and civilians whose homes and belongings were destroyed during the conflict are unable to recover, and supports the recommendations of the ICRC, which call for import restrictions to be eased to allow vital infrastructure to be rebuilt, access to essential medical supplies and for action to be taken to rebuild the shattered economy that has led to the alarming level of poverty within Gaza.
