Business Bulletin No. 18/2002

Wednesday 30 January 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-2662 Margaret Smith: energywatch "Stop Now!" campaign—That the Parliament welcomes the energywatch "Stop Now!" campaign which is designed to put a stop to unscrupulous sales tactics used by some gas and electricity companies which are on the increase and which, customers report, can include lying, bullying, misleading requests and intimidation; supports the demands made by energywatch to ensure that the gas and electricity companies’ house is put in order by (a) the Office of Gas and Electricity Markets (Ofgem) keeping and strengthening the operating licensing conditions which the energy companies must comply with and which are due to expire in March 2002, (b) Ofgem using its power to fine unscrupulous energy companies, (c) the energy companies establishing a "rogues’ register" of sales agencies and individuals who fail to meet acceptable standards, (d) the energy companies paying proper compensation to consumers for any distress and inconvenience caused by their sales malpractices and (e) the establishment of a standard format for energy supply contracts so that consumers are able to understand contracts and compare them with other suppliers; welcomes the commitment of energywatch to work with the police to combat bogus caller crime; urges the Scottish Executive to work in partnership with Scottish energy companies to improve consumer relations, and commends energywatch for raising the profile of such important consumer issues.

*S1M-2657 Robin Harper: energywatch "Stop Now!" Campaign—That the Parliament notes with concern the rising trend of complaints against energy supply companies for malpractice and maladministration relating to their sales techniques and transfer processes; welcomes the launch of the energywatch "Stop Now!" campaign aimed at tackling this trend, and calls upon the energy supply companies to co-operate fully with energywatch and for the Office of Gas and Electricity Markets to use its available powers to ensure that customers are no longer transferred from supplier to supplier against their will and without adequate information or under duress.

*S1M-2656 Mr Kenneth Macintosh on behalf of the Procedures Committee: Changes to Standing Orders—That the Parliament (a) approves the recommendations of the Procedures Committee’s 5th Report 2001, Changes to the Standing Orders of the Scottish Parliament (SP Paper 441) and agrees to amend the Standing Orders in accordance with Annex A to the Report; (b) approves the recommendations of the Procedures Committee’s 1st Report 2002, Changes to the Standing Orders of the Scottish Parliament (SP Paper 495) and agrees to amend the Standing Orders in accordance with Annex A to the Report, and (c) agrees that these amendments to the Standing Orders should come into force on 4 February 2001.

Supported by: Mr Gil Paterson*

*S1M-2655 Tommy Sheridan: Representation on the European Union Convention on the Future of Europe—That the Parliament notes with regret that Scotland will have no representation at the European Union Convention on the Future of Europe and calls on the Scottish Executive to make representations to Her Majesty’s Government and the European Union to secure direct representation on the convention.
