Business Bulletin No. 127/2006: Thursday 14 September 2006
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 21 September 2006

Question Time

General

1. Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what powers it has to ensure that a local authority contributes fairly to the education of children with additional support needs who attend school in another local authority area. (S2O-10605)

2. Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive how it intends to improve sporting attainment. (S2O-10562)

3. Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the targets set for its Antisocial Behaviour Roadshow were met. (S2O-10599)

4. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in tackling youth disorder in the Glasgow Springburn parliamentary constituency. (S2O-10602)

5. Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to evaluate the impact of Transport Scotland’s decisions on the community and economic life of towns and villages whose main streets form part of the trunk road network. (S2O-10558)

6. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will support legislation to regulate the subdivision of property. (S2O-10608)

7. Mr Andrew Arbuckle: To ask the Scottish Executive whether pressure can be put on statutory consultative bodies to reduce their response times with regard to planning applications. (S2O-10566)

8. Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what funds remain available to Transport Scotland for infrastructure investment in the current financial year. (S2O-10561)

9. Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what arrangements are in hand to procure the rolling stock required for the Airdrie to Bathgate railway line. (S2O-10587)

10. Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive how much investment is planned for schools and college facilities in the Clydebank and Milngavie parliamentary constituency between 2006 and 2010. (S2O-10609)

Environment and Rural Development

1. Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive how much recyclable household waste, sorted for recycling by householders and collected by local authorities, goes to landfill. (S2O-10607)

2. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether Scotland is able to set its own modulation rate to fund the Scottish Rural Development Plan. (S2O-10576)

3. Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive whether its Environment and Rural Affairs Department was advised at the time about any risk of impact on the marine environment from serious and significant "nuclear safety events" at Faslane and Coulport which are reported to have significantly increased in number and, if so, what information it received. (S2O-10550)

4. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is assisting local authorities to reduce their environmental footprint. (S2O-10571)

5. Mark Ballard: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to support organic agriculture and to meet demand for organic food. (S2O-10617)

6. Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive what controls are available to prevent pollution from the activity of mobile crushing plants used to crush demolition arisings for the purposes of recycling. (S2O-10583)

7. Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any concerns about the effect of milk prices on the dairy industry in Dumfries and Galloway. (S2O-10613)

8. David McLetchie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it welcomes the decision of Tesco to reward customers who use alternatives to plastic bags. (S2O-10581)

9. Euan Robson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to review the Rural Stewardship Scheme. (S2O-10567)

10. Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps its Environment and Rural Affairs Department is taking to aid rural development in South Lanarkshire by securing additional resources for rural roads. (S2O-10594)

Health and Community Care

1. Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive when the number of cases of mesothelioma is expected to peak in Scotland and how many people are expected to be diagnosed in each of the peak years. (S2O-10560)

2. Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will instigate a review of policy in respect of the provision of wigs to patients undergoing chemotherapy. (S2O-10627)

3. Mr Alasdair Morrison: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it can give an update on dental services provision in the Western Isles. (S2O-10610)

4. Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there will be a reduction in training posts for junior doctors under the Modernising Medical Careers initiative. (S2O-10557)

5. Mr John Swinney: To ask the Scottish Executive what its plans are for the development of GP services. (S2O-10575)

6. Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to address the shortage of NHS dentists in Caithness and Sutherland. (S2O-10573)

7. John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there has been any progress in improving care home provision in the Highlands. (S2O-10565)

8. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will publish a response to Health Scotland’s Review of the Scottish Diet Action Plan. (S2O-10623)

9. Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied that the current relationship between the NHS and GPs is sufficiently flexible to allow the provision or modernisation of local health facilities, such as health centres or GP surgeries, to go ahead in an expeditious manner. (S2O-10574)

10. Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive whether geographical considerations will be taken into account under new bed blocking guidelines if local care for vulnerable elderly people is unavailable. (S2O-10621)
