Business Bulletin No. 45/2009: Thursday 12 March 2009
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 18 and 19 March 2009

18 March

Question Time

Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body

1. Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body, in addition to considering a cycle to work scheme, whether it will consider implementing personalised travel plans for staff to encourage more sustainable transport choices and promote cycling and walking to work. (S3O-6466)

2. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body what changes and improvements are planned to the Parliament’s IT systems in the Holyrood complex and in constituency and regional offices. (S3O-6463)

3. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body, in light of its proposals for the reorganisation of SPCB supported organisations, whether it can guarantee the continuation of the work carried out by the office of the Commissioner for Children and Young People in Scotland. (S3O-6457)

4. David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body what plans it has to equip all committee rooms for video conferencing. (S3O-6461)

5. Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body how it ensures that it complies with its equal opportunities responsibilities. (S3O-6462)

6. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body what evidence it collected and consultation with stakeholders it undertook prior to proposing a merger of the office of the Commissioner for Children and Young People in Scotland with the Scottish Human Rights Commission. (S3O-6464)

7. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body what plans it has to review the support provided to MSPs by its broadcasting and website facilities. (S3O-6465)

8. Shirley-Anne Somerville: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body what mechanisms are in place to promote volunteering to staff. (S3O-6459)

9. Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body what its reasons are for not renewing its contract with the Scottish Tourist Guides Association. (S3O-6458)

10. John Wilson: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body how many staff have taken voluntary redundancy since 2003, broken down by directorate. (S3O-6460)

19 March

Question Time

General

1. Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will seek a joint legal opinion with the UK Government on the Scottish Government’s fishing quota and licensing proposals. (S3O-6276)

2. John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to attract tourism business to Ayrshire and in particular southern Ayrshire. (S3O-6257)

3. Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will re-examine the operation of legal aid rules that in practice mean that many mortgage borrowers facing repossession are not eligible for assistance or, where they are eligible, that many lawyers will not assist because they are required to recover fees from clients if successful. (S3O-6290)

4. Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive, in the light of the findings of the Scottish Environmental Attitudes and Behaviours Survey 2008 that the main barriers to green behaviour are cost, convenience, a lack of alternative options and practical considerations, how it plans to tackle these barriers. (S3O-6303)

5. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to contribute towards the maintenance of the Clyde Tunnel in Glasgow. (S3O-6319)

6. Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will renew funding to the Scottish Fair Trade Forum to support the small grants scheme. (S3O-6316)

7. Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make representations to the Royal Mail regarding its decision to withdraw five postbus services from the north and west Highlands. (S3O-6288)

8. John Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it monitors the performance of local authorities that have moved to fortnightly refuse collections. (S3O-6270)

9. John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive when it intends to announce the membership and remit of the Bull Hire Scheme review group. (S3O-6280)

10. George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that there is an imbalance in the grant allocation for faith-based organisations. (S3O-6302)

Health and Wellbeing

1. Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to support the City of Edinburgh Council in tackling the city’s acute housing issues. (S3O-6286)

2. Aileen Campbell: To ask the Scottish Executive what arrangements are in place to ensure the safe delivery of medicines for patients who receive them by post. (S3O-6333)

3. Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made with the fuel poverty programme. (S3O-6292)

4. Ian McKee: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in immunising eligible women against the human papilloma virus. (S3O-6348)

5. Christopher Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether statistical information is available on complaints raised under the dignity at work programme and their outcomes for representative authorities within NHS Scotland and what external monitoring or mediation is being undertaken in contentious cases. (S3O-6342)

6. Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to encourage the deployment of junior doctors to work and train in Ayrshire and Arran. (S3O-6339)

7. Anne McLaughlin: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in reducing any democratic deficit in the provision of healthcare by establishing elected NHS boards. (S3O-6347)

8. Tricia Marwick: To ask the Scottish Executive what proposals it has to improve levels of dental provision in Fife. (S3O-6345)

9. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive what further steps have been taken to ensure that the deployment, training and recruitment of health visitors are at the centre of the early years strategy. (S3O-6273)

10. Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive what it is doing to ensure that junior doctors are given the right support to allow them to carry out their duties. (S3O-6277)

11. Not Lodged

12. Joe FitzPatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive what further action ministers are taking to combat hospital-acquired infections. (S3O-6338)

13. Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive what organisations it has met to discuss the need to raise awareness of oesophageal cancer. (S3O-6296)

14. Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has plans to screen for asbestos-related diseases. (S3O-6352)

15. Christina McKelvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on ensuring that the 2014 Commonwealth Games in Glasgow will produce a lasting legacy. (S3O-6349)

16. Bill Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of Richard Wilkinson’s research comparing inequality between societies and which shows that in the most unequal societies the detrimental effects become so great that the health and wellbeing of even the wealthiest are damaged, what value it places on the importance of continuing to tackle inequality as a priority and in a coordinated manner. (S3O-6357)

17. Not Lodged

18. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the UK Government concerning the need for anti-discrimination protection for the Scottish Gypsy/Traveller community and whether it would support legislation in this area. (S3O-6274)

19. Alison McInnes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the new Care Environment Inspectorate regime to address hospital-acquired infections will be extended to care homes. (S3O-6279)

20. Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive how it intends to respond to the latest quarterly housing statistics showing the lowest number of new build starts since 1980. (S3O-6301)
