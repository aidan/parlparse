BUSINESS BULLETIN 98 / 2001

Thursday 14 June 2001

Section E - Written questions lodged on 13 June 2001

 

 

S1W-16249 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it was consulted by the Ministry of Defence on the environmental or other implications of the Interim Storage of Laid-up Submarines Project and whether it will place in the Scottish Parliament Information Centre any documents and communications in respect of such consultation.

S1W-16250 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what position it took on the choice of Mellon Charles near Aultbea in Wester Ross as the location for a repository of nuclear waste material from decommissioned nuclear submarines in any consultation with the Ministry of Defence on the environmental and other implications of the proposed location.

S1W-16251 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what advice Scottish Natural Heritage has given on whether Mellon Charles near Aultbea in Wester Ross should be chosen as the location for a repository of nuclear waste material from decommissioned nuclear submarines.

S1W-16252 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will next meet representatives of BP to discuss ways in which the work connected with the development of the Clair field for which contracts will shortly be issued can contribute to the Scottish economy, in particular with regard to employment opportunities.

S1W-16253 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to make funding available to Lothian Health Board and Lothian University Hospitals NHS Trust for the provision of continuous positive airway pressure treatment to relieve sleep apnoea.

S1W-16254 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people suffering from sleep apnoea in the Borders and East Lothian are on a waiting list for a continuous positive airway pressure machine.

S1W-16255 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people in the Borders and East Lothian currently suffer from sleep apnoea.

S1W-16256 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people suffering from sleep apnoea in the Borders and East Lothian are currently receiving treatment by way of a continuous positive airway pressure machine.

S1W-16257 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive how many road accidents have occurred at the Ballinluig junction on the A9 in each of the last five years.

S1W-16258 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive what the cost was of each improvement scheme undertaken on the A9 between Dunkeld and Pitlochry during 1999-2000 and 2000-01 and what the cost will be of any improvement schemes planned for 2001-02.

S1W-16259 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-15831 by Sarah Boyack on 11 June 2001, whether it will publish the report and recommendations of the road safety investigation into the Ballinluig junction on the A9.

S1W-16260 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-15831 by Sarah Boyack on 11 June 2001, when it expects to be able to announce what measures it is planning to implement as a result of the road safety investigation into the Ballinluig junction on the A9.

S1W-16261 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-14663 by Sarah Boyack on 23 May 2001, how many grants have been awarded under the Powershift grants scheme in each of the last five years for the purchase or conversion of local authority vehicles in (a) Clackmannanshire, (b) Stirling, (c) Perth and Kinross, (d) Angus and (e) Fife.

S1W-16262 Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many responses to its consultation on The Nature of Scotland: A Policy Statement were received by 29 May 2001 and how many of these were broadly in favour of the proposals outlined in the statement.

S1W-16263 Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-15917 by Rhona Brankin on 5 June 2001, when it expects to have considered the consultation responses to The Nature of Scotland: A Policy Statement and when it expects to announce its legislative plans in respect of the proposals outlined in the statement.
