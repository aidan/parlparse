Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally printed the day after they are lodged. If an amendment is lodged to a motion then the original motion will appear alongside the amendment, along with any support lodged that day, in this section.

Each Monday, this section also contains all motions and amendments lodged the previous week that are still live and any motions and amendments that have been lodged for debate in the coming week. A motion or amendment is live if it has not been debated, withdrawn or otherwise deleted from the list.

Where a motion or amendment has been withdrawn, it is indicated in this section the day after it has been withdrawn. Support for motions and amendments received after they are lodged is shown at the end of this section the day after such support is received.

Motions and amendments can be published with various symbols:

  • Asterisks before the motion or amendment number indicate a motion or amendment published for the first time;

  • Asterisks also identify alterations to the text of a motion or amendment made since it was first published;

  • A hash symbol identifies motions eligible for debate at Members’ Business;

  • A diamond symbol identifies motions lodged for Members’ Business that have not yet attracted the required cross-party support;

  • An "R", identifies motions or amendments in which the Member who lodged it has a registrable interest.

The Parliamentary Bureau periodically deletes motions or amendments not scheduled for debate and which are over six weeks old.

A full list of current motions is available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/motions.htm.

A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/index.htm.

Any questions in relation to this section should be directed to the Chamber Desk, for which contact details are provided at the end of the Bulletin.

New and altered motions and amendments

*S3M-7895 Marilyn Livingstone: Congratulations to Kinghorn Lifeboat Station—That the Parliament notes that Kinghorn lifeboat station was the second busiest station in Scotland last year, with the boat being launched on 70 occasions, including 25 call-outs in the dark, and rescuing 92 people; understands that the volunteer crew at Kinghorn spent nearly 77 hours at sea and many more hours training and preparing for the next call-out; commends the fact that, despite the recent severe weather, Kinghorn lifeboat station has not had a single day off service because of the conditions; believes that it was due to the determination of the volunteer crew that they and the lifeboat were able to go out to sea whatever the weather, and applauds the generosity of people in the Kirkcaldy constituency, even during difficult economic times, which funds all rescues carried out by Kinghorn RNLI.

*S3M-7894 Linda Fabiani: Congratulations to Christopher Kane—That the Parliament congratulates Christopher Kane on winning the British Fashion Council/Vogue Designer Fashion Fund, which will allow this Scots designer the opportunity to develop his business into a global brand; compliments Vogue and the British Fashion Council on their forward-looking attitude in running the fund, and looks forward to Christopher Kane’s continued success in the world of fashion.

*S3M-7893 Linda Fabiani: St Vincent’s Primary Top Micro-tyco—That the Parliament congratulates Robyn Conlan, Bethan Malley, Olivia Beaton, Stephanie Devine, Astrid Corkill and Amna Assed of St Vincent’s Primary School in East Kilbride on winning the micro-tyco challenge with an incredible performance of raising more than £4,000 for WildHearts in Action from a £1 start-up fund; praises their efforts and their imagination and endorses the work of the charity, which provides microloans to women in West Africa to help them start their own businesses, and further looks forward to these pupils continuing to make a contribution to society throughout their lives.

*S3M-7892 Kenneth Gibson: Alcohol Minimum Pricing—That the Parliament welcomes news that the UK Government plans to introduce a minimum price for alcohol in England and Wales later in 2011; recognises that there are plans for a trial in Manchester with alcohol at 50p per unit; understands that, even with this relatively modest increase in the basic price, research indicates that this would result in 3,000 fewer deaths every year and 41,000 fewer cases of chronic illness each year if implemented across England and Wales; further welcomes the news that Labour has backed these proposals, which it believes will have a huge positive impact on the health and wellbeing of English and Welsh citizens; believes that the unionist parties in Scotland should now back proposals of the Scottish Government to introduce a minimum price for alcohol in order to tackle Scotland’s unhealthy relationship with alcohol, and believes that a new Bill specifically with that intention should be introduced early in the next session.

*S3M-7891 Kenneth Gibson: Disability Living Allowance Reform—That the Parliament condemns the decision by the UK Government to reform the Disability Living Allowance benefit in the way that it has; recognises the importance of this benefit to thousands of disabled people across the country and believes that the proposed reforms will have a negative impact on those who currently receive it; considers that there are serious flaws in the consultation paper and that there is little evidence to support the Department for Work and Pensions’ case for such reform; further believes that it is likely that benefit expenditure will fall but that health and social care expenditure will inevitably increase, thus transferring the burden of cost to areas that the Scottish Parliament is responsible for, and, as such, calls on the Minister for Disabled People to withdraw the proposals.

*S3M-7870 Bill Kidd: Support for the Egyptian People—That the Parliament wishes to express its full support for the demonstrations by the Egyptian people by stating its unequivocal belief that all peoples have the right to live in a free and democratic society unshackled by dictatorial oppression and the fear of arrest, detention and torture by a private army disguised as a police force, as it considers is the case of the 30-year rule of Egypt’s Hosni Mubarak, and calls on President Mubarak to halt what it sees as the thuggish behaviour of his supporters and to step aside now, rather than holding off until September, in order to avert further bloodshed of innocent Egyptians and allow a peaceful transition to democracy.

Supported by: Mike Pringle*, Sandra White*, Hugh O’Donnell*, Gil Paterson*, Stuart McMillan*, Linda Fabiani*, Kenneth Gibson*, Rob Gibson*, Christine Grahame*, Patrick Harvie*, Bob Doris*, Joe FitzPatrick*, Bill Wilson*

*S3M-7869 Cathy Peattie: The Helix Project—That the Parliament welcomes the commencement of work on the Helix project on 2 February 2011 on a 300-hectare site between Falkirk and Grangemouth; notes that the area will be transformed into a park featuring an enclosed lagoon and outdoor events space, pathways, cycle networks and a range of community facilities, with a new canal link connecting Grangemouth and the Firth of Forth to Scotland’s extensive canal network, the centrepiece of which will be the Kelpies lock, featuring two 30-metre high sculptures in the shape of horses’ heads, and believes that the Helix will provide a much-needed recreational facility for local people and further boost the tourist potential of the area.
