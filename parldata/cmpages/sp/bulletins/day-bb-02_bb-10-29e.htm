Business Bulletin No. 152/2002

Tuesday 29 October 2002

Section E : Written questions lodged on 28 October 2002

  

Questions marked with a triangle () are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S1W-30937 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive what mechanism is in place to make public whether Scottish Enterprise has been successful in achieving its performance targets.

S1W-30938 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive what mechanism is in place to make public whether Scottish Enterprise Glasgow has been successful in achieving its performance targets.

S1W-30939 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive what monitoring mechanism has been put in place to judge the effectiveness of the free concessionary travel scheme for elderly and disabled people.

S1W-30940 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any monitoring of the free concessionary travel scheme for elderly and disabled people will include an analysis of any problems encountered regarding the bus companies’ understanding of the guidance issued in respect of what constitutes the free concessionary travel area.

S1W-30941 Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-29905 by Malcolm Chisholm on 8 October 2002, what facilities ambulances described as having thrombolytic capability are equipped with for assessing the need for, and delivering, thrombolytic treatment.

S1W-30942 Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to questions S1W-29905 and S1W-29906 by Malcolm Chisholm on 8 October 2002, what the difference is between the Cardiac Response Unit (CRU) and the Rapid Response Unit (RRU) and why the RRU is permanently on-call while the CRU only operates during peak times.

S1W-30943 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive when the legal aid exemption limit in most matrimonial cases was first set at £2,500; when it was first announced that this limit would rise to £4,200, and when this increase will come into force.

S1W-30944 David McLetchie: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will announce its decision on the Colinton flood prevention scheme submitted to it for approval by the City of Edinburgh Council.

S1W-30945 Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will review the level of financial support made available to Dumfries and Galloway Constabulary given the increase in the number of asylum applications lodged at Stranraer over the last year.

S1W-30946 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive how many redundancies have been notified in Ayrshire since the launch of Partnership Action for Continuing Employment in March 2000, broken down by company, sector and earnings level.

S1W-30947 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people made redundant in Ayrshire since March 2000 have found alternative employment, broken down by sector and earnings level of new jobs.

S1W-30948 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people made redundant in Ayrshire since March 2000 have found alternative employment (a) in Ayrshire, (b) elsewhere in Scotland and (c) outwith Scotland.

S1W-30949 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive how many acute psychiatric beds have been available in each NHS board area in each of the last five years.

S1W-30950 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people have been unable to access acute psychiatric beds in each NHS board area in each of the last five years.

S1W-30951 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive how many voluntary patients were treated in acute psychiatric beds in each NHS board area in each of the last five years.

S1W-30952 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has had any discussions with Lothian Primary Care NHS Trust regarding the closure of 20 acute beds at the Royal Edinburgh Hospital and, if so, what was discussed and what the outcome was.

S1W-30953 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has had any discussions with Argyll and Clyde NHS Board or any of the NHS trusts in its area regarding current issues with the mental health service in the Helensburgh, Dumbarton and Vale of Leven areas and, if so, what was discussed and what the outcome was.

S1W-30954 Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive what its policy is in relation to educational tourism.

S1W-30955 Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken by VisitScotland to promote educational tourism.

S1W-30956 Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment has been made of the value to the higher education sector of fees from overseas students.

S1W-30957 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Presiding Officer why the seating provision in the public gallery of the chamber in the new Parliament building will not be at least 250 as stated in press release no. 026/2000 issued on 2 June 2000; on what date approval was given to reduce the total number of seats below 250, and whether the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body will provide a full explanation of why this decision was taken.

S1W-30958 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how much new money will be made available to the children’s hearing system in each of the areas selected for the fast track pilot schemes for under-16s announced by the Minister for Education and Young People on 23 October 2002.

S1W-30959 Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will hold a public inquiry into plans for infrastructure work at the Auchenkilns roundabout, Cumbernauld.

S1W-30960 Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will rule out applying road tolls or other charges for the use of any planned new motorway along the A80/M80 route and for what period any such restriction would apply.

S1W-30961 Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what changes in the definitions or categories of environmental impact have taken place in any official guidelines affecting the assessment of potential road improvements and new road building in the last 10 years and from what dates any such changes applied.

S1W-30962 Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether plans for a motorway bypass of Cumbernauld were part of the Cumbernauld New Town Development Corporation’s plans for the expansion of Cumbernauld at any time.

S1W-30963 Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive which towns with a population of more than 5,000 have a motorway passing through the town and what routes are involved.

S1W-30964 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to secure the jobs at Richard Roberts Knitwear in Ayrshire.

S1W-30965 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many students from (a) Scottish addresses and (b) in total have applied for entry to Scottish universities in each of the last three years.

S1W-30966 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there has been a recent reduction in the number of students taking science and engineering courses in Scottish universities.

S1W-30967 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to tackle levels of illiteracy amongst young adults in South Lanarkshire.

S1W-30968 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Scottish Information Commissioner will be appointed.

S1W-30969 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what the outcomes were of the first meeting of the Sustainable Development Forum on 24 July 2002.

S1W-30970 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what its proposals are for the composition and remit of the Sustainable Development Forum.

S1W-30971 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive how it proposes to engage a wider group of stakeholders in achieving sustainable development.

S1W-30972 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how many young people have entered work under Phase 1 of the New Futures Fund since the inception of the fund.

S1W-30973 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how much Phase 1 of the New Futures Fund cost.

S1W-30974 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how many young people entered work under the Beattie Inclusiveness Projects in year 1.

S1W-30975 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how much the Beattie Inclusiveness Projects cost in year 1.

S1W-30976 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive what the differences are between the New Futures Fund and the Beattie Inclusiveness Projects.

S1W-30977 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people have secured open employment through the New Deal for Disabled People.

S1W-30978 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive what the mechanisms are for competitive tendering when selecting employment programmes for disabled people.

S1W-30979 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive how many children left school with no qualifications in each year from 1992-93 to 1996-97, broken down by local authority area.

S1W-30980 Mr Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take in response to the situation regarding audiology staffing as reported in A Survey of Staffing in NHS Hospital Audiology Departments: An Interim Report from the Audiology Needs Assessment Group.

S1W-30981 Mr Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take following its decision to suspend tendering for a BSc course in audiology after tenders had been submitted.

S1W-30982 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are no facilities available in any hospital to allow a mother with post-natal depression to continue to care for her baby whilst undergoing treatment.

S1W-30983 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive what the reasons are for any lack of dedicated mother and baby services for women with post-natal depression.

S1W-30984 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it intends to take to direct NHS boards to ensure that any lack of provision of dedicated mother and baby units for women with post-natal depression is addressed swiftly.

S1W-30985 Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-28610 by Malcolm Chisholm on 17 September 2002, how much money it actually made available to the National Review of Resource Allocation for the NHS in Scotland.

S1W-30986 Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average Scottish Ambulance Service response time is for calls that are (a) life-threatening, (b) serious but not immediately life-threatening and (c) neither serious nor life-threatening, broken down by NHS board area.

S1W-30987 Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-29330 by Malcom Chisholm on 27 September 2002, why the Information and Statistics Division of the Common Services Agency has no plans to compile key health indicators using the same criteria as the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development.

S1W-30988 Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many locum consultant doctors there currently are working in each NHS board area.

S1W-30989 Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many GP lists are currently closed, broken down by NHS board area.

S1W-30990 Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average number of days lost due to ill health was in each of the last five years for NHS (a) medical and (b) nursing staff.

S1W-30991 Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the Scottish Executive what the (a) final outturn expenditure for 2000-01, (b) planned outturn expenditure for 2001-02 and (c) actual final outturn expenditure for 2001-02 were, broken down by NHS board area.

S1W-30992 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average number of offences committed by persons released on bail after release was in each of the last four years.

S1W-30993 Brian Fitzpatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-30462 by Ross Finnie on 28 October 2002, how many of the 471 payments over £100,000 made to agricultural producers were in the bands (a) £100,001-£150,000, (b) £150,001-£200,000, (c) £200,001-£250,000, (d) £250,001-£300,000, (e) £300,001-£350,000, (f) £350,001-£400,000, (g) £400,001-£450,000, (h) £450,001-£500,000 and (i) over £500,000.
