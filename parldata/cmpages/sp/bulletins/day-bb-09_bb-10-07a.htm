Section A – Today’s Business

Meetings of Committees

All meetings take place in the Scottish Parliament, unless otherwise specified. Contact details for Committee Clerks are provided at the end of the Bulletin.

Economy, Energy and Tourism Committee
26th Meeting, 2009

The Committee will meet at 9.30 am in Committee Room 1

1. Decision on taking business in private: The Committee will decide whether to take item 4 in private.

2. Draft Budget Scrutiny 2010-11: The Committee will take evidence on the Scottish Government’s Draft Budget 2010-11 from—

Philip Riddle, Chief Executive, VisitScotland.

3. Arbitration (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will consider the Bill at Stage 2.

4. The way forward for Scotland’s banking, building society and financial services sector inquiry: The Committee will consider written evidence received for this inquiry and agree a list of witnesses to invite to appear before the Committee.

Rural Affairs and Environment Committee
24th Meeting, 2009

The Committee will meet at 9.30 am in Committee Room 3

1. Public Services Reform (Scotland) Bill (in private): The Committee will consider a draft report to the Finance Committee.

2. Draft Budget scrutiny 2010-11: The Committee will take evidence on the Scottish Government’s budget proposals 2010-11 from—

Richard Lochhead MSP, Cabinet Secretary for Rural Affairs and Environment, David Barnes, Deputy Director, Agriculture and Rural Development Division, Paul Gray, Director General Environment, and Ross Scott, Finance Team Leader, Rural Affairs and Environment Portfolio, Scottish Government.

3. Draft Budget scrutiny 2010-11 (in private): The Committee will review the evidence heard earlier in the meeting.

4. Marine (Scotland) Bill (in private): The Committee will consider a draft Stage 1 report.

Health and Sport Committee
26th Meeting, 2009

The Committee will meet at 9.45 am in Committee Room 5

1. Public Services Reform (Scotland) Bill (in private): The Committee will consider a draft report to the Finance Committee.

2. Budget Process 2010-11 (Stage 2): The Committee will take evidence on the Scottish Government’s Draft Budget 2010-11 from—

Jon Ford, Head of Health Policy and Economic Research, British Medical Association;
Theresa Fyffe, Director, Royal College of Nursing Scotland;
John Gallacher, Regional Organiser and Secretary, UNISON;

and then from—

Tim Davison, Chief Executive, NHS Lanarkshire;
Malcolm Iredale, Director of Finance, NHS Highland.

3. Subordinate legislation: The Committee will take evidence on the draft Health Board Elections (Scotland) Regulations 2009 from—

Nicola Sturgeon MSP, Cabinet Secretary for Health and Wellbeing, Kathleen Preston, Solicitor, Solicitors Health and Community Care Division, Legal Directorate, and Robert Kirkwood, Business Planning Executive, Scottish Government.

That the Health and Sport Committee recommends that the draft Health Board Elections (Scotland) Regulations 2009 be approved.

5. Subordinate legislation: The Committee will consider the following negative instrument—

the National Health Service (Discipline Committees) (Scotland) Amendment Regulations 2009 (SSI 2009/308).

Education, Lifelong Learning and Culture Committee
27th Meeting, 2009

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in Committee Room 4

Keith Brown MSP, Minister for Schools and Skills, David Roy, Policy Officer, Teacher Development Team, Schools Directorate, and Neel Mojee, Principal Legal Officer, Solicitors Childrens, Education, Enterprise and Pensions Division, Scottish Government.

Local Government and Communities Committee
25th Meeting, 2009

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in Committee Room 6

1. Decision on taking business in private: The Committee will decide whether to take item 5 in private.

2. Draft Budget Scrutiny 2010-11: The Committee will take evidence on the Scottish Government’s Draft Budget 2010-11 from—

Nicola Sturgeon MSP, Cabinet Secretary for Health and Wellbeing, Mike Foulis, Director for Housing and Regeneration, and Ann Thomson, Head of Social Inclusion and Voluntary Issues, Scottish Government.

Debate on the subject of—

S3M-4851 Mike Rumbles: Kintore Station and Aberdeen Crossrail—That the Parliament welcomes the announcement that passenger numbers at the reopened Laurencekirk station have exceeded estimations by around 80%; notes that the decision by the previous Scottish Executive to reopen the Laurencekirk station followed a long community-based campaign; believes that a reopened Kintore station would be similarly successful; expresses its concern that progress on the Aberdeen Crossrail project has stalled under the current Scottish Government, and would welcome funding being brought forward to reopen Kintore station as part of the Aberdeen Crossrail project.
