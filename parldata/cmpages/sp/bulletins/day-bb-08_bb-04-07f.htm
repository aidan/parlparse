Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (♦).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S3M-1674 Christina McKelvie: Lanarkshire Supershow—That the Parliament congratulates the high school pupils from North and South Lanarkshire for the spectacular Lanarkshire Supershow performed at Motherwell Concert Hall on the 31 March 2008; commends the work of New Lanarkshire Ltd in bringing this show to the stage and recognising the amazing talents of all the young people in Lanarkshire, and commends the dedication and commitment of the young people in demonstrating to the tabloid media that the majority of Scotland’s young people are thoroughly decent, talented human beings.

*S3M-1673 Patricia Ferguson: Congratulations to Allied Vehicles Ltd—That the Parliament notes that Allied Vehicles Ltd is the United Kingdom’s leading manufacturer of wheelchair accessible cars, taxis and minibuses and has recently been recognised for its achievements in both the Glasgow Business Awards and the Scottish Enterprise Glasgow Awards; congratulates Michael and Gerry Facenna and their company for their commitment to the Possilpark area of Glasgow, and congratulates the company on its recent acquisition of land to allow it to build a new factory in the area, from which it has recruited 85% of its workforce.

*S3M-1672 John Scott: Church of Scotland Housing and Loan Fund—That the Parliament notes that Church of Scotland ministers, upon their retirement, having been required during the course of their working lives to reside in tied accommodation, are often obliged to seek a loan from the Church of Scotland Housing and Loan Fund in order to secure a home for their retirement; further notes that The Employer-Financed Retirement Benefits (Excluded Benefits for Tax Purposes) Regulations 2007 (SI 2007/3537), exempts accommodation provided to retired ministers of religion from the terms of the Income Tax (Earnings and Pensions) Act 2003 but that no corresponding exemption is made in respect of beneficial loans; recognises that as a result many retired ministers and their families applying to the Housing and Loan Fund in order to help secure accommodation upon leaving their tied home are penalised as a result of this lack of exemption, and calls on the UK Government to exempt beneficial loans from the Income Tax (Earnings and Pensions) Act 2003.

*S3M-1671 Hugh O’Donnell: Council for Scottish Archaeology Changes its Name—That the Parliament welcomes the new name and re-branding of the Council for Scottish Archaeology (CSA) to Archaeology Scotland (AS), from 1 April 2008; agrees with the CSA Board that the new name and the changes associated with it will give a new impetus to the protection and promotion of Scotland’s rich archaeological heritage, and welcomes the fact that CSA/AS have been assured that protection of all archaeological and historical sites is now a condition of grant for all recipients of agri-environment grants under Tier 2 - Land Management Options and Tier 3 - Rural Priorities.

*S3M-1670 Ted Brocklebank: 90th Anniversary of the Formation of the Royal Air Force—That the Parliament celebrates the 90th Anniversary of the formation of the Royal Air Force (RAF); notes that the RAF, the world’s first independent air force, was created on 1 April 1918; notes with considerable pride the achievements and professionalism of our armed forces’ men and women; further notes the significant role which bases such as RAF Leuchars in North East Fife play in the military, economic and social fabric of Scotland, and notes that as a nation we can, therefore, be justifiably proud of our RAF, both past and present.

Supported by: Nanette Milne*, Elizabeth Smith*, Jamie McGrigor*, Jamie Stone*, John Park*, Robert Brown*, John Lamont*, Jackson Carlaw*, John Scott*, Keith Brown*, Mary Scanlon*, Kenneth Gibson*, Bill Aitken*, Ken Macintosh*

*S3M-1669♦ Bill Wilson: Commemorative Stamps for 200th Anniversary of Scots Language Dictionary and Funding for Complete Modern Scots Dictionary—That the Parliament notes that 2008 is the 200th anniversary of John Jamieson’s extensively researched An Etymological Dictionary of the Scottish Language, a book dedicated to the Baron of Renfrew, known also as the Prince of Wales, which included words from Renfrewshire and elsewhere in Scotland; notes that this dictionary set new standards in lexicography and has influenced two centuries of lexicographers, including Sir James Murray, the founding father of the Oxford English Dictionary, and those presently working for the Scots language dictionaries organisation; regrets, however, that two centuries after the publication of An Etymological Dictionary there is still no complete modern dictionary of the Scots language which includes words Scots shares with the related language, English; notes that this anomaly prevents the production of an effective spell-checker for Scots and makes automated translation impossible; believes that the existence of such a complete dictionary would raise the status of the Scots language and help prevent possible discrimination against the language and its speakers in the west of Scotland and elsewhere, and would welcome investigation of the feasibility of both providing adequate funds for the production of such a dictionary and the issuing of stamps to commemorate John Jamieson’s remarkable achievement.

*S3M-1668 Stuart McMillan: 100 Years of History for 3rd Gourock Company Boys’ Brigade—That the Parliament welcomes the historic milestone reached by the 3rd Gourock Company Boys’ Brigade of being in existence for 100 years and acknowledges that this is a great achievement for the organisation which will be showcasing memorabilia charting their 100 years of history at the Islay Hall in the Old Church Gourock and Ashton Church during April 2008.

Supported by: Jamie McGrigor*, Bashir Ahmad*, Ross Finnie*, Bill Kidd*, Elizabeth Smith*, Nanette Milne*, Jamie Stone*, Roseanna Cunningham*, Jamie Hepburn*

*S3M-1667# Sandra White: Congratulations to Glaswegian Newspaper—That the Parliament congratulates The Glaswegian newspaper for its campaign against post office closures and in particular its involvement of the public and Glasgow City Council and sincerely hopes that all interested parties will work together to stop the further erosion of post office services in Glasgow and Scotland.

Supported by: Jamie Hepburn*, Bashir Ahmad*, Rob Gibson*, Bill Kidd*, Kenneth Gibson*, Stuart McMillan*, Brian Adam*, Bob Doris*

*S3M-1666 Keith Brown: World Cross Country Championships—That the Parliament recognises the success of the International Association of Athletics Federation’s 36th World Cross Country Championships held in Scotland on 30 March 2008; believes that this reflects well on Scottish Athletics, the many volunteers and participants involved, and the country as a whole, and extends its congratulations to all those responsible for further highlighting Scotland’s professionalism and ability to host international sporting events to the wider world.

Supported by: Bill Kidd*, Bashir Ahmad*, Jamie McGrigor*, Nanette Milne*, Hugh O’Donnell*, Shirley-Anne Somerville*, Jamie Stone*, Kenneth Gibson*, Roseanna Cunningham*, John Lamont*, Brian Adam*, Stuart McMillan*, Mike Pringle*, Jamie Hepburn*, Joe FitzPatrick*

*S3M-1665 Robin Harper: Congratulations to Chris Hoy, the Real McHoy!—That the Parliament congratulates Chris Hoy on his truly inspirational World Championship performance and notes that his two gold medals for individual sprint and keirin events, added to silver in the team sprint event, take his tally of Olympic and World Championship medals to an incredible total of  20, not including his notable collection of World Cup and Commonwealth Games successes; acknowledges the diverse range of his prodigious talent; recognises the valuable role of his inspirational career in encouraging active and sporting lifestyles; acknowledges the important role that the velodrome at Meadowbank Stadium played in his development as a track cyclist; notes that without the provision of a track cycling facility in the city many would lose the opportunity of involvement in one of Scotland’s most successful sports, and agrees that Scotland and Edinburgh, his native city, should  honour his achievements by providing and enhancing track cycling facilities in the capital.

*S3M-1664 Hugh O’Donnell: Merger of Epilepsy Scotland and Enlighten - Tackling Epilepsy—That the Parliament welcomes the merger of Epilepsy Scotland and Enlighten – Tackling Epilepsy, based in the Lothians; welcomes this progressive move; believes that this merger of two excellent services will bring added support and quality to services that help nearly 40,000 people affected by this common neurological condition, and hopes that it will secure new funding and build on the existing innovative projects that already assist households living with epilepsy and continue to make a difference and transform people’s lives.

Supported by: James Kelly*

*S3M-1663 Stuart McMillan: Greenock Academy Hailed as Best in Scotland—That the Parliament welcomes the recent announcement that Greenock Academy has been recognised as one of the best schools in Scotland; acknowledges the report from HM Inspectorate of Education that it was rated the best non-denominational school in Scotland, and recognises that within the Inverclyde area there is a high standard of education provision which is reflected in the recent glowing appraisal of Greenock Academy.

Supported by: Aileen Campbell*, Bashir Ahmad*, Bill Kidd*, Rob Gibson*, Kenneth Gibson*, Ross Finnie*, Bob Doris*, Jamie McGrigor*
