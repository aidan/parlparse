Business Bulletin No. 137/2001

Tuesday 25 September 2001

Section E : Written questions lodged on 24 September 2001
  

S1W-18463 Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will continue the policy of prescribing beta interferon through the NHS to those people diagnosed with multiple sclerosis who it is considered could benefit from the drug.

S1W-18464 Colin Campbell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many children identified as having Waardenburg Syndrome have a Record of Needs, broken down by local authority area.

S1W-18465 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will detail all secondary legislation provided for under the Housing (Scotland) Act 2001 and the timetable for such legislation coming into force.

S1W-18466 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Housing Improvement Task Force is the only group looking at the issue of sellers' surveys and, if not, whether it will give details of any other such groups, including their remit, membership and meetings held to date, and details of when the task force and any other groups are due to report.

S1W-18467 Mr Duncan Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether responses to its consultation paper A Modern Complaints System: Consultation Paper for Public Sector Ombudsmen in Scotland favoured the inclusion of enterprise external adjudicators in a "one stop shop", whether it intends to create such posts and, if so, (a) when the adjudicators will be appointed; (b) where they will be located; (c) what their salary scale will be; (d) to whom they will be responsible, and (e) when and how often they will report the details of their findings.

S1W-18468 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to establish rehabilitation units for people with epilepsy.

S1W-18469 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether selenium deficiency is a cause of any medical conditions and, if so, what conditions it causes.

S1W-18470 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the domiciliary eye test is available to all those who would benefit from the service.

S1W-18471 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is currently being taken to encourage more people to state their intentions regarding organ transplantation.

S1W-18472 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any statistics are available which provide evidence that a ban on tobacco advertising reduces the amount of tobacco smoked and consequently the death rate from smoking-related diseases.

S1W-18473 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people were given a domiciliary eye test in each health board area in each of the last five years.

S1W-18474 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it has taken, or intends to take, in light of the findings of the publication The Effectiveness of Public Health Nursing: A Review of Systematic Reviews.

S1W-18475 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider recognising rural health as a separate speciality in the undergraduate curriculum for medical students.

S1W-18476 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive how much revenue has been raised by the sale of Bangour Village Hospital.

S1W-18477 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive when it plans to respond to the proposal by West Lothian Health Care NHS Trust and West Lothian Council with regard to the building of a new hospital for mental health patients.

S1W-18478 Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to investigate the possibility of setting up a centre based on the Son-Rise Program run by the Autism Treatment Centre of America.

S1W-18479 Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to collate information regarding the cost of caring for and educating persons with an autistic spectrum disorder over their lifetime.

S1W-18480 Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive what the take-up figures were for the measles, mumps and rubella vaccination in the most recent period for which figures are available.

S1W-18481 Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many residential places are available across Scotland for the treatment of drug and alcohol addiction, broken down by health board area.

S1W-18482 Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many residential places have been utilised for the treatment of drug and alcohol addiction since May 1999, broken down by health board area.

S1W-18483 Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people in Scotland are (a) drugs dependent and (b) alcohol dependent, broken down by age, gender and health board area.

S1W-18484 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-16758 by Susan Deacon on 31 July 2001, whether any revenue and capital resources within the health budget remain unallocated following the realignment of resources to support the implementation of free personal care.

S1W-18485 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-16758 by Susan Deacon on 31 July 2001, whether there are any as yet unallocated capital and revenue resources within budget headings other than health.

S1W-18486 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-16764 by Jackie Baillie on 31 July 2001, when level III information on the social justice budget will be published.

S1W-18487 Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-15496 by Mr Jim Wallace on 6 June 2001, what penalties were imposed on the 1,081 offenders found guilty of driving whilst disqualified in 1999 whose convictions did not result in custody, a community service order or admonition.

S1W-18488 Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive what range of fines was imposed on drivers found guilty of driving whilst disqualified in 1999, what total amount of fines was levied and what monies in respect of those fines have been received to date.

S1W-18489 Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-15495 by Mr Jim Wallace on 6 June 2001, what penalties were imposed on the 10,669 offenders found guilty of driving without insurance in and whose convictions did not result in custody, a community service order or admonition.

S1W-18490 Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive what range of fines was imposed on drivers found guilty of driving without insurance in 1999, what total amount of fines was levied and what monies in respect of those fines have been received to date.

S1W-18491 Mr Andy Kerr: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to close the "digital divide".

S1W-18492 Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to reform the law on psychiatric injury.

S1W-18493 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive why the national strategy recommended in the Report of the Advisory Group on Youth Crime which had a deadline for implementation no later than April 2001 has not yet been issued for consultation.

S1W-18494 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive what the amended timescale is for drawing up, issuing for consultation and implementing the national strategy as recommended in the Report of the Advisory Group on Youth Crime.
