*S1M-2889 Rhoda Grant: Screentime-That the Parliament congratulates the project director and trustees of Screentime on achieving charitable status for the organisation; supports their plans for providing people in the Highlands and Islands with access to film and media professionals, to new-media technology, to industry training workshops and to community and career development opportunities; welcomes the success of the two pilot programmes in 1999 and 2001; recognises the contribution that Screentime could make to the sustainable development of the region, and wishes it well in raising funds for the programme of events later this year.

*S1M-2888# Mr Andrew Welsh: End-of-Life Refrigerators and Freezers-That the Parliament understands that from January 2002 EC Regulation 2037/2000 requires all ozone-depleting substances to be recovered from domestic refrigerators and freezers before they are recycled or sent for disposal; notes that minimal facilities exist in Scotland for the removal of such substances and that local authorities are having to stockpile end-of-life refrigerators and freezers until adequate facilities are available, and is concerned at the Scottish Executive's lack of preparedness concerning the implementation of this legislation and its failure to ensure that adequate facilities are available for this purpose.

*S1M-2887# Tricia Marwick: GM Trials, Newport-That the Parliament welcomes the formation of a steering group to oppose the proposed GM field trial in Newport, Fife; notes the statement made by Mr Charles Saunders, consultant in public health for Fife Health Board and a member of the BMA that the Scottish Executive is gambling with public health, and considers that the Minister for Environment and Rural Development, who is responsible for deciding whether the trials go ahead, should withhold agreement until the seed manufactures can offer conclusive proof that there will be no health or environmental damage from GM Crops.

*S1M-2886 Mr Duncan Hamilton: Rural Postal Services-That the Parliament recognises the vital role that the postal service has to play in our rural communities, particularly those in the Highlands and Islands; supports the principle of universal postal tariffs and services throughout Scotland, regardless of location; notes the potential threat posed to rural postal services by the Postal Services Commission's proposals, and calls upon the relevant parliamentary committee to conduct an immediate and thorough investigation into those proposals and into the long-term future of the postal service in rural areas.

*S1M-2885# Cathy Peattie: Hamish Henderson-That the Parliament notes with regret the death of Hamish Henderson and wishes to place on record its appreciation of his lifetime devotion to international solidarity, peace and socialism, his many contributions to Scottish culture and politics, including his role in gathering traditional songs, his support for other artists and his authorship of the song that many believe should be Scotland's (inter)national anthem, The Freedom Come All Ye.

*S1M-2884 Mr Andy Kerr: Scottish Qualifications Authority Bill: Financial Resolution-That the Parliament, for the purposes of any Act of the Scottish Parliament resulting from the Scottish Qualifications Authority Bill, agrees to the following expenditure out of the Scottish Consolidated Fund-

  1. expenditure of the Scottish Ministers in consequence of the Act; and

(b) increases attributable to the Act in the sums payable out of that Fund under any other enactment.

Supported by: Cathy Jamieson*, Nicol Stephen*, Peter Peacock*

*S1M-2883 Bruce Crawford: Nuclear Power Stations-That the Parliament notes that consent for nuclear and other electricity power stations over 50 megawatts, under section 36 of the Electricity Act 1989, is a responsibility devolved to Scottish Executive Ministers and believes that the final decision over consent for nuclear power stations, now and in the future, must remain with Scottish Executive Ministers accountable to the Parliament.

Supported by: Michael Russell*, Alex Neil*, Mr John Swinney*

*S1M-2882 Nicola Sturgeon: National Health Service-That the Parliament notes that there are fewer nurses and fewer acute beds in the NHS now than in 1999, that the NHS is treating fewer out-patients, in-patients and day case patients now than in 1999, that fewer patients are being seen within the Scottish Executive's target times than was the case in 1999, that there are 16,000 more people on NHS waiting lists now than in 1999 and that median waiting times are 10 days longer than in 1999; is concerned that the NHS is shrinking, and calls upon the Scottish Executive to pursue policies that will reverse this reduction in capacity and deliver an NHS of the standard that patients in Scotland are entitled to expect.

Supported by: Shona Robison*

*S1M-2881 Malcolm Chisholm: Food Protection (Emergency Prohibitions) (Amnesic Shellfish Poisoning) (West Coast) (No.2) (Scotland) Order 2002-That the Health and Community Care Committee, in consideration of the Food Protection (Emergency Prohibitions) (Amnesic Shellfish Poisoning) (West Coast) (No.2) (Scotland) Order 2002 (SSI 2002/65), recommends that the order be approved.

Supported by: Mrs Mary Mulligan*, Hugh Henry*

*S1M-2880 Ms Margo MacDonald: Palestine-That the Parliament condemns the policies of the Israeli government towards Palestinians; urges the Prime Minister to use his best efforts to convince the government of Ariel Sharon that the continued brutality in territory occupied illegally by Israel under resolutions of the United Nations has resulted in the inevitability of the establishment of the State of Palestine, and counsels that this Israeli government and future governments should seek for this to be realised in peaceful co-operation between the State of Israel and the future State of Palestine.

*S1M-2879 Michael Russell: Sportscotland Relocation to North Ayrshire-That the Parliament notes the requirement on sportscotland to consider relocating from Edinburgh; recognises the potential of North Ayrshire as a place for growth in government related jobs and the poor record to date of such job creation in the area; urges North Ayrshire Council to take positive and determined steps to attract such jobs and especially those that would be created by the move of sportscotland to the area; observes that sportscotland already has facilities in North Ayrshire, and therefore calls on the Scottish Executive to direct sportscotland to consider North Ayrshire as the preferred site for its relocation.

*S1M-2878 Tommy Sheridan: Legalisation of Cannabis and Drug Laws-That the Parliament notes and welcomes the resolutions at the Liberal Democrat spring conference to support the legislation of cannabis and issuing of heroin on prescription to registered addicts; further notes that this policy position has been promoted by the Scottish Socialist Party since the Parliament's inception, and believes that it is time to radically overhaul the unrealistic drugs laws and invest in treatment, rehabilitation and community facilities as part of a social and medical approach to drugs policy, as opposed to the "Just Say No" rhetoric of the past.
