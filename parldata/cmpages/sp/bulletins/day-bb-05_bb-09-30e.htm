Section E – Written questions lodged on 29 September 2005

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-19549Δ Christine May: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will give a progress report on the implementation of the recommendations made in the Review of Historic Scotland published in February 2004.

S2W-19550 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure that meat coming into Scotland is free from Creutzfeldt-Jakob Disease prions.

S2W-19551 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has taken steps to ensure that meat coming into Scotland undergoes the same rigorous safety checks as meat produced in Scotland.

S2W-19552 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has taken, or will take, steps to ensure that meat coming into Scotland comes from animals that have not been fed on bonemeal which may contain Creutzfeldt-Jakob Disease prions.

S2W-19553 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has undertaken, or will undertake, research into the pathologies of spongiform encephalopathies, and in particular the pathology of Bovine Spongiform Encephalopathy and its link with the new variant of Creutzfeldt-Jakob Disease, to determine the safety of meat imports for human consumption.

S2W-19554 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has undertaken, or will undertake, research into links between bonemeal which may contain human remains and the pathologies of spongiform encephalopathies.

S2W-19555 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what account it took of epidemiological studies into the links between cholesterol and dementia when framing guidance for doctors on cholesterol levels in patients.

S2W-19556 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considered possible links between low cholesterol and dementia when framing policy on the use of statins.

S2W-19557 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration it has given to research showing that high cholesterol in later life can be associated with a decreased risk of dementia.

S2W-19558 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-19262 by Lewis Macdonald on 26 September 2005, whether all care homes should receive a gross fee equivalent to the full cost of providing the service on a level comparable with that of local authority-run care homes.

S2W-19559 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive how many appeals have been lodged with it against the erection of TETRA masts; how many were lodged by (a) individuals, (b) communities and (c) local authorities, and how many in each category were successful.

S2W-19560 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide the names of and background information on, the members of the Scotland-Malawi projects.

S2W-19561 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive how much has been (a) spent on or (b) allocated to the Scotland-Malawi projects, broken down by project or service.

S2W-19562 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide details of the members of the task force addessing employment opportunities in Moray; how much expenditure has been involved so far; what interaction there has been between Executive departments, and what contact there has been between the Executive, the Ministry of Defence and the Department of Trade and Industry on this issue.

S2W-19563 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has made any applications for funding under Strand A of INTERREG III.

S2W-19564 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what applications for funding it has made under Strand B of INTERREG III.

S2W-19565 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what applications for funding it has made under Strand C of INTERREG III.

S2W-19566 John Swinburne: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-19005 by Robert Brown on 20 September 2005, what impact the additional £12 million in funding will have on kinship carers who do not currently receive any payment in areas such as Glasgow.

S2W-19567 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the geographical boundaries in which area-based policies will be applied under the draft Crofting Reform Bill have been delineated.

S2W-19568 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what different types and amounts of funding it provides for the support and promotion of Gaelic.
