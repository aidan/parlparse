Business Bulletin No. 164/2002

Thursday 14 November 2002

Section D : Oral Questions selected for answer on 21 November 2002
  

Question Time

1. Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what business advice it is supplying to urban sub-post offices to expand their role. (S1O-5935)

2. Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to expand the Ayrshire economy. (S1O-5934)

3. Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to promote the development of small towns. (S1O-5907)

4. Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will give an up-to-date assessment of the contingency arrangements for the fire services. (S1O-5946)

5. Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will revise the Less Favoured Area Support Scheme with regard to minimum stocking densities. (S1O-5927)

6. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any new scientific work has been commissioned with regard to white fish stocks. (S1O-5918)

7. Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to ensure the safety of children conveyed by school transport. (S1O-5943)

8. Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any provision is in place to assist local authorities with additional gritting should there be a particularly severe winter. (S1O-5925)

9. Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made on improving transport infrastructure in Ayrshire. (S1O-5908)

10. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the existing powers of councils with regard to the regulation of the sale of unofficial merchandise by street traders outside football grounds are inadequate and, if so, whether it will strengthen such powers. (S1O-5921)

11. Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will now seek to negotiate a reinstatement of the 10% reduction in the west of Scotland nephrops quota. (S1O-5911)

12. Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether rough sleeping will have ended by 1 May 2003. (S1O-5905)

13. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it or its agencies and non-departmental public bodies have had with Her Majesty's Government or the Royal Navy regarding the use of low frequency active sonar and its effect on whales and dolphins. (S1O-5926)

14. Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will rule out imposing tolls or charges on any new or upgraded roads. (S1O-5914)

15. Mr John McAllion: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with local authorities with regard to using joint venture companies as a vehicle for investment in schools. (S1O-5924)

16. Mr Keith Raffan: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it intends to take to tackle binge drinking, particularly among younger women, and when it plans to seek a parliamentary debate on its Plan for Action on alcohol problems. (S1O-5948)

17. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what concerns it has regarding the funding of free personal care for elderly people. (S1O-5922)

18. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether licenses for houses in multiple occupation are being issued for properties for which appropriate planning permission has not also been sought. (S1O-5951)

19. Brian Fitzpatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive how it ensures that regional selective assistance funding contributes to its overall objectives of sustainability and closing the opportunity gap. (S1O-5950)

20. Cathie Craigie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the designation order for the Cairngorms National Park will adhere to the Sandford principles. (S1O-5941)

21. Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will give assurances that the children of asylum seekers living in Scotland will continue to be educated in existing state-sector schools and not through separate, segregated education. (S1O-5920)

22. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether adequate alcohol detoxification services are in place. (S1O-5917)

23. Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive how all stakeholders will derive benefit from the ending of the two-tier workforce in new public private partnerships. (S1O-5936)

24. John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is with regard to the relocation of the headquarters of Scottish Natural Heritage to Inverness. (S1O-5928)

25. Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to improve services to children at risk. (S1O-5944)

26. Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has for ending the over 30-month rule on the slaughter of cattle. (S1O-5909)

27. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met representatives of the Scottish fishing industry and what matters were discussed. (S1O-5919)

28. Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to promote the establishment of a single airport for central Scotland. (S1O-5955)

29. Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will support the Gaelic Language (Scotland) Bill introduced on 13 November 2002. (S1O-5916)

30. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in encouraging recycling. (S1O-5942)
