Business Bulletin No. 96 / 2004

Monday 7th June 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-1420 Rhona Brankin: Gardening Scotland 2004 - 4 to 6 June-That the Parliament wishes the Gardening Scotland 2004 event at the Royal Highland Centre at Ingliston every success; congratulates the organisers on attracting 400 exhibitors to what is Scotland's biggest gardening exhibition; notes that over 30,000 visitors are expected to attend the event; acknowledges the many benefits of gardening, particularly in keeping older people active, and recognises the important contribution that horticulture makes to the Scottish economy.

*S2M-1419 Shiona Baird: Crisis FareShare-That the Parliament recognises the valuable contribution made by the two existing Crisis FareShare projects in Scotland, whose network of volunteers collects and redistributes surplus fresh food from manufacturers and retail outlets to centres and shelters for homeless people; notes the direct benefits of these projects in providing nutritious food to homeless people while helping to minimise food waste and its subsequent disposal to landfill sites; recognises the additional benefits of such schemes in allowing resources to be diverted into vital counselling, resettlement and other support services and in encouraging homeless people to gain social contact and new skills by participating as volunteers; commends the scheme as an excellent example of socially and environmentally responsible partnership between food manufacturers and the voluntary sector, and calls upon the Scottish Executive to make a firm financial commitment to ensure the continuation of existing projects and the further development of fareshare initiatives across Scotland.

*S2M-1418 Trish Godman: Tolled Bridges-That the Parliament notes the comments by the Deputy First Minister and Minister for Enterprise and Lifelong Learning in the debate on the economy of the Highlands and Islands on Thursday 3 June 2004: "However, I reaffirm and reassure members and John Farquhar Munro that the Executive is committed to ending the discredited tolling regime on the Skye Bridge. Professional advisers have been appointed and discussions with Skye Bridge Ltd have already begun. Having conferred with the Minister for Transport on the issue in anticipation of such a question, I believe that we can achieve our goal by the end of this year." (Official Report, c 8834 - 35) and seeks assurances from the Scottish Executive that, if the tolls on the Skye Bridge are lifted in the timescale suggested, tolls on the Erskine Bridge and other tolled bridges are lifted simultaneously in the interest of consistency and fairness.

*S2M-1417 John Swinburne: D-Day Commemoration and the Treatment of Veterans and Widows-That the Parliament congratulates the First Minister on his decision to attend the D-Day 60th anniversary remembrance ceremony on the Normandy beaches; hopes that he takes this opportunity to remind the Prime Minister, Tony Blair, that far too many of the D-Day veterans and the immediate relatives of those who gave their lives for our democracy are currently living below the Government's own poverty level and that this generation is entitled to much more than a free television license on reaching 75 years old or a 25p increase in their pension on reaching the age of 80 years old, and therefore believes that it is unacceptable in 2004 that Scotland is still not a land where heroes can afford to live with the dignity that was earned by the ultimate sacrifices paid during World War Two.

*S2M-1416# Mr Kenny MacAskill: International Refugee Week-That the Parliament notes that 14 to 20 June 2004 is International Refugee Week; welcomes the great social, cultural and economic benefits that refugees have brought to Scotland over the years; recognises that they continue to bring these benefits and in many instances arrive in Scotland as a result of war, and destitution caused thereby; notes that events are taking place worldwide to mark the week, and welcomes the establishment of the Edinburgh Refugee Centre at St George's West Church in Shandwick Place, Edinburgh, providing humanitarian support, advice and assistance for refugees and asylum seekers.

S2M-1415 Ms Rosemary Byrne: Belmont Academy Parliament Success-That the Parliament congratulates the pupils and staff of Belmont Academy in Ayr for winning the National Youth Parliament competition, Holyrood Model, where the pupils took on roles of MSPs and conducted their own parliamentary sessions, bringing to life the process of parliament and devolved government.

Supported by: Christine Grahame*

S2M-1413# Carolyn Leckie: Assault on Lanarkshire NHS-That the Parliament is extremely concerned at the announcement by NHS Lanarkshire that it will make cuts of between 5 and 10% of its budget this year in order to address a projected deficit of £20 million; is alarmed that the Scottish Executive refused to assist in avoiding such draconian measures by insisting that the board reach financial balance this year and by rejecting the board's proposals for a two-year recovery plan; notes concern that legislation and changes to be made by the Executive to consultant contracts, the new deal for junior doctors, the new GP contract and other such initiatives have not been fully funded by the Executive's allocation to NHS boards, thereby putting the service under intolerable strain; believes that the Executive should take immediate steps to avoid cuts in NHS Lanarkshire by making adequate funding available; believes that the two PFI hospitals at Hairmyres and Wishaw have placed unacceptable pressure on the board's revenue budget to the detriment of patient care and have led directly to bed cuts, reduced staffing levels and threats to the level of services provided from the non-PFI hospital at Monklands whilst the PFI consortia rake in huge profits; condemns specifically the consortia responsible for the Hairmyres PFI who are additionally set to benefit to the tune of nearly £1 billion from a re-financing deal, and believes that PFI is a wasteful means of funding public building projects, that it only benefits banks and big business and should be abolished and that all public buildings should be financed via direct public funding and the public works loan board.

S2M-1412 Mary Scanlon: Scottish First Division Champions-That the Parliament fully condemns the decision taken on 1 June 2004 by the Scottish Premier League (SPL) to deny Scottish First Division champions, Inverness Caledonian Thistle, entry into Scotland's top league next season, meaning that, for the second successive year, the team finishing bottom of the SPL has not been relegated and the First Division winners have not been rewarded for their success by rightfully gaining promotion to the SPL; notes that this is despite the club reaching a suitable ground-sharing agreement with Aberdeen Football Club to play at Pittodrie Stadium for one season, and believes that the decision takes away the basic principle of being rewarded for success.

S2M-1406# Helen Eadie: Reserved Contracts to Support Remploy-That the Parliament recognises the work of Remploy in Scotland in supporting 700 people with special needs in employment and congratulates the organisation on its training and development programme which has seen the creation of dedicated learning rooms at all 10 of its employment sites, with 275 employees currently undertaking skills training in language and mathematics and a further 135 involved in other learning activity, and a commitment of at least 5% of working time over a year to be dedicated to personal development; notes that this programme has been implemented in just over two years, and believes that the Scottish Executive and all publicly-funded organisations should examine their procurement policies with a view to reserving contracts for supported employment organisations such as those working with Remploy.

Supported by: Mark Ballard*

S2M-1405 Mr Stewart Maxwell: Norway's Ban on Smoking in Public Places-That the Parliament congratulates Norway on its decision to ban smoking in all public places; notes that the proposal comes after heavy pressure from restaurant workers' unions who say that their members have a higher incidence of lung cancer than others due to passive smoking and that the Norwegian Health Ministry states that between 300 and 500 people die of passive smoking in Norway each year; notes that, even before the ban came into force, the smoking rates had dropped from 29% to 26%, suggesting that many smokers are looking for a reason to give up smoking and that a ban such as this assists them in doing so, and recommends that members follow the example set by Norway by supporting such measures as the Prohibition of Smoking in Regulated Areas (Scotland) Bill to protect the health of both workers and patrons of eating establishments.

Supported by: Michael Matheson, Brian Adam, Richard Lochhead, Carolyn Leckie, Rob Gibson, Jim Mather, Eleanor Scott, Alex Neil, Chris Ballance, Christine Grahame, Bruce Crawford, Mark Ballard*, Robin Harper*

S2M-1397 Rosie Kane: Gulf War Syndrome-That the Parliament expresses its deep concern for Mr Alexander Izett, 34, a Scottish veteran of the first Gulf War, who has been on hunger strike since 1 May 2004 to demand an independent public inquiry into the causes and medical treatment of Gulf War Syndrome; notes that, in preparation for deployment, he received nine vaccinations in 24 hours, and that since 1993 he has suffered ill-health, including autoimmune osteoporosis which a 2003 court ruling found was caused by this "concoction of drugs"; further notes that many other veterans have suffered similar ill-health and even death, and calls on Her Majesty's Government to set up an independent public inquiry into all aspects of Gulf War Syndrome.

S2M-1396 Rosie Kane: Mordechai Vanunu and Israel's Secret Nuclear Weapons Programme-That the Parliament condemns the Israeli Government's treatment of Mordechai Vanunu who was released from prison on 21 April 2004 after being held in prison for 18 years, 11 of which were in solitary confinement, for revealing the existence of the Israeli secret nuclear weapons programme; furthermore strongly opposes restrictions placed upon Mr Vanunu by the Israeli Government whereby Mr Vanunu is not allowed to leave the country, visit any embassy or speak to foreigners or media; believes that it is ironic that Mr Vanunu was jailed for exposing secret weapons of mass destruction whilst Iraq was attacked and occupied for supposedly not revealing weapons of mass destruction; considers that the First Minister should write to Ariel Sharon inviting Mr Vanunu to Scotland, a safe haven where he can live in freedom and safety having suffered long enough as a result of his brave stance in relation to weapons of mass destruction and the Israeli Government's secret programme; notes that should Mr Vanunu reach these shores it would not be appropriate to hold him in Dungavel House Immigration and Removal Centre, and supports the Scottish Socialist Party in taking this initiative if the First Minister feels that for any reason he cannot.
