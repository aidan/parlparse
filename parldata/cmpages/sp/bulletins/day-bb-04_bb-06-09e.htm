Business Bulletin No. 98 / 2004

Wednesday 9th June 2004

Section E : Written questions lodged on Wednesday 8th June 2004
  

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-8822 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Crofters Commission is to remove the bull from its coat of arms in consequence of the abolition of the Livestock Improvement Scheme.

S2W-8823 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-5591 by Mr Andy Kerr on 28 January 2004, why the outcome of the review of the police funding formula has not been published and what its findings were with specific reference to the impact of the review on funding for Grampian Police.

S2W-8824 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to S2W-7721 by Mr Andy Kerr on 5 May 2004, whether it was consulted by the Electoral Commission before the announcement on the referendum was made.

S2W-8825 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what its recommended funding levels for roads maintenance have been, broken down by local authority, in (a) 1999-2000, (b) 2000-01, (c) 2001-02, (d) 2002-03 and (e) 2003-04.

S2W-8826 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Scottish Medicines Consortium (SMC) will consider its position on Zevalin; how long thereafter guidance will be issued on its use, and whether Zevalin, which has a UK and European Commission licence, can be prescribed by NHS Lothian or NHS Borders without first being assessed by the SMC.

S2W-8827 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive which local authorities maintain a Common Housing Register and whether all local authorities should be under an obligation to maintain such a register.

S2W-8828 Bristow Muldoon: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on any evaluations that have been carried out on the Agenda for Change early implementation sites in England and whether any evaluations have been carried out on the project sites in Scotland, in particular in relation to the management of additional annual leave entitlement.

S2W-8829 Bristow Muldoon: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are in place to retain nurses in the NHS and how any retention policy is monitored.

S2W-8830 Bristow Muldoon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many nurses and midwives have returned to the NHS since the introduction of the Return to Practice programme.

S2W-8831 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many victims of crime have been waiting for (a) less than one year, (b) one to two years, (c) two to three years and (d) more than three years for the Criminal Injuries Compensation Authority to settle their claim, broken down by sheriff court district.

S2W-8832 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of its budget was spent on non-departmental public bodies in (a) 1999-2000, (b) 2000-01, (c) 2001-02, (d) 2002-03 and (e) 2003-04.

S2W-8833 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people are currently (a) on waiting lists for housing and (b) homeless, broken down by local authority area.

S2W-8834 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) bankruptcies, (b) receiverships and (c) liquidations there were in (i) 2000-01, (ii) 2001-02, (iii) 2002-03 and (iv) 2003-04, broken down by sheriff court district.

S2W-8835 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many civil servants there are, broken down by local authority area in which they are located.

S2W-8836 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many warrants allowing the interception of communications were issued in (a) 2000, (b) 2001, (c) 2002 and (d) 2003.

S2W-8837 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people were convicted of drink-driving in (a) 2002-03 and (b) 2003-04.

S2W-8838 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how many additional allowances have been granted to dentists who take up their vocational training in a designated area and how much has been awarded in such allowances in each year since the scheme was introduced, broken down by designated area.

S2W-8839 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how many grants have been awarded to established dental practitioners setting up or expanding approved vocational training practices in each year since the scheme was introduced, broken down by NHS board area.

S2W-8840 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) children and (b) adults have received dental care from the Community Dental Service, in each year since 1999, broken down by NHS board area and expressed also as a percentage of the number of children and adults in each area.

S2W-8841 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how many vocational training places there have been for (a) all dentists and (b) community dental services, in each year since 1999.

S2W-8842 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive what average number of sessions per dentist was taken up with postgraduate education in each year since 1999.

S2W-8843 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how many clinical assistant posts for dentists there have been in each year since 1999.

S2W-8844 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how many, and what percentage of, dental nurses had any qualifications in each year since 1999, broken down by NHS board area.

S2W-8845 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how many dental therapists there have been in each year since 1999, broken down by NHS board area.

S2W-8846 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how many dental students have graduated in each year since 1999, broken down by institution.

S2W-8847 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive what level of funding has been identified in order to meet the cost of each scheme it has introduced to improve dental health.

S2W-8848 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how many false (a) payment claims have been made by dental practitioners in the NHS and (b) claims of exemption from dental charges have been made by patients in each year since 1999, broken down by NHS board area and detailing the amounts in each case.

S2W-8849 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how much has been spent on general dental services and how much, and what percentage of this amount, was paid directly by patients in each year since 1999.

S2W-8850 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how many student dental (a) hygienists, (b) therapists, (c) technicians and (d) nurses there have been in each year since 1999, broken down by institution.

S2W-8851 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how many dental (a) hygienists, (b) therapists, (c) technicians and (d) nurses graduated in each year since 1999, broken down by institution.

S2W-8852 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of five-year-olds in 2002-03 were free from dental caries, as referred to in Social Justice: A Scotland where everyone matters - Indicators of Progress 2003.

S2W-8853 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure that all its business suppliers' invoices are paid on time.

S2W-8854 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to reach its target of paying 100% of undisputed bills within 30 days.

S2W-8855 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive how much interest it (a) has paid (i) in each of the last three years and (ii) this year so far and (b) is liable for in relation to late payments of business suppliers' invoices.

S2W-8856 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of its business suppliers' invoices have been paid within 30 days of receipt since 1 January 2004.

S2W-8857 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Minister for Communities will reply to my letter dated 30 April 2004 regarding Mr A Hepburn of Peterhead.

S2W-8858 Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are being taken to ensure that land is identified and set aside for the provision of new waste recycling and treatment infrastructure for the delivery of area waste plans in (a) Edinburgh and (b) Scotland in order to meet EU landfill directive waste diversion targets.

S2W-8859 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will begin work on its next 10-year transport plan.

S2W-8860 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how much was spent to support the development of the Inverness to Stockholm route and on its marketing by VisitScotland or any other public body.

S2W-8861 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-8211 by Nicol Stephen on 3 June 2004, whether the franchise agreements can be viewed at Strategic Rail Authority offices in (a) Glasgow, (b) London or (c) both; why the agreements are not available in the Scottish Parliament Information Centre (SPICe), and whether steps will be taken to ensure that current and future agreements are made available in SPICe.

S2W-8862 Colin Fox: To ask the Scottish Executive how many primary schools are being considered for closure in the next two years.

S2W-8863 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many Scottish students have studied at foreign universities under the Erasmus programme in each of the last five years for which figures are available.

S2W-8864 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are in place to ensure that care workers and related staff are suitably vetted before being assigned to work with vulnerable elderly and disabled people.

S2W-8865 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what national standards are in place to vet care workers and whether such standards are sufficient.

S2W-8866 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what monitoring it undertakes of the Care Commission's regulation of private care companies.

S2W-8867 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive how it monitors the Care Commission's regulation of the quality of care offered in care homes.

S2W-8870 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what requirements are in place to ensure that care workers have undertaken appropriate training.

S2W-8871 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive whether current vetting procedures are sufficient for care workers who work in nursing homes and those who work in the patient's own home.

S2W-8872 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the Care Commission regarding alleged incidents of abuse, theft and bullying suffered by residents of care homes.

S2W-8873 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to introduce a pay scale for professional care workers with pay levels rising dependent on experience and qualifications.

S2W-8874 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria must be met by a private care company before a local authority can employ its services and whether present monitoring of these criteria is effective.

S2W-8875 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are in place to ensure that elderly people who are vulnerable through mental illness are assisted in making complaints about the quality of care they receive.

S2W-8876 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to promote the role of the Care Commission.

S2W-8878 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps have been taken to ensure that the circumstances surrounding the invitation to the First Minister to visit Normandy on the 60th anniversary of the D-Day landings are not repeated.

S2W-8879 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what its response is to the Remote and Rural Areas Resource Initiative's report on the provision of a pilot televisiting service for psychiatric in-patients in Aberdeen who originate from remote areas and whether it will support expansion of the scheme.

S2W-8880 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive from which budget heading its additional funding proposals for Scottish Opera will be sourced.

S2W-8881 Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it will take to reduce the number of women in prison.

S2W-8882 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are any plans to introduce a maritime equivalent of the rail company revenue support grant as part of its multimodal policies for removing freight from roads.
