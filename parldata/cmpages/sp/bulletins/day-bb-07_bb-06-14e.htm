Business Bulletin No. 88/2007: Thursday 14 June 2007

Section E – Written questions lodged on 13 June 2007

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-900 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the proposals to improve public transport provision for residents living in rural communities in south and east Ayrshire in order to access NHS services, as agreed by the Minister for Health and Community Care and the chair of NHS Ayrshire and Arran on 15 December 2006, will proceed as planned, in light of the decision to retain accident and emergency services at Ayr Hospital.

S3W-901 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive how it intends to improve transport provision for residents of South Carrick to enable them to access NHS services.

S3W-902 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive how it intends to improve transport provision for residents of the Doon Valley to enable them to access NHS services.

S3W-903 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive when the independent panel established to scrutinise revised proposals for health services in Ayrshire and Arran will be appointed.

S3W-904 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria will be used to appoint members to the independent panel established to scrutinise revised proposals for health services in Ayrshire and Arran.

S3W-905 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what public consultation will take place about the revised proposals for health services in Ayrshire and Arran.

S3W-906 David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-259 by Stewart Stevenson on 11 June 2007, how these statistics compare with accident rates on other major trunk roads in Scotland and what the respective statistics were for serious injuries and fatalities on such roads in the last five years.

S3W-907 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive on how many occasions and on what dates the Joint Ministerial Committee has met since 1999, excluding any sub-committee meetings.

S3W-908 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has received any communication from the UK Government since May 2007 to propose that a meeting of the Joint Ministerial Committee be convened.

S3W-909 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take to ensure the correct application of the Land Reform (Scotland) Act 2003.

S3W-910 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance it intends to issue to Scottish sheriffs regarding the interpretation of the Scottish Outdoor Access Code.

S3W-911 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any concerns regarding the Land Reform (Scotland) Act 2003 and its compliance with the European Convention on Human Rights.

S3W-912 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any requirements for the Scottish Agricultural College to obtain consent from the Secretary of State for Scotland in terms of the memorandum of association dated 17 November 1987 and incorporated under the Companies Act 1985 have been formally transferred to the Scottish Ministers and whether the Secretary of State for Scotland could overrule any decision made by the Scottish Ministers in this respect.

S3W-913 John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive what role it will have in the implementation of the Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment Regulations 2006.

S3W-914 John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive what position it plans to take on the banning of animal containment systems, such as freedom fences.

S3W-915 Ian McKee: To ask the Scottish Executive how many nursing hours were worked in NHS 24 between 8.00 am and 6.00 pm on weekdays in the most recent year for which figures are available.

S3W-916 Ian McKee: To ask the Scottish Executive what the total staff costs were of operating NHS 24 between 8.00 am and 6.00 pm on weekdays in the most recent year for which figures are available.

S3W-917 Ian McKee: To ask the Scottish Executive how many individual caller contacts were received by NHS 24 between 8.00 am and 6.00 pm on weekdays in the most recent year for which figures are available.

S3W-918 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to review the functions or structures of Scottish Natural Heritage and the Scottish Environment Protection Agency; whether any such review will include other bodies and agencies; what consultation it is planning with stakeholders, and when it will announce its intentions.

S3W-919 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive how many individual farmers or crofters are registered in Scotland to receive subsidies; what total amount was paid in single farm payments and other subsidies to these farmers and crofters in each of the last three years, and what proportion of these payments was subject to conditions other than good agricultural and environmental condition.

S3W-920 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive how many registered fishing boats there are in Scotland; what the estimated GDP contribution was by the fish catching sector in each of the last three years, and what proportion of total Scottish GDP this represents.

S3W-921 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list the commercial fish stocks in Scottish waters that are considered to be below safe biological limits and what steps it is taking to restore these stocks to favourable status.

S3W-922 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with Her Majesty’s Government concerning the provisions of the UK Marine Bill or any proposed Scottish Marine Bill and when it will announce its legislative intentions with regard to the protection of the marine environment.

S3W-923 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to reconvene the Sea Fisheries Advisory and Reference Group and when it will announce its intentions with regard to the Sea Fisheries Strategy.

S3W-924 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to reconvene the Advisory Group on Marine and Coastal Strategy and when it will announce its intentions with regard to the implementation of this group’s recommendations.

S3W-925 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive on how many occasions since his appointment the Cabinet Secretary for Rural Affairs and the Environment has (a) met or spoken to representatives of the Scottish Fishermen’s Federation and (b) met representatives of other marine industries or relevant stakeholders.

S3W-926 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive how many responses it received from (a) organisations and (b) individuals to its consultation on a coastal and marine national park; in each case, how many of these responses were (i) in favour of, (ii) against or (iii) expressed no opinion about such a park and, in the case of responses from organisations, what number of people were represented by (A) organisations in favour of and (B) organisations against a park.

S3W-927 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to appoint a new chair of the Scottish Environment Protection Agency.

S3W-928 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list the organisations that the Cabinet Secretary for Rural Affairs and the Environment has met to discuss fisheries matters since his appointment.

S3W-929 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure that, during any fisheries negotiations he attends and at other times, the Cabinet Secretary for Rural Affairs and the Environment is aware of environmental, scientific and community perspectives as well as those of the fishing industry.

S3W-930 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive when it plans to convene the next meetings of the Sea Fisheries Advisory and Reference Group and Scottish Inshore Fisheries Advisory Group; whether the membership of these groups has been reaffirmed and, if there are no plans to reconvene the group, what the reasons are for its position on the matter.

S3W-931 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it remains committed to the objectives of the existing Sea Fisheries Strategy.

S3W-932 John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure the full traceability of beef imports.

S3W-933 John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure that imported beef is free of hormones.

S3W-934 John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to reduce the risk of importing foot and mouth disease from South America.

S3W-935 John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive whether similar traceability and welfare standards are in place for beef and lamb imported from outside the EU as are required of Scottish, UK and EU farmers.

S3W-936 John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to raise the issues of traceability and method of production of imported beef with the European Union in the near future.

S3W-937 John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that importing beef from South America increases the risk of foot and mouth disease occurring in Scotland.

S3W-938 John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with (a) supermarkets and (b) the European Union about the equivalence of production standards for beef within and outwith the EU.

S3W-939 Bill Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether unqualified interpreters are employed at police stations or courts in Scotland and, if so, how many are employed and what proportion of the total number of interpreters employed these represent.

S3W-940 Derek Brownlee: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will determine the application by Airtricity for its proposed Clyde Wind Farm.
