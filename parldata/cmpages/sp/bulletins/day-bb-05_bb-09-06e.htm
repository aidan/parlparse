Section E – Written questions lodged on 5 September 2005

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-18900 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to review its anti-racism campaign and wider strategy, in light of the statement in Positive Action in Housing’s 10th Annual Report 2005 that the campaign has stalled.

S2W-18901 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what mechanisms it has to monitor whether racism is getting worse.

S2W-18902 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive how much funding it has allocated to community-led anti-racist initiatives in each year since 2002, broken down by initiative.

S2W-18903 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied that public procurement policies were adhered to during the tendering process for the Scottish Intelligence Database system in 2001.

S2W-18904 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Scottish Intelligence Database system was installed on time.

S2W-18905 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Scottish Intelligence Database system was operational on time.

S2W-18906 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Scottish Intelligence Database system was installed and operational within budget.

S2W-18907 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive which police force led the exercise, on behalf of the Association of Chief Police Officers in Scotland and the Scottish Police Service, of inviting tenders for the Scottish Intelligence Database system.

S2W-18908 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether ministers were involved in the decision to award the final contract for the Scottish Intelligence Database.

S2W-18909 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria were applied in the selection of a winning bid for the tender for the Scottish Intelligence Database.

S2W-18910 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what advantage a graduate applying for a visa under the Fresh Talent: Working in Scotland scheme will have over other similar graduates who do not apply through the scheme.

S2W-18911 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to expand the Fresh Talent initiative to allow graduates from previous years to apply.

S2W-18912 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive how many graduates it expects to apply for the Fresh Talent: Working in Scotland scheme in each of the next five years.

S2W-18913 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-14460 by Mr Tom McCabe on 14 March 2005, when the construction procurement manual will be published.

S2W-18914 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is confident that the six-month waiting time target for in-patient and day care treatment to be implemented from December 2005 will be achieved by orthopaedic departments.

S2W-18915 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive how many orthopaedic surgeons are employed and how many vacancies there are in each NHS board area.

S2W-18916 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it plans to improve the funding of Council for Voluntary Service social care providers.

S2W-18917 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will extend the two-tier workforce agreement to include Council for Voluntary Service employees who provide statutory and social care services.

S2W-18918 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive how many sexually-transmitted disease clinics there are in the NHS Lanarkshire area, giving (a) staffing levels, (b) consulting hours per week and (c) average waiting time for an appointment.

S2W-18919 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there have been cutbacks in the provision of sexually-transmitted disease clinics within the NHS Lanarkshire area since 1999.

S2W-18920 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive how many sexually-transmitted disease clinics there are in each NHS board area, giving (a) staffing levels, (b) consulting hours per week and (c) average waiting time for an appointment and (d) the ratio of consulting hours to head of population.

S2W-18921 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied with the rate of progress made by BT in respect of broadband provision in rural areas; what measures are in place to monitor BT’s progress towards installing cabling to connect rural households to broadband, and when it expects such cabling to be installed in Carrbridge.

S2W-18922 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what definition of community ownership it uses in respect of community-owned housing.

S2W-18923 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what role tenants have in the local application of community ownership.

S2W-18924 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to institute a statutory right of secondary transfer for former council house properties which have been transferred under whole stock transfer.

S2W-18925 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it is offering the Commissioner for Children and Young People in Scotland to ensure that the office remains impartial and free from political pressure.

S2W-18926 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it expects the Speed Enforcement Camera System on the A77 to reduce the need for police officers to patrol this stretch of road.

S2W-18927 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are classes or types of vehicles which cannot be captured by the Speed Enforcement Camera System on the A77.

S2W-18928 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what monitoring is in place to judge the effect of the Speed Enforcement Camera System on the A77.

S2W-18929 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are any plans to introduce the Speed Enforcement Camera System to more roads in Scotland.

S2W-18930 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body what measures are in place to protect parliamentary appointees from political pressure.

S2W-18931 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body what support it gives to parliamentary appointees to resist political pressure.

S2W-18932 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body what avenues are open to parliamentary appointees to refer to the Parliament for assistance.

S2W-18933 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body what avenues are open to parliamentary appointees to refer to the Parliament approaches made to them seeking to influence their decisions for political ends.

S2W-18934 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive which NHS boards have provided sleep services in each year since 1999 and what type of service each board has provided.

S2W-18935 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many patients have been on waiting lists for sleep services in each year since 1999, broken down by NHS board area.

S2W-18936 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people have been sent outwith their own NHS board area for sleep services in each year since 1999, broken down by board area.

S2W-18937 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive which treatment centres have provided sleep services to patients outwith their own NHS board area in each year since 1999 and how many such patients each centre has treated.

S2W-18938 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration it is giving to the reintroduction of grant funding to private developers for the provision of low-cost housing.

S2W-18939 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what pilot schemes have been completed where grant funding was given to private developers for the provision of low-cost housing.

S2W-18940 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether stocks of vaccine against avian influenza will be stocked near to areas where migratory birds arrive in Scotland.

S2W-18941 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what quantities of avian influenza vaccine will be held centrally in Scotland and what quantities will be dispersed around the country.

S2W-18942 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the quantity of avian influenza vaccine to be stockpiled in Scotland is sufficient to protect the whole population and, if not, when sufficient quantities will be stockpiled.

S2W-18943 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the lifespan is of avian influenza vaccine to be stockpiled in Scotland.

S2W-18944 Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is encouraging the use of solar panels in new-build properties.

S2W-18945 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to introduce a statutory duty on non-departmental public bodies to have an equalities policy.

S2W-18946 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive how many of its officials are currently working on the national language strategy.

S2W-18947 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what the provisional date is for publishing the national language strategy.

S2W-18948 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the third generation term maintenance contracts for management and maintenance of the Scottish trunk road network will include provision for the regular inspection and maintenance of those sections of cycle route that are located (a) adjacent to trunk roads and (b) within the trunk road boundary.

S2W-18949 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive why, according to the Scottish Enterprise Annual Report 2004-05, Scottish Enterprise spent £13.9 million less on skills and learning in 2004-05 than was allocated under this budget heading in the Executive’s Draft Budget 2004-05.

S2W-18950 Derek Brownlee: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list all Executive-owned properties detailing (a) their staff capacity, (b) their current capacity, (c) what functions are currently carried out in each building, (d) the approximate cash value of each property and (e) the running costs of each property.

S2W-18951 Derek Brownlee: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list all properties that the Executive rents detailing (a) their staff capacity, (b) their current capacity, (c) what functions are currently being carried out in each building and (d) the running costs of each property.

S2W-18958 Derek Brownlee: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide a breakdown of what the 2007-08 allocation of £12 million, shown under the "Reducing re-offending" category in table 1.13 of its Draft Budget 2006-07, will be used for.

S2W-18959 Derek Brownlee: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide a breakdown of what the 2007-08 allocation of £4,325,000, shown under the "Festivals" category in table 4.06 of its Draft Budget 2006-07, will be used for.

S2W-18961 Derek Brownlee: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide a breakdown of what the 2007-08 allocation of £100,975,000, shown under the "Departmental Unallocated Provision" category in table 5.03 of its Draft Budget 2006-07, will be used for.

S2W-18967 Derek Brownlee: To ask the Scottish Executive why the budget allocation for Level 3 spending category "International Relations", shown in table 10.07 of its Draft Budget 2006-07, has increased by the amount that it has in the three years from 2005-06.
