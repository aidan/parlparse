Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 10 February 2005

Question Time

Enterprise, Lifelong Learning and Transport

1. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to assist the manufacturing sector. (S2O-5404)

2. Cathie Craigie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take following the publication of, and formal consultation on, draft road orders for the A80. (S2O-5399)

3. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will take forward the recommendations of the Forum for Renewable Energy Development in Scotland’s Biomass Energy Group Report. (S2O-5356)

4. Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations have been made to the Office of Fair Trading regarding the newspaper and magazine supply chain. (S2O-5392)

5. Jim Mather: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it will take in light of the publication of Peter Wood’s issues paper for the Cross Cutting Expenditure Review on Economic Development, published on 1 February 2005. (S2O-5386)

6. Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it expects the wave power industry to contribute to the target of generating 40% of electricity from renewables by 2020. (S2O-5440)

7. Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to increase trade with developing world markets. (S2O-5405)

8. Janis Hughes: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to promote community transport projects across Scotland. (S2O-5408)

9. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what service and supply contracts it has with social enterprises. (S2O-5368)

10. John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will help Highland Council to complete the Inverness Southern Distributor Road by providing a canal and river crossing. (S2O-5448)

11. Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive how the renewable energy industry will benefit from changes to the rating system. (S2O-5334)

12. Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many Scottish businesses are trading on the internet. (S2O-5330)

Justice and Law Officers

1. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what confidence it has in the accuracy of the latest figures on youth offending produced by the Scottish Children’s Reporter Administration. (S2O-5434)

2. Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to improve communication with victims in the justice system. (S2O-5336)

3. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive what additional training will be provided to police officers and social workers to help them deal with sex offenders. (S2O-5421)

4. Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to improve delivery of the procurator fiscal service in Dumbarton. (S2O-5413)

5. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent discussions it has had with the UK Government regarding the implications for Scots law of Home Office legislative proposals. (S2O-5321)

6. Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what the current timescale is for establishing a Scottish Human Rights Commission. (S2O-5349)

7. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to improve delivery of the procurator fiscal service in Dunfermline. (S2O-5425)

8. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will act on the recommendations contained in Review of Marches and Parades in Scotland. (S2O-5430)

9. Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive what further action it will take to tackle crimes involving firearms. (S2O-5316)

10. Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive what value it places on rehabilitation programmes in prisons to tackle drug and alcohol abuse. (S2O-5370)

11. Colin Fox: To ask the Scottish Executive what lessons have been learned from recent high-profile miscarriage of justice cases and whether it is satisfied that no other injustices persist. (S2O-5331)

12. Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to crack down on drug dealing in communities. (S2O-5427)

General

1. Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will reform the way in which the Land Register operates. (S2O-5383)

2. Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to address concerns about the provision of NHS dentistry services. (S2O-5367)

3. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will take forward the implementation of Strategy and Action Plan for Improving Sexual Health. (S2O-5398)

4. David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it supports the Environment Agency’s plans to introduce rod licensing charges on the Scottish section of the River Esk. (S2O-5388)

5. Bristow Muldoon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many local authorities have approached it with a view to establishing pressurised area status in respect of the right to buy for council houses. (S2O-5344)

6. Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what funds it has made available for the development of youth and grassroots football. (S2O-5364)

7. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive how the members of the National Sexual Health Advisory Committee will be appointed and when the Committee will hold its first meeting. (S2O-5389)

8. Chris Ballance: To ask the Scottish Executive how many public sector jobs have been relocated in line with Executive policy and how many of these jobs have been relocated to Dumfries and Galloway. (S2O-5314)

9. Mr John Swinney: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has concerns in respect of the level of ambulance cover. (S2O-5340)

10. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will take any steps to assist local authorities and housing associations to acquire land for new affordable housing projects. (S2O-5327)

11. Mark Ballard: To ask the Scottish Executive what further progress is being made on its review of social work. (S2O-5441)

12. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact centralisation of cardiothoracic services at the Golden Jubilee National Hospital will have on NHS Greater Glasgow’s acute services review. (S2O-5360)

13. Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive what assistance is being given to arable farmers to encourage diversification and innovation. (S2O-5395)

14. Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with NHS Dumfries and Galloway regarding the provision of dental services in the area. (S2O-5376)

15. Mrs Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive what vocational courses are being offered to intelligent pupils who lack interest in traditional academic subjects. (S2O-5400)
