BUSINESS BULLETIN 156 / 2000

Thursday 19 October 2000

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 26 October 2000

 

 

Question Time

1. Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to introduce legislation to tighten controls over fireworks. (S1O-2381)

2. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to regulate property management factors in Scotland. (S1O-2403)

3. Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has for promotion and encouragement of a Scottish slate industry. (S1O-2387)

4. Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to respond to calls from people in Cumbernauld and Kilsyth for there to be a new local authority separate from North Lanarkshire Council. (S1O-2426)

5. Kay Ullrich: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to introduce a National Adoption Register. (S1O-2398)

6. Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it is drawing up to address the needs of homeless people over the Christmas period. (S1O-2422)

7. Mr Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will set a target date to achieve access to NHS dental treatment for all who need it. (S1O-2404)

8. Mr Keith Harding: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to encourage local authorities to address the issues outlined in its Statistical Bulletin Housing Series (October 2000). (S1O-2402)

9. Ms Margaret Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to assist women returning to work after career breaks. (S1O-2411)

10. Nick Johnston: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidelines are given to universities regarding the commercialisation of university research. (S1O-2384)

11. Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether its policies on health are consistent with those in the speech made by the Prime Minister to the Royal College of Surgeons on 17 October 2000. (S1O-2425)

12. Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive on what date this year it expects to make arable aid payments to farmers. (S1O-2406)

13. Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made by the review group set up to consider the impact of Part V of the Police Act 1997 in relation to payment for police vetting checks on adult volunteers. (S1O-2393)

14. Elaine Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to support children and young people in making the transition from primary to secondary education. (S1O-2410)

15. Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to ensure that rural communities benefit from the Scottish Rural Partnership Fund. (S1O-2412)

16. Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects the working group on deferred entry to pre-school education to submit its report to Ministers. (S1O-2390)

17. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether Health Trusts are applying a sufficient proportion of their budget to the reduction of waiting times. (S1O-2405)

18. Mr John McAllion: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has held recently with Health Boards and NHS Trusts relating to the Tayside Acute Services Review. (S1O-2392)

19. Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met representatives of Scottish rugby and what issues were discussed. (S1O-2401)

20. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what the current position is with regard to the cashmere industry in Scotland. (S1O-2419)

21. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are in place to monitor and prevent clinical accidents in the NHS. (S1O-2383)

22. Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what stage it has reached in its consideration of the restructuring of police forces. (S1O-2418)

23. Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to address the financial position of Disability Scotland. (S1O-2382)

24. Miss Annabel Goldie: To ask the Scottish Executive how long the Scottish Qualifications Authority must retain examination papers before they can be destroyed. (S1O-2386)

25. Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive what powers local authorities have in relation to privately rented houses which are found to be unsafe for habitation but are not covered by the legislation affecting houses in multiple occupation. (S1O-2394)

26. Ms Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has for a public debate on the use of cannabis. (S1O-2408)

27. Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made towards creating a community legal service in Scotland. (S1O-2413)

28. Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what financial provision it makes for essential lifeline bridge projects and what funding is available when structures reach the end of their lifespan. (S1O-2420)

29. David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in reviewing the procedures for approving signposting of tourist attractions from motorways and trunk roads. (S1O-2409)

30. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment has been made of the impact the extension of the M74 will have on traffic usage on the Kingston Bridge. (S1O-2423)
