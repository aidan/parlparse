BUSINESS BULLETIN 74 / 2000

Thursday 4 May 2000

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 11 May 2000

 

 

1. Mr Keith Raffan: To ask the Scottish Executive what its current policy is in regard to civil service job dispersal within Scotland. (S1O-1657)

 

2. Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will ask the appropriate joint ministerial committee to initiate a review of the New Deal. (S1O-1679)

 

3. Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive what estimate it has made of the proposed Glasgow Housing Association’s VAT liability. (S1O-1694)

 

4. Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with Glasgow City Council regarding demolition of public housing within the city of Glasgow. (S1O-1684)

 

5. Mrs Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive what role the Scottish Arts Council will play in the development of the creative industries. (S1O-1663)

 

6. Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it plans to take as a result of the recent survey into begging and homelessness carried out by Glasgow University on behalf of the Joseph Rowntree Foundation. (S1O-1665)

 

7. Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive whether funding for the new mart facility on Tiree will be made available. (S1O-1688)

 

8. Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to address the financial situation of industrial museums in Scotland, particularly the Scottish Mining Museum at Newtongrange, Midlothian. (S1O-1646)

 

9. Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1O-1544 by Sarah Boyack on 6 April 2000, whether the necessary funding will be made available for resurfacing the main runway at Wick Airport. (S1O-1659)

 

10. David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has had any discussions with Her Majesty’s Government about the application in Scotland of the funds raised during the current mobile phone license auction. R (S1O-1647)

 

11. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has for walk-in, walk-out hospitals and one stop clinics in Scotland. (S1O-1686)

 

12. Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has made or proposes to make regarding the electrification of the East Coast main line. (S1O-1664)

 

13. Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make a statement about productivity levels in Scotland’s manufacturing industry and any work it is undertaking in conjunction with industry to improve productivity in Scotland. (S1O-1691)

 

14. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with Her Majesty’s Government or other bodies on the use of private land for defence training purposes and what consideration has been given to this in the draft Land Reform (Scotland) Bill. (S1O-1655)

 

15. Elaine Thomson: To ask the Scottish Executive, in the light of the findings of the recent study Road Accidents and Children Living in Disadvantaged Areas, what steps are being taken to address the link between child pedestrian accident rates and social exclusion. (S1O-1683)

 

16. Mr John McAllion: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent initiatives it has taken to encourage the dispersal of civil service jobs outwith the central belt axis of Edinburgh and Glasgow. (S1O-1658)

 

17. Janis Hughes: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to ensure that the unique nature of paediatric health services is taken into account when it considers the future of child health services in Glasgow and in Scotland as a whole. (S1O-1689)

 

18. Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to reconsider the planning guidelines for opencast mining operations. (S1O-1667)

 

19. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to introduce a water service charges benefit scheme aligned to the existing Council Tax Benefit Scheme. (S1O-1660)

 

20. Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to further encourage farmers to diversify into timber production. (S1O-1662)

 

21. Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has discussed with the Scottish Tourist Board or local tourist boards the need to support and retain manned tourist information centres. (S1O-1649)

 

22. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has made to Her Majesty's Government in support of relocating offshore-related civil service jobs to Scotland. (S1O-1652)

 

23. Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive how many houses in multiple occupation will be licensed by the end of October this year. (S1O-1671)

 

24. Mr Duncan Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive what the future role of Caledonian MacBrayne will be in relation to the Gourock-Dunoon ferry route. (S1O-1678)

 

25. Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to ensure the continued survival and health of the colony of bottle nosed dolphins in the Moray Firth. (S1O-1673)

 

26. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to increase awareness of mental health difficulties amongst looked after children. (S1O-1693)

 

27. George Lyon: To ask the Scottish Executive what national guidelines are in place for local authorities in determining the criteria for rural schools closures. (S1O-1670)

 

28. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will give an update on the progress of the evaluation of the Glasgow Airport rail link. (S1O-1648)

 

29. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what consultations it has held with youth organisations about the possible introduction of a voluntary identity card scheme for young people. (S1O-1675)

 

30. Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are available to provide protection to witnesses in cases of anti-social behaviour. (S1O-1641)
