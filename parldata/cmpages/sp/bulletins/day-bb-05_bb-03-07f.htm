Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

*S2M-2529 Stewart Stevenson: Aberdeenshire Sports and Recreation Facilities—That the Parliament deplores the inappropriate sense of priorities displayed by Aberdeenshire Liberal Democrat councillors in choosing to donate a six-figure sum to the development of swimming facilities for a city university outwith its own area while their own Mintlaw Academy has none and while Peterhead pool is in urgent need of new investment.

*S2M-2528 Shiona Baird: Aberdeen Western Peripheral Route and the Scottish Parliament’s Climate Change Inquiry—That the Parliament notes with concern that the Scottish Executive has today launched a public consultation into the route options for the Aberdeen Western Peripheral Route (WPR); recognises that the WPR and other major road projects will increase traffic and increase greenhouse gas emissions that will contribute to climate change; condemns Scottish ministers for going ahead with major new road projects, including the WPR, before the Environment and Rural Development Committee has made its report to the Parliament on its inquiry into climate change; recognises that the WPR has been estimated to cost between £200 and £400 million whilst only reducing congestion by 2%, whereas government-commissioned research has demonstrated that improving public transport and other measures could achieve congestion reductions of 5 to 21%, giving returns of £10 in reduced congestion for every £1 spent; calls on the Executive to seriously consider alternatives to the WPR, and further calls on the Executive to recognise the seriousness of proceeding with projects that will contribute to climate change and to respect the Parliament by halting any decision on the WPR, at least until after the Committee has reported on its climate change inquiry.

*S2M-2527 Mr Brian Monteith: Criminals Should Not Be Rewarded For Crime—That the Parliament condemns the actions of the BBC in paying convicted burglar, Brendan Fearon, £4,000 to appear in a BBC documentary while the victim of the burglary received no fee; believes that this is a wholly inappropriate use of the TV licence fee and that the BBC’s actions will disgust the majority of TV licence fee payers who have no control over how the licence fee money is spent, and calls for legislation to ensure that convicted criminals cannot make money from their crimes.
