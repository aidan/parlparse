Business Bulletin No.170/2006: Friday 24 November 2006
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 23 November 2006

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-30102 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive when the women’s unit at Craiginches prison, Aberdeen, closed.

S2W-30103 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive when the refurbishment of the women’s unit at Craiginches prison, Aberdeen, was completed.

S2W-30104 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive how much the refurbishment of the women’s unit at Craiginches prison, Aberdeen, cost.

S2W-30105 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive when the women’s unit at Craiginches prison, Aberdeen, will reopen.

S2W-30106 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what additional costs were incurred as result of having women remand prisoners transported to courts during the closure of the women’s unit at Craiginches prison, Aberdeen.

S2W-30107 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has for provision for women prisoners in any new prison in the north east.

S2W-30108 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Scottish Red Squirrel Action Plan 2006-11 will be directed by one nominated individual with responsibility for its implementation.

S2W-30109 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will undertake a mapping programme for grey squirrels to identify the exact whereabouts and extent of the spread of grey squirrels.

S2W-30110 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are, or will be, funds available to carry out research into the immuno-contraception of grey squirrels.

S2W-30111 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the European Union in relation to the spread of grey squirrels and their impact on biodiversity.

S2W-30112 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with squirrel organisations and local authorities in the north of England regarding the spread and control of grey squirrels and what action it has taken in collaboration with these organisations.

S2W-30113 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to stop the illegal release of grey squirrels into the wild.

S2W-30114 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many cautions were issued in each police force area for (a) violence against the person, (b) theft and handling stolen goods, (c) sexual offences, (d) criminal damage, (e) robbery and (f) fraud and forgery in each year since 1990.

S2W-30115 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has made representations to Her Majesty’s Government about increasing the level of duty on high-alcohol-by-volume ciders in line with that on beer and, if so, what these representations were and what response was received.

S2W-30116 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has made representations to Her Majesty’s Government about duty levels on fortified wines and, if so, what these representations were and what response was received.

S2W-30117 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many official overseas trips were made by senior staff of the Scottish Prison Service in each year since 1999, broken down by (a) member of staff, (b) destination, (c) date travelled and (d) cost.

S2W-30118 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how much it has paid to Dundas and Wilson in respect of legal and other advice received from it or agencies working on its behalf in each year since 1999.

S2W-30119 Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to include prescribing rates of new treatments for psoriatic arthritis in its programme of public annual reviews of local NHS boards.

S2W-30120 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive when an announcement will be made on funding for a regional sports centre for north-east Scotland.

S2W-30121 Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive how many trained signers are available to provide sign language assistance to students in schools and higher education institutions.

S2W-30122 Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people are undergoing sign language training to qualify to operate as signers in classrooms and where they receive such training.

S2W-30123 Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding it provides for training sign language assistants to operate in classrooms in school and higher education institutions.

S2W-30124Δ Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will make a decision about the roll out of youth courts.

S2W-30125 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-20130 by Mr Andy Kerr on 7 November 2005, whether the business case has been considered by the minister; if not, when he will consider it, and what the up-to-date position is on the matter.

S2W-30126 Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people have been charged with possession of crystal methamphetamine since 2001.

S2W-30127 Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people have been convicted of possession of crystal methamphetamine since 2001.

S2W-30128 Derek Brownlee: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-29259 by Mr Tom McCabe on 7 November 2006, whether it will provide a breakdown of the payments made under the category "other" to Holyrood Communications in 2004-05 and 2005-06.

S2W-30129 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many families were in temporary accommodation in each year since 1990.

S2W-30130 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many homes were sold to first-time buyers in each of the last 10 years and what percentage of all homes were sold in this way.

S2W-30131 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many value-for-money exercises on the use of (a) management consultants and (b) professional advisers it conducted in each of the last five years.

S2W-30132 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive which external consultants were used by each of its (a) departments and (b) agencies on projects relating to (i) private finance initiatives and (ii) public private partnerships in each year since 1999 and what the (1) nature and (2) cost of the consultants’ work was in each case.

S2W-30133 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive which external consultants were used by each of its (a) departments and (b) agencies on projects excluding private finance initiatives and public private partnerships in each year since 1999 and what the (i) nature and (ii) cost of the consultants’ work was in each case.

S2W-30134 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) adults and (b) children were registered as NHS patients with dental practices in each year since 1996, broken down by (i) NHS board and (ii) parliamentary constituency.

S2W-30135 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how much it has spent on external media trainers in each year since 1999, broken down by supplier.

S2W-30136 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how much it has spent on organising and hosting press conferences in each year since 1999.

S2W-30137 Ms Maureen Watt: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) supermarkets, (b) fast food outlets, (c) independently owned shops and (d) independently owned cafes have been located in each local authority area in each year since 1999, showing year-on-year and overall percentage changes.

S2W-30138 Miss Annabel Goldie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many areas in Scotland are complying timeously with the Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network’s national clinical guideline on the management of harmful drinking and alcohol dependence in primary care, with specific regard to section 4 on detoxification.

S2W-30139 Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to raise the profile and availability of sign language and recruitment of signers to enable the deaf community to participate fully in the educational system.

S2W-30140 Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure staff safety in the Scottish Prison Service.

S2W-30141 Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prison officers have been employed in the Scottish Prison Service in (a) Scotland and (b) Saughton prison in Edinburgh in each year from 1999.

S2W-30142 Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prison officers are projected to be employed in the Scottish Prison Service in (a) Scotland and (b) Saughton prison in Edinburgh in each year from 2007 to 2011.

S2W-30143 Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what the projected average daily prison population is for (a) Scotland and (b) Saughton prison in Edinburgh in each year from 2007 to 2011.

S2W-30144 Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to retain staff and boost morale in the Scottish Prison Service.

S2W-30145 Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive what consultations were undertaken before the responsibility for contacting patients receiving flu vaccinations was transferred from NHS boards to Practitioner Services.

S2W-30146 Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many patients have received from Practitioner Services multiple or inaccurate copies of letters of notification to attend to receive flu vaccinations.

S2W-30147 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what the level of child mortality has been in each year since 1999, also broken down by local authority area.

S2W-30148 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of the population have been in receipt of income support in each year since 1999, also broken down by local authority area.

S2W-30149 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many lone parent households there have been in each year since 1999, also broken down by local authority area.

S2W-30150 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average household incomes have been in each year since 1999, also broken down by parliamentary constituency.

S2W-30151 Mr Mark Ruskell: To ask the Scottish Executive how the Moray Firth Seal Management Plan is being monitored.

S2W-30152 Mr Mark Ruskell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any evidence that implementation of the Moray Firth Seal Management Plan has resulted in an increase in salmon populations.

S2W-30153 Mr Mark Ruskell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many seals have been killed under the Moray Firth Seal Management Plan since its inception.

S2W-30154 Mr Mark Ruskell: To ask the Scottish Executive how the quotas for (a) common and (b) grey seals to be killed under the Moray Firth Seal Management Plan were calculated.

S2W-30155 Mr Mark Ruskell: To ask the Scottish Executive which non-lethal seal management options have been trialled as part of the Moray Firth Seal Management Plan.
