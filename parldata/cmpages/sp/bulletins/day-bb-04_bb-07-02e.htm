Business Bulletin No. 115 / 2004

Friday 2nd July 2004

Section E : Written questions lodged on Thursday 1st July 2004
  

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-9514 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied with the arrangements whereby the Culture Commission can be contacted and when its contact details will be published.

S2W-9515 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Culture Commission has been asked by the Minister for Tourism, Culture and Sport to take evidence from individuals and organisations and, if so, in what way this will be done.

S2W-9516Δ Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive when a consultation paper on reform of the laws of personal bankruptcy and diligence will be published.

S2W-9517Δ Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive when the outcome of the review of Part 5 of the Adults with Incapacity (Scotland) Act 2000 will be announced.

S2W-9518 Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with Network Rail regarding the future maintenance of the Forth Rail Bridge.

S2W-9519 Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to replace the Forth Rail Bridge.

S2W-9520 Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive what the annual maintenance costs of the Forth Rail Bridge have been in each year since 1998-99 and what the projected maintenance costs are for (a) 2004-05, (b) 2005-06 and (c) 2006-07.

S2W-9521 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-8628 by Mr Tom McCabe on 25 June 2004, what advice it has received on the legality of two people paying different amounts for the same service.

S2W-9522 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria will be applied in any decision on relocating public sector jobs to Moray and the Highlands and Islands.

S2W-9523 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of companies from which it procures goods provide independently verified reports on their social and environmental performance.

S2W-9524 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body what percentage of companies from which it procures goods provide independently verified reports on their social and environmental performance.

S2W-9525 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to combat counterfeit medicines and to ensure patient safety.

S2W-9526 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to compensate businesses that might be adversely affected by any ban on smoking in public places which results from its current public consultation on the issue.

S2W-9527 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive how many civil servants it expects will retire in each of the next five years.

S2W-9528 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive what costs for civil service redundancies it has made budgetary provision for in each of the next three years, in light of the ministerial statement on effective government on 24 June 2004.

S2W-9529 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average age is of civil servants.

S2W-9530 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive what directives that are (a) binding and (b) advisory have been issued to (i) hospitals, (ii) NHS boards, (iii) schools, (iv) housing associations, (v) police forces and (vi) fire brigades in each of the last five years.

S2W-9531 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to include home zones in new developments and establish them in existing communities.

S2W-9532 Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive how much funding it has allocated for consultation and research as part of its campaign to promote Scotland's international image and how this funding will be broken down.

S2W-9533 Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-8835 by Mr Andy Kerr on 23 June 2004, in which of its departments, agencies and associated departments the 121 full-time posts in Fife are located.

S2W-9534 Carolyn Leckie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many staffed beds there are in the NHS.

S2W-9535 Carolyn Leckie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many staffed beds there in each category of care in the NHS, broken down by NHS board area.

S2W-9536 John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has had any discussions with the Ministry of Defence regarding the impact of possible storage of submarines at (a) Aultbea, (b) Dounreay, (c) Nigg and (d) Ardoyne Point.

S2W-9537 John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on other sites, not referred to in Project ISOLUS - Consultation on Outline Proposals (CIOP): Final Report, that the Ministry of Defence is considering using for the storage of nuclear waste.

S2W-9538 John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive whether, if Her Majesty's Government (HMG) has made any proposals to store waste from nuclear submarine reactors at any site in Scotland, it will make representations to HMG regarding the social and economic impact of such proposals.
