Business Bulletin No. 11/2002

Monday 21 January 2002

Section E : Written questions lodged on 18 January 2002
  

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S1W-22028 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to grant franchises for the management of poorly performing hospitals.

S1W-22029 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to introduce Foundation Hospitals with autonomous decision-making and management similar to those announced by the Secretary of State for Health.

S1W-22030 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to introduce and publish a star rating system for NHS hospitals.

S1W-22031 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people are (a) known to be suffering from Hepatitis C and officially recorded as such and (b) estimated to be suffering from Hepatitis C but not officially recorded as such.

S1W-22032 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what the most up-to-date figures are for recorded incidence of dental fluorosis in (a) fluoridated and (b) non-fluoridated areas.

S1W-22033 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive what programmes it has initiated that promote the inclusion of people with both learning difficulties and physical disabilities in the community.

S1W-22034 Mr Keith Harding: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to encourage local authorities to reduce local authority housing rent arrears.

S1W-22035 Mr Keith Harding: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to encourage local authorities to complete the sale of local authority houses within the national target time of 26 weeks.

S1W-22036 Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider making respiratory illness a national health priority.

S1W-22037 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to its press release SE1935/2000 on 3 July 2000, what improvements to people's health the Public Health Institute for Scotland has achieved since January 2001.

S1W-22038 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what targets, and timescales for achieving these targets, have been set for the Public Health Institute for Scotland.

S1W-22039 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Diabetes Framework will be published and what the reason is for any delay in its publication.

S1W-22040 Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance it has given to its staff on the operation of the concordats between it and departments of Her Majesty's Government and on the operation of the Memorandum of Understanding between Her Majesty's Government, Scottish ministers and the Cabinet of the National Assembly for Wales.

S1W-22041 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many officials within its Environment and Rural Affairs Department had responsibility in relation to the bee-keeping profession in the years (a) 2001, (b) 1995 and (c) 1990.

S1W-22042 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is its policy to release consultation papers during the winter holiday season.

S1W-22043 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether its consultation paper of 1 November 2001 on the draft Guidance on the Circumstances in which Parents may Choose to Educate their Children at Home was released at the end of December 2001 and what the reasons are for the position on this matter.

S1W-22044 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether its consultation paper of 1 November 2001 on the draft Guidance on the Circumstances in which Parents may Choose to Educate their Children at Home was available or referred to on its website at the time of publication; what the reasons are for the position on this matter, and why the paper does not state to whom or by when responses to the paper should be made.

S1W-22045 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what research has been carried out into the needs of older women in relation to domestic abuse and other forms of violence.

S1W-22046 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans there are to establish sexual assault treatment centres.

S1W-22047 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans there are to relax the rules on the coaching of witnesses prior to court proceedings.

S1W-22048 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many drug-assisted sexual assaults took place in the most recent year for which figures are available, broken down by the gender of the assaulted persons.

S1W-22049 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what support is available for male victims of rape.

S1W-22050 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to highlight drug-assisted assaults and the measures that can be taken to prevent them.

S1W-22051 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what methods are available to the police to screen for drugs.

S1W-22052 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to improve access for pensioners to information about older people's groups.

S1W-22053 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider setting up a database listing all older people's groups which could be accessed by pensioners.

S1W-22054 Ben Wallace: To ask the Scottish Executive how many Healthy Living Centres have been established since January 2001.

S1W-22055 Ben Wallace: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people over 65 were screened for breast cancer in 2001 compared to 2000, expressed also as a percentage of the total number of people screened for breast cancer in each year.

S1W-22056 Ben Wallace: To ask the Scottish Executive what the definition is of "inappropriate settings" as used in section 2.6 of Working together for Scotland: A Programme for Government.

S1W-22057 Ben Wallace: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it has taken to improve health education for older people since January 2001.

S1W-22058 Ben Wallace: To ask the Scottish Executive how many reforming drug misusers were prepared for training and employment in 2001.

S1W-22059 Ben Wallace: To ask the Scottish Executive how support for families of drug misusers has increased since January 2001.

S1W-22060 Ben Wallace: To ask the Scottish Executive how it has improved local involvement in the development of health services since January 2001.

S1W-22061 Ben Wallace: To ask the Scottish Executive how many staff posts have been or will be lost as a result of the establishment of the new NHS boards.

S1W-22062 Ben Wallace: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people received treatment for the first time from their local drug treatment service in 2001.

S1W-22063 Ben Wallace: To ask the Scottish Executive how many toothbrushes have been provided free to children since January 2001.

S1W-22064 Fiona McLeod: To ask the Scottish Executive what directions it has issued to the Scottish Environment Protection Agency on prioritising data gathering for the Priority Waste Stream Projects.

S1W-22065 Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive (a) what surveys other than the Central Scotland Transport Corridor Study (CSTCS) have been undertaken regarding road safety on the A80 at Cumbernauld, (b) how many accidents were reported on this stretch of trunk road in each of the last five years and (c) when it will publish the CSTCS report into the future development and upgrading of this route.

S1W-22066 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many referrals were made alleging that a child aged between eight and 12 had committed a criminal offence in Edinburgh to (a) the Reporter to the Children's Panel and (b) the Procurator Fiscal; how many of these referrals were subsequently prosecuted (i) at court and (ii) at a Children's Panel Hearing, and what sentence was imposed in each case, all in each year since 1997.

S1W-22067 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of the meeting between the Minister for Enterprise, Transport and Lifelong Learning and the Secretary of State for Transport, whether the Strategic Rail Authority's strategic plan will be amended to confirm that airport links to Edinburgh Airport and Glasgow Airport will go ahead within five years and what the reasons are for the position on this matter.

S1W-22068 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it made to the Strategic Rail Authority to seek its agreement for the inclusion of particular rail projects in the authority's strategic plan; what timescales it sought for the completion of any such rail project, and whether any such projects were included within the published strategic plan.

S1W-22069 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive which police force divisions have specialist sexual offence officers and how many such officers each division has.

S1W-22070 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive in which police force areas women doctors are available at all times to conduct medical examinations following a sexual assault.

S1W-22071 Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-19180 by Iain Gray on 15 November 2001, what further research has been carried out into "attrition rate" as recommended by Dr Lynn Jamieson and Dr Michelle Burman in the Central Research Unit report The 'Attrition' of Sexual Offences in the Criminal Justice System.

S1W-22072 Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it holds on the numbers of patients removed from GP lists in each year and what reasons other than a patient's death are given in such cases.

S1W-22073 Cathie Craigie: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects the Central Scotland Transport Corridor Studies to be completed.

S1W-22074 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to allocate an animal welfare portfolio to a specific minister.

S1W-22075 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to conduct a review of animal welfare legislation similar to that announced by the Department for the Environment, Food and Rural Affairs on 2 January 2002.

S1W-22076 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive how it can ensure that local authorities enforce the Weeds Act 1959 with regard to ragwort.

S1W-22077 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive which individuals and organisations have been sent copies of Forth Estuary Transportation Authority Report on Public Consultation.
