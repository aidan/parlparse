Business Bulletin No. 100 / 2003

Monday 7th July 2003

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-222# John Swinburne: Scottish Lottery—That the Parliament notes the recent news that the National Lottery is facing financial difficulty under its current operation following a steady drop in sales; further notes with concern the resulting decrease in money for good causes; recognises some of the reasons for the fall of interest in the National Lottery; welcomes Her Majesty’s Government’s pledge to "give the lottery back to the people", and therefore considers that a not-for-profit Scottish lottery should be set up with the money spent by lottery customers in Scotland exclusively used for the benefit of the people and communities of Scotland.

*S2M-221 Jeremy Purvis: Flooding in Selkirk and Yarrow Valley—That the Parliament notes the major damage done to properties in Selkirk and the Yarrow valley caused by major floods that occurred on 30 May 2003; condemns the slow response of many private insurers to support clients in the immediate aftermath; recognises that there is still considerable damage to private homes, rented accommodation and public property that needs to be repaired and that over 70 households need to be decanted; is concerned that there is an acute shortage of alternative accommodation, and calls on the Scottish Executive to work with all the agencies, including housing associations, to act urgently in ensuring that people suffer as little distress and disruption as possible.

*S2M-220 Jackie Baillie: Children's Hospice at Balloch—That the Parliament welcomes the commencement of construction work on Scotland's second children's hospice at Balloch; recognises the considerable work of the Children's Hospice Association Scotland (CHAS) in providing a caring and supportive environment for terminally-ill children and their families; applauds the tremendous effort of volunteers in Dunbartonshire and throughout Scotland who have worked so hard to raise funds; notes that £7 million of the £10 million required to build, equip and maintain the new hospice at Balloch has been raised; further notes that an additional £3 million is needed to ensure that CHAS has sufficient reserves to underpin the cost of running Robin House, and recognises that, with continued voluntary effort and the generosity shown by many people, this target will be achieved by the completion date for the building of Robin House Children's Hospice in August 2004.

*S2M-219 Rosie Kane: Citizen Y Youth Project—That the Parliament congratulates the young people involved in the Edinburgh youth project, Citizen Y, on their mural at St John’s Church called "Who are you calling a ned?"; welcomes such positive images of young people in the current climate of scapegoating and stigma and acknowledges that building productive relationships with young people depends upon giving them the respect and opportunities that they appear to lack from their elected representatives at the moment.

*S2M-218 Richard Lochhead: Save Scotland's Ink Recycling Industry—That the Parliament notes with concern the potential impact that the implementation of EU Directive 2002/96/EC on waste electrical and electronic equipment could have on the business of ink cartridge refill shops and the prices of printer ink and toner for the Scottish consumer; endorses the petition under way at cartridge refill shops across Scotland, and calls on the Scottish Executive to make representations to the United Kingdom's Department for Trade and Industry to ensure that the transposition of the directive into domestic Scots law does not penalise Scottish businesses or consumers in favour of multinational manufacturers of ink cartridges.

*S2M-217 Richard Lochhead: Freedom to Express Scottish Identity—That the Parliament notes with dismay that the University of Aberdeen Careers Advisory Service advises students that, when applying for jobs, they should define themselves as "British" rather than "Scottish" in order to enhance their employment prospects; believes that this advice is misplaced and symptomatic of the Scottish cringe which has held Scotland back for decades and undermines the confidence of young Scots; calls on any advice providers to recognise that increasing numbers of Scots view themselves as Scottish first and foremost and welcomes this increasing self-confidence, and believes that, in the unlikely event that any employer were to downgrade applicants because they took pride in their Scottish identity, such an action would be utterly foolish and counterproductive.

Supported by: Michael Matheson*, Ms Sandra White*, Campbell Martin*, Mr Adam Ingram*, Brian Adam*, Christine Grahame*, Jim Mather*, Mr Stewart Maxwell*

*S2M-216 Margo MacDonald: Use of Cannabis for the Relief of Pain—That the Parliament salutes the courage, integrity and tenacity of Biz Ivol in her campaign to change the laws on cannabis in order to enable MS sufferers such as herself to legally use the substance for the relief of pain; regrets that the Crown Office and Procurator Fiscal Service judged her prosecution to be in the public interest when common sense and humanity dictate otherwise, and calls on the Scottish Executive to instigate an immediate public consultation on changing the provisions of the criminal law in Scotland in respect of the use of cannabis for the relief of pain.

Supported by: Rosie Kane*, Eleanor Scott*, Tommy Sheridan*, Robin Harper*, Nicola Sturgeon*, Christine Grahame*, Kate Maclean*

*S2M-215 Malcolm Chisholm: National Health Service Reform (Scotland) Bill—That the Parliament agrees to the general principles of the National Health Service Reform (Scotland) Bill.

Supported by: Mr Tom McCabe*

*S2M-214 Rhona Brankin: National Endometriosis Awareness Day—That the Parliament welcomes National Endometriosis Awareness Day on 2 July 2003; notes that endometriosis is a gynaecological disease that affects up to two million women in the United Kingdom causing extreme pain, infertility, depression and incapacity for work or social interaction, and recognises the need to raise awareness of this condition.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan*, Rosie Kane*

*S2M-213 Mr Andy Kerr: Primary Medical Services (Scotland) Bill - Financial Resolution—That the Parliament for the purposes of any Act of the Scottish Parliament resulting from the Primary Medical Services (Scotland) Bill, agrees to any increase in expenditure of the Scottish Ministers payable out of the Scottish Consolidated Fund in consequence of the Act.

*S2M-212 Donald Gorrie: West of Scotland Racial Equality Council—That the Parliament congratulates the West of Scotland Race Equality Council on the successful launch of its anti-racism pack for school managers working with children and young people and recognises the positive contribution the council has made to addressing race issues in Scotland, its willingness to work in partnership with other agencies in tackling the problems of racism in Scotland and the valuable contribution made to the development of the council by its Director, Maggie Chetty, who is retiring after 18 years’ commitment to the organisation.

Supported by: Ms Sandra White*, Tommy Sheridan*, Rosie Kane*

*S2M-211 Maureen Macmillan: Parliament's In-house Mail Services—That the Parliament welcomes the recent announcement that its in-house mail service will be transferred to the responsibility of the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body and congratulates all members of the Royal Mail staff for the professional service provided to MSPs and staff since 1999.

Supported by: Colin Fox*, Robin Harper*, Jackie Baillie*, Janis Hughes*, Irene Oldfather*, Margaret Jamieson*, Helen Eadie*, Ms Sandra White*, Tommy Sheridan*, Rosie Kane*

*S2M-210 Mr Kenneth Macintosh: Respect for Shopworkers Day, 17 September 2003—That the Parliament notes with concern the level of violence and abuse that shopworkers deal with on a daily basis; further notes the recent survey by the Union of Shop, Distributive and Allied workers (USDAW) that found that 47% of those responding to the survey reported physical attacks or assaults on staff in the last 12 months, that 72% reported staff being threatened with violence, that in one in four stores threats are being made every week, that in one-third of stores verbal abuse is a daily event, that almost 50% of staff have taken time off work as a result of violence and that stress-related problems are common and include sickness, insomnia, headaches and clinical depression; commends the USDAW campaign, "Freedom from Fear"’ and recognises its main objectives as raising awareness of the level of violence and abuse, making the workplace safer and protecting both shoppers and staff, and supports "Respect for Shopworkers Day" on 17 September 2003 aimed at preventing abusive behaviour, highlighting the support that retail companies can, and do, give their staff and encouraging the public to show respect for shopworkers.

Supported by: Karen Whitefield*, Marlyn Glen*, Dr Elaine Murray*, Pauline McNeill*, Karen Gillon*, Scott Barrie*, Paul Martin*, Elaine Smith*, Maureen Macmillan*, Susan Deacon*, Michael McMahon*, Sarah Boyack*, Mr Alasdair Morrison*, Kate Maclean*, Cathie Craigie*, Jackie Baillie*, Marilyn Livingstone*, Dr Sylvia Jackson*, Helen Eadie*, Des McNulty*, Christine May*, Bill Butler*, Mr Richard Baker*, Bristow Muldoon*, Cathy Peattie*, Mr John Home Robertson*, Margaret Jamieson*, Rhona Brankin*, Ms Wendy Alexander*, Janis Hughes*, Trish Godman*, Johann Lamont*, Tommy Sheridan*, Rosie Kane*

*S2M-209 Eleanor Scott: Marine National Parks—That the Parliament is inspired by the vision of the Wester Ross Marine Reserve Partnership, the Hebridean Marine National Park Partnership and the Fair Isle Marine Environment and Tourism Initiative; agrees with these groups that it is vital to achieve an integrated coastal zone management around Scotland with local communities playing a lead role, and recommends the commencement of formal consultations with these local communities on the establishment of marine national parks.
