Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-2620# Janis Hughes: Special Olympics Great Britain National Summer Games 2005—That the Parliament notes that the Special Olympics Great Britain National Summer Games 2005 are to be held in Glasgow from 2 to 9 July 2005; welcomes to Scotland the 2,500 athletes with learning disabilities who will participate in 23 sports at 11 venues throughout the city; notes that this will be the biggest sporting event to take place in Great Britain in 2005, and hopes that the games will demonstrate Glasgow’s ability to host such events in support of the city’s potential bid to host the 2014 Commonwealth Games.

*S2M-2619 Margo MacDonald: Meeting the Needs and Aspirations of the People of Scotland—That the Parliament agrees that its members’ primary function is to reflect and address the needs and aspirations of the people of Scotland.

*S2M-2618 Ms Sandra White: Pension Rebate Gender Discrimination—That the Parliament notes the one-off council tax rebate promised to pensioners by the Chancellor of the Exchequer; further notes that this will not apply to roughly 40,000 female pensioners in Scotland; concurs with the view expressed by Help The Aged Scotland that "This one-off payment is a grotty little bribe, but even worse it is also sexist and ageist", and believes that, if a rebate is to be given to pensioners, it should be done in a more equal fashion and should not discriminate against one group in this way.

*S2M-2617 Mr Tom McCabe: Housing (Scotland) Bill: Financial Resolution—That the Parliament, for the purposes of any Act of the Scottish Parliament resulting from the Housing (Scotland) Bill, agrees to any expenditure of a kind referred to in paragraph 3(b)(iii) of Rule 9.12 of the Parliament’s Standing Orders arising in consequence of the Act.

*S2M-2616 Ms Sandra White: Bridging the Gap—That the Parliament notes the research report by the Scottish Consumer Council, Bridging the gap: Improving access to primary healthcare services for disabled people, which examined how primary healthcare providers have responded to the needs of disabled people; notes with some concern the disappointment of the Scottish Consumer Council that it did not find more evidence of good practice in this area; calls on all of Scotland’s primary healthcare providers to implement the checklist aimed at primary care practitioners as a step to improving good practice, and urges the Scottish Executive to do far more to promote change among primary healthcare providers as a way of ensuring the needs of disabled people are heard and adhered to.

*S2M-2615# Mike Watson: Protecting Land Used for Organised Sport and Other Forms of Physical Activity—That the Parliament notes with concern the continuing diminution in the number of sports pitches and open space across Scotland, despite the efforts of sportscotland and the National Playing Fields Association Scotland; believes that this will make it more difficult for the Scottish Executive to achieve its stated aim of improving the health of young people in Scotland and reducing levels of obesity; endorses the need, as expressed in the report of the Physical Activity Task Force, for all primary and secondary pupils to have a minimum of two hours each week of quality physical education, and considers that the Executive should ensure that more robust measures are introduced to the planning process to protect land used for organised sport and other forms of physical activity.

S2M-2614 Christine Grahame: People’s Victory for Borders Rail—That the Parliament notes the recent funding announcement by the Scottish Executive following a petition signed by thousands in support of the Waverley Line and unanimous support from the Scottish Parliament in June 2000 for full reinstatement of the line from Edinburgh to Carlisle; recognises that this latest announcement represents a people’s victory for everyone from Campaign for Borders Rail to individuals such as Madge Elliot from Hawick, and calls on the Executive to ensure that the track for the railway is laid from the Borders towards Edinburgh in recognition of the sustained efforts of Borderers to right this historic wrong.

S2M-2613 Rosie Kane: Stop the M74—That the Parliament reaffirms the principles of the Universal Declaration of Human Rights; notes and recognises the primacy of Article 3, the right to life; acknowledges that clean water, clean air and adequate nourishment are essential for human life; further acknowledges that any individual or organisation that prevents the access of another individual to clean water, clean air or adequate nourishment is clearly in breach of that person’s human rights; accepts that the M74 extension will prevent access to clean air for the communities affected by the road and thus that the Scottish Executive is breaching the human rights of its own citizens, and therefore calls on the Executive to stop its plans for this road extension immediately.

S2M-2612# Alex Neil: Crisis of Management in Kilmarnock Prison—That the Parliament considers that the Minister for Justice should adopt a proactive approach, rather than her current complacent one, to dealing with the crisis of management in Kilmarnock Prison; believes that, if she continues to ignore this problem, she will lose the confidence of the Scottish people and this Parliament; further believes that the Chief Executive of the Scottish Prison Service has failed to ensure that the contract between Premier Prison Services and the Scottish Prison Service for the running of Kilmarnock Prison is being properly implemented and that he has totally ignored the breaches of contract, illegal activities and morally-decadent behaviour which are occurring in the prison, as well as failing to ensure that suicide watches have been properly carried out and monitored, as a result of which there has been an unnecessary and avoidable loss of life; believes that he should resign forthwith and that the Chief Inspector of Prisons should launch an immediate, open and transparent investigation into the allegations made in the BBC documentary into Kilmarnock Prison, broadcast on 9 March 2005, and further believes that the latest report of the Chief Inspector of Prisons into Kilmarnock Prison should be published immediately.

Supported by: Rob Gibson*, Christine Grahame*, Fiona Hyslop*, Campbell Martin*, Mr John Swinney*, Ms Sandra White*, Brian Adam*

S2M-2611# Colin Fox: Our Prisons are not for Profit—That the Parliament believes that all of Scotland’s prisons belong in the public sector; supports the Prisons are not for Profit campaign, and believes that any partial-privatisation plans, such as the bridging the gap initiatives, undermine the prison service’s commitment to correctional excellence.

Supported by: Rosie Kane*, Christine Grahame*, Fiona Hyslop*, Ms Sandra White*, Frances Curran*, Patrick Harvie*

S2M-2610 Alex Neil: Measuring Scotland’s Economic Performance—That the Parliament believes that the Scottish Executive should take urgent steps to establish a much more reliable, accurate and comprehensive set of economic statistics for Scotland than is currently available and that it should ensure that such statistics are as robust as those for competitor nations and are totally independent of undue and prejudicial government interference.

S2M-2609 Alex Neil: Scottish Economic Performance Study—That the Parliament believes the Scottish Executive should undertake a detailed study on how Scotland can outperform the Irish economy.

S2M-2608 Alex Neil: North Sea Oil Revenues—That the Parliament demands that the extra £1.5 billion to be raised from North Sea oil revenues identified in the Chancellor of the Exchequer’s budget be allocated for spending in Scotland.

S2M-2607 Mike Pringle: Zimbabwe Elections—That the Parliament welcomes the elections in Zimbabwe on 31 March 2005; calls on the people of Zimbabwe to turn out and exercise their hard-won and democratic right; notes that previous elections have been invalidated by intimidation and vote-rigging by the ruling Zanu-PF party of Robert Mugabe, and further calls on the international community to monitor that this election is carried out in a free and fair way so that the result fully reflects the future hopes and aspirations of the people of Zimbabwe.

S2M-2606 Frances Curran: Jamie Oliver’s School Meals Programme and On-line Petition—That the Parliament congratulates Jamie Oliver and Channel 4 on the excellent programme "Jamie’s School Dinners" which concluded on 16 March 2005; believes that Mr Oliver has put the Westminster Government to shame in highlighting the devastating state of school food served to children in England and the consequent threat to the future health of an entire generation and, in doing so, has underlined the urgent need for government intervention; notes the petition launched by Mr Oliver’s website which urges people to sign up to the following statement: "I believe kids need a healthy diet so we need to change school dinners to ban the junk and get fresh, nutritious meals back on the plate. I support the Feed Me Better campaign"; further notes that within two days of the petition being launched, well over 50,000 people have already signed, with the number of signatures going up by the second; congratulates Hull City Council on its three-year pilot programme of providing free and healthy school breakfasts, dinners and after-school snacks to all primary schoolchildren, and the Welsh Assembly on its free school breakfasts policy for all children, and looks forward to Glasgow City Council carrying out its manifesto promise to provide free, nutritious school meals to all primary schoolchildren in Glasgow by 2007.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan, Colin Fox, Robin Harper, Ms Sandra White, Rosie Kane, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Shiona Baird*, Fiona Hyslop*, Carolyn Leckie*, Bristow Muldoon*, Dr Jean Turner*, Eleanor Scott*

S2M-2605 Robin Harper: Simon Pepper OBE, Retiral from WWF Scotland—That the Parliament applauds the contribution of Simon Pepper OBE to the environmental movement in Scotland and internationally for the last two decades and more; notes his announcement that he will retire from his post as Director of WWF Scotland in June 2005; recognises his unique contribution which has enabled and inspired many individuals and organisations to take action, not just on environmental policy but right across the sustainable development and public policy agenda, and particularly applauds his widely-respected work in supporting and building initiatives such as Scottish Environment LINK and the Millennium Forest for Scotland and his vigorous efforts to help Scotland become a forward-looking, globally-responsible and sustainable country.

Supported by: Richard Lochhead, Shiona Baird, Bruce Crawford, Ms Sandra White, Patrick Harvie, Sarah Boyack, Mr John Swinney, Mark Ballard, Tommy Sheridan, Des McNulty, Eleanor Scott*

S2M-2604# Irene Oldfather: Treatment of Alzheimer’s Disease—That the Parliament notes with regret the recent decision taken by the National Institute for Clinical Excellence (NICE) not to recommend the use of donepezil, rivastigmine, galantamine and memantine in the treatment of newly-diagnosed sufferers of Alzheimer’s disease in England; acknowledges the uncertainty and concern that this decision will cause the families of Alzheimer’s sufferers in Scotland; recognises that NHS Quality Improvement Scotland will take any such decision in Scotland; is aware that there are 62,000 sufferers of Alzheimer’s disease in Scotland and that a significant proportion of these people are currently being prescribed these drugs; accepts that the drugs are widely available in the United States and across the rest of Europe to successfully treat Alzheimer’s disease; acknowledges the work of Age Concern and Alzheimer Scotland in raising awareness of the disease and providing advice and support for sufferers and their families; is aware that Alzheimer’s disease is a recognised terminal illness; notes that NHS Quality Improvement Scotland has not yet approved the use of memantine and has not yet taken a final decision on prescribing practices for Alzheimer’s disease on the basis of the NICE recommendations, but considers that the Scottish Executive should look at the wider picture and take into account the benefits that the use of these drugs have been proven to have for sufferers and their families across the rest of Europe and beyond.

Supported by: Dr Elaine Murray*

S2M-2603# Shiona Baird: Disability Rights Commission Employment Campaign—That the Parliament congratulates the Disability Rights Commission (DRC) on its new employment campaign aimed at increasing confidence among small employers in the benefits of recruiting and retaining disabled staff and exploding some popular myths about the costs to businesses of doing so; believes that more support should be offered to the one million disabled people in Britain who are unemployed but who want to find work; recognises that, while people who become disabled during their working years may have to alter their employment patterns, they retain highly-valuable and transferable skills and experience, and considers that all employers should fully embrace the DRC campaign, recognise that disabled people represent a vast, untapped source of talent, and take positive steps to address under-representation of disabled people in the workplace.

Supported by: Chris Ballance, Mark Ballard, Bruce Crawford, Rob Gibson, Robin Harper, Alex Neil, Patrick Harvie, Mr Adam Ingram, Mr Kenneth Macintosh, Nora Radcliffe, Mr Mark Ruskell, Eleanor Scott, Elaine Smith, Ms Sandra White, Mike Pringle, Tommy Sheridan, Dr Jean Turner*, Christine Grahame*

S2M-2602# John Swinburne: Treatment of Alzheimer’s Disease—That the Parliament notes with concern the preliminary recommendations of the National Institute for Clinical Excellence (NICE) which, while recognising the effectiveness of three dementia drugs, donepezil, rivastigmine and galantamine, to many sufferers, recommends withdrawing these drugs on the grounds of cost to the NHS in England and Wales; endorses the call by Alzheimer Scotland that all patients with Alzheimer’s disease who are considered suitable by their doctors for these treatments should receive them until it can be shown that either they are not or are no longer benefiting, and considers that the Scottish Executive should ensure that the NICE recommendations are not adopted by NHS Scotland to avoid a disastrous decision for the 62,000 sufferers of dementia in Scotland and their families.

Supported by: Alex Neil, Rob Gibson, Tricia Marwick, Stewart Stevenson, Brian Adam, Ms Sandra White, Frances Curran, Carolyn Leckie, Tommy Sheridan, Murray Tosh*, Dr Jean Turner*, Mr David Davidson*, Christine Grahame*

S2M-2601# Roseanna Cunningham: City of Perth—That the Parliament notes that the foundation of Perth as a royal burgh dates back to a charter granted by King William the Lion in 1210 and that the first reference to Perth as a city came in the Golden Charter granted by King James VI in 1600; recognises that between 1600 and the early 19th century the use of the terms "city", "burgh" and "town" was interchangeable and that from the early 19th century Perth was consistently referred to as a city in official documents such as Acts of Parliament; further recognises that this use continued in the 20th century when the terms "City of Perth" and "Royal Burgh of Perth" appeared in many official documents, and welcomes Perth and Kinross Council’s intention to reaffirm Perth’s status as one of Scotland’s historic and modern-day cities with a declaration in the old Council Chambers and a series of events leading up to the celebration of the 800th anniversary of the City of Perth in 2010.

Supported by: Michael Matheson, Christine Grahame*

S2M-2601.1# Murdo Fraser: City of Perth—As an amendment to motion (S2M-2601) in the name of Roseanna Cunningham, insert at end "but regrets that Perth is not recognised as having legal city status by the Scottish Executive and accordingly considers that the Executive should accept Perth as having city status on a par with Aberdeen, Dundee, Edinburgh, Glasgow, Inverness and Stirling."

S2M-2583 Bill Butler: Drumchapel High School Pupils’ Act of Solidarity—That the Parliament expresses its admiration for the pupils of Drumchapel High School in Glasgow who organised a petition which attracted hundreds of signatures in support of their friends and fellow pupils, Agnesa, aged 15, and Gentian Murselaj, aged 12, who were taken from their home in Scotstoun at 7.00 am on Sunday 6 March 2005 following the rejection of their family’s asylum application; conveys concern at the apparently heavy-handed nature of the family’s eviction, which saw Mr Murselaj handcuffed by one of several immigration officials drafted in to extract the family of five, including a nine-year-old child, from their home; notes that the pupils’ petition may have played a significant role in influencing the Home Office’s subsequent decision to grant the family a stay of removal pending a case review; agrees that these acts of solidarity and support not only exemplify the goodwill which exists towards asylum seekers in Scotland but also demonstrate the philosophy behind the Scottish Executive’s One Scotland Many Cultures campaign, and considers that the pupils should be congratulated for their stance.

S2M-2581# Christine Grahame: Scotland Day - 6 April—That the Parliament notes that 6 April 2005 will mark the 685th anniversary of the signing of the Declaration of Arbroath, a formal declaration of Scottish independence and the seminal document in our nation’s history which proclaimed in 1320 that "It is in truth not for glory, nor riches, nor honours that we are fighting, but for freedom"; notes that the Declaration of Arbroath was the inspiration behind the American declaration of independence from Britain and that the date chosen to mark Tartan Day, 6 April, recognises the important part played by Scots in the establishment of the United States as a nation free from the "chains and slavery" of "English rule"; acknowledges the heroic struggle of our ancestors who freed our nation from tyranny and oppression, and believes that Scottish ministers should recognise that struggle and formally establish a "Scotland Day" on 6 April each year which will allow Scots to celebrate our history and inject greater confidence and optimism as a nation, one of the oldest in Europe, and allow us once again to take our rightful place amongst the family of nations.
