Business Bulletin No. 159/2001

Tuesday 6 November 2001

Section E : Written questions lodged on 5 November 2001
  

S1W-19678 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in dealing with unemployment in North Ayrshire.

S1W-19679 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what practical measures it will consider taking to attract employment to North Ayrshire.

S1W-19680 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive whether its plans for the dispersal of civil service jobs will extend to all areas of the country.

S1W-19681 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in relation to any timetable it has for the dispersal of civil service jobs.

S1W-19682 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what the criteria are for the selection of destinations for the relocation of civil service jobs.

S1W-19683 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive whether areas with above average levels of unemployment will be given priority in the dispersal of civil service jobs.

S1W-19684 Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-18877 by Ross Finnie on 2 November 2001, what revenue it expects to be allocated from the Aggregates Tax Sustainability Fund in 2002-03.

S1W-19685 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-17971 by Jackie Baillie on 1 October 2001, how the £0.5 million funding to boost college provision specifically for asylum seekers will be spent, broken down by individual colleges, over the current financial year and over future financial years.

S1W-19686 Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive what advice or guidance it has provided to education authorities on how to deal with teaching materials concerning genetically modified crops.

S1W-19687 Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive what authorisation it has given to companies involved with genetically modified (GM) crops or pro-GM organisations regarding the distribution of information on GM crops to schools.

S1W-19688 Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make available in the Scottish Parliament Information Centre any material that it has authorised that can be distributed to schools on the use of genetically modified crops.

S1W-19689 Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive what advice or guidance it has issued to civil emergency planners and local authorities regarding potential terrorist threats to nuclear material stored or used at Sellafield, Torness, Hunterston, Dounreay, Faslane, Chapelcross, Rosyth and Coulport.

S1W-19690 Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive what advice or guidance it has issued to civil emergency planners and local authorities regarding the length of warning times which should be given to enable the evacuation of the general public from affected areas in the event of a terrorist attack on Sellafied or Dounreay.

S1W-19691 Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has calculated what length of warning people in Dumfries and Galloway would require to enable a safe public evacuation in the event of a terrorist attack on the nuclear installation at Sellafied.

S1W-19692 Kay Ullrich: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of comments made by Chief Superintendent David Johnstone of Strathclyde Police in the Ardrossan and Saltcoats Herald on 2 November 2001 that, in a context of limited resources, the force had failed the people of Ardrossan, whether additional resources will be made available to U Division of Strathclyde Police in order to ensure that crime and disorder in the town is tackled.

S1W-19693 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken to ensure that there is no repetition on the wider motorway network of the traffic gridlocks recently experienced by drivers on the M8.

S1W-19694 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-18665 by Allan Wilson on 26 October 2001, what specific additional safety measures have been introduced through the British Boxing Board of Control following the Paul Ingles fight and how information on these measures will be disseminated.

S1W-19695 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will publish full details of the income and expenditure of the Scottish Qualifications Authority for the last two years.

S1W-19696 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many social work assistants have been employed by each local authority in each of the last five years, and what financial and other support is available for social work assistants to enable them to gain appropriate qualifications.

S1W-19697 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many social workers specialising in childcare have been employed by each local authority in each of the last five years.

S1W-19698 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many posts for social workers specialising in childcare were vacant in each local authority in each of the last five years.

S1W-19699 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to address any shortage in foster carers.

S1W-19700 John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive, in respect of composting bins issued by local authorities, whether it has issued any guidelines on benchmark figures for the amount of compost produced per bin that should form the basis of statistical calculations.

S1W-19701 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will provide substantive answers to questions S1W-17798, S1W-17799, S1W-17802 and S1W-18076.

S1W-19702 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to review the core funding arrangements for women’s refuges.
