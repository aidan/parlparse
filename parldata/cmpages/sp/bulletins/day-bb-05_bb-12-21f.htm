Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-3767 Carolyn Leckie: Blood Products—That the Parliament recognises the dogged efforts of patients, relatives, campaigners and all those who have highlighted the issues around the contamination of blood products, including those with viruses such as Hepatitis C and HIV, in the past and the serious consequences for the recipients of these products and their families; acknowledges the previous and continuing work of the Parliament’s Health Committee and MSPs; accepts that campaigners’ demands for a full, independent, public inquiry remain outstanding; believes that there is now more information in the public domain and that the debate has moved on, and agrees that there should now be a full, independent public inquiry.

Supported by: Colin Fox*

*S2M-3766 Frances Curran: "Torture Flights" on Scottish Soil—That the Parliament considers, in light of recent decisions by the governments of Sweden, Spain and Iceland, by the European Union and now by Michael Todd, Chief Constable of Greater Manchester Police in England, to undertake inquiries into rendition flights, that the Scottish Executive should follow these examples; believes that there are sufficient grounds to justify an independent inquiry into the possible use of Scottish airports by CIA rendition flights, and further believes that the Lord Advocate should instruct the police to investigate without delay whether there has been a breach of Scots Law.

Supported by: Ms Rosemary Byrne*

*S2M-3764 Fiona Hyslop: Glasgow’s Gaelic School—That the Parliament notes the intention to make Woodside School in Glasgow the first all-through Gaelic school and the Scottish Executive’s commitment of £2.75 million towards the renovation of the school; further notes that construction industry inflation has increased the cost of the project from £3.5 million to the latest estimate of £4 million, and calls on the Executive to increase its support for the project in order to help address these cost increases.

Supported by: Ms Sandra White*

*S2M-3761 Mark Ballard: Art Not Oil Exhibition: 15 December 2005 to 16 January 2006—That the Parliament welcomes the exhibition, "Art not Oil", running at the Bongo Club in Edinburgh from 15 December 2005 to 16 January 2006; acknowledges that Tony Blair has said that climate change is the most important environmental issue facing the world today and that the UK Government’s Chief Scientific Officer, Sir David King, describes climate change as a bigger threat than global terrorism; believes that the crisis in Iraq is symptomatic of a greater battle for control of scarce resources, and calls on the Scottish Executive to step up its measures to tackle climate change, including using its influence to promote environmental justice and expressing the will of the Scottish people on reserved issues such as defence and energy, to set binding emissions reduction targets for Scotland and to take action to cut Scotland’s greenhouse gas emissions by cutting airport expansion and road building, investing in public transport, supporting environmentally sustainable renewable energy developments and working towards energy efficient housing in Scotland.

*S2M-3760 Rob Gibson: Buy Local for the Best Food—That the Parliament agrees with Rick Stein’s Christmas message that to source the best food, families should buy local produce, praising the heroism of those who persist in growing and making quality traditional food that is gaining increased recognition for its tasty, healthy qualities but as yet is unavailable in quantities that supermarkets deal in; notes the prolonged battle to win government support for a favourable rate of carriage for small quantities of product to reach markets, seeks simplification of regulations and deplores the lack of production facilities such as local abattoirs in an agricultural system in Scotland that is largely geared to quantity production dictated by supermarket specifications rather than taste, and calls on all MSPs to champion the Food Heroes Christmas Message.

*S2M-3753 Patrick Harvie: Civil Partnership in the Western Isles—That the Parliament condemns Comhairle nan Eilean Siar for its decision not to perform civil partnership ceremonies, making it the only local authority in the United Kingdom to take this stance; recognises that for many couples a public ceremony in front of family and friends is an important aspect of any act of union; notes that, although ceremonies are discretionary, both for civil partnership and for civil marriage, no local authority has refused to offer them for the latter; considers the council’s decision to be an insult to all same-sex couples and that such couples will be less likely to exercise their right to registration as a result, and calls on the Scottish Executive to contact Comhairle nan Eilean Siar to explain the meaning of the council’s duty under the Local Government in Scotland Act 2003 to discharge its functions in a manner which encourages equal opportunities, as the council has clearly demonstrated a limited understanding of this duty.

Supported by: Mr Mark Ruskell*, Margaret Smith*, Carolyn Leckie*, Robin Harper*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*
