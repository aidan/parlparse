BUSINESS BULLETIN 100 / 2000

Tuesday 13 June 2000

Section E - Written questions lodged on 12 June 2000

 

 

S1W-7841 Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether local Abbeyfield societies or Abbeyfield Scotland were included in the consultation list for Regulating Care and the Social Services Workforce.

S1W-7843 Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to allocate additional funding to the Scottish Ambulance Service in order to reduce on-call working.

 

S1W-7844 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it will take to promote improvements in the standard of bathing waters at the seven Scottish bathing beaches recently found to have failed to meet the European Union’s minimum standards.

 

S1W-7845 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many Gaelic speakers it expects to be resident in Scotland in (a) 2005, (b) 2010 and (c) 2015.

 

S1W-7846 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many Gaelic speakers aged 15 or less it expects to be resident in Scotland in (a) 2005, (b) 2010 and (c) 2015.

 

S1W-7847 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive what expert advice from language planners and other linguistic authorities has been taken with regard to any official projections of the number of Gaelic speakers resident in Scotland in 2005, 2010 and 2015.

 

S1W-7848 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many parental requests for Gaelic-medium education have been received by local authorities in Scotland in the last five years.

 

S1W-7849 Mr Duncan Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive what specific measures it will implement to encourage the take-up of flu vaccinations and what resources will be used for a public awareness campaign.

 

S1W-7850 Mr Duncan Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-6937 by Sarah Boyack on 31 May 2000, whether the research papers on Caledonian MacBrayne which have been placed in the Scottish Parliament Information Centre include all Scottish Office and Scotland Office research papers and, if not, whether these will be placed there as soon as possible.

 

S1W-7851 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive how the roles of the Consumers’ Committee and the related Committee of Investigation for Great Britain have developed since they were established under the Agricultural Marketing Act 1958.

 

S1W-7852 Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what responses it has had to its consultation on Towards an Equality Strategy.

 

S1W-7853 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has been consulted on the costs of implementation of the proposed Climate Change Levy to Scotland and whether it will publish these costs.

 

S1W-7854 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it intends to take to advise businesses which are likely to be exempt from the Climate Change Levy to apply for licences.

 

S1W-7855 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is invited to attend meetings between the Department of the Environment, Transport and the Regions and the Gas Forum in order to ensure that the interests of Scottish business and the Scottish consumer are protected and, if so, whether it will report the progress of any such meetings to the Parliament.

 

S1W-7856 Mr Murray Tosh: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-4928 by Ms Wendy Alexander on 9 June 2000, whether it will identify the specific land or property disposed of by Dumfries and Galloway Council, East Lothian Council, Scottish Borders Council and South Ayrshire Council.

 

S1W-7857 Janis Hughes: To ask the Scottish Executive how it supports the role played by guide/communicators in the day-to-day life of deafblind people.

 

S1W-7858 Janis Hughes: To ask the Scottish Executive which local authorities in Scotland do not currently provide guide/communicator support for deafblind people.

 

S1W-7859 Janis Hughes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to provide funding for guide/communicators for deafblind people in areas which do not fund such a service.

 

S1W-7860 Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it has made on preparations for the Housing Bill including that made on its documents Housing and Anti-Social Behaviour – The Way Ahead and A New Single Housing Tenancy for Scotland: Rights, Obligations and Opportunities.

 

S1W-7861 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will detail the membership of the National Steering Group announced as part of the Rural Partnership for Change Initiative in March.
