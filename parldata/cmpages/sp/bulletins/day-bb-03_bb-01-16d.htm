Business Bulletin No. 9/2003

Thursday 16 January 2003

Section D : Oral Questions selected for answer on 23 January 2003

  

Question Time

1. Mr John McAllion: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with Her Majesty’s Government regarding an early and just settlement to the fire service dispute. (S1O-6265)

2. Mr Tom McCabe: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to prevent domestic abuse. (S1O-6292)

3. Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met Dundee City Council and what issues were discussed. (S1O-6260)

4. Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what concerns it has regarding the presence of mercury in some infant vaccines. (S1O-6290)

5. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to increase the numbers of drug seizures and arrests. (S1O-6277)

6. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the provision of car parking for commuters at railway stations. (S1O-6286)

7. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with Scottish Borders Council regarding any application by the council under the Bellwin scheme and any other sources of funding for repairs to the A72. (S1O-6289)

8. Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what extra resources are being provided to the procurator fiscal service. (S1O-6295)

9. Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive what its plans are to encourage entrepreneurial education in secondary schools. (S1O-6269)

10. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will report on the Review of the First Year of the Mandatory Licensing of Houses in Multiple Occupation in Scotland. (S1O-6296)

11. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive what its priorities are for improving the NHS. (S1O-6291)

12. Mr Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive what its timetable is for considering the recommendations of the audiology services review. (S1O-6287)

13. Ms Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive whether City of Edinburgh Council has requested any meetings with it following the cities review. (S1O-6270)

14. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it offers lower income homeowners. (S1O-6281)

15. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to encourage young people to continue to participate in sport after leaving school. (S1O-6279)

16. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to improve services for those suffering from chronic pain. (S1O-6288)

17. Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether its expenditure is greater or less than the revenue raised and whether its spending plans would be affected if expenditure is matched to revenue. (S1O-6283)

18. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive what it is doing to improve audiology services. (S1O-6272)

19. George Lyon: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to improve single track roads in the Highlands and Islands. (S1O-6256)

20. Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will publish the report of its short-life action group on myalgic encephalomyelitis. (S1O-6299)

21. Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will publish a bill to reform the record of needs procedure. (S1O-6257)

22. John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will reform the system for identifying and protecting vulnerable children. (S1O-6267)

23. Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to reduce the incidence of skin cancer. (S1O-6297)

24. Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the appointment of active members of the Labour Party to the boards of public bodies. (S1O-6262)

25. Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will produce a consultation paper on urban regeneration companies. (S1O-6293)

26. Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has for Scottish Water. (S1O-6259)

27. Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in assessing the economic impact that reductions in fishing quotas will have on fishing communities such as Arbroath. (S1O-6278)

28. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in relation to the introduction of the European Union law that requires the recycling of unwanted cars and other vehicles. (S1O-6282)

29. Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive when Scottish gross domestic product per head will reach the same level as UK average gross domestic product per head. (S1O-6264)

30. Mr Keith Harding: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will next meet the Convention of Scottish Local Authorities and what issues will be discussed. (S1O-6266)
