Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-3940 John Swinburne: Congratulations to Motherwell FC and the Maggie’s Centre—That the Parliament congratulates Motherwell FC on its proposed assistance to the new Maggie’s Centre at Wishaw General Hospital, which seeks to offer help to those with cancer; notes that all profits from the forthcoming game against Falkirk FC on 11 March 2006 will be donated to the centre, including revenues from the gates, catering and programmes, and further notes that this should give a tremendous boost to the centre. R

*S2M-3939 Robin Harper: Save the Palais—That the Parliament notes with alarm that the Fountainbridge Mecca Palais bingo hall, an Edinburgh institution which once attracted young dancers in their droves, is to be demolished to make way for housing, business units and eateries; believes that this building, with its landmark Art Deco frontage, should be preserved at a time when live music and dance venues in Edinburgh are at an all-time low, and recognises that we should encourage the resurgence of dancing generated by recent television programmes as a means of promoting physical exercise in adults.

*S2M-3938 Ms Sandra White: Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual and Transgender Youth Scotland’s Progress Report 2006—That the Parliament welcomes the 2006 progress report from LGBT Youth Scotland; notes that, while there may be as many as 100,000 young people who are lesbian, gay, bisexual or transgender, only around 6% have come out by the time they are 16; applauds the positive work done by the group in liaising with local authorities, the police, education authorities, health services, government and others to help shape policy, and fully supports it in its quest to help ease the transition of young people into adulthood.

*S2M-3937 Donald Gorrie: Funding for 7:84 Theatre and Scottish Opera Programmes—That the Parliament commends the good educational work done by 7:84 Theatre Company in touring the play, "Tipping Point", and discussion about the G8 summit around 24 secondary schools and other school and community projects, stimulating students and adults to think and argue about important issues; further commends the well-established programme by Scottish Opera of touring do-it-yourself operas around primary schools, and urges that sufficient funding should be made available by the Scottish Arts Council and from the Scottish Executive’s culture, educational and community budgets to enable these programmes to continue and expand so that many more young people can benefit from these life-enhancing experiences.

*S2M-3936 Mr John Swinney: Temporary Relocation of the Stone of Destiny to Scone Palace in May 2006—That the Parliament expresses its regret at the decision of the First Minister not to permit the relocation of the Stone of Destiny to Scone Palace for a temporary period in May 2006 in connection with the 700th anniversary of the coronation of Robert the Bruce at Scone Palace; applauds Scone Palace on the series of events which it has planned in relation to this event in May to boost the local economy; believes that this is an important opportunity to increase awareness of Scottish history that would have been enhanced by the presence of the Stone of Destiny, and appeals to the First Minister to reconsider his decision.

*S2M-3935 Mr Ted Brocklebank: 500th Anniversary of the Discovery of Tristan Da Cunha—That the Parliament notes that this year is the 500th anniversary of the discovery of the South Atlantic British Overseas Dependency of Tristan da Cunha; acknowledges the historic ties between Scotland and the island, from the founder settler in 1816, Corporal William Glass of Kelso, to the visit in 1867 of HRH Prince Alfred, Duke of Edinburgh, and the subsequent naming of the settlement community as Edinburgh, and suggests that the Scottish Executive should recognise this anniversary by presenting a commemorative gift to this isolated community which is fiercely proud of its connections with the Scottish people and Scotland.
