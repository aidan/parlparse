BUSINESS BULLETIN 83 / 2001

Tuesday 22 May 2001

Section E - Written questions lodged on 21 May 2001

 

 
S1W-15872 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many special educational needs lecturing staff are employed (a) in each further education (FE) college and (b) per thousand FE college students.

S1W-15873 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many special educational needs support staff are employed (a) in each further education (FE) college and (b) per thousand FE college students.

S1W-15874 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance is given to further education colleges regarding support for students with special educational needs.

S1W-15875 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive how the performance of further education colleges is monitored with regard to students with special educational needs.

S1W-15876 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what targets are set for further education colleges with regard to students with special educational needs.

S1W-15877 Ben Wallace: To ask the Scottish Executive how much money the NHSiS has recovered from April 1999 to date from insurance companies for the treatment of casualties of road traffic accidents.

S1W-15878 Ben Wallace: To ask the Scottish Executive how many invoices have been raised against insurance companies for the treatment of casualties of road traffic accidents under the centralised cost recovery arrangements announced in April 1999 to replace the Emergency Treatment Fee.

S1W-15879 Ben Wallace: To ask the Scottish Executive how much has been raised from April 1999 to date from insurance companies for the treatment of road accident patients (a) in accident and emergency departments and (b) who were admitted to hospital following the accident.

S1W-15880 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether health boards are required to replace current out of hours services with the NHS24 service.

S1W-15881 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive what the timescale is for the introduction of the NHS24 service.

S1W-15882 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive how much has been spent to date on preparations for the introduction of the NHS24 service and what the total cost of implementation will be.

S1W-15883 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive what evidence exists to show that a centralised NHS24 doctor on call system delivers a better service to patients than the current local provision.

S1W-15884 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive what consulation has taken place with NHSiS staff, unions, patients or patient representative groups regarding the introduction of the NHS24 service.

S1W-15885 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive what is being done to raise public awareness of the NHS24 service.

S1W-15886 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the NHS24 service will provide better value for money than the present system and, if so, on what figures this assertion is based.

S1W-15887 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive why information about traditional arts, culture and the Scots language is not available on the visitscotland website.

S1W-15888 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether visitscotland will maximise the potential of Scottish traditional arts, culture and language to attract overseas visitors by highlighting them on its website.

S1W-15889 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-13848 by Susan Deacon on 11 May 2001, whether it will provide figures on the expected increase in the number of students entering medical training in (a) 2001-02, (b) 2002-03, (c) 2003-04 and (d) 2004-05.

S1W-15890 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-15518 by Rhona Brankin on 15 May 2001, when the draft designation order for the Cairngorms National Park will be published and who will be included in the further consultation process.
