Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (♦).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S3M-4590 Jackie Baillie: Club of Champions—That the Parliament congratulates Helensburgh Sailing Club on the opening of its new clubhouse; notes that the club can claim Olympic gold medalist Mike McIntyre, round the world yachtswoman Emma Richards and Stephen Park OBE as former members; understands that the opening is a culmination of years of fundraising by club members, and wishes the club every success with its new facilities that will undoubtedly give rise to a new generation of Scottish champions.

*S3M-4589 Alex Johnstone: Aberdeen Bus Strike—That the Parliament expresses concern at the possibility of strike action by First Aberdeen staff; notes that public transport is a lifeline to many people in the city of Aberdeen, especially the elderly and vulnerable; further notes that passengers may be obliged to leave buses and be left at the side of the road; urges the union not to undertake strike action, and hopes that all parties will negotiate an amicable solution that avoids a detrimental impact on communities.

*S3M-4588 David Stewart: Eigg Crowned North Regional Winner of Scottish Calor Village of the Year 2009—That the Parliament congratulates the community of the island of Eigg on being named North Regional Winner in the Scottish Calor Village of the Year 2009; welcomes the award of £2,000 for community investment; further notes the comments of Stephen Rennie, Managing Director of Calor Gas, which recognised that the islanders had demonstrated a unique ability to sustain an almost completely self-sufficient island lifestyle with outstanding community spirit, providing everything from electricity to employment, and wishes the people of Eigg well in the next round of the competition.

Supported by: Bill Kidd*, Stuart McMillan*, Sandra White*, Mr Frank McAveety*, Rob Gibson*, Hugh Henry*, Ken Macintosh*

*S3M-4587# Ms Wendy Alexander: Robert Tannahill—That the Parliament commemorates the life of Robert Tannahill (1774-1810) also known as "Paisley’s Son"; celebrates the bicentenary of his death; recognises the contribution that Robert Tannahill made to Scotland’s cultural heritage; notes the impact that his poetry and music has made on Scotland and Paisley, and welcomes the efforts being made by David Mullen to commemorate his anniversary for next year.

Supported by: Jamie McGrigor*, Hugh O’Donnell*, Sandra White*, Kenneth Gibson*, Ross Finnie*, Robin Harper*, Trish Godman*, David Whitton*, Malcolm Chisholm*, Mr Frank McAveety*

*S3M-4586 Malcolm Chisholm: School Closures in North Edinburgh and Leith—That the Parliament is concerned by the proposal to close Royston Primary School and Fort Primary School in the Edinburgh North and Leith constituency; notes that the average number of pupils per primary school in Edinburgh is already the second highest in Scotland; is dismayed that almost two thirds of the proposed revenue savings from primary school closures in Edinburgh are stated by the council to arise from reducing staff costs; believes that closures will therefore increase class sizes and pupil teacher ratios, and calls on the council to concentrate instead on reversing the decline in teacher numbers in Edinburgh and recognising the important and successful role that Royston and Fort primaries play in their respective communities.

*S3M-4585 Pauline McNeill: Work Begins on Aircraft Carriers—That the Parliament warmly welcomes the first cut of steel for the aircraft carriers that will become HMS Queen Elizabeth and HMS Prince of Wales, large sections of which will be built on the Clyde and at Rosyth Dockyard, providing work for the foreseeable future for nearly 4,000 shipyard workers on the Clyde; further welcomes the commitment of the UK Government to the aircraft carriers that will come into service in 2016; notes the UK Government’s view that this work provides the basis for a longer-term sustainable future, thus securing jobs and skills on the Clyde, and further notes that these carriers highlight the performance and ability in Scottish shipbuilding and ship design, which continues to be of a world-class standard, and that this success allows for the largest apprenticeship programme in Scotland and as such represents a major contribution to the Scottish economy.

Supported by: John Park*, Duncan McNeil*, Ms Wendy Alexander*, Helen Eadie*, Cathie Craigie*, Ken Macintosh*, Andy Kerr*, David Whitton*, Dr Richard Simpson*, David Stewart*, Trish Godman*, Mr Frank McAveety*, Bill Butler*

*S3M-4584 Mike Pringle: A Mixed Plastics Reprocessing factory for Scotland—That the Parliament welcomes WRAP Scotland’s invitation for bids for £5 million of funding towards the construction of Scotland’s first mixed plastics reprocessing factory; notes that when a factory is established it will allow Scotland to recycle an estimated 40,000 tonnes of plastic waste a year, 27,500 tonnes more that is currently recycled; further notes that plastic waste will no longer have to be sent elsewhere in the United Kingdom or abroad to be recycled, further reducing Scotland’s carbon footprint; acknowledges the vital importance of increasing recycling levels and reducing the volume of plastic waste that is sent to landfill as part of a wider environmental strategy, and hopes that Scotland can continue to increase its plastic recycling capacity to tackle the estimated 200,000 tonnes of plastic that is thrown away across the nation each year.

*S3M-4583 Christina McKelvie: Congratulations to Team Lanarkshire—That the Parliament congratulates Team Lanarkshire, Scotland’s only representatives at the International Children’s Games in Athens, on its magnificent tally of one gold, two silver and four bronze medals; notes that the girls’ 4 x 100 metre relay team stormed home to win gold; further notes that the silver medal-winning boys’ football team only narrowly missed out on gold following a penalty shoot-out; further notes that East Kilbride athlete Rachel Whittle not only contributed to the gold medal relay performance but also took silver in the individual 100 metres and bronze in the high jump, and believes that Rachel’s achievements along with those of Emma Brown and Ciaran Johnston from East Kilbride, Mhairi Hendry from Hamilton, Arlene Barr from Cambuslang, Darren Brownlie from Bellshill, Dean McLean from Carluke, Craig Moore from Uddingston, Jack Smith from Carfin, Anthony Brady from Glenboig, Ross Burnett and Calum Baxter from Airdrie, Euan Alston, Lynn Macarthur and Leanna Marshall from Cumbernauld, Jonathan Stuart from Blantyre, Rochelle Callan from Coatbridge, Emily Lauder from Bargeddie and Charlotte Walker from Bothwell reflect highly on the quality of Scotland’s young athletes and bode well for the success of the 2011 International Children’s Games when it takes place in their home county of Lanarkshire.

*S3M-4582 Jackie Baillie: You Could be Heroes - Just for One Day—That the Parliament congratulates Helensburgh Heroes on its successful recording of the legendary David Bowie song Heroes; notes that the non–profit organisation set up last year has recorded its own version of the popular song and is planning on releasing it for charity; understands that David Bowie’s record company are delighted with the new version and have given the community group permission to release it commercially; further understands that the Helensburgh Heroes group will be unveiling its efforts at the Victoria Halls in Helensburgh on 1 August 2009, and wishes it every success with the world premiere of the new Heroes recording and film of its journey.

*S3M-4580# Alasdair Morgan: Whithorn as Cradle of Christianity and Place of Modern Pilgrimage—That the Parliament recognises that Whithorn is the earliest known centre of Christianity in Scotland and that Whithorn has already produced archaeological artefacts of immense historical significance; notes that, while in ancient times Ninian was Scotland’s premier saint and remains commemorated in many sites throughout the nation, the historical significance of Whithorn and Ninian’s story is largely forgotten by a modern generation; recognises that the recognition of Whithorn as Scotland’s cradle of Christianity will bring economic benefit to a depressed area, and supports the efforts of the Whithorn Trust to publicise Whithorn’s unique contribution to Scottish history more widely, not least for the benefit of its present-day inhabitants.

*S3M-4579 Tricia Marwick: 40th Anniversary of Tanshall Primary School—That the Parliament congratulates Tanshall Primary School in Glenrothes on its 40th anniversary; notes that since 1969 the school has been at the centre of the west of Glenrothes community and has provided a fantastic primary education for a countless number of local children; commends head teacher Elaine Davidson and her staff for their excellent recent HM Inspectorate of Education report, and believes this is the ideal present for the school’s 40th anniversary.

Supported by: Brian Adam*, Bill Kidd*, Stuart McMillan*, John Park*, Sandra White*

*S3M-4573 Bill Kidd: US-Russia Nuclear Agreement Confirms the Need to Scrap Trident—That the Parliament welcomes the expressed determination of US President Barack Obama and Russian President Dmitry Medvedev to cut back on their nations’ stockpiles of nuclear weapons; considers this diplomatic breakthrough, between two powers that were previously engaged in a massive conventional and nuclear arms race with one another, to be a constructive step towards realising a world free of nuclear weapons, and believes this to be an excellent opportunity for the UK Government to affirm its own commitment toward nuclear disarmament, in compliance with its obligations under the Nuclear Non-Proliferation Treaty, by ditching the wasteful and ill-advised plans to renew the Trident weapons programme.
