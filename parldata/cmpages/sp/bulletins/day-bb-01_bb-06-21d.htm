BUSINESS BULLETIN 103 / 2001

Thursday 21 June 2001

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 28 June 2001

 

 

Question Time

1. Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what research it is undertaking into any link between road works carried out by utility companies and levels of city congestion. (S1O-3672)

2. Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met representatives of Argyll and Clyde Health Board. (S1O-3646)

3. Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made by the Scottish Advisory Committee on Alcohol Misuse. (S1O-3660)

4. Mr John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will take into account the cost implications for rural local authorities of implementing the recommendations in the report by the Discipline Task Force, Better Behaviour – Better Learning. (S1O-3639)

5. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive when it proposes to inform the Parliament of its legislative intentions for 2001-02. (S1O-3666)

6. Mr Andy Kerr: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure proper monitoring of the current trunk roads maintenance contracts. (S1O-3635)

7. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact early intervention schemes are having on literacy and numeracy standards. (S1O-3675)

8. Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will evaluate the impact of Freight Facilities Grants in diverting freight from road to rail. (S1O-3647)

9. Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to support a private storage scheme for the lamb market. (S1O-3677)

10. Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what its target rate of growth is for the economy. (S1O-3669)

11. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any concerns with regard to the provision of education in the Scottish Borders. (S1O-3650)

12. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to ensure that patient care does not suffer as a result of any cuts to be made by Grampian University Hospitals NHS Trust. (S1O-3636)

13. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has been consulted by Her Majesty's Government on the potential impact on the coalition partnership of any proposals to change the method of electing additional members of the Scottish Parliament. (S1O-3655)

14. Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made of the economy of Dumfries and Galloway following the foot-and-mouth disease outbreak. (S1O-3659)

15. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive when the independent committee which is to review alcohol licensing legislation, as announced on 7 December 2000, will begin work and what its timetable will be. (S1O-3657)

16. Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to issue new guidance to protect children from the dangers of the internet. (S1O-3645)

17. Cathie Craigie: To ask the Scottish Executive when the target of having a nursery place available for all three-year-olds whose parents want one will be met. (S1O-3638)

18. Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to support the roles and functions of community hospitals. (S1O-3641)

19. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made by the Improving Regulation in Scotland Unit in cutting the burden of red tape on Scottish business. (S1O-3676)

20. Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether Scotland currently has a structural fiscal deficit with the rest of the United Kingdom. (S1O-3643)

21. Mr John McAllion: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent discussions have taken place with the Scotland Office regarding amending the Scotland Act 1998. (S1O-3640)

22. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive what factors led to the recently announced increase in core funding for Childline Scotland. (S1O-3664)

23. Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making in discussions with the European Commission over the sheep export trade. (S1O-3654)

24. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in attracting new jobs to Kintyre. (S1O-3633)

25. Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there will be a resumption of livestock auction sales this autumn. (S1O-3653)

26. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what further progress has been made in implementing measures to tackle youth crime since the publication of the report of the Advisory Group on Youth Crime and the Executive’s response to the report last year. (S1O-3671)

27. Mrs Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to tackle youth crime. (S1O-3642)

28. Mr Duncan Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met officials from Argyll and Bute Council and what issues were discussed. (S1O-3649)

29. Colin Campbell: To ask the Scottish Executive when a report of the negotiations and consultations between Her Majesty's Government and the Scottish Executive in preparation for the 2000 Inter-governmental Conference in Nice will be in the public domain. (S1O-3658)

Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it supports the marketing of Badenoch and Strathspey as "Monarch Country" and whether it will urge visitscotland to investigate the feasibility of purchasing brief television advert slots at the end of episodes of "Monarch of the Glen" shown in other countries.
