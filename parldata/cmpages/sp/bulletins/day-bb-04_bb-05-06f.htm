Business Bulletin No. 76 / 2004

Thursday 6th May 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-1277# Colin Fox: Prescription Charges and Penalty Charges-That the Parliament believes that significant numbers of patients are not accessing the health care they need because they cannot afford the £6.40 charged for each prescription item; further notes the Citizens Advice Bureau report, Penalising prescription poverty, in January 2004 which suggests that many people on incapacity benefits are confused as to whether or not they qualify for free prescriptions and are falling foul of penalty charges introduced by the Health Act 1999; believes that patients in poor health and on low incomes are being severely penalised by the present system, and considers that the Scottish Executive should look at outright abolition of prescription charges as one of the options in its forthcoming review.

*S2M-1276 Patricia Ferguson on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Membership of Committee-That the Parliament agrees that Margaret Smith be appointed to replace Mike Pringle on the Waverley Railway (Scotland) Bill Committee.

Supported by: Tavish Scott*

*S2M-1275 Patricia Ferguson on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: National Health Service Reform (Scotland) Bill - Business Motion-That the Parliament agrees that, during the Stage 3 proceedings of the National Health Service Reform (Scotland)  Bill, debate on each part of those proceedings shall be brought to a conclusion by the time-limits indicated (each time-limit being calculated from when Stage 3 begins and excluding any periods when other business is under consideration or when the meeting of the Parliament is suspended or otherwise not in progress):

Groups 1 and 2 - no later than 25 minutes

Groups 3 and 4 - no later than 1 hour

Groups 5, 6 and 7 - no later than 1 hour 50 minutes

Motion to pass the Bill - no later than 2 hours 30 minutes

Supported by: Tavish Scott*

*S2M-1274 Donald Gorrie: Big in Falkirk Arts Event-That the Parliament congratulates the town of Falkirk on the recent success of its Big in Falkirk street and community arts festival; acknowledges the hard work of all those people who contributed to the event, and thanks the various sponsors and artists who contributed to its success.

*S2M-1273 Mr Brian Monteith: Daily Newspapers-That the Parliament regrets the decision of the Royal Mail to cease carrying small circulation newspapers by air, in particular, removing from newsagents supplies of The Morning Star, Britain's sole international socialist daily newspaper, and the Daily Jang, an Urdu daily paper serving, amongst others, Scotland's Muslim population; regards a diverse range of newspapers as being healthy for democracy, and urges the Royal Mail to reconsider its decision.

*S2M-1272 Mr Brian Monteith: City of Stirling Ale-That the Parliament congratulates the Bridge of Allan Brewery on fighting off competition from 100 other United Kingdom breweries to secure Tesco's "Beer Challenge Winner" title and therefore securing a contract to supply all Tesco's UK stores with the speciality City of Stirling ale and believes a similar blind beer-tasting competition should be held for a Parliamentary ale that could be available in the new Parliament Building and on sale in the Parliament shop.

Supported by: Murdo Fraser*, Mr Ted Brocklebank*

*S2M-1271 Mr Brian Monteith: Kids' Lit Quiz-That the Parliament congratulates Wayne Mills from New Zealand for his efforts in making the Kids' Lit Quiz a global literary challenge event for school students by expanding from New Zealand to the UK in 2003; further congratulates the students, and supporting staff, at Dunblane High School on their tremendous achievement in coming first and second in the Scottish heat and going on to win the UK final, and wishes the team all the best for the international final in New Zealand in June this year.

*S2M-1270 Mary Scanlon: Autism Awareness Week 2004 - 17 to 24 May 2004-That the Parliament joins with The National Autistic Society Scotland, Partners in Autism and all other autism organisations in Scotland in marking Autism Awareness Week 2004; recognises the everyday challenges faced by children and adults with autistic spectrum disorders, their families and carers; further recognises the Scottish Executive's announcement of investment in services and support, focusing, in particular, on adults in the Glasgow and Edinburgh areas, and looks forward to an enhancement in services for children and adults affected by autistic spectrum disorders across Scotland.

Supported by: Murdo Fraser*

*S2M-1269 Ms Rosemary Byrne: Congratulations to Nicola Benedetti-That the Parliament congratulates Nicola Benedetti of West Kilbride in Ayrshire on her well-deserved achievement of becoming the BBC Young Musician of the Year; regards a good musical education as a fundamental right for all Scottish schoolchildren, and urges the Scottish Executive to ensure that sufficient funds are made available for all children in state schools who wish to learn to play a musical instrument free of charge.

*S2M-1268 Karen Whitefield: Airdrie United Football Club-That the Parliament congratulates the players, management, staff and supporters at Airdrie United on gaining promotion to the First Division only two years after the inception of the club and welcomes the club's commitment to working in partnership with the community by including a representative from the Supporters' Trust on the Board of Directors.

*S2M-1266.1 Alex Johnstone: Scotland's Beaches - A National Resource-As an amendment to motion (S2M-1266) in the name of Allan Wilson, insert at end "but notes the importance of continuing improvement in bathing water quality in Scotland to the quality of our beaches and believes that further progress is now dependent on addressing the limitations of Scottish Water and the planning system."

*S2M-1266.2 Mr Mark Ruskell: Scotland's Beaches - A National Resource-As an amendment to motion (S2M-1266) in the name of Allan Wilson, leave out from "recognises" to end and insert ", while welcoming the results of the recent Keep Scotland Beautiful survey of resort beaches with the granting of Seaside Awards to 40 Scottish beaches including 14 new recipients, acknowledges the impact of beach litter as highlighted in the recent Marine Conservation Society's Beachwatch 2003 Report; notes that the report's findings include a net increase in beach litter and that sewage-related debris accounts for a higher proportion of beach litter in Scotland than elsewhere in the United Kingdom; further notes that, while encouraging the public to take more responsibility for its own litter, the report also states that determined efforts are required to reduce pollution at source and that local authorities, water authorities and Her Majesty's Government must play their part in reducing and cleaning up litter; further notes that the environmental quality of beaches encompasses much more than litter alone, and calls upon the Scottish Executive to display a strong lead following the consultation on the best strategy for protecting and enhancing all of Scotland's coastline as pledged in A Partnership for a Better Scotland, including consideration of an integrated Marine Act."

*S2M-1266.3 Rob Gibson: Scotland's Beaches - A National Resource-As an amendment to motion (S2M-1266) in the name of Allan Wilson, insert at end "and calls on the Executive to step up pressure against illegal disposal of waste at sea including discharges by the nuclear industry."
