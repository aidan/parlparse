BUSINESS BULLETIN 54 / 2000

Thursday 23 March 2000

Section D –  Oral questions selected for answer on 30 March 2000

 

 

1. Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has received concerning the proposal to relocate The Royal Hospital for Sick Children, Yorkhill. (S1O-1452)

 

2. Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive how much it is currently spending to combat the effects of coastal erosion and how much it has spent in each of the last three years. (S1O-1469)

 

3. Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to make an announcement about supporter involvement in football clubs following the deadline for responses to the leaflet New Mutualism – A Golden Goal. (S1O-1460)

 

4. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to promote physical activity among young people in order to improve their general health. (S1O-1454)

 

5. Mr Keith Raffan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make a statement on the current status, funding and role of the Civic Forum and when it expects it to become fully operational. (S1O-1484)

 

6. Mr Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to exclude from the proposed extension to the right to buy those rural communities where social housing provision is already at a very low level. (S1O-1446)

 

7. Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it intends to make to the Strategic Rail Authority regarding electrification of railways in Scotland. (S1O-1466)

 

8. Bristow Muldoon: To ask the Scottish Executive what NHS per capita expenditure in Scotland will be compared to England and Wales and compared to the European Union average, following the Chancellor of the Exchequer’s Budget announcement. (S1O-1486)

 

9. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what assistance is given to people wishing to train as lip-reading tutors. (S1O-1462)

 

10. Mrs Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has for the future operation of the District Court service. (S1O-1448)

 

11. Ian Jenkins: To ask the Scottish Executive what detailed plans it has to improve the planning system to increase public participation and ensure decisions reflect the principles of sustainable development. (S1O-1491)

 

12. Mr John Swinney: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions there have been in its Cabinet regarding the implementation of the tax varying powers. (S1O-1475)

 

13. Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has made to Her Majesty’s Government on the need to conclude and agree the assisted area status map with the European Commission to allow financial assistance by public sector agencies to be extended beyond the minimum €100,000 over three years. (S1O-1456)

 

14. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it remains committed to progress on local government electoral reform following The Report of the Commission on Local Government and the Scottish Parliament and what timescale it envisages for such progress. (S1O-1485)

 

15. Mr John McAllion: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met Railtrack to discuss the completion of the electrification of the east coast main line. (S1O-1453)

 

16. David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many Scottish pupils have a school-provided e-mail address and what proportion of the primary and secondary school populations respectively these figures represent. R (S1O-1471)

 

17. Tricia Marwick: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has undertaken any assessment of the impact on social inclusion of the abolition of Mortgage Interest Relief at Source (MIRAS). (S1O-1479)

 

18. Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures have been considered in order to alleviate any problems of dairy farmers on Islay following the closure of the island’s creamery. (S1O-1473)

 

19. George Lyon: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to restructure the dairy industry on Islay following the closure of the Islay Creamery. (S1O-1474)

 

20. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact the Chancellor of the Exchequer’s budget announcement regarding the New Deal Programme will have on the number of long-term unemployed people in Scotland. (S1O-1482)

 

21. Mr John Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to remove or increase the ceiling on agri-environment schemes to bring Scotland into line with the rest of the United Kingdom. (S1O-1489)

 

22. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are being taken to assist local authorities and owner-occupiers to tackle disrepair of older tenement properties. (S1O-1458)

 

23. Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what financial assistance it makes available to help companies set up renewable energy schemes. (S1O-1451)

 

24. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will announce its decision on the future of Boharm School. (S1O-1447)

 

25. Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in tackling stalking. (S1O-1492)

 

26. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Minister for Health last met Greater Glasgow Health Board and what matters were discussed. (S1O-1463)

 

27. Ms Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will outline the discussions it has had with the City Of Edinburgh Council about the impact of the Holyrood Project on traffic and the source of funding for any road realignments required as a result of the project. (S1O-1490)

 

28. Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people are living in poverty in Scotland. (S1O-1468)

 

29. Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is committed to maintaining the existing bio-diversity within Scotland’s countryside. (S1O-1480)

 

30. Allan Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact the Chancellor of the Exchequer’s budget will have on employment, enterprise and economic growth in Scotland. (S1O-1455)
