Section A – Today’s Business

Meetings of Committees

All meetings take place in the Scottish Parliament, unless otherwise specified. Contact details for Committee Clerks are provided at the end of the Bulletin.

Equal Opportunities Committee
8th Meeting, 2006

The Committee will meet at 9.30 am in Committee Room 5

1. Disability inquiry: The Committee will take evidence from—

Patricia Ferguson MSP, Minister for Tourism, Culture and Sport

and then from—

Professor Nick Watson and Dr Hazel McFarlane, Strathclyde Centre for Disability Research, University of Glasgow

and then from—

Tom Berry, Disability Rights Commission;

Gerard Hastings, The Institute for Social Marketing, University of Stirling;

Christine Twine, Learning and Teaching Scotland; and

Linda Dunion, ‘see me’ Campaign.

Finance Committee
12th Meeting, 2006

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in Committee Room 1

1. Legal Profession and Legal Aid (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will take evidence on the Financial Memorandum from—

Chris Graham, Team Leader, Access to Advice; Louise Miller, Legal Profession and Legal Aid (Scotland) Bill Co-ordinator; Mike West, Team Leader, Legal Services Team; and Elaine Hamilton, Legal Services Team, Scottish Executive.

2. Tourist Boards (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will take evidence on the Financial Memorandum from—

John Brown, Head of Tourism Policy, Scottish Executive.

3. Accountability and Governance inquiry: The Committee will consider its informal seminar.

4. Items in private: The Committee will decide whether to consider its draft report on the Financial Memorandum of the Criminal Proceedings etc. (Reform) Bill and a paper from the Committee’s adviser on the review of Scottish Executive management of public finances in private at its next meeting.

5. Accountability and Governance inquiry (in private): The Committee will consider potential witnesses for its inquiry.

Procedures Committee
7th Meeting, 2006

The Committee will meet at 10.15 am in Committee Room 4

1. Declaration of interests: Robin Harper will be invited to declare any relevant interests.

2. Annual report: The Committee will consider a draft annual report for the Parliamentary year from 7 May 2005 to 6 May 2006.

3. Review of Parliamentary Time: The Committee will consider timescales for the completion of the inquiry.

4. Members’ Bills - member in charge: The Committee will consider whether Rule-changes are needed to limit the ability of MSPs who are members in charge of a Member’s Bill to participate as members of committees scrutinising the Bill.

5. Committee annual reports: The Committee will consider written evidence received and decide what further action to take.

6. Proposed Transport and Works Bill: The Committee will consider a draft letter in response to the Executive’s consultation.

7. Motions and decisions: The Committee will consider a draft letter to all MSPs seeking their views on various changes proposed.

Subordinate Legislation Committee
13th Meeting, 2006

The Committee will meet at 10.30 am in Committee Room 6

1. Delegated powers scrutiny: The Committee will consider the delegated powers provisions in the following bill—

Crofting Reform etc. Bill at Stage 1.

2. Executive response: The Committee will consider the response from the Executive to points raised on the following—

The Avian Influenza (H5N1 in Wild Birds) (Scotland) Order 2006, (SSI 2006/196).

3. Draft instruments subject to approval: The Committee will consider the following—

the Joint Inspections (Scotland) Amendment Regulations 2006, (SSI 2006/draft);

the Private Water Supplies (Notices) (Scotland) Regulations 2006, (SSI 2006/draft); and

the Public Appointments and Public Bodies etc. (Scotland) Act 2003 (Treatment of Office or Body as Specified Authority) Order 2006, (SSI 2006/draft).

4. Instruments subject to annulment: The Committee will consider the following—

the Private Water Supplies (Scotland) Regulations 2006, (SSI 2006/209); and

the Private Water Supplies (Grants) (Scotland) Regulations 2006, (SSI 2006/210).

5. Instruments not laid before the Parliament: The Committee will consider the following—

Act of Adjournal (Criminal Procedure Rules Amendment No. 2) (Financial Reporting Orders) 2006, (SSI 2006/205);

Act of Sederunt (Rules of the Court of Session Amendment No. 3) (Family Law (Scotland) Act 2006) 2006, (SSI 2006/206); and

Act of Sederunt (Ordinary Cause Rules) Amendment (Family Law (Scotland) Act 2006 etc.) 2006, (SSI 2006/207).

6. Conferring powers on the Scottish Parliament: The Committee will consider the following—

Interests of Members of the Scottish Parliament Bill at Stage 3.

7. Inquiry into the regulatory framework in Scotland (in private): The Committee will consider a draft report.

Standards and Public Appointments Committee
4th Meeting, 2006

The Committee will meet at 11.00 am in Committee Room 3

1. Review of the Code of Conduct for MSPs: The Committee will consider a paper on the structure of the Code and a paper on guidance for MSPs in dealing with unacceptable actions.

2. Annual Report: The Committee will consider a draft annual report.

Communities Committee
13th Meeting, 2006

The Committee will meet at 2.00 pm in Room UB.03

1. Planning etc. (Scotland) Bill (in private): The Committee will consider its draft report on the Bill at Stage 1.

Enterprise and Culture Committee
11th Meeting, 2006

The Committee will meet at 2.00 pm in Committee Room 6

1. Scottish Enterprise budget and restructuring plans: The Committee will take evidence on proposed budget and restructuring plans at Scottish Enterprise from—

Sir John Ward, Chairman, Jack Perry, Chief Executive, and Ian Carmichael, Director of Finance, Scottish Enterprise;

and then from—

Charlie Morrison, Chairman, and Fred Hallsworth, Deputy Chairman, Audit Committee, Scottish Enterprise.

2. Bankruptcy and Diligence etc. (Scotland) Bill: The adviser will brief the Committee at Stage 1 on part 13 (Debt Arrangements Scheme) and part 15 (Disclosure of Information) of the Bill.

3. Bankruptcy and Diligence etc. (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will take evidence at Stage 1 on part 13 (Debt Arrangements Scheme) and part 15 (Disclosure of Information) of the Bill from—

Susan McPhee, Head of Social Policy and Public Affairs, and Beccy Reilly, Money Advice Consultant, Citizens Advice Scotland, Hillary Wilson, Vice President of the Institute of Revenues (Scottish Association) and Head of Revenue and Housing Management, Midlothian Council, Yvonne Gallacher, Chief Executive, Money Advice Scotland, and Mr John Campbell, President, Society of Messengers-at-Arms and Sheriff Officers.

4. Bankruptcy and Diligence etc. (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will consider issues emerging from evidence taken so far.

5. National anthem: The Committee will consider a request by Michael Matheson that the Committee should discuss a national anthem for Scotland.

European and External Relations Committee
6th Meeting, 2006

The Committee will meet at 2.00 pm in Committee Room 3

1. Declaration of interests: The new member of the Committee will be invited to declare any relevant interests.

2. The Committee’s Work Programme 2006: The Committee will consider a briefing paper.

3. The European Commission’s Work Programme 2006 – Tracking issues identified by the Committee: The Committee will consider an update paper.

4. Pre- and post-EU Council scrutiny: The Committee will consider the agendas and information received from the Scottish Executive on the following meetings of the Council of the EU.

Forthcoming Councils

Agriculture and Fisheries Council, 25 April

Justice and Home Affairs Council, 27-28 April

ECOFIN Council, 5 May

Previous Councils

General Affairs and External Relations Council, 27 February

Environment Council, 9 March

Competitiveness (Internal Market, Industry and Research) Council, 13 March

ECOFIN Council, 14 March

Agriculture and Fisheries Council, 20 March

General Affairs and External Relations Council, 20 March

Transport, Telecommunications and Energy Council, 27 March

5. Sift of EC/EU documents and draft legislation: The Committee will consider the latest list of EC/EU documents and draft legislative proposals received for this meeting.

6. Convener’s Report: The Convener will update the Committee on the—

Letter from Tom McCabe MSP, Minister for Finance and Public Service Reform, regarding the Scottish Executive’s response to the European Commission’s ‘Plan D’;

Letter from Douglas Alexander MP, UK Minister for Europe, regarding the UK Government’s response to the European Commission’s ‘Plan D’;

Letter from Ross Finnie MSP, Minister for Environment & Rural Development, to Sarah Boyack MSP, Convener of the Environment and Rural Development Committee, regarding the use of Section 57(1) of the Scotland Act 1998 in relation to the Intellectual Property (Enforcement etc.,) Regulations 2006;

Letter from Ross Finnie MSP, Minister for Environment & Rural Development regarding the Greenhouse Gas Emissions Trading Scheme (Amendment) Regulations 2006;

Response from the Scottish Executive regarding points raised under Pre- and post-EU Council scrutiny;

Letter from Tom McCabe MSP regarding an updated Scottish Executive response to the recommendations in the Committee’s report on the G8 Summit and the UK Presidency of the European Union; and

Civicus World Assembly, Glasgow, 21-25 June 2006.
