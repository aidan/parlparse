Section E – Written questions lodged on 15 March 2005

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-15258 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-33414 by Mr Jim Wallace on 5 February 2003, how many, and what percentage of, persons convicted of drug offences were convicted for (a) supplying and trafficking and (b) possession of (i) cannabis, (ii) cocaine, (iii) ecstasy, (iv) heroin, (v) amphetamines and (vi) other illegal drugs in each of the last five years, broken down by police force area.

S2W-15261 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied that information on the condition of dams and reservoirs is easily accessible to the public, local planners and other interested parties.

S2W-15262 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive to what extent information and assessments on the flood risk relating to each dam and reservoir are publicly available and where such information can be obtained.

S2W-15263 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive which public authorities are responsible for carrying out safety inspections of dams and reservoirs; what the scope is of such inspections, and where the relevant reports can be obtained.

S2W-15264 Colin Fox: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S20-5331 by Cathy Jamieson on 10 February 2005, whether it will support Miscarriages of Justice Organisation (Scotland) (MOJO) in its attempt to establish a counselling scheme for those released from prison after being found to be the victim of a miscarriage of justice.

S2W-15265 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive when guidance on infection control standards in adult care homes will be published.

S2W-15266 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) adults and (b) children have been registered with a dentist in each year since 1985.

S2W-15267 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive how many health inspection nurses have been employed in each of the last five years.

S2W-15268 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive how much gross and net revenue has been raised in each of the last three years from toll charges on the (a) Forth, (b) Tay and (c) Erskine road bridges.

S2W-15269 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive what the actual spending per head is in (a) nursery, (b) primary and (c) secondary schools, (d) further education colleges and (e) universities, shown also as a percentage of the OECD average.

S2W-15270 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the industrial action being taken by Careers Scotland with effect from 14 March 2005.

S2W-15271 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive how many households have received a (a) 25% council tax rebate, (b) 50% council tax rebate or (c) qualified for 100% council tax benefit in each of the last five financial years, broken down by local authority.

S2W-15272 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive what the cost of funding council tax rebates was in each of the last five financial years and is in the current financial year.

S2W-15273 Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive what studies it has completed, is currently undertaking or is planning to undertake relating to the number of people arrested who test positively for alcohol.

S2W-15274 Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people arrested have tested positively for alcohol since 1997, broken down by police force area.

S2W-15275 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps have been taken to assess and estimate the future impact of climate change on each dam and reservoir; whether such information is available to the public and, if so, how it can be obtained.

S2W-15276 Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of (a) offenders committing and (b) victims of violent offences have tested positively for alcohol when the offence was committed since 1997, broken down by police force area.

S2W-15277 Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will report on the review of the children’s hearings system.

S2W-15278 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) police officers and (b) other public sector workers contracted (i) HIV, (ii) hepatitis B or (iii) hepatitis C following exposure to the bodily fluids of another person as a result of that person committing or allegedly committing an offence in the last year.

S2W-15279 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what expert opinions it has relied on to inform its position that wind power is significantly effective in reducing CO2 emissions.

S2W-15280 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Minister for Transport will respond to my letter of 5 January 2005, acknowledged on 11 January, regarding the future of the Clyde and Hebridean ferry services.
