Business Bulletin No. 115 / 2004

Friday 2nd July 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-1560 Chris Ballance: Future of Chapelcross-That the Parliament regards the closure of Chapelcross nuclear power station as a significant milestone along the road to developing nuclear-free sources of energy and employment longer term in the south of Scotland; notes that over the next few years there will be more jobs created in decommissioning the plant than there would be in operating a replacement nuclear power station, and encourages  the Corridor Regeneration Strategy Steering Group to convert the site into a centre for renewable energy as part of Scotland's long-term renewable energy future.

*S2M-1558 Tommy Sheridan: No Tour Charges in the People's Parliament-That the Parliament condemns any attempt to charge the people of Scotland to go on tours of their own new Parliament building, the people having previously paid for it with over £430 million of public money; views any such proposal as being in direct contradiction to the Parliament's founding principles of access and participation and opposes any attempt to impose such a charge; believes that the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body (SPCB), in agreeing to recommend the imposition of such charges, has once again demonstrated its complete inability to take into account the views of the ordinary people of Scotland, and calls on the SPCB to be expanded to include elected representatives from civic Scotland to ensure that such views are at the heart of future decision-making on the governance of the people's parliament.

Supported by: Ms Rosemary Byrne*

S2M-1556 Rhona Brankin: Physiotherapy Week - 5 to 9 July 2004-That the Parliament welcomes the Chartered Society of Physiotherapy's Physiotherapy Week from 5 to 9 July 2004; notes that 850,000 working days were lost due to musculoskeletal disorders last year in Scotland; recognises the value of workplace physiotherapy in the fight against work-related ill health, and agrees with the Chartered Society of Physiotherapy that prevention is better than cure.

Supported by: Linda Fabiani, Mike Pringle, Brian Adam, Sarah Boyack, Janis Hughes, Richard Lochhead, Murdo Fraser, Richard Baker, Dr Sylvia Jackson, Eleanor Scott, Pauline McNeill, Mrs Margaret Ewing, Rob Gibson, Mr Kenny MacAskill, Bruce Crawford, Bill Butler, Dr Elaine Murray, Tommy Sheridan, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Colin Fox*, Karen Whitefield*, Robert Brown*, Margaret Jamieson*, Christine May*, Stewart Stevenson*, Christine Grahame*, Margaret Mitchell*, Elaine Smith*, Robin Harper*, Tricia Marwick*, Margo MacDonald*, Cathie Craigie*, Ms Sandra White*

S2M-1555 Mr Stewart Maxwell: New York Introduces Self-Extinguishing Cigarettes-That the Parliament congratulates New York State on the ban of sales of non-self-extinguishing cigarettes within New York State; appreciates that this measure will help in saving the lives of some of the 900 Americans who die each year by fires started by cigarettes left unattended, and, given that smoking-related fires are the most common reason for preventable deaths reported by the Fire Service in Scotland and that under the Scotland Act 1998 this issue is reserved to Westminster, urges the Scottish Executive to make representations to Her Majesty's Government to introduce similar legislation in Scotland.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson*, Mr Kenny MacAskill*, Jim Mather*, Brian Adam*, Christine Grahame*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Richard Lochhead*, Ms Sandra White*, Tommy Sheridan*

S2M-1554 Robin Harper: Protection of Scotland's Coasts-That the Parliament notes the first anniversary of the sinking of the MV Jambo in the Minch; regrets the failure to protect one of Scotland's most important and fragile marine areas and regards the overall protection of Scotland's coasts as inadequate; calls on the Scottish Executive to introduce further measures to ensure the conservation and protection of Scotland's marine environment and that all the recommendations of the Donaldson report which followed the Braer disaster are fully implemented, and calls on the Scottish Executive to give further consideration to the appointment of a dedicated minister for the marine environment to deliver coherent government action on the seas.

Supported by: Mark Ballard, Eleanor Scott, Campbell Martin, Rob Gibson, Ms Rosemary Byrne, John Farquhar Munro, Chris Ballance, Richard Lochhead, Tommy Sheridan, Rosie Kane*

S2M-1553 Carolyn Leckie: Iraq Remains Occupied-That the Parliament regards the alleged handover of sovereignty from coalition occupying forces in Iraq as completely fictitious; views the new Iraqi government as an unelected puppet of the US regime, its sovereignty guaranteed by an army of 160,000 foreign fighters and 3,000 US diplomats; notes that the Iraqi Prime Minister is a former CIA agent; notes that the new US embassy in Baghdad will be the largest in the world and bigger than the Iraqi government headquarters; notes that the US State Department has taken $184m earmarked for drinking water projects and moved it to the budget for the embassy; notes that the United States will continue to control all aspects of Iraqi life through "inspectors" appointed by the US; is outraged that 80,000 Western private mercenaries operating on behalf of Western companies in Iraq have been given immunity from prosecution for any crimes that they may commit including murder; notes that the US will continue to detain some 5,000 Iraqis without trial, and reaffirms its call for the immediate withdrawal of all coalition troops from Iraq and the ending of the privatisation of Iraq's infrastructure.

Supported by: Colin Fox, Rosie Kane, Tommy Sheridan, Ms Rosemary Byrne

S2M-1552 Tommy Sheridan: Condolences to the Family of Gordon Gentle-That the Parliament expresses its deepest condolences to the family of Gordon Gentle who was tragically killed in Iraq on 28 June 2004 at the age of only 19, and conveys our thoughts, love and solidarity to Rose and George, his mother and father, and Pamela and Maxine, his two sisters.

S2M-1545 David Mundell: Cessation of Power Generation at Chapelcross - The World's Oldest Operational Nuclear Power Station-That the Parliament notes with regret the formal announcement that the Chapelcross Power Station, the longest-serving nuclear power station in the world, has ceased power generation, having provided a safe and reliable supply of electricity to the south of Scotland since 1959; believes that the engineering and scientific accomplishments of designing, building and operating Chapelcross and Calder Hall in Cumbria were the 20th century equivalent of the Forth Rail Bridge or the invention of the steam engine and congratulates the management and workforce on their skill and dedication in operating successfully the Calder Hall and Chapelcross power stations for a combined total of more than 90 years; further notes the fudge and delay inherent in Her Majesty's Government's energy policy; expresses concern at the prospects of a looming "energy gap" in the United Kingdom that cannot realistically be filled by renewable sources, and believes that the success of Chapelcross would be best recognised by the early commissioning of a replacement nuclear power facility on the same site.

Supported by: Mary Scanlon, Mr Ted Brocklebank, Alex Johnstone, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton, Alex Fergusson, John Scott, Murdo Fraser, Mr David Davidson, Mrs Nanette Milne, Murray Tosh, Mr Brian Monteith, Phil Gallie*

S2M-1544 Richard Baker: International Youth Parliament-That the Parliament congratulates Matthew Middler and Fiona Smith of Aberdeen on being selected to represent Scotland at an international youth parliament event in Sydney organised by Oxfam International; welcomes the fact that Fiona Smith's attendance at the event is being supported by UNISON, and notes that the event will discuss key international issues including poverty, social justice, and the environment.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan
