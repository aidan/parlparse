Business Bulletin No. 98/2010: Thursday 10 June 2010
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 9 June 2010

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-34272 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what accreditations or professional memberships heating engineers must have in order to install boilers under its boiler scrappage scheme.

S3W-34273 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what protections are in place for successful applicants to the boiler scrappage scheme if their engineer does not have the required accreditation or professional memberships to install the system under its rules.

S3W-34274 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there is a time limit on the installation of a new boiler under its boiler scrappage scheme.

S3W-34275 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that the operation of its boiler scrappage scheme favours large engineering companies over small, independent engineers.

S3W-34276 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to encourage primary school-age children to walk or cycle to school.

S3W-34277 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many miles it expects primary school children to walk to school before being offered transport.

S3W-34278 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to increase the number of schoolchildren who travel to school by school bus in order to reduce the number travelling by car.

S3W-34279 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive how much was spent on encouraging primary school children to walk or cycle to school in (a) 2007-08, (b) 2008-09 and (c) 2009-10.

S3W-34280 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive how much was spent on primary school bus transport in each local authority area in (a) 2007-08, (b) 2008-09 and (c) 2009-10.

S3W-34281 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many primary school children travelled to school by school bus in each local authority area in each of the last three years.

S3W-34282 Hugh O’Donnell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many murders have been recorded in Strathclyde in each six-month period in each year since 2007, broken down by police division.

S3W-34283 Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many patients had organs accidentally punctured or cut during surgery in 2009, broken down by NHS board.

S3W-34284 Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many patients had a foreign body accidentally left in a body cavity during a surgical procedure in 2009, broken down by (a) NHS board and (b) type of foreign body.

S3W-34285 Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many patients underwent an inappropriate operation in 2009, broken down by NHS board.

S3W-34286 Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many surgical errors occurred in 2009, broken down by (a) NHS board and (b) type of error.

S3W-34287 John Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many properties are currently subject to secondary stock transfer.

S3W-34288 John Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many social housing units are (a) scheduled to be demolished and (b) available for social rent.

S3W-34289 John Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many secondary housing stock transfers have been subject to ballots since 2006.

S3W-34290 John Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the result has been of each secondary housing stock transfer ballot, including turnout figures, since 2006.

S3W-34291 John Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what registered social landlords were involved in each secondary housing stock transfer since 2006.

S3W-34292 John Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many properties were transferred in each secondary stock transfer since 2006.

S3W-34293 John Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many secondary housing stock transfers have taken place since 2006 that were not subject to ballot.

S3W-34294 John Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the value was of each secondary stock transfer bid that has gone to ballot since 2006.

S3W-34295 John Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive how much funding it has provided to assist in the consultant process relating to proposed secondary stock transfer ballots in each year since 2006.

S3W-34296 John Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to monitor the transfer of assets from local authorities to local communities under the auspices of the Development Trusts Association Scotland.

S3W-34297 John Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it plans to give to the Development Trusts Association Scotland in Central Scotland.

S3W-34298 John Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the budget of the Development Trusts Association Scotland was in (a) 2008-09 and (b) 2009-10

S3W-34299 John Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what projects the Development Trusts Association Scotland is undertaking, broken down by location.

S3W-34300 John Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the membership is of the Development Trusts Association Scotland.

S3W-34301 Stuart McMillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what assistance it provides to local authorities in the West of Scotland for befriending services for (a) adults and (b) young people.

S3W-34302 Stuart McMillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it offers to voluntary organisations and services in the West of Scotland to expand their remit.

S3W-34303 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive on what date it expects to publish the annual accounts of the Scottish Futures Trust for 2009-10.

S3W-34304 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive on what date ministers received a copy of the annual accounts of the Scottish Futures Trust for 2009-10.

S3W-34305 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive how many members of staff at (a) Scottish Enterprise, (b) Scottish Futures Trust and (c) Skills Development Scotland were awarded a car allowance in 2009-10.

S3W-34306 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive how much (a) Scottish Enterprise, (b) Scottish Futures Trust and (c) Skills Development Scotland spent on car allowances in 2009-10.

S3W-34307 John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to support children attending Junior Academy at the Royal Scottish Academy of Music and Drama, which recently had local authority funding withdrawn.

S3W-34308 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on whether the Public Interest Disclosure Act (1998) adequately protects individuals who make disclosures of information in the public interest.

S3W-34309 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether guidance is offered to individuals who make disclosures of information in the public interest regarding the NHS and, if so, where this information can be found.

S3W-34310 John Park: To ask the Scottish Executive how many livestock farms are inspected each year by Animal Health as per the Welfare of Farmed Animals (Scotland) Regulations 2000.

S3W-34311 John Park: To ask the Scottish Executive how many incidents of non-compliance with the Welfare of Farmed Animals (Scotland) Regulations 2000 come to the attention of Animal Health each year.

S3W-34312 John Park: To ask the Scottish Executive how many incidents of non-compliance with the Welfare of Farmed Animals (Scotland) Regulations 2000 were investigated by Animal Health between 2006 and 2009.

S3W-34313 John Park: To ask the Scottish Executive with what frequency incidents of non-compliance with the Welfare of Farmed Animals (Scotland) Regulations 2000 occurred between 2006 and 2009.

S3W-34314 John Park: To ask the Scottish Executive what the nature was of incidents of non-compliance with the Welfare of Farmed Animals (Scotland) Regulations 2000 between 2006 and 2009.

S3W-34315 John Park: To ask the Scottish Executive whether a record is kept of (a) farmers who lose animals through non-compliance with the Welfare of Farmed Animals (Scotland) Regulations 2000 and (b) the reasons for such non-compliance.

S3W-34316 John Park: To ask the Scottish Executive on what dates (a) special advice from advisory expert groups and (b) the Severe Weather Grant were made available to farmers during the severe weather over the 2009-10 winter period.

S3W-34317 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive under what circumstances a public local inquiry would have to be held before a decision could be taken on an application for a development that has national development status under the National Planning Framework for Scotland 2.

S3W-34318 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what evidence it used in determining that there was a national need for new baseload generating capacity at existing power station sites when identifying national developments for inclusion in the National Planning Framework for Scotland 2.

S3W-34319 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what evidence it used in determining that there was a national need for new baseload generating capacity at Hunterston in addition to that which would be provided at other power station sites when identifying national developments for inclusion in the National Planning Framework for Scotland 2.

S3W-34320 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive whether objections to the proposed coal-fired station at Hunterston received prior to the opening of the public consultation will be taken into account on the same basis as those received after the consultation formally began.

S3W-34321 Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive when the new carers and young carers strategies will be published.

S3W-34322 Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive how the planned new carers and young carers strategies will support the sustainability of carers organisations.

S3W-34323 Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will recognise the contribution of unpaid carers and young carers during Carers Week and beyond.

S3W-34324 Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will acknowledge the work of carers organisations and young carers projects and ensure the sustainability of the support that these organisations offer to unpaid carers.

S3W-34325 Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive how the new carers and young carers strategies will address the funding challenges faced by carers and young carers projects and organisations across the country.

S3W-34326 Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether Carers’ Information Strategy funding will continue in 2011-12.

S3W-34327 Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will ensure that local authorities (a) take on board and (b) implement the new carers and young carers strategies.

S3W-34328 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive when it announced funding for a new Dalkeith Medical Centre.

S3W-34329 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether construction work on the new Dalkeith Medical Centre has started and when it will open for patients.

S3W-34330 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what broadband connection speeds it considers are a satisfactory minimum for rural communities.

S3W-34331 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that a 512kb broadband connection speed is appropriate for (a) households and (b) businesses.

S3W-34332 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are plans to enable full platform ADSL for the Butterstone telephone exchange in Perth and Kinross.

S3W-34333 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what assistance is available to rural households to be broadband-enabled.
