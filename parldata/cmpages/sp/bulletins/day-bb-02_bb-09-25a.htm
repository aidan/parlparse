Business Bulletin No. 136/2002

Wednesday 25 September 2002

Section A : Daily Business List
  

Meetings of Committees

All meetings take place in Committee Chambers, Edinburgh unless otherwise indicated.

Transport and the Environment Committee

26th Meeting, 2002

The Committee will meet at 9.15 am in Committee Room 2

1. Water Environment and Water Services (Scotland) Bill (in private): The Committee will consider possible lines of questioning for witnesses.

2. Water Environment and Water Services (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will take evidence at stage 1 on the general principles of the Water Environment and Water Services Bill from—

Willie Halcrow, Director of Operations, Scottish Environment Protection Agency

Martin Marsden, Water Unit Manager, Scottish Environment Protection Agency

Ross Finnie MSP, Minister for Environment and Rural Development.

3. Subordinate Legislation: The Committee will consider the following negative instruments-

The Road Traffic ( Permitted Parking Area and Special Parking Area) (Perth and Kinross Council) Designation Order 2002, (SSI 2002/398)

The Parking Attendants (Wearing of Uniforms) (Perth and Kinross Council Parking Area) Regulations 2002, (SSI 2002/399)

The Road Traffic (Parking Adjudicators) (Perth and Kinross Council) Regulations 2002, (SSI 2002/400).

4. Highlands and Islands Ferry Services: The Committee will consider a reporters’ paper on the draft service specification for Highlands and Islands ferry services.

5. Water Environment and Water Services (Scotland) Bill (in private): The Committee will consider the evidence it has taken.

6. Building (Scotland) Bill (in private): The Committee will consider a paper on the arrangements for its consideration of the bill.

Health and Community Care Committee

23rd Meeting, 2002

The Committee will meet at 9.30 am in Committee Room 1

1. Mental Health (Scotland) Bill (in private): The Committee will receive a briefing from the Committee adviser on the bill.

2. Mental Health (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will take evidence at Stage 1 from—

Dr Jim Dyer and Dr Juliet Cheetham, Mental Welfare Commission

Bruce Millan, former Chair, Millan Committee and Jim Kiddie, Millan Committee member

Dr Denise Coia, Chair, Royal College of Psychiatrists.

Justice 2 Committee

32nd Meeting, 2002

The Committee will meet at 9.45 am in the Chamber, Assembly Hall, The Mound, Edinburgh

1. Land Reform (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will consider the Bill at Stage 2 (Day 6).

Standards Committee

13th Meeting, 2002

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in Committee Room 3

1. Cross-Party Groups: The Committee will consider a proposal to establish a Cross-Party Group on Kidney Disease.

2. Conference: The Committee will give further consideration to an invitation to a conference on lobbying in Budapest.

3. Lobbying: The Committee will consider a draft motion.

4. Alleged Unauthorised Disclosure of Draft Committee Report: The Committee will consider correspondence from Pauline McNeill MSP, Convener of the Justice 2 Committee, concerning an alleged unauthorised disclosure of the Justice 2 Committee’s draft report on the Criminal Justice (Scotland) Bill.

5. Replacing the Members’ Interest Order: The Committee will consider a paper on paid advocacy and a draft motion in support of its proposal for a Committee Bill.

Meeting of the Parliament

2:30 pm Time for Reflection – Alison Twaddle, General Secretary, Church of Scotland Guild

followed by Parliamentary Bureau Motions

followed by Education, Culture and Sport Committee Debate on the Committee's Report on proposed Commissioner for Children and Young People Bill—

S1M-3322 Karen Gillon on behalf of the Education, Culture and Sport Committee: Commissioner for Children and Young People Bill—That the Parliament agrees to the proposal for a Committee Bill under Rule 9.15 contained in the 11th Report 2002 of the Education, Culture and Sport Committee, Report on Proposed Commissioner for Children and Young People Bill (SP Paper 617).

followed by Parliamentary Bureau Motions

5:00 pm Decision Time

followed by Members’ Business

debate on the subject of—

S1M-3357 Mary Scanlon: Importance of Primary Care—That the Parliament recognises the importance of primary care in managing 90% of patient contact with the NHS in Scotland; appreciates the vital role GPs play in providing primary care services as part of the wider primary care team; supports the statement that "if it can be done in primary care, it should be done in primary care", and acknowledges the urgent need for reform and support to invigorate staffing levels, facilities, IT provision and morale for this to become a reality.
