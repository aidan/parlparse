Business Bulletin No. 82/2011: Monday 27 June 2011
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 24 June 2011

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S4W-00950 Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the percentage change in emergency admissions has been for the NHS in each year since 2008-09, also broken down by (a) NHS board, (b) community health partnership, (c) GP practice and (d) per 100 patients identified by Scottish Patients at Risk of Readmission or Admission (SPARRA).

S4W-00951 Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure that every patient identified by Scottish Patients at Risk of Readmission or Admission (SPARRA) with more than two unplanned admissions receives a (a) personalised health and social care programme and (b) structured discharge plan.

S4W-00952 Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to maintain its commitment to keep the level of delayed discharges at zero.

S4W-00957 Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether Social Care and Social Work Improvement Scotland (SCSWIS) measures the rates of unplanned admissions in its criteria for good care in care homes.

S4W-00960 Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to monitor (a) the uniformity of approach by NHS boards to the new exceptional needs system and (b) whether the scheme’s processes are (i) transparent and (ii) acceptable to applicants and clinicians.

S4W-00962 Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria are used by each NHS board when considering whether to accept clinical recommendations to fund the use of Eculizumab in the treatment of paroxysmal nocturnal haemoglobinuria.

S4W-00963 Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason treatments recommended by the centrally funded UK-designated specialist service outreach clinic at Monklands Hospital for the treatment of paroxysmal nocturnal haemoglobinuria are not funded nationally.

S4W-01190 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many specialist asthma nurses are employed by each NHS board.

S4W-01191 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how regularly an asthma patient has their condition reviewed.

S4W-01192 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what must be included in an asthma review in order for a GP to be eligible for the Quality and Outcomes Framework payment.

S4W-01193 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what categories of patients are eligible for the Minor Ailments Service.

S4W-01194 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what changes are proposed to the eligibility criteria for the Minor Ailments Service to ensure that all those who will benefit can access the service.

S4W-01195 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will review the £250 annual payment to ambulance staff for opting out of paid meal breaks.

S4W-01205 Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive what the role is of the Scottish Fuel Poverty Forum.

S4W-01206 Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive when will it appoint a chair to the Scottish Fuel Poverty Forum.

S4W-01207 Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it remains its target to eradicate fuel poverty by 2016.

S4W-01208 Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to eradicate fuel poverty.

S4W-01209 Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will publish an action plan on how it will meet its target of eradicating fuel poverty by 2016.

S4W-01210 Margaret McDougall: To ask the Scottish Executive what initiatives it, or any of its agencies, has undertaken to tackle social deprivation in the west of Scotland since 2007.

S4W-01211 Margaret McDougall: To ask the Scottish Executive what initiatives it, or any of its agencies, has undertaken to tackle health inequalities in the west of Scotland since 2007.

S4W-01212 Margaret McDougall: To ask the Scottish Executive how it takes (a) social deprivation and (b) unemployment into account when allocating funding to NHS boards.

S4W-01214 Margaret McDougall: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average life expectancy is for (a) men and (b) women in the west of Scotland, broken down by Scottish Index of Multiple Deprivation data zone.

S4W-01216 Margaret McDougall: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures NHS (a) Ayrshire and Arran and (b) Greater Glasgow and Clyde are taking to promote preventative healthcare.

S4W-01217 Margaret McDougall: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures the NHS is taking to tackle the effects of unemployment in (a) health and (b) mental health services.

S4W-01218 Margaret McDougall: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to review NHS Ayrshire and Arran’s eligibility for island adjustment funding, as stipulated by the NHSScotland Resource Allocation Committee formula.

S4W-01219 Margaret McDougall: To ask the Scottish Executive how many frontline staff have left NHS Ayrshire and Arran through (a) redundancy, (b) ending of temporary contracts and (c) retirement.

S4W-01220 Margaret McDougall: To ask the Scottish Executive how many frontline staff have left NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde through (a) redundancy, (b) ending of temporary contracts and (c) retirement.

S4W-01221 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what its timetable is for introducing the warm homes fund.

S4W-01222 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive how the warm homes fund will operate.

S4W-01223 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive how the warm homes fund will be targeted to benefit the people most affected by fuel poverty.

S4W-01224 Willie Coffey: To ask the Scottish Executive how many homes in each local authority area are served by water supply systems containing lead pipes.

S4W-01225 Willie Coffey: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure the removal of lead piping from domestic water supply systems.

S4W-01226 Willie Coffey: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is one of its policy objectives to have lead piping removed from domestic water supply systems.

S4W-01227 Willie Coffey: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance it has issued to (a) Scottish Water and (b) local authorities regarding the removal of lead piping from domestic water supply systems.

S4W-01228 Willie Coffey: To ask the Scottish Executive how many local authorities operate grant schemes to assist owner-occupiers to remove lead pipes from their homes.

S4W-01229 Willie Coffey: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent discussions it has had with the UK Government regarding the availability of social tariffs from energy providers.

S4W-01230 Willie Coffey: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the extent to which existing social tariffs from energy companies protect consumers from fuel poverty.

S4W-01231 Willie Coffey: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has asked the UK Government to make changes to the social tariffs provided by UK energy providers and, if so, what changes.

S4W-01232 Willie Coffey: To ask the Scottish Executive how much funding it has provided to (a) sportscotland and (b) sport governing bodies in each year since 1999.

S4W-01233 Willie Coffey: To ask the Scottish Executive how much of the funding provided to (a) sportscotland and (b) sport governing bodies in each year since 1999 has been spent on (i) administration, (ii) capital investment, (iii) support for local sports development and (iv) support for individual competitors.

S4W-01234 Willie Coffey: To ask the Scottish Executive how much of the funding provided to (a) sportscotland and (b) sport governing bodies in each year since 1999 was awarded (i) without competition and (ii) following a competitive bid process.

S4W-01235 Willie Coffey: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures of effectiveness it applies to funding awarded to (a) sportscotland and (b) sport governing bodies.

S4W-01236 Willie Coffey: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on whether the funding provided to (a) sportscotland and (b) sport governing bodies helps to improve the results obtained in sporting competitions.

S4W-01237 Willie Coffey: To ask the Scottish Executive how many of the (a) chief executives, (b) senior staff and (c) board members of (i) sportscotland and (ii) sport governing bodies that receive Scottish Government funding receive a salary of over £100,000 per annum for those roles.

S4W-01238 Willie Coffey: To ask the Scottish Executive what financial assistance it or its agencies provided to sporting bodies in Kilmarnock and Irvine Valley in (a) 2008-09, (b) 2009-10 and (c) 2010-11.

S4W-01240 Kezia Dugdale: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide details of all meetings it has had with the Child Poverty Unit since 2009.

S4W-01241 Kezia Dugdale: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide details of all meetings it has had with the Four Nations Forum on Child Poverty since 2009.

S4W-01243 Kezia Dugdale: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide details of the steps that it has taken to raise awareness of childcare vouchers among private sector employers since August 2009.

S4W-01244 Kezia Dugdale: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide details of the steps that it has taken to raise awareness of childcare vouchers among voluntary sector employers since August 2009.

S4W-01245 Kezia Dugdale: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide the results of any assessment undertaken of the provision of childcare vouchers since August 2009.
