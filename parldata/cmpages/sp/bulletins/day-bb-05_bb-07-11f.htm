Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

* S2M-3089 Chris Ballance: 20th Anniversary of the Sinking of the Rainbow Warrior—That the Parliament commemorates the 20th anniversary of the sinking of the Rainbow Warrior on 10 July 1985 by the French secret service, causing the death of Greenpeace photographer, Fernando Pereira; hails the continuing voyages of Rainbow Warrior II to protect the Pacific Ocean; remembers that the first Rainbow Warrior was on its way to campaign against testing of nuclear weapons in the South Pacific when it was sunk; notes with grave concern the proliferation of nuclear weapon states in the last 20 years, and calls on the UK Government to meet its obligation as a signatory to Article 6 of the Nuclear Non-Proliferation Treaty by abandoning plans to replace the Trident nuclear arsenal.

Supported by: Robin Harper*

* S2M-3088 Chris Ballance: Nuclear Non-Proliferation Treaty—That the Parliament welcomes the opening statement from the UK delegation to the Non-Proliferation Treaty Review Conference at the United Nations on 5 May 2005 of “our unequivocal undertaking to accomplish the elimination of nuclear arsenals”; recognises that such a worthy commitment is incompatible with plans to build a successor to the existing Trident nuclear missile system, and calls on the UK Government to abandon any such plans.

Supported by: Shiona Baird*, Richard Lochhead*, Patrick Harvie*, Mr Mark Ruskell*, Robin Harper*, Alex Neil*, Eleanor Scott*, Mark Ballard*

* S2M-3087 Mr Jamie McGrigor: Well Done to London—That the Parliament congratulates London on winning the right to host the 2012 Olympic Games; notes the hard work and dedication of all those involved in the successful bid, in particular of bid-leader Lord Coe whose determination and tenacity has been so important in winning crucial votes; further notes that Scotland could provide ideal venues for a number of sports, such as Fort William where the World Mountain Biking Championships are to be held, and hopes that Scotland will have the opportunity to play its part in welcoming the world to the United Kingdom and the Games of the XXX Olympiad.

Supported by: Michael McMahon*, Murdo Fraser*, Mr Ted Brocklebank*, John Scott*, Mrs Nanette Milne*, Mr David Davidson*, Nora Radcliffe*, Phil Gallie*, David McLetchie*, Margaret Mitchell*, Dennis Canavan*, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton*, Mary Scanlon*, Dr Jean Turner*

* S2M-3086 Shiona Baird: Support for Renewable Energy—That the Parliament welcomes the current review of the Renewables Obligation (Scotland) Order; notes that, whilst the technology-neutral nature of the ROCs system has encouraged the development of onshore wind power, it is not providing adequate support to emerging technologies such as biomass, offshore wind, wave and tidal power; acknowledges that the renewables industry has been calling for increased support for these sectors for several years; feels that the Scottish Executive has yet to grasp the full potential of this industry, estimated by the World Energy Council to be worth £500 billion for wave energy alone; regrets that Ocean Power Delivery’s first commercial contract was in Portugal and not Scotland, and calls on the Executive to provide increased support for emerging renewable technologies as a matter of urgency.

* S2M-3085 Mark Ballard: Protests, Policing and Public Safety—That the Parliament notes the eyewitness reports of the protest in Edinburgh on 4 July 2005; is concerned that groups of local youths drinking alcohol were allowed to walk into the areas where there were disturbances and that innocent bystanders were also able to wander unknowingly into the protests and were then contained by police officers and not allowed to move away from the unrest; is further concerned that there was a lack of clear communication by the police, resulting in confusion and panic among protesters and the public; recognises the difficult situation faced by police officers on the ground; questions, however, the Justice Minister’s description of police tactics as prioritising the maintenance of public safety and public order, and appeals for calm from both police and protesters ahead of the organised peaceful march planned near Gleneagles Hotel on 6 July.

* S2M-3084 Mr Kenny MacAskill: Peaceful Protest—That the Parliament congratulates the organisers and support staff of the Make Poverty History march on 2 July 2005 for organising and hosting the event; thanks the police for doing an exemplary job and ensuring that the day passed without serious incident, and further thanks the estimated 225,000 participants for coming to Edinburgh to march together in peaceful protest against the scourge of world poverty.

Supported by: Fiona Hyslop*, Michael McMahon*, Linda Fabiani*, Rob Gibson*, Susan Deacon*, Christine Grahame*, Mrs Margaret Ewing*, Nora Radcliffe*, Jim Mather*, Mike Pringle*, Des McNulty*, Tricia Marwick*, Jackie Baillie*, Chris Ballance*, Cathie Craigie*, Bruce Crawford*, Mr David Davidson*, Stewart Stevenson*, Dennis Canavan*, Mr John Swinney*, Brian Adam*, Richard Lochhead*, Shona Robison*, Alex Neil*, Shiona Baird*, Robin Harper*, Dr Jean Turner*, Eleanor Scott*
