Business Bulletin No. 56/2011: Wednesday 18 May 2011
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 17 May 2011

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S4W-00047 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria are used when assessing planning applications for businesses to which the access roads are also junctions from trunk roads.

S4W-00048 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive where it will set up the national lending unit proposed in the Housing (Scotland) Act 2006.

S4W-00049 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive how the contracts with (a) surveyors, (b) installers and (c) suppliers providing services under its Energy Assistance Package are allocated.

S4W-00050 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive how many businesses in Dumfries and Galloway are contracted to (a) survey properties, (b) install insulation and central heating systems and (c) supply materials under its Energy Assistance Package.

S4W-00051 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how it ensures that the NHS and local authorities provide a co-ordinated service when dealing with mental health patients.

S4W-00052 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how it ensures that mental health patients receive personalised care packages.

S4W-00053 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many mental health patients have received self-directed support for community care in each of the last five years, also expressed as a percentage of all recipients of self-directed support.

S4W-00054 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how GPs are made aware of guided self-help services and support groups for patients with mental health issues.

S4W-00055 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how it creates and makes accessible exercise referral schemes for people with mental health issues.

S4W-00056 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what it is doing to reduce sickness absence from work on the grounds of poor mental health and how it helps employees improve mental health and remain in work.

S4W-00057 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make representations to Jobcentre Plus to develop local schemes to support health, employment and fitness advice that assist people with mental health issues in finding and remaining in employment.

S4W-00058 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how it ensures that the voluntary sector is included in the provision of services for mental health patients at local and national level.

S4W-00059 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether mental health awareness and promotion is included in teacher training and, if so, whether this is made available for continuing professional development.

S4W-00060 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether training in mental health awareness is offered to GPs as part of their continuing professional development.

S4W-00061 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many GPs have undergone training on mental health issues as part of their continuing professional development in each of the last five years.

S4W-00062 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what it will do to increase the number of law firms in Scotland that sign up to the Law Society’s mental health accreditation scheme.

S4W-00063 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what proportion of the mental health budget is allocated to the promotion of good mental health and whether it will review spending on early intervention and appropriate treatment for mental health patients.

S4W-00064 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many children have been admitted to adult psychiatric wards in each of the last five years.

S4W-00065 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how sections 25 and 26 of the Mental Health (Care and Treatment) (Scotland) Act 2003 are evaluated; who is responsible for this evaluation, and what action is taken if local authorities have not fulfilled their duties in relation to this legislation.

S4W-00066 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how it monitors whether people with long-term health conditions regularly have their mental health needs assessed and how mental health or psychological support is then provided.

S4W-00067 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what the waiting times are for access to all mental health services, broken down by NHS board.

S4W-00068 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether patients who have been prescribed antidepressants are given regular reviews to ensure that they receive appropriate and personalised care and treatment and, if so, how the NHS monitors these reviews.

S4W-00069 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how it ensures that police forces receive the support needed to deal with people with mental health problems.

S4W-00070 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how it ensures that prisoners are able to access mental health treatments.

S4W-00071 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what work is being undertaken to establish whether there are links between alcohol use and underlying mental health issues.

S4W-00072 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what it is doing to assist unemployed people with mental health problems in finding training and employment.

S4W-00073 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to create new jobs and tackle unemployment in West Dunbartonshire, given that research by the TUC indicates that the area has the highest rate of claimants per vacancy in Britain, with over 40 claimants applying for every vacancy.

S4W-00074 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take to implement the recommendations in the Poverty Alliance’s The 100 Day Challenge: Anti-Poverty Priorities for the next Scottish Government.

S4W-00075 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether responsibility for tackling poverty will be co-ordinated by the First Minister’s office.

S4W-00076 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will establish a Scottish living wage unit, as recommended in the Poverty Alliance’s The 100 Day Challenge: Anti-Poverty Priorities for the next Scottish Government.

S4W-00077 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what (a) existing and (b) additional funding it will provide for services such as (i) childcare, (ii) support for sick and disabled people and (iii) independent advice to mitigate the impact of budget reductions on vulnerable groups, as recommended in the Poverty Alliance’s The 100 Day Challenge: Anti-Poverty Priorities for the next Scottish Government.

S4W-00078 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will initiate a campaign to raise awareness of the reality of living in poverty and to tackle prejudice toward people living in poverty, as recommended in the Poverty Alliance’s The 100 Day Challenge: Anti-Poverty Priorities for the next Scottish Government.

S4W-00079 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will reconvene the Tackling Poverty Board and broaden its membership to involve people in poverty and other stakeholders in the ongoing development of anti-poverty policy, as recommended in the Poverty Alliance’s The 100 Day Challenge: Anti-Poverty Priorities for the next Scottish Government.

S4W-00080 Iain Gray: To ask the Scottish Executive what amounts in Freight Facilities Grant it has awarded to Lafarge Cement in each year since 1999.

S4W-00081 Iain Gray: To ask the Scottish Executive what financial support it has given to Lafarge Cement to move its freight by rail rather than road in each year since 1999.

S4W-00082 Iain Gray: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will (a) determine and (b) publish the names of recipients of a Freight Facilities Grant from the 2011-12 budget.

S4W-00083 John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans there are to update Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network (SIGN) Guideline 75, Epithelial ovarian cancer, in light of the new National Institute for Clinical Excellence (NICE), guideline, Ovarian cancer: the recognition and initial management of ovarian cancer, issued in April 2011.

S4W-00084 Alasdair Allan: To ask the Scottish Government what action it will take to stop depredation of crofting land in the Western Isles following reported changes in the migration patterns of the greylag goose.

S4W-00085 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has carried out or plans to carry out an investigation of the impact on island-based voluntary organisations of the exclusion of business travel from the Air Discount Scheme.

S4W-00086 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has carried out or plans to carry out an investigation of the impact on island-based businesses of the exclusion of business travel from the Air Discount Scheme.

S4W-00087 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take to help island-based voluntary organisations to participate in national events on the Scottish mainland following the exclusion of business travel from the Air Discount Scheme.

S4W-00088 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has received regarding the impact on (a) voluntary organisations and (b) businesses of the exclusion of business travel from the Air Discount Scheme.

S4W-00089 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what its target dates are to roll out superfast broadband to rural areas in each region.

S4W-00090 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what its target dates are for the development of a local broadband plan for each region.

S4W-00091 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what stakeholders will be involved in the development of a local broadband plan for Orkney.

S4W-00092 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive to what parts of (a) Orkney, (b) Shetland and (c) the Western Isles next generation broadband will be rolled out by 2015.

S4W-00093 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria will be used to prioritise the roll-out of superfast broadband to each (a) region and (b) local area in each region.

S4W-00094 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to address the needs of people with rare medical conditions.

S4W-00095 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has set a date for complying with the European Council recommendation on rare diseases.

S4W-00096 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason not all patients with paroxysmal nocturnal haemoglobinuria have access to treatment.

S4W-00097 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria are used to decide on access to treatment for paroxysmal nocturnal haemoglobinuria.
