Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally printed the day after they are lodged. If an amendment is lodged to a motion then the original motion will appear alongside the amendment, along with any support lodged that day, in this section.

Each Monday, this section also contains all motions and amendments lodged the previous week that are still live and any motions and amendments that have been lodged for debate in the coming week. A motion or amendment is live if it has not been debated, withdrawn or otherwise deleted from the list.

Where a motion or amendment has been withdrawn, it is indicated in this section the day after it has been withdrawn. Support for motions and amendments received after they are lodged is shown at the end of this section the day after such support is received.

Motions and amendments can be published with various symbols:

  • Asterisks before the motion or amendment number indicate a motion or amendment published for the first time;
  • Asterisks also identify alterations to the text of a motion or amendment made since it was first published;
  • A hash symbol identifies motions eligible for debate at Members’ Business;
  • A diamond symbol identifies motions lodged for Members’ Business that have not yet attracted the required cross-party support;
  • An "R", identifies motions or amendments in which the Member who lodged it has a registrable interest.

The Parliamentary Bureau periodically deletes motions or amendments not scheduled for debate and which are over six weeks old.

A full list of current motions is available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/motions.htm.

A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/index.htm.

Any questions in relation to this section should be directed to the Chamber Desk, for which contact details are provided at the end of the Bulletin.

New and altered motions and amendments

*S4M-00044 Murdo Fraser: Back Perth City Status Bid 2012—That the Parliament recognises that 2012 will mark Her Majesty the Queen’s Diamond Jubilee; notes that, as part of the celebrations to mark the anniversary, there will be a UK-wide competition to award city status and that the closing date for applications is set for 27 May 2011; acknowledges that, although considered to be a historic city, Perth does not share legal city status on a par with Aberdeen, Dundee, Edinburgh, Glasgow, Inverness and Stirling; congratulates Perth and Kinross Council for all its work and effort to promote Perth’s city status bid and its 50-page submission document; considers and welcomes that Perth’s general public, businesses and civic society all strongly support official city status and believes that it would boost Perth’s national standing and the local economy, and supports Perth’s bid to become a city during Her Majesty the Queen’s Diamond Jubilee.

*S4M-00043 Alasdair Allan: Coastguard Stations Enquiry—That the Parliament notes that the UK Parliament’s Transport Select Committee is scheduled to visit Stornoway on 19 May 2011 to gather evidence on the UK Government’s plans to reduce the number of coastguard stations in Scotland; considers these to be short-sighted plans that will amount to little more than the withdrawal of yet another emergency service at sea; is of the view that any decisions impacting on maritime safety are of acute interest to the many coastal communities around Scotland, and urges the Transport Select Committee to listen to the evidence relating to the concerns that a reduction in maritime safety cover could have for shipping around Scotland.

*S4M-00042 Joe FitzPatrick: SAMH, What’s It Worth Now?—That the Parliament commits to promoting good mental health in Scotland; notes that one in four people in Scotland will experience mental health problems during their lives; welcomes the recent publication of What’s it worth now?, commissioned by the Scottish Association for Mental Health (SAMH), which estimates that the social and economic cost of mental health problems in Scotland has reached £10.7 billion per year; recognises that the current mental health strategy for Scotland, Delivering for Mental Health, comes to an end in 2011, and, in light of what it considers this moral and economic imperative, calls for a new strategy to improve Scotland’s mental health.

*S4M-00040 Christine Grahame: Parliament Noticeboard—That the Parliament considers that the reconvened Standards, Procedures and Public Appointments Committee, or its successor committee, should review Standing Orders in relation to the admissibility of motions in order that those of a congratulatory nature be deemed inadmissible and that a procedure be established for a publicly available noticeboard where such motions can be placed for a period of, for example, two weeks.

*S4M-00039 Kenneth Gibson: Ardrossan Winton Rovers Clinch League Title—That the Parliament congratulates Ardrossan Winton Rovers on clinching the Ayrshire District League title; commends the commitment and ability of the Winton players in gaining promotion to the Stagecoach Super League First Division, and wishes them and their fans all the best in the forthcoming Evening Times Cup as they bid to bring more silverware to Winton Park.

Supported by: Jim Eadie*, Jamie McGrigor*, Chic Brodie*

*S4M-00037 Linda Fabiani: Italian Cloister Garden and Arandora Star Memorial—That the Parliament congratulates Archbishop Mario Conti and the Garden Committee on the opening of the Italian Cloister Garden at St Margaret’s Cathedral in Glasgow; notes that the garden incorporates the monument designed by Maria Giulia Chiarini Testa in memory of more than 700 deportees, among them 446 Italians, who died when the Arandora Star sunk on 2 July 1940; celebrates the links between Italy and Scotland; recognises the work of the Scots-Italian community in bringing this project to fruition, and welcomes the Cloister Garden as a place of memory, contemplation and peace.

Supported by: Paul Wheelhouse*, Chic Brodie*, Drew Smith*, Clare Adamson*

*S4M-00036 Kenneth Gibson: NHS Ayrshire and Arran Leads the Way in the Nutritional Care of People Affected by Cancer—That the Parliament congratulates NHS Ayrshire and Arran’s dietetic department on winning an Advancing Healthcare Award 2011 for the development of its innovative education programme, Nutritional Care of People Affected by Cancer; notes that this e-learning course is the first of its kind in the United Kingdom and has been developed with funding from Macmillan Cancer Support; recognises that it is important for cancer patients to have a healthy and balanced diet in order to maintain their strength and energy, help to promote healing and ensure that they avoid the complications that can be brought on by malnutrition, including severe weight loss; believes that by educating healthcare staff and giving them knowledge, skills and confidence, this course will help to deliver good nutritional care and advice to people affected by cancer; considers that NHS Ayrshire and Arran’s healthcare professionals provide world-class cancer treatment and support, and commends their hard work and commitment.

Supported by: Jackie Baillie*, Chic Brodie*, Derek Mackay*, Roderick Campbell*

*S4M-00035 Tavish Scott: Port Authority and Boating Club’s Fruitful Cooperation in Lerwick—That the Parliament notes what it considers the successful cooperation between the Lerwick Port Authority and the Lerwick Boating Club that came to fruition with the recent celebration to mark the completion of the new boat deck and launching ramps in the small boat harbour in Lerwick; welcomes the development of what it sees as this much improved facility for the storage and launching of sailing boats, which was combined with the port authority’s other work to improve the amenities in the vicinity of the harbour; congratulates the port authority, the boating club, their designers, Arch Henderson LLP, and the contractor, Tulloch Development, on their successful cooperation on this project, and hopes that the new facility will help to maintain the boating club’s long tradition of sailing based in Lerwick’s historic centre and assist its training programme, which, over the years, has introduced many young people to the joys of sailing, some of whom have gone on to represent Shetland internationally in the Inter-Island Games.

*S4M-00034 Marco Biagi: International Day of Action Against Homophobia—That the Parliament notes that 17 May is the International Day of Action against Homophobia (IDAHo); supports and congratulates the NUS Scotland LGBT campaign for marking the occasion; notes that in 76 countries homosexuality remains illegal and in five of those it is punishable by death; commits to working for greater acceptance in Scotland, not least in schools, and looks forward to a time when equality means that such days are no longer necessary.
