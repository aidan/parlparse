Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally printed the day after they are lodged. If an amendment is lodged to a motion then the original motion will appear alongside the amendment, along with any support lodged that day, in this section.

Each Monday, this section also contains all motions and amendments lodged the previous week that are still live and any motions and amendments that have been lodged for debate in the coming week. A motion or amendment is live if it has not been debated, withdrawn or otherwise deleted from the list.

Where a motion or amendment has been withdrawn, it is indicated in this section the day after it has been withdrawn. Support for motions and amendments received after they are lodged is shown at the end of this section the day after such support is received.

Motions and amendments can be published with various symbols:

  • Asterisks before the motion or amendment number indicate a motion or amendment published for the first time;
  • Asterisks also identify alterations to the text of a motion or amendment made since it was first published;
  • A hash symbol identifies motions eligible for debate at Members’ Business;
  • A diamond symbol identifies motions lodged for Members’ Business that have not yet attracted the required cross-party support;
  • An "R", identifies motions or amendments in which the Member who lodged it has a registrable interest.

The Parliamentary Bureau periodically deletes motions or amendments not scheduled for debate and which are over six weeks old.

A full list of current motions is available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/motions.htm.

A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/index.htm.

Any questions in relation to this section should be directed to the Chamber Desk, for which contact details are provided at the end of the Bulletin.

New and altered motions and amendments

*S4M-00032 Mary Scanlon: Congratulations to Nairn County FC—That the Parliament congratulates Nairn County FC on its 4-0 victory over Fraserburgh FC in the Fosters Highland League Cup final; notes that the final match pitted the Highland Football League’s two longest serving managers against each other, with Les Fridge and his Nairn County side emerging as victors; praises the management, players and supporters of Nairn County FC for achieving this result, and congratulates all the teams and match officials involved with the Highland Football League who have once again provided a memorable season for supporters of all clubs.

*S4M-00031 Marco Biagi: Play Fair with Donaldson’s—That the Parliament notes the submission by Cala Homes to the Lands Tribunal for Scotland to remove restrictions on developments in front of the former Donaldson’s School; considers that, regardless of planning controls, there would be no need to remove these restrictions if there were no desire to develop the site, and believes that the site’s historic and scenic beauty should be preserved.

*S4M-00030 Jackie Baillie: Poverty in Scotland—That the Parliament is deeply disappointed with figures from the Scottish Government indicating that progress on tackling poverty in Scotland has stalled; notes that more than 200,000 children are still living in poverty across the country; further notes that, in 2009-10, the proportion of working age adults in relative poverty increased; considers that cuts being made to public services across Scotland will have a significant impact on people already struggling on low incomes; supports the 100 Day Challenge proposed by the Poverty Alliance that calls on the Scottish Government to make tackling poverty a priority by putting concern for poverty and inequality at the heart of government and for it to tackle low pay and in-work poverty, to promote fairer welfare, to address socioeconomic inequality, to change perceptions of people living in poverty and involve people with direct experience of poverty in the development of policy, and calls on the Scottish Government to take urgent and sustained action to address what it considers to be the increasing problem of poverty in Scotland.

*S4M-00029 Jamie Hepburn: Cumbernauld and Kilsyth’s Rugby Stars —That the Parliament offers its congratulations to St Joseph’s, Chapelgreen, St Patrick’s, Ravenswood, and Carbrain and Cumbernauld primaries on successfully qualifying for the North Lanarkshire area primary six and seven rugby finals, which are to take place in Coatbridge on 3 June 2011; applauds the hard work of all those who have taken part in the tournament so far, and wishes the best of luck to those competing in the finals.

*S4M-00028 Jim Eadie: Child Poverty and The Hunter Foundation Initiative—That the Parliament supports the launch of the charitable initiative by The Hunter Foundation (THF) in association with Scottish Television that will partner with the following leading charities in Scotland, Aberlour, Action for Children, Barnardo’s Scotland, CHILDREN 1st, Save the Children Scotland and One Parent Families Scotland; welcomes the venture’s focus in its first year on children affected by poverty; notes that 200,000, one in four children in Scotland, still live in relative poverty despite a 10,000 drop on the previous year; further notes that the venture will seek to create long-term sustainable change in the lives of Scotland’s most at-risk children and young people; applauds the focus of the campaign on raising awareness, challenging stereotypes and bringing attention to the real extent of child poverty, aiming to tackle both the causes and effects of poverty and disadvantage; recognises the fact that all operational costs of the charitable venture will be covered by THF, meaning that every penny donated by the public will go directly to support those children most in need, and looks forward to the success of this innovative and collaborative approach involving the public, private and third sectors in being able to provide a strong and lasting partnership that can make a difference to the lives of Scotland’s most vulnerable children and young people.

*S4M-00027 Aileen Campbell: Commemorating the Cameronian Rifles—That the Parliament notes that Saturday 15 May 2011 saw the annual commemoration of the Cameronian Scottish Rifles at Douglas, South Lanarkshire, including a raising of the regimental flag at the Douglas Victoria Bowling Green, a prayer service in St Bride’s Church, Douglas, and a conventicle at the disbandment cairn beside the Castle Dangerous site near the town; notes that the origins of the Cameronian Regiment can be traced as far back as 1689 and that the Cameronians (Scottish Rifles) were formed as the only rifle regiment of the Scottish infantry in 1881, a role maintained until their disbandment in 1968, and commends all those who work to honour and preserve the memory of the Cameronians at this and other annual events.

*S4M-00026 Aileen Campbell: Biggar Museum Trust Stephens Garage Appeal—That the Parliament congratulates Biggar Museum Trust on its effort to purchase the town’s Stephens Garage as a new premises in order to create a modern museum; notes that the trust’s plans include consolidating existing exhibits and collections and integrating the site into the town’s High Street as an attraction for both tourists and residents; welcomes the progress that has already been made toward the fundraising target of £385,000, and encourages people in Clydesdale and beyond to support ongoing fundraising efforts and events to make the trust’s vision for the town a reality.

Supported by: Jamie McGrigor*, Brian Adam*, Bob Doris*, Humza Yousaf*, Stuart McMillan*, Kenneth Gibson*, Bill Kidd*, Alasdair Allan*

*S4M-00025 Alasdair Allan: Derek Cooper Collection—That the Parliament expresses its gratitude to the writer and broadcaster, Derek Cooper, for making his extensive collection of more than 500 books and related items about the Highlands and Islands, history, food and drink, available to the public by gifting them to the Ravenspoint Centre in Kershader, Isle of Lewis; welcomes this public-spirited act and congratulates all those who have made the deposit and care of what is considered this valuable archive possible, and looks forward to many generations of people being able to view this archive in the future.

Supported by: Bill Kidd*, Aileen Campbell*, Jackson Carlaw*, Jamie McGrigor*, Rob Gibson*, Roderick Campbell*, Brian Adam*, Mary Scanlon*

*S4M-00024 Kezia Dugdale: Living Wage for Interns—That the Parliament considers that interns make a valuable contribution to the workings of the Parliament; recognises, however, that unpaid positions are accessible only to those who can afford to work for free; believes that the Parliament must lead the way to a more equal and fair society by breaking down barriers, and hopes that MSPs resolve to pay their interns serving one month or more a living wage of £7.15 an hour.

Supported by: Neil Findlay*, John Park*

S4M-00024.1 Marco Biagi: Living Wage for Interns—As an amendment to motion S4M-00024 in the name of Kezia Dugdale (Living Wage for Interns), leave out from "unpaid" to end and insert "full-time unpaid positions are accessible only to those who can afford to work for free; believes that the Parliament must lead the way to a more equal and fair society by breaking down barriers, and hopes that MSPs resolve to pay their interns serving one month or more a living wage of £7.15 an hour or provide volunteering arrangements that allow those who are studying or working elsewhere to participate equally."

*S4M-00023 Rob Gibson: Easter Ross Farmers Club 200 Years Young—That the Parliament congratulates the members of Easter Ross Farmers Club on celebrating the club’s bicentenary; notes that, over these 200 years, the club has been active in aiming to improve agricultural practices in the Easter Ross area; further notes that, to mark this milestone and these activities, the club was presented with a special trophy from the Royal Highland and Agricultural Society of Scotland at a recent dinner in Tain; considers that, due to climate change, these are testing times that require the secure provision of food as never before, and wishes the farmers of Easter Ross every good fortune for the future in sustaining their skills and successes.

*S4M-00022 John Park: Dunfermline Heading to the SPL—That the Parliament celebrates the recent success of Dunfermline Athletic Football Club on winning the First Division championship; is delighted that the victory secures Dunfermline a place in the Scottish Premier League (SPL); congratulates Jim McIntyre and his players on their achievement after what it considers was a difficult start to the season; further congratulates Pars fans for keeping faith with the East End Park side over this period, and looks forward to the side’s continued success in the SPL.

Supported by: Jamie McGrigor*, Kenneth Gibson*, Alex Johnstone*, Bill Kidd*, Jackie Baillie*, Tavish Scott*, Willie Rennie*

*S4M-00021 Kenneth Gibson: Congratulations to Dalry Thistle FC—That the Parliament congratulates Dalry Thistle FC on gaining promotion to the Super League First Division in a season that also saw the Ayrshire club reach the Scottish Junior Cup semi-final for the first time in its 91-year history and wishes all at Dalry Thistle FC the very best for season 2010-11.

Supported by: Stewart Maxwell*, Aileen Campbell*, Alasdair Allan*, Bob Doris*, John Lamont*, Bill Kidd*, Brian Adam*, Stuart McMillan*, Humza Yousaf*, David Torrance*, Colin Beattie*, Jamie McGrigor*, Willie Coffey*, Jim Eadie*

*S4M-00019 Brian Adam: Three in a Row for Dyce Boys FC—That Parliament sends its warmest congratulations to Dyce Boys Club under-16 football team on what it considers to be its stunning third successive victory in the Scottish Youth Football Association Scottish Cup final; notes that the club also won the competitions at under-14 and under-15 level, and wishes the club all the very best for the future.
