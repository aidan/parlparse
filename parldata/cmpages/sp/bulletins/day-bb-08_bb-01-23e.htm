Section E – Written questions lodged on 22 January 2008

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-8724 John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what total costs have been calculated to be attributable to the outbreak of foot-and-mouth disease in 2007. R

S3W-8725 John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to protect buyers’ and sellers’ animals from bluetongue at the forthcoming Perth Bull Sales. R

S3W-8726 John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive how the new double tagging system for sheep will improve traceability. R

S3W-8727 John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what it is doing to reduce the costs to the red meat sector of the meat hygiene service, in light of the BSE crisis coming to an end. R

S3W-8728 John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made on limiting the risk of bluetongue being imported to Scotland from continental Europe. R

S3W-8729 John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to keep sheep and cattle on Less Favoured Area land in the highlands and islands where sheep and cattle are presently coming off that land. R

S3W-8730 John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what it is doing to limit the spread of bracken in the north and west of Scotland. R

S3W-8731 John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what it is doing to ensure that compound fertilisers will be available in the spring. R

S3W-8732 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what its plans are to improve the provision of mental health services in (a) Aberdeen, (b) Aberdeenshire and (c) Angus in 2008 to 2011.

S3W-8733 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what actions it plans for raising awareness of mental health in local communities.

S3W-8734 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what actions it plans for educating school children about mental health.

S3W-8736 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how much funding it intends to allocate for delivering mental health services in (a) Aberdeen, (b) Aberdeenshire and (c) Angus during the period of the spending review.

S3W-8738 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to offer financial assistance to the Royal Observatory in Edinburgh.

S3W-8739 Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive why priority need for housing assessment is to be phased out by 2012 and how it will deliver on this commitment.

S3W-8740 Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive whether local connection is to be removed as a criterion for social housing allocation; whether it considers that this will cause concern in communities and, if so, whether it will reconsider its position on this matter.

S3W-8741 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will highlight in its road safety campaigns dangers caused by diesel and oil spills.

S3W-8742 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it plans to take on the enforcement of legislation in respect of diesel and oil spills on roads.

S3W-8743 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to introduce a safety campaign to highlight the danger of over-filling diesel tanks, given that diesel and oil spills on roads have led to a number of fatalities in the north east this year.

S3W-8749 Mr Frank McAveety: To ask the Scottish Executive how many of the responses to its internal review on the future of sportscotland called for the absorption of the Scottish Institute of Sport into sportscotland.

S3W-8750 Mr Frank McAveety: To ask the Scottish Executive when the decision was made to remove the Chairs of sportscotland and the Scottish Institute of Sport from their posts.

S3W-8758 Mr Frank McAveety: To ask the Scottish Executive when a new full-time Chair of the merged boards of sportscotland and the Scottish Institute of Sport will be in place.
