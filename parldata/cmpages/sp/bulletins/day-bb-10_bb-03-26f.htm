Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (♦).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S3M-6073 Aileen Campbell: Biggar Archaeology Group Members Become Mudlarks at Elsrickle—That the Parliament congratulates the Biggar Archaeology Group, along with colleagues from University of Stirling, which has been doing important exploratory work at Elsrickle, South Lanarkshire; commends this search for evidence about the environment in which Scotland’s earliest inhabitants lived 14,000 years ago; recognises Biggar Archaeology Group’s growth from humble beginnings to a highly developed archaeological team; looks forward to Biggar Archaeology Group’s continued contributions in future excavations, and notes the importance of archaeological findings in better understanding Scotland’s long and rich history.

*S3M-6072 John Park: Carbon Capture and Storage at Longannet—That the Parliament welcomes news that Longannet Power Station has been selected as one of the final bids in the UK Government’s carbon capture and storage competition; congratulates the Scottish Power-led Carbon Capture and Storage Consortium on getting through to the final stage of funding, sharing a £90 million UK Government fund with e-on to finance design and development studies; hopes that Longannet is successful as one of the two contenders for a possible further award of a £1 billion subsidy in 2011; considers supporting the development of carbon capture and storage important in delivering the low-carbon energy of the future; understands that some estimates, including that of WWF, suggest that the successful demonstration of this technology at Longannet could result in a reduction in CO2 emissions of over 14 million tonnes by 2025; further welcomes the prospects for a new industry that this announcement affords, including an estimated 10,000 jobs over the next 10 to 15 years, and looks forward to Scotland leading the way in delivering clean coal technology.

*S3M-6071 Mike Pringle: Congratulations to Liberton High School and its Head Teacher, Donald Macdonald—That the Parliament congratulates Liberton High School and its head teacher, Donald Macdonald, in the vast improvement evident at the school since Donald first took charge in June 2005; notes that, following its most recent inspection in 2009, HM Inspectorate of Education (HMIE) described Liberton High as the most improved school in Scotland; further notes that, in 2009, 52 pupils from the school attended university, an increase from only seven who attended in 2005 when HMIE deemed overall school attainment unsatisfactory; considers this incredible improvement to be a just reward for all the hard work put in by the pupils, teachers, staff and parents at Liberton High in turning the school around, and wishes Liberton High and Donald Macdonald every success in the future as it continues to improve.

*S3M-6070 Sarah Boyack: Boroughmuir High’s Quiz Champions—That the Parliament congratulates the pupils of Boroughmuir High School on winning the Scottish Young Consumer of the Year competition; notes that the worthy winners were Boroughmuir pupils Imogen Ross, Elspie Horne, Alice Mackenzie and team captain Douglas Boubert, and considers their excellent knowledge and skill to be a credit to both the individual pupils and teachers involved and to the whole school.

*S3M-6069 Liam McArthur: Sudden End to Home Renewables Grant and Home Loans Schemes—That the Parliament believes that the Scottish Government’s decision to bring forward the deadline for applications under the change to the Energy Saving Scotland (ESS) home renewables grant and home loans schemes without consultation or warning will unfairly penalise the very many innovative individuals looking to take forward microrenewables projects; is concerned that the way in which grants were removed and loans reduced under the ESS schemes risks undermining public confidence in schemes of this type in the future, and calls on the Scottish Government to reconsider how applicants and prospective applicants affected by the premature closure of the scheme can be helped.

*S3M-6068 Jackie Baillie: Obama Health Reforms Welcome—That the Parliament congratulates US President Barack Obama and the US House of Representatives for the successful passage of the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act, which will bring substantial reform to health care in America; welcomes the ending of pre-existing condition exclusions for health insurance coverage; further welcomes the extension of insurance to all low-income groups; warmly acknowledges the improved access and inclusion of more individuals and families in the US health care system, and, in doing so, celebrates the NHS in Scotland and across the United Kingdom, where health care remains free at the point of need.

*S3M-6067 John Swinney: Forth Crossing Bill - Financial Resolution—That the Parliament, for the purposes of any Act of the Scottish Parliament resulting from the Forth Crossing Bill, agrees to any expenditure of a kind referred to in Rules 9C.16.3(b)(ii) and (iii) of the Parliament’s Standing Orders arising in consequence of the Act.

*S3M-6066 Helen Eadie: Fair Fares for Fifers—That the Parliament is dismayed to learn that Fife Council is increasing by 1,340% the concessionary rail fares for all Fife senior citizens who, it is considered, have over many years benefited enormously from the previous Labour-controlled administration’s policy of 50p journeys to Glasgow, Edinburgh or Dundee in Fife; considers that, in many instances, this will mean that pensioners will have to severely limit their contact with the close family in these cities and face insurmountable costs of travel to the major hospitals for outpatient treatment or visits to patients, and hopes that the council will reverse this monumentally wrong decision and restore this policy that is the envy of pensioners across other parts of Scotland and that this policy may be rolled out right across Scotland, furthering the sustainability of public transport while at the same time recognising that, in the majority of cases, all those disabled people who are unable to mount the steps of intercity coaches find the relatively easy access of trains a major boon to making travel much easier for them.

*S3M-6065 Willie Coffey: Rathbone Achievers Awards—That the Parliament congratulates all of the winners in this year’s Rathbone Scotland Achievers of the Year 2010 awards, including young achievers Emily Paterson from Kilmarnock, Liam Murray and Lisa Keogh from Dundee, Thomas Hodge from Fife and Jake Duncanson from Edinburgh, Rathbone’s Employee and Volunteer of the Year, Niki Gray and Junior Robertson, respectively, and employers Arnold Clark of Dundee and Johnston Brown of Glasgow; recognises the achievement of the young award-winners who have overcome difficult circumstances to build a positive future for themselves; believes that the commitment of Rathbone’s staff and volunteers plays a vital role in helping these young people succeed; notes the importance of Rathbone’s partnerships with employers in providing young people with the opportunity to progress, and wishes Rathbone every success in continuing to improve the life chances of young people in Scotland.

*S3M-6064 Stuart McMillan: Able2Sail—That the Parliament welcomes the work carried out by Able2Sail; notes that Able2Sail is a volunteer-run charity based in Inverkip Marina that provides free sailing to any disabled people, the families of disabled people and armed forces veterans; further notes the charity’s central belief that every individual should have the opportunity to sail a modern yacht; commends Able2Sail on its excellent work in the community, and wishes it every success in the future.

Supported by: Brian Adam*, Joe FitzPatrick*, Anne McLaughlin*, Dave Thompson*, Kenneth Gibson*, Christine Grahame*, Rob Gibson*, Jackie Baillie*, Sandra White*, Bob Doris*

*S3M-6063 Christina McKelvie: Making Children Participants, Not Pawns, in School Closure Consultations—That the Parliament welcomes the publication of the Participants, Not Pawns guidance by Scotland’s Commissioner for Children and Young People (SCCYP); believes that this guidance acts as an important complement to the Schools Consultation Act (Scotland) 2010 in helping local authorities to ensure that their schools closure consultations abide by the United Nations Convention on the Rights of the Child, under which children are entitled to make their views known and have them taken seriously; agrees with SCCYP that it makes sense to give a central position to the views of children within a process that directly affects them, and commends SCCYP’s ongoing efforts to help Scotland’s public bodies engage more effectively, fairly and meaningfully with children and young people of all ages and abilities.

*S3M-6062 Dr Richard Simpson: Excellent Work by the Scottish Driving Assessment Service—That the Parliament commends the excellent work of the Scottish Driving Assessment Service (SDAS), which has carried out over 12,000 assessments in its 25 years; notes the increasing number of referrals to SDAS and the consequential increase in waiting times; recognises the contribution of Citroën, Nissan, Ford and Toyota in lending SDAS vehicles, and believes that the timely provision of SDAS assessment, advice and support is an essential part of the administration.

*S3M-6061 Dr Richard Simpson: Welcoming the Fourth Anniversary of the Smoking, Health and Social Care (Scotland) Act 2005—That the Parliament welcomes the fourth anniversary of the coming into force of the Smoking, Health and Social Care (Scotland) Act 2005, which it considers has significantly improved Scotland’s health, setting the precedent for the rest of the United Kingdom; considers that, on its fourth anniversary, the effects of its introduction so far include reducing Scotland’s heart attack rate by around 14%; believes that, in the coming years, this Act will continue to have a positive effect in reducing smoking and smoking-related diseases in Scotland, and considers that further reform in smoking legislation passed this year will build on that landmark Act.
