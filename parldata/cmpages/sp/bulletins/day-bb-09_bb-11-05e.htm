Business Bulletin No. 161/2009: Thursday 5 November 2009
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 4 November 2009

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-28622 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many doses of the influenza A (H1N1) vaccine have been provided to (a) NHS and social care staff and (b) identified at-risk categories in each week since the start of the vaccination programme.

S3W-28623 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will amend the priority categories for the influenza A (H1N1) vaccination programme to include nought to four-year-olds.

S3W-28624 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many doses of the influenza A (H1N1) vaccine were provided by GPs to priority categories of patients before the start of the predicted peak period of the virus, from end October to mid-November 2009.

S3W-28625 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many doses of the influenza A (H1N1) vaccine will be available to GPs for each week until Christmas 2009.

S3W-28626 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it has taken since the Cabinet Secretary for Health and Wellbeing’s statement in the chamber on 29 October 2009 to accelerate the delivery of the influenza A (H1N1) vaccination programme.

S3W-28627 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the publication of its Recorded Crimes and Offences Involving Firearms, Scotland, 2008-09 statistical bulletin, what disposals were issued to those offenders who committed (a) homicide, (b) attempted murder, (c) serious assault, (d) robbery, (e) vandalism, (f) reckless conduct with firearms, (g) minor assault and (h) other crimes and offences in the Lothian and Borders police force area.

S3W-28628 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the publication of its Recorded Crimes and Offences Involving Firearms, Scotland, 2008-09 statistical bulletin, what action it will take to tackle the increase in the number of attempted murders involving firearms in the Lothians and Borders police force area from one in 2007-08 to 11 in 2008-09.

S3W-28629 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the publication of its Recorded Crimes and Offences Involving Firearms, Scotland, 2008-09 statistical bulletin, what firearms were used by those offenders who committed (a) homicide, (b) attempted murder, (c) serious assault, (d) robbery, (e) vandalism, (f) reckless conduct with firearms, (g) minor assault and (h) other crimes and offences in the Lothian and Borders police force area.

S3W-28630 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive, in relation to the publication of its Recorded Crimes and Offences Involving Firearms, Scotland, 2007-08 statistical bulletin, what firearms were used by those offenders who committed (a) homicide, (b) attempted murder, (c) serious assault, (d) robbery, (e) vandalism, (f) reckless conduct with firearms, (g) minor assault and (h) other crimes and offences in the Lothian and Borders police force area.

S3W-28631 Stuart McMillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the National Lottery in relation to funding and supporting renewable projects in the West of Scotland.

S3W-28632 Stuart McMillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what assistance is available to organisations that wish to invest in renewable technologies to make their businesses more environmentally friendly.

S3W-28633 Stuart McMillan: To ask the Scottish Executive when it plans to meet the European Commissioner for Competition Policy to discuss competition within the banking sector in Scotland.

S3W-28634 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list the 236 new or refurbished schools completed since April 2007 referred to in its news release of 8 October 2009, broken down, where applicable, by (a) procurement method, (b) date when the outline business case was approved and (c) date when construction commenced.

S3W-28635 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list the 36 new or refurbished schools referred to in its news release of 8 October 2009 as completed since publication of SPICe Briefing 09/72, broken down, where applicable, by (a) procurement method, (b) date when the outline business case was approved and (c) date when construction commenced.

S3W-28636 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive for how many and which of the 236 new or refurbished schools completed since April 2007 referred to in its news release of 8 October 2009 construction commenced in financial years budgeted for by the current administration.

S3W-28637 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive for how many and which of the 236 new or refurbished schools completed since April 2007 referred to in its news release of 8 October 2009 construction commenced in financial years budgeted for by the previous administration.

S3W-28638 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason the online School Estate Statistics 2009 were altered between the evening of 7 October 2009 and the morning of 8 October 2009 and what changes were made.

S3W-28639 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is aware of any case of online official statistics referred to in a motion for parliamentary debate being altered on the evening prior to such debate.

S3W-28640 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive when the influenza A (H1N1) vaccination will be available for people with an egg allergy.

S3W-28641 Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive what mechanisms are in place to monitor the contribution of community planning partnerships to tackling unemployment.

S3W-28642 Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive by what means the contributions of community planning partnerships to tackling unemployment are being measured.

S3W-28643 Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive what evidence there is that community planning partnerships are contributing to tackling unemployment.

S3W-28644 Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what the cost to date is of the Aberdeen Western Peripheral Route public consultation.

S3W-28645 Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what the total cost to date is of the Aberdeen Western Peripheral Route, including the cost of the public consultation.
