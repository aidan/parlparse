BUSINESS BULLETIN 89/1999

Thursday 11 November 1999

Section E - Written questions lodged on 10 November 1999

 

 

S1W-2323 Mr Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether, when work contracts have been awarded by NHS Trusts, any such contracts have attracted capital charges to the National Health Service.

 

S1W-2324 Mr Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether, in awarding work contracts, any NHS Trust requiring a private build solution has awarded a contract to an organisation already utilising NHS property.

 

S1W-2447 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied that the water supply at Bracara does not pose any health risk to consumers.

 

S1W-2448 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider raising the school entry age to six.

 

S1W-2449 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to limit the sale of school playing fields.

 

S1W-2450 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what statistics are available, by sex, on the percentage of young people aged 18 or under smoking in the years 1996-97, 1997-98 and 1998-99.

 

S1W-2451 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will seek the discontinuation of PVC saline bags following withdrawal of such bags by the US Food and Drug Administration over concerns regarding the release of toxins.

 

S1W-2452 Dr Winnie Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it has in place to help pensioners during any excessively cold weather.

 

S1W-2453 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of secondary schools in each local authority offer courses in Modern Studies to Higher level.

 

S1W-2454 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to establish a Civil Justice Forum along similar lines to the existing Criminal Justice Forum.

 

S1W-2455 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to legislate to address the implications of the decision of Sheriff Principal Bowen in the case of Conway versus Glasgow City Council by providing hostel dwellers with the right not to be evicted without legal process.

 

S1W-2456 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any audit is made of the adequacy or otherwise of care plans made in connection with child supervision orders.

 

S1W-2457 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to encourage cycling and walking and how much financial support it is providing for the promotion of cycling and walking in each of the next three financial years.

 

S1W-2458 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has received the annual report of the Accountant of Bankruptcy for 1998-99 and whether it is considering reform of the law of sequestration in relation to protected trust deeds.

 

S1W-2459 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive how many protected trust deeds were registered in the financial years 1995-96, 1996-97, 1997-98 and 1998-99.

 

S1W-2460 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider introducing for those debtors whose total unsecured debts do not exceed a specified and relatively small limit, an alternative to sequestration such as the administration order which is available in England, or a procedure which provides a respite within which to determine whether a sequestration decree is necessary.

 

S1W-2462 Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made on the introduction of individual learning accounts in Scotland.

 

S1W-2463 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what its policy is on visits to schools by school dentists and health visitors and in particular on the recommended frequency of such visits.

 

S1W-2464 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there is a national policy to refuse heart transplants to patients beyond a certain age and, if so, what age that is.

 

S1W-2465 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what the projected overspend is in the current financial year for acute hospitals, broken down by hospital.

 

S1W-2466 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it can take to ensure that all imported pig meat has been produced to the same health and welfare standards as that produced in the United Kingdom.

 

S1W-2467 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to include resource transfer within Scottish Health Service Costs, prepared by the Information and Statistics Division and published annually.

 

S1W-2468 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive in what budgets it identified savings of £2.1 million in order to fund its rescue package for Scottish Opera.

 

S1W-2469 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what the rights are of separated parents to access to their children’s clinical notes.

 

S1W-2470 Dorothy-Grace Elder: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding is being provided for the NHS Direct initiative due to be piloted in Scotland next year.

 

S1W-2471 Mrs Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive how many patients in each local authority in Scotland were transferred from hospital directly into home care, residential care and nursing homes in each year from 1993-4 to present.

 

S1W-2472 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to provide assistance to the Glasgow Highlanders’ Institute, in the light of the difficulties it presently faces without permanent premises.

 

S1W-2473 Mike Watson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will begin to publish bilateral concordats agreed between Whitehall Departments and their counterparts in the Executive.
