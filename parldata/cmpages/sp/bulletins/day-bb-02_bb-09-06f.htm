Business Bulletin No. 123/2002

Friday 6 September 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-3361 Tommy Sheridan: Scottish Gulf War Veterans-That the Parliament recognises the deep concern over the health conditions of Scottish Gulf War veterans and calls on the Scottish Executive to recognise their illness and to ensure that they receive the appropriate treatment.

Supported by: Mr John McAllion*, Dennis Canavan*, Dorothy-Grace Elder*, John Farquhar Munro*, Elaine Smith*, Ms Sandra White*, Tricia Marwick*, Mr Lloyd Quinan*, Tavish Scott*, Ms Margo MacDonald*, Dr Winnie Ewing*, Alex Neil*

*S1M-3360 Mr Brian Monteith: Doors Open Day 2002-That the Parliament welcomes the Doors Open Days 2002 initiative taking place in September 2002, co-ordinated by the Scottish Civic Trust, which gives people the opportunity to visit and explore for a day, free of charge, various buildings across Scotland such as castles, stately homes and law courts, many of which are not normally open to the public, and believes the initiative gives people the opportunity to improve their understanding of their local architectural environment and the country's rich built heritage.

Supported by: Mr Keith Harding*, Murdo Fraser*

*S1M-3359 Mr John McAllion: Safe Child Scheme-That the Parliament welcomes the Safe Child Scheme launched in Milton of Craigie Retail Park, Dundee; notes that the use of contact cards allows a lost child to be reunited with his or her parents quickly, and further notes that this is part of a wider scheme aimed at providing a safe environment for the community in the east end of Dundee.

*S1M-3358 Patricia Ferguson on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Committee Membership-That the Parliament agrees the following committee membership change-

Duncan Hamilton to replace Kenneth Gibson on the Local Government Committee.

Supported by: Euan Robson*

*S1M-3357# Mary Scanlon: Importance of Primary Care-That the Parliament recognises the importance of primary care in managing 90% of patient contact with the NHS in Scotland; appreciates the vital role GPs play in providing primary care services as part of the wider primary care team; supports the statement that "if it can be done in primary care, it should be done in primary care", and acknowledges the urgent need for reform and support to invigorate staffing levels, facilities, IT provision and morale for this to become a reality.

*S1M-3356 Ms Margo MacDonald: Drug Testing Regime of the International Olympic Committee-That the Parliament urges the International Olympic Committee to adopt a more rigorous regime that will conclusively and unambiguously identify methamphetamine in tests for banned substances carried out on athletes to ensure that no athlete is automatically banned from competing with the subsequent loss of reputation, earnings and, in the case of Alain Baxter, an Olympic medal which was won fairly and without the use of a performance-enhancing drug, as objective scientific opinion now verifies.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie*

S1M-3354# Crisis in Borders Public Sewerage System (lodged on 4 September 2002) Shona Robison*, Fiona Hyslop*, Alex Neil*, Mr Kenneth Gibson*

S1M-3352*#* Tavish Scott: Support for Crofting in the Highlands and Islands-That the Parliament notes the importance of crofting to the Highlands and Islands; welcomes the publication of the White Paper on crofting reform and encourages crofters to participate in the consultation now underway; further notes the level of agricultural returns to crofting businesses and supports the agri-environmental focus that the Environmentally Sensitive Area scheme has brought to many crofting counties, and encourages continuing support to crofting through such measures as the Crofting Counties Agricultural Grants scheme.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie*
