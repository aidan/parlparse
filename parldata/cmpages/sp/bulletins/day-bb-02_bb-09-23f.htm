Business Bulletin No. 134/2002

Monday 23 September 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-3414 Mr Brian Monteith: The Menteith Shinty Festival, Callander-That the Parliament congratulates the Menteith Camanachd Club on holding yet another successful shinty festival in Callander, West Perthshire, this year; welcomes the efforts of the organisers and the boys and girls in promoting the cause of shinty; applauds the success of Aberdour Under 9s, Rockfield Under 12s and Menteith Under 15s in winning their sections, and looks forward to the festival growing from strength to strength in future years.

Supported by: Mr Keith Harding*

S1M-3413# Karen Whitefield: Eco-Schools-That the Parliament recognises the value of the Eco-Schools programme in promoting environmental education and encouraging young people to make a difference to their local communities; congratulates those teachers who, through their hard work and enthusiasm, are making the programme a success across Scotland; hopes that more schools will register an interest in the project, and considers that the Executive should continue to give its support to such initiatives which encourage citizenship and environmental awareness.

Supported by: Sarah Boyack*

S1M-3412# Bill Butler: Glasgow Airport Rail Link-That the Parliament welcomes the announcement by the Minister for Finance and Public Services on 12 September 2002 that resources are to be made available to make possible the construction and completion of a Glasgow Airport rail link; recognises the significance of this rail link as part of a modern, 21st century transport infrastructure for the whole of Scotland; believes that the most sensible option for this new rail link is one routed via Paisley Gilmour Street and Paisley St James, and considers that the Minister for Enterprise, Transport and Lifelong Learning should announce, as a matter of urgency, both his favoured option for this development and a clear timetable for its speedy implementation.

*S1M-3412.1 Ms Sandra White: Glasgow Airport Rail Link-As an amendment to motion (S1M-3412) in the name of Bill Butler, leave out from "believes" to end and insert "however notes with concern that there is still no commitment to the Glasgow CrossRail scheme which has been widely recognised as a key component to the success of the Glasgow Airport rail link opening up links to the airport not only from Glasgow city centre but also improving accessibility from the entire country, and considers that the Scottish Executive should immediately make the resources available and announce as a matter of urgency the earliest possible commencement date for work on the implementation of the Glasgow Airport link and the Glasgow CrossRail scheme."

S1M-3410 Ms Margaret Curran: Building (Scotland) Bill - Stage 1-That the Parliament agrees to the general principles of the Building (Scotland) Bill.

Supported by: Hugh Henry

S1M-3408 Robin Harper: Soil Association Report on GM Crops-That the Parliament calls upon the Scottish Executive to examine the findings of the recent report by the Soil Association, Seeds of Doubt - North American Farmers' Experiences of GM Crops, paying particular attention to the impact of genetically modified crops on conventional and organic farming, and to hold a full public consultation on the growing of genetically modified crops that addresses the concerns raised in the report.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan

S1M-3407# Rhoda Grant: European Capital of Culture Bid - Inverness Highland 2008-That the Parliament notes the on-going competition to decide the United Kingdom's nominee for the European Capital of Culture in 2008; further notes that Inverness Highland 2008 is the sole Scottish bid; recognises the imaginative plans that the bid team have proposed including capitalising on the Highlands' unique culture and the development of a cultural plan; further recognises the immense economic, social and cultural benefits that a successful bid would bring to Inverness and the Highlands, and considers that, as this is the sole Scottish bid, the Scottish Executive, MSPs, government agencies and the wider Scottish society should do all in their power to support the bid and promote a successful outcome.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie

S1M-3406# Sarah Boyack: Edinburgh Waverley Station-That the Parliament notes the importance of Edinburgh Waverley Station to the national rail network; welcomes plans for the development of regional and local services across central Scotland; further notes that current projections state that the station will reach its capacity in 2003; recognises the station's significance as part of the historic core of Scotland's capital city and its potential as a modern, accessible transport interchange; further welcomes proposals to upgrade the station to enable the significant expansion of rail provision on the east coast, and encourages the Scottish Executive to continue to work in partnership with Her Majesty's Government, City of Edinburgh Council, the Strategic Rail Authority and the railway industry to accelerate the delivery of this vital project.

Supported by: Rhoda Grant, Nora Radcliffe, Des McNulty, Margaret Smith, Trish Godman, Bristow Muldoon, Mr Jamie Stone, Mr John Home Robertson, Brian Fitzpatrick, Scott Barrie, Mr Tom McCabe, Maureen Macmillan, Susan Deacon, Mr Alasdair Morrison, Jackie Baillie, Helen Eadie, Karen Gillon, Donald Gorrie

S1M-3400# Mr John McAllion: Whitfield-That the Parliament congratulates the people of Whitfield in Dundee for their outstanding efforts in regenerating their community over the last few years; notes that Whitfield, like many other parts of Scotland, has its fair share of problems but recognises the great progress made by community-based organisations; however, further notes the Save the Children report, The Well-being of Children in the UK, that highlights the persistence of poverty and other problems in parts of the estate; regrets that the Whitfield Partnership was wound up by the Scotland Office without a proper exit strategy, and believes that the Scottish Executive and Her Majesty's Government should redouble their efforts to eradicate child poverty from Whitfield and every other community in Scotland.

Supported by: Alex Neil, Kate Maclean, Mr Kenneth Gibson, Scott Barrie, Tommy Sheridan, Mr Kenneth Macintosh, Elaine Smith*

S1M-3399# Brian Fitzpatrick: Douglas Andrew Kilgour Black-That the Parliament recognises the lifelong commitment of Douglas Andrew Kilgour Black to the medical profession, the cause of social justice and the campaign to reduce health inequalities across the United Kingdom and the rest of the world; pays tribute to his contribution to the National Health Service as a physician, scientist, professor of medicine, President of the Royal College of Physicians and President of the British Medical Association and also as a steadfast campaigner for recognition of the links between poverty and ill-health, most notably in his scholarship and research in the pioneering work comprised in the report, Inequalities in Health, which provided evidence that at every important stage of life people of the lowest incomes suffered disproportionately from ill-health and premature death; extends its thanks for his work to build a better future, and extends its deepest sympathy and condolences to Sir Douglas' wife, children, family and many friends on his death on 13th September 2002.

Supported by: Ms Wendy Alexander, Rhoda Grant, Margaret Jamieson, Cathie Craigie, Helen Eadie, Mr Kenneth Macintosh, Mr Michael McMahon, Rhona Brankin, Johann Lamont, Karen Gillon, Maureen Macmillan, Mr John McAllion, Scott Barrie, Des McNulty, Tommy Sheridan

S1M-3398 Malcolm Chisholm: Mental Health (Scotland) Bill-That the Parliament agrees to the general principles of the Mental Health (Scotland) Bill.

Supported by: Mrs Mary Mulligan, Mr Frank McAveety, Dr Richard Simpson

Supported by: Hugh Henry

Supported by: Allan Wilson

S1M-3394 Johann Lamont: Challenging Sectarianism in Glasgow-That the Parliament congratulates Glasgow City Council on its important and pioneering work in challenging sectarianism; notes in particular the powerful partnership it has forged with Strathclyde Police, the Church of Scotland, the Roman Catholic Church, Celtic Football Club, Rangers Football Club and voluntary organisations in developing the "Sense over Sectarianism" campaign; welcomes the decision to commission major research into the extent of sectarianism and Glaswegians' perception of it and acknowledges that this research will allow the development of meaningful action to tackle sectarianism; further welcomes the initiative by the council in bringing together all the major faiths in the city to launch the "Forum of Faiths" on 23 September 2002, an initiative which will work to foster respect and understanding amongst people of all religious beliefs and none in Scotland's largest city; congratulates all those involved in this important work, and welcomes this measured approach which looks beyond simplistic rhetoric and provides a model for those who seek to eradicate sectarianism across Scotland.

Supported by: Trish Godman, Brian Fitzpatrick

S1M-3393# Alex Neil: Firefighters and Emergency Fire Control Staff-That the Parliament recognises the valuable and courageous work undertaken by the fire service and believes that a new pay formula together with a commensurate level of pay for firefighters and emergency fire control staff should be introduced to ensure that these accurately reflect the highly skilled and professional role which they undertake.

Supported by: Tricia Marwick, Ms Margo MacDonald, Mrs Margaret Ewing, Michael Russell, Mr Kenneth Gibson, Ms Sandra White, Fergus Ewing, Mr John McAllion, Tommy Sheridan, Elaine Smith*, Fiona McLeod*

S1M-3392 David Mundell: International Forest Fest 2002-That the Parliament notes that the International Forest Fest 2002 will be held at Lockerbie on 25 to 28 September 2002; further notes that the event will not only be the flagship of Treefest Scotland 2002 but will also bring together for the first time in the UK two major biennial events, the 25th World Logging Championships and the APF International Forest Machinery Exhibition, along with many other prestigious competitions and varied forest events; congratulates all those involved in organising this unique forest spectacular and bringing it to Scotland, and sends its best wishes for a successful event, not only to the organisers, but also to the many thousands of competitors, exhibitors and visitors attending the event.

Supported by: Mary Scanlon, Brian Fitzpatrick, Mr Kenneth Gibson, Phil Gallie, Mr Murray Tosh, Murdo Fraser, Mrs Lyndsay McIntosh, John Scott, Alex Fergusson, Mr Jamie Stone, Alex Neil, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton, Michael Russell, Robin Harper

S1M-3391 Mr John McAllion: Mary Slessor Foundation-That the Parliament welcomes the work of the Mary Slessor Foundation in continuing the work done in Calabar, Nigeria, by Mary Slessor of Dundee in the 19th century; notes the excellent work being done by the foundation to defeat poverty and disease in Nigeria, and further welcomes this commemoration of Mary Slessor and the encouragement of links between Scotland and Africa.

Supported by: Alex Neil, Mr Kenneth Gibson, Tommy Sheridan
