Business Bulletin No. 65/2008: Thursday 24 April 2008

Section E – Written questions lodged on 23 April 2008

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-12184 Jim Tolson: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Fuel Poverty Forum will next meet.

S3W-12185 Jim Tolson: To ask the Scottish Executive what stakeholders it has asked to take part in the Fuel Poverty Forum.

S3W-12186 Jim Tolson: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it has taken to develop a one-stop-shop approach on fuel poverty to reflect the decision taken by the Parliament on 13 March 2008.

S3W-12187 Jim Tolson: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is considering the introduction of a local tax rebate as part of its strategy on fuel poverty to reflect the decision taken by the Parliament on 13 March 2008.

S3W-12188 Jim Tolson: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is considering changes to planning rules to make it easier to install micropower as part of its strategy on fuel poverty to reflect the decision taken by the Parliament on 13 March 2008.

S3W-12189 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-11689 by Nicola Sturgeon on 16 April 2008, how many whole-time-equivalent hours have been worked by bank and agency staff, excluding bank and agency nurses, in each NHS board in each of the last five years, broken down by discipline.

S3W-12192 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many council houses will be built in South Ayrshire in (a) 2009, (b) 2010 and (c) 2011.

S3W-12194 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many affordable homes for sale will be built in in East Ayrshire in (a) 2009, (b) 2010 and (c) 2011.

S3W-12195 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many affordable homes for sale will be built in South Ayrshire in (a) 2009, (b) 2010 and (c) 2011. 

S3W-12196 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many homes for rent will be built by housing associations in East Ayrshire in (a) 2009, (b) 2010 and (c) 2011.

S3W-12197 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many homes for rent will be built by housing associations in South Ayrshire in (a) 2009, (b) 2010 and (c) 2011.

S3W-12199 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive how much funding will be allocated to South Ayrshire Council in (a) 2008, (b) 2009, (c) 2010 and (d) 2011 for the building of council houses.

S3W-12201 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many new schools, other than those already under construction, will be built in East Ayrshire in (a) 2008, (b) 2009, (c) 2010 and (d) 2011. 

S3W-12202 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many new schools, other than those already under construction, will be built in South Ayrshire in (a) 2008, (b)2009, (c) 2010 and (d) 2011.

S3W-12203 Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has proposed a pig compensation scheme to the European Commission.

S3W-12204 Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive why the Cabinet Secretary for Rural Affairs and the Environment felt unable to attempt to construct a successful case for headage payments for the pig sector.

S3W-12205 Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that the outbreak of foot-and-mouth disease in 2007 caused a welfare issue for the pig sector in respect of cull sows.

S3W-12206 Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive what study it has made of the French Government’s support package for pig farmers.

S3W-12207 Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on the breakdown of the French Government’s support package for pig farmers.

S3W-12208 Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on how much of the French Government’s support package for pig farmers will be available to individual farmers.

S3W-12209 Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has noted the conclusion of Commissioner Mariann Fischer Boel that the French Government’s support package for pig farmers appears valid under the "de minimis" rule.

S3W-12210 Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that there is scope for direct action to provide practical assistance to the Scottish pig industry.

S3W-12211 Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made of the possibility of introducing a soft loans scheme for Scottish pig farmers.

S3W-12212 Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive what direct action measures to assist the pig industry will be examined by the short-life task force on the industry.

S3W-12213 Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive what indirect action measures to assist the pig industry will be examined by the short-life task force on the industry.

S3W-12214 Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the short-life task force on the pig industry will be guided by the example of the French Government’s support package for pig farmers and the feasibility of introducing a similar measure in Scotland.

S3W-12215 Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the short-life task force on the pig industry will assess the feasibility of introducing a new on-goers scheme, with possible restructuring of the scheme’s definitions to circumvent the 10-year condition of the previous scheme, as referred to by the Cabinet Secretary for Rural Affairs and the Environment in evidence to the Rural Affairs and Environment committee on 16 April 2008.

S3W-12216 Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive what the remit is of the short-life task force on the pig industry.

S3W-12217 Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects the short-life task force on the pig industry to report.

S3W-12219 Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive how soon it expects to act on the recommendations of the short-life task force on the pig industry.

S3W-12220 Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive which stakeholders were invited to contribute to the short-life task force on the pig industry.

S3W-12221 Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive how it decided which stakeholders were invited to contribute to the short-life task force on the pig industry.

S3W-12222 Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has commissioned a long-term review from the Farmed Animal Welfare Council into whether pig produce from abroad meets the same welfare standards as Scottish pig produce, as referred to by the Cabinet Secretary in evidence to the Rural Affairs and Environment Committee on 16 April 2008.

S3W-12223 Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive how the review from the Farmed Animal Welfare Council into whether pig produce from abroad meets the same welfare standards as Scottish pig produce will contribute to the National Food Policy.

S3W-12224 Derek Brownlee: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-10895 by John Swinney on 19 March 2008, how much an increase in the UK income tax personal allowance of (a) £100, (b) £200 and (c) £500 would reduce the projected yield from local income tax for (i) 2008-09, (ii) 2009-10 and (iii) 2010-11.

S3W-12225 Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-11202 by Stewart Maxwell on 15 April 2008, how the savings of over £1 million per annum in staffing costs from the abolition of Communities Scotland will be secured.

S3W-12226 Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive whether, in proposals anent GP opening hours, consideration has been given to addressing the issue of patients who do not attend or cancel booked appointments.

S3W-12227 Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive whether, given that proposals anent GP opening hours will include other members of practice teams, hospital technicians will be available during evenings and weekends to process blood tests carried out during GPs’ clinics.

S3W-12228 David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Executive what guarantees it can provide that the commitment to halt the loss of Scotland’s biodiversity will be sufficiently funded, following the reduction in Scottish Natural Heritage’s budget, the loss of the Biodiversity Action Grants Scheme and the  pressure on  Scottish Rural Development Programme resources.

S3W-12229 David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied that the current funding levels for biodiversity will enable Scotland to meet the 2010 targets to halt to the loss of biodiversity.

S3W-12230 David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making towards developing plans to deliver the next phase of the Scottish Biodiversity Strategy by Spring 2008, as set out by the Cabinet Secretary for Rural Affairs and the Environment on 13 June 2007, and what funding will be made available to deliver those plans.

S3W-12231 Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Aberdeen Western Peripheral Route will be financed using the same method of public finance that will be used to fund the New Southern General Hospital.

S3W-12232 Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what the uptake of school meals by secondary pupils has been in each year since the introduction of the Hungry for Success: A Whole School Approach to School Meals in Scotland initiative, broken down by local authority.

S3W-12233 Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what the uptake of school meals by primary pupils has been in each year since the introduction of the Hungry for Success: A Whole School Approach to School Meals in Scotland initiative, broken down by local authority.

S3W-12234 Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to legislate to ban irresponsible promotions of alcohol.
