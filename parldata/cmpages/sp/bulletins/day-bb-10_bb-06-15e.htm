Business Bulletin No. 101/2010: Tuesday 15 June 2010
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 14 June 2010

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-34468 Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive what average price per house housing associations paid for land for housing in 2009.

S3W-34469 Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive what the timetable is for the evaluation of the Small Business Bonus Scheme in 2010.

S3W-34470 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-33993 by Roseanna Cunningham on 9 June 2010, how the responses to its consultation on the contents of guidance on applications for licences to control birds of prey were taken into account and how many of each organisation’s suggested amendments to the guidance were accepted.

S3W-34471 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-33993 by Roseanna Cunningham on 9 June 2010, how each of the organisations listed was consulted and how often discussions were held with each.

S3W-34472 Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-33970 by John Swinney on 9 June 2010, whether it will list the contracts that it has entered into that do not make specific reference to contractors complying with the national minimum wage.

S3W-34473 Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has considered making the living wage a requirement of its procurement practices.

S3W-34474 Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive what average price housing associations paid for the 1,699 off-the-shelf houses purchased in 2009-10.

S3W-34475 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Control of Dogs (Scotland) Act 2010 will be fully enacted.

S3W-34476 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will publish its guidance to local authorities on the implementation of the Control of Dogs (Scotland) Act 2010.

S3W-34477 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-33328 by Richard Lochhead on 5 May 2010, whether it considers that the 2008 amendment to the Scottish farmed salmon protected geographical indication describing the stages of the production process required to take place within the designated area is no longer valid or does not apply to non-organic salmon.

S3W-34478 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-33328 by Richard Lochhead on 5 May 2010, whether the unamended Scottish farmed salmon protected geographical indication describing stages of the production process that must take place within the designated area is no longer valid or whether the Scottish Government considers that all stages of the production process are required to take place within the designated area.

S3W-34479 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-33328 by Richard Lochhead on 5 May 2010, which stages of the production process for Scottish farmed salmon must take place in (a) Scotland and (b) the protected geographical indication-designated area in order for the product to be classified as Scottish farmed salmon.

S3W-34480 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to participate in the United Nations International Year of Cooperatives 2012.

S3W-34481 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive whether Scottish Enterprise will have a role in delivering activity during the United Nations International Year of Cooperatives 2012.

S3W-34482 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive whether VisitScotland will have a role in delivering activity during the United Nations International Year of Cooperatives 2012.

S3W-34483 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive which minister will be responsible for the delivery of activity during the United Nations International Year of Cooperatives 2012.

S3W-34484 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will deliver activities in schools to mark the United Nations International Year of Cooperatives 2012.

S3W-34485 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive when all applicants to the seventh round of the Rural Priorities scheme will be advised of the success or otherwise of their application.

S3W-34486 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive when a full list of the successful applicants to the seventh round of the Rural Priorities scheme will be made public.

S3W-34487 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive how many full-time, permanent procurators fiscal were employed as of 1 April 2009, broken down by area.

S3W-34488 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive how many full-time, permanent procurators fiscal were employed as of 1 April 2010, broken down by area.

S3W-34489 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to recruit additional procurators fiscal in 2010-11.

S3W-34490 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive how many full-time, permanent procurators fiscal it expects to employ as at 31 March 2011.

S3W-34491 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is planning for an increase in cases taken to court during 2010-11.

S3W-34492 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to manage a freeze on the number of cases taken to court in 2010-11.

S3W-34493 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to increase the number of fixed penalty fines in 2010-11.

S3W-34494 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive whether fixed penalty fines and other measures will be used to minimise the number of cases taken to court in 2010-11.

S3W-34495 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive when the intelligence-sharing service for public sector agencies to determine suitability of potential contractors that is being considered by the Serious Organised Crime and Procurement Sub Group will be operational and how it will operate.

S3W-34496 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Serious Organised Crime and Procurement Sub Group will report on the sectors of public procurement that are most vulnerable to exploitation by serious and organised crime groups.

S3W-34497 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress the Serious Organised Crime and Procurement Sub Group has made in scoping the feasibility of automating the validation process for previous convictions, in relation to which information is sought in the pre-qualification questionnaire for public contracts.

S3W-34498 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it can take in addition to the work being undertaken by the Serious Organised Crime and Procurement Sub Group to limit public sector contracts being exploited by serious and organised crime groups.
