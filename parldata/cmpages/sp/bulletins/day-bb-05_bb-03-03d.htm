Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 10 March 2005

Question Time

Enterprise, Lifelong Learning and Transport

1. Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive what performance indicators it has set for ministers with responsibility for developing the economy. (S2O-5684)

2. Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to increase the graduate endowment above the rate of inflation. (S2O-5722)

3. Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will ensure that the European Commission is provided speedily with the full information that it needs to complete the audit into ERDF funding in the Highlands and Islands. (S2O-5712)

4. Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to ensure that businesses have access to support and advice on energy efficiency, waste minimisation and improved resource efficiency. (S2O-5778)

5. Christine May: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it has made in its priority of growing the economy. (S2O-5757)

6. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to promote the development of wave and tidal power. (S2O-5758)

7. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met BEAR Scotland to discuss the Route Accident Reduction Plan for trunk roads in Fife. (S2O-5717)

8. Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to extend opportunities to enter the workforce to people with disabilities. (S2O-5723)

9. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance it has given to Scottish Enterprise about the level of fees paid to celebrities for appearances at events organised by the Scottish Enterprise Network. (S2O-5676)

10. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to improve road safety on the trunk road network. (S2O-5753)

11. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the viability of Heriot Watt University maintaining its campus in Galashiels and on the consequences for Borders College should the shared campus proposals fall through. (S2O-5710)

12. Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making in relation to the introduction of the national concessionary travel scheme for older and disabled people. (S2O-5772)

Justice and Law Officers

1. Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it has taken to improve the way victims are notified about the release of offenders from prison. (S2O-5746)

2. Mark Ballard: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made on security arrangements for the G8 summit. (S2O-5769)

3. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is publicising the campaign to encourage reporting drug dealing by telephoning Crimestoppers 0800 555 111. (S2O-5743)

4. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is monitoring incidences of arrest warrants not being executed because delays in processing have lead to them overrunning their time limits. (S2O-5721)

5. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive what average time is served by female prisoners serving life sentences. (S2O-5679)

6. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what sanctions it takes through the justice system against those who supply alcohol to, or purchase alcohol on behalf of, under-18s. (S2O-5699)

7. Mr Mark Ruskell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make a ministerial statement on security arrangements for the G8 summit. (S2O-5771)

8. Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive what facilities are available for deaf people in courts. (S2O-5728)

9. Christine May: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will publish guidance on good practice in tackling antisocial behaviour. (S2O-5748)

10. Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied with security arrangements for the G8 summit. (S2O-5767)

11. Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to review the guidelines for the siting of speed cameras. (S2O-5695)

12. Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it supports the right of householders to defend their property from intruders. (S2O-5701)

General

1. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take in respect of its 32 alleged breaches of European environmental law currently being investigated by the European Commission. (S2O-5788)

2. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has had any discussions with the Scottish Agricultural Wages Board about its powers to investigate the employment of casual labour in agricultural businesses. (S2O-5690)

3. Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to improve the quality of care and treatment for people with asthma to enable them to lead their lives free from the symptoms of asthma. (S2O-5708)

4. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that the answer to question 208258 by Dr Denis MacShane MP on 17 January 2005 shows that the European Constitution does not ensure the involvement of the Scottish Executive and the Parliament in the EU legislative process to a greater degree than at present. (S2O-5681)

5. Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is aware of any up-to-date studies relating to teacher stress in the educational system. (S2O-5675)

6. Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will report on progress made by the short life working group on dementia. (S2O-5715)

7. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it intends to evaluate the impact of its media campaign to promote race equality and tackle racism. (S2O-5789)

8. Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what further action it is taking to support the installation of new generation artificial football pitches in Scotland. (S2O-5693)

9. Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will reform planning guidelines to ensure that new housing includes provision for kerbside collection of recyclable material. (S2O-5688)

10. Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance it has issued to education authorities since March 2003 regarding the safe use of the internet in schools. (S2O-5726)

11. Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance has been issued to education authorities on staffing levels for providing special needs education. (S2O-5686)

12. Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the recently-announced review of NHS 24 will consider whether NHS 24 is the appropriate vehicle for delivering out-of-hours GP services. (S2O-5702)

13. Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is in respect of the implications for health of living near high voltage transmission lines. (S2O-5733)

14. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what professional development opportunities currently exist for nurses wishing to specialise in ophthalmology. (S2O-5687)

15. Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive why it is under investigation by the European Commission for allegedly breaching environmental law in 32 cases. (S2O-5791)
