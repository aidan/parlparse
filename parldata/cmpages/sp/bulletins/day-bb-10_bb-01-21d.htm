Business Bulletin No. 13/2010: Thursday 21 January 2010
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D – Oral questions selected for answer on 28 January 2010

Question Time

General

1. Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to promote Scottish produce. (S3O-9343)

2. Ted Brocklebank: To ask the Scottish Executive when it next plans to meet NHS Fife. (S3O-9261)

3. Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to make decisions on the future development of forensic and fingerprint services. (S3O-9293)

4. Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether secondary schools receiving funding under the school building programme are being replaced on a like-for-like basis, including space for sports and community centres, and, if not, what the reasons are for its position on this matter. (S3O-9321)

5. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what support is available for colleges to cope with additional demand for places. (S3O-9320)

6. Maureen Watt: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact it considers a supermarket ombudsman will have on farmers and consumers. (S3O-9352)

7. Anne McLaughlin: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the use of Naloxone for problem drug users. (S3O-9345)

8. Elizabeth Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it is giving to help landscape partnerships access rural priority funding. (S3O-9256)

9. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that the Protection of Children and Prevention of Sexual Offences (Scotland) Act 2005 is being applied effectively. (S3O-9325)

10. Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive how it supports housing co-operatives. (S3O-9334)

Finance and Sustainable Growth

1. Christina McKelvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what South Lanarkshire Council’s financial commitment to PFI/PPP contracts is expected to be in 2010-11 and 2011-12. (S3O-9347)

2. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions the Cabinet Secretary for Finance and Sustainable Growth has had with Glasgow City Council regarding the allocation of additional funding to tackle deprivation. (S3O-9297)

3. Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Cabinet Secretary for Finance and Sustainable Growth last met housing sector representatives to discuss the 2010-11 budget. (S3O-9299)

4. Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Cabinet Secretary for Finance and Sustainable Growth last met COSLA and what was discussed. (S3O-9288)

5. Bill Aitken: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met Strathclyde Partnership for Transport and what issues were discussed. (S3O-9265)

6. Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Cabinet Secretary for Finance and Sustainable Growth has discussed with the Minister for Housing and Communities proposals to change its guidance explaining ministerial priorities for the Fairer Scotland Fund. (S3O-9295)

7. Shirley-Anne Somerville: To ask the Scottish Executive what reports have been received from Network Rail and First ScotRail regarding performance in the operation of train services and infrastructure during the recent period of severe weather. (S3O-9350)

8. Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what its preferred option is for improvements at the Haudagain roundabout and when work on this will commence. (S3O-9269)

9. Elaine Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions the Cabinet Secretary for Finance and Sustainable Growth has had with North Lanarkshire Council during the budget process about provision of essential services. (S3O-9306)

10. Jamie Hepburn: To ask the Scottish Executive when Transport Scotland expects to publish the results of its Developing Rail Freight in Scotland consultation. (S3O-9340)

11. Charlie Gordon: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria it uses in deciding to fund some rail projects from borrowing via Network Rail’s regulated asset base. (S3O-9282)

12. Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what it would cost to introduce a boiler scrappage scheme similar to that in place in England and Wales. (S3O-9294)

13. Andy Kerr: To ask the Scottish Executive how much money has been committed to the Glasgow Airport Rail Link. (S3O-9287)

14. Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what work is required to reinforce the Dounreay to Beauly high voltage power line and when it is envisaged that this work will start. (S3O-9337)

15. Cathie Craigie: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason the budget for public service reform and efficiency has increased to £23.9 million in the 2010-11 draft budget. (S3O-9275)

16. Not Lodged

17. Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent discussions it has had with East Ayrshire Council with regard to the council’s capital spending programme. (S3O-9285)

18. Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive, in addition to the responses given to the Economy, Energy and Tourism Committee on 13 January 2010 and in light of a subsequent meeting between ministers and Scottish and Southern Energy, under what circumstances additional statutory consents will be required to allow the proposed Beauly to Denny power line development to proceed. (S3O-9311)

19. David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Executive what negotiations it has had with the European Union to extend the Public Services Obligation contract for air services to Barra. (S3O-9307)

20. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to have discussions with the Welsh Assembly Government over the potential for direct rail services between Edinburgh and Cardiff. (S3O-9326)
