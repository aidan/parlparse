Business Bulletin No. 73/2010: Tuesday 4 May 2010
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section A – Today’s Business

Meetings of Committees

All meetings take place in the Scottish Parliament, unless otherwise specified. Contact details for Committee Clerks are provided at the end of the Bulletin.

Equal Opportunities Committee
8th Meeting, 2010

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in Committee Room 1

1. Decision on taking business in private: The Committee will decide whether to take item 5 in private. The Committee will also decide whether its consideration of the main themes arising from the evidence heard and a draft report on its inquiry into migration and trafficking should be taken in private at future meetings.

2. Budget Strategy Phase 2011-12: The Committee will take evidence at the Budget Strategy Phase 2011-12 from—

John Swinney MSP, Cabinet Secretary for Finance and Sustainable Growth, Russell Bain, Head of Public Service Reform Policy Team, and Yvonne Strachan, Head of Equality Unit, Scottish Government.

3. Inquiry into migration and trafficking: The Committee will take evidence from—

Deputy Chief Constable Gordon Meldrum, Chair, Serious Organised Crime Portfolio, ACPOS;
Lorraine Cook, Policy Officer, COSLA’s Strategic Migration Partnership;
Helen Baillot, Senior Asylum Support Adviser, and Simon Hodgson, Director of Policy & Communication, Scottish Refugee Council;
Brian Craven, acting Lead Manager, United Kingdom Human Trafficking Centre;

and then from—

Michael Emberson, Chief Executive Officer, Migrant Helpline;
Abigail Stepnitz, National Coordinator, the Poppy Project;
Simon Chorley, Advocacy & Partnerships Officer, Stop the Traffik.

4. Annual report: The Committee will consider a draft annual report for the parliamentary year from 9 May 2009 to 8 May 2010.

5. Inquiry into migration and trafficking: The Committee will consider the main themes arising from the evidence heard.

6. Budget Strategy 2011-12 (in private): The Committee will consider the main themes arising from the evidence heard.

Justice Committee
15th Meeting, 2010

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in Committee Room 4

1. Criminal Justice and Licensing (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will consider the Bill at Stage 2 (Day 6).

European and External Relations Committee
7th Meeting, 2010

The Committee will meet at 10.30 am in Committee Room 2

1. Decision on taking business in private: The Committee will decide whether to take item 5 and future consideration of a draft report on its Treaty of Lisbon inquiry in private.

2. Decision on taking business in private: The Committee will decide whether to take items 6 and 7 in private.

3. Scottish Government correspondence: The Committee will consider correspondence received from the Scottish Government.

4. Brussels Bulletin: The Committee will consider the Brussels Bulletin.

5. Treaty of Lisbon inquiry: The Committee will consider the key issues arising from its inquiry.

6. Annual report: The Committee will consider a draft annual report for the parliamentary year from 9 May 2009 to 8 May 2010.

7. Informal planning session: The Committee will consider its approach to its informal planning session on 18 May.

Transport, Infrastructure and Climate Change Committee
12th Meeting, 2010

The Committee will meet at 1.45 pm in Committee Room 2

1. Decision on taking business in private: The Committee will decide whether to take item 3 and any future consideration of draft reports on the Budget Strategy Phase 2011-12 in private.

2. Budget Strategy Phase 2011-12: The Committee will take evidence at the Budget Strategy Phase 2011-12 from—

John Barrett, Senior Research Associate, Stockholm Environment Institute;
Phil Matthews, Senior Policy Advisor, Sustainable Development Commission Scotland;

and then from—

Dave Watson, Scottish Organiser, UNISON;
Duncan McLaren, Chief Executive, Friends of the Earth Scotland;
Chas Booth, Senior Press and Parliamentary Officer, Association for the Conservation of Energy;

and then from—

Stewart Stevenson MSP, Minister for Transport, Infrastructure, and Climate Change, David Middleton, Chief Executive, Transport Scotland, Jonathan Pryce, Director of Transport, and Gavin Barrie, Head of Unit, Climate Change Policy, Scottish Government.

3. Budget Strategy Phase 2011-12: The Committee will consider the evidence heard during the Budget Strategy Phase 2011-12.

Finance Committee
12th Meeting, 2010

The Committee will meet at 2.00 pm in Committee Room 4

1. Budget Strategy Phase 2011-12: The Committee will take evidence on its inquiry into efficient public services from—

Ruth Hancock, Professor in the Economics of Health and Welfare, University of East Anglia;
Dr Michael Harris, National Endowment for Science, Technology and the Arts;
Brian McKechnie, Senior Studies Institute, University of Strathclyde.

2. Scottish Parliamentary Commissions and Commissioners etc. Bill: The Committee will consider the Bill at Stage 2.

3. Work programme (in private): The Committee will consider its work programme.

7th Meeting, 2010

PE1323 Community Centres for Scottish Shia Muslims

PE812 Stirling before pylons

PE1159 Healthcare for Gulf war veterans
PE1179 Acquired brain injury services
PE1189 Standards of social rented housing

PE1249 Party flats
PE1252 Convicted police officers

PE1257 Solicitors acting as court reporters

4. Forthcoming work programme: The Committee will consider a paper by the Clerk.

Subordinate Legislation Committee
14th Meeting, 2010

The Committee will meet at 2.15 pm in Committee Room 6

1. Draft instruments subject to approval: The Committee will consider the following—

the Climate Change (International Aviation and Shipping) (Scotland) Order 2010 (SSI2010/draft);
the Protection of Vulnerable Groups (Scotland) Act 2007 (Automatic Listing) (Specified Criteria) Order 2010 (SSI2010/draft);
the Protection of Vulnerable Groups (Scotland) Act 2007 (Modification of Regulated Work with Adults) Order 2010 (SSI2010/draft);
the Protection of Vulnerable Groups (Scotland) Act 2007 (Modification of Regulated Work with Children) Order 2010 (SSI2010/draft);
the Protection of Vulnerable Groups (Scotland) Act 2007 (Relevant Offences) (Modification) Order 2010 (SSI2010/draft).

2. Instruments subject to annulment: The Committee will consider the following—

the Additional Support for Learning (Co-ordinated Support Plan) (Scotland) Amendment Regulations 2010 (SSI2010/149);
the Parole Board (Scotland) Amendment Rules 2010 (SSI2010/164);
the Victims’ Rights (Prescribed Bodies) (Scotland) Order 2010 (SSI2010/165).
