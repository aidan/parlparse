*S1M-2791 Mr Duncan Hamilton: Travis Success At Brit Awards—That the Parliament congratulates Travis for its success at the recent Brit Awards in winning the Best British Band category; notes the tremendous achievements of Travis and other Scottish bands in the music industry, and looks forward to more success from Scottish bands in the future.

*S1M-2790# Mr Jamie McGrigor: Environmentally Designated Areas—That the Parliament notes with alarm concerns expressed by local communities highlighted by recent petitions to the public petitions committee (PE462 to 464) about procedures and scientific data used by Scottish Natural Heritage (SNH) for environmentally designated areas, such as Special Protection Areas, Special Areas of Conservation and Sites of Special Scientific Interest and believes that a programme of comprehensive consultation will result in more effective participation and co-operation between SNH and local people within the proposed areas of designation, which will result in better relations between SNH and local communities.

Supported by: Phil Gallie*, Murdo Fraser*, Mr David Davidson*, Alex Fergusson*, John Scott*, John Young*, Mrs Lyndsay McIntosh*

*S1M-2789 Robin Harper: Protection of Scottish Coral Reefs—That the Parliament notes with concern the recent research from the Marine Biological Station on Cumbrae, published by the Royal Society, which shows that large and diverse coral reefs off the north-west of Scotland which are more than 4,500 years old and the habitat of diverse and unique marine species are suffering wide-scale fishing damage and requests that the Scottish Executive and Her Majesty’s Government take urgent action to protect the "Darwin Mounds" and other environmentally significant areas of deep-sea coral, including designating them as marine Special Areas of Conservation.

*S1M-2788# Angus MacKay: The Tourism Industry—That the Parliament recognises that the economy of Edinburgh and the whole of Scotland depends greatly on not only attracting tourists but also encouraging them to return; further recognises the importance of tourist accommodation and attractions being of the highest possible quality, and considers that the Scottish Executive should encourage and support the maintenance of high standards in the tourism industry to ensure that visitors to Scotland have a memorable experience and return.

*S1M-2787 Mr Gil Paterson: Stop Indecent Displays in Corner Shops—That the Parliament recognises that the public sale and display of pornographic material in our local stores, newsagents and garages is completely unacceptable; further recognises that pornography eroticises violence against women and children and desensitises the public to the meaning of violence towards women in real life, and calls on the Executive to ensure that the Civic Government (Scotland) Act 1982 is fully and effectively enforced and to review this legislation to ensure that it is adequate.

*S1M-2786# Miss Annabel Goldie: Renfrewshire Economy—That the Parliament notes with pleasure the very positive Special Report on the Renfrewshire economy in business a.m. on 25 February 2002; congratulates all agencies involved in creating this economic success, and commends the Renfrewshire model to the rest of Scotland as an example of good practice.

Supported by: David Mundell*, Murdo Fraser*, Phil Gallie*, John Young*, Mr Murray Tosh*, Mrs Lyndsay McIntosh*, Mr Jamie Stone*

*S1M-2785# Elaine Smith: World Day for Water - 22 March 2002—That the Parliament notes the resolution A/RES/47/193 adopted by the United Nations General Assembly on 22 December 1992, which declared 22 March of each year as World Day for Water; further notes that this year the highly pertinent theme is "Water and Health"; expresses concern that many of our children do not have access to free drinking water whilst at school; commends North Lanarkshire Council and the South Coatbridge Social Inclusion Partnership for initiating pilot schemes in four Coatbridge primary schools to supply free, accessible drinking water to pupils, and considers that the Scottish Executive should recognise and promote the importance for our children’s health and well-being of providing free, accessible drinking water in all schools.

Supported by: Mr Kenneth Gibson*

*S1M-2784 Johann Lamont: International Women's Day - 8 March 2002—That the Parliament notes that 8 March 2002 is International Women's Day; congratulates the Women Together group in the Pollok constituency and women across Scotland and around the world who are organising events to celebrate the work of women past, present and future; recognises the central role of women in our communities in meeting needs today and creating change for tomorrow and beyond, and urges the Scottish Executive and the Parliament's committees, in partnership with women's organisations, to examine how women's needs and rights might more effectively be addressed across the range of services, departments and organisations that impact on women's lives.

Supported by: Sarah Boyack*, Mr Kenneth Gibson*, Brian Fitzpatrick*

*S1M-2783# Marilyn Livingstone: National Strategy for Survivors of Childhood Sexual Abuse—That the Parliament welcomes the work of the Cross-Party Group on Survivors of Childhood Sexual Abuse in co-ordinating and developing an outline national strategy for children and adults who have experienced sexual abuse during childhood and, in light of recommendations made by the independent inquiry commissioned by Fife Council following the conviction of David Logan Murphy, believes that the Scottish Executive should actively develop a national strategy, based on the work of the Cross-Party Group on Survivors of Childhood Sexual Abuse, that considers the wider social, health, mental and legal implications of sexual abuse and its impact on survivors and society as a whole.

Supported by: Mr Kenneth Gibson*
