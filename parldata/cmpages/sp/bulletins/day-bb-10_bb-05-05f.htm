Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally printed the day after they are lodged. If an amendment is lodged to a motion then the original motion will appear alongside the amendment, along with any support lodged that day, in this section.

Each Monday, this section also contains all motions and amendments lodged the previous week that are still live and any motions and amendments that have been lodged for debate in the coming week. A motion or amendment is live if it has not been debated, withdrawn or otherwise deleted from the list.

Where a motion or amendment has been withdrawn, it is indicated in this section the day after it has been withdrawn. Support for motions and amendments received after they are lodged is shown at the
end of this section the day after such support is received.

Motions and amendments can be published with various symbols:

  • Asterisks before the motion or amendment number indicate a motion or amendment published for the first time;

  • Asterisks also identify alterations to the text of a motion or amendment made since it was first published;

  • A hash symbol identifies motions eligible for debate at Members’ Business;

  • A diamond symbol identifies motions lodged for Members’ Business that have not yet attracted the required cross-party support;

  • An "R", identifies motions or amendments in which the Member who lodged it has a registrable interest.

The Parliamentary Bureau periodically deletes motions or amendments not scheduled for debate and which are over six weeks old.

A full list of current motions is available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/motions.htm.

A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/index.htm.

Any questions in relation to this section should be directed to the Chamber Desk, for which contact details are provided at the end of the Bulletin.

*S3M-6252 Ian McKee: Edinburgh Best Tourist Destination in Britain—That the Parliament notes with interest the recent TripAdvisor poll of millions of travellers worldwide that puts Edinburgh at the top of the 25 most popular tourist destinations in the United Kingdom, 21st in the world and seventh-best European nightlife destination; believes that this is yet more evidence of a successful tourism industry that has continued to attract tourists from across the globe despite the economic recession; considers that Edinburgh’s hospitality and tourism sectors are rising to the challenge in making Scotland’s capital one of the most visited cities in the world, and hopes that the survey will encourage other Scottish cities to compete and sell themselves as international destinations of choice.

Supported by: Bill Kidd*

*S3M-6251 Anne McLaughlin: Congratulations to GESH—That the Parliament congratulates the Greater Easterhouse Supporting Hands project (GESH) on being shortlisted as one of three finalists for the Scottish Government’s Community Service Award for improving the local community; notes that GESH is run entirely by an army of dedicated volunteers who work tirelessly to make their community a better place; commends the excellent work that they do in helping some of the most disadvantaged communities in Glasgow, and wishes the project every success for the future.

Supported by: Rob Gibson*, Bob Doris*, Margaret Mitchell*, Sandra White*, Hugh O’Donnell*, Gil Paterson*, Kenneth Gibson*, Bill Kidd*

*S3M-6250 Gavin Brown: Edinburgh Sevens Festival—That the Parliament congratulates Scottish Rugby, the organisers of the IRB Edinburgh Sevens festival, which is to be held at Murrayfield on 29 and 30 May 2010; believes that the sevens tournament is a fantastic advert for rugby in Scotland; notes that Scotland rugby legends, Roy Laidlaw and John Rutherford, are the festival’s ambassadors; considers that this event enhances Edinburgh and Murrayfield’s reputation as a world-class destination for events; welcomes the significant boost to the Edinburgh economy that the annual tournament brings, and wishes the Scotland Sevens team all the best of luck in the attempts to improve on its outstanding cup semi-final finish in the 2009 event.

*S3M-6249 Hugh Henry: Communication Workers Union Ballot—That the Parliament welcomes the vote of the Communication Workers Union to agree a three-year deal that will give Royal Mail staff a wage rise worth 6.9% and job security; hopes that this will lead to improved industrial relations; recognises the willingness of staff to actually participate in the transformation of a publicly owned Royal Mail, and hopes that early steps will be taken to resolve the issue of the Royal Mail pension debt.

*S3M-6248 Gil Paterson: Beardmore Hotel Strikes Gold at Scottish Hotel Oscars—That the Parliament congratulates the Beardmore Hotel in Clydebank on being named the Scottish Business Hotel of the Year 2010; praises the staff and management at the hotel for all their hard work, and wishes them every success for the year ahead.

*S3M-6247 Dave Thompson: Buckie Thistle, 2010 Highland League Champions—That the Parliament congratulates Buckie Thistle on its success at being crowned Highland League Champions 2010; notes that this is its first Highland League title since 1958, winning 3-0 against Wick on Saturday 1 May 2010; hails this as a historic achievement for the town and club, which adds further testimony to a strong showing from Highland football over recent weeks, and finally wishes these dedicated players, who train evenings and weekends in addition to full-time jobs, the best of luck in their last game against Brora.

Supported by: Rob Gibson*, Kenneth Gibson*, Bill Kidd*, Nigel Don*

*S3M-6246 Robin Harper: Restrictions on Access to Books for Schools—That the Parliament urges the Scottish Government to review the present arrangements for the centralised procurement of books for school libraries on the grounds that it is considered that the arrangements will be to the severe disadvantage of small suppliers across Scotland, will restrict the range of books available, will in effect restrict the ability of schools to inspire and challenge Scotland’s young people through reading and exploring new ideas and the full range of writing that should be available to them and will diminish and restrict the freedom of thought and action of teachers and librarians across the country in a fashion not acceptable to a free society.

*S3M-6245 Patrick Harvie: Govanhill Streetland Festival—That the Parliament congratulates the hard working organisers of Govanhill’s Streetland festival, which took place over May Day weekend, on a highly successful event; notes that Streetland has been a highlight of the excellent Glasgow International Festival of Visual Art, and thanks all the artists, poets, musicians, cooks, DJs, tour guides, cake bakers and hula-hoopers who generously gave their time and talents to turn Govanhill into a buzzing centre of community spirit, culture and colour.

*S3M-6244 Ross Finnie: Welcoming the Retention of the Inverclyde League of Hospital Friends—That the Parliament congratulates all the volunteers involved in the Inverclyde League of Hospital Friends, and the local community of Inverclyde, on their success in persuading NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde to reverse its previous decision and allow the League of Hospital Friends to continue its fantastic charity work of providing the tea bar service at Inverclyde Royal Hospital, raising funds to purchase vital additional equipment for the hospital and providing its vital volunteer transport service.
