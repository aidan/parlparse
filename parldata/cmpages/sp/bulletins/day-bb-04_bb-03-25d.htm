Business Bulletin No. 55 / 2004

Thursday 25th March 2004

Section D : Oral questions selected for answer on Thursday 1st April 2004
  

Question Time

Enterprise, Transport and Lifelong Learning

1. Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it is giving to the science centres in the four main cities. (S2O-1884)

2. John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the Highland Council regarding the completion of the southern distributor route linking the A9 to the A82. (S2O-1866)

3. Miss Annabel Goldie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is intended to create any new accesses to, or egresses from, that part of the M8 that passes through Renfrewshire. (S2O-1840)

4. Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in the action to recover the £4 million spent on repairs to the Erskine Bridge following the incident involving an offshore floating structure. (S2O-1817)

5. Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive what changes will require to be made in higher education to ensure that it is competitive in the European Higher Education Area. (S2O-1883)

6. Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to ensure that industry benefits fully from the commercialisation of wave power. (S2O-1938)

7. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on plans to reorganise Glasgow's further education colleges. (S2O-1929)

8. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it will provide for improving access at Haymarket station. (S2O-1850)

9. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive whether all budgets have been set for local enterprise companies. (S2O-1880)

10. Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has made to Cogent, the Sector Skills Council, in respect of the promotion of diversification into new technology and more sustainable production in the oil and gas sector. (S2O-1934)

11. Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what relative financial contributions it and the Strategic Rail Authority will make to the upgrade at Waverley Station. (S2O-1865)

12. Mr Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive when further improvement of the A830 trunk road from Arisaig to Loch nan Uamh will commence, following the recent commemoration of the A830 trunk road from Arisaig to Kinsadel improvement. (S2O-1879)

Justice and Law Officers

1. Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive, following the closure of the Airborne Initiative, what programmes it supports that provide residential care for young people and are designed to divert them from crime and addiction problems. (S2O-1924)

2. Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether security will be increased at consulates and other major buildings in Edinburgh as a result of recent world events and what the cost of such increased security would be. (S2O-1869)

3. Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive what support is available to those freed from prison after a miscarriage of justice. (S2O-1841)

4. Miss Annabel Goldie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will deliver on its commitment to reduce the public fear of crime and to monitor it statistically. (S2O-1826)

5. Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive what legal restrictions exist on direct home deliveries of alcoholic drinks. (S2O-1913)

6. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of the recent announcement by the Home Secretary that additional funding will be made available to the police to tackle terrorism, whether Scottish police forces will receive any additional funding. (S2O-1953)

7. Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to implement the provision in the Partnership Agreement to provide sufficient secure accommodation and allow children who might otherwise be in secure accommodation to remain in the community through the use of electronic tagging. (S2O-1855)

8. Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it has made in ending slopping out in all prisons. (S2O-1910)

9. John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive what action police forces are taking to tackle livestock theft. (S2O-1868)

10. Tricia Marwick: To ask the Scottish Executive when it next intends to meet Fife Constabulary. (S2O-1909)

11. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to reduce the number of offences committed by individuals already granted bail. (S2O-1904)

12. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is considering any early release schemes, in light of overcrowding in prisons. (S2O-1925)

General

1. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to increase the level of blood donations in Scotland. (S2O-1891)

2. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the indicators that underpin the funding formula for local government are being reviewed. (S2O-1861)

3. Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the importance of rural schools in maintaining sustainable rural communities. (S2O-1827)

4. Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the NHS regional planning groups have made any progress in making Mohs micrographic surgery more widely available. (S2O-1821)

5. Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment has been made of the social and economic impact on rural communities of the rural post office network. (S2O-1890)

6. Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects the results of farm scale trials on genetically modified winter-sown oilseed rape. (S2O-1951)

7. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will establish a scheme for housing key workers in areas of high economic pressure, such as Edinburgh. (S2O-1902)

8. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are any plans to extend its anti-smoking campaign to young people, particularly young women. (S2O-1843)

9. George Lyon: To ask the Scottish Executive how small tourism providers will engage with the new tourist hubs. (S2O-1887)

10. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Care Commission will be self-funding. (S2O-1923)

11. Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether coastal super quarries are still seen as an essential part of Scottish mineral development policies. (S2O-1856)

12. Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it has taken to address concerns about guidelines on school rationalisation since its letter of 12 March 2003 to the Education, Culture and Sport Committee and what impact such action will have on the current consultation on school closures in the rural communities of Midlothian. (S2O-1886)

13. Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact television has upon culture and what strategy it has in place to use television to promote Scotland. (S2O-1870)

14. Colin Fox: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the decision to close six schools in Edinburgh and five schools in Midlothian has been taken with the best educational interests of children in mind. (S2O-1892)

15. Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many vessels constitute the Scottish white fish fleet. (S2O-1888)
