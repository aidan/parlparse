Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an “R”.

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

*S2M-3688 Frances Curran: Save Leven Cottage Care Home—That the Parliament condemns the decision of West Dunbartonshire Council to close Leven Cottage care home to save money; notes that it is the only council-run home left in the Vale of Leven and has lost patience with elderly people’s rights being trampled under foot; supports 75-year-old Robert Toole and respects his right to refuse to leave what is his home of 10 years, and further supports local campaigners taking part in a sit-in in support of Robert and to prevent the boarding up and demolition of the home.

*S2M-3687 Frances Curran: People Power Works—That the Parliament congratulates the parents, pupils, staff, trade unions and local community of Renton and Christie Park primary schools in West Dunbartonshire which the council had earmarked for closure but which have been saved by a grass roots campaign forcing the council to think again and notes that this campaign highlights yet again that people power and grass roots campaigning can win.

*S2M-3686 Frances Curran: Stop Violence Against Women in East Renfrewshire—That the Parliament welcomes the launch of the East Renfrewshire Domestic Abuse Website which coincided with the 16 days of activism to end violence against women; acknowledges the need to tackle violence against women wherever it is exposed, and further acknowledges that, although progress is being made, there is still a long way to go in raising understanding especially amongst those in public life.

*S2M-3685 Marilyn Livingstone: 50th Anniversary of NCH Scotland—That the Parliament congratulates NCH Scotland, the children’s charity, on its 50th anniversary; welcomes the anniversary event in the Parliament’s garden lobby on 13 December 2005, with 7:84 Theatre Outreach and Community Action Group showcasing the talent of young people in Scotland; recognises the value of the work NCH undertakes through its 63 projects and services across Scotland in helping some of the most vulnerable children and young people in our society achieve their full potential and make the best of their lives, and further recognises the major contribution NCH Scotland makes across a wide variety of services, including family centres that support families living in some of the most deprived areas in Scotland, respite centres that support families of children with disabilities, residential and support services for young homeless people, foster care projects that are finding family homes for young people in difficulty, criminal justice services that are helping to reduce youth offending, and services that provide support to young people who are the main carers for sick or disabled relatives.

*S2M-3684 Mr Andy Kerr: Future Arrangements for Health Services in the Argyll and Clyde Area—That the Parliament supports the decisive action announced by the Scottish Executive on 19 May 2005 to dissolve Argyll and Clyde NHS Board and to redraw the boundaries of the neighbouring boards to take over its responsibilities from April 2006; believes that these steps will address the board’s structural and financial problems; applauds the continued successful efforts of staff in NHS Argyll and Clyde to maintain comprehensive healthcare services for the people of the area; notes that a full public consultation has been held on the boundary option to be adopted, and supports NHS Highland and NHS Greater Glasgow in their task of returning services to financial balance while maintaining high standards of quality and access.

Supported by: Lewis Macdonald*

*S2M-3683 Colin Fox: Support for Union Members at First Bus in Aberdeen—That the Parliament supports the Transport and General Workers Union members at First Bus in Aberdeen who have taken industrial action in pursuit of a pay claim; believes that the strike action reflects a widespread discontent among drivers in Aberdeen about their low levels of pay and proposed changes to pension rights and working conditions; believes that there is widespread anger at the glaring disparity within the different areas of First Group in relation to drivers’ wage rates; notes that in a ballot drivers voted by 96% to support the current strike action; notes that similar action at Lothian Regional Transport and First Bus South East Scotland earlier this year resulted in an improved pay offer for workers there, and regrets the considerable inconvenience the strike has caused to the general public but believes that the onus is on the company to come forward with an improved offer to end the dispute, one which does not involve a reduction in the present conditions of employment of drivers.

Supported by: Carolyn Leckie*

*S2M-3682 Rob Gibson: Caithness Maternity Saved!—That the Parliament congratulates the successful, persistent and popular campaign by the North Action Group to secure consultant-led maternity services in Wick that can now serve the people of Caithness and north Sutherland for the foreseeable future; notes the role of the Kerr report which seeks to establish a workplan that contributes to the recently accepted national priority of sustaining rural general hospitals; wishes the board of NHS Highland success in seeking to recruit three permanent consultant obstetricians to be based in Caithness; welcomes the aim of catering for the work needs of clinicians’ partners; notes that the communities’ wishes will be met by the appointment of these consultants to back up a midwife team whose task is to provide quality services that are women and family-centred, essentially community-based and midwife-managed while demonstrating a shared philosophy of care irrespective of risk, and hopes that this will encourage young families to remain in the far north and attract more economically active people to choose to live and work there.

Supported by: Jim Mather*, Richard Lochhead*, Mrs Nanette Milne*, Brian Adam*

*S2M-3681 Ms Sandra White: Lack of Scrutiny of Scottish Ministers—That the Parliament notes the continuing poor quality of ministerial answers to parliamentary questions; notes that the regular inability of ministers to provide adequate answers to questions by MSPs is detrimental to democratic accountability and scrutiny and is undermining the parliamentary process, and calls on the Scottish Executive to embrace democracy fully in order to become truly accountable, transparent and open in the range of areas for which it has direct responsibility by establishing a central statistical department which can collate and ultimately monitor the performance of Scottish ministers.

*S2M-3680 Carolyn Leckie: Congratulations to John Aberdein—That the Parliament congratulates John Aberdein for winning the Saltire Society/Royal Mail Scottish First Book of the Year Award for Amande’s Bed;believes that this book marks an important milestone in promoting the dialects and languages of Scotland and in enriching Scotland’s culture, and believes that Scottish literary works should be encouraged, promoted and protected from big business interests which undermine diversity and lead to the encroachment of a monoculture.

*S2M-3674 Cathie Craigie: Celebrating 25 Years of TPAS Scotland—That the Parliament congratulates the Tenant Participation Advisory Service (TPAS) in its 25th year; notes its successful recent conference with a record number of delegates; recognises the work TPAS Scotland has done over the years in promoting tenants’ interests and providing training for tenants to ensure that they are aware of their rights, and hopes that TPAS Scotland goes from strength to strength over the next 25 years.

*S2M-3673 Alex Neil: Anniversary of Finland’s Independence—That the Parliament sends its cordial best wishes to Finland which celebrates its 88th year of independence from Russia on 6 December 2005; notes that this independent country is able to celebrate its national day with a public holiday; congratulates the country for the massive improvements it has made to public health, and looks forward to the day that Scotland can also celebrate its independence and has the powers of a proper parliament to tackle adequately poverty and poor health.

*S2M-3672 Chris Ballance: CIA Rendition Flights—That the Parliament considers that there are sufficient grounds to justify a full police inquiry into the possible use of Scottish airports by CIA rendition flights; believes that the Lord Advocate should instruct the police to instigate that inquiry without delay, and further believes that he should instruct the police to conduct searches of planes landing at Scottish airports suspected of being operated by the CIA to ensure that no one aboard is being tortured, being transported in an inhumane manner or being illegally detained against their will.

*S2M-3671 Mr Kenneth Macintosh: Children’s Cancer Awareness Month—That the Parliament notes that December is Children’s Cancer Awareness Month; recognises the medical, emotional and financial impact that cancer and leukaemia have on children and young people and on their families, friends and carers; congratulates CLIC Sargent for its work as the only national charity dedicated solely to raising awareness of cancer and leukaemia in children and young people and providing clinical, emotional and financial support services to child cancer patients and their families, whether in hospital, at home or in the community across Scotland, and urges the Scottish Executive to raise public awareness of cancer in children and young people and to ensure that research into effective treatment of cancer and leukaemia is adequately funded.

*S2M-3670.1 Margaret Mitchell: Violence Against Women—As an amendment to motion (S2M-3670) in the name of Malcolm Chisholm, insert after “Rape Crisis Specific Fund;” “recognises the expertise and contribution the voluntary sector can provide in dealing with domestic abuse; acknowledges that usually the best outcome for women and children is one where they can remain in the family home;”

*S2M-3670.2 Christine Grahame: Violence Against Women—As an amendment to motion (S2M-3670) in the name of Malcolm Chisholm, insert at end “, and further, in challenging the attitudes which place the blame on the woman and not the perpetrator, condemns the overt sexualised representation of young women prevailing in a range of publications, electronic or paper, driven by commercialism, which presents women as objects rather than individuals.”

*S2M-3670.3 Frances Curran: Violence Against Women—As an amendment to motion (S2M-3670) in the name of Malcolm Chisholm, insert at end “recognises that the normalisation of sexualised images of women and girls in magazines that are on general sale promotes sexist attitudes about women and girls and contributes to ideas that allow violence towards women to occur without challenge, and further recognises that the Violence Against Women Fund is grant allocated and that mainstream funding is still not a reality for many non-governmental organisations, local authorities and the NHS and therefore that violence against women initiatives are treated as fringe activities as opposed to core strategic planning.”

S2M-3117# Mike Pringle: Benefits of Dialogue between Steiner and Mainstream Education Sectors—That the Parliament commends Steiner Schools in Scotland, including the Edinburgh Rudolf Steiner School, for providing education which focuses on a child’s spiritual, physical and moral well-being as well as academic progress; notes the recent research publication, Steiner Schools in England, by Professor Woods of the University of West of England, which compared Steiner Schools with those in the state sector; agrees with him that “there is great potential benefit from mutual dialogue and professional interaction between Steiner and mainstream educators”; welcomes high-level dialogue between the Steiner Fellowship (the UK Steiner accreditation body), local education authorities in England and the UK Government which has led to the prospect of the United Kingdom’s first publicly funded Steiner Academy in Hereford, and *considers that* the Scottish Executive *should* engage actively with local authorities in Scotland and encourage the schools to be brought within the publicly funded sector, in a similar fashion to other European countries such as Norway, Sweden, Finland and Denmark.
