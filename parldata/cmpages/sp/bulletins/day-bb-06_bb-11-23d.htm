Business Bulletin No. 169/2006: Thursday 23 November 2006
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 30 November 2006

Question Time

General

1. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent discussions it has had with the alcohol industry regarding the impact of tonic wine on underage drinking. (S2O-11305)

2. Mr Charlie Gordon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it monitors the effectiveness in Scotland of the UK Fireworks Act 2003. (S2O-11270)

3. Mr Frank McAveety: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in empowering local authorities and the police to tackle antisocial behaviour. (S2O-11279)

4. Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde is taking to sustain the provision of anaesthetics at the Vale of Leven hospital. (S2O-11260)

5. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive how many community police officers have been deployed to Faslane over the last 12 months. (S2O-11277)

6. Gordon Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to address the increase in sexually transmitted diseases over the past 18 months. (S2O-11273)

7. Colin Fox: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact its plans for the release and post-custody management of offenders will have on the prison population. (S2O-11296)

8. Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive how many local authorities will meet its recycling targets and what examples of good practice could be disseminated to other local authorities. (S2O-11287)

9. John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to assist the dental practice in Prestonpans to meet the growing demand for NHS dental checks and treatment of patients from East Lothian and elsewhere whose dentists refuse to work for the NHS. (S2O-11271)

10. Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will have completed its assessment of the proposal to construct a dynamic loop on the railway line between Kilmarnock and Glasgow to increase the frequency of rail services. (S2O-11274)

Finance and Public Services and Communities

1. Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it or Communities Scotland has had with the Department of Trade and Industry regarding financial assistance for the provision of services from public post offices to ensure the future of the rural sub-post office network. (S2O-11255)

2. Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Minister for Communities last met representatives of Scottish Gas to discuss the progress of the central heating programme. (S2O-11252)

3. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure that funding and support are reaching those communities with the greatest need. (S2O-11275)

4. Tricia Marwick: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will announce a timetable for the second stage transfer of Glasgow housing stock. (S2O-11257)

5. Mr Alasdair Morrison: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to improve public services in the Western Isles. (S2O-11284)

6. Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there is a shortfall in the local government finance settlement in respect of the financing of social work services. (S2O-11304)

7. David McLetchie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will publish its official response to the recommendations of the Local Government Finance Review Committee prior to April 2007. (S2O-11292)

8. Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to develop affordable housing in rural and remote communities. (S2O-11315)

9. Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made of the financial provision that will be needed in future years to support a growth in the elderly population and increases in the numbers of children identified as being in need of support. (S2O-11298)

10. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the cost has been of preparations carried out by it and its agencies for the United Kingdom to join the euro. (S2O-11243)

Education and Young People, Tourism, Culture and Sport

1. John Swinburne: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take to prevent local authorities increasing swimming pool charges for senior citizens to an unaffordable level. (S2O-11242)

2. Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive what action Scottish Screen is taking to promote film-making in Renfrewshire. (S2O-11259)

3. Rosie Kane: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding, initiatives and support the Minister for Education and Young People is putting in place to address the issue of suicide among young people. (S2O-11254)

4. Bill Aitken: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to review the use of setting or streaming in secondary schools. (S2O-11240)

5. Frances Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is aware of the research conducted by academics at the University of Dundee on the efficiency of universal benefits compared with means-tested targeting in relation to free school meals. (S2O-11253)

6. Not Lodged

7. Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans are in place to reverse the decline in playing fields in local authority areas and how much money is earmarked for the provision of sports facilities and coaching in local communities. (S2O-11246)

8. Ms Maureen Watt: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to ensure that all pupils have equality of access to a full and varied curriculum. (S2O-11231)

9. Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to promote marine tourism to the benefit of coastal communities and the marine environment. (S2O-11303)

10. Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is measuring potential destitution among asylum seeker children. (S2O-11297)
