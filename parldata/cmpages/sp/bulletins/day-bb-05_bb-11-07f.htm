Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week. Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section. Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-3523 Hugh Henry: The Draft Victim Statements (Prescribed Courts) (Scotland) Revocation Order 2005—That the Justice 1 Committee recommends that the draft Victim Statements (Prescribed Courts) (Scotland) Revocation Order 2005 be approved.

Supported by: Cathy Jamieson*

*S2M-3522 Christine Grahame: Book of Remembrance for the Victims of the Lancastria—That the Parliament notes the Lancastria exhibition which will take place in the garden lobby of the Parliament on Thursday 10 November 2005, the eve of Armistice Day; notes that the exhibition will commemorate the worst ever maritime disaster in British history and also the worst single loss of life for British forces in the whole of World War Two when the troopship, Lancastria, was attacked by enemy aircraft on 17 June 1940 off the French coastal town of St Nazaire; notes that the 16,242 ton Clyde-built liner took just 20 minutes to sink, claiming more than 4,000 lives, twice the number of victims in the Titanic and Lusitania disasters combined, yet has remained virtually unheard-of for more than 65 years, and encourages members to sign a book of condolence which acknowledges this considerable human sacrifice at the exhibition which will later be placed in the Scottish National War Memorial at Edinburgh Castle.

*S2M-3521 Ms Sandra White: Protocol Regarding Removal of Children of Asylum Seekers—That the Parliament notes that the Scottish Executive is yet to put in place a formal written protocol to clarify the role of Scottish children’s services in future removals of asylum seeker children, despite widespread concerns related to child welfare from a number of expert sources, including the Commissioner for Children and Young People; notes that Scottish ministers have a clear remit to intervene to ensure the welfare of the child under the Children (Scotland) Act 1995, and further notes that images of children being dragged from their beds in the early hours of the morning and deported back to a country they barely know will do little for Scotland’s wider reputation abroad and indeed appears to run contrary to the Executive’s declared policy of trying to encourage families to stay and contribute to Scotland.

*S2M-3520# Alex Fergusson: A76 - The Case for Improvement—That the Parliament notes with regret the two recent deaths on the A76 in separate incidents north of Sanquhar and near Auldgirth; recognises that the upgrading of the A77 to motorway standard from Glasgow to Kilmarnock has brought about a steady increase in the amount of traffic using the A76; notes that there have been three deaths in five years on the same stretch north of Sanquhar which would have been upgraded by now had the Scottish Executive not shelved plans agreed in the route action plan in favour of an easier option south of Sanquhar; recognises the formation of the A76 Action Group, and considers that the Executive should identify priority stretches of the A76 for urgent upgrading on safety grounds before further lives are lost.

*S2M-3519 Richard Lochhead: Call for Withdrawal of Motion to Remove Haddock From Sale—That the Parliament condemns the early day motion tabled on 1 November 2005 at Westminster by Liberal Democrat MP, Norman Baker, and supported by Dan Rogerson regarding the need for the House of Commons authorities to remove haddock from sale in food outlets; believes that such a move would be extremely damaging to an industry that has already suffered significant hardships in recent years; notes that scientific advice states that North Sea haddock stocks are robust with the International Council for the Exploration of the Sea recently advising the European Union that there is a healthy stock of haddock; further notes that Liberal Democrat and Scottish fisheries minister, Ross Finnie, has condemned the proposed 40% reduction in the haddock quota for 2006, and calls on Norman Baker and Dan Rogerson to withdraw their motion and commit to supporting the Scottish fishing industry as it continues the sustainable fishing of the North Sea.

S2M-3518 Carolyn Leckie: Haemophilia Blood Product Disaster—That the Parliament recognises the continuing flow of new information pointing to complacency, neglect and culpability at the root of the haemophilia blood product disaster, including the recent film by Kelly Duda, Factor 8: The Arkansas Prison Blood Scandal; believes that this film is essential viewing for the mainstream media in Scotland, not least for the information it provides to haemophiliacs, especially since the Government has admitted that it has destroyed significant and relevant documents, and further believes that nothing less than a full independent public inquiry is necessary to get to the truth of the circumstances that have caused horrendous levels of infection from blood products in the haemophiliac population and that final settlement in relation to compensation should only be expected to occur when the full, truthful information is in the public domain.

Supported by: Brian Adam*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*

S2M-3513 Shiona Baird: Scottish Parliament Visitor and Outreach Services—That the Parliament congratulates members of the Visitor and Outreach Services team on their work in taking the Scottish Parliament out to the people of Scotland; believes that, by facilitating greater participation by individuals, organisations and communities, the outreach team has already made significant progress towards achieving its aim of encouraging greater political awareness and engagement by the public across Scotland; notes the overwhelmingly positive and enthusiastic response by members of the public who attended recent outreach events in Arbroath, Inverurie and Peterhead in the north-east region; recognises that community-based parliamentary events have an important role to play in helping MSPs reach out to constituents and in educating the general public about the work of the Parliament; considers that outreach events have already helped increase public confidence in, and accessibility to, the Parliament and may ultimately help boost electoral turnout, and believes that continuing support for outreach services should remain a key priority for the Parliament if it is to succeed in becoming a truly inclusive institution.

S2M-3512 Mr Mark Ruskell: Climate Change - Prime Minister’s U-turn—That the Parliament notes the comments on climate change made by Tony Blair to a summit of G8 energy and environment ministers in London earlier this week; condemns Mr Blair’s craven submission to those misguided nations who wrongly believe that binding targets for the emissions of greenhouse gases will somehow undermine their economies; strongly believes that a sustainable environment need not be incompatible with a strong and sustainable economy, and acknowledges that, while reducing such emissions is a challenge, it is one that must be faced if we are to avoid the far greater challenge of a rapidly changing climate.
