Business Bulletin No. 30 / 2004

Friday 13th February 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-915 Donald Gorrie: Environmental Justice Fund-That the Parliament welcomes the proposed environmental justice fund and the compensation it proposes for communities blighted by industrial and domestic waste dumping in, or close to, them; regrets the decision of Scottish Ministers to grant planning permission for an extension to the Greengairs landfill site in Lanarkshire, and expresses a desire that the communities in and around the Greengairs site are amongst the first to see real benefit from the proposed fund.

*S2M-914# Mr John Home Robertson: Better Access to Small Claims Courts-That the Parliament believes that the thresholds of £750 for small claims and £1,500 for summary cause are unrealistically low and that these limits are preventing many Scottish citizens from securing the settlement of legitimate claims; notes that the equivalent procedure in England can be used for claims up to the value of £5,000, and believes that the Scottish Executive should amend the regulations to set more appropriate thresholds for the small claims procedure in Scotland.

*S2M-913# Dr Sylvia Jackson: Commonwealth Day 2004-That the Parliament recognises the valuable role of the Commonwealth in strengthening relationships between nations across the world; welcomes the continued contribution of Scotland and its people to those relationships; reaffirms its support for the work of the Commonwealth Parliamentary Association, and commends the theme of Commonwealth Day this year, "Building a Commonwealth of Freedom", that raises important issues of governance, democracy, human rights, citizenship, building communities and development that are relevant to all peoples of the Commonwealth.

Supported by: Cathy Peattie*, Karen Gillon*, Brian Adam*, Kate Maclean*, Mike Pringle*, Murray Tosh*, Helen Eadie*, Robert Brown*, Christine May*, Cathie Craigie*, Michael Matheson*, Jackie Baillie*, Maureen Macmillan*, Mr Alasdair Morrison*, Bill Butler*

*S2M-912 Mike Rumbles: British Heart Foundation "How Big is Your Heart" Campaign-That the Parliament notes that, despite substantial progress, coronary heart disease remains one of Scotland's biggest killers, causing too many Scots to die prematurely each year; congratulates the British Heart Foundation on its "How Big is Your Heart" Valentine's Day campaign and particularly for the success it has had in raising funds to carry out much-needed research for the fight against heart disease in Scotland; believes that campaigns such as these provide an ideal opportunity to raise public awareness about the nature and extent of the disease, and urges MSPs and members of the public to support the "How Big is Your Heart" campaign.

*S2M-911# Robin Harper: Airborne Initiative-That the Parliament regrets the Scottish Executive's decision to withdraw funding from the Airborne Initiative; commends the staff for the excellent work that they have put in over the last 10 years; recognises that the initiative's strategy, in terms of what was possible, has been a huge success in that at least half of the participants completed the course and at least a quarter of these have not reoffended; notes that recent success rates show that up to 50% of those who completed the course have not re-offended; further notes that the scheme has saved the Executive between £2.8 and £4.2 million in prison costs over the last 10 years, and believes that the Executive should consider whether it has any other scheme that is likely to be anywhere near as effective in helping serious and persistent offenders back into society and out of prison on a permanent basis.

*S2M-910 Mr Stewart Maxwell: Smoking and Fertility-That the Parliament congratulates the British Medical Association (BMA) on its most recent report, Smoking and reproductive life - the impact of smoking on sexual, reproductive and child health; notes with concern the fact that women who smoke are three times more likely to have children with low birth weight and that smoking during pregnancy increases the risk of miscarriage, stillbirth and death of the newborn; takes cognisance of the fact that over a quarter of Scottish mothers smoke during pregnancy; asks the Scottish Executive to take on board the remarks made by Dr John Garner, chairman of the BMA Scottish Council, on the fact that the Scottish Executive's target to reduce smoking has not changed despite the release of its recent Tobacco Action Plan, and, in light of these comments, urges the Scottish Executive to set much more ambitious targets for the reduction of smoking in this group.

*S2M-909# Elaine Smith: Financial Management of Further Education Colleges-That the Parliament notes the substantial growth in funding and participation rates in the Scottish further education sector in recent years; recognises the measures initiated by the Scottish Executive and the Scottish Further Education Funding Council aimed at improving the financial health of the sector; notes with concern, however, that the deadline imposed on all colleges to achieve financial security by July 2006 puts significant pressure on those colleges who have suffered as a result of mismanagement and/or underfunding in recent decades, and stresses that such financial strategies should not work to compel colleges to cut back on courses or services and should not have a detrimental impact upon current students.

S2M-908 Support for the Ay Family Threatened with Imminent Deportation to Turkey (lodged on 11 February 2004) Nicola Sturgeon*, Campbell Martin*, Frances Curran*, Dennis Canavan*, Patrick Harvie*

*S2M-907# Mr Alasdair Morrison: Fish Farming in Scotland-That the Parliament welcomes the results of a study into the presence of chemical residues in seabed sediments near fish farms, published by the Scottish Environment Protection Agency (SEPA); notes that 89% of sample measurements did not detect any residues of chemicals used to treat sea lice; further notes that, in the 11% of analysis where there were measurable concentrations of active ingredients, residues were restricted to areas of seabed below and close to fish farms and that all results from outwith these immediate areas were below SEPA's safe environmental standards; welcomes the continuing commitment of the Scottish aquaculture industry to minimising the environmental impact of fish farming, and commends SEPA on its use of sound science, robust regulation and partnership working to ensure the protection of Scotland's marine resources.
