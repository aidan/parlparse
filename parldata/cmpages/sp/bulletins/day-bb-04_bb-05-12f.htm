Business Bulletin No. 80 / 2004

Wednesday 12th may 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-1296 Maureen Macmillan: Christian Aid Week-That the Parliament commends Christian Aid who work in over 55 countries helping people, regardless of religion or race, to improve their lives and tackle the causes of poverty and injustice; notes that Christian Aid week is 9 to 15 May 2004 and highlights the need to help those in the world's poorest countries to help themselves; further notes, in particular, the work Christian Aid is undertaking in Sierra Leone, the world's poorest country, to help ensure sustainable peace, and calls on the Scottish Executive to make representations to Her Majesty's Government to make available further resources to assist overseas development in order to eradicate inequality in the world's poorest countries.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie*
