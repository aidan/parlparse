Business Bulletin No. 31 / 2004

Monday 16th February 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-917# Rosie Kane: Parkhead Youth Project-That the Parliament notes the invaluable contribution that Parkhead Youth Project has made to life in the East End of Glasgow; notes the success of the project in making up to 13,000 contacts with young people each year, offering them advice and support; further notes the recent decision by the East End Social Inclusion Partnership (SIP) to withdraw core funding from the project despite its success in helping young people and the efforts of the staff to attract additional grants; deplores the fact that the project will be forced to close, denying the young people of the East End the opportunity to take part in empowering educational activities; notes that the project has complied with every piece of developmental and organisational advice offered to it by the local SIP; believes that East End SIP should reconsider the decision to withdraw funding from the Parkhead Youth Project, and considers that the Scottish Executive should take steps to end the funding lottery for community organisations by adopting the principle that funding is guaranteed for all types of social inclusion projects where need has been demonstrated.

*S2M-916# Alex Fergusson: Fuel Prices in Rural Scotland-That the Parliament notes with concern the widening gap between fuel prices in urban and rural areas; further notes that between the fuel crisis in September 2000 and summer 2003 the gap remained at a relatively constant two to three pence per litre but has increased since then to a differential of at least six pence per litre, and therefore strongly believes that the Scottish Executive should enter into talks with the major fuel suppliers to ascertain why the differential cannot be maintained at a level that will be less damaging to private, business and tourism interests in rural areas than the current discriminatory level.

S2M-915 Donald Gorrie: Environmental Justice Fund-That the Parliament welcomes the proposed environmental justice fund and the compensation it proposes for communities blighted by industrial and domestic waste dumping in, or close to, them; regrets the decision of Scottish Ministers to grant planning permission for an extension to the Greengairs landfill site in Lanarkshire, and expresses a desire that the communities in and around the Greengairs site are amongst the first to see real benefit from the proposed fund.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan*

S2M-914# Mr John Home Robertson: Better Access to Small Claims Courts-That the Parliament believes that the thresholds of £750 for small claims and £1,500 for summary cause are unrealistically low and that these limits are preventing many Scottish citizens from securing the settlement of legitimate claims; notes that the equivalent procedure in England can be used for claims up to the value of £5,000, and believes that the Scottish Executive should amend the regulations to set more appropriate thresholds for the small claims procedure in Scotland.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan*, Sarah Boyack*, Karen Gillon*, Dr Elaine Murray*

S2M-913# Dr Sylvia Jackson: Commonwealth Day 2004-That the Parliament recognises the valuable role of the Commonwealth in strengthening relationships between nations across the world; welcomes the continued contribution of Scotland and its people to those relationships; reaffirms its support for the work of the Commonwealth Parliamentary Association, and commends the theme of Commonwealth Day this year, "Building a Commonwealth of Freedom", that raises important issues of governance, democracy, human rights, citizenship, building communities and development that are relevant to all peoples of the Commonwealth.

Supported by: Cathy Peattie, Karen Gillon, Brian Adam, Kate Maclean, Mike Pringle, Murray Tosh, Helen Eadie, Robert Brown, Christine May, Cathie Craigie, Michael Matheson, Jackie Baillie, Maureen Macmillan, Mr Alasdair Morrison, Bill Butler, Chris Ballance*, Mr Jamie Stone*, Phil Gallie*, Mike Watson*, Sarah Boyack*, Robin Harper*, Murdo Fraser*, Richard Lochhead*, Margaret Jamieson*, Alex Neil*, Mrs Nanette Milne*, Fiona Hyslop*, Dr Elaine Murray*

S2M-912 Mike Rumbles: British Heart Foundation "How Big is Your Heart" Campaign-That the Parliament notes that, despite substantial progress, coronary heart disease remains one of Scotland's biggest killers, causing too many Scots to die prematurely each year; congratulates the British Heart Foundation on its "How Big is Your Heart" Valentine's Day campaign and particularly for the success it has had in raising funds to carry out much-needed research for the fight against heart disease in Scotland; believes that campaigns such as these provide an ideal opportunity to raise public awareness about the nature and extent of the disease, and urges MSPs and members of the public to support the "How Big is Your Heart" campaign.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan*

S2M-911# Robin Harper: Airborne Initiative-That the Parliament regrets the Scottish Executive's decision to withdraw funding from the Airborne Initiative; commends the staff for the excellent work that they have put in over the last 10 years; recognises that the initiative's strategy, in terms of what was possible, has been a huge success in that at least half of the participants completed the course and at least a quarter of these have not reoffended; notes that recent success rates show that up to 50% of those who completed the course have not re-offended; further notes that the scheme has saved the Executive between £2.8 and £4.2 million in prison costs over the last 10 years, and believes that the Executive should consider whether it has any other scheme that is likely to be anywhere near as effective in helping serious and persistent offenders back into society and out of prison on a permanent basis.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan*

S2M-910 Mr Stewart Maxwell: Smoking and Fertility-That the Parliament congratulates the British Medical Association (BMA) on its most recent report, Smoking and reproductive life - the impact of smoking on sexual, reproductive and child health; notes with concern the fact that women who smoke are three times more likely to have children with low birth weight and that smoking during pregnancy increases the risk of miscarriage, stillbirth and death of the newborn; takes cognisance of the fact that over a quarter of Scottish mothers smoke during pregnancy; asks the Scottish Executive to take on board the remarks made by Dr John Garner, chairman of the BMA Scottish Council, on the fact that the Scottish Executive's target to reduce smoking has not changed despite the release of its recent Tobacco Action Plan, and, in light of these comments, urges the Scottish Executive to set much more ambitious targets for the reduction of smoking in this group.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan*

S2M-909# Elaine Smith: Financial Management of Further Education Colleges-That the Parliament notes the substantial growth in funding and participation rates in the Scottish further education sector in recent years; recognises the measures initiated by the Scottish Executive and the Scottish Further Education Funding Council aimed at improving the financial health of the sector; notes with concern, however, that the deadline imposed on all colleges to achieve financial security by July 2006 puts significant pressure on those colleges who have suffered as a result of mismanagement and/or underfunding in recent decades, and stresses that such financial strategies should not work to compel colleges to cut back on courses or services and should not have a detrimental impact upon current students.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan*

S2M-908 Tommy Sheridan: Support for the Ay Family Threatened with Imminent Deportation to Turkey-That the Parliament notes with grave concern the imminent threat to deport the Ay family from Germany to Turkey and the unusually speedy rejection of their last appeal; further notes the disappearance of Mr Ay on his return to Turkey in 2002 and the fact that he remains missing to this day; cautions the Home Office that failure to note this evidence of danger to the family could lead to their death or similar disappearance and represents a breach of their human rights guaranteed under the United Nations Charter and European Convention on Human Rights; calls upon the Scottish Executive to make urgent representations to Home Office ministers to intercede immediately on behalf of the Ay family and use their discretionary powers to grant the family leave to return to the UK, and further calls upon the Executive to work with the Home Office to develop an impartial asylum assessment system.

Supported by: Rosie Kane, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Carolyn Leckie, Ms Sandra White, Nicola Sturgeon, Campbell Martin, Frances Curran, Dennis Canavan, Patrick Harvie, Chris Ballance*

S2M-907# Mr Alasdair Morrison: Fish Farming in Scotland-That the Parliament welcomes the results of a study into the presence of chemical residues in seabed sediments near fish farms, published by the Scottish Environment Protection Agency (SEPA); notes that 89% of sample measurements did not detect any residues of chemicals used to treat sea lice; further notes that, in the 11% of analysis where there were measurable concentrations of active ingredients, residues were restricted to areas of seabed below and close to fish farms and that all results from outwith these immediate areas were below SEPA's safe environmental standards; welcomes the continuing commitment of the Scottish aquaculture industry to minimising the environmental impact of fish farming, and commends SEPA on its use of sound science, robust regulation and partnership working to ensure the protection of Scotland's marine resources.

S2M-895# Fiona Hyslop: Missing Link Completion and Upgrade of the A801 Avon Gorge Road-That the Parliament acknowledges the tireless campaigning efforts over many decades by local communities and individuals of Westfield and Avonbridge to bring about the much-needed upgrading of the A801 Avon Gorge road; notes with concern the significant number of deaths and accidents on the road over the years; recognises that the road is a key link between the M8 and M9 in the very centre of Scotland and that completion of the missing link part of the road would allow safe and more environmentally responsible north-south transportation in Scotland, avoiding one of the most severe gradients, of approximately 1 in 6, of any road in Scotland and so eliminating the 40 miles currently taken by many heavy goods lorries from Whitburn to Grangemouth via Newbridge, and believes that the Scottish Executive should recognise the important national strategic importance of improving this road in considering its decision in relation to planning and should acknowledge its responsibility to help complete and upgrade the road in terms of support for funding to West Lothian and Falkirk councils.

Supported by: Michael Matheson, Stewart Stevenson

S2M-894# Sarah Boyack: Bus Services for Holyrood and the New Scottish Parliament-That the Parliament notes with dismay the removal of Lothian Buses Service 64 running between Holyrood and Princes Street; further notes that the only remaining service, Lothian Buses Service 35 serving the Holyrood and Abbeyhill areas, is a temporary service which does not operate into the evenings; understands that local residents, along with staff from a range of companies including Scotsman Publications, are adversely affected; welcomes plans for the high frequency Lothian Buses Service 100 to be extended from the current service from Waverley Station; notes with regret that due to the lack of an agreed route with the Traffic Commissioner there will be no extension of the Lothian Buses Service 100 until autumn 2004 at the earliest; notes with concern the fact that there is now no direct bus route between the city centre and the new Parliament at Holyrood, and considers that the relevant authorities, including the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body, the City of Edinburgh Council and Lothian Buses, should resolve this problem urgently.

Supported by: Trish Godman, Jackie Baillie, Maureen Macmillan, Iain Smith, Helen Eadie, Elaine Smith, Karen Gillon, Marlyn Glen, Margaret Jamieson, Margo MacDonald, Rosie Kane, Christine May, Mike Watson, Cathie Craigie, Scott Barrie, Tommy Sheridan*

S2M-885 Richard Lochhead: Days at Sea and the Safety of Our Fishermen-That the Parliament recognises the dangers that fishermen face in the pursuit of their work to provide us with high quality produce; deplores the fact that the deal reached at the EU Agriculture and Fisheries Council in Brussels failed to take into account force majeure, the impact of bad weather on fishing time or time taken for vessels to steam to and from fishing grounds; notes the impact that this will have on the safety of our fishermen at sea, and calls upon Her Majesty's Government to negotiate an increase in days at sea to ensure that the safety of our fishermen is not further compromised by the Common Fisheries Policy.
