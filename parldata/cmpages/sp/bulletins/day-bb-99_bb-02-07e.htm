BUSINESS BULLETIN 30/1999

Friday 2  July 1999

Section E –  Written questions lodged on 1 July 1999
 

S1W-353 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive what is the breakdown by local authority area of the poindings and warrant sales which have taken place in Scotland in each of the past three years.

 

S1W-354 Tricia Marwick: To ask the Scottish Executive what is the estimated cost to the voluntary sector in Scotland of criminal record checks on volunteers who work with children and young people.

 

S1W-355 Fiona McLeod: To ask the Scottish Executive how many schools will have access to the internet before the end of October 1999.

 

S1W-356 Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what was the value of land transferred out of public ownership in the Edinburgh Royal Infirmary Private Finance Initiative agreement.

 

S1W-357 Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive to list (a) the public sector capital projects involving private finance both planned and under way giving both date of start and estimated completion, (b) the total estimated capital investment included in the projects and (c) the annual public expenditure required to service these projects in cash and real terms over the life of the projects.

 

S1W-358 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive to state the number of new build and renovated properties which will be affected by the Housing (Right to Buy)(Cost Floor)(Scotland) Order (S.I. 1999/611).

 

S1W-359 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive the rate of interest at which it can secure borrowings from the Secretary of State and whether such borrowings can be used for investment in public service projects.

 

S1W-360 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what is the location of the nearest NHS Dexa scanner to residents within the Highland Council area and whether it will provide additional funding to enable a Dexa scanner and the appropriate associated staffing to be provided at Raigmore Hospital.

 

S1W-361 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has regarding the provision of community legal services and what the timetable is for progressing the proposals contained in the document "Access to Justice: a consultation paper on civil legal aid".

 

S1W-362 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what estimates have been made of the level of use of the powers proposed to be granted to consumer bodies, including the citizens advice bureaux, to take legal action in relation to unfair contract terms.

 

S1W-363 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what proposals it has to fund citizens’ advice bureaux in order that they can provide services and in particular to ensure that they can exercise the powers proposed to be granted to them in relation to unfair contract terms.

 

S1W-364 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what proposals it has to introduce a statutory obligation on local authorities to fund providers of free independent consumer advice.

 

S1W-365 Ms Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what arrangements for the pension rights of the new staff, as opposed to those transferring from the present Royal Infirmary of Edinburgh, have been negotiated with the company contracted to supply ancillary services to the NHS.

 

S1W-366 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any local authority is likely to be capped this year.

 

S1W-367 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make a statement about future arrangements for joint working with Her Majesty’s Government.
