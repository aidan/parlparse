Business Bulletin No. 175/2010: Friday 26 November 2010
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 25 November 2010

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-37846 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether UK residents based in England are entitled to free dental and eye checks in Scotland.

S3W-37847 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the development of an integrative care approach for people with long-term conditions.

S3W-37848 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to implement an integrative care approach for people with long-term conditions.

S3W-37849 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers homeopathy to fall within its definition of integrative care.

S3W-37850 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it can confirm reports that there has been a net decrease in woodland area of 8,000 hectares in the last two years and whether it considers that there will be a net increase or decrease in 2010-11.

S3W-37851 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive how many hectares of woodland area have been cleared in the last two years as a result of (a) wind farm developments and (b) bog restorations and how this compares with the number of hectares planted in the same period.

S3W-37852 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to change the definition of a woodland area from land with a 20% cover of trees to land with a 10% cover of trees and, if so, how much additional woodland area this would result in.

S3W-37853 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it expects the Native Woodland Survey of Scotland to identify additional woodland area and, if so, approximately how many additional hectares.

S3W-37854 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects the results of the Native Woodland Survey of Scotland to be published.

S3W-37855 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has received representations from stakeholders suggesting that its tree-planting provisions are inadequate and, if so, from what organisations.

S3W-37856 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration it has given to recent developments in the field of osmotic power generation.

S3W-37857 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has undertaken research into the potential advantages and disadvantages of deploying osmotic power generating facilities and, if so, with what result.

S3W-37858 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive whether an assessment has been made of Scotland’s osmotic power generation potential and, if so, with what result.

S3W-37859 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it has made available to organisations or individuals looking to develop osmotic power generation techniques or facilities.

S3W-37860 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive how much paper has been used by public sector bodies in each year since 2007.

S3W-37861 Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive how much Departmental Expenditure Limit underspend was returned to HM Treasury in 2007-08.

S3W-37862 Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive how much Departmental Expenditure Limit underspend was returned to HM Treasury in 2008-09.

S3W-37863 Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive how much Departmental Expenditure Limit underspend was returned to HM Treasury in 2009-10.

S3W-37864 Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive how much it would have cost to prepare a state of readiness for the Scottish variable rate in 2007-08.

S3W-37865 Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive how much it would have cost to prepare a state of readiness for the Scottish variable rate in 2008-09.

S3W-37866 Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive how much it would have cost to prepare a state of readiness for the Scottish variable rate in 2009-10.

S3W-37867 Willie Coffey: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the national waiting time standard applies to patients waiting for treatment from the national scoliosis service.

S3W-37868 Willie Coffey: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure that the national scoliosis service meets the waiting time standard of 12 weeks for inpatient and day-care treatment.

S3W-37869 Willie Coffey: To ask the Scottish Executive what alternative services are available to patients with scoliosis who cannot be treated by the national scoliosis service within the 12-week national standard.

S3W-37870 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-36953 by Nicola Sturgeon on 9 November 2010, whether it will commit to using the Barnett consequential funding resulting from the £200 million per year cancer drug fund for cancer drugs.

S3W-37871 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-36953 by Nicola Sturgeon on 9 November 2010, how much it will receive in Barnett consequentials resulting from the £200 million per year cancer drug fund.

S3W-37872 Tom McCabe: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-37685 by Stewart Stevenson on 25 November 2010 and given that the inquiry was concluded two years ago, when it expects to announce the outcome of the public local inquiry into the M8 Baillieston to Newhouse scheme.

S3W-37873 John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what reason it has made a reduction of 12.5% in the veterinary surveillance budget before the Kinnaird review of veterinary surveillance reports in January 2011. R
