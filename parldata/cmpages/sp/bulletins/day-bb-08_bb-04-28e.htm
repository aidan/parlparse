Business Bulletin No. 67/2008: Monday 28 April 2008
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 25 April 2008

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-12322 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has carried out an evaluation of the impact of existing policies limiting the number of houses in multiple occupation (HMO) licences in Glasgow and Edinburgh with the aim of informing Scottish Planning Policy 3: Planning for Housing (SPP 3) and, if so, what the outcomes of the evaluation were.

S3W-12323 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what evidence is available to suggest that houses in multiple occupation (HMO) quotas are successful in tackling antisocial behaviour and other local housing problems.

S3W-12324 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what evidence is available to suggest that a concentration of houses in multiple occupation (HMOs) would have a negative impact on local amenities.

S3W-12325 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any record of any business or other public amenity that has been forced to close due to a concentration of houses in multiple occupation (HMO) in the locality.

S3W-12326 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers houses in multiple occupation (HMO) quotas to be useful in addressing antisocial behaviour and, if so, whether it considers them to be more effective than the provisions of the Antisocial Behaviour etc. (Scotland) Act 2004.

S3W-12327 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers the legislation relating to houses in multiple occupation (HMO) to be adequately enforced.

S3W-12328 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact it considers that the use of houses in multiple occupation (HMO) quotas has on the affordability of housing to buy and rent.

S3W-12329 Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive how many forensic accountants are employed by the Scottish Crime and Drug Enforcement Agency and how they have been funded.

S3W-12330 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what extra funding will be provided for any increase in the use of community sentence orders over the next three years, broken down by local authority area.

S3W-12331 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in procuring broadband access for the village of Ewes, near Langholm.

S3W-12332 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what the staff sickness and turnover rates are for each prison.

S3W-12333 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what its policy is on prison officers undertaking other employment in their time off.

S3W-12334 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what the underspend or overspend is in the budget of each prison.

S3W-12335 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive how many visits have been made by HM Chief Inspector of Prisons for Scotland to each prison in the last year.

S3W-12336 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what overseas visits the Scottish Prison Commission has undertaken and what, and to whom, it has reported on any such visits.

S3W-12337 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people under the age of 16 there are in each adult prison.

S3W-12338 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people have moved from prison into employment in the last year, broken down by prison.

S3W-12339 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prisoners in each prison undertook (a) paid employment and (b) community work while in prison in the most recent year for which figures are available.

S3W-12340 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prosecutions there have been since the law was changed regarding physical punishment of children by their parents.

S3W-12341 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many students in full time education are paying council tax.

S3W-12342 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many higher education students in (a) full-time and (b) part-time education are paying council tax.

S3W-12343 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many further education students in (a) full-time and (b) part-time education are paying council tax.

S3W-12344 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many night class students are paying council tax.

S3W-12345 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many distance learning students are paying council tax.

S3W-12346 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many Open University students are paying council tax.

S3W-12347 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how council tax is calculated for full-time students.

S3W-12348 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the First Minister’s statement on 24 April 2008 that some 400,000 students in Scotland are currently liable for the council tax (Official Report c. 7934), which categories of student are included in this calculation and how many students there are in each category.

S3W-12349 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the First Minister’s statement on 24 April 2008 that some 160,000 students in Scotland are currently not liable for the council tax (Official Report c. 7934), which categories of students are included in this calculation and how many students there are in each category.

S3W-12350 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive whether full-time students living in shared accommodation with other full-time students are liable to pay council tax.

S3W-12351 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the First Minister’s statement on 17 April 2008 that higher education students pay council tax dependent on where they stay (Official Report c. 7686), what the relationship is between the location or type of accommodation of full-time higher education students and their liability to pay council tax

S3W-12352 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the First Minister’s statement on 17 April 2008 that higher education students pay council tax dependent on where they stay (Official Report c. 7686), what analysis it has made of where students live.

S3W-12353 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what formal arrangements are in place for Scottish Development International’s work with (a) Scottish Enterprise, (b) Event Scotland, (c) VisitScotland and (d) Highlands and Islands Enterprise.

S3W-12354 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects that a new chief executive of Scottish Development International will be appointed and what role ministers will have in this appointment.

S3W-12355 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what role ministers had in the decision not to appoint any of the applicants for the post of chief executive of Scottish Development International.

S3W-12356 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive how the restructuring of the enterprise network has impacted on the remit and operations of Scottish Development International.

S3W-12357 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is planning any changes to the remit and operations of Scottish Development International.

S3W-12358 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is planning any changes to the arrangements for Scottish Development International’s work with (a) Scottish Enterprise, (b) Event Scotland, (c) VisitScotland and (d) Highlands and Islands Enterprise.

S3W-12359 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to increase Scottish Development International’s overseas postings, co-location of offices and representation in other countries.

S3W-12360 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what Scottish Development International’s annual budget has been for each of the last three years and what it will be for each of the next three years, broken down by funding stream.

S3W-12361 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive how much income Scottish Development International received from (a) Scottish Enterprise, (b) the Executive and (c) any other sources in each of the last three years and what it will receive from these sources over each of the next three years.

S3W-12362 Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people the Mortgage to Rent scheme had assisted in each year since 2003.

S3W-12363 Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average value of grant towards the purchase of houses under the Mortgage to Rent scheme has been in each year since 2003.

S3W-12364 Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive what level of funding was allocated for the Mortgage to Rent scheme in (a) 2003-04, (b) 2004-05 and (c) 2005-06.
