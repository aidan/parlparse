Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (♦).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

*S3M-3514 Ian McKee: Smoking Kills, Irrespective of Social Class—That the Parliament welcomes the recent study carried out by Health Scotland’s Director of Public Health Science, Dr Laurence Gruer, and published in the British Medical Journal, which examines the long-term impact of smoking on older men and women, showing that smoking itself is a greater source of health inequalities than social position; welcomes the findings that death rates of ex-smokers were close to those of never-smokers, demonstrating that quitting does make a difference regardless of social position, and considers that this research reinforces the Scottish Government’s stance and commitment to target smoking prevention among young people and provide effective support to encourage smokers to quit, taking us along the path to a non-smoking Scotland and improving the nation’s public health.

*S3M-3500 Bill Butler: Fairtrade Fortnight—That the Parliament notes that Fairtrade Fortnight runs from 23 February until 8 March 2009; applauds the efforts over the last decade of charities, church groups and individuals in promoting the benefits of fair trade in Scotland; considers that the fairtrade movement has already made a significant difference to the lives of many thousands of workers, their families and communities across the globe; recognises that the fairtrade concept is based on traditional cooperative principles of community ownership, democratic membership control and concern for communities; notes that sales of fairtrade products currently total an estimated £700 million across the United Kingdom, and encourages the Scottish Government to work with schools, local authorities, further and higher education establishments and businesses to ensure that as many of them as possible achieve fairtrade status.

Supported by: Shirley-Anne Somerville*, Elaine Murray*, Michael McMahon*, Trish Godman*, Sandra White*, Claire Baker*, Patricia Ferguson*, David Stewart*, Mary Mulligan*, Kenneth Gibson*, Paul Martin*, Bill Kidd*, Marlyn Glen*, Andy Kerr*

*S3M-3499 Aileen Campbell: Launch of the Fairtrade Fortnight—That the Parliament celebrates the launch of Fairtrade Fortnight 2009, which runs from 23 February to 8 March 2009; encourages the people of Scotland to support and take part in Fairtrade Fortnight, whether by simply choosing to buy fairtrade goods or through holding their own fairtrade events; acknowledges the need to raise awareness and make a positive impact on the unfair trade rules that keep two billion people, a third of humanity, in poverty, and commends the invaluable work undertaken by the Fairtrade Foundation to improve the trading position of producer organisations around the world and deliver sustainable livelihoods for farmers, workers and their communities.

*S3M-3498 Hugh O’Donnell: Voice of Experience Forum Home Safety Campaign—That the Parliament congratulates the Voice of Experience Forum (VOEF) on its recent home safety campaign, aimed at raising awareness of cold calling, in partnership with Trading Standards and the North Lanarkshire Council Bogus Caller Task Force, and commends the work of VOEF on promoting the inclusion of older people in the community and articulating the needs and wishes of older people in North Lanarkshire.

*S3M-3497 Hugh O’Donnell: Fairbridge Works—That the Parliament congratulates the youth work charity, Fairbridge, on the vital work that it undertakes to ensure that every young person has a fair chance to succeed in life; notes the strong contribution that Fairbridge makes to the More Choices, More Chances strategy and the Curriculum for Excellence, by working with over 680 disengaged or excluded young people each year across Dundee, Edinburgh and Glasgow, many of whom face issues such as low self-esteem, poor social and life skills, mental health issues or drug and alcohol addictions; recognises that Fairbridge offers individuals a fresh start, someone to believe in them and a chance to develop the skills and confidence that they need to change their own lives, and considers that ongoing investment in early intervention and first-step services at a local level is essential to closing the opportunity gap among Scotland’s young people.

*S3M-3496 Jackie Baillie: Keeping Services Local at the Vale of Leven Hospital—That the Parliament regrets the recommendations being presented to a meeting of the board of NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde that, if accepted, will result in the removal of the 24/7 anaesthetics service and consequent transfer of coronary care and treatment for strokes, gastrointestinal bleeds and epileptic seizures from the Vale of Leven Hospital to the Royal Alexandra Hospital in Paisley; further regrets the recommendation to close the Christie Ward, which provides invaluable inpatient acute mental health services to the local community; strongly considers that the views of the local community will have been totally ignored if these recommendations are approved by the board; further considers that NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde has failed to integrate health services properly in this area; believes that as many inpatient services as possible should be delivered at the Vale of Leven Hospital but considers that insufficient attention has been given to a reconfiguration of services north of the river based on a revised catchment area and including access to the Golden Jubilee National Hospital; further notes that the recommendations to the board are not keeping services local, as promised by the Scottish Government, and therefore calls on the board of NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde to reject these proposals, to retain 24/7 anaesthetics and the Christie Ward at the Vale of Leven Hospital and to reconfigure services to be delivered north of the river.

Supported by: David Stewart*, Ross Finnie*, Paul Martin*, Dr Richard Simpson*

*S3M-3495 Liam McArthur: Future of Scotland’s Retained Firefighters—That the Parliament notes with surprise and regret that not a single SNP MP turned up to debate the future of the Scottish Retained Fire Service in Westminster Hall on 11 February 2009 and that only one Scottish Labour MP was present; is disappointed that the SNP in particular seem to prefer engaging in debate in the press rather than in the parliamentary chamber; views the protection of our local communities and retained fire crews as something that requires careful, responsible joint working, and calls on SNP and Labour parliamentarians in Westminster and Holyrood to participate in the democratic process for which they were elected and to press the UK and Scottish governments to ensure that a means of implementing any changes is found that will allow the continued operation of a fire service relying on retained firefighters.

*S3M-3494 Kenneth Gibson: Not a Penny More—That the Parliament notes the cost of the Edinburgh trams project to be £545 million, of which £500 million is to be provided by the Scottish Government; considers that this sum is enough to complete the project, as previously agreed by the contractor; totally rejects any demands for additional funding, which would impact on projects elsewhere, and believes that not a single extra penny should be forthcoming.

Supported by: Bill Kidd*, Christina McKelvie*, Shirley-Anne Somerville*

*S3M-3493 David Stewart: ParentLine Scotland—That the Parliament congratulates ParentLine Scotland, the national free and confidential helpline for parents and carers, on celebrating its 10th anniversary in 2009 and on receiving more than 85,000 calls since its inception; commends the helpline’s support for parents and carers through highly trained volunteers listening and offering information, support and advice so that parents are better able to cope with issues such as family relationships, child ill-health, bullying and behaviour issues; notes that a significant proportion of calls to ParentLine Scotland are from parents of teenagers, and recognises the particular importance of ParentLine Scotland as a source of easily accessible support for these parents.

*S3M-3492 Patrick Harvie: European Resolution on Depleted Uranium Weapons—That the Parliament acknowledges the European Parliament’s resolution on "Depleted uranium weapons and their effect on human health and the environment - towards a global ban on the use of such weapons"; recognises the serious health concerns about the use of depleted uranium weapons on both military personnel and civilians, concerns that are widely acknowledged by many including the UN General Assembly; further recognises the serious environmental hazards posed by depleted uranium such as the contamination of soil and groundwater, and strongly urges the Scottish Government to offer its moral and, where appropriate, practical support for a global ban on the use of depleted uranium weapons.

*S3M-3491 John Park: Apprenticeships Week for Scotland—That the Parliament notes that the second annual Apprenticeships Week in England takes place from 23 to 27 February 2009; recognises the opportunities that this week will provide to highlight the vital role that apprenticeships play in developing a skilled workforce; further recognises that many successful individuals and business leaders began their careers as apprentices; believes that many businesses understand the importance of training workers in order to remain competitive in the current economic climate and beyond; further notes the recent agreement by the Scottish Government, following the Scottish Budget, to hold an apprenticeship summit, create 7,800 new apprenticeship starts in the next year and provide support for apprentices facing redundancy, and calls on the Scottish Government to consider establishing an apprenticeships week for Scotland.

Supported by: Bill Butler*, Johann Lamont*, Paul Martin*, Mary Mulligan*, Lewis Macdonald*, Bill Kidd*, Andy Kerr*, Michael McMahon*, Trish Godman*, Des McNulty*, Claire Baker*, David Stewart*, Marlyn Glen*

*S3M-3490 Aileen Campbell: Travis Trek, The Legends—That the Parliament congratulates Larkhall resident Scott Cunningham and his guide dog, Travis, on embarking on their fifth long-distance fundraising walk, following the route of the West Highland Way from north to south in aid of the Guide Dogs charity; notes that the pair have so far raised over £60,000 for the charity in their previous expeditions and have set new records as the first blind man and guide dog pair to walk each of the West Highland Way, the Southern Upland Way, the Speyside Way and the Pennine Way; further notes that on this trip they will be joined by football legends and supported by the Royal Marines, and looks forward to the continuing success of Scott and Travis on their treks.

Supported by: Bill Kidd*, Alasdair Allan*, Kenneth Gibson*, Joe FitzPatrick*, Sandra White*

*S3M-3489 Karen Gillon: Congratulations to Scott Cunningham and Travis—That the Parliament congratulates Larkhall man Scott Cunningham on his amazing fundraising efforts in aid of Guide Dogs; congratulates Scott on raising over £60,000 for Guide Dogs, which has so far helped to sponsor nine guide dog pups, and wishes him and his guide dog, Travis, every success in their forthcoming walk from north to south on the West Highland Way, making him the first blind man to cover the route and on which he will be accompanied by a number of former Rangers players.

Supported by: Shirley-Anne Somerville*, Michael McMahon*, Elaine Murray*, Sandra White*, Trish Godman*, Jamie McGrigor*, David Stewart*, Bill Kidd*
