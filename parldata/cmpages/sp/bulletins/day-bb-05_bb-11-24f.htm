Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday.  That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an “R”.

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm).  A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-3625 Carolyn Leckie: Stop Violence Against Women - Support Amnesty International—That the Parliament congratulates Amnesty International on its Stop Violence Against Women campaign and its latest initiative, “16 days of Activism to Stop Violence Against Women”; regrets, however, that such campaigns are still necessary and is particularly concerned that recent attitude surveys reflect that an unacceptably large proportion of the population blame women for rapes perpetrated by men; is further concerned that conviction rates for rape have decreased and that those rapes that end up in court are a tiny proportion of rapes actually committed; believes and affirms that rape is a brutal expression of power over, and violence against, women, that it is always the responsibility of the perpetrator and never the victim, that the media contribute to a climate where sexist and misogynist attitudes to women are given credence, and further believes that fundamental attitudinal change must be achieved throughout the criminal justice system for women to have the confidence to report and pursue rape allegations and that the Parliament has the responsibility to lead that attitudinal change in the criminal justice system and wider society.

*S2M-3624 Margo MacDonald: Urgent Request for a Ministerial Statement—That the Parliament requests that the First Minister makes a statement on the discussions that have taken place between himself and Home Office ministers concerning the methods used to remove failed asylum seekers from Scotland, in light of his spokesman’s statement at lunchtime on 23 November 2005 which made it clear that the First Minister had not reflected the Parliament’s opposition to the “dawn raids” in his meetings with Home Office ministers to date and believes that, in advance of his meeting on 24 November 2005 with the Home Office Minister, Tony McNulty, he must re-state the Scottish Executive’s position on this matter.

*S2M-3623 Carolyn Leckie: Scottish Executive Failing Women on Equal Pay—That the Parliament is concerned that the Scottish Executive in its local government finance settlement fails to comply with its obligations to do all that it can to achieve equal pay; recognises that these obligations are the encouragement of equal opportunities, in particular the observance of equal opportunities requirements and the imposition of duties on Scottish public authorities and cross-border authorities in relation to their Scottish functions; believes that, given that local authorities in Scotland are resisting full settlement of equal pay for women on the grounds of insufficient funding and the risk to council services and jobs and recommending acceptance of compensation arrangements that fall well below what women would be confident of achieving by pursuing their legal entitlement, the Executive could and should have made the necessary funds available to achieve equal pay, both retrospectively and prospectively, but has not; further believes, therefore, that the Executive has not met the financial, legal, political or moral challenge of achieving equal pay and has stood by whilst the unequal pay of women has subsidised public services, and considers that only urgent direct action by the Executive on this issue, including the allocation of sufficient resources, will deliver results that match the Executive pronouncements on equality and preserve public services and the jobs of local authority workers.

*S2M-3622 Ms Margaret Curran: The Draft Scotland Act 1998 (Modifications of Schedule 5) (No. 3) Order 2005—That the Equal Opportunities Committee recommends that the draft Scotland Act 1998 (Modifications of Schedule 5) (No. 3) Order 2005 be approved.

Supported by: George Lyon*

*S2M-3621 Lewis Macdonald: The Food Protection (Emergency Prohibitions) (Amnesic Shellfish Poisoning) (West Coast) (No. 17) (Scotland) Order 2005—That the Health Committee recommends that the Food Protection (Emergency Prohibitions) (Amnesic Shellfish Poisoning) (West Coast) (No. 17) (Scotland) Order 2005 (SSI 2005/585) be approved.

Supported by: Mr Andy Kerr*

*S2M-3607.1 Murdo Fraser: Congratulations to Glas Cymru—As an amendment to motion (S2M-3607) in the name of Rob Gibson, leave out from first “Scottish” to end and insert “, if Scottish Water was transformed into a mutual company or not-for-profit trust in the private sector along the lines of Glas Cymru, it could reduce costs to Scottish customers instead of expecting to charge excessive rates to domestic and, especially, business water users, a practice that will continue with the Scottish Executive’s chosen model, and concludes that the benefits to be gained from adopting the Welsh model would be lost if Scottish Water were to be retained in the public sector.”

*S2M-3592.1 Sarah Boyack: Every Penny Counts for Mature Students—As an amendment to motion (S2M-3592) in the name of Mike Pringle, insert at end “and further notes that the “Every Penny Counts” campaign also calls on the Minister for Transport and Telecommunications to take action, given that mature students are an integral part of the Scottish Executive’s lifelong learning strategy.”

*S2M-3438.1 Mary Scanlon: Housing (Scotland) Bill—As an amendment to motion (S2M-3438) in the name of Malcolm Chisholm, insert at end “but, in so doing, expresses opposition to the scheme for the provision of information about houses on the market in Part 3 of the Bill, which provides for a single seller survey.”
