Business Bulletin No. 35/2009: Thursday 26 February 2009
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 25 February 2009

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-21322 Christina McKelvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the Scottish Funding Council on the teaching of the languages of China and Japan.

S3W-21323 Christina McKelvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure adequate teaching of the languages of China and Japan in tertiary education.

S3W-21324 Christina McKelvie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it intends to support Japanese studies and Chinese studies in universities.

S3W-21325 Christina McKelvie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to offer to facilitate improvements in relations between Japanese and Chinese cultural organisations and tertiary education institutions.

S3W-21326Δ Stuart McMillan: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will provide the Parliament with a more detailed analysis of the likely cost implications of the Damages (Asbestos-related Conditions) (Scotland) Bill, in accordance with the motion passed at the conclusion of Stage 1.

S3W-21327 Jamie Hepburn: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it holds on the availability of cardiology services in each general hospital in each NHS board area.

S3W-21328 Jamie Hepburn: To ask the Scottish Executive what arrangements are in place to support patients waiting in one hospital for treatment or examination in another hospital.

S3W-21329 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many police officers carry a Taser gun, broken down by police board area.

S3W-21330 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many police officers have received training in the use of Taser guns, broken down by police board area.

S3W-21331 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive on how many occasions police officers have used a Taser gun to help apprehend a suspect.

S3W-21332 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether anyone has been significantly injured by a Taser gun used by a police officer.

S3W-21333 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are restrictions on using Taser guns on people under the age of 18.

S3W-21334 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether police forces are allowed to use Taser guns other than in deployments involving conventional firearms.

S3W-21335 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many times Taser guns have been used on people under the age of 18.

S3W-21336 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-14927 by John Swinney on 29 July 2008, whether it now intends to legislate further to protect the status of common good assets.

S3W-21337 Angela Constance: To ask the Scottish Executive how many women have received smear tests for the detection of cervical cancer in the last three months and how this figure compares with that for the same period in 2008.

S3W-21338 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many offences of drunk driving per 10,000 head of population there were in each of the last five years, broken down by local authority area, and when it expects new statistics to be available.

S3W-21339 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many publicly funded schools offered (a) higher and (b) advanced higher courses in each of the last four years, broken down by local authority.

S3W-21340 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average class size was for each secondary school age group in each of the last three years, broken down by local authority.

S3W-21341 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average class size was for (a) maths and (b) English in S1 and S2 in each of the last three years, broken down by local authority.

S3W-21342 Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many vacancies there were for junior doctor posts as of February 2009 and how many there were in each of the preceding four years, broken down by NHS board.

S3W-21343 Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive what it estimates the shortfall in the hours of junior doctors will be on introduction of the European working time directive.

S3W-21344 Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of the introduction of the European working time directive, how it plans to deal with any shortfall in the hours of junior doctors.

S3W-21345 Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many junior doctors work over 48 hours a week, broken down by NHS board.

S3W-21346 Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of the introduction of the European working time directive, what it estimates the cost will be to the NHS to cover any shortfall in the hours of junior doctors.

S3W-21347 Willie Coffey: To ask the Scottish Executive how many hours of study per week qualify as full-time study for students applying for Education Maintenance Allowance to study for highers in (a) schools and (b) colleges.

S3W-21348 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prisoners have been held at each young offenders institution in each month in the last three years for which information is available.

S3W-21349 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what the (a) design capacity and (b) safe operating limits are for each young offenders institution.

S3W-21350 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what formula is used in the allocation of funding for young offenders institutions.

S3W-21351 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many offenders are incarcerated in young offenders institutions for knife crime offences.

S3W-21352 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prisoners are held in each young offenders institution and how many have a previous history of incarceration.

S3W-21353 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people are serving short-term prison sentences of under (a) three and (b) six months and, of these, how many have a previous history of incarceration.

S3W-21354 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what alcohol rehabilitation programmes are available to prisoners under 21 years old who offended while under the influence of alcohol and how many places on such programmes are available in (a) Scotland and (b) YOI Polmont.

S3W-21355 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what educational and employment programmes are available in young offenders institutions and how many places on such programmes are available in (a) Scotland and (b) YOI Polmont.

S3W-21356 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many young offenders in each young offenders institution have a learning disability.

S3W-21357 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how much funding it provides for programmes in young offenders institutions aimed at tackling prisoners’ learning disabilities.

S3W-21358 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive when the improvements to the A77 around Ayr will be completed.

S3W-21359 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what budget has been allocated for the improvements to the A77 around Ayr.

S3W-21360 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on plans for a rail station at Ayr Hospital.

S3W-21361 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive when its guidance on patient confidentiality was last updated.

S3W-21362 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive when information for patients on patient confidentiality was last updated.

S3W-21363 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what protocol is in place regarding the transfer of patient information between NHS Scotland and the NHS in the rest of the United Kingdom.

S3W-21364 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive when the protocol regarding the transfer of patient information between NHS Scotland and the NHS in the rest of the United Kingdom will be updated.

S3W-21365 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what the discharge rate from general hospitals was for alcohol-related conditions in each year from 2003-04 to 2007-08, broken down by community health partnership, and when it expects new statistics to be published.

S3W-21366 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what the (a) male and (b) female mortality rate was where alcohol was the underlying cause in each of the last five years, broken down by community health partnership area, and when it expects new statistics to be available.

S3W-21367 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what the (a) male and (b) female mortality rate was where alcohol was a contributory factor in each of the last five years, broken down by community health partnership area, and when it expects new statistics to be available.

S3W-21368 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with Islamic funding sources in relation to (a) the Scottish Futures Trust or (b) other Scottish Executive spending; which sources have been involved, and when it will be in a position to indicate what use is expected to be made of such sources.

S3W-21369 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the level of homelessness was for the last quarter for which information is available and how this compares with the (a) previous quarter and (b) same quarter in 2008.

S3W-21370 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the level of rough sleeping was for the last quarter for which information is available and how this compares with the (a) previous quarter and (b) same quarter in 2008.

S3W-21371 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that it is on track to achieve the 2012 homelessness target.

S3W-21372 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people have registered as homeless in (a) Argyll and Bute and (b) West Dunbartonshire in the last quarter for which information is available.

S3W-21373 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people were sleeping rough in (a ) Argyll and Bute and (b) West Dunbartonshire in the last quarter for which information is available.

S3W-21374 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of the economic downturn, what additional measures are being taken to prevent homelessness and rough sleeping.

S3W-21375 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many urinary tract infections have occurred in hospital patients in each year since 2007.

S3W-21376 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is concerned about the number of patients with catheters who contract urinary tract infections and what action it is taking to address this.

S3W-21377 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people were tested negative for Clostridium difficile but subsequently died of the infection at (a) the Vale of Leven Hospital and (b) other hospitals in each year since 2007.

S3W-21378 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-20031 by Nicola Sturgeon on 29 January 2009, whether it will collect data on the (a) minimum, (b) maximum and (c) average turnaround time for medical laboratories testing samples from patients with suspected hospital-acquired infections.

S3W-21379 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-20032 by Nicola Sturgeon on 29 January 2009, whether it will list those laboratories that test for hospital-acquired infections and specifically Clostridium difficile.

S3W-21380 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-20033 by Nicola Sturgeon on 21 January 2009, whether it will collect and publish the information on which hospitals have reported the 078 strain of Clostridium difficile since 2007.

S3W-21381 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is undertaking research into the mechanism of transmission of Clostridium difficile.

S3W-21382 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what records are kept of the transmission of Clostridium difficile in patients being transferred between hospitals.

S3W-21383 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether all patients transferring between hospitals or entering hospitals from a community setting are routinely tested for healthcare associated infections and, if so, how this is carried out in each hospital.

S3W-21384 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-19730 by Shona Robison on 21 January 2009, whether it will outline the scope and remit of the The Same As You research proposal.

S3W-21385 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-19730 by Shona Robison on 21 January 2009, who it has consulted on the detail of the The Same As You research proposal.

S3W-21386 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has undertaken, is undertaking, or plans to undertake a survey into the availability across Scotland of schemes for the provision of free home insulation and whether it will outline the results of any such survey undertaken.

S3W-21387 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has undertaken, is undertaking, or plans to undertake a survey into the variation across Scotland of the cost of installing home insulation and whether it will outline the results of any survey undertaken.

S3W-21388 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list all the home insulation grants that it makes available to households, either directly or through third parties, outlining (a) what is available in each case, (b) any restrictions on who can apply and (c) whether any cap on the maximum value of the grant offered varies across Scotland to take into account the needs of households in areas with higher installation costs.

S3W-21389 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has made representations to Her Majesty’s Government or to utility companies to ensure that schemes for the provision of free home insulation are available to households across the whole of Scotland and, where schemes offer free insulation, that households in areas with higher installation costs are not penalised by being excluded or offered only partial grants.

S3W-21390 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has been consulted by the Secretary of State for Energy and Climate Change on the Nuclear Industry Association’s application to justify new nuclear power stations, either in accordance with justification regulations or otherwise and, if so, what response it has made or intends to make.

S3W-21391 Joe FitzPatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it intends to take following the meeting between the First Minister, the Prime Minister and leaders of the devolved administrations on 25 February 2009 to discuss the current economic crisis.
