Business Bulletin No. 101 / 2004

Monday 14th June 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-1461 Mike Rumbles: Men's Health Week, Scotland: 14 to 20 June 2004-That the Parliament pledges support for Men's Health Week, Scotland, from 14 to 20 June 2004; acknowledges efforts by all sectors to publicise Men's Health Week, Scotland and recognises and welcomes the work undertaken by the Men's Health Forum Scotland generally; recognises that men have a lower life expectancy, higher suicide rate and a higher death rate from diseases such as cancer and heart disease than women; further recognises that men are less likely to have chronic diseases identified and treated early and that men attend their GPs less than half as often as women; notes that as the population of Scotland ages the burden of treating and caring for increasing numbers of men living longer with chronic disease will be resource-intensive; believes that prevention of such diseases should be a focus for Scottish health policy, and calls upon MSPs to assist ongoing collaborations by promoting positive messages about healthy ageing, men's health and Men's Health Week, encouraging more men to take responsibility for their own health and well-being.

*S2M-1460 Christine Grahame: Roxburgh Citizens Advice Bureau: 30 Years of Serving the Community-That the Parliament recognises the invaluable work of Citizens Advice Bureau offices Scotland-wide and notes that, in the past year of its work, Roxburgh Citizens Advice Bureau, like bureaux Scotland-wide, has offered free, confidential and independent information, advice and advocacy on any subject to anyone who requests it and also helps the community by campaigning on social issues; notes that this year alone it helped the public to claim £805,658 to which they were entitled, and congratulates all volunteers, past and present, as it celebrates 30 years of service in the community.

S2M-1459 Mr Jamie Stone: Improvement in Scottish Small Business Sector-That the Parliament welcomes the publication of the Federation of Small Businesses in Scotland membership survey, Lifting Barriers to Growth in UK Small Businesses 2004, in particular the findings that 58% of its members had experienced an increase in turnover in the past 12 months, that nearly a third have increased their number of employees, that there has been a rapid rise in uptake of broadband from 3% in 2002 to 21% in 2003 and an increase in the number of women who own a small business and that more than half expect their business to grow within the next two years and, furthermore, supports the work of the Scottish Executive, Scottish Enterprise and the Business Gateways in helping small businesses to achieve growth.

S2M-1458 Chris Ballance: Call for Resignation of Scottish Opera Chairman, Duncan McGhie-That the Parliament supports the chorus of Scottish Opera and the union, Equity, in their fight to retain jobs for singers in Scotland and believes that the chairman of Scottish Opera, Duncan McGhie, who has failed to ensure that the company spends according to its income, failed to ensure the continuity of productions and failed to retain jobs for his staff, should resign.

Supported by: Ms Rosemary Byrne*

S2M-1457# Dr Jean Turner: Glasgow Marriage Counselling Service - Couple Counselling Scotland-That the Parliament recognises the excellent work done by Glasgow Marriage Counselling Service (GMCS) in association with Couple Counselling Scotland; notes, in particular, their expertise and involvement with families where children are drawn into partnership dysfunction that requires the assistance of well-trained couple counsellors; is aware that training costs around £5,500 per counsellor, of which £3,500 is paid by counsellors who later give their time on a voluntary basis; is astonished that such an essential service to families which is in line with the principles set out in the Scottish Executive consultation paper, Family Matters: Improving Family Law in Scotland, does not have direct funding and depends on varying levels of support for such services from local authorities, and notes with concern that one local authority has indicated its intention to reduce substantially their vital support of GMCS with attendant consequences for the service.

S2M-1456# Mark Ballard: Congratulations to Scottish Nappy Company for Winning a Scottish Business in the Community Award-That the Parliament notes that the Scottish Nappy Company was presented with an Award for Excellence at the Scottish Business in the Community celebration dinner on 3 June 2004; commends the excellent service that companies like the Scottish Nappy Company offer to parents in Scotland who are seeking an uncomplicated and inexpensive means to reuse cotton nappies; recognises that, on average, 60% of a disposable nappy is non-biodegradable and that their usage creates 1,000,000 tonnes of waste in Great Britain every year, much of it ending up in landfill, and recognises that reusable cotton nappies provide a practical and environmentally-sustainable alternative to disposable nappies.

S2M-1454 Donald Gorrie: Dalziel High School, Motherwell-That the Parliament congratulates Dalziel High School in Motherwell on the success of its development and marketing of the school's own-label healthy options of low-fat and low-salt potato crisps and bottled water; believes that this enterprise is a fine example of the entrepreneurial spirit of Scotland's young people and potentially a positive step in the battle to provide healthy eating options for our young people, and calls for similar projects to be explored and instigated by Scotland's other local authorities.

S2M-1453 Patrick Harvie: Parkhead Citizens Advice Bureau-That the Parliament regrets Glasgow City Council's decision to award Parkhead Citizens Advice Bureau (CAB) only £65,969 of the £104,845 requested in the service level agreement that was submitted for the running of core services; notes that this will result in a 40% reduction in Parkhead CAB's service to clients and will have a significant impact on the lives of people within the east end of Glasgow; further notes that last year the bureau assisted with over 7,000 client contacts and over 10,000 problems, formally represented over 300 clients at tribunals and in the Sheriff Court and secured over £830,000 in financial gain for clients; is extremely concerned that other Glasgow citizen's advice bureaux and advice providers are facing problems in securing funding for their valuable services, and therefore urges Glasgow City Council to increase substantially Parkhead CAB's grant to allow the bureau to continue to operate at its current level of service.

Supported by: Rob Gibson*, John Swinburne*, Mr Mark Ruskell*

S2M-1451 Mr Kenneth Macintosh: Heart Week 5 to 13 June 2004-That the Parliament congratulates the British Heart Foundation on its Heart Week campaign, the Big Red Fightback, from 5 to 13 June 2004, against coronary heart disease (CHD); notes that, while heart disease rates in Scotland are falling, more people than ever before are living with the disease; further notes that lack of physical activity is one of the key causes of CHD, yet only around one in three men and one in four women achieves the recommended 30 minutes of exercise, five times a week, and therefore believes that the Heart Week campaign's focus on encouraging people to increase their level of physical exercise will be an important contribution to fighting the causes of heart disease in Scotland.

S2M-1450# Jackie Baillie: NHS Argyll and Clyde's Clinical Strategy Review-That the Parliament rejects the inadequate and short-sighted proposals expressed in Shaping the Future, the clinical strategy for NHS Argyll and Clyde, and specifically its proposals to close both the Dumbarton Joint Hospital and the Helensburgh Victoria Infirmary and to further downgrade the Vale of Leven Hospital while centralising services at the Royal Alexandra Hospital (RAH) in Paisley; notes that, in a recent consultation held by Jackie Baillie MSP and John McFall MP with nurses, consultants, voluntary groups and the local community, 88% of those who responded said that they wanted services delivered north of the river Clyde; agrees that travelling long journeys to reach suitable health care should be the exception and not the rule; further notes with concern the apparent lack of detailed regional planning in arriving at these proposals, and therefore considers that these proposals should be rejected and that local services should be maximised as the first priority and thereafter, for those services that cannot be delivered locally, that serious consideration should be given to the use of Gartnavel Hospital and the Golden Jubilee Hospital.

Supported by: Elaine Smith*, Helen Eadie*, Mr Duncan McNeil*

S2M-1449 Mike Rumbles: RCN Scotland's Right for Nurses, Right for Patients Campaign-That the Parliament notes the recent launch of the Royal College of Nursing Scotland's Right for Nurses, Right for Patients campaign that aims to focus attention on five key nursing issues, including staffing levels and workload, pay modernisation, professional development, specialist nurses and nurse leadership; welcomes the Scottish Executive's positive response to the Facing the Future Nursing and Midwifery Workload and Workforce Planning Project report in accepting all of its 20 recommendations, and further welcomes the forthcoming Facing the Future national convention on Monday 28 June 2004 in Edinburgh and the opportunity it presents for nurses across Scotland to influence future policy on nursing.

S2M-1448 Mr Duncan McNeil: NHS Argyll and Clyde's Draft Clinical Strategy-That the Parliament notes the publication of NHS Argyll and Clyde's draft clinical strategy, which proposes to move in-patient and emergency services from Inverclyde Royal Hospital to the Royal Alexandra Hospital (RAH) in Paisley; expresses concern that the RAH, which is already struggling to cope with the increased numbers of patients that it is required to treat as a result of previous centralisation exercises, will be even more severely stretched; condemns the denial of access to NHS services for people in Inverclyde, Largs, Dunoon and beyond, and calls on the NHS board to consider alternative options which may be brought forward for dealing with the real external pressures it faces.

S2M-1446 Mr Stewart Maxwell: Dying from Cancer is No Pleasure-That the Parliament notes with dismay the comments made by Dr John Reid, UK Secretary of State for Health, that "people from those lower socio-economic categories have very few pleasures in life and one of them they regard as smoking"; notes that the people Dr Reid speaks of are those most at risk of dying of smoking-related illnesses; considers these remarks to be ill-judged and dangerous and also that these comments are unhelpful to the Scottish Executive's proclaimed strategy to cut smoking levels among all categories in society, and urges its members to make it clear that the health of those in the lower socio-economic groups is equally as important as those in other socio-economic categories and support speedy legislation to get anti-smoking legislation on the statute book as soon as possible to protect the health of all the people of Scotland.
