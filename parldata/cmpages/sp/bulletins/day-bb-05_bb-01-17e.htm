Section E – Written questions lodged on 14 January 2005

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-13466 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how much has been spent on the recruitment of special constables in each year since 1999, broken down by police force area.

S2W-13467 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many special constables there have been in each year since 1999, broken down by police force area.

S2W-13468 Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will make a statement on the actions it plans to take following the completion in 2004 of the consultation, Modernising NHS Dental Services in Scotland.

S2W-13469 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will reply to my letter of 18 November 2004 regarding the Protection of Children (Scotland) Act 2003.

S2W-13470 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has made to the UK Government regarding protecting Scottish regional air routes.

S2W-13471 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken, or will take, to ensure that Scotland has strong air links with the capitals of Europe.

S2W-13472 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what the implications would be for Scotland’s air links if changes are made to the weight charge formula for airlines.

S2W-13473 Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive what benefits it expects the roadworks provisions in the Transport (Scotland) Bill to bring; how these benefits will be measured, and how it will demonstrate the effectiveness of these provisions.

S2W-13474 Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive what estimate it has made of the costs for utilities of the roadworks provisions in the Transport (Scotland) Bill.

S2W-13475 Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made in respect of any impact on fuel poverty of costs incurred by utility companies under the Transport (Scotland) Bill being passed on to the consumer.

S2W-13476 Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration it has given to the impact of the roadworks provisions in the Transport (Scotland) Bill on urban regeneration.

S2W-13477 Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive what the impact of the Transport (Scotland) Bill will be on utilities’ infrastructure investment programmes, including water and gas mains replacement and renewal and upgrading of electricity cables.

S2W-13478 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it has taken to ensure the correct and timely implementation of EC Urban Waste Water Treatment Directive 91/271/EEC in light of the decision by the European Commission on 12 January 2005 to send the United Kingdom a final written warning ordering it to comply with the requirements of the directive in relation to the waste water treatment plant in Lerwick.

S2W-13479 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) serious accidents and (b) fatalities have occurred on the A77 between Kilmarnock and Newton Mearns in each of the last five years.

S2W-13480 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) serious accidents and (b) fatalities have occurred on the A77 between Ayr and Stranraer in each of the last five years.

S2W-13481 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive how many applications have been (a) received and (b) approved for regional selective assistance in each Scottish parliamentary constituency; what the value was of the total investment in each constituency; what total amount of funding was (a) applied for and (b) approved in each constituency; what the estimate was of the number of jobs to be (a) created and (b) safeguarded as a result of the assistance in each constituency, and how many jobs have actually been (a) created or (b) safeguarded in each constituency, in each of the last five years.

S2W-13482 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-10833 by Margaret Curran on 12 October 2004, whether it has sought and obtained the views of the Presiding Officer, other members of the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body (SPCB) and other interested parties on whether to pursue the recommendation in Lord Fraser’s report to amend the Scotland Act 1998 in respect of the powers of the SPCB; if such views have not yet been obtained, when it will seek them, and, if such views have been sought, whether it will place the responses received in the Scottish Parliament Information Centre.

S2W-13483 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list all the changes that have been made since 10 September 2002, or are in the process of being made, to Schedule 5 of the Scotland Act 1998.

S2W-13484 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive how much has been spent during the current financial year by each of the three intermediate technology institutes; whether it anticipates that the institutes will underspend their £15 million budgets and, if so, by how much, and how any monies resulting from any underspend will be reallocated within the enterprise network budgets.

S2W-13485 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will establish its group to look into bioethoanol and biodiesel as alternative sources of energy.

S2W-13486 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will introduce a Vulnerable Adults Bill.

S2W-13487 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will carry out a national review of chiropody services for older people, including into whether the existing criteria for the availability of free services are adequate to meet the needs of older people on low incomes.

S2W-13488 Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people it estimates have asthma, broken down by NHS board area.

S2W-13489 Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people it estimates have been diagnosed with asthma in each year since 1999.

S2W-13490 Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive what new practices or procedures it intends to implement to help reduce the number of people who commit suicide.

S2W-13491 Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive what practical help it intends to provide to help people in Stirlingshire who have been affected by recent flooding events.

S2W-13492 Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will request the Scottish Environment Protection Agency to provide technical advice on appropriate flood prevention measures to people in the Mid Scotland and Fife region who are affected by flooding.

S2W-13493 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what assistance is to be offered to Inverness Airport to address issues relating to the PFI contract.

S2W-13494Δ Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on meeting the Partnership Agreement commitment to review student bursaries.

S2W-13495 Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-12956 by Mr Andy Kerr on 23 December 2004 regarding the committee of the National Framework for Service Change, what the geographical base is of each member of the sub-group.

S2W-13496 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will publish the Annual Report of the Scottish Children’s Reporter Administration.

S2W-13497 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will place a copy of the draft Annual Report of the Scottish Children’s Reporter Administration (SCRA) in the Scottish Parliament Information Centre along with correspondence between it and the SCRA relating to the annual report.

S2W-13498 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive how many children have been reported for committing 10 or more offences in each of the last five years and this year to date and, in respect of these offences, how many disposals there were in each year.

S2W-13499 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive how many referrals have been made to children’s panels in each of the last five years.

S2W-13500 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive what its targets are for reducing youth crime.

S2W-13501 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive how many young people have been reported for committing five or more offences within a six-month period in each of the last five years and this year to date and, in respect of these offences, how many disposals there were in each year.

S2W-13502 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive on how many occasions in each local authority area pupils have been sent home from school because of a shortage of teachers in (a) 2002, (b) 2003, (c) 2004 and (d) 2005.

S2W-13503 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive how many vacancies there are in each local authority area for teachers in (a) primary schools, (b) English, (c) modern languages, (d) mathematics, (e) science subjects, (f) technical subjects, (g) art, (h) geography, (i) history (j) computer studies and (k) physical education.

S2W-13504 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to address the needs of (a) children and (b) adults with autistic spectrum disorders who have gastrointestinal disorders.

S2W-13505 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what training is given to clinical (a) psychiatrists and (b) psychologists on diagnosing autistic spectrum disorders.

S2W-13506 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive how many serious assaults were reported between 24 December 2004 and 5 January 2005 and how many arrests were made relating to such assaults, broken down by police force.

S2W-13507 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive how many full-time equivalent police officers (a) were employed in each of the last five years and (b) are currently employed, broken down by police force.

S2W-13508 Mr Mark Ruskell: To ask the Scottish Executive on what date it intends to bring forward a revised code of practice for the safe use of plant protection products.

S2W-13509 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how many priority one calls were received by NHS 24 over the Christmas and New Year period.

S2W-13510 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average response time was by NHS 24 to priority one calls over the Christmas and New Year period.

S2W-13511 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive what the maximum waiting time was for a priority one call to be dealt with by NHS 24 over the Christmas and New Year period.

S2W-13512 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive what the recommended maximum waiting time is for NHS 24 to deal with priority one calls.

S2W-13513 Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive what it estimates the cost to be of removing the requirement for people with asthma to pay prescription charges.

S2W-13515 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that social capital can be developed in an area that lacks the infrastructure and the will of the community to support it.

S2W-13516 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive what action can be taken to address social exclusion within vulnerable communities.

S2W-13517 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has a definition of a "model citizen".

S2W-13518 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive what indicators are used in relation to (a) social exclusion, (b) urban regeneration and (c) social capital.

S2W-13519 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive what its definition is of "community".

S2W-13520 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive what role social capital plays in social inclusion and what impact any such role has on the role of the state in social inclusion.

S2W-13521 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers the Granton waterfront consultation to be an example of a grass-roots community consultation.

S2W-13522 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive what community or communities were involved in the Granton waterfront consultation or will be affected by its development.

S2W-13523 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive how it monitors the effectiveness of community participation and the extent to which it increases democratic accountability and empowers communities.

S2W-13524 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to ensure that community consultation is conducted as effectively as possible and is seen as a priority over the need to meet short deadlines.
