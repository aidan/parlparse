Business Bulletin No. 78/2007: Thursday 31 May 2007
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 30 May 2007

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S3W-334 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to introduce local referenda for onshore wind farm developments and, if so, (a) what the terms of the referenda will be with regard to the minimum size of wind farm for which a referendum is called, the selection of the electorate entitled to participate in a given referendum and the arrangements for voting, (b) whether any consultation has been or will be carried out into the terms of such referenda and (c) what weight will be given to the outcomes of the referenda.

S3W-335 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to change the current approach to state aid compliance and approval in respect of the fisheries and agricultural sectors.

S3W-336 Derek Brownlee: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) fatal and (b) non-fatal accidents there were on each trunk road in 2006.

S3W-337 Derek Brownlee: To ask the Scottish Executive how much has been spent on upgrading each trunk road in each year since 1999.

S3W-338 Derek Brownlee: To ask the Scottish Executive how much was spent on road safety improvements on each trunk road in 2006.

S3W-339 Derek Brownlee: To ask the Scottish Executive how much was spent on pedestrian safety improvements on each trunk road in 2006.

S3W-340 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to increase the allocation of private sector housing grant to Shetland Islands Council.

S3W-341 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the registration and inspection regime pursued by Communities Scotland as it affects rural and island housing associations.

S3W-342 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what consultation the Office of the Scottish Charity Regulator has carried out, and with whom, about its current policy in respect of housing associations that operate as registered charities.

S3W-343 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive when Shetland Islands Council and Hjaltland Housing Association will be given approval to operate the agreed Tenants Incentive Scheme and what the reasons are for any delay in approval.

S3W-344 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what its policy is on the Cod Recovery Plan 2 which the European Commission wishes to introduce in 2009 and whether the Executive supports the reorganisation of the Cod Recovery Plan.

S3W-345 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what its policy and objectives are in respect of the annual EU/Norway fishing negotiations which take place in advance of the EU Fisheries Council in December.

S3W-346 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what investigations and research are being considered into informal mobility, such as car sharing, in respect of demand-responsive transport.

S3W-347 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive how many consultations there have been on the flexibility and transferability of pelagic and demersal pressure stock licences and what period has passed since the initial proposals for changes were made.

S3W-348 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what the policy objective is of allowing unused pelagic and demersal pressure stock licences to be cancelled if not used for a period of three years.

S3W-349 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on no timescale limit being attached to unused pelagic and demersal pressure stock licences that are currently cancelled if not used for a period of three years.

S3W-350 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive when a decision will be made on the flexibility and transferability of pelagic and demersal pressure stock licences and why no decision has been taken to date.

S3W-351 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) pelagic and (b) demersal pressure stock licences have been cancelled in each of the last 10 years, also showing the reasons for cancellation.

S3W-352 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to minimise bureaucracy for housing associations which are registered charities and wish to operate the Homestake scheme, given the current position of the Office of the Scottish Charity Regulator.

S3W-353 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to allow housing associations which are registered charities to operate the Homestake scheme.

S3W-354 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the UK Marine Bill in respect of devolution of nature conservation responsibilities out to the 200-mile limit.

S3W-355 Alasdair Allan: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has received from GP practices in respect of the level of global sum funding.

S3W-356 Alasdair Allan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is aware of any instances of GP practices citing the level of global sum funding as a reason for a practice being financially unsustainable.

S3W-357 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive how fishing stakeholders will be directly involved in international negotiations and EU Fisheries Councils.

S3W-358 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive whether producer organisations will be defined as "active fishermen" and "coastal communities" in respect of the allocation of fishing entitlement.

S3W-359 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will address the complex regulation of the fishing industry.

S3W-360 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to fund research and development of Sweet Gale and other non-food products in Scotland.

S3W-361 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of the recent Scottish Consumer Council research, what action is being taken to ensure that all NHS boards develop a policy and strategy on how they handle public enquiries to the NHS.

S3W-362 Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what recommendations of Missing Out – Young Runaways in Scotland it has implemented.

S3W-363 Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what resources are available for local authorities to safeguard young runaways who are at risk of harm under section 38 of the Children (Scotland) Act 1995.

S3W-364 Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive which local authorities have taken steps to provide short-term refuges for children and whether it plans to issue guidance to local authorities on this matter.

S3W-365 Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive who the members are of the Working Group on Young Runaways and Children Abused Through Prostitution.

S3W-366 Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive how often the Working Group on Young Runaways and Children Abused Through Prostitution has met and what results have emerged from its meetings.

S3W-367 Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Working Group on Young Runaways and Children Abused Through Prostitution last met and what matters were discussed.

S3W-368 Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidelines are available to local authorities on young runaways under the age of 16.

S3W-369 Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the results were of the three-year reform programme for child protection services.

S3W-370 Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has increased the level of investment in helplines for young people, including the provision of funding to allow ChildLine Scotland to increase by up to 60% since 2003 the number of children it helps.

S3W-371 Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether ChildLine Scotland has increased the number of children it helps since 2003.

S3W-372 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what its policy is on cod discards.

S3W-373 Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it endorses the Reaching Higher: Building on the Success of Sport 21 strategy and, if so, what plans it has to take the strategy forward.

S3W-374 John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive how it intends to increase the number of NHS dentists in Roxburgh and Berwickshire.

S3W-375 John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive how it intends to extend access to dentistry in Roxburgh and Berwickshire.

S3W-376 John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide additional funding to NHS Borders to fund a new full-time NHS dentist for Newcastleton.

S3W-377 David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of land required for the dualling of the A9 is currently owned by the roads authority.

S3W-378 David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent action has been taken to meet the aims of the EU Water Framework Directive.

S3W-379 David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Executive what actions under the new Scottish Rural Development Plan are aimed at meeting the Kyoto Protocol on climate change.

S3W-380 David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Executive what timescale is envisaged for a decision to be reached on the voluntary modulation rate.

S3W-381 David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Executive how many representations it has received over the last 12 months on the environmental requirements of the Scottish Rural Development Plan.

S3W-382 Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to upgrade the A82 Tarbet to Crianlarich trunk road.

S3W-383 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive how many 16 to 18 year-olds live in the (a) Dundee City, (b) Angus and (c) Aberdeenshire local authority areas.

S3W-384 Mr Charlie Gordon: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will abolish prescription charges for people with chronic health conditions and whether it will list the health conditions affected.

S3W-385 Mr Charlie Gordon: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will now award the main roadworks contract for the completion of the M74.

S3W-386 Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive what estimates were made of the number of homeowners eligible for its central heating programme (a) in its original form and (b) since its subsequent extension in 2004 and how many homeowners have taken advantage of the scheme to date.

S3W-387 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the timescales are for ministerial decisions on the approval of regional transport plans.

S3W-388 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what powers ministers have in relation to the amendment, rejection or resubmission of regional transport plans.

S3W-389 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether transport infrastructure projects outlined in regional transport plans must also be reflected in relevant structure plans before they can be approved.

S3W-390 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what standard and detail of public consultation is required by regional transport partnerships in preparing plans for submission to ministers.

S3W-391 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what effect the removal of tolls from the Erskine Bridge has had on traffic levels across it.

S3W-392 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it intends to address any increased traffic levels as a result of scrapping tolls on the Forth and Tay road bridges.

S3W-393 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made of funding requirements for bridge maintenance and repairs on the Forth and Tay road bridges.

S3W-394 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made, including any related, initial or previous research, of the possible costs of (a) dualling the A9, (b) dualling the A96, (c) dualling the A77, (d) building the Aberdeen Western Peripheral Route, (e) building the M74 and (f) building a second crossing over the Forth.
