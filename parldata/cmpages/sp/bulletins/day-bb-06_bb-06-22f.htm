Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-4596 Richard Lochhead: Misleading Birds Eye Advertisements—That the Parliament considers that the latest Birds Eye advertisement which suggests that frozen fish is superior to fresh fish leaves a bad taste in the mouth; believes that the company should withdraw the advertisement; further believes that the advertisement is more than likely an attempt to justify the company’s practice of using only imported frozen fish in its products; considers that the idea that frozen fish tastes better than fish freshly landed at Scottish ports is clearly ludicrous and that consumers should not be subjected to this kind of advertising, and calls on Birds Eye to be careful that it is not seen as anti-Scottish and to stop using its advertising budget to belittle Scotland’s fishing industry.

*S2M-4595 Cathy Peattie: Timebanks—That the Parliament welcomes the growth of "timebanks" which enable people to help each other by exchanging time rather than money; notes that there are now active timebanks in Clackmannanshire and Castlemilk, with others under development in Argyll and Bute, Glasgow, Johnstone and Livingston; notes that timebanks bring together people from diverse backgrounds, sharing their experiences and skills within mutual support networks, and that they can be used to help tackle health inequalities, facilitate education, regenerate communities and build social capital in communities across Scotland and the UK; believes that timebanking complements the Scottish Executive’s volunteering strategy, and considers that the Executive should promote awareness of the potential of timebanks and develop a strategy to support the extension of timebanking.

Supported by: Mark Ballard*, Jackie Baillie*, Dr Elaine Murray*, Roseanna Cunningham*, Patrick Harvie*, Eleanor Scott*, Robin Harper*, Ms Maureen Watt*, Trish Godman*, Murray Tosh*, Christine May*, Sarah Boyack*, Cathie Craigie*

*S2M-4594 Dr Sylvia Jackson: United Nations Universal Declaration for Animal Welfare—That the Parliament believes that animals are sentient beings capable of suffering and experiencing pain; recognises the need for a United Nations universal declaration for animal welfare to acknowledge this fact globally; welcomes the recently launched World Society for the Protection of Animals’ "Animals Matter to Me" petition aimed at collecting 10 million signatures at www.animalsmatter.org in support of such a universal declaration; supports the work of the Scottish SPCA in promoting the campaign and its petition in Scotland, and calls on the Scottish Executive to urge the relevant UK Minister to attend a ministerial conference to support the proposed universal declaration in New York in 2007.

*S2M-4593 Richard Lochhead: Genetic Testing Waiting Times—That the Parliament welcomes the report by Breakthrough Breast Cancer, Testing Times - Waiting for Genetic Test Results; recognises that there are 3,600 diagnosed cases of breast cancer every year in Scotland, with 180 women developing breast cancer as a result of an inherited DNA defect; condemns the current situation where women are forced to wait for up to three years for genetic test results; further condemns current procedures which mean that those waiting for life-altering test results are not kept up to date with progress; supports the aims of the report to reduce waiting times for test results to between two and eight weeks, and calls on NHS boards to meet the targets set out in the report.

*S2M-4592# Miss Annabel Goldie: Regeneration of Inverclyde—That the Parliament notes the various initiatives being undertaken in Inverclyde to regenerate the economy and to improve the physical and social environment; congratulates all relevant agencies and organisations involved in these initiatives, and *considers that* the Scottish Executive *should* ensure that the needs of Inverclyde remain paramount when considering any other national or strategic objectives.

Supported by: Mr David Davidson*
