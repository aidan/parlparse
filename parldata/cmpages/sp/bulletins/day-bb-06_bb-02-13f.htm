Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-3949 Alex Neil: Demand for a Public Inquiry into Shirley McKie Case—That the Parliament notes that the Scottish Executive has agreed to make a settlement in the compensation claim by Shirley McKie against it; considers that this issue of compensation could have been resolved much sooner following her acquittal in 1999 of having committed perjury; considers that the failure to resolve the issue in a more timely fashion has resulted in years of misery for Shirley McKie and her family; notes that, despite her acquittal in 1999, failure to resolve the issue sooner may have led to a tarnishing of her reputation; commends the McKie family for their bravery in fighting for justice, and calls for a full public inquiry into the role of the police, Scottish Criminal Records Office, the Crown Office and the Executive in this case, in particular their reason for bringing an action of perjury against Shirley McKie in the first instance and their failure to resolve the situation in a timely manner following her acquittal.

*S2M-3948 Mr Tom McCabe: Scottish Commissioner for Human Rights Bill: Financial Resolution—That the Parliament, for the purposes of any Act of the Scottish Parliament resulting from the Scottish Commissioner for Human Rights Bill, agrees to any expenditure of a kind referred to in paragraph 3(b)(ii) of Rule 9.12 of the Parliament’s Standing Orders arising in consequence of the Act.

*S2M-3947 Irene Oldfather: North Ayrshire Schools Concert—That the Parliament congratulates North Ayrshire Council’s educational services department on hosting the forthcoming 10th Anniversary Flagship Concert at the Royal ConcertH Hall in Glasgow, due to take place on Friday 24 February 2006; notes that this year’s concert will feature the North Ayrshire junior, intermediate and senior choirs, concert band, training band, string orchestras, symphony orchestra, traditional music students and the Stanecastle Band, meaning that over 400 students from across North Ayrshire will take to the stage to showcase their musical talents; acknowledges the hard work and practice that all these young people will have committed in recent months to prepare for the event, and sends its best wishes to North Ayrshire Council, the pupils and teaching staff involved for a very successful event.

*S2M-3946 Fergus Ewing: Fuel Tax Concessions for Scottish Highlands and Islands—That the Parliament notes that more tax is paid on a litre of fuel in the Highlands and Islands than any other area of the United Kingdom; notes the French Government’s recent decision to allow its regional councils to introduce reduced rates of duty for road fuel consumed within their territories; further notes that this decision was reached with the prior approval of the Council of Ministers which included ministers from the UK Government; observes that the European Commission elected to allow this derogation from the Energy Products Directive to permit this measure; condemns Her Majesty’s Government for its refusal to countenance any move to reduce fuel prices in rural or island communities of Scotland; notes that Her Majesty’s Government has ruled out any assistance for rural and island Scotland whilst supporting a similar policy of the French Government which would see reductions of fuel duty by 3% until 2011; believes that this refusal shows that Her Majesty’s Treasury, led by a Scot, is totally out of touch with the needs of rural and island communities of Scotland and has no understanding of how high fuel taxes affect areas of Scotland like Na h-Eileanan an Iar; further believes that, since these powers exist to assist regional economies, the UK Government should consider at least a 3% reduction in the rate of duty on petrol and diesel for extension across other areas of the Highlands and Islands and other rural and remote parts of Scotland, where it is required; calls on the Scottish Executive, including the Liberal Democrat Minister for Transport and Telecommunications, the Liberal Democrat Minister for Enterprise and Lifelong Learning and all Liberal Democrat MSPs, to give its backing to the campaign to see in Scotland the same powers available to protect rural and island communities from higher fuel prices as are to be enjoyed by French communities, and further calls on Liberal Democrat MSPs to set a new precedent by adopting the same policy in Scotland as that of Liberal Democrat MPs in London.

S2M-3945 Alex Fergusson: Scottish Meat and Sainsbury’s—That the Parliament notes the 30% rise in red meat sales at Sainsbury’s since November 2005, including a 55% rise in the sale of Scotch beef, following a change in policy to promote Scottish beef, lamb and pork more actively; further notes that the consumer is prepared to spend more on high-quality Scottish produce when it is correctly presented and robust information on the origin of the product is available, and believes that Sainsbury’s has introduced a model of retailing Scottish meat which should be followed by all other retailers.

S2M-3944# Carolyn Leckie: Lanarkshire United Health for All—That the Parliament notes NHS Lanarkshire’s consultation, A Picture of Health; is concerned that the board has written off the status quo as "unsustainable" without giving the public the opportunity to make that judgement about their health service; believes that to consult on options which exclude the possibility of a community retaining emergency and planned services at their local hospital is no choice at all; questions the rationale and evidence that the consultation is predicated on, such as whether planned capacity will be sufficient; is concerned that health inequalities will not be addressed and may indeed be perpetuated; recognises the growing anger of communities throughout Lanarkshire, and believes that the board and politicians alike have a duty to explore all options and strategies required to meet the needs of Lanarkshire’s citizens and unite with them to secure full and equal health services for all.

Supported by: Frances Curran, Tommy Sheridan, Ms Rosemary Byrne*

S2M-3943 Carolyn Leckie: Catering Equality—That the Parliament believes that the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body’s (SPCB) current policy towards catering in the Parliament is elitist and exclusive; further believes that congestion in the canteen could be minimised if the first-floor restaurant was opened up; considers that the disproportionate subsidy of the currently named Members’ Restaurant for MSPs on already comfortable salaries, who can also claim expenses for meals when staying overnight, should cease; considers that access to the restaurant should be open at all times to everyone, and believes that the SPCB should consider also opening it to the public so that its capacity can be fully utilised.

S2M-3942 Pauline McNeill: Celebrating Part 1 of the Land Reform Act—That the Parliament commemorates the first anniversary of the access provisions of the Land Reform (Scotland) Act 2003 coming into force and congratulates all the agencies involved in securing a successful first year; welcomes and supports initiatives by the Scottish Executive and local authorities to resource access and calls on them to continue to increase the core path network and improve signage; further calls on all public and other agencies to continue to defend and enforce the provisions of the Act to secure the preventative health and social justice benefits of the outdoors for all, and continues to recognise the contribution of land reform campaigners in improving the nation’s quality of life.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie

S2M-3941<> Ms Sandra White: 7:84 Theatre Company—That the Parliament applauds the work done throughout the years by Glasgow-based 7:84 Theatre Company; reaffirms the valuable artistic and theatrical contribution it has made to the life of Glasgow and Scotland as a whole; condemns the statements contained in Scottish Arts Council (a publicly funded body) reports that 7:84 "has no national role", that there are "serious concerns regarding…the quality of its work", that it is "unclear if the company’s work engages audiences"; further condemns the reports which say the company is "turning sharp political satire into panto for the working classes" and that their audience is "blissfully unassuming"; recognises the fine relationship that the company has with local councils, especially in regard to its excellent outreach programme, and believes that the Scottish Arts Council should commit itself fully to future funding of the company, thereby, not only empowering 7:84 to maintain its high level of contribution to the arts scene, but also discharging its own duties in helping to deliver a pluralistic and artistically diverse Scotland.

Supported by: Mr Kenny MacAskill, Carolyn Leckie, Christine Grahame, Patrick Harvie, Michael Matheson, Frances Curran, Brian Adam, Linda Fabiani, Mr Adam Ingram, Colin Fox, Alex Neil, Rob Gibson, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Rosie Kane, Chris Ballance*, Tommy Sheridan*, Mr Mark Ruskell*, Eleanor Scott*

S2M-3940 John Swinburne: Congratulations to Motherwell FC and the Maggie’s Centre—That the Parliament congratulates Motherwell FC on its proposed assistance to the new Maggie’s Centre at Wishaw General Hospital, which seeks to offer help to those with cancer; notes that all profits from the forthcoming game against Falkirk FC on 11 March 2006 will be donated to the centre, including revenues from the gates, catering and programmes, and further notes that this should give a tremendous boost to the centre. Supported by: Donald Gorrie

S2M-3939 Robin Harper: Save the Palais—That the Parliament notes with alarm that the Fountainbridge Mecca Palais bingo hall, an Edinburgh institution which once attracted young dancers in their droves, is to be demolished to make way for housing, business units and eateries; believes that this building, with its landmark Art Deco frontage, should be preserved at a time when live music and dance venues in Edinburgh are at an all-time low, and recognises that we should encourage the resurgence of dancing generated by recent television programmes as a means of promoting physical exercise in adults.

S2M-3938 Ms Sandra White: Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual and Transgender Youth Scotland’s Progress Report 2006—That the Parliament welcomes the 2006 progress report from LGBT Youth Scotland; notes that, while there may be as many as 100,000 young people who are lesbian, gay, bisexual or transgender, only around 6% have come out by the time they are 16; applauds the positive work done by the group in liaising with local authorities, the police, education authorities, health services, government and others to help shape policy, and fully supports it in its quest to help ease the transition of young people into adulthood.

Supported by: Iain Smith

S2M-3937 Donald Gorrie: Funding for 7:84 Theatre and Scottish Opera Programmes—That the Parliament commends the good educational work done by 7:84 Theatre Company in touring the play, "Tipping Point", and discussion about the G8 summit around 24 secondary schools and other school and community projects, stimulating students and adults to think and argue about important issues; further commends the well-established programme by Scottish Opera of touring do-it-yourself operas around primary schools, and urges that sufficient funding should be made available by the Scottish Arts Council and from the Scottish Executive’s culture, educational and community budgets to enable these programmes to continue and expand so that many more young people can benefit from these life-enhancing experiences.

Supported by: Rosie Kane, Alex Neil, Colin Fox, Chris Ballance, Mr Mark Ruskell, Mike Pringle, Patrick Harvie, Carolyn Leckie, Tommy Sheridan, Mr Jim Wallace, Trish Godman*, Robin Harper*, Eleanor Scott*

S2M-3936 Mr John Swinney: Temporary Relocation of the Stone of Destiny to Scone Palace in May 2006—That the Parliament expresses its regret at the decision of the First Minister not to permit the relocation of the Stone of Destiny to Scone Palace for a temporary period in May 2006 in connection with the 700th anniversary of the coronation of Robert the Bruce at Scone Palace; applauds Scone Palace on the series of events which it has planned in relation to this event in May to boost the local economy; believes that this is an important opportunity to increase awareness of Scottish history that would have been enhanced by the presence of the Stone of Destiny, and appeals to the First Minister to reconsider his decision.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie

S2M-3935 Mr Ted Brocklebank: 500th Anniversary of the Discovery of Tristan Da Cunha—That the Parliament notes that this year is the 500th anniversary of the discovery of the South Atlantic British Overseas Dependency of Tristan da Cunha; acknowledges the historic ties between Scotland and the island, from the founder settler in 1816, Corporal William Glass of Kelso, to the visit in 1867 of HRH Prince Alfred, Duke of Edinburgh, and the subsequent naming of the settlement community as Edinburgh, and suggests that the Scottish Executive should recognise this anniversary by presenting a commemorative gift to this isolated community which is fiercely proud of its connections with the Scottish people and Scotland.

Supported by: Mr Jamie McGrigor, Murray Tosh, Murdo Fraser, Brian Adam, John Scott, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton, Mrs Nanette Milne*, Derek Brownlee*, Bill Aitken*, Robin Harper*, Phil Gallie*

S2M-3933# Alasdair Morgan: Wanlockhead Museum Trust and Museum of Lead Mining—That the Parliament notes that 2006 is the 250th anniversary of the founding of Wanlockhead Miners’ Library, established by the lead miners as part of their educational and cultural activity and now under the care of the Wanlockhead Museum Trust; recognises that the award-winning museum itself is a unique monument to industrial workers in the Lowther Hills, as well as being a valuable economic asset to today’s economy, and believes that the Scottish Executive should act to ensure that arrangements are in place so that the museum’s trustees can plan for the future with some degree of certainty instead of encountering the intermittent financial uncertainty which, on occasion, threatens the very existence of the museum.

S2M-3929 Colin Fox: Fuel Poverty Requires Energy Price Freeze—That the Parliament notes with alarm the statement by the Secretary of State for Trade and Industry, Alan Johnson, that one million people face poverty as a result of energy price rises; believes that this will mean 100,000 Scots facing fuel poverty, and urges the UK Government to recognise that the energy companies have earned massive profits from rising prices and order an immediate energy price freeze to protect those facing fuel poverty.
