Business Bulletin No. 62/2002

Friday 19 April 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-3010 Ms Margo MacDonald: Statement on Holyrood Project—That the Parliament requests that the Presiding Officer, as Chair of the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body, make an accurate statement to the Parliament on the expected completion date, entry date, estimated cost of the new Parliament building and costs associated with any slippage in the timetable for completion and entry previously intimated to the Parliament.

Supported by: Mr Lloyd Quinan*, Alex Neil*

*S1M-3009# Helen Eadie: Dunfermline Press and West Fife Advertiser and Scottish Local Newspaper of the Year Award 2002—That the Parliament congratulates the Dunfermline Press and West Fife Advertiser on winning the Scottish Local Newspaper of the Year Award 2002; recognises the massive contribution that the paper makes to the life of the community in west Fife, and acknowledges the vital role of local newspapers throughout Scotland in providing news and a voice that is sensitive to local concerns.

*S1M-3008 Scott Barrie: Dunfermline Press and West Fife Advertiser—That the Parliament congratulates the Dunfermline Press and West Fife Advertiser on winning the Scottish Local Newspaper of the Year Award 2002; believes that the newspaper has played a valuable role in Dunfermline and west Fife over the past year; looks forward to another year of excellent service, and hopes that it will continue to be Fife’s best selling weekly for many more years to come.

*S1M-3007 Colin Campbell: Anti-social Behaviour Orders—That the Parliament believes that anti-social behaviour orders are not working effectively and that many social housing providers are having difficulty implementing policies and procedures to deal speedily with tenants’ complaints and, in order to meet the needs of complainants, calls upon the Scottish Executive to take urgent action at the earliest possible opportunity to deal with anti-social behaviour.

*S1M-3006 Murdo Fraser: Perthshire Gardens Collection—That the Parliament welcomes the establishment of the Perthshire Gardens Collection; congratulates all of the nine gardens involved in this new initiative on developing this innovative approach to marketing these gardens as attractions and as an excellent means of promoting Perthshire as a short break tourist destination, and wishes them a successful season.

Supported by: Mr Brian Monteith*, Mr Keith Harding*, Mary Scanlon*, John Young*

*S1M-3005 Michael Russell: Queen of the South Football Club Promotion to Divison One—That the Parliament congratulates Queen of the South Football Club, its players, management, board of directors and loyal fans, for their success in securing promotion to the Scottish First Division, a feat which has escaped them for the last 16 years; is certain that this achievement will help to secure the club’s future; looks forward to further success for the club, and commends the contribution made by the manager, John Connolly, who has in two years transformed a football club in financial difficulty and facing relegation into one which is now on the way up and which also has continued ambition to go on climbing.
