Business Bulletin No. 129/2009: Thursday 10 September 2009

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 17 September 2009

Question Time

General

1. Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what importance it places on valuing Care Commission and NHS staff. (S3O-7882)

2. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met representatives of NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde and what issues were discussed. (S3O-7871)

3. Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures contained in the draft Housing (Scotland) Bill are intended to reform the situation surrounding the licensing of houses in multiple occupancy. (S3O-7847)

4. Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide an update on the consequences of the closure of the A83 at the Rest and Be Thankful. (S3O-7791)

5. Nigel Don: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to tackle drugs in Scotland’s prisons. (S3O-7825)

6. Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the forthcoming Housing Bill will tackle public concern that factors continue to rely on responsible tenants to meet the full costs of communal repairs and are not effectively pursuing owners unwilling to contribute. (S3O-7849)

7. Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will support the provision of a Kirkwall-based aircraft providing air ambulance cover as well as inter-island transport for other health services. (S3O-7809)

8. Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what additional transport infrastructure will be in place before the next Offshore Europe Oil and Gas Conference and Exhibition in 2011. (S3O-7874)

9. Not Lodged

10. Not Lodged

Europe, External Affairs and Culture

1. Not Lodged

2. Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the National Trust for Scotland regarding the future of the Bannockburn Heritage Centre. (S3O-7800)

3. Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions are taking place with Glasgow City Council and the architecture and design professions regarding the future of the Lighthouse in Glasgow. (S3O-7879)

4. Bill Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that, to make an intelligent choice about our future as a nation, we should be knowledgeable about our history and culture and, if so, that libraries should play an important role in this. (S3O-7848)

5. Not Lodged

6. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions the Minister for Culture, External Affairs and the Constitution has had with the organisers of major cultural events regarding the need to reduce carbon emissions in organising such events. (S3O-7853)

7. Not Lodged

8. Not Lodged

9. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is supporting initiatives that export Scottish culture and teaching, such as the Scotland-Malawi Partnership run by lead partner Adam Smith College in conjunction with Scotland’s Colleges International. (S3O-7870)

10. Shirley-Anne Somerville: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact the Expo Fund had in improving opportunities for Scotland-based artists at this year’s Edinburgh festivals. (S3O-7843)

Education and Lifelong Learning

1. Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is concerned about the uptake of school meals. (S3O-7881)

2. Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to ensure that teachers who have successfully completed their probationary year are able to secure teaching posts in Scottish schools. (S3O-7866)

3. Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent discussions it has had with the City of Edinburgh Council regarding education. (S3O-7790)

4. Jamie Hepburn: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration it has given to proposals to give parents control over their children’s individual education budgets from local education authorities. (S3O-7832)

5. John Park : To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it has made on delivering 7,800 extra modern apprenticeships. (S3O-7883)

6. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied with the provision and cost of childcare for students in further education. (S3O-7796)

7. Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will use primary or secondary legislation to ensure lower class sizes in schools. (S3O-7880)

8. Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to provide a mechanism to facilitate the funding of replacement buildings for the secondary schools at Laurencekirk and Kemnay in the Aberdeenshire Council area. (S3O-7794)

9. Christopher Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are plans to offer a social or community year along European lines to young people between school and higher education, in order to enhance social responsibility and illustrate career possibilities, and to offer educational credits for the first year in higher education in accordance with the provisions of the Bologna Process. (S3O-7831)

10. Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to assist vulnerable adults involved in the children’s hearing system. (S3O-7835)
