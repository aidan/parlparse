Business Bulletin No. 140 / 2003

Monday 27th October 2003

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-511# Ms Sandra White: Review of Maternity Services in Glasgow-That the Parliament notes the decision of Greater Glasgow Health Board to endorse the recommendations of the maternity working group to close the Queen Mother's Hospital and transfer services to the Southern General Hospital; further notes the concerns of the public and hospital health professionals at the closure of the Queen Mother's Hospital and subsequently services at the Royal Hospital for Sick Children and the adverse effect that this will have on maternity and children's services throughout Scotland, and considers that the Minister for Health and Community Care, Malcolm Chisholm, should launch an immediate investigation into the maternity services review by Greater Glasgow Health Board.

*S2M-510 Marlyn Glen: Stobswell Pharmacy, Dundee-That the Parliament congratulates the Stobswell Pharmacy, Dundee, after its first successful year as a model for community pharmacies; notes the valuable role that pharmacies play in promoting a healthy style of living, and congratulates all those associated with the pharmacy on their work in promoting health in the local community and promoting the name of Dundee through another new project tested in the city to benefit the rest of Scotland.

*S2M-509# Carolyn Leckie: Nursery Nurses' Dispute Still Unresolved-That the Parliament notes the persistent refusal by COSLA to conduct meaningful negotiations with UNISON to resolve the nursery nurses' dispute; recognises that recruitment and retention difficulties will be exacerbated without an acceptable solution and that differences in pay grades and career structure across authorities will lead to local authorities competing with each other for staff, undermining continuity of education for children; further recognises the need for an agreed national settlement binding on local authorities that meets the moderate demands of nursery nurses, and considers that the Scottish Executive should intervene, albeit belatedly, to secure settlement of the dispute.

*S2M-508 Murdo Fraser: Charitable Status of Independent Schools in Perth and Kinross-That the Parliament recognises the economic contribution made by independent schools to Perth and Kinross and the contribution of some 700 jobs and £13 million per annum; further recognises that independent schools currently benefit from charitable status without which many would struggle to continue in existence, and accordingly calls on the Scottish Executive to safeguard the charitable status of independent schools, thus protecting parental choice and safeguarding jobs in Perth and Kinross.

*S2M-507# Helen Eadie: Scottish White Stuff Milk Moustache Campaign-That the Parliament welcomes the launch of the Scottish White Stuff Milk Moustache campaign, a joint campaign of the Milk Development Council and the Scottish Dairy Marketing Company; supports its aims of promoting increased consumption of low fat, semi-skimmed milk as part of a balanced diet, targeted especially at young people who are known to have, on average, lower intakes of vitamins and minerals; applauds the campaign for tailoring its message to the perceptions and interests of young people such as by emphasising the benefits of milk for skin complexion; praises celebrities who have supported the campaign, and acknowledges the complimentarity between the Scottish White Stuff Milk Moustache campaign and the Scottish Executive's Healthy Living campaign.

*S2M-506 Rosie Kane: Support for David Amess MP's Early Day Motion on Asylum Seekers and the Right to Work-That the Parliament congratulates David Amess, MP for Southend West, who has laid an Early Day Motion (EDM) before the Houses of Parliament regretting Her Majesty's Government's decision to deny the right to work to asylum seekers, the right to apply for permission to work having been withdrawn from asylum seekers on 23 July 2002; supports the EDM which states, "That this House regrets the decision in 2002 by the Secretary of State for the Home Department to prevent asylum seekers from working while their applications for residency are under consideration; urges the Home Secretary to reconsider that decision; recognises that issuing temporary work permits would reduce the temptation to work illegally, and believes that allowing asylum seekers to contribute to the British economy would reduce tensions within the local communities where they are located", and calls on the Scottish Executive to make representations to Her Majesty's Government to reverse its decision of 23 July 2002 and allow asylum seekers throughout the UK to seek work while waiting for their claims to be considered.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan*, Carolyn Leckie*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*

*S2M-505 Richard Lochhead: Statues of Justice and Mercy-That the Parliament notes the existence of the statues Justice and Mercy, sculpted in 1637 by Alexander Mylne of Perth, that stood above the great door of Parliament Hall within the old Scottish Parliament; further notes that the statues are currently under the care of the Faculty of Advocates and Court of Session in Parliament House; regrets the lack of awareness and knowledge of these statues; believes that prominently displaying them in the new Parliament building at Holyrood would be an ideal way of linking the old Parliament with the new, and calls on the Presiding Officer to investigate, along with the Faculty of Advocates and Court of Session, the possibility of installing these historical artefacts in a suitably cherished position within the new parliament building.

Supported by: Patrick Harvie*, Jim Mather*, Fergus Ewing*

Supported by: Rosie Kane*, Tommy Sheridan*, Carolyn Leckie*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*
