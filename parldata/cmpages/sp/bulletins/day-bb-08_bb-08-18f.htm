Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (♦).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S3M-2401 Rob Gibson: Highlands and Islands-Friendly Quality Mark—That the Parliament supports a proposal from Sanday Community Council in Orkney for the Scottish Government to award a Highlands and Islands-friendly quality mark for those mail order companies and couriers that do not charge extra for delivering goods to postcodes in the Highlands and Islands; considers that the extra charges that some firms impose for customers in remote and rural areas are unfair, unjust and effectively treat those residents as second-class citizens; welcomes the idea of promoting firms that practise a "one price for all" policy, and calls on those firms that impose extra costs for deliveries to rural areas and islands to follow best practice examples such as Amazon and the Royal Mail.

*S3M-2400 Ian McKee: Farming Concerns—That the Parliament notes with concern the effects of the rising input costs of feed, fuel and fertiliser on Scottish farmers; recognises that the return many farmers make does not cover the costs of production; further recognises the role that farmers play in feeding Scotland’s population, maintaining Scotland’s famous countryside and underpinning rural communities and that they contributed £2.1 billion directly to the Scottish economy in 2007, and welcomes the Scottish Government’s undertaking to meet supermarket bosses to discuss how government and retailers can work together to ensure a sustainable and profitable future for Scottish farming. 

*S3M-2399 Stuart McMillan: Sail Clyde Strategy Launch—That the Parliament congratulates the Scottish Enterprise Sail Clyde Steering Group on the launch of its new strategy, which is to take place on Friday 29 August 2008 in Largs; notes that Sail Clyde is a business development strategy to stimulate expansion of the marine leisure industry and improve facilities for recreational boating in the Firth of Clyde; further notes that Sail Clyde aims to double the number of yachts and motor cruisers on the Clyde in the next decade and to increase significantly marine tourism and marine business of all kinds, and acknowledges that implementing this strategy will help provide more money and jobs in the local economy.

Supported by: Bob Doris*, Jamie McGrigor*, Bill Kidd*, Bashir Ahmad*, Alasdair Allan*, Brian Adam*

*S3M-2398 Robert Brown: National Allotments Week 2008—That the Parliament welcomes National Allotments Week 2008, being held from 11 to 17 August 2008; believes that allotments can make a big contribution to healthier living and knowledge of the environment and the seasonality of food; notes the potential for positive links with local schools, and urges all local authorities to identify more high quality sites to satisfy the growing demand for allotments in Scotland.

*S3M-2397 Bob Doris: Helping Cambuslang Allotments Forum Grow—That the Parliament commends the work of the Cambuslang Allotment Forum and the Scottish Allotments and Gardens Society; welcomes National Allotment Week as an opportunity to highlight the immense physical and mental health benefits tending allotments can bring; believes that by growing fresh produce people can play an important part in improving the nutrition of Scots and help tackle serious issues such as obesity; supports, in particular, the efforts of the Cambuslang Allotment Forum in its attempt to identify suitable land to deal with the list of local residents currently waiting to secure allotments; calls on South Lanarkshire Council to acknowledge that allotments make a significant contribution to 14 of the 45 National Outcomes as detailed in the Scottish Government’s historic concordat with local authorities and therefore strongly encourages South Lanarkshire Council to work constructively with the forum to ensure suitable sites are located, and calls on all budding green-fingered allotment enthusiasts in Rutherglen and Cambuslang to get in contact with the Cambuslang Allotment Forum to find out how they can help the campaign and hopefully have their own allotments in the future.

Supported by: Jamie Hepburn*, Christina McKelvie*, Alasdair Allan*, Bashir Ahmad*, Bill Kidd*, Kenneth Gibson*, Rob Gibson*, Brian Adam*

*S3M-2396 George Foulkes: Students Hit by Credit Crunch—That the Parliament notes with concern the rising cost of rent for students studying in Scotland and that the £71.06 spent on average on rent in Edinburgh has risen by 20% in the last four years alone; recognises that the scrapping of the graduate endowment does nothing to directly help those students most in need of financial help while at university; supports those Labour MSPs calling for student support funding to be boosted to bring it into line with England and Wales; believes that the SNP grandstands about its achievements in higher education while backroom cuts and efficiency savings leave Scotland’s poorest students worse off than their English and Welsh counterparts, and recognises that rent is the single biggest expense for students and that the Scottish Government must do more to alleviate the strains on the student housing market by ruling out HMO quotas, incentivising universities to build more publicly-owned student housing and helping first-time buyers so that there is less competitive pressure on the housing market.

Supported by: Des McNulty*, Mike Pringle*, Ken Macintosh*, Iain Gray*, David Whitton*, Dr Richard Simpson*

*S3M-2395 Cathy Jamieson: Summer Academy at Strathclyde—That the Parliament celebrates the 10th anniversary of the Summer Academy at Strathclyde (S@S), which seeks to widen access to further and higher education and challenge educational inequality in Scotland; notes that over the last 10 years S@S has raised the aspirations of over 9,000 young people drawn from over 135 schools, including Auchinleck, Cumnock and Doon academies, across 13 local authorities through its dynamic, interactive and thought-provoking range of activities; welcomes the fact that the S@S concept has been adopted by educational institutions across Europe and that several hundred international students have visited Scotland to participate, and praises the commitment of the course director, Christine Percival, her staff and the hundreds of mentors who have worked tirelessly over the past 10 years to create such a Scottish success story.

*S3M-2394 Jamie Hepburn: Scottish Youth Parliament Independence Consultation—That the Parliament congratulates the Scottish Youth Parliament on the launch of its consultation on independence and Scotland’s constitutional future; welcomes the non-partisan basis on which the survey is being conducted, and encourages young people across the country to take part and make their views heard.

Supported by: Bill Kidd*, Bob Doris*, Kenneth Gibson*, Alasdair Allan*, Bashir Ahmad*, Nigel Don*, Christina McKelvie*, Stuart McMillan*, Angela Constance*, Rob Gibson*, Brian Adam*

*S3M-2393 Bashir Ahmad: Congratulating Pakistan and India on 61 Years of Independence—That the Parliament congratulates India and Pakistan on the 61st anniversary of their independence from British rule; applauds both countries for their many successes over the last 61 years, including India on becoming the world’s second fastest growing economy and Pakistan for its flourishing research institutes; notes and celebrates the extensive contribution of Indians and Pakistanis living in Scotland; applauds the significant steps both countries have taken in working closer with one another, and wishes them both continued success in the years to come.

Supported by: Elaine Murray*, Bob Doris*, Bill Kidd*, Jim Tolson*, Alasdair Allan*, Ken Macintosh*, Joe FitzPatrick*, Stuart McMillan*, Brian Adam*, Shirley-Anne Somerville*

*S3M-2393.1 David Whitton: Congratulating Pakistan and India on 61 Years of Independence—As an amendment to motion (S3M-2393) in the name of Bashir Ahmad, leave out from "and Pakistan for" to end and insert ", leading the way in many fields of science, technology, mathematics, commerce and trade, and Pakistan for its flourishing research institutes, such as the Applied Economics Research Centre in Karachi and the National Centre of Excellence in Geology in Peshawar; notes and celebrates the extensive contribution of Indians and Pakistanis living in Scotland; recognises that the road to independence was not always smooth, with the largest and most rapid population transfer in history, with an estimated 20 million people leaving their homes; also recognises the loss of life, notably the Jallianwala Bagh massacre, among the non-cooperation movements and during the salt march and civil disobedience; applauds the significant steps both countries have taken in working closer with one another, and wishes them both continued success in the years to come."

Supported by: Bashir Ahmad*, Jackie Baillie*, Mr Frank McAveety*, Ken Macintosh*, Des McNulty*

*S3M-2392 Dr Richard Simpson: Congratulations to the Scottish Rugby Union—That the Parliament notes that the Scottish Rugby Union (SRU) has now appointed 56 development officers across Scotland; welcomes the increasing opportunities being provided by rugby clubs and schools for young people to participate in rugby; acknowledges the important role played by volunteers in developing and sustaining this effort and welcomes the use of funds from confiscation of criminal assets to support the SRU’s work in this field; recognises the increasing ties between the SRU, its clubs and touch rugby; congratulates the Scottish teams participating in the European championship in Paris on 11 to 12 July 2008 on achieving one gold, two silver and a bronze, and calls on businesses to consider developing touch rugby teams as an excellent way of promoting a healthy, social and sporting lifestyle.

*S3M-2391 Willie Coffey: Lt Col Blair Mayne - Posthumous Award of Victoria Cross—That the Parliament recognises the close association between the town of Darvel in the Loudoun Valley and the Special Air Service Regiment; commends the bravery displayed by Lt Col Blair Mayne of that regiment during the Second World War, and joins the Northern Ireland Assembly in calling on the Ministry of Defence to reconsider the case for the posthumous award of the Victoria Cross.

*S3M-2390 Lewis Macdonald: Congratulations to David Florence—That the Parliament congratulates Olympic canoeist, David Florence, a former pupil of Mile End Primary School in Aberdeen, on winning a silver medal for Team GB in the men’s C1 single slalom final; wishes all athletes representing Team GB the best of luck, and looks forward to further success in forthcoming events.

Supported by: Alasdair Allan*, Jamie McGrigor*, Marlyn Glen*, Elaine Murray*, Bashir Ahmad*, Jackie Baillie*, Ken Macintosh*, Bob Doris*, Karen Gillon*, Sandra White*, Rhona Brankin*, Mary Mulligan*, Bill Butler*, John Park*, David Whitton*, Mary Scanlon*, Johann Lamont*, Jamie Hepburn*, Dr Richard Simpson*, Mike Pringle*, Des McNulty*, Jim Tolson*, Alison McInnes*, Nanette Milne*, James Kelly*, Marilyn Livingstone*, Patricia Ferguson*

*S3M-2389 Elaine Smith: Baby Ban on Buses—That the Parliament notes with concern reports that Lothian Buses has introduced what appears to be a ban on prams on its buses; considers the bus company’s apparent decision to ensure that the dedicated space provided on its newer vehicles should not be used for prams but should be left vacant for wheelchair users, even where no one requires it, to be discriminatory towards young families and infants; recognises the vital importance of making public transport more accessible to disabled users, but feels strongly that it would be inappropriate for a service provider to exclude other service users when the space is available; suggests that it is not acceptable that a large number of parents, the vast majority of whom are women, may be deprived of the ability to use their local bus service because Lothian Buses does not seem to be taking a common-sense approach to the use of space on its buses; notes that any exclusion of prams from Lothian Buses’ services is likely to impact worst on poor families and single parents with no access to other forms of transport, resulting in the further exclusion of an already severely marginalised group, and, therefore, urges the Scottish Government to ensure that Lothian Buses, or any other bus service provider, is required to revisit any decision to implement unnecessary and potentially discriminatory access restrictions on its services.

Supported by: Marilyn Livingstone*, Bill Kidd*, Angela Constance*, James Kelly*, Patricia Ferguson*, Shirley-Anne Somerville*

*S3M-2388 Bob Doris: Congratulations to Councillor Dornan—That the Parliament congratulates Councillor James Dornan on his election to the post of leader of the SNP group on Glasgow City Council; notes that the vacancy was created by the stunning success of the SNP in having former group leader, John Mason, elected as Scotland’s newest Member of the UK Parliament; wishes Councillor Dornan and his team well; is confident that he can lead the group to victory in Glasgow at the next council elections; calls on all council groups in Glasgow City Council to work with the Scottish Government to deliver on the joint concordat commitments; hopes that the ruling Labour group in Glasgow City Council will make a genuine attempt to implement the historic concordat with the Scottish Government, and is confident that the SNP opposition group will keep the pressure on Labour in Glasgow to deliver on a range of commitments including reducing class sizes and kinship care payments, two areas where Glasgow Labour has been too slow and SNP MSPs and councillors have been pushing for action.

Supported by: Michael Matheson*, Joe FitzPatrick*, Bashir Ahmad*, Stuart McMillan*, Alasdair Allan*, Jamie Hepburn*, Ian McKee*, Bill Kidd*, Kenneth Gibson*, Gil Paterson*

*S3M-2387 John Scott: Traditional Herbal Medicinal Products Directive—That the Parliament notes that herbal remedies and food supplements are widely used by many people in the United Kingdom; further notes that since the adoption of the traditional herbal medicinal products directive (THMPD) by the European Parliament only 14 products have so far been approved for use; expresses grave concern that the THMPD will lead to thousands of safe and popular herbal remedies and food supplements becoming unavailable, and calls on Her Majesty’s Government to urge the European Union to review this legislation to remove the threat it poses to the herbal remedies sector.

*S3M-2386 Alasdair Allan: Association of Harris Tweed Weavers—That the Parliament congratulates Harris Tweed weavers in the Western Isles on forming the Association of Harris Tweed Weavers; welcomes the formation of a body that can represent not only the interests of weavers but work to promote the industry as a whole, and looks forward to cross-industry co-operation in partnership with the Scottish Government and other agencies to ensure the bright future which Harris Tweed deserves.

Supported by: Jamie McGrigor*, John Wilson*, Mike Pringle*, Dave Thompson*, Bashir Ahmad*, Sandra White*, Bob Doris*, Stuart McMillan*, Michael Matheson*, Bill Kidd*, Rob Gibson*, Kenneth Gibson*, Brian Adam*, Gil Paterson*, Jim Tolson*

*S3M-2386.1 David Whitton: Association of Harris Tweed Weavers—As an amendment to motion (S3M-2386) in the name of Alasdair Allan, insert at end "and calls on the new association to support calls for all entries to the new national tartan register to provide a piece of woven cloth in support of their application, to help boost the weaving industry."

Supported by: Jackie Baillie*, Sarah Boyack*, Karen Gillon*, Jim Tolson*, Dr Richard Simpson*, Elaine Murray*, Mary Mulligan*, Des McNulty*, Marilyn Livingstone*

*S3M-2385 Bashir Ahmad: Fighting in South Ossetia and Georgia—That the Parliament strongly condemns the fighting in South Ossetia and Georgia which has led to over 2,000 reported civilian deaths; calls on both sides involved in the conflict to take up an immediate ceasefire to allow negotiations to begin; views the actions of the Russian Federation as unacceptable and wholly disproportionate; notes with extreme concern the escalation in violence which has spread beyond South Ossetia, and calls upon the United Nations, European Union and other influential partners to intervene in the region to prevent further unnecessary deaths.

Supported by: Bob Doris*, Alasdair Allan*, Bill Kidd*, Michael Matheson*, Cathy Peattie*, Alex Neil*, Sandra White*, Kenneth Gibson*, Joe FitzPatrick*, Dave Thompson*, Mike Pringle*, Christina McKelvie*

*S3M-2384 Jackie Baillie: One Jumper or Two?—That the Parliament expresses concern at Centrica’s recent announcement that the price of gas for consumers will rise by 35% and electricity by 9% while the company’s profits for the first half of 2008 amounted to £992 million; notes that for every 1% rise in energy prices 8,000 more households are pushed into fuel poverty and condemns the comments by Centrica that, in effect, it is time for people to wear two jumpers instead of one as being completely out of touch with the day-to-day struggle of increasing numbers of households to heat their homes and pay their energy bills, and calls on all suppliers to offer meaningful support to low-income households, including offering a social tariff that is lower than the lowest tariff on offer.

Supported by: Des McNulty*, Elaine Murray*, Malcolm Chisholm*, Bill Butler*, Alasdair Allan*, Paul Martin*, Kenneth Gibson*, David Whitton*, Mary Mulligan*, Stuart McMillan*, Elaine Smith*, Iain Gray*, Ken Macintosh*, John Park*, Michael McMahon*, Gil Paterson*, Sarah Boyack*, Angela Constance*, James Kelly*, Marilyn Livingstone*, Patricia Ferguson*

*S3M-2383 Nigel Don: Aberdeen Post Offices—That the Parliament notes with disappointment the decision to close three post offices in Aberdeen, at Mount Street, Mannofield and Nigg, and to consult on a further replacement closure at Bieldside by way of replacing the saved Middlefield post office; congratulates the campaigners who helped to save Middlefield; considers that the inclusion of Bieldside as an additional closure proposal demonstrates that the closure campaign is driven by an artificial quota rather than a robust business case; regrets that Aberdeen’s Labour MPs did not vote to halt the closure campaign when given the opportunity in March 2008; hopes that the community of Bieldside will rally to save their post office, and calls on the UK Government to take urgent action to prevent any further culling of post offices by securing the long-term future of the Post Office card account.

Supported by: Kenneth Gibson*, Alasdair Allan*, Bashir Ahmad*, Stuart McMillan*, Bob Doris*, Sandra White*, Rob Gibson*, Bill Kidd*, Brian Adam*, Gil Paterson*
