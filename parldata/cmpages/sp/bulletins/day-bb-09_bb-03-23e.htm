Business Bulletin No. 52/2009: Monday 23 March 2009
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 20 March 2009

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-22164 John Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3O-6270 by Richard Lochhead on 19 March 2009, where in the 32 single outcome agreements there is reference to fortnightly refuse collections.

S3W-22165 Andy Kerr: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it acknowledges that Strathclyde Partnership for Transport considers that the opening of a direct Ayr to Edinburgh rail service, as contained in the Glasgow Crossrail proposals, would result in an additional 93 million rail passenger kilometres every year and what evidence the Scottish Executive has that its alternative proposals for rail in Glasgow, as set out in the Strategic Transport Projects Review, will produce similar benefits.

S3W-22166Δ Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects responsibility for crofting development to transfer from the Crofters Commission to Highlands and Islands Enterprise and how much resource it proposes to transfer with it. 

S3W-22167 Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what it is doing to increase the eligibility weighting given to local connection in housing allocations.

S3W-22168 Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what incentives it provides to encourage private landlords to let their properties for long-term lets instead of holiday lets.

S3W-22169 Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive how many households are receiving rural home ownership grants, broken down by local authority.

S3W-22170 Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria is used to identify what housing stock is bought from the private sector through the accelerated housing funding.

S3W-22171 Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the housing stock bought from the private sector through the accelerated housing funding is required to meet the Scottish Housing Quality Standard.

S3W-22172 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in the procurement of air ambulances.

S3W-22173 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what definition of physical activity it uses when communicating with schools.

S3W-22174 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what its rationale is for it being illegal for under-18s to buy alcohol but not cigarettes.

S3W-22175 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what the eligibility criteria are for transport provision by ambulance to and from hospital.

S3W-22176 Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many leaflets have been distributed explaining the Energy Assistance Package.

S3W-22177 Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many posters have been distributed explaining the Energy Assistance Package.

S3W-22178 Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many application forms have been distributed explaining the Energy Assistance Package.

S3W-22179 Andy Kerr: To ask the Scottish Executive how many civil servants have worked on its local income tax proposals since May 1997, also broken down by pay grade.

S3W-22180 Andy Kerr: To ask the Scottish Executive how many civil servants are working on its local income tax proposals, also broken down by pay grade.

S3W-22181 Andy Kerr: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reasons it did not publish the most recent version of its local income tax proposals following the Cabinet Secretary for Finance and Sustainable Growth’s announcement on 11 February 2009 (Official Report c. 14893) that they would not be taken forward in the current parliamentary session.

S3W-22182 Andy Kerr: To ask the Scottish Executive what the most recent revenue projections are for a 3p in the pound local income tax rate and how they compare with council tax revenue projections.

S3W-22183 Andy Kerr: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it calculated revenue projections for its proposed local income tax at rates other than 3p in the pound and, if so, what the projections are.

S3W-22184 Andy Kerr: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-18225 by John Swinney on 5 December 2008, whether it will publish the financial memorandum and regulatory impact assessment prepared for its proposed council tax abolition Bill.

S3W-22185 Andy Kerr: To ask the Scottish Executive what it estimated the (a) upfront and (b) annual costs to employers would be of introducing its local income tax.

S3W-22186 Andy Kerr: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it decided what groups of people were to be exempt from the proposed local income tax and, if so, what groups these are.

S3W-22187 Andy Kerr: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it received legal confirmation that its proposed local income tax was compliant with Article 9 of the European Charter of Local Self-Government.

S3W-22188 Andy Kerr: To ask the Scottish Executive what its latest thinking is on how water and sewerage charges would be collected under a local income tax.

S3W-22189 Andy Kerr: To ask the Scottish Executive what its latest thinking is on whether and how second homes would be taxed under a local income tax.

S3W-22190 Andy Kerr: To ask the Scottish Executive what its latest thinking is on whether people living in England but working in Scotland would be required to pay a Scottish local income tax.

S3W-22191 Jamie Hepburn: To ask the Scottish Executive how many incidents of internet fraud have been reported to police in each of the last five years.

S3W-22192 Jamie Hepburn: To ask the Scottish Executive what concerns it has about internet fraud.

S3W-22193 Jamie Hepburn: To ask the Scottish Executive what action police forces are taking to tackle internet fraud.

S3W-22194 Jamie Hepburn: To ask the Scottish Executive whether agencies other than the police are involved in tackling internet fraud.
