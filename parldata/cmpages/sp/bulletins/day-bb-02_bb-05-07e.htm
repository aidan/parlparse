Business Bulletin No. 73/2002

Tuesday 7 May 2002

Section E : Written questions lodged on 3 May 2002
  

Questions marked with a triangle () are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S1W-25599 Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in reducing the number of adults lacking basic literacy and numeracy skills.

S1W-25600 Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive what support the Crown Office and procurator fiscal service give to the victims of, and witnesses to, domestic violence who are fearful of reprisals.

S1W-25601 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to promote the use of home produced food and drink to visitors and tourists to Scotland and to increase the economic value of such produce.

S1W-25602 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-24755 by Mr Jim Wallace on 29 April 2002, how many prison officers attended the risk workshop referred to in section 2.7 of the Financial Review of Scottish Prisons Service Estates Review prepared by PricewaterhouseCoopers, broken down by rank.

S1W-25603 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-24755 by Mr Jim Wallace on 29 April 2002, how many members of staff from the Scottish Prison Service (SPS) who attended the risk workshop referred to in section 2.7 of the Financial Review of Scottish Prisons Service Estates Review prepared by PricewaterhouseCoopers were attached to SPS headquarters.

S1W-25604 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-24755 by Mr Jim Wallace on 29 April 2002, whether anyone who attended the risk workshop referred to in section 2.7 of the Financial Review of Scottish Prisons Service Estates Review prepared by PricewaterhouseCoopers (PwC), was attached to the Scottish Prison Service (SPS) headquarters but was not employed either by the SPS or by PwC and, if so, what organisation they came from.

S1W-25605 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-24755 by Mr Jim Wallace on 29 April 2002, how many members of staff in total from the Scottish Prison Service attended the risk workshop referred to in section 2.7 of the Financial Review of Scottish Prisons Service Estates Review prepared by PricewaterhouseCoopers.

S1W-25606 Bill Aitken: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to remove the right to buy fishing rights from part three of the Land Reform (Scotland) Bill.

S1W-25607 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will provide a substantive answer to question S1W-21658 lodged on 10 January 2002.

S1W-25608 John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive how many children have been diagnosed with myalgic encephalomyelitis and chronic fatigue syndrome in each of the past five years.

S1W-25609 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance it has issued to local authorities on the licensing of taxis and private hire cars with regard to the Disability Discrimination Act 1995.

S1W-25610 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has consulted the Scottish Taxi Federation about provisions relating to taxis in the Disability Discrimination Act 1995.

S1W-25611 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration has been given to standardising the fees for taxi and private hire car licenses under section 12 of the Civic Government (Scotland) Act 1982.

S1W-25612 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to promote throughout Scotland the practice of Trinity Academy in Leith of capping class sizes at 20 and what the reasons are for the position on this matter.

S1W-25613 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many and what percentage of the people undertaking modern apprenticeships are (a) male and (b) female.

S1W-25614 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) males and (b) females have gained permanent employment as a result of the modern apprenticeship scheme.

S1W-25615 Mrs Lyndsay McIntosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what mechanism and procedure it has in place to target specific sectors of the population who may be especially at risk from fuel poverty.

S1W-25616 Mrs Lyndsay McIntosh: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to promote the fuel poverty initiative amongst landlords.

S1W-25617 Mrs Lyndsay McIntosh: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will measure any changes in the numbers of those suffering from fuel poverty from now until 2006.

S1W-25618 Mrs Lyndsay McIntosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what its definition is of fuel poverty.

S1W-25619 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive what national guidelines exist on the installation of traffic lights in towns and, in particular, on the maximum number of sets of traffic lights practicable within a defined area.

S1W-25620 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive what studies it has carried out or commissioned into the effectiveness of bus lanes in speeding up traffic flows in town centres.

S1W-25621 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has carried out or commissioned any independent audit of the effectiveness of town centre traffic management arrangements instigated by local authorities and, if not, how it monitors such arrangements.

S1W-25622 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has evaluated the effectiveness of the SCOOT traffic management system deployed in Dumfries town centre; if so, what the results of this evaluation were and, if not, what plans it has to carry out or commission any such evaluation.

S1W-25623 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will respond to the recent letter from Mr Watrett of Dumfries to the First Minister in connection with the planning system.

S1W-25624 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive whether risks of delays in the construction of the Loch Katrine Water Project are borne by (a) Scottish Water or (b) its contractors.

S1W-25625 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive whether risks of potential fines for non-compliance with any relevant legislation and EU directives are borne by (a) it, (b) Scottish Water or (c) its contractors.

S1W-25626 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive how many staff at director level left West of Scotland Water Authority for roles at Thames Water in the last year.
