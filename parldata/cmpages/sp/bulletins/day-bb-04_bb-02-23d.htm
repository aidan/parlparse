Business Bulletin No. 32 / 2004

Monday 23 February 2004

Section D : Oral Questions
  

Question Time

1. Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Minister for Tourism, Culture and Sport next plans to meet Scottish Opera and what issues will be discussed. (S2O-1357)

2. Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive what research is being undertaken into the incidence of asthma in Scotland. (S2O-1375)

3. Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has received representations about the City of Edinburgh Council's school catchment areas' review. (S2O-1366)

4. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether ministers have any plans to visit galleries or museums in Fife. (S2O-1350)

5. Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will support the Scottish skiing industry. (S2O-1361)

6. Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to promote and encourage participation in the European Year of Education through Sport. (S2O-1354)

7. Mr Mark Ruskell: To ask the Scottish Executive what public investment the commitment to build 300 new schools by 2009 represents and what possibilities such investment offers in respect of sustainable energy. (S2O-1369)

8. Mr Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive on what date it will publish the review of area tourist boards. (S2O-1347)

9. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive what effect a European Commission decision making state aid to out-of-town airports illegal would have on the Ayrshire economy. (S2O-1331)

10. Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what the role is of Mohs micrographic surgery in the management of skin cancer in the NHS. (S2O-1334)

11. Chris Ballance: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration it has given to setting interim targets as part of its policy to stabilise road traffic at 2001 levels by 2021. (S2O-1370)

12. Bill Aitken: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will discuss with Glasgow City Council the council's level of uncollected council tax. (S2O-1341)

13. Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive how many patients are currently awaiting transfer from NHS hospitals to care homes. (S2O-1352)

14. Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will liaise with mobile phone operators to promote greater awareness of mobile phone theft. (S2O-1372)

15. Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to provide funding to the Loch Lomond Rescue Service in line with the recent funding announcement for mountain rescue teams. (S2O-1346)

16. Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it has made in its review of NHS prescription charges. (S2O-1340)

17. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the review of science centres will include the role of centres such as Satrosphere in Aberdeen. (S2O-1336)

18. Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive what its policy is on the reintroduction of native species to Scotland. (S2O-1355)

19. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive when its review of local government finance will commence and whether it will address the specific issues faced by pensioners in paying their council tax. (S2O-1365)

20. Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to support social firms and community businesses. (S2O-1335)

21. Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on sectarianism in sport in Scotland and its implications for tourism and inward investment. (S2O-1333)

22. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in reforming the criminal justice system. (S2O-1373)

23. Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to further encourage the development of broadband telecommunications services in rural areas in north east Scotland. (S2O-1349)

24. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is supporting the reduction of excessive packaging on products. (S2O-1356)

25. George Lyon: To ask the Scottish Executive when a decision will be taken on the relocation of jobs to Tiree. (S2O-1353)

26. Elaine Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to protect sex industry workers from exploitation. (S2O-1363)

27. Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive what the next steps in the Clyde waterfront regeneration project will be and what level of investment, over what period, is being committed to the project. (S2O-1343)

28. Mark Ballard: To ask the Scottish Executive whether congestion charging has the potential to help alleviate problems of congestion in Edinburgh, enable essential funds to be raised to support public transport initiatives across south east Scotland, such as the proposed Edinburgh trams scheme, and enhance the region's environment through reducing noise and air pollution. (S2O-1368)

29. Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive how many abattoirs there currently are and how many have closed down in the last 20 years. (S2O-1371)

30. Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making in helping local authorities develop sustainable strategies for dealing with waste. (S2O-1374)
