Business Bulletin No. 172/2010: Tuesday 23 November 2010
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 22 November 2010

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-37717 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will protect funding for new tree planting to meet its target of 7,500 hectares of new trees in the next 12 months, as referred to by the Cabinet Secretary for Rural Affairs and the Environment at the Forestry in the Low Carbon Economy conference on 2 June 2010.

S3W-37718 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive, following the announcement of the draft budget for 2011-12, how the planting of extra hectares of trees required to meet the target of 100 million trees by 2015 will be financed.

S3W-37719 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to reach its target of planting 100 million trees by 2015, which is equivalent to planting 10,000 hectares of trees per year.

S3W-37720 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that it will meet its target of planting 100 million trees by 2015, given that the number of trees planted annually since 2007 is estimated at 6.7 million.

S3W-37721 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive on what date the short-life working group established to review school meal and school clothing grant provision submitted its report.

S3W-37722 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive on what date it expects to complete its consideration of the report of the short-life working group established to review school meal and school clothing grant provision.

S3W-37723 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people have been prosecuted for possession of illegal drugs in (a) 2008-09 and (b) 2009-10, broken down by (i) police force area and (ii) type of drug.

S3W-37724 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people have been convicted of possession of illegal drugs in (a) 2008-09 and (b) 2009-10, broken down by (i) police force area and (ii) type of drug.

S3W-37725 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people convicted of possessing cocaine received (a) a custodial sentence, (b) a community sentence order, (c) a probation order, (d) a drug treatment and testing order, (e) a fine or (f) another disposal in (i) 2008-09 and (ii) 2009-10, broken down by police force area.

S3W-37726 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people convicted of possessing ecstasy received (a) a custodial sentence, (b) a community sentence order, (c) a probation order, (d) a drug treatment and testing order, (e) a fine or (f) another disposal in (i) 2008-09 and (ii) 2009-10, broken down by police force area.

S3W-37727 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people convicted of possessing heroin received (a) a custodial sentence, (b) a community sentence order, (c) a probation order, (d) a drug treatment and testing order, (e) a fine or (f) another disposal in (i) 2008-09 and (ii) 2009-10, broken down by police force area.

S3W-37728 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people convicted of possessing amphetamines received (a) a custodial sentence, (b) a community sentence order, (c) a probation order, (d) a drug treatment and testing order, (e) a fine or (f) another disposal in (i) 2008-09 and (ii) 2009-10, broken down by police force area.

S3W-37729 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people convicted of possessing cannabis received (a) a custodial sentence, (b) a community sentence order, (c) a probation order, (d) a drug treatment and testing order, (e) a fine or (f) another disposal in (i) 2008-09 and (ii) 2009-10, broken down by police force area.

S3W-37730 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it took legal advice regarding its decision to cease payments to HM Revenue and Customs to maintain the database required to implement the Scottish variable rate.

S3W-37731 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it sought the advice of the Council of Economic Advisors prior to suspending its right to exercise the Scottish variable rate and, if so, whether it will publish this advice.

S3W-37732 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive whether its decision to cease payments to HM Revenue and Customs to maintain the database required to implement the Scottish variable rate was discussed at Cabinet and, if so, on what dates.

S3W-37733 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive whom it consulted prior to its decision to suspend its right to exercise the Scottish variable rate.

S3W-37734 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason it did not place information regarding its decision to suspend its right to exercise the Scottish variable rate on its website.

S3W-37735 Aileen Campbell: To ask the Scottish Executive under what circumstances a planning authority will advise a developer to change the name or description of a planning proposal and what the relevant legislation or regulations would be.

S3W-37736 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether Barnett consequentials for the recently announced additional £400 million for short breaks for carers in England will be reflected in the Scottish budget.

S3W-37737 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what it will do to prevent Scotland falling behind in short breaks provision following the announcement of an additional £400 million for short breaks in England.

S3W-37738 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will ensure that the £5 million of Carer Information Strategy funding in the Scottish budget will be used to directly benefit unpaid carers and young carers.

S3W-37739 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-37161 by Shona Robison on 11 November 2010, how much has been allocated for the anticipated cost of procuring a commercial host broadcast service provider.

S3W-37740 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-37161 by Shona Robison on 11 November 2010, how much it has estimated that the sale of the domestic and international broadcaster rights will generate.

S3W-37741 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what preparation the Court of Session Rules Council is making regarding new rules governing the award of protective cost orders in environmental cases, following the Scottish Government’s response to the Report of the Scottish Civil Courts Review.

S3W-37742 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects the Court of Session Rules Council to promulgate new rules governing the award of protective cost orders in environmental cases.

S3W-37743 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Court of Session Rules Council will take account of the recent decision of the Aarhus Convention Compliance Committee regarding protective cost orders in environmental cases in its consideration of new rules governing the award of such orders.

S3W-37744 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers protective cost orders to be sufficient to meet the requirements of access to justice without prohibitive cost in environmental matters as set out in the Aarhus Convention on Access to Information, Public Participation in Decision-making and Access to Justice in Environmental Matters.

S3W-37745 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration it will give to the access to justice requirements of the Aarhus Convention on Access to Information, Public Participation in Decision-making and Access to Justice in Environmental Matters when replacing the current tests of title and interest with a single and simpler test of sufficient interest, as stated in its response to the Report of the Scottish Civil Courts Review.

S3W-37746 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether communities of interest and non-governmental organisations will have greater access to justice as required by the broad access to justice requirements of the Aarhus Convention on Access to Information, Public Participation in Decision-making and Access to Justice in Environmental Matters when it replaces the current tests of title and interest with a single and simpler test of sufficient interest, as stated in its response to the Report of the Scottish Civil Courts Review.

S3W-37747 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration it will give to the timely access to justice requirements of the Aarhus Convention on Access to Information, Public Participation in Decision-making and Access to Justice in Environmental Matters when introducing a time limit to bring a case as stated in its response to the Report of the Scottish Civil Courts Review.

S3W-37748 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will take account of the legal system of England and Wales and the Aarhus Compliance Committee’s recent ruling in relation to non-compliance with the Aarhus Convention on Access to Information, Public Participation in Decision-making and Access to Justice in Environmental Matters when implementing the reforms outlined in paragraphs 168 to 175 of its response to the Report of the Scottish Civil Courts Review.

S3W-37749 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive when a decision will be issued on the compulsory purchase order for Corunna Street, Glasgow.

S3W-37750 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive how long the reporter has taken to report on the compulsory purchase order at Corunna Street, Glasgow.

S3W-37751 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-37254 by John Swinney on 22 November 2010, whether it will provide a (a) job description and (b) salary for each of the posts listed.
