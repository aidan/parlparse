Business Bulletin No. 92 / 2004

Tuesday 1st June 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-1373 Michael Matheson: Falkirk Mini-Olympics-That the Parliament congratulates Falkirk Council on organising a mini-Olympic games where 1,800 pupils from 51 primary schools will compete together in a variety of sports; welcomes the fact that each school will represent a chosen country; wishes the participants well at the centre of excellence in Grangemouth Stadium, and believes that the pupils will enter into the true spirit of the Olympic movement.

*S2M-1372 Carolyn Leckie: Money Owed to Nursery Nurses-That the Parliament notes that large numbers of nursery nurses do not receive holiday pay, despite their entitlement under the working time directive and regulations; believes that nursery nurses should have been in receipt of full holiday entitlement since 1998; condemns COSLA and individual employers for not correcting this wrong and withholding monies that nursery nurses are lawfully entitled to, and believes that COSLA should seek to ensure that local authorities reimburse nursery nurses in full for all outstanding holiday entitlement and offer a full explanation and apology for its omissions.

*S2M-1371# Elaine Smith: Launch of Osteoporosis Mobile Scanner in Coatbridge-That the Parliament welcomes the launch in Coatbridge of the first NHS mobile scanner for osteoporosis; recognises that one in three women in Scotland suffer from osteoporosis, but that many women are not aware that they have reduced bone density; notes that there are 20,000 osteoporotic fractures in Scotland every year, which cost the NHS £1.7 billion per annum and that this figure will continue to rise as we live longer; recognises that this is a health issue of particular importance to the women of Scotland; congratulates NHS Lanarkshire for supporting this innovative project; further congratulates local Coatbridge GP, Dr Eamonn Brankin, the project leader, for his vision and tenacity in securing the loan of this mobile service; notes, however, that this is a temporary solution, only available for 16 weeks, and believes that the Scottish Executive should consider ways of securing future funding for this example of best practice in Coatbridge and pursue the option of rolling it out across Scotland.

*S2M-1370 Frances Curran: US Aggression Towards Cuba-That the Parliament condemns the report announced on 6 May 2004 by the misnamed "Commission for Assistance to a Free Cuba", appointed by President George W Bush, which contains measures to further tighten the US Government's hostile policy against Cuba; notes that the measures include new restrictions on Cuban-born citizens living in the US travelling to Cuba and spending money on their families there and, even more worryingly, measures aimed at subverting the Cuban Government by increasing the funds provided to counter-revolutionary groups, developing a global media campaign to undermine Cuba's image at international level and creating a worldwide disinformation campaign aimed at discrediting the Cuban tourist industry, thereby affecting the Cuban economy; further notes that some $59 million has been earmarked for these measures, which also include promises of assistance to a "post-Castro government"; considers that these measures are part of a longer-term plan to recolonize Cuba; condemns the US Government's attempt to increase the extremely difficult conditions already imposed on the Cuban people by the illegal economic and investment blockade it has enforced for 45 years; declares that this further attempt to subdue the Cuban population through hunger and disease is a violation of human rights and constitutes a flagrant breach of international law; notes that the Cuban Government fears that these measures are a prelude to invasion by the United States, and calls on Her Majesty's Government to distance itself urgently from the US policy of aggression towards Cuba and join with countries, including Spain and Mexico, in denouncing this report and the measures contained within.

Supported by: Carolyn Leckie*, Campbell Martin*

*S2M-1369 Donald Gorrie: South Lanarkshire Council Planning for Sydes Braes-That the Parliament deeply regrets the decision of South Lanarkshire Council's planning committee to approve the application for the construction of a crematorium at the Sydes Braes site in High Blantyre; notes that this decision was taken in spite of almost 2,000 letters of objection from local residents, and believes that any further action in relation to the application should not be taken without a public inquiry which takes full and proper account of local views on the proposals.

S2M-1368 Mike Watson: Incitement to Racial Hatred-That the Parliament notes with disgust the British National Party's election leaflet for the European Parliament elections, delivered to every household in Scotland; is appalled that its xenophobic content is designed to stir up racial hatred and division; further notes that the Scottish Refugee Council has issued a detailed rebuttal of the assertions which it contains, and urges the Lord Advocate to examine the content of the leaflet with a view to bringing a prosecution under the appropriate legislation.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson*, Michael Matheson*, Frances Curran*, Carolyn Leckie*

S2M-1367 Chris Ballance: Joint ScotRail and FirstBus Initiative-That the Parliament welcomes the joint initiative by ScotRail and FirstBus to provide a connecting bus and rail service with through-ticketing between Haddington and Edinburgh; further welcomes the initial special offer of free travel for a trial period between 24 May and 20 June 2004, and recommends that other bus companies enter into similar arrangements with ScotRail and the new rail franchise holder.

Supported by: Mr Kenny MacAskill*, Patrick Harvie*, Mike Pringle*, Tommy Sheridan*

S2M-1366 Frances Curran: Racist Election Literature-That the Parliament notes that*, in the opinion of Positive Action in Housing and other anti-racism campaigners,* the British National Party's current European election leaflet amounts to an incitement to racial hatred which is an offence under the Public Order Act 1986; is concerned that tens of thousands of pounds of public money have been used to deliver this vile, racist message of hatred to hundreds of thousands of homes across Scotland; further notes that Royal Mail employees, who have objected to delivering the leaflet, have been threatened with disciplinary action and that, so far, despite dozens of complaints by members of the public, the police have failed to bring a prosecution; believes that democratic principles are undermined when minority ethnic communities are placed in a state of fear by literature of this nature, and calls on the Scottish Executive's Law Officers to urgently investigate the possibility of prosecuting the British National Party for public order offences.

Supported by: Carolyn Leckie, John Swinburne, Campbell Martin, Tommy Sheridan, Rosie Kane*, Alex Neil*, Elaine Smith*, Michael Matheson*, Linda Fabiani*

S2M-1365 Ms Rosemary Byrne: Depleted Uranium-That the Parliament congratulates Dr Helen Caldicott, a paediatrician and president of the American Nuclear Policy Research Institute on her calls for the US military to clean up the depleted uranium scattered across Iraq to prevent future health problems such as cancer and birth defects; notes that the depleted uranium ammunition currently being used by the US military could seriously harm both service personnel and civilians living in the area for decades to come; further notes Dr Caldicott's calls for the US Government to compensate people with cancer related to depleted uranium, and calls on the Scottish Executive to put pressure upon the Ministry of Defence to stop the testing of depleted uranium at Dundrennan as a matter of urgency, undertake a full health investigation into the local area and fully compensate those individuals whose health has been affected by this substance.

Supported by: Frances Curran*, Carolyn Leckie*

S2M-1364 Ms Rosemary Byrne: Tweed Forum-That the Parliament congratulates the Tweed Forum on its Bridging the Border Education Project, a project that helps young people to enjoy and understand their natural and cultural heritage; notes that the work of the forum involves educational activities and events for young people, youth leaders and teachers; further notes the innovative way in which this project combines environmental awareness with educational activities, and urges the Scottish Executive to support all similar projects that have both educational and emotional benefits for youth leaders and young people.

Supported by: Frances Curran*, Carolyn Leckie*

Supported by: Michael Matheson

S2M-1360 Carolyn Leckie: Salute to the Nursery Nurses-That the Parliament salutes the struggle of nursery nurses, the majority of whom enter their 13th week of all-out indefinite action; is dismayed by the intransigence and lack of care displayed by COSLA, the individual local authorities and the Scottish Executive, despite the nursery nurses having an obviously just case and the spectre of thousands of children not receiving an education for over three months; believes that nursery nurses have demonstrated great dignity in the pursuit of their campaign, in direct contrast to their employers and the Executive who have treated them with contempt by refusing to use the power and means at their disposal that would have resolved this dispute without the costs incurred by nursery nurses, children and parents, and further believes that the Executive should take immediate steps to ensure that a national pay and grading structure is put in place for nursery nurses.

Supported by: Campbell Martin, Rosie Kane*, Frances Curran*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Colin Fox*

S2M-1359 Tricia Marwick: Neurological Alliance of Scotland-That the Parliament welcomes the launch on 26 May 2004 of the Neurological Alliance of Scotland; notes that an estimated one million people in Scotland live with a neurological condition, a tenth of whom are disabled by that condition; while encouraged by the recent increase in the number of neurologists, deplores the long waiting times to see a specialist and the continuing geographical inconsistencies across Scotland in the provision of neurological services, and supports the alliance's call for a strategic approach to developing services to meet identified neurological needs throughout Scotland.

Supported by: Michael Matheson

S2M-1357 Michael McMahon: Scottish Premier League Deal with Setanta-That the Parliament notes with disappointment the decision by the Scottish Premier League to agree an exclusive four-year deal with pay-per-view company, Setanta; believes that this will restrict the ability of ordinary football fans to watch Scotland's most popular spectator sport; considers this to be yet another short-sighted decision by those who run Scottish football, and urges the Scottish Executive to make representations to Her Majesty's Government so that the necessary steps are taken to ensure that there will be live football available on terrestrial television in Scotland once this restrictive deal ends.

Supported by: Campbell Martin

S2M-1356 Mark Ballard: Statutory Regulation of Acupuncture-That the Parliament notes the consultation Regulation of herbal medicine and acupuncture: Proposals for statutory regulation being undertaken by Her Majesty's Government's Department of Health, and notes that this encompasses regulation of complementary and alternative medicine in Scotland; notes that these proposals for statutory regulation favour the establishment of a shared Complementary and Alternative Medicine Council for herbal medicine and traditional acupuncture which could then be extended to other unregulated complementary and alternative medicine professions; further notes that health delivery in Scotland is different to that in the rest of the United Kingdom and, therefore, may require different measures to the rest of the UK; notes that traditional acupuncture is a central part of the system of Chinese medicine, which is a holistic approach to health based on over 2000 years of observation and analysis, and has a distinct theoretical basis; notes the high standards of regulation, health and safety provision, extensive training and professional codes of practice of those practitioners who are accredited by the British Acupuncture Council, the UK's main regulatory and representative body for the practice of acupuncture involving over 2,400 professionally-qualified acupuncturists; believes that Chinese medicine, as a separate system of medical practice, should be defined, governed and accredited separately and according to the current standards of the British Acupuncture Council, British Acupuncture Accreditation Board and World Health Organization; believes that medical and other practitioners working within the western medical tradition should avoid the term acupuncture unless it is used as part of the Chinese medicine medical tradition, and believes that the distinct benefits and attributes of Chinese medicine should be recognised by the creation of a separate statutory body to accredit, govern and act as a professional body to these activities.

S2M-1355 Mr Kenny MacAskill: Murrayfield Alcohol Ban-That the Parliament recognises the vital role played by the Scottish Rugby Union in promoting rugby both at international and grassroots level; further recognises the financial difficulties and burdens faced by, and placed upon, it; is aware of the proposal within the Nicholson report to trial alcohol sales at a sports ground; notes the success of the BT Cup Final and the Scotland versus Barbarians matches at Murrayfield at which alcohol was sold; recalls that there has never been any history of crowd trouble at rugby internationals in Scotland; notes that alcohol may be sold at pop concerts, rugby league games and American football matches but not at rugby internationals at the stadium; further notes that Murrayfield is the only major venue in the Six Nations rugby tournament to have alcohol provision precluded at international matches, and therefore calls on the Scottish Executive to review its proposal to continue the ban on the selling of alcohol at international games at Scottish rugby's national stadium.

Supported by: Mr Stewart Maxwell

S2M-866# Des McNulty: Asbestos-Related Illnesses-That the Parliament notes with deep concern recent projections regarding the increasing prevalence of asbestos-related illnesses in Scotland; notes that the historic concentration of heavy industry on Clydeside has left a devastating health legacy, affecting many former shipyard and engineering workers and their families; notes that although former shipyard communities show the highest incidence of asbestos-related disease, evidence now shows that ever-increasing numbers of workers from other parts of Scotland have also become victims of asbestos-related illnesses due largely to the past use of asbestos in construction; considers that the Scottish Executive should give urgent consideration to bringing forward an integrated strategy to assist all those affected by asbestos-related illnesses, which should combine screening and testing people who may have been at risk in order to ensure early diagnosis, counselling and support for victims and their families, and the provision of specialist palliative care, and further considers that the Scottish Executive should work along with COSLA, NHS boards and other key bodies, including the Health and Safety Executive to ensure that necessary information is made available to potential sufferers from asbestos-related illnesses and all those professionals in health, local government and other services who may be in the position of assisting victims and their families.
