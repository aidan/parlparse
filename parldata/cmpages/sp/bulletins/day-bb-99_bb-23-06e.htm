BUSINESS BULLETIN 23/1999

Wednesday 23 June 1999

Section E –  Written questions lodged on 22 June 1999
 

S1W-163 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to make available information relating to the number of people detained in Scotland under immigration or asylum legislation in the last three years, the establishments in which they have been detained and for how long, and whether it intends to provide additional accommodation for such people.

 

S1W-164 Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to recognise the importance of rural areas and to ensure that rural development is given a high priority over the next four years.

 

S1W-165 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide information on the estimated costs of the consultation process and investigation into its proposal to impose a toll on the M8 motorway.

 

S1W-166 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide information on the estimated annual administrative costs of operating a toll on the M8 motorway.

 

S1W-167 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide information on the status of New Housing Partnership bids rejected under the last round of awards.

 

S1W-168 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the relevant Scottish Minister will be willing, if requested, to visit Tillydrone to discuss with the local community their bid for assistance for local housing improvements.

 

S1W-169 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether, after 1 July, it will provide information regarding the process for consideration of the bid lodged by Aberdeen City Council on behalf of the Tillydrone community for cash aid towards housing improvements under the New Housing Partnership initiative.

 

S1W-170 Mrs Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to consider re-classifying the A801 as a trunk road, given its strategic importance to the economy of West Lothian, the Falkirk area, and the Central Belt of Scotland as a whole.

 

S1W-171 Bill Aitken: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to increase the level of home ownership in Scotland.

 

S1W-172 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to establish a forum for dialogue with the film and television industry.

 

S1W-173 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to review the role in the economy of Scotland’s film and television industry.

 

S1W-174 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive within what timescale and under what procedure it intends to consider the City of Glasgow Council’s proposals for stock transfer under the New Housing Partnership initiative.

 

S1W-175 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what financial and administrative criteria it intends to use in assessing the viability of proposals submitted by local authorities under the New Housing Partnership initiative.

 

S1W-176 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to enable greater Glasgow Health Board to modernise or replace its hospital buildings.

 

S1W-177 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive when it intends to make a decision as to the location of the Food Standards Agency in Scotland, and what criteria it intends to apply in making this decision.

 

S1W-178 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it intends to take to address public concern over the potential health risks in relation to mobile telephone telecommunication masts.

 

S1W-179 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive when it envisages the Food Standards Agency being operational in Scotland.

 

S1W-180 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive to detail with whom it intends to consult prior to making decisions regarding the Food Standards Agency in Scotland.

 

S1W-181 Mr Keith Harding: To ask the Scottish Executive whether or not it intends to introduce any system of proportional representation voting for local government elections in Scotland.

 

S1W-182 Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans a review of the Crofters Commission.

 

S1W-183 Miss Annabel Goldie: To ask the Scottish Executive if it intends to give consideration to the current absence of a Right of Appeal to an unsuccessful objector against a planning application in which planning permission has been granted by the planning authority.

 

*S1W-108 Winnie Ewing: Withdrawn
