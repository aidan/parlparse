Business Bulletin No. 72/2011: Monday 13 June 2011
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 10 June 2011

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S4W-00671 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what annual unitary charge payments have been made by Aberdeen City Council for the 3Rs Project in Aberdeen each year to date.

S4W-00672 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive how much Aberdeen City Council will pay in annual unitary charge payments under the 3Rs Project in Aberdeen over the lifetime of the project.

S4W-00673 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding it has provided for the 3Rs Project in Aberdeen in each year to date.

S4W-00674 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding it will provide over the lifetime of the 3Rs Project in Aberdeen.

S4W-00675 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what the capital value is of schools built as part of the 3Rs Project in Aberdeen.

S4W-00676 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive how many 999 calls have been diverted away from the ambulance crew nearest to the incident in the last year.

S4W-00677 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive how many staff in each ambulance station have opted to be available to be called out during their rest break.

S4W-00678 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive how many staff in each ambulance station have opted not to be available to be called out during their rest break.

S4W-00679 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive how many staff in each ambulance station are entitled to a rest break.

S4W-00680 Margaret McDougall: To ask the Scottish Executive what (a) discussions, (b) correspondence and (c) meetings it has had with GlaxoSmithKline regarding the location of its proposed new plant.

S4W-00681 Margaret McDougall: To ask the Scottish Executive what (a) discussions, (b) correspondence and (c) meetings it has had with North Ayrshire Council regarding the location of GlaxoSmithKline’s proposed new plant.

S4W-00682 Margaret McDougall: To ask the Scottish Executive what (a) discussions, (b) correspondence and (c) meetings it has had with Angus Council regarding the location of GlaxoSmithKline’s proposed new plant.

S4W-00683 Margaret McDougall: To ask the Scottish Executive what (a) discussions, (b) correspondence and (c) meetings it has had with the Department of Business, Innovation and Skills regarding the location of GlaxoSmithKline’s proposed new plant.

S4W-00684 Margaret McDougall: To ask the Scottish Executive what (a) discussions, (b) correspondence and (c) meetings it has had with the Scotland Office regarding the location of GlaxoSmithKline’s proposed new plant.

S4W-00685 Margaret McDougall: To ask the Scottish Executive how it supports job creation in North Ayrshire.

S4W-00686 Margaret McDougall: To ask the Scottish Executive how Skills Development Scotland supports job creation in North Ayrshire.

S4W-00687 Margaret McDougall: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps Scottish Development International is taking to encourage GlaxoSmithKline to locate its proposed new plant in Scotland.

S4W-00689 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it remains its position that the net funding gap between English and Scottish universities is £93 million.

S4W-00691 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on whether university tuition fees in England will rise with inflation and what impact such rises will have on the net funding gap between English and Scottish institutions.

S4W-00692 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will bring forward legislation to increase tuition fees for students from the rest of the United Kingdom.

S4W-00693 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it expects the number of fees only places in Scottish universities provided in 2011-12 to be replicated every year until the end of the current parliamentary session.

S4W-00694 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many Scottish students dropped out of Scottish universities in each of the last four years, broken down by year of degree.

S4W-00695 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will legislate to introduce a minimum income of £7,000 a year for students in full-time higher education.

S4W-00696 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will introduce a higher education Bill and what measures it will contain.

S4W-00698 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will legislate to set targets for widening access to universities.

S4W-00699 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive whether statutory widening access targets for universities will apply to the sector as a whole or individual institutions.

S4W-00700 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will publish its discussion paper for reforms to the college sector and what issues will be included.

S4W-00702 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will publish a map of course provision for the (a) further and (b) higher education sector.

S4W-00704 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will publish legal advice that it has received on the possibility of implementing a service charge for EU students studying at Scottish institutions.

S4W-00705 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what modern languages will be offered in schools under its proposals to introduce a scheme for modern language learning based on the EU 2+1 model.

S4W-00706 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how much funding has been allocated for the teaching of modern languages at higher education institutions in each year since 2007, broken down by funding stream.

S4W-00707 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many Scotland-domiciled students have taken modern languages courses at Scottish universities in each year since 2007.

S4W-00708 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many Scotland-domiciled entrants there have been to modern languages courses at each Scottish university in each year since 2007, broken down by course.

S4W-00710 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many and which higher education institutions offer combined honours courses in modern languages and education.

S4W-00711 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many Scotland-domiciled entrants there have been to combined honours courses in modern languages and education in each year since 2007, broken down by (a) institution and (b) course.

S4W-00712 Neil Findlay: To ask the Scottish Executive what the total cost of the modern apprenticeship programme was in 2010-11.

S4W-00713 Neil Findlay: To ask the Scottish Executive how many targeted pathways to apprenticeships were or will be funded in (a) Scotland, (b) Lothian and (c) West Lothian in (i) 2010-11 and (ii) 2011-12.

S4W-00714 Neil Findlay: To ask the Scottish Executive how many participants of the targeted pathways to apprenticeship programme were registered as unemployed prior to joining it in 2010-11.

S4W-00715 Neil Findlay: To ask the Scottish Executive how many participants of the targeted pathways to apprenticeship programme progressed into a modern apprenticeship in 2010-11.

S4W-00717 Neil Findlay: To ask the Scottish Executive what the cost was of the targeted pathways to apprenticeship programme for 2010-11.

S4W-00722 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what the level of sickness absence in the NHS has been in each of the last five years, expressed as a percentage.

S4W-00723 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what its investment plans are for improvements to the A9.

S4W-00724 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what work it has done with train operating companies to roll out the availability of wi-fi on trains.

S4W-00725 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will ensure that next generation broadband is available to all of Scotland by 2020.

S4W-00726 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what its action plan is for organic (a) food and (b) farming.

S4W-00727 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what changes it will make to national planning guidance for onshore wind turbines to enhance the role of local communities in the determination of wind turbine sites.

S4W-00729 Derek Mackay: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it can provide to Regenersis, based in Inchinnan, in light of reports that staff might be made redundant as a result of its repair contract with Hutchison 3G UK not being renewed.
