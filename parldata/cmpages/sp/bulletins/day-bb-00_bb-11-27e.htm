BUSINESS BULLETIN 182 / 2000

Monday 27 November 2000

Section E - Written questions lodged on 24 November 2000

 

 

S1W-11467 Dorothy-Grace Elder: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will enable geriatric nurses’ duties to be split between geriatric nursing and other nursing areas with a view to boosting recruitment of nurses to geriatric care.

S1W-11537 Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-11022 by Ms Wendy Alexander on 22 November 2000, whether its regular discussions with the Department of Trade and Industry and the energy industry have included any specific discussions on the possibility of increasing the nuclear electric power generation capacity in Scotland.

S1W-11538 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what it will do to ensure that the new Scottish Household Survey provides information about groups which are vulnerable to poverty including women, lone parents, people with disabilities and ethnic minorities.

S1W-11539 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive how the £22.5 million funding to improve adult literacy and numeracy announced on 25 September 2000 will be spent.

S1W-11540 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to record centrally, in order to ensure the availability of reliable data on disabled children, all assessments of needs of children affected by disability carried out under section 23 of the Children (Scotland) Act 1995.

S1W-11541 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to ensure that the per capita resources dedicated to learning disability services, including community services, are raised to at least the level of expenditure in England and Wales.

S1W-11542 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive which local authorities have not changed their policies on helping young homeless people since the Children (Scotland) Act 1995 introduced a duty on Scottish local authorities to accommodate homeless 16 and 17 year olds.

S1W-11543 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive how Learning and Teaching Scotland recruited the members of its senior management team; in particular, whether the posts were advertised externally; if so, in which journals and on what dates, and whether internal applicants were required to apply using the same process as external applicants.

S1W-11544 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive what methods Learning and Teaching Scotland used to assess the applications it received for posts in its senior management team.

S1W-11545 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive why it announced a delay in the 2002 local government elections through an answer to question S1W-11507, before the question had been published in the Business Bulletin and after decision time on the day of a by-election, rather than by a Ministerial statement or following a parliamentary debate.

S1W-11546 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether a system of proportional representation will be utilised in the next local governmental elections, given that these elections will now be postponed to 2003.

S1W-11547 John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what the current average value is of discretionary rebates on business rates for small businesses.

S1W-11548 John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of small businesses currently receive a discretionary rebate on their business rates.

S1W-11549 Mr Murray Tosh: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-10164 by Sarah Boyack on 28 October 2000, when it will announce the criteria for defining which areas should be targeted for extra resources under the Powershift programme.

S1W-11550 Mr Murray Tosh: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-10649 by Jackie Ballie on 9 November 2000, whether any specific projects approved under (a) the empty homes initiative and (b) the rough sleeper initiative have been cancelled and, if so, what these projects and their approved costs were and how it has reallocated, or intends to reallocate, unused resources under these headings.

S1W-11551 Mr Murray Tosh: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the Minister for Transport’s comments to the Transport and the Environment Committee on 8 November 2000 (official report col. 1155), whether it will confirm that it will not approve any road user charging scheme which is paper based.

S1W-11552 Mr Murray Tosh: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the Minister for Transport’s comments to the Transport and the Environment Committee on 8 November 2000 (official report col. 1155) whether any proposed electronic charging schemes will require any equipment to be fitted to motor vehicles, and whether road users will be expected to pay to acquire and fit such equipment.

S1W-11553 Mr Murray Tosh: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the Minister for Transport’s comments to the Transport and the Environment Committee on 8 November 2000 (official report col. 1155) whether electronic charging schemes will require road users to pay only on roads within designated areas and not for crossing cordons on the perimeter of designated areas.

S1W-11554 Tricia Marwick: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Lord Advocate will reply to my letter of 7 July 2000.

S1W-11555 Tricia Marwick: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Deputy First Minister and Minister for Justice will reply to my letter of 1 September 2000 and reminder letters of 6 October and 10 November 2000.
