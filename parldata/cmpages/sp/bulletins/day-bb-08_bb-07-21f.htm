Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (♦).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S3M-2340 Bill Kidd: Time to End Discrimination Against All Unpaid Carers—That the Parliament welcomes the European Court of Justice’s decision that the laws that protect disabled people against discrimination apply not only to the person themselves, but also to their unpaid carer; notes that this follows the historic case taken by carer, Sharon Coleman, who claimed that she was discriminated against and harassed because she had a disabled son and was treated less favourably than employees whose children were not disabled; further notes that this could give new rights to millions of unpaid carers who are struggling to combine caring with paid work, and agrees with Carers Scotland’s view that, although this ruling appears to apply only to parents of disabled children, government should ensure that it is applied to all people providing unpaid care to others and take a proactive approach to include all carers, rather than awaiting further cases to be taken through the courts.

Supported by: Alasdair Allan*, Alex Neil*, Kenneth Gibson*, Sandra White*, Cathy Peattie*, Bill Wilson*

*S3M-2339 Helen Eadie: Salmond’s Government Has No Compassion for Disabled People—That the Parliament deplores that in the 21st century the clock is being turned back so that disabled people are apparently the least cared about by this SNP government; further deplores that all those disabled people in Scotland who now for the first time, under a Nationalist government, will face assessments of income in order to determine which scale of fees will apply for essential services that, under the previous Labour-led Scottish Executive, were provided free of charge and without any age discrimination; recognises that the age discrimination against disabled people must be investigated as a matter of urgency, and hopes that all those disabled people in the Fife region and, in particular, the towns of Lochgelly and Dalgety Bay, will act as champions supported by their local elected representatives and pursue this very important issue with every relevant agency conceivable until this disgraceful policy is reversed.

*S3M-2338# Christina McKelvie: Deserving Dignity - Children Seeking Sanctuary in Scotland—That the Parliament welcomes the publication of Deserving Dignity, the third report from the Independent Asylum Commission, in particular the recommendations relating to the treatment of children in the asylum system; notes the commission’s recognition of the additional support and opportunities offered to the children seeking sanctuary in Scotland, and encourages the Scottish Government to continue in its efforts to end the detention of children at the Dungavel detention centre, to work to implement the recommendations of the report and to continue in its efforts to improve the lives and opportunities of children seeking sanctuary in Scotland.

*S3M-2337 Dr Richard Simpson: Educational Seminars from Fife Constabulary—That the Parliament congratulates all those involved in Operation Eden, the educational seminars from police education liaison officers from Fife Constabulary and Glenrothes schools, for their success in delivering 250 seminars resulting in a substantial reduction in youth crime, and looks forward to the longer-term outcomes being evaluated and, if these indicate success, the roll out of this programme both across Fife and nationally.

*S3M-2336 George Foulkes: 400th Stonewall Diversity Champion—That the Parliament congratulates the British Army on becoming the 400th Stonewall Diversity Champion, demonstrating its commitment to supporting all of its current and future employees; notes that this a remarkable achievement in light of the fact that less than 10 years ago lesbian and gay people were banned from the army; believes that the road to equality is a long one but recognises the substantial achievements of the Labour government in ensuring that equality and diversity are valued and protected at all levels of our society, and supports the UK Equality Bill which aims to make it easier for people to access their rights and be afforded the dignity and respect that they rightfully deserve.

Supported by: Dr Richard Simpson*, David Stewart*, Ken Macintosh*, Michael McMahon*, Robin Harper*, Bill Butler*, Cathy Peattie*, Rhona Brankin*

*S3M-2335 Karen Whitefield: Lanarkshire Success in Hosting the 2011 International Children’s Games—That the Parliament congratulates North and South Lanarkshire and all members of the New Lanarkshire Limited bid team on their recent success in bidding to host the 2011 International Children’s Games; notes that this is the first time that a county rather than a city has been chosen to host the games; looks forward to welcoming the 1,760 participants who will take part in 10 sports; recognises the long-term benefits that can accrue in improving health by becoming involved in sporting activities; also recognises the benefits to young people from meeting people from diverse cultures, and wishes everyone concerned in hosting the games every success in planning for this important event.

*S3M-2334 Mike Pringle: Congratulations to Lothian Buses—That the Parliament congratulates Lothian Buses on winning the Bus Operator of the Year Award at the National Transport Awards 2008, adding to its two UK Bus Awards accolades of City Operator of the Year and Bus Operator of the Year; acknowledges the vital role Lothian Buses plays in Edinburgh and the wider Lothian region’s public transport network, as well as the hard work of its staff in delivering an excellent service that has seen passenger numbers rise by over 35% during the last six years; wishes Lothian Buses all the best for the future, and looks forward to the arrival of the tram network to further compliment the excellent service it provides.

Supported by: Patrick Harvie*, Jim Hume*, Keith Brown*, Robin Harper*, Liam McArthur*, George Foulkes*, David McLetchie*

*S3M-2333 Dr Richard Simpson: Central Scotland Police Annual Report—That the Parliament notes the last annual report to be delivered by Central Scotland’s Chief Constable, Andrew Cameron, covering the year 2007-08 and commends him and his force on their increasing success in delivering police services and increasing community safety across central Scotland; recognises that Central Scotland Police has one of the highest detection rates in the United Kingdom at 60% and that the force is reducing crime in all three command areas, especially drug-related crime, with more than 300 dealers being charged in 2007-08, and acknowledges, in particular, Andrew Cameron’s 40 years of service and his efforts to involve communities and their representatives in tackling all manner of crimes in central Scotland.

*S3M-2332 Sandra White: Glasgow University - Far From a Lonely Place to be on the Planet!—That the Parliament welcomes the groundbreaking publication of Study Glasgow, a guide to studying at one of the world’s top universities, in one of its exciting and dynamic cities, produced by Lonely Planet in collaboration with the University of Glasgow; notes that the guide is intended to give those considering undertaking a course at the university the chance to get a taste of what the city is like to live in, and congratulates the University of Glasgow for its significant rise in higher education league tables, making it an attractive destination to study for students from across the globe.

Supported by: Kenneth Gibson*, Patrick Harvie*, Rob Gibson*, Bill Kidd*, Alasdair Allan*, Angela Constance*, Bob Doris*, Stuart McMillan*

*S3M-2331 Mike Pringle: Congratulations to Marks & Spencer on Success of Plastic Bag Charging Scheme—That the Parliament congratulates Marks & Spencer (M&S) on the success of its carrier bag charging scheme; notes that since its introduction in May 2008 the scheme, which charges 5p for every disposable bag issued, has succeeded in reducing the number of disposable bags used by 80% and raised significant sums for charitable urban regeneration projects, including one in Edinburgh; acknowledges that the success of the M&S scheme is further evidence that a plastic bag levy is a viable option for Scotland and would be successful in significantly reducing the number of disposable bags issued by retailers, and calls on supermarkets and other retailers to follow M&S’s example and introduce charging for plastic bags to further reduce plastic waste.

Supported by: Jim Tolson*, Bill Kidd*, Jim Hume*, Patrick Harvie*, Robin Harper*

*S3M-2330 Dr Richard Simpson: Beauly to Denny Power Line—That the Parliament notes that, in Historic Scotland’s consultation for an inventory of historic battlefields, responses show that the Sheriffmuir battlefield meets all the proposed criteria for the inventory, which propose that battlefields will receive the same protection as historic gardens and designed landscapes; further notes that the proposed Beauly to Denny power line passes through the centre of the Sheriffmuir battlefield; also notes that this important protective measure has come too late to be considered by the inquiry reporters, and calls on the Scottish Executive to take this into account when reaching its decision on the route and nature of the proposed power line.

*S3M-2329 Christina McKelvie: Lanarkshire Children’s Games—That the Parliament congratulates North and South Lanarkshire councils on beating off competition from Singapore and Daegu in South Korea to stage the International
Children’s Games in 2011; notes that up to 2,000 athletes aged 12 to 15 years, as well as coaches and officials from more than 70 countries, will begin preparations for the event; further notes that track and field, football, golf, tennis and rowing are among the sports to take place at venues such as Hamilton Palace sports ground, Strathclyde Park, the John Wright sports centre and Dollan Baths in East Kilbride, and warmly congratulates the bid team, John Scott and all the staff at New Lanarkshire Limited for working together to bring this fantastic event to Lanarkshire.

*S3M-2328 Kenneth Gibson: Brussels to Allow USA Access to Private Data—That the Parliament condemns EU proposals to allow law enforcement and security agencies in the United States of America access to private information on EU citizens; acknowledges that this will include personal data from banks, credit card companies and other agencies; notes that firms that supply information to US agencies at present, in breach of data protection laws, can be prosecuted but that if this agreement is signed and ratified such firms will be protected; regrets the lack of public awareness and debate on this issue, and believes that access to such private data is in breach of EU law and should be vetoed by the governments of member states to prevent final agreement and implementation later this year.

Supported by: John Wilson*, Mike Pringle*, Bill Kidd*, Bill Wilson*, Elaine Smith*, Brian Adam*, Alex Neil*

*S3M-2327 Kenneth Gibson: Selling Tobacco Products to Developing Countries—That the Parliament notes with concern the marketing strategies of British American Tobacco (BAT) and other tobacco companies in promoting their products in the developing world where health concerns are already acute, health resources are overstretched and where there are far fewer restrictions on the marketing, promotion and selling of tobacco products; is aware that smoking rates in Africa, Asia and Latin America are rapidly increasing with all the appalling consequences one might anticipate; acknowledges the World Health Organization’s research that recognises that more deaths will occur globally as a result of smoking-related diseases than AIDS, tuberculosis and malaria combined, and believes that British companies selling tobacco products overseas should market their products responsibly and avoid targeting young people unscrupulously, as they have often been accused.

Supported by: Mike Pringle*, Rob Gibson*, Brian Adam*

*S3M-2326 Robin Harper: Federation of Master Builders and Climate Change—That the Parliament congratulates the Federation of Master Builders (FMB) on its recent successful policy conference in London on 9 July 2008; recognises that, in 2050, 80% of our present housing stock will still be in use and that improvements to the insulation, draft proofing and energy efficiency of our present housing stock are the greatest urgency in the context of the challenge of climate change, and calls on the Scottish Government to work as closely as possible with the FMB and small and medium sized construction firms to start a process of innovation, skills development and capacity building in the construction industry, moving towards a future standard for housing refurbishment that is consistent with an 80% reduction in CO2 emissions by 2050.

Supported by: Patrick Harvie*, Bill Wilson*, Bill Kidd*, Mike Pringle*, David Stewart*, Elaine Smith*, Angela Constance*, Cathy Peattie*, Nigel Don*

*S3M-2244 Bill Kidd: Congratulations to Glasgow City Council—That the Parliament congratulates Glasgow City Council on its findings that 72% of Glaswegians would be better off under local income tax as opposed to the unfair council tax, and believes that, given the persistent high levels of multiple deprivation in Glasgow under successive UK Governments, the introduction of a local income tax would contribute substantially to combating poverty in Glasgow and consequently would also combat poverty in all other areas of Scotland, both urban and rural, experiencing high levels of poverty. Supported by: Jamie Hepburn, Christina McKelvie, Rob Gibson, Kenneth Gibson, Aileen Campbell, Bill Wilson, Alasdair Allan, Alex Neil, Bob Doris, Bashir Ahmad, Brian Adam, Christine Grahame, Stuart McMillan, Gil Paterson, Joe FitzPatrick, Michael Matheson

*S3M-2244.1 James Kelly: Congratulations to Glasgow City Council—As an amendment to motion (S3M-2244) in the name of Bill Kidd, leave out from "72% of Glaswegians" to end and insert "the proposals for a local income tax (LIT) set at 3p in the pound would lead to a funding gap for Glasgow City Council of between £60 million and £133 million per annum and that, accordingly, the Scottish Government would have to set the LIT at around 4.5% to avoid this gap and maintain current levels of council services, therefore hitting hard-working families; welcomes Glasgow City Council’s findings that, because of the number of households in Glasgow in receipt of full council tax benefit, the poorest households would see no change under the LIT proposals and the problem of such households continuing to incur water and waste charges would not be resolved by the Scottish Government’s plans, and congratulates the Labour government at Westminster since 1997 on its record of tacking social injustice and poverty, noting in particular the record employment levels, the introduction of the National Minimum Wage, which will rise to £5.73, the cutting of the basic rate of income tax from 22p to 20p, the lowest for 75 years, and the introduction of tax and benefit measures, which mean that, in real terms, families with children in the poorest fifth of the population will be on average £4,050 per year better off than before 1997 and that 600,000 children have been lifted out of poverty since 1997."

Supported by: Ken Macintosh*, Marlyn Glen*, David Whitton*, Michael McMahon*, Iain Gray*, Karen Gillon*, Dr Richard Simpson*, Cathy Peattie*, David Stewart*
