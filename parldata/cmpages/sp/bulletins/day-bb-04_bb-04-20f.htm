Business Bulletin No. 65 / 2004

Tuesday 20th April 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-1179 Carolyn Leckie: Nursery Nurses' Just Pay Claim-That the Parliament demands the immediate settlement of the nursery nurses' dispute in light of the news that the UK's richest people have seen their wealth increase by nearly 30%; recognises that Scotland's nursery nurses have been denied a national settlement to their just demand for a decent living wage at the same time as the 100 richest Scots are now worth over £11 billion, and regards it as a disgrace that our nursery nurses are having to battle for a decent living wage while the excessively rich sit back and watch their bank balances increase daily.

*S2M-1178 Mr Ted Brocklebank: Congratulations to Fife Constabulary-That the Parliament congratulates Fife Constabulary for the successful pilot scheme of housing satellite police stations in rural post offices; notes how this programme has benefited communities in Fife by providing a police presence in remote areas as well as maintaining postal services at a time when many rural post offices are being closed, and hopes that this scheme will be rolled out to other rural areas throughout Scotland.

*S2M-1177 Mr Jamie McGrigor: National Gallery of Modern Art-That the Parliament congratulates the National Gallery of Modern Art for its exhibition of the first ever museum showing of Lucian Freud's complete etchings; recognises that this exhibition provides the public with the opportunity to view a unique collection of works of an outstanding artist, and applauds the gallery for bringing forward exhibitions of this calibre that can only enhance the reputation of Scotland in the field of visual arts.

*S2M-1176# Colin Fox: Abolish Prescription Charges-That the Parliament believes that charging patients for their medicines breaches the principle of universally free health care that underpins the NHS; notes that Unhealthy Charges, published by the National Association of Citizens Advice Bureaux in July 2001, indicates that as many as 70,000 Scots per year may have gone without vital medicines because they could not afford the £6.30 charge for each item; believes that the logic behind the charging criteria, with one patient exempt and another with a very similar condition paying in full, cannot be sustained, and believes that the Scottish Executive should follow the example of the National Assembly for Wales which voted recently to abolish prescription charges.
