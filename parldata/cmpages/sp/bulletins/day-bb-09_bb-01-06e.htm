Business Bulletin No. 2/2009: Tuesday 6 January 2009
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 5 January 2009

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-19353 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will issue guidance under the Housing (Scotland) Act 2006.

S3W-19354 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the maintenance order provisions of the Housing (Scotland) Act 2006 are adequate when dealing with owners who can pay but will not.

S3W-19355 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will empower local authorities or other agencies as well as tenants to make complaints to the Private Rented Housing Panel.

S3W-19356 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will strengthen the powers available to local authorities to certify that houses are overcrowded, are in multiple occupation or should be registered under the landlord registration requirements.

S3W-19357 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it intends to take to tackle alleged problems of gross overcrowding and abuse of housing protections in the Govanhill area of Glasgow.

S3W-19358 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide increased resources to tackle housing disrepair in Govanhill.

S3W-19359 Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive how much it anticipates each local authority will save as a direct result of the abolition of ringfencing in (a) 2008-09, (b) 2009-10 and (c) 2010-11.

S3W-19360 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive how many objections to the Aberdeen Western Peripheral Route came from addresses without an AB postcode; what proportion of the total number of objections that constitutes; what the cost of handling these objections is likely to be; what part of that cost will be borne by Aberdeen City Council as part of its contribution to the project cost, and what delays have derived from such objections.

S3W-19361 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive when the National Residential Child Care Initiative will report.

S3W-19362 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many staff of residential homes and schools are suspended, broken down by suspension of (a) up to six months, (b) over six months to one year, (c) over one year to two years and (d) over two years.

S3W-19363 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are directions from the Lord Advocate on the prosecution of child-care staff.

S3W-19364 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to agree and implement a standard definition of incidents recorded by the police.

S3W-19365 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what the highest number of prisoners was in each prison in each of the last three months. 

S3W-19366 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will review the arrangements for paying travel allowances to students.

S3W-19367 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make child care support for college students an entitlement rather than it being provided through a discretionary fund.

S3W-19368 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will introduce a tenancy deposit scheme in terms of the Housing (Scotland) Act 2006.

S3W-19369 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will establish a widening access unit to improve university uptake rates from across the social spectrum.

S3W-19370 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Minister for Enterprise, Energy and Tourism was aware of the £30,000 donated to the SNP by Mr Donald Macdonald of Aviemore Highland Resort when meeting representatives of Macdonald Hotels, the Cairngorms National Park Authority, Highland Council and the Minister for Community Safety in Aviemore on 26 October 2007.

S3W-19371 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Minister for Enterprise, Energy and Tourism, with or without officials, met Mr Donald Macdonald of Aviemore Highland Resort or representatives of Macdonald Hotels at any time between his appointment as a minister and his meeting with representatives of Macdonald Hotels, the Cairngorms National Park Authority, Highland Council and the Minister for Community Safety in Aviemore on 26 October 2007 and, if so, on what dates.

S3W-19372 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what level of additional greenhouse gas emissions it expects will be generated by aviation resulting from Homecoming Scotland 2009.

S3W-19373 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it has assessed the level of greenhouse gas emissions expected to be generated by aviation resulting from Homecoming Scotland 2009.

S3W-19374 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what proportion of the promotional work for Homecoming Scotland 2009 will focus on domestic and European tourism rather than on increasing the number of visitors who rely on international aviation.

S3W-19375 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what efforts will be made to maximise the use of surface-based transport modes rather than aviation by domestic and European tourists coming to Homecoming Scotland 2009.

S3W-19376 Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive whether funding for the Retired and Senior Volunteer Programme (RSVP) was factored into the estimated funding for community partnerships in the concordat between the Scottish Government and COSLA and, if not, whether it is concerned at the possibility that the work of RSVP may cease in most local authority areas.

S3W-19377 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will publish the figures for suspensions and special leave in the health service, broken down by NHS board.

S3W-19378 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what directions have been issued to the NHS for the treatment of deep vein thrombosis and pulmonary embolism.

S3W-19379 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it records the number of inpatients who develop deep vein thrombosis or pulmonary embolism following joint replacement and, if so, what these figures are, broken down by NHS board.

S3W-19380 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance has been issued to NHS boards on undertaking risk assessments of inpatients developing deep vein thrombosis or pulmonary embolism.

S3W-19381 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to protect patients in light of the 2007 National Institute for Health and Clinical Excellence (NICE) guidelines on venous thromboembolism and recommendations from the Department of Health and the House of Commons Health Committee.

S3W-19382 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to update Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network (SIGN) Guideline 62, Prophylaxis of Venous Thromboembolism, published in 2002.

S3W-19383 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what official Christmas gifts have been received by the First Minister and how each one has been disposed of.

S3W-19384 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive how many and which of its or its agencies’ senior officials receive private medical insurance as part of their remuneration packages.

S3W-19385 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive how many and which of its or its agencies’ senior officials are on sabbatical leave and what the nature of the sabbatical leave is.

S3W-19386 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive which agencies, local authorities or other public bodies had investments in a vehicle under the control of Bernard Madoff and what the total amount was of those investments.

S3W-19387 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive which of its agencies has financial dealings with the Tosca hedge fund.

S3W-19388 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide an estimate of the range of the financial cost of answering parliamentary questions, from simple to complex.

S3W-19389 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what the financial limit is beyond which it considers that the cost for answering a written parliamentary question would be disproportionate.

S3W-19390 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prosecutions there have been since May 2007 for the discharge of pollution into rivers and how many have been successful.

S3W-19391 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive which banks operate accounts for it and whether it has changed any of the banks that it has used since May 2007.

S3W-19392 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive how many snow ploughs and gritters are available to keep trunk roads open in the event of severe snow conditions and what change in those numbers there has been since May 2007.

S3W-19393 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what procedure is in place to ensure that neither it nor its agencies employ illegal immigrants and what changes there have been in these procedures since May 2007.

S3W-19394 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what official engagements were undertaken by each minister during the 2008 Christmas and New Year parliamentary recess.

S3W-19395 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive how and to whom the chief executive of Transport Scotland intimated his intention to retire and when the retirement will take effect.

S3W-19396 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what the arrangements are for the appointment of a new chief executive of Transport Scotland.

S3W-19397 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what arrangements it has in place for reviewing the implementation of Audit Scotland reports (a) one, (b) two and (c) three years after their publication.

S3W-19398 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to review the local ward and local authority area boundaries prior to the council elections in 2012.

S3W-19399 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what its policy towards written parliamentary questions is and whether there has been any change since May 2007.

S3W-19400 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what changes there have been in the provision of telephone services to its offices since May 2007.

S3W-19401 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what examination has been carried out of the financial interests of members of the Council of Economic Advisers since their appointment and any potential conflicts of interest.

S3W-19402 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it made and to whom concerning the closure of the Sun Microsystems operation in Linlithgow.

S3W-19403 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive when and for what purpose the First Minister next expects to visit the United States of America.

S3W-19404 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive on how many occasions the Minister for Europe, External Affairs and Culture has visited the (a) London and (b) East Kilbride offices of the Department for International Development and in each case whom she met and what issues and countries were discussed.

S3W-19405 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what issues it has referred to the Scots Law Commission since May 2007.

S3W-19406 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects a contract for the construction of a replacement Portobello High School to be approved.

S3W-19407 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it expects to convert the Scottish Futures Trust into a non-departmental body and, if so, when this will take place.

S3W-19408 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what arrangements it proposes for a period of purdah during the next UK general election.

S3W-19409 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive how much it estimates that it owes to suppliers for the provision of goods and services and what action it is taking to speed up payment.

S3W-19410 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive how much is owed to the Public Works Loan Board for construction and how much interest is paid to the board.

S3W-19411 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive how many officials it provides credit cards to for payment of official expenses and purchases and what it estimates the total amount outstanding on all such cards to be.

S3W-19412 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions ministers have had with executives of Lloyds TSB following the approval by Lloyds TSB and HBOS shareholders of the proposed takeover and what its policy is on the takeover.

S3W-19413 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive which of the Scottish Prison Commission’s recommendations it considers to have been implemented.

S3W-19414 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what the latest cost of the Borders rail scheme is projected to be and whether it is appropriate to reconsider whether this represents value for money.

S3W-19416 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what transfer of financial resources there has been from prison to community sentences and prevention of crime since May 2007 and what effect this has had on the projected prison population.

S3W-19417 Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied that the existing A82 trunk road through Inverness and connecting to the A9 is adequate for current circumstances and for projected traffic growth up to 2020.

S3W-19418 Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive what the projected traffic growth forecasts are for the A82 through Inverness for the period up to 2020.

S3W-19419 Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive on which criteria the Inverness trunk link route between the A9 and A82 fell short in determining whether it should be funded under plans announced as part of the Strategic Transport Projects Review.

S3W-19420 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what budget has been allocated to the proposed upgrading of the Dalry bypass.

S3W-19421 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive when it anticipates that work will begin on the proposed upgrading of the Dalry bypass.

S3W-19422 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects work to be completed on the proposed upgrading of the Dalry bypass.
