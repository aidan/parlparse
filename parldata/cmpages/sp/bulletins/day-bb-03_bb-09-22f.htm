Business Bulletin No. 123/2003

Monday 22 September 2003

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-368# John Swinburne: National Concessionary Travel Scheme for Older People-That the Parliament acknowledges the importance of affordable public transport to the quality of life of older people in every part of Scotland and believes that the Scottish Executive should introduce a national concessionary travel scheme which includes bus, rail, ferry and air services.

*S2M-367# Fiona Hyslop: Congestion Charging-That the Parliament considers that the Scottish Executive should reject the congestion charging scheme if submitted to it in its current form by the City of Edinburgh Council for approval, as the scheme discriminates against residents of Midlothian, West Lothian and surrounding areas commuting to Edinburgh for employment purposes and will result in such residents paying disproportionately for Edinburgh's transport problem without commensurate improvement in public transport in Midlothian, West Lothian and surrounding areas.

*S2M-366 Linda Fabiani: Scottish Traditional Music Awards-That the Parliament welcomes the establishment of the annual Scottish traditional music awards; congratulates all winners at the inaugural event which took place on 13 September 2003 in Edinburgh; notes in particular the posthumous award to Hamish Henderson for his outstanding contribution to Scottish culture, and appreciates the attendance of the Minister for Tourism, Culture and Sport at this important celebration of contemporary Scottish music.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan*, Rosie Kane*

*S2M-365# Bristow Muldoon: Edinburgh's Proposals for Congestion Charging-That the Parliament notes that the draft congestion charging scheme approved by the City of Edinburgh Council proposes to exempt Edinburgh residents who live outwith the proposed outer cordon from paying a congestion charge, whilst still proposing to levy a charge on residents of other local authorities; believes that such a proposal is discriminatory and unfair, thus failing to meet the requirement of "fair treatment" emphasised by the Scottish Executive in giving approval in principle to a charging scheme, and therefore considers that the Scottish Executive should reject any subsequent proposal from the City of Edinburgh Council which is based on this form of charging regime.

Supported by: Cathie Craigie*, Dr Elaine Murray*, Helen Eadie*

S2M-364# Scott Barrie: Children's Panel Membership-That the Parliament recognises the huge contribution made to public life by volunteers; notes that the children's hearing system has now been in operation in Scotland for over 30 years; applauds the dedication and commitment of children's panel members in undertaking a difficult and demanding task; recognises the Scottish Executive's current recruitment campaign for additional panel members, and encourages people from all sections of the community, but in particular men, to consider applying for membership of the children's panel in their local area.

Supported by: Margaret Jamieson, Rhona Brankin, Christine May, Tommy Sheridan*, Rosie Kane*

S2M-363 Eleanor Scott: Hi-Arts Broadband Britain Challenge Award-That the Parliament congratulates Hi-Arts on winning the public sector category of the Broadband Britain Challenge Awards; notes the diversity of the arts and their pivotal role in our cultural identity and our economy, and welcomes the potential that broadband offers to offset some of the difficulties faced by arts makers and other small businesses in remote and rural areas and thereby support sustainable development in those areas.

S2M-362 Eleanor Scott: Tartan Day, Paris, 4 April 2004-That the Parliament notes that Tartan Day is now celebrated in France as well as in the United States of America and urges the First Minister to attend the Tartan Day celebrations in Paris on 4 April 2004, thereby cementing the Auld Alliance and our historic and ongoing friendship with Old Europe.

S2M-361# Richard Lochhead: Lack of Affordable Rural Housing in North East Scotland-That the Parliament is concerned by the lack of affordable housing available for renting or buying in rural communities in the north east and elsewhere in the country; notes that in Aberdeenshire alone 6000 households await suitable housing; further notes that local people are often priced out of their own communities due to the rising and exorbitant cost of property and land, and considers that the Scottish Executive should bring forward radical and innovative measures to address those issues at the root of this situation such as a review of planning regimes, diversification of land ownership, appropriate funding mechanisms and the need for more compulsory purchase orders.

Supported by: Mr Adam Ingram, Stewart Stevenson*, Tommy Sheridan*, Rosie Kane*

S2M-360# Nicola Sturgeon: Open Source Software-That the Parliament congratulates the SNP Members of the European Parliament, their colleagues in the Green/European Free Alliance group and, in particular, Prof Neil MacCormick MEP for being at the forefront of the campaign in the European Parliament to defend the interests of the open-source software industry; notes that a number of public bodies across Europe have switched to open source software systems; further notes that the SNP group in the European Parliament has called on the EU institutions to look into switching to open source software, and considers that the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body should investigate the feasibility of the Parliament making greater use of such software with a view to switching to open source software in the future.

Supported by: Mr Rob Gibson, Mr Stewart Maxwell, Mark Ballard, Alasdair Morgan, Christine Grahame, Mr Kenny MacAskill, Brian Adam, Richard Lochhead, Michael Matheson, Stewart Stevenson, Ms Sandra White, Mr Adam Ingram, Shiona Baird, Tommy Sheridan*, Rosie Kane*, Mrs Margaret Ewing*, Mr Bruce McFee*

S2M-359# Shona Robison: Availability of Organ Donor Forms-That the Parliament is concerned that the scheme to include organ donor forms with voter registration forms has been halted; believes that the scheme is a successful way of recruiting badly-needed new donors as demonstrated by Dundee City Council, the only Scottish local authority that took part in the scheme, and considers that the Scottish Executive should enter into discussions with the Electoral Commission and Her Majesty's Government in order to try to resolve any legal difficulties involved in the interpretation of the Representation of the People (Amendment) Regulations 2002.

Supported by: Frances Curran, Mr Adam Ingram, Carolyn Leckie, Kate Maclean, Eleanor Scott, Stewart Stevenson, Mr Stewart Maxwell, Brian Adam, Shiona Baird, Ms Sandra White, Marlyn Glen, Mike Pringle, Murray Tosh, Michael Matheson, Robin Harper, Alasdair Morgan*, Phil Gallie*, Mrs Margaret Ewing*, Tommy Sheridan*, Rosie Kane*, Margo MacDonald*

S2M-358 Campbell Martin: Dr Henry Faulds - Pioneer of Fingerprinting-That the Parliament acknowledges the pioneering work carried out by Scotsman Dr Henry Faulds in the scientific study of fingerprints; recognises that Dr Faulds, who was born in Beith, Ayrshire, in 1843, first published his findings on the forensic application of fingerprinting in the Nature Magazine in 1880, some years ahead of contemporaries in the field, and therefore should be recognised as the true pioneer of fingerprinting, and acknowledges the work of the Dr Henry Faulds - Beith Commemorative Society in seeking to recognise the work and achievements of Dr Faulds by securing funds to provide a permanent memorial in the town.

Supported by: Alex Neil, Brian Adam, Fergus Ewing, Phil Gallie, Christine Grahame, Richard Lochhead, Tricia Marwick, Michael Matheson, Mr Stewart Maxwell, Ms Sandra White, Mr Adam Ingram, Mr Rob Gibson, Robin Harper, Christine May, Mr Jamie Stone, Chris Ballance*, Margo MacDonald*

S2M-349 Mike Pringle: Dedicated Bus Service to St. Peter's Primary School, Edinburgh-That the Parliament welcomes the agreement between the City of Edinburgh Council and contractors to allow the refurbishment of St. Peter's Primary School, Morningside, to start immediately; notes that the lack of a dedicated school bus service for the current school year to take pupils to their temporary school near Tollcross has caused traffic congestion at this site and overcrowding on buses running on the A702 into the city, and calls on the City of Edinburgh Council to introduce one immediately to stop travel problems for teachers, pupils, parents and commuters.

S2M-348# Mike Pringle: Transport in Southern Edinburgh-That the Parliament notes with concern the continued traffic congestion on main routes into Edinburgh from the south, including the problem of parking by city centre commuters in residential streets in southern Edinburgh; further notes with concern the proposal for an out-of-town football stadium at Straiton that can only exacerbate existing traffic problems; considers that improved transport infrastructure, such as the proposed park-and-ride site at Burdiehouse, is essential to help tackle the problem, and considers that the City of Edinburgh and Midlothian councils and the Scottish Executive should ensure that these transport problems in southern Edinburgh can be resolved with added benefit to the wider Lothian and Borders region.

S2M-347 Chris Ballance: Redundancy Notices at the National Library of Scotland-That the Parliament condemns the redundancy notices to six senior heads of department at the National Library of Scotland and regrets the fact that the skills of the six most knowledgeable librarians in Scotland are potentially to be lost to the nation.

Supported by: Mark Ballard, Eleanor Scott, Shiona Baird, Alex Neil, Mike Pringle, Mr Mark Ruskell, Robin Harper, Dr Jean Turner, Colin Fox, Mr Keith Raffan, Frances Curran, Carolyn Leckie, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Tommy Sheridan*, Rosie Kane*

S2M-346 Christine Grahame: Borders Railway Funding-That the Parliament welcomes the lodging of the Waverley Railway (Scotland) Bill; notes that Stow is not to be included as a stop, although the projected time for the journey without that stop is now some 61 minutes from Galashiels to Edinburgh when in 1954 the same journey took 49 minutes; is concerned that this line requires to meet some, as yet unspecified, business case, a test not put with regard to other announced rail, road or tram developments and that accordingly no firm commitment has been given to this funding, and asks the Scottish Executive to detail the criteria for this business case now.

Supported by: Mr Stewart Maxwell, Mr Rob Gibson, Ms Sandra White, Brian Adam, Michael Matheson, Stewart Stevenson, Alex Neil, Tommy Sheridan*, Rosie Kane*

S2M-345 Murdo Fraser: Swedish Referendum on Euro Membership-That the Parliament welcomes the convincing result of the Swedish referendum on euro membership; congratulates the people of Sweden for having the courage to vote "No" to the euro in such large numbers against the weight of the entire political, business and media establishment; agrees with the Swedish people that joining the euro would not be in the economic interests of Sweden and would have meant a substantial loss of national sovereignty, and looks forward to Her Majesty's Government calling a euro referendum here at the earliest opportunity in order that the people of Scotland can demonstrate that their opposition to the euro is every bit as strong as that of the Swedes.

Supported by: Mary Scanlon, Mr Ted Brocklebank, David Mundell, Alex Johnstone, Phil Gallie, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton, Mr Brian Monteith*, Margo MacDonald*

S2M-344# Phil Gallie: Congratulations to Sweden-That the Parliament congratulates the Swedish nation for standing up to the pressure of politicians at home and abroad whose aim, as stated by the Italian Ambassador to the United Kingdom, is to give birth to a European super state and considers that the Scottish Executive should apply pressure on Her Majesty's Government to hold referenda on both the European constitution and the common European currency at an early date in order that uncertainties within the Scottish economy are removed in line with the democratic wishes of Scots and UK citizens as a whole.

Supported by: Mark Ballard, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton, Mary Scanlon, Mr Ted Brocklebank, Murdo Fraser, Dr Jean Turner, Mr Brian Monteith*

S2M-343# Mr Jamie McGrigor: Scottish Food Fortnight-That the Parliament congratulates the organisers of Scottish Food Fortnight, the first ever national celebration of Scotland's best regional produce, to take place between 20 September and 5 October 2003 which will highlight the world-class quality of Scottish produce and advertise Scotland as a prime food destination in Western Europe.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie, Robin Harper, Mr Ted Brocklebank, David Mundell, Mr David Davidson, Fergus Ewing, Murray Tosh, Nora Radcliffe, Christine May, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton, Mr Jamie Stone, Mr Brian Monteith*

S2M-210# Mr Kenneth Macintosh: Respect for Shopworkers Day, 17 September 2003-That the Parliament notes with concern the level of violence and abuse that shopworkers deal with on a daily basis; further notes the recent survey by the Union of Shop, Distributive and Allied workers (USDAW) that found that 47% of those responding to the survey reported physical attacks or assaults on staff in the last 12 months, that 72% reported staff being threatened with violence, that in one in four stores threats are being made every week, that in one-third of stores verbal abuse is a daily event, that almost 50% of staff have taken time off work as a result of violence and that stress-related problems are common and include sickness, insomnia, headaches and clinical depression; commends the USDAW campaign, "Freedom from Fear"' and recognises its main objectives as raising awareness of the level of violence and abuse, making the workplace safer and protecting both shoppers and staff, and supports "Respect for Shopworkers Day" on 17 September 2003 aimed at preventing abusive behaviour, highlighting the support that retail companies can, and do, give their staff and encouraging the public to show respect for shopworkers.

Supported by: Karen Whitefield, Marlyn Glen, Dr Elaine Murray, Pauline McNeill, Karen Gillon, Scott Barrie, Paul Martin, Elaine Smith, Maureen Macmillan, Susan Deacon, Michael McMahon, Sarah Boyack, Mr Alasdair Morrison, Kate Maclean, Cathie Craigie, Jackie Baillie, Marilyn Livingstone, Dr Sylvia Jackson, Helen Eadie, Des McNulty, Christine May, Bill Butler, Mr Richard Baker, Bristow Muldoon, Cathy Peattie, Mr John Home Robertson, Margaret Jamieson, Rhona Brankin, Ms Wendy Alexander, Janis Hughes, Trish Godman, Johann Lamont, Tommy Sheridan, Rosie Kane, Irene Oldfather, Frances Curran, Carolyn Leckie, Ms Rosemary Byrne
