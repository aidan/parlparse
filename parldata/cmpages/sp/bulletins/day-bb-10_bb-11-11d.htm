Business Bulletin No. 164/2010: Thursday 11 November 2010
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D – Oral questions selected for answer on 18 November 2010

Question Time

General

1. Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to revise its decision to introduce free parking at hospital car parks. (S3O-12046)

2. Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making on ensuring access to infertility treatment across Scotland. (S3O-11998)

3. Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken to ensure that public services such as health services are made fully accessible to disabled people, particularly when new facilities are designed. (S3O-12001)

4. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to protect communities in Edinburgh from flooding. (S3O-11970)

5. Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to protect frontline policing from budgetary pressures. (S3O-11972)

6. Bill Kidd: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to improve the provision of and access to occupational health services. (S3O-12023)

7. Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has agreed a remit for Lord Carloway’s review announced in the context of the emergency legislation on the detention of suspects. (S3O-12037)

8. Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on local authorities refusing additional alcohol licences in areas deemed to be oversubscribed in licensed premises. (S3O-12031)

9. Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what is being done to tackle youth violence. (S3O-12048)

10. Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) farmers and (b) crofters have notified a reduction in their eligible land areas for the purposes of claiming under Single Farm Payment or Less Favoured Area Support schemes since new guidance was issued by ministers in May 2010. (S3O-12040)

Finance and Sustainable Growth

1. Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent discussions it has had on reviewing the local government funding formula. (S3O-11956)

2. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it supports increased taxation powers for Scotland and, if so, for what purpose. (S3O-12009)

3. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will encourage public sector clients in the construction industry to break down large contracts into smaller packages to allow local contractors to tender and how it will introduce carbon footprint criteria into public sector procurement. (S3O-11986)

4. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how its budget will impact on the Highlands and Islands. (S3O-11961)

5. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the UK Government regarding high speed rail. (S3O-11994)

6. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive what degree of impartiality public sector planning officials are expected to show toward planning developments under consideration. (S3O-12008)

7. Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive how much the decision to lower the poundage rate to match that of England has saved for businesses in Angus. (S3O-12034)

8. Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions the Cabinet Secretary for Finance and Sustainable Growth has had regarding plans for the future of the urban regeneration companies. (S3O-12038)

9. Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive what financial incentives are in place to promote renewable energy. (S3O-11981)

10. Bill Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that other sectors of the economy might benefit by following VisitScotland’s example in encouraging tourism-related enterprises to use their environmental credentials to boost business. (S3O-12036)

11. Derek Brownlee: To ask the Scottish Executive what its projected underspend is for the current year. (S3O-11957)

12. Bob Doris: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in developing a scheme to incentivise local authorities to improve the collection of non-domestic rates. (S3O-12016)

13. Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures the Cabinet Secretary for Finance and Sustainable Growth plans to take to tackle the record level of youth unemployment. (S3O-11990)

14. Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive what the implications of the Scottish budget will be for Lanarkshire, Falkirk and other areas in Central Scotland. (S3O-11963)

15. Peter Peacock: To ask the Scottish Executive what costs and reductions in budget, beyond those to the Scottish block grant, are being fed through directly to local authorities from the UK Government. (S3O-12000)

16. Nigel Don: To ask the Scottish Executive what the effects of the Scottish budget will be on local authorities. (S3O-12015)

17. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive from which budget lines the £70 million for the National Renewables Infrastructure Fund will be taken and from what current projects or initiatives resources will be redirected. (S3O-12047)

18. Jim Tolson: To ask the Scottish Executive what support is being given to new enterprises in the renewable energy sector in Fife. (S3O-12051)

19. Andy Kerr: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Cabinet Secretary for Finance and Sustainable Growth last met COSLA. (S3O-11984)

20. Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to ensure that Scotland reaps the maximum benefit from offshore renewable energy. (S3O-12050)
