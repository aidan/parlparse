Business Bulletin No. 125/ 2004

Wednesday 8 September 2004

Section E : Written questions lodged on 7 September 2004
  

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-10281 Colin Fox: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it will take to end the shortage of NHS consultants.

S2W-10282 Colin Fox: To ask the Scottish Executive what approaches it has made to NHS Lothian on the implications for surgical services of Improving Care, Investing in Change 2004.

S2W-10283 Colin Fox: To ask the Scottish Executive what services will be removed from St John’s Hospital in Livingston and located elsewhere if Improving Care, Investing in Change 2004 is implemented.

S2W-10284 Colin Fox: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact the removal of orthopaedic trauma and emergency general surgery from St John’s Hospital in Livingston will have on intensive care and high dependency units in the hospital.

S2W-10285 Colin Fox: To ask the Scottish Executive in which (a) hospitals and (b) departments the last 50 consultants recruited by NHS Lothian were deployed.

S2W-10286 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with train operating companies regarding the provision of no-smoking carriages on all trains.

S2W-10287 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive how it monitors the activities of the Victim Information and Advice service.

S2W-10288 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people benefited from the support offered by the Victim Information and Advice service in the last year.

S2W-10289 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive how many court cases were adjourned in 2003-04 because the accused were deemed to be medically unfit to stand trial.

S2W-10290 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what procedures are in place to verify claims from defence teams that the accused is medically unfit to stand trial.

S2W-10291 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what procedures are in place to ensure that victims are kept informed of the progress of their cases in the event of adjournment due to the ill-health of the accused.

S2W-10292 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider adding portable oxygen conservers to the Scottish drugs tariff and, if so, what the likely timescale is.

S2W-10293 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people are registered as having a need for clinical oxygen therapy.

S2W-10294 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people are registered as having a need for clinical oxygen therapy for eight hours a day or more.

S2W-10295 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average cost per patient has been of the provision of portable oxygen conservers in each of the last four years.

S2W-10296 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is monitoring the use made by local authorities of the provisions within the Dog Fouling (Scotland) Act 2003 since it came into force.

S2W-10297 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many criminal justice social workers have been employed by local authorities in each of the last five years and how many are currently employed.

S2W-10298 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many local authorities own, or have a controlling interest in, an outdoor education centre.

S2W-10299 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any of the law officers have attended a political party conference in their official capacity in the last five years and, if so, in which year, which political party conference and for what purpose.

S2W-10300 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive what improvements in journey times between Edinburgh and Glasgow it has secured under the new ScotRail franchise.

S2W-10301 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to follow up any of the ministerial and other activities scheduled as part of its contribution to the Scotland in the Netherlands series of events.

S2W-10302 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will place in the Scottish Parliament Information Centre a report regarding the visits undertaken by four of its ministers as part of the Scotland in the Netherlands series of events.

S2W-10303 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what follow-up activity has been undertaken of its involvement in the (a) Scotland with Catalonia and (b) Scotland in Sweden series of events and whether it will place in the Scottish Parliament Information Centre a report on the work undertaken by it, or its agencies, since the end of the initial series of events.

S2W-10304 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will publish the planned work programme of Councillor Eric Milligan’s "first impressions" review and whether Councillor Milligan or the Executive will place in the Scottish Parliament Information Centre regular reports on the success of this review.

S2W-10305 Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will submit a separate return for Scotland each year to the World Health Organization’s European health for all database to allow adequate analysis of the health problems facing Scotland.

S2W-10306 Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive what the mid-year population was in each year since 1999, calculated on the same basis as the World Health Organization’s European health for all database.

S2W-10307 Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of the population was aged (a) 0 to 14 and (b) 65 and over in each year since 1999, calculated on the same basis as the World Health Organization’s European health for all database.

S2W-10308 Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many live births there were in each year since 1999, expressed also per 1,000 head of population and calculated on the same basis as the World Health Organization’s European health for all database.

S2W-10309 Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive what the crude death rate per 1,000 head of population was in each year since 1999, calculated on the same basis as the World Health Organization’s European health for all database.

S2W-10310 Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive what the total fertility rate was in each year since 1999, calculated on the same basis as the World Health Organization’s European health for all database.

S2W-10311 Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of the population was urban in every year since 1999, calculated on the same basis as the World Health Organization’s European health for all database.

S2W-10312 Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average population density per square kilometre was in each year since 1999, calculated on the same basis as the World Health Organization’s European health for all database.

S2W-10313 Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive what the total area of Scotland is in square kilometres, calculated on the same basis as the World Health Organization’s European health for all database.

S2W-10314 Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive what the percentage unemployment rate was in each year since 1999, calculated on the same basis as the World Health Organization’s European health for all database.

S2W-10315 Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive what the labour force as a percentage of the population was in each year since 1999, calculated on the same basis as the World Health Organization’s European health for all database.

S2W-10316 Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive what the annual percentage average rate of inflation was in each year since 1999, calculated on the same basis as the World Health Organization’s European health for all database.

S2W-10317 Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive what the gross (a) national and (b) domestic product was in each year since 1999, expressed in US dollars per capita and calculated on the same basis as the World Health Organization’s European health for all database.

S2W-10318 Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive what the mid-year population was in each year since 1999, calculated on the same basis as the World Health Organization’s European health for all database.

S2W-10319 Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive what the real gross domestic product was for each year since 1999 in purchasing power parity per capita expressed in US dollars, calculated on the same basis as the World Health Organization’s European health for all database.

S2W-10320 Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive what the percentage literacy rate was for people aged 15 or over in each year since 1999, calculated on the same basis as the World Health Organization’s European health for all database.

S2W-10321 Bristow Muldoon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many rotas covering junior doctors in NHS Lothian are compliant with the EU working time directive, broken down by speciality.

S2W-10322 Bristow Muldoon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many rotas covering junior doctors in NHS Lothian are not compliant with the EU working time directive, broken down by speciality.

S2W-10323 Bristow Muldoon: To ask the Scottish Executive which operating divisions of NHS Lothian did not meet the budget they were set for 2003-04 and what the level of overspend was in each case.

S2W-10324 Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to review the allocation procedures for rural housing associations.

S2W-10325 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-9957 by Malcolm Chisholm on 6 September 2004, whether the £9,882,454 cost of negligence claims in 2003-04 was met by NHS boards or by insurers and, if the cost was not met by insurers, how much has been paid in premiums (a) by each NHS board and (b) in total.
