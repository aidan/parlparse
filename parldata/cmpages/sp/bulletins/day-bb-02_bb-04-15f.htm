Business Bulletin No. 58/2002

Monday 15 April 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-2975 Tommy Sheridan: Post Office Privatisation—That the Parliament supports the Communication Workers Union campaign against privatisation of the Post Office and their lobby of the Scottish Parliament planned for Thursday 25 April 2002; condemns the privatisation of the Post Office being pursued by the New Labour government resulting in thousands of post offices and depots closures and related job loss threats amounting to 40,000, and resolves to put maximum pressure on Westminster to halt the privatisation of the Post Office and to invest appropriately instead in what is potentially a hugely efficient public service, deserving of protection and promotion within the public sector.

*S1M-2974# Tommy Sheridan: Labour Against the Euro Campaign—That the Parliament notes the launch of the Labour Against the Euro campaign in Westminster on Wednesday 10 April 2002 which involved 30 Labour MPs declaring their opposition both to a euro referendum before the next general election and their opposition to joining the euro, should such a referendum be called; further notes the release of a major survey at the meeting illustrating that 75% of Labour voters were opposed to joining the euro and that expenditure on public services was the key preoccupation of Labour voters, and, while welcoming the launch of the Labour Against the Euro campaign, believes it is time for a progressive cross-party and labour movement campaign against the euro to be established in Scotland.

*S1M-2973 Tommy Sheridan: James Sabzali—That the Parliament condemns the conviction of Canadian-born businessman James Sabzali in the United States of America for the violation of Washington’s economic blockade against Cuba by selling water purification chemicals to the Cuban government; notes that the water purification chemicals sold to Cuba are used to purify the water which goes to Cuban schools and homes; further notes that James Sabzali could face a sentence of up to 205 years in prison and that he is the only foreign national ever to be prosecuted for alleged violation of the 1917 Trading with the Enemy Act; believes that this conviction is yet another reason why the United States of America’s illegal economic blockade of Cuba must be lifted immediately, and recognises that the USA is in breach of international law and opinion by refusing to accept UN resolutions passed over the last decade demanding an end to its destructive and damaging economic blockade of Cuba.

*S1M-2972# Shona Robison: SPL-TV and the Financial Future of Football Clubs—That the Parliament regrets the uncertain financial future facing the 10 non-Old Firm Scottish Premier League Clubs following the decision by Rangers and Celtic to effectively veto plans for SPL-TV; notes that the 10 clubs whose revenue risks being adversely impacted in a very significant way employ in the region of 1000 full-time staff, thousands more on a casual or part-time basis and contribute significantly to their surrounding local economies, and considers that the Scottish Executive should intervene so that the clubs can look forward to a degree of financial stability and the public can enjoy watching their teams on television.

*S1M-2971 Janis Hughes: National Epilepsy Week, 19-25 May 2002—That the Parliament notes that 19-25 May 2002 is National Epilepsy Week, recognises the excellent work being done by the Glasgow based Epilepsy Action Scotland in supporting those with epilepsy, notes that there are almost 30,000 people with epilepsy in Scotland, and calls upon the Scottish Executive to support those with epilepsy by providing high standards of medical care and easy access to information and by working to end the discrimination and stigma suffered by people with epilepsy.

Supported by: Karen Whitefield*

*S1M-2970# Dr Sylvia Jackson: MS Awareness Week, 14-21 April 2002—That the Parliament welcomes MS Awareness Week, 14-21 April 2002, which aims to raise the profile of the disease of multiple sclerosis; recognises the role that the health service in Scotland and the MS Society Scotland play in improving the lives of people affected by MS; notes the recent progress which will ensure that beta interferon is made available to all those people with MS who can benefit from it, and considers that the Scottish Executive should work closely with the MS Society in developing a national standard of care for people with MS.

Supported by: David Mundell*, Karen Whitefield*

*S1M-2969 David Mundell: Lockerbie to Leeds Rail Service—That the Parliament notes that the Strategic Rail Authority is establishing a new Northern England franchise which will include the existing Glasgow to Leeds service; notes that this service currently passes through Lockerbie Station without stopping; further notes that Lockerbie Station is a major regional railhead for much of the south of Scotland and that there is no direct service between the station and West Yorkshire, and calls upon the Scottish Executive to do all that it can to urge the Strategic Rail Authority and the six pre-qualified bidders for the Northern England franchise to agree to stop at Lockerbie Station on the Glasgow to Leeds service as part of the new franchise.

Supported by: Phil Gallie*, Michael Russell*, Mrs Lyndsay McIntosh*, Mr David Davidson*
