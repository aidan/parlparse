Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 3 February 2005

Question Time

Environment and Rural Development

1. Mr Mark Ruskell: To ask the Scottish Executive what its response is to the possible impact on the agricultural and fisheries sectors of the Scottish European Structural Funds Forum’s assessment that European structural funds for Scotland will be cut by between 15 and 50% from 2007 . ( S2O-5302)

2. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in developing a timber transport infrastructure, in accordance with Delivering the Scottish Forestry Strategy. ( S2O-5267)

3. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is promoting agricultural diversification . ( S2O-5237)

4. Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with interested bodies about the future of the Royal Highland Show showground. ( S2O-5209)

5. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive what resources are in place to enable local authorities to take flood prevention measures. ( S2O-5219)

6. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in reducing instances of fly-tipping. ( S2O-5277)

7. Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the environmental costs and benefits are of building power generation capacity immediately adjacent to cities. ( S2O-5192)

8. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers the declarations and resolutions of the World Parks Congress 2003, the OSPAR Commission Bremen Statement 2003, the World Summit on Sustainable Development 2002 and the UN Convention on Biological Diversity 2004 on marine protected areas to be proportionate and based on sound science. ( S2O-5291)

9. Susan Deacon: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to modernise Scotland’s water and sewage infrastructure. ( S2O-5276)

10. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to increase water and sewage capacity in areas where acute housing shortages have been identified. ( S2O-5266)

11. Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what proposals it has to promote recycling. ( S2O-5256)

12. Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to aid the development of rural Scotland, for example by increasing the amount of quality rented housing in rural areas. ( S2O-5191)

Health and Community Care

1. Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what effect increasing PFI/PPP costs have on the budgets of NHS boards. ( S2O-5214)

2. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many NHS patients have been referred to the private sector in the last year. ( S2O-5173)

3. Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has carried out of hospital services that were centralised in 2003. ( S2O-5183)

4. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is taking any action on recent research that some cancer in children is caused by pollution. ( S2O-5284)

5. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it is currently providing to people who wish to give up smoking. ( S2O-5269)

6. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is reviewing the performance of NHS 24. ( S2O-5189)

7. Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive how the NHS is responding to the additional pressures that the service experiences over the winter period. ( S2O-5246)

8. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many in-patient psychiatric beds for children and young people will be provided by October this year to comply with the implementation of the Mental Health (Care and Treatment) (Scotland) Act 2003. ( S2O-5226)

9. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking over recent research regarding the contribution of pollution to cancer in children. ( S2O-5283)

10. Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied with the changes to the GP out-of-hours service. ( S2O-5194)

11. Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will bring forward the date for a recommendation in respect of two-view mammography as the standard breast-screening method so that the procedure can take place with immediate effect. ( S2O-5169)

12. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in providing specialised stroke and heart services. ( S2O-5270)

General

1. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is supporting community transport schemes. ( S2O-5298)

2. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to increase the availability of affordable housing. ( S2O-5175)

3. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is encouraging the use of community planning powers by local authorities to protect and develop rural services. ( S2O-5306)

4. Mr John Swinney: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made on restructuring the tourism network. ( S2O-5200)

5. Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to hold a public inquiry into Scottish Water’s planning application to build a waste transfer station at Cowie, near Stonehaven. ( S2O-5307)

6. Christine May: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will announce its area infrastructure investment plan. ( S2O-5304)

7. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive what role it will play at the G8 summit to be held in Scotland later this year. ( S2O-5297)

8. Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what importance it places on preserving Scotland’s maritime heritage. ( S2O-5225)

9. Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive how the needs of road users, other than drivers, will be provided for when the A96 is upgraded. ( S2O-5300)

10. Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to counter the anticipated economic slowdown in global growth in 2005. ( S2O-5252)

11. Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made towards the reopening of a railway station at Laurencekirk. ( S2O-5228)

12. Mr Alasdair Morrison: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that Scottish companies can benefit from the London 2012 Olympic bid. ( S2O-5263)

13. Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to extend concessionary bus travel to young people. ( S2O-5210)

14. Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with regard to the sale of alcohol in sports grounds. ( S2O-5251)

15. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive what consultations are being undertaken on a review of the right of tenants to buy houses belonging to local authorities and housing associations. ( S2O-5206)
