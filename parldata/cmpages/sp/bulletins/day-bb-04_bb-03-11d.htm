Business Bulletin No. 45/2004

Thursday 11 March 2004

Section D : Oral Questions selected for answer on 18 March 2004
  

Question Time

Education, Tourism, Culture and Sport

1. Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to encourage tourism in Cunninghame South. (S2O-1583)

2. Christine May: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it is giving to non-traditional sports in schools in order to motivate and encourage children to become more physically active. (S2O-1628)

3. Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is in respect of young people sitting Standard Grade exams in S3 rather than S4. (S2O-1626)

4. Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to promote shinty. (S2O-1608)

5. Mr Mark Ruskell: To ask the Scottish Executive to what extent it considers the quality of the environment to be important to tourism. (S2O-1651)

6. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive how many schools will have access to Enterprise in Schools material. (S2O-1661)

7. Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had in respect of the future of the Ratho Adventure Centre. (S2O-1580)

8. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive what analysis it has made in respect of the impact of London’s bid for the Olympic Games in 2012 on Scottish sport. (S2O-1634)

9. Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive how many extra physical education teachers it will recruit over the next academic year and whether it has any plans to create more places at the Scottish Centre for Physical Education, Sport and Leisure Studies. (S2O-1627)

10. Mr Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it will take to encourage Historic Scotland to focus on building partnerships with stakeholders and customers and be open to input from external bodies. (S2O-1638)

11. Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is in respect of the importance of music, physical education and drama teaching within schools. (S2O-1632)

12. Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive what priority will be given to the educational value of rural schools in decisions in respect of realising the asset value of rural school buildings for the financing of its second phase PPP/PFI projects. (S2O-1561)

Finance and Communities

1. Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will announce its position on council tax rebate on second homes. (S2O-1577)

2. Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will report on the voter education campaign for the 2003 local government elections. (S2O-1642)

3. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to enable asylum seekers and refugees to contribute to their new communities in Scotland. (S2O-1640)

4. Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has examined the advantages of the Irish model of staff relocation policy when considering its own policy. (S2O-1607)

5. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive how women victims of trafficking for the purposes of sexual exploitation will be supported by the pilot Support Service for Victims of Trafficking for which it announced £100,000 funding on 8 March 2004. (S2O-1572)

6. Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive what importance it places on the provision of appropriate school, health and transport facilities to support major housing developments, such as the Heartlands project at Polkemmet in West Lothian which has the fastest growing population in Scotland. (S2O-1667)

7. Mr Mark Ruskell: To ask the Scottish Executive how successful the voter education campaign for the 2003 local government elections was. (S2O-1641)

8. George Lyon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider changes to the planning system to address the situation in respect of the supply of affordable housing. (S2O-1657)

9. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any studies are planned to assess the impact of rising house prices on first time buyers in rural areas. (S2O-1563)

10. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive what resources are available to assist the continued opening of post offices serving communities in urban areas. (S2O-1559)

11. Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is supporting the work of credit unions. (S2O-1655)

12. Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive what it is doing to provide and promote training in the use of British Sign Language. (S2O-1605)

General

1. Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it endorses the change in strategy by the Scottish Drugs Enforcement Agency. (S2O-1587)

2. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what the implications are for the Scottish Agricultural College of the recent research organisation assessment exercise in respect of its funding and for the timing of the publication of its business plan. (S2O-1618)

3. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking in respect of the report to the Minister for Health and Community Care on the meeting held on 4 March 2004 between South Glasgow University Hospitals NHS Trust and Bankvale Associates Ltd. (S2O-1616)

4. Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what its policy is in respect of whether swing bridges should be used on main trunk routes where they cross canals or waterways and whether it will provide any incentive to avoid retention of swing bridges when trunk road improvements are carried out. (S2O-1609)

5. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will support greater use of mediation in dispute resolution. (S2O-1666)

6. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will begin consulting on proposals for the introduction of a national free off-peak bus scheme as part of its commitment to concessionary travel. (S2O-1573)

7. Chris Ballance: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration it has given to the proposed landfill site at Aucheninnes Moss in Dumfries and Galloway and its impact on the survival of the bog bush-cricket and the sorrel pygmy moth. (S2O-1645)

8. Mr Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure that people living in remote and rural areas are adequately served under the new GP contract. (S2O-1617)

9. George Lyon: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the European Commission regarding the tendering of Caledonian MacBrayne routes. (S2O-1662)

10. Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the recent decision by Moray Council on a £95 million flood management scheme complies with the Water Environment and Water Services (Scotland) Act 2003. (S2O-1643)

11. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is addressing the issue of litter. (S2O-1560)

12. Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to roll out the restorative justice pilot scheme in Glasgow across the rest of Scotland. (S2O-1663)

13. Tricia Marwick: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will next meet Fife Constabulary and what issues it intends to discuss. (S2O-1557)

14. Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive what the completion rate is for people enrolling on the modern apprenticeship programme. (S2O-1620)

15. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it would support an association of Scottish prisoners. (S2O-1668)
