Business Bulletin No. 148/2003

Thursday 6 November 2003

Section E : Written questions lodged on 5 November 2003
  

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-3832 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive who will be consulted on the inclusion of annual check ups for over-75s in the new GP contract.

S2W-3846 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of operational capacity the cardiac unit of the Golden Jubilee Hospital has been running at in each month from May to October 2003.

S2W-3866 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Care Commission applies the same standards to schools as it does to organisations using school premises.

S2W-3867 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive which new community schools have been developed so far and how many pupils and teachers have been allocated to each school.

S2W-3868 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what the optimum size is of a new community school.

S2W-3869 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to review the organisation of pre-school and out-of-school care in order to provide a more effective "wraparound" service.

S2W-3870 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what the membership is of the Cabinet Sub-Committee on Sustainable Scotland.

S2W-3871 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will continue to support Eco-Schools after 2005.

S2W-3872 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to support sustainable development in education.

S2W-3873 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of GPs have opted out of providing out of hours health services in all NHS board areas in the Highlands and Islands.

S2W-3874 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to encourage all local authorities to provide assistance for direct payments support organisations in their areas.

S2W-3875 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to address low uptake of direct payments in local authority areas.

S2W-3876 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how many linear accelerators are (a) needed to meet patient demands and (b) currently in operation, (i) in total and (ii) in each NHS board area.

S2W-3877 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive on how many days linear accelerators were not in use due to (a) maintenance, (b) breakdown and (c) lack of staff to operate them in each of the last five years in each NHS board area.

S2W-3878 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how many full-time equivalent radiotherapists there are in each NHS board.

S2W-3879 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how many radiotherapists are required to ensure that the linear accelerators are used to their full capacity.

S2W-3880 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how many linear accelerators there are per million of the population.

S2W-3881 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how many expert groups it has set up since May 1999.

S2W-3882 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how many expert groups set up by it have reported to it since May 1999.

S2W-3883 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how many reports from expert groups have been published since May 1999.

S2W-3884 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how many of the expert groups it has set up are still due to report to it and when it expects to receive their reports.

S2W-3885 Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Time Out centre being piloted in Glasgow as an alternative to prison for women was opened; how many (a) residential and (b) day places are available in the centre; how much funding the Executive has allocated to Glasgow City Council to establish this service, and how long the service will run as a pilot before evaluation and possible roll out to other parts of the country.

S2W-3886 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive why annual check-ups for over-75s have been postponed pending consultation on the new GP contract.

S2W-3887 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what the annual income of the Golden Jubilee Hospital is from "marginal hotel" charges made to NHS trusts that send patients to the hospital for cardiac surgery.

S2W-3888 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the heart team of the Golden Jubilee Hospital is deployed to work elsewhere in the NHS at times when the team is not working to capacity at that hospital.

S2W-3889 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive at what rate, per patient, Scottish NHS trusts are charged for the "marginal hotel" costs levied by the Golden Jubilee Hospital for those sent for cardiac treatment.

S2W-3890 Mrs Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of household waste was recycled in (a) Scotland and (b) the City of Edinburgh Council area in each year from 1985 to 1995.

S2W-3891 Mrs Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average air quality levels were in (a) Scotland and (b) the City of Edinburgh Council area in each year from 1985 to 1995.

S2W-3892 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is acceptable for a paramedic or nurse to attend to cover an out-of-hours call in the absence of a GP.

S2W-3893 Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it supports COSLA guidance issued to local authorities in respect of party representation on council committees.

S2W-3894 Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is considering introducing legislation in respect of party representation on council committees as part of its consultation on the draft Local Governance (Scotland) Bill.

S2W-3895 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what the cost was to Scottish Enterprise and Highlands and Islands Enterprise of the rebranding of (a) Business Shops as The Small Business Gateway in 2001 and (b) The Small Business Gateway as The Business Gateway in 2003.

S2W-3897 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Presiding Officer whether the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body (SPCB) has made all documentary and other evidence that it has available to the Holyrood inquiry; whether the SPCB requested that any particular items be kept confidential, and, if so, whether the nature of such items can be identified and the reasons for them being kept confidential.

S2W-3898 Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Environment and Rural Affairs Department has been invited to participate in the UK working group on the equine industry and, if so, whether it will participate.

S2W-3899Δ Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it has made in establishing a Financial Service Strategy Group.

S2W-3900 Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive whether its Environment and Rural Affairs Department has commissioned any research on the equine industry.

S2W-3901 Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made of the economic importance and potential of the equine industry.

S2W-3902 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps have been taken to implement its policy on architecture; what further steps will be taken to implement the policy, and what funding has been allocated for this purpose.
