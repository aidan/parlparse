Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-4988 Colin Fox: House Price Rises and Lack of Affordable Housing—That the Parliament believes that the recent rises in average house prices in Scotland are unsustainable and that lack of new social housing is fuelling inflation in house prices, making both buying and renting increasingly unaffordable to those on low incomes, and therefore calls for an end to right-to-buy and for substantial investment by the Scottish Executive in the building of new local authority and housing association properties for affordable rents to meet the obvious need that rising house prices signify.

*S2M-4987 Dr Elaine Murray: A Manifesto for Life Sciences in Scotland—That the Parliament notes the recent document produced by the BioIndustry Association Scotland, entitled Manifesto for Life Sciences in Scotland, which sets out key recommendations for all Scotland’s political parties to follow in order to create a healthier and more prosperous Scotland; recognises that the life sciences sector is a success for Scotland, with 592 companies and institutions employing over 20,700 people and contributing over £800 million per annum to the Scottish economy; appreciates that the sector is highly competitive and that the right conditions are important for the sector to grow and for such important jobs to stay here, and considers that the Scottish Executive should support this manifesto in order to continue the success of life sciences as part of a thriving Scottish economy and to realise the human healthcare benefits that life sciences promise.

*S2M-4986 Rosie Kane: Ann Gloag and the Right to Roam—That the Parliament supports Ramblers Scotland in its court battle against Ann Gloag; believes that no landowner, however wealthy, should place themselves above the law of the land and that right to roam legislation will have little meaning if landowners use their wealth to inflict onerous costs on those wishing to gain access to their land, and believes that, if right to roam access is to be maintained, the Scottish Executive needs to make itself a party to any future appeals that arise from the Gloag case.

*S2M-4985 Ms Rosemary Byrne: European Week for Safety and Health 2006—That the Parliament notes this year’s European Week for Safety and Health 2006 - Safe Start for young workers; notes that young workers are often more susceptible to damage from exposure to toxic substances which may not become apparent until much later in life; further notes that young people’s bones and muscles are still developing and that heavy repetitive work can lead to muscular and skeletal problems and impaired growth; further notes that in the European Union the injury rate for workers aged 18 to 24 is more than 40% higher than other age groups, that in just five years it is estimated that 50 under-18s have lost their lives in UK workplaces and nearly 13,000 were badly injured and that at least 45 children and young people have died and more than 400 have been seriously injured on farms in the last 10 years; recognises that over a quarter of a million young people are involved in apprenticeship programmes in the UK, some of which involve the 14 to 16 age group; notes with concern that a recent TUC poll found that 37% of 15 to 24-year-olds had not had any health and safety training, and therefore calls for the Scottish Executive to do all it can to ensure that Scottish employers understand and implement the Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974 for all employees, including providing a safe workplace, safe systems of work, safe plant and equipment, instruction, training and supervision, and safe use, handling, storage and transport of articles and substances.

*S2M-4984 Frances Curran: Giveme5—That the Parliament supports the "giveme5" campaign, launched by The Richmond Fellowship and FOCUS to increase the permitted earnings disregard for people on means-tested benefits; notes that the permitted earnings disregard has not been increased for five years and supports the call for the Westminster Government to increase it by £5 per week to £25, and further notes that the permitted earnings disregard enables people to increase their skills and confidence in the knowledge that their benefits will not be affected and can be a stepping stone to future employment.

*S2M-4983 Sarah Boyack: Energy Saving Week - 23 to 29 October 2006—That the Parliament congratulates the Energy Saving Trust for organising its 10th annual Energy Saving Week from 23 to 29 October; urges all MSPs to make a pledge to adopt at least one of 10 small measures to reduce the amount of energy they consume on a daily basis: boiling only as much water as they need, turning down thermostats by 1°C, switching off appliances and not leaving them on standby, installing cavity wall insulation, topping up loft insulation to 270mm, replacing light bulbs with energy saving recommended ones, installing a condensing boiler, buying energy saving recommended appliances, washing laundry at 30°C and leaving cars at home for short journeys, and further notes that since the Energy Saving Trust was established it has made lifetime savings of around 20 million tonnes of carbon.
