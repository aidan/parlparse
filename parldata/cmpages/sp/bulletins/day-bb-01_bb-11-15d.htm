Business Bulletin No. 166/2001

Thursday 15 November 2001

Section D : Oral Questions
  

Question Time

1. Mr Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it has made towards improving the quality of NHS dental services in the Grampian Health Board area. (S1O-4121)

2. Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to promote culture and the arts as being integral to the regeneration of communities. (S1O-4148)

3. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to announce final details of the wind-up of the Scottish Transport Group pension schemes. (S1O-4156)

4. Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether its programme for road projects will include upgrading the remaining single track section of the A830 between Fort William and Mallaig and whether it will seek any European transitional funding for such an upgrade. (S1O-4154)

5. Brian Fitzpatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making with its policy of improving the Scottish diet. (S1O-4150)

6. Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to promote physical education within the school curriculum. (S1O-4122)

7. Mr John McAllion: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to introduce legislation to facilitate competition in the delivery of water and sewerage services. (S1O-4128)

8. Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the Department of Culture, Media and Sport in relation to the powers granted to the Secretary of State for that department to determine the boundary between English and Scottish territorial waters by virtue of section 1 (3) of the National Heritage Bill. (S1O-4120)

9. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to prevent any deterioration of coastal waters resulting from expansion of the fish farming industry. (S1O-4125)

10. Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what priority the reopening of the Stirling-Alloa-Dunfermline line has in relation to other rail projects. (S1O-4135)

11. Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made of the potential for developing renewable energy generation in Dumfries and Galloway. (S1O-4155)

12. Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how many homes owned by elderly people in Dundee have been fitted with central heating under the central heating initiative. (S1O-4144)

13. Mr Keith Raffan: To ask the Scottish Executive what its spending is in the current financial year on tackling alcohol misuse and what its estimate is of the numbers suffering from severe or chronic misuse. (S1O-4145)

14. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1O-3917 by Iain Gray on 25 October 2001, what steps it will take to meet the requirements of those who seek access to police road traffic accident reports. (S1O-4130)

15. David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in discussions with Her Majesty’s Government on the possibility of a national top-up to the Sheep Annual Premium. (S1O-4134)

16. Mr Frank McAveety: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to promote and support cultural tourism. (S1O-4143)

17. Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made with the New Strategy for Golf Tourism. (S1O-4147)

18. Mrs Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive what actions it plans to take following the publication of its report, Estimating the National and Local Prevalence of Problem Drug Misuse in Scotland. (S1O-4157)

19. Janis Hughes: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making with its plans to recruit and retain additional nurses. (S1O-4146)

20. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to reduce the number of children abused and neglected in Scotland and improve services offered to those children. (S1O-4137)

21. Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans there are to produce guidelines limiting the number of cases, particularly child protection cases, allocated to individual social workers. (S1O-4129)

22. Bristow Muldoon: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made with the development of a form of diligence to replace poindings and warrant sales. (S1O-4152)

23. Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive what its next steps will be to assist tourism. (S1O-4153)

24. Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has held any discussions with Dr Andrew Walker of the Robertson Centre for Biostatistics in the University of Glasgow, in light of his report on hospital-acquired infections, and what the outcome of any such discussions was. (S1O-4126)

25. Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to receive payments in respect of the costs of repair of the Erskine Bridge from those parties considered responsible for damaging the bridge. (S1O-4127)

26. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to improve the services available on the NHS to people with diabetes. (S1O-4139)

27. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive whether, in light of recent difficulties facing the electronics industry in the Scottish Borders, it will address the state of the area’s economy as a matter of urgency. (S1O-4131)

28. Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what preparations it has made for the introduction of EC Regulation 2037/2000 concerning the recycling of domestic refrigerators and freezers. (S1O-4141)

29. Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what its current position is on the relocation of jobs in non-departmental public bodies from Edinburgh and Glasgow to other locations in Scotland. (S1O-4140)

30. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to provide funding to support additional places in women’s refuges. (S1O-4158)
