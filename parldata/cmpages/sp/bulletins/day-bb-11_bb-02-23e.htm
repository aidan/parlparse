Business Bulletin No. 29/2011: Wenesday 23 February 2011
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 22 February 2011

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-39740 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding has been provided by sportscotland to the Scottish Football Association in each of the last four years.

S3W-39741 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive when the local action plans relating to the Change Fund are due to be submitted.

S3W-39742 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many local action plans relating to the Change Fund have been submitted.

S3W-39743 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what process will be followed to agree the local action plans relating to the Change Fund.

S3W-39744 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason the Intellectual Assets Centre’s budget has been reduced from £1.35 million in 2010-11 to £720,000 for 2011-12.

S3W-39745 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Intellectual Assets Centre’s staff will be subject to compulsory redundancy.

S3W-39746 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason it is proposing to relocate the Intellectual Assets Centre.

S3W-39747 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Intellectual Assets Centre is a fully owned subsidiary of Scottish Enterprise.

S3W-39748 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive how much the Intellectual Assets Centre has contributed to the economy in each of the last four years.

S3W-39749 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive under what circumstances ministers can call in a planning application regarding the demolition of a listed building.

S3W-39750 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether ministers can call in a planning application regarding the demolition of a listed building when Historic Scotland has not requested such action.

S3W-39751 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment has been made of the compliance of (a) its directorates, (b) its agencies and (c) non-departmental public bodies with the UK Government’s Central Point of Expertise on Timber’s criteria for sourcing sustainable timber.

S3W-39752 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment has been made of the proportion of wood-based products procured by (a) it and (b) its agencies in the last 12 months that come from (i) Forest Stewardship Council-certified, (ii) Programme for the Endorsement of Forest Certification scheme-certified, (iii) other certified and (iv) non-certified sources.

S3W-39753 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to update the procurement policy note on timber and timber products on its website.

S3W-39754 Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with housing organisations regarding the infrastructure loans fund announced in the budget for 2011-12.

S3W-39755 Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many houses were sold under shared equity schemes in 2009-10 and have been sold in 2010-11.

S3W-39756 Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many houses it anticipates will be sold under shared equity schemes in 2011-12.

S3W-39757 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure that NHS boards are implementing the clinical standards for neurological services.

S3W-39758 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance it has issued to alcohol and drug partnerships regarding meeting the new HEAT targets for drugs and alcohol.

S3W-39759 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will monitor alcohol and drug partnerships to ensure that punitive discharge of users receiving treatment is not used as a way to free up places for new users in order to meet HEAT targets for drugs and alcohol.

S3W-39760 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-36623 by Nicola Sturgeon on 25 October 2010, how many (a) bed days were lost and (b) procedures were cancelled due to ward closures due to hospital-acquired infection in (i) 2006-07, (ii) 2007-08, (iii) 2008-09 and (iv) 2009-10.

S3W-39761 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether all schools maintain an up-to-date asthma register.

S3W-39762 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what national enhanced services are undertaken by GP practices in each NHS board area.

S3W-39763 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the cost to the NHS has been of national enhanced services in the last two years for which information is available.

S3W-39764 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the cost was to the NHS of each national enhanced service (NES) in the last two years for which information is available; in how many cases the fee included a per-patient element, and how many patient fees were paid for each NES.

S3W-39765 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the cost was of each national enhanced service, broken down by (a) NHS board and (b) 1,000 population, in the last two years for which information is available.

S3W-39766 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what local enhanced services in primary care were delivered by each NHS board in the last year for which information is available.

S3W-39767 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the cost was of each local enhanced service in each NHS board in the last year for which information is available.

S3W-39768 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what evidence has been collected on national enhanced services in primary care that demonstrates the objective of shifting care from secondary to primary services.

S3W-39769 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what definition of homeless is used in determining the per-patient fee as part of the national enhanced service for care for homeless people.

S3W-39770 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding it will provide to the Scottish Business Crime Centre for the three-year period following March 2011.

S3W-39771 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding it provided to the Association of Scottish National Neighbourhood Watches in 2009-10 and has provided for (a) 2010-11 and (b) 2011-12.

S3W-39772 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what changes have been made to prison rules since May 2007 to ensure that the most effective contact is sustained between parents and children.

S3W-39773 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what requirements it has introduced in relation to the development or refurbishment of prison facilities to ensure that the needs and rights of children are taken into account.

S3W-39774 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure that a visitor centre separate from formal visitor facilities in prisons is provided for support before and after formal visits.

S3W-39775 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the national standards for social enquiry reports include a requirement to assess the impact on children.

S3W-39776 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many family contact development officers or equivalent there are in each prison.

S3W-39777 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken since May 2007 to ensure that children with a parent in prison are recorded as being in need in terms of section 22 of the Children (Scotland) Act 1995.

S3W-39778 Jamie Hepburn: To ask the Scottish Executive whether proposed housing developments by a registered social landlord must receive planning permission before grant support to assist with construction costs can be awarded.

S3W-39779Δ Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will meet its obligations under section 71 of the Climate Change (Scotland) Act 2009 regarding permitted development rights for microgeneration equipment on non-domestic properties.

S3W-39780 Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will require a public local inquiry to be held into the proposal for the Viking wind farm on Shetland and what the reasons are for its position on this matter.

S3W-39781 Shirley-Anne Somerville: To ask the Scottish Executive how many jobs it estimates will be created under its plans for a low-carbon economy by 2014-15.

S3W-39782 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding will be provided for the Choose Life programme in 2011-12.

S3W-39783 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how funding for the Choose Life programme has been distributed in each year since May 2007.

S3W-39784 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what monitoring arrangements are in place for the Choose Life programme.

S3W-39785 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how funding from the Choose Life programme has been spent by NHS Health Scotland in each year since May 2007.

S3W-39786 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the announcement of £400 million for a new mental health strategy by the UK Government will have a Barnett consequential and, if so, (a) what this will be and (b) how it will allocate it.
