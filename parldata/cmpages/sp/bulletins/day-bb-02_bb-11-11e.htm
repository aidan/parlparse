Business Bulletin No. 161/2002

Monday 11 November 2002

Section E : Written questions lodged on 8 November 2002

  

Questions marked with a triangle () are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S1W-31356 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive how many consultant forensic pathologist posts there are currently; where the posts are located, and which of them are currently vacant.

S1W-31357 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive how many training posts in forensic medicine there are currently; where the posts are located, and which of them are currently vacant.

S1W-31369 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to build up community organisations in deprived areas to enable them to deliver community plans.

S1W-31392 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many places are available annually on programmes for (a) drug and (b) alcohol rehabilitation.

S1W-31393 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average waiting time is for a place on (a) drug and (b) alcohol rehabilitation programmes in each NHS board.

S1W-31394 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the rationale for the methadone programme is (a) clinical effectiveness or (b) to achieve a reduction in crimes committed to fund drug habits.

S1W-31395 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the report commissioned by its Environment and Rural Affairs Department and compiled by the Macaulay Land Use Research Institute and others, referred to in page 10 of The Press and Journal on 6 November 2002, has been made available to (a) the Parliament and (b) the media and, if so, on what dates and what the reasons are for its position on this matter.

S1W-31396 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what its policy is with regard to the publication of reports commissioned by its Environment and Rural Affairs Department.

S1W-31397 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessments have been made of the effectiveness of agri-environmental schemes currently operating in Scotland, with particular reference to the Environmentally Sensitive Areas schemes; which bodies have carried out any such assessments, and when they were commissioned to carry out any such work.

S1W-31399 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consult YouthLink Scotland in order to identify the administrative costs that national youth organisations claim to incur in the administration of Disclosure Scotland checks on voluntary youth workers.

S1W-31400 Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive how many NHS boards in Scotland currently prescribe the anti-TNF drugs etanercept and infliximab and when any such boards started prescribing these drugs.

S1W-31401 Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance has been given to NHS boards about prescribing new drugs that have been endorsed by the Health Technology Board for Scotland.

S1W-31402 Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive how much funding has been given to each NHS board to take account of the costs of prescribing drugs in (a) 2001-02 and (b) 2002-03.

S1W-31403 Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is normal practice for NHS boards, following recommendations by the Health Technology Board for Scotland that a new drug should be prescribed, to receive additional funding to ensure that the drug can be made available.

S1W-31404 Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average annual cost to the NHS is of caring for someone with arthritis.

S1W-31405 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive when it was first advised of the outcome of the appeal to the Competition Commission Appeal Tribunal in the case of BetterCare Group Limited v the Director General of Fair Trading and what steps it has subsequently taken to consider the implications of the tribunal’s findings.

S1W-31406 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive when guidance will be issued to local authorities and other public bodies on the implications for public services of the Competition Commission Appeal Tribunal’s ruling in the case of BetterCare Group Limited v the Director General of Fair Trading.

S1W-31407 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive when it was first advised of the appeal to the Competition Commission Appeal Tribunal in the case of BetterCare Group Limited v the Director General of Fair Trading and what steps it was taken to be kept up to date with developments in this case.

S1W-31408 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive which minister is leading discussions with Her Majesty’s Government on the implications for public services of the Competition Commission Appeal Tribunal’s ruling in the case of BetterCare Group Limited v the Director General of Fair Trading.

S1W-31409 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Presiding Officer what the unit cost per PC will be of purchasing Microsoft Office XP for use by general users of parliamentary PCs and whether any other industry standard packages compatible at file level with Microsoft Office, such as StarOffice, were considered as an alternative.

S1W-31410 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-28515 by Mr Jim Wallace on 4 November 2002, why the answer did not indicate what plans are in place to evaluate prisoners considered eligible for the Retox Programme prior to release and what any such plans are.

S1W-31411 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any assessment has been made of the responses given by Mr Tony Cameron, Chief Executive of the Scottish Prison Service in answers to written parliamentary questions, in particular of whether such responses address the specific questions posed.

S1W-31412 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prisoners were held on remand in Scottish prisons in (a) 1999, (b) 2000 and (c) 2001; how long on average each such prisoner spent in prison; what the average cost was per prisoner, and how many such prisoners received a prison sentence.

S1W-31413 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prisoners were held on remand in bail hostels in (a) 1999, (b) 2000 and (c) 2001; what the average cost was per prisoner, and how many bail hostel places are currently available.

S1W-31414 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive who is responsible for ensuring that people sent to prison are properly advised about their housing position and, in particular, about whether they should cancel their tenancy in order to avoid accumulating debts from unpaid rent.

S1W-31415 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to establish in other prisons centres based on the Throughcare Centre pilot at HM Prison Edinburgh.

S1W-31416 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will place a duty on local authorities to work with prisons in providing after-prison throughcare.

S1W-31417 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will give grants to voluntary organisations providing restorative justice schemes.

S1W-31418 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide core funding to the Safeguarding Communities, Reducing Offending in Scotland (SACRO) organisation for its work with young people, given that it provides funding for SACRO's work with adults.

S1W-31419 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will ensure that youth crime teams are set up in every local authority.

S1W-31420 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is its policy that bills must be paid to businesses by public bodies within 30 days.

S1W-31421 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there is any evidence of late payment of bills to businesses by public bodies.

S1W-31422 Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive what emergency planning measures have been put in place for incidents involving nuclear-powered and nuclear-armed submarines and ships and whether it will make details of any such measures publicly available.

S1W-31423 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive what success it has had in achieving reductions in the levels of street crime in (a) Scotland and (b) Renfrewshire since 1999 and what targets it has set for such reductions in this period.

S1W-31424 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive what success it has had in achieving reductions in the levels of violent crime related to drug use in (a) Scotland and (b) Renfrewshire since 1999 and what targets it has set for such reductions in this period.

S1W-31425 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive what policies to tackle youth crime in Renfrewshire it (a) has implemented and (b) is planning to implement and what associated investment is needed to implement such policies.

S1W-31426 Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive what incentives there are for local authorities and NHS boards to implement the recommendations set out in The same as you? and what action will be taken if they do not do so.

S1W-31427 Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider withholding Learning Disability Services Change Fund monies from any local authorities that do not implement the commitments contained in the Partnership in Practice agreements.

S1W-31428 Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it holds any information on local authorities’ use of Learning Disability Services Change Fund monies and, if so, when such information will be published.

S1W-31429 Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average annual spend per capita on learning disability services is in Scotland as compared with levels of such spend in England and Wales.
