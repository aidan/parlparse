Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 9 June 2005

Question Time

General

1. Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive when it intends to legislate against kerb crawling. (S2O-7043)

2. George Lyon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is aware of the concerns of people on the Isle of Mull regarding Argyll and Bute Council’s proposals to impose maximum plated weight restrictions on the island’s roads. (S2O-7008)

3. Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive how much it would cost to give business rate exemptions to all new businesses in their first two years. (S2O-7012)

4. Gordon Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the timetable is for construction of the M77 Seaward Street diversion scheme. (S2O-7051)

5. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will support the bid by St Andrews Links Trust for St Andrews to be the Olympic golf venue if London is successful in its attempt to host the 2012 Olympic Games and golf is included as an event at these games. (S2O-7013)

6. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to encourage more people to volunteer for projects in their communities. (S2O-7007)

7. Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive what its latest estimate is of the number of 16 to 19-year-olds who are not in education, training or employment. (S2O-7034)

8. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive what confidence it has in the Chair of the Scottish Scientific Advisory Committee. (S2O-6993)

9. Not Lodged

10. Not Lodged

Environment and Rural Development

1. Not Lodged

2. Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to help prevent future flooding damage to homes and businesses in Tayside. (S2O-6999)

3. Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to promote greenspace areas in urban landscapes. (S2O-7032)

4. Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance it intends to issue on the use of pesticides, in light of the findings of the Geoparkinson study. (S2O-7025)

5. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take following the publication of the Report on the Inquiry into Climate Change by the Environment and Rural Development Committee. (S2O-7042)

6. Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will bring forward a specific sustainable strategy for the treatment and disposal of sewage sludge. (S2O-7026)

7. Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in improving the international competitiveness of Scottish aquaculture. (S2O-6996)

8. Mr Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it has taken to address any issues arising from the review of agri-environment payments. (S2O-7003)

9. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions have taken place with port authorities across Scotland regarding the environmental impact of ship-to-ship oil transfers. (S2O-7045)

10. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will report on the consultation on Developing a Strategic Framework for Scotland’s Marine Environment. (S2O-7056)

Health and Community Care

1. Bristow Muldoon: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made by NHS Lothian in recruiting additional consultants to support the obstetrics and gynaecology department at St. John’s Hospital in Livingston. (S2O-7066)

2. Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the timetable for constructing the new Renfrew Health Centre will be affected by the plans for NHS Argyll and Clyde. (S2O-7039)

3. Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making on the review of the Arbuthnott formula for allocating funding to NHS boards. (S2O-7040)

4. Kate Maclean: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is aware of the challenges of managing type 1 diabetes and, in particular, the difficulties faced by young people in trying to control the disease. (S2O-7055)

5. Jim Mather: To ask the Scottish Executive whether, when implementing the recommendations of the Kerr report and, in particular, the recommendation that training arrangements should be developed to ensure a steady supply of remote and rural practitioners, it will consider the methodologies presented to the Finance Committee by Professor Michael Barber on 17 May 2005. (S2O-7031)

6. Mr Alasdair Morrison: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide an update on the provision of dental services on the Isle of Lewis. (S2O-7065)

7. Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive what changes are planned for the operation of NHS 24. (S2O-7035)

8. Mr Andrew Arbuckle: To ask the Scottish Executive how the proposals in the Kerr report will improve services for patients in Mid Scotland and Fife. (S2O-7005)

9. Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive how often the Minister for Health and Community Care meets individual NHS boards. (S2O-7002)

10. Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive how many school nurses there are; whether it considers that there are sufficient numbers of such nurses; whether it has a target for the number that there should be, and what its policy is in this regard. (S2O-7020)
