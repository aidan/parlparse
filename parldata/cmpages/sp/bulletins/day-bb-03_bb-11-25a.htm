Business Bulletin No. 161 / 2003

Tuesday 25th November 2003

Section A : Daily Business List
  

Meetings of Committees

All meetings take place in Committee Chambers, Edinburgh unless otherwise indicated.

Audit Committee

8th Meeting, 2003

The Committee will meet at 9.30 am in Committee Room 2

1. Items in private: The Committee will consider whether to hold items 6, 7, 8 and 9 in private.

2. Catering for Patients: The Committee will receive a briefing from the Auditor General for Scotland (AGS) on the report by the AGS entitled 'Catering for Patients' (AGS/2003/10).

3. Work programme: The Committee will consider its work programme for the period December 2003 to January 2004.

4. Subordinate legislation: The Committee will consider the following Negative Instrument -

The Public Finance and Accountability (Scotland) Act 2000 (Access to Documents and Information) (Relevant Persons) Order 2003, (SSI 2003/530).

5. Moving to Mainstream: The Convener will report to the Committee on the outcome of his meeting with the Conveners of the Finance and Procedures Committees in respect of its forthcoming inquiry into the scrutiny of financial consequences of proposed legislation, following publication of the report by the AGS entitled 'Moving to Mainstream - The inclusion of pupils with special educational needs in mainstream schools' (AGS/2003/4).

6. Individual Learning Accounts in Scotland: The Committee will consider a draft report on its inquiry into the report by the AGS entitled 'Individual Learning Accounts in Scotland' (AGS/2003/3).

7. Supporting Prescribing in General Practice: The Committee will consider a draft report on its inquiry into the report by the AGS entitled 'Supporting Prescribing in General Practice - A Progress Report' (AGS/2003/5).

8. Inquiry into the AGS Report on the Scottish Further Education Funding Council (AGS/2003/7): The Committee will consider the evidence taken at its meeting held on 11 November 2003.

9. Catering for Patients: The Committee will consider its approach to the report by the AGS entitled 'Catering for Patients' (AGS/2003/10).

Equal Opportunities Committee

8th Meeting, 2003

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in The Chamber, Assembly Hall, The Mound, Edinburgh

1. Items in private: The Committee will consider whether to take items 3, 4 and 5 in private.

2. Gender Recognition: The Committee will consider a paper.

3. Education (Additional Support for Learning) (Scotland) bill: The Committee will consider an approach paper.

4. Civil partnership registration: The Committee will consider a draft response to the Scottish Executive consultation.

5. European Year of Disabled People: The Committee will consider its lines of questioning for its meeting on 2 December.

Subordinate Legislation Committee

14th Meeting, 2003

The Committee will meet at 10.30 am in Committee Room 3

1. Executive responses: The Committee will consider responses from the Scottish Executive to points raised on the following-

the Public Finance and Accountability (Scotland) Act 2000 (Access to Documents and Information) (Relevant Persons) Order 2003, (SSI 2003/530)

the Control of Pollution (Silage, Slurry and Agricultural Fuel Oil) (Scotland) Regulations 2003, (SSI 2003/531)

the Agricultural Holdings (Scotland) Act 2003 (Commencement No.3, Transitional and Savings Provisions) Order 2003, (SSI 2003/548).

2. Draft instruments subject to approval: The Committee will consider the following-

the Producer Responsibility Obligations (Packaging Waste) Amendment (Scotland) Regulations 2003, (draft).

3. Instruments subject to approval: The Committee will consider the following-

the Food Protection (Emergency Prohibitions) (Amnesic Shellfish Poisoning) (West Coast) (No.11) (Scotland) Order 2003, (SSI 2003/561).

4. Instruments subject to annulment: The Committee will consider the following-

the Food (Brazil Nuts) (Emergency Control) (Scotland) Amendment Regulations 2003, (SSI 2003/558)

the Prohibition of Keeping or Release of Live Fish (Specified Species) (Scotland) Order 2003, (SSI 2003/560).

5. Instruments not subject to Parliamentary procedure: The Committee will consider the following-

the Food Protection (Emergency Prohibitions) (Amnesic Shellfish Poisoning) (Orkney) (No.3) Revocation Order 2003, (SSI 2003/557).

6. Instruments not laid before the Parliament: The Committee will consider the following-

Act of Sederunt (Summary Applications, Statutory Applications and Appeals etc. Rules) Amendment (International Protection of Adults) 2003, (SSI 2003/556).

7. Proposed inquiry: The Committee will consider a scoping paper for a proposed inquiry into the regulatory framework in Scotland.

8. Correspondence: The Committee will consider a letter from FSA Scotland.
