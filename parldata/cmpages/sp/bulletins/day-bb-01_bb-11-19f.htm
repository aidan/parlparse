Business Bulletin No. 168/2001

Monday 19 November 2001

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-2459 Mr Jim Wallace: Sexual Offences (Procedure and Evidence) (Scotland) Bill-That the Parliament agrees to the general principles of the Sexual Offences (Procedure and Evidence) (Scotland) Bill.

Supported by: Iain Gray*

*S1M-2458 Susan Deacon: NHS Reform and Health Care Professions Bill - UK Legislation-That the Parliament endorses the principle that the Council for the Regulation of Healthcare Professionals, which is to be created to oversee the regulatory bodies for healthcare professionals under the NHS Reform and Health Care Professions Bill, should also have power to oversee any regulatory bodies which might be established in future and which fall within the devolved competence of the Scottish Parliament; agrees that the Council be made accountable to the Scottish Parliament in respect of such of its functions as fall within devolved competence; agrees that the function should be conferred upon Scottish ministers of appointing one of the members of the Council, agrees that power should be conferred on Scottish ministers to make payments and loans to the Council, and finally agrees that the relevant provisions to achieve this end in the Bill should be considered by the UK Parliament.

Supported by: Malcolm Chisholm*

*S1M-2457# Christine Grahame: Borders Tourism-That the Parliament notes with concern the report in The Scotsman on 15 November 2001 about falling visitor numbers and the consequential loss of around £30,000 in "gate receipts" at the former home of Sir Walter Scott, Abbotsford, near Galashiels, following the outbreak of foot-and-mouth disease and recent events in the USA; draws the attention of the Scottish Executive to the report of 7 November 2001 by the Director of Lifelong Care at Scottish Borders Council on the impact in the area of the foot-and-mouth disease outbreak and in particular the estimated total loss for the coming year to Borders tourism of £30 million (paragraph 12.1); considers that the £440,000 delivered by the Executive so far for marketing purposes is insufficient to redress this loss, and calls upon the Executive, on presentation of the Scottish Borders Tourist Board's forthcoming report, to provide realistic funding to redress this spiralling decline.

Supported by: Mr Lloyd Quinan*, Roseanna Cunningham*, Ms Sandra White*, Michael Russell*, Ms Margo MacDonald*, Fiona Hyslop*

*S1M-2456 Mr Adam Ingram: Disposal of Remains from the Foot-and-Mouth Disease Outbreak-That the Parliament deplores the decision by the Scottish Executive to dispose of two thousand tonnes of ash from the incineration of sheep and cattle culled in Dumfries and Galloway and the Scottish Borders during the foot-and-mouth outbreak earlier this year at Garlaff in East Ayrshire; supports East Ayrshire Council in its opposition to the decision which results in the import of waste material in the form of ash from the outbreak into a non-infected area without guarantees that the temperature of incineration reached 1000 degrees centigrade, which is the temperature required to kill the BSE prion, and calls on the Executive to arrange for the transportation of the waste for disposal at an engineered site at Carlisle failing the provision of such a site within previously infected areas in Scotland.

S1M-2454 Trish Godman: Retirement of John Hume and Seamus Mallon-That the Parliament notes the retirement of John Hume and Seamus Mallon from the leadership of the Social Democratic and Labour Party; acknowledges the supremely important part which they have both played in the Northern Ireland peace process and, in particular, in the creation of the Northern Ireland Assembly and the new police service; believes that they are amongst the finest and most honourable politicians of their generation; pays handsome tribute to their remarkable political abilities and unflinching personal courage in the face of brutal adversity, and believes that their sustained and deep opposition to all kinds of violence should guide the conduct of all present and future political activists in democracies throughout the world.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson*

S1M-2450 Hugh Henry: World AIDS Day: 1 December 2001-That the Parliament notes World AIDS Day on 1 December 2001 and the "Out of sight, out of mind" campaign; further notes that recent figures published show that 91 people have been diagnosed as HIV positive in the first six months of this year compared with 154 in total for the previous year and that, if this trend continues for the rest of the year, it would represent an 18% increase over last year; further notes that, if the number of people aged 15 to 24 being diagnosed as HIV positive continues at the current rate, there will be a 47% increase by the end of the year, and therefore urges the Scottish Executive to undertake a high profile national campaign on HIV and AIDS awareness.

Supported by: Trish Godman, Mike Watson, Janis Hughes

S1M-2449 Mr Murray Tosh: Convenerships and vice-convenerships of committees-That the Parliament notes that the convenerships and vice-convenerships of the Parliament's committees were allocated initially on a proportional basis; agrees that these posts should always be allocated proportionally; recognises that the Labour Party, with 42.6% of the seats in the Parliament, now holds 53% of the committee convenerships and 47% of the vice-convenerships, and calls upon the Parliamentary Bureau to take the initiative in restoring proportionality following any personnel changes arising from a reshuffle of Scottish Executive ministers.

Supported by: Phil Gallie, Brian Adam, Mr David Davidson, Mr Mike Rumbles, John Scott, Mrs Lyndsay McIntosh*, Mary Scanlon*, Fergus Ewing*, Stewart Stevenson*, Fiona McLeod*

S1M-2448 Dennis Canavan: Election of First Minister-That the Parliament agrees that members be given the opportunity to question those nominated for the post of First Minister before the Parliament votes to decide on the successful nominee.

Supported by: Mr Murray Tosh, Tommy Sheridan, Alex Neil, Robin Harper*

S1M-2447# Mr Kenny MacAskill: Congestion and Pollution caused by Edinburgh "Bus Wars"-That the Parliament notes with concern the escalation of the "bus wars" in the city of Edinburgh, a situation which is detrimental to routes, services and passengers and which results in congestion and pollution in some areas, at the same time as routes and services are lost in others, and urges all responsible parties to take appropriate action to address this situation and to allow Scotland's capital city to develop an integrated transport network in the 21st Century.

Supported by: Andrew Wilson, Fiona McLeod*

S1M-2446# Shona Robison: National Mouth Cancer Awareness Week-That the Parliament recognises the importance of National Mouth Cancer Awareness Week in highlighting the need for people to visit the dentist regularly to ensure early detection and treatment of oral cancer; is concerned that the incidence of oral cancer has increased by one third over the last fifteen years; is aware of the research by the British Dental Association which showed that one in two people are more likely to visit a dentist regularly if there is no charge for an NHS dental check up, and believes that the Scottish Executive should consider the restoration of free dental check ups as part of its review of charging policy within the health service.

Supported by: Andrew Wilson, Fiona McLeod*

S1M-2440 Angus MacKay: Scottish Local Government (Elections) Bill: Financial Resolution-That the Parliament, for the purposes of any Act of the Scottish Parliament resulting from the Scottish Local Government (Elections) Bill, agrees to any increase in expenditure payable out of the Scottish Consolidated Fund in consequence of the Act.

Supported by: Peter Peacock

S1M-2439 Kate MacLean on behalf of the Equal Opportunities Committee: Inquiry into Gypsy Travellers and Public Sector Policies-That the Parliament notes the 1st Report 2001 of the Equal Opportunities Committee on the Inquiry into Gypsy Travellers and Public Sector Policies (SP Paper 356).

S1M-2437 John Farquhar Munro: Internet Access-That the Parliament notes with concern that, according to a MORI survey conducted for Oftel, the number of households in the UK with access to the internet has fallen for the first time, from 40% to 39% of households, welcomes the slight increase in the proportion of Scottish homes with internet access, but regrets that the Scottish figure is only 29%, which is still 10% lower than the UK average; recognises the critical role that the internet plays in promoting business and as a key access tool for vulnerable groups; notes that these vulnerable groups are still lagging behind the general population with regard to internet access, the number of households with annual incomes lower than £17,500 with internet access having dropped from 21% to 19%; welcomes the Scottish Executive's Digital Communities initiative, but believes that the Executive should ensure that all low income and other vulnerable groups in Scotland are given every opportunity to connect to the internet, particularly by encouraging the use of fixed-price packages, in order to ensure that technology in the 21st century narrows the social and digital divide rather than expanding it.

Supported by: Mr Jamie Stone, Iain Smith, Nora Radcliffe, Michael Russell

S1M-2436# Mr Mike Rumbles: Digital Hearing Aids and Review of Audiology Services-That the Parliament notes that the Scottish Executive is conducting an audiology services review; recognises that many hard of hearing and profoundly deaf people are still provided with out-dated analogue hearing aids; is aware that new digital hearing aids can improve the quality of life for those who need them; realises that digital hearing aids are not widely available on the NHS in Scotland and are expensive to purchase privately; understands that the cost can be dramatically reduced by a system of bulk-buying; further notes that such a scheme has been introduced into 20 NHS hospitals in England, and considers that the Scottish Executive should make a commitment to provide digital hearing aids on the NHS in Scotland.

Supported by: Iain Smith, Nora Radcliffe, John Farquhar Munro, Mr Adam Ingram, Mr Kenneth Gibson, Mr Murray Tosh, Fergus Ewing, David Mundell, Mr Keith Raffan, Brian Adam, Mr David Davidson, Alex Neil, Ms Sandra White, John Scott, Robert Brown, Robin Harper*

S1M-2435# Michael Matheson: European Day of Disabled People: 3 December 2001-That the Parliament notes the European Day of Disabled People on 3 December 2001; recognises the contribution made by people with disabilities to Scottish society; further notes with concern the continuing exclusion of disabled people from full participation within our society due to barriers created by poor design and inaccessible buildings in towns and cities across Scotland, and supports the principle of "Design for All" by asking the Holyrood Progress Group to ensure that the new Parliament building at Holyrood is accessible to all.

Supported by: Bruce Crawford, Fergus Ewing, Richard Lochhead, Mr Andrew Welsh, Mr Kenny MacAskill, Robert Brown, Colin Campbell, Brian Adam, Roseanna Cunningham, Mr Kenneth Gibson, Mr Gil Paterson, Ms Sandra White, Andrew Wilson, Shona Robison, Mr Michael McMahon, Mrs Margaret Ewing, Mr Murray Tosh, Stewart Stevenson, Nora Radcliffe, Pauline McNeill, Fiona McLeod, Mr George Reid, Alex Neil, Michael Russell, Robin Harper*, Irene McGugan*
