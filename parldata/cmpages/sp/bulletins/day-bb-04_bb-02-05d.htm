Business Bulletin No. 24/2004

Thursday 5 February 2004

Section D : Oral Questions
  

Question Time

1. John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is aware of any potential threat to the environment from unregulated tanker traffic in the Minch. (S2O-1294)

2. Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive what the reasons are for it not achieving its target of ending mixed-sex wards in hospitals by March 2002. (S2O-1301)

3. Chris Ballance: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has a policy to encourage staff to travel by train where appropriate. (S2O-1314)

4. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average reduction in waiting times for drug treatment services has been in the last year. (S2O-1323)

5. David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to restructure area tourist boards. (S2O-1290)

6. Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will remove tolls from the Erskine Bridge. (S2O-1283)

7. Frances Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is acceptable for an elderly couple to be without electricity for almost a year. (S2O-1300)

8. Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on whether Scottish fishermen should be allowed to utilise the unused days at sea, under the recently agreed quota arrangements, of other Scottish vessels that for any reason cannot use their allocation. (S2O-1291)

9. Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are being taken to protect children from viewing inappropriate material on the internet. (S2O-1272)

10. Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to set targets for waste prevention for household and commercial waste. (S2O-1310)

11. Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what restrictions are placed on private landlords whose tenants have had central heating installed as part of its central heating programme. (S2O-1282)

12. Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to respond to NHS Quality Improvement Scotland's report, Specialist Palliative Care - National Overview, regarding services for patients with incurable illnesses. (S2O-1284)

13. Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive how much agricultural land has been let under the terms of limited duration tenancies and short limited duration tenancies created by the Agricultural Holdings (Scotland) Act 2003. (S2O-1321)

14. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will increase the participation of 16 to 19-year-olds leaving care in education, employment and training. (S2O-1271)

15. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with Her Majesty's Government with regard to amending the Scotland Act 1998. (S2O-1319)

16. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the City of Edinburgh Council's consultation process on congestion charging is adequate. (S2O-1322)

17. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive how the Tenements (Scotland) Bill will benefit homeowners. (S2O-1288)

18. Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent steps it has taken regarding the high cost of fuel paid by rural drivers. (S2O-1327)

19. Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive what is being done to ensure that woman are offered breast cancer screening at the age of 50. (S2O-1295)

20. Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on which organisations are opposed to the introduction of legislation to regulate smoking. (S2O-1285)

21. Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to appoint a new head of the Scottish Drug Enforcement Agency. (S2O-1320)

22. Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations have been made to the Medicines Control Agency regarding access to medicines through the internet. (S2O-1278)

23. Mr Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive what it is doing to promote the aims of the Livestock Improvement Scheme for crofting townships. (S2O-1325)

24. Mr Mark Ruskell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether concerns over wind farm developments could be allayed by additional planning guidelines. (S2O-1313)

25. Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what effect Norwegian ownership of Scottish salmon farms has on possible co-operation between Scotland and Norway in securing a long-term future for Scotland's aquaculture sector. (S2O-1286)

26. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken to ensure that the interests of objectors in the planning system are taken account of in its preparations for consulting on rights of appeal in planning. (S2O-1312)

27. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will continue with the hedgehog cull in the Western Isles this year. (S2O-1280)

28. Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is currently supporting the arts. (S2O-1273)

29. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to tackle business crime. (S2O-1308)

30. Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it will take to improve the enforcement of bus regulation by traffic commissioners. (S2O-1289)
