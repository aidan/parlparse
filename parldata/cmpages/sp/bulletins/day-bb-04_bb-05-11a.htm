Business Bulletin No. 79 / 2004

Tuesday 11th May 2004

Section A : Daily Business List
  

Meetings of Committees

All meetings take place in Committee Chambers, Edinburgh unless otherwise indicated.

Procedures Committee

8th Meeting, 2004

The Committee will meet at 9.30 am in Committee Room 4

1. Item in private: The Committee will decide whether to take Item 5 in private.

2. Bills - timescales and Stages: The Committee will take evidence from-

Margo MacDonald MSP, former Convener of the Subordinate Legislation Committee and Alasdair Rankin, Clerk to the Committee; and

Pauline McNeill MSP, former Convener of the Justice 2 Committee;

and then from-

Dave Morris, Director, Ramblers' Association Scotland;

Dr Maurice Hankey, Director General, Scottish Rural Property and Business Association; and

John Mackay, formerly National Strategy Manager, Scottish Natural Heritage,

in relation to the Land Reform (Scotland) Bill, one of the case-study Bills in the inquiry.

3. Question Time Review: The Committee will consider a paper on its forthcoming review of the changes it recommended to the timing of First Minister's Question Time and the structure and duration of Question Time.

4. Minor procedural issues: The Committee will consider a paper on minor procedural issues that have been raised.

5. Non-Executive Bills: The Committee will continue its consideration of possible changes to procedures governing the introduction of Non-Executive Bills.

Audit Committee

10th Meeting, 2004

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in Committee Room 2

1. Items in private: The Committee will consider whether to take agenda items 4, 5, 6, 7 and 8 today in private. The Committee will also consider whether to take in private lines of questioning for the witnesses at its evidence session on 25 May 2004 on the report by the Auditor General for Scotland entitled "Better Equipped to Care? - follow-up report on managing medical equipment" (AGS/2004/2).

2. Auditor General for Scotland Strategic Statement: The Committee will receive a briefing from the Auditor General for Scotland on the report by the Auditor General for Scotland entitled "Holding to account and helping to improve: a strategic statement for public audit in Scotland 2004 to 2006".

3. Day Surgery in Scotland: The Committee will receive a briefing from the Auditor General for Scotland on the report by the Auditor General for Scotland entitled "Day Surgery in Scotland: reviewing progress" (AGS/2004/3).

4. Audit Scotland Consultation Paper: The Committee will receive a briefing from the Auditor General for Scotland on the consultation paper on Audit Scotland's future programme of performance studies.

5. Medical Equipment: The Committee will receive a briefing from the Auditor General for Scotland on the report by the Auditor General for Scotland entitled "Better Equipped to Care? - follow-up report on managing medical equipment" (AGS/2004/2).

6. NHS Overview: The Committee will consider an issues paper on its inquiry into the report by the Auditor General for Scotland entitled "Overview of the NHS in Scotland" (AGS/2003/12).

7. Day Surgery in Scotland: The Committee will consider its approach to the report by the Auditor General for Scotland entitled "Day Surgery in Scotland: reviewing progress" (AGS/2004/3).

8. Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body: The Committee will consider possible findings and recommendations on the report by the Auditor General for Scotland entitled "The 2002-03 Audit of the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body".

Finance Committee

15th Meeting, 2004

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in Committee Room 1

1. Subordinate legislation: The Committee will consider the following affirmative instrument-

the Budget (Scotland) Act 2004 Amendment Order 2004 (draft);

and will take evidence from-

Tavish Scott, MSP, Deputy Minister for Finance and Public Services;

Richard Dennis, Finance Co-ordination Team Leader, Scottish Executive; and

Paul Grice, Clerk/ Chief Executive and Lisbeth Craig, Financial Controller, Scottish Parliament.

Peter Wood, Tribal HCH.

4. Emergency Workers (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will take evidence on the Bill's Financial memorandum from-

Richard Scott, Head, Criminal Justice Division and Gery McLaughlin, Bill Team Leader, Criminal Justice Division, Scottish Executive.

5. Items in private: The Committee will decide whether to consider the draft report on the Financial Memorandum of the Emergency Workers (Scotland) Bill and an issues paper on the Committee's Inquiry into the Relocation of Public Sector Jobs in private at its next meeting.

6. School Education (Ministerial Powers and Independent Schools) (Scotland) Bill (in private): The Committee will consider its draft report on the Bill's Financial Memorandum.

7. Breastfeeding etc. (Scotland) Bill (in private): The Committee will consider its draft report on the Bill's Financial Memorandum.
