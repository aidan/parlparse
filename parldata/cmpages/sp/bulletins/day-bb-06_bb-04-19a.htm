Section A – Today’s Business

Meetings of Committees

All meetings take place in the Scottish Parliament, unless otherwise specified. Contact details for Committee Clerks are provided at the end of the Bulletin.

Communities Committee
12th Meeting, 2006

The Committee will meet at 9.30 am in Committee Room 3

1. Item in private: The Committee will consider whether to take agenda item 5 and all future considerations of its draft report on the Planning etc. (Scotland) Bill at Stage 1 in private.

2. Subordinate legislation: Johann Lamont MSP, Deputy Minister for Communities, to move the following motion—

S2M-4174—That the Communities Committee recommends that the draft Planning and Compulsory Purchase Act 2004 (Commencement No.2 and Consequential Provisions) (Scotland) Order 2006 be approved.

3. Finance Committee inquiry into Accountability and Governance: The Committee will consider a letter from the Convener of the Finance Committee.

4. Petition PE946: The Committee will consider petition PE946 on behalf of the Old Musselburgh Club, calling on the Scottish Parliament to consider and debate the process of local engagement and consultation in local planning issues.

5. Planning etc. (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will consider its draft report on the Bill at Stage 1.

Education Committee
9th Meeting, 2006

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in Committee Room 5

1. Subordinate legislation: The Committee will consider the following negative instrument—

the Children (Protection at Work) (Scotland) Regulations 2006 (SSI 2006/140)

2. Subordinate legislation: The Committee will take evidence on the draft Joint Inspections (Scotland) Regulations 2006 from—

Robert Brown MSP, Deputy Minister for Education and Young People;

Jackie Brock, Children and Families Division, Scottish Executive, Education Department;

Sara Davies, Medical Adviser, Health Planning and Quality Division, Scottish Executive, Health Department; and

Douglas Tullis, Office of the Solicitor to the Scottish Executive.

3. Subordinate legislation: Robert Brown MSP, Deputy Minister for Education and Young People, to move S2M-4144—That the Education Committee recommends that the draft Joint Inspections (Scotland) Regulations 2006 be approved.

4. Scottish Schools (Parental Involvement) Bill: The Committee will consider the Bill at Stage 2 (Day 2).

5. Adoption and Children (Scotland) Bill (in private): The Committee will agree its approach to Stage 1 consideration of the Bill.

12th Meeting, 2006

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in Committee Room 1

1. Crofting Reform etc. Bill: The Committee will take evidence at Stage 1 from—

Panel 1

Mike Watson, Crofting Reform etc. Bill Manager, Scottish Executive;

Shane Rankin, Crofting Reform etc. Bill Leader, Scottish Executive;

Aileen Imrie and Ethel Burt, Solicitors, Legal and Parliamentary Services, Scottish Executive;

Brian Wilson;

Professor Jim Hunter;

Panel 3

Hughie Donaldson, Director, Scottish Crofting Foundation;

Andy Robertson, Chief Executive, NFU Scotland; and

Ian Cairns, Area Consultant for Lewis and Harris, Scottish Agricultural College.

2. Subordinate Legislation: The Committee will consider the following negative instruments—

the Pesticides (Maximum Residue Levels in Crops, Foods and Feeding Stuffs) (Scotland) Amendment Regulations 2006, (SSI 2006/151); and

the Products of Animal Origin (Third Country Imports) (Scotland) Amendment Regulations 2006, (SSI 2006/156).

3. Work programme: The Committee will consider its future work programme.

Public Petitions Committee
7th Meeting, 2006

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in Committee Room 6

1. New Petitions: The Committee will consider the following new petitions—

Consultation on school closures.

The Committee will take evidence on PE945 from:

Susan Green; and

Pauline Gilgallan

and on PE955 from

Catriona Lessani; and

Patrick Strickland.

PE947 To give communities the right to ownerless land. Diane Huddleston will make a brief statement to the Committee in support of her petition.

PE925 Not to extend the tolling regimes on the remaining Scottish toll bridges.

PE951 Amendment of legislation to ensure higher parliamentary standards.

2. Current Petitions: The Committee will further consider the following current petitions—

PE869 & PE926 Removing the tolls from the Erskine Bridge. The Committee will consider a response from the Scottish Executive.

PE734 Global Campaign for Education - the Millennium Development Goals. The Committee will consider a response from the Scottish Executive.

PE768 Agenda for Change - Legislation for Speech and Language Therapy Services. The Committee will consider a response from Amicus.

PE806 Closure of Community Hospitals. The Committee will consider a response from the petitioner.

PE807 The influence of supermarkets on the food chain. The Committee will consider responses from the Scottish Executive and the Office of Fair Trading.

PE875 Common good assets. The Committee will consider responses from the Accounts Commission; Audit Scotland; COSLA; Historic Scotland; Registers of Scotland; and the Minister for Finance.

PE896 Consultation before disposal of listed buildings or common land. The Committee will consider responses from Historic Scotland, the Scottish Civic Trust and the Scottish Executive.

PE886 The treatment of Alzheimer’s Disease and other forms of dementia. The Committee will consider a response from NICE.

PE862 Review of the current system for dealing with and monitoring convicted child sex offenders. The Committee will consider a response from the petitioner.

3. Report of the Public Petitions Committee Event, Dunfermline, 30 January 2006: The Committee will consider a draft report.

4. Revised Administrative Procedures for Dealing with Public Petitions: The Committee will consider a paper from the Clerk.

Waverley Railway (Scotland) Bill Committee
9th Meeting, 2006

The Committee will meet at 1.30 pm in Committee Room 5

1. Items in private: The Committee will consider whether to take items 3 and 4 in private and whether to consider its draft Consideration Stage and appropriate assessment reports in private at future meetings.

2. Approach to oral evidence on Stow station proposal: The Committee will consider a paper on its approach to oral evidence on the objection to the proposal for a station at Stow.

3. Draft Consideration Stage report: The Committee will consider a draft Consideration Stage report.

4. Draft Appropriate assessment report: The Committee will consider a draft appropriate assessment report.
