Business Bulletin No. 59/2002

Tuesday 16 April 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-2984 Ross Finnie: The Draft Air Quality (Scotland) Amendment Regulations 2002-That the Transport and Environment Committee recommends that the draft Air Quality (Scotland) Amendment Regulations 2002 be approved.

Supported by: Allan Wilson*

*S1M-2983 Mr Jim Wallace: Enterprise Bill - UK Legislation-That the Parliament agrees that provisions in the Enterprise Bill that relate to the devolved matter of receivership should be considered by the UK Parliament.

Supported by: Dr Richard Simpson*

*S1M-2982 Iain Gray: Housing Improvement Taskforce-That the Parliament welcomes the publication of the first report of the Housing Improvement Task Force Issues in Improving Quality in Private Housing as an important step in meeting the Scottish Executive's commitment, as set out in Working Together for Scotland: A Programme for Government, to address the significant problems of housing quality in the private sector stock.

Supported by: Ms Margaret Curran*

*S1M-2981 Ms Margo MacDonald: Scottish Swimming Achievements-That the Parliament congratulates Alison Sheppard on her new Commonwealth record time of 24.96 seconds for the 50 metres freestyle and records its appreciation of the role she has played in inspiring swimmers like Kirsty Balfour and Gregor Tait from the City of Edinburgh Club to win British championships, thus enhancing the current improvement in the standard of Scottish swimming.

*S1M-2980 Ms Margo MacDonald: World Curling Championships 2002-That the Parliament congratulates the Scottish Women's Curling Team on winning the World Curling Championships 2002 and thanks Jackie Lockhart, Sheila Swan, Katriona Fairweather, Anne Laird, Edith Louden and their coach, Isobel Hannen, for their superb ambassadorial work for their country and sport.

*S1M-2979# Bill Aitken: Partick Thistle FC-That the Parliament congratulates the management, staff, players and supporters of Partick Thistle FC whose tremendous efforts over the last five years have brought the club back from the verge of extinction and who have seen these efforts result in the club being promoted for the second time in two years, achieving once again the status of a Premier League side and reaching the semi-final of the Scottish Cup, thus ensuring that this well-loved Glasgow institution, which has contributed so much, over many years, has regained its natural place at the top level of Scottish football.

*S1M-2978# Karen Whitefield: Social Workers-That the Parliament appreciates the positive difference that social work services make to the lives of the people of Scotland; recognises the increasingly difficult and complex role that social workers play; further recognises the current serious problem in recruiting and retaining social workers in Scotland, particularly within certain specialisms; acknowledges the need to challenge the often negative perception of social work and to attract more people into the profession; believes in the importance of high quality generic social work training at undergraduate level with the opportunity for post-graduate specialisation, and further believes that the social work qualification should be upgraded.

*S1M-2977 Irene McGugan: Hugh Miller (1802 - 1856)-That the Parliament notes the bicentenary of one of Scotland's greatest 19th century "lads o' pairts", the geologist and writer, Hugh Miller; further notes that his fossil collection of over 6,000 specimens became the founding core of what is today's Scottish national collection in the Royal Scottish Museum in Edinburgh and that his books, such as The Old Red Sandstone, The Cruise of the Betsey, Testimony of the Rocks, Scenes and Legends of the North of Scotland and his autobiography My Schools and Schoolmasters became best-sellers in many editions, and sends good wishes to all events and celebrations in his honour taking place throughout Scotland this year and, in particular, to Hugh Miller's Cottage, his birthplace in Cromarty, cared for by the National Trust for Scotland on behalf of the nation and to the community of Cromarty for its many celebratory events.

*S1M-2976# Cathie Craigie: The Forth and Clyde and Union Canals-That the Parliament notes the successful reopening of the Forth and Clyde Canal; further notes that the reopening of the canal has given the communities on its banks a major boost; looks forward to the next step in this project which, on 24 May 2002, sees the opening of the Falkirk Wheel and the resulting link between the Forth and Clyde Canal and the Union Canal; congratulates the board of British Waterways, the Forth and Clyde Canal Society, the Scottish Inland Waterways Association, Scottish Enterprise, North Lanarkshire Council, Falkirk Council, Glasgow City Council, the City of Edinburgh Council, West Lothian Council, East Dunbartonshire Council and West Dunbartonshire Council on working in partnership in order to make this ambitious project a reality, and believes that the Scottish Executive should work together with VisitScotland and Scottish Enterprise in order to maximise the benefits of this project for the local economies, especially local tourism.
