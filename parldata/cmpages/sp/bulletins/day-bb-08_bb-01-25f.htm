Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (♦).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S3M-1220 Tom McCabe: The Haven in Blantyre—That the Parliament congratulates The Haven Caring Counselling Communication Centre in Blantyre on being awarded a Life Transitions grant of £283,677; notes that its success in receiving this award recognises the inspirational work done by the centre and the services it provides for people who are affected by life-limiting illnesses in the communities which it serves; commends all the staff of The Haven Caring Counselling Communication Centre for the care and compassion which they give to their clients during the times that they need it most, and notes that this funding will support the continuation of this crucial service for people not only in South Lanarkshire, but also in North Lanarkshire and north Glasgow.

*S3M-1219 Jackie Baillie: Dumbarton Academy—That the Parliament acknowledges that, despite the SNP promising to match Labour’s school regeneration programme "brick for brick" in April 2007, the young people of Dumbarton have been denied a new much-needed school; believes that Dumbarton Academy should become the first priority for rebuilding in West Dunbartonshire, and calls on the Scottish Government to intervene with the local authority to ensure that a new Dumbarton Academy is built by the originally proposed date of Spring 2009 in order that the full curriculum can be delivered.

*S3M-1218 Cathy Jamieson: Appointment of George Burley as the Scotland National Football Team Manager—That the Parliament welcomes the appointment of Cumnock-born George Burley as the manager of the Scottish national football team; recognises his contribution and achievements as both a player and a manager, and wishes him and the players well in the qualification campaign for the 2010 World Cup in South Africa.

*S3M-1217 Willie Coffey: Commemoration by the Bank of England of the 250th Anniversary of the Birth of Robert Burns—That the Parliament recognises the outstanding life and work of Scotland’s bard, Robert Burns; notes that the man, born in Ayrshire on 25 January 1759 to a Kincardineshire father and an Ayrshire mother, combined his inheritance of Scotland’s strong oral and musical traditions with a wide-ranging but restricted formal education; recognises his emergence as a poet through the publication of the Kilmarnock Edition of his poems on 31 July 1786 as a milestone in the development of Scottish culture; notes that, following his death in Dumfries on 21 July 1796, Burns was widely recognised as Scotland’s bard, a position he has commanded ever since; acknowledges that, through works such as A Man’s A Man for a’ That and Auld Lang Syne, Burns’ reputation and influence has spread across the globe, and, recognising the common usage of Bank of England notes in the Scottish economy and the bank’s previous commemoration of the 200th anniversary of the death of economist Adam Smith, cordially invites the Bank of England to mark the 250th anniversary of the birth of Robert Burns through the issuing of suitable commemorative notes.

*S3M-1214 Willie Coffey: Commemoration by the Scottish Clearing Banks of the 250th Anniversary of the Birth of Robert Burns—That the Parliament recognises the outstanding life and work of Scotland’s bard, Robert Burns; notes that the man, born in Ayrshire on 25 January 1759 to a Kincardineshire father and an Ayrshire mother, combined his inheritance of Scotland’s strong oral and musical traditions with a wide-ranging but restricted formal education; recognises his emergence as a poet through the publication of the Kilmarnock Edition of his poems on 31 July 1786 as a milestone in the development of Scottish culture; notes that, following his death in Dumfries on 21 July 1796, Burns was widely recognised as Scotland’s bard, a position he has commanded ever since; acknowledges that, through works such as A Man’s A Man for a’ That and Auld Lang Syne, Burns’ reputation and influence has spread across the globe, and calls on each of the note-issuing Scottish clearing banks to mark the 250th anniversary of the birth of Robert Burns through the issuing of suitable commemorative notes.

*S3M-1213♦ Elaine Murray: Marketing and Communications Sector in Scotland—That the Parliament notes that the marketing and communications sector in Scotland is worth £350 million a year and employs around 40,000 people, making the industry an important service sector for Scotland, including in rural areas such as Dumfries and Galloway; welcomes the presence of the Scottish Marketing Communications Action Group in the Parliament on 19 February 2008; notes that the group is requesting the Scottish Government to enter into a partnership to work with the industry to build on its strengths and agrees that work should continue between the government and the industry to increase the income retained by businesses in Scotland and to increase the skills within the sector, generating a potential increase in income of over £100 million; furthermore recognises that marketing and communications, as part of Scotland’s service sector, is an essential element in driving Scotland’s business growth and attractiveness to inward investors, and therefore pledges to do what it can to assist in this initiative.

*S3M-1212 Aileen Campbell: Townhead Cafe in Biggar Wins UK Award—That the Parliament congratulates Biggar’s Townhead Cafe for winning the finals of the UK Fish and Chip Shop of the Year competition; notes that the last time a Scottish champion won the award was 10 years ago; further notes that, for the first time in the competition’s history, entrants were judged on their commitment to building a sustainable future for the industry, and congratulates owners Sandra and Gordon Hillan for their accomplishment and wishes them every success in the future.

S3M-1207 Ian McKee: Loanhead Community Learning Centre—That the Parliament congratulates Loanhead Community Learning Centre (LCLC), Midlothian, on winning the International Play Association’s Right to Play Award 2008 at an awards ceremony in Hong Kong; applauds the hard work done by staff and volunteers in providing educational, social welfare and recreational opportunities for over 1,000 people who use the centre every week; notes the success of the LCLC in providing the focal point to bring people together from new housing schemes, and wishes it every success in continuing to provide this service well into the future.

Supported by: Roseanna Cunningham*, Kenneth Gibson*, Joe FitzPatrick*, Stuart McMillan*, Christine Grahame*, George Foulkes*, Aileen Campbell*, Jamie Stone*, Bashir Ahmad*, Bill Kidd*, Angela Constance*

*S3M-1207.1 George Foulkes: Loanhead Community Learning Centre—As an amendment to motion (S3M-1207) in the name of Ian McKee, insert at end "recognises the value which a Labour-led Midlothian Council places on education in stark contrast to the SNP/Liberal Democrat administration in the City of Edinburgh Council; condemns the City of Edinburgh Council for its proposed cuts to the community and learning development budget; is concerned that a review designed to look at organisational efficiencies will ultimately lead to frontline cuts in services and potential job losses, and supports all 44 community centres in the great work that they do across the city of Edinburgh."
