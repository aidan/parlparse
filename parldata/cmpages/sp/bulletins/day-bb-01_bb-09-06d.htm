Business Bulletin No. 124/2001

Thursday 6 September 2001

Section D : Oral Questions selected for answer on 13 September 2001
  

Question Time

1. Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive what proportion of Scottish Enterprise's current budget is spent specifically on social justice issues and what steps it is taking to ensure that Scottish Enterprise is involved in supporting the Executive's commitments in this area. (S1O-3766)

2. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to encourage young people to take up opportunities available through the European Union's ERASMUS, LEONARDO and European Voluntary Service education and training programmes. (S1O-3742)

3. Elaine Thomson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to support the oil and gas industry to ensure that it continues to play an integral part in the economy. (S1O-3744)

4. Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in encouraging local authorities to adopt the guidelines under the Scottish Diet Action Plan on nutritional standards in school meals. (S1O-3768)

5. Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made on simplifying the national qualifications framework. (S1O-3767)

6. Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress the Crown Office and the Association of Chief Police Officers in Scotland have made in setting up their joint strategy for the investigation and prosecution of sexual exploitation of children on the internet. (S1O-3738)

7. Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-15533 by Mr Jim Wallace on 14 June 2001, what heraldic conventions the flags flown from its buildings observe and whether any heraldic definition of colours for use in the national flag of Scotland exists. (S1O-3759)

8. Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met representatives of the Crown Estate and what matters were discussed. (S1O-3748)

9. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive who is liable for damage to organic farms and crops resulting from field trials of GM crops. (S1O-3753)

10. Bill Aitken: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the present children's hearing system is effective in respect of young offenders. (S1O-3735)

11. Janis Hughes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether, as part of its review of cities, it intends to redraw local authority boundaries. (S1O-3765)

12. Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive whether prescribing practices will be addressed in developments in clinical governance. (S1O-3762)

13. Bristow Muldoon: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will make an announcement on the future of the Scottish Qualifications Authority. (S1O-3749)

14. David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to measure the continuing economic impact of the foot-and-mouth disease outbreak in the south of Scotland. (S1O-3760)

15. Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it plans to have with local authorities regarding possible changes to local authority boundaries. (S1O-3733)

16. Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to improve public transport provision in Clydesdale. (S1O-3764)

17. Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration it has given to allowing Glasgow City Council to retain all business rates collected in the city. (S1O-3747)

18. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to review the effectiveness and value for money of VisitScotland's Ossian website. (S1O-3763)

19. Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to support the Angus economy. (S1O-3741)

20. Mr Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to tackle coronary heart disease. (S1O-3758)

21. Ian Jenkins: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the current formula used to fund further education colleges has any adverse effect on those colleges with a limited pool of potential students. (S1O-3743)

22. Mr John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will publish the site-specific risk assessment, the appropriate assessment and all information that pertains to its recent decision to grant consent to the release of genetically-modified oilseed rape at Roskill Farm, Munlochy, Ross-shire. (S1O-3761)

23. Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration has been given to the upgrading of the A9 from Helmsdale to Ord of Caithness during the development of its transport delivery plan. (S1O-3754)

24. Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it envisages any changes in the financial assistance available to small businesses following its recent impact study of the effect of the foot-and-mouth disease outbreak. (S1O-3769)

25. Mr Keith Harding: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Minister for Finance and Local Government last met CoSLA, what issues were discussed and when he next plans to meet CoSLA. (S1O-3756)

26. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met representatives of Scottish Borders Council and what issues were discussed. (S1O-3737)

27. Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is addressing the childcare needs of lone parents seeking to attend college or university. (S1O-3752)

28. Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive what further funding will be made available to local authorities to ensure that the Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network guidelines on attention deficit and hyperkinetic disorders in children and young people are fully implemented. (S1O-3740)

29. Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in analysing the 3,500 representations received during its consultation on the draft Land Reform (Scotland) Bill. R (S1O-3734)

30. Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to re-establish a war crimes unit. (S1O-3739)
