Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-2525 Richard Baker: Davidson Mill, Aberdeen—That the Parliament notes with deep regret and concern the announcement by BPB Paperboard of the proposed closure of the Davidson Mill in Aberdeen; notes the significant contribution that the mill has made to employment and the economy in north-east Scotland, currently employing 276 people; calls for a full consultation between the management of BPB Paperboard and trade union representatives at the mill, and urges all relevant local agencies to take the appropriate actions to support staff at the mill at this difficult time and to take action to address the impact of the proposed closure on the local economy.

*S2M-2524# Elaine Smith: International Women’s Day, 8 March 2005—That the Parliament notes the significance of 8 March 2005 as International Women’s Day; endorses the role which this day plays in recognising, promoting and celebrating women’s issues world-wide; congratulates the many groups and organisations who strive to create a fairer society for women here in Scotland; recognises, in particular, the work of the Equal Opportunities Commission and its highly-successful Pregnant and Productive campaign which aims to highlight the problems that pregnant woman encounter in the workplace and ensure that pregnant women and their employers are fully aware of their rights and responsibilities under existing sexual discrimination laws; notes that an estimated 30,000 working women leave their jobs each year because of pregnancy discrimination; considers that the Scottish Parliament and the Scottish Executive can play a key role in improving the situation in Scotland, and believes that continual support should be given to the development of programmes that educate women and employers on maternity-related issues, including their legal rights.

*S2M-2523 Shiona Baird: Developing Better Legal Responses to Rape—That the Parliament welcomes the many activities and events organised as part of the International Women’s Day celebrations in 2005; commends the work over the last 30 years and more by the rape crisis movement in Scotland, which has helped dramatically to change attitudes towards rape and sexual abuse by raising public awareness about how widespread sexual violence is and contributing to changes, such as improvements to the way the health service and the criminal justice system respond; is appalled that, despite the successes that have been achieved, Scotland still has one of the lowest conviction rates for rape in Europe; recognises that two reviews have been established in Scotland, in 2003 and 2004, which intend to develop better legal responses to rape, and urges the Scottish Executive to act promptly on the reviews’ findings in order to improve the criminal justice system for survivors of rape and sexual abuse.

*S2M-2522# Mark Ballard: Wipe Out Debt—That the Parliament notes the Wipe Out Debt 2005 petition and its call for full cancellation of the unpayable debts of the world’s poorest countries by fair and transparent means; further notes that, despite the work of campaigners and politicians worldwide, 88% of the debts of the poorest countries remain uncancelled; further notes that full cancellation of debt, when allied to reform of unfair trade rules, would act to lift millions of the world’s poorest people out of poverty, and believes that the G8 summit at Gleneagles presents an excellent opportunity to encourage the governments of rich countries to make good on their promises and deliver the resources needed to make poverty history.

*S2M-2521 Donald Gorrie: First-time House Purchasers—That the Parliament urges the Scottish Executive to press the Chancellor of the Exchequer to raise the threshold at which house-buyers have to pay stamp duty from £60,000, the figure that it has been since 1993, to the equivalent related figure for current house prices, estimated to be £156,000; notes that 59% of Scottish first-time buyers now pay stamp duty compared to virtually none in the 1990s, and believes that this change would help new first-time buyers to enter the housing market.

*S2M-2520# Bristow Muldoon: Outsourcing of Rail Call Centre Work from Dingwall and Edinburgh—That the Parliament condemns the proposed outsourcing from the United Kingdom, and outwith the European Union, of call centre work at Dingwall and Edinburgh carried out on behalf of Virgin Trains through Trainline and Vertex, putting in jeopardy hundreds of jobs; notes that Virgin Trains is the main shareholder of Trainline, that Virgin Trains received £578 million in subsidies from the taxpayer in 2003-04 and that the Dingwall call centre also received taxpayers’ support to be established through Highlands and Islands Enterprise; supports the campaign of the transport union, the Transport Salaried Staffs’ Association, on behalf of the call centre workers, and therefore considers that the Scottish Executive should use its influence directly, and through the UK Government, to encourage the three companies to reverse the decision to transfer jobs out of the United Kingdom that are supported by taxpayers’ money.

Supported by: Dr Elaine Murray*, Pauline McNeill*, Elaine Smith*, Bill Butler*, Tommy Sheridan*, Mike Pringle*, Eleanor Scott*, Mark Ballard*, Mr Kenneth Macintosh*, Alex Neil*, Robert Brown*, Mr Kenny MacAskill*, Scott Barrie*, Fiona Hyslop*

*S2M-2515.1 Tommy Sheridan: Profits of Royal Bank of Scotland Group and HBOS plc—As an amendment to motion (S2M-2515) in the name of Murdo Fraser, leave out from "management and staff" to end and insert "the improved performance and profit generation of these two banks is due to the dedication and commitment of employees to deliver a service to the public, despite damaging cutbacks in employee numbers and conditions, and believes that these banks and the financial industry as a whole should be publicly-owned and democratically-controlled so that surpluses generated can be ethically invested at home and overseas, pension funds can be used productively and guarantee proper returns to provide security for all pensioners and society can benefit from social and health investment instead of the tiny minority who currently receive the majority of the profits through dividend payments and obscene salaries, bonuses and pensions."

Supported by: Frances Curran*

*S2M-2471.1 Tommy Sheridan: Debt Relief—As an amendment to motion (S2M-2471) in the name of Murdo Fraser, leave out from "have voiced a commitment" to end and insert "are committed to the promotion of free markets, privatisation, anti-trade union practices and protection of multi-national corporations who plunder and rape resources in pursuit of maximum profits with wanton disregard to individual nation’s natural mineral wealth and other resources, and believes that the IMF and the World Bank represent the rich and powerful across the world, present a real obstacle to tackling world poverty and should be completely restructured or disbanded."

*S2M-2352.1 Tommy Sheridan: Charities and Trustee Investment (Scotland) Bill—As an amendment to motion (S2M-2352) in the name of Malcolm Chisholm, insert at end "but, in so doing, supports the removal of private and other fee-paying schools from the artificial cover of charitable status which saves these elite institutions thousands of pounds in various taxes every year and calls on the Scottish Executive to bring forward amendments during Stage 2 of the Bill to achieve this objective whilst maintaining the status of independent special schools."

Supported by: Colin Fox*
