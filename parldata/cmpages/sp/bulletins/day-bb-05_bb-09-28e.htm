Section E – Written questions lodged on 27 September 2005

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-19499 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will begin work on its protocol with the Home Office on the removal of asylum seekers.

S2W-19500 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to complete work on its protocol with the Home Office on the removal of asylum seekers.

S2W-19501 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive which ministers will lead negotiations with the Home Office in respect of the protocol on the removal of asylum seekers.

S2W-19502 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is bound by the terms of the United Nations Convention on the Rights of the Child.

S2W-19503 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether Dundee Dental Hospital has a resident consultant in oral medicine; if not, for how long it has not had one and when one will be appointed.

S2W-19504 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that the facilities at the Glasgow Dental School are fit for purpose and whether there are any plans to upgrade student and staff facilities.

S2W-19505 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive whether NHS boards are meeting the recommended response time of 60 minutes to administer thrombolytics for those suffering heart attacks and what strategies are in place to help meet this response time in rural areas.

S2W-19506 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has met its 2005 targets on (a) physical activity, (b) diet and (c) alcohol.

S2W-19507 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to encourage a higher uptake of the use of statins for those who could benefit from treatment but are not yet accessing the medicines.

S2W-19508 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what stage discussions relating to increasing the NHS pension age from 60 to 65 have reached.

S2W-19509 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what its estimate is of the number of environmental health officers required in each local authority area to police the smoking restrictions provided for by the Smoking, Health and Social Care (Scotland) Act 2005 and whether it considers that the current number of officers is sufficient to achieve a satisfactory level of policing.

S2W-19510 Janis Hughes: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it is offering to local drugs forums in communities throughout Scotland.

S2W-19511Δ Christine May: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will publish the key performance targets set for Historic Scotland for 2005-06.

S2W-19512 Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive what the reasons are for the Forestry Commission’s plans to relocate administrative and training operations from Ae, in Dumfries and Galloway, to Edinburgh at a time when the Executive has announced its commitment to decentralising posts away from Edinburgh.

S2W-19513 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to encourage Scots, in particular schoolchildren, to learn a second language.

S2W-19514 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has set any targets for Scotland’s population to speak a second language.

S2W-19515 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has carried out a study to ascertain the percentage of Scots who can speak a second language; if so, what the results were and, if not, whether it will do so.

S2W-19516 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what the financial benefits to Scotland are of having more citizens who have bilingual or multilingual skills.

S2W-19517 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures and actions it has implemented to ensure that victims of domestic violence are at less risk of being abused again.

S2W-19518 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to develop a National Family Strategy for Scotland, as referred to in the Equal Opportunities Commission’s report, Britain’s Hidden Brain Drain.

S2W-19519 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans ministers have to extend the provisions of Part 2 of the Land Reform (Scotland) Act 2003 to urban areas with populations of over 10,000 and whether it will provide details and any estimated timescale.

S2W-19520 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how much compensation was paid to landowners by Scottish Water in each of the last three years, broken down by reason for compensation where appropriate, such as that arising from the installation of new water and sewerage systems, and showing year-on-year percentage increases.

S2W-19521 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how much compensation was paid to landowners by it in each of the last three years, broken down by reason for compensation where appropriate, such as that arising from the building and improvement of the trunk road network, and showing year-on-year percentage increases.

S2W-19522 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how much compensation was paid to landowners by local authorities in each of the last three years, broken down by reason for compensation where appropriate, such as that arising from the building and improvement of the road network, and showing year-on-year percentage increases.

S2W-19523 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average price paid by Communities Scotland for housing plots was in each of the last five years, showing year-on-year percentage increases.

S2W-19524 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what proportion of Communities Scotland’s budget has been used for the purpose of purchasing land in each of the last five years, showing the amount in each case and broken down by category where appropriate.

S2W-19525 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average cost of, and increase in, land prices of housing plots funded through the rural home ownership scheme was in each of the last five years.

S2W-19526 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what the design capacity is of each prison.

S2W-19527 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what the maximum number of prisoners is that each prison is contracted to take under the new contracts introduced this year by the Scottish Prison Service.
