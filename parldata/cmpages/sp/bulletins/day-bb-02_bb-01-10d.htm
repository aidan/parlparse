BUSINESS BULLETIN No. 4/2002

Thursday 10 January 2002

Section D : Oral Questions selected for answer on 17 January 2002
  

Question Time

1. Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the pension arrangements currently in place for former First Ministers and Scottish Ministers. (S1O-4439)

2. Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there will be any reform of the Less Favoured Area Support Scheme for 2003 and whether any such reform will involve crofters, farmers and local authorities. (S1O-4451)

3. Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with The Highland Council about the recent winter maintenance of non-trunk roads. (S1O-4427)

4. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive what action has been taken during the last year to address any illegal landings and processing of fish. (S1O-4458)

5. John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with The Highland Council with a view to finding solutions to closures of the A890 between Stromeferry and Achintee owing to landslides. (S1O-4430)

6. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making in addressing waiting and discharge times in the NHS. (S1O-4443)

7. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to help Scotland prepare for the euro. (S1O-4450)

8. Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in its negotiations with Her Majesty's Government in respect of continuing the payment of Attendance Allowance to those people who will become eligible for free personal care in April 2002. (S1O-4423)

9. Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will next meet representatives of the Scottish Football Association and what issues will be discussed. (S1O-4431)

10. Mr Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking in order to reduce waiting times for out-patient appointments at diabetic clinics. (S1O-4466)

11. Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to support economic regeneration in Scotland in 2002. (S1O-4463)

12. Bristow Muldoon: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to assist disabled people to live independently. (S1O-4446)

13. Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to consult on improving educational provision for children with special needs. (S1O-4434)

14. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in improving adoption services. (S1O-4432)

15. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made by the Child Health Support Group. (S1O-4447)

16. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps have been taken to measure the performance of BEAR Scotland Ltd in response to the recent spate of winter weather. (S1O-4422)

17. Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has agreed to write off the capital housing debt of each local authority actively considering whole stock transfer. (S1O-4428)

18. Brian Fitzpatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive what proposals it has in respect of improving the inspection of child care facilities. (S1O-4444)

19. Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to improve cancer services for patients in Eastwood. (S1O-4465)

20. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is assisting adult education projects. (S1O-4442)

21. Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with West of Scotland Water about the provision of sewage treatment in the south west of Scotland. (S1O-4461)

22. Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to reduce the incidence of chlamydia among young people. (S1O-4421)

23. Janis Hughes: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to improve out-of-school care provision. (S1O-4449)

24. David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the maintenance of trunk roads in the South of Scotland by Amey Highways Ltd under recent winter conditions has been adequate. (S1O-4435)

25. Elaine Thomson: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are being taken in order to develop the youth justice system, with particular regard to repeat offenders. (S1O-4464)

26. Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is undertaking in order to improve children's services. (S1O-4441)

27. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking in order to protect children using the internet. (S1O-4453)

28. George Lyon: To ask the Scottish Executive what powers local authorities have to ensure that utilities companies, such as West of Scotland Water, restore road surfaces after being dug up for installation and maintenance purposes. (S1O-4462)

29. Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive what its priorities are regarding fire safety. (S1O-4429)

30. Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether Scotland's current economic growth performance is adequate. (S1O-4424)
