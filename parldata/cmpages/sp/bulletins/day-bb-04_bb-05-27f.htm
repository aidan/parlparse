Business Bulletin No. 91 / 2004

Thursday 27th May 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-1368 Mike Watson: Incitement to Racial Hatred-That the Parliament notes with disgust the British National Party's election leaflet for the European Parliament elections, delivered to every household in Scotland; is appalled that its xenophobic content is designed to stir up racial hatred and division; further notes that the Scottish Refugee Council has issued a detailed rebuttal of the assertions which it contains, and urges the Lord Advocate to examine the content of the leaflet with a view to bringing a prosecution under the appropriate legislation.

*S2M-1367 Chris Ballance: Joint ScotRail and FirstBus Initiative-That the Parliament welcomes the joint initiative by ScotRail and FirstBus to provide a connecting bus and rail service with through-ticketing between Haddington and Edinburgh; further welcomes the initial special offer of free travel for a trial period between 24 May and 20 June 2004, and recommends that other bus companies enter into similar arrangements with ScotRail and the new rail franchise holder.

*S2M-1366 Frances Curran: Racist Election Literature-That the Parliament notes that the Commission for Racial Equality believes that the British National Party's current European election leaflet amounts to an incitement to racial hatred which is an offence under the Public Order Act 1986; is concerned that tens of thousands of pounds of public money have been used to deliver this vile, racist message of hatred to hundreds of thousands of homes across Scotland; further notes that Royal Mail employees, who have objected to delivering the leaflet, have been threatened with disciplinary action and that, so far, despite dozens of complaints by members of the public, the police have failed to bring a prosecution; believes that democratic principles are undermined when minority ethnic communities are placed in a state of fear by literature of this nature, and calls on the Scottish Executive's Law Officers to urgently investigate the possibility of prosecuting the British National Party for public order offences.

Supported by: Carolyn Leckie*, John Swinburne*, Campbell Martin*, Tommy Sheridan*

*S2M-1365 Ms Rosemary Byrne: Depleted Uranium-That the Parliament congratulates Dr Helen Caldicott, a paediatrician and president of the American Nuclear Policy Research Institute on her calls for the US military to clean up the depleted uranium scattered across Iraq to prevent future health problems such as cancer and birth defects; notes that the depleted uranium ammunition currently being used by the US military could seriously harm both service personnel and civilians living in the area for decades to come; further notes Dr Caldicott's calls for the US Government to compensate people with cancer related to depleted uranium, and calls on the Scottish Executive to put pressure upon the Ministry of Defence to stop the testing of depleted uranium at Dundrennan as a matter of urgency, undertake a full health investigation into the local area and fully compensate those individuals whose health has been affected by this substance.

*S2M-1364 Ms Rosemary Byrne: Tweed Forum-That the Parliament congratulates the Tweed Forum on its Bridging the Border Education Project, a project that helps young people to enjoy and understand their natural and cultural heritage; notes that the work of the forum involves educational activities and events for young people, youth leaders and teachers; further notes the innovative way in which this project combines environmental awareness with educational activities, and urges the Scottish Executive to support all similar projects that have both educational and emotional benefits for youth leaders and young people.

*S2M-1363 David McLetchie: Sale of Alcohol at Murrayfield Stadium-That the Parliament notes that the sale of alcohol within Murrayfield Stadium, Edinburgh was licensed on the occasion of the BT Cup Finals on 1 May 2004 and the Scotland versus Barbarians match on 22 May 2004 and that both events were well conducted and gave rise to no problems of crowd disorder; believes that international rugby union matches played at Murrayfield Stadium should cease to be designated sporting events for the purposes of the Criminal Law (Consolidation) (Scotland) Act 1995, and calls on the Scottish Executive to bring forward amending legislation to that effect.

Supported by: Mike Pringle*, Tricia Marwick*, David Mundell*, Murdo Fraser*, Mr Kenny MacAskill*, Bill Aitken*, Mr Brian Monteith*, Margaret Mitchell*, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton*

S2M-1354# Rob Gibson: Local Healthy Food for Highland Schools-That the Parliament congratulates Highland Council for its decision to source locally as much of its £2.5 million contracts for foodstuffs, including fresh fruit and vegetables, as possible, to promote food education for children and parents alike; welcomes the decision of the council's Education, Culture and Sport Committee to raise awareness among local suppliers whose successful tenders included the supply of organic fruit and vegetables; believes that the proposed pilot in Strathpeffer Primary School based on the principles of "Food for Life" is a most welcome step that sets a good example by insisting that 50% or more food is sourced locally, with ingredients to include 30% organic and 75% unprocessed foodstuffs, and praises the council's public procurement policy as part of major moves towards the aim of maximum local sourcing to provide best quality school meals that can be produced within budget constraints.
