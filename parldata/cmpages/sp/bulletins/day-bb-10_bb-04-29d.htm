Business Bulletin No. 71/2010: Thursday 29 April 2010
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 5 May 2010

Question Time

General

1. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive, following the roundtable meeting on 29 March 2010, what action it is undertaking in partnership with the City of Edinburgh Council, local agencies and local communities to tackle antisocial behaviour and community safety issues arising from short-term holiday lets. (S3O-10370)

2. Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive which local authorities have housing stock that it categorises as being at high risk of not achieving the Scottish Housing Quality Standard by 2015 and what investment per housing unit is required to deliver compliance of this housing stock. (S3O-10395)

3. John Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking regarding the amount of derelict and vacant urban land throughout Scotland. (S3O-10355)

4. James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to monitor the expenditure of the Scottish Futures Trust. (S3O-10383)

5. Charlie Gordon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to rule out delaying the completion of the M74 Junction 5 Raith Scheme until 2013-14. (S3O-10380)

6. John Park: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to assist unemployed people in Mid Scotland and Fife. (S3O-10399)

7. Aileen Campbell: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to tackle the effects of the recession. (S3O-10408)

8. Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to tackle fuel poverty in the year ahead. (S3O-10426)

9. Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive when it plans to name its proposed minimum unit price for alcohol. (S3O-10367)

10. Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what the benefits will be for employment as a result of the announcement of a £3.2 million new freight facilities grant to Montrose Port Authority. (S3O-10430)

Rural Affairs and the Environment

1. Shirley-Anne Somerville: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it is providing to the Keep Scotland Beautiful National Spring Clean campaign. (S3O-10427)

2. Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure best practice in deer management. (S3O-10413)

3. Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what its environmental policy is on the weekly collection of household waste. (S3O-10390)

4. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what alternative means of funding are available to subsidise nurseries to grow bog myrtle plants or fund growers to purchase plants, given that funding towards the purchasing of such plants is not eligible under the Rural Priorities scheme. (S3O-10356)

5. Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making on meeting its targets on waste management. (S3O-10392)

6. Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the Scottish Environment Protection Agency regarding a review of environmental protection legislation. (S3O-10434)

7. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration has been given to planning and flood risk as a consequence of the Flood Risk Management (Scotland) Act 2009. (S3O-10375)

8. Alison McInnes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that Scotland will meet its European air quality targets this year. (S3O-10438)

9. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects the restructuring of Scottish Environment Protection Agency’s environmental science services to be completed. (S3O-10440)

10. Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to address the problem of flood damage to homes in the Falkirk Council area. (S3O-10363)

Justice and Law Officers

1. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to reduce the incidence of drug taking in prisons. (S3O-10443)

2. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will support the Kirkcaldy Business Improvement District’s aims of creating a safe and welcoming town centre and reducing crime through collaboration with local police and joint initiatives such as Pubwatch, Radio Link and Child-Safe. (S3O-10386)

3. Bob Doris: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on tackling knife crime. (S3O-10412)

4. Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to tackle racially aggravated crime. (S3O-10436)

5. Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to tackle knife crime in Grampian. (S3O-10368)

6. Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Cabinet Secretary for Justice last met representatives of Lothian and Borders Police. (S3O-10371)

7. Stuart McMillan: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of any financial constraints expected to be imposed by the next UK Government, what action it is taking to make sure that Scotland’s streets remain safe. (S3O-10425)

8. Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Minister for Community Safety monitors how many people have been referred for methadone and detox-based rehabilitation by the alcohol and drug partnerships and have sought such treatment. (S3O-10357)

9. Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what its response is to the recent HM Inspectorate of Prisons report on Perth Prison. (S3O-10359)

10. Ian McKee: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidelines the law officers will provide regarding the potential prosecution of a person assisting another to end his or her life. (S3O-10423)
