BUSINESS BULLETIN 76 / 2001

Friday 11 May 2001

Section E - Written questions lodged on 10 May 2001

 

 

S1W-15712 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many families with children presented themselves as homeless to local authorities in each year from 1996-97 to 2000-01.

S1W-15713 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many homeless families with children were accommodated in permanent accommodation under (a) section 31 of the Housing (Scotland) Act 1987, (b) section 22 of the Children (Scotland) Act 1995 and (c) section 25 of the Children (Scotland) Act 1995 in each year from 1996-97 to 2000-01.

S1W-15714 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many homeless families with children were deemed intentionally homeless by local authorities and not accommodated in permanent accommodation in each year from 1996-97 to 2000-01.

S1W-15715 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what resources were made available to assist local authorities to provide accommodation for homeless families with children under the Children (Scotland) Act 1995 in each year since the Act’s introduction.

S1W-15716 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many modern apprenticeship schemes were in operation in each year since 1997 in each local authority area.

S1W-15717 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will provide the information sought by Iain McKie on behalf of Shirley McKie, most recently in Mr McKie’s letter of 6 May 2001 to the Minister for Justice.

S1W-15718 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to improve welfare standards in the pig industry.

S1W-15719 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance has been issued on the use of the brain cancer drug temozolomide.

S1W-15720 Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average period is (a) between a child’s parents or school requesting remedial help for the child and an assessment being made by an educational psychologist and (b) from a child being diagnosed as requiring remedial help to actually receiving such help, broken down by local authority.

S1W-15721 Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-10039 by Iain Gray on 30 October 2000, what progress has been made in ensuring that people with a disability continue to have access to the support and advisory services provided by Disability Scotland.

S1W-15722 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will detail (a) the targets set for the ratio between the projected elderly population and available sheltered and very sheltered housing and any changes made to these targets, (b) the level of borrowing consent for sheltered and very sheltered housing per 1,000 elderly population and (c) the ratio between borrowing consent and local government funding for sheltered housing funding, in (i) 1999-2000, (ii) 2000-01 and (iii) 2001-02, broken down by local authority.

S1W-15723 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will detail (a) the level of New Housing Partnership funding allocated, (b) any changes made to these allocations and (c) actual or projected underspend against those allocations in (i) 1999-2000, (ii) 2000-01 and (iii) 2001-02, both overall and broken down by local authority.

S1W-15724 Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive how much of the Barnett Formula increase in funding to the Scottish Departmental Expenditure Limits in the current financial year was a result of increases in comparable expenditure on the provision of water services in England and Wales.

S1W-15725 Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the total planned and actual expenditure on water services was in each year since 1990 in both cash and real terms, broken down by local authority and water authority area.

S1W-15726 Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many contracts for goods and services were agreed between it and private contractors who were (a) based in Scotland and (b) located outside Scotland in each year since 1999.

S1W-15727 Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many contracts for goods and services were agreed between its associated departments and executive agencies and private contractors who were (a) based in Scotland and (b) located outside Scotland in each year since 1999.

S1W-15728 Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what proportion of the overall number of UK university students are educated at universities in Scotland.

S1W-15729 Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many hours of community service sentences were undertaken in the latest year for which figures are available.

S1W-15730 Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will detail any unlimited contingent liabilities entered into by it, its associated departments and executive agencies, specifying the amounts involved in each case.

S1W-15731 Mr Keith Harding: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will reconsider its decision not to agree to Fife Council’s request for new signposting on the A985 to the historic villages of Limekilns and Charlestown.

S1W-15732 Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many permanently employed teachers there are in (a) primary and (b) secondary schools.

S1W-15733 Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many male teachers there are in (a) primary and (b) secondary schools.

S1W-15734 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to review the Grant for Rent Ownership scheme following the recent European decision on a similar English scheme.

S1W-15735 Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making in identifying schools where there is little or no provision of cycle training, and what steps it will take to encourage cycle training in such schools.

S1W-15736 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress the Lord Advocate is making regarding possible prosecutions consequent to the complaints against the Scottish Criminal Records Office by Ms Shirley McKie.

S1W-15737 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive which public body has legal responsibility for the acts and omissions of the Scottish Criminal Records Office in relation to complaints and civil actions against (a) civilian employees and (b) police employees.

S1W-15738 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are planned to increase public transport access to Edinburgh from towns and villages which do not have connections.

S1W-15739 Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has for the National Transport Forum for Scotland and its subgroups and when the forum or its subgroups will next meet.

S1W-15740 Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has allocated funding for future work by the National Transport Forum for Scotland or its subgroups.

S1W-15741 Mr Frank McAveety: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Minister for Education, Europe and External Affairs will next make a report to the Parliament on the Scottish Qualifications Authority’s progress in delivering the Diet of examinations for 2001.

S1W-15742 Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will recommend that professional training for physiotherapists includes a module on hypermobility syndromes.

S1W-15743 Mr Keith Harding: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to reverse any decline in the bird population.
