BUSINESS BULLETIN 79/1999

Thursday 28 October 1999

Section D –  Oral questions selected for answer on 4 November 1999

   

Question Time

1. Bill Aitken: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration it has given to restricting the terms of Deeds of Conditions whereby the appointment of a named property manager cannot be made mandatory. (S1O-527)

 

2. Dorothy-Grace Elder: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has made or will make representations to Her Majesty’s Government regarding the immediate removal of the Trident installation in Scotland. (S1O-495)

 

3. Elaine Thomson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to review the current planning regulations with respect to landfill sites and their proximity to residential accommodation. (S1O-543)

 

4. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many time off in lieu hours were due to Scottish Prison Service officers as at 1 September 1999 or the latest date for which figures are available. (S1O-511)

 

5. Mr Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will identify the factors that have resulted in the improvements in survival of breast cancer patients in Scotland. (S1O-522)

 

6. Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with pig meat retailers about the labelling of pig meat products. (S1O-541)

 

7. Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive how many ambulances are provided in Angus between the hours of 6.00pm and 8.00am. (S1O-513)

 

8. Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what further consideration it is giving to the relocation of public service jobs, particularly in relation to departments or agencies with rural responsibilities, to the Scottish Highlands. (S1O-519)

 

9. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will call for an independent consultant to carry out a design survey regarding the proposal to reduce the size of the Ambulatory Care and Diagnostic Unit at Stobhill Hospital. (S1O-521)

 

10. Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to increase expenditure on policing in financial year 2000-2001. (S1O-529)

 

11. Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what it considers to be the economic contribution to the economy of the pig industry. (S1O-542)

 

12. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make representations to Her Majesty’s Government to encourage the National Lottery Board to expedite the release of the £17.25 million cancer initiative funding for Scotland from the New Opportunity Fund. (S1O-516)

 

13. Mr John Swinney: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make a statement on the future of the neurosurgical unit at Ninewells Hospital, Dundee. (S1O-528)

 

14. Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will outline the role, remit and objectives of the new steering group set up to oversee the next phases of work on stock transfer of Glasgow council housing. (S1O-547)

 

15. Miss Annabel Goldie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to launch an initiative to raise the profile of blood donation in Scotland given that only 5 per cent of the Scottish population currently donates blood. (S1O-509)

 

16. Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-385 by the Minister for Transport and Environment on 9 August 1999, whether it has yet received advice from its nature conservation advisers and whether Scottish Natural Heritage has any plans to designate the seas around Fair Isle as a Marine Special Protection Area under the Birds Directive. (S1O-523)

 

17. Mr Murray Tosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what proposals it has to encourage householders to minimise their output of domestic waste and to encourage waste recycling. (S1O-498)

 

18. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to write to local authorities and other public authorities highlighting the quality of pig meat produced in Scotland and urging them to support the industry by sourcing their orders from local producers. (S1O-510)

 

19. Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive how it envisages the work of organisations offering a holistic advice service to young people, such as ‘Off the Record’ in Stirling and ‘The Corner’ in Dundee, being supported in their pioneering work to provide a confidential, accessible and multi-agency approach to help young people deal with increasing drug related problems. (S1O-502)

 

20. Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it regards recent school closures to be as a result of the current local government spending allocation. (S1O-546)

 

21. Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive what preliminary discussions have been held with the designated assemblies and Her Majesty’s Government concerning the structure, functions and membership of the proposed Council of the Isles. (S1O-533)

 

22. Bristow Muldoon: To ask the Scottish Executive what role it envisages the establishment of Scotland House playing in promoting the interests of Scotland within Europe. (S1O-526)

 

23. Cathie Craigie: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made to secure a Scotland wide concessionary transport scheme for pensioners. (S1O-530)

 

24. Ms Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive what provision is being made to ensure that professional interpreting services are provided in all cases in the Scottish criminal justice system where an accused person cannot understand or speak the language used in court. (S1O-524)

 

25. Janis Hughes: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has had from the business community in Glasgow Rutherglen constituency regarding the proposed M74 extension. (S1O-544)

 

26. Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the streetlighting in Greengairs is currently in a state of repair which meets the standards required by Scotland’s local authorities. (S1O-500)

 

27. Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the statement by Sam Galbraith on 22 September 1999, when it intends to bring forward proposals to remove the statutory basis of the Scottish Joint Negotiating Committee. (S1O-505)

 

28. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with Her Majesty’s Government to ensure that the two European Football Championship play-offs proceed without disorder, both inside the stadiums and elsewhere. (S1O-532)

 

29. Ms Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive how many entrants there are in the current academic year for the Scottish Universities’ Speech and Language Therapy degree courses. (S1O-520)

 

30. Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive how it proposes to address the needs of young carers in Scotland. (S1O-548)

 

 

Open Question Time

1. Mr Alex Salmond: To ask the Scottish Executive when the First Minister last met with the Secretary of State for Scotland and what issues they discussed. (S1O-517)

 

2. David McLetchie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will outline its law and order policy priorities. (S1O-512)

 

3. Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is promoting joint working by local government, the National Health Service and Scottish Homes. (S1O-534)
