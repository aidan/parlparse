Business Bulletin No. 130/2008: Wednesday 17 September 2008
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 16 September 2008

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-16262 John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will confirm its continuing commitment to fox clubs.

S3W-16320 Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidelines there are for police forces in relation to the translation into English of evidence in other languages.

S3W-16321 Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidelines there are for police forces in relation to the independent scrutiny of the translation into English of evidence in other languages.

S3W-16322 Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidelines there are for procurators fiscal in relation to the translation into English of evidence in other languages.

S3W-16323 Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidelines there are for procurators fiscal in relation to the independent scrutiny of the translation into English of evidence in other languages.

S3W-16324 Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration it has given to the use of Ethos FR and other fuel additives in the public sector and more widely and what tests or reviews of tests it has undertaken on these products.

S3W-16325 Joe FitzPatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive what projections it has made of the impact on the Scottish block grant of a 4p in the pound reduction in the basic rate of income tax during the current spending review period and what representations it has made to Her Majesty’s Government on the matter.

S3W-16326 Joe FitzPatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive what projections it has made of the impact on the budget of an (a) 1p and (b) 2p in the pound reduction in income tax using the Parliament’s taxation powers under the Scotland Act 1998.

S3W-16327 Hugh O’Donnell: To ask the Scottish Executive how it recognises the role that the codes of practice published by the Scottish Social Services Council play in the values of social services’ work and what priority it puts on the codes in its guidance to local authorities and the single outcome agreements.

S3W-16328 Hugh O’Donnell: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are being taken to ensure that codes of practice published by the Scottish Social Services Council are upheld during any re-tendering of services.

S3W-16329 Hugh O’Donnell: To ask the Scottish Executive what practical steps it will take to ensure that the process of re-tendering for social care providers does not result in poorer quality service for users.

S3W-16331 Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made on the Stranraer regeneration project.

S3W-16332 Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding package will be available for the Stranraer regeneration project.

S3W-16333 Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will make the Stranraer regeneration project a national priority.

S3W-16334 Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive how the £1.4 million being made available to assist NHS boards that currently charge for car parking will be distributed.

S3W-16335 Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that the £1.4 million being made available to assist NHS boards that currently charge for parking is adequate to cover the ongoing costs of providing and managing hospital car parks.

S3W-16336 Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive what additional costs it estimates that NHS Highland will incur in running its car parking facilities after 31 March 2009.

S3W-16337 Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will ensure that front-line patient services will not be affected when NHS boards have to fully fund the costs of maintaining their own car parks after the transitional funding period ends on 31 March 2009.

S3W-16338 Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-12705 by Stewart Stevenson on 15 May 2008, what supplementary grant payments have been made to CalMac Ferries Ltd to cover increases in the cost of fuel.

S3W-16339 Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-9536 by Stewart Stevenson on 5 March 2008 and the intervention made by the Minister for Transport, Infrastructure and Climate Change during the debate on ferry services on 10 September 2008 (Official Report c. 10640), whether there remains scope in the budget for the proposed Lochboisdale to Mallaig ferry service.

S3W-16340 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive how many times Scottish ministers have met the Scottish Football League to discuss sectarianism since 17 May 2007.

S3W-16341 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive how many times Scottish ministers have met the Scottish Premier League to discuss sectarianism since 17 May 2007.

S3W-16342 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive how many times it has met with the STUC to discuss sectarianism since 17 May 2007.

S3W-16343 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has received representations since 17 May 2007 from the Irish Government in respect of concerns about offensive songs being sung in public places in Scotland and, if so, whether it will publish its response.

S3W-16344 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the First Minister plans to make a ministerial statement to the Parliament about sectarianism in Scotland and, if so, when.

S3W-16345 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to produce detailed annual reports on crime aggravated by religious prejudice and develop a programme of rehabilitation for offenders convicted under section 74 of the Criminal Justice (Scotland) Act 2003.

S3W-16346 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive how many twinning arrangements were in place between denominational and non-denominational schools at the start of 2008-09 school year, broken down by local authority.

S3W-16347 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is taking forward the development of open-market Homestake.

S3W-16348 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will make open-market Homestake available in Glasgow.

S3W-16349 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what eligibility criteria are used to assess access to open-market Homestake.

S3W-16350 Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive what the outcome was of the legal pursuit of the recovery of the cost of the repairs to the Erskine Bridge following the incident involving an offshore structure.

S3W-16351 Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive what companies were deemed to be responsible for the damage caused to the Erskine Bridge by an offshore structure and how much money was recovered from each of the organisations concerned.

S3W-16352 Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on progress in the rehabilitation, rehousing and, where appropriate, employment and vocational training of service personnel wounded in Iraq and Afghanistan and what monitoring there is to ensure that their concerns are addressed by the Scottish Government and other interested parties.

S3W-16353 Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is undertaking work in conjunction with the Health and Safety Executive to increase employee on-site safety in Scotland’s open cast coal mines and, if so, what the nature is of this work.

S3W-16354 Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is carrying out work in conjunction with the Health and Safety Executive to reduce and minimise company safety failures on opencast mines and, if so, what the nature is of this work.

S3W-16355 Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will restrict any planned development of existing opencast mines where it has been clearly established in a court of law that the operating company has previously committed a breach of on-site safety regulations leading to on site employee accidents and deaths.

S3W-16356 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what financial support can be given to commercial companies to assist in the setting up and provision of NHS dental services.

S3W-16357 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what financial support can be given to dental bodies corporate to assist in the setting up and provision of NHS dental services.

S3W-16358 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the provision in the proposed Health Bill to keep GP practices in the NHS will be extended to commercial companies providing NHS dental services.
