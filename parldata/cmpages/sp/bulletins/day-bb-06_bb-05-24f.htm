Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-4442 Mr Kenneth Macintosh: Offside! Labour Rights and Sportswear Production in Asia—That the Parliament welcomes Oxfam International’s report, Offside! Labour Rights and Sportswear Production in Asia; notes that, as top sportswear brands prepare to showcase their wares at this year’s FIFA World Cup finals in Germany, many sportswear workers in Asia face discrimination, intimidation, violence and dismissal for joining unions to fight for better pay and conditions; further notes that the majority of these workers are women from poor communities supporting children and families and who are being paid as little as 32 pence an hour; urges global sports brands to do more to improve labour conditions, and believes that Scotland should show leadership in the fight for trade justice by realising the Scottish Executive’s international development policy ambition for Scotland to become a fair trade country.

*S2M-4441 Donald Gorrie: The Big Lottery Young People’s Fund—That the Parliament congratulates the Big Lottery Fund on successfully setting up 31 local panels of young people across Scotland to promote and process local applications to the Young People’s Fund and on developing schemes to explain their processes to applicants and to train the participants on the panels and suggests that the Scottish Executive should develop a similar process to help to direct grants to voluntary organisations to produce the best results for their community.

*S2M-4435 Donald Gorrie: Competition Commission Decision on Merger of Waterstone’s and Ottakar’s—That the Parliament is concerned about the Competition Commission’s statement accepting the merger between Waterstone’s and Ottakar’s bookshops, ignoring the strong arguments opposing the merger put forward by many interested bodies in Scotland, the different position in Scotland in relation to competition between booksellers and the treatment of Scottish titles by some major retailers; regards this as yet another example of the UK system favouring the growth of large retail businesses and causing the demise of more and more small, independent shops in Scotland, and commits itself to pressing the UK Government, the Westminster Parliament and the Scottish Executive to promote cultural and social diversity, and calls on the UK Government to direct the Competition Commision to take account of these issues.
