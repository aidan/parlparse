Business Bulletin No. 78/2006: Tuesday 16 May 2006
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 15 May 2006

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-25915 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider bringing forward the implementation date of the requirement to supply free tap water on request under the Licensing (Scotland) Act 2005.

S2W-25916 David McLetchie: To ask the Scottish Executive what powers are available to recover unpaid fines from the salaries of employed persons and on how many occasions such powers were exercised in the last year for which figures are available.

S2W-25917 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive what financial support it is providing this year to organisations offering financial literacy training.

S2W-25918 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on how many money advisers there are in each local authority area.

S2W-25919 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on the number of reports of adverse drug reactions received via the Yellow Card Scheme in each of the last 10 years, broken down by NHS board.

S2W-25920 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidelines are offered to (a) GPs, (b) nurses, (c) pharmacists and (d) hospital-based doctors in respect of reporting adverse drug reactions.

S2W-25921 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on the number of reports of adverse drug reactions in Scotland made by (a) GPs, (b) hospital-based doctors, (c) nurses, (d) pharmacists and (e) patients.

S2W-25922 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people died as a result of adverse drug reactions in each of the last 10 years, broken down by NHS board.

S2W-25923 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people were admitted to hospital as a result adverse drug reactions in each of the last 10 years, broken down by NHS board.

S2W-25924 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidelines are offered for monitoring adverse drug reactions in children.

S2W-25925 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive what literature or guidance it provides to patients to ensure that they know how to report an adverse drug reaction.

S2W-25926 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider researching the barriers to reporting adverse drug reactions, as suggested in the recent British Medical Association report, Reporting Adverse Drug Reactions.

S2W-25927 Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there is an increased risk of the H5N1 flu virus spreading to reared game birds in Scotland through the importing of game birds from Europe for release in Scotland for sport shooting and, if so, what measures it will take in order to counter this risk.

S2W-25928 Murray Tosh: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the decisions on 4 May 2006 by the Court of Justice of the European Communities in respect of cases C-290/03 and C-508/03, published on 10 May 2006, have implications for the law on environmental impact assessment in Scotland and whether it will amend the law in this regard through the Planning etc. (Scotland) Bill.

S2W-25929 Ms Maureen Watt: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-25540 by Mr Andy Kerr on 10 May 2006, what ambulance cover has been available in each town in Aberdeenshire and Moray in each year since 1999.

S2W-25930 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prisoners were parents of dependent children in each year since 1999, broken down by prison.

S2W-25931 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many new drug treatment centres it plans to create.

S2W-25932 Euan Robson: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures NHS Scotland has introduced to ensure that people with severe mental illness are encouraged to join programmes to combat obesity, increase exercise or promote smoking cessation, in light of the levels of physical health difficulties among this group, evidence that those with severe mental illness live ten years less than other people and Rethink’s current campaign highlighting mental health as something which should feature in mainstream public health reforms.

S2W-25933 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made towards more secure recording of licensed firearms.

S2W-25934 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people have had firearm licences withdrawn in each year since 1999.

S2W-25935 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive what correspondence it has had in respect of, and what difficulties have resulted from, implementing a more secure recording of licensed firearms.

S2W-25936 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive whether its strategy to improve the quality of health care for older people includes commitment to establishing a national resource, such as the Caledonian Virtual Practice Development College, which has pioneered evidence-based nursing care and promotes better standards of care for older people.

S2W-25937 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how long women needed to wait to receive treatment for post-natal depression in (a) Scotland, (b) each NHS board and (c) each local authority area in each of the last five years.

S2W-25938 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how many women were treated with (a) prescribed medication and (b) cognitive behavioural therapy for post-natal depression in (i) Scotland, (ii) each NHS board and (iii) each local authority area in each of the last five years.
