Business Bulletin No. 162/2010: Tuesday 9 November 2010
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 8 November 2010

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-37332 Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will amend planning regulations in order to help restrict the installation of mobile telephone base stations.

S3W-37333 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in expanding school-college partnerships for 14 to 16-year-olds.

S3W-37334 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what it has done to support efforts to attract more international students to higher and further education institutions.

S3W-37335 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it has taken since May 2007 to help colleges develop their role in the (a) economy and (b) community.

S3W-37336 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what financial support it has provided since May 2007 to support research and development.

S3W-37337 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will develop care hubs so that independent and voluntary sector care homes for older people can also provide (a) day care, (b) assessment and rehabilitation, (c) respite and crisis care, (d) meals and laundry, (e) podiatry and (f) support for self-help and mutual care services.

S3W-37338 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether Creative Scotland will introduce a grant enabling artists to reclaim the cost, up to a maximum of £15,000, of tax paid on work that they have sold.

S3W-37339 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding models for the creative sector Creative Scotland has examined.

S3W-37340 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive how much has been reinvested in the creative sector from Scottish Government profits from a stake in artists’ work.

S3W-37341 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has undertaken research into film tax incentives or explored the operation of such schemes in Ireland, Canada and the United States of America.

S3W-37342 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in embedding cultural strategies in the local community planning process.

S3W-37343 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures have been taken to encourage BBC or private sector programme makers to market Scotland’s culture internationally.

S3W-37344 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with BBC Scotland or other parties since May 2007 regarding the future availability of BBC Alba on Freeview.

S3W-37345 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it has made toward the target of ensuring that the proportion of Gaelic speakers is back to at least 2001 levels by the 2021 census.

S3W-37346 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made toward improving direct contact with the European Commission, as stated in the 2007 SNP manifesto.

S3W-37347 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many meetings have been held with the Norwegian Government and European Commission to explore options for a North Sea supergrid since May 2007.

S3W-37348 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it has taken to monitor the effectiveness of the Charter for Grandchildren.

S3W-37349 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has received from Grandparents Apart Self Help Group regarding the Charter for Grandchildren.

S3W-37350 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met Grandparents Apart Self Help Group and what matters were discussed.

S3W-37351 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent guidance it has issued to local authorities regarding the Charter for Grandchildren.

S3W-37352 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) females and (b) males have myasthenia gravis.

S3W-37353 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many babies have been born with congenital myasthenic syndrome in each of the last three years.

S3W-37354 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people aged (a) under 18, (b) 18 to 30, (c) 31 to 40, (d) 41 to 50, (e) 51 to 60 and (f) over 60 have myasthenia gravis.

S3W-37355 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many specialist myasthenia gravis nurses there are and where they are located.

S3W-37356 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to extend the Climate Challenge Fund.

S3W-37357 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it was responsible for the Health + website and, if so, for what reason it has been removed.

S3W-37358 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how much has been spent on developing the NHS Inform website, including all IT costs.

S3W-37359 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how much will be spent per annum to maintain the NHS Inform website, including all IT costs.

S3W-37360 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the original budget was for the (a) development and (b) maintenance of NHS Inform.

S3W-37361 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how the development of the NHS Inform website was project managed; what project plans existed, and what budget controls were in place.

S3W-37362 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how NHS Inform relates to NHS24.

S3W-37363 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how much of the content of the NHS Inform website has been taken from other NHS websites and what these websites are.

S3W-37364 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment of best value has been made of the procurement and development of NHS Inform.

S3W-37365 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how project spend on NHS Inform was monitored.

S3W-37366 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how much was spent at the launch event for NHS Inform on 3 August 2010.

S3W-37367 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the budget for NHS Inform was in (a) 2008-09 and (b) 2009-10 and is in 2010-11.

S3W-37368 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether work on the NHS Inform website is complete and, if not, what further work is required.

S3W-37369 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the original completion date was for NHS Inform.

S3W-37370 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it requires NHS boards to engage with voluntary sector organisations in meeting the needs of people living with and affected by blood-borne viruses.

S3W-37371 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it recognises the contribution that the voluntary sector can make in keeping people with blood-borne viruses on their treatment pathway.

S3W-37372 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it recognises the contribution that the voluntary sector can make in educating the public regarding blood-borne viruses and what funding it provides for this.

S3W-37373 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding is available for voluntary sector organisations that help people with blood-borne viruses.

S3W-37374 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to help people on long-term therapies to treat blood-borne viruses.

S3W-37375 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it gives to people who have completed their treatment for blood-borne viruses.

S3W-37376 Peter Peacock: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects the result of the public inquiry into a flood alleviation scheme in Forres to be made public.

S3W-37377 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what level of efficiency savings NHS boards will be required to find in 2011-12.

S3W-37378 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether NHS boards will be able to retain the efficiency savings that they generate in 2011-12.

S3W-37379 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether additional funding will be provided to achieve the NHSScotland Resource Allocation Committee (NRAC) targets for each NHS board and what the likely timescale is for NHS boards to meet their targets.

S3W-37380 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to evaluate the performance of community health partnerships and community health and care partnerships.

S3W-37381 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to introduce talking guides on any bus services that it subsidises.

S3W-37382 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive how it uses public procurement contracts to encourage public transport providers to introduce talking guides on services.

S3W-37383 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive on what research evidence NHS Lothian bases its proposal to reduce homeopathy services.

S3W-37384 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive which NHS boards provide homeopathy services.

S3W-37385 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what the annual cost to the NHS is of providing homeopathy services, also broken down by NHS board.

S3W-37386 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive how it determined the target to reduce Scottish final energy consumption by 12% by 2020, as set out in Conserve and Save: A Consultation on the Energy Efficiency Action Plan for Scotland.

S3W-37387 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive how the target to reduce Scottish final energy consumption by 12% by 2020 from the average 2005 to 2007 consumption levels, as set out in Conserve and Save: A Consultation on the Energy Efficiency Action Plan for Scotland, relates to the EU target of reducing energy consumption by 20% against a business-as-usual scenario through increased efficiency by 2020.

S3W-37388 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what final energy consumption figure for Scotland was assumed for 2020 under a business-as-usual scenario in determining the target to reduce Scottish final energy consumption by 12% by 2020, as set out in Conserve and Save: A Consultation on the Energy Efficiency Action Plan for Scotland.
