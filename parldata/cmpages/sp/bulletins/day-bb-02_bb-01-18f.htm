Business Bulletin No. 10/2002

Friday 18 January 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-2623# Tavish Scott: Lifeline Air Links to the Islands—That the Parliament recognises that the lifeline air services to the Scottish islands are necessities, not luxuries; believes that the commercial air fares charged by the airlines are a tax on island life, handicapping individuals, businesses and public bodies alike, and considers that the Scottish Executive should enter into discussions with Her Majesty's Government and the airlines with a view to using a Public Service Obligation subsidy to reduce the airfares to a more reasonable level.

*S1M-2622 Marilyn Livingstone: Cross-Party Support for a Review of the Construction Industry in Scotland—That the Parliament recognises the importance of the construction industry to the Scottish economy and the contribution it makes to supporting employment and vocational education and training; notes that nine out of every 10 construction firms employ less than eight people; acknowledges that the reputation of the whole industry is damaged by the actions of rogue traders, particularly within the residential sector, and that small businesses contend that their profitability and competitiveness is adversely affected by the system of cash retentions, and believes that the Scottish Executive should initiate a strategic review of construction in Scotland in order to (a) identify how best to combat the "cowboy" element, (b) support the education and training infrastructure so that it is able to meet the skill requirements of the industry, (c) examine the possibility of developing an inspection and assessment regime to evaluate the competence of construction companies, (d) address the system of cash retentions and (e) ensure that the provisions within the existing construction contracts legislation deliver a process that is fair, effective and easy to understand.

Supported by: Miss Annabel Goldie*, Tavish Scott*, Alex Neil*, Robin Harper*

*S1M-2621# Cathy Peattie: Fair Trade Fortnight—That the Parliament notes that 4-17 March 2002 is Fair Trade Fortnight and considers that the Scottish Executive should endorse the use of Fair Trade produce, in particular by encouraging schools to incorporate Fair Trade as an element within their curricula and should promote it through other activity.

Supported by: Robin Harper*

*S1M-2620 Tricia Marwick: Miners' Pension Scheme—That the Parliament welcomes the £8 million released from Her Majesty’s Treasury to miners' pension schemes; notes that many ex-miners or widows of ex-miners are living in poverty while HM Treasury receives £250 million per year from the profits of the schemes; further notes that Her Majesty's Government will take over £4 billion from the schemes, and demands that Her Majesty's Government return all of the profits to the pension schemes to allow the ex-miners and their families to receive the benefits of the scheme to which they contributed.

Supported by: Robin Harper*

*S1M-2619 Alex Neil: Public Appointments (Parliamentary Approval) (Scotland) Bill—That the Parliament agrees to the general principles of the Public Appointments (Parliamentary Approval) (Scotland) Bill.

*S1M-2618 Mr Kenneth Gibson: Remove the Shackles—That the Parliament notes with regret the increasingly partisan approach of Labour members to "take note" motions and motions for members’ business; is aware that of the 14 outstanding such motions submitted by Liberal Democrat MSPs to attract signatures at 11 January 2002, 13 have attracted support from SNP members but none have the support of their Labour coalition partners; realises that, in contrast, of the 33 Labour motions, 23 have attracted Liberal Democrat, 31 SNP and 19 Conservative support; acknowledges that of 70 SNP motions outstanding, 31 obtained Liberal Democrat, 19 Conservative but only 11 Labour support; recognises that, when given, Labour support is token and comes from only one or two members with specific constituency or special interests; is surprised that even motions previously signed by Labour members do not attract the same signatures when subsequently re-submitted indicating a tightening of the Labour Whips’ control; further acknowledges that such an approach has meant no Labour backing at all for motions on such issues as Female Genital Mutilation, International Year of Mountains and Donald Whyte, Genealogist; believes that Labour Whips are preventing Labour members from signing perfectly innocuous, non-partisan motions and thus undermining the very ethos of the "new politics" the Parliament should represent; expresses surprise at the lack of self-confidence, lack of faith and insecurity that this approach displays; accepts that while all parties have a right to express their views robustly in the Chamber, in committees and in the media, there is still room for cross-party co-operation on a host of issues, not least those raised in members’ business motions; commends Conservative, Green, Independent, Liberal Democrat, SNP and SSP members for acting in the true spirit of the Parliament in this regard but expresses disappointment that Labour control freakery prevents Labour members from doing likewise; considers that Labour members cannot reasonably expect support from non-Labour members for their motions when they have no intention of reciprocating, and therefore appeals to Labour Whips to remove the shackles on their members to allow free expression of support for non-partisan motions, and indeed members’ Bills, on their merits, before colleagues in other parties, irritated by Labour’s politically sectarian attitude, feel obliged to react in similar fashion.

Supported by: Mr Adam Ingram*, Mr Lloyd Quinan*, Ms Sandra White*, Colin Campbell*, Mr Kenny MacAskill*, Fiona McLeod*, Fergus Ewing*, Richard Lochhead*, Michael Matheson*
