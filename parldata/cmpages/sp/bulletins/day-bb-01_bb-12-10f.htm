Business Bulletin No. 182/2001

Monday 10 December 2001

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-2534# Dorothy-Grace Elder: Save Post Office Jobs-That the Parliament views with grave concern the cutbacks in postal services in Scotland being proposed by Consignia, including the closure in Glasgow of Scotland's only Parcel Distribution Office and only remaining International Mail Centre and a potential one in ten job losses in other areas, threatening business and domestic services, with transfer of work to newly built centres in Coventry and Berkshire; opposes any plan to sell off Post Office Vehicle Services which could result in further savage job cuts; upholds strongly the Post Office and Royal Mail as respected national institutions; deplores the rundown of a trusted service which commands public respect and affection thanks to the exceptional dedication of its workforce, and urges all who benefit from the dedication of postal workers, especially at this time of year, to realise their present plight and support their campaign to retain decent standards of service and maintain a public service.

*S1M-2533# Donald Gorrie: Musical Instrument Instructors-That the Parliament notes with concern reports of impending changes in the contracts, status and conditions of musical instrument instructors in schools, designed to make them casual, lower grade employees; believes that giving pupils the chance to learn to play a musical instrument and to take part in orchestras and groups is an essential part of educational and cultural life, and considers that the Executive should ensure that musical instrument instructors retain the contracts and status of full teaching members of the school community.

Supported by: John Farquhar Munro*, Mr Jamie Stone*

*S1M-2532 Mr Andy Kerr: Land Reform (Scotland) Bill: Financial Resolution-That the Parliament, for the purposes of any Act of the Scottish Parliament resulting from the Land Reform (Scotland) Bill, agrees to any increase in expenditure payable out of the Scottish Consolidated Fund in consequence of the Act.

Supported by: Mr Jim Wallace*, Ross Finnie*, Allan Wilson*, Peter Peacock*

*S1M-2531 Malcolm Chisholm: The Food Protection (Emergency Prohibitions) (Amnesic Shellfish Poisoning) (West Coast) (No.14) (Scotland) Order 2001-That the Health and Community Care Committee recommends that the Food Protection (Emergency Prohibitions) (Amnesic Shellfish Poisoning) (West Coast) (No.14) (Scotland) Order 2001 (SSI 2001/451) be approved.

Supported by: Mrs Mary Mulligan*, Hugh Henry*

S1M-2530 Karen Gillon: Railtrack and the Larkhall Project-That the Parliament notes with dismay the decision by Railtrack plc not to support the Scottish Executive funded rail link between Larkhall and Milngavie; recognises that this decision has the potential to have a particular impact on Larkhall and the surrounding communities as it jeopardises the future of a joint project between the Scottish Executive and Strathclyde Passenger Transport; re-iterates its view that this project would have brought substantial economic and social benefits to Clydesdale, and urges Railtrack plc to reconsider urgently their decision in order to ensure that this project proceeds as planned to enable the economic and social regeneration of the Larkhall area.

S1M-2529# Karen Gillon: New Lanark-That the Parliament recognises the significant contribution that New Lanark has made to the social, cultural and educational fabric of Scotland; congratulates the New Lanark Conservation Trust on the excellent work that it has done in restoring the village to its natural beauty, and calls on UNESCO to grant the village full World Heritage Status.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie*

S1M-2528# Mr Kenneth Gibson: Young Runaways-That the Parliament welcomes the report Missing Out - Young Runaways in Scotland; commends the University of York, Aberlour Child Care Trust, Extern, Children's Promise and The Children's Society for producing this report, the most extensive piece of research yet undertaken into young people under 16 who run away or are forced to leave home; notes that this is the first comprehensive research to provide both an overall picture of the scale and extent of the problem while suggesting workable strategies for responding to the needs of this very vulnerable group of young people, and asks the Executive to look closely at the findings and recommendations of the report and act accordingly.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie*

S1M-2527 Tommy Sheridan: Sentencing for Breach of the Peace at Faslane Blockade-That the Parliament condemns the disgraceful three month prison sentence handed down to Jenny Gaiawyn on 29 November 2001 by Sheriff Morag Galbraith for a breach of the peace at the blockade in February 2001 of the Faslane nuclear base; considers this sentence to be an outrageous attack on this young woman's human right to protest peacefully, which is a fundamental human right enshrined in the European Convention on Human Rights and as such should be upheld in law; further considers the sentence to be a scandalous reflection of the arbitrariness of the Scottish legal system, and calls on the Minister for Justice to set up an inquiry into the inconsistencies of sentencing in the courts, which have seen people acquitted, fined or even sentenced to a prison term for the same "crime" of breach of the peace.

S1M-2526 Pauline McNeill: Breastfeeding Clinic at Yorkhill, Glasgow-That the Parliament congratulates the Queen Mother's Hospital at Yorkhill NHS Trust in establishing Scotland's first breastfeeding clinic where breastfeeding mothers can attend every Monday between 8.00 am and 6.00 pm without an appointment; notes that this is the first open clinic of its kind in Scotland, and supports the encouragement which the clinic will offer to mothers to continue breastfeeding beyond the initial two weeks in order to benefit both their health and that of the baby.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie*

S1M-2525# Mr Duncan McNeil: Railtrack and the Gourock Project-That the Parliament notes with dismay the decision by Railtrack plc not to support four Scottish Executive funded enhancement projects, including the project at Gourock train station; recognises that this decision has a particular impact on the Gourock project as it jeopardises the future of the joint capital project between Railtrack, the Scottish Executive, Inverclyde Council and private companies to redevelop the Gourock waterfront; further notes that this project, which would include an integrated transport hub, offers substantial benefits to the Inverclyde economy, and asks Railtrack, as a matter of urgency, to reconsider this short-sighted decision before the project is lost and the people of Inverclyde are robbed of a major economic opportunity.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie*

S1M-2524 Mr Andy Kerr: Scottish Local Government (Elections) Bill-That the Parliament agrees that the Scottish Local Government (Elections) Bill be passed.

S1M-2519# Andrew Wilson: Scottish Number Plates-That the Parliament considers that the Scottish Executive should recognise the importance of national symbols and the absolute right of people to identify with their country of choice and should detail what action it has undertaken thus far with the Driver and Vehicle Licensing Agency and Department for Transport, Local Government and the Regions to protect the rights of Scotland's people with vehicles registered here to display a Saltire and/or "SCO" on their vehicle number plates, and affirms that from this date the Scottish Executive should not accept or endorse any situation whereby these rights might be eroded.

Supported by: Bruce Crawford, Alasdair Morgan, Fiona McLeod, Michael Matheson, Christine Grahame, Mr Andrew Welsh, Dr Winnie Ewing, Tricia Marwick, Mr Kenny MacAskill, Mr Duncan Hamilton, Richard Lochhead, Linda Fabiani, Colin Campbell, Ms Sandra White, Mr Kenneth Gibson, Roseanna Cunningham, Mr Gil Paterson, Mr Lloyd Quinan, Mr Adam Ingram, Ms Margo MacDonald, Dorothy-Grace Elder, Nicola Sturgeon, Shona Robison, Michael Russell, Fergus Ewing, Stewart Stevenson

S1M-2516 Ms Margo MacDonald: National Asthma Campaign Scotland-That the Parliament congratulates National Asthma Campaign Scotland on its work over the past year in drawing public and parliamentary attention to the long-term condition of asthma which affects over 400,000 people in Scotland; welcomes the National Asthma Campaign's Asthma Audit 2001 and its survey of asthma services in Scotland of winter 2000 which together, for the first time, give a true indication of the work that still needs to be done to tackle the condition, and calls on the Scottish Executive to work with the National Asthma Campaign Scotland in its mission to conquer asthma.

Supported by: Michael Matheson, Fergus Ewing, Michael Russell, Donald Gorrie*, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton*

S1M-2515 John Scott: Beatson Oncology Centre, Glasgow-That the Parliament notes with extreme concern and regret the decision by four senior consultants to leave the Beatson Oncology Centre in Glasgow as a result of major difficulties in the provision of cancer care services and low morale; notes that this leaves the Beatson Oncology Centre with 10 vacant posts which seriously impedes its ability to deliver cancer care and treatment, and calls on the Scottish Executive to take decisive action to address the situation.

S1M-2514 Irene McGugan: Tartan Army Award-That the Parliament congratulates the Tartan Army on winning the Belgian Olympic Committee's Fair Play Prize 2001 for the excellent behaviour displayed by Scotland fans in Brussels when visiting for the Belgium v Scotland world cup qualifier in September; notes that this award is the latest in a string of accolades bestowed on Scottish football fans; further notes that a record 15,000 Scotland supporters travelled to the match; commends those supporters for the good name they give Scotland throughout the world, and looks forward to the presentation of the award to representatives of the Scotland Travel Club and the SFA in Brussels on 7 December 2001.

Supported by: Michael Matheson, Fergus Ewing, Mr Andrew Welsh, Michael Russell, Donald Gorrie*

S1M-2513 Fergus Ewing: Inverness Highland European Capital of Culture 2008 Bid -That the Parliament congratulates all those involved in the Inverness Highland bid 2008 for the title of European Capital of Culture; notes that the Inverness Highland 2008 bid is the only Scottish one, and recognises that a successful bid would bring important social, economic and cultural rewards for Scotland in general and for Inverness and the Highlands in particular.

Supported by: Kay Ullrich, Stewart Stevenson, Murdo Fraser, Mr Kenny MacAskill, Mr Adam Ingram, Michael Matheson, Mrs Margaret Ewing, Brian Adam, Richard Lochhead, Mr Andrew Welsh, Tommy Sheridan, Mr Gil Paterson, Roseanna Cunningham, Fiona McLeod, Mary Scanlon, Mr Kenneth Gibson, Ian Jenkins, Dr Winnie Ewing, Ms Sandra White, Michael Russell, Dennis Canavan, John Farquhar Munro

S1M-2512 Mr Gil Paterson: Breaking the Organised Child Sex Rings-That the Parliament recognises that well organised and financed people are systematically abusing children in the sex trade using games, alcohol and drugs to groom and coerce children into sex rings who are then exploited, degraded and abused by paedophile adults for money and calls on the Scottish Executive to resource the police to allow them to set up a task force to combat child abuse in the sex trade, tackle the provision of children for sex and paying for sex with children by adults and target crimes against children on the internet in order to ensure a course of action is followed that will break organised child sex rings and bring the perpetrators of child sex abuse to justice.

Supported by: Mr Andrew Welsh, Michael Matheson, Mr Lloyd Quinan, Mr Adam Ingram, Fergus Ewing, Stewart Stevenson, Kay Ullrich, Fiona McLeod, Brian Adam, Ms Margo MacDonald, Ms Sandra White, Dr Winnie Ewing, Alex Fergusson, Richard Lochhead, Dennis Canavan, Mr Kenneth Gibson, Donald Gorrie*

S1M-2511 Michael Russell: Head of Communications at North Ayrshire Council-That the Parliament congratulates North Ayrshire Council on appointing Mr Gerry O' Brien as its new Head of Communications; notes Mr O' Brien's previous posts with the Conservative Party and the Conservative Group in the Scottish Parliament; expects that Mr O' Brien will have few ideological problems in helping to implement new Labour policies in North Ayrshire as they are mostly identical to failed Tory policies, and hopes that Mr O' Brien's efforts are rewarded with the same success as the Tories in recent years, who have all but disappeared from the electoral map in most of Scotland.

S1M-2510# Linda Fabiani: Annual St Andrew's Day Cultural Celebration-That the Parliament congratulates Children in Scotland (Clann an Alba) on the success of its Equal Futures event held on 30 November 2001 in Glasgow; notes that this was attended by schoolchildren from all over Scotland with a view to building racial equality and believes that St Andrew's Day should be designated as an annual day of celebration of Scotland's cultural diversity, with consideration given to public holiday status so that everyone in Scotland can participate.

Supported by: Mr Kenneth Gibson, Mr Kenny MacAskill, Bruce Crawford, Andrew Wilson, Mr Gil Paterson, Mr Andrew Welsh, Tommy Sheridan, Michael Matheson, Mr Lloyd Quinan, Mr Adam Ingram, Michael Russell, Donald Gorrie*

S1M-2509 Michael Russell: Ministerial Responsibility for Education and Schools-That the Parliament notes that the principal day-to-day responsibility for education and schools now lies with the Deputy Minister for Education and Young People and not with the Minister for Education and Young People; regrets that this change in responsibilities was not announced to the Parliament before the vote on the appointment of Ministers, which is contrary to the principles of openness and accountability on which the Parliament was founded; expresses concern that there will now be no Minister in the Cabinet who has hands-on responsibility for the major devolved area of responsibility that covers schools and the investment that education of young people makes in our society, and calls on the First Minister to bring this issue to the Parliament for full debate.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson, Irene McGugan, Mr Andrew Welsh, Tommy Sheridan, Michael Matheson, Mr Lloyd Quinan, Mr Adam Ingram, Mr Gil Paterson

S1M-2508 Fiona McLeod: GM Cross Contamination-That the Parliament recognises the Mexican Government's ban on all genetically modified material; is distressed that cross-contamination from GM crops banned eight years ago is having a direct effect on current crops; believes that the only answer is a moratorium on GM field trials, and calls on the Scottish Executive to reverse its decision to carry out field trials at Munlochy and other sites in Scotland.

Supported by: Bruce Crawford, Mr Andrew Welsh, Tommy Sheridan, Michael Matheson, Mr Lloyd Quinan, Mr Adam Ingram, Mr Gil Paterson, Michael Russell

S1M-2507 Cathy Jamieson: School Education (Amendment) (Scotland) Bill-That the Parliament agrees that the School Education (Amendment) (Scotland) Bill be passed.

Supported by: Nicol Stephen

S1M-2506 Mr Jim Wallace: Land Reform (Scotland) Bill-That the Parliament agrees to the general principles of the Land Reform (Scotland) Bill.

S1M-2505 Fergus Ewing: Aggregates Tax: Lack of Scottish Exemptions-That the Parliament notes that special exemptions from the aggregates tax are to be granted to Northern Ireland following HM Treasury's pre-budget report; believes that the imposition of the tax on Scotland from 1 April 2002 will be damaging to the Scottish economy, especially in rural areas and will not result in any environmental gain but will lead to the closure of some smaller quarries, will place a greater burden of traffic on our roads and will place a particular burden upon local authorities which could lead to higher council taxes and/or cuts in building and roads repairs programmes; further believes that the failure of the Scottish Executive to accept in any way the widespread concerns on the impact of the aggregates tax and its total refusal to stand up to Her Majesty's Government on this issue contrasts poorly with the united approach pursued by politicians in the Northern Ireland Assembly and that, had the coalition parties campaigned against this tax instead of refusing to voice any word of criticism, Scotland too may have received valuable exemptions, and therefore calls upon the Scottish Executive to state exactly what, if anything, it plans to do in relation to the imposition of this tax on Scotland and to estimate what the cost to the economy will be from it.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson, Michael Matheson, Mr Lloyd Quinan, Mr Adam Ingram, Mr Gil Paterson

S1M-2504# Mr Duncan Hamilton: Minister for Highlands and Islands-That the Parliament recognises that the Highlands and Islands have unique needs; notes that the case for the needs of the Highlands and Islands areas needs to be made at the highest levels of government; regrets the decision of the First Minister to drop the role of 'Minister for the Highlands and Islands' from the Scottish Executive and calls upon the First Minister to reinstate this role.

Supported by: Fergus Ewing
