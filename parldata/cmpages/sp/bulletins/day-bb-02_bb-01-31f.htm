Business Bulletin No. 19/2002

Thursday 31 January 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-2668 Rhoda Grant: Campbeltown - Ballycastle Ferry-That the Parliament welcomes the announcement by the Scottish Executive of the call for tenders for the Campbeltown - Ballycastle ferry; recognises that this announcement is the product of joint working between the Scottish Executive, the Northern Ireland Executive and the Scotland Office, and urges the Scottish Executive to ensure that the tendering process is concluded as soon as is practically possible to ensure that the Campbeltown area can benefit from a ferry link with Northern Ireland.

*S1M-2667 Christine Grahame: Freedom of Information (Scotland) Bill-That the Justice 1 Committee considers the Freedom of Information (Scotland) Bill at Stage 2 in the following order: sections 1 to 3, schedule 1, sections 4 to 8, sections 10 and 11, sections 14 to 42, schedule 2, sections 43 to 54, schedule 3, sections 55 to 61, section 9, sections 12 and 13, sections 62 to 73.

*S1M-2666 Mr Brian Monteith: Innovative Philosophy Classes at Sunnyside Primary School, Alloa-That the Parliament warmly congratulates the headteacher, Paul Cleghorn, and the teaching staff at Sunnyside Primary School, Alloa, Clackmannanshire, for being the first school in the country to introduce an innovative programme of philosophy classes aimed at developing pupils' thinking and questioning skills.

Supported by: Mr Keith Harding*

*S1M-2665 Ms Margo MacDonald: New Hospitals-That the Parliament, whilst welcoming the new Royal Infirmary of Edinburgh, notes that it will cost £184 million to build and will cost taxpayers some £1 billion to rent over its lifetime; agrees with the report by NHS consultant Dr Matthew Dunnigan, The Downsized Hypothesis: Value for Money, that the outdated analysis of patients' needs in the Lothian area used to determine the hospital's size has resulted in an underestimate of the number of acute beds required; regrets that the Scottish Executive has adopted the policy of previous Conservative Governments in Westminster which enriched the private sector and impoverished the NHS, and therefore calls on the Scottish Executive to review its opposition to establishing a Scottish trust for public investment to fund future projects similar to the new Royal Infirmary of Edinburgh.

*S1M-2664 Donald Gorrie: UK Life League-That the Parliament condemns unreservedly the tactics employed by the UK Life League, particularly in terms of the horrific practice of using photos of aborted foetuses and the personal tragedy of individuals in the public eye, for extremely dubious political ends; regrets that any group can deliberately misuse such material, particularly with the specific intention of inducing fear, misinformation and confusion amongst women; notes that the UK Life League website is a haven for such propaganda, which is almost entirely based on falsehood and lies, including the appropriation of other groups' logos and misrepresentation of them to imply their support for the "values" of the UK Life League, and reaffirms its commitment to freedom of speech for all pressure groups, including legitimate pro-life organisations, while remaining absolutely committed to the principle that it is a woman's right to choose.

*S1M-2663 Roseanna Cunningham: Mike Tyson-That the Parliament notes and applauds the decision of the Nevada Athletic Commission in the United States of America not to approve an application from Mike Tyson for a licence to box in Las Vegas; expresses its concern at speculation that the fight might be re-arranged to a UK venue; recollects the anger that was caused by the Home Secretary's decision to allow Mike Tyson, a convicted rapist, to enter the UK for a fight in Glasgow on 24 June 2000; further notes that Mike Tyson has at no time expressed public remorse for the crimes of violence for which he has been convicted; expresses the hope that boxing promoters and venue managers will resist suggestions that a second Tyson fight be held in Scotland, and calls upon the Scottish Executive to make a firm commitment that it will oppose the licensing of any boxing match involving Mike Tyson in Scotland and that it will make representations to oppose any visa application that might be made.
