Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (♦).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S3M-3654 Nicola Sturgeon: The Draft Health Care and Associated Professions (Miscellaneous Amendments and Practitioner Psychologists) Order 2009—That the Health and Sport Committee recommends that the draft Health Care and Associated Professions (Miscellaneous Amendments and Practitioner Psychologists) Order 2009 be approved.

Supported by: Shona Robison*

*S3M-3653 Nicola Sturgeon: Welfare Reform Bill - UK Legislation—That the Parliament agrees that the relevant provisions of the Welfare Reform Bill, introduced in the House of Commons on 14 January 2009, relating to a Right to Control for disabled people, so far as these matters fall within the legislative competence of the Scottish Parliament, should be considered by the UK Parliament.

Supported by: Shona Robison*

*S3M-3652 Kenny MacAskill: Borders, Citizenship and Immigration Bill - UK Legislation—That the Parliament agrees that the relevant provisions of the Borders, Citizenship and Immigration Bill, introduced in the House of Lords on 14 January 2009, relating to the power of detention for individuals subject to an outstanding arrest warrant and their enforcement, so far as these matters fall within the legislative competence of the Scottish Parliament, should be considered by the UK Parliament.

Supported by: Fergus Ewing*

*S3M-3651 Bruce Crawford on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Damages (Asbestos-related Conditions) (Scotland) Bill—That the Parliament agrees that, during Stage 3 of the Damages (Asbestos-related Conditions) (Scotland) Bill, debate on groups of amendments shall, subject to Rule 9.8.4A, be brought to a conclusion by the time limit indicated, that time limit being calculated from when the Stage begins and excluding any periods when other business is under consideration or when a meeting of the Parliament is suspended (other than a suspension following the first division in the Stage being called) or otherwise not in progress:

Groups 1 and 2: 40 minutes.

Supported by: Fergus Ewing*

*S3M-3650 John Park: Donibristle Primary School’s Achievements in Sport—That the Parliament congratulates Donibristle Primary School on winning the title of Dunfermline and West Fife School of the Year for Sport for a second consecutive year; recognises the school’s success in winning the regional indoor athletics finals at Monifieth on 19 February 2009 when the school represented Fife, competing against another seven teams and winning by a margin of 65 points, and applauds the hard work of students and staff at Donibristle Primary School for their ongoing dedication to and achievements in sport.

*S3M-3649 Robert Brown: Scrutiny of the Scottish Government’s Alcohol Reforms—That the Parliament expresses its serious concern over the Scottish Government’s intention to introduce its alcohol framework without comprehensive scrutiny by the Parliament; recognises that elements of the proposals, for example the introduction of minimum retail pricing for alcohol, are deeply controversial and could potentially have a substantial impact on Scottish industry, the retail trade and consumer choice; considers that it is unacceptable for major reforms of this nature to bypass full public and parliamentary scrutiny; believes that measures to tackle Scotland’s complex alcohol problems require a considered approach and should not be brought forward without due and democratic consideration, and therefore calls on the Scottish Government to bring forward all of its key alcohol proposals in a parliamentary Bill so that they can be subjected to full and detailed public consultation, parliamentary scrutiny and debate and to detailed votes by a parliamentary committee and by MSPs in the Chamber.

Supported by: Ross Finnie*, Liam McArthur*

*S3M-3648 Angela Constance: Congratulations to WELFEHD—That the Parliament congratulates the West Lothian Food and Health Development (WELFEHD) on its recent award of £915,978 from the Growing Community Assets programme of the Big Lottery Fund, which will allow it to purchase new premises and expand its operations; notes that the project will continue to offer quality fruit and vegetables to over 6,000 people from West Lothian with inadequate access to affordable produce; welcomes WELFEHD’s efforts in buying local produce, which benefits local farmers and the local economy; welcomes the project’s creation of volunteer and employment opportunities for individuals who have a history of unemployment, giving them the skills and confidence that they need to move to the wider workplace, and wishes the project continued success.

Supported by: Bob Doris*, Alasdair Allan*, Patrick Harvie*, Bill Kidd*

*S3M-3647 Pauline McNeill: Supermarket Packaging—That the Parliament notes the Friends of the Earth Scotland’s national waste-prevention project and that local volunteer waste-prevention champions will be carrying out a packaging take-back protest at their local Morrison’s store in Partick on Saturday 14 March 2009; further notes that food packaging costs households £470 per year, which is one sixth of the average food budget, and that over 40% of household rubbish that goes into landfill is supermarket packaging, and calls on supermarkets, which control 80% of the grocery market spend, to use their purchasing influence to reduce excessive packaging and also to continue to work to reduce the number of plastic carrier bags distributed to shoppers.

Supported by: Bill Kidd*, Jackie Baillie*, Bob Doris*, Patricia Ferguson*, Patrick Harvie*, Kenneth Gibson*

*S3M-3646 Aileen Campbell: Black Watch Scoops Four Olivier Awards—That the Parliament congratulates the play, Black Watch, on receiving four awards, for Best New Play, Best Director, Best Sound Design and Best Theatre Choreographer, at the 2009 Lawrence Olivier Awards; notes the worldwide success of Black Watch and its ability to bring to life vividly the experiences of Black Watch soldiers in Iraq, and recognises that, since its inception in February 2006, the National Theatre of Scotland has supported and produced high quality work, which continues to entertain, move and excite audiences.

*S3M-3645 Bill Kidd: Workfare and Labour Privatisation—That the Parliament notes with concern reports that private contractors only placed 6% of the Incapacity Benefit claimants on their books into work by October 2008 compared to 14% by public service Jobcentres over the same period and calls on the UK Labour Government to abandon its workfare policy and its moves to further privatise Jobcentre services.

*S3M-3644 John Scott: Congratulations to Ayr Rugby Football Club—That the Parliament congratulates Ayr Rugby Football Club on clinching the premiership division 1 title following its 20-10 win over Edinburgh Academical Football Club on Saturday 7 March 2009 and sends best wishes to 1st XV coach Kenny Murray and to all the players and supporters of Ayr Rugby Football Club.

*S3M-3643 Karen Whitefield: Greengairs Primary School Permanent Eco-School Status—That the Parliament congratulates the staff and pupils of Greengairs Primary School on obtaining permanent Eco-School status, which follows the award of a fourth Green Flag; recognises the hard work carried out by staff and pupils, both past and present, to achieve this award, and values the role of the Eco-Schools awards in raising the awareness of children about environmental and conservation issues.

*S3M-3642 Christina McKelvie: Well Done Motherwell College—That the Parliament congratulates Motherwell College on becoming the first and only state-funded education establishment in Scotland to be authorised to deliver the International Baccalaureate Diploma course and looks forward to seeing the teaching for this qualification delivered at the new £70 million campus at Ravenscraig.

*S3M-3641 Stewart Maxwell: Scotland 21 - 17 Australia—That the Parliament congratulates the Scotland team on beating Australia by 21 points to 17 to win the Plate competition at the 2009 Rugby World Cup Sevens.

Supported by: Jamie McGrigor*, Bill Kidd*, Jackie Baillie*, Kenneth Gibson*, Rob Gibson*, Willie Coffey*, Patricia Ferguson*, John Lamont*, Bill Wilson*, Christina McKelvie*, Bob Doris*, John Scott*, Jim Tolson*, Brian Adam*, David Whitton*, Robin Harper*
