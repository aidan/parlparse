Business Bulletin No. 36 / 2004

Friday 27th February 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-971# Rhona Brankin: World Health Organisation's Bone and Joint Decade 2000 to 2010-That the Parliament welcomes the World Health Organisation's Bone and Joint Decade 2000 to 2010; recognises that musculoskeletal disorders are the most common cause of severe long-term pain and physical disability affecting hundreds of millions of people including many young people around the world; further recognises that joint diseases account for more than half of all chronic conditions in persons aged 60 years and over and that back pain is the second leading cause of sick leave, and believes that the Scottish Executive should consider how best to raise awareness of musculoskeletal disorders in Scotland such as rheumatoid arthritis and osteoarthritis, osteoporosis and ankylosing spondylitis and their impact both on peoples' lives and on the Scottish economy.

*S2M-970# Richard Baker: Association of University Teachers' Industrial Dispute and Strike Action-That the Parliament supports the campaign by the Association of University Teachers (AUT) for better pay for academic and related staff; notes that the AUT is currently taking strike action, supported by NUS Scotland, and boycotting assessment activity from 1 March 2004 onwards; further notes that since 1980 academic salaries have increased by an average of only 20% compared with a rise in average earnings of 60% and of graduate earnings by still more; believes that fair pay in universities and colleges is essential for recruitment of well-motivated academic staff on whom we rely to create a highly skilled economy in Scotland, and believes that the Scottish Executive should act on the recommendations in the Enterprise and Culture Committee's Scottish Solutions Inquiry to ensure that investment in higher education in the future will enable staff to be better paid and address problems of recruitment and retention of academic staff.

Supported by: Robin Harper*

*S2M-969# Michael McMahon: Recognising the Contribution of Scottish Lithuanians-That the Parliament congratulates the Scottish Lithuanian Cultural and Recreational Social Club in Mossend, Lanarkshire, on the occasion of its 25th anniversary; notes the positive contribution that Lithuanian immigrants have made to Scotland's economy, local communities and special causes; applauds the commitment of members of the club to maintaining a sense of their history; recognises that now, with fifth and sixth generations, the Lithuanian community has integrated successfully but is at risk of losing its cultural identity and language; calls, therefore, for a recognition that, with the accession of Lithuania to the EU, the time is right to welcome further immigrants with much needed skills to Scotland from Lithuania and build on the links already important to, and embedded in, Lanarkshire's culture and history, and hopes that any new immigrants are treated with respect and given a warm welcome in recognition that different communities can and do integrate successfully in Scotland.

Supported by: Hugh Henry*

*S2M-966 Cathy Jamieson: The Draft Civil Legal Aid (Financial Conditions) (Scotland) Regulations 2004-That the Justice 2 Committee recommends that the draft Civil Legal Aid (Financial Conditions) (Scotland) Regulations 2004 be approved.

Supported by: Hugh Henry*

*S2M-965 Ms Margaret Curran: The Draft Debt Arrangement Scheme (Scotland) Regulations 2004-That the Communities Committee recommends that the draft Debt Arrangement Scheme (Scotland) Regulations 2004 be approved.

Supported by: Mrs Mary Mulligan*

*S2M-964 Patrick Harvie: Maryhill Citizens Advice Bureau's Initiative for Asylum Seekers-That the Parliament applauds a radical new initiative to integrate asylum seekers into a deprived area of Glasgow by Maryhill Citizens Advice Bureau (CAB); welcomes the news that Maryhill CAB is poised to embark upon an 18-month drive to recruit refugees as volunteer CAB advisers; notes that up to 80% of the refugees the Bureau sees are professionals such as doctors, teachers, accountants and bankers; offers its congratulations to this project, which is backed by the European Social Fund and is the culmination of almost two years work by Citizens Advice Scotland, Maryhill CAB and other Glasgow bureaux; calls upon the Executive to encourage similar projects throughout Scotland, and believes that such attempts to bring the energy and skills of asylum seekers into Scottish life are a welcome change from the authoritarian treatment that asylum seekers receive.

Supported by: Eleanor Scott*, Nicola Sturgeon*, Chris Ballance*, Frances Curran*, Carolyn Leckie*, Mr Stewart Maxwell*

*S2M-963 Roseanna Cunningham: Caithness Glass-That the Parliament notes with concern the decision by Royal Worcester and Spode Ltd to call the receivers into the glass-making firm Caithness Glass; recognises the importance of Caithness Glass as an employer, particularly in Perth and Wick; believes that the Caithness Glass name is world-renowned in the manufacture of decorative glass, and hopes that the Scottish Executive and Scottish Enterprise will give all possible assistance to the receivers in their attempts to secure a single buyer for the business as a going concern.

Supported by: Rob Gibson*, Jim Mather*

*S2M-962 Eleanor Scott: Highland Peace Movement-That the Parliament welcomes the Lochinvar Declaration for Peace launched on 14 February 2004; commends all those people who have signed up to it and pledged to act for peace; supports the growing movement for peace across Scotland, and notes that this movement will converge on Inverness on 28 February 2004 to urge Tony Blair to do more to find non-violent resolutions to international conflicts.

*S2M-961# Karen Whitefield: Landfill at Greengairs-That the Parliament recognises the sense of frustration felt by the people of Greengairs following the recent announcement that yet another landfill site is to be opened near to their village; believes that there must be changes made to planning law to ensure that the views of communities are more effectively taken into consideration during the planning process, both at the time of application and following the approval of any applications; welcomes the recent announcement by the First Minister that an environmental justice fund will be established to assist communities such as Greengairs, and believes that representatives from communities such as Greengairs must be involved in the process of designing this fund.

*S2M-960 Mr Ted Brocklebank: Support for Scottish Farmed Salmon Industry-That the Parliament congratulates Scottish Quality Salmon for the responsible manner in which it represented this vital Scottish industry during the recent food scare, following the publication of an American research paper that made highly misleading claims about Scottish farmed salmon and calls for all political parties to support publicly this important developing industry against any further dubious or commercially inspired attacks.
