Business Bulletin No. 112/2002

Friday 26 July 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-3291 Malcolm Chisholm: The Food Protection (Emergency Prohibitions) (Amnesic Shellfish Poisoning) (West Coast) (No.9) (Scotland) Order 2002—That the Health and Community Care Committee, in consideration of The Food Protection (Emergency Prohibitions) (Amnesic Shellfish Poisoning) (West Coast) (No.9) (Scotland) Order, recommends that the order be approved.

Supported by: Mr Frank McAveety*, Mrs Mary Mulligan*

*S1M-3290 Pauline McNeill: Hunterian Museum and Art Gallery—That the Parliament congratulates the Hunterian Museum and Art Gallery at the University of Glasgow on its ranking for its collection amongst the top three in the Scottish Museums Council National Audit.

*S1M-3289 Pauline McNeill: Partick 150th Anniversary—That the Parliament notes the 150th anniversary of the burgh of Partick; sends its warmest greetings to its citizens present and past, and notes the rich history of Partick from fishing village outside Glasgow in 1652 to its crucial role in the development of the Clyde shipbuilding industry.

*S1M-3288 Pauline McNeill: HMI Inspection of Glasgow College of Building and Printing —That the Parliament congratulates Glasgow College of Building and Printing on its excellent outcome in the HM Inspectorate of Education subject review; notes that, in particular, the college deserves praise for its monitoring of student destinations, its outreach initiatives for black and ethnic minority women, its commitment to lifelong learning and its efforts to ensure that 42% of students come from Social Inclusion Partnership areas, and offers its best wishes to the Principal and staff in meeting the challenge of Glasgow’s construction skills shortage.R

*S1M-3287 Brian Adam: Western Peripheral Route Funding—That the Parliament notes the Chancellor's spending review announcement; further notes that the Scottish Executive has transport as one of its five priorities, and calls upon the Executive to make the allocation of resources for the construction of the western peripheral route around Aberdeen, with a third bridge over the River Don, as its top priority.

*S1M-3286 Brian Adam: Pedestrian Crossing on North Anderson Drive, Aberdeen—That the Parliament calls on the Scottish Executive to instruct BEAR Scotland to install a pedestrian crossing on North Anderson Drive, Aberdeen, at a point at the northern end of Smithfield Drive and to the south of Hilton Drive.

*S1M-3285 Brian Adam: "Ditch the Dirt" Campaign—That the Parliament congratulates the Aberdeen Evening Express for launching its "Ditch the Dirt" campaign, which aims to change the attitudes of irresponsible dog owners who do not clean up their dog’s mess, and calls for a change in legislation to allow for on-the-spot fines for those people that refuse to clean up after their dogs.
