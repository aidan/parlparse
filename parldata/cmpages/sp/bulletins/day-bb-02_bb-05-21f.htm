Business Bulletin No. 83/2002

Tuesday 21 May 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-3136 Donald Gorrie: Royal Opening of Millennium Wheel, Falkirk-That the Parliament congratulates British Waterways, Falkirk Council and all the organisations and people who contributed to the creation and building of the Falkirk Millennium Wheel, which is a testament to Scotland's well-renowned and innovative engineering skills. and welcomes Her Majesty the Queen, who will officially open the Wheel on 24 May 2002, and whose presence is an indication of the significance of this imaginative project as a new landmark in central Scotland.

*S1M-3135 Donald Gorrie: Volunteers Week 2002-That the Parliament welcomes the efforts of Volunteer Development Scotland to encourage people to be volunteers during this year's Volunteers Week from 7 to 13 June 2002; congratulates and welcomes those who will be volunteering for the first time during that week; appreciates that very many of those volunteering will already be regular and committed volunteers who support individuals and communities in many indispensable ways, and commends the valuable contribution to Scottish life and communities made by volunteers from all walks of society.

*S1M-3134 Andrew Wilson: Transfers Under PFI/PPP - Protection of Staff Rights and Conditions-That the Parliament recognises concerns expressed by many employees, trade union members, commentators and concerned citizens about the operation of PFI/PPP contracts initiated by the public sector in Scotland; acknowledges that these concerns are based on a number of factors, including the excessive cost of capital provided, the quality of public service provided and the rights and conditions of staff employed under PFI/PPP contracts; further recognises that many of the alleged efficiency gains in PFI/PPP contracts in comparison to the public sector are made at the expense of staff rights and conditions, which, in turn, can have a significant negative impact on the quality of ongoing service; further recognises that there is growing concern that the rights and conditions of employees transferred out of the public sector in Scotland through PFI/PPP deals are less favourably protected than elsewhere in the UK; resolves that any future PFI/PPP contracts should make the detail of alleged efficiency savings publicly available and specify the impact that such savings have on staff rights and conditions so that public bodies entering into such agreements are fully accountable to the people that they serve, and further resolves that, in respect of employee transfers under the TUPE regulations from public bodies for which the Scottish Executive has any responsibility, transfer practice should adhere to the Cabinet Office Statement of Practice as a minimum standard.
