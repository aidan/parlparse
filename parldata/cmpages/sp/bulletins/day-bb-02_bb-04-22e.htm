Business Bulletin No. 63/2002

Monday 22 April 2002

Section E : Written questions lodged on 19 April 2002
  

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S1W-25229 Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people completing modern apprenticeships in the last two years have obtained national qualifications.

S1W-25230 Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessments are made of those companies and organisations which provide modern apprenticeship schemes for young men and women and how often such assessments take place.

S1W-25231 Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive what assistance is given to those organisations and companies which offer modern apprenticeship schemes to enable them to meet adequate skill and training standards.

S1W-25232 Brian Fitzpatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive what intimation it received, and when, of the e-mail message of 12.58 pm on 21 March 2002 to Scottish Prison Service (SPS) staff by Mr Tony Cameron, Chief Executive of the SPS, regarding the prison estate review.

S1W-25233 Brian Fitzpatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive what reports it has received or requested from the Scottish Prison Service (SPS) on the e-mail message of 12.58 pm on 21 March 2002 to SPS staff by Mr Tony Cameron, Chief Executive of the SPS, regarding the prison estate review.

S1W-25234 Brian Fitzpatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive, with reference to the e-mail message of 12.58 pm on 21 March 2002 to Scottish Prison Service (SPS) staff by Mr Tony Cameron, Chief Executive of the SPS, regarding the prison estate review, which of the proposals contained in its consultation on the review are elements of Executive policy or of SPS practices.

S1W-25235 Brian Fitzpatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure that key stakeholders and social partners are able fully to participate in discussions on its consultation on the prison estate review.

S1W-25236 Brian Fitzpatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has received from the Scottish Prison Service the report, Constructing the Future, prepared by Mr Eric Murch of HM Prison Low Moss on or about 21 April 2000 and, if so, when it received it.

S1W-25237 Brian Fitzpatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment has been made on its behalf of the report, Constructing the Future, prepared by Mr Eric Murch of HM Prison Low Moss on or about 21 April 2000.

S1W-25238 Brian Fitzpatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive what financial review, if any, has been made on its behalf of the investment decisions proposed in the report, Constructing the Future, prepared by Mr Eric Murch of HM Prison Low Moss on or about 21 April 2000.

S1W-25239 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will publish the annual accident figures for roads where route accident reduction plans have been implemented within the past three years.

S1W-25240 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of the recent interim report by the Chief Inspector of Prisons on HM Prison Kilmarnock, whether it will now publish full details of the number and categories of staff employed at that prison.

S1W-25241 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of the publication by Her Majesty's Government of the report Review of PFI and Market Testing in the Prison Service in regard to prisons in England and Wales, what its policy is in regard to the publication of staff numbers in any private prison in Scotland.

S1W-25242 Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive under what criteria the Rent Registration Service sets rent levels for Housing Benefit purposes.

S1W-25243 Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidelines there are for the Rent Registration Service on reducing rent levels previously set for Housing Benefit purposes in respect of buildings that do not meet acceptable standards.

S1W-25244 Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether local authorities can reduce rent levels set for Housing Benefit purposes by the Rent Registration Service in respect of properties that do not meet acceptable standards.

S1W-25245 Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it accepts the number of 1,416 acute hospital beds occupied full-time by patients with hospital-acquired infection (HAI) as estimated by Dr Andrew Walker in his report into HAI and bed use in the NHS.

S1W-25246 Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what account will be taken of Dr Andrew Walker's report into hospital-acquired infection and bed use in the NHS.

S1W-25247 Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact actions taken to tackle hospital-acquired infection are having.

S1W-25248 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has received about introducing capital city policing in Edinburgh; from whom any such representations have been received; whether capital city policing will be introduced in Edinburgh and, if so, what funding will be made available.

S1W-25249 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to promote or fund further bus park and ride schemes similar to Ferry Toll in Fife; what sites have been selected under any such plans, and what (a) funds have provided and (b) steps have been taken to date.

S1W-25250 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what its current position is on the financing of the M74 northern extension.

S1W-25251 Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the block grant is received in one payment to the Scottish Consolidated Fund or in instalments over the course of the financial year.

S1W-25252 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the private build, private operate option for prisons and, in particular, the projected more flexible use of staff and reduced labour costs under that option are consistent with the Memorandum of Understanding between the Executive and the Scottish Trades Union Congress.

S1W-25253 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive how much PricewaterhouseCoopers was paid for its Financial Review of Scottish Prison Service Estates Review and whether this fee was included in the costings for the Public Private Partnership options in the prison estates review.

S1W-25254 Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding it has received for the support of post offices in deprived urban areas in 2002-03 and how such funding will be utilised.

S1W-25255 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Minister for Tourism, Culture and Sport (a) was invited to and (b) attended the recent tourism summit hosted by the Secretary of State for Culture, Media and Sport and what the reasons are for the position on this matter.

S1W-25256Δ Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive when the consultation paper on the review of the law of diligence will be published.

S1W-25257 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what monitoring has taken place over the last three years of resources devoted by local authorities to the provision of an archaeological service in line with the National Planning Policy Guideline 5 and whether such resources are adequate, given the remit of local authorities in respect of archaeological issues raised on unscheduled monuments under both the local authority planning function and Executive initiatives on forestry and agri-environment schemes.

S1W-25258 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken in each of the last three years in order to encourage any local authorities that do not have a professional archaeology service in line with National Planning Policy Guideline 5 to establish such a service.

S1W-25259 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what monitoring has taken place of the effectiveness of National Planning Policy Guideline 5 and Planning Advice Note 42 and whether it will publish any statistics that it has gathered in this respect over the last three years.
