Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-4305 Rosie Kane: Charles Clarke Should Resign—That the Parliament is shocked by the announcement this week that the Home Office has not followed procedure regarding the release of foreign prisoners who have been convicted of murder, rape, sexual assault and paedophilia; is concerned that Charles Clarke, the Secretary of State for the Home Office, has accepted responsibility but has not yet resigned; demands a full and thorough independent investigation into these matters; questions the fact that often 20 agents from the Home Office are involved in every dawn raid, arrest and removal of asylum seekers where innocent women, men and children are taken into detention, many of whom are tagged, where cautioners are forced to pay substantial amounts of bail money to have asylum seekers released from detention and where these innocent individuals and families who have harmed no one are forced to sign on every week at the Home Office building in Brand Street, Glasgow, or at local police stations, and believes that this misplaced use of resources and poor management has contributed to the current situation and that Charles Clarke should resign immediately.

*S2M-4304 Michael Matheson: Scotland’s Support for the Paralympic Movement—That the Parliament recognises the growing development of the paralympic movement; believes that promoting and hosting international disability sporting events and paralympic training camps in Scotland in the run-up to the 2012 London Paralympics can help this development, will encourage more disabled people in Scotland to participate in sport and will provide excellent opportunities for Scotland’s elite disabled sportspeople to compete in their home country; recognises that participation in sport produces physical, psychological, and social benefits; believes that promoting disability sport may help change attitudes and foster a more inclusive society; notes also the potential economic benefits for local communities from hosting major disability sporting events and training camps; recognises that Scotland already has facilities which would provide ideal locations for such events and camps, and calls on the Scottish Executive to explore how Scotland can be promoted as a suitable venue for hosting international disability sporting events and to begin discussions with the London Organising Committee of the Olympic Games (LOCOG) on Scotland providing paralympic training camps before London 2012.

*S2M-4303 Jim Mather: Payment of Public Money to Lobbying Organisations—That the Parliament notes the payment of public money through non-departmental public bodies such as Scottish Enterprise and Scottish Water to private lobbying organisations such as the Confederation of British Industry (CBI), largely through subscriptions; is concerned that in the case of Scottish Enterprise £194,796.77 was paid by a public body to the CBI over six years; welcomes the decision of Scottish Water to stop paying money to the CBI, and recommends that, to avoid any potential conflict of interest, all public bodies reconsider the practice of supporting private lobbying organisations through membership subscriptions.

*S2M-4302 Richard Baker: North East Footballing Success—That the Parliament congratulates Deveronvale FC on winning the Highland League for the second time in four years; recognises the achievements of both Fraserburgh FC and Cove Rangers FC in reaching the final of the Highland League Cup; pays tribute to players and staff at all three clubs for their terrific achievements this season; believes that these achievements reflect the quality and strength of football in the north east, and wishes the teams well in the future.

*S2M-4301 Susan Deacon: OKtoAsk Gateway Helpline—That the Parliament welcomes the recent launch of the OKtoAsk Gateway helpline, which will improve access to information and support for parents across Scotland; congratulates Parenting Across Scotland’s partner organisations, One Parent Families Scotland, Capability Scotland, Children 1st, the Scottish Adoption Association, Couple Counselling Scotland and the Aberlour Childcare Trust, on this initiative; commends Scotland’s parents for the work that they do in bringing up the next generation; recognises that parenting and family life in modern societies is demanding, and believes that a tapestry of information and support should be available to parents across Scotland.

Supported by: Margaret Smith*, Mike Pringle*, Bruce Crawford*, Chris Ballance*, Maureen Macmillan*, Jackie Baillie*, Marlyn Glen*, Dr Sylvia Jackson*

*S2M-4300 Ross Finnie: The Draft Private Water Supplies (Notices) (Scotland) Regulations 2006—That the Environment and Rural Development Committee recommends that the draft Private Water Supplies (Notices) (Scotland) Regulations 2006 be approved.

Supported by: Rhona Brankin*

*S2M-4299 Peter Peacock: The Draft Joint Inspections (Scotland) Amendment Regulations 2006—That the Education Committee recommends that the draft Joint Inspections (Scotland) Amendment Regulations 2006 be approved.

Supported by: Robert Brown*

*S2M-4298 Cathy Jamieson: The Draft Public Appointments and Public Bodies etc. (Scotland) Act 2003 (Treatment of Office of Body as Specified Authority) Order 2006—That the Justice 2 Committee recommends that the draft Public Appointments and Public Bodies etc. (Scotland) Act 2003 (Treatment of Office or Body as Specified Authority) Order 2006 be approved.

Supported by: Hugh Henry*

*S2M-4297 Ms Maureen Watt: Promoting Doric—That the Parliament notes that the Doric language plays a vital communication role in the north east, which adds greatly to Scotland’s national heritage and further advances the cultural diversity of Scotland, and calls on the Scottish Executive to actively promote Doric and Lallan Scots as living languages.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson*, Alex Johnstone*, Roseanna Cunningham*, Fergus Ewing*, Linda Fabiani*, Rob Gibson*, Eleanor Scott*, Robin Harper*, Mr Andrew Welsh*, Fiona Hyslop*, Jim Mather*, Christine Grahame*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Brian Adam*, Bruce Crawford*
