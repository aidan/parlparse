Business Bulletin No. 146/2006: Monday 23 October 2006
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 26 October 2006

Question Time

General

1. Mr Alasdair Morrison: To ask the Scottish Executive how many eligible residents in the Western Isles have registered for the Air Discount Scheme and how many have used the scheme since its introduction. (S2O-10877)

2. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to regulate property factors. (S2O-10870)

3. Mr Andrew Arbuckle: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it still expects the final report of the Local Government Finance Review Committee to be published by the end of October. (S2O-10838)

4. Chris Ballance: To ask the Scottish Executive whether environmental or economic considerations are given greater weight when making decisions on routes to support through the Air Route Development Fund. (S2O-10810)

5. Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has for improving the railway line between Inverness and Aberdeen. (S2O-10813)

6. Rosie Kane: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the Home Secretary in relation to continued dawn raids in Scotland. (S2O-10805)

7. Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive how the additional funding to improve school facilities will directly raise the standard of education delivered within our schools. (S2O-10872)

8. Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans there are to improve the gender-proofing of policies across departmental portfolios. (S2O-10862)

9. Janis Hughes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to increase the number of neighbourhood wardens in light of their performance in tackling antisocial behaviour in our communities. (S2O-10865)

10. John Swinburne: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will address the issue of means-tested pensioners who are required to pay the water and sewerage element of the council tax, despite being exempt from the need to pay council tax. (S2O-10799)

Health and Community Care

1. Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to prevent further centralisation of the health service, in light of the paper by the Academy of Medical Royal Colleges, Centralisation and Specialisation of Hospital Services - bigger is not necessarily better for rural and remote communities. (S2O-10849)

2. Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has concerns about delays being experienced by cancer patients in receiving their treatment. (S2O-10800)

3. Mr Jim Wallace: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made of local authority requirements for residential care places for elderly people between now and 2014. (S2O-10842)

4. Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive what its estimate is of the number of NHS employees in Monklands Hospital in each of the next three years. (S2O-10832)

5. Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive how many dental students graduated from Scottish dental schools in each year from 2004 to 2006 but did not take up dental vocational training posts in Scotland. (S2O-10814)

6. Frances Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive why the integrated care pilot at Vale of Leven hospital has ended and what implications this has for the integrated care model in other areas. (S2O-10804)

7. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many additional community and residential care places it plans to create in the next four years to help people with alcohol and other addictions. (S2O-10848)

8. Mr Ted Brocklebank: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with NHS Fife regarding the timescale for the new Community Hospital and Health Centre for St Andrews to be operational. (S2O-10829)

9. Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive how it ensures that nutritional standards in hospitals are met. (S2O-10845)

10. Mr Frank McAveety: To ask the Scottish Executive what further plans are being considered to tackle obesity in Scotland’s population. (S2O-10871)

Environment and Rural Development

1. Mr Charlie Gordon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider funding the upgrading of waste management facilities in the City of Glasgow. (S2O-10863)

2. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what the future is of the Crofting Counties Agricultural Grants Scheme. (S2O-10869)

3. Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider the introduction of a new legal obligation to remedy long-running odour problems from the waste water treatment plant at Dalmuir experienced by the people of Clydebank. (S2O-10876)

4. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment has been made of the environmental impact of leaks from Scottish nuclear power stations. (S2O-10840)

5. Euan Robson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to review the fees for licences issued by the Scottish Environment Protection Agency and, if so, when. (S2O-10836)

6. Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will announce the outcome of the consultation on bye-laws for Loch Lomond. (S2O-10854)

7. Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive what analysis it has undertaken of the impact on the environment if security of energy supply from indigenous sources cannot be guaranteed in the foreseeable future. (S2O-10873)

8. Derek Brownlee: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Water Environment (Controlled Activities) (Scotland) Regulations 2005 are working satisfactorily. (S2O-10828)

9. Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what final proposals have been agreed to replace the interim bull hire arrangements for crofters and when they will be published. (S2O-10823)

10. Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to support dairy farming in Clydesdale. (S2O-10861)
