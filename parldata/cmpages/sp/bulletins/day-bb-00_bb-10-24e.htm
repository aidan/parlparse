BUSINESS BULLETIN 158 / 2000

Tuesday 24 October 2000

Section E - Written questions lodged on 23 October 2000

 

 

S1W-10553 Dorothy-Grace Elder: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it plans to take to ensure that OKI Data Corporation in Cumbernauld offers maximum support to the 240 full time and 80 part-time employees who will lose their jobs as a result of the company relocating its production lines.

S1W-10554 Dorothy-Grace Elder: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to prevent international corporations relocating their manufacturing away from Scotland as a result of the level of the pound.

S1W-10555 Dorothy-Grace Elder: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has made to Her Majesty's Government regarding any damage caused to Scottish manufacturing due to international companies relocating their production centres as a direct result of the level of the pound.

S1W-10556 Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive, following sight of the most recent scientific studies in the Inner Sound and Lochs Torridon, Carron, Kishorn, Duich, Alsh and Horn, what measures it proposes to take with regard to creel and trawl fishing in these areas.

S1W-10557 Mrs Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive what arrangements it has put in place to monitor and report on progress towards the implementation of the key challenges and targets of Sport 21, the national strategy for sport in Scotland.

S1W-10558 Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what analysis has been undertaken of the economic impact of any increased incidence of heather beetle on moorland.

S1W-10559 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with Her Majesty’s Government regarding any delays to the new Scottish Air Traffic Control Centre in Prestwick and their effect on local jobs and whether it will place copies of any relevant correspondence in the Scottish Parliament Information Centre.

S1W-10560 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to monitor the number of local authorities that make cycle training available and the number of primary six and seven pupils who benefit from such training, and what action it is taking to monitor the effectiveness and take-up of cycle training schemes generally.

S1W-10561 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what charge was paid by airlines per passenger and by category of passenger at each of the airports run by Highlands and Islands Airports Ltd, broken down for each of the last five years.

S1W-10562 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what the passenger numbers were at each airport run by Highlands and Islands Airports Ltd; what the income was from (a) passenger charges and (b) all charges, and what the running costs at each airport were, broken down for each of the last five years.
