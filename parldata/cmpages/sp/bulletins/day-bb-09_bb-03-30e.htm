Business Bulletin No. 57/2009: Monday 30 March 2009
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 27 March 2009

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-22418Δ Joe FitzPatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive what goals it expects Registers of Scotland to achieve in 2009-10.

S3W-22419Δ Bob Doris: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to increase the cost of applications for criminal record checks carried out by Disclosure Scotland under Part V of the Police Act 1997.

S3W-22421 Jamie Hepburn: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are specific standards for the education of children with an autistic spectrum disorder and, if so, how these standards are monitored.

S3W-22422 Jamie Hepburn: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people there are with an autistic spectrum disorder, broken down by (a) age group, (b) local authority area and (c) NHS board area, also expressed as a percentage of the population.

S3W-22423 Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what conclusions were reached about postbuses at the meeting between the Minister for Enterprise, Energy and Tourism and the Royal Mail on 16 March 2009.

S3W-22424 Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to protect tenants in the private rented sector from the threat of homelessness due to landlords defaulting on their mortgages.

S3W-22425 Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive how much funding Glasgow Housing Association has received in each year since 2003, broken down by type of funding.

S3W-22426 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-21881 by Keith Brown on 26 March 2009, whether it agrees that there has been no increase in the percentage of pupils in primaries one to three in classes of size 18 or less in Dumfries and Galloway between 2006 and 2008.

S3W-22427 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-21886 by Keith Brown on 27 March 2009, whether it is aware that the decision to procure replacement buildings for Lincluden, Cargenbridge and Troqueer primary schools via traditional procurement methods was taken by the education and community services committee of Dumfries and Galloway Council on 27 June 2006 and, if so, on what basis it considers that the current administration commissioned these schools.
