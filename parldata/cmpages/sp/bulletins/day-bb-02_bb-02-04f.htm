Business Bulletin No. 21/2002

Monday 4 February 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-2690 Michael Russell: "Scottish Six" News Bulletin-That the Parliament supports a "Scottish Six" main evening BBC television news bulletin, produced and edited in Scotland rather than in London and mixing an appropriate blend of Scottish, UK and international news, similar to the excellent format of BBC Radio Scotland; notes that the Broadcasting Council for Scotland is in favour of a "Scottish Six", that 51 BBC Scotland journalists have publicly supported this important broadcasting reform and that opinion surveys show that a "Scottish Six" is supported by 69 per cent of the people of Scotland; believes that it is absurd that, in post-devolution Scotland, viewers north of the border are still subject to London-based stories about English health and education issues, which are very often misleading for a Scots audience, while Scottish health and education are ignored by the BBC's London-centric output, and expresses astonishment that Tom Harris MP should describe Scottish-based news programming as "parochial" (The Herald, 1 February 2002), regarding this as a slur on the professionalism, editorial judgement, and abilities of BBC Scotland journalists and management.

*S1M-2689 Christine Grahame: Radio Borders-That the Parliament congratulates Radio Borders on celebrating its 12th anniversary and recognises the important service provided by local radio stations in airing matters of concern to their broadcasting constituencies, thus making them integral to the political and democratic process.

*S1M-2688 Janis Hughes: Public Concern at Work-That the Parliament welcomes the establishment of an office in Scotland by Public Concern at Work, congratulates the charity on the launch of a dedicated Scottish helpline number (0141 550 7572) offering confidential advice to Scottish workers about malpractice in the workplace; urges employers from all sectors in Scotland to work constructively with Public Concern at Work, and calls upon the Scottish Executive to recognise the role that public interest whistleblowing can and does play in increasing openness and accountability in the workplace.

*S1M-2687# Alex Neil: Untenable Position of Independent Assessors-That the Parliament notes with concern the untenable position of the 12 new Independent Assessors appointed on 29 January 2002; believes that none of the 12 can command public confidence and that these appointments should be annulled, and considers that it should investigate this shoddy process and, in particular, should examine (a) why half the Independent Assessors appointed were active Labour Party members and (b) why only 27 people applied for these vacancies.

*S1M-2686 Cathy Jamieson: Scottish Qualifications Authority Bill-That the Parliament agrees to the general principles of the Scottish Qualifications Authority Bill.

Supported by: Nicol Stephen*

*S1M-2685# Mr Brian Monteith: Funding the Loch Lomond and Trossachs National Park-That the Parliament notes with concern that the level of funding to be given by the Scottish Executive for the first year of operation of its proposed Loch Lomond and Trossachs National Park at £2.8 million is half the amount of
£5.6 million required by the Park's interim committee; further notes that this contrasts sharply with the £14 million of taxpayers money which is being spent on landscaping the new Parliament building at Holyrood, and believes that the Scottish Executive should, without delay, reconsider its grant aid to the Loch Lomond and Trossachs National Park and should commission research to establish how many additional tourists and what impact on traffic flows there will be following the creation of the National Park, so that any further demands on the local transport infrastructure can be determined.

S1M-2684# Cathy Peattie: Draft Guidance on the Circumstances in which Parents may Choose to Educate their Children at Home-That the Parliament welcomes the Scottish Executive's consultation on the draft Guidance on the Circumstances in which Parents may Choose to Educate their Children at Home; believes that this will provide an opportunity for the views of children and parents, who clearly have concerns about the content of the draft guidance, to be taken into account, and further believes that the right to choose education outwith school should be facilitated with due respect for data protection and human rights legislation and that the guidance should not discriminate unreasonably between those children who have never attended and those who are removed from state schools.

S1M-2683 Trish Godman: Scottish Veterans of the International Brigade-That the Parliament offers warm greetings to the very small band of Scottish veterans of the British Battalion of the International Brigade; notes that they and their late Scottish comrades formed a full 25% of the British Battalion; acknowledges their courage and radical determination to assist the democratically elected Spanish Government in stemming the ugly tide of fascism so many years ago, at a time when some European governments sought to appease the fascists in Spain, Germany and Italy; recalls that these Scottish veterans, along with other veterans of the International Brigade, were honoured by the Spanish Government on the 60th Anniversary of the Spanish Civil War with honorary Spanish citizenship, and offers its best wishes to these distinguished Scottish veterans of a distant war against fascism.

Supported by: Dorothy-Grace Elder*

S1M-2682 Trish Godman: The Co-op and Labelling in Braille-That the Parliament commends the Co-operative Society (Co-op) on the introduction of labelling in braille for products sold in its retail stores and shops; believes that this initiative will be welcomed by blind and partially-sighted customers of the Co-op, and urges other large retailers to follow the example set by the Co-op in providing this kind of assistance to such members of the community, whose needs are too often ignored.

Supported by: Dorothy-Grace Elder*

S1M-2681 Mr Duncan Hamilton: Rural Postal Services-That the Parliament notes the decision by the Postal Services Commission to bring an end to Royal Mail's monopoly over letter deliveries; expresses its concern over the threat that this may pose to postal services in rural areas, and calls upon the Scottish Executive to do all that it can in order to ensure that these services are protected.

Supported by: Dorothy-Grace Elder*

S1M-2680 Tommy Sheridan: Funds Paid to the Third Age Group-That the Parliament notes with grave concern the most recent allegations regarding links between the constituency office of former First Minister Henry McLeish in Glenrothes and the Third Age Group and regarding the payment of £40,000 in grants by Fife Council to the charity for two years after it ceased to exist; believes that, once again, activities in the constituency of the former First Minister are likely to cast a deep shadow over his activity as a member of the Scottish Parliament, and calls for an independent inquiry into relations between Fife Council, the Third Age Group and the former First Minister's constituency office in order to determine who was responsible for granting such funds to the Third Age Group and what they were used for.

S1M-2679 Iain Gray: The Draft Town and Country Planning (Fees for Applications and Deemed Applications) (Scotland) Amendment Regulations 2002-That the Transport and the Environment Committee recommends that the draft Town and Country Planning (Fees for Applications and Deemed Applications) (Scotland) Amendment Regulations 2002 be approved.

Supported by: Ms Margaret Curran

S1M-2678 Malcolm Chisholm: Community Care and Health (Scotland) Bill-That the Parliament agrees that the Community Care and Health (Scotland) Bill be passed.

Supported by: Hugh Henry, Mrs Mary Mulligan

S1M-2677 Mr Brian Monteith: Widening Access Accolade for Stirling and other Universities-That the Parliament congratulates the University of Stirling on coming first in The Times Higher Education Supplement's "Access Elite" list of universities in the UK that perform best at widening access to pupils from poorer backgrounds, based on four criteria, namely (a) inclusive access, (b) low dropout rates, (c) quality of teaching and (d) research and applauds the University of Strathclyde, Glasgow School of Art, Heriot-Watt University and the University of Dundee for taking the next four places to give Scotland a clean sweep of the UK's top five higher education institutions in this survey.

Supported by: Mr Keith Harding, Mr David Davidson, Alex Johnstone, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton

S1M-2676 Mr Keith Harding: Freemasons-That the Parliament notes the declaration of Mr Keith Harding MSP as a Freemason in the register of members' interests on 31 January 2002 and encourages all other MSPs who are members of the craft, a body of men dedicated to charitable good works, to make similar voluntary declarations in support of the Consultative Steering Group's principles of openness, accountability and transparency.

Supported by: Mr David Davidson, Mr Brian Monteith*, Mr Jamie McGrigor*, Dorothy-Grace Elder*

S1M-2675 Ross Finnie: Water Industry (Scotland) Bill-That the Parliament agrees that the Water Industry (Scotland) Bill be passed.

Supported by: Allan Wilson

S1M-2674 Mr Andy Kerr: Budget (Scotland) (No.3) Bill-That the Parliament agrees that the Budget (Scotland) (No.3) Bill be passed.

Supported by: Mr Jack McConnell, Mr Jim Wallace, Patricia Ferguson, Peter Peacock

S1M-2673 Michael Russell: OECD Programme for International Student Assessment: Scottish Report-That the Parliament notes the OECD Programme for International Student Assessment: Scottish Report which confirmed that Scotland's young people have the potential to be the best in the world in terms of school attainment but that they have not yet reached that position; congratulates students and teachers on their performance in the light of continual interference from a Scottish Executive which is not meeting its own targets in education, and notes that the report specifically states that "the performance in reading literacy of students (at age 15) varied substantially with the amount of time they read for enjoyment", thus confirming the importance of enjoyment and personal satisfaction in educational attainment in contrast to imposing the treadmill of over assessment which remains the Scottish Executive's policy.

S1M-2672 Mr Brian Monteith: Mansfield Place Church-That the Parliament congratulates the Mansfield Traquair Trust for its success to date in restoring the fabric of the Catholic Apostolic Church at Mansfield Place, Edinburgh, which is an excellent example of romanesque style by Sir Robert Rowand Anderson and home to the most uplifting, scintillating and luminescent murals from the arts and craft movement by Phoebe Anna Traquair, completed in 1901, but now, sadly, in great need of careful restoration; notes that the Scottish Council for Voluntary Organisations has already agreed a long term lease to use the building as its new headquarters, thereby providing a secure basis for the future, and calls upon Historic Scotland, national lottery organisations and other public agencies to continue to give the Mansfield Traquair Trust every assistance so that the building and its superior decorative illuminations are available for the enjoyment of the Scottish people and art lovers world-wide.

S1M-2671 Roseanna Cunningham: Perth Bid for New SNH Headquarters-That the Parliament recognises that Scottish Natural Heritage intends to move its headquarters out of Edinburgh; notes previous commitments by the Scottish Executive on the dispersion of civil service and other public sector jobs, and welcomes the submission from Perth and Kinross Council highlighting the suitability of a number of sites in Perthshire.

Supported by: Mr John Swinney

S1M-2670 Tommy Sheridan: Asylum Policy of Australian Government-That the Parliament condemns the Australian Government for its inhumane asylum policy which has led directly to over 300 desperate and imprisoned asylum seekers embarking on hunger strikes to highlight their case for asylum and their appalling treatment by the Australian Government; finds this inhumane treatment particularly offensive having recently witnessed Holocaust memorial services across Scotland; regards it as a shameful fact of history that, had those seeking asylum from Nazi Germany been allowed into neighbouring countries, many lives could have been saved, and therefore urges the Scottish Executive to make representations to the Australian Government to register our opposition to its asylum policy.

Supported by: Dorothy-Grace Elder*

S1M-2669# Elaine Thomson: World Heritage Site Status for the Cairngorms-That the Parliament congratulates the United Nations on its co-ordination of the International Year of Mountains; believes that an appropriate way to celebrate the year would be to ensure that the Cairngorm Mountain Range, the largest continuous area of high ground above 1,000 metres in Britain, is designated a World Heritage Site, and therefore considers that the Scottish Executive should speedily propose discussions with Her Majesty's Government to achieve the ultimate international acclaim of World Heritage Status for this beautiful and environmentally unique part of our heritage.

Supported by: Maureen Macmillan*, Rhoda Grant*, Mr Kenneth Macintosh*, Marilyn Livingstone*, Scott Barrie*, Jackie Baillie*, Dr Sylvia Jackson*, Dorothy-Grace Elder*

S1M-2668 Rhoda Grant: Campbeltown - Ballycastle Ferry-That the Parliament welcomes the announcement by the Scottish Executive of the call for tenders for the Campbeltown - Ballycastle ferry; recognises that this announcement is the product of joint working between the Scottish Executive, the Northern Ireland Executive and the Scotland Office, and urges the Scottish Executive to ensure that the tendering process is concluded as soon as is practically possible to ensure that the Campbeltown area can benefit from a ferry link with Northern Ireland.

S1M-2667 Christine Grahame: Freedom of Information (Scotland) Bill-That the Justice 1 Committee considers the Freedom of Information (Scotland) Bill at Stage 2 in the following order: sections 1 to 3, schedule 1, sections 4 to 8, sections 10 and 11, sections 14 to 42, schedule 2, sections 43 to 54, schedule 3, sections 55 to 61, section 9, sections 12 and 13, sections 62 to 73.

S1M-2666 Mr Brian Monteith: Innovative Philosophy Classes at Sunnyside Primary School, Alloa-That the Parliament warmly congratulates the headteacher, Paul Cleghorn, and the teaching staff at Sunnyside Primary School, Alloa, Clackmannanshire, for being the first school in the country to introduce an innovative programme of philosophy classes aimed at developing pupils' thinking and questioning skills.

Supported by: Mr Keith Harding, Mr George Reid

S1M-2665 Ms Margo MacDonald: New Hospitals-That the Parliament, whilst welcoming the new Royal Infirmary of Edinburgh, notes that it will cost £184 million to build and will cost taxpayers some £1 billion to rent over its lifetime; agrees with the report by NHS consultant Dr Matthew Dunnigan, The Downsized Hypothesis: Value for Money, that the outdated analysis of patients needs in the Lothian area used to determine the hospital's size has resulted in an underestimate of the number of acute beds required; regrets that the Scottish Executive has adopted the policy of previous Conservative Governments in Westminster which enriched the private sector and impoverished the NHS, and therefore calls on the Scottish Executive to review its opposition to establishing a Scottish trust for public investment to fund future projects similar to the new Royal Infirmary of Edinburgh.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan, Michael Matheson, Alex Neil, Stewart Stevenson, Dorothy-Grace Elder*

S1M-2664 Donald Gorrie: UK Life League-That the Parliament condemns unreservedly the tactics employed by the UK Life League, particularly in terms of the horrific practice of using photos of aborted foetuses and the personal tragedy of individuals in the public eye, for extremely dubious political ends; regrets that any group can deliberately misuse such material, particularly with the specific intention of inducing fear, misinformation and confusion amongst women; notes that the UK Life League website is a haven for such propaganda, which is almost entirely based on falsehood and lies, including the appropriation of other groups' logos and misrepresentation of them to imply their support for the "values" of the UK Life League, and reaffirms its commitment to freedom of speech for all pressure groups, including legitimate pro-life organisations, while remaining absolutely committed to the principle that it is a woman's right to choose.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan, Mr Kenneth Gibson, Linda Fabiani, Mr Jamie Stone, Fiona McLeod, Nora Radcliffe

S1M-2663 Roseanna Cunningham: Mike Tyson-That the Parliament notes and applauds the decision of the Nevada Athletic Commission in the United States of America not to approve an application from Mike Tyson for a licence to box in Las Vegas; expresses its concern at speculation that the fight might be re-arranged to a UK venue; recollects the anger that was caused by the Home Secretary's decision to allow Mike Tyson, a convicted rapist, to enter the UK for a fight in Glasgow on 24 June 2000; further notes that Mike Tyson has at no time expressed public remorse for the crimes of violence for which he has been convicted; expresses the hope that boxing promoters and venue managers will resist suggestions that a second Tyson fight be held in Scotland, and calls upon the Scottish Executive to make a firm commitment that it will oppose the licensing of any boxing match involving Mike Tyson in Scotland and that it will make representations to oppose any visa application that might be made.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson, Tommy Sheridan, Michael Matheson, Alex Neil, Dorothy-Grace Elder*

S1M-2662 Margaret Smith: energywatch "Stop Now!" campaign-That the Parliament welcomes the energywatch "Stop Now!" campaign which is designed to put a stop to unscrupulous sales tactics used by some gas and electricity companies which are on the increase and which, customers report, can include lying, bullying, misleading requests and intimidation; supports the demands made by energywatch to ensure that the gas and electricity companies' house is put in order by (a) the Office of Gas and Electricity Markets (Ofgem) keeping and strengthening the operating licensing conditions which the energy companies must comply with and which are due to expire in March 2002, (b) Ofgem using its power to fine unscrupulous energy companies, (c) the energy companies establishing a "rogues' register" of sales agencies and individuals who fail to meet acceptable standards, (d) the energy companies paying proper compensation to consumers for any distress and inconvenience caused by their sales malpractices and (e) the establishment of a standard format for energy supply contracts so that consumers are able to understand contracts and compare them with other suppliers; welcomes the commitment of energywatch to work with the police to combat bogus caller crime; urges the Scottish Executive to work in partnership with Scottish energy companies to improve consumer relations, and commends energywatch for raising the profile of such important consumer issues.

Supported by: Robert Brown, Tommy Sheridan, Dorothy-Grace Elder*

S1M-2657 Robin Harper: energywatch "Stop Now!" Campaign-That the Parliament notes with concern the rising trend of complaints against energy supply companies for malpractice and maladministration relating to their sales techniques and transfer processes; welcomes the launch of the energywatch "Stop Now!" campaign aimed at tackling this trend, and calls upon the energy supply companies to co-operate fully with energywatch and for the Office of Gas and Electricity Markets to use its available powers to ensure that customers are no longer transferred from supplier to supplier against their will and without adequate information or under duress.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson, Sarah Boyack, Tommy Sheridan

S1M-2655 Tommy Sheridan: Representation on the European Union Convention on the Future of Europe-That the Parliament notes with regret that Scotland will have no representation at the European Union Convention on the Future of Europe and calls on the Scottish Executive to make representations to Her Majesty's Government and the European Union to secure direct representation on the convention.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson, Dorothy-Grace Elder*

S1M-2653 David McLetchie: New Royal Infirmary of Edinburgh-That the Parliament congratulates all those involved in the new Royal Infirmary of Edinburgh project, which has now started to treat patients and was completed on time and within budget; notes that this essential development would not have proceeded without the foresight of successive Conservative Secretaries of State for Scotland nor without the use of the innovative PFI scheme, and calls on Lothian University Hospitals NHS Trust to retain the historic name of "Simpson" for its maternity unit.

Supported by: Mr Keith Harding, Mr Brian Monteith, Mary Scanlon

S1M-2652 Johann Lamont: Digital Audio in Visual Impairment Education (DAVIE)-That the Parliament congratulates Darnley Primary School in the Glasgow Pollok constituency, Glasgow City Council, and the Royal National Institute for the Blind Scotland (RNIB) on their excellent, pioneering and challenging work in supporting young people with visual impairment in mainstream education through the use of the innovative and much-praised RNIB equipment, Digital Audio in Visual Impairment Education (DAVIE); celebrates the work of the staff involved and the benefit experienced by all pupils in Darnley because of this project; notes that RNIB revealed in a recent research report, Shaping the Future, that, while 70% of blind and partially-sighted pupils in Scotland attend their local primary school, almost a quarter of such pupils do not receive the support they require; further notes the support of Scottish Power plc in underpinning the start of the Scottish roll-out of this initiative through the award of a £132,000 grant, following an initial £42,000 grant awarded by the Scottish Executive, to pilot DAVIE in Darnley Primary School, and urges the Executive and the appropriate committees of the Parliament to consider how this crucial work in the integration of, and support for, visually impaired pupils and students in mainstream education might best be supported and developed across Scotland.

Supported by: Michael Matheson, Scott Barrie, Tommy Sheridan, Trish Godman

S1M-2651 Ms Wendy Alexander: The Draft Forth Estuary Transport Authority Order 2002-That the Transport and the Environment Committee recommends that the draft Forth Estuary Transport Authority Order 2002 be approved.

Supported by: Lewis Macdonald
