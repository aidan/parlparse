Business Bulletin No. 14/2009: Thursday 22 January 2009
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 29 January 2009

Question Time

General

1. Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to its announcement on 24 August 2007 that it intends to replace the prison in Peterhead and close Craiginches prison in Aberdeen, what plans it has to establish new community prison facilities in Aberdeen. (S3O-5754)

2. Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many houses for social rent it expects to be completed in 2008-09 and 2009-10. (S3O-5705)

3. Ian McKee: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to encourage physical activity among school children. (S3O-5734)

4. Jamie Hepburn: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made by the Working Group on Scotland Without Nuclear Weapons. (S3O-5730)

5. Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on analysing the responses to the consultation on draft guidance for local authorities on the use of biometric technology in schools. (S3O-5739)

6. Keith Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will give an update on what progress has been made with the UK Government on allowing fatal accident inquiries to be held in Scotland for Scottish armed services personnel and others killed in accidents overseas. (S3O-5718)

7. Alison McInnes: To ask the Scottish Executive what the estimated completion date is for the replacement of HMP Peterhead. (S3O-5749)

8. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive how scrutiny and other services in relation to the NHS would be protected in the proposed merger of NHS Quality Improvement Scotland, the Mental Welfare Commission for Scotland and the Care Commission. (S3O-5672)

9. Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive, following the Public Petitions Committee debate on 23 January 2009, what plans the Scottish Government has to reduce knife carrying and knife crime, especially among young people. (S3O-5661)

10. Not Lodged

Finance and Sustainable Growth

1. Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to tackle the effect of the global economic downturn on employment in light of the latest employment statistics. (S3O-5745)

2. Stuart McMillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what the benefits have been of its six-point economic recovery programme. (S3O-5736)

3. Peter Peacock: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made since May 2007 on dualling the A9 north of the Drumochter pass. (S3O-5709)

4. James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in employing a chief executive for the Scottish Futures Trust. (S3O-5692)

5. Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met representatives of ScotRail to discuss ticket pricing. (S3O-5733)

6. Margaret Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Cabinet Secretary for Finance and Sustainable Growth last met representatives from Glasgow City Council. (S3O-5683)

7. Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress was made in respect of the Glasgow Crossrail project at the meeting on 7 January 2009 between Scottish Government officials and Strathclyde Partnership for Transport. (S3O-5680)

8. Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to reduce levels of sickness absence in the public sector. (S3O-5658)

9. Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive how much it spent on private consultants in 2008. (S3O-5663)

10. Tom McCabe: To ask the Scottish Executive to what extent the M80 Stepps to Haggs project will improve journey times, reliability and safety. (S3O-5698)

11. Nicol Stephen: To ask the Scottish Executive which other local authorities are being considered for extra funding of the kind given to the City of Edinburgh Council to reflect its status as capital city. (S3O-5757)

12. Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is promoting economic growth in the south of Scotland. (S3O-5747)

13. Alasdair Allan: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken to monitor the changeover from British Airways to Flybe on island air routes to ensure that service levels have been maintained. (S3O-5716)

14. Mr Frank McAveety: To ask the Scottish Executive what meetings the Cabinet Secretary for Finance and Sustainable Growth has had with Glasgow City Council to discuss the Scottish Futures Trust. (S3O-5697)

15. Bill Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that parameters like gross national product or gross domestic product, in a European context, cannot on their own measure sustainable growth and that parameters that take account of externalities such as happiness, wellbeing and environmental impacts should be given at least equal weight. (S3O-5744)

16. Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it will take to increase the numbers of installations of microgeneration devices. (S3O-5687)

17. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive what will be on its list of priorities for development across Scotland when it next meets leaders of industry and local authorities. (S3O-5684)

18. Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance it has given to public bodies on selling land at the lowest point in the market. (S3O-5659)

19. Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to consider project bank accounts for its own construction contracts. (S3O-5665)

20. Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will announce its decision on the Beauly to Denny power line application. (S3O-5704)
