Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an “R”.

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

* S2M-2288 Cathy Jamieson:The Draft Land Reform (Scotland) Act 2003 (Modification) Order 2005—That the Justice 1 Committee recommends that the draft Land Reform ( Scotland) Act 2003 (Modification) Order 2005 be approved.

Supported by: Hugh Henry*

* S2M-2287# Margaret Smith:Shared Equity—That the Parliament commends Communities Scotland and the City of Edinburgh Council for the efforts they are making to deal with the problems of lack of affordable housing in Edinburgh, particularly for first time buyers, essential public sector workers and those on low incomes; welcomes the Scottish Executive’s discussion paper on shared equity, published in November 2004; considers that the Executive should implement shared equity pilot schemes as a matter of urgency, noting that such schemes may represent a solution for owners in Pennywell faced with the demolition of their homes as part of the council’s PPP 2 proposals for Craigroyston High School, and believes that the Executive should act immediately to review home loss payments paid under the Land Compensation (Scotland) Act 1973 with a view to at least bringing them into line with minimum and maximum amounts paid in England.

* S2M-2286 Scott Barrie:Pride in the Job—That the Parliament congratulates Laing O’Rourke Scotland Ltd’s Gillian Jenkins from Crossford, Dunfermline, on winning the National House-Building Council’s Pride in the Job Supreme Award; recognises that she is the first woman to win this highest accolade in the house-building industry in the award’s 24-year history; notes that Pride in the Job recognises excellence amongst site managers in UK house-building; encourages best practice and drives up standards in the industry, and hopes that Gillian’s award will act as a shining example of achievement for all women working in the construction industry.

* S2M-2285 Richard Lochhead:Good Luck Message to Scottish Schools Debating Team—That the Parliament notes the commencement of the World Schools Debating Championships between 9 and 18 February 2005 in Calgary, Alberta, Canada; further notes that Scotland has won the competition on two previous occasions and reached the quarter-finals of the 2004 tournament; believes that debating is an activity which should be encouraged at school level due to the positive impacts it has on knowledge and understanding, confidence and critical thinking and the way in which it promotes positive engagement in society, and takes the opportunity to wish the Scottish team of Gabriel Brady (George Heriot’s School), Rose Grogan (Aberdeen Grammar School), Gareth Jones (Robert Gordon’s College), Jonathan Lafferty (St Aloysius’ College) and Beki Muinde (St Margaret’s School for Girls) the very best of luck in the competition and is certain that they will do Scotland proud.

Supported by: Jim Mather*, Brian Adam*, Bill Butler*, Eleanor Scott*, Ms Sandra White*, Linda Fabiani*, Fergus Ewing*, Mr Stewart Maxwell*

* S2M-2284 Mr Tom McCabe:Smoking, Health and Social Care (Scotland) Bill: Financial Resolution—That the Parliament, for the purposes of any Act of the Scottish Parliament resulting from the Smoking, Health and Social Care (Scotland) Bill, agrees to any expenditure or increase in expenditure of a kind referred to in Rule 9.12.3(b)(ii) or (iii) of the Parliament’s Standing Orders arising in consequence of the Act.

Supported by: Mr Andy Kerr*, Tavish Scott*, Rhona Brankin*

* S2M-2283 Mr Ted Brocklebank:60th Anniversary of the Liberation of Auschwitz—That the Parliament recognises the suffering of all those who died at the Nazis’ hands in Auschwitz, which was liberated by Allied troops 60 years ago this month, and particularly recognises, and pays tribute to, the courage and fortitude of the survivors of Auschwitz, including Zoë Polanska Palmer of Broughty Ferry, whose story of personal survival against all the odds is told in her autobiography, Yalta Victim.

* S2M-2282 Mr David Davidson:Eating Disorders Awareness Week—That the Parliament notes Eating Disorders Awareness Week from 6 to 12 February 2005; welcomes the Health Committee’s inquiry into eating disorder services in Scotland, taking evidence from sufferers, carers, support groups and health professionals in the field, and further asks that the Scottish Executive takes note of the evidence and proceeds to take action on any recommendations of the Health Committee.

* S2M-2281 Dr Jean Turner:Lack of NHS Board Funding—That the Parliament urges NHS boards to carry out immunisation, especially for mumps in the 13 to 25 years age group and for 17 to 20-year olds who were only offered a single dose of MMR through a catch-up campaign in 1988, who have not been offered a second dose and who have not been exposed to an infection of mumps due to low levels of infection during their primary school years; believes that GPs should not be hindered in administering immunisation due to the financial constraints of NHS boards, and further believes that the Scottish Executive should intervene to prevent delay in order to protect these young people.

* S2M-2280 Dr Jean Turner:Drug Rehabilitation versus Damage Limitation—That the Parliament commends the work of organisations like the New Horizons project, which offers drug users treatment and rehabilitation and links to training and employment, steering them to steady jobs away from addiction, and the Maxie Richards Foundation for its work with drug addicts in getting them away from a life of drug-taking and crime, providing residential care and supporting drug addicts to give up the habit forever; believes that methadone can become a substitute of one drug for another if used inappropriately; calls on the Scottish Executive to tackle drug addiction strongly by exploring all avenues of treatment and rehabilitation, especially detox, residential approaches and particularly by encouraging abstinence rather than damage limitation; notes the increased use of cocaine and crack cocaine in younger age groups and also the spread of drug misuse outwith the cities, and believes that the focus should be on the prevention of the spread of drugs within all our communities.
