BUSINESS BULLETIN 56 / 2000

Monday 27 March 2000

Section E - Written questions lodged on 24 March 2000

 

 

S1W-5580 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive which members and staff of the EU Commission Mr Jack McConnell met on 21 March 2000 during his visit to Brussels, and what issues were discussed.

 

S1W-5581 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive by what process external finance limits and other borrowing limits for public agencies within its responsibility are decided and, in particular, what role Her Majesty’s Treasury has in the process.

 

S1W-5582 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of mental health spending is allocated to (a) institutionalised care and (b) care in the community.

 

S1W-5583 Mr Keith Harding: To ask the Scottish Executive what total amount was raised in non-domestic rates in 1999-2000, and what total amount will be raised in 2000-01.

 

S1W-5584 George Lyon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will give details of the financial package outlined in the National Goose Forum report.

 

S1W-5585 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list the meetings at which it has been represented of (a) the European Union Council of Ministers and (b) other European Union bodies since 1 July 1999.

 

S1W-5586 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what share of the £280 million additional funding on roads recently announced by the Secretary of State for Transport, Environment and the Regions will accrue to Scotland.

 

S1W-5587 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Digital Scotland Taskforce will report, whether the report will contain recommendations and what the arrangements will be for its publication. R

 

S1W-5588 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive what consultation process will follow the publication of the report of the Digital Scotland Taskforce and how long any consultation process is likely to take. R

 

S1W-5589 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive what proportion of its communications with local authorities are carried out electronically and what targets it has to increase this proportion. R

 

S1W-5590 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive what mechanisms are in place to monitor how much of its correspondence and publication and distribution of documents is carried out electronically (a) internally and (b) to other bodies and individuals. R

 

S1W-5591 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any of the additional resources for the NHS announced by the Chancellor of the Exchequer will be used in Scotland to (a) alleviate NHS Trust financial deficits, (b) finance the 1270 unfilled vacancies for nurses or (c) enable bed-blocking patients to access care in the community.

 

S1W-5592 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how much of the additional money being received as a result of the Chancellor of the Exchequer’s budget will be allocated to each health board in Scotland and for what purpose the money will be used.

 

S1W-5593 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how much was spent on mental health in (a) 1999-2000, (b) 1998-99 and (c) 1997-98.

 

S1W-5594 Mr Keith Harding: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Minister for Finance will provide the additional information on non-domestic rate calculation as referred to in column 544 of the Official Report of the Local Government Committee, 8th meeting, on 7 March 2000.

 

S1W-5595 Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive how it proposes to support and expand the school library service, particularly in relation to the function of school libraries as a central IT resource for schools and the role of librarians in facilitating use of the Internet and CD-ROMS.

 

S1W-5596 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will complete the selection of Special Protection Areas under the EC Wild Birds Directive (79/409/EEC) on land in Scotland.

 

S1W-5597 Tricia Marwick: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to improve the rights of private rented sector tenants in the forthcoming housing bill and to give local authorities the power to prosecute private landlords in cases of alleged illegal eviction.

 

S1W-5598 Ms Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-5135 by Susan Deacon on 22 March 2000, (a) who the members of the Scottish Rehabilitation Technology Forum are, (b) from where the members have been drawn; (c) whether there are any end user members in the Forum and (d) how long the Forum has been established.

 

S1W-5599 Ms Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-5135 by Susan Deacon on 22 March 2000, what provisions are made to allow individuals to continue to receive long-term treatment by a specialist health board area other than their local one.

 

S1W-5600 Ms Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding it has given to the NHS to allow the introduction of lighter and more modern materials for orthotic equipment.

 

S1W-5601 Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will publish the Action Plan detailing how it proposes to achieve the social justice targets set out in Social Justice… a Scotland where everyone matters.

 

S1W-5602 Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it has made towards developing the Annual Scottish Social Justice Report and when it expects this report to be published.

 

S1W-5603 Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to launch its Social Neighbourhoods Initiative.

 

S1W-5604 Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to begin training for the Community Participation Skills Development Programme.

 

S1W-5605 Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects the report of the Homelessness task force due in February 2000 to be published.

 

S1W-5606 Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive to specify its total expenditure on housing, including grants to local authorities, Scottish Homes, the rent assessment panel and voluntary housing organisations, for financial years 1999-2000 and 2000-01.

 

S1W-5607 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what the split, in terms of the total amount collected via water charges, between domestic and non-domestic water charge payers will be in the next two years and what the equivalent figures were in each of the last four years.

 

S1W-5608 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average increase in water charges for 2000-01 is as (a) a percentage of the rise in the state pension and (b) a percentage of average household income.

 

S1W-5609 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has a legal obligation to licence the carrying out of genetically modified crop trials if an application is submitted.

 

S1W-5610 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive, in the light of the Chancellor of the Exchequer’s budget statement on 21 March 2000, whether it intends to extend the Education Maintenance Allowance Scheme for over 16s.
