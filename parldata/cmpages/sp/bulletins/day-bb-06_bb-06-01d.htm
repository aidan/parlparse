Business Bulletin No. 88/2006: Thursday 1 June 2006
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 8 June 2006

Question Time

General

1. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to ensure that reparation actions in Scottish courts are processed efficiently. (S2O-10160)

2. Dave Petrie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide a progress report on the commitment in A Partnership for a Better Scotland to reduce S1 and S2 class sizes in English and Maths to 20 by 2007. (S2O-10102)

3. Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive what the specific programmed dates are for the start of construction of the six improvement schemes on the A75 between Stranraer and Dumfries. (S2O-10080)

4. Mr John Swinney: To ask the Scottish Executive what issues are being considered in its consultation on the National Transport Strategy. (S2O-10085)

5. Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will consider the outcome of the GRIP stage 4 assessment of the Dynamic Loop on the Kilmarnock to Glasgow rail line. (S2O-10125)

6. Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what importance it places on encouraging enterprise in education. (S2O-10141)

7. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to carry out an audit of youth facilities across Scotland. (S2O-10110)

8. Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide an update on the Edinburgh Airport Rail Link. (S2O-10091)

9. Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is confident that rurality is given adequate recognition when making funding allocations for public service delivery across Scotland. (S2O-10166)

10. Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it has taken since the success of Scotland at the Melbourne Commonwealth Games to ensure that Scottish athletes compete successfully at an international level. (S2O-10096).

Environment and Rural Development

1. Not Lodged

2. Not Lodged

3. Chris Ballance: To ask the Scottish Executive what importance it attaches to the contribution of incineration or energy from waste to diversion from landfill. (S2O-10149)

4. Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive whether Scottish National Heritage and the Scottish Environment Protection Agency have adequate resources in order for them to make representations to public inquiries on planning applications for large renewable projects. (S2O-10092)

5. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with Her Majesty’s Government about radioactive waste on the beach at Dalgety Bay. (S2O-10118)

6. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans for action to deter the practice of fly-tipping and discarding litter at roadsides and lay-bys. (S2O-10122)

7. Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive what action its Environment and Rural Affairs Department is taking to deal with wildlife crime. (S2O-10161)

8. Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made on the Aberdeen Beach Recharge Scheme. (S2O-10113)

9. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps its Environment and Rural Affairs Department will take to ensure that people with concerns about the environment have opportunities to comment on the environmental aspects of public transport projects. (S2O-10155)

10. Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to encourage the export of Scottish beef. (S2O-10094)

Health and Community Care

1. Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive what action the NHS is taking to promote well-being. (S2O-10153)

2. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in reducing waiting times for digital hearing aids. (S2O-10156)

3. John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive how many physicians’ assistants are being recruited for NHS Highland. (S2O-10162)

4. Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has received from Highland Council in respect of the council’s plans to put out to tender the provision of care for elderly people who are currently resident in local authority homes, such as Invernevis House, Fort William, the Wade Centre, Kingussie and Ach an Eas and Burnside, Inverness, and whether it considers that the savings which the council has predicted are realistic. (S2O-10087)

5. Jim Mather: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures the Minister for Health and Community Care will focus on when he reviews the performance of NHS boards during the summer. (S2O-10088)

6. Mr Andrew Arbuckle: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are any plans to introduce NHS specialist facilities to treat and rehabilitate young people addicted to drugs. (S2O-10163)

7. Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in reducing waiting times for NHS audiology services. (S2O-10134)

8. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are being taken to improve access to primary care in Glasgow Springburn. (S2O-10129)

9. Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to improve the service for people who suffer from osteoporosis in the west of Scotland. (S2O-10097)

10. Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive what action will be taken to ensure that waiting time targets for urgent referrals for people with cancer are met. (S2O-10082).
