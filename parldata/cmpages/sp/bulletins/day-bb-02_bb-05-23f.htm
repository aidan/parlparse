Business Bulletin No. 85/2002

Thursday 23 May 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-3144 Tommy Sheridan: Glasgow Housing Association and European Commission Rules—That the Parliament expresses its concern that the transfer of Glasgow council housing to the Glasgow Housing Association may breach European Commission rules on state aid; notes the inadequate answers given so far on this matter by the Scottish Executive, and calls for an early ministerial statement on the issue.

*S1M-3143# Elaine Smith: Promoting Scotland's Industrial Heritage—That the Parliament recognises that our industrial heritage is of interest to many people from other countries as well as Scots, and believes that the Scottish Executive should look at ways to market ex-industrial areas throughout Scotland such as Coatbridge and give consideration to exploring the idea of state-run hotels, along the line of Spanish paradors, where listed buildings could be restored to provide accommodation for tourists thus allowing itineraries to include non-traditional tourist facilities such as Summerlee Heritage Park in Coatbridge which is home to the only working trams in Scotland.

*S1M-3142 Michael Russell: Conservative Support for Creationist Schools—That the Parliament notes with astonishment the remarks made by Mr Murdo Fraser MSP, the Deputy Education Spokesperson for the Tory Party in Scotland, on BBC Scotland Television on 17 May 2002, in which he expressed support for groups promoting "creationist" views on science running state schools in Scotland; notes that members of such groups totally reject evolution and have nothing to do with the great religious faiths in Scotland and the wider world; further notes that creationists have taken control of a state-funded secondary school in Gateshead, where they are undermining the teaching of science; rejects the foolish position of the Tory Party in Scotland, which seems now to be not only against devolution but also evolution, and asserts that misguided creationist groups should not be placed in charge of children’s education in Scotland.

*S1M-3141 Mr Adam Ingram: Larkhall to Milngavie Rail Line—That the Parliament notes the comments of South Lanarkshire councillor David McLachlan, vice-convener of Strathclyde Passenger Transport (SPT), in relation to the Larkhall to Milngavie rail line that "all we need is the Parliament to give us the power to head up the project", and calls on the Scottish Executive to make a decision over whether the British Railways (No.3) Order Confirmation Act 1994 can be transferred to SPT or whether a new Bill needs to be introduced into the Parliament to allow for this link, which is vital to the future economic prosperity of Larkhall, Stonehouse and Leshmahagow, to be progressed.

S1M-3138 Fiona Hyslop: Congratulations to Linlithgow Rose—That the Parliament congratulates Linlithgow Rose junior football club on winning the Scottish Junior Cup for the first time since *1965*; acknowledges the hard work of the players, the backroom staff and the committee in building this success, and congratulates the club’s supporters on helping to make Sunday a celebration of junior football.
