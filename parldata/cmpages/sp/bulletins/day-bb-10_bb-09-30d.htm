Business Bulletin No. 142/2010: Thursday 30 September 2010
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 7 October 2010

Question Time

General

1. Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure that the National Dementia Strategy’s aim of strengthening the integration of health and social care services is met. (S3O-11654)

2. Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it is having with NHS Highland regarding the future of Dunbar hospital. (S3O-11656)

3. David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to support victims and witnesses. (S3O-11613)

4. Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it takes to ensure that facilities that are open to the public, including new health facilities, are made fully accessible. (S3O-11611)

5. Malcolm Chisholm: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take to ensure that people traffickers are prosecuted and that their victims are protected and supported. (S3O-11583)

6. Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on developing the carers and young carers strategies and when the implementation group will be set up. (S3O-11605)

7. Aileen Campbell: To ask the Scottish Executive what requirements local authorities must meet when consulting on proposed school mergers. (S3O-11618)

8. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive when a minister last visited a supported employment workshop. (S3O-11585)

9. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has had discussions with the UK Government regarding health and safety legislation and the police service. (S3O-11616)

10. David McLetchie: To ask the Scottish Executive what estimate it has made of the additional revenue and capital costs to local authorities of the policy of imposing a maximum number of 25 pupils in primary 1 classes. (S3O-11573)

Finance and Sustainable Growth

1. Not Lodged

2. Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to support a high speed rail link to Scotland. (S3O-11644)

3. Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Cabinet Secretary for Finance and Sustainable Growth has met the Cabinet Secretary for Education and Lifelong Learning to discuss tackling youth unemployment. (S3O-11581)

4. Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive on what date it estimates that work is likely to commence on the construction of the Aberdeen Western Peripheral Route. (S3O-11578)

5. Alasdair Allan: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration it is giving to bringing about closer working between local authorities and NHS boards and other public authorities in island areas. (S3O-11617)

6. Elaine Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the fairness of the system for rebanding of council tax. (S3O-11612)

7. Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on supporting a council tax freeze in 2011-12. (S3O-11571)

8. Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to its decision to support tax increment financing for developments on Edinburgh’s waterfront, whether it is aware of the plan by XStream Scotland to build the world’s first indoor white-water rafting facility there. (S3O-11575)

9. Stuart McMillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what actions it has taken to ensure that large-scale infrastructure projects benefit workers in local communities. (S3O-11636)

10. Maureen Watt: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the Ofgem Transmission Charging Review and the potential outcomes. (S3O-11639)

11. Bill Kidd: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken to encourage better use of underdeveloped commercial and industrial land in urban areas. (S3O-11629)

12. Nicol Stephen: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is considering changing the local government funding formula in order to assist poorly funded local authorities. (S3O-11655)

13. George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration it has given to using the Scottish Parliament’s tax-varying powers to increase revenue. (S3O-11587)

14. Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive what the estimated value is of the cooperative sector to the economy. (S3O-11582)

15. Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in securing the fossil fuel levy monies held by HM Treasury. (S3O-11625)

16. Nigel Don: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations ministers have made to the UK Government regarding the Renewable Heat Incentive. (S3O-11621)

17. Derek Brownlee: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people have been recruited externally and internally by it and its agencies since February 2010, and how many of these posts were permanent. (S3O-11572)

18. Not Lodged

19. Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with organisations and representatives from the business community following the business rates revaluation that came into effect in April 2010. (S3O-11646)

20. Hugh O’Donnell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the £111 million that has been reported as being saved by the Scottish Futures Trust has been or will be deployed. (S3O-11649)
