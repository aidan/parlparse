Business Bulletin No. 153/2004

Thursday 28th October 2004

Section D : Oral questions selected for answer on Thursday 4 November 2004
  

Question Time

Enterprise, Lifelong Learning and Transport

1. Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to achieve a significant increase in the number of domestic wind turbines in domestic and industrial premises. (S2O-3817)

2. Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on any difficulties faced by foreign students seeking to study at colleges in Scotland. (S2O-3812)

3. Mike Watson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take to ensure that the recent funding settlement secures pay modernisation in higher education in accordance with the framework agreement between employers and trade unions and what timescale for completion of this process is now envisaged. (S2O-3790)

4. Mrs Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to adopt quality bus contracts across Scotland. (S2O-3844)

5. Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the new national concessionary travel scheme for elderly and disabled people will use smart cards. (S2O-3859)

6. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has received from road transport organisations following the conference organised by Cumbernauld Community Council’s Joint Action Group on the proposals for the upgrade of the A80. (S2O-3801)

7. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure proper competition and satisfactory standards in areas where both bus and rail services are provided by First Group plc. (S2O-3787)

8. Mr Mark Ruskell: To ask the Scottish Executive how the Integrated Transport Fund is supporting projects that include the provision of infrastructure for cycling. (S2O-3899)

9. Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there has been any progress in relation to the six road improvement schemes planned for the A75. (S2O-3839)

10. Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive what part the Minister for Enterprise and Lifelong Learning will play in ensuring that Scottish Enterprise delivers environmentally sustainable development. (S2O-3895)

11. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Airdrie to Bathgate rail link will be completed and when the new service will be operational. (S2O-3867)

12. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to help to reduce congestion on the Forth Road Bridge. (S2O-3860)

Justice and Law Officers

1. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will further develop community-based alternatives to imprisonment. (S2O-3874)

2. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will deal with prosecutions relating to the fishing industry. (S2O-3902)

3. Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take to improve access to justice for adults with incapacity. (S2O-3878)

4. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on the review of drug treatment and rehabilitation. (S2O-3901)

5. Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Scottish Law Commission review of rape and sexual offences law will be completed. (S2O-3836)

6. Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria it uses when deciding whether to pursue legal challenges to court rulings. (S2O-3809)

7. Colin Fox: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to amend the law to allow for the disclosure during a trial of the previous convictions of defendants. (S2O-3808)

8. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will expand the use of community-based alternatives to prison. (S2O-3875)

9. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to introduce a licensing system to regulate private security firms. (S2O-3797)

10. Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to ensure that sex offenders are properly supervised in the community. (S2O-3877)

11. Chris Ballance: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to implement the commitment in the Partnership Agreement to expand the role of restorative justice as a means of improving the range and quality of the sentences available to courts. (S2O-3892)

12. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will ensure that delays in processing applications by Disclosure Scotland do not recur. (S2O-3831)

General

1. Susan Deacon: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent action it has taken to reduce the incidence of chlamydia and other sexually transmitted infections. (S2O-3820)

2. Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to improve fisheries research. (S2O-3852)

3. George Lyon: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to increase the affordable housing supply in Argyll and Bute. (S2O-3846)

4. Colin Fox: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact user charges have on the level of access to NHS services by patients. (S2O-3803)

5. David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied with the proposed GP out-of-hours arrangements in rural areas. (S2O-3806)

6. Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what support is available to assist community groups in acquiring premises for the provision of facilities for young people. (S2O-3794)

7. Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to assist local authorities in upgrading recycling points and centres. (S2O-3854)

8. Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in reducing deaths from cancer. (S2O-3861)

9. Mr Frank McAveety: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making on the decommissioning of the Glasgow homeless hostels. (S2O-3865)

10. Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to ensure that development of brownfield sites provides for satisfactory disposal of asbestos. (S2O-3823)

11. Miss Annabel Goldie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the normal timescale is between a drug-addicted person first consulting a GP and being referred for clinical rehabilitation. (S2O-3784)

12. Chris Ballance: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is in respect of the proposal to develop a landfill site at Shewalton in North Ayrshire based on planning consent granted in 1978 and without an environmental impact assessment. (S2O-3886)

13. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in increasing the number of doctors employed by the NHS. (S2O-3857)

14. Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to encourage NHS Argyll and Clyde to work with NHS Greater Glasgow in the interests of patients. (S2O-3868)

15. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive what proportion of Scottish tourism revenue is generated from tourists from England. (S2O-3849)
