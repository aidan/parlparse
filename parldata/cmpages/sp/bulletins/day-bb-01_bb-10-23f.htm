Business Bulletin No. 149/2001

Tuesday 23 October 2001

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions.

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-2343 Mr Jim Wallace: Publication of the Reports into the Investigation, Legal Proceedings and Family Liaison Arrangements in the Case of the Murder of Surjit Singh Chhokar-That the Parliament notes that the Scottish Ministers intend to lay in English and other languages the Report of the Inquiry into the Liaison Arrangements Between the Police, the Procurator Fiscal Service and the Crown Office and the Family of the Deceased Surjit Singh Chhokar in Connection with the Murder of Surjit Singh Chhokar and the Related Prosecutions by Dr Raj Jandoo and the Report of the Inquiry into Crown Decision-Making in the Case of the Murder of Surjit Singh Chhokar by the Rt. Hon. Sir Anthony Campbell before the Parliament and orders the Clerk to publish the Reports and their translations.

Supported by: Iain Gray*

*S1M-2342 Mr Jack McConnell: Adoption and Children Bill-That the Parliament accepts the need to provide for continued mutual cross-border recognition of orders properly made by the separate GB jurisdictions in respect of adoption procedures; supports proposals for reform of recognition of overseas adoption orders, and agrees that the relevant provisions to achieve these ends in the Adoption and Children Bill should be considered by the UK Parliament.

Supported by: Nicol Stephen*

*S1M-2341 Mr Jim Wallace: Proceeds of Crime Bill-That the Parliament endorses the principles of the Proceeds of Crime Bill and agrees that the provisions in the Bill that relate to devolved matters should be considered by the UK Parliament.

Supported by: Iain Gray*

*S1M-2340 Mr Tom McCabe: Membership of the Committee of the Regions-That the Parliament endorses the Executive's proposal to nominate as representatives of the Parliament Nicol Stephen MSP and Hugh Henry MSP as full members and Jack McConnell MSP and Irene McGugan MSP as alternate members on the UK delegation to the Committee of the Regions for the forthcoming session from 2002 to 2006 and notes that the representation from local government will be Councillor Christine May and Councillor Keith Brown as full members and Councillor Corrie McChord and Councillor Hugh Halcro-Johnston as alternate members.

Supported by: Mr Jack McConnell*

*S1M-2339# Elaine Smith: Violence Against Women - 16 Days Campaign 2001, 25 November - 10 December 2001-That the Parliament congratulates and supports the Scottish women's groups organising activities for the 16 Days Campaign 2001 which highlights the issue of violence against women as a world-wide human rights issue and aims to mobilise women as a social force, building an international women's movement to demand women's rights in this context; notes that over the 16 day period of the campaign its international nature will be promoted with the theme "Think Global - Act Local", and further notes that flags from 14 different countries will be displayed with relevant facts and statistics on violence against women outside the Parliament on the morning of the campaign launch date, 25 November 2001.

*S1M-2338# Irene McGugan: Pay scales for new teachers-That the Parliament notes that trainee primary and secondary school teachers were encouraged into the profession by assurances that age and experience would be recognised on qualification; is concerned that the Scottish Negotiating Committee for Teachers is considering making changes to teachers' pay scales as they apply to probationary and recently qualified staff which would impact significantly on the situation of mature students; considers that these changes can only have a generally detrimental impact on the national teacher recruitment campaign; believes that the Scottish Executive should consider the implications which such a proposal will have, particularly on mature students who are currently part of the way through their training and many of whom gave up well-paid careers, and recommends that such students be given assurances that what they were promised will be honoured.

*S1M-2337# Robin Harper: Scottish Science Library-That the Parliament notes the irreplaceable and valuable service that the Scottish Science Library contributes to the advancement of science in Scotland; further notes the Scottish Executive's recently declared support for science through the first ever science strategy, A Science Strategy for Scotland, including encouragement of public interest in science; further notes with concern the announcement of the imminent closure of the library, and expresses the hope that the Executive will take action to prevent the library from closing.

Supported by: Bruce Crawford*
