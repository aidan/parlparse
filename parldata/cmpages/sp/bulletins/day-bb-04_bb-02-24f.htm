Business Bulletin No. 33 / 2004

Tuesday 24th February 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-936 Cathy Jamieson: Justice (Northern Ireland) Bill - UK Legislation-That the Parliament agrees that an amendment should be made to the Justice (Northern Ireland) Bill to provide for the compulsory transfer of prisoners from Northern Ireland to Scotland and that the amendment should be considered by the UK Parliament.

Supported by: Hugh Henry*

*S2M-935# Carolyn Leckie: Government Overspend-That the Parliament notes the ongoing concern at the cost of building the new Scottish Parliament going over budget; believes that those costs should be considered in the context of the Ministry of Defence's overspend of £3 billion in the last year alone; notes that this included an overspend of £1.3 billion on the Astute Class submarine; further notes that despite the overspend the Ministry of Defence was incapable of providing its employees with basic equipment necessary for its illegal war on Iraq, and believes that Her Majesty's Government should cancel its top 22 defence projects, saving the UK taxpayer £65 billion.

*S2M-934 Mr Stewart Maxwell: Methadone Prescribing-That the Parliament expresses its condolences to the family of Craig Winstone who tragically died after taking a combination of methadone and valium; notes that during the Fatal Accident Inquiry the general practitioner concerned stated that she prescribed methadone to Craig without carrying out a urine test; is concerned that methadone prescribing practice varies widely across Scotland with no consistent approach between different NHS boards; calls on the Scottish Executive to help those working with patients with drug problems by ensuring that there are strong national guidelines in place for the prescribing of methadone, and further calls on the Executive to enforce minimum standards based on best practice across Scotland in order to avoid the possibility of differing standards resulting in avoidable deaths.

*S2M-933 Mr Duncan McNeil: MMR Vaccine-That the Parliament notes that the research by Dr Andrew Wakefield linking the MMR vaccine to autism has been discredited by what the editor of The Lancet describes as a "fatal conflict of interest"; regrets that this flawed research led to many parents refusing to vaccinate their children; notes, however, that this impact was compounded by the irresponsible use of the study's findings by certain politicians, and invites those politicians who seek to use questionable medical research to create public disquiet or further a political agenda to reflect on their actions.

*S2M-932# Colin Fox: Paul Robeson US Commemorative Stamp-That the Parliament welcomes the US Postal Service's decision to issue a stamp in commemoration of the life of the singer, athlete and political activist Paul Robeson and believes that this is a fitting tribute to a man who made an enormous contribution to the cultural life of the United States of America and Britain throughout the 1930s, 1940s and 1950s and yet paid a heavy price as a victim of the McCarthyite witch-hunt that was rife in the USA in the 1950s.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan*, Rosie Kane*

*S2M-931# Colin Fox: Fountainbridge Brewery Closure-That the Parliament condemns the decision of Scottish and Newcastle Breweries to close their Fountainbridge facility; believes that the decision has less to do with the current state of the United Kingdom beer market and more to do with cashing in on the Edinburgh property boom; notes the assurances the company gave the workforce only recently that their jobs were safe when it asked for, and received, "greater employee flexibility" and opened a £10 million state of the art kegging operation, and considers that the Scottish Executive should give its full support to the campaign to save the facility's 170 jobs.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan*, Rosie Kane*

*S2M-930 Christine Grahame: Consumer Credit Licensing-That the Parliament notes with concern an article in the Mail on Sunday on 22 February 2004 reporting that a journalist, using his bona fide personal details, was granted a consumer credit licence permitting him, inter alia, to offer loans, offer debt counselling and arrange consolidated debt schemes after the most minimal inquiry by a trading standards officer, namely a brief telephone call confirming these personal details; notes that neither the trading standards officer nor the application form for the licence made any inquiry as to the applicant's current employment status or experience in the field; notes the Department of Trade and Industry's White Paper Fair, Clear and Competitive - The Consumer Credit Market in the 21st Century issued in December 2003 and, in particular, the section on the licensing regime on page 44 and the proposed reforms; notes that, under devolution, the fall-out from personal debt, for example bankruptcies, relationship breakdown and ill-health, all has its cost to the Scottish budget, and therefore urges the Scottish Executive to make representations in the strongest terms to ensure that the loopholes so apparent in the reported example cannot be repeated and further to make urgent representations that, pending a change in the law, procedures are tightened.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan*, Rosie Kane*

*S2M-929 Donald Gorrie: Support for Local Sports Clubs and Activities-That the Parliament urges sportscotland, the Scottish Executive and local authorities to work together to develop a decentralised structure that will support and encourage the development of the approximately 13,000 sports clubs, especially in promoting more sport for young people and the sporting activities of other organisations, using the existing network of local sports councils.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan*, Rosie Kane*

*S2M-928 Donald Gorrie: Developing Football in Scotland-That the Parliament urges the Scottish Football Association and all the other organisations involved in managing or promoting football in Scotland for all ages and both sexes to work together to develop a more coherent and effective structure for developing football in Scotland.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan*, Rosie Kane*
