Business Bulletin No. 66/2003

Friday 9 May 2003

Section E : Written questions lodged on 8 May 2003
  

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-17 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Presiding Officer when the Holyrood Progress Group will next report to the Finance Committee on the progress and costs of the new Parliament building.

S2W-18 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Presiding Officer when the new Parliament building at Holyrood will be open for use.

S2W-19 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Presiding Officer whether the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body will publish details of progress in letting service contracts for the new Parliament building.

S2W-20 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Presiding Officer when the Holyrood Progress Group will terminate its activities.

S2W-21 Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has received from Fife Council regarding a case for a new secondary school to serve north Fife.

S2W-22 Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will make a decision on the business case for a new hospital and health centre in St Andrews.

S2W-23 Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will grant further powers to local authorities to enable them to increase the number of 20 mph zones outside schools.

S2W-24 Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what investigations it has carried out into any potential health risks associated with radio masts being supplied by Airwave MM02 Ltd for the new police forces communications service.

S2W-25 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will increase funding to Grampian NHS Board following reductions in funding under the Arbuthnott formula and, if so, when it will do so.

S2W-26 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has set a target date for the commencement of the building of the Aberdeen peripheral route.

S2W-27 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will conduct a safety audit of the A90 junction at Muchalls with a view to providing safer road access for residents wishing to travel north.

S2W-28 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive what actions it will take following the recent deaths on the A90 in south Aberdeenshire.

S2W-29 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will improve safety at the three A90 junctions at Laurencekirk.

S2W-30 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will give details of any meetings it has held with Aberdeenshire Council with regard to the reopening of Laurencekirk Station.

S2W-31 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will investigate the continuing issue of sewage escaping into the River Dee around Banchory.

S2W-32 Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive what duties are implied by the requirement placed on the contractor to comply with the Freedom of Information Act 2000 as referred to in clause 59.7.1 of the Scottish Schools Standard PPP Contract.

S2W-33 Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive what obligations education authorities will have under the (a) Code of Practice on Government Information (1997) and (b) Freedom of Information Act 2002 as referred to in clause 59.7.2 of the Scottish Schools Standard PPP Contract.

S2W-34 Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive what minimum procedural safeguards for suspects and defendants in criminal proceedings it considers necessary as part of judicial cooperation in the European Union.

S2W-35 Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will report on the discussion of minimum procedural safeguards for suspects and defendants in criminal proceedings at the Council of the European Union's informal meeting on justice and home affairs held on 28 and 29 March 2003.

S2W-36 Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive what response it will make to the European Commission's green paper, Procedural Safeguards for Suspects and Defendants in Criminal Proceedings throughout the European Union.

S2W-37 Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive what role it has in the appointment of Europol directors.

S2W-38 Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken to ensure that all countries of the European Union recognise an order made in Scotland for seizure of the proceeds of crime where there is no preceding criminal conviction.

S2W-39 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the most recent annual figures are for successful prosecutions under section 76 of the Licensing (Scotland) Act 1976 on selling alcoholic liquor to a drunken person and the penalties imposed, broken down by sheriffdom.
