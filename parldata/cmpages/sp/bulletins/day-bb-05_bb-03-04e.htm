Section E – Written questions lodged on 3 March 2005

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-14980 Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it employs to evaluate the proportion of time children spend on the internet for educational activities.

S2W-14981 Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what benchmarks it plans to use to measure the educational successes of its Broadband for Scotland campaign.

S2W-14982 Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will publish any figures or research showing the number of children who have access to broadband internet at (a) home and (b) school.

S2W-14983 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive when Scottish Enterprise will inform New Futures Fund projects whether they will continue to receive funding until March 2006.

S2W-14984 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the peak staffing levels of New Futures Fund projects were in the last year.

S2W-14985 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the estimated staffing levels are for New Futures Fund projects.

S2W-14986 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the estimated client level is within New Futures Fund projects.

S2W-14987 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the peak level of client contact was within New Futures Fund projects in the last year.

S2W-14988 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive when the consultation process on fertility treatment will commence.

S2W-14989 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the life expectancy is of (a) males and (b) females who (i) drink alcohol above recommended levels, (ii) drink alcohol at recommended levels, (iii) drink alcohol below recommended levels and (iv) abstain from alcohol.

S2W-14990 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many females were convicted of drink-driving offences in each year since 1997, broken down by police force area.

S2W-14991 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many drink-driving convictions there were in (a) 1997 and (b) 1998, broken down by police force area.

S2W-14992 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of assault victims reported their attacker to have been under the influence of alcohol in each year since 1997, broken down by type of assault.

S2W-14993 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many reports procurators fiscal received from each police force in 2003-04 and, of these, how many (a) were marked "no proceedings" on account of delay on the part of the reporting agency, (b) proceeded to trial, (c) resulted in a conviction and (d) are currently pending.

S2W-14994 Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive what the costs have been of storing the material referred to as "Political Papers of David McLetchie MSP" in the National Library of Scotland and what the additional costs have been of splitting them into two categories, personal files and party files.

S2W-14995 Jim Mather: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it took to ensure that the draft interim report on the Objective One funding projects in the Highlands and Islands, received by the Executive from the European Commission on 27 September 2004, remained confidential until the completion of the audit process.

S2W-14996 Jim Mather: To ask the Scottish Executive which organisations in Scotland had access to the draft interim report on the Objective One funding projects in the Highlands and Islands delivered to the Executive by the European Commission on 27 September 2004.

S2W-14997 Jim Mather: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to investigate how the draft interim report on the Objective One funding projects in the Highlands and Islands, delivered to it by the European Commission on 27 September 2004, came to be in the hands of the BBC.

S2W-14998 Jim Mather: To ask the Scottish Executive what preparations it has made to comply with the follow-up audit of the Objective One projects in the Highlands and Islands and to ensure that the information requested by the European Commission is provided in a timeous and efficient manner.

S2W-14999 Mr Mark Ruskell: To ask the Scottish Executive what its views are on the model of housing development known as "cohousing".

S2W-15000 Mr Mark Ruskell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has examined the potential benefits to communities of promoting cohousing as a model for housing and community development and, if not, whether it will consider doing so.

S2W-15001Δ Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive when it plans to publish revised arrangements for the governance of the Scottish Prison Service.

S2W-15002 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive whether NHS board action plans produced in line with the findings of the fatal accident inquiry about the death of my constituent, James Mauchland, will be publicly available.

S2W-15003 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive whether NHS board action plans produced in line with the findings of the fatal accident inquiry about the death of my constituent, James Mauchland, will be evaluated to ensure that people with learning disabilities and other vulnerable patients are treated with dignity, respect and equality.

S2W-15004 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will ensure that any good practice guidance arising from the evaluation of NHS board action plans produced in line with the findings of the fatal accident inquiry into the death of James Mauchland is disseminated across all NHS boards and that appropriate training will be in place.

S2W-15005 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-11821 by Mr Jim Wallace on 10 November 2005, what the drop-out rate for students was from Scottish universities in 2002-03.

S2W-15006 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-1233 by Malcolm Chisholm on 17 July 2003, what action has been taken by each NHS board on the recommendations of the fatal accident inquiry into the death of James Mauchland.

S2W-15008 Jim Mather: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will support the Highlands and Islands European Partnership’s (HIEP) update of the Oviedo Declaration for Statistical Effect Regions.

S2W-15009 Jim Mather: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has had any contact with European Regional Commissioner, Danuta Hubner, and, if so, what the details were of such contact.

S2W-15010 Jim Mather: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the First Minister or any other Scottish Minister plans to meet Danuta Hubner, European Regional Commissioner, and, if so, what the subject of the discussions will be.

S2W-15011 Jim Mather: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will release details of all discussions held about European Regional Development Funding for Scotland (a) with the Department for Trade and Industry, (b) with HM Treasury, (c) as part of any joint ministerial committee meeting and (d) with the European Commission.

S2W-15012 Jim Mather: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has made to the European Commission about regional funding in the 2007-13 funding period.
