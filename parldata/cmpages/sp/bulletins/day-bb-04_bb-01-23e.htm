Business Bulletin No. 15 / 2004

Friday 23rd January 2004

Section E : Written questions lodged on Thursday 22nd January 2004
  

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-5630 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive what (a) treatments and (b) drugs are available for people with severe Alzheimer's disease.

S2W-5653 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive how planning guidelines for wind farms could be altered to take greater account of local concerns and natural heritage issues.

S2W-5654 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-2864 by Malcolm Chisholm on 6 October 2003, how many calls there have been to the healthyliving line in each month since September 2003.

S2W-5655 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-4938 by Mr Jim Wallace on 20 January 2004, whether any evaluation has been carried out on the impact of inward investment during the years referred to, by either Scottish Enterprise or the Executive and, if so, whether it will place a copy of any such evaluation in the Scottish Parliament Information Centre.

S2W-5656 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive, in respect of each executive Non-departmental Public Body (NDPB), advisory NDPB, tribunal, public corporation, nationalised industry and NHS body for which ministers are accountable, what external legal advice each organisation receives and from whom, what the jurisdiction is of each such legal advice provider, and what statutes govern the operation of each NDPB or other organisation and any contracts that they may enter into with third parties.

S2W-5657 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive, in respect of each executive Non-departmental Public Body (NDPB), advisory NDPB, tribunal, public corporation, nationalised industry and NHS body for which ministers are accountable, how much each organisation has spent on external legal advice in each year since 1980, (a) in total and (b) adjusted for inflation with 1980 as the base year, broken down by legal jurisdiction of the invoicing provider.

S2W-5658 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to update The Way Forward: Framework for Economic Development in Scotland and, if so, who will undertake this work and when it will be published.

S2W-5659 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to update A Smart, Successful Scotland: Ambitions for the Enterprise Networks and, if so, who will undertake this work and when it will be published.

S2W-5660 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in arrangements for holding an annual business forum; what the format will be; what discussions it has had with representatives of the Parliament in respect of the forum, and when invitations will be issued to the forum and who will issue them.

S2W-5661 Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration has been given to the judgement of the Court of Appeal in the case of Angela Cannings and what implications it will have for similar cases in Scotland.

S2W-5662 Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive how many dairy farmers there were in (a) 1990 and (b) the most recent year for which figures are available.

S2W-5663 Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average size of dairy herds was in (a) 1990 and (b) the most recent year for which figures are available.

S2W-5664 Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive how many, and what percentage of, dairy farm businesses have gone out of business in each of the last five years for which figures are available.

S2W-5665 Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive what proportion of dairy processing businesses is (a) farmer-owned co-operatives and (b) private companies.

S2W-5666 Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Scottish Higher Education Funding Council has reduced its grant to the Open University in Scotland for this year and, if so, by how much.

S2W-5667 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with Her Majesty's Government and other stakeholders in respect of developing the use of marine energy.

S2W-5668 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is supporting research into the development of marine energy technology.

S2W-5669 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact the creation of an Energy Intermediary Technology Institute in Aberdeen will have on the development of marine energy technology.

S2W-5670 Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many cases for shared residency and contact orders have been dealt with by Edinburgh Sheriff Court in the last year.

S2W-5671 Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many shared residency and contact orders were granted by Edinburgh Sheriff Court in the last year.

S2W-5672 Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many individuals, granted shared residency and contact orders by Edinburgh Sheriff Court in the last year, had to return to court to have the orders enforced.

S2W-5673 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive when the final report of the task group reviewing the licensing provisions contained in the Civic Government (Scotland) Act 1982 will be published.

S2W-5674Δ Cathie Craigie: To ask the Scottish Executive what further support it will provide to the money advice sector in Scotland.

S2W-5675Δ Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made to establish the ex-gratia payment scheme for people with hepatitis C through blood and blood products.

S2W-5676 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive what (a) its and (b) its agencies policies are in respect of recruiting employees over 60 and whether such policies are consistent with other policies in regard to age discrimination.

S2W-5677Δ Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to improve services for victims of rape and sexual abuse.

S2W-5678 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken to promote genealogy as a reason for visiting Scotland.

S2W-5679 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive what its response will be to the proposal of the European Parliament's Committee on Industry, External Trade, Research and Energy to set up deep geological disposals as an effective solution for high-level and long-lived radioactive waste.

S2W-5680 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make representations to the European Parliament's Committee on Industry, External Trade, Research and Energy in respect of the committee's proposed two step approach towards the development of deep geological disposal sites for high-level and long-lived radioactive waste and whether it will seek a full explanation of what such an approach entails.

S2W-5681 John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it has been made towards building a grade separated junction on the A9 at North Kessock.

S2W-5682 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive when the report of the Summary Justice Review Committee will be published.

S2W-5683 Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the Scottish Executive what the "limitations in terms of our resources and negotiating flexibility", referred to in its European strategy, are.

S2W-5684 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to make an announcement regarding the implementation of the reform of the Common Agricultural Policy and what the timetable is for implementation.

S2W-5685 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive how many responses it received to the consultation on the proposed Gaelic Language Bill.

S2W-5686 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive when it intends to publish the Gaelic Language Bill.

S2W-5687 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what importance it places on the provision of Gaelic translations on signage in public offices.

S2W-5688 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what the estimated cost will be of the nationwide roll-out of the Education Maintenance Allowance scheme.

S2W-5689 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what the estimated cost will be for the North Ayrshire Council area of funding the Educational Maintenance Allowance scheme in 2004-05.

S2W-5690 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take to encourage as many people as possible to make a submission to the consultation on smoking in public places, as announced in A Breath of Fresh Air for Scotland.
