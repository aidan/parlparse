Business Bulletin No. 133/2002

Friday 20 September 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-3413# Karen Whitefield: Eco-Schools-That the Parliament recognises the value of the Eco-Schools programme in promoting environmental education and encouraging young people to make a difference to their local communities; congratulates those teachers who, through their hard work and enthusiasm, are making the programme a success across Scotland; hopes that more schools will register an interest in the project, and considers that the Executive should continue to give its support to such initiatives which encourage citizenship and environmental awareness.

*S1M-3412# Bill Butler: Glasgow Airport Rail Link-That the Parliament welcomes the announcement by the Minister for Finance and Public Services on 12 September 2002 that resources are to be made available to make possible the construction and completion of a Glasgow Airport rail link; recognises the significance of this rail link as part of a modern, 21st century transport infrastructure for the whole of Scotland; believes that the most sensible option for this new rail link is one routed via Paisley Gilmour Street and Paisley St James, and considers that the Minister for Enterprise, Transport and Lifelong Learning should announce, as a matter of urgency, both his favoured option for this development and a clear timetable for its speedy implementation.

*S1M-3410 Ms Margaret Curran: Building (Scotland) Bill - Stage 1-That the Parliament agrees to the general principles of the Building (Scotland) Bill.
