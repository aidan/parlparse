Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday.  That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an “R”.

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm).  A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-5167 Tommy Sheridan: Reverse the Manufacturing Slump—That the Parliament notes the dramatic collapse in manufacturing industry as far as share of gross domestic product (GDP) and percentage of jobs across the economy are concerned, as reflected in the most recent statistics which reveal that manufacturing’s share of the United Kingdom’s GDP has slumped from 21% in 1997 to only 13.6% in 2006, that the number of workers employed in the manufacturing sector in Scotland is now only 235,000, representing 10.3% of all jobs, and that manufacturing’s share of GDP in Scotland has dropped from 21.8% in 1997 to less than 15% today; believes that manufacturing is a critical sector for economic development and success but that without more direct public control and democratic administration of wealth-creating manufacturing concerns, allied to significant investment in research, development and skills improvements, our ability to provide a decent, improved and sustained standard and quality of life for all our citizens will be diminished, and calls for public ownership of our financial sector to facilitate real investment in our manufacturing capacity.

*S2M-5166 Tommy Sheridan: Oppose Politically Driven Public Service Cuts—That the Parliament notes the report from the House of Commons Public Accounts Committee (PAC) into the effect of the £375 million cost-cutting exercise by the Department for Work and Pensions (DWP) which has decimated the service to the public so much that the PAC Chairman, Edward Leigh MP, described the service as “unresponsive, overcomplicated and unreliable” and that the “people left hanging on the phone would have met with derisive laughter the claim by the DWP that it was modernising the delivery of welfare benefits”; also notes that a total of 21 million calls to the DWP helplines went unanswered, representing almost half, 44%, of all calls to Jobcentre Plus, the Pension Service and the Disability and Carers Service (DCS) and that the worst problems were experienced by callers to DCS helplines, with inquiries about Disability Living Allowance and Attendance Allowance getting through to a member of staff in only 21% of cases; condemns the New Labour government for implementing such politically motivated and damaging cuts in essential services for the most vulnerable citizens, and supports the campaign of the Public and Commercial Services (PCS) union and other welfare and pensioners’ groups to reverse these cuts.

*S2M-5165 Tommy Sheridan: World Support for Cuba—That the Parliament notes last week’s United Nations General Assembly vote, by 183 votes to 4 with one abstention, to urge the United States of America to end its 45-year-old trade blockade against Cuba; further notes that last week’s vote represents the 15th year in a row that the UN General Assembly has voted against the US trade blockade and that this year’s vote was a one vote improvement on last year and was secured after the Cuban Foreign Minister, Felipe Perez Roque, addressed the assembly in the following terms: “The economic war unleashed by the US against Cuba, the longest and cruellest ever known, qualifies as an act of genocide and constitutes a flagrant violation of international law and the Charter of the United Nations”; concurs fully with Felipe Perez Roque’s remarks; condemns the US for continuing to ignore the will of the world, as expressed via the UN General Assembly, and adds its support to the world’s call for economic justice for Cuba and an end to the illegal blockade by the US.

*S2M-5164 Tommy Sheridan: Solidarity with RMT and NUJ Members—That the Parliament recognises and congratulates those trade union members organised by the Rail, Maritime and Transport Union (RMT), who are employed as divers by oil companies in the North Sea, and journalists in the National Union of Journalists (NUJ), employed by the Telegraphtitles, who were compelled to take part in industrial action recently to defend living standards and working conditions and who secured significant victories; hopes that these two recent victories for organised trade unionists will encourage other workers involved in struggles against arrogant bosses and employers who believe that vast wealth is fine for them but that their employees should be content with buttons, and therefore expresses solidarity with all those workers forced to embark on industrial action, including withdrawing their labour to defend their working conditions or secure a decent wage rise.

*S2M-5163 Dennis Canavan: Centenary of the Death of Michael Davitt—That the Parliament hails the enormous contribution of Michael Davitt to land reform in his native Ireland; notes the great acclaim with which he was greeted during his visit to Scotland in 1887 to pledge support for Scottish crofters in their struggle for land reform; congratulates the organisers of recent events in Portree to commemorate Davitt’s visit and the centenary of his death, and believes that the Scottish Executive and the Parliament together must ensure that land reform continues to be on our agenda.

Supported by: John Farquhar Munro*, Mr Alasdair Morrison*, Jim Mather*, Bruce Crawford*, Rob Gibson*

*S2M-5162 Carolyn Leckie: Resist the NHS Profiteers—That the Parliament expresses profound concern about the decision by NHS Lanarkshire to put out to tender the provision of primary medical care at Harthill health centre; recognises that it is Scottish Executive policy and legislation that has facilitated this situation; believes that the experience of England’s primary care services at the hands of predatory companies like US multinational United Health should have acted as a brake on any such developments in Scotland; condemns NHS Lanarkshire for choosing to go out to tender rather than seeking to provide the services in the normal way; believes that other primary medical services will be put at risk by this development, which signifies a further escalation of privatisation of the NHS, and calls on NHS Lanarkshire to recall the tendering process and for the Executive to change policy direction and ensure that no further primary medical services are put out to tender.

Supported by: Frances Curran*, Rosie Kane*, Dr Jean Turner*, Tommy Sheridan*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*

*S2M-5161 Margaret Jamieson on behalf of the Glasgow Airport Rail Link Bill Committee: Glasgow Airport Rail Link Bill—That the Parliament agrees that the Glasgow Airport Rail Link Bill be passed.

*S2M-5160 Chris Ballance: Shipping of Prawns—That the Parliament condemns the decision of Young’s Seafood Limited to ship prawns from the Solway up to 12,000 miles and back in order to be hand-shelled in Thailand and then returned to Scotland, without regard to the environmental damage caused by this unnecessary transportation and the loss of over a hundred Scottish jobs, and calls on the Scottish Executive to hold urgent talks with the company to discuss ways of reversing the decision.
