Business Bulletin No. 123/2004

Monday 6 September 2004

Section D : Oral Questions selected for answer on 8 September 2004

  

First Minister's Question Time

1. Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the First Minister what issues will be discussed at the next meeting of the Scottish Executive’s Cabinet. ( S2F-997)

2. David McLetchie: To ask the First Minister when he will next meet the Prime Minister and what issues will be discussed. ( S2F-1002)

3. Robin Harper: To ask the First Minister when he will next meet the Secretary of State for Scotland and what issues he intends to discuss. ( S2F-1019)

4. Susan Deacon: To ask the First Minister what steps are being taken to reform and modernise the civil service in Scotland. ( S2F-1006)

5. Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the First Minister what the Scottish Executive’s plans are in respect of the introduction of a smoking ban in enclosed spaces in Scotland. ( S2F-1025)

6. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the First Minister how the Scottish Executive is encouraging participation in sport and promoting excellence at the highest, and all, levels throughout Scotland following the Athens Olympic Games. ( S2F-1024)

Question Time

Enterprise , Lifelong Learning and Transport

1. Susan Deacon: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to work with employers to address demographic shifts. ( S2O-3116)

2. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to provide affordable English language courses for immigrants and to address any shortage of English language teachers for immigrants whose first language is not English. ( S2O-3016)

3. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to encourage corporate social responsibility in businesses. ( S2O-3078)

4. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to address any economic changes resulting from the implementation of Her Majesty’s Government’s proposals in Delivering Security in a Changing World: Future Capabilities. ( S2O-3023)

5. Mr John Swinney: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to upgrade the A9 between Perth and Inverness. ( S2O-3056)

6. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance it issues to local authorities on road safety near sheltered housing complexes. ( S2O-3066)

7. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will announce its conclusions following receipt of the Airdrie to Bathgate rail line engineering feasibility study. ( S2O-3050)

8. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has had any discussions with the City of Edinburgh Council regarding the recent feasibility study report into the Edinburgh South Suburban Railway. ( S2O-3014)

9. George Lyon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is prepared to reallocate the funding that it has made available to Argyll and Bute Council for building slipways on the Luing ferry route for building a bridge. ( S2O-3112)

10. Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive what locations have been agreed for junctions on the Aberdeen Western Peripheral Route and where the southern link with the A90 will be sited. ( S2O-3000)

11. Bristow Muldoon: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on the reopening of the Bathgate to Airdrie railway line. ( S2O-3025)

12. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects the Borders Railway to be operational. ( S2O-3027)

Justice and Law Officers

1. Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to prevent crimes associated with aggressive driving. ( S2O-3033)

2. Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take to ensure that the Scottish Prison Service recognises the Prison Governors Association. ( S2O-3007)

3. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive how many responses it has received to the consultation on the recommendations of the McInnes Review. ( S2O-3012)

4. Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent discussions it has had with the Scottish Prison Service regarding HM Prison Peterhead. ( S2O-3030)

5. Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive on how many occasions in 2003-04 procurators fiscal recommended no action on pollution cases forwarded to them by the Scottish Environment Protection Agency and what proportion of cases this represented. ( S2O-3131)

6. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the latest available figures are for the number of cases of offences aggravated by religious hatred coming before the courts and how many convictions there have been for such offences. ( S2O-3017)

7. Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive what specific resources it gives to police forces to provide protection for the royal family. ( S2O-3001)

8. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met COSLA to discuss the work of district courts. ( S2O-3052)

9. Mark Ballard: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will report on the proposal to establish a Scottish human rights commission. ( S2O-3128)

10. Frances Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive why asylum seekers who have no criminal charges or convictions against them are being held in HM Prison Greenock and who took the decision to detain them. ( S2O-3119)

11. Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is in respect of whether prison governors are entitled to be represented by a trade union of their choice. ( S2O-3054)

12. Elaine Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take as a result of the Mott MacDonald report, The Future of Fire Service Control Rooms in Scotland. ( S2O-3041)

General

1. Mr John Swinney: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to review levels of service delivery by the Scottish Ambulance Service. ( S2O-3058)

2. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive when the review of the Scottish Diabetes Framework will be published. ( S2O-3013)

3. Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive when results from its consultation on the review of the children’s hearing system will be made public. ( S2O-3123)

4. Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive what advice it has given to GPs regarding the provision of the new thiomersal-free vaccine for whooping cough, polio, diphtheria, tetanus and haemophilus influenzae in advance of its own statement on the product. ( S2O-3002)

5. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making in promoting Fife and central Scotland as a tourist destination for international visitors arriving by ferry at Rosyth. ( S2O-3085)

6. Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to protect Scotland's architectural heritage. ( S2O-3069)

7. Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to review the right to buy legislation for local authority housing. ( S2O-3043)

8. Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in addressing and implementing the recommendations of the Public Health Institute of Scotland’s Needs Assessment Report on Child and Adolescent Mental Health. ( S2O-3081)

9. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive what support is available for the building of houses to rent in areas where there are shortages of affordable housing, such as the Scottish Borders. ( S2O-3011)

10. Frances Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive why the board of the Central College of Commerce in Glasgow has not implemented the recommendations of an industrial tribunal to reinstate the National President of the Further Education Lecturers’ Association of the Educational Institute of Scotland who the tribunal found had been unfairly dismissed and how much money was spent from the college budget in legal advice and representation at the tribunal. ( S2O-3120)

11. Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to ensure that Loch Katrine remains one of Scotland's most desirable fishing areas. ( S2O-3005)

12. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with NHS Fife regarding accident and emergency services in Fife. ( S2O-3093)

13. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how patients will benefit from the implementation of the new out-of-hours proposals in the NHS. ( S2O-3080)

14. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any further plans to reduce the incidence of disease and death arising from smoking tobacco. ( S2O-3046)

15. Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met NHS board Chairs and what issues were discussed. ( S2O-3107)
