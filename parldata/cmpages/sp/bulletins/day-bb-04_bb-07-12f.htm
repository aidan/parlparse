Business Bulletin No. 116 / 2004

Monday 12th July 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-1577 Phil Gallie: Princess Anne's Visit to Gibraltar-That the Parliament commends Princess Anne's welcome and successful visit to Gibraltar in June 2004 and urges the First Minister, in his role as a Privy Councillor, to encourage Her Majesty the Queen to visit Gibraltar for the first time since her visit of 50 years ago.

*S2M-1576 Mr Stewart Maxwell: Cancer Patients & Unclaimed Benefits-That the Parliament strongly congratulates Macmillan Cancer Relief on funding financial advice and support for cancer patients in Scotland; is concerned that 64% of cancer sufferers in Scotland do not claim the benefits to which they are entitled, amounting to almost £15 million in unclaimed benefits; considers it a disgrace that the benefit system is so confusing and hostile that charitable organisations have to fund guidance on claiming for cancer sufferers, and calls on the Scottish Executive to take swift action to guarantee that those people in Scotland who suffer from cancer receive the full support to which they are entitled and to make immediate and strong representations to Her Majesty's Government in support of simplifying the benefits system for people diagnosed with serious illnesses.

*S2M-1575 Mr Brian Monteith: Stirling - The Worst Local Roads in Scotland-That the Parliament notes that the recent Scottish road condition survey for 2003-04, carried out by consultant engineers for the Society of Chief Officers of Transport in Scotland using the latest laser technology on more than 10,000 miles of A, B and C roads, established an accurate and objective audit of the state of Scotland's local road network; notes that Stirling was found to have the worst roads in Scotland with almost two-thirds requiring either immediate repair or further investigation; notes from the research submitted in November 2003 by the AA Motoring Trust to the Local Government and Transport Committee on roads maintenance expenditure in Scotland that Stirling Council spent £5.1 million less on its local roads network from 2001 to 2003 than it received in central government grant, in contrast to the majority of Scottish councils which spent more than they received; further notes that the council's own scrutiny group published research in February 2003 showing that £9.8 million would have been invested in local roads had the local authority and its predecessor Central Region spent their full allocation from central government over the previous six years; acknowledges that Stirling's current Labour administration has consistently rejected alternative budget proposals to spend more on roads; believes that Stirling Council has neglected its responsibility to maintain Stirling's roads to a proper standard, and calls on the Scottish Executive to communicate to Scottish local authorities, such as Stirling Council's Labour administration, the seriousness of the problems that can result from failing to spend residents' and drivers' taxes on the proper maintenance of local roads.

Supported by: Alex Johnstone*, Mr David Davidson*

*S2M-1574# Ms Sandra White: Congratulations to Camcorder Guerillas-That the Parliament congratulates Camcorder Guerillas for the production of "Welcome", a film depicting the plight of asylum seekers in Glasgow who due to government policy are made homeless; notes that Glasgow City Council, whilst evicting asylum seekers from their homes, currently receives funding from the Home Office for houses earmarked for asylum seekers which in many cases are lying empty, and believes that, on humanitarian grounds, the Scottish Executive should enter into talks with the Home Office and Glasgow City Council to put an end to the practice of making asylum seekers homeless and take full responsibility for asylum seekers who are on Scottish soil.

*S2M-1573 Mr Stewart Maxwell: Smoking Deaths per Constituency-That the Parliament notes the high mortality rates attributable to smoking in every constituency throughout Scotland; further notes with concern the wide variance between constituencies and, in particular, the effect that smoking has on mortality rates in the west of Scotland and Glasgow, and urges all members, particularly those from the most badly-affected areas, to appreciate the deaths and illness caused by both primary and secondary smoking and to take the first possible opportunity to vote for legislation that will assist in reducing these horrific figures, thereby helping to create a more equal society for all the people of Scotland.

*S2M-1572 Christine Grahame: The Unclaimed Millions-That the Parliament welcomes the research commissioned by Macmillan Cancer Relief into the extent of unclaimed disability living allowance by people with a terminal diagnosis of cancer, undertaken by the Centre for Economics of Health at the University of Wales, Bangor; notes with concern the figures that show that 64% of people with terminal cancer in Scotland did not claim, amounting to nearly £15 million unclaimed and affecting 9,783 people; notes also that many others living long term with cancer are also eligible for benefit so the true amount going unclaimed, and the number of people affected, is much higher; further notes that Scotland has the lowest claim rate in the whole of the United Kingdom and that someone in Scotland with cancer is twice as likely not to claim benefit as someone in Wales and almost three times as likely not to claim as someone in Northern Ireland, and calls on the Scottish Executive to improve the targeting of funds to ensure that this vulnerable group receive the access to benefits they are entitled to and, further, to work with local NHS boards to introduce measures to address patients' financial concerns.

*S2M-1571 Mr Brian Monteith: Queen Victoria School Appeal-That the Parliament congratulates Queen Victoria School in Dunblane and recognises its success as the only school of its type in Scotland to fill the role for which it was founded, providing high quality education in a stable environment for the sons and daughters of members of all three services, and additionally in making its facilities available to young persons' organisations, including the National Youth Orchestras of Scotland; wishes the school every success with its £2.5 million appeal as it approaches its centenary celebrations in 2008, and welcomes plans to mark the centenary with the building of an auditorium, suitable for assembly, musical and theatrical performances and to be made available to other groups and to the local community for public performances and conferences, which will be the visible symbol of the gratitude of the school's supporters for the sacrifices of servicemen and woman during the past hundred years, just as the foundation of the school was a tribute to the soldiers and sailors killed in the South African wars.

*S2M-1570 Mr Jamie McGrigor: Scotland's Regiments-That the Parliament notes with pride and admiration the professionalism of the British Army's Scottish regiments in both their historical and current roles; notes with concern reports that treasury-forced cuts in defence spending may lead to the amalgamation of all of Scotland's regiments; believes that such savage cuts would lead to severe difficulties in recruitment in Scotland; further believes that the prospect of such cuts to Britain's already stretched armed forces at a time of global insecurity when peacekeeping operations are essential is reckless; condemns any decision that would see any of Scotland's historic regiments disappear, and calls on the Scottish Executive to make clear to Her Majesty's Government the very strong opposition throughout Scotland to any such cuts to the Scottish regiments.

Supported by: Lord James Douglas-Hamilton*, Murdo Fraser*, Alex Johnstone*, Mr Ted Brocklebank*, David Mundell*, Phil Gallie*, Mr David Davidson*, Margaret Mitchell*, Bill Aitken*, John Scott*, David McLetchie*, Murray Tosh*, Mrs Nanette Milne*, Mr Brian Monteith*, Alex Fergusson*, Mary Scanlon*, Miss Annabel Goldie*

*S2M-1569 Robin Harper: World Heritage Site for St. Kilda-That the Parliament congratulates the Scottish Executive, Scottish Natural Heritage, the National Trust for Scotland and other bodies involved in securing extension of St. Kilda's World Heritage Site status to the marine environment; notes that decisions on cultural heritage aspects of the site have been deferred pending comparative studies and notes the difficulty of conducting such studies given the archipelago's uniqueness, and calls on the Executive to act to ensure that all Scotland's marine environment receives appropriate levels of protection, starting with the creation of a department of marine affairs with a dedicated minister.

*S2M-1568 Mr Jamie McGrigor: Competitive Sport for Children-That the Parliament congratulates Portugal on hosting such a memorable and successful European Football Championships; further congratulates Greece on its victory in the tournament; notes that Greece has a population of just over 10 million people but has 2 million football players of all ages actively participating in official competitions of every kind; is concerned at reports that Edinburgh, Glasgow, Dundee, North Lanarkshire, Fife, Aberdeen and Falkirk local authorities have introduced the "potted" sports system that prevents children from losing in sporting competitions; supports comments by rugby legend, John Jeffrey, that Scotland has to encourage a winning culture if we want to have any success on the international sporting stage; further supports the comments of Olympic bronze medallist, Yvonne Murray, that she learnt more from losing than winning, and calls on the Scottish Executive to ensure that all children of all ages in Scotland are given access to competitive sports and the opportunity to learn from the experiences of winning and losing.

Supported by: Mr Ted Brocklebank*, David McLetchie*, Alex Johnstone*, Mike Pringle*, Mr David Davidson*, Murdo Fraser*, Mr Brian Monteith*, Mrs Nanette Milne*, Phil Gallie*, John Scott*

*S2M-1567 Mr Andy Kerr: Water Services etc. (Scotland) Bill - Financial Resolution-That the Parliament, for the purposes of any Act of the Scottish Parliament resulting from the Water Services etc. (Scotland) Bill, agrees to-

(a) any expenditure or increase in expenditure of a kind referred to in Rule 9.12.3(b)(ii) or (iii) of the Parliament's Standing Orders, and

(b) any payments in relation to which Rule 9.12.4 of the Standing Orders applies,

arising in consequence of the Act.

*S2M-1566 Mr Jamie McGrigor: Care Home Funding in Scotland-That the Parliament notes that the difference between what local authorities pay to private care homes and what the actual cost per person, per week amounts to is an average of £71 per person, per week across Scotland; further notes that the cost to the taxpayer of care in council residential homes is some £200 per person, per week, more than is received by the private care homes from the same local authorities; acknowledges that private care homes cannot be expected to provide care at less than the actual cost and are therefore forced to charge top-up fees; further acknowledges that independent care homes cannot bring forward improvements with such inequitable funding; recognises that private homes care for some 26,000 elderly people in long-term care in Scotland and employ a work force of 50,000, and calls on the Scottish Executive to review funding for all care homes in Scotland and ensure that there is a fairer solution.

Supported by: Alex Johnstone*, Murdo Fraser*, Phil Gallie*, John Scott*

*S2M-1565 Richard Lochhead: Congratulations to Peterhead Academy-That the Parliament congratulates Peterhead Academy on taking second place in the Northern Open Rock Challenge Final with its "Maid in Scotland" production and for being excellent ambassadors for Scotland while attending the final in Grimsby; notes the excellent work undertaken by the Be Your Best Foundation in organising the 2004 Rock Challenge for school students, allowing them to use and develop music, dance, drama and production skills with the aim of encouraging young people to maintain a healthy lifestyle by making a commitment not to take drugs and avoid drinking and smoking; further notes that there is only one event of this nature in Scotland at present, and calls for there to be more opportunities for other parts of Scotland to experience this event.

Supported by: Brian Adam*, Mr Jamie Stone*, Mr David Davidson*, Ms Sandra White*, Robin Harper*, Mr Brian Monteith*, Rosie Kane*

*S2M-1564 Mr Brian Monteith: Smoking in Pubs-That the Parliament believes that the people best placed to know how to run their pubs and to assess how to accommodate and meet their customers' needs most appropriately are the local landlords; believes that a ban on smoking in pubs would be illiberal and would remove the right of millions of law-abiding people to enjoy a relaxing drink and smoke in their local pubs; believes that a smoking ban would have a seriously detrimental effect on the licensed trade; understands that many staff who work in pubs smoke themselves; notes that a number of smaller country pubs do not actually employ any staff at all; recognises that people considering working in pubs make that choice knowing that they may well encounter smoky atmospheres at work; further recognises that people going into pubs for social purposes make that same choice; believes that preventing people from smoking in pubs in order to protect those who work in pubs, who may be smokers themselves anyway, is inconsistent with, for example, allowing people to work down coal mines and with chemicals; believes that, if there is a demand for non-smoking pubs or pubs which have both smoking and non-smoking areas, the free market would deliver such establishments, and therefore totally rejects the idea of a ban on smoking in pubs, believing that it is the responsibility of pub landlords to decide what kind of establishment they wish to run, having due regard to their customers and staff.

Supported by: Phil Gallie*, Mr Jamie McGrigor*, Murdo Fraser*, Alex Johnstone*

*S2M-1563 Mr Stewart Maxwell: Clear Labelling for Scotch Beef-That the Parliament welcomes the confirmation of the new rules governing Protected Geographic Indicator status for Scotch beef through the European Commission's Official Journal on 1 July 2004; recognises that these rules will ensure that any beef labelled as Scotch must be sourced from cattle that are born, bred and slaughtered in Scotland and have been reared exclusively through farm assurance schemes; congratulates Quality Meat Scotland (QMS) on its continuing hard work in promoting Scotch beef and believes that this decision can only assist QMS in further promoting Scotch beef worldwide, and agrees with NFU Scotland that this decision will protect and enhance the integrity of the world-leading Scotch beef brand in the face of an even more market-oriented environment by showing the value and strength of Scottish farm assurance schemes.

Supported by: Ms Sandra White*

*S2M-1562 Richard Lochhead: Physiotherapy Week 2004-That the Parliament notes Physiotherapy Week 2004 from 5 to 9 July; believes that physiotherapy is extremely important in helping people of all ages to overcome injuries and resume their regular lives as quickly as possible; further believes that physiotherapy also plays a vital role in terms of assisting those with physical disabilities to live their lives confidently; recognises the pressures and demands placed on the service that are being exacerbated in some areas by shortages; believes that action should be taken to ensure that more people enter the profession, alleviating pressures on those physiotherapists currently practicing and ensuring that the highest standards are attained throughout Scotland, and congratulates the physiotherapists of Scotland for all their hard work in this regard.
