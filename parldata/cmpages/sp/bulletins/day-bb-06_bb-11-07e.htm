Business Bulletin No. 157/2006: Tuesday 7 November 2006
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 6 November 2006

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-29617 Dave Petrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many NHS staff disciplinary cases in 2003 resulted in a suspended employee being (a) reinstated and (b) permanently dismissed, broken down by NHS board.

S2W-29618 Dave Petrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many NHS staff grievances were upheld by the investigating body in 2003, broken down by NHS board.

S2W-29619Δ Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has decided what action it will take to address the issue of trustee remuneration and the provision of trustee indemnity insurance under the Charities and Trustee Investment (Scotland) Act 2005.

S2W-29620 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body what ethical guidelines it uses when considering the issuing of contracts to outside companies supplying the Parliament.

S2W-29621 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body whether it has any contracts with the Eden Springs water company and, if so, whether it is aware of reports that Eden Springs is in violation of international law by exploiting the water resources of the Golan Heights and, if so, whether it will reconsider its contracts on that basis and, if not, whether it will reconsider its contract should it become aware of any such allegations.

S2W-29622 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what ethical guidelines it uses when considering the issuing of contracts to outside companies.

S2W-29623 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any contracts with the Eden Springs water company and, if so, whether it is aware of reports that Eden Springs is in violation of international law by exploiting the water resources of the Golan Heights and, if so, whether it will reconsider its contracts on that basis and, if not, whether it will reconsider its contract should it become aware of any such allegations.

S2W-29626 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive how many midwifery graduates there have been in each of the last three years, broken down by university.

S2W-29627 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive how many new midwifery graduates found employment in the NHS in each of the last three years.

S2W-29628 Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive in which areas of policy or legislation it requires to seek the approval of, or consult, the Privy Council.

S2W-29629 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what level of support has been provided to (a) Strathclyde Passenger Transport Authority, or its successor organisation and (b) Lothian Buses in each year since 1999.

S2W-29630 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how much support and funding has been provided for bus routes and services in (a) Scotland and (b) Edinburgh in each year since 1999.

S2W-29631 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how much funding has been provided to establish park-and-ride schemes for Edinburgh in each year since 1999.

S2W-29632 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration it has given to re-opening a railway station in Portobello; what it estimates the cost would be of opening such a station, and what its reasons are for not yet having opened a station.

S2W-29633 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many dispersal orders it estimates will be used annually by each local authority.

S2W-29634 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what level of support and financial subsidy it has allocated to support and promote public transport links to the site of the new Royal Infirmary of Edinburgh in each year since 1999.

S2W-29635 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-1303 by Malcolm Chisholm on 28 July 2003, how many episodes of care English residents have received as out-patients, in-patients or day cases in NHS Scotland facilities in each year since 2002.

S2W-29636 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-28683 by Mr Andy Kerr on 24 October 2006, how many cases of MRSA and MSSA there were for the third quarter of 2006, broken down by NHS board.

S2W-29637 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the central stockpile of antiviral agents for avian influenza has been completed and, if so, whether each NHS board has been allocated its share.

S2W-29638Δ Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will begin the consultation on the National Fostering Strategy and who will be consulted.

S2W-29639 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive what the (a) main items of expenditure and (b) total costs were in respect of the operation, maintenance, rent, upgrade and any other associated costs of Bute House, the official residence for the First Minister, in each of the last five years.

S2W-29640 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive how many citizens have information held about them by police that may be made available through disclosure or enhanced disclosure requests.

S2W-29641 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive how many applications to become foster carers have been made in each year since 1999.

S2W-29642 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive how many applications to become foster carers were refused on the basis of standard or enhanced disclosures in each year since 1999.

S2W-29643 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact the introduction of disclosure and enhanced disclosure searches has had on the selection of foster carers.

S2W-29644 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what procedures are in place to ensure that information or intelligence held by the police, which might be disclosed through a standard or enhanced disclosure request, has not been maliciously supplied.

S2W-29645 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider placing a requirement on the police to give reasons for the retention of information or intelligence which might be disclosed through standard or enhanced disclosure requests.

S2W-29646 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive, with regard to standard or enhanced disclosure requests, whether it will consider placing a duty on bodies, to whom information is disclosed, to justify the action they take following receipt of information that does not relate to a conviction.
