Business Bulletin No. 178/2004

Thursday 2 December 2004

Section D : Oral questions selected for answer on 9 December 2004
  

Question Time

Environment and Rural Development

1. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to address global warming. (S2O-4503)

2. Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many households have had a water meter installed. (S2O-4472)

3. Mr John Swinney: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken by Scottish Water to address development constraints created by lack of water and sewerage capacity. (S2O-4442)

4. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Over Thirty Months scheme for beef cattle will be ended. (S2O-4423)

5. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to reduce packaging waste. (S2O-4502)

6. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made on achieving an improved nephrops total allowable catch for next year. (S2O-4459)

7. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it has made in planning the core path network in order to promote and provide access to the countryside and mountain areas throughout Scotland. (S2O-4495)

8. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what assistance it provides for farmers seeking to identify new markets for their products. (S2O-4470)

9. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what action local authorities can take to reduce greenhouse gas emissions. (S2O-4494)

10. Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive what support is being given to the wider use of wood chip for a range of uses such as sheep bedding, cattle corrals and wood fuel. (S2O-4475)

11. Bristow Muldoon: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on increasing recycling of domestic waste. (S2O-4516)

12. Carolyn Leckie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to prevent companies found to be causing environmental pollution from avoiding Scottish Environment Protection Agency enforcement notices by making multiple appeals while continuing to pollute the environment. (S2O-4497)

Health and Community Care

1. Christine May: To ask the Scottish Executive how Agenda for Change will assist front-line NHS services. (S2O-4525)

2. Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it supports the principle that parents should have an input into the assessment of the health care of their children. (S2O-4521)

3. Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to develop children’s services at Perth Royal Infirmary. (S2O-4440)

4. Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will complete its review of NHS prescription charges and publish its conclusions. (S2O-4499)

5. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to improve health care in the north of Scotland. (S2O-4526)

6. Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what input it has to the scheme for training medical personnel in rural areas being developed by the UHI Millennium Institute and NHS Western Isles. (S2O-4522)

7. Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are being put in place to prevent the spread of hospital-acquired infections. (S2O-4513)

8. Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to address out-patient waiting times. (S2O-4511)

9. Elaine Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to promote healthy living and reduce childhood obesity. (S2O-4510)

10. George Lyon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is considering writing off NHS Argyll and Clyde’s debt. (S2O-4528)

11. Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Mental Health (Care and Treatment) (Scotland) Act 2004 will still come into force in April 2005. (S2O-4466)

12. Bristow Muldoon: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to address the shortage of consultants in the NHS. (S2O-4509)

General

1. Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent discussions it has had with Network Rail regarding rail electrification. (S2O-4489)

2. Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will appoint a single minister to deal with European and external relations. (S2O-4433)

3. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will respond to the performance inspection report on criminal justice social work services in Glasgow. (S2O-4541)

4. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with chief constables regarding the use of weapons by the police. (S2O-4422)

5. Bristow Muldoon: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on enhancing rail services on the Edinburgh to Glasgow via Shotts line. (S2O-4481)

6. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration it has given to the consultation on the proposed Christmas and New Year’s Day Trading in Scotland Bill. (S2O-4488)

7. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what assistance is being given to headteachers to help them manage school budgets more effectively. (S2O-4496)

8. Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has received regarding the dispute between football clubs and the Pools Promoters’ Association. (S2O-4474)

9. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to tackle antisocial behaviour in private housing. (S2O-4424)

10. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in ensuring that railway stations are accessible. (S2O-4482)

11. Christine May: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will promote the development of marine energy. (S2O-4535)

12. Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures can be put in place to improve school discipline. (S2O-4483)

13. Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration it has given to the introduction of tolling on new roads. (S2O-4479)

14. Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what priority it attaches to addressing the issue of underage drinking. (S2O-4427)

15. Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken in schools to help people who have dyslexia. (S2O-4478)
