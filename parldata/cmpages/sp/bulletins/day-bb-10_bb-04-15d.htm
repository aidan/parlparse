Business Bulletin No. 61/2010: Thursday 15 April 2010

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 22 April 2010

Question Time

General

1. George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent discussions ministers have had with the solicitors acting on behalf of Abdelbaset Ali Mohmed Al Megrahi. (S3O-10181)

2. Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will access the funds held by Ofgem in the Fossil Fuel Levy account. (S3O-10212)

3. Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the UK Government regarding the high speed rail link. (S3O-10246)

4. Elizabeth Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met representatives of Perth and Kinross Council and what issues were discussed. (S3O-10159)

5. Not Lodged

6. Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive what it has done to encourage the efforts of the Machrihanish Airbase Community Company to buy out the ex-RAF base at Machrihanish near Campbeltown for the community. (S3O-10166)

7. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to support businesses affected by increases in their non-domestic rates bills. (S3O-10220)

8. Not Lodged

9. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will intervene to ensure that the concessionary rail travel scheme in Fife is retained. (S3O-10179)

10. Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met representatives of West Lothian Council and what issues were discussed. (S3O-10201)

Europe, External Affairs and Culture

1. Charlie Gordon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is aware of any proposed film or television productions that have been lost to Scotland due to the lack of derogation that would permit characters to be depicted smoking on screen. (S3O-10184)

2. Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will provide an update on its North American plan. (S3O-10160)

3. Willie Coffey: To ask the Scottish Executive what implications the publication of Ginealach Ùr na Gàidhlig, an Action Plan to increase the number of Gaelic speakers will have for Gaelic education in Kilmarnock and Loudoun. (S3O-10228)

4. Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will assess the benefits to Scotland of its engagement in Scotland Week in North America. (S3O-10157)

5. Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive what has been achieved as a result of the recent ministerial visits to the United States of America. (S3O-10180)

6. Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive how it supports community arts initiatives. (S3O-10190)

7. Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has made to the BBC Trust regarding BBC Alba being made available on Freeview. (S3O-10185)

8. Bill Kidd: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations the Minister for Culture, External Affairs and the Constitution has made to Culture and Sport Glasgow regarding its decision to close 12 community centres throughout Glasgow and what the current situation is regarding community buy-outs of these facilities. (S3O-10238)

9. Dave Thompson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that the development of Gaelic is progressing at a satisfactory pace. (S3O-10248)

10. Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will measure the success of its India plan. (S3O-10240)

Education and Lifelong Learning

1. Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the adequacy of arrangements for placing children with additional support needs across local authority boundaries and for funding their needs. (S3O-10211)

2. Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it remains its policy to maintain teacher numbers at the levels prevailing in 2007, as proposed in the SNP manifesto. (S3O-10169)

3. Shirley-Anne Somerville: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to prevent homophobic bullying in schools. (S3O-10247)

4. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in bringing together family mediation and couple counselling services across Scotland and the long-term strategy for delivery of these services. (S3O-10225)

5. Angela Constance: To ask the Scottish Executive whatis being done to address bullying in schools. (S3O-10229)

6. Anne McLaughlin: To ask the Scottish Executive what importance it places on the arts in education. (S3O-10242)

7. Malcolm Chisholm: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take to address the decline in teacher numbers. (S3O-10176)

8. Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it remains its position that the commitment in the SNP manifesto to provide free school lunches to all primary 1 to 3 pupils by August 2010 has been fully funded and whether it expects this pledge to be met. (S3O-10207)

9. Christopher Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to safeguard and expand the provision of artistic and cultural education in schools. (S3O-10236)

10. John Park: To ask the Scottish Executive what it is doing to support musical instrument tuition. (S3O-10204)
