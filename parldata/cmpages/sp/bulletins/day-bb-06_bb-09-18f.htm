Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-4807 Jackie Baillie: The Loch Lomond Food and Drink Festival—That the Parliament welcomes the second Loch Lomond Food and Drink Festival being held at Lomond Shores from 17 to 19 September 2006; congratulates the organisers for the success of last year’s event which was visited by 25,000 people; believes that the wide range of activities including whisky tasting, the real ale festival, the chocolate extravaganza, the Loch Fyne oysters and champagne bar, the farmers’ market and cookery demonstrations from some of the country’s top chefs, will attract more visitors than before, and acknowledges that festivals such as this are vital to our tourism industry and promote the best that Scotland has to offer in food, drink and scenery.

*S2M-4806 Mr Mark Ruskell: The Future Starts Here - The Route to a Low Carbon Economy—That the Parliament welcomes the report, The Future Starts Here: The Route to a Low Carbon Economy, based on research commissioned by the Tyndall Centre for Climate Change Research; recognises the report as the United Kingdom’s first comprehensive roadmap to a low-carbon economy which would deliver on government commitments to keep temperatures from rising beyond a critical point; acknowledges the report’s conclusion that the UK Government has only four years to implement a major new programme of action to cut carbon emissions if the UK is to play its part in keeping global temperatures below danger levels; notes that UK carbon emissions have not fallen since 1990 if emissions from international shipping and aviation are taken into account; notes that, as a first step towards a low-carbon economy, Friends of the Earth is calling for a climate change bill to be introduced at Westminster which would commit the Government to reducing the carbon dioxide emissions by at least 3% year on year; recognises the parallel proposal by Mark Ruskell MSP for a climate change bill in the Scottish Parliament, and calls on MSPs of all parties to back calls for Scottish Ministers to commit to a 3% annual reduction in climate change emissions.

*S2M-4805 Ms Sandra White: Hidden Cost of Nursing Grants—That the Parliament welcomes the scheme to offer grants to internationally qualified nurses to overcome NHS skill shortages in rural areas but notes the damaging impact on developing countries of losing skilled doctors and nurses; further notes the example of Ghana, where life expectancy is 57 years and there are only nine doctors per 100,000 citizens, which since 1999 has lost more than it has trained, and believes that greater assistance must be offered to developing countries so that the healthcare of the most vulnerable people worldwide does not suffer as a result of the recruitment activity of wealthier nations.

Supported by: Chris Ballance*, Frances Curran*

*S2M-4804 Richard Lochhead: Duffus Wins Britain in Bloom Award—That the Parliament congratulates Duffus in Moray for winning the award for best small village at the Britain in Bloom Awards 2006; commends the residents of Duffus for the hard work and dedication required to achieve this highly prestigious award, and recognises that the award is a great asset to the village and will provide a boost to tourism and the area’s image.

*S2M-4803 Richard Lochhead: Forres Wins Britain in Bloom Award—That the Parliament congratulates Forres in Moray for winning the award for the best medium town at the Britain in Bloom Awards 2006; commends the residents of Forres for the hard work and dedication required to achieve this highly prestigious award, and recognises that the award is a great asset to the town and will provide a boost to tourism and the area’s image.

S2M-4802 Euan Robson: One Planet Food: Good for You, Good for the Planet—That the Parliament warmly welcomes the focus on food as part of WWF Scotland’s One Planet Living exhibition; notes that 20% of our ecological footprint arises from the impacts of our food choices; encourages everyone to do what they can to reduce their "food footprint" and help us move towards "one planet living" by choosing more local, fresh, seasonal, fair trade and organic produce and less processed and packaged foods; notes that by making these changes we can make significant reductions in our "food footprint"; celebrates the fact that these choices are better for the planet and better for our health, and looks forward to making these links in the Schools (Health Promotion and Nutrition) (Scotland) Bill.

Supported by: Nora Radcliffe, Mike Pringle*

S2M-4801 Murdo Fraser: HBOS’s Commitment to Scotland—That the Parliament congratulates Halifax Bank of Scotland (HBOS) for pledging to install 50 new fee-free ATMs in deprived areas across Scotland, which will provide thousands of people in Scotland with free access to their money; believes that the pledge demonstrates HBOS’s corporate social responsibility to the Scottish people; notes that HBOS provides more than half of all social banking accounts in the United Kingdom and works continuously to reduce the number of "unbanked" people in Scotland, underlining its commitment to the financial inclusion of all Scots; congratulates the bank on its contribution to the Scottish economy, and calls on the Scottish Executive to continue to work closely with HBOS and all of Scotland’s banks to increase financial inclusion in Scotland.

S2M-4790 Eleanor Scott: Organic Aid Scheme—That the Parliament notes with concern that, out of 314 applications to the Organic Aid Scheme in 2006, only 157 applications were approved, of which only 55 were for ongoing maintenance payments as compared to 102 for conversion from conventional to organic agriculture; compares and contrasts this 50% approval rate with 97% for the preceding year; questions whether this decrease in the approval rate can be reconciled with the aspiration in the Scottish Executive’s Organic Action Plan "to see Scottish organic products grow in market penetration so that they can meet at least 70% by value of overall Scottish consumer demand for organic products which can be sourced in Scotland", and calls on the Executive to take immediate action, both to support farmers and to meet market demand for Scottish organic produce.

S2M-4785 Stewart Stevenson: Record Prisoner Numbers—That the Parliament notes the continuous increase in prisoner numbers in Scotland’s prisons from an average of 5,362 in 2000-01 to an actual number of 7,178 as at 1 September 2006, an increase of over 33.86%; recognises that the record increase in prisoner numbers over the last six years has taken place during a period of sustained operational efficiency cuts that have resulted in significant reductions in the Scottish Prison Service (SPS) staff complement at all SPS establishments; acknowledges the additional strain placed on staff and the prison system by such record prisoner numbers; recognises that such overcrowding in Scotland’s prisons is having a detrimental effect on the health and safety of staff and prisoners, and calls on the Scottish Executive to review urgently the current operational budget allocation of the SPS.

Supported by: Michael Matheson

S2M-4783 Mr Kenneth Macintosh: Action on Anti-Semitism—That the Parliament notes the findings of Westminster’s All Party Parliamentary Inquiry into Anti-Semitism which was established to investigate the nature and extent of contemporary anti-Semitism throughout the United Kingdom; is disturbed at the rise in anti-Semitic incidents in the UK and the fact that this has been exacerbated by the war in the Middle East, and calls on the Scottish Executive to implement the group’s recommendations, including applying the same victim-orientated standard to identifying anti-Semitic incidents as to other racist incidents, better education about anti-Semitism in schools, a crackdown on anti-Jewish activity on university campuses, action to prevent the spread of anti-Semitic material online, the provision of funding for physical security measures at minority places of worship and the provision of funding for projects that aim to develop good interfaith and intercommunal relations.

S2M-4781 Frances Curran: Scotland’s 10-Year Health Failure—That the Parliament notes the review of the Scottish Diet Action Plan which has found that, despite numerous high-profile and costly information and advertising campaigns, the Scottish diet is today just as bad as it was 10 years ago; further notes that the bright spot on the horizon is the suggestion that healthier school meals and the introduction of free fruit in schools is beginning to alter children’s eating habits but it is not enough as less than half our children take school meals; believes that the introduction of free healthy schools meals for all pupils 10 years ago would have had a big impact on the eating habits of a new generation; considers that the evidence is there internationally that Scotland needs less talk and more action; further considers that the introduction of free healthy school meals for all children would be a first important step to improving the health of our children and the next generation of adults, and believes that MSPs need to support this initiative now as we are running out of time.
