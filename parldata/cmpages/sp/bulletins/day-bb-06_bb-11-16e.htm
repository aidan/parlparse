Business Bulletin No. 164/2006: Thursday 16 November 2006
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 15 November 2006

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-29884 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many fiscal fines have been issued in each year since 1999.

S2W-29885 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) qualified and (b) unqualified staff have been employed in the Procurator Fiscal Service in each year since 1999.

S2W-29886 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many children’s panel members there have been in each year since 1999.

S2W-29887 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many foreign drivers have been prosecuted for road traffic offences in each year since 1999.

S2W-29888 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) passengers and (b) vehicles were carried on the North Channel ferry services in each year since 1999.

S2W-29889 Dr Jean Turner: To ask the Scottish Executive why users of Glasgow hospitals, who are not residents of Glasgow city, are not able to access the recently launched Evening Visitor Scheme and when this situation will be rectified.

S2W-29890 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many children under the age of 18 have been admitted to hospital because of alcohol-related problems in each year since 1997, broken down by NHS hospital.

S2W-29891 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prisoners in each prison were sentenced for drug-related offences, including offences to fund drug habits, (a) in total and (b) broken down by category of offence in each of the last five years, expressed also as a percentage of all prisoners in each category.

S2W-29892 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the presence of parasites of the Argulus species will be included as a matter to be notified to its Environment and Rural Affairs Department (SEERAD), as a notifiable disease or parasite, under the terms of the Aquaculture and Fisheries (Scotland) Bill; what steps are being taken to ascertain how this parasite is spreading, and whether the Executive will publish all the information held by SEERAD and other public agencies in relation to Argulus.

S2W-29893 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-29406 by Lewis Macdonald on 14 November 2006, how many children suffering from mental ill-health have been admitted to adult hospital wards in each year since 1999, broken down by NHS board.

S2W-29894 Chris Ballance: To ask the Scottish Executive what restrictions are placed on the activities of visitscotland.com.

S2W-29895 Colin Fox: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to extend the national concessionary travel scheme to individuals who are not senior citizens.

S2W-29896 Miss Annabel Goldie: To ask the Scottish Executive what procedures are followed on admission to prison to assess whether a prisoner is a drug addict or receiving treatment for drug addiction; how long this assessment takes, and what practices are followed to address any addiction or to continue existing treatment programmes.

S2W-29897 Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent representations have been made anent the Medical Research Council’s (MRC) strategic review of virology and its likely implications for the MRC Virology Unit in Glasgow.

S2W-29898 Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to make a submission to the Medical Research Council’s strategic review of virology.

S2W-29899 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will publish its response to the Local Government Finance Review Committee report.

S2W-29900 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive how much the Burt review into local government finance cost.

S2W-29901 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average domestic water and sewage bills have been in each year since 1999, broken down by local authority and showing year-on-year percentage changes.

S2W-29902 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average wage is in Scotland and how many households have (a) one working adult, (b) two working adults, (c) three working adults and (d) more than three working adults, also expressed as a percentage of all households with working adults and indicating how many of those working adults earn more than the average wage.

S2W-29903 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many individuals are considered to be low paid, broken down by local authority area.

S2W-29904 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-29360 by Mr Andy Kerr on 10 November 2006, what the statement “the Chief Medical Officer will then consider what might need to be done” means in terms of action and timescale.

S2W-29905 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that there should be national guidance on the diagnosis of deep vein thrombosis.

S2W-29906 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that more action should be taken to raise public awareness of deep vein thrombosis.

S2W-29907 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact the new age discrimination legislation will have on the level of bursaries available for nursing students.

S2W-29908 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what provision is made for identifying learning difficulties such as dyslexia in students in Gaelic medium education.

S2W-29909 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive in what year of primary school the identification of students with learning difficulties in Gaelic medium education commences.

S2W-29910 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what support has been provided for students with learning difficulties in Gaelic medium education in the last 10 years, broken down by  local authority.

S2W-29911 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what strategies and resources are available to students with learning difficulties in Gaelic medium education, broken down by local authority.
