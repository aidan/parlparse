Business Bulletin No. 26/2004

Monday 9 February 2004

Section E : Written questions lodged on 6 February 2004
  

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-5955 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what powers it has regarding the granting of rebates in respect of modes of transport other then buses, including aviation fuel for piston engines.

S2W-6014 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average number is of NHS patients on dental practice lists, expressed also as a percentage of the average total number of patients on such lists.

S2W-6033 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive under what authority housing associations, such as the Ayrshire North Community Housing Organisation, charge owner-occupiers an annual estate management fee where no such charge has been levied before.

S2W-6034 Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration it has given to primary care services providing carers with free influenza vaccination at a local GP surgery.

S2W-6035 Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration it has given to the training of carers in lifting and handling techniques.

S2W-6036 Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of police officers who have retired in the last five years on ill health grounds were (a) diagnosed with stress or a mental illness, (b) injured while on duty and (c) injured while off duty.

S2W-6037 Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance exists in respect of serving police officers who are experiencing stress, depression or other mental health illness.

S2W-6038 Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance exists in respect of serving police officers who have experienced stress, depression or other mental health illness.

S2W-6039 Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance exists in respect of the recruitment of police officers who have experienced stress, depression or other mental health illness.

S2W-6040 Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has considered funding free mountain weather forecasts, as currently being trialled in Snowdonia and the Brecon Beacons.

S2W-6041 Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration it has given to the reclassification of Alzheimer's disease as a terminal illness.

S2W-6042 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive when work on the Larkhall to Milngavie rail link is expected to commence.

S2W-6043 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive how many expert advisers are currently working as temporary civil servants; how many such advisers are full-time; what grade they are; how they are distributed across departments, and how these numbers have changed on an annual basis since the establishment of the Parliament.

S2W-6044 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-5522 by Mr Tom McCabe on 3 February 2004, why the average weekly cost per resident with a mental illness in residential care units operated by local authorities is higher than the equivalent average weekly cost per resident in (a) private and (b) voluntary sector residential care units.

S2W-6045 David McLetchie: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-3405 by Mr Andy Kerr on 7 November 2003, where each joint ministerial committee meeting took place; which ministers attended, and what issues were discussed.

S2W-6046 Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has received from dog walkers in respect of the Scottish Outdoor Access Code and whether it intends to make any changes to the code as a result.

S2W-6047 Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has issued guidance to NHS boards to discontinue the prescription of nicotine patches to people who wish to stop smoking and, if so, what the reasons are for such guidance.

S2W-6048 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-2866 by Malcolm Chisholm on 6 October 2003, how much the healthyliving line cost to run in each month since September 2003.

S2W-6049 Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive how many undergraduates have declared themselves bankrupt in each year for which figures are available.

S2W-6050 Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive how many undergraduates have been declared bankrupt in each year for which figures are available.

S2W-6052 Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive how many student loan borrowers have been declared bankrupt in each year since student loans were introduced.

S2W-6053 Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive what the total outstanding debt is to the student loans company.

S2W-6055 Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive how many student loans have been taken out in each year since they were introduced.

S2W-6056 Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive what estimate it has made of the average cost to a person of undertaking further education, taking into account any (a) contribution towards fees, (b) graduate endowment obligation, (c) foregone earnings whilst studying and (d) other costs.

S2W-6057 Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive what estimate it has made of the average cost to a person of undertaking higher education, taking into account any (a) contribution towards fees, (b) graduate endowment obligation, (c) foregone earnings whilst studying and (d) other costs.

S2W-6063 Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any student loan debts have been sold to the private sector.
