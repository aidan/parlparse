A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-3334 Dorothy-Grace Elder: Save the Planet Summit—That the Parliament applauds the intent of the Save the Planet summit in South Africa but expresses disgust and revulsion at the lavish expenditure on delegates in view of the key topic of world starvation and urges the UK delegation to scorn the hospitality of the pâté de foie gras and vintage champagne circuit, as exposed by the Sun newspaper, and come to urgent conclusions about ending the horrific conditions suffered by the deprived.

S1M-3329 Mr Lloyd Quinan: Batasuna Ban—That the Parliament regrets the decision of the Spanish Government to disenfranchise the voters, members and supporters of the Basque nationalist party Batasuna by the imposition of an undemocratic ban on its legitimate activities in furthering the cause of Basque independence.

Supported by: Mr Adam Ingram*

S1M-3327# Karen Gillon: Attacks on Emergency Service Staff—That the Parliament condemns the mindless attacks against emergency services workers; recognises the vital role that fire-fighters, ambulance staff and police officers provide in supporting the local community and ensuring public safety, and considers that the Scottish Executive should take all necessary steps to ensure that the safety of all staff who work within the emergency services is safeguarded.

Supported by: Mr Michael McMahon, Shona Robison, Robin Harper, Kate Maclean, Johann Lamont, Stewart Stevenson, Mr Tom McCabe, Dr Sylvia Jackson, Brian Fitzpatrick, Elaine Smith, Janis Hughes, Rhoda Grant, Mr Keith Raffan, Fiona Hyslop, Alex Johnstone, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton, Sarah Boyack, Christine Grahame, Mr Kenneth Gibson, Irene McGugan, Mary Scanlon, Robert Brown, Mr Kenneth Macintosh, Alasdair Morgan, Fiona McLeod, Karen Whitefield, Michael Matheson, Mrs Lyndsay McIntosh, Elaine Thomson*, Jackie Baillie*, Richard Lochhead*, Mr Andrew Welsh*

S1M-3326 Mr Lloyd Quinan: MMR and TaqMan PCR Blood Test—That the Parliament recognises the recent findings of Professor John O’Leary following his use of the DNA blood test, Taqman PCR, which has provided further evidence of a link between the MMR vaccine and autism; believes that the Scottish Executive should provide funding to enable all autistic children to have this test on the NHS, and supports the family of Angus Kyle, who paid for this test privately, in its legal case against the manufacturers of the MMR vaccine.

Supported by: Adam Ingram*

S1M-3325# Mary Scanlon: Ferry Services to the Northern Isles—That the Parliament notes with concern the on-going situation surrounding the transfer of the contract for ferry links to the northern isles; recognises that these services provide a crucial lifeline for island communities and businesses, particularly the agriculture and fishing industries, through a substantial volume of passenger and freight transport, and considers that the Scottish Executive should ensure that ferry links between the mainland, Orkney and Shetland are maintained into the future.

S1M-3324 Mr Brian Monteith: The Threat To Haggis, Scotland's National Dish—That the Parliament notes with concern that the Food Standards Agency has made a formal request to the European Commission to ban the use of sheep’s intestines across the European Union without any acknowledgement of its unique role in Scotland’s national dish, the haggis; further notes the lack of any scientific link between the possible existence of BSE in sheep and its transference to humans through sheep’s intestine; believes that using the same weak criteria could see future bans extended to the offal content of haggis and that such unsubstantiated health scares only serve to undermine the genuine need for effective public health campaigns; recognises the crucial importance of haggis to Scotland’s national culture, its popularity at home and abroad and the enjoyment it brings to so many people, and further believes that, given the absence of any hard evidence and the exceedingly low theoretical risk to public health, Scots should be free to choose how they like their haggis cooked and served.

S1M-3323 Nicola Sturgeon: Glasgow Acute Services Review—That the Parliament notes the outcome of the acute services review in Glasgow; welcomes the proposed £700 million investment in hospital services; expresses concern about aspects of the review, including the proposed number and location of in-patient hospitals and the planned reduction of accident and emergency departments from five to two, and calls on the Scottish Executive to reconsider its approval of Greater Glasgow NHS Board’s proposals.

Supported by: Ms Sandra White, Linda Fabiani

S1M-3322 Karen Gillon on behalf of the Education, Culture and Sport Committee: Commissioner for Children and Young People Bill—That the Parliament agrees to the proposal for a Committee Bill under Rule 9.15 contained in the 11th Report 2002 of the Education, Culture and Sport Committee, Report on Proposed Commissioner for Children and Young People Bill (SP Paper 617).

S1M-3319.1 Brian Adam: Aberdeen City Council Planning Application for a Football Stadium and Other Facilities at Kingswells—As an amendment to motion (S1M-3319) in the name of Mr Mike Rumbles, leave out from "Aberdeen" to end and insert "that only two of the twelve Liberal Democrat councillors on Aberdeen City Council supported a proposal to the Scottish Executive for a public inquiry into a planning application for a new football stadium and other facilities at Kingswells; further notes with concern the conduct of one of Mr Mike Rumbles MSP's employees, who is a member of the council; agrees that the recent allegations of wrongdoing within the council have undermined the public’s confidence in the openness and transparency of the planning process, and calls on the Scottish Executive to hold a public inquiry on the application as a matter of urgency."

S1M-3318.1 Mr Brian Monteith: Scotland vs Israel Under 21s Football Match—As an amendment to motion (S1M-3318) in the name of Pauline McNeill, leave out from "expresses" to end and insert "acknowledges the importance that sport plays in building relationships between different cultures and nations all over the world and therefore supports the decision of the Scottish Football Association to arrange a friendly fixture between the Scottish and Israeli under-21 football teams; recalls that Scottish teams have in the past played the Soviet Union, Iran, Saudi Arabia, Israel, Argentina, East Germany and Egypt and many other teams where grounds for political objections existed without so much as a whimper; suggests that politicising Scottish sporting events could leave a Scottish-based Euro 2008 tournament open to political blackmail by other nations just as the 1986 Commonwealth Games was; celebrates the right to peaceful protest outside a sporting fixture as an integral part of any democracy but unreservedly condemns as undemocratic and counterproductive any attempts to stop a sporting event by physical or violent means, and believes that ultimately participation is a matter for the individual conscience of any sportsmen and women involved."
