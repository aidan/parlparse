Business Bulletin No. 29/2009: Thursday 12 February 2009
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 11 February 2009

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-20789 Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken to explore opportunities to develop an apprenticeship route into the creative media industries.

S3W-20821 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-18600 by Nicola Sturgeon on 15 December 2008, when the review of NHS board compliance with the Protocol for the Scottish Surveillance Programme for Clostridium Difficile Associated Disease by the HAI National Taskforce will be (a) completed and (b) published.

S3W-20822 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-18599 by Nicola Sturgeon on 15 December 2008, where the study of Clostridium difficile ribotypes will take place; when it will start and finish; when it will be published on the Health Protection Scotland website, and when the results are expected to be published.

S3W-20823 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of the emergence of the 078 strain of Clostridium difficile and its potentially higher level of toxicity, whether it has issued new guidelines to replace those requiring samples to be submitted where (a) a patient has developed a severe disease, including (i) admission to hospital or an intensive care unit with, (ii) surgery for or (iii) death following a diagnosis of Clostridium difficile associated disease (CDAD), (b) an outbreak of CDAD is suspected or (c) infections or outbreaks of ribotype 027 are suspected.

S3W-20824 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what level of funding it allocated to the National Galleries of Scotland and the National Museum of Scotland in 2008-09.

S3W-20825 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what projected level of funding it will allocate to the National Galleries of Scotland and the National Museum of Scotland in 2009-10.

S3W-20826 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what level of funding it allocated to the Museums Galleries Scotland Recognition Scheme in 2008-09 and how much of this was allocated to museums and galleries in Glasgow.

S3W-20827 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what projected level of funding it will allocate to the Museums Galleries Scotland Recognition Scheme in 2009-10 and how much of this is to be allocated to museums and galleries in Glasgow.

S3W-20828Δ Christina McKelvie: To ask the Scottish Executive when it intends to publish the guidance, Safeguarding Children in Scotland who may have been Trafficked.

S3W-20829 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken to raise public awareness of its mortgage to shared equity scheme.

S3W-20830 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive, in the 60th year of the Scottish Parent Teacher Council, whether it recognises the value of an independent voice for parents.

S3W-20831 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many offences of selling alcohol to people under 18 have been detected in the Lothians region in the last 12 months as a result of the national roll-out of the test purchasing of alcohol.

S3W-20832 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many alcohol-related incidents have taken place in schools in the Lothians region in each of the last five years, broken down by (a) type of incident and (b) local authority.

S3W-20833 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how much alcohol-related illness cost NHS Lothian in each of the last five years.

S3W-20834 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many crimes were committed by people under 18 in the Lothians region in 2008 and how many of these were by (a) first-time offenders and (b) repeat offenders.

S3W-20835 Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive what the purpose is of section 50 of the Children Act 1975.

S3W-20836 Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that section 50 of the Children Act 1975 has been successfully implemented.

S3W-20837 Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive what the (a) initial revenue and (b) capital allocations have been to NHS (i) Highland, (ii) Orkney, (iii) Shetland, (iv) Western Isles and (v) Grampian in each financial year since 2004, also expressed in real terms.

S3W-20838 Angela Constance: To ask the Scottish Executive what specialist weaning programmes are available on the NHS for tube-fed infants.

S3W-20839 Angela Constance: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the NHS offers weaning programmes for tube-fed infants equivalent to the one provided in the Kinderpsychosomatik ward in the Graz children’s hospital in Austria.

S3W-20840 Angela Constance: To ask the Scottish Executive what financial support can be offered to families requiring access to the specialist tube-fed weaning programme offered in the Kinderpsychosomatik ward in the Graz children’s hospital in Austria.

S3W-20841 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to publish the findings of its review of the National Physical Activity Strategy.

S3W-20842 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list the organisations, groups or agencies that have been awarded funds to participate in the National Conversation, detailing the value and terms of each award.

S3W-20843 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will detail its spending commitments to the National Conversation for 2009-10.

S3W-20844 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list the travel costs, including foreign trips, associated with the National Conversation, indicating the minister and officials involved.

S3W-20845 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will detail (a) the total cost of hospitality for and (b) how many people attended each National Conversation event.

S3W-20846 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-18397 by Nicola Sturgeon on 7 January 2009, when it expects to (a) evaluate and (b) publish responses to the National Conversation; how it plans to do this, and how much it anticipates that this will cost.

S3W-20847 John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prisoners were in custody in each prison on 11 February 2009, broken down by local authority of residence at the time of reception in prison.

S3W-20848 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-20223 by Linda Fabiani on 4 February 2009, when it will announce its final decisions on applications for funding from Scottish-based organisations that wish to assist in providing emergency relief in Gaza.

S3W-20849 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prisoners in HMP Aberdeen come from (a) Aberdeen city, (b) Aberdeenshire, (c) Moray, (d) elsewhere in Scotland and (e) elsewhere in the United Kingdom.

S3W-20850 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prisoners in HMP Aberdeen come from an address with an AB postcode.

S3W-20851 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prisoners from Aberdeen city are held in (a) HMP Aberdeen, (b) HMP Peterhead, (c) another prison in Scotland for adult males, (d) HMP and YOI Cornton Vale and (e) YOI Polmont.

S3W-20852 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prisoners from Aberdeenshire are held in (a) HMP Aberdeen, (b) HMP Peterhead, (c) another prison in Scotland for adult males, (d) HMP and YOI Cornton Vale and (e) YOI Polmont.

S3W-20853 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prisoners from Moray are held in (a) HMP Aberdeen, (b) HMP Peterhead, (c) another prison in Scotland for adult males, (d) HMP and YOI Cornton Vale and (e) YOI Polmont.

S3W-20854 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prisoners with an AB postcode are held in (a) HMP Aberdeen, (b) HMP Peterhead, (c) another prison in Scotland for adult males, (d) HMP and YOI Cornton Vale and (e) YOI Polmont.

S3W-20855 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the evidence given to the Public Petitions Committee on 10 February 2009 by the Cabinet Secretary for Justice, what plans there are for the site of HMP Aberdeen.

S3W-20856 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the evidence given to the Public Petitions Committee on 10 February 2009 by the Cabinet Secretary for Justice, whether there are technical reasons for which the site of HMP Aberdeen can not be used for a new community prison.

S3W-20857 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the evidence given to the Public Petitions Committee on 10 February 2009 by the Cabinet Secretary for Justice, whether there are technical reasons for which the site of HMP Aberdeen can not be used for a new remand facility.
