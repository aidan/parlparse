Business Bulletin No. 162/2002

Tuesday 12 November 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-3563 Irene McGugan: Mouth Cancer Awareness Week-That the Parliament recognises that the second Mouth Cancer Awareness Week runs from 10-16 November 2002; congratulates the Tayside Community Dental Service and other agencies throughout Scotland for their efforts to inform the public about this disease; notes with concern that in Scotland there are approximately 500 new cases of oral cancer and 250 deaths per year, that less than 50% of cases will survive five years and that the incidence of oral cancer is on the increase in younger people, and encourages the Scottish Executive to assist in raising public awareness of preventative measures such as giving up smoking, reducing alcohol consumption and, most importantly, visiting a dentist at least once a year.

*S1M-3562 Pauline McNeill: Maggie's Centre - Westen Infirmary, Glasgow-That the Parliament warmly welcomes the opening of the new Maggie's Centre in the Western Infirmary gatehouse in Glasgow which will specialise in supporting people with cancer, carers and their families and includes an extensive cancer information library and internet access; expresses its delight at the overwhelming public support in Glasgow for the Maggie's Centre, and congratulates all those who have participated in fund-raising efforts to make the centre possible.

*S1M-3561 Ms Margo MacDonald: Ability of Holyrood Progress Group-That the Parliament has no confidence in the ability of the Holyrood Progress Group to achieve any of its own declared aims which are to deal competently with question and answer sessions for MSPs, monitor and report on the progress of the Holyrood project and control expenditure so as to ensure that the Parliament takes delivery of a world-class building on budget and on time, and resolves to exert a greater measure of client control over the project by dissolving the group and appointing an experienced building industry professional to represent the Parliament and the public interest on the Holyrood Project Team in place of the current Project Director.

*S1M-3560 Ross Finnie: The Draft Genetically Modified Organisms (Deliberate Release) (Scotland) Regulations 2002-That the Transport and the Environment Committee recommends that the draft Genetically Modified Organisms (Deliberate Release) (Scotland) Regulations 2002 be approved."

Supported by: Allan Wilson*

*S1M-3559 Irene McGugan: Street Football for All-That the Parliament notes the work of the C.A.F.E. Project, a highly successful and nationally recognised voluntary organisation which provides a one-stop shop for leisure, culture, health, education and employment facilities and outdoor activities for people under 25 in the Arbroath area, which was recently described by Tony Andrews, Director of the British Council in Germany as "extraordinary, innovative and inspiring in the way it reconnects young people with the mainstream - a model for working with disadvantaged young people"; congratulates the project on its plans to develop "Street Football For All" alongside the national "Dreams and Teams" project, which seeks to promote sport, health and active citizenship by using football to bring together young people from Scotland and Germany and 15 other countries of the world to increase understanding and tolerance, and wishes the projects every success with this excellent initiative which meaningfully embodies equality and social inclusion
