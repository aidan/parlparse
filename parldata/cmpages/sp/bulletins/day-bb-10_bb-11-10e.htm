Business Bulletin No. 163/2010: Wednesday 10 November 2010
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 9 November 2010

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-37389 Charlie Gordon: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-37107 by Stewart Stevenson on 3 November 2010, whether work on the transport plan for the 2014 Ryder Cup will include an evaluation of access to Gleneagles station for passengers with a mobility impairment.

S3W-37390 Charlie Gordon: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-37107 by Stewart Stevenson on 3 November 2010, whether work on the transport plan for the 2014 Ryder Cup will include an evaluation of road links serving Gleneagles station.

S3W-37391 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what the cost has been of advocates appearing in the sheriff courts in (a) criminal, (b) civil and (c) children’s cases in the last year.

S3W-37392 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many hours advocates have spent in the sheriff courts in the last year.

S3W-37393 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how appearance rates for solicitors compare with those for advocates.

S3W-37394 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether Scottish Prison Service Industries products are manufactured in all prisons.

S3W-37395 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prisoners have worked in Scottish Prison Service Industries production workshops in the last year.

S3W-37396 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what kind of work opportunities are available in Scottish Prison Service Industries production workshops.

S3W-37397 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many hours per day on average prisoners carry out (a) work and (b) training or education, broken down by (i) length of sentence and (ii) prison.

S3W-37398 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what the income of Scottish Prison Service Industries has been in the last year; what the cost of production has been, and how the money has been used.

S3W-37399 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether prisoners working in Scottish Prison Service Industries production workshops gain qualifications.

S3W-37400 Charlie Gordon: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-37107 by Stewart Stevenson on 3 November 2010, whether work on the transport plan for the 2014 Ryder Cup will include consideration of the reopening of Blackford railway station.

S3W-37401 Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had regarding the proposed European Year of Active Ageing 2012.

S3W-37402 Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what the criteria are for applications for funding from the European Union for the proposed European Year of Active Ageing 2012.

S3W-37403 Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what applications it has made for available funding for the proposed European Year of Active Ageing 2012.

S3W-37404 Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive how many distinction award holders there are following the 2010 awards round.

S3W-37405 Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-36476 by John Swinney on 30 September 2010, whether a decision has been taken on providing an analysis of the implementation of the 2009-10 single outcome agreements.

S3W-37406 Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will discuss with the voluntary sector the implications on that sector of the UK Government’s proposal to require recipients of benefits to work unpaid as a condition of continuing to receive benefits.

S3W-37407 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) announced and (b) unannounced Care Commission visits to care homes for older people are planned in the next year.

S3W-37408 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what fee is paid to the Care Commission by a care home for older people with (a) 20, (b) 30, (c) 50 and (d) 60 beds.

S3W-37409 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what advances have been made in adult care homes as a result of the publication in May 2006 of Making good care better.

S3W-37410 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether independent and voluntary sector care homes for older people could be used instead of emergency hospital admissions for non-specialist assessment, intervention and care.

S3W-37411 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether independent and voluntary sector care homes for older people could be used for continuing care and rehabilitation of older patients rather than hospital places.

S3W-37412 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether more use could be made of the care home sector to provide more choice to people in need of palliative and end-of-life care.

S3W-37413 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether independent and voluntary sector care home staff could be used to provide palliative and end-of-life care in patients’ homes.

S3W-37414 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what is being done to reduce the time taken to access an appropriate care package for older people in (a) the community and (b) a residential care home.

S3W-37415 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will assist GPs to refer older people to care homes as well as hospitals when considered appropriate to meet patient needs.

S3W-37416 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many older people in care homes are cared for by (a) local authorities and (b) the independent and voluntary sector.

S3W-37417 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider piloting telehealth links with care homes for older people to help monitor older people in the community.

S3W-37418 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how independent and voluntary sector care homes will be used to provide community, respite and day-care support.

S3W-37419 Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive how many hoax emergency calls there have been in each of the last 10 years, broken down by (a) police force and (b) fire and rescue service areas.

S3W-37420 Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people have been prosecuted for making hoax emergency calls in each of the last 10 years, broken down by Sheriff Court area.

S3W-37421 David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to introduce a bus scrappage scheme to enable bus operators to trade in older, polluting buses for new, low-emission vehicles.
