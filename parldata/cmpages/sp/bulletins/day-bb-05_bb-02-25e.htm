Section E – Written questions lodged on 24 February 2005

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-14712 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how many local authority employees were given retirement on health grounds in each of the last five years, broken down by local authority and expressed also as a percentage of the number of people employed by each local authority.

S2W-14713 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive how many cases have been brought to court under the Adults with Incapacity (Scotland) Act 2000.

S2W-14714 Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied with the operation of the Adults with Incapacity (Scotland) Act 2000 and what difficulties have been encountered since the Act came into force.

S2W-14715 John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will introduce legislation to give local authorities the power to cut high hedges.

S2W-14716 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of patients suffering from rheumatological conditions are prescribed anti-TNF drugs; what information it has on how this figure compares with international data in respect of the prescription of anti-TNF drugs for patients suffering from rheumatological conditions, and what the reasons are for any variance in these figures.

S2W-14717 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance it issues to NHS boards on the prescription of insulin pumps for diabetics.

S2W-14718 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people are registered as diabetic (a) in total and (b) broken down by NHS board area.

S2W-14719 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive how many diabetics have been issued with insulin pumps to treat their condition (a) in total and (b) broken down by NHS board area.

S2W-14720 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what the cost per patient is of providing an insulin pump for the treatment of diabetes.

S2W-14721Δ Christine May: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to publish its first Scottish Infrastructure Investment Plan by the end of February.

S2W-14722 Dr Jean Turner: To ask the Scottish Executive what the research findings are on new approaches to changing eating behaviour by targeting groups such as schoolchildren, pregnant women and elderly people, as referred to in Eating for Health: A Diet Action Plan for Scotland.

S2W-14723 Dr Jean Turner: To ask the Scottish Executive what action has been initiated through the Chief Pharmacist and the Scottish Health Education Department on identifying methods to accelerate the introduction of low-sugar or sugar-free paediatric medicines, as referred to in Eating for Health: A Diet Action Plan for Scotland.

S2W-14724 Dr Jean Turner: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are in place for NHS boards and local authorities to ensure that health professionals, residential staff and day care staff with care responsibilities for children under five have a working knowledge of the dietary and nutritional needs of young children.

S2W-14725 Dr Jean Turner: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there have been any special initiatives for local authorities and NHS boards to encourage under-fives to eat healthily.

S2W-14726 Dr Jean Turner: To ask the Scottish Executive how many home economists and dieticians have been employed in each year since 1996 to provide advice and support on diet and nutritional matters to families with young children, broken down by NHS board.

S2W-14727 Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what sources of funding are available to assist community halls to comply with legislative requirements.

S2W-14728 Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether community groups using local authority-owned premises are required by law to provide their own insurance and, if so, under what legislation.

S2W-14729 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of prisoner inductions is for prisoners serving (a) less than three months, (b) three to six months and (c) six months to one year.

S2W-14730 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what the (a) initial cost was and (b) estimated annual cost is of the national prison induction programme referred to in the Scottish Prison Service’s oral evidence to the Justice 1 Committee’s inquiry into the effectiveness of rehabilitation in prisons (Official Report, c. 1076).

S2W-14731Δ Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made towards implementation of the Vulnerable Witnesses (Scotland) Act 2004.

S2W-14732Δ Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what capital funds will be made available to NHS boards for (a) 2005-06, (b) 2006-07 and (c) 2007-08.

S2W-14733 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how it measures the number of immigrants who enter Scotland originating from the EU accession states.

S2W-14734 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what specific methods it uses to attract economic migrants entering the United Kingdom from the EU accession states to Scotland and what funding has been allocated to support any such methods.

S2W-14735 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to develop specific policies to attract economic migrants from the EU accession states and, if so, what these plans are.

S2W-14736 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what arrangements are in place between it and the Home Office to promote Scotland as a place to settle for immigrants from the EU accession states.

S2W-14737 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2O-1997 by Allan Wilson on 22 April 2004, what further discussions it has had with the Environment Agency with regard to new byelaws for the Border Esk.

S2W-14738 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive what mechanisms will be used to prosecute anyone breaching the Environment Agency’s rod licence arrangements on the Scottish section of the Border Esk.
