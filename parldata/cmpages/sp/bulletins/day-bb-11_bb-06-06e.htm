Business Bulletin No. 67/2011: Monday 6 June 2011
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 3 June 2011

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S4W-00453 Liz Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of the level of proposed tuition fees in England being set at an approximate average of £8,500, whether it will revise its estimate of the funding gap facing universities in Scotland.

S4W-00454 Liz Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what the impact will be on the number of students attending universities in Scotland from the rest of the United Kingdom of its proposal to increase their tuition fees by up to 250%.

S4W-00471 Neil Findlay: To ask the Scottish Executive what the predicted level of financial support provided to each university by the Scottish Funding Council will be in each of the next four years.

S4W-00472 Neil Findlay: To ask the Scottish Executive whether withdrawing courses in geography, sociology, community education and music at the University of Strathclyde will result in a reduction in its funding from the Scottish Funding Council.

S4W-00473 Neil Findlay: To ask the Scottish Executive whether withdrawing courses in geography, sociology, community education and music at the University of Strathclyde will result in fewer opportunities for people from low-income backgrounds to access degree programmes in these disciplines.

S4W-00474 Neil Findlay: To ask the Scottish Executive, using the weighted student units of measurement methodology, how much funding from the Scottish Funding Council is predicted for each college in each of the next four years.

S4W-00475 Neil Findlay: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made to ensure that a £10.4 million reduction in funding to the further education sector in the 2011-12 budget will not result in a reduction in further education places.

S4W-00476 Neil Findlay: To ask the Scottish Executive, using the weighted student units of measurement methodology, how much funding the Scottish Funding Council provided to each college in the last five years.

S4W-00478 Neil Findlay: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the weighted student units of measurement methodology is disadvantageous to some colleges if it is not providing a sufficient level of financial support required to meet their obligations.

S4W-00479 Neil Findlay: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will report to the Parliament proposals to reform the weighted student units of measurement.

S4W-00480 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive how many teacher training course places (a) it and (b) the Scottish Funding Council have funded in each of the last five years.

S4W-00481 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive how many teacher training course places (a) it and (b) the Scottish Funding Council will fund in 2011-12.

S4W-00482 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people have taken teacher training courses in each year since 2009-10, broken down by institution.

S4W-00483 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive how many applications for places on teacher training courses there have been in each of the last five years.

S4W-00484 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to match the number of places allocated for teacher training with the number of teaching posts available.

S4W-00485 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive whether every trainee teacher who successfully completes their probation at the end of 2010-11 will be guaranteed a post in 2011-12.

S4W-00486 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive how many trainee teachers will be on their probationary year in 2011-12.

S4W-00487 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) trainee teachers will complete their probationary year in 2011-12 and (b) teaching posts will be available.

S4W-00488 Bill Kidd: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are plans to issue hollowed-out ammunition to firearms officers and, if so, whether it (a) has sought information on research in relation to such ammunition and (b) considers that its distribution contravenes the Hague Declaration of 1899.

S4W-00491 Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is aware of any proposals to relocate 45 Royal Marine Commando from RM Condor in Arbroath, in light of the potential impact on jobs in the local community.

S4W-00492 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive whether, before it decided to exclude business travel from the Air Discount Scheme, it surveyed the terms of similar schemes in other EU member states and, if so, what schemes it surveyed and whether they included business travel.

S4W-00493 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding it has provided to the UK Supreme Court in each year since it started operating in 2009.

S4W-00494 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-40242 by Kenny MacAskill on 14 March 2011, when it will next publish an audit of community service.

S4W-00495 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive how many whole-time equivalent police support staff were employed in each quarter from May 2007 to May 2011, broken down by police force area.

S4W-00496 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will publish an updated Scottish Strategy for Victims.

S4W-00497 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Scottish Sentencing Council will be set up.

S4W-00498 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to legislate to create a presumption against custodial sentences of six months or less.

S4W-00499 James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to abolish prison visiting committees.

S4W-00500 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to reduce the number of emergency admissions for over-65s to acute hospitals.

S4W-00501 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many older people received care in their own homes in each of the last five years.

S4W-00502 John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive how it supports apprenticeships in the textile industry.

S4W-00503 John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive how many apprenticeships in the textile industry it has funded in the Scottish Borders in each of the last four years.

S4W-00504 John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive how it supports apprenticeships in (a) carpet fitting and (b) flooring.

S4W-00505 John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive how it promotes skills development in the textile industry in the Scottish Borders.
