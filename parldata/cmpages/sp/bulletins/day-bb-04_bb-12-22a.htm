Business Bulletin No. 191/2004

Wednesday 22 December 2004

Section A : Daily Business List
  

Meetings of Committees

All meetings take place in the Scottish Parliament, Edinburgh unless otherwise indicated.

Education Committee

28th Meeting, 2004

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in Committee Room 6

1. Protection of Children (Scotland) Act 2003: The Committee will discuss the arrangements proposed by the Scottish Executive for the implementation of the Act and take evidence from—

Euan Robson MSP, Deputy Minister for Education and Young People.

2. Subordinate legislation: The Committee will consider the Protection of Children (Scotland) Act 2003 Determination Regulations 2004 (SSI 2004/523).

3. Subordinate legislation: The Committee will consider the Police Act 1997 (Criminal Records) (Protection of Children) (Scotland) Regulations 2004 (SSI 2004/526).

4. Forward work programme: The Committee will discuss its forward work programme to summer 2005.

5. Gaelic Language (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will discuss the scope of its Stage 1 report.

6. Items in private: The Committee will consider whether to consider its draft Stage 1 report on the Gaelic Language (Scotland) Bill in private at its next meeting.

 

Justice 1 Committee

40th Meeting, 2004

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in Committee Room 1

1. Subordinate legislation: Hugh Henry MSP, Deputy Minister for Justice, to move the following motion—

S2M-2170 Cathy Jamieson—That the Justice 1 Committee recommends that the draft Criminal Procedure (Amendment) (Scotland) Act 2004 (Incidental, Supplemental and Consequential Provisions) Order 2005 be approved.

2. Subordinate legislation: The Committee will consider the following negative instrument—

Act of Sederunt (Fees of Sheriff Officers) 2004 (SSI 2004/513).

3. Protection of Children and Prevention of Sexual Offences (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will take evidence from—

Tam Baillie, Assistant Director (Policy), Barnardo’s Scotland; and

James Chalmers, University of Aberdeen.

4. Security of tenure and stability of rents: The Committee will consider what action to take in relation to the security of tenure and stability of rents for those who occupy properties built on leased land.

5. Civil Partnership Act 2004 (UK legislation): The Committee will note the enacting of UK legislation to create a status of civil partnerships for same-sex couples.

6. Protection of Children and Prevention of Sexual Offences (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will consider whether to delegate authority in respect of witness expenses to the Convener.

Public Petitions Committee

20th Meeting, 2004

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in Committee Room 2

1. New petitions: The Committee will consider the following new petitions—

PE799 Trial of a No Take Zone and Marine Protected Area in Lamlash Bay. Tom Vella-Boyle will make a brief statement in support of the petition, accompanied by Don Macneish and Howard Wood.

PE796 Introduction of a pedestrian crossing and speed cameras on the A78 Trunk Road at Fairlie.

2. Current petitions: The Committee will further consider the following current petitions—

PE535 Inquiry into past institutional child abuse. The Committee will hear from—

Chris Daly (Petitioner);

Alan Draper, Chair, In Care Abuse Survivors (INCAS); and

Helen Holland, Vice-Chair, INCAS.

PE531 Alcohol abuse. The Committee will consider options for further action.

PE573 Amending the Adults with Incapacity (Scotland) Act 2002 and relevant code of practice. The Committee will consider responses from the Scottish Executive.

PE580 Introducing preventative safety measures to help combat solvent abuse. The Committee will consider responses from the Deputy Minister for Justice and the Department of Trade and Industry.

PE687 Bone Marrow Register. The Committee will consider a response from the Minister for Health and Community Care.

PE775 Highland Movement Against Water Fluoridation. The Committee will consider a response from the Minister for Health and Community Care.

3. Promoting the Public Petitions System: The Committee will consider a draft report on its recent participation event in Inverness.
