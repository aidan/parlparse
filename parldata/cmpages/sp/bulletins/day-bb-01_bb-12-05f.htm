*S1M-2520 Cathy Jamieson: Improving Scotland's Youth Justice System to Build Safer Communities-That the Parliament confirms its support for the Executive's objective of decreasing offending and disorder among young people, particularly persistent offending by a very small minority of young people; recognises the importance of early intervention measures, of youth work and of increased sports, leisure and educational opportunities in enabling all young people to fulfil their potential and to steering many young people at risk of offending away from a life of crime; welcomes the proposals to achieve these objectives including (a) mapping the coverage and effectiveness of community-based programmes to tackle persistent offending, (b) using the partnership opportunities provided by the multi-agency youth justice teams to develop more systematic early intervention measures and to improve the quality of information provided to the children's hearing about the offending behaviour and how re-offending could be prevented and (c) identifying how restorative justice approaches can prevent the offender re-offending and build the confidence of victims in our youth justice system and increase the safety of communities.

*S1M-2519# Andrew Wilson: Scottish Number Plates-That the Parliament considers that the Scottish Executive should recognise the importance of national symbols and the absolute right of people to identify with their country of choice and should detail what action it has undertaken thus far with the Driver and Vehicle Licensing Agency and Department for Transport, Local Government and the Regions to protect the rights of Scotland's people with vehicles registered here to display a Saltire and/or "SCO" on their vehicle number plates, and affirms that from this date the Scottish Executive should not accept or endorse any situation whereby these rights might be eroded.

Supported by: Bruce Crawford*, Alasdair Morgan*, Fiona McLeod*, Michael Matheson*, Christine Grahame*, Mr Andrew Welsh*, Dr Winnie Ewing*, Tricia Marwick*, Mr Kenny MacAskill*, Mr Duncan Hamilton*, Richard Lochhead*, Linda Fabiani*, Colin Campbell*, Ms Sandra White*, Mr Kenneth Gibson*, Roseanna Cunningham*, Mr Gil Paterson*, Mr Lloyd Quinan*, Mr Adam Ingram*, Ms Margo MacDonald*, Dorothy-Grace Elder*, Nicola Sturgeon*, Shona Robison*, Michael Russell*

*S1M-2517 Patricia Ferguson on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Approval of SSIs-That the Parliament agrees that The Food Protection (Emergency Prohibitions) (Amnesic Shellfish Poisoning) (West Coast) (No. 13) (Scotland) Order 2001, (SSI 2001/425) be approved.

Supported by: Euan Robson*

*S1M-2516 Ms Margo MacDonald: National Asthma Campaign Scotland-That the Parliament congratulates National Asthma Campaign Scotland on its work over the past year in drawing public and parliamentary attention to the long-term condition of asthma which affects over 400,000 people in Scotland; welcomes the National Asthma Campaign's Asthma Audit 2001 and its survey of asthma services in Scotland of winter 2000 which together, for the first time, give a true indication of the work that still needs to be done to tackle the condition, and calls on the Scottish Executive to work with the National Asthma Campaign Scotland in its mission to conquer asthma.

*S1M-2515 John Scott: Beatson Oncology Centre, Glasgow-That the Parliament notes with extreme concern and regret the decision by four senior consultants to leave the Beatson Oncology Centre in Glasgow as a result of major difficulties in the provision of cancer care services and low morale; notes that this leaves the Beatson Oncology Centre with 10 vacant posts which seriously impedes its ability to deliver cancer care and treatment, and calls on the Scottish Executive to take decisive action to address the situation.

*S1M-2514 Irene McGugan: Tartan Army Award-That the Parliament congratulates the Tartan Army on winning the Belgian Olympic Committee's Fair Play Prize 2001 for the excellent behaviour displayed by Scotland fans in Brussels when visiting for the Belgium v Scotland world cup qualifier in September; notes that this award is the latest in a string of accolades bestowed on Scottish football fans; further notes that a record 15,000 Scotland supporters travelled to the match; commends those supporters for the good name they give Scotland throughout the world, and looks forward to the presentation of the award to representatives of the Scotland Travel Club and the SFA in Brussels on 7 December 2001.
