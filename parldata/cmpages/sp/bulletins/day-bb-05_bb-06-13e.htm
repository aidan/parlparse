Section E – Written questions lodged on 10 June 2005

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-17208 Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to map the needs of people with communication impairments but who do not have sensory impairments.

S2W-17209 Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to determine the numbers of people with communication impairments but who do not have sensory impairments.

S2W-17222 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on the (a) numbers and (b) unemployment rates of claimants in each ward of (i) Glasgow City Council and (ii) Aberdeenshire Council in each year since 1999, using claimant count as the basis for such calculations.

S2W-17239 Mr Bruce McFee: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any consideration has been given to amending the advice provided to planning authorities regarding the determination of planning applications in relation to telecommunication masts.

S2W-17240 Mr Bruce McFee: To ask the Scottish Executive what information is collated locally or nationally on how many telecommunication masts are sited in each local authority area.

S2W-17241 Mr Bruce McFee: To ask the Scottish Executive what advice is provided to planning authorities regarding the siting of telecommunication masts.

S2W-17242 Carolyn Leckie: To ask the Scottish Executive what standards it requires from Reliance when escorting prisoners to hospital appointments, investigations and treatments.

S2W-17243 Carolyn Leckie: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria are applied in defining a healthy option on prison menus.

S2W-17244 Carolyn Leckie: To ask the Scottish Executive under what circumstances it would be appropriate to remove standard electrical equipment from prison cells.

S2W-17245 Carolyn Leckie: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance it offers to prisons on the management of long-term prisoners in relation to the proportion of the year that these prisoners must spend participating in (a) work parties and (b) the formal prison regime.

S2W-17246 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive , further to the answers to questions S2W-8485, S2W-10935, S2W-16507 and S2W-16955 by Cathy Jamieson on 8 June 2004, 19 October 2004, 23 May 2005 and 6 June 2005 and given that the contract with Reliance Secure Task Management Ltd for the provision of prisoner escort and court custody services is now fully rolled out, how many prison officer hours have now been released from prisoner transport duties as a result of the contract.

S2W-17247 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive , further to the answer to question S2W-16507 by Cathy Jamieson on 23 May 2005, whether the contract with Reliance Secure Task Management Ltd for the provision of prisoner escort and court custody services was fully rolled out by that date and, if so, why no substantive answer was given to that question.

S2W-17248 Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive what the remit of the Accounts Commission is and how much financial support it received in 2004-05.

S2W-17249 Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive what the remit of Audit Scotland is and how much financial support it received in 2004-05.

S2W-17250 Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive what the remit is of the Improvement Service and how much financial support it will receive in 2005-06.

S2W-17251 Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive how many local authorities provide facilities for the recycling of plastic bottles.

S2W-17252 Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to encourage local authorities to provide recycling facilities for plastic bottles.

S2W-17253 Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive how many local authorities provide facilities for the recycling of household batteries.

S2W-17254 Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to encourage local authorities to provide recycling facilities for household batteries.

S2W-17255 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive how many educational psychologists have been employed by each local authority in each year since 1999.

S2W-17256 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive what the current definition is of dyslexia.

S2W-17257 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria are applied in defining dyslexia.

S2W-17258 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive how many pupils are diagnosed as dyslexic in each local authority area, expressed also as a percentage of all schoolchildren in the local authority area.

S2W-17259 Rosie Kane: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to address the current levels of nitrogen dioxide in Glasgow.

S2W-17260 Rosie Kane: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will produce an air safety plan to address nitrogen dioxide levels in Glasgow, as requested by the European Commission.

S2W-17261 Rosie Kane: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will publish its plan to address nitrogen dioxide levels.

S2W-17262 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-16847 by Mr Andy Kerr on 7 June 2005, why NHS Western Isles received £497 per person more than NHS Orkney in 2004-05.

S2W-17263 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive , further to the answers to questions S1W-33583 and S2W-7812 by Mr Jim Wallace and Cathy Jamieson on 10 February 2003 and 19 May 2004, what the total delegated budget for each prison and young offenders’ institution has been in (a) 2003-04 and (b) 2004-05 to date and what the expenditure outturn has been in each case.

S2W-17264 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive , further to the answer to question S2W-15432 by Cathy Jamieson on 14 April 2005, whether it will designate the operators of future privately managed prisons as public authorities under section 5 of the Freedom of Information (Scotland) Act 2002.

S2W-17265 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive , further to the answer to question S2W-15432 by Cathy Jamieson on 14 April 2005, whether the new prison at Low Moss will be provided and managed by the private sector under contract to the Scottish Prison Service.

S2W-17266 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive what costs it incurred in respect of legal representation at the fatal accident inquiry into the death of James Barclay in HM Prison Kilmarnock in January 2002.

S2W-17267 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive , further to the answer to question S2W-16642 by Cathy Jamieson on 31 May 2005, which part of Schedule D of the contract with Premier Prison Services provides details that indicate whether it gave prior written approval of the training given by instructors to new employees shown in the BBC documentary on HM Prison Kilmarnock and, if no part of Schedule D provides such details, whether it is satisfied with the answer to question S2W-16642.

S2W-17268 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive what the actual relative cost savings per prisoner have been for privately operated prisons in comparison with publicly operated prisons in Scotland for each of the last five years and how these figures are calculated.

S2W-17269 Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take in response to the Sentencing Commission’s Report on Bail and Remand , with particular reference to sex offenders who offend against children.

S2W-17270 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the Identity Cards Programme Team, what issues were discussed and what decisions were taken.

S2W-17271 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether football organisations other than the Scottish Football Association are entitled to seek financial assistance from sportscotland and what the reasons are for its position on the matter.

S2W-17272 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive , further to the answer to question S1W-9902 by Sarah Boyack on 5 October 2000, how many claims for compensation for damage to vehicles and injuries to passengers and pedestrians caused by dangerous and/or poorly maintained trunk roads and motorways there have been in each of the last four years and what the total value was of such claims.

S2W-17273 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of secondary school graduates (a) enrolled in a university in Scotland, (b) enrolled in a university elsewhere and (c) did not enrol in a university.

S2W-17274 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many cases of medical malpractice have been filed in the last five years, broken down by NHS board area.

S2W-17275 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many crimes of (a) abuse, (b) disorderly conduct, (c) theft, and (d) murder were reported to have taken place in licensed premises in the last five years.

S2W-17276 Carolyn Leckie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has carried out a cost analysis of the implementation of the Kerr report’s recommendations and, if so, whether it will place it in the Scottish Parliament Information Centre.

S2W-17277 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive how many children of asylum seekers there were in schools in Scotland in (a) 2001, (b) 2002, (c) 2003 and (d) 2004, broken down by local authority area and (i) primary and (ii) secondary school.

S2W-17278 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive how many children of refugees there were in schools in Scotland in (a) 2001, (b) 2002, (c) 2003 and (d) 2004, broken down by local authority area and (i) primary and (ii) secondary school.

S2W-17279 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive how many equality impact assessment staff were allocated to each (a) primary and (b) secondary school in (i) 2001, (ii) 2002, (iii) 2003 and (iv) 2004, broken down by local authority area.
