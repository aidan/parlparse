Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-3285 Mr Kenny MacAskill: An Impartial Service?—That the Parliament notes with concern the implications of the opening of National Archives of Scotland file, SEP 10/556, for the perception of an impartial civil service; notes that for 30 years the British state concealed a report by Professor Gavin McCrone that strongly supported the economic case for independence, and calls for an assurance from the Scottish Executive that it has never used the civil service to try and undermine any political party or movement in the partisan way that the Scottish Office and Cabinet Office used the civil service to try and undermine the Scottish National Party in 1975.

Supported by: Mr Stewart Maxwell*, Nicola Sturgeon*, Alex Neil*, Roseanna Cunningham*, Linda Fabiani*, Fiona Hyslop*, Ms Sandra White*

*S2M-3284 Roseanna Cunningham: A Warneing to England—That the Parliament congratulates the England cricket side on their success in regaining the Ashes from Australia; notes that Australian generosity will not begrudge them a win once in 18 years, but expresses every confidence that Australia will regain the Ashes at the next meeting between the two countries.

*S2M-3283 Mr Jamie McGrigor: Christine’s Wide Ball—That the Parliament notes the comments of Christine Grahame MSP that cricket is a sport of "only marginal interest in Scotland"; believes that her comments ignore the facts that cricket is celebrating the 220th anniversary of the first-recorded match in Scotland in 1785, that there are an estimated 12,000 active cricketers of all ages in Scotland, that a sell-out crowd of 4,500 paid to see Scotland play Australia earlier this summer and that it is estimated that three times that number could have packed the Grange in Edinburgh had there been capacity for them and that the BBC considered interest in the match to be sufficient to show the game live, that eight of the 20 players from the smaller countries which qualified for the ICC World Cup invited to a pre-World Cup training camp are Scottish and three are Scots-born Asians, and celebrates Scottish cricket’s proud record in helping people from across the world integrate into their local communities in Scotland.

Supported by: Mr David Davidson*, Alex Johnstone*, Scott Barrie*, Roseanna Cunningham*, Margaret Smith*, Mr Ted Brocklebank*, Mr Andrew Arbuckle*, Robin Harper*, Mrs Nanette Milne*, Murdo Fraser*, Alex Fergusson*, Bill Aitken*

*S2M-3282# Bill Butler: Drumchapel High School Pupils’ Support for the Vucaj Family—That the Parliament expresses its admiration for the pupils of Drumchapel High School in Glasgow who have organised a petition which has already attracted hundreds of signatures in support of their friends and fellow pupils, Nimet, aged 16, and Saida Vucaj, aged 13, who were taken from their home in Scotstoun at 7.00 am on Tuesday 13 September 2005 following the rejection of their family’s asylum application; conveys concern at the apparently heavy-handed nature of the family’s eviction which saw Elvis Vucaj handcuffed by one of several immigration officials drafted in to extract the family from their home and the youngest child, Saida Vucaj, removed in her pyjamas; hopes that the pupils’ petition will play an important part in attempting to convince the Home Office to grant the family a stay of removal pending a case review; agrees that these acts of solidarity and support, including staff members at Drumchapel High School writing to Charles Clarke, the Home Secretary, in support of the Vucaj family’s case, not only exemplify the goodwill which exists towards asylum seekers in Scotland but also demonstrate the philosophy behind the Scottish Executive’s "One Scotland, Many Cultures" campaign, and considers that the pupils should be congratulated for their stance.

Supported by: Ms Sandra White*, Nicola Sturgeon*, Tommy Sheridan*, Mr John Swinney*, Stewart Stevenson*, Rob Gibson*, Des McNulty*, Dr Elaine Murray*, Linda Fabiani*, Jackie Baillie*, Tricia Marwick*, Kate Maclean*, Robin Harper*, Chris Ballance*

*S2M-3281 Dr Jean Turner: Scottish Medicines Consortium - Breast Cancer Drug, Arimidex, Approved Ahead of the Rest of the United Kingdom—That the Parliament congratulates the Scottish Medicines Consortium (SMC) for backing, ahead of the rest of the United Kingdom, the use in Scotland of the breast cancer drug, anastrazole (Arimidex) for first-line therapy in the adjuvant treatment of postmenopausal women with oestrogen receptor-positive early invasive breast cancer; notes that in trials this drug cut the risk of cancer returning in older woman to less than one in four, and further notes that the backing is ahead of the appraisal by the National Institute for Health and Clinical Excellence (NICE) who may not be appraising this drug until November 2006 due to funding cuts of £3.5 million.

Supported by: Alex Neil*, Mrs Margaret Ewing*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Ms Sandra White*, Mike Pringle*, Shiona Baird*

*S2M-3280 Mr Jamie McGrigor: Congratulations on a Great Summer for British Cricket—That the Parliament congratulates England’s men’s cricket team on winning the Ashes for the first time in 18 years, England’s women’s team for winning their Ashes series against Australia for the first time in over 40 years and Scotland’s cricketers for winning the ICC Trophy and qualifying for the 2007 Cricket World Cup; further congratulates both the Australian men’s and women’s cricket teams for playing their part in exciting Ashes series, and hopes that what was a superb summer for British cricket will help the further development of the game in Scotland.

Supported by: Murray Tosh*, Murdo Fraser*, Mr Ted Brocklebank*, John Scott*, Alex Johnstone*, Mr Jamie Stone*, Bill Aitken*, Mr Alasdair Morrison*, Christine May*, Mr Andrew Arbuckle*, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton*, Chris Ballance*, Scott Barrie*, David McLetchie*, Robin Harper*, Mrs Nanette Milne*

*S2M-3279 Dr Jean Turner: NHS Greater Glasgow - Bed Review Consultation Time Should Be Longer—That the Parliament urges NHS Greater Glasgow to reconsider the consultation period for the future of acute in-patient beds in greater Glasgow; notes that the bed-modelling document was only made public at a NHS Greater Glasgow board meeting on 26 July 2005, with views and comments to be received by mid-September 2005; further notes that a consultation of this magnitude needs more that seven weeks, especially given that the consultation started during the summer, and believes that NHS Greater Glasgow’s own statement that "developing proposals for service redesign and the related requirement for in-patient beds for a decade in advance is a difficult and challenging endeavour" should reinforce the call for a longer consultation period so that all interested parties and members of the public should be able to have their views considered.

Supported by: Ms Sandra White*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Mrs Margaret Ewing*, Murray Tosh*, Patrick Harvie*, Mr Stewart Maxwell*

*S2M-3278.1 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: Quality Teachers for the 21st Century—As an amendment to motion (S2M-3278) in the name of Peter Peacock, leave out from first "welcomes" to end and insert "while recognising with concern the increasing number of teaching posts vacant for more than three months, welcomes the implementation of radically improved teacher induction arrangements; acknowledges the widening of opportunities and improved quality of Continuing Professional Development for teachers and the development of chartered teacher programmes, but notes the low uptake of these programmes among teachers; recognises the importance of teaching standards in delivering higher educational attainment, and welcomes the Executive’s commitment to secure quality teachers for the 21st century but notes that, with 40% of teachers due to retire within 10 years, the issue of teacher retention must be addressed with some urgency."

*S2M-3278.2 Mr Adam Ingram: Quality Teachers for the 21st Century—As an amendment to motion (S2M-3278) in the name of Peter Peacock, leave out from "the significant" to end and insert "that Scottish teachers are the key drivers for excellence in our education system; further recognises that there will be a continuing need for significant investment in teacher recruitment and induction, initial teacher education and continuous professional development; notes the challenging nature of the Scottish Executive’s pledge to increase teacher numbers to 53,000 by 2007, given that 40% of Scottish teachers are due to retire in the next 10 years; is concerned that the recruitment of mathematics teachers is falling behind targets, threatening promised class-size reductions; commends the work of the General Teaching Council for Scotland in developing quality teaching, and calls on the Executive to fully fund the initial part of the chartered teacher programme for all Scottish teachers".

*S2M-3278.3 Ms Rosemary Byrne: Quality Teachers for the 21st Century—As an amendment to motion (S2M-3278) in the name of Peter Peacock, leave out from "significant" to end and insert "shortage of certain subject teachers in the secondary education sector; acknowledges that teacher morale has declined significantly due to recent changes in promoted post structures, and calls on the Scottish Executive to implement a national strategy on promoted posts structures in conjunction with professional teaching organisations."

*S2M-3274.1 Mr Brian Monteith: It’s Simply Not Cricket—As an amendment to motion (S2M-3274) in the name of Christine Grahame, leave out from first "notes" to end and inserts "believes that the English victory in the Ashes Test, coupled with Scotland’s 10th place in the world rankings, is helping to deliver a resurgence of interest in playing cricket in Scotland; further believes that there is something truly civilised about cricket, from the dulcet tones of Richie Benaud to the Yorkshire drawl of Geoffrey Boycott, the sense of fair play and sportsmanship between competing participants, the inclusive mixture of accents of all classes and regional accents and the physical bravery of facing a hard ball travelling at over 90 mph, to the skill of delivering a googly and the chess-like tactical fielding so that batsmen will play straight to silly point, all of which were on display during the Ashes Test; accepts that one group’s minority sport is another community’s central interest and that all should be given their place in the sun with excellence in any sport being reported by public service and commercial broadcasters; notes that this year’s Ashes Test brought a tear to many an eye, including many Scots’, and, in a world full of doom and gloom, made many people the world over feel proud to be British, and calls on members of the Scottish National Party to display greater generosity of spirit and for them to acknowledge that British sporting achievements which encourage young people to become physically active are to be welcomed rather than cheaply disparaged."

Supported by: Mrs Nanette Milne*, Alex Johnstone*, Robin Harper*, Murdo Fraser*

*S2M-3274.2 Iain Smith: It’s Simply Not Cricket—As an amendment to motion (S2M-3274) in the name of Christine Grahame, leave out from first "notes" to end and insert "congratulates the England cricket team for its magnificent performances over the summer which led to victory in regaining the Ashes from Australia; commends the way that both sides approached the series as a model of commitment and sportsmanship; recognises that the resurgence in cricket extends not just to England but throughout the United Kingdom; deplores the petty and narrow-minded nationalism of the terms of motion S2M-3274 as lodged, which is an insult to the thousands who play cricket in Scotland, the Scottish Cricket Team which recently qualified for the World Cup, those who hoped to see Scotland play Australia at the Grange in August, a game which sold out three months in advance, and the millions of Scots who enjoyed the recent Ashes Test on television and radio; regrets that future test cricket will not be available on free-to-view television next year; recognises the importance of physical exercise which can be achieved through ensuring that people have access to a range of sports, and therefore urges sportscotland and the Scottish Executive to continue to support the development of cricket in Scotland, particularly in our schools."

Supported by: Mr Andrew Arbuckle*, Scott Barrie*, Dr Elaine Murray*, Nora Radcliffe*, Mike Pringle*, Alex Fergusson*

S2M-2911# John Home Robertson: Affordable Rented Housing—That the Parliament is concerned about the shortage of affordable rented housing in *East Lothian and* many communities and the fact that some applicants in overcrowded or unsuitable accommodation can be stuck on waiting lists for years, while others may be compelled to buy or rent housing that they cannot afford; suggests that there is a need for a clearer definition of affordability for the purposes of housing policy; welcomes initiatives by the Scottish Executive to help councils and registered social landlords to increase the supply of affordable housing, including prudential borrowing to fund new building; recognises that the high cost of development land is hindering that policy, and believes that there is a case for further measures to help local authorities to acquire and secure land for affordable housing in the Executive’s proposed Planning Bill.
