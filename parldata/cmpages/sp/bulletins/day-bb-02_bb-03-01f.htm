Business Bulletin No. 35/2002

Friday 1 March 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-2815 Ms Sandra White: Glasgow University Student Accomodation Sell Off-That the Parliament notes with concern the plans of the University of Glasgow to sell off over half of its student accommodation to a private housing association; further notes the concerns of students with regard to resulting rent increases, the lack of consultation with student bodies on campus and their exclusion from key talks on the issue; believes that this can only result in increased hardship for students already burdened with debt from loans and tuition fees, and therefore calls on the University of Glasgow to reconsider the decision to sell off its student accommodation.

*S1M-2814 Ms Sandra White: Sun-Tanning Parlours-That the Parliament notes with concern recent reports linking the operation of sun-tanning parlours with criminal activities; further notes the recent proliferation of such parlours particularly in Glasgow, and urges the Scottish Executive to instigate an immediate moratorium on the operation of sun-tanning parlours until such an investigation has been completed.

*S1M-2813 Mr Lloyd Quinan: New Housing Partnership-That the Parliament notes the motion passed by Labour-controlled Renfrewshire Council whereby the council fully supports the retention of its housing stock, supports the tenants' position that debt associated with the council's houses should be written off without the need to transfer ownership of housing stock, endorses the recommendations contained within the Board Report and seeks an early meeting with Ministers in order to develop options for retaining the housing stock within council ownership with affordable rent levels, whilst delivering the repairs and improvements identified within its consultant's report; congratulates Renfrewshire Council for adopting SNP policy, and calls on the Scottish Executive to make representations to Her Majesty's Government in order to ensure that funds are available to write off the housing debt for councils that do not opt for housing stock transfer in order to ensure that they are able to carry out any repairs needed.

Supported by: Ms Sandra White*, Michael Matheson*, Alex Neil*, Ms Margo MacDonald*, Mr Gil Paterson*, Christine Grahame*, Brian Adam*, Mr Adam Ingram*

*S1M-2812 Mr Duncan McNeil: Euro 2008-That the Parliament welcomes the announcement that the Scottish Football Association and the Football Association of Ireland are to launch a joint bid to host the Euro 2008 football championships; notes that every major party in the Parliament has, at least in the past if not at present, supported such a joint bid; further notes the potential of the bid to bring all the benefits for tourism and the economy that would have resulted from a solo bid by Scotland without burdening the country with four expensive and unneeded 30,000-seater stadiums; looks forward to the opportunity of working and renewing ties with our Irish neighbours, and urges the whole country to back the bid.

Supported by: Mr John McAllion*, Bristow Muldoon*, Johann Lamont*, Trish Godman*, Rhoda Grant*, Dr Sylvia Jackson*, Margaret Jamieson*

*S1M-2811 Robin Harper: Spike Milligan 1918-2002-That the Parliament notes with sadness the death of Spike Milligan at the age of 83; recognises his extraordinary contribution to the development of satire, the extensive contribution he made in support of a large number of charities and campaigning organisations including Greenpeace, Friends of the Earth and the Society for the Preservation of Victorian Lamposts; further notes that he was fiercely against fox hunting, and conveys its sympathy to all of his family. *S1M-2810 Mr Mike Rumbles: Code of Conduct for Members of the Scottish Parliament: Confidentiality-That the Parliament agrees to amend the Code of Conduct for Members of the Scottish Parliament by replacing Section 9.4 with Section 9.4 as set out in Annex D of the Standards Committee's 8th Report 2001, Report on the Investigation of Unauthorised Disclosures.

*S1M-2809 Christine Grahame: Sport for Disabled People in the Scottish Borders and the Waterside Fitness Club, Jedburgh-That the Parliament congratulates the representatives from the Scottish Borders who will be taking part in the World Special Olympics in Dublin in 2003, namely badminton players Ronald Young, Graeme Lang and Steven George, swimmer Alan German, Gordon Yule the UK badminton coach and Jed Renilson the Assistant Head of Delegation; notes with concern that Jedburgh swimming pool is currently under threat of closure due to the £5.9 million cuts by Scottish Borders Council's administration at a time when athletes from all over the Borders attend the pool for Special Olympics Regional Squad sessions on a weekly basis and that the pool is essential not only for those athletes, but the entire community, with swimming lessons taking place Monday to Saturday with 16 classes and 10 pupils to a class, disabled lessons with 1 to 1 tuition and waiting lists for all swimming lessons, parent and baby classes, fun sessions for children and crash swimming courses; further notes that the pool is used by over 600 pupils per week from the primary schools Parkside, Howdenburn, Hobkirk, Keilder/Bryness, Glendouglas, Jedburgh Grammar, Earlston High and Howdenburn School House Special Needs with disabled dippers' groups, community education group canoeing, Jedburgh Rugby Football Club and Women's Borders Aid also as user groups, and calls upon the Scottish Executive to ensure that this essential community facility does not close.

*S1M-2808 Robert Brown: Ratification of the Optional Protocol to the UN Convention on the Rights of the Child on the Involvement of Children in Armed Conflict-That the Parliament notes that Her Majesty's Government has not yet ratified the Optional Protocol to the UN Convention on the Rights of the Child on the Involvement of Children in Armed Conflict; notes the interest of this Parliament in protecting the rights of children; believes that children under 18 are entitled to the protection of the safeguards in the Protocol, and urges Her Majesty's Government to ratify the Protocol as soon as possible.

*S1M-2807 Mr Duncan Hamilton: Lack of General Practitioner in Helmsdale-That the Parliament regrets the recent decision by Highland NHS Board not to seek a replacement general practitioner for Helmsdale; notes the negative impact which this will have on the local community; condemns the Board's consultation process as deeply flawed; deplores the fact that some residents must now have to travel up to fifty miles for the services of a GP, and calls upon the Scottish Executive to do all it can to ensure that a GP is appointed in Helmsdale as soon as possible.
