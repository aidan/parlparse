Business Bulletin No. 183/2009: Tuesday 8 December 2009
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 7 December 2009

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-29740 Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive whether, between 9 May and 2 July 2007, advice on class sizes in primary one to primary three was given to special advisers but not ministers.

S3W-29741 Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Chief Medical Officer for Scotland is a civil servant.

S3W-29742 Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many hospital admissions have resulted from deliberate self-harm by people under 16 in each year since 1999, broken down by NHS board.

S3W-29743 Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many hospital admissions have resulted from deliberate self-harm by people aged 16 and over in each year since 1999, broken down by NHS board.

S3W-29744 Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive what estimate it has made of the cost to the NHS of treating obesity-related illness and how this cost has changed over the last 10 years.

S3W-29745 Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive what estimate it has made of the cost of obesity to the NHS and how this cost has changed over the last 10 years.

S3W-29746 Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many deaths due to obesity-related illness there have been in each of the last five years, broken down by NHS board.

S3W-29747 Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to reduce healthcare associated infections in community healthcare settings.

S3W-29748 Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive who was involved in making the decision that district nurses would administer the influenza A (H1N1) vaccine to at-risk groups who are housebound or in care homes and whether the NHS partnership process was followed in making this decision.

S3W-29749 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what matters were discussed at the last meeting of the Small Business Consultative Group.

S3W-29750 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to publish the minutes of the last meeting of the Small Business Consultative Group.

S3W-29751Δ Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to consult any bodies about possibly extending to them the coverage of the Freedom of Information (Scotland) Act 2002.

S3W-29752 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is aware of the World Business Angels Association conference being held in Beijing.

S3W-29753 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what representation Scotland has at the World Business Angels Association conference being held in Beijing.

S3W-29754 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on the representation that other parts of the United Kingdom have at the World Business Angels Association conference being held in Beijing.

S3W-29755 Peter Peacock: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made with the independent review of the origins of the recent outbreak of infectious salmon anaemia in Shetland.

S3W-29756 Peter Peacock: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has access to independent findings on infectious salmon anaemia outbreaks prior to authorising any payments to affected fish farms.

S3W-29757 Peter Peacock: To ask the Scottish Executive under what circumstances and for what purposes it can authorise payments to fish farms affected by infectious salmon anaemia and what considerations it must take into account in doing so.

S3W-29758 Peter Peacock: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions have been held with the Norwegian Government on infectious salmon anaemia and what lessons have emerged.

S3W-29759 Peter Peacock: To ask the Scottish Executive what contingency plans exist to deal with unacceptably high levels of sea lice infestation on salmon farms.

S3W-29760 Peter Peacock: To ask the Scottish Executive when the review of non-domestic permitted development rights will (a) commence and (b) be completed.

S3W-29761 Peter Peacock: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to review the requirement to notify planning authorities of the construction of hill tracks for agricultural purposes.

S3W-29762 Peter Peacock: To ask the Scottish Executive what evidence is required by local authorities to satisfy themselves that a hill track is for agricultural use.

S3W-29763 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether every senior charge nurse has direct responsibility for the deployment of cleaning staff.

S3W-29764 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether every senior charge nurse has a budget and direct responsibility for deploying it.

S3W-29765 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the revised estimate is of the cost for (a) small and (b) large retailers of compliance with the ban on the display of tobacco as contained in the Tobacco and Primary Medical Services (Scotland) Bill.

S3W-29766 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the timetable is for the regulations on banning tobacco displays for (a) small and (b) large retailers and what time will be provided for consultation.

S3W-29767 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will issue clear guidance to GPs on the diagnosis of oesophageal cancer to encourage quicker referral of symptomatic patients for testing.

S3W-29768 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many endoscopy clinics are located in primary care settings.

S3W-29769 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to increase the number of endoscopy clinics in primary care settings.

S3W-29770 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the five-year survival rates are for people diagnosed with oesophageal cancer, broken down by gender, and how this compares with other cancers.

S3W-29771 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive where light-induced fluorescence endoscopy systems are available in the NHS

S3W-29772 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason the real-time data on infection rates by hospital is not available on the NHS web portal and whether it will list the hospitals for which no data has been supplied.

S3W-29773 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason the real-time data on infection rates is not provided by every NHS board and whether it will list those boards for which no data has been supplied.

S3W-29774 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason the real-time data on infection rates by specialty is not available on the NHS web portal and whether it will list those specialties by hospital for which no data has been supplied.

S3W-29775 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason the real-time data on infection rates on the NHS web portal is on average two months old.

S3W-29776 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many individuals were infected with Clostridium difficile during the period (a) December 2007 to December 2008 and (b) December 2008 to December 2009, broken down by hospital.

S3W-29777 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many individual fatalities from Clostridium difficile there were during the period (a) December 2007 to December 2008 and (b) December 2008 to December 2009, broken down by hospital.

S3W-29778 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many individuals were infected with norovirus during the period (a) December 2007 to December 2008 and (b) December 2008 to December 2009.

S3W-29779 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what wards were closed or had visitor access restricted due to infection during the period (a) December 2007 to December 2008 and (b) December 2008 to December 2009, broken down by hospital.

S3W-29780Δ Ian McKee: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to take action in response to the Scottish Law Commission reports on succession and on damages.

S3W-29781 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what age group is targeted in the Scottish Breast Screening Programme.

S3W-29782 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of women eligible for the Scottish Breast Screening Programme takes up the service.

S3W-29783 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of women from deprived areas eligible for the Scottish Breast Screening Programme takes up the service.

S3W-29784 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the incidence of breast cancer was in (a) 2006-07, (b) 2007-08, (c) 2008-09 and (d) April to October 2009.

S3W-29785 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the survival rate for those diagnosed with breast cancer was in (a) 2006-07, (b) 2007-08, (c) 2008-09 and (d) April to October 2009.

S3W-29786 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to facilitate the early diagnosis of breast cancer.

S3W-29787 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will extend the age range for automatic screening for breast cancer.

S3W-29788 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the review of the role of a senior charge nurse is now complete and, if so, what key changes have arisen from the review.

S3W-29789 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many nurses are employed in the NHS and how many are (a) registered and (b) not registered.

S3W-29790 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many alcohol liaison nurses are employed in the NHS, broken down by (a) NHS board, (b) hospital and (c) primary care setting.

S3W-29791 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take in response to the recommendations in the Mental Welfare Commission for Scotland’s report, Investigation into the care and treatment of Mr Q.

S3W-29792 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it took to improve service provision in the NHS in response to the report, Psychiatric services for adolescents and adults with Asperger syndrome and other autistic-spectrum disorders, published by the Royal College of Psychiatrists in April 2006.

S3W-29793 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what specialist hospital provision is available for people with Asperger syndrome, broken down by NHS board.

S3W-29794 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will ensure that those people with Asperger syndrome who are placed in non-specialist hospital facilities receive treatment appropriate to their needs.

S3W-29795 Peter Peacock: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take to better protect golden eagles.

S3W-29796 Peter Peacock: To ask the Scottish Executive in what regions it or Scottish Natural Heritage considers golden eagle populations to be in an unsatisfactory or vulnerable state and what the reasons for this are.

S3W-29797 Peter Peacock: To ask the Scottish Executive how many hen harriers are known to nest in Scotland, broken down by local authority area, and when these data were collected.
