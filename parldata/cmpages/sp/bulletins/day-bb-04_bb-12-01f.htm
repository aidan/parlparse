Business Bulletin No. 177/2004

Wednesday 1 December 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-2096 Lewis Macdonald: Towards a Sustainable Aquaculture Industry—That the Parliament welcomes the Scottish Executive commitment, as set out in A Partnership for a Better Scotland, to support a sustainable aquaculture industry, through the implementation of the Strategic Framework for Scottish Aquaculture and other measures, including trade defence and a review of the regulatory procedures and associated costs and of the scope for improved access to veterinary medicines, all of which are designed to protect and improve employment and investment opportunities in many parts of the Highlands and Islands, including many of our most remote rural and island communities.

Supported by: Ross Finnie*

*S2M-2093 Mr Tom McCabe: Efficient Government—That the Parliament welcomes the publication of Building a Better Scotland: Efficient Government – Securing Efficiency, Effectiveness and Productivity; recognises the need to increase the efficiency, effectiveness and productivity of the Scottish public sector, and endorses the Scottish Executive’s ambition to make the Scottish public sector the most productive and innovative in the United Kingdom.

Supported by: Tavish Scott*

*S2M-2092 Scott Barrie: Lauder College, Dunfermline - Scottish Employer of the Year—That the Parliament congratulates Lauder College in Dunfermline on being named Working Families/Lloyds TSB Scotland Scottish Employer of the Year 2004; considers that this important accolade demonstrates that the further education sector contains good and progressive employers who can demonstrate excellence in management and governance, and hopes that other employers in both the public and private sectors will follow the excellent example of Lauder College as an employer.

*S2M-2091 Ms Margaret Curran on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Business Motion—That the Parliament agrees—

(c) that the Justice 1 Committee reports to the Justice 2 Committee by 3 December 2004 on the Civil Legal Aid (Scotland) Amendment (No.2) Regulations 2004 (SSI 2004/491); the Advice and Assistance (Scotland) Amendment (No.3) Regulations 2004 (SSI 2004/492); and the Legal Aid (Scotland) Act 1986 Amendment Regulations 2004 (SSI 2004/493).
