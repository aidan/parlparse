Business Bulletin No. 4/2009: Thursday 8 January 2009
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 15 January 2008

Question Time

General

1. Bashir Ahmad: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last had discussions with representatives of the Foreign and Commonwealth Office regarding the situation in Gaza and any implications for the Scottish Government’s International Development Policy. (S3O-5524)

2. Bill Kidd: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to encourage the spread and development of new and existing woodland areas in urban environments. (S3O-5538)

3. Dave Thompson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Strategic Transport Projects Review is the only means by which it will progress transport developments. (S3O-5547)

4. Keith Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers the compensation arrangements for small businesses in the event of electricity supply interruptions to be adequate. (S3O-5525)

5. John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it will give to Scottish Borders Council to develop public transport in Roxburgh and Berwickshire. (S3O-5456)

6. Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to support shopworkers recently made redundant as a result of the closure of several leading high street stores. (S3O-5469)

7. Elizabeth Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that it is doing enough to tackle indiscipline in schools. (S3O-5451)

8. John Park: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent meetings it has had with energy companies to discuss consumer energy bills. (S3O-5497)

9. Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to deal with retailers selling alcohol to under-18s. (S3O-5508)

10. Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to support retail businesses in rural town centres in light of the current economic slowdown. (S3O-5458)

Health and Wellbeing

1. Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive what it is doing to support housing associations. (S3O-5494)

2. Bill Aitken: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Cabinet Secretary for Health and Wellbeing will next meet the chief executive of NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde. (S3O-5459)

3. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive what it is doing to tackle poverty and income inequality. (S3O-5485)

4. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with NHS Fife, Fife Council and nursing trade unions on training carers in homes for people with complex needs to replace fully qualified nursing staff. (S3O-5473)

5. James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on the planned new build of the Southern General Hospital. (S3O-5481)

6. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in the use of telehealth and telemedicine. (S3O-5461)

7. Charlie Gordon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that the operation of GHA (Management) Ltd should be regulated and, if so, how. (S3O-5477)

8. Christina McKelvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in providing specialist nurses for motor neurone disease sufferers. (S3O-5542)

9. Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had in relation to the provision of alcohol misuse services in Aberdeen. (S3O-5465)

10. Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to increase access to NHS dentistry in Grampian in light of an increase to 30,936 in the number of people in Grampian waiting to be registered with an NHS dentist. (S3O-5516)

11. David Whitton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide an update on the measures that it is taking to help those facing repossession of their houses. (S3O-5503)

12. Derek Brownlee: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take to respond to the challenges highlighted in the Audit Scotland report Financial overview of the NHS in Scotland 2007-08. (S3O-5460)

13. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to tackle overcrowding in social housing. (S3O-5492)

14. Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will ensure that housing associations are enabled to build new developments in response to local demands, in light of recent cuts to the Housing Association Grant subsidy. (S3O-5478)

15. Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive how much NHS Grampian will save in 2009-10 as a consequence of the reduction in VAT announced by the Chancellor of the Exchequer on 24 November 2008. (S3O-5488)

16. Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive what its reasons are for not introducing a dedicated webpage that would provide at one source publicly-accessible information on hospital-acquired infections and mortality figures for every hospital. (S3O-5463)

17. Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met money advice organisations and what issues were discussed. (S3O-5483)

18. Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress there has been on the construction of the new Midlothian community hospital. (S3O-5468)

19. Willie Coffey: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure that long-serving members of staff who have retired from the NHS after the introduction of Agenda for Change are receiving their full pension entitlements based on their revised salaries. (S3O-5527)

20. John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with NHS boards regarding the provision of specialist Parkinson’s disease nurses. (S3O-5453)
