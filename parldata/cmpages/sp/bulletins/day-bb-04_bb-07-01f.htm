Business Bulletin No. 114 / 2004

Thursday 1st July 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-1557 Patricia Ferguson on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Approval of SSI-That the Parliament agrees that the Scottish Outdoor Access Code: Proposed Code (SE/2004/101) be approved.

Supported by: Tavish Scott*

*S2M-1556 Rhona Brankin: Physiotherapy Week - 5 to 9 July 2004-That the Parliament welcomes the Chartered Society of Physiotherapy's Physiotherapy Week from 5 to 9 July 2004; notes that 850,000 working days were lost due to musculoskeletal disorders last year in Scotland; recognises the value of workplace physiotherapy in the fight against work-related ill health, and agrees with the Chartered Society of Physiotherapy that prevention is better than cure.

Supported by: Linda Fabiani*, Mike Pringle*, Brian Adam*, Sarah Boyack*, Janis Hughes*, Richard Lochhead*, Murdo Fraser*, Richard Baker*, Dr Sylvia Jackson*, Eleanor Scott*, Pauline McNeill*, Mrs Margaret Ewing*, Rob Gibson*, Mr Kenny MacAskill*, Bruce Crawford*, Bill Butler*, Dr Elaine Murray*, Tommy Sheridan*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*

*S2M-1555 Mr Stewart Maxwell: New York Introduces Self-Extinguishing Cigarettes-That the Parliament congratulates New York State on the ban of sales of non-self-extinguishing cigarettes within New York State; appreciates that this measure will help in saving the lives of some of the 900 Americans who die each year by fires started by cigarettes left unattended, and, given that smoking-related fires are the most common reason for preventable deaths reported by the Fire Service in Scotland and that under the Scotland Act 1998 this issue is reserved to Westminster, urges the Scottish Executive to make representations to Her Majesty's Government to introduce similar legislation in Scotland.

*S2M-1554 Robin Harper: Protection of Scotland's Coasts-That the Parliament notes the first anniversary of the sinking of the MV Jambo in the Minch; regrets the failure to protect one of Scotland's most important and fragile marine areas and regards the overall protection of Scotland's coasts as inadequate; calls on the Scottish Executive to introduce further measures to ensure the conservation and protection of Scotland's marine environment and to that all the recommendations of the Donaldson report which followed the Braer disaster are fully implemented, and calls on the Scottish Executive to give further consideration to the appointment of a dedicated minister for the marine environment to deliver coherent government action on the seas.

Supported by: Mark Ballard*, Eleanor Scott*, Campbell Martin*, Rob Gibson*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, John Farquhar Munro*, Chris Ballance*, Richard Lochhead*, Tommy Sheridan*

*S2M-1553 Carolyn Leckie: Iraq Remains Occupied-That the Parliament regards the alleged handover of sovereignty from coalition occupying forces in Iraq as completely fictitious; views the new Iraqi government as an unelected puppet of the US regime, its sovereignty guaranteed by an army of 160,000 foreign fighters and 3,000 US diplomats; notes that the Iraqi Prime Minister is a former CIA agent; notes that the new US embassy in Baghdad will be the largest in the world and bigger than the Iraqi government headquarters; notes that the US State Department has taken $184m earmarked for drinking water projects and moved it to the budget for the embassy; notes that the United States will continue to control all aspects of Iraqi life through "inspectors" appointed by the US; is outraged that 80,000 Western private mercenaries operating on behalf of Western companies in Iraq have been given immunity from prosecution for any crimes that they may commit including murder; notes that the US will continue to detain some 5,000 Iraqis without trial, and reaffirms its call for the immediate withdrawal of all coalition troops from Iraq and the ending of the privatisation of Iraq's infrastructure.

Supported by: Colin Fox*, Rosie Kane*, Tommy Sheridan*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*
