Business Bulletin No. 62/2009: Monday 6 April 2009
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 3 April 2009

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-22655 Mr Frank McAveety: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-21583 by Keith Brown on 25 March 2009, how much additional funding has been provided to subsidise five days of outdoor education for schools in deprived communities and how many schools in the 10 most deprived council wards provide five days of outdoor education.

S3W-22656 Mr Frank McAveety: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-21582 by Shona Robison on 18 March 2009, how many school sports clubs receive subsidised sports lets and what direct funding has been given to schools for this purpose.

S3W-22657 Mr Frank McAveety: To ask the Scottish Executive what volunteering programmes it has initiated to introduce young people to local sporting programmes.

S3W-22658 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people were declared bankrupt in the last quarter of 2007, also broken down by sheriffdom.

S3W-22659 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people were declared bankrupt in the last quarter of 2006, also broken down by sheriffdom.

S3W-22660 Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive whether responsibility for the administration of the Rural Empty Properties Grant scheme has been transferred from Communities Scotland to local authorities and, if so, (a) when it was transferred, (b) what action was taken to ensure that expertise and skills built up by employees at Communities Scotland in relation to the scheme were passed on to local authorities, (c) what the Scottish Government’s response is to concerns that some local authorities are unwilling or unable to administer or promote the scheme, (d) how local authorities promote the scheme and (e) whether it will encourage local authorities to do more to promote the scheme.

S3W-22661 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive how many applications were received under the (a) agri-environment, (b) business development and (c) forestry elements of the Rural Priorities scheme.

S3W-22662 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of applications were successful under the (a) agri-environment, (b) business development and (c) forestry elements of the Rural Priorities scheme.

S3W-22663 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what the level of spending has been on successful applications under the (a) agri-environment, (b) business development and (c) forestry elements of the Rural Priorities scheme.

S3W-22664 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what the original budget allocation was for the (a) agri-environment, (b) business development and (c) forestry elements of the Rural Priorities scheme.

S3W-22665 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations ministers have received from farming industry representatives regarding ongoing support to maintain a national scheme for dealing with fallen stock.

S3W-22666 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what estimate has been made of the costs of maintaining a national scheme for dealing with fallen stock.

S3W-22667 David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to reopen the women-only facility at HM Prison Inverness.

S3W-22668 Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects the Registration Office in Bellshill to have its family history section ready to go online.

S3W-22669 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it agrees with the estimate that one in seven couples need help in conceiving and rely on treatment.

S3W-22670 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many patients have received IVF treatment in each of the last five years.

S3W-22671 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many patients have received assisted conception services in each of the last five years.

S3W-22672 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is aware that 80% of couples have been required to pay for assisted conception services because of a lack of NHS provision.

S3W-22673 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many times guidance on infertility services has been issued to NHS boards in the last five years.

S3W-22674 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive on what dates guidance on infertility services was issued to NHS boards in each of the last five years.

S3W-22675 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether NHS boards are required to submit plans on implementing guidance to the Cabinet Secretary for Health and Wellbeing.

S3W-22676 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it can take when its guidance is not implemented by NHS boards.

S3W-22677 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to assist NHS boards to implement its guidance in areas where waiting times have reached unacceptable levels.

S3W-22678 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met the Expert Advisory Group on Infertility Services in Scotland.

S3W-22679 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has made any assessment of the cost of implementing the National Institute for Health and Clinical Excellence (NICE) guidelines for infertility services.

S3W-22680 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will set targets for NHS boards to deliver assisted conception services in each board area.

S3W-22681 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive what its views are of the six-year waiting time for assisted conception services identified in an Expert Advisory Group on Infertility Services in Scotland report.

S3W-22682 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people registered as homeless in the Lothians region in each of the last four quarters for which information is available, broken down by local authority.

S3W-22683 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people have registered as homeless in the Lothians region in each month since May 2007.

S3W-22684 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people have registered as homeless in the Lothians region in each year since 2002, broken down by local authority.

S3W-22685 Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria were used to allocate invitations to the Apprenticeship Summit and whether all major stakeholders will have the opportunity to attend.

S3W-22686 Ted Brocklebank: To ask the Scottish Executive how much it has spent on advertising in newspapers in each month since June 2007, broken down by (a) campaign and (b) newspaper.

S3W-22687 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive whether ministers or Transport Scotland have considered bringing the Edinburgh south suburban railway network back into use for either heavy or light rail.

S3W-22688 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions have taken place to progress tram line 3 to link Edinburgh city centre with the Royal Infirmary of Edinburgh.

S3W-22689 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any discussions regarding tram line 3 have considered extending the tram line to Midlothian.

S3W-22690 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the expected start date for the Waverley railway project remains as 2010.

S3W-22691 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive whether market-testing discussions with contractors relating to construction work for the Waverley railway project are about the Waverley to Gorebridge section or the whole route.

S3W-22692 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what conclusions have been reached from the latest market-testing discussions regarding the Waverley railway project between Transport Scotland and contractors.

S3W-22693 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide an update on the market interest in the Waverley railway project.

S3W-22694 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reasons it did not hold the planned event with potential bidders for the Waverley railway project in 2008 and when this event will take place.

S3W-22695 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what professional advice it has sought on the feasibility of borrowing from financial markets for the Waverley railway project.

S3W-22696 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of the report on the Waverley railway project for Transport Scotland by Cyril Sweet, whether all the concerns about the technical and financial elements of the business case have been addressed.

S3W-22697 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive how much has been spent on land acquisition in connection with the Waverley railway project and whether the land acquisition has been completed.

S3W-22698 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive whether provision has been made in the revised costings for the Waverley railway project for buying the land needed to connect the new line to existing tracks.

S3W-22699 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive whether Network Rail has agreed to connect the Waverley railway project to the rail network; what costs have been identified, and who is responsible for meeting these costs.

S3W-22700 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the estimated cost of the Waverley railway project remains as between £235 and £295 million.

S3W-22701 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reasons the publication of the official notice advertising the contract for the Waverley railway project has been delayed until May 2009.

S3W-22702 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the delay in advertising the contract for the Waverley railway project will result in extra costs.

S3W-22703 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Scottish Council of Economic Advisers has discussed the Waverley railway project and, if so, what its views are.

S3W-22704 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-20117 by Stewart Stevenson on 30 January 2009, whether it will provide up-to-date information on projected (a) patronage levels and (b) train timetables for the Waverley railway project.

S3W-22705 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what the latest benefit-to-cost ratio is for the Waverley railway project and whether these estimates have been affected by the current economic downturn and problems in the financial markets.

S3W-22706 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is confident in the accuracy of its earlier projections of the demand for new homes in the Scottish Borders and whether these projections are reflected in the strategic development plans proposed by the City of Edinburgh, Midlothian and Scottish Borders councils and approved by ministers.
