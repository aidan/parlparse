Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week. Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section. Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-3224 Shiona Baird: Don’t Shop at Supermarkets Day—That the Parliament welcomes Don’t Shop at Supermarkets Day 2005 on 3 September which encourages consumers to purchase goods at alternative food retailers such as farmers’ markets and small, independent stores; further welcomes the start of Organic Week 2005, from 3 to 11 September, and the annual Dundee Flower and Food Festival, from 2 to 4 September, which play an important part in promoting a vibrant, mixed food-production system and market in Scotland; notes the plight of Scottish dairy farmers, who do not receive a fair price for their goods, and the concerns voiced by the North West Cattle Producers’ Association that cattle farming in north Scotland is increasingly under threat from cheap imports, and calls on the Scottish Executive to respond urgently to concerns regarding the monopoly of supermarkets and the impact that supermarket price wars have on Scottish small businesses and producers in the light of the recent decision by the Office of Fair Trading not to improve the supermarkets’ code of practice.

*S2M-3223 Michael Matheson: Support for the Scottish Football Team—That the Parliament recognises the importance of the Scottish Football Association (SFA) as the governing body of football within Scotland; praises its role within FIFA and UEFA; strongly agrees with the SFA’s views on a British football team for the 2012 Olympic Games, and disagrees with Sepp Blatter, FIFA President, for his recent misguided and ill-thought-out remarks which will have little or no support amongst those who run football in Scotland and, more importantly, those who support it.

Supported by: Bruce Crawford*, Linda Fabiani*, Fiona Hyslop*, Christine Grahame*

*S2M-3222 Tommy Sheridan: Aftermath of Hurricane Katrina—That the Parliament congratulates President Chavez of Venezuela for his prompt offer of oil reserves and food aid for the flood and storm-hit areas of the United States of America; notes that the eminent climate specialist, Professor Kerry Emanuel, published research in the magazine, Nature, on 4 August 2005 which concluded that the destructive potential of tropical storms, such as Hurricane Katrina, had doubled over the last 30 years primarily because ocean surfaces have grown warmer; further notes that the majority of scientific opinion believes that such rises in temperature are attributable to the climate change caused by greenhouse gas emissions; notes that federal budgets for flood control in Louisiana have been slashed and that oil company canals in Louisiana have contributed significantly to the loss of over 40 miles of wetland which provided a natural buffer to storms and flood; notes that national guardsmen and helicopters from the hurricane-affected areas of Louisiana, Alabama and Mississippi are currently deployed in Iraq and therefore unavailable for search and rescue, evacuation and distribution of aid relief work, and believes, therefore, that far from being a natural disaster, the scale of human tragedy now unfolding is largely man-made and attributable to the bankrupt policies pursued by US President George W Bush.

*S2M-3221 Patrick Harvie: Removal of Asylum Seekers’ Children—That the Parliament congratulates the Commissioner for Children and Young People in Scotland, Professor Kathleen Marshall, for raising the issue of the inhumane methods used by the UK Government in the removal and detention of children prior to deportation; endorses Article 19 of the UN Convention on the Rights of the Child (UNCRC) which states that governments should protect children from all forms of physical or mental violence, injury or abuse; considers that the early morning raids being carried out on vulnerable families by officers in body armour violate this article as well as the spirit of the UNCRC; agrees with Professor Marshall that this treatment amounts to terrorising families; is concerned that Section 9 of the Asylum and Immigration Act 2004 will worsen this situation and lead to many more families being subjected to this inhumane treatment, and calls on the UK Government to repeal this legislation and to ensure that all asylum and immigration policy is consistent with the UNCRC.

Supported by: Fiona Hyslop*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Campbell Martin*, Mr John Swinney*, Shiona Baird*, Chris Ballance*, Mark Ballard*, Rob Gibson*, Robin Harper*, Alex Neil*

*S2M-3220# John Scott: The Future Provision of Emergency and Unscheduled Care Services in Ayrshire and Arran—That the Parliament notes the plans by NHS Ayrshire and Arran to institute a public consultation on the future provision of emergency and unscheduled care in Ayrshire; expresses concern that this consultation only includes options to downgrade the existing accident and emergency unit at Ayr Hospital, and expects NHS Ayrshire and Arran to abide by the spirit of the report, Building a Health Service Fit for the Future, by Professor David Kerr by listening to the views of both the public and health professionals in Ayrshire and taking full account of these views before reaching a decision on the future of accident and emergency care provision in the county.

*S2M-3219# John Farquhar Munro: Future of Crofting—That the Parliament recognises the importance of crofting to the social and economic life of the Highlands and Islands and wishes to see the continuation of sustainable crofting communities; shares the concerns of crofting communities, such as those in Skye and Lochalsh and throughout the crofting counties, regarding the threat to crofting communities from the lack of action by the Crofters Commission over the decrofting of land and sale of crofts, and from absentee crofters and dereliction; believes that crofting tenancies should not be sold on the open market which is currently allowing local people to be outbid by those with no understanding of, or long-term commitment to, crofting and the crofting community, and further believes that if this continues it will, in a generation, lead to the end of crofting in everything but name.

*S2M-3218 Mr Mark Ruskell: Stop Climate Chaos—That the Parliament welcomes the launch of Stop Climate Chaos, the coalition of environment, development and faith organisations which has been formed to tackle climate change; acknowledges the strength of concern about climate change which exists within many organisations in the voluntary sector; believes that effective international action to reduce the emissions of greenhouse gases across all sectors is urgently required, and urges the Scottish Executive to act without delay to ensure that all its policies are climate proofed.

Supported by: Shiona Baird*, Mark Ballard*, Patrick Harvie*, Robin Harper*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Chris Ballance*, Mr John Swinney*

*S2M-3217 Mark Ballard: Folding Bicycles—That the Parliament notes that folding bicycles are a useful part of an integrated, sustainable transport system; further notes that such folding bicycles reduce to the size of a medium suitcase; additionally notes that, while other objects of similar size are allowed onto buses, travellers with folding bicycles are frequently refused access to buses with their bicycles, and calls on bus companies to revise any such policy that operates to the detriment of integrated sustainable transport.

*S2M-3216 Frances Curran: Condolences to the People of Baghdad—That the Parliament expresses its condolences to the people of Baghdad, where over 600 people were killed when panic swept through a crowd of pilgrims over claims that suicide bombers were present; notes that this tragic event underlines the fear and lack of security in Iraq, and offers sympathy to the ordinary people who live with this insecurity every day.

*S2M-3215 Christine Grahame: Goats, A Pair of Oxen and a Plough—That the Parliament, particularly in the year of the Make Poverty History campaign, recognises the important contribution made by Veterinary Assistance and International Development (VETAID), a member of VSF Europa (Veterinaries Sans Frontiers), located at Pentlands Science Park, Penicuik, and a not-for-profit charitable organisation whose remit is working for a reduction in poverty and an increase in food security of people dependent on livestock; commends projects such as the Machakos Project in Kenya, which works with a group of widows who support 40 child or grandparent-led households by providing in some instances two or three goats, a small breeding flock of chickens and training in livestock-keeping and notes that locally-based organisations will receive a pair of oxen and a plough; notes that this typifies the small-scale modestly resourced project which develops self-reliance, and therefore commends VETAID for its simple, direct and effective interventions in making poverty history for some.

*S2M-3214 Eleanor Scott: Sustainable Timber Certification Standards—That the Parliament supports the Forest Stewardship Council (FSC) certification scheme for sustainably produced timber; notes that this is the only certification scheme that is generally accepted by all stakeholders, including environment groups, as ensuring environmentally and socially responsible timber sourcing; further notes its concern that alternative certification schemes, including the Programme for the Endorsement of Forest Certification (PEFC) and Sustainable Forestry Initiative (SFI), represent a dilution of standards for sustainability as assessed by the Central Point for Expertise on Timber, established by the UK Government, and calls on the Scottish Executive to ensure that FSC certification remains the standard for public procurement in Scotland.

*S2M-3213 John Swinburne: Big Lottery Seniors Fund—That the Parliament welcomes the introduction of the Young People’s Fund by the Big Lottery Fund, allowing young people to access directly the grant distribution process and encouraging the youth of Scotland to contribute positively to their communities; notes, however, that no similar scheme is to be set up for the older sections of society; further notes the sizable proportion of lottery players who are senior citizens, and, recognising the wide network of pensioners’ groups and elderly forums who could make a positive input, urges the Scottish Executive to encourage the Big Lottery Fund to introduce a Seniors Fund, to be set up with the same purpose as the Young People’s Fund, allowing our older citizens to use their experience for the positive benefit of our communities.

*S2M-3212 Shiona Baird: Eco Prize for Creativity—That the Parliament congratulates Dundee University-based artists, Dalziel and Scullion, on winning the Eco Prize for Creativity 2005; welcomes the new annual award established by the Eco Trust, Friends of the Earth Scotland and Scotland’s Centre for Architecture, Design and the City at The Lighthouse for contributing to a much-needed informed public debate on the environment in Scotland, and recognises the vital role that visual art has to play in stimulating that debate.

Supported by: Rob Gibson*, Patrick Harvie*, Mark Ballard*, Robin Harper*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Chris Ballance*, Shona Robison*

*S2M-3204# Murdo Fraser: Planning Applications for Wind Power Developments—That the Parliament notes that 17 planning applications for onshore wind power developments have been referred to ministers on appeal after being refused by local authorities since 1999; is concerned at the high number of applications that are referred to ministers after they are democratically rejected at local level; believes that, when a rejected application is referred to ministers on appeal, the Scottish Executive should ensure that a public inquiry is held in order to have all issues considered and aired in public; further believes that all groups involved with wind farm applications, which include local authorities, community councillors, anti and pro-wind farm action groups, wind farm developers and the general public, agree that the current planning strategy and guidelines are inadequate, and considers that Scotland needs a national renewable energy strategy with up-to-date national guidelines for the siting of onshore wind farms.

Supported by: Alex Johnstone*, Phil Gallie*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Derek Brownlee*

*S2M-3202 Ms Sandra White: Costs of Backing the British Council—That the Parliament notes that more than £2,136,000 has been spent by the Scottish Executive since 2002 on a range of joint events with the British Council, including £5,600 for a "crossroads for ideas" workshop and a five-day arts presentation in Sweden which cost £60,000; notes that many of the events held were either closed to the public or to the press and that no report of these activities was made available; expresses concern that the Executive was unable to provide a total cost to the taxpayer for 24 of the 49 events held, and urges the Executive to review its auditing procedures to ensure greater transparency and accountability in the disposal of public funds.
