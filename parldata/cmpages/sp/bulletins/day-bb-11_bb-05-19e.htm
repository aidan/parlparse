Business Bulletin No. 57/2011: Thursday 19 May 2011
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 18 May 2011

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S4W-00098 Kezia Dugdale: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-40420 by Nicola Sturgeon on 16 March 2011, how many NHS staff have received a wage increase as a result of the living wage introduced on 1 April 2011.

S4W-00099 Kezia Dugdale: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-35494 by John Swinney on 25 August 2010, what aspects of EU law prevent community benefit clauses being used to progress the case for a living wage.

S4W-00100 Kezia Dugdale: To ask the Scottish Executive for what government agencies and bodies it can directly set the wages and salaries.

S4W-00101 Kezia Dugdale: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will seek to meet the London Olympic Delivery Authority to discuss how it progressed the case for a living wage to be paid by contractors within the constraints of EU law.

S4W-00103 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on a review of the regulation of letting agents.

S4W-00104 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will provide funds to Strathclyde Partnership for Transport for the refurbishment of the Glasgow subway.

S4W-00105 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is in relation to the creation of a multi-modal smartcard system for public transport and whether this will be achieved within the current parliamentary session.

S4W-00106 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will develop a peak oil policy.

S4W-00107 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will use procurement policy as an incentive to the private sector to provide a living wage to all employees.

S4W-00108 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what actions it will take to improve independent scrutiny of its record on sustainable development in the absence of the Sustainable Development Commission.

S4W-00109 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the development of alternative economic indicators as a replacement for or supplement to GDP.

S4W-00110 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will consult on same-sex marriage and the registration of civil partnerships.

S4W-00111 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take to improve mobile 3G coverage across the country.

S4W-00112 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will work with First ScotRail to ensure the provision of wi-fi services on its trains.

S4W-00113 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many incidents of disability-related hate crime have been reported to police since March 2010.

S4W-00114 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people have been (a) prosecuted and (b) convicted of aggravated offences under the Offences (Aggravation by Prejudice) (Scotland) Act 2009.

S4W-00115 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people convicted of aggravated offences under the Offences (Aggravation by Prejudice) (Scotland) Act 2009 have received a (a) community or (b) custodial sentence.

S4W-00116 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance has been produced to assist judges in sentencing aggravated offences underthe Offences (Aggravation by Prejudice) (Scotland) Act 2009.

S4W-00117 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many compulsory redundancies have been made by organisations and institutions for which it provided funding (a) 2010 and (b) 2011, broken down by (i) organisation or institution, (ii) parliamentary region and (iii) parliamentary constituency.

S4W-00118 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many compulsory redundancies have been made by each (a) university and (b) college in 2011.

S4W-00119 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many compulsory redundancies have been proposed by each (a) university and (b) college for 2011-12.

S4W-00120 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many compulsory redundancies have been proposed for 2011-12 by organisations and institutions for which it provided funding for (a) 2010 and (b) 2011, broken down by (i) organisation or institution, (ii) parliamentary region and (iii) parliamentary constituency.

S4W-00121 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will publish proposals on addressing the university funding gap.

S4W-00122 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will bring forward measures to improve support for college students.

S4W-00123 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will bring forward measures to improve support for university students.

S4W-00124 Alison Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it has made on the commitments set out in the 2003 guidance, Vulnerable Children and Young People: Sexual Exploitation Through Prostitution, and what actions remain outstanding.

S4W-00125 Alison Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to update the 2003 guidance, Vulnerable Children and Young People: Sexual Exploitation Through Prostitution, to include all forms of child sexual exploitation and reflect the growing use of mobile and internet technologies in such crimes.

S4W-00126 Alison Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) prosecutions and (b) convictions there have been and how many cautions have been issued for the offence of causing or inciting provision by child of sexual services or child pornography under the Protection of Children and Prevention of Sexual Offences (Scotland) Act 2005 in each police board area in each year since 2005.

S4W-00127 Alison Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) prosecutions and (b) convictions there have been and how many cautions have been issued for the offence of controlling a child providing sexual services or involved in pornography under the Protection of Children and Prevention of Sexual Offences (Scotland) Act 2005 in each police board area in each year since 2005.

S4W-00128 Alison Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) prosecutions and (b) convictions there have been and how many cautions have been issued for the offence of arranging or facilitating provision by child of sexual services or child pornography under the Protection of Children and Prevention of Sexual Offences (Scotland) Act 2005 in each police board area in each year since 2005.
