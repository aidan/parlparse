*S1M-3401 Mike Watson: The Role of Culture in the Educational Development of Young People-That the Parliament recognises the value of participation in cultural activities by Scotland's young people; believes that the development of creativity and the expressive arts is essential to Scotland's success in the 21st century, and welcomes the commitment of the Scottish Executive to increase the opportunities for all young people to take action to engage with Scotland's rich and vibrant culture.

Supported by: Nicol Stephen*

*S1M-3400# Mr John McAllion: Whitfield-That the Parliament congratulates the people of Whitfield in Dundee for their outstanding efforts in regenerating their community over the last few years; notes that Whitfield, like many other parts of Scotland, has its fair share of problems but recognises the great progress made by community-based organisations; however, further notes the Save the Children report, The Well-being of Children in the UK, that highlights the persistence of poverty and other problems in parts of the estate; regrets that the Whitfield Partnership was wound up by the Scotland Office without a proper exit strategy, and believes that the Scottish Executive and Her Majesty's Government should redouble their efforts to eradicate child poverty from Whitfield and every other community in Scotland.

*S1M-3399# Brian Fitzpatrick: Douglas Andrew Kilgour Black-That the Parliament recognises the lifelong commitment of Douglas Andrew Kilgour Black to the medical profession, the cause of social justice and the campaign to reduce health inequalities across the United Kingdom and the rest of the world; pays tribute to his contribution to the National Health Service as a physician, scientist, professor of medicine, President of the Royal College of Physicians and President of the British Medical Association and also as a steadfast campaigner for recognition of the links between poverty and ill-health, most notably in his scholarship and research in the pioneering work comprised in the report, Inequalities in Health, which provided evidence that at every important stage of life people of the lowest incomes suffered disproportionately from ill-health and premature death; extends its thanks for his work to build a better future, and extends its deepest sympathy and condolences to Sir Douglas' wife, children, family and many friends on his death on 13th September 2002.

*S1M-3394 Johann Lamont: Challenging Sectarianism in Glasgow-That the Parliament congratulates Glasgow City Council on its important and pioneering work in challenging sectarianism; notes in particular the powerful partnership it has forged with Strathclyde Police, the Church of Scotland, the Roman Catholic Church, Celtic Football Club, Rangers Football Club and voluntary organisations in developing the "Sense over Sectarianism" campaign; welcomes the decision to commission major research into the extent of sectarianism and Glaswegians' perception of it and acknowledges that this research will allow the development of meaningful action to tackle sectarianism; further welcomes the initiative by the council in bringing together all the major faiths in the city to launch the "Forum of Faiths" on 23 September 2002, an initiative which will work to foster respect and understanding amongst people of all religious beliefs and none in Scotland's largest city; congratulates all those involved in this important work, and welcomes this measured approach which looks beyond simplistic rhetoric and provides a model for those who seek to eradicate sectarianism across Scotland.

S1M-3393*#* Alex Neil: Firefighters and Emergency Fire Control Staff-That the Parliament recognises the valuable and courageous work undertaken by the fire service and *believes that a new pay formula together with a commensurate level of pay for firefighters and emergency fire control staff should be introduced* to ensure that these accurately reflect the highly skilled and professional role which they undertake.
