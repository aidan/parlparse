Business Bulletin No. 136/2004

Thursday 23 September 2004

Section D : Oral Questions selected for answer on 30 September 2004
  

Question Time

Enterprise, Lifelong Learning and Transport

1. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive what the impact on productivity was last year of days lost to industry through ill health. (S2O-3459)

2. Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking in respect of the governance of Glasgow Central College of Commerce. (S2O-3407)

3. Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will support students at the Netherdale Campus, Galashiels, in their campaign to keep the School of Textiles and Design in Galashiels. (S2O-3492)

4. Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has made to the Department for Transport regarding the circumstances in which Trans-European Network funding would be applied for. (S2O-3476)

5. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on improving rail links to airports. (S2O-3501)

6. John Swinburne: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will introduce the proposed national concessionary scheme for people who are elderly or disabled. (S2O-3494)

7. Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive when a decision will be taken on the exact location of the Aberdeen Western Peripheral Route. (S2O-3434)

8. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made of the projected demand for passenger rail services between Fife and Edinburgh up to 2009. (S2O-3474)

9. Chris Ballance: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is ensuring that trains run on time. (S2O-3513)

10. Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on how many aircraft registered to Scottish addresses and with a maximum take-off weight over 5,700kg are operating public transport services. (S2O-3530)

11. Mr Mark Ruskell: To ask the Scottish Executive how it funds infrastructure projects for cycling. (S2O-3516)

12. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it would support the creation of a direct rail or tram link from Glasgow to Braehead. (S2O-3515)

Justice and Law Officers

1. Mr Mark Ruskell: To ask the Scottish Executive what the maximum fine level is for breaches of environmental legislation. (S2O-3510)

2. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will implement a new funding formula for police forces and what the basis will be for any such formula. (S2O-3414)

3. Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is monitoring the performance of Reliance in escorting prisoners in Dumfries and Galloway. (S2O-3444)

4. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps have been taken to improve the level of drug rehabilitation services in prisons. (S2O-3440)

5. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive what concerns it has in respect of the statement in the Scottish Legal Services Ombudsman’s Annual Report 2003-04 that, in 42% of full complaint investigations carried out into Law Society of Scotland decisions, the Ombudsman recommended that the Law Society should investigate the complaint again or reconsider its decision. (S2O-3396)

6. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is addressing concerns expressed over reports that Reliance will be responsible for the prison and court escort of sex offenders. (S2O-3461)

7. Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will involve communities in the licensing process. (S2O-3473)

8. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive why, under Scots law, the spouse of a person accused of abusing their own child cannot be compelled to give evidence. (S2O-3443)

9. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will outline its programme in respect of the implementation of the draft Protection of Children and Prevention of Sexual Offences (Scotland) Bill. (S2O-3425)

10. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether, following the recent settlement in the fire-fighters’ pay agreement, it will allocate the agreed funding to develop services in the Highlands and Islands. (S2O-3454)

11. Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to reduce drug-related deaths. (S2O-3463)

12. Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it has made on implementing its commitment to expanding the role of restorative justice as a means of improving the range and quality of services available to courts. (S2O-3511)

General

1. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive what procedures are applied when testing for metal content in sewage sludge used for land regeneration. (S2O-3395)

2. Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects free eye and dental checks to be implemented. (S2O-3448)

3. John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take to ensure that all elderly people in hospital are properly fed. (S2O-3477)

4. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will take action to help people in fuel poverty due to rising energy prices. (S2O-3449)

5. Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to improve the accountability of NHS boards. (S2O-3404)

6. Bristow Muldoon: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take to ensure that St John’s Hospital in Livingston continues to be able to support its accident and emergency department by retaining on-site emergency surgery. (S2O-3499)

7. Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive who has ultimate managerial responsibility for staff working for the Scottish Executive. (S2O-3392)

8. Mr Mark Ruskell: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to protect native biodiversity from the impact of non-native species. (S2O-3522)

9. Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to support the Scottish film and television industry. (S2O-3455)

10. Miss Annabel Goldie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average waiting time is for a patient to get an initial appointment with an audiologist after being referred by a general practitioner. (S2O-3485)

11. Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what safeguards are in place to prevent loss of playing fields as a result of school rebuilding schemes carried out under public private partnerships. (S2O-3481)

12. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans there are for the long-term retention of Scottish opera chorus singers after 2005. (S2O-3408)

13. Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what effect the level of the fee for regulation with the care commission is having on smaller care providers. (S2O-3523)

14. Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Inquiry Reporters Unit is required to take into account the previous environmental record of an applicant when deciding on a planning application. (S2O-3389)

15. Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to support young carers in Aberdeen and the north east of Scotland. (S2O-3435)
