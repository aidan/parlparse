Business Bulletin No. 81/2002

Friday 17 May 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-3126# Christine Grahame: Re-opening of Reston Railway Station-That the Parliament supports the campaign by RAGES for the re-opening of a railway station at Reston in Berwickshire, the former station having closed in 1964; notes that many Borderers currently commute by car to either Berwick or Dunbar to then travel citywards by train; further notes that there is agricultural land available for a new-build station and parking and that for a small sum, in the order of £10,000, a feasibility study into the re-opening of a station at Reston could be undertaken, and considers that the Scottish Executive should commission such a study now.

Supported by: Brian Adam*, Mr Kenneth Gibson*, Fergus Ewing*, Colin Campbell*, Michael Matheson*, Bruce Crawford*, Michael Russell*, Roseanna Cunningham*

*S1M-3125# Johann Lamont: National Carers' Week, 10-16 June 2002-That the Parliament notes that 10-16 June 2002 has been designated as National Carers' Week and welcomes the opportunity to acknowledge and celebrate the crucial role of carers within the Glasgow Pollok constituency, in Glasgow and across Scotland; recognises the central role of carers in providing support for people who are frail or who have a disability which otherwise would cost social and health care services in Scotland in excess of £5 billion and, as shown in recent research by Carers Scotland, in contributing to the broader life of local communities; accepts that the demands of caring can often lead to social exclusion; invites the Scottish Executive and MSPs to ensure that the role of carers is addressed across all Scottish Executive departments and across the responsibilities of the committees of the Parliament, and asks all those with a statutory responsibility to consult and engage with carers so as to do everything in their power to ensure that the expertise, knowledge and commitment of carers are harnessed in the important work of securing social justice for carers and those for whom they care.

Supported by: Karen Whitefield*

*S1M-3124# Bruce Crawford: Closure of Longannet Deep Coal Mine-That the Parliament regrets the closure of Scotland's last deep coal mine at Longannet in Fife; is deeply disappointed that over 500 mining jobs were lost and that Scotland has also lost its most valuable low sulphur coal reserve; is concerned that the pit flooded at a time when Scottish Coal (Deep Mine) Ltd was experiencing financial and production difficulties and that the reasons for the flooding have not yet been adequately explained; is further concerned that, despite the substantial public investment, in excess of £45 million, inadequate steps were taken by Her Majesty's Government to protect the public's investment in the mine, save the jobs or retain this valuable energy resource for the nation, and therefore believes that the Scottish Executive should carry out a full public inquiry to determine how the flooding was allowed to take place that resulted in the mine's closure.

Supported by: Fiona McLeod*

*S1M-3123 Brian Adam: Family History Societies Support-That the Parliament recognises the excellent work being done by the many volunteer genealogists within the network of family history societies throughout Scotland, despite facing problems of unsuitable accommodation and lack of funding and support, and asks the Scottish Executive to consider how it might best support this network as part of the geneaology tourism niche market strategy.
