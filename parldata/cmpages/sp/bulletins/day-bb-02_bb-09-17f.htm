Business Bulletin No. 130/2002

Tuesday 17 September 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-3393 Alex Neil: Firefighters and Emergency Fire Control Staff-That the Parliament recognises the valuable and courageous work undertaken by the fire service and calls for the introduction of a new pay formula together with a commensurate level of pay for firefighters and emergency fire control staff to ensure that these accurately reflect the highly skilled and professional role which they undertake.

*S1M-3392 David Mundell: International Forest Fest 2002-That the Parliament notes that the International Forest Fest 2002 will be held at Lockerbie on 25 to 28 September 2002; further notes that the event will not only be the flagship of Treefest Scotland 2002 but will also bring together for the first time in the UK two major biennial events, the 25th World Logging Championships and the APF International Forest Machinery Exhibition, along with many other prestigious competitions and varied forest events; congratulates all those involved in organising this unique forest spectacular and bringing it to Scotland, and sends its best wishes for a successful event, not only to the organisers, but also to the many thousands of competitors, exhibitors and visitors attending the event.

*S1M-3391 Mr John McAllion: Mary Slessor Foundation-That the Parliament welcomes the work of the Mary Slessor Foundation in continuing the work done in Calabar, Nigeria, by Mary Slessor of Dundee in the 19th century; notes the excellent work being done by the foundation to defeat poverty and disease in Nigeria, and further welcomes this commemoration of Mary Slessor and the encouragement of links between Scotland and Africa.
