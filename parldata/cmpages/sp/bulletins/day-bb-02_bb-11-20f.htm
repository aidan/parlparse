Business Bulletin No. 168/2002

Wednesday 20 November 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-3608 Mr Jim Wallace: Extradition Bill - UK Legislation-That the Parliament notes the provisions of the Extradition Bill and agrees that those provisions conferring Executive functions on the Scottish Ministers in relation to extradition should be considered by the UK Parliament.

Supported by: Dr Richard Simpson*

*S1M-3607 Patricia Ferguson on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Designation of Lead Committee-That the Parliament agrees that the Education, Culture and Sport Committee be designated as lead committee in consideration of the Gaelic Language (Scotland) Bill and that the Local Government Committee be a secondary committee.

Supported by: Euan Robson*

*S1M-3606 Patricia Ferguson on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Designation of Lead Committees-That the Parliament agrees that the Rural Development Committee be designated as lead committee in consideration of the following regulations-

the draft Cairngorms National Park Designation, Transitional and Consequential Provisions (Scotland) Order 2003, and

the draft Cairngorms National Park Elections (Scotland) Order 2003

Supported by: Euan Robson*

*S1M-3605 Patricia Ferguson on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Designation of Lead Committees-That the Parliament agrees that the Justice 1 Committee be designated as lead committee in consideration of the following regulations-

the Civil Legal Aid (Scotland) Regulations 2002 (SSI 2002/494);

the Advice and Assistance (Scotland) Amendment Regulations 2002 (SSI 2002/495); and

*S1M-3604 Patricia Ferguson on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: May 2003-That the Parliament agrees to meet in The Hub, Castlehill, Edinburgh during the period from 12 - 29 May 2003.

Supported by: Euan Robson*

*S1M-3603 Tommy Sheridan: Strike Action by Staff at North Glasgow NHS Trust-That the Parliament supports the clerical and ancillary staff at North Glasgow NHS Trust who are currently taking strike action; recognises that these workers have given up to 20 years' service to the NHS but are only earning an average of £11,000 per year and are badly in need of a wage rise, and calls on the trust to open serious negotiations with the employees with a view to securing a real improvement in their wages.

*S1M-3602 Ross Finnie: Scottish Executive Response to Foot-and-Mouth Disease Inquiries-That the Parliament notes the conclusions and recommendations of the Lessons to be Learned, Royal Society and Royal Society of Edinburgh inquiries into foot-and-mouth disease and welcomes the Scottish Executive response which builds on their recommendations and aims to develop a framework for reducing the risk of importing infectious diseases, reducing the vulnerability of livestock and minimising the impact of any future outbreak to give better protection to Scotland's valuable livestock industry.

Supported by: Allan Wilson*

*S1M-3601# John Farquhar Munro: Sabhal Mòr Ostaig Awarded Queens Anniversary Prize-That the Parliament congratulates Sabhal Mòr Ostaig on its outstanding achievement in winning the Queen's Anniversary Prize for Higher Education, a prize which is awarded in recognition of excellence, initiative and innovation; recognises the great progress Sabhal Mòr Ostaig has made since its origins in a derelict farm steading on Skye so that it is now Scotland's leading Gaelic education college, and considers that the Scottish Executive should honour Sabhal Mòr Ostaig by recognising it as a National Centre of Excellence for Gaelic Language and Culture.

*S1M-3600# Dennis Canavan: Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease-That the Parliament welcomes the launch of the European Chronic Pulmonary Disease (COPD) Patient Manifesto supported by seventeen European nations in recognition of the increasing incidence of COPD across the world; notes that Scotland has one of the highest incidences of COPD in Europe, and considers that the Scottish Executive should ensure that the necessary preventative action is taken to reduce the incidence of COPD and to guarantee adequate standards of treatment for COPD patients.

Supported by: Mr Adam Ingram*, Michael Russell*, Mr George Reid*, Brian Fitzpatrick*, Mr John McAllion*, Dorothy-Grace Elder*, Colin Campbell*, Michael Matheson*, Bruce Crawford*, Tommy Sheridan*

*S1M-3599 Patricia Ferguson on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Approval of SSI-That the Parliament agrees that the draft Budget (Scotland) Act 2002 Amendment Order 2002 be approved.

Supported by: Euan Robson*
