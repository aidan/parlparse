Business Bulletin No. 001/2002

Monday 7 January 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

S1M-2575# Irene McGugan: Recruitment and Retention Crisis in Social Work-That the Parliament notes with concern that the crisis of recruitment and retention in the social work profession is now acute, as evidenced by the fact that 78% of local authorities who responded to a recent SNP survey highlighted this as a serious concern; further notes that there has been an increase in referrals and statutory obligations to childcare social work without resources to meet the needs and that, where child poverty and deprivation is highest, the crisis worsens, with some areas recently recording a vacancy rate of 24% with 29% of cases unallocated; is concerned that there has been a steady fall in applications to social work departments over the last five years and that in consequence Scotland's most vulnerable children and young people are being failed at a time when they are most in need of support, and considers that the Executive should take action to ensure that the role of childcare social workers is valued and should as a matter of urgency make a commitment to a comprehensive review of the terms and conditions of social workers which will address the particular circumstances of the profession and improve the current poor public image of social work for the benefit of all children in need.

S1M-2574 Roseanna Cunningham: Imprisonment of Ngawang Sangdrol-That the Parliament recognises the plight of Buddhist nun Ngawang Sangdrol who, along with her fellow nuns from the Garu monastery, was arrested and imprisoned in August 1990 for attending a pro-independence demonstration in Lhasa; notes reports which indicate that Ngawang Sangdrol is currently due to be released in 2013 by which time she will be only 36 years old and yet will have been imprisoned for 21 years, and expresses its support for Amnesty International's campaign to have Ngawang Sangdrol released from prison in Tibet.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson*, Brian Adam*, Mr Duncan Hamilton*

S1M-2571 Michael Russell: Fèisean nan Gàidheal-That the Parliament congratulates Fèisean nan Gàidheal, the National Association of Gaelic Arts Youth Tuition Festivals, on its work in the last year in involving over 3,600 young people in Gaelic cultural events, and commends the hard work by all in the organisation in order to involve a new generation of young people in Scotland's Gaelic heritage despite the present range of difficult circumstances which face Gaelic, not least the fact that the present shortage of Gaelic teachers results in the language relying heavily on the goodwill of volunteers, such as those in Fèisean nan Gàidheal.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson*, Brian Adam*, Mr Duncan Hamilton*

S1M-2570 Michael Russell: Act of Settlement-That the Parliament notes the resolution of the House of Commons on 19 December 2001 giving leave for the Introduction of a Bill by Kevin McNamara MP, and to be supported by Alex Salmond MP, which would abolish the deeply offensive anti-Catholic provisions of the Act of Settlement; congratulates the sponsors of this proposed Bill and is pleased that they have paid tribute to the Scottish Parliament for its resolution of 16 December 1999 on this matter and to the Guardian and Scotland on Sunday newspapers for actively promoting the cause for repeal; regrets that Her Majesty's Government has taken no action on this matter despite indicating general support, and renews its call for UK legislation at the earliest possible date.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson*, Brian Adam*, Mr Duncan Hamilton*

S1M-2568 Mr Mike Rumbles: International Year of Mountains-That the Parliament notes that 2002 is the International Year of Mountains; supports the event's aim of raising public awareness of the high ecological value and wider socio-economic significance of mountains; recognises the global importance of Scotland's mountainous areas, particularly their role in shaping the nation's image and culture, and therefore urges the Scottish Executive to support the year's celebrations by fostering debate between local communities, farming interests, rural businesses, public bodies, and non-governmental organisations on how to maximise the mutual benefit from activities aimed at safeguarding, enhancing and promoting enjoyment of Scotland's mountain heritage.

Supported by: Mary Scanlon, Mr Jamie McGrigor, Michael Matheson, Mr Duncan Hamilton*

S1M-2567 Scott Barrie: Death of Stuart Adamson-That the Parliament notes the untimely death of Stuart Adamson; acknowledges the invaluable contribution he made to contemporary popular music in Scotland, particularly as lead singer with Big Country and guitarist with The Skids; recognises the part he played in promoting his home town of Dunfermline, and sends its condolences to his family.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson, Mr Jamie McGrigor, Michael Matheson, Mr Duncan Hamilton*

S1M-2565 Margaret Smith on behalf of the Health and Community Care Committee: Report on Hepatitis C-That the Parliament notes, and calls upon the Scottish Executive to act upon, the recommendations contained within the 17th Report 2001 of the Health and Community Care Committee, Report on Hepatitis C (SP Paper 398).

S1M-2564 Irene McGugan: Citizens of a Multilingual World-That the Parliament welcomes the findings in the report by the Action Group on Languages, Citizens of a Multilingual World and the supportive statements made by Jack McConnell MSP when Minister for Education, Europe and External Affairs; notes however the concerns of the Scottish Association of Language Teaching and others regarding the delay in making the revised 5-14 curriculum guidance available, which has hindered progress and much needed development in modern languages in both the primary and secondary school sectors; recognises that this regrettable delay in responding to the Group's report, and the publication of Circular 3/2001, Guidance on Flexibility in the Curriculum, in advance of this response, have led to widespread speculation and misinterpretation of the principle of "entitlement" as it is described in the Group's report, and calls upon the Scottish Executive to end this uncertainty and pursue the proposals in the report by means of a strategic implementation plan without further delay and to provide local authorities and schools with the responsibility and opportunity to improve language learning throughout Scotland, so that each pupil and student is enabled to fulfil his or her potential as a citizen of this multilingual world.

Supported by: Michael Matheson, Ms Sandra White*, Mr Duncan Hamilton*

S1M-2563 Cathy Jamieson: Education (Disability Strategies and Pupils' Records) (Scotland) Bill-That the Parliament agrees to the general principles of the Education (Disability Strategies and Pupils' Records) (Scotland) Bill.

Supported by: Nicol Stephen, Ms Margaret Curran

S1M-2557# Johann Lamont: Citizens Advice Bureau, Greater Pollok-That the Parliament welcomes the opening of a Citizens Advice Bureau in Greater Pollok; notes that it has received a remarkable number of enquiries already, 370 in its first month of being open; further notes that local people have come forward to volunteer and currently form 90% of the workforce in the bureau; congratulates Greater Pollok Social Inclusion Partnership and Citizens Advice Scotland on their partnership approach in working together to provide the necessary funding, and believes that the bureau will complement the excellent work of the seven other bureaux in Glasgow in tackling social exclusion through the provision of independent advice, assistance and representation services.

S1M-2556# Dr Sylvia Jackson: Sheep Farming around Loch Katrine-That the Parliament notes West of Scotland Water's decision to cease its sheep farming operations around Loch Katrine; recognises the potential effects this could have on the Trossachs community; further notes the discussions that West of Scotland Water is now having with local community groups, and urges West of Scotland Water to work with all organisations, including the Scottish Executive, to fully investigate the environmental, social and economic impact this decision will have on the area and to use the integrated catchment management plan as a basis to find solutions to sustain the local community.

Supported by: Mr Brian Monteith, Mr Jamie McGrigor

S1M-2553 Dorothy-Grace Elder: First Aid in Schools-That the Parliament calls on the Scottish Executive to make basic first aid a compulsory part of the school curriculum, as it is in several other European countries; believes that secondary school pupils could be a major new force in life saving and recognises that even two hours training in a lifetime has led to lives being saved; agrees with the ambulance service that lives are being wasted because of a lack of rudimentary first aid in the first few minutes after cardiac arrest; cites the splendid example of a 17 year-old first aider, Scott Gavin of Holyrood RC Secondary School in Glasgow, who rushed to the aid of a maths teacher, Brian Innes, when he collapsed during a lesson and kept him alive until an ambulance arrived, and applauds all voluntary training offered by the Red Cross and youth organisations while believing that a national programme instilling skill and confidence in every secondary pupil in the country is needed and that this could be accomplished with minimal time and expense.

Supported by: Mr Adam Ingram, Mr Gil Paterson, Mr Lloyd Quinan, Michael Russell, Mr Brian Monteith, Ms Sandra White, Robin Harper*, Mr Duncan Hamilton*

S1M-2553.1 Mr Brian Monteith: First Aid in Schools-As an amendment to motion (S1M-2553) in the name of Dorothy-Grace Elder, after "curriculum" insert "as a component of guidance teaching".

S1M-2552 Brian Adam: House Building in the Bridge of Don Area-That the Parliament condemns the undemocratic procedures adopted by Aberdeen City Council Labour group in proposing an additional 700 or more houses in the Bridge of Don area despite a lack of adequate roads and other infrastructure; welcomes the Group's incompetence in failing to persuade all its colleagues to vote for the proposal, and calls on the Scottish Labour Party to investigate the practices of its councillors in Aberdeen.

Supported by: Michael Matheson
