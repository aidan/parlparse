Business Bulletin No. 91/2009: Monday 1 June 2009
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 29 May 2009

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-24426 Alison McInnes: To ask the Scottish Executive what the reasons are for the rise in private car journeys made by its officials and staff in 2007-08, as indicated in the Scottish Government Environmental Performance Annual Report 2007/08.

S3W-24427 Alison McInnes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide a breakdown of the private car journeys made by its officials and staff in 2007-08, as indicated in the Scottish Government Environmental Performance Annual Report 2007/08, showing how many of the journeys covered distances of (a) less than one, (b) between one and five, (c) between five and 50, (d) between 50 and 100 and (e) more than 100 miles.

S3W-24428 Alison McInnes: To ask the Scottish Executive what provision it makes for car-sharing schemes for users of the Scottish Government estate.

S3W-24429 Alison McInnes: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of private car journeys made by its officials and staff in 2007-08, as indicated in the Scottish Government Environmental Performance Annual Report 2007/08, was made by participants in car-sharing schemes.

S3W-24430 Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is aware of suggestions by the National Institute for Health and Clinical Excellence (NICE) that acupuncture, structured exercise and chiropractic services should be offered as treatment for back pain.

S3W-24431 Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers it a worthwhile use of NHS funds to offer complementary treatments such as physical therapy and massage to people with chronic back pain.

S3W-24432 Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to follow the example of the NHS in England and Wales, where people with persistent back pain may be treated by chiropractors, osteopaths and acupuncturists.

S3W-24433 Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will confirm that all of the £45 million in consequentials from Her Majesty’s Government’s budget announcement on housing will be used on housing and whether it will set out how it plans to spend this money.

S3W-24434 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it is giving in schools to children with type 1 diabetes.

S3W-24435 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive how many children in primary schools are registered as having diabetes.

S3W-24436 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what financial support it has given to third sector organisations to support children with diabetes.

S3W-24437 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what training is given to teachers to help them assist children who have diabetes and require additional help during the school day.

S3W-24438 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive at what date and time the First Minister first became aware that prisoner John Burt Brown had absconded from HMP Castle Huntly open prison on 27 May 2009.

S3W-24439 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive at what date and time the Cabinet Secretary for Justice first became aware that prisoner John Burt Brown had absconded from HMP Castle Huntly on 27 May 2009.

S3W-24440 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive at what date and time government officials first became aware that prisoner John Burt Brown had absconded from HMP Castle Huntly on 27 May 2009.

S3W-24441 Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive how many registered NHS dentists there have been in each year since 1999, broken down by Scottish Parliament constituency.

S3W-24442 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prisoners in the open estate were serving (a) life, (b) long-term and (c) short-term sentences, as at May 2009.

S3W-24443 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prisoners in the open estate were serving sentences, broken down by (a) length of sentence and (b) main charge proven, as at May 2009.

S3W-24444 Elizabeth Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive how many police stations have been manned at evenings and weekends in each police force area in each year since 1999.

S3W-24445 Elizabeth Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive which police stations are manned at evenings and weekends in each police force area.

S3W-24446 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive how it proposes to develop the national strategy for dementia and what will be involved in the wide-ranging consultation process referred to in its news release of 28 May 2009.

S3W-24447 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what the timescale is for agreeing a national strategy for dementia.

S3W-24448 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to discuss with Alzheimer Scotland how its Words into Action on Dementia consultation with carers and people with dementia that will inform the work of the Cross-party Group on Alzheimer’s could be used to shape the development of the national strategy on dementia.

S3W-24449 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will confirm the commitment made by the Minister for Public Health and Sport on Good Morning Scotland on 28 May 2009 that adequate additional resources would be made available to implement a national strategy for dementia.

S3W-24450 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what the role of the dementia forum will be in the development of a national strategy for dementia.

S3W-24451 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it sees a role for the charter of rights being developed by the Parliament’s Cross-party Group on Alzheimer’s in informing the recommendations for the development of a national strategy on dementia.

S3W-24452 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive how the Parliament’s Cross-party Group on Alzheimer’s can contribute to the development of the national strategy on dementia.

S3W-24453 John Park: To ask the Scottish Executive how many redundancies were caused by the removal of the tolls from the Forth and Tay bridges.

S3W-24454 John Park: To ask the Scottish Executive what the annual running costs of the Forth and Tay bridges (a) were before and (b) have been since the removal of tolls.

S3W-24455 John Park: To ask the Scottish Executive what the cost was of (a) installing and (b) removing the refurbished toll plaza on the south side of the Forth Bridge.

S3W-24456 John Park: To ask the Scottish Executive how much income was generated annually by the tolls on the (a) Forth and (b) Tay bridges.

S3W-24457 David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Executive when the traffic capacity study for the A9 Longman roundabout was commissioned.

S3W-24458 David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects the conclusions of the traffic capacity study carried out by Scotland Transerv on the A9 Longman roundabout to be published.

S3W-24459 David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Executive what budget allocations have been made for improvements to the A9 Longman roundabout.

S3W-24460 David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Executive what timescale is anticipated for improvements to the A9 Longman roundabout.

S3W-24461 David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are in place to reduce traffic congestion on the Kessock Bridge, particularly at peak times.

S3W-24462 David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Executive what maximum daily traffic movements have been recorded on the Kessock Bridge.

S3W-24463 David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Executive what design capacity was planned in terms of maximum vehicle movements for the Kessock Bridge when it opened in 1982.

S3W-24464 David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Executive what projections it has for vehicle movements per day on the Kessock Bridge over the next five years.

S3W-24465 David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans are in place for developing long-term park-and-ride solutions for either side of the Kessock Bridge.

S3W-24466 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what the opening hours are for a grade two VisitScotland tourist information centre and whether they change according to the time of year.

S3W-24467 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-18812 by Richard Lochhead on 8 January 2009, what progress it has made in implementing the 16 of the 17 recommendations made in the report, A review of the European Union Agricultural Subsidy Appeals Procedure in Scotland, that it has accepted and when an announcement will be made of its detailed proposals for reform and for setting up an independent appeals procedure.
