BUSINESS BULLETIN 92/1999

Tuesday 16 November 1999

Section E - Written questions lodged on 15 November 1999

 

 

S1W-2461 Shona Robison: To ask the Presiding Officer whether the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body will be giving consideration to the budgetary and service implications of holding meetings of the Parliament in Dundee during the period in May 2000 when the Church of Scotland will be using the Assembly Hall.

 

S1W-2527 Mrs Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what options will be investigated in relation to the upgrading of the A800 and in particular whether these options will include (a) increased tolls on the Forth Road Bridge, (b) tolls on the road itself, (c) joint management and operation of the Forth and Kincardine crossings and relevant road links and (d) increased financial assistance to the relevant local authorities.

 

S1W-2528 Mrs Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive why improvement to the A8000 was not included in the Strategic Roads Review, given its potential cost benefit ratio in relation to some of the schemes included.

 

S1W-2529 Mrs Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether, if there are trunk roads or motorways on either side of a bridge, responsibility for the bridge must rest with central government rather than a bridge board.

 

S1W-2530 Ms Margaret Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive what the Warm Deal budget will be in 2000-01 and 2001-02.

 

S1W-2531 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive what contingency plans are being put in place to deal with the situation arising out of the recent ruling against the use of temporary sheriffs.

 

S1W-2532 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive, following recent discussions between the Lord Advocate and the Sheriff Principle, what further measures, if any, in addition to any contingency plans will or may be put in place following the recent ruling against the use of temporary sheriffs.

 

S1W-2533 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is considering the possibility of emergency legislation following the recent ruling against the use of temporary sheriffs, and whether there will be an appeal against the ruling.

 

S1W-2534 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive, following the suspension of Scotland’s temporary sheriffs, whether a guarantee will be given that all of those charged with crimes of violence will have their cases brought to court satisfying the 110 day rule.

 

S1W-2535 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of the time of the sheriff courts was taken up with cases before temporary sheriffs for each of the last three years, how many full time sheriffs would have to be appointed to meet the shortfall arising as a result of the recent ruling against the use of temporary sheriffs, and whether it will ensure that there will be sufficient full time sheriffs to cover the withdrawal of temporary sheriffs.

 

S1W-2536 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it agrees with the recent ruling that temporary sheriffs cannot be independent.

 

S1W-2537 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Lord Advocate’s role in the appointment of all judges will be reviewed, and whether any possible changes are under consideration.

 

S1W-2538 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive, in the light of the recent ruling against the use of temporary sheriffs, whether it will review the role of the Lord Advocate as a member of the Scottish Executive.

 

S1W-2539 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is under an obligation to implement any and every ruling in Scotland arising out of the European Convention on Human Rights.

 

S1W-2540 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will guarantee that convicted and sentenced prisoners will not be released into the community as a result of the recent ruling against the use of temporary sheriffs, and whether it will detail what percentage of prisoners convicted within the last year appeared before temporary sheriffs.

 

S1W-2541 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will guarantee that the recent ruling against the use of temporary sheriffs will not prevent criminal and civil cases being heard owing to a paucity of judges.

 

S1W-2542 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-58 by Iain Gray on 8 November 1999, what specific consultations it has had with the Home Secretary about the arrangements arising from the Immigration and Asylum Act (1999) for looking after asylum seekers in Scotland while they await the outcome of applications for asylum.

 

S1W-2543 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with COSLA, local authorities, health boards, other Scottish public bodies and voluntary bodies about the arrangements needed to look after asylum seekers in Scotland under the Immigration and Asylum Act (1999) and what concerns these bodies have expressed to the Scottish Executive on these matters.

 

S1W-2544 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any additional capital funding will be made available to assist local authorities in providing housing for asylum seekers.

 

S1W-2545 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how administration costs incurred by local authorities in connection with the implementation of the Immigration and Asylum Act 1999 will be funded.

 

S1W-2546 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any additional funding will be made available to local authorities to cover the cost of providing furnishings and clothing to asylum seekers.

 

S1W-2547 Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make increased funding available to the Scottish Refugee Council to provide one-stop shops and continuing care for asylum seekers.

 

S1W-2548 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether temporary sheriffs sat more in certain areas of Scotland than others, and whether the recent ruling against the use of temporary sheriffs will result in a disproportionate amount of pressure in the courts of certain areas and, if so, whether it will take immediate action to stop this happening.

 

S1W-2549 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive what the immediate financial implications are of the recent ruling against the use of temporary sheriffs.

 

S1W-2550 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive what would be the estimated expenditure required to fill the gap left by the recent ruling against the use of temporary sheriffs if permanent sheriffs are employed in their place, and from where this funding could come.

 

S1W-2551 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make a statement to the Parliament once the full implications of the ruling against the use of temporary sheriffs are clear.

 

S1W-2553 Malcolm Chisholm: To ask the Scottish Executive what the number and the average pay scales of childcare workers employed in Scotland are, broken down by (a) gender, (b) sector (private, public, non profit making, self employed) and (c) part-time/full-time employment for the years 1997, 1998 and 1999.

 

S1W-2554 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many loans the Student Loans Company in Scotland has dispersed in each year over the last three years; how many loans have been issued late in each year over the last three years, and what is the average waiting time for the receipt of late loans.

 

S1W-2555 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive what its definition is of poverty in relation to income in Scotland today and, within the terms of its definition, how many families, pensioners and children live in poverty in Scotland today.

 

S1W-2556 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive to provide a breakdown of the premature deaths in Scotland in each of the last five years to date related to each of tobacco, alcohol, cannabis, amphetamines, heroin, ecstasy and cocaine.

 

S1W-2557 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many recorded convictions there have been for trafficking in cannabis, amphetamines, heroin, ecstasy and cocaine in each of the last five years to date.

 

S1W-2558 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people are employed in total in Scottish call centres; what the average wage is of such employees; what is the trade union density in this sector, and whether there is any monitoring of the conditions experienced by call centre staff.

 

S1W-2559 Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made with implementing the Food Standards Act 1999 and setting up the Food Standards Agency in Scotland

 

S1W-2560 Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made so far to implement its Land Reform Action Plan, published in August 1999.

 

S1W-2561 Ms Margaret Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to monitor the disposal of natural waste, particularly at landfill sites close to residential areas.

 

S1W-2562 Ms Margaret Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive what the plans are for the continued evaluation and assessment of landfill sites within the Greater Glasgow area.

 

S1W-2563 Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria will be used in deciding how and where the £6 million for the Domestic Abuse Service Development Fund will be spent.

 

S1W-2564 Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria will be used in deciding how and where the additional funding granted to Scottish Homes for the provision of refuge places will be spent.

 

S1W-2565 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what has been the rate of progress on the decontamination or treatment of contaminated land in each of the last three years in each local enterprise company area and, at the current rate of dealing with identified contaminated land in each area, how long it will take to decontaminate or treat currently identified contaminated land in each area.

 

S1W-2566 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what the permitted maximum daily level of dioxin release is from waste incinerators.

 

S1W-2567 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance has been issued to Scottish local authorities to assist them in considering applications for the siting of waste incinerators and whether it will place a copy of this guidance in the Scottish Parliament Information Centre.

 

S1W-2568 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what budget has been allocated to support the operation of each of the Parliament’s Committees.

 

S1W-2570 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive what implications there are arising from the judgement on temporary sheriffs for (a) adjourned trials and (b) continued civil cases, and whether it will list the current numbers in each category.

 

S1W-2571 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive what estimates were made during 1997-98 and 1998-99 in relation to the cost to the civil and criminal justice systems in Scotland of implementation of the European Convention of Human Rights.

 

S1W-2572 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive what the actual costs of the implementation of the European Convention of Human Rights have been during 1997-98, 1998-99 and 1999-2000 to date.
