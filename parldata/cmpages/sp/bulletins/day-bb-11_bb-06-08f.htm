Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally printed the day after they are lodged. If an amendment is lodged to a motion then the original motion will appear alongside the amendment, along with any support lodged that day, in this section.

Each Monday, this section also contains all motions and amendments lodged the previous week that are still live and any motions and amendments that have been lodged for debate in the coming week. A motion or amendment is live if it has not been debated, withdrawn or otherwise deleted from the list.

Where a motion or amendment has been withdrawn, it is indicated in this section the day after it has been withdrawn. Support for motions and amendments received after they are lodged is shown at the end of this section the day after such support is received.

Motions and amendments can be published with various symbols:

  • Asterisks before the motion or amendment number indicate a motion or amendment published for the first time;
  • Asterisks also identify alterations to the text of a motion or amendment made since it was first published;
  • A hash symbol identifies motions eligible for debate at Members’ Business;
  • A diamond symbol identifies motions lodged for Members’ Business that have not yet attracted the required cross-party support;
  • An "R", identifies motions or amendments in which the Member who lodged it has a registrable interest.

The Parliamentary Bureau periodically deletes motions or amendments not scheduled for debate and which are over six weeks old.

A full list of current motions is available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/motions.htm.

A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/index.htm.

Any questions in relation to this section should be directed to the Chamber Desk, for which contact details are provided at the end of the Bulletin.

New and altered motions and amendments

*S4M-00235 John Swinney: Scotland Bill – Borrowing Powers and Growing the Scottish Economy—That the Parliament notes the Scotland Bill proposals on capital borrowing and the recommendations of the Scotland Bill Committee in that regard; calls on the UK Government and Scottish Government to undertake joint work to agree a clear, long-term and principles-based approach to capital borrowing and the sources of borrowing including the potential for bonds, and calls for the implementation of capital borrowing powers at an accelerated timescale to that proposed in the Scotland Bill to support economic recovery and enhance Scotland’s infrastructure.

Supported by: Fergus Ewing*

*S4M-00234 Jackie Baillie: Caring for Scotland’s Older People—That the Parliament welcomes the Scottish Government’s new focus on the care for older people; further welcomes the commitment given by the First Minister that vulnerable residents in Southern Cross homes will not be compromised as a result of Southern Cross’s business model; notes with concern the report by the Mental Welfare Commission regarding the appalling treatment of Mrs V at Ninewells Hospital and the recent disturbing events at the Elsie Inglis Nursing Home in Edinburgh where standards of care were totally inadequate; further notes that one in 10 of the city’s care homes have been criticised and deemed weak or unsatisfactory in at least one area of assessment in the past year; believes that the 25% cut to the budget of Social Care and Social Work Improvement Scotland (SCSWIS), charged with the inspection and improvement of care standards, should be reversed; agrees that radical reform of community health partnerships is urgently required following the serious failings identified in a recent Audit Scotland report; recognises that funding prevention work will delay services for older people requiring formal care; believes that the care and safety of Scotland’s older and vulnerable people must be a major priority for the Scottish Government, and therefore calls on it to come forward urgently with plans to integrate health and social care so that Scotland’s older people and their families can have full confidence that they will receive the best possible standard of care when they need it.

*S4M-00231 Margaret McDougall: Award for Kilwinning Main Street—That the Parliament congratulates Kilwinning Main Street on being awarded a Commendation in the Public Realm/Landscaping category of the Scottish Design Awards 2011; notes the positive changes that the redevelopment has seen for the local area and economy, including a raft of new jobs and investment; considers that it has made improvements to the physical environment and enhanced the identity and image of the historic abbey town centre as a commercial and community destination, and thanks the Irvine Bay Regeneration Company, North Ayrshire Council, Scottish Enterprise and the Scottish Government for making this project possible.

*S4M-00230 Margaret McDougall: Congratulations to Kilwinning Segdoune Carnival—That the Parliament congratulates Kilwinning Segdoune Carnival on organising this annual event, which it considers is of great importance to the local community; congratulates all the primary school pupils who took part in the day, including Karah Murphy, who was crowned queen; thanks community activist Ann Robertson MBE for crowning the queen; celebrates the return of the carnival to the newly revamped Kilwinning Main Street, and hopes that the event will go from strength to strength.

*S4M-00229 Jackie Baillie: Living Legend Lollipop Man Retires—That the Parliament commends Alex Donald who retired last month after four years of service helping children get safely across the road to and from Knoxland Primary school in Dumbarton; notes that the 85-year-old was named Scotland’s living legend in 2004 for his lifetime campaigning on road safety issues; further notes that he was appointed an MBE for his work on these issues; understands that Mr Donald campaigned successfully for a number of road safety measures over the last 40 years, including the introduction of a 20 mph speed limit in local housing estates and zig zag markings at pelican crossings, and acknowledges his enormous contribution to improving road safety in Scotland.

*S4M-00228 Jackie Baillie: Apprentice of the Year at Clydebank College—That the Parliament congratulates David McLean on being named the apprentice of the year at Clydebank College; notes that the 19-year-old from Tullichewan won the college’s painting and decorating second year award; understands that Mr McLean is currently employed by Baxter and Gillespie painting contractors and hopes to continue his apprenticeship next summer, and acknowledges the importance of apprenticeships in equipping young people with the skills and knowledge to benefit the economy of Scotland.

*S4M-00227 Siobhan McMahon: Emergency Lifesaving and Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation Training in Schools—That the Parliament is concerned that heart and circulatory disease remains Scotland’s biggest killer; notes that, for every minute that passes after a cardiac arrest without defibrillation, the chances of survival are reduced by around 10%, but that immediate cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) can triple the chances of survival; supports the British Heart Foundation (BHF) Scotland’s campaign for every child to receive emergency lifesaving (ELS) and CPR training in school as part of the Curriculum for Excellence; congratulates North Lanarkshire Council for providing ELS and CPR training through BHF’s Heartstart training programme in every secondary school and welcomes its aspiration to achieve the same in every primary school, and urges other local authorities to follow this example by affiliating to Heartstart and ensuring that ELS and CPR training is available in schools throughout Scotland.

*S4M-00226 Mary Fee: Fairtrade in Renfrew—That the Parliament congratulates the town of Renfrew on meeting the criteria required to become a Fairtrade Town; notes the dedication and hard work of the community council; acknowledges the importance of the Fairtrade Foundation in tackling poverty and injustice in trade, and encourages other towns in Scotland to work toward Fairtrade status.

*S4M-00225 Stuart McMillan: Bee’s Knees Awards 2011—That the Parliament welcomes the recent Bee’s Knees Awards 2011, which was held at Greenock Town Hall on Friday 3 June to showcase the diversity of the Inverclyde local business community and the talent driving it; notes that BPI, Europe’s largest manufacturer of polythene films, won the Best of the Best award for 2011; further notes that awards were presented to On-Site Scanningas Emerging Business 2011, Inverclyde Tourist Group as Community Spirit and Cardwell Nursery Garden Centre as Proud to be Inverclyde; acknowledges the achievements of the winners; also congratulates those who were nominated for an award, and wishes them all every success for the future.

*S4M-00223 Christina McKelvie: Official Opening of Townhill Primary School, Hamilton—That the Parliament congratulates the pupils and staff of Townhill Primary School, Hamilton, on what it considers was a highly successful official opening of their new school building on 30 May 2011; further considers that pupils of all ages were a credit to their school and that they provided a superb welcome for their guests with songs, a guided tour and walls decorated with inspirational affirmations about how much the new school is appreciated by all who learn and work in it; notes that, throughout its history, over 20,000 local children have been Townhill pupils; further notes that the new school was built without the use of the private finance initiative, and considers that, therefore, this will provide value to the public purse, and wishes the new Townhill Primary a long and successful future.

*S4M-00222 Marco Biagi: Edinburgh’s Festival of Sport—That the Parliament welcomes the third Edinburgh Festival of Sport, which will run from 6 to 19 June 2011; congratulates the organisers on the festival’s success since the inaugural event in 2009 and notes that this has allowed the festival to expand across the entire east of Scotland; welcomes this year’s expanded programme of events; considers that this will allow greater participation and offer more opportunities for people across all age groups, fitness levels and abilities to experience the range of sporting activities on offer; acknowledges the importance of the event in the run-up to the 2014 Commonwealth Games in Glasgow; believes that the festival makes a crucial contribution to improving national and local awareness of the broad range of sporting activities available across Edinburgh and Scotland; welcomes the important role that the festival plays as part of Edinburgh’s Activcity programme, and looks forward to the festival’s success and continued contribution to Scotland’s health and sporting community.

Supported by: Derek Mackay*, Mike MacKenzie*, John Wilson*, Richard Lyle*, Stewart Maxwell*, Kenneth Gibson*, Mark McDonald*, Rob Gibson*

*S4M-00218 Jackie Baillie: Employment Project Ontowork Scrapped in West Dunbartonshire—That the Parliament expresses concern at news that West Dunbartonshire Council has withdrawn all funding provided to the Ontowork project; notes that, as of 30 June 2011, the council will cease to fund the project, which has served the West Dunbartonshire community for 22 years; understands that Ontowork has a proven track record of success and in the last two years has supported 477 West Dunbartonshire residents into employment, 260 into training and currently has over 680 clients; condemns the decision to withdraw what it considers an essential service at a time when West Dunbartonshire has been named by the TUC as the most difficult place in the United Kingdom to find a job, and calls on West Dunbartonshire Council to reverse its decision and support Ontowork in finding jobs for local residents in West Dunbartonshire.

*S4M-00217 Stuart McMillan: The Success of Prostate Cancer Awareness Month 2011—That the Parliament understands that prostate cancer is the most commonly diagnosed cancer among Scottish men, with 2,700 diagnoses every year and 19,000 Scots currently living with the disease; understands that March 2011 marked the most successful prostate cancer awareness month in Scotland to date; thanks MSPs of all parties and of none for getting behind the month so extensively by wearing The Prostate Cancer Charity’s blue man badge during First Minister’s Question Time on 3 March, by backing motion S3M-08022 in support of the month and by signing the pledge for prostate cancer during the spring party conference season; further thanks The Prostate Cancer Charity, Scotland’s trained volunteers and support group members for their tireless awareness and fundraising endeavours during Prostate Cancer Awareness Month; believes that the success of the month can act as an ideal springboard for The Prostate Cancer Charity Scotland to step up its fight for a world where lives are no longer limited by prostate cancer, and looks forward to further joint working with the charity as it seeks to achieve this objective by tackling the disease through its ongoing research, support, information and campaigning work.
