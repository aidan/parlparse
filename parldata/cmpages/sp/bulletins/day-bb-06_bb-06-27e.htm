Business Bulletin No. 106/2006: Tuesday 27 June 2006
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 26 June 2006

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-27097Δ Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to consult on the law relating to the compellability of spousal witnesses.

S2W-27098Δ Janis Hughes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make a statement on the future of the blood processing activities of the Scottish National Blood Transfusion Service, including the Protein Fractionation Centre.

S2W-27099 Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will publish the STAG1 report on the Maybole bypass commissioned by the Minister for Transport in 2003.

S2W-27100 Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on accidents involving motor cyclists on motorways or duelled sections of road where a wire rope rather than solid section central reservation division is provided.

S2W-27101 Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive what research has been carried out into motor cyclist safety regarding the provision of wire rope central reservation divisions on Scottish roads.

S2W-27102 Cathie Craigie: To ask the Scottish Executive what timetable is envisaged for implementing the recommendations in Moving Forward: Review of NHS Wheelchair and Special Seating Services in Scotland.

S2W-27103 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many internal leak inquiries it has conducted in each year since 1999, broken down by department.

S2W-27104 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many women aged under 30 are smokers and what the figure was (a) five and (b) 10 years ago.

S2W-27105 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average time was for results of (a) breast cancer and (b) genetic tests in each of the last five years, broken down by NHS board.

S2W-27106 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many NHS beds in specialist facilities are available for spinal cord-injured patients and how many were available in (a) 1990, (b) 1995, (c) 2000 and (d) 2005.

S2W-27107 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive which accident and emergency departments have been (a) closed and (b) downgraded in each of the last 10 years.

S2W-27108 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average percentage increases in houses prices have been in (a) 2004, (b) 2005 and (c) 2006 in the (i) IV52 (Plockton), (ii) IV9 (Avoch), (iii) KW1 (Wick), (iv) KW12 (Halkirk), (v) KW9 (Brora), (vi) IV17 (Alness), (vii) IV27 (north-west Sutherland), (viii) HS1 (Stornoway), (ix) IV51 (Portree), (x) IV12 (Cawdor), (xi) IV31 (Lossiemouth), (xii) KW17 (Westray), (xiii) ZE2 (west Shetland) and (xiv) PA34 (Oban) postcode areas.

S2W-27109 Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will ensure that there are sufficient permanent posts available for new teachers at the end of their probationary year.

S2W-27110 Elaine Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to disseminate information throughout the NHS on current advances in research into, and treatment of, Duchenne muscular dystrophy.

S2W-27111 Elaine Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what research it has funded in the last six years involving Duchenne muscular dystrophy.

S2W-27112 Elaine Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding streams are available for research into Duchenne muscular dystrophy and what action it will take to encourage research in this area.

S2W-27113 Ms Maureen Watt: To ask the Scottish Executive what targets have been set to ensure that people having seizures will not have to wait more than six months for intervention and treatment after seeing an epilepsy specialist.

S2W-27114 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answers to questions S2W-26773 and SW2-26744 by Rhona Brankin on 23 June 2006, whether it will provide a breakdown by (a) breed and (b) township of the number of bulls hired to crofters in (i) 2004, (ii) 2005 and (iii) 2006 under the interim arrangements following withdrawal of the Bull Hire Scheme, also showing which breeds were ordered by each township.

S2W-27115 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-26773 by Rhona Brankin on 23 June 2006, how many bulls were considered to have poor estimated breeding value in (a) 2004, (b) 2005 and (c) 2006, broken down by breed.
