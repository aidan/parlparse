Business Bulletin No. 9/2009: Thursday 15 January 2009
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 14 January 2009

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-19721 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made towards turning Scotland into a "Celtic lion" economy, described by the First Minister in a speech at Harvard University on 31 March 2008 as the "defining mission" of the Scottish Government, and what contribution the Small Business Bonus Scheme will make in this area.

S3W-19722 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made towards achieving the First Minister’s commitment to "building an economy that is the envy of Europe", as outlined in a speech at Harvard University on 31 March 2008, and what contribution the Small Business Bonus Scheme will make in this area.

S3W-19723 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what targets have been set in relation to the First Minister’s commitment to "building an economy that is the envy of Europe" and to turning Scotland into a "Celtic lion" economy as outlined in a speech at Harvard University on 31 March 2008, and how success in this area will be measured.

S3W-19729 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on achieving the 29 recommendations contained in the review document, The Same as You.

S3W-19730 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will evaluate the impact of its The Same As You policy on services for people with learning disabilities before 2010 to inform policy development.

S3W-19731 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what will replace partnership in practice agreements to ensure that people with learning disabilities continue to be involved in the development of local services.

S3W-19732 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what it will bring forward to replace its The Same As You policy to ensure continuing focus on services for people with learning disabilities.

S3W-19733 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive which local authorities’ single outcome agreements contain specific reference to services for people with learning disabilities.

S3W-19734 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will monitor local provision and development of services for people with learning disabilities.

S3W-19735 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what work is being done on the introduction of processes for monitoring and measuring the provision and development of services for people with learning disabilities, broken down by local authority.

S3W-19736 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to expand the Scottish Driving Assessment Service.

S3W-19737 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider establishing a permanent base for the Scottish Driving Assessment Service in the west of Scotland.

S3W-19738 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many patients were referred to the Scottish Driving Assessment Service in 2007-08 and for the first nine months of 2008-09, broken down by NHS board.

S3W-19739 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many patients had to wait for an assessment from the Scottish Driving Assessment Service in 2007-08 and for the first nine months of 2008-09, also showing the waiting period, broken down by NHS board.

S3W-19740 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average annual pattern and duration is of visits by the mobile unit of the Scottish Driving Assessment Service.

S3W-19741 Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive what the planned date is for holding IslamFest, which the Scottish Islamic Foundation was granted funding to organise.

S3W-19742 Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list the dates of meetings of the National Group to Address Violence Against Women since May 2007.

S3W-19743 Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive when the National Group to Address Violence Against Women will next meet.

S3W-19744 Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive when in 2009 the conference on race, referred to in the Scottish Government Race Equality Statement published on 8 December 2008, will take place.

S3W-19745 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what the rate of still births per 1,000 live births was in the last year for which information is available, broken down by NHS board.

S3W-19746 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive how much will be allocated to improve transport links between North Ayrshire and Glasgow in (a) 2009, (b) 2010 and (c) 2011.

S3W-19747 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions the Cabinet Secretary for Health and Wellbeing has had with NHS Ayrshire and Arran regarding the timescale for setting up a new community unit at Ayrshire Central Hospital.

S3W-19748 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive how many deaths have occurred as a result of Clostridium difficile in NHS Ayrshire and Arran in each of the last six months.

S3W-19749 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive how many cases of Clostridium difficile have been reported by NHS Ayrshire and Arran in each of the last six months.

S3W-19750 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what the rate of still births per 1,000 live births was in each of the last five years for which information is available, broken down by NHS board.

S3W-19751 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what the rate of late fetal deaths per 1,000 live births was in each of the last five years for which information is available, broken down by NHS board.

S3W-19752 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what the rate of neonatal deaths per 1,000 live births was in each of the last five years for which information is available.

S3W-19753 Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive how many accident and emergency staff were employed by the Scottish Ambulance Service in NHS (a) Highland, (b) Grampian, (c) Western Isles, (d) Orkney and (e) Shetland in (i) 2007 and (ii) 2008, broken down by month.

S3W-19754 Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive how many staff were employed in each emergency medical dispatch centre in (a) 2007 and (b) 2008, broken down by month.

S3W-19755 Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive how many staff vacancies there were in the Scottish Ambulance Air Wing in (a) 2006, (b) 2007 and (c) 2008, broken down by month.

S3W-19756 Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive on how many occasions air ambulance aircraft have been grounded due to staff shortages in each of the last six months.

S3W-19757 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list meetings that it has had with representatives of the Royal Scottish Academy of Music and Drama since May 2007, broken down by date and location.

S3W-19758 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive how many accounts submitted to it by contractors had not been settled within a 10-day period as at 10 December 2008, also expressed as a percentage of the transactions paid within that period.

S3W-19759 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive how many accounts submitted to it by contractors had not been settled within a 10-day period as at 10 January 2009, also expressed as a percentage of the transactions paid within that period.

S3W-19760 John Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the anticipated schedule is to deliver the enhanced park-and-ride facilities at Croy railway station.

S3W-19761 Peter Peacock: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied that the existing A96 trunk road through Elgin is an adequate trunk road for current circumstances and for the projected traffic growth.

S3W-19762 Peter Peacock: To ask the Scottish Executive what the traffic growth forecasts are for the A96 through Elgin for the period up to 2020.

S3W-19763 Peter Peacock: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will set out its reasons for not including the Elgin bypass as a priority project for completion in the Strategic Transport Projects Review.

S3W-19764 Peter Peacock: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will publish the advice Scottish ministers received on the case for an Elgin bypass as a priority project in the Strategic Transport Projects Review.

S3W-19765Δ Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will publish its response to the recommendations made in the First Annual Report of the Scottish Council of Economic Advisers: December 2008.

S3W-19766 Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure that bus operators comply with the rules and regulations of the national concessionary travel scheme.

S3W-19767 Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken to ensure that bus operators submit correct information on the revenue forgone or average fare element of their travel claims under the national concessionary travel scheme.

S3W-19768 Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive why some bus operators do not have to record boarding and alighting points when submitting claims for concessionary travel.

S3W-19769 Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to require bus operators to record the origin and destination of journeys under the national concessionary travel scheme.

S3W-19770 Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it can intervene in a local authority planning decision on a retrospective application to install double glazing.

S3W-19771 Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it can intervene in a local authority decision to grant planning permission for white uPVC windows in an area where planning policy has deemed this to be inappropriate.

S3W-19772 Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive what action members of the public can take if they object to planning permission being granted for white uPVC windows in an area where local planning policy has deemed this to be inappropriate.

S3W-19773 Bill Aitken: To ask the Scottish Executive, excluding the standard induction meeting, how many people sentenced to community service orders begin work within (a) one week, (b) one month, (c) two months, (d) three months and (e) six months.

S3W-19774 Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making in re-establishing a ferry service between Campbeltown and Ballycastle.

S3W-19775 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps were taken by Scottish Government officials to bring forward Less Favoured Area Support Scheme (LFASS) payments before the end of 2008.

S3W-19776 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken to ensure that the next round of Less Favoured Area Support Scheme (LFASS) payments and single farm payments can be made in 2009.

S3W-19777 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what advice it has taken on the possibility of making the Less Favoured Area Support Scheme (LFASS) payment and the single farm payment at the same time.

S3W-19778 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the European Commission on the possibility of making the Less Favoured Area Support Scheme (LFASS) payment and the single farm payment in the same year.

S3W-19779 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that there is any legal impediment to making the Less Favoured Area Support Scheme (LFASS) payment and the single farm payment in the same year.

S3W-19780 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make an announcement on the outcome of any investigation into making the Less Favoured Area Support Scheme (LFASS) payment and the single farm payment at the same time and, if so, when.

S3W-19781 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has made any assessment of the potential benefits for farmers and crofters from the Less Favoured Area Support Scheme (LFASS) payment and the single farm payment being made in the same year.

S3W-19782 Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there is a code of good practice on the siting of fin fish farm cages in (a) salt and (b) fresh water.

S3W-19783 Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive whether a code of good practice or regulation exists on how long fin fish cages can be used on a single site without being moved in (a) salt and (b) fresh water.

S3W-19784 Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive whether its review of aquaculture will consider the appropriateness of existing fin fish farm sites in relation to the impact that they may have had on wild fish populations in (a) salt and (b) fresh water.
