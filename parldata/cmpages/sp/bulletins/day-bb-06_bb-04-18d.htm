Business Bulletin No. 59/2006: Tuesday 18 April 2006

Section D – First Minister’s and Oral questions selected for answer on 20 April 2006

First Minister’s Question Time

1. Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the First Minister when he will next meet the Prime Minister and what issues they will discuss. (S2F-2230)

2. Miss Annabel Goldie: To ask the First Minister what issues will be discussed at the next meeting of the Scottish Executive’s Cabinet. (S2F-2231)

3. Colin Fox: To ask the First Minister when he next intends to meet the Chancellor of the Exchequer and what issues they will discuss. (S2F-2244)

4. Helen Eadie: To ask the First Minister whether enough precautions are in place to combat bird flu. (S2F-2236)

5. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the First Minister what guidance the Scottish Executive will issue to police forces in relation to the new offence of glorifying terrorism, created by the Terrorism Act 2006. (S2F-2243)

6. Fergus Ewing: To ask the First Minister what improvements have been made in respect of the diagnosis, treatment and prevention of osteoporosis; what further plans the Scottish Executive considers should now be made, and over what time period they will be implemented. (S2F-2233)

Question Time

General

1. Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it agrees with the advice from Commissioner Mariann Fischer Boel that Article 45 of EC Regulation 1782/2003 could be used in order to ensure that farmers in Scotland who were deer farming prior to 2002 can receive a Single Farm Payment under new EU support mechanisms. (S2O-9537)

2. Mrs Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive how it ensures that people placed temporarily in a nursing or residential home are still able to access primary healthcare services. (S2O-9570)

3. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive what economic benefits there are in proposals for a second runway at Edinburgh Airport. (S2O-9515)

4. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how it considers that the proposed closure of Jedburgh and Coldstream cottage hospitals complies with the spirit of the Kerr report. (S2O-9579)

5. Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to promote good practice on caring for elderly people with dementia. (S2O-9572)

6. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to be able to announce the outcome of its examination of the economic, social and environmental impact and costs of retaining or removing tolls from the Forth and Tay road bridges, as recommended by the Parliament on 30 March 2006. (S2O-9543)

7. Euan Robson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider creating a new national healthcare priority to combat respiratory diseases. (S2O-9523)

8. Mr Ted Brocklebank: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is able to provide any further information about the Office of Fair Trading investigation into supermarket trading. (S2O-9589)

9. Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what further progress it is making on the Aberdeen Crossrail project. (S2O-9544)

10. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to minimise the economic impact on the East Neuk of Fife following the discovery of a dead swan infected with the H5N1 strain of avian influenza. (S2O-9524)

Education and Young People, Tourism, Culture and Sport

1. Mr Mark Ruskell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many new schools being built under PPP contracts are installing biomass heating systems. (S2O-9581)

2. Mr Jim Wallace: To ask the Scottish Executive what key lessons have been learned from the Commonwealth Games in Melbourne in developing Glasgow’s bid for the 2014 Commonwealth Games. (S2O-9518)

3. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met the Scottish Tourism Forum and what issues were discussed. (S2O-9578)

4. John Swinburne: To ask the Scottish Executive what cultural events it plans to hold to celebrate National Veterans Day on 27 June 2006. (S2O-9513)

5. Janis Hughes: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on the average age of young people leaving care from (a) foster care, (b) residential units and (c) children’s units. (S2O-9554)

6. Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive how many American tourists visited Scotland in the last year. (S2O-9587)

7. Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are any plans to improve the tracking by local education authorities of the final educational destination of school leavers. (S2O-9551)

8. Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it supports schools developing curricular specialities to promote higher attainment. (S2O-9564)

9. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what changes it will make to schools’ procurement guidelines to ensure that they contribute to the Executive’s Climate Change Programme objectives. (S2O-9545)

10. Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has had, or plans to have, any discussions with VisitScotland and the City of Edinburgh Council regarding creating a distinct Edinburgh tourist board. (S2O-9533)

Finance and Public Services and Communities

1. Not Lodged

2. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met COSLA to discuss the issue of budgets for the Supporting People initiative. (S2O-9549)

3. Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in resolving the local government pensions dispute. (S2O-9542)

4. Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on the reform of public services. (S2O-9530)

5. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to review the formula whereby Revenue Support Grant funding is redistributed in favour of areas of deprivation. (S2O-9553)

6. Frances Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive how much it has already allocated, and how much it will allocate, to fund PPP/PFI schools and hospital contracts. (S2O-9535)

7. Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met the President of COSLA and what issues were discussed. (S2O-9586)

8. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will call in the proposal for the development at Musselburgh Racecourse. (S2O-9585)

9. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether, learning from the English experience with parish councils, it will discuss with community councils and other community groups an increase in the powers, resources and opportunities of community councils. (S2O-9514)

10. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are being taken to make non-departmental public bodies more open and accountable. (S2O-9560)
