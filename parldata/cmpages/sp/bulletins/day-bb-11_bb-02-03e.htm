Business Bulletin No. 19/2011: Thursday 3 February 2011

Section E – Written questions lodged on 2 February 2011

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-39276 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the usefulness of homeopathy in relation to the Scottish Government’s integrative approach to patient care.

S3W-39277 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the cost-effectiveness of homeopathy in relation to the Scottish Government’s integrative approach to patient care.

S3W-39278 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers homeopathy to have a place in relation to the Scottish Government’s integrative approach to patient care.

S3W-39279 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-37848 by Nicola Sturgeon on 25 November 2010, what its position is on whether the withdrawal of homeopathic services in NHS Lothian is consistent with "person-centredness and compassion" and "a holistic approach to people’s healthcare needs."

S3W-39280 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that a shift towards personalised services, as outlined in the Better Health, Better Care: Action Plan, should include homeopathic options.

S3W-39281 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is monitoring the implementation by each NHS board of the report, Getting to GRIPS with Chronic Pain in Scotland.

S3W-39282 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what pain management services there are in each NHS board.

S3W-39283 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive which NHS boards have sent patients to pain management programmes outside Scotland and how much this cost each board.

S3W-39284 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many patients are offered a multidisciplinary pain management programme after attending a local pain clinic, broken down by NHS board.

S3W-39285 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance it issues to ensure the independence and impartiality of witness statements.

S3W-39286 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive how it ensures that police officers completing witness statements do so impartially and without consulting with colleagues.

S3W-39287 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive whether procurators fiscal have a responsibility to examine the content and impartiality of witness statements recorded bypolice officers.

S3W-39288 Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what the age-standardised mortality rate from prostate cancer has been in each year since 1997-98, also broken down by (a) NHS board and (b) parliamentary constituency.

S3W-39289 Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive how many deaths from prostate cancer there have been in each year since 1997-98, also broken down by (a) NHS board and (b) parliamentary constituency.

S3W-39290 Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what the (a) age-standardised incidence for prostate cancer and (b) number of diagnoses of prostate cancer there have been in each year since 1997-98, also broken down by (i) NHS board and (ii) parliamentary constituency.

S3W-39291 Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prostate-specific antigen (PSA) tests there have been in each year since 1997-98, also broken down by (a) NHS board and (b) parliamentary constituency.

S3W-39292 Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made of reports made by prostate cancer patients regarding their treatment and care and what steps are being taken to addressing variations experienced.

S3W-39293 Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive how many attendances at GP surgeries by prostate cancer patients there have been in each year since 1997-98, also broken down by (a) NHS board and (b) parliamentary constituency.

S3W-39294 Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive how many emergency admissions by patients with a primary diagnosis of prostate cancer there have been in each year since 1997-98, also broken down by (a) NHS board and (b) parliamentary constituency.

S3W-39295 Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it takes to (a) monitor and (b) improve the experience of the treatment and care of cancer patients.

S3W-39296 Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken to increase awareness of prostate-specific antigen (PSA) tests, including the risks and benefits, and whether it will provide information regarding these to (a) GPs and (b) the public.

S3W-39297 Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure that men are made aware of the prostate-specific antigen (PSA) test and are given sufficient information to make a choice regarding taking it.

S3W-39298 Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what proportion of (a) men and (b) GPs was aware of the availability of the prostate-specific antigen (PSA) test in each year since 1997, also broken down by (i) NHS board and (ii) parliamentary constituency.

S3W-39299 Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to establish a test for prostate cancer that would be suitable as a universal screening tool.

S3W-39300 Hugh O’Donnell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to develop the Scottish Book Trust’s book gifting scheme to include pupils in other years.

S3W-39301 Hugh O’Donnell: To ask the Scottish Executive how much it is allocating to the Scottish Book Trust’s book gifting scheme in 2011-12.

S3W-39302 Hugh O’Donnell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has conducted an impact evaluation of the Scottish Book Trust’s book gifting scheme and, if so, when this will be made available.

S3W-39303 Hugh O’Donnell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has conducted research into the levels of extracurricular reading among school-age children and, if so, when this will be made available.

S3W-39304 Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the document, A Policy for Sika Deer in Scotland, published by the Forestry Commission and Scottish Natural Heritage in 1997 reflects Scottish Government policy.

S3W-39305 Hugh O’Donnell: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-38849 by Michael Russell on 17 January 2011, how much funding has been set aside for the Literacy Action Plan.

S3W-39306 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many applications for support have been made in the Clydesdale parliamentary constituency under the new entrants scheme for farming since the scheme was introduced.

S3W-39307 Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many applications for support in the Clydesdale parliamentary constituency under the new entrants scheme for farming have been successful since the scheme was introduced.

S3W-39308 Elizabeth Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what changes were made to the methodology used to determine the natural heritage indicator for the area of Scotland unaffected by built development; for what reason, and how comparable the 2009 results are with the results between 2002 and 2008 as a consequence of these changes.

S3W-39309 Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to secure the future of jobs at the Skykon wind tower factory at Macrihanish.

S3W-39310 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will confirm that the amount budgeted for distinction awards in the 2010-11 draft budget is £30 million in cash and £29.6 million in real terms and that this represents a 7.1% and 5.6% increase respectively on the amount budgeted in 2009-10.

S3W-39311 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive, given the increase in the workforce line for health and sport in the draft budget for 2011-12 to £28.5 million in cash terms, what regulatory bodies will receive lower payments.

S3W-39312 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many student nurses have enrolled in each of the last five academic years.

S3W-39313 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to increase the demand-led funding for general (a) medical and (b) dental services.

S3W-39314 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason it has reduced, in the draft budget for 2011-12, the increase planned for eHealth for 2010-11.

S3W-39315 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason the national indicator connected with reducing coronary heart disease in under-75s in deprived areas contained in the HEAT targets for 2008-09 is absent from the current list.

S3W-39316 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason only four HEAT targets in the 2011-12 draft budget correlate with the Scottish Government’s performance indicators.

S3W-39317 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason there is no target to tackle NHS staff sickness absence in the draft budget for 2011-12.

S3W-39318 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason there is no emergency ambulance response rate target in the draft budget for 2011-12.

S3W-39319 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason there is no accident and emergency waiting time target in the draft budget for 2011-12.

S3W-39320 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason it has decided to create HEAT standards to replace HEAT targets.

S3W-39321 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason there are targets to measure performance against budget for only three of its key spending priorities for health and sport outlined in the draft budget for 2011-12.

S3W-39322 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will confirm that in its draft budget for 2011-12 the spending on health will be £190 million less than planned in the budget for 2010-11.

S3W-39323 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will confirm that, in comparing the draft budget for 2011-12 with that for 2010-11, £95 million less is budgeted for theNHS boards and Special Health Boards.

S3W-39324 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the capital expenditure line in the draft budget for 2011-12 takes account of the repayment of the £50 million in capital expenditure brought forward to 2010-11.

S3W-39325 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will confirm that the capital investment in the draft budget for 2011-12 shows a cash-terms decrease of £105 million and a real-terms decrease of £113.5 million compared with the draft budget for 2010-11 and that this will covered by means of the accumulated underspend.

S3W-39326 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will confirm that, when comparing the draft budgets for 2011-12 and 2010-11 for health and sport, spending plans for education and training are reduced at a proportionally higher rate under the workforce line than the nursing line.

S3W-39327 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether, when comparing the education and training spending plans across the workforce and nursing lines in the draft budget for 2011-12 and 2010-11 for health and sport, the nursing line will see the larger decrease in cash terms.

S3W-39328 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether, in the draft budget for 2011-12 for health and sport, the workforce budget line has decreased by 17.2% and the nursing budget line by 7% compared with the draft budget for 2010-11.

S3W-39329 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list the eHealth strategy commitments that it plans to defer under its draft budget for 2011-12.

S3W-39330 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has assessed the impact of the proposed reduction in the health screening budget for 2011-12 by almost £6 million to £8.4 million in real terms compared with 2010-11.

S3W-39331 Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason it transferred the budget line for cervical cancer screening to miscellaneous other services.
