Business Bulletin No. 190/2001

Thursday 20 December 2001

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-2569 Patricia Ferguson on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Membership of Committees-That the Parliament agrees that the following Members be appointed to Committees-

Duncan Hamilton to replace Margaret Ewing on the Justice 2 Committee,

Andrew Wilson to replace Duncan Hamilton on the Enterprise and Lifelong Learning Committee.

Supported by: Euan Robson*

*S1M-2568 Mr Mike Rumbles: International Year of Mountains-That the Parliament notes that 2002 is the International Year of Mountains; supports the event's aim of raising public awareness of the high ecological value and wider socio-economic significance of mountains; recognises the global importance of Scotland's mountainous areas, particularly their role in shaping the nation's image and culture, and therefore urges the Scottish Executive to support the year's celebrations by fostering debate between local communities, farming interests, rural businesses, public bodies, and non-governmental organisations on how to maximise the mutual benefit from activities aimed at safeguarding, enhancing and promoting enjoyment of Scotland's mountain heritage.

*S1M-2567 Scott Barrie: Death of Stuart Adamson-That the Parliament notes the untimely death of Stuart Adamson; acknowledges the invaluable contribution he made to contemporary popular music in Scotland, particularly as lead singer with Big Country and guitarist with The Skids; recognises the part he played in promoting his home town of Dunfermline, and sends its condolences to his family.

*S1M-2566 Patricia Ferguson on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Business Motion-That the Parliament agrees that for Stage 3 of the Scottish Local Government (Elections) Bill and Stage 3 of the School Education (Amendment) (Scotland) Bill, debate on each part of the proceedings, if not previously brought to a conclusion, shall be brought to a conclusion at the following times (calculated from the time when Stage 3 of the Scottish Local Government (Elections) Bill begins)-

Scottish Local Government (Elections) Bill

Consideration of Amendments - no later than 1 hour

Motion to pass the Bill - no later than 1 hour 30 minutes

School Education (Amendment) (Scotland) Bill

Consideration of Amendments - no later than 2 hours 30 minutes

Motion to pass the Bill - no later than 3 hours

Supported by: Euan Robson*

*S1M-2565 Margaret Smith on behalf of the Health and Community Care Committee: Report on Hepatitis C-That the Parliament notes, and calls upon the Scottish Executive to act upon, the recommendations contained within the 17th Report 2001 of the Health and Community Care Committee, Report on Hepatitis C (SP Paper 398).

*S1M-2564 Irene McGugan: Citizens of a Multilingual World-That the Parliament welcomes the findings in the report by the Action Group on Languages, Citizens of a Multilingual World and the supportive statements made by Jack McConnell MSP when Minister for Education, Europe and External Affairs; notes however the concerns of the Scottish Association of Language Teaching and others regarding the delay in making the revised 5-14 curriculum guidance available, which has hindered progress and much needed development in modern languages in both the primary and secondary school sectors; recognises that this regrettable delay in responding to the Group's report, and the publication of Circular 3/2001, Guidance on Flexibility in the Curriculum, in advance of this response, have led to widespread speculation and misinterpretation of the principle of "entitlement" as it is described in the Group's report, and calls upon the Scottish Executive to end this uncertainty and pursue the proposals in the report by means of a strategic implementation plan without further delay and to provide local authorities and schools with the responsibility and opportunity to improve language learning throughout Scotland, so that each pupil and student is enabled to fulfil his or her potential as a citizen of this multilingual world.

*S1M-2563 Cathy Jamieson: Education (Disability Strategies and Pupils' Records) (Scotland) Bill-That the Parliament agrees to the general principles of the Education (Disability Strategies and Pupils' Records) (Scotland) Bill.

Supported by: Nicol Stephen*, Ms Margaret Curran*
