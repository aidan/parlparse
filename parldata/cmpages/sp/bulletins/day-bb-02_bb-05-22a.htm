Business Bulletin No. 84/2002

Wednesday 22 May 2002

Section A : Daily Business List
  

Meetings of Committees

All meetings take place in Committee Chambers, Edinburgh unless otherwise indicated.

Health and Community Care Committee
15th Meeting, 2002

The Committee will meet at 9.30 am in Committee Room 4

1. Items in private: The Committee will consider whether to take items 4-7 in private.

2. Subordinate Legislation: The Committee will consider the following affirmative instrument -

  • The Food Protection (Emergency Prohibitions) (Amnesic Shellfish Poisoning) (West Coast) (No.4) (Scotland) Order 2002, (SSI 2002/231)

and the following negative instruments -

  • The Adults with Incapacity (Medical Treatment Certificates) (Scotland) Regulations 2002, (SSI 2002/208)
  • The National Health Service (Optical Charges and Payments) (Scotland) Amendment (No.2) Regulations 2002, (SSI 2002/224).

3. Petitions Report: The Committee will consider a report on petitions.

4. School Meals (Scotland) Bill (Stage 1): The Committee will consider a draft Stage 1 on the School Meals (Scotland) Bill.

5. MMR: The Committee will consider the MMR Expert Group report.

6. Local Government Bill in Scotland: The Committee will consider a list of witnesses.

7. Proposed Bill on Mental Health: The Committee will consider a list of possible advisers on forthcoming mental health legislation.

Justice 2 Committee

21st Meeting, 2002


The Committee will meet at 9.30 am in Committee Room 2

1. Criminal Justice (Scotland) Bill (in private): The Committee will consider lines of questioning for item 4.

2. Item in private: The Committee will consider whether to take item 5 in private.

3. Subordinate legislation: The Committee will consider the following negative instruments:

  • The Regulation of Investigatory Powers (Cancellation of Authorisations) (Scotland) Regulations 2002 (SSI 2002/207);
  • The Regulation of Investigatory Powers (Juveniles) (Scotland) Order 2002 (SSI 2002/206);
  • The Regulation of Investigatory Powers (Source Records) (Scotland) Regulations 2002 (SSI 2002/205).

4. Criminal Justice (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will take evidence on the general principles of the Bill at Stage 1 from-

  • Helen Stirling, British Psychological Society;
  • Rosemarie McIlwhan, Director, Scottish Human Rights Centre;
  • Kelly Bayes, Barnardo's Scotland,
  • Susan Elsley, Save the Children Scotland,
  • Margaret McKay, Children First and
  • Anna Daley, Community Practitioners and Health Visitors Association;
  • Jennifer Bairner, Chair, Justice Committee, Scottish Youth Parliament;

Not before 2.00pm

  • Sam Campbell and Trudy Kinloch;
  • Judith Gillespie and Eleanor Coner, Scottish Parent Teacher Council;
  • Norman Wells, Families First,
  • Jeremy Balfour, CARE and
  • Christine Dodd, Churches Network for non-violence.

5. Criminal Justice (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will consider the payment of witness expenses.

Transport and the Environment Committee

17th Meeting, 2002


The Committee will meet at 9.30 am in Committee Room 1

1. Aquaculture Inquiry (in private): The Committee will consider possible areas of questioning for witnesses.

2. Item in Private: The Committee will consider whether to take agenda item 4 in private.

3. Aquaculture Inquiry: The Committee will take evidence as part of its inquiry into aquaculture from-

  • David North, Director for Government and Industry Affairs, Tesco
  • Jeremy Hooper, Lead Technical Manager, Fish and Poultry, Tesco
  • Allan Wilson MSP, Deputy Minister for Environment and Rural Development

4. Aquaculture Inquiry: The Committee will consider evidence taken at Phase 2 of its inquiry into aquaculture.

Not before 11.30am

5. Subordinate Legislation: The Committee will consider the following draft affirmative instruments-

  • The Scotland Act 1998 (Modifications of Schedule 5) Order 2002.
  • The Scotland Act 1998 (Transfer of Functions to the Scottish Ministers etc.) Order 2002.

6. Highlands and Islands Ferry Services: The Committee will consider a reporters' paper on the progress of its inquiry into Highlands and Islands Ferry Services.

7. Public Petitions: The Committee will consider the following public petitions:

  • PE 421 by Mr Alasdair Nicholson on Road Equivalent Tariffs.
  • PE 425 by Mrs Anne-Marie Glashan on the siting of mobile phone masts.
  • PE 357 by Aberdeen City Council on Investment Transport Infrastructure.

Standards Committee

9th Meeting, 2002


The Committee will meet at 10.30 am in Committee Room 3

1. Declaration of Interests: The new Member of the Committee will be invited to declare any registrable interests.

2. Item in Private: The Committee will consider whether to take item 4 in private.

3. Replacing the Members' Interest Order: The Committee will consider a short paper on the requirement to register heritable property.

4. Scottish Parliamentary Standards Commissioner Bill: The Committee will consider papers concerning issues at Stage 2.
