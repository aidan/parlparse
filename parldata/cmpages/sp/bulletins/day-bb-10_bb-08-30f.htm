Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally printed the day after they are lodged. If an amendment is lodged to a motion then the original motion will appear alongside the amendment, along with any support lodged that day, in this section.

Each Monday, this section also contains all motions and amendments lodged the previous week that are still live and any motions and amendments that have been lodged for debate in the coming week. A motion or amendment is live if it has not been debated, withdrawn or otherwise deleted from the list.

Where a motion or amendment has been withdrawn, it is indicated in this section the day after it has been withdrawn. Support for motions and amendments received after they are lodged is shown at the end of this section the day after such support is received.

Motions and amendments can be published with various symbols:

  • Asterisks before the motion or amendment number indicate a motion or amendment published for the first time;
  • Asterisks also identify alterations to the text of a motion or amendment made since it was first published;
  • A hash symbol identifies motions eligible for debate at Members’ Business;
  • A diamond symbol identifies motions lodged for Members’ Business that have not yet attracted the required cross-party support;
  • An "R", identifies motions or amendments in which the Member who lodged it has a registrable interest.

The Parliamentary Bureau periodically deletes motions or amendments not scheduled for debate and which are over six weeks old.

A full list of current motions is available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/motions.htm.

A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/index.htm.

Any questions in relation to this section should be directed to the Chamber Desk, for which contact details are provided at the end of the Bulletin.

*S3M-6880 Jamie Hepburn: Vigil to highlight Freedom of Religion and Imprisonment of Bahá’ís in Iran—That the Parliament sends its support to the organisers of a vigil, to be held in St Mungo Museum in Cathedral Square, Glasgow, on Saturday 28 August 2010 from 3 pm until 10 pm, to highlight freedom of religion in Iran and the plight of seven prominent Bahá’ís, who were this month sentenced to 20 years imprisonment by the Iranian authorities; agrees with the comments of Foreign Secretary William Hague that the sentencing is "a shocking example of the Iranian state’s continued discrimination against the Bahá’ís"; believes that people across Scotland should show their support for the vigil as a means of showing solidarity with the Bahá’í community and their commitment to the right of freedom of religious expression, and joins Foreign Secretary William Hague’s in calling on the Iranian authorities to consider any appeal against this sentencing of these seven individuals and to immediately cease discrimination against the Bahá’í community.

*S3M-6879 Bill Kidd: The UK June 2010 Budget and Equality Assessments—That the Parliament expresses concern, following remarks by the Equality and Human Rights Commission that the UK Government must consider the effect of budget cuts on vulnerable groups and that "Under equality legislation, the Treasury, like all public bodies, has a legal duty to pay "due regard" to equality and consider any disproportionate impact on vulnerable groups when making decisions, including decisions about the budget", at the apparent failure by the UK Government to carry out assessments of the effects of the June 2010 Budget on vulnerable groups and considers in particular that the severe cut-backs on disability living allowance, expressly to save money rather than based on the principle of fairness, would appear to fall disproportionably heavily on disabled people as a vulnerable group.

*S3M-6878 Sandra White: International Day of the Disappeared—That the Parliament notes the International Day of the Disappeared on Monday 30 August 2010, which is a United Nations-recognised annual commemoration day for individuals who have gone missing during armed conflicts or other situations of violence and whose fate is unresolved; recognises that hundreds of thousands of families across the world are still unaware of the fate of their loved ones who are missing; supports the work of the Red Cross in putting missing people back in touch with loved ones and commends the work of the Red Cross International Tracing and Message Service based at the organisation’s Refugee Unit in Glasgow, and further notes that over the past year this service has seen over 500 people, worked on 120 new tracing cases and has had 30 successful cases, which led to the reconnection of over 80 people and the exchange of 90 Red Cross messages between separated families.

*S3M-6875 Elaine Smith: NHS Lanarkshire is UK Pioneer for New Osteoporosis Service—That the Parliament congratulates NHS Lanarkshire on being the first NHS board in Scotland to offer a new service for osteoporosis across both primary and secondary care; recognises that osteoporosis is a devastating condition affecting many more women than men and causing a high risk of fractures in the elderly; understands that bisphosphonates are types of drugs that make bones stronger, prevent breaks and relieve bone pain; notes that the Lanarkshire Intravenous Bisphosphonate Service will transform the way that this type of medication is delivered to the people who need it most; further notes that the drug will be administered by a nurse as a once yearly intravenous infusion; considers that this innovative approach, promoted by Dr Eamonn Brankin, will mean that those who were unable to benefit from the existing treatment of a weekly tablet due to other medical conditions, health issues, or an inability to comply with the requirement to remain upright for a couple of hours after taking the tablet will now be able to benefit from this medication, and welcomes the fact that NHS Lanarkshire have recognised the worth in spending on this new service to save money in the longer term and, most importantly, to save lives.
