Business Bulletin No. 148/2002

Friday 11 October 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments

  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-3485# Mr Keith Harding: Breast Cancer Awareness—That the Parliament congratulates all the voluntary and research agencies involved for their efforts in highlighting Breast Cancer Awareness Month; recognises the importance of continuing research into the diagnosis and treatment of breast cancer, building upon major advances achieved over past decades; records its thanks to all the medical and ancillary staff involved for their unstinting kindness and support to patients and their families, and considers that the Scottish Executive should ensure that funding is made available for increasing awareness, research, providing treatment and support and all other aspects of breast cancer care.

Supported by: Mrs Margaret Ewing*, Tommy Sheridan*, Margaret Smith*

*S1M-3484 Mr Andrew Welsh: Angus Youth Congress—That the Parliament congratulates the Angus Youth Congress for its role in encouraging young people to become active citizens and, in so doing, play an active and constructive role in the democratic decision-making process; notes that a conference will take place on 11-13 October 2002 in which 70 delegates from Angus will meet in Dunoon to discuss the way forward for their organisation; looks forward to the outcome of the conference, and wishes the delegates every success in continuing to promote the voice of young people throughout the Angus Council area.

Supported by: Brian Adam*

*S1M-3483 Tommy Sheridan: Homeless Persons Unit, Glasgow City Council—That the Parliament supports the strike by 70 members of staff in the Homeless Persons Unit in the housing department of Glasgow City Council; appeals to the council to meet the union's demands; notes that the workers are taking indefinite strike action not on pay and conditions but in defence of the specialist service that they provide to the city's growing homeless population; is concerned by the council's policy of introducing generic workers in a whole series of departments, rather than using the specialist skills that staff have to the benefit of the public; recognises that the very specific skills required for working with homeless single persons, as distinct with homeless families, cannot be sustained under the council's restructuring, which indiscriminately groups all staff together in an unworkable fashion, and condemns the council's high-handed approach in advertising the workers' jobs in the press on the eve of the legally organised strike.

*S1M-3482 Elaine Smith: In-House Provision of Public Services—That the Parliament congratulates Lanarkshire Acute Hospitals NHS Trust on its decision to transfer the catering, portering and domestic staff at Monklands Hospital, formerly employed under Medirest’s facilities management contract, back into the employment of the NHS Trust on 6 October 2002, a decision which was taken with a view to achieving improved patient care and best value; further congratulates UNISON on its ‘Positively Public’ campaign, which includes support for the principle of having public services of the highest quality provided by an in-house team, and believes that the in-house model of public service provision should be extended right across Scotland.

*S1M-3481# Bill Butler: Timetable for the Glasgow Airport Rail Link—That the Parliament welcomes the response made by the Minister for Enterprise, Transport and Lifelong Learning in the debate on the future of air transport in Scotland to a question regarding the much-needed Glasgow Airport rail link; recognises that his reply, which talked of being able to come forward with "the preferred option…in the next few weeks" and a "starting date for design and construction" of "2005" [Official Report c 14500], marks real progress on this matter; notes that the Minister’s statement relates directly to an earlier members’ motion, S1M-3412, which urged the Minister to come forward "as a matter of urgency" with his "favoured option for this development and a clear timetable for its speedy implementation", and stresses the absolute support of Glasgow’s MSPs, Glasgow City Council, Strathclyde Passenger Transport and Glasgow Chamber of Commerce for the successful implementation of the "ambitious timetable" [Official Report c 14500] announced on 9 October 2002 by the minister.
