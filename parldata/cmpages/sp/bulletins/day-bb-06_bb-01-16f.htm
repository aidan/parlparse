Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday.  That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an “R”.

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm).  A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-3819 Richard Baker: Beating Bowel Cancer - 7th Loud Tie Campaign—That the Parliament notes that, despite increased efforts to raise awareness of bowel cancer, 3400 new cases are diagnosed every year in Scotland; believes that, because of the importance of comprehensive screening and early diagnosis, progress made though the new national screening programme will be crucial in reducing the number of deaths from bowel cancer; recognises that the screening programme, already piloted in Grampian, Tayside and Fife, will see over 650,000 potentially vulnerable people screened from 2009; supports further improvements in bowel cancer services, building on the increased patient involvement and public awareness being delivered though managed clinical networks; believes that more needs to be done to raise awareness of bowel cancer; welcomes and supports Beating Bowel Cancer’s seventh “Loud Tie Campaign”, and urges MSPs to wear a loud tie or scarf between 30 January and 5 February 2006 and help beat bowel cancer.

*S2M-3818 Tommy Sheridan: End Illegal Blockade of Cuba—That the Parliament notes the UN General Assembly vote of 182 to 4 to demand that the United States of America ends “as soon as possible” its 45-year-old economic and commercial embargo against Cuba taken last month; further notes that last month’s vote at the UN General Assembly was the 14th successive year that the vast majority of the world’s nations have voted to condemn and demand an end to the USA’s illegal economic and commercial blockade of the Socialist Republic of Cuba; regrets that the USA, Israel, Palau and the Marshall Islands voted against the resolution and Micronesia abstained but believes that these countries are completely out of step with the majority of the world who believe that the USA’s continued blockade is legally, morally, politically and ethically unacceptable, and resolves to write to the US Embassy in Edinburgh and in London to demand that the USA adhere to the overwhelming will of the United Nations and respects resolutions passed by the United Nations in the same way as it expects other countries to do.

*S2M-3817 Tommy Sheridan: Socialism Breeds Across Latin America—That the Parliament notes the overwhelming victory of Evo Morales, the Movement Towards Socialism (MAS) presidential candidate, in Bolivia last month and resolves to send a message of congratulations and solidarity to him and his new administration for their inauguration on 22 January 2006; notes that a centrepiece commitment of Evo Morales was to nationalise Bolivia’s vast natural gas holdings, the second largest in South America, and to reject the US-inspired neo-liberal economics of privatisation and inequality; welcomes the growing strength of the socialist movement across Latin and South America, indicated by the growing strength of Cuba, the rise of Hugo Chavez’s socialist Venezuela, the election of Evo Morales in Bolivia and the lead that left candidate, Ollanta Humala, currently commands in the run-up to Peru’s presidential elections in April, and recognises that new winds are blowing across Latin and South America and that these winds spell the end of imperialist neo-liberal subjugation of that sector of the world in favour of wealth redistribution, public ownership, national sovereignty and anti-poverty policies.

*S2M-3816 Tommy Sheridan: Boycott Coca-Cola—That the Parliament notes the growing campaign to boycott Coca-Cola based on serious claims of violence, including murder, against employees in bottling plants in Colombia and environmental destruction in villages surrounding its facilities in India; notes that universities like Oxford in England and New York and Michigan in the United States of America have voted to boycott Coca-Cola, and calls on the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body to organise a boycott of Coca-Cola within the Parliament in solidarity with trade unionists in Colombia and India.

*S2M-3815 Tommy Sheridan: Impeach the Prime Minister—That the Parliament notes the recent comments on BBC Radio 4 of General Sir Michael Rose, former Commander of Forces in Bosnia, in relation to Prime Minister Tony Blair’s responsibility for committing British troops to what many believe to have been the illegal invasion of Iraq that “certainly from a soldier’s perspective there can’t be any more serious decision taken by a Prime Minister than declaring war…and then to go to war on what turns out to be false grounds is something that no-one should be allowed to walk away from”; further notes General Rose’s specific statement in The Scotsman on 9 January 2006 that “the politicians should be held to account, and my own view is that Blair should be impeached. That would prevent politicians treating quite so carelessly the subject of taking a country into war”, and believes that General Sir Michael Rose is, on this occasion, absolutely right and that Tony Blair should be impeached for, as many believe, misleading Britain into an illegal war under cover of lies, distortions and deceit.

*S2M-3814 Mrs Margaret Ewing: Scottish Environment Week 2006—That the Parliament welcomes the forthcoming Scottish Environment Week which will take place from 6 to 12 February 2006; notes that the event will highlight the importance of our environment and its value to Scotland’s society and economy; further notes that as a nation Scotland has awoken to the importance of protecting our rich and diverse environment, and recognises the dedication of individual people, workplaces and communities in striving to safeguard our environment so that future generations can benefit from and enjoy it.

*S2M-3813 Ms Rosemary Byrne: MilkLink Job Losses—That the Parliament notes with regret the decision of Tesco to move UHT milk production abroad, jeopardising jobs at the Kirkcudbright factory; supports and applauds the hard work and dedication of workers and unions at MilkLink in fighting to save jobs; notes that the decision of Tesco is a direct result of the current free market economic policy in world food production, which is based on profit rather than promoting locally grown produce, and calls on the Scottish Executive to promote Scottish farming in an environmentally, economically and socially just manner to produce healthy Scottish produce and protect and promote jobs.

S2M-3812 Carolyn Leckie: Equal Treatment for Local Government Pension Scheme Members—That the Parliament is concerned that local government workers are faced with proposals to diminish their existing pension arrangements; believes that local government workers deserve at least equal treatment with other public sector workers in relation to pensions; is concerned that, despite the fact that the Scottish Executive has responsibility for the Scottish Public Pensions Agency, it has no direct input into the negotiations that will impact on Scottish local government pensions; is further concerned that the Scottish Public Pensions Agency has declared its intention to follow policy established in England and Wales, where the proposals for local government involve the removal of Rule 85, no protection for existing scheme members, a 1% increase in employee contributions and an increase in the age at which benefits can be taken from 50 to 55, and believes that the Executive should exercise its full powers and responsibilities in order to achieve a fair deal and equal treatment for local government pension scheme members and not leave their fate in the hands of negotiations being conducted in England.

S2M-3811 Dr Sylvia Jackson: Commonwealth Games 2014 in Stirling—That the Parliament congratulates the Scottish Executive on encouraging major world sporting events to come to Scotland; welcomes the benefits that will accrue to provincial towns and cities and all other areas of our country, and asks that Stirling is considered to provide hotel and training base facilities for the national sports teams competing in the Commonwealth Games 2014.

S2M-3810# Campbell Martin: North Ayrshire Schools PPP Project—That the Parliament notes that North Ayrshire Council, through a PPP project, intends to amalgamate St Andrew’s Academy in Saltcoats with St Michael’s Academy in Kilwinning and to build a “superschool” to be erected on Laighdykes playing fields in Saltcoats, the only playing fields serving the towns of Saltcoats and Ardrossan; is concerned that the council’s plans will impact adversely on the educational attainment of pupils and on the provision of open space and sports pitches in Saltcoats and Ardrossan; is further concerned that questions remain over aspects of the bidding and procurement processes adopted by North Ayrshire Council in its PPP for Schools Project, and considers that public private partnerships do not represent best value for local taxpayers and local communities.

Supported by: Margo MacDonald*, Alex Neil*, Frances Curran*, Carolyn Leckie*, Fiona Hyslop*

S2M-3809 Sarah Boyack: Scotland’s Ecological Footprint—That the Parliament acknowledges the value of the ecological footprint as a resource management tool that is easily understood and can help guide us to reduce our impact on the planet; notes that the size of Scotland’s ecological footprint currently represents Scotland as using and polluting much more than our fair share of the global environment and that, if everyone on the planet consumed and polluted at this rate, we would need three planets to sustain us; therefore welcomes the Scottish Executive’s commitment to reduce Scotland’s ecological footprint and to use the footprint as a key tool for communicating and delivering sustainable development; further welcomes the first ecological footprint exercise by the Parliament and commends Scotland’s Global Footprint Project, involving the local authorities in North Lanarkshire, Aberdeen City and Aberdeenshire, for leading the way in the United Kingdom by using the footprint to reduce their global environmental impacts; looks forward to all local authority area results being published in spring 2006, and encourages everyone to calculate their own footprint at www.scotlandsfootprint.org and set out to reduce it.

S2M-3808 Colin Fox: Abolition of NHS Prescription Charges (Scotland) Bill—That the Parliament agrees to the general principles of the Abolition of NHS Prescription Charges (Scotland) Bill.

S2M-3807 Pauline McNeill: Labour Students, “Sex, Lives and Politics” Campaign—That the Parliament welcomes the “Sex, Lives and Politics” campaign run by Scottish Labour Students and the Terrence Higgins Trust; welcomes the £15 million investment to improve equality of access to sexual health care in Scotland; further welcomes the creation of Scotland’s first-ever National Sexual Health Advisory Committee; calls on the Scottish Executive to extend chlamydia postal testing across Scotland, and further calls on the Executive to meet the NHS Quality Improvement Scotland target of achieving clinical standards for waiting times in cases of sexually transmitted infection.

Supported by: Mr Jamie Stone, Sarah Boyack, Nora Radcliffe, Eleanor Scott, Patrick Harvie, Dr Elaine Murray, Marlyn Glen, Robin Harper, Mr Frank McAveety, Marilyn Livingstone, Bill Butler, Trish Godman, Richard Baker, Jackie Baillie, Susan Deacon, Cathie Craigie*, Mr Kenneth Macintosh*

S2M-3805 Carolyn Leckie: Detention of Tehran Bus Workers Union Leader—That the Parliament notes with concern the continued detention of Mansour Osanloo, leader of the Tehran bus workers union, the Sherkate Vahed syndicate, following a strike on 25 December 2005 over wages and conditions, notes that the International Confederation of Free Trade Unions has written to Mr Mahmoud Ahmadjinejad, President of the Islamic Republic of Iran, expressing great concern about the health of Mansour Osanloo and his continued detention, and calls on the Iranian authorities to release him immediately.

Supported by: Frances Curran, Ms Sandra White, Rosie Kane, Colin Fox, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Tommy Sheridan

S2M-3804 Eleanor Scott: Coastal Flood Protection—That the Parliament notes the first anniversary of the deaths of five members of the MacPherson/Campbell family on South Uist on 11 January 2005 as a result of a severe storm and sends heartfelt sympathies to relatives and friends of the family; welcomes the funding package from the Scottish Executive which, according to Hugh Henry’s reply to question S2W-19378 on 6 October 2006, amounts to £9 million; welcomes the final agreement by Comhairle nan Eilean Siar to a study of the Stoneybridge shingle bank and other vulnerable coastal areas, with a view to giving recommendations as to an appropriate, costed engineered solution, eligible, hopefully, for grant aid under the Coastal Protection Act 1949; calls on all coastal local authorities to consider providing specific support for communities to develop and protect natural storm defences, and calls for the speedy installation of an early coastal flood-warning system to prevent further avoidable loss of life.

Supported by: Rosie Kane, Mark Ballard, Rob Gibson, Shiona Baird, Patrick Harvie, Mr Mark Ruskell, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Chris Ballance, Robin Harper

S2M-3800 Patrick Harvie: Congratulations to Project HAPPY—That the Parliament congratulates Project HAPPY (Help And Protect Prisoners’ Youngsters) for raising £11,000 to provide a free minibus transport service for prisoners’ children and families from Glasgow city centre to HM Prison Kilmarnock and back every Saturday and Sunday, 52 weeks of the year; commends the former children of prisoners for setting up the project to meet the needs of current prisoners’ youngsters; encourages families to use this service, and urges the Scottish Executive to pay attention to the results of this 12-month pilot project and to do more to support the children and families of prisoners by extending the service to HM Prisons Cornton Vale and Shotts.

Supported by: Ms Sandra White, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Chris Ballance, Shiona Baird, Frances Curran, Mark Ballard, Rosie Kane, Rob Gibson, Eleanor Scott, Robin Harper, Nora Radcliffe*, Fiona Hyslop*

S2M-3794 Donald Gorrie on behalf of the Procedures Committee: Private Bill Committee Assessors—That the Parliament notes the Procedures Committee’s 1st Report, 2006 (Session 2), Private Bill Committee assessors (SP Paper 481), and agrees that the changes to Standing Orders set out in Annexe A to the report be made with effect from 19 January 2006.

Supported by: Karen Gillon

S2M-3793 Mark Ballard: Support Town Centres—That the Parliament welcomes calls from the Forum of Private Business for shoppers to support their town centre shops and shun “parasitic” out-of-town shopping centres and retail parks; supports efforts to protect small and medium-sized retailers which are vital to ensuring the diversity and independence of the retail sector; notes that money spent in town centre shops is retained in the community, bringing growth and prosperity to the local area, whereas money spent in retail parks disappears out of the area, supporting only a limited number of low-paid jobs and leading to local economic decline; condemns the short-sighted policies that have led 20,000 high street shops to close in the United Kingdom since 1997 and laments the consequent emergence of “clone town Britain”, and calls on the Scottish Executive and local authorities to do everything in their powers to support locally owned businesses and ensure that they are able to compete with retail parks on a truly level playing field.

Supported by: Mr Mark Ruskell, Robin Harper, Patrick Harvie, Rob Gibson, Chris Ballance, Donald Gorrie, Eleanor Scott, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Shiona Baird

S2M-3791 Ms Sandra White: Congratulations to the Refugees into Teaching Project—That the Parliament notes the success of and congratulates the Refugees Into Teaching project based at the University of Strathclyde, which has just been awarded 5-star beacon status by the Home Office, the highest status that can be achieved, for its work with refugee and asylum seeker teachers; notes the comments of the award’s assessor that the partnership project, which is jointly funded by the University of Strathclyde, the University of Paisley, the University of Glasgow, Anniesland College, Glasgow City Council, the General Teaching Council for Scotland, the Bridges Project, the West of Scotland Wider Access Forum and the Scottish Refugee Council, “shows early indications that it will result in positive personal and professional outcomes for refugees”, and notes that this successful model of integration for refugees and asylum seekers is one which can be recommended to other professional groups.

Supported by: Michael Matheson, Fiona Hyslop

S2M-3790 Carolyn Leckie: Health Services in Lanarkshire: Make it a Genuine Choice—That the Parliament notes the launch of NHS Lanarkshire’s formal consultation, A Picture of Health; believes that, for such a consultation to be genuine and meaningful, all alternative proposals for services, including acute services, should be put before local communities and all background information, including financial information and research, should be placed in the public domain; notes that NHS Lanarkshire’s own option appraisal exercise cites the status quo of maintaining three general hospitals providing the full range of services as the second most popular option and the cheapest capital option; recognises the desire of communities to maintain the full range of services locally; expresses support for the communities served by Monklands and Hairmyres hospitals in their efforts to maintain all services in their areas, and believes that the will of communities to maintain and improve the full range of emergency, intensive and trauma care at all three general hospitals within NHS Lanarkshire should provide the impetus to the board and politicians to take the necessary action to ensure that a new improved status quo is a genuine potential outcome of the consultation.

Supported by: Frances Curran, Tommy Sheridan, Colin Fox, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Rosie Kane

S2M-3789 Shiona Baird: Project StarSight—That the Parliament commends the Dundee-based Abertay Centre for the Environment and its partner, Fife-based Compliance Technology, on their joint participation in the innovative project StarSight which will see the installation of solar-powered street lighting, incorporating wireless internet access, on the Abertay University campus in Dundee; notes the significant environmental benefits of these “smart” lights which operate by way of light-emitting diode technology developed in Singapore and which use a low amount of power derived from solar cells and recharged by daylight; further notes the additional benefits to communities of enjoying reliable, economic street lighting, coupled with easily accessible internet access; believes that potential worldwide sales related to the project StarSight pilot underline the importance of investment in the renewables industry; welcomes the showcasing of this innovative photovoltaic technology in Scotland, and wishes the project every success.

Supported by: Brian Adam, Mark Ballard, Patrick Harvie, Nora Radcliffe, Mr David Davidson, Mike Pringle, Donald Gorrie, Eleanor Scott, Mr Andrew Welsh, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Frances Curran, Kate Maclean, Chris Ballance

S2M-3788 Margaret Mitchell: Lanarkshire Health Accident and Emergency Consultation—That the Parliament  is disappointed at the decision taken by the board of NHS Lanarkshire to omit from A Picture of Health, its accident and emergency care public consultation document, the option to keep all three accident and emergency departments within Lanarkshire open; believes that it is essential that the consultation process should include the proper scrutiny and appraisal of the possibility of retaining all three accident and emergency departments; further believes that, if NHS Lanarkshire has a strong and overwhelming case for the retention of only two accident and emergency departments, then it should have no difficulty in convincing the public that this is the way forward, and therefore calls on the board to ensure that it is included as an option for the reconfiguration of services in Lanarkshire in order to give patients an influential voice in the future of the health service and their own care.

Supported by: Alex Johnstone, Mrs Nanette Milne, Murdo Fraser, Derek Brownlee, Mr Jamie McGrigor, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton, Phil Gallie, Carolyn Leckie, Mary Scanlon
