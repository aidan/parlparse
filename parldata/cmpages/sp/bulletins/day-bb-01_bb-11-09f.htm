Business Bulletin No. 162/2001

Friday 9 November 2001

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions.

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-2430 Tommy Sheridan: Funding of Further Education Colleges-That the Parliament notes the increasing number of industrial disputes affecting Scotland's further education colleges; believes that these disputes are overwhelmingly the result of a flawed funding system arising from colleges being removed from local authority control, thus compelling colleges to adopt aggressive cuts packages in order to satisfy their banks at the expense of proper further education provision; further believes that our further education colleges are tremendous assets to the local communities they serve and to Scotland as a whole, and, in recognition of their value to Scotland, resolves to investigate the re-incorporation of Scotland's colleges under local authority control with democratic and accountable management to ensure secure, long-term funding dictated by educational priorities and not commercial concerns.

*S1M-2429 Maureen Macmillan: Future of Air Links to Brussels-That the Parliament notes with concern the news that Sabena has filed for bankruptcy and the uncertainties over direct flights from Scotland to Brussels; recognises the importance of these links to Scotland's economic, cultural and political life, and urges the Scottish Executive to work with Her Majesty's Government to safeguard Scotland's links to the heart of Europe.

*S1M-2428# Mr Kenneth Macintosh: 2002, Autism Awareness Year-That the Parliament joins with the Scottish Society for Autism, the National Autistic Society, Autism Alliance Scotland, the Learning Disabilities Trust, the British Institute for Brain Injured Children and all other autism organisations across Scotland in declaring 2002 as Autism Awareness Year; recognises the everyday challenges faced by children and adults on the autistic spectrum, and their families, in gaining appropriate support from the statutory services, and further recognises and congratulates those voluntary organisations, including the East Renfrewshire Autism Support Group, which work tirelessly to support all of those affected by autistic spectrum disorders.

*S1M-2427# Marilyn Livingstone: Maggie's Cancer Centre Charity-That the Parliament recognises, welcomes and supports the Maggie's Cancer Centre charity, founded by Maggie Keswick Jencks in 1996, for its work in providing extra medical care, support and assistance to cancer sufferers, their families and friends and which seeks to ensure that cancer sufferers are as healthy in mind and body as possible, by enabling them to participate actively in the treatment of their disease and to share their experiences with other sufferers; considers that the Scottish Executive should actively promote the development of Maggie's Centres throughout Scotland, and wishes the campaign to develop a Maggie's Centre in Kirkcaldy every success.

S1M-2422 Scotland's Oil (lodged on 7 November 2001) Mr Kenneth Gibson*, Mr Kenny MacAskill*, Brian Adam*, Mr Duncan Hamilton*, Colin Campbell*, Ms Sandra White*, Bruce Crawford*

*S1M-2418# Irene Oldfather: Bicycle Helmets-That the Parliament notes that the majority of deaths in cycling accidents occur as a result of head injuries; recognises that wearing a correctly fitted helmet has been shown to reduce the risk of severe head injury by 63 to 88%; further recognises that cost is a major barrier, particularly for families on a low income, and therefore *believes that the Scottish Executive should* promote a campaign of responsible cycling, including dissemination of information on wearing helmets correctly and measures to reduce the cost of bicycle helmets to the public.
