BUSINESS BULLETIN 170 / 2000

Thursday 9 November 2000

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 16 November 2000

 

 

Question Time

1. Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether adequate information on public transport timetables was available during recent severe weather conditions and, if not, what plans it has to address this issue. (S1O-2542)

2. Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of total anti-drugs spending was spent on (a) education and (b) rehabilitation in 1997-98 to 1999-2000 and what the projected figures are for 2000-01. (S1O-2539)

3. Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive how the Republic of Ireland’s economic performance will influence its policies for the Scottish economy. (S1O-2537)

4. Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the drug beta interferon is subject to "postcode prescribing". (S1O-2538)

5. Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made of the economic costs of recent transport disruptions. (S1O-2552)

6. Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will publish the review of implementation of Higher Still and, if so, when. (S1O-2519)

7. Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive what up-to-date guidance has been given to local authorities with a view to ensuring that they put in place effective permanent flood prevention measures. (S1O-2515)

8. Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in teaching modern languages in primary schools. (S1O-2549)

9. Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met the Department of Social Security and what issues were discussed. (S1O-2516)

10. Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to encourage more young people to study science and technology subjects. (S1O-2544)

11. Mr Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to promote the welfare of children. (S1O-2550)

12. Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Minister for Education, Europe and External Affairs will consider visiting St Mary’s Episcopal Primary School in Dunblane to meet parents, pupils and teachers. (S1O-2521)

13. David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it has made in disposing of properties acquired by the Scottish Office during the construction of the M74. (S1O-2523)

14. Kay Ullrich: To ask the Scottish Executive why planning consent was refused for the retail development at Heathfield in Ayr while consent was granted for the associated construction of a football ground at the same site. (S1O-2536)

15. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what priorities it has set to improve standards in schools. (S1O-2531)

16. Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to review the pre-designation public consultation procedures by Scottish Natural Heritage in respect of Special Areas of Conservation and Sites of Special Scientific Interest. (S1O-2533)

17. Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive why the level of health spending in Scotland is rising less rapidly than in England. (S1O-2551)

18. Elaine Thomson: To ask the Scottish Executive in what ways it is listening to the concerns of the business community. (S1O-2545)

19. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will make an announcement on the future funding of current Rough Sleeper Initiative projects. (S1O-2524)

20. Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has reviewed the status of the merger between the Scottish Council for Educational Technology and the Scottish Consultative Council on the Curriculum. (S1O-2522)

21. Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many disabled people have been appointed to public bodies since March 2000. (S1O-2547)

22. Mrs Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to review the training given to nursery nurses. (S1O-2529)

23. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive what weight is given to the recommendations of planning inquiry reporters when making planning decisions and what costs were incurred by the Executive with regard to the recent Ayr United stadium and retail project applications. (S1O-2540)

24. Mr John Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider providing a fast track procedure in the free central heating programme for pensioners who have been diagnosed with terminal illness. (S1O-2520)

25. Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to meet its target of providing every school pupil with internet access and a personal email address. (S1O-2517)

26. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive how its equality strategy will assist its drive for social justice. (S1O-2543)

27. Elaine Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to support local partnerships which will tackle social exclusion. (S1O-2532)

28. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the impact of the Chancellor of the Exchequer’s pre-budget statement will be in Scotland. (S1O-2548)

29. Tricia Marwick: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it proposes to reconsider or review the Police (Scotland) Act 1967. (S1O-2512)

30. Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has made any representations to Her Majesty's Government regarding the potential impact on Scotland’s environment of any plans to make mixed oxide fuel for overseas customers at Sellafield. (S1O-2525)
