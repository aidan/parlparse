Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-2373 Trish Godman: Ferguson’s Shipyard, Port Glasgow—That the Parliament is of the view that Ferguson’s Shipyard, Port Glasgow, is one of the very best shipyards in Europe, is an important element in the local economy in an area of population decline and cannot be allowed to slip away and believes that it is of the utmost importance that the Scottish Executive does all in its power to ensure that both management and workforce are given a fair share of government contracts which, going by the yard’s excellent record, would be built on time, to cost and to the highest levels of design and construction.

*S2M-2372# Roseanna Cunningham: G8 Summit—That the Parliament recognises that the G8 Summit being held in Gleneagles, Perthshire, from 6 to 8 July 2005, offers an opportunity for Scotland to debate issues related to international aid, trade justice and debt relief; acknowledges that there will be inconvenience and disruption to many communities as a result of hosting this summit, and is of the view that such disruption can only be justified if the representatives of those countries with the greatest economic power arrive at decisions which will allow countries in the developing world to grow and prosper, at their own pace and in their own way, by the provision of appropriately-targeted aid, debt relief and a just framework of trade rules.

Supported by: Linda Fabiani*, Rob Gibson*

*S2M-2371 Ms Wendy Alexander: Firefighters’ Dispute at Glasgow Airport—That the Parliament notes with concern the firefighters’ dispute at Glasgow International Airport which has led to fire cover currently not being provided by the in-house team; regrets that the strikers have been taken to the Court of Session for engaging in peaceful picketing, and urges both sides to get back around the table and secure a resolution to this dispute that does not compromise public safety.

Supported by: Dr Elaine Murray*, Maureen Macmillan*, Bill Butler*, Elaine Smith*, Richard Baker*, Mr Frank McAveety*, Jackie Baillie*, Trish Godman*, Mr Duncan McNeil*, Mike Watson*

*S2M-2370 Rosie Kane: Alaska’s Arctic National Wildlife Refuge—That the Parliament recognises the international importance of all national parks; acknowledges that development in any national park can adversely affect the whole planet; agrees that the Sandford Principle should be the founding principle of all national parks and that they should be afforded maximum protection under the law, and calls on the Scottish Executive to urge the US House of Representatives Resources Committee not to vote to revive the energy bill that would allow oil drilling in Alaska’s Arctic National Wildlife Refuge.

*S2M-2369 Margaret Mitchell: Recovery, Reuse and Recycling of Mobile Phones and Printer Cartridges—That the Parliament commends the work of Eurosource, a Falkirk-based company, which has become an international leader in the recovery, reuse and recycling of mobile phones and printer cartridges; welcomes its innovative approach and individual customer-tailored collection programmes which have resulted in a significant reduction in environmental damage; applauds the company’s contributions of almost £1.5 million to charities, schools and scout groups since its inception five years ago; recognises the benefits of the company’s corporate social responsibility ethos and its work with schools which helps youngsters to learn about the environment, enterprise and citizenship, and considers that, within the Scottish Executive and Scottish local authorities, everything possible should be done to encourage the recovery, reuse and recycling of mobile phones and printer cartridges.

Supported by: Robert Brown*, Richard Lochhead*, Rob Gibson*, Michael Matheson*, Alex Johnstone*, Nora Radcliffe*, Rosie Kane*, Mr David Davidson*, Roseanna Cunningham*, Alex Neil*, Linda Fabiani*, Mrs Margaret Ewing*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Mrs Nanette Milne*, Eleanor Scott*, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton*, Shiona Baird*, Ms Sandra White*

*S2M-2368 Carolyn Leckie: Neo-Nazi at St Andrews—That the Parliament notes that it is barely one week from the commemoration of the 60th anniversary of the liberation of Auschwitz; believes that it is an insult to the memories of the millions who died in the Holocaust that Nick Griffin, a man proud to stand in the tradition of Hitler and the Nazis, has been invited to speak at St Andrews University debating society; condemns the unfeeling contempt for the victims of the Holocaust displayed by those who extended an invitation to this contemptible extremist, and considers that all MSPs should apply as much pressure as possible to halt this visit and obstruct it by all means possible.

*S2M-2367# Mrs Nanette Milne: Call for Action on the A90—That the Parliament notes with concern the number of road accidents on the A90 north of Aberdeen between Tipperty and Balmedie; further notes the significant number of new houses in and around the Ellon area which have added to the high volumes of traffic on this already congested road, and considers that the Scottish Executive should take action to upgrade this stretch of road as a matter of extreme urgency.

Supported by: Richard Lochhead*, Ms Sandra White*, Alex Johnstone*, David Mundell*, Mr David Davidson*, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton*, Margaret Mitchell*

*S2M-2366# Mark Ballard: Norwegian Smoking Ban Not Bad for Business—That the Parliament notes the forecast in Norway of an increase in the number of pub, bar and restaurant businesses that would face bankruptcies due to the introduction of a smoking ban in public places; notes that these fears have proved to be unfounded; notes that the number of pub, bar and restaurant businesses facing bankruptcy declined during the first seven months of the ban; believes that the introduction of a smoking ban in Scotland would not necessarily lead to increased bankruptcies in the bar, pub, hotel and restaurant industries, and congratulates Norway for achieving a reduction in adult smoking in every year since 1998.

*S2M-2263.2# Mr John Home Robertson: The Closure of Rural Primary Schools in Scotland—As an amendment to motion (S2M-2263) in the name of Alex Johnstone, leave out from "calls on" to end and insert "deplores the fact that the Conservative-dominated administration on the Scottish Borders Council is currently seeking to close several excellent village schools".
