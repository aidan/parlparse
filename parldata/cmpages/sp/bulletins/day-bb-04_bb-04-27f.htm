Business Bulletin No. 70 / 2004

Tuesday 27th April 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-1207 Dr Elaine Murray: Congratulations to the Solway Sharks-That the Parliament congratulates Dumfries and Galloway's ice hockey team, the Solway Sharks, on its victory in winning the 2004 Scottish Ice Hockey Association's Scottish Cup final.

*S2M-1206# Dr Elaine Murray: Harestanes Wind Farm Planning Application-That the Parliament notes with concern Scottish Power's planning application to erect 94 wind turbines, each 411 feet high, in the Forest of Ae in Dumfries and Galloway; further notes that this is one of several industrial-scale wind farm planning applications in the region, and believes that a public inquiry should be held in advance of the determination of this application by Scottish Ministers.

*S2M-1205 Carolyn Leckie: Identity Cards-That the Parliament is opposed to the introduction of identity cards as a clear attack on the civil liberties of our citizens; pledges that MSPs will not use identity cards in the event of them becoming compulsory, and calls on the people of Scotland to join in a campaign of defiance by refusing to use identity cards.

*S2M-1204 Mr David Davidson: Newton Dee Village Community-That the Parliament recognises the huge investment in, and the excellent and unique work accomplished at, the Newton Dee Village Community since 1940 and also at the 11 other centres of the Camphill Village Trust through the provision of fulfilling work opportunities and homes for adults with learning disabilities; commends the pioneering health care provision available to residents and the local community; recognises the impact this has on the development of wide-ranging interests and participation in cultural and social activities, and believes that everything possible should be done to allow the community to continue undisturbed and to develop over future years.

*S2M-1203 Christine Grahame: Foster Care Fortnight-That the Parliament welcomes the annual Foster Care Fortnight organised by the Fostering Network that is to take place between 10 and 24 May 2004; further welcomes the excellent work undertaken by the Fostering Network in supporting fostering within Scotland; notes with concern the shortfall of 650 foster carers across Scotland and the report Progress with Complexity by Angus Skinner, Chief Social Work Inspector, which gives a headline figure of 367 children and young people still awaiting a placement; notes also the figure of 20 to 50% of all children in foster care being required to move three or more times in inappropriate placements to the detriment of their educational and emotional well-being; further notes the calls for a uniform minimum allowance scheme across Scotland to provide significant help in boosting the recruitment and retention of foster carers, and calls on the Scottish Executive to ensure that local authorities are in a position to pay the Fostering Network's minimum recommended allowances, recognised by the Inland Revenue as the basis for new tax exemptions introduced in 2003, as the first step in introducing a nationwide "Payment for Skills" scheme.

*S2M-1202 Cathy Jamieson: Civil Partnership Bill - UK Legislation-That the Parliament endorses the principle of giving same sex couples in Scotland the opportunity to form a civil partnership and agrees that the provisions in the Civil Partnership Bill that relate to devolved matters should be based on Scots law and considered by the UK Parliament.

Supported by: Hugh Henry*

*S2M-1201# Linda Fabiani: Local Television-That the Parliament congratulates Thistle Television, the only local television station in Scotland, on commencing local television broadcasting throughout Lanarkshire with a potential audience of 400,000 local people; welcomes Thistle Television as a valuable addition to local media networks and creative employment, and looks forward to its contribution to local business, culture, education, and community involvement.

*S2M-1200 Pauline McNeill: 10th Anniversary of Democracy in South Africa-That the Parliament congratulates the South African people and government on the 10th anniversary of a democratic South Africa; notes that, despite the threats and predictions of some, South Africa's transition from apartheid to democracy was achieved with a minimum of bloodshed; believes that this owes much to the political maturity and leadership of the liberation movement and the strength, understanding and patience of the people; further believes that the rainbow nation has become a beacon against racism for the world; specifically congratulates the South African people and government on considerable achievements since the overthrow of apartheid, including (a) water - during 2004 the 10 millionth connection to clean water over the past decade will be made, (b) sanitation - in 1994 some 22 million people had no access to clean water, today it is down to 18 million, (c) housing - 1.6 million new houses built since 1994, (d) electricity - in 1994 some 60% of South Africa's population had no access to electricity, now more than 70% have access and (e) education - in 1994 a divisive education system provided secondary education for 70%, now an integrated system covers 85%; acknowledges the terrible legacy of colonialism and apartheid which remains a huge challenge; shares the grave concern over the HIV/AIDs pandemic and the huge cost in suffering to the people of South Africa; pledges continuing solidarity in the struggle to overcome the legacy of colonialism and apartheid, especially given Britain's responsibilities; congratulates ACTSA Scotland (Action for Southern Africa, the successor to the Anti-Apartheid Movement) for the part it played in the South African people's liberation from apartheid and its continuing role to develop links and facilitate solidarity with the people of South Africa; welcomes the joint activity by Scottish and South African organisations, including local authorities, trade unions, students and churches, to build mutually beneficial contacts and activities in various spheres, including those of economic, cultural, educational and sporting activity, and considers that MSPs, the Scottish Parliament and Scottish Ministers should assist in the facilitation of these links with the people and government of South Africa.

*S2M-1199 Pauline McNeill: Closure of the Queen Mother's Hospital-That the Parliament regrets Greater Glasgow NHS Board's decision of 20 April 2004 to close the Queen Mother's Hospital in Glasgow; notes that the Queen Mother's Hospital is an integral part of the Yorkhill campus model of care, deliberately co-locating specialist maternity and paediatric care and attracting world class medical and surgical expertise; further notes the proven record of excellence at Yorkhill in the development of ultrasound, innovative foetal therapy and developing a breast milk bank; believes that this service model has benefited women with difficulties in their pregnancies, sick babies and families throughout Scotland as well as providing an important general maternity service for the north west Glasgow corridor and that the hospital has been the hospital of choice for many more women in the west of Scotland for over 40 years; believes that the quality of specialist baby and maternal care services is threatened by this decision as reconfiguration may prove costly and difficult to implement; is concerned that the board has taken this decision in the face of overwhelming clinical, political and public opposition; believes that the decision was reached unfairly using inaccurate assumptions and that no other option was seriously considered, and considers that the Minister for Health and Community Care must (a) take steps to restore public confidence in consultation and decision-making processes in the NHS, (b) thoroughly review all of the factors that led to the Greater Glasgow NHS Board's decision on maternity services, (c) reject the decision to close the Queen Mother's hospital and (d) ensure that the integrated specialist care model is protected to continue to serve Scotland with the same quality of service and innovation that has been achieved over the last 40 years.

*S2M-1198 Scott Barrie: Queen Anne High School and Fife Miners' Banner-That the Parliament congratulates the students and staff on the Art and Design department of Queen Anne High School, Dunfermline, for their winning design for a new banner to commemorate the Fife Miners' Gala in recognition of Anne Picking MP's former role as the National President of UNISON in 1999-2000. *S2M-1197# Brian Adam: 1911 and 1921 Census Records-That the Parliament recognises that the Census records for 1841 to 1891 were released after less than 82 years of closure and commends this practice; regrets the introduction of a 100-year rule in 1974, and considers that the Scottish Executive should review the position for the 1911 and 1921 Census records to allow greater access for those who wish to pursue their family history.
