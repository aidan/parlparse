Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an “R”.

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

*S2M-3619 Christine Grahame: Scott’s Selkirk—That the Parliament recognises the benefits that Scott’s Selkirk contributes to the local economy, running as it does on Saturday and Sunday, 3 and 4 December 2005; notes that it commemorates the ride of Sir Walter Scott into Selkirk from Clovenfords, and then from Abbotsford, to dispense justice in the courthouse that still dominates Selkirk’s market place; further notes these words from Marmion “Heap on more wood! - the wind is chill; But let it whistle as it will, We’ll keep our Christmas merry still... While music, mirth, and social cheer speed on their wings the passing year.”; further notes that during the weekend there are living history performances including the re-enactment of Scott’s court cases, together with street theatre and stalls, and congratulates the Souters of Selkirk on celebrating this extraordinary Borderer with such panache.

Supported by: Bruce Crawford*

*S2M-3618 Mr Kenny MacAskill: FSB Report - The Future of the High Street—That the Parliament welcomes the Federation of Small Businesses’ (FSB) report, The Future of the High Street; acknowledges its 10 pragmatic recommendations to support small independent retailers, and believes that vibrant and healthy independent retailers are vital to the distinct character of Scotland’s towns and cities.

Supported by: Mike Pringle*, Tricia Marwick*, Jim Mather*, Nora Radcliffe*, Christine Grahame*, Fiona Hyslop*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Bruce Crawford*, Brian Adam*

*S2M-3616 Patrick Harvie: Ministerial Commitments on Asylum Protocol—That the Parliament notes the groundswell of opposition to current asylum removal practices being carried out by the Home Office in Scotland; reasserts its view, as expressed in motion S2M-3323 agreed by the Parliament on 22 September 2005, that an agreement is needed between the Home Office and the Scottish Executive which would protect the welfare of children facing removal; notes the commitment made in the Parliament on the same day by the First Minister to seek a “protocol” for this purpose and to report back to the Parliament on progress; notes the First Minister’s statement on 29 September 2005 that the Home Secretary “has agreed that the establishment of such an agreement in Scotland and, perhaps, elsewhere would be advisable”; notes the Deputy First Minister’s assurances to the Parliament on the 27 October 2005 that “the First Minister and the Home Secretary have reached an agreement in principle on the issue” and that the protocol would change removal practices rather than describe existing practice; notes with dismay that the Home Office has this week briefed journalists to the effect that no such protocol will be agreed with the Executive; considers that Scottish ministers have a responsibility to speak honestly in the Parliament and elsewhere, and considers that the contradiction between the ministers’ statements and the Home Office briefing raises the possibility that the First Minister may have misled the Parliament.

Supported by: Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Rob Gibson*
