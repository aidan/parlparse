Business Bulletin No. 151/2006: Monday 30 October 2006
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 27 October 2006

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-29320 Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is possible for an individual charged with a number of serious offences in separate incidents to be given bail in respect of each offence.

S2W-29321 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has made to HM Treasury to request its support for the European Parliament Committee on Economic and Monetary Affairs’ amendment to the proposed European Directive on payment services in the international market in order to exclude credit unions from its scope.

S2W-29322 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has asked HM Treasury to seek derogation from the proposed European Directive on payment services in the international market in order to exclude credit unions from its scope.

S2W-29323 Colin Fox: To ask the Scottish Executive when it now expects to publish its report on its review of NHS prescription charges and exemption arrangements in Scotland.

S2W-29324 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people in the Dumbarton parliamentary constituency are waiting for installation work under the central heating programme and the Warm Deal scheme.

S2W-29325 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive when it anticipates that installation work under the central heating programme and the Warm Deal scheme will be completed for those on the waiting list in the Dumbarton parliamentary constituency.

S2W-29326 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how much funding has been allocated to rebuilding and refurbishing schools in the (a) Argyll and Bute and (b) West Dunbartonshire local authority area in each year since 1999 and how the money has been used.

S2W-29327 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how much has been allocated under New Community School projects to (a) Argyll and Bute Council and (b) West Dunbartonshire Council in each year since 1999 and on what the money was spent.

S2W-29328 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive which projects were undertaken to improve higher and further education and how much money was allocated for higher or further education in the (a) Argyll and Bute and (b) West Dunbartonshire local authority area in each year since 1999.

S2W-29329 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average class sizes were in (a) primary and (b) secondary schools in the (i) Argyll and Bute and (i) West Dunbartonshire local authority area in 2005-06.

S2W-29330 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) teachers and (b) classroom assistants there have been in schools in the (i) Argyll and Bute and (ii) West Dunbartonshire local authority area in each year since 2003-04.

S2W-29331 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many school leavers continued their studies at higher or further education level in the (a) Argyll and Bute and (b) West Dunbartonshire local authority area in each year since 2003-04.

S2W-29332 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people from the (a) Argyll and Bute and (b) West Dunbartonshire local authority area were paid Education Maintenance Allowance in 2005-06.

S2W-29333 Frances Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are any proposals to limit to five the daily journeys which may be made by senior citizens under the concessionary travel scheme with effect from April 2007.

S2W-29335 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what input it has into road traffic regulations and laws; whether there is a standing committee on this issue on which it is represented, and what representations it has made, and on what subjects, about road traffic regulations and laws to Her Majesty’s Government since 1999.

S2W-29336 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what the estimated annual costs of Addiewell prison are from 2007 to 2017.

S2W-29337 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how much security at sheriff courts has cost in each year since 1999.

S2W-29338 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many police officers have been on duty at Scottish courts in each year since 1999.

S2W-29339 Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive what advice or guidance it has provided to NHS boards about supporting the transportation of patients who have been referred to remote centres for specialist consultations and thus have long journeys to attend hospital appointments.

S2W-29340 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how many applications have been received for the Rural Stewardship Scheme 2006, broken down by local authority area or nearest geographical equivalent and showing the number and percentage of successful applications in each case.

S2W-29341 Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-28233 by Tavish Scott on 21 September 2006, whether the discussions between Transport Scotland, Network Rail and Dumfries and Galloway Council have now concluded and when a decision is expected on further funding to enable disabled access to both platforms at Lockerbie Station.

S2W-29342 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on whether there are any proposals to transfer from the UK Government to the Executive further powers over the marine environment.

S2W-29343 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it provides to organisations offering unpaid work placements to graduates to enable them to gain the necessary experience to find full-time employment.

S2W-29344 Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the commercial sale of animals at pet fairs is banned under the Animal Health and Welfare (Scotland) Act 2006.

S2W-29345 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent research it has (a) commissioned and (b) evaluated into the effect of exposure to unsafe levels of pesticide residues in food.

S2W-29346 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what actions are being taken to address unsafe levels of pesticides in food.

S2W-29347 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is aware of the survey published by the Pesticide Action Network that found unsafe levels of pesticides in various foods and what plans it has to take action on the matter.

S2W-29348 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what the (a) incidence and (b) level has been of pesticide residue in food in each of the last five years.

S2W-29349 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the latest research published by the Pesticide Action Network in respect of pesticide residues in food.

S2W-29350 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) reported cases of and (b) successful prosecutions for breaches of maximum pesticide residue levels in food there have been in each of the last five years.

S2W-29351 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what redress is available to people who are wrongfully refused entry to the police force on medical grounds.

S2W-29352 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what legal force the police recruitment mandatory eyesight standards requirements, contained in an attachment to police circular 8/2003, have.

S2W-29353 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-21022 by Cathy Jamieson on 5 December 2005, what degree of discretion chief constables have in determining what parts of the guidance issued by ministers should be followed.

S2W-29354 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-21023 by Cathy Jamieson on 5 December 2005, whether the failure of a chief constable to follow this guidance would be a breach of the Disability Discrimination Act 1995.

S2W-29355 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to answer to question S2W-21023 by Cathy Jamieson on 5 December 2005, how it ensures that this guidance is complied with by police forces.
