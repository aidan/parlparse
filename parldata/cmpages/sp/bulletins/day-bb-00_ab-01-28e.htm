BUSINESS BULLETIN 16 / 2000

Friday 28 January 2000

Section E -   Written questions lodged on 27 January 2000

 

 

S1W-3998 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-1605 by Mr Iain Gray on 17 January 2000, what percentage of the £140 and £200 average weekly contributions to the cost of long term residential care or long term nursing care made by older people who receive contributions to the costs of their care only from the Department of Social Security was accounted for by (a) nursing care costs and (b) other care costs.

 

S1W-3999 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether extra funding in respect of DNA will be made available comparable to the additional funding in England.

 

S1W-4002 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive how many Business Steps there are and to list their location and the total annual cost of their operation.

 

S1W-4003 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what economic development services are provided by Business Steps; whether any analysis has been done or will be done as to whether the nature and range of services provided vary and whether it will place a copy of such analysis in the Scottish Parliament Information Centre.

 

S1W-4004 Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive how much money is involved in the double payment of subsidy to some 120 farming participants in agri-environmental schemes, which schemes are involved, and whether these participants will have future payments withheld if repayment is not sought.

 

S1W-4005 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how much it would cost to extend the fuel duty rebate to taxis.

 

S1W-4006 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether fuel duty rebate will be extended to mobile shops.

 

S1W-4007 Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive what assistance will be given to Glasgow City Council to assist it in dealing with repair and improvement grant work in the private housing sector.

 

S1W-4008 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive how it intends to develop proposals for legislation on the law of the foreshore and seabed.

 

S1W-4009 Mr Murray Tosh: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-2858 by Sarah Boyack on 12 January 2000, when and by whom the decision to abandon the Crossrail project was made; whether it made a unilateral decision to withdraw its support from Crossrail, and whether it has advised the promoters of Crossrail, including Strathclyde Passenger Transport Executive, of its decision.

 

S1W-4010 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to respond to the criticisms of the National Health Service Appeals System made in the Annual Report of the Scottish Committee of the Council on Tribunals.

 

S1W-4011 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to consult on any proposals to amend the National Health Service Appeals System and, if so, when such consultation is expected to take place.

 

S1W-4012 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any consultation on proposals to amend the National Health Service Appeals System will include the Health and Community Care Committee and the Scottish Committee of the Council on Tribunals.
