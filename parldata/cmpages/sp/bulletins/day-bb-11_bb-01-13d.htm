Business Bulletin No. 4/2011: Thursday 13 January 2011
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D – Oral questions selected for answer on 20 January 2011

Question Time

General

1. John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is managing the supplies of salt and grit. (S3O-12703)

2. Derek Brownlee: To ask the Scottish Executive how frequently the forecast income from devolved taxes and charges is monitored and how accurate published forecasts have been. (S3O-12695)

3. Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking in response to the annual report of the Chief Medical Officer for Scotland 2009, in particular regarding the promotion of a salutogenic asset-based approach to future health creation. (S3O-12692)

4. Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive how it supports small and medium-sized enterprises. (S3O-12674)

5. Peter Peacock: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking and what progress is being made in examining the UK Government’s proposals for the reform of UK consumer and protection bodies; what devolved implications there are for Scotland and its consumers, and when it will report its findings on the proposals. (S3O-12661)

6. Joe FitzPatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to make changes to free personal care. (S3O-12675)

7. Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions are being held with carers and carers organisations to ensure their involvement in the planning and delivery of the Change Fund in the NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde area. (S3O-12652)

8. Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met the COSLA leadership. (S3O-12654)

9. Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met Supporters Direct. (S3O-12634)

10. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will support the creation of a not-for-profit bidder for the ScotRail franchise and what action it has taken to explore the practicality of this. (S3O-12626)

Rural Affairs and the Environment

1. Alasdair Allan: To ask the Scottish Executive what infraction proceedings are associated with failure to meet European Commission policies regarding the designation of marine special areas of conservation. (S3O-12670)

2. Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will publish a timetable for the implementation of the actions for 2011 laid out in its publication, Low Carbon Scotland: Public Engagement Strategy. (S3O-12662)

3. Maureen Watt: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that a successful farmed salmon industry is an important part of the economy. (S3O-12690)

4. Tom McCabe: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it can take to ensure the elimination of the noxious odours that emit from the sewerage treatment works at Bothwell Road in Hamilton. (S3O-12650)

5. Willie Coffey: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it has made in encouraging large food retailers to offer outlets in their stores to local food producers. (S3O-12671)

6. Nicol Stephen: To ask the Scottish Executive what advice has it issued to residents whose household waste and recycling was not collected and whose recycling centres were overflowing during the recent cold weather conditions. (S3O-12717)

7. George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what consultation it has had with the UK Government regarding the provisions for forestry in the Public Bodies (Reform) Bill. (S3O-12639)

8. Mr Frank McAveety: To ask the Scottish Executive what the Minister for Environment is doing to ensure that the carbon emissions impact of the 2014 Commonwealth Games is minimised. (S3O-12649)

9. Elizabeth Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it provided to farmers during the recent severe weather. (S3O-12696)

10. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive what target has been set to continue to reduce plastic bag use, given that the Welsh Assembly Government is planning to introduce a levy on all disposable plastic bags. (S3O-12712)

Justice and Law Officers

1. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what action the police can take against those responsible for oil or diesel spills on roads. (S3O-12669)

2. Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prosecutions there have been in Scotland under the provisions of the Corporate Manslaughter and Corporate Homicide Act 2007. (S3O-12640)

3. James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment has been made of the impact on public safety of the implementation of a presumption against custodial sentences of three months or less. (S3O-12645)

4. Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has for future provision of police and fire services in Angus. (S3O-12691)

5. Andy Kerr: To ask the Scottish Executive how many knives and other weapons have been seized by the Scottish Court Service since 2007. (S3O-12646)

6. Ian McKee: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions the Cabinet Secretary for Justice has had with the UK Secretary of State for Justice regarding the value of short prison services. (S3O-12685)

7. Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to roll out the domestic abuse court to other parts of Scotland. (S3O-12643)

8. Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many parenting orders have been issued in the Linlithgow constituency. (S3O-12657)

9. Anne McLaughlin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will ban the use of Mosquito ultrasonic youth deterrents. (S3O-12684)

10. Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to increase public confidence in community sentences. (S3O-12704)
