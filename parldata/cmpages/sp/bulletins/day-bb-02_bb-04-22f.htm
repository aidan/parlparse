Business Bulletin No. 63/2002

Monday 22 April 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-3011 Mr Adam Ingram: Schizophrenia-That the Parliament notes the report by the Clinical Standards Board for Scotland (CSBS) on schizophrenia which shows that there is a postcode lottery in the provision of mental health services; further notes the comments made by Lord Patel, chairman of the CSBS at the launch of the report on schizophrenia that "Mental health has become a bit of a Cinderella", and calls on the Scottish Executive to set up a mental health task force to ensure that mental health is regarded as a national clinical priority by health boards in the same way as it is regarded as one of only three national clinical priorities by the Executive.

S1M-3010 Ms Margo MacDonald: Statement on Holyrood Project-That the Parliament requests that the Presiding Officer, as Chair of the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body, make an accurate statement to the Parliament on the expected completion date, entry date, estimated cost of the new Parliament building and costs associated with any slippage in the timetable for completion and entry previously intimated to the Parliament.

Supported by: Mr Lloyd Quinan, Alex Neil

S1M-3009# Helen Eadie: Dunfermline Press and West Fife Advertiser and Scottish Local Newspaper of the Year Award 2002-That the Parliament congratulates the Dunfermline Press and West Fife Advertiser on winning the Scottish Local Newspaper of the Year Award 2002; recognises the massive contribution that the paper makes to the life of the community in west Fife, and acknowledges the vital role of local newspapers throughout Scotland in providing news and a voice that is sensitive to local concerns.

S1M-3008 Scott Barrie: Dunfermline Press and West Fife Advertiser-That the Parliament congratulates the Dunfermline Press and West Fife Advertiser on winning the Scottish Local Newspaper of the Year Award 2002; believes that the newspaper has played a valuable role in Dunfermline and west Fife over the past year; looks forward to another year of excellent service, and hopes that it will continue to be Fife's best selling weekly for many more years to come.

S1M-3005 Michael Russell: Queen of the South Football Club Promotion to Division One-That the Parliament congratulates Queen of the South Football Club, its players, management, board of directors and loyal fans, for their success in securing promotion to the Scottish First Division, a feat which has escaped them for the last 16 years; is certain that this achievement will help to secure the club's future; looks forward to further success for the club, and commends the contribution made by the manager, John Connolly, who has in two years transformed a football club in financial difficulty and facing relegation into one which is now on the way up and which also has continued ambition to go on climbing.

S1M-3002# Alasdair Morgan: Current Situation in the Middle East-That the Parliament expresses its deep concern about the situation in the Middle East; recognises the key role of the United Nations and the rule of international law, and affirms that the immediate cessation of violence, the recognition and protection of human rights and the need for negotiation are essential in order to secure just and long lasting peace in the region.

Supported by: Richard Lochhead, Mr Kenny MacAskill, Christine Grahame, Mr Andrew Welsh, Michael Matheson, Michael Russell, Stewart Stevenson, Shona Robison, Bruce Crawford, Fiona McLeod*, Linda Fabiani*

S1M-3001 Fiona Hyslop: Middle East-That the Parliament condemns all acts of violence in the Middle East, whether they be acts of state violence from Israel or terrorist attacks from Palestine factions; calls for the immediate cessation of all acts of violence; supports the codification of the Saudi initiative as a UN Security Council resolution that would involve Israeli troops withdrawing to pre-1967 boundaries, acceptance of the legitimacy of the Israeli state by the Arab world and the establishment of a viable, self-governing Palestine state; recognises that the UK should freeze all military exports to Israel until it agrees to abide by UN Security Council resolutions; calls for wider economic sanctions to be imposed by the US and EU, preferably under the terms of a UN resolution, and further recognises that the Prime Minister should resolve his contradictory stance by accepting that a new UN Security Council resolution is necessary before embarking on any military action in Iraq.

Supported by: Christine Grahame, Richard Lochhead, Michael Matheson, Michael Russell, Shona Robison, Bruce Crawford, Fiona McLeod*, Linda Fabiani*

S1M-3000 Mr Kenneth Gibson: Congratulations to Colin MacIver Campbell MSP-That the Parliament congratulates Colin MacIver Campbell MSP on the birth of Ruairidh Alasdair MacLean Campbell, 7lb 13 oz, his eighth grandchild and fifth grandson, born on 16 April 2002 at 19.45 at Paisley Maternity Hospital to his third son Laurie and daughter-in-law Allison, their second child and first son and is pleased that mother and baby are doing fine.

S1M-2989 Mr Gil Paterson: Domestic Abuse Courts-That the Parliament welcomes the call by Lord Carloway for the setting up of specialist courts to deal with domestic abuse; notes the effectiveness of this system in dealing with the root causes as well as the symptoms of domestic abuse along with programmes to tackle abusive behaviour as demonstrated in Canada; further notes the cluster courts set up in Leeds which have helped to speed up the court process for cases involving domestic abuse and have made those abused more willing to come forward due to the support they receive and the clear message that is given that abuse is not acceptable, and calls on the Scottish Executive to make the changes necessary to set up these specialist courts in Scotland which will be able to deal with both criminal and civil matters relating to the family.

Supported by: Mr Lloyd Quinan, Mr Adam Ingram, Richard Lochhead, Stewart Stevenson, Colin Campbell, Mr George Reid, Dorothy-Grace Elder, Roseanna Cunningham, Ms Sandra White, Michael Matheson, Brian Adam, Mr Kenny MacAskill, Christine Grahame, Tommy Sheridan*

S1M-2987# Pauline McNeill: International Workers' Memorial Day 28 April 2002-That the Parliament supports International Workers' Memorial Day on 28 April 2002, a date important to trade unionists the world over; notes that, every year, over 1.3 million workers across the world die from accidents and diseases at work, nearly twice the number of deaths caused by war; further notes that many such deaths could be prevented by better health and safety management; welcomes the theme of occupational health in this year's activities, and believes that Members should actively support this important day by wearing the purple ribbon.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson, Michael Matheson, Ms Margo MacDonald, Dorothy-Grace Elder, Christine Grahame, Tommy Sheridan*

S1M-2986# Alex Neil: STUC Emergency Motion on Prison Privatisation-That the Parliament supports the Scottish Trade Union Congress's emergency motion on prison privatisation which backs the findings contained in Privatised Prisons and Detention Centres in Scotland: An Independent Report by Phil Taylor and Christine Cooper; believes that the Scottish Executive should consider seriously how this report impacts on the Scottish Prison Service estates review; agrees that it is hard to believe that three new public prisons would cost £700 million more to build and run than three new private prisons, and calls for a full public debate on the philosophy and financial implications of prison privatisation.

Supported by: Ms Margo MacDonald, Mr Lloyd Quinan, Mr Adam Ingram, Mr Gil Paterson, Stewart Stevenson, Michael Matheson, Ms Sandra White, Dorothy-Grace Elder, Christine Grahame, Tommy Sheridan*

S1M-2985 David Mundell: Dumfries and Galloway Managed Clinical Network for Heart Disease-That the Parliament recognises that the Dumfries and Galloway Managed Clinical Network for Heart Disease was the first of its kind in the United Kingdom and is a way of focusing and providing care across the boundaries between general practice and hospital and between different health professions with a focus on patients and their needs; notes that, although the network has only been in existence for nine months, it is having a tremendous impact on the quality of care provided, with patients, doctors and nurses involved actively in decision-making about the future of service and on the use of scarce resources; further notes that the pre-hospital thrombolysis service in the area was also the first of its kind in the United Kingdom and will allow life-saving clot-dissolving drugs to be given to patients suffering from a heart attack before they get to hospital, and congratulates all those who have been involved in Dumfries and Galloway in bringing about these cutting edge developments in health services provision.

Supported by: Phil Gallie, Alex Johnstone, Mr Murray Tosh, Alasdair Morgan, John Scott, Alex Fergusson, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton, Mrs Lyndsay McIntosh, Mr Keith Harding, Murdo Fraser, Dorothy-Grace Elder, Christine Grahame, Ian Jenkins*
