BUSINESS BULLETIN 44 / 2000

Thursday 9 March 2000

Section D –  Oral questions selected for answer on 16 March 2000

 

  

1. Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has had from local authorities regarding this year’s local government financial settlement. (S1O-1380)

 

2. Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to invest in research and development for organic agriculture. (S1O-1371)

 

3. George Lyon: To ask the Scottish Executive what Scottish Homes’ spending plans for rural areas are for the coming year. (S1O-1376)

 

4. Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive whether Danish pig meat, fed with meat and bone meal, meets the same safety standards as that produced by the Scottish industry. (S1O-1383)

 

5. Nicola Sturgeon: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met officials from the Scottish Qualifications Authority and what issues were discussed. (S1O-1362)

 

6. Ben Wallace: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects the plans for the new round of European Structural Funds (2000-06) to be approved by the European Commission. (S1O-1366)

 

7. Bristow Muldoon: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to promote enterprise and encourage innovation in the business and academic communities. (S1O-1393)

 

8. Janis Hughes: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in modernising the infrastructure of the NHS in Scotland. (S1O-1384)

 

9. David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether properly maintained roads are essential to economic development in rural areas; whether there is any point at which it will deem the level and quality of road maintenance provided by local authorities in the South of Scotland to be unacceptable for economic development purposes, and, if so, what steps it would take to deal with such roads. (S1O-1385)

 

10. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent consultations it has had about the statutory right of access to the countryside, including access to water as well as to land. (S1O-1354)

 

11. Ms Margaret Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive what has been the progress to date of the New Futures Fund and whether it has assisted the most excluded people in society. (S1O-1400)

 

12. Dorothy-Grace Elder: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with environmental bodies and the local community of Cape Wrath over the impact of the bombing of the coastal area by the navies of the United States, the Netherlands and Spain and what assessment it has carried out of any environmental damage caused. (S1O-1388)

 

13. Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive under what circumstances it would seek additional resources for the Scottish Consolidated Fund from Her Majesty's Government. (S1O-1367)

 

14. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many state school playing fields have been sold for private development in the period 1990-97 and since 1997. (S1O-1396)

 

15. Mrs Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what will be the remit of the Prevention and Effectiveness Unit to be established within the Public Health Policy Unit. (S1O-1397)

 

16. Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has received regarding its proposals to alter the structure and operation of the General Teaching Council. (S1O-1369)

 

17. Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact increased access to credit unions will have on communities which currently have no access to low cost financial services. (S1O-1365)

 

18. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are being taken to alleviate long term unemployment. (S1O-1390)

 

19. Ian Jenkins: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it will make to the Department of Culture, Media and Sport regarding the National Lottery licence renewal and any effect this may have on the allocation and distribution of funding to Scottish charities. (S1O-1391)

 

20. Mr Keith Raffan: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects the joint working group established with the Convention of Scottish Local Authorities to look at issues such as three-year planning, hypothecation, salaries and guidelines to report, whether the report will be published and whether it will be available in time to affect the way next year’s local government financial settlement is decided. (S1O-1375)

 

21. Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it intends to take to address the increase in excess winter deaths. (S1O-1370)

 

22. Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in the Joint Action Committee on child poverty. (S1O-1358)

 

23. Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what is being done to reduce any stigma associated with mental health. (S1O-1398)

 

24. Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive how much land and property Scottish Enterprise and the local enterprise companies have sold or disposed of since May 1997; what was the total monetary worth of the sales or disposals, and which government departments have been in receipt of the revenues raised from the sales or disposals. (S1O-1374)

 

25. Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will confirm that it will provide no special deficit funding for Scottish Opera for the financial year 2000-01. (S1O-1364)

 

26. Mr John Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will ensure that local government funding will increase over the next few years and what representations it has made to Her Majesty’s Government about increasing the block grant so that a larger amount of money can be available in future local government settlements. (S1O-1402)

 

27. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to promote community development in coalfield communities. (S1O-1363)

 

28. Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will achieve its targets to speed treatment and shorten waiting times in the NHS. (S1O-1401)

 

29. Ms Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive when it was made aware of the current state of repair of the roof at the Royal Hospital for Sick Children, Edinburgh. (S1O-1359)

 

30. Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people died because of the incidence of flu this winter. (S1O-1386)
