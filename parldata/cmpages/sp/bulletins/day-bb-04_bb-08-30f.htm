Business Bulletin No. 122/2004

Monday 30 August 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-1627 Ms Sandra White: Yorkhill Hospital Football Team—That the Parliament sends a message of support to the Yorkhill Hospital staff football team as it heads off to represent Scotland in the Eurospital 2004 Tournament in San Marino this September; notes that the team is made up of dedicated staff members from the Royal Hospital for Sick Children at Yorkhill in Glasgow and will be playing against teams from hospitals representing other nations across Europe in the spirit of international friendship, and wishes the team every success.

*S2M-1626# Brian Adam: Proposed National Front March in Aberdeen—That the Parliament expresses great sympathy for the councillors in Aberdeen who have to determine the application for a National Front march in Aberdeen; notes the advice given by the council’s legal department that councillors voting for a ban on the march may expose themselves to disqualification of up to five years, a draconian punishment; further notes the considerable potential for public disorder as evidenced by similar events elsewhere, and believes that the Scottish Executive should take all steps, including legislation, to ensure that local councillors representing local opinion can make decisions on local issues without fear of oppressive punishment.
