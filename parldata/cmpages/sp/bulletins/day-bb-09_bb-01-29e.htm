Business Bulletin No. 19/2009: Thursday 29 January 2009
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 28 January 2009

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-20220 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the 22% efficiency savings required by the Office of Rail Regulation and the announcement by Network Rail to defer 28% of planned track renewals across the United Kingdom will have an impact on track renewals, inspection and maintenance programmes in Scotland.

S3W-20221 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the redundancies announced by rail company DB Schenker, previously the English Welsh and Scottish Railway Ltd, will have an impact on employment in the rail industry and freight services in Scotland.

S3W-20222 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of recent announcements by rail companies, Network Rail and the Office of Rail Regulation, what steps it is taking to protect train lines and services.

S3W-20223 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what assistance it will make available to Scottish aid organisations that are not members of the Disasters Emergency Committee (DEC) and wish to assist in the humanitarian effort in Gaza.

S3W-20224 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what mechanisms are in place to enable NHS Scotland to accept medical evacuations from Gaza.

S3W-20225 Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive what the effect will be of the withdrawal of direct payments in the Orkney Islands council area.

S3W-20226 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to ensure that no data remains on IT equipment that has been used in the NHS and that such equipment is resold or deployed across other public sector bodies.

S3W-20227 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what parliamentary scrutiny was undertaken prior to implementation of the new grading system for nursery schools.

S3W-20228 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people receive direct payments for their care in each local authority area.

S3W-20229 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what consultation took place with parents prior to defining the categories of care by nursery providers.

S3W-20230 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive, following the new rating categories due to be implemented in April 2009, what level of care, development and education will be provided to children aged three and four.

S3W-20231 Stuart McMillan: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-16179 by Stewart Stevenson on 24 September 2008, what the outcome was of the assessment into the effectiveness of the speed camera system on the A77.

S3W-20232 Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people have been fined for littering offences in each of the last five years for which information is available, broken down by local authority.

S3W-20233 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-18831 by Stewart Stevenson on 8 January 2009, whether it will be re-advertising for new members of the Mobility and Access Committee for Scotland in order to make the further appointments expected.

S3W-20234 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-18835 by Stewart Stevenson on 8 January 2009, how much funding the Mobility and Access Committee for Scotland (MACS) will receive from the 2008-09 budget and how this compares with the funding that MACS has received in the past.

S3W-20235 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what costs were associated with the production of the A77 Maybole Transport Study - Design Manual for Roads and Bridges - Stage 2 Report.

S3W-20236 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-16656 by Stewart Stevenson on 27 October 2008, whether further engineering works are planned to improve the safety of the A77 in south Ayrshire.

S3W-20237 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are plans for engineering works on the A77 south of Ayr.

S3W-20238 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will extend the use of average speed cameras on the A77 to include the sections south of Girvan not currently covered.

S3W-20239 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on the commitment to deliver an additional 10,000 respite weeks for carers by 2010-11.

S3W-20240 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-16718 by Shona Robison on 28 October 2008, whether it will take steps to ensure that information on the number of young carers waiting for support services in each local authority area is available centrally.

S3W-20241 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-13230 by Rhona Brankin on 3 June 2008, what action it has taken to ensure that school pupils receive two hours of physical education each week.

S3W-20242 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many Life Begins health checks for people over 40 have been performed in 2008-09, broken down by NHS board.

S3W-20243 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many physiotherapists are employed in the NHS and how many were employed in May 2000 and May 2007.

S3W-20244 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any funding was ring-fenced for the development of mental health and wellbeing services in 2008-09, broken down by NHS board.

S3W-20245 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive which community-based alcohol rehabilitation services and programmes receive support.

S3W-20246 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive which community-based programmes to combat underage drinking it supports.

S3W-20247 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how much alcohol-related illness cost the NHS in 2008.

S3W-20248 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it supports the Community Alcohol Partnership project in north Glasgow.

S3W-20249 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will support the introduction of community alcohol partnerships of the kind established in areas like St Neots, Cambridgeshire.

S3W-20250 John Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive how much Housing Association Grant funding was allocated per registered social landlord in North Lanarkshire and South Lanarkshire council areas in 2007-08 and 2008-09.

S3W-20251 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to investigate the disposals that have the most successful outcomes in preventing reoffending by young people charged with criminal offences.

S3W-20252 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria it has adopted in deciding at what level to set the age of criminal responsibility.

S3W-20253 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive which criminal charges against young people under-16 are regarded as appropriate for prosecution rather than reference to the children’s hearing system and for what reasons.

S3W-20254 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Crown Office has issued guidance as to the circumstances in which young people under-16 charged with criminal offences should be prosecuted rather than referred to the children’s hearing system.

S3W-20255 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many students receive the full Young Students’ Bursary, broken down by assessed income.

S3W-20256 Angela Constance: To ask the Scottish Executive how many compulsory purchase orders of domestic residencies there were in each year since 1999.

S3W-20257 Angela Constance: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average compensation was for domestic properties obtained via compulsory purchase order in each year since 1999.

S3W-20258 Angela Constance: To ask the Scottish Executive what proportion of domestic houses subject to compulsory purchase order was bought at market value in each year since 1999.

S3W-20259 Angela Constance: To ask the Scottish Executive how many houses have been built using siporex, also broken down by local authority area.

S3W-20260 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Scottish national entitlement card will be compulsory for individuals seeking access to local leisure services.

S3W-20261 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what alternatives are available to individuals who would like to access leisure services, including swimming pools, community centres and libraries, but do not wish to obtain a Scottish national entitlement card.

S3W-20262 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what precautionary measures have been put in place to safeguard the privacy of the data gathered through the Scottish national entitlement card.
