Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-3861 Alex Neil: Scotland’s Renewable Energy Opportunity—That the Parliament welcomes the findings of new research by Dr Shimon Awerbuch of the University of Sussex which shows that increasing the proportion of Scotland’s electricity generated from wind would mean an overall reduction in Scotland’s future electricity costs; notes that such an increase in wind electricity generation will not only reduce overall costs but will improve the security of Scotland’s electricity supply, and calls on the Scottish Executive to consider revising upwards its current targets for renewable energy to take advantage of the findings of this research.

*S2M-3860 Christine Grahame: Keeping Children Safe—That the Parliament, while recognising the importance of the sex offenders’ register and the Disqualified from Working with Children List, accepts that most abused children are abused by someone from within their immediate circle and not by strangers; believes that an approach to child protection that relies on procedures, lists and rigid rules will not keep many of our nation’s children safe but that society at large has a responsibility and a duty to protect the nation’s children; further believes that the criteria and the process for inclusion on the sex offenders’ register should be reconsidered, noting that Children 1st specifically recommends that not all people on this register should automatically be disqualified from working with children and that the criteria for so banning someone should be in the public domain to increase confidence in the child protection system, and therefore urges the Scottish Executive to review the operation of both registers, while being mindful that it is not stranger danger but familiar faces which pose most risk to children, and to instigate a public awareness campaign to this end.

*S2M-3859 Carolyn Leckie: Scottish Prison Service’s Telephone System—That the Parliament is extremely concerned that prisoners in Scotland, who receive extremely limited income, pay inflated rates for telephone calls in comparison with prisoners in England; notes that the rate is 11p per minute in Scotland and 6.5p per minute in England; recognises that maintaining relationships is key to increasing chances of rehabilitation; finds it abhorrent that Siemens is making excessive profits from a literally captive market, and believes that this inequality and exploitation should stop and that the Scottish Executive should seek to eradicate the inequity and unfairness of the Scottish Prison Service’s telephone systems.

Supported by: Frances Curran*, Patrick Harvie*, Robin Harper*

*S2M-3858 Colin Fox: Support for Sacked Worker—That the Parliament believes that the chain store firm, Dunnes Stores, which has outlets throughout Scotland, has achieved a new low in European industrial relations by sacking shop steward, Joanne Delaney; notes that 22-year-old Ms Delaney was elected shop steward by her fellow members in the Ashleaf store in Crumlin, Dublin, after four years’ service with the firm, but was sacked shortly afterwards for wearing her Mandate trade union badge on her store uniform; further notes that Dunnes then compounded its anti-trade union stance by refusing to attend a meeting about the disciplinary action with Ms Delaney because she was accompanied by her trade union official; utterly condemns Dunnes’ behaviour towards Ms Delaney, believing that it directly contravenes her basic human rights of freedom of association and belief, and urges the firm to reinstate her forthwith.

*S2M-3857 Christine Grahame: Rendition Flights to Wick and Inverness Airports?—That the Parliament notes that the disclosure of documents, obtained by Amnesty International from the US Federal Aviation Administration and separately by the Danish Government, has revealed inter alia that seven alleged rendition flights operated by the CIA have been made through two Highlands airports, two at Wick and five at Inverness; further notes that following these reports the Danish Government announced on 22 August 2005 that the CIA could no longer use Danish airspace; recognises that both Wick and Inverness airports are operated by Highlands and Islands Airports Limited (HIAL), a company wholly owned by the Scottish Executive with the Scottish Ministers the sole shareholder, represented by the Minister for Transport, at one time the current Deputy First Minister, Nicol Stephen, and currently Tavish Scott MSP, and, given that ministerial relationship, calls on the Scottish Ministers to use their powers to instigate a full and thorough investigation to determine what associated documentation HIAL may, or may not, have in respect of these alleged flights.
