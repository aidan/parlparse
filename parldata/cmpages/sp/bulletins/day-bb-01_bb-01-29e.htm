BUSINESS BULLETIN 17 / 2001

Monday 29 January 2001

Section E - Written questions lodged on 26 January 2000

 

 

S1W-12848 Elaine Thomson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to address any future skills shortages and labour market issues.

S1W-12849 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it now has to support and promote credit unions.

S1W-12850 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what public money has been expended on the City of Edinburgh Rapid Transit project in addition to the transport challenge fund project allocations in 1997-98, 1998-99 and 1999-2000 and whether it will provide a detailed breakdown of these costs.

S1W-12851 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide a breakdown of the estimated costs to it in respect of planning applications, appeals and inquiries in relation to the City of Edinburgh Rapid Transit project, taking into account both costs directly incurred and the use of staff resources.

S1W-12852 Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive when the central heating initiative working group last met, when the group is next due to meet and what progress has been made to date.

S1W-12853 Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive which local authorities will be able to access funds from the central heating initiative from 1 April 2001.

S1W-12854 Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-12419 by Jackie Baillie on 25 January 2001, whether it will give an actual date for the publication of the criteria for entitlement to assistance under the central heating initiative.

S1W-12855 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what the implications of Article 6 of the European Convention on Human Rights are for education appeals.

S1W-12856 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what the implications of Article 6 of the European Convention on Human Rights are for rating valuation appeals.

S1W-12857 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive how much was spent on research into the treatment of and possible cures for (a) multiple sclerosis and (b) myalgic encephalomyelitis in (i) 1997-98, (ii) 1998-99 and (iii) 1999-2000 and how much is expected to be spent in 2000-01 and 2001-02.

S1W-12858 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive how much was spent on treatment of those suffering from (a) multiple sclerosis and (b) myalgic encephalomyelitis in (i) 1997-98, (ii) 1998-99 and (iii) 1999-2000, broken down by health board.

S1W-12859 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people were diagnosed with (a) multiple sclerosis and (b) myalgic encephalomyelitis in (i) 1998, (ii) 1999 and (iii) 2000, broken down by health board.

S1W-12860 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made with the review of audiology services.

S1W-12861 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive who currently serves on the working group looking at hearing services and what the group’s remit is.

S1W-12862 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it consulted Her Majesty's Government on 25 January 2001 prior to the Minister for Parliament’s ministerial statement on long term care and, if so, whom it consulted and on what basis.

S1W-12863 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it notified the media on 25 January 2001 that the Minister for Parliament would be making a ministerial statement on long term care and, if so, at what time.

S1W-12864 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make any representations to Her Majesty's Government in support of designating areas adjacent to the proposed M74 north extension as an enterprise zone.

S1W-12865 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has made or plans to make to Her Majesty's Government regarding a review of OSPAR 98/3 on the decommissioning of oil platforms.

S1W-12866 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what input it has into meetings of the Oslo Paris agreement on the protection of the environment in the North Sea (OSPAR); how many meetings have taken place and how many it or the Scottish Office has attended since 1997, and in what capacity it or the Scottish Office attended these.

S1W-12867 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Presiding Officer whether the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body will make available access by MSPs to videoconferencing facilities.

S1W-12868 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether clinical geneticists are required to be medically qualified and, if not, what qualifications are necessary to carry out genetic screening.

S1W-12869 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is still the case that the Health Technology Board for Scotland will appraise and evaluate new drugs, as envisaged in the Scottish Office document Designed to Care.

S1W-12870 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will review current financial targets for NHSiS Trusts, given that eight out of 28 had a financial deficit at 31 August 2000.

S1W-12871 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive why more resources are being allocated to organic foods and organic farming.

S1W-12872 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive what studies it has made into the effect on the local food chain of depleted uranium shells fired into the Solway Firth.

S1W-12873 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether all patients in psychiatric hospitals have the right and the opportunity to speak with a chaplain when requested.

S1W-12874 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide any assistance to prevent the possible closure of the Community Service Volunteers in Clyde Street, Glasgow.

S1W-12875 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many groups in (a) Glasgow and (b) Glasgow Kelvin have been unsuccessful in their bids for Objective 3 funding.

S1W-12876 Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there is a significant difference in the number of Catholic prisoners as compared with those from other religious groupings and, if so, whether it will investigate the reasons for any such differences.
