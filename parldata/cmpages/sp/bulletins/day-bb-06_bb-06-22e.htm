Business Bulletin No. 103/2006: Thursday 22 June 2006
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 21 June 2006

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-27003 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prosecutions there have been for rape in each of the last 20 years.

S2W-27004 Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-20004 by Peter Peacock on 8 November 2005, what progress it has made in securing data on how many local authority-employed (a) teachers and (b) additional support assistants have (i) a Bachelor of Education, (ii) a Post-Graduate Diploma in Education and (iii) other qualifications in (1) inclusive and special education, (2) specific learning difficulties, (3) hearing impairment, (4) visual impairment, (5) autistic spectrum disorder and (6) behavioural support, broken down into primary, secondary and special schools and by local authority area.

S2W-27005 Murray Tosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what amounts at current prices were paid in government grants in each year since 1997 to meet any annual deficits where the cost of pensions exceeded the amount of contributions made in respect of each of the pay-as-you go pension schemes identified in Public sector pension schemes in Scotland by Audit Scotland.

S2W-27006 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how many teachers in each local authority will have completed their probationary year in 2005-06 and how many positions for which they may apply are available (a) nationally and (b) in each local authority.

S2W-27007 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many full-time equivalent communications, public relations and marketing staff it has employed in each year since 1999.

S2W-27008 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many faulty (a) fire extinguishers and (b) smoke alarms were identified in schools in the each of the last five years.

S2W-27009 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive what proportion of fires in (a) schools, (b) higher education institutions and (c) further education colleges in each of the last 10 years were started deliberately.

S2W-27010 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive what proportion of emergency fire service call-outs to schools in each of the last 10 years resulted from hoax calls.

S2W-27011 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many fires there were in (a) primary and (b) secondary schools in each of the last 10 years, broken down by local education authority.

S2W-27012 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many individuals were treated by the NHS for wounds caused by (a) a knife, (b) a dagger, (c) a sword, (d) other types of bladed article and (e) a firearm in each year since 1997; of these, what proportion were (i) male and (ii) female, and how many of these were admitted to hospital as emergency cases, broken down by NHS board.

S2W-27013 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many small businesses that began trading since 1997 have subsequently ceased trading, broken down by local authority area.

S2W-27014 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on how many people resident in Scotland are qualified to drive, broken down by gender.

S2W-27015 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people have been asked to take a breathalyser test in each of the last five years for which information is available, broken down by gender.

S2W-27016 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people have failed a breathalyser test in each of the last five years for which information is available, broken down by gender.

S2W-27017 Colin Fox: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to enact sections 25 to 29 of the Law Reform (Miscellaneous Provisions) (Scotland) Act 1990.

S2W-27018 Colin Fox: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to make legal provisions for "Mackenzie friends" or "people’s attorneys".

S2W-27019 Colin Fox: To ask the Scottish Executive what comparative research it has conducted on legal rights of audience between Scotland and other countries.

S2W-27020 Colin Fox: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it accepts the case for a greater choice in legal representation in Scotland.

S2W-27021 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive which local authority areas have a befriending service for children and young people and who operates those services in each area.

S2W-27022 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body what steps it is taking to review the provision of facilities in the Parliament complex for people with disabilities and their carers.

S2W-27023 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on the number of unaccompanied asylum seeker children in Scotland and whether this information is held on a central register.

S2W-27024 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will publish the results of the review of the action plan of the Scottish Refugee Integration Forum.

S2W-27025 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive how many task forces, working groups or other similar bodies it has convened for purposes connected with its race equality strategy; what the (a) purposes and (b) membership of any such bodies were or are, and in what capacity each member served or serves.

S2W-27026 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider making Education Maintenance Allowance available to children of asylum seekers and to unaccompanied asylum seeker children.

S2W-27027 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider changing the terms of the bursary regulations to include children of asylum seekers and unaccompanied asylum seeker children.

S2W-27028 Mr Ted Brocklebank: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-26411 by Tavish Scott on 16 June 2006, whether it will meet Fife Council as a matter of urgency to discuss the five sets of traffic lights in the centre of Kincardine-on-Forth, in light of their impact on traffic flows, pollution, the convenience of pedestrians and potential accidents.
