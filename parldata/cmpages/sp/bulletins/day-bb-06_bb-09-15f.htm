Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-4802 Euan Robson: One Planet Food: Good for You, Good for the Planet—That the Parliament warmly welcomes the focus on food as part of WWF Scotland’s One Planet Living exhibition; notes that 20% of our ecological footprint arises from the impacts of our food choices; encourages everyone to do what they can to reduce their "food footprint" and help us move towards "one planet living" by choosing more local, fresh, seasonal, fair trade and organic produce and less processed and packaged foods; notes that by making these changes we can make significant reductions in our "food footprint"; celebrates the fact that these choices are better for the planet and better for our health, and looks forward to making these links in the Schools (Health Promotion and Nutrition) (Scotland) Bill.

Supported by: Nora Radcliffe*

*S2M-4801 Murdo Fraser: HBOS’s Commitment to Scotland—That the Parliament congratulates Halifax Bank of Scotland (HBOS) for pledging to install 50 new fee-free ATMs in deprived areas across Scotland, which will provide thousands of people in Scotland with free access to their money; believes that the pledge demonstrates HBOS’s corporate social responsibility to the Scottish people; notes that HBOS provides more than half of all social banking accounts in the United Kingdom and works continuously to reduce the number of "unbanked" people in Scotland, underlining its commitment to the financial inclusion of all Scots; congratulates the bank on its contribution to the Scottish economy, and calls on the Scottish Executive to continue to work closely with HBOS and all of Scotland’s banks to increase financial inclusion in Scotland.

*S2M-4800 Margaret Smith: Changing Faces—That the Parliament acknowledges the launch of the Scottish branch of the charity, Changing Faces, on Tuesday 19 September 2006 at Edinburgh City Chambers; notes that over 400,000 people in the United Kingdom live with a disfigurement and that Changing Faces is a charity which, through extensive experience and research, has developed techniques to allow people living with disfigurement to build their confidence and self-esteem; recognises that Changing Faces has been at the forefront of efforts to ensure that the emotional and social needs of people living with disfigurement are addressed, and urges MSPs to support the charity.

*S2M-4799 Maureen Macmillan: Beautiful Scotland in Bloom Awards 2006—That the Parliament congratulates the Alness in Bloom team for winning the top award in the prestigious Beautiful Scotland in Bloom campaign; notes that this is the fifth time in 10 years that Alness, Ross-shire, has taken the coveted rose bowl trophy, having won previously in 1997, 1998, 2000 and 2004; further notes that, in addition to the rose bowl trophy, Alness has also won the small town trophy, the horticultural award and the David Welch Memorial Award for Something Special; notes that, in addition to Alness, both Forres and Duffus, in Moray, were also honoured, with Forres winning the medium town trophy and Duffus the small village trophy; pays tribute to the hard work and enthusiasm of all the teams involved, and wishes Alness every success for the results of the first-ever Britain in Bloom Champion of Champions title, to be announced in Perth on Thursday 21 September 2006.

*S2M-4798 Robin Harper: Hungry for Success: Securing the Double Dividend—That the Parliament notes the report published by WWF Scotland, The Footprint of Scotland’s Diet - The environmental burden of what we eat, which sets out the reduction in the ecological footprint that has been achieved by schools through the introduction of healthier menus under the Hungry for Success programme; recognises that a healthy diet can also be better for the planet, and therefore encourages the Scottish Executive to make the links between improvements in nutritional standards and sustainability standards in requirements and guidance which it establishes for all public procurement of food, including the provisions in the Schools (Health Promotion and Nutrition) (Scotland) Bill.

Supported by: Patrick Harvie*, Mr Kenneth Macintosh*

*S2M-4797 Pauline McNeill: One Planet Living—That the Parliament acknowledges that, based on the average ecological footprint of current lifestyles in Scotland, if everyone in the world lived this way we would need three planets to survive; recognises that this cannot be sustainable and that moving towards "one planet living" is the only solution; warmly welcomes the constructive WWF Scotland package which promotes simple ways for each individual and government to reduce their footprint and move towards "one planet living"; recognises that in areas like food, transport and energy we can all make significant reductions to our footprint just by making different choices like buying local, fresh, seasonal, organic, fair trade food when we can, and notes that these choices are not only better for us, but better for the planet and the Scottish economy.

Supported by: Margaret Jamieson*, Mrs Nanette Milne*, Christine May*, Dr Elaine Murray*, Murray Tosh*, Murdo Fraser*, Richard Lochhead*, Roseanna Cunningham*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Mr Mark Ruskell*, Bruce Crawford*, Dr Jean Turner*, Maureen Macmillan*, Sarah Boyack*, Eleanor Scott*, Kate Maclean*, Robin Harper*, Cathy Peattie*, Chris Ballance*, Cathie Craigie*, Bristow Muldoon*, Margaret Smith*, Alex Fergusson*, Rob Gibson*, Ms Sandra White*, Nora Radcliffe*, Mr Kenneth Macintosh*, Trish Godman*

*S2M-4796 Mr Tom McCabe: Edinburgh Airport Rail Link Bill: Financial Resolution—That the Parliament, for the purposes of any Act of the Scottish Parliament resulting from the Edinburgh Airport Rail Link Bill, agrees to any expenditure of a kind referred to in Rule 9A.14.3(b)(ii) of the Parliament’s Standing Orders arising in consequence of the Act.

*S2M-4795 Roseanna Cunningham on behalf of the Health Committee: Care Inquiry—That the Parliament notes the conclusions and recommendations contained in the Health Committee’s 10th Report, 2006 (Session 2): Care Inquiry (SP Paper 594).

Supported by: Janis Hughes*

*S2M-4794.1 David McLetchie: Congratulations to Standard Life—As an amendment to motion (S2M-4794) in the name of Jim Mather, insert at end "but notes that on 31 March 2004 Alex Salmond MP described the demutualisation of Standard Life, without which it could not have joined the FTSE 100, as "a sad, bad day for the Scottish financial sector", and calls on the SNP to state whether this remains the official view of the party."

*S2M-4769.1# Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: Airdrie to Bathgate Rail Line—As an amendment to motion (S2M-4769) in the name of Karen Whitefield, insert at end "and believes that the project should include a station at Blackridge which would serve the communities of Blackridge, Greenrigg and Harthill."

Supported by: Alex Johnstone*, Murdo Fraser*, Dave Petrie*, Chris Ballance*, Robin Harper*
