Business Bulletin No. 139/2001

Thursday 27 September 2001

Section D : Oral Questions selected for answer on 4 October 2001
  

Question Time

1. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it plans to take to reduce the number of missed NHS appointments. (S1O-3866)

2. Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making in recruiting extra teachers. (S1O-3872)

3. Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to restore the overall level of improvement and repair grants to what it was in 1996-97. (S1O-3864)

4. Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making in meeting its target for flu vaccinations. (S1O-3912)

5. Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what research it is undertaking into any link between increased levels of skin cancer and the use of sun tanning parlours. (S1O-3891)

6. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in ensuring that the international ferry link between Rosyth and continental Europe will be operational by next year. (S1O-3897)

7. Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether inspections carried out by HM Inspectorate of Education on the education functions of local authorities will improve standards of education delivered in schools. (S1O-3908)

8. Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will make a decision on writing off the housing debt of Shetland Islands Council. (S1O-3879)

9. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will investigate the circumstances surrounding the appeals procedure used by Scottish Borders Council under the Education (Scotland) Act 1980 to refer the case of Victoria Nichol from Peebles, who suffers from cerebral palsy, to HM Inspectorate of Education. (S1O-3880)

10. Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will detail its plans for the marketing of Scotland in other countries as a tourist destination following the recent terrorist attacks in the United States of America. (S1O-3910)

11. Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it plans to take to limit the costs associated with quality assurance so that Scottish farmers are not placed at any competitive disadvantage with their English counterparts. (S1O-3893)

12. Bill Aitken: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will conduct an audit on the effect on the resources of the Crown Office and the Procurator Fiscal Service of incorporating the European Convention on Human Rights into Scots Law. (S1O-3870)

13. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people are currently on NHS patient waiting lists. (S1O-3878)

14. Mr Andy Kerr: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making in improving energy efficiency in buildings. (S1O-3873)

15. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to recruit and train in Glasgow the additional skilled workers needed for the construction of the M74 extension, the Glasgow Harbour Development, the schools building programme and the housing repairs programme following any housing stock transfer. (S1O-3911)

16. David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive what funds it will make available to Dumfries and Galloway Council to implement the outcome of its schools option appraisal process. (S1O-3907)

17. Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive how shared equity within sheltered and retirement housing will be dealt with in its proposed Title Conditions (Scotland) Bill. (S1O-3909)

18. Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what support is being made available for farm tourism. (S1O-3888)

19. Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made by the Motorola task force. (S1O-3889)

20. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to improve the professional standards of teachers. (S1O-3884)

21. Colin Campbell: To ask the Scottish Executive how it ensures that all answers to parliamentary questions and letters from MSPs are detailed, informed and transparent and that the minimum constraints on providing information are imposed. (S1O-3915)

22. Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will reconsider its decision to close its Environment and Rural Affairs Department office in Stirling given the current situation in the agriculture industry. (S1O-3894)

23. Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to increase the impact of the Child Strategy Statement. (S1O-3876)

24. Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether its Freedom of Information Bill will include provisions to improve access to police road traffic accident reports. (S1O-3898)

25. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will publish details of any breaches by it of European environmental law currently being investigated by the European Commission and what action it is taking both in respect of any such breaches and to prevent any in future. (S1O-3901)

26. Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the current position is in its discussions with CoSLA on the procedures for the closure of rural schools. (S1O-3875)

27. Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration it will give in its review of the cities to the development of rural areas surrounding cities. (S1O-3896)

28. Mrs Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to assist local authorities to enable them to comply with the recommendations in the report of the Scottish Waste Advisory Group. (S1O-3904)

29. Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what its policy is on the development of light railways and tram systems. (S1O-3905)

30. Tricia Marwick: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance it has given the Health Technology Board for Scotland in relation to their consideration of prescribing beta interferon to people with multiple sclerosis. (S1O-3867)
