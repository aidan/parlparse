Business Bulletin No. 160/2010: Friday 5 November 2010

Section E – Written questions lodged on 4 November 2010

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-37244 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-36750 by Kenny MacAskill on 7 October 2010, whether the announcement will be made to the Parliament.

S3W-37245 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-36816 by Michael Russell on 29 October 2010, what methodology was used to calculate the value to the economy of Scottish universities’ international activities.

S3W-37246 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what overseas trips by ministers are (a) booked and (b) planned before May 2011, broken down by (i) minister involved, (ii) purpose and (iii) number of officials expected to attend.

S3W-37247 Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people under the age of 36 have died of a condition recognised as sudden cardiac death in each of the last five years, broken down by NHS board.

S3W-37248 Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive how much funding the NHS allocated to investigating the causes and prevention of sudden cardiac deaths in (a) 2006-07, (b) 2007-08, (c) 2008-09 and (d) 2009-10.

S3W-37249 Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) electrocardiogram and (b) echocardiogram machines are in operation in the NHS, broken down by NHS board.

S3W-37250 Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive what the estimated cost is per patient of operating an (a) electrocardiogram and (b) echocardiogram machine.

S3W-37251 Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people have been screened in each year since the launch of the Cardiac Assessment in Young Athletes programme in 2008.

S3W-37252 Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people have been referred for further investigation or treatment following screening by the Cardiac Assessment in Young Athletes programme, in each year since its launch.

S3W-37253 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-6845 by John Swinney on 6 December 2007, what the value is of the pay ranges that apply in its core directorates and the percentage of its total staff in each pay range.

S3W-37254 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-36778 by John Swinney on 26 October 2010, what the membership is of the Infrastructure Investment Group, broken down by (a) name, (b) job role and (c) employer.

S3W-37255 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-36778 by John Swinney on 26 October 2010, how often the Infrastructure Investment Group has met; what was discussed in each case; where each meeting was held, and at what cost.

S3W-37256 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-36778 by John Swinney on 26 October 2010, what the relationship is of the Scottish Futures Trust and the Infrastructure Investment Group and how their roles differ.

S3W-37257 Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people retain the right to buy (RTB) their home either through preserved or modernised RTB.

S3W-37258 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answers to questions S3W-35045 and S3W-36170 by Fiona Hyslop on 23 July and 16 September 2010 respectively, on what date it plans to publish its report into museum funding.

S3W-37259 Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive how many community planning partnerships have (a) expressed an interest in and (b) committed to participating in the Low Carbon Vehicle Procurement Support Scheme.

S3W-37260 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what evidence supports the banning of people with myalgic encephalomyelitis from giving blood.

S3W-37261 Jamie Hepburn: To ask the Scottish Executive, under the terms of the Schools (Consultation) (Scotland) Act 2010, whether representatives of parents or community groups are entitled to appear before local authority bodies making decisions on school closures.

S3W-37262 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many suicides have been recorded in each year since 1999.

S3W-37263 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding is provided to the Samaritans.

S3W-37264 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it provides funding to the Samaritans’ telephone helpline.

S3W-37265 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has made changes to the number of new entrants to trainee nursing programmes for 2010-11.

S3W-37266 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has made changes to the number of new entrants to trainee doctor programmes for 2010-11.

S3W-37267 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many places were provided for new entrants to trainee nursing programmes in (a) 2008-09 and (b) 2009-10.

S3W-37268 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many places were provided to new entrants to the trainee doctor programmes in (a) 2008-09 and (b) 2009-10.

S3W-37269 Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive when funds will be available to Pentland Housing Association Ltd for adaptations to social rented housing.

S3W-37270 Cathie Craigie: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to improve broadband services in urban areas.

S3W-37271 Cathie Craigie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many breaches of section 61 orders under the Control of Pollution Act 1974 have taken place in the last three years.

S3W-37272 Cathie Craigie: To ask the Scottish Executive in relation to how many breaches of section 61 orders under the Control of Pollution Act 1974 action has been taken in the last three years.

S3W-37273 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding has been made available to carers centres through carers information strategies for 2010-11, broken down by carers centre.

S3W-37274 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the level of local authority funding for carers centres was in (a) 2008-09, (b) 2009-10 and (c) 2010-11.

S3W-37275 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is aware of local authorities (a) reducing respite services and (b) providing emergency respite services only.

S3W-37276 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the new care standards for dementia will include a requirement for consultation with family and carers before the prescription of psychoactive medication.

S3W-37277 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what training will be made available to care home and hospital staff following the publication of Scotland’s National Dementia Strategy.

S3W-37278 Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive how much has been spent on (a) public housing projects and (b) shared equity schemes in each year since 2007, broken down by local authority.

S3W-37279 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the Supported Employment Workplaces debate on 7 October 2010 (Official Report c. 29383), whether it has convened a meeting with the City of Edinburgh Council and other interested parties regarding the proposed closure of Blindcraft in Edinburgh.

S3W-37280 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the Supported Employment Workplaces debate on 7 October 2010 (Official Report c. 29383), whether it has invited all interested parties, including the City of Edinburgh Council, MSPs and MPs, to meet to discuss the future of Blindcraft in Edinburgh and, if so, on what date the invitations were issued.

S3W-37281 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the Supported Employment Workplaces debate on 7 October 2010 (Official Report c. 29383), whether it will provide details of correspondence between it, the City of Edinburgh Council and MSPs regarding the proposed closure of Blindcraft in Edinburgh.

S3W-37282 Hugh O’Donnell: To ask the Scottish Executive what costs have been incurred by Disclosure Scotland in preparing for the introduction of the Protecting Vulnerable Groups Scheme.

S3W-37283 Hugh O’Donnell: To ask the Scottish Executive who was employed by Disclosure Scotland to write the 136-page guidance to the new rules for the Protecting Vulnerable Groups Scheme and what the cost was.

S3W-37284 Hugh O’Donnell: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-29201 by Jim Mather on 2 December 2009, whether it will issue a Scottish energy key facts publication before the end of 2010.

S3W-37287 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many businesses in (a) South and (b) East Ayrshire will be eligible for the small business bonus scheme in 2010-11.

S3W-37288 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive who was on the panel that awarded the contract for the additional support needs national advocacy service.

S3W-37289 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria were used to award the contract for the additional support needs national advocacy service and whether this included having experience of representing parents or families at the Additional Support Needs Tribunal.

S3W-37290 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what arrangements are in place to assist families in receipt of support from the advocacy service provided by Independent Special Education Advice (Scotland) for whom a tribunal has yet to be set or will be held after 14 November 2010.

S3W-37298 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to ensure that the Education (Additional Support for Learning) (Scotland) Act 2004 is being implemented in a consistent way across Scotland.
