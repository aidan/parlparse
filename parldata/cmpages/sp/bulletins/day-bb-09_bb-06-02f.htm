Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (♦).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S3M-4289 Ross Finnie: Celebrate Barnardo’s Works Renfrewshire’s 10th Anniversary—That the Parliament celebrates the 10th anniversary of children’s charity Barnardo’s Works Renfrewshire, formerly called Youthbuild, the project that enables disadvantaged young people from Renfrewshire’s most deprived communities to access sustainable employment, and congratulates all who have successfully run the project, including Barnardo’s, Renfrewshire Council and the local employers, and the young people themselves who have been helped into employment through the programme.

*S3M-4288 Sarah Boyack: The Edinburgh Diversity Cup 2009—That the Parliament welcomes the third annual Edinburgh Diversity Cup, a five-a-side charity football competition to be held on Saturday 20 June 2009 at the Edinburgh Corn Exchange; notes that the proceeds from this event will go towards the Hope into Homes project in Malawi, an Edinburgh-based project operating with the assistance of the Edinburgh Inter-Faith Association in partnership with Habitat for Humanity to fund a multi-faith and multi-cultural team from Edinburgh to travel to Malawi to work in partnership with local families to build new homes; further notes that the 2008 event involved 19 teams and 130 players representing diverse backgrounds, beliefs and professions from across Edinburgh, raising £770 in the process, and lauds the event for its core aims of bringing people from different cultures and faiths together through sport, building friendship and cooperation, promoting respect and equality and celebrating diversity.

*S3M-4287 Jamie Hepburn: Cumbernauld Community Park—That the Parliament congratulates Friends of Cumbernauld Community Park on the organisation of the second Fun in the Park day, held on 31 May 2009; notes that this highly successful event builds on last year’s equally successful Fun in the Park day and follows on from the Walk in the Park event on 26 April 2009, which launched the series of recently constructed pathways through Cumbernauld Community Park; further congratulates Friends of Cumbernauld Community Park for all its endeavours, and hopes that these events hasten the formal designation of Cumbernauld Community Park.

*S3M-4286 Patrick Harvie: Offences (Aggravation by Prejudice) (Scotland) Bill—That the Parliament agrees that the Offences (Aggravation by Prejudice) (Scotland) Bill be passed.

*S3M-4285 Cathy Jamieson: Away the Bot, Congratulations to Auchinleck Talbot FC—That the Parliament congratulates all the players and staff of Auchinleck Talbot FC on their historic eighth victory in the 2009 Citilink Scottish Junior Cup final; further congratulates Auchinleck Talbot FC’s manager, Tommy Sloan, on his second victory in the competition as the club’s manager; recognises the significance of this achievement in Auchinleck Talbot FC’s centenary year; praises the crowd of 8,122, fans of both Auchinleck Talbot FC and Clydebank FC, for creating a fantastic atmosphere at the final in Rugby Park, Kilmarnock; recognises the hard work of the Auchinleck Talbot committee and its strong links with the local community; thanks the people of Mauchline, Catrine and Auchinleck who turned out to welcome the team back to Beechwood Park, and also thanks everyone at the Scottish Junior Football Association and all those involved in junior football at a local level for their support for the game.

Supported by: John Scott*, Sarah Boyack*, Mary Mulligan*, Des McNulty*, Kenneth Gibson*

*S3M-4284 Jamie Hepburn: Congratulations to Winners of Amnesty International Scottish Media Awards—That the Parliament congratulates Lucy Adams on winning the Amnesty International Scottish Media Award for the second time in three years; notes that her winning article, The Fight for Justice, was published in The Herald Magazine in September 2008 and looked at the use of rape as a weapon of war in conflict in the Democratic Republic of Congo, examining its impact on the lives of women and their communities and the coping mechanisms that have been developed; also congratulates the runners-up in the competition from the BBC Scotland Eòrpa Gaelic language programme, Hedda MacLeod, Catriona MacKinnon, Colin MacLeod, Annie Cheape, Iain Macinnes, Darren Laing and Alasdair Fraser, for three short films looking at immigration issues in Europe, and hopes that the award will continue to inspire journalists in Scotland to work for better understanding and awareness of human rights issues in Scotland and around the world. *S3M-4283 Dave Thompson: Congratulations to Karen Gillan—That the Parliament congratulates Karen Gillan on securing her role in the much-loved BBC series, Doctor Who; notes the wealth of talent that Scotland has in the drama industry; believes that it is important that young Scottish talent is supported, encouraged and given the recognition it deserves, and wishes Karen the best of luck in the future.

Supported by: Bob Doris*, Bill Wilson*, Jamie McGrigor*, Christina McKelvie*

*S3M-4282♦ Rhoda Grant: Giving Blood, Giving the Gift of Life—That the Parliament notes with concern the decline in the number of blood donors in Scotland, a number that has decreased by 30,000 in the last decade; further notes that in the Highlands alone, the service requires 23,000 donors to give blood in 2009; supports the appeal by the Scottish National Blood Transfusion Service for donors to give blood at least once in 2009 to reverse the decline in the donor base; believes that there would be benefit in employers giving their staff paid time off to donate blood, and hopes that Scots who have not already done so will register to give blood and give the gift of life.

Supported by: Peter Peacock*, David Stewart*

*S3M-4281 Kenneth Gibson: Congratulations to Largs Thistle FC—That the Parliament congratulates Largs Thistle FC, its fans, players, committee and backroom staff on winning the Stagecoach West Region First Division title in style; commends the inspirational leadership of management team Sandy MacLean and Paddy McIlvogue, and wishes Largs Thistle all the best in next season’s Superleague Premier League, which The Theesel will grace for the first time.

Supported by: Alasdair Allan*, Stuart McMillan*, Stewart Maxwell*, Aileen Campbell*, Sandra White*, Ross Finnie*

S3M-4269 *#* Elaine Murray: Wildlife Week, 6 to 14 June 2009—That the Parliament commends the Scottish Wildlife Trust (SWT) for organising Wildlife Week, 6 to 14 June 2009; notes that there are over 230 events and activities taking place throughout Scotland, including the celebration of peatlands, woodlands and marine environments in Dumfries and Galloway, and appreciates the efforts by SWT, its sister charities and non-governmental organisations in Scotland in raising public awareness of the richness of Scotland’s natural environment and the need to preserve and enhance habitats to conserve Scotland’s wildlife and ensure that future generations can also enjoy its diversity.

Supported by: Ms Wendy Alexander*, Nanette Milne*, Christine Grahame*, Robin Harper*, Jackie Baillie*, Karen Whitefield*, Patricia Ferguson*, Des McNulty*, Alison McInnes*

S3M-4267 *#* Trish Godman: Erskine Week, Supporting Veterans—That the Parliament supports Erskine Week, from 1 to 8 June 2009, organised by Erskine, the charity that cares for ex-servicemen and women throughout Scotland; congratulates all those who are taking part to raise money for the charity; notes that Erskine Week aims to get people of all ages involved to raise awareness and funds to help Erskine provide the very high standards of care that Scotland’s veterans so eminently deserve; offers congratulations to Colonel Martin Gibson, the outgoing chief executive of Erskine, who retires on 8 June, for all his hard work and dedication; and wishes him well in all his future endeavours; wishes to ensure that the care, treatment and rehabilitation of ex-servicemen and women is given the highest priority, and further notes that the world would be a very different place without the dedication and courage of veterans, such as those in the care of Erskine Home in West Renfrewshire.

Supported by: Jamie McGrigor*, Margaret Mitchell*, Bill Kidd*
