Wednesday 3 October 2001

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

The full text of all outstanding motions and amendments will appear in the Business Bulletin every Monday.

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-2285 Allan Wilson: Sports Promotion in Scotland's Schools—That the Parliament recognises the contribution sport makes to both the physical and mental health of young people as well as providing a positive and attractive alternative to anti-social and offending behaviour; endorses the various initiatives, such as the School Sports Co-ordinator and Active Primary School Programmes put in place by the Scottish Executive through sportscotland and the increasing opportunities for young people to participate in sports in schools, and calls on local authorities, and others, to engage fully with these initiatives.

*S1M-2284# Mr Kenneth Gibson: Recognition of Parkinson's Disease—That the Parliament recognises that Parkinson’s Disease is a progressive neurological condition affecting 13,500 people across Scotland and that one in seven people diagnosed will be under the age of 50; notes that every element of normal movement, speech and swallowing is affected by the disease, which often has a devastating impact on both immediate and extended family; believes it is important to establish models of good clinical and social care; further recognises and commends the excellent work being done by the Parkinson’s Disease Society in helping people and their carers with problems arising from the disease, and considers that the Scottish Executive should as a matter of urgency establish a national network of Parkinson’s Disease specialist nurses to provide a national service framework for neurological conditions including Parkinson’s Disease and should eliminate any inequities in health and social care access in areas such as Glasgow, Ayrshire, Lanarkshire and the Highlands and Islands where the shortage of specialist nurses is most acute.

Supported by: Tricia Marwick*, Mr Kenny MacAskill*, Bruce Crawford*, Ms Sandra White*, Mr Gil Paterson*, Michael Russell*, Brian Adam*, Alasdair Morgan*
