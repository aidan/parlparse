Meetings of Committees

All meetings take place in the Scottish Parliament, Edinburgh unless otherwise indicated.

Public Petitions Committee

17th Meeting, 2004

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in Committee Room 6

1. New Petitions: The Committee will consider the following new petitions—

PE778 Upgrading the Laurencekirk/Maryhill junction of the A90. Jill Campbell will make a brief statement in support of her petition accompanied by Norman Banski.

PE780 Immediate restructuring of the Scottish Football Team’s management regime.

PE782 Modernising the treason law in the United Kingdom.

PE783 Supporting the establishment of the Golden Eagle as the national bird of Scotland.

PE781 Commemorating the 700th anniversary of Sir William Wallace’s death, marking the date as an annual event in the Scottish calendar and urging that he be exonerated of the charge of treason.

2. Current Petitions: The Committee will further consider the following current petitions—

PE558 Including a bypass for Elgin within the programme for improvements to the A96. The Committee will consider an update from the Scottish Executive.

PE571 Implementing recommendations from the National Medical Advisory Committee on complementary medicine within the NHS. The Committee will consider an update from the Scottish Executive.

PE583 Governance and management of Scotland’s further education colleges. The Committee will consider responses from the petitioner, NUS Scotland, EIS FE Lecturers’ Association and the Association of Scottish Colleges.

PE702 Reviewing the Code of Conduct for Councillors 2003 relating to planning applications. The Committee will consider a response from the Scottish Executive.

PE726 Regulating and training Returning Officers for local government elections. The Committee will consider responses from the Scottish Executive, Electoral Commission, Renfrewshire Council, Society of Local Authority Chief Executives, and COSLA.

PE745 Amending the Scottish Public Services Ombudsman Act 2002 and introducing guidelines on the procedure for investigating complaints. The Committee will consider a response from the Scottish Public Services Ombudsman.

PE749 Conducting an inquiry into the safety of the spreading of sewage sludge. The Committee will consider responses from the Minister for Environment and Rural Development, Scottish Water and Scottish Environment Protection Agency.

PE751 Conducting an inquiry into the procedures and practices of the Scottish Legal Aid Board. The Committee will consider responses from the Disability Rights Commission, Scottish Executive, Scottish Legal Aid Board and Law Society of Scotland.

S2M-1840 Dr Elaine Murray: Science and the Parliament Event, 10 November 2004—That the Parliament welcomes the Science and the Parliament 2004 event being held on 10 November 2004, organised by the Royal Society of Chemistry in association with Scotland’s leading science organisations; notes the contribution of Scotland's 40,000 scientists to our economic, environmental and social development; notes that Scotland is a world leader in many scientific disciplines; further notes the Scottish Executive’s efforts to foster an environment that enhances pupil and student participation in science, to invest in the science infrastructure and equipment of our educational establishments, to increase investment in research along with supporting greater industrial research and assist in the practical application of our world-beating research, and welcomes the review of the Executive’s review, A Science Strategy for Scotland.
