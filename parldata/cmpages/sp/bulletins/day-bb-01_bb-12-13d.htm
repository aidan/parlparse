Business Bulletin No. 185/2001

Thursday 13 December 2001

Section D : Oral Questions
  

1. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any concerns following the abandonment in July 2001 of the trial of six men in Ayrshire charged with sexual abuse and what steps it is planning to take to ensure that justice is served in all such cases. (S1O-4339)

2. Mr John McAllion: To ask the Scottish Executive how much funding has been made available to local authorities for the appointment of consultants to develop proposals for public private partnerships in education. (S1O-4354)

3. Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the additional money allocated from Her Majesty’s Treasury under the recent Pre-Budget Report will be used to initiate a programme of upgrade and renewal of NHS equipment. (S1O-4347)

4. Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it plans to take to attract independent dental practitioners back into the NHS. (S1O-4351)

5. Cathie Craigie: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to integrate ex-offenders into the labour market. (S1O-4377)

6. Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in taking forward the recommendations in the report of the Action Group on Languages, Citizens of a Multilingual World. (S1O-4337)

7. Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether Scottish Homes tenants in Glasgow will be eligible for the same repair and improvement grants as will be made available to all former council tenants who bought their home under the right to buy legislation should the proposed Glasgow housing stock transfer take place. (S1O-4329)

8. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are being taken to improve the prosecution service. (S1O-4358)

9. Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Minister for Enterprise, Transport and Lifelong Learning last met Her Majesty's Treasury ministers and what issues were discussed. (S1O-4341)

10. David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive how transfer of Dumfries and Galloway’s local authority housing stock to the Dumfries and Galloway housing partnership would benefit tenants. (S1O-4349)

11. John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the monitoring by the Performance Audit Group of BEAR (Scotland) Ltd’s trunk road maintenance contract has found any shortcomings in their winter road maintenance performance on the Highland trunk road network during the last six weeks. (S1O-4350)

12. Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what preparations have been made for the introduction of EC Regulation 2037/2000 concerning the recycling of refrigerators and freezers. (S1O-4360)

13. Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what further assistance it plans to make available to Angus Council to help it meet the costs of the replacement bridge over the South Esk at Montrose. (S1O-4352)

14. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in combatting domestic abuse. (S1O-4379)

15. Janis Hughes: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to improve adult numeracy and literacy standards. (S1O-4372)

16. Mr Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact the merger of the two leading cancer charities, the Imperial Cancer Research Fund and the Cancer Research Campaign, will have on cancer care. (S1O-4381)

17. Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to ensure that there is an adequate supply of affordable housing in rural areas. (S1O-4328)

18. Kate MacLean: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to introduce a programme of eyesight screening for all schoolchildren when they start their primary and secondary education. (S1O-4331)

19. Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive when it next plans to meet representatives of the Scottish School Board Association. (S1O-4353)

20. Elaine Thomson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is undertaking to reduce traffic congestion in Aberdeen. (S1O-4368)

21. Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria it uses in assessing requests from local authorities for additional funding to prevent the closure of rural schools. (S1O-4370)

22. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people are currently on waiting lists for heart by-pass surgery. (S1O-4359)

23. Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people are currently being prescribed anti-depressants. (S1O-4365)

24. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive whether its recent Action Plan on nurse recruitment and retention includes specific measures to retain nurse specialists. (S1O-4342)

25. Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to assess the impact on its policy on fuel poverty resulting from the proposal by the Office of Gas and Electricity Markets to remove price controls from the electricity market. (S1O-4327)

26. Ian Jenkins: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will ensure that bus companies provide routes and timetables that reflect the expectations of existing and potential passengers. (S1O-4362)

27. Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has made to Her Majesty’s Government regarding its policies on competition and their effect on the operation of Scottish transport. (S1O-4340)

28. George Lyon: To ask the Scottish Executive how its proposals for land reform will benefit rural communities. (S1O-4366)

29. Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it will take to ensure that additional monies made available for human resources development within universities are used to enhance the job security of contract research staff. (S1O-4334)

30. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to improve the level of public transport services in rural communities. (S1O-4345)
