Business Bulletin No. 69/2002

Tuesday 30 April 2002

Section E : Written questions lodged on 29 April 2002
  

Questions marked with a triangle () are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S1W-25459 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-23884 by Nicol Stephen on 21 March 2002, how many proposals which would involve the loss of a playing field have been referred to it by planning authorities as a result of Sportscotland advising against the proposal.

S1W-25460 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-23884 by Nicol Stephen on 21 March 2002, what guidelines Sportscotland follow when considering planning applications and developments likely to prejudice or lead to the loss of playing fields and what guidance it gives to Sportscotland on this issue.

S1W-25461 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive what assistance, financial or otherwise, will be available to community sports clubs which are not registered charities following the restriction of water rates relief to charities under the Water Industry (Scotland) Act 2002.

S1W-25462 Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has made to (a) the City of Edinburgh Council and (b) Sportscotland in regard to the future of Scotland's only velodrome at Meadowbank Sports Centre, Edinburgh.

S1W-25463 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive, following the First Minister's speech to the Committee of the Regions’ Commission for Constitutional Affairs and European Governance on 26 April 2002, what "greater role" the First Minister envisages for the Parliament in Europe and when this greater role will be outlined to (a) the Convention on the Future of Europe and (b) the Parliament.

S1W-25464 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how much earmarked funding has been received, or will be received, by each NHS board for drug treatment services under the Arbuthnott Substance Misuse Formula (a) since the formula came into use and (b) in future financial years for which budgets are available, expressed in cash terms and per capita.

S1W-25465 Mr Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will give an itemised breakdown of the total transport subsidy to Shetland in each of the past five years.

S1W-25466 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any NHS boards have placed restrictions on the prescribing of Glivec as a treatment for chronic myeloid leukaemia, and, if so, which boards.

S1W-25467 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what the (a) target and (b) actual current waiting times are for a neurological examination by a consultant neurologist in each NHS board area.

S1W-25468 Susan Deacon: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-20118 by Mr Andy Kerr on 26 March 2002, what progress has been made in finalising the list of public appointees who declared political activity in the five-year period prior to their appointment.

S1W-25469 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-24633 by Cathy Jamieson on 23 April 2002 and following the meeting of Minecor on 25 April 2002, whether it is now able to give its position on Scottish representation on the Youth Convention on the Future of Europe.

S1W-25470 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-24633 by Cathy Jamieson on 23 April 2002, what further information it is awaiting in respect of the proposed Youth Convention and what the relevance is of the European Youth Gathering in Murcia, Spain, to the proposed Youth Convention and to potential Scottish participation in the convention.

S1W-25471 John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive when the £4.5 million funding package for the wave test centre in Orkney will be made available.

S1W-25472 Bristow Muldoon: To ask the Scottish Executive when the report of the MMR expert group will be made public and when it will respond to the Public Health Institute of Scotland needs assessment report on autistic spectrum disorders.
