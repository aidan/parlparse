Business Bulletin No. 85/2004

Wednesday 19 May 2004

Section E : Written questions lodged on 18 May 2004
  

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-8269 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it has taken to monitor any increase in occurrences of ragwort in each year since 1999.

S2W-8270 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive how many enforcement notices requiring the clearance of ragwort under the Weeds Act 1959 were served in 2003.

S2W-8271 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prosecutions for the non-clearance of ragwort under the Weeds Act 1959 have taken place in each year since 1999 and what percentage was successful.

S2W-8272 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the trunk road contract contains a specific requirement for the removal of ragwort by contract holders when it arises in their area of operation and what monitoring controls the Executive has in place to ensure that any occurrence of ragwort is reported to it and removed.

S2W-8273 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it has taken to monitor occurrences of ragwort arising on property under the control of Network Rail and its predecessors in each year since 1999 and how many enforcement notices and prosecutions there have been in each year as a result.

S2W-8274 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it has taken to monitor occurrences of ragwort arising on property under the control of Amey Highways Ltd in each year since the trunk road contract was awarded and how many enforcement notices and prosecutions there have been in each year as a result.

S2W-8275 Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it has taken to monitor occurrences of ragwort arising on property under the control of BEAR Scotland Ltd in each year since the trunk road contract was awarded and how many enforcement notices and prosecutions there have been in each year as a result.

S2W-8304 Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-7205 by Malcolm Chisholm on 28 April 2004, how much surplus has been generated by car parking charges at the Royal Infirmary of Edinburgh and whether Consort, the City of Edinburgh Council and NHS Lothian receive equal shares of this surplus.

S2W-8305 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-7936 by Mr Andy Kerr on 17 May 2004, whether it will break down by (a) country, (b) year and (c) type the requests it received seeking its support for events outwith Scotland requesting Scottish participation in each year from 1999 to 2004.

S2W-8306 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-7855 by Mr Jim Wallace on 11 May 2004, why it does not set targets for those they expect to be in full-time employment upon completion of their modern apprenticeship scheme and, in the absence of such targets, how it measures the success of the scheme.

S2W-8307 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-7853 by Lewis Macdonald on 11 May 2004, how much the research project awarded to DTZ Pieda Consulting and the Science, Technology and Policy Research Unit at the University of Sussex will cost.

S2W-8308 Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive how much has been paid in community benefits to local communities where wind farms are sited.

S2W-8309 Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive how the 40% target for renewable electricity generation in 2020 was arrived at.

S2W-8310 Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there are subsidiary targets within the 18% and 40% targets for different renewable generation technologies.

S2W-8311 Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive what proportion of the 18% and 40% renewable electricity generation targets should be capable of meeting base-load demand.

S2W-8312 Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on what proportion of intermittent renewable generation can be accommodated in the Scottish grid without disruption to a secure electricity supply.

S2W-8313 Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on what proportion of intermittent renewable generation can be accommodated in the Scottish grid without incurring extra costs whilst maintaining a secure electricity supply.

S2W-8314 Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the 40% target for renewable energy generation refers to 40% of the total installed capacity of generation, 40% of installed capacity to meet Scottish peak demand plus a suitable load margin, 40% of peak Scottish power demand, 40% of maximum Scottish energy demand or any other measure of electricity generation.

S2W-8315 Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make representations to the Joint Energy Security of Supply Working Group (JESS) in respect of the possibility of publishing data on electricity supply and demand forecasts for Scotland, similar to those published in the JESS reports for England and Wales.

S2W-8316 Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive whether its representatives on the Joint Energy Security of Supply Working Group have raised any concerns regarding a potential shortfall in electricity generation in Scotland in light of the planned closures of Cockenzie, Longannet and Hunterston B power stations.

S2W-8317 Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive what reports its representatives on the Joint Energy Security of Supply Working Group have given to it in respect of new electricity generating capacity in Scotland.

S2W-8318 Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the definition of "value for money" used in the Scottish Public Finance Manual in respect of procurement policies includes consideration of the costs of greenhouse gas emissions from appliances and energy installations.

S2W-8319 Frances Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions have been held with the Department of Trade and Industry and other government departments in respect of General Agreement on Trade in Services negotiations relating to the water industry; where such discussions took place; who attended the meetings, and whether it will publish any minutes of such discussions.

S2W-8320Δ Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive what form the investigation into the tragic incident in Maryhill last week will take and whether there will be a public inquiry.
