Business Bulletin No. 165/2002

Friday 15 November 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-3585 Ben Wallace: Armed Forces Personnel and Firefighting Duties-That the Parliament supports the brave young men and women of the armed forces in Scotland who are putting their lives at risk in order to cover the reckless and dangerous strike by Scottish firefighters; demands that the Scottish Executive immediately steps up concurrent training for armed forces personnel to allow them to use better firefighting equipment and, in addition, gives them access to basic protective clothing; believes that public safety and the safety of the armed forces personnel must come before trade union demands, and notes that the armed forces carry out these essential services ill-equipped and for considerably lower pay than firefighters and with no right to strike.

Supported by: Mrs Lyndsay McIntosh*, Bill Aitken*, Phil Gallie*, John Young*, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton*, Mr Jamie McGrigor*, John Scott*, Mr David Davidson*

*S1M-3584# Margaret Smith: Edinburgh Park Railway Station-That the Parliament notes the recent announcement that construction of the Edinburgh Park Railway Station can begin; welcomes the funding supplied by the Strategic Rail Authority, City of Edinburgh Council and New Edinburgh Ltd; further welcomes the investment in new rolling stock from the Scottish Executive; is concerned that agreement on the project was delayed by performance targets; considers that the Scottish Executive should examine how this station can serve the Edinburgh to Glasgow rail line, and asks the Executive to ensure that construction of the station and the provision of additional rolling stock is completed as soon as possible.

*S1M-3583 Mr Gil Paterson: White Ribbon Campaign to End Violence Against Women-That the Parliament supports the White Ribbon Campaign, which leads up to the UN International Day Against Women on 25 November 2002; recognises the white ribbon as the international symbol for a world where women and girls can live free from the fear of violence; supports the work of the UK White Ribbon Campaign, co-ordinated by WOMANKIND Worldwide, to raise funds and awareness to end violence against women; notes with alarm the prevalence and gravity of violence against women, with one in four women in the UK experiencing domestic violence at some point in their lives, and one in 20 women suffering rape, and at least one in every three women around the world being beaten, coerced into sex, or abused in some other way, and urges MSPs and others to support this campaign by wearing a white ribbon to show their support for ending violence against women.

*S1M-3582 Fergus Ewing: Personal Care-That the Parliament notes with concern that The Highland Council has considered tightening the rules covering eligibility for free personal care because of a funding shortfall of about £300,000; further notes that more than 75,000 people in Scotland are estimated to be eligible for free care following the decision by the Parliament and the Scottish Executive to provide such free care; recognises that the council received just over £4 million from the Executive to meet the costs of providing free personal and nursing care in its area but that demand has proven to be higher than predicted, and calls on the Executive to fulfil its commitment to free personal care by meeting the shortfall in funding as soon as possible.

*S1M-3581# Bill Butler: Schooling of Children of Asylum Seekers-That the Parliament notes with concern the requirement contained within the Nationality, Immigration and Asylum Act 2002, which stipulates that the children of asylum seekers be schooled outwith the mainstream local authority education system in purpose-built accommodation centres; fears that such a condition, which requires separate educational provision for the children of asylum seekers, will in effect lead to segregation rather than integration; acknowledges that the present arrangement, as evidenced by a recent survey conducted in Glasgow, is both inclusive and successful, and considers that the Scottish Executive should make representations to the Home Secretary, the purpose of which would be to arrive at an agreement whereby the current distinctive approach pertaining to the schooling of asylum seeker children in Scotland is allowed to continue.
