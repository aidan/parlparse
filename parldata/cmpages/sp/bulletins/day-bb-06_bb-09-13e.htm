Business Bulletin No. 126/2006: Wednesday 13 September 2006
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 12 September 2006

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-28273 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has a policy of supporting the installation of fingerprint systems in schools and, if so, what the objective is of this policy.

S2W-28274 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it provides funding for the installation of fingerprint systems in schools and, if so, how much has been spent on this in the last three financial years.

S2W-28275 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that parental consent should be required before schools take the fingerprints of pupils.

S2W-28276 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what correspondence it has had with (a) schools and (b) local authorities regarding the use of fingerprint systems in schools.

S2W-28277 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what correspondence it has had with the (a) UK Government and (b) UK Information Commissioner regarding the use of fingerprint systems in schools.

S2W-28278 Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether carer’s allowance and pension credit should be included in the assessment of an individual’s income for the purpose of calculating entitlement to legal aid.

S2W-28279 Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive why recipients of income support or income-based jobseeker’s allowance qualify automatically for free advice and assistance from the Scottish Legal Aid Board, whereas those in receipt of pension credit do not.

S2W-28280 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive how many responses it has received to its consultation on the future position of Careers Scotland.

S2W-28281 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many retail shop closures there have been in the Scottish Borders in each year since 1999 and what the estimated year-on-year economic losses have been to local communities as a result of these closures.

S2W-28282 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how much has been raised in business rates from the premises of firms in the Scottish Borders with a rateable value of below £8,000 in each year since 1999.

S2W-28283 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how much has been raised in business rates from the premises of firms in the Scottish Borders with a rateable value of between £7,999 and £15,000 in each year since 1999.

S2W-28284 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many redundancies there have been in the Scottish Borders since January 1998.

S2W-28285 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many of its employees resided in the Scottish Borders region in each year since 1999.

S2W-28286 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many ministerial visits have taken place in each year since 2002, broken down by local authority area.

S2W-28287 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many economic task forces it has established in each year since 1999.

S2W-28288 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on how many accidents were recorded on construction sites in (a) the Scottish Borders and (b) Scotland in each year since 1999.

S2W-28289 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many accidents were recorded by accident and emergency units to have taken place in the home in (a) the Scottish Borders and (b) Scotland in each year since 1999.

S2W-28290 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how much funding has been paid by Scottish Enterprise Borders and its predecessor organisations directly to local firms in each year since 1999.

S2W-28291 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many accidents involving children under 16 occurred in the home in (a) the Scottish Borders and (b) Scotland in each year since 1999.

S2W-28292 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many retail shop closures there have been in East Lothian in each year since 1999 and what the estimated year-on-year economic losses have been to local communities as a result of these closures.

S2W-28293 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how much has been raised in business rates from the premises of firms in East Lothian with a rateable value of below £8,000 in each year since 1999.

S2W-28294 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how much has been raised in business rates from the premises of firms in East Lothian with a rateable value of between £7,999 and £15,000 in each year since 1999.

S2W-28295 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many redundancies there have been in East Lothian since January 1998.

S2W-28296 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on how many accidents were recorded on construction sites in East Lothian in each year since 1999.

S2W-28297 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many accidents were recorded by accident and emergency units to have taken place in the home in East Lothian in each year since 1999.

S2W-28298 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many accidents involving children under 16 occurred in the home in East Lothian in each year since 1999.

S2W-28299 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many firearms certificates have been revoked in each police force area in each year since 1999.

S2W-28300 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many shotgun certificates were revoked in each police force area in each year since 1999.

S2W-28301 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many armed police raids have taken place in each police force area in each year since 1999.

S2W-28302 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many incidents at which armed police have attended have resulted in officers discharging their firearms in each year since 1999, broken down by police force area.

S2W-28303 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many illegal firearms have been recovered in each year since 1999, broken down by police force area.

S2W-28304 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many vacant consultant posts were filled by locums on (a) 1 September 2005 and (b) 1 September 2006, broken down by NHS board.

S2W-28305 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what skills will be sought from candidates in the recruitment process for a new chairman of the Crofters Commission.

S2W-28306 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is progressing with implementing the recommendations of Chronic Pain Services in Scotland.

S2W-28307 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-27870 by Mr Andy Kerr on 11 September 2006, how many pain clinics there are in each NHS board.

S2W-28308 Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive how it interprets the recommendation of the Kerr report that effective long-term condition management should be based on a generic approach geared to the management of the totality of a person’s needs, in the community and without hospitalisation wherever possible, and how this is implemented to provide adequate chronic pain self-management programmes.

S2W-28309 Mr Jim Wallace: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made towards implementation of the Partnership Agreement commitment progressively to introduce a scheme of national bus, rail and ferry concessionary travel for young people, initially for all in full-time education or training.

S2W-28310 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has sought a derogation from EC Regulation 853/2004 for Scottish minced beef.

S2W-28311 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-27675 by Ross Finnie on 29 August 2006, what bearing the decision to set aside the convictions referred to will have on those who, as a result of these convictions, also had penalties applied to their single farm payments for breaching subsidy guidelines.

S2W-28312 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-27675 by Ross Finnie on 29 August 2006, what bearing the decision to set aside the convictions referred to will have on those who were wrongly convicted during the reference period for the single farm payment.

S2W-28313 Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to S2W-27685 by Mr Tom McCabe on 7 September 2006, why no budget was set for the criminal history system replacement project, given that it followed the Prince methodology.

S2W-28314 Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to S2W-27685 by Mr Tom McCabe on 7 September 2006, who made the decision that the criminal history system replacement project could proceed beyond the planning stage without a budget being set.

S2W-28315 Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to S2W-27685 by Mr Tom McCabe on 7 September 2006, what additional controls and reporting mechanisms were put in place to compensate for the lack of a designated budget against which to monitor project spending in respect of the criminal history system replacement project.

S2W-28316 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive what trends are evident in the incidence of plagiocephaly in Scotland and what causes underlie these trends.

S2W-28317 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive what treatment is available within the NHS for plagiocephaly, broken down by NHS board area.

S2W-28318 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive when the First Minister intends to reply to my letter of 10 August 2006 regarding the subject "The Public Interest".
