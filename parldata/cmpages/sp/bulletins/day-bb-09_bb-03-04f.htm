Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (♦).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

New and altered motions and amendments

*S3M-3609 Johann Lamont: Scottish Government Failures—That the Parliament notes that SNP manifesto promises have been broken on a wide range of issues including health, housing, community safety and education; further notes the absence of a credible strategy to address the needs of people facing difficult economic circumstances and to tackle poverty and disadvantage; regrets that the Scottish Government prefers to focus its attention on the powers it does not have in order to pursue its party’s agenda of separation, and urges the Scottish Government to examine how it might effectively use the powers at its disposal to meet the needs of people by sustaining economic activity and employment and supporting communities across Scotland.

*S3M-3608 Kenneth Gibson: Ayrshire Macmillan Ayrshire Appeal—That the Parliament congratulates Macmillan Cancer Support on the official launch of the Ayrshire Macmillan Money Matters Service on 3 March 2009; notes that even before the official launch, since opening in November 2008, 164 people with cancer and their families in Ayrshire have secured over £183,000 in additional benefits; recognises that Macmillan now runs 26 such centres across Scotland and, to date, has generated some £30 million in additional benefits, successfully assisting some 76% of all those who have called on their services; acknowledges that Macmillan also awards small hardship grants totalling around £1.5 million a year in Scotland for some 4,000 people; is aware that Ayrshire Money Matters was established with the support of East Ayrshire, North Ayrshire and South Ayrshire councils, NHS Ayrshire and Arran and the Pensions Service, with £300,000 from Macmillan itself to fund full-time welfare rights workers in each local authority area; commends the excellent work that Macmillan does in providing information on all aspects of finance, and wishes Macmillan Cancer Support every success in raising £500,000 through its Ayrshire Appeal to support the Ayrshire Macmillan Money Matters Service for the next three years and beyond.

*S3M-3607 Jamie Hepburn: 60th Anniversary of NATO—That the Parliament notes that 4 April 2009 marks the 60th anniversary of the founding of the North Atlantic Treaty Organisation (NATO); recognises that the continued presence of NATO serves as a destabilising factor in the West’s relationship with Russia; notes that NATO relies heavily on the continued use of nuclear weapons as part of its operational capacity; recalls that the first Secretary General of NATO, Lord Ismay, described the organisation’s purpose as being "to keep the Russians out, the Americans in, and the Germans down"; further recognises that the world has fundamentally changed since the founding of NATO in 1949, and, given that the Cold War is meant to be over, believes that the organisation has no useful purpose in the modern world.

*S3M-3606 Christine Grahame: Cruelty of Snaring Exposed—That the Parliament considers that authenticated footage, filmed on Tuesday 24 February 2009 and published by Advocates for Animals, showing a young badger caught in a legal snare has demonstrated that these traps are cruel, distressing and indiscriminate; notes that disturbance of the surrounding area and the fact that the badger had almost chewed through the steel cable of the snare, injuring its mouth in the process, indicated that it had been struggling desperately for many hours before its discovery; believes that had this badger, a protected species, not been found, it might have died a lingering and agonising death; believes that this incident is not isolated but is proof that regulating snares will not work, and therefore urges the Scottish Government to legislate to ban snares without delay.

*S3M-3605 Jackie Baillie: Citizens of the Year—That the Parliament congratulates Barbara Barnes and Elizabeth Hartley on winning the Vale and Dumbarton citizens of the year respectively; applauds Barbara Barnes for her work with various groups within the area, including Macmillan Cancer Support and Alzheimer Scotland’s Carers Group among others; notes the contribution that she has made to charity, taking part in many fund-raising events such as parachute jumps, zip slides and fire walking; praises Elizabeth Hartley on running a companion club for pensioners and her personal care for an elderly friend, despite her own ill-health, and welcomes the deserved recognition bestowed on these two model citizens by the local community.

*S3M-3604 Michael Matheson: Time to Abandon GB Football Team—That the Parliament notes the comments of FIFA President Sepp Blatter that the independence of separate Scottish, Welsh, Northern Irish and English football teams will be jeopardised if the UK Government persists with a Great Britain football team for the 2012 London Olympics; further notes that the 1908 London Olympics provide a precedent for all four home nations to be allowed to participate in their own right; considers that it is time to stop any pretence that a GB team will not have an effect on the status of the four associations and to take heed of the comments of the President of FIFA, who stated on 9 March 2008, "if you start to put together a combined team for the Olympic Games, the question will automatically come up that there are four different associations so how can they play in one team", and calls for the UK Government to start listening to the Scottish Football Association, FIFA and the Tartan Army on this matter.

*S3M-3603♦ Willie Coffey: Survey of Scottish Manufactured Exports 2007/08—That the Parliament welcomes the publication by the Scottish Council for Development and Industry (SCDI) of its Survey of Scottish Manufactured Exports 2007/08; notes with concern the finding reported in the survey that Scotland’s manufacturing exports continue to lag behind their peak of almost £20 billion in 1999; welcomes the signs of increased diversity of exports, with a rise of over 8% in non-electronics exports in 2007 and with food products and beverages performing particularly strongly at a 12% increase compared with a decline in total exports of 2.4%; notes that the current economic downturn is having an adverse impact on companies engaged in manufacturing, including Vesuvius, Mahle, and Diageo, which all operate in the Kilmarnock and Loudoun constituency; commends the SCDI for its commitment to assisting Scottish manufacturing, including its support for the recently launched BusinessClub Scotland, and welcomes the First Minister’s undertaking to have the Council of Economic Advisers examine how best Scotland’s manufacturing companies can be supported through and beyond the current recession.

*S3M-3602 Bruce Crawford on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Designation of Lead Committee—That the Parliament agrees that the Health and Sport Committee be designated as the lead committee in consideration of the Tobacco and Primary Medical Services (Scotland) Bill at Stage 1.

*S3M-3601 Bruce Crawford on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Business Motion—That the Parliament agrees that consideration of the Sexual Offences (Scotland) Bill at Stage 2 be completed by 1 May 2009.

*S3M-3599 Willie Coffey: Ayrshire Employment Task Force—That the Parliament recognises that the economic downturn has been felt strongly across Ayrshire, particularly in the manufacturing and retail sectors; welcomes the establishment of the Ayrshire Employment Task Force through which a range of agencies will be working together, including the three Ayrshire councils, JobcentrePlus, Scottish Enterprise, Business Gateway, Ayrshire Chamber of Commerce and Industry, the Federation of Small Businesses and Kilmarnock College; notes that the task force will gather and share information about companies that are experiencing trading difficulties to ensure that effective, coordinated support is made available to those businesses and their employees; further welcomes the business summit to be held in the Park Hotel, Kilmarnock, on Tuesday 24 March, and congratulates all of the agencies for their commitment to working together in the interests of Ayrshire’s communities.

*S3M-3598 Willie Coffey: East Ayrshire Sports Council Awards—That the Parliament congratulates the winners of the East Ayrshire Sports Council Awards for 2009; notes the high standard of winners, including Scott Connelly who won Disabled Sportsperson of the Year and has been involved for over 14 years with the Sports for Special Needs club, which was awarded Sports Club of the Year, Lyndsay McCreath of the Aquarius Gymnastics Club who won the Jim Higgins Youth Sports Personality of the Year award and Peter Kirkbride of Kilmarnock Amateur Weightlifting Club who won the Individual Performance of the Year award; recognises in particular the contribution of Eddie Cassidy, recipient of the award for Service to Sport after 52 years’ involvement with the Cluaran Kwai Judo Club, and commends all winners and nominees for their determination, dedication and commitment to fair play.

*S3M-3597 Margaret Mitchell: Drivers’ Awareness Evening—That the Parliament congratulates Strathclyde Police and the Wishaw Crime Prevention Panel on their recent drivers’ awareness evening held at a local garage and designed to be particularly useful to female drivers; recognises that the evening’s presentation was designed to increase awareness of potentially dangerous situations, focusing on areas such as planning a journey, road rage and safe parking as well as practical car checks including tyre pressure and treads, lights, oil and engine fluids and changing tyres; notes that the feedback from the evening has been very positive, and welcomes the fact that another event has been planned for later in March.

*S3M-3596 Shirley-Anne Somerville: Leith Docks to Retain Name—That the Parliament welcomes the decision by Forth Ports to drop its plans to rebrand part of the waterfront development at Leith Docks as Edinburgh Harbour; recognises the historical significance of the Port of Leith and the strong and distinct identity of the area, and welcomes the decision of Forth Ports to listen to the opinion of the thousands of Leith residents who ran a successful campaign against the name change.

*S3M-3595 Willie Coffey: See Me Campaign—That the Parliament notes that one in four of the population will experience mental health problems, while many more will suffer from poor mental wellbeing, at some time in their lifes; commends those companies signing up to tackle discrimination against those with mental ill-health; notes that the recent NHS Ayrshire and Arran annual workplace conference had the theme of mental wellbeing in the workplace; welcomes the news that, following the conference, a further nine firms, including Johnnie Walker whisky makers Diageo and Kilmarnock firm New Horizons Creative Solutions, signed up for the see me campaign, bringing the number of Ayrshire firms that have signed up to 26, and endorses the message from Suzie Vestri, director of the see me campaign, that we can all play a part in stamping out the stigma of mental ill-health.

*S3M-3594 Gil Paterson: Thanks to Scottish Gymnastics—That the Parliament congratulates all the coaches, volunteers and others from the world of Scottish gymnastics who participated in the Beginner and Intermediate Floor and Vault Competition for girls aged from eight to 16, held at the Bell’s Sports Centre in Perth, which attracted individuals and teams from Shetland to Dumfries and all points in between and thanks all those who give up their time to ensure that events such as this are a success but, more importantly, to provide the expertise and advice on a long-term basis to encourage our young sports enthusiasts, which assists in keeping Scotland’s young people fit in body and mind.

*S3M-3593 Jack McConnell: Sick Kids Hospital Edinburgh New Pyjamas Campaign—That the Parliament welcomes the launch of the new pyjamas campaign, which aims to raise a £15 million contribution to the new Sick Kids hospital in Edinburgh; notes that all money raised by the new pyjamas campaign will pay for equipment and services that are not covered by the NHS budget; further notes that this will supply the hospital with equipment that is at the forefront of paediatric healthcare; welcomes that money raised by the campaign will also be used in a variety of ways to support parents and patients; commends the new pyjamas campaign on breaking new ground for charities by building a social networking site on the fundraising website, www.newpyjamas.org, so that the campaign will be able to contact an ever-increasing number of potential supporters of the new Sick Kids hospital, and wishes the campaign every success with its fundraising.

*S3M-3592 Shirley-Anne Somerville: Eco-driving Challenge—That the Parliament welcomes the Energy Saving Trusts’ Eco-driving initiative; notes that the three-year-long campaign aims to inform drivers of more economical driving techniques that result in energy savings and reduced fuel consumption; recognises the benefit of taking small steps to save energy in our everyday lives in addition to cutting fuel costs and promoting safer driving, and encourages people to take their own virtual eco-drive at www.energysavingtrust.org.uk/scotland.

*S3M-3591 Bob Doris: Rebuilding the Botany—That the Parliament notes that, eight years ago, tenants in the Botany area in Maryhill were decanted from their homes with the promise of returning to new-build homes within a few years; recognises the frustration of tenants and the local housing association at the delays in delivering these homes; therefore congratulates in particular Willy Briody and Donna Birrell, of Maryhill Housing Association, and Patricia Wilson, the tenant chair of the Botany tenants’ consultation group, in overcoming the many financial and logistical problems faced in pursuing delivery of these new homes, and also congratulates them on the ground-breaking ceremony, held on 4 March 2009, marking the start of work to deliver 35 new-build homes to tenants.

*S3M-3590 John Lamont: Firefighter Safety and Data Collection—That the Parliament applauds the selfless commitment of firefighters to their role in what can so often be very dangerous and extreme circumstances; notes with concern the increased number of firefighter deaths as highlighted in the Fire Brigades Union report, In the Line of Duty; further notes the lack of safety-critical operational guidance for fire authorities highlighted in that report and the deficiency of data collection in respect of firefighter deaths and other serious incidents, and calls for greater clarity and standardisation in the recording and investigation of firefighter fatalities and injuries and for more comprehensive safety guidance for fire authorities.

Supported by: Jackson Carlaw*, Bill Aitken*, Sandra White*, Mary Scanlon*, Jamie Hepburn*, John Scott*, Jamie McGrigor*, Murdo Fraser*

*S3M-3588 Kenneth Gibson: Swimathon 2009—That the Parliament encourages active participation in Swimathon 2009, the world’s biggest swimming fundraising event, organised by Marie Curie Cancer Care; notes that this event will take place from 16 to 19 April 2009 at over 500 pools across the United Kingdom; recognises that over the last two years 30,000 swimmers took part, raising over £3 million, 70% of which went to help fund the work of more than 2,000 Marie Curie nurses who provide care for terminally ill people, the remainder going to the Swimathon Foundation to promote the health benefits of and increased participation in swimming for people of all ages, and acknowledges that further information can be found at www.swimathon.org.

S3M-3559*#* Kenneth Gibson: The Future of the Sunday Mail and Daily Record—That the Parliament notes with deep concern the decision of Trinity Mirror to merge the editorial content of the Daily Record and Sunday Mail with the loss of up to 30% of journalistic staff, based primarily in Glasgow and the West of Scotland; recognises that both newspapers are an integral part of Scottish culture and that to diminish their individual identities would be a serious blow to the diversity, vibrancy and quality of the Scottish media; believes that the loss of their separate identities would undoubtedly lead to the erosion of circulation at both titles, leading inevitably to further job losses; considers that Trinity Mirror is highly profitable and that there is no need to undertake such drastic restructuring, and therefore very much hopes that good sense may prevail over the future of two of Scotland’s leading titles.

Supported by: Jamie Hepburn*
