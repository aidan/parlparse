Business Bulletin No. 72/2002

Friday 3 May 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-3065 Karen Whitefield: Airdrieonians Football Club—That the Parliament expresses deep regret at the news that Airdrieonians Football Club has been placed into full liquidation; recognises the important part that the club has played in maintaining a sense of community in Airdrie and surrounding villages since its inception in 1878; commends the supporters of the club who have endeavoured to keep the club alive over the past two years, and wishes the supporters and members of the provisional supporters’ trust every success in their efforts to keep a footballing identity within the town.

S1M-3046# Mr Mike Rumbles: Prescription Charges—That the Parliament recognises that the criteria used to confer exemption from prescription charges were agreed in 1968; notes that since then many more conditions have been recognised as lifelong and life-threatening; agrees that the state has a responsibility towards those who suffer from these types of condition, and *considers that the Scottish Executive should* carry out immediately a review of the list of conditions that are exempt from prescription charges.
