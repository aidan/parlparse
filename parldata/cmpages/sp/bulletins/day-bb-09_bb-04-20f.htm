Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (♦).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S3M-3921 Christine Grahame: Scottish SPCA’s Rehoming Week—That the Parliament welcomes and supports the Scottish SPCA’s Rehoming Week from 20 to 26 April 2009, which hopes to increase awareness of the variety of animals that are in care and in need of homes; recognises that the Scottish SPCA, a purely Scottish charity, is the only animal welfare charity that rescues every kind of animal in Scotland, including dogs, cats, rabbits and less common pets such as reptiles, rodents, birds and exotic animals, and notes that rehoming is predicated on suitable and responsible ownership.

*S3M-3920 Bill Kidd: Carers’ Poverty, Enough is Enough—That the Parliament welcomes the carers’ poverty protest on 22 April 2009; considers that the Carer’s Allowance, at £53.10 per week, is the lowest of the earnings replacement benefits; initially calls on the UK Government to make an urgent increase to this allowance to bring it more in line with other earnings replacements benefits, and calls for a wider review of the benefits for carers and, in particular, the position of pensioners with regard to the overlapping rule between the Carer’s Allowance and the state retirement pension.

*S3M-3919 Johann Lamont: Congratulations to James Lillis, Winner of the 4th STUC Helen Dowie Award for Lifelong Learning—That the Parliament congratulates James Lillis of Unite the Union on winning the prestigious Scottish Trades Union Congress (STUC) Helen Dowie Award for Lifelong Learning in recognition of his tireless work in supporting and encouraging others to participate in learning; notes in particular his immense contribution to supporting a learning culture in his own workplace at First Glasgow; celebrates his commitment to integration in the workplace and his key role in helping migrant workers integrate into the broader community; appreciates his efforts in partnership with Unite the Union, First Glasgow, Renfrewshire Buddies, Adam Smith College and the learners themselves in making his workplace learning centre a success; salutes James Lillis as an inspiration in making possible for his fellow workers the reality of learning and community involvement, and congratulates the STUC and trade unionists across Scotland on the priority that they give to learning in the workplace.

Supported by: Helen Eadie*, Rhona Brankin*, Dr Richard Simpson*, Elaine Murray*, Claire Baker*, Marlyn Glen*, James Kelly*, Mary Mulligan*, Mr Frank McAveety*, Robin Harper*, Jackie Baillie*, Bill Butler*, John Park*

*S3M-3918 Patricia Ferguson: Lambhill Stables—That the Parliament notes the groundbreaking ceremony that took place at Lambhill Stables on 16 April 2009; also notes the innovative environmental-based learning that the project seeks to make available to the local community, and wishes the project well in its bid to obtain funding to allow the restoration of the historic Lambhill Stables.

Supported by: Mary Mulligan*, Bill Butler*, Mr Frank McAveety*

*S3M-3917 Patricia Ferguson: Proposed School Closures by Glasgow City Council—That the Parliament applauds the Save Our Schools campaign mounted by the parents of the five schools proposed for closure in the Glasgow Maryhill constituency; acknowledges the detrimental impact that such closures would have on local communities throughout the area; considers the arguments against closure to be compelling and well made, and hopes that, even at this late stage in the process, those arguments will prevail at the council meeting on 23 April 2009.

Supported by: Bill Butler*, Elaine Smith*, Kenneth Gibson*, Robert Brown*

*S3M-3916 Robert Brown: School Closures by Glasgow City Council’s Labour Administration—That the Parliament is appalled at the decision of Glasgow City Council’s Labour administration to recommend the closure of 20 primary and nursery schools across the city and to disregard the views of local communities, particularly in Wyndford and Cadder, which continue to campaign so persuasively against the closures; notes the apparent abandonment by the council of the commitment in its single outcome agreement to provide parents and carers with access to high quality education and childcare within "buggy pushing distance" of their homes; recognises the need to reduce under-occupancy in city schools but considers that this should have been done on a basis that builds on local communities and retains a community school comprising both non-denominational and denominational traditions, and urges all councillors to support this approach at the council meeting on 23 April 2009.

Supported by: Ross Finnie*, Robin Harper*, Bill Kidd*

*S3M-3915 Stuart McMillan: Inverclyde Schools Nominated for Honours at the 2009 Scottish Education Awards—That the Parliament congratulates the Inverclyde schools of Notre Dame High School and St Kenneth’s Primary School, both in Greenock, for being nominated for the forthcoming 2009 Scottish Education Awards, which take place in Glasgow on Tuesday 16 June; notes that Gerard Gordon, a principal music teacher at Notre Dame High School, has been selected as a finalist for Teacher of the Year and that staff and pupils from St Kenneth’s Primary School have been nominated for the Health and Wellbeing Award to celebrate their proactive and successful approach to improving the health of children; commends all those who have been involved in receiving nominations, and wishes them the very best at the awards ceremony in Glasgow.

Supported by: Alasdair Allan*, Bill Kidd*, Robin Harper*

*S3M-3914 Karen Whitefield: Shotts Shakers Success—That the Parliament congratulates members of the Shotts Shakers cheerleading group for winning the Reclaim Cheer Cup, a competition organised by Sidekix; recognises the important role of the Getting Better Together project in supporting the group; notes that the Shotts Shakers received their trophy on 9 April 2009 from Ally McCoist at the Reclaim Award Ceremony in the Mitchell Theatre Glasgow, and recognises the success of the Sidekix programme in providing diversionary activities for young people.

*S3M-3913 Jamie Hepburn: Public Opposition to Privacy Infringement—That the Parliament notes the results of a recent survey, conducted on behalf of the Metro newspaper by www.PoliticsHome.com, which indicates that 60% of those surveyed disapprove of a new EU directive that requires that all communications, including phone calls, emails and internet usage, are recorded and stored for one year and that 58% of those surveyed agreed with the statement that "There is no such thing as secure data storage. It inevitably gets into the wrong hands"; believes that the concerns of the general public are well placed considering a spate of data blunders by UK Government departments in recent years, which includes the loss in 2007 of data discs that contained the personal details of 25 million people; believes that the UK Government must take heed of the growing public concern at moves to curb personal privacy and individual civil liberties, and calls on the UK Government to work against efforts at wide-scale monitoring of personal communications.

Supported by: Bill Kidd*, Robin Harper*, Christina McKelvie*, Kenneth Gibson*

*S3M-3912 Dr Richard Simpson: Congratulations to Scottish School of Primary Care—That the Parliament congratulates Professor Frank Sullivan and colleagues from the Scottish School of Primary Care, Scotland’s collaborative primary care research vehicle, for being awarded the British Medical Journal’s research paper of the year for a paper on the treatment of Bell’s palsy, published in the New England Journal of Medicine, and notes that the research will benefit all those who contract this potentially disfiguring condition across the world.

*S3M-3911 Jackson Carlaw: Beatson Pebble Appeal—That the Parliament notes that 54 people are diagnosed with cancer every day in the west of Scotland alone; further notes the outstanding work being done by the Beatson Institute and the Beatson West of Scotland Oncology Centre in researching cancer and treating cancer patients respectively; acknowledges the requirement for a Translational Research Centre to bridge the gap between these two existing facilities and finalise the Glasgow Centre for Cancer Research, the largest comprehensive cancer centre in Scotland, serving almost two-thirds of the population; congratulates The Evening Times on its Beatson Pebble Appeal campaign, designed to raise £10 million for this new research centre, and encourages as many people as possible to back the campaign by visiting www.beatsonpebbleappeal.com to ensure that the new research centre can become both a reality and a key player in speeding up the process of finding new drugs and better methods of prevention and diagnosis in the international battle against cancer.

*S3M-3910 Cathy Jamieson: National Osteoporosis Society Manifesto for Scotland—That the Parliament welcomes the publication of the National Osteoporosis Society’s manifesto for Scotland; is concerned that osteoporosis is a long-term condition that is currently putting around 250,000 people in Scotland at risk of painful fractures, which are costly for the NHS to treat; notes that much can be done to prevent these fractures through the proper identification, treatment and care of people who have osteoporosis; considers that a fracture liaison service linked to every hospital that receives fragility fractures would ensure that every fragility fracture patient gets the treatment and care that they need; calls for coherent clinical guidance on the prevention, diagnosis and care of falls, fragility fractures and osteoporosis; also calls for the quality and systems of care for people at risk of falls, fragility fractures and osteoporosis to be measured on a regular basis to stimulate best practice; further calls for healthcare professionals working in primary care to be offered meaningful financial incentives to identify and treat people at a high risk of fragility fractures, and finally calls for measures to improve understanding of bone health among individuals of all ages and especially among children in primary and secondary school settings.

*S3M-3909 Marilyn Livingstone: Kofi Annan’s Visit to Scotland—That the Parliament welcomes Kofi Annan, former Secretary-General of the United Nations, to Scotland and in particular to Adam Smith College in Kirkcaldy on invitation from Gordon Brown MP, the college chancellor, to deliver the 2009 Adam Smith Lecture; recognises Mr Annan’s achievements to date, which include being Secretary-General of the United Nations from 1997 to 2006, receiving a Nobel Peace Prize in 2001 for his work and currently being on the board of directors of the United Nations Foundation, a public charity that builds and implements public private partnerships to address the world’s most pressing problems; congratulates the coordinated efforts of the Adam Smith College and the Post Secondary International Network in organising the lecture and securing Mr Annan’s attendance; recognises that the Adam Smith Lecture, designed to commemorate the life and work of the world’s first economist, as one of Scotland’s key events, and commends the Adam Smith College for delivering excellent learning opportunities for the people of Fife and beyond.

Supported by: Elaine Murray*

*S3M-3904 Brian Adam: Neil MacCormick, 1941 to 2009—That the Parliament notes the death of Professor Sir Neil MacCormick, MEP for Scotland from 1999 to 2004 and Regius Professor of Public Law and the Law of Nature and Nations at Edinburgh University from 1972 to 2008, and pays tribute to the significant contribution that he made to promoting Scotland’s interests in Europe and to his international standing as one of the world’s leading legal scholars.

*S3M-3903 Robert Brown: Mental Health Action Week 2009—That the Parliament supports Mental Health Action Week 2009, run by the Mental Health Foundation from 12 to 18 April; notes that this year’s campaign is raising awareness about the impact that fear and anxiety can have on our lives; expresses concern that up to one in 10 people in Scotland experience a problem with anxiety and that one in four will experience a serious problem with mental wellbeing at some point in life; welcomes the availability of the free campaign action pack from the Mental Health Foundation, and calls on the Scottish Government to support community mental health facilities to ensure that throughout Scotland everyone has access to adequate support facilities and resources at times when fear and anxiety is having a debilitating impact on lives.

*S3M-3900 Robin Harper: Marine and Coastal Litter Strategy—That the Parliament welcomes the launch of the Marine Conservation Society 2008 Beachwatch report; expresses alarm that 2,581 items of litter were recorded per kilometre of Scottish beach surveyed, the highest average of any UK country; acknowledges that, despite many voluntary efforts and schemes such as Fishing for Litter, the problem of marine debris continues to damage the Scottish marine and coastal environment; notes that the US Commission on Ocean Policy recognised marine debris as a serious threat to marine resources and human health and that US legislation tasks the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration and the United States Coast Guard to deal specifically with these dangers; understands that marine debris does not obey political boundaries, and therefore calls on the Scottish Government to ensure that a comprehensive marine and coastal litter strategy is developed in partnership with appropriate agencies across the United Kingdom in order to tackle this scourge of Scotland’s coastlines.

Supported by: Christopher Harvie*, Hugh O’Donnell*, Bill Kidd*, Des McNulty*, Cathy Peattie*, Elaine Murray*

*S3M-3899 Ken Macintosh: No to Compulsory Redundancies at the Daily Record and Sunday Mail—That the Parliament expresses its strong concern at the decision by Trinity Mirror to issue compulsory redundancies to some staff at the Daily Record and Sunday Mail despite a number of voluntary redundancies already having been agreed; notes the strong circulation of both these Scottish titles and their highly profitable contribution to the financial strength of the parent company; notes too the contribution of the Daily Record and Sunday Mail to Scottish public life; worries that drastic cuts in staff to both papers will affect the quality and breadth of their coverage of Scottish affairs, and calls on the management of Trinity Mirror to reinstate those individuals identified for compulsory redundancy, keeping the door open for voluntary agreement instead, and to negotiate constructively with the National Union of Journalists on the future of these vital Scottish newspapers.

S3M-3894*#* Cathy Jamieson: RNID Hearing Matters Campaign—That the Parliament welcomes RNID Scotland’s campaign, Hearing Matters; notes that there are 758,000 deaf and hard of hearing people in Scotland, the majority of whom are aged over 55; further notes that it takes people on average 10 to 15 years to address hearing loss and that there are around 350,000 people in Scotland who could benefit from a hearing aid but are not currently using one; considers that some GPs in the NHS Ayrshire and Arran area are screening adults for their hearing in the community rather than making a straight referral to audiology or ENT, and believes that hearing loss should be identified and treated at the earliest opportunity and the introduction of hearing screening on the NHS for those aged 55 and over considered.

S3M-3559# Kenneth Gibson: The Future of the Sunday Mail and Daily Record—That the Parliament notes with deep concern the decision of Trinity Mirror to merge the editorial content of the Daily Record and Sunday Mail with the loss of up to 30% of journalistic staff, based primarily in Glasgow and the west of Scotland; recognises that both newspapers are an integral part of Scottish culture and that to diminish their individual identities would be a serious blow to the diversity, vibrancy and quality of the Scottish media; believes that the loss of their separate identities would undoubtedly lead to the erosion of circulation at both titles, leading inevitably to further job losses; considers that Trinity Mirror is highly profitable and that there is no need to undertake such drastic restructuring, and therefore very much hopes that good sense may prevail over the future of two of Scotland’s leading titles.
