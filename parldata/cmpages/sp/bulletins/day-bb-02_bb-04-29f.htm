Business Bulletin No. 68/2002

Monday 29 April 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-3044# Helen Eadie: Westfield Development Proposals-That the Parliament applauds the Scottish Executive's determination to produce an effective and sustainable National Waste Strategy and the First Minister's support for "environmental justice" principles; notes that environmental justice principles were promoted by Dr Robert Bullard following a study of waste disposal in the USA; considers that Dr Bullard's conclusions highlighted the fact that the poorest areas of a nation carried the major burden of "toxic pollution"; further notes that, in any implementation plans for recycling and waste disposal, the poorest and most deprived communities should not become "dumping grounds" for other major areas of Scotland; expresses concern at potential health issues, and regrets that current proposals for Fife's Westfield Recycling initiative by Alba will have major adverse implications for the surrounding towns and villages as a result of heavily increased road traffic pressurising in an unacceptable way already over-committed local authority roads budgets, further endangering numerous weak bridges scattered right across Fife, adding new traffic volumes to an already severely congested Forth Rail Bridge and causing unacceptably high levels of noise pollution and diminished air quality and visual amenity to a countryside that has the potential for a major green-field project similar to the widely acclaimed land restoration project at Lochore Meadows, which has been in place for more than 20 years and was a former major pit bing site.

*S1M-3043 Fiona Hyslop: European Parliamentary Report on Multi-Annual Guidance Programme for Fishing Fleets-That the Parliament congratulates the SNP members of the European Parliament and their European Free Alliance Colleagues on their successful amendment in the European Parliament on 25 April 2002 to the parliamentary report on multi-annual guidance programme for fishing fleets which instructs the President of the European Parliament "to forward its resolution to the governments and parliaments in the Member States", ensuring that the Scottish Parliament will be informed directly of this report; notes that this sets a precedent for this Parliament to be informed of further important deliberations in the European Parliament as of right; further notes that the proposal was opposed by the Scottish Labour MEP Catherine Stihler at the fisheries committee meeting in March 2002, and welcomes the volte-face of Scottish Labour MEPs in the plenary session.

*S1M-3042 Mr Gil Paterson: Sexual Assaults-That the Parliament notes the increase in the number of sexual assaults carried out in 2001; welcomes the Lord Advocate's Review on the definition of rape and the conclusion which it reached which now firmly places rape law around consent; further notes the announcement by Harriet Harman of the intention to introduce a national network of specialist rape prosecutors in England and Wales, and calls on the Scottish Executive to look at conducting an overarching review of the current procedures set up to deal with sexual assault cases and of all legislation covering sexual assault cases in Scotland in order to combat current trends in sexual assault cases as changes should not be carried out in a piecemeal manner.

*S1M-3041# Rhona Brankin: Scottish Mining Museum-That the Parliament recognises the importance of the industrial museum sector and, with the closure of the last deep mine in Scotland at Longannet, the crucial role played by the Scottish Mining Museum at Newtongrange in preserving the heritage of former coal mining communities.

*S1M-3040 Mr Duncan Hamilton: Criminal Record Checks for Students-That the Parliament supports the Association of Scottish Colleges (ASC) in its condemnation of Scottish Executive proposals to impose charges for criminal record checks for students; notes that such checks would be in respect of courses involving work placements with children and vulnerable adults; supports the view that the charges will impact most negatively on students who are unemployed or from disadvantaged backgrounds; recognises the ASC's concerns that the inclusion of students in such a regime could lead to a decline in the availability of people suitable and properly qualified for working with children and vulnerable adults, and suggests that, in order to avoid such a decline, the charges should remain, as they are at present, free

*S1M-3039 Richard Lochhead: Taxation on Oil and Gas Profits-That the Parliament notes the decision by Her Majesty's Government to increase tax on North Sea oil and gas profits by 10% but believes that any provision to this effect should not be introduced until a full economic assessment has been conducted of the impact of any changes on current and future employment and exploration related to North Sea activities.

S1M-3038 Mr Duncan Hamilton: Inverness and District Friends of ARMS-That the Parliament congratulates Inverness and District Friends of ARMS group on its successful bid for NHS Highland funding; notes the group's efforts in the delivery of an invaluable physiotherapy service to Inverness and the rest of the Highlands; welcomes the fact that the provision of an MS specialist nurse will be additional to the direct funding; recognises the part played by the MS Society Scotland, and undertakes to keep the issue of specialised, rural health provision at the forefront of national debate

S1M-3037 Andrew Wilson: Motherwell Football Club-That the Parliament notes with alarm the current crisis at Motherwell Football Club; recognises that it is a symptom of the financial crisis in the professional organisation of Scotland's national game and that the current structure of its organisation is unsustainable; appreciates the importance of Motherwell Football Club to the Scottish game and to the people of Lanarkshire as well as the needs of the employees of the club; acknowledges and further appreciates the many attempts made by the outgoing ownership at Motherwell to invest in the club and the game, but recognises that such investment is unsustainable unless the overall structure of the Scottish professional game is sustainable and viable, and calls for all leaders of Scottish Football to consider a wide-ranging and radical overhaul of the structure of the Scottish game and leagues in order to ensure competitiveness at all levels and the growth of community-based clubs encouraging domestic talent and to ensure that there are opportunities for clubs to grow and progress through the leagues from all levels and, if necessary, to move on to larger, non-domestic leagues if that is to their financial and sporting advantage.

S1M-3036 Brian Adam: A90 Dualling from Tipperty to Balmedie-That the Parliament notes the dangerously high levels of congestion on the A90 north of Aberdeen, the expected increase in traffic from the proposed new housing developments which will rely on this route and the poor accident record, and calls on the Scottish Executive to upgrade the single carriageway Tipperty to Balmedie stretch of the A90 to dual carriageway.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson*

S1M-3035 Alasdair Morgan: PGCE Secondary Courses in Geography, Drama, Biology and Chemistry at Jordanhill Campus-That the Parliament deplores the decision of the Scottish Executive to direct the Scottish Higher Education Funding Council to limit the recruitment of students at Jordanhill Campus of the University of Strathclyde in the areas of geography, drama, biology and chemistry to not more than 85, having the month before indicated to the University that they should expect to recruit some 175 such students, a decision which has resulted in the University issuing 136 legally binding offers for such courses for which they will now receive funding for only 85; notes that the Principal of the University of Strathclyde has described the Executive's decision as "capricious and ill-considered", and calls upon the Executive to review its decision.

S1M-3034# Margaret Jamieson: Meares - Irlen Syndrome-That the Parliament notes that Meares-Irlen syndrome affects up to one-third of all young people and, in the worst cases, has a serious impact on their learning in the early years; further notes that, for many children, its effects can be mitigated by colour overlays or lenses but that the facilities within the Scottish health service to diagnose and treat this syndrome are few and far between, and therefore considers that the Scottish Executive should work with the NHS and the education authorities to ensure early diagnosis of this syndrome and to put in place cost-effective methods of treating it.

S1M-3032# Irene Oldfather: Scotland and Europe Day-That the Parliament is proud to mark Europe Day as a celebration of the founding of the European Union (EU); endorses the fundamental principles of the EU as set out in the Declaration by Robert Schuman on 9 May 1950; welcomes the contribution that the EU has made towards peace and solidarity across Europe; notes the relevance of the EU to the working of the Scottish Parliament and to the everyday lives of Scots, and encourages the people of Scotland, young and old, to join with citizens across Europe, in the celebration of our common values and to participate in the debate on the future of Europe.

Supported by: Trish Godman, Robert Brown*, Sarah Boyack*

S1M-3032.1 Michael Russell: Scotland and Europe Day-As an amendment to motion (S1M-3032) in the name of Irene Oldfather, leave out from "encourages" to end and insert "looks forward to this relevance being enhanced when Scotland participates enthusiastically in the EU on the basis of equality as a member state, with its own direct representation on the Council of Ministers and European Commission."

S1M-3018# John Young: The Links Between Domestic Violence, Child Abuse and Animal Abuse-That the Parliament recognises the groundbreaking research by the Royal (Dick) School of Veterinary Studies at the University of Edinburgh into non-accidental injury (NAI) in companion animals which showed parallels with NAI suffered by children; welcomes the work already done by the Scottish Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals, through its "First Strike Scotland" campaign, to raise awareness of the links between animal abuse and violence towards humans; considers that the Scottish Executive should commission research into (a) the correlation between domestic violence, child abuse and animal abuse and (b) therapeutic programmes to address children's abuse of animals, and believes that a protocol for the reporting of such incidents should be introduced across all respective agencies.

S1M-3017 Fiona McLeod: Removal of Dr Robert Watson from the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change-That the Parliament expresses concern at the removal of Dr Robert Watson as Chair of the UN's Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change following last year's publication of its Third Assessment Report (TAR) which states that global temperatures are rising nearly twice as fast as previously thought, predicts that temperatures could rise by as much as 5.8 degrees celsius by the end of this century and claims that industrial pollution is the main cause; notes that the TAR report has the unqualified backing of more than 3,000 scientists; expresses dismay at President Bush's continued refusal to recognise the link between pollution and global warming; urges the President to reinstate America's support for the Kyoto Protocol, and further notes Scotland's determination to play its full part in tackling the threat of global warming

Supported by: Michael Matheson, Tommy Sheridan, Ms Sandra White*
