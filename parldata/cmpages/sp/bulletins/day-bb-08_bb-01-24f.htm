Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (♦).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S3M-1211 Ted Brocklebank: St Andrew’s Society of Detroit—That the Parliament commends the work of the St Andrew’s Society of Detroit; notes that, since its founding in 1849, it has raised significant funds for charity, currently as much as $40,000 per annum to support worthy causes, including the Erskine Home in Glasgow; acknowledges that the society hosts the longest-running Highland Games in North America, and congratulates it on almost 160 years of celebrating Scotland’s heritage and culture and fostering ties between the old country and our cousins in the United States of America.

*S3M-1210 Nanette Milne: Lift the Ban on Haggis Imports to the United States—That the Parliament notes with regret the continued ban on haggis imports to the United States of America; recognises that expatriate Scots are currently unable to enjoy their native dish; further notes that the US represents a major potential market for Scottish-produced haggis, and calls on the Scottish Government to make representations to the US Government to ensure that the ban is lifted by next Burns Night.

*S3M-1209 Jeremy Purvis: Happy Birthday Radio Borders—That the Parliament congratulates Radio Borders on reaching maturity with the celebrations this week of its 18th birthday; recognises that it is one of the country’s most successful commercial radio stations; notes the important service that Radio Borders has provided over the last 18 years for communities across the borders, and wishes the presenters, production team and all involved with Radio Borders’ operation the best of luck for the future.

*S3M-1208 Michael Matheson: Cowdenhill Quarry, Banknock—That the Parliament applauds the decision by the Scottish Government’s Directorate for Planning and Environmental Appeals, based in Falkirk, to uphold the decision by Falkirk Council not to allow the expansion of the Cowdenhill Quarry in Banknock; believes that this is the right decision; recognises the work done by Banknock Residents Against Cowdenhill Expansion (BRACE) in campaigning against the development over a number of years, and congratulates the people of Banknock who have shown real community spirit in their fight against this development.

*S3M-1207 Ian McKee: Loanhead Community Learning Centre—That the Parliament congratulates Loanhead Community Learning Centre (LCLC), Midlothian, on winning the International Play Association’s Right to Play Award 2008 at an awards ceremony in Hong Kong; applauds the hard work done by staff and volunteers in providing educational, social welfare and recreational opportunities for over 1,000 people who use the centre every week; notes the success of the LCLC in providing the focal point to bring people together from new housing schemes, and wishes it every success in continuing to provide this service well into the future.

*S3M-1206 Ted Brocklebank: Congratulations to AL Kennedy—That the Parliament congratulates Dundee-born writer and former lecturer in creative writing at the University of St Andrews, (Alison) AL Kennedy, on winning this year’s prestigious Costa Book of the Year Award for her fifth novel, Day; notes that the novel triumphed over the books of established authors such as Simon Sebag Montefiore, and endorses the words of the chair of the final judges, Joanna Trollope, that "Day is an example of excellence in its category and a book ultimately to recommend. It is perfectly and beautifully written by an author who is an extraordinary stylist."

*S3M-1205 Cathy Peattie: UN International Year of Languages—That the Parliament welcomes the decision by the United Nations General Assembly to designate 2008 as the International Year of Languages; recognises the role of UNESCO in co-ordinating activities during the year; notes the views of the Director-General of UNESCO, Mr Koichiro Matsuura, that every linguistic community should be encouraged and permitted to use its mother tongue as widely as possible, including within education; calls on the Scottish Executive to mark and celebrate the international year of languages in ways which encourage the use of the Scots and Gaelic languages, and believes that the Executive should respond positively to UNESCO’s call for International Mother Language Day on 21 February 2008 to be used as an appropriate date for the introduction of language initiatives.

*S3M-1204.1 David Stewart: Scottish Biodiversity Strategy Report—As an amendment to motion (S3M-1204) in the name of Michael Russell, insert at end "and calls on the Scottish Government to confirm the commitment to meeting the 2010 EU Gothenburg biodiversity targets."

Supported by: Karen Gillon*

*S3M-1204.2 Jim Hume: Scottish Biodiversity Strategy Report—As an amendment to motion (S3M-1204) in the name of Michael Russell, insert at end "notes that land users, land managers, inshore fishery interests, sea fishing interests and land practitioners are charged with delivering environmental benefits, and recognises that there is a need for these stakeholders in particular to be formally involved in the strategy at all levels, to understand the objectives, and thereafter to contribute practical solutions to the strategy and its overall aim."

S3M-1202*#* Nanette Milne: Alternative and Augmentative Communication—That the Parliament expresses concern following the findings by the Royal College of Speech and Language Therapists, Capability Scotland and Augmentative Communication in Practice whose recent survey of alternative and augmentative communication (AAC) equipment and speech and language therapy support provision in Scotland revealed that eight out of 15 NHS boards, including NHS Grampian, are providing a poor service; notes that only 16% of potential beneficiaries are accessing specialist AAC services in Scotland and that AAC provision is a postcode, age, advocacy and impairment lottery, and considers that an AAC strategy for Scotland should be developed and implemented to provide for a national standard of specialist speech and language therapy provision.

Supported by: Elizabeth Smith*, Robin Harper*, Jamie McGrigor*, Ted Brocklebank*, John Lamont*, Jackson Carlaw*, Murdo Fraser*, Rob Gibson*, Ken Macintosh*, Hugh Henry*

*S3M-1200.1 Jackie Baillie: Dormant Bank and Building Society Accounts Bill - UK Legislation—As an amendment to motion (S3M-1200) in the name of Jim Mather, insert at end "and, in so doing, calls on ministers to ensure a full and open consultation involving all interests with a social or environmental purpose and, in particular, organisations providing services, facilities or opportunities to meet the needs of young people; notes that a further vote will be required in the Scottish Parliament, and calls on ministers to return to the Parliament at the conclusion of the consultation exercise for further consideration of the distribution of the funds not later than September 2008."

*S3M-1200.2 Jeremy Purvis: Dormant Bank and Building Society Accounts Bill - UK Legislation—As an amendment to motion (S3M-1200) in the name of Jim Mather, insert at end "and, in so doing, calls on ministers to return to the Parliament following Royal Assent to the Bill and make a statement that the funds to be distributed in Scotland will be diverted towards youth services and facilities for all parts of Scotland."
