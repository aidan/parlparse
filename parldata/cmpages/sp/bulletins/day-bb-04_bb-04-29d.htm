Business Bulletin No. 72/2004

Thursday 29 April 2004

Section D : Oral Questions
  

Question Time

Enterprise, Transport and Lifelong Learning

1. Christine May: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to improve rail travel to Edinburgh . (S2O-2348)

2. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has with ScotRail regarding rail journey times between Glasgow and Edinburgh. (S2O-2347)

3. Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether, in light of the report of the Scottish Affairs Committee at Westminster last week, it will make further representations to the Chancellor of the Exchequer to ensure that strip stamps are not imposed on the Scotch whisky industry. (S2O-2270)

4. Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to support and encourage the Scotch whisky industry. (S2O-2268)

5. Colin Fox: To ask the Scottish Executive what advantages a road equivalent tariff scheme has for transport costs to and from island communities. (S2O-2330)

6. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive why rail passenger numbers are falling in the Strathclyde Passenger Transport area, given the Executive's policy of transferring road travellers to rail. (S2O-2239)

7. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will announce the preferred bidder for the ScotRail franchise. (S2O-2263)

8. Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of recent media reports, what plans it has to counter any possible boom and bust situation in the Scottish economy. (S2O-2342)

9. Mark Ballard: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make representations to Her Majesty's Government to ensure that the City of Edinburgh Council is allowed to use the full electoral register to hold a referendum on congestion charging. (S2O-2307)

10. Bristow Muldoon: To ask the Scottish Executive how it intends to improve local bus services. (S2O-2350)

11. Janis Hughes: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to make public transport less vulnerable to crime. (S2O-2346)

12. Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive how the Modern Apprenticeship programme is developing and whether standards are improving. (S2O-2344)

Justice and Law Officers

1. Christine May: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to address the issue of violent attacks on transport workers. (S2O-2365)

2. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of Lord Bonomy's recent judgement, what action is being taken to address any issues of inadequate regimes, overcrowding and slopping out for prisoners suffering from a mental health condition. (S2O-2276)

3. Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to ensure that environmental crimes are prosecuted in the courts. (S2O-2275)

4. Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what mechanisms are in place under the Debt Arrangement and Attachment (Scotland) Act 2002 for people affected by Scotland's growing level of debt. (S2O-2352)

5. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the implications would be for the Scottish justice system if the draft European constitution were to be implemented as proposed. (S2O-2240)

6. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to ensure that as many prisoners as possible benefit, while in prison and after release, from effective educational and training for work programmes. (S2O-2266)

7. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what lessons have been learned from HM Inspectorate of Prisons: Report on HM Prison Shotts. (S2O-2357)

8. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will publish the results of the consultation exercise on the Nicholson Committee report on liquor licensing. (S2O-2336)

9. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive when the combined sheriff court and police station in Peebles will be operational. (S2O-2364)

10. Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to improve family mediation services. (S2O-2361)

11. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will provide funding for family support organisations. (S2O-2332)

12. Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take following the judgement of Lord Bonomy in the Napier case concerning slopping out in prisons. (S2O-2356)

General

1. Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive what the implications are for the NHS of the recent legal rulings regarding the European Working Time Directive that resident on-call time will now be considered as work when calculating junior doctors working hours. (S2O-2326)

2. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the Scottish Ambulance Service with regard to the possible introduction of advanced care teams. (S2O-2324)

3. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to combat climate change and protect biodiversity, in light of recent research results on the early arrival of spring. (S2O-2259)

4. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to assist first-time buyers looking to access the property market in their local area. (S2O-2258)

5. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is improving primary care services. (S2O-2315)

6. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making with its review of freshwater fishing. (S2O-2234)

7. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will meet organisers of local public festivals and local authorities to discuss any methods of providing insurance cover for such festivals. (S2O-2255)

8. Cathie Craigie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to ensure that adequate sports facilities are available in all Scotland's communities. (S2O-2319)

9. Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive how it proposes to triple the number of businesses and organisations in the accreditation scheme for green tourism, as stated in the ministerial statement on tourism on 11 March 2004. (S2O-2310)

10. Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive how long the waiting list is for orthopaedic out-patients in Argyll and Clyde and what steps it is taking to reduce orthopaedic waiting times in Argyll and Clyde and across Scotland. (S2O-2328)

11. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making with its cancer strategy. (S2O-2323)

12. Janis Hughes: To ask the Scottish Executive what evaluation it has made of the impact of its mental health awareness campaign. (S2O-2321)

13. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will take account of local concerns regarding the provision of healthcare services in the Lothians. (S2O-2293)

14. Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in releasing public land for the development of affordable housing. (S2O-2318)

15. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many young people are educated at home and what the implications are for child protection. (S2O-2271)
