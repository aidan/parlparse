Business Bulletin No. 114/2002

Friday 9 August 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-3301 Mr Brian Monteith: Scotland's Commonwealth Games Team—That the Parliament extends its warm congratulations to members of Scotland’s Commonwealth Games team for their achievement in getting selected and accomplishing personal bests and winning 30 gold, silver and bronze medals, a total of one fewer than the record attained at the Edinburgh Games in 1986; extends its thanks also to their support network of relatives, friends, coaches, trainers and sports administrators who helped the participants to represent Scotland so well, and calls on the Scottish Executive and sportscotland to use this opportunity to review the success or otherwise of lottery funding for various sports so that any necessary changes can be made for the future.

Supported by: Robin Harper*

*S1M-3300 Rhona Brankin: 2002 Commonwealth Games—That the Parliament congratulates the Scottish team on its tremendous performance in winning 30 medals at the 2002 Commonwealth Games in Manchester; welcomes the support for the team from the Scottish Executive and the National Lottery through sportscotland and the Scottish Institute of Sport; recognises the importance of sport in shaping a healthier Scotland and, following the successful hosting of the games by Manchester, notes the potential benefits of staging major sporting events in Scotland such as the European Football Championships in 2008 and the Ryder Cup.

Supported by: Robin Harper*

*S1M-3299 Irene Oldfather: Closure of Sanmina-SCI Irvine—That the Parliament notes the announcement made on 31 July 2002 of the owner’s intention to close the Sanmina-SCI electronics plant in Irvine, resulting in the loss of 750 jobs to the local community; recognises that the closure is a result of a severe global downturn in the electronics market and in no way reflects upon the highly skilled and well motivated workforce at the plant; further notes that unemployment is already substantially higher in the north Ayrshire area than the Scottish national average; calls on the Scottish Executive to offer all possible support to those facing redundancy as a result of this closure and urges the Executive to address the north Ayrshire unemployment situation with the utmost of urgency.

*S1M-3298 Mr David Davidson: Edinburgh Military Tattoo 2002—That the Parliament recognises the role of the Edinburgh Military Tattoo in promoting Scottish history, tradition and pageantry and congratulates the board, staff and performers in creating a fantastic spectacle for the Queen's Jubilee Tattoo which is a complete sell-out, contributing greatly to Scottish tourism.

*S1M-3297# Mr John McAllion: Broughty Ferry Beach and the Designation Process Under the EC Bathing Water Directive —That the Parliament welcomes the significant progress in water quality at Broughty Ferry beach following the implementation of the new Tayside sewage treatment scheme; congratulates Friends of the Earth and the Broughty Ferry Community Council for their ongoing campaign to have the beach designated under the EC Bathing Water Directive, and invites the Scottish Executive to use its existing powers to designate the beach accordingly.

Supported by: Robin Harper*

*S1M-3296 Robin Harper: Banking Standards—That the Parliament calls on the Scottish Executive to consult with banks operating in Scotland on issues relating to competition, the quality of information available on banking services, and the levels of financial support available for small businesses.
