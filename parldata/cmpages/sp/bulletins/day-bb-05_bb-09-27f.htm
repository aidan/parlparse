Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday.  That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (u).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an “R”.

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm).  A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-3344 George Lyon: Membership of Committee of the Regions—That the Parliament endorses the Scottish Executive’s proposal to nominate as representatives of the Parliament Jack McConnell MSP and Irene Oldfather MSP as full members and George Lyon MSP and Richard Lochhead MSP as alternate members on the UK delegation to the Committee of the Regions for the forthcoming session from 2006 to 2010 and notes that the representation from local government will be Councillor Corrie McChord and Councillor Andrew Campbell as full members and Councillor Graham Garvie and Councillor Jim McCabe as alternate members.

Supported by: Mr Tom McCabe*

*S2M-3343u Ms Sandra White: Sex Discrimination - Ending the Public Sector Pay Gap—That the Parliament notes that November will mark the 30th anniversary of the passing of the Sex Discrimination Act 1975 which, at the time, represented a watershed for equal opportunities campaigners; notes that, despite the Equal Pay Act 1970 and an equal pay strategy agreed by unions and local authorities in 1999, more than 50,000 female local authority workers in Scotland have been paid less than their entitlement and are now threatening to take court action over their claims; notes that the total cost could be as much as £500 million unless an amicable solution can be found by local authorities; notes that the Scottish Executive failed to issue any guidelines to local authorities on the issue of fair pay; notes that it is often women who are among the lowest-paid workers in local authorities working often part-time and that currently their collective bargaining punch is seen as limited by employers; further notes that independent nations, like Norway and Sweden, place a high premium on childcare, removing employment barriers for pregnant women and recognise the need for flexibility among female workers as a means to narrowing the gender gap, and considers that Scottish ministers should engage with local authorities and ensure that resources are in place so that women are fully paid and properly valued.

*S2M-3342 Mr Tom McCabe: Waverley Railway (Scotland) Bill: Financial Resolution—That the Parliament, for the purposes of any Act of the Scottish Parliament resulting from the Waverley Railway (Scotland) Bill, agrees to any expenditure of a kind referred to in Rule 9A.14.3(b)(ii) of the Parliament’s Standing Orders arising in consequence of the Act.

*S2M-3341 Bill Aitken: Barnardo’s/HBOS Tug of War—That the Parliament notes that the Barnado’s Parliament Tug of War Competition, held in Holyrood Park on Thursday 15 September 2005, helped raise £2,750 for the charity; congratulates the members and staff of the Scottish Executive, the Green, SNP and Liberal Democrat parties, The Scotsman, the BBC, the Scottish Conservatives and the other teams for their sterling efforts and great commitment; further congratulates Barnado’s for all the hard work which went into organising such an enjoyable event and HBOS for its generous support, and hopes that next year’s event will be even more successful, with teams from all parties taking part.

*S2M-3340 Mr Brian Monteith: Skateraw Project—That the Parliament congratulates the Demarco European Art Foundation which, in collaboration with Johnny and Sandra Watson of John Watson Seeds, is presenting the Skateraw Project, an exhibition which celebrates the 75th birthday of artist and impresario, Ricky Demarco, by bringing together under one roof his 40-year-old archive of artworks of all kinds, photographic records, catalogues, correspondence and other artistic manuscripts of the Scottish and European avant-garde; marvels at the use of an insulated seed barn set in the inspiring East Lothian countryside to bring together the worlds of farmer and artist under the concept of Agri-Culture, where work can be seen, conceived and created, allowing people to discover that we are all artists; encourages members of the Scottish Parliament to make the journey on the road to Meikle Seggie and see how new horizons are in the offing at Skateraw; further congratulates other sponsors, such as Lloyds TSB (Scotland), British Energy, the Russell Trust, and Prestonfield House for their support, and wishes the event, which runs until 29 October 2005 at the Barn Gallery, Skateraw Farm, Dunbar, every success.

S2M-3073# Brian Adam: Met Office Aberdeen—That the Parliament expresses grave concern at proposals by the Met Office to close its office in Aberdeen and transfer the work to Exeter; notes that the Aberdeen office is commercially profitable and that its integrated approach to forecasting and commercial activities is key to that success; further notes its important role in adding value to raw data by providing significant interpretation through local knowledge and by successfully engaging face to face with many of its customers, and *considers that* the Scottish Executive *should* engage in the consultation recently announced by the Met Office with the intention of retaining all its activities in Aberdeen and seeking consideration of the devolution of the civil and commercial activities.
