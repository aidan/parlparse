Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<> ).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-4217 Ms Margaret Curran on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Establishment of Committee—That the Parliament agrees to establish a committee of the Parliament as follows:

Name of Committee: Edinburgh Airport Rail Link Bill Committee;
Remit: To consider and report to the Parliament on the Edinburgh Airport Rail Link Bill;
Duration: Until the Bill has received Royal Assent, falls or is withdrawn;
Convenership: The Convener will be a member of the Labour Party and the Deputy Convener will be a member of the Scottish Conservative and Unionist Party;
Membership: Christine Grahame, Jamie McGrigor, Iain Smith, Scott Barrie, Mr Charlie Gordon.

Supported by: George Lyon*

*S2M-4216 Richard Lochhead: The Spiralling Cost of the Nuclear Clean-Up—That the Parliament notes with great concern the news that the clean-up cost for existing nuclear facilities is set to rise by £9 billion to £65 billion; believes that this highlights that nuclear power is expensive as well as dangerous and dirty; considers that Scotland has no need for new nuclear power stations which, according to the UK Government-appointed Committee on Radioactive Waste Management, if built, would produce radioactive waste nine times stronger than currently exists and will cost billions of pounds to both build and clean up, and also believes that we should reject Labour’s nuclear ambitions, while promoting clean and safe energy technologies with which Scotland has the potential to lead the world.

*S2M-4215 Carolyn Leckie: Solidarity with Local Government Workers in Their Fight to Defend Their Pensions—That the Parliament congratulates the trade unions and their members for having the courage and strength to stand up to government attacks on their contractual pension rights and entitlements; admires the show of solidarity displayed by over 200,000 workers in Scotland and over one million more across Britain; regrets that the actions of the Scottish Executive and the Westminster Government have led to strike action being necessary; believes that the Executive should reconsider its stance on the legal position of the Rule of 85, given the conflicting advice from COSLA and UNISON and the contradictory statements of the Westminster Government; further believes that the Executive should reconsider its position in relation to ensuring that local government workers suffer no detriment in terms of their pension rights or entitlements; considers that, as a matter of urgency, the Executive should publish its proposals for resolution of this dispute, whether or not the Westminster Government approves, and believes that the only fair and just way to settle this dispute is for the Executive and employers to offer a solution acceptable to local government trade union members.

Supported by: Campbell Martin*, Ms Sandra White*

*S2M-4214 Donald Gorrie: Caledonia Youth—That the Parliament notes with regret that decisions by NHS Greater Glasgow and NHS Tayside have resulted in the closure of Caledonia Youth clinics and sexual health education and advice services, first in Glasgow and now in Dundee; further notes that these services have been widely praised, including by HM Inspectorate of Education, as providing much-needed individual support to young people facing unplanned pregnancy, relationship problems and issues of sexuality and low self-esteem and proving successful in reducing such problems among Caledonia Youth’s clients, and urges the Scottish Executive and the Parliament to show that they take seriously the Executive’s national sexual health strategy, Respect and Responsibility, by ensuring that Caledonia Youth is sustained financially and allowed to contribute towards the national policy.

*S2M-4213 Shiona Baird: DC Thomson Takeover of Aberdeen Journals—That the Parliament notes the takeover by DC Thomson of Aberdeen Journals, publisher of the Press & Journal and the Evening Express; welcomes this development as securing Scottish ownership of Scottish-based newspapers; congratulates DC Thomson on avoiding any major staffing cuts, despite recent drastic cuts in staffing levels at other national and regional newspapers; urges DC Thomson to take all measures possible to secure current levels of employment, and wishes the company and its staff a very prosperous future.

*S2M-4212 Shiona Baird: More Overseas Success for Scottish Marine Renewables—That the Parliament congratulates Inverness-based company, Wavegen, on the successful development of the Limpet onshore wave energy device; welcomes the news that this pioneering technology could be installed in a harbour development in northern Spain; hopes that there will soon be significant numbers of both onshore and offshore wave energy devices commercially installed in Scottish waters, and calls on the Scottish Executive to commit greater financial resources to support the development of the Scottish marine renewable energy sector that promises so much for the Scottish economy.

*S2M-4211 Maureen Macmillan: Publication of a Consultation Paper on the Proposed UK Marine Bill—That the Parliament notes the publication of a consultation paper on the proposed UK Marine Bill by Her Majesty’s Government; welcomes this step forward towards comprehensive marine legislation for the United Kingdom including for many reserved issues in Scottish waters; encourages as many stakeholders from Scotland as possible to participate in the consultation exercise; commends the consultation paper’s recognition of the devolution context, in particular the possibility of devolving further or new powers; further commends the parallel work being carried out by the Scottish Executive under the marine and coastal strategy, Seas the Opportunity, and being further developed by the Ministerial Advisory Group; supports this advisory group’s work on identifying any need for parallel legislation in Scotland, and urges those responsible for the legislative programme after May 2007 to accord a high priority to marine legislation in Scotland to complement that being delivered at UK level.

*S2M-4210 Cathy Jamieson: The Draft Maximum Number of Part-Time Sheriffs (Scotland) Order 2006—That the Justice 2 Committee recommends that the draft Maximum Number of Part-Time Sheriffs (Scotland) Order 2006 be approved.

Supported by: Hugh Henry*

*S2M-4209 Ms Sandra White: No Place for a Child: Stop Detaining Children Now!—That the Parliament welcomes the "No Place for a Child: Stop Detaining Children Now! Campaign" launched on 27 March 2006 by Save the Children UK and the Scottish Refugee Council along with the Welsh Refugee Council and Bail for Immigration Detainees; notes that the UK Government locks up thousands of children and babies each year; further notes that parents and children are not told how long they will be detained for and observes that this can be for over a year; is concerned that internment can be physically and mentally traumatic for children, with long-term negative repercussions; agrees that most of these families have complied with immigration regulations, and suggests that there are other, less expensive, ways of dealing with immigration which protect both the rights of those seeking immigration and the rights of the child not to be scarred for life.

*S2M-4208 Dennis Canavan: Scottish Record at the Commonwealth Games—That the Parliament congratulates all those who represented Scotland at the Commonwealth Games in Melbourne, particularly the medal winners who did us proud by achieving a Scottish record of 11 gold medals out of an impressive total of 29.

*S2M-4207 Dr Elaine Murray: The Meteoric Rise of Gretna Football Club—That the Parliament congratulates Gretna Football Club on winning the Scottish League Second Division title and on its promotion to the First Division; notes that it is the first UK club this season to secure its league championship; notes that Gretna FC joined the Scottish Football League as recently as 2002 and that it secured the third division championship last season; commends the club on its community programme involving outreach work in schools in Dumfries and Galloway and Cumbria and a programme of events for young people, and wishes Gretna Football Club good luck in the First Division next year.

*S2M-4206 Brian Adam: Congratulations to Ross Gazey and Cameron Fraser—That the Parliament congratulates Ross Gazey from Aberdeen’s Robert Gordon University and SiGen Ltd and Cameron Fraser from Heriot-Watt University and Caledonian Aerotech Ltd for their success at the Knowledge Transfer Partnerships (KTP) annual awards in London and furthermore commends SiGen for promoting the development of hydrogen from renewable energy sources as carbon-free fuel vector for the remote and island communities around Scotland.

S2M-4199 Irene Oldfather: James Watt College—That the Parliament recognises the contribution that James Watt College makes to the local communities and economies of Kilwinning, Greenock and Largs; welcomes the educational opportunities that the college and its staff provide for, on average, *29,000* students per year, including international students and those with additional support needs; notes with regret the present operational difficulties facing the college; further regrets the breakdown of communication in informing staff and stakeholders of these difficulties; calls for urgent action and partnership working by all parties involved, and urges all parties to get back round the table to resume discussions in order to avoid damaging industrial action.

*S2M-4197.1 Murdo Fraser: Bridge Tolls—As an amendment to motion (S2M-4197) in the name of Shona Robison, insert at end "and notes that in so doing the costs of relocating the toll plaza from the north end of the bridge to the south would be saved."

*S2M-4197.2 Shiona Baird: Bridge Tolls—As an amendment to motion (S2M-4197) in the name of Shona Robison, insert at end "for public transport in addition to the existing exemptions, that a scheme of "smart tolls" should be introduced, which takes into account factors such as occupancy levels and peak hour traffic flows, and that the Tay Road Bridge Joint Board should be given more flexibility to use toll revenues to deal with transport issues in the vicinity."

*S2M-4197.3 Tavish Scott: Bridge Tolls—As an amendment to motion (S2M-4197) in the name of Shona Robison, leave out from "agrees" to end and insert "notes the responses, analysis and conclusions set out in the recent consultation and review of Scotland’s tolled bridges, including the Tay Road Bridge Joint Board’s response; notes that the bridges review met all of the Board’s requests, and recommends an examination of the economic, social and environmental impact and cost of retaining or removing tolls from the Tay and Forth bridges, on Fife and Dundee, the proposals for which will be reported on as soon as possible."

Supported by: Ms Margaret Curran*

*S2M-4196.1 Mr Jamie McGrigor: Congratulations Team Scotland—As an amendment to motion (S2M-4196) in the name of Michael Matheson, insert at end "notes, however, the words of Simon Clegg, the Chief Executive of the British Olympic Association, who said that "the British Olympic Association...strongly believes that we are stronger collectively than as individual countries…Any distractions, dilution or further fragmentation of sport in the UK will seriously undermine the once in a lifetime opportunity British sport has to capitalise on the unique benefits of hosting an Olympic Games", and therefore believes that there is a need to support both grass-roots sport infrastructure and elite athletes through the valuable work of the Scottish Institute of Sport which aims to change the culture and raise the ambitions and aspirations within sport in Scotland."

*S2M-4196.2 Patricia Ferguson: Congratulations Team Scotland—As an amendment to motion (S2M-4196) in the name of Michael Matheson, leave out from "; believes" to end and insert "and the valuable support provided by the Institute network, sportscotland and the World Class Performance Programme; believes that Team Scotland’s success in Melbourne should act as a springboard to ensure even greater success for our sportsmen and women in the international sporting arena and will also inspire many young Scots to participate in sport; recognises the significant benefits to be gained for Scotland from London 2012 and a successful bid for Glasgow to host the Commonwealth Games in 2014; acknowledges that access to good sporting facilities plays a significant role in the development of our nation’s sporting talent; welcomes the investment in facilities through the Executive’s National and Regional Facilities Strategy which will provide a first-class setting for our elite and developing athletes and which can also be enjoyed by local communities, and welcomes the imminent publication of the reports on the audit of local facilities."

Supported by: Ms Margaret Curran*
