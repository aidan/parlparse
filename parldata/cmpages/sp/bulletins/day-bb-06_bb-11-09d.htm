Business Bulletin No. 159/2006: Thursday 9 November 2006

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 16 November 2006

Question Time

General

1. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is supporting employment in the Dunfermline area. (S2O-11097)

2. Euan Robson: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent representations it has made to the UK Government regarding the planning-gain supplement. (S2O-11089)

3. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to introduce a high-speed rail service between Glasgow and Edinburgh. (S2O-11127)

4. Not Lodged

5. Gordon Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being considered to improve road safety at accident black spots. (S2O-11109)

6. Ms Maureen Watt: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to conduct a national audit of sports facilities. (S2O-11076)

7. Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive what proportion of road fatalities in rural Scotland took place between sunset and sunrise in the last two years. (S2O-11074)

8. Dave Petrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people in the Western Isles constituency have been placed on a waiting list for installation and assessment work under its central heating programme and Warm Deal scheme as a result of the transition of the contract from Eaga Partnership Ltd to Scottish Gas. (S2O-11061)

9. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what the outcome has been of any representations it has made about the future of the post office network and what assessment it has made of how any decision to close local post offices is likely to impact on community development priorities. (S2O-11075)

10. Mr Alasdair Morrison: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress there has been in relation to the schools public private partnership in the Western Isles. (S2O-11121)

Environment and Rural Development

1. Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive what factors currently inhibit growth in Scotland’s rural communities. (S2O-11067)

2. Carolyn Leckie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to assess the environmental impact of the use of biomass heating systems, such as that recently introduced at Taylor High School in Motherwell, and whether it has any plans to roll out the use of such systems. (S2O-11082)

3. Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what monitoring is carried out of the effect of depleted uranium contamination on the environment. (S2O-11080)

4. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will monitor the effectiveness of the new funding provided for the provision of backcourt recycling in Glasgow. (S2O-11119)

5. Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it shares the Prime Minister’s reported view that the future of agriculture for this country is more likely to be in organic niche farming than in extensive tracts of genetically modified crops. (S2O-11136)

6. Cathie Craigie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it supports the creation of green spaces and parkland. (S2O-11101)

7. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent developments have taken place regarding the proposed ship-to-ship transfer of oil in the Firth of Forth. (S2O-11111)

8. Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in encouraging young people to participate in the "It’s Our Future" campaign. (S2O-11100)

9. Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure that sustainable rural development and, in particular, rural employment is supported by the rural road network. (S2O-11104)

10. Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what resources have been committed to local authorities to improve recycling. (S2O-11096)

Health and Community Care

1. Mr Mark Ruskell: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure that NHS boards and local authorities address the need to make commitments to support community-led and voluntary sector health initiatives in their strategic planning processes. (S2O-11135)

2. Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what annual funding it makes available, for the provision of long-term care services, to housing organisations with charitable status. (S2O-11046)

3. Mrs Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are being taken to increase the provision of respite care for those being cared for at home. (S2O-11123)

4. Susan Deacon: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to communicate, and to promote understanding about, recent changes to the BCG immunisation programme. (S2O-11102)

5. Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to make NHS boards more accountable to the patients and communities that they serve. (S2O-11065)

6. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what the latest position is regarding the provision of NHS dental services in the Highlands. (S2O-11112)

7. Mr John Swinney: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will publish the contract that has been awarded to establish the Scottish Regional Treatment Centre at Stracathro Hospital. (S2O-11070)

8. Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what drug treatments are currently available on the NHS in Scotland for the treatment of mild, moderate and severe Alzheimer’s disease and whether this position is likely to change in the near future. (S2O-11124)

9. Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Minister for Health and Community Care will announce his decision regarding the closure of the Aboyne maternity unit. (S2O-11090)

10. Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive how the new investment in Stracathro Hospital will benefit patients in Tayside. (S2O-11058).
