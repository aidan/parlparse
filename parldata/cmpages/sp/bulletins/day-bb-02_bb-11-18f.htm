Business Bulletin No. 166/2002

Monday 18 November 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-3589# Karen Gillon: The Sale of The Herald, Sunday Herald and Evening Times by SMG-That the Parliament notes the proposed sale by Scottish Media Group of the titles The Herald, Sunday Herald and Evening Times; recognises the contribution these titles make in providing Scotland with diverse media, and therefore considers that the Scottish Executive should make representations to Her Majesty's Government to ensure that any sale does not lead to a monopoly situation.

Supported by: Brian Fitzpatrick*, Michael Matheson*, Mr Michael McMahon*, Irene McGugan*, Mr John McAllion*

*S1M-3588 Mr Andy Kerr: Title Conditions (Scotland) Bill: Financial Resolution-That the Parliament, for the purposes of any Act of the Scottish Parliament resulting from the Title Conditions (Scotland) Bill, agrees to any increase in the sums payable out of the Scottish Consolidated Fund in consequence of the Act.

Supported by: Peter Peacock*, Mr Jim Wallace*, Dr Richard Simpson*

*S1M-3587# Christine Grahame: Grooming of Children and Vulnerable Adults-That the Parliament notes with concern the increasing use of internet chat rooms and other methods by adults to deceive, beguile and lure children into compromising situations with a view to sexual gratification, sometimes known as "grooming"; further notes that there may be a lacuna in existing criminal law in this regard; notes that the Scottish Executive is investigating whether a new statutory offence is required, and considers that the Executive should extend its inquiry and any potential new statutory offence to cover vulnerable adults.

*S1M-3586 Mr Adam Ingram: Heartstart-That the Parliament welcomes Heartstart, the British Heart Foundation's Scottish schools initiative, which aims to develop emergency life support training programmes in our schools; congratulates those schools throughout Scotland that have already signed up to this programme and have benefited from teacher training free of charge and generous grants for training equipment, and urges local authorities to ensure that those schools that have not yet affiliated are encouraged to do so, in order to provide young people with invaluable lifesaving skills.

S1M-3585 Ben Wallace: Armed Forces Personnel and Firefighting Duties-That the Parliament supports the brave young men and and women of the armed forces in Scotland who are putting their lives at risk in order to cover the reckless and dangerous strike by Scottish firefighters; demands that the Scottish Executive immediately steps up concurrent training for armed forces personnel to allow them to use better firefighting equipment and, in addition, give them access to basic protective clothing; believes that public safety and the safety of the armed forces personnel must come before trade union demands, and notes that the armed forces carry out these essential services ill-equipped and for considerably lower pay than firefighters and with no right to strike.

Supported by: Mrs Lyndsay McIntosh, Bill Aitken, Phil Gallie, John Young, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton, Mr Jamie McGrigor, John Scott, Mr David Davidson

S1M-3584# Margaret Smith: Edinburgh Park Railway Station-That the Parliament notes the recent announcement that construction of the Edinburgh Park Railway Station can begin; welcomes the funding supplied by the Strategic Rail Authority, City of Edinburgh Council and New Edinburgh Ltd; further welcomes the investment in new rolling stock from the Scottish Executive; is concerned that agreement on the project was delayed by performance targets; considers that the Scottish Executive should examine how this station can serve the Edinburgh to Glasgow rail line, and considers that the Executive should ensure that construction of the station and the provision of additional rolling stock is completed as soon as possible.

S1M-3583 Mr Gil Paterson: White Ribbon Campaign to End Violence Against Women-That the Parliament supports the White Ribbon Campaign, which leads up to the UN International Day for the Elimination of Violence Against Women on 25 November 2002; recognises the white ribbon as the international symbol for a world where women and girls can live free from the fear of violence; supports the work of the UK White Ribbon Campaign, co-ordinated by WOMANKIND Worldwide, to raise funds and awareness to end violence against women; notes with alarm the prevalence and gravity of violence against women, with one in four women in the UK experiencing domestic violence at some point in their lives, and one in 20 women suffering rape, and at least one in every three women around the world being beaten, coerced into sex, or abused in some other way, and urges MSPs and others to support this campaign by wearing a white ribbon to show their support for ending violence against women.

Supported by: Ms Sandra White*, Mr Lloyd Quinan*, Mr Adam Ingram*, Andrew Wilson*, Michael Matheson*

S1M-3582 Fergus Ewing: Personal Care-That the Parliament notes with concern that The Highland Council has considered tightening the rules covering eligibility for free personal care because of a funding shortfall of about £300,000; further notes that more than 75,000 people in Scotland are estimated to be eligible for free care following the decision by the Parliament and the Scottish Executive to provide such free care; recognises that the council received just over £4 million from the Executive to meet the costs of providing free personal and nursing care in its area but that demand has proven to be higher than predicted, and calls on the Executive to fulfil its commitment to free personal care by meeting the shortfall in funding as soon as possible.

Supported by: Christine Grahame*

S1M-3581# Bill Butler: Schooling of Children of Asylum Seekers-That the Parliament notes with concern the requirement contained within the Nationality, Immigration and Asylum Act 2002, which stipulates that the children of asylum seekers be schooled outwith the mainstream local authority education system in purpose-built accommodation centres; fears that such a condition, which requires separate educational provision for the children of asylum seekers, will in effect lead to segregation rather than integration; acknowledges that the present arrangement, as evidenced by a recent survey conducted in Glasgow, is both inclusive and successful, and considers that the Scottish Executive should make representations to the Home Secretary, the purpose of which would be to arrive at an agreement whereby the current distinctive approach pertaining to the schooling of asylum seeker children in Scotland is allowed to continue.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson*, Christine Grahame*

S1M-3580# Bill Aitken: Glasgow Acute Health Services Review-That the Parliament notes the continued and growing concern of Glasgow residents regarding the Scottish Executive's proposals following the recent Acute Health Services Review in Glasgow.

S1M-3579*#* Brian Adam: Centre of Excellence in Genealogy-That the Parliament recognises the long-established position of Scotland as a country with a history of genealogical research, development and expertise; notes recent technological developments in the study of genealogy which will allow greater access to Scotland's excellent records; congratulates the General Register Office for Scotland on its digitising programme, and urges the Scottish Executive to facilitate the emergence of Scotland as a leading centre in Europe for the academic study of family history research and genealogy by building partnerships with all organisations that hold records of interest in Scotland to maximise public access.

Supported by: Michael Matheson, Ms Sandra White*, Mr Kenny MacAskill*, Andrew Wilson*, Colin Campbell*, Mr John McAllion*, Murdo Fraser*, Richard Lochhead*, Stewart Stevenson*

S1M-3578# Christine Grahame: Bah Humbug-That the Parliament notes that the advertising, promotion and sale of Christmas as a commercial calendar event is now encroaching earlier and earlier in the year and that such activities put unacceptable pressures particularly on parents with young children, leading some understandably into financial difficulties; further notes that retail staff and shoppers are subjected to endless jingles before even Halloween masks have been reduced to half price and pumpkins are buy one get six free, and therefore urges the Scottish Executive to take such steps as are required to consign Christmas to where it belongs, no earlier than the month of December.

Supported by: Mr John McAllion, Mr Jamie Stone, Margaret Smith, Michael Matheson, Robin Harper*

S1M-3568 Murdo Fraser: Result of Gibraltar Referendum-That the Parliament acknowledges the right of the people of Gibraltar to self-determination; welcomes the overwhelming vote on 7 November 2002 of the people of Gibraltar against shared sovereignty of the Rock between the United Kingdom and Spain, and calls on Her Majesty's Government to terminate forthwith all negotiations with the Spanish Government that would progress the rejected principle of shared sovereignty.

Supported by: Mrs Lyndsay McIntosh, Alex Johnstone, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton, Mr Brian Monteith, Mr Keith Harding, Ben Wallace

S1M-3567 Mr Andy Kerr: The Draft Budget (Scotland) Act 2002 Amendment Order 2002-That the Finance Committee recommends that the draft Budget (Scotland) Act 2002 Amendment Order 2002 be approved.

Supported by: Peter Peacock

S1M-3566 Mr Andy Kerr: The Draft Scottish Local Government Elections Regulations 2002-That the Local Government Committee, in consideration of the draft Scottish Local Government Elections Regulations 2002, recommends that the instrument be approved.

Supported by: Peter Peacock

S1M-3565# Mr John Swinney: A9 - Perth to Inverness-That the Parliament notes with concern the unacceptable level of death and injury caused by road traffic accidents on the A9 from Perth to Inverness; recognises that the design of the A9, particularly the frequent switching between single and dual carriageway, contributes to this level of danger; welcomes improvements made to the road design to improve safety, particularly measures already agreed at the Bankfoot and Ballinluig junctions, but recognises that, until the A9 from Perth to Inverness is re-constructed as a dual carriageway with safe junctions, it will continue to present road safety dangers to the local community and the significant number of visitors to the area.

Supported by: Kay Ullrich, Brian Adam, Mr Kenneth Gibson, Kate Maclean, Colin Campbell, Stewart Stevenson, Michael Russell, Irene McGugan, Ms Sandra White, Mr Lloyd Quinan, Richard Lochhead, Murdo Fraser, Mr Brian Monteith, Tricia Marwick, Mr Kenny MacAskill, Fergus Ewing, Bruce Crawford, Alex Johnstone, Shona Robison, Christine Grahame, Mr Duncan Hamilton, Dr Winnie Ewing, Michael Matheson, Alex Neil, Mary Scanlon

S1M-3564 Dorothy-Grace Elder: Congratulations to Titanic Historian-That the Parliament congratulates the German historian Susanne Stormer on her superb scholarship in respect of the Titanic disaster and her efforts to tell the truth about the heroic actions of First Officer William McMaster Murdoch of Dalbeattie; records the gratitude of the people of Dalbeattie; supports Ms Stormer in her continuing fight against a wrongful portrayal of Mr Murdoch in James Cameron's film, Titanic, a portrayal that 20th Century Fox eventually admitted was untrue; congratulates Ms Stormer on her latest scholarly book William McMaster Murdoch - a Career at Sea which shows that, but for Mr Murdoch's calmness and courage, even greater loss of life could have occurred on Titanic, and notes that the sailor from Dalbeattie stayed to the end, going down with the Titanic after giving up his life jacket to another.

Supported by: Mr Brian Monteith, Alex Neil

S1M-3563 Irene McGugan: Mouth Cancer Awareness Week-That the Parliament recognises that the second Mouth Cancer Awareness Week runs from 10-16 November 2002; congratulates the Tayside Community Dental Service and other agencies throughout Scotland for their efforts to inform the public about this disease; notes with concern that in Scotland there are approximately 500 new cases of oral cancer and 250 deaths per year, that less than 50% of cases will survive five years and that the incidence of oral cancer is on the increase in younger people, and encourages the Scottish Executive to assist in raising public awareness of preventative measures such as giving up smoking, reducing alcohol consumption and, most importantly, visiting a dentist at least once a year.

Supported by: Alex Neil, Mary Scanlon, Christine Grahame, Michael Matheson

S1M-3562 Pauline McNeill: Maggie's Centre - Westen Infirmary, Glasgow-That the Parliament warmly welcomes the opening of the new Maggie's Centre in the Western Infirmary gatehouse in Glasgow which will specialise in supporting people with cancer, carers and their families and includes an extensive cancer information library and internet access; expresses its delight at the overwhelming public support in Glasgow for the Maggie's Centre, and congratulates all those who have participated in fund-raising efforts to make the centre possible.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson, Mary Scanlon, Christine Grahame

S1M-3561 Ms Margo MacDonald: Ability of Holyrood Progress Group-That the Parliament has no confidence in the ability of the Holyrood Progress Group to achieve any of its own declared aims which are to deal competently with question and answer sessions for MSPs, monitor and report on the progress of the Holyrood project and control expenditure so as to ensure that the Parliament takes delivery of a world-class building on budget and on time, and resolves to exert a greater measure of client control over the project by dissolving the group and appointing an experienced building industry professional to represent the Parliament and the public interest on the Holyrood Project Team in place of the current Project Director.

S1M-3560 Ross Finnie: The Draft Genetically Modified Organisms (Deliberate Release) (Scotland) Regulations 2002-That the Transport and the Environment Committee recommends that the draft Genetically Modified Organisms (Deliberate Release) (Scotland) Regulations 2002 be approved."

Supported by: Allan Wilson

S1M-3559 Irene McGugan: Street Football for All-That the Parliament notes the work of the C.A.F.E. Project, a highly successful and nationally recognised voluntary organisation which provides a one-stop shop for leisure, culture, health, education and employment facilities and outdoor activities for people under 25 in the Arbroath area, which was recently described by Tony Andrews, Director of the British Council in Germany as "extraordinary, innovative and inspiring in the way it reconnects young people with the mainstream - a model for working with disadvantaged young people"; congratulates the project on its plans to develop "Street Football For All" alongside the national "Dreams and Teams" project, which seeks to promote sport, health and active citizenship by using football to bring together young people from Scotland and Germany and 15 other countries of the world to increase understanding and tolerance, and wishes the projects every success with this excellent initiative which meaningfully embodies equality and social inclusion

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson, Mr Kenneth Gibson, Mr John McAllion, Fiona McLeod, Colin Campbell, Brian Adam, Scott Barrie, Ms Sandra White, Dennis Canavan, Michael Russell, Alex Neil, Mr Brian Monteith, Mr Kenny MacAskill, Mr Kenneth Macintosh, Mr Andrew Welsh, Fergus Ewing, Michael Matheson, Robin Harper*, Ian Jenkins*

S1M-3538# John Farquhar Munro: Affordable Rural Housing-That the Parliament notes the shortfall in affordable housing in rural areas for rent or for purchase particularly for young families; acknowledges that, in part, the cause of this can be traced to second home ownership; believes that in order to maintain a sustainable future for rural Scotland measures must be taken to encourage young people and families to remain in their communities, and considers that the Parliament should review the provision of socially rented housing as well as the extent of second home ownership throughout rural Scotland.

Supported by: Dr Sylvia Jackson, Mr Mike Rumbles, Fergus Ewing, Robert Brown, Nora Radcliffe, Brian Adam*

S1M-3486# Mr Duncan McNeil: Mis-selling of Utilities-That the Parliament notes the proposal of energy regulator, Ofgem, to penalise London Electricity for failing to prevent its sales staff from mis-selling products to customers; expresses concern over the high-pressure selling tactics employed by representatives of certain utility companies; believes that vulnerable members of the public are entitled to protection from such practices; seeks clarification over what safeguards are currently in place and how these are enforced, and considers that the industry, the Scottish Executive and all interested parties should undertake a concerted effort to put an end to underhand sales practices and restore public confidence in the utilities market.
