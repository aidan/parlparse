Business Bulletin No. 85/2007: Monday 11 June 2007
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 8 June 2007

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S3W-790 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of the call by the EIS for a ban on recruitment by the armed forces in schools, whether ministers will issue immediate guidance to education authorities to ensure that this practice ceases.

S3W-791 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many NHS employees have been recruited into the British armed forces following recruitment activities in hospitals and what the resulting impact has been on the delivery of health services to patients in each year since 2003.

S3W-792 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider banning the practice of recruitment visits to hospitals by the armed forces targeting NHS staff, in light of pressure on NHS boards to deliver high-quality health provision to patients.

S3W-793 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider issuing guidance to all bodies for which it has responsibility to ensure that recruitment shortages in the British armed forces do not lead to diminished public services through significantly increased recruitment activity by the armed forces in devolved areas.

S3W-794 Joe FitzPatrick: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body what plans it has to replace ordinary light bulbs in the Parliament building with energy saving bulbs.

S3W-795 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive who will be consulted as part of a review of the existing proposals for the Aberdeen Western Peripheral Route.

S3W-796 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what the cost will be of a review of existing proposals for the Aberdeen Western Peripheral Route.

S3W-797 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what the timescale will be for a review of existing proposals for the Aberdeen Western Peripheral Route.

S3W-798 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive which agencies will carry out a review of existing proposals for the Aberdeen Western Peripheral Route.

S3W-799 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will report to the Parliament on a review of existing proposals for the Aberdeen Western Peripheral Route.

S3W-800 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the Minister for Transport, Infrastructure and Climate Change’s comments on 7 June 2007 in response to an intervention from Patrick Harvie (Official Report c. 458) and the answer to the second supplementary question to question S3F-29 by Alex Salmond on 7 June 2007, what further financial review is planned of the Aberdeen Western Peripheral Route.

S3W-801 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has assessed the risk of any delay to completion of the Aberdeen Western Peripheral Route by instituting a further financial review of the project and, if so, what the level of risk was found to be.

S3W-802 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what the timescale is for the 2007 spending review.

S3W-803 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether Professor Lorne Crerar’s independent review of scrutiny of public services in Scotland has been completed.

S3W-804 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will publish the findings of Professor Lorne Crerar’s independent review of scrutiny of public services in Scotland.

S3W-805 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making in establishing baselines in the forthcoming Scottish budget to enable overall savings to be quantified and to ensure that the Executive pursues internal efficiency savings to a level comparable with those required of local authorities.

S3W-806 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what improvements it intends to make to the presentation of equalities information in the Scottish budget.

S3W-807 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-45 by Fiona Hyslop on 6 June 2007, whether the Cabinet Secretary for Education and Lifelong Learning, a member of her ministerial team or the First Minister has met the Scottish Funding Council to discuss the suspension of the University of Glasgow’s intake of undergraduate students at the Crichton Campus in Dumfries.

S3W-808 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-45 by Fiona Hyslop on 6 June 2007, whether the Cabinet Secretary for Education and Lifelong Learning, a member of her ministerial team or the First Minister has met the Principal of the University of Glasgow to discuss the suspension of the university’s intake of undergraduate students at the Crichton Campus in Dumfries.

S3W-809 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the circumstances which led to vaccines being stored at the wrong temperature in a medical practice in Aberdeen have arisen elsewhere in Scotland.

S3W-810 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the results of its national audit of vaccine storage procedures will be placed in the public domain.

S3W-811 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive whether its national audit of vaccine storage procedures has led to revaccination of any patients, other than patients of the medical practice in Aberdeen where the issue of vaccines being stored at the wrong temperature first arose.

S3W-812 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will assist private and NHS dentists to implement health and safety measures, including decontamination, in order to meet legislative requirements.

S3W-813 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to introduce a funded comprehensive oral health assessment for all patients.

S3W-814 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will review the criteria for determining dentists’ commitment to the NHS.

S3W-815 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to ensure that patients access orthodontic services at the correct stage in their dentition.

S3W-816 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-152 by Nicola Sturgeon on 4 June 2007, whether hospital parking charges can be considered to be excessive when they are used to cover the cost of providing parking facilities.

S3W-817 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is considering adding Parkinson’s disease to the list of conditions exempt from prescription charges.

S3W-818 John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to establish a dedicated fund to refurbish or modernise village halls and other community buildings.

S3W-819 Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive what support is available to re-establish piers in the highlands and islands.

S3W-820 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive why its targets for efficiency savings are 1.5% per annum when the targets for the rest of the United Kingdom are 3%.

S3W-821 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive why, when the UK overall efficiency savings targets have risen between September 2004 and September 2007, there has not been a commensurate rise in Scotland.

S3W-822 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-79 by John Swinney on 5 June 2007, what the clear, strategic outcomes at national level will be and what the Executive’s national policy outcomes are.

S3W-823 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-85 by Jim Mather on 31 May 2007, with which small European nations it aims to bring Scotland’s growth rate into line.

S3W-824 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance it issues to nursing homes to ensure that dementia patients receive adequate exercise.

S3W-825 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures can be taken to encourage bone density, thereby reducing fractures, in elderly nursing home residents.

S3W-826 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance it issues to encourage mobility in elderly residents of nursing homes.

S3W-827 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance it issues in relation to the use of Kirkton chairs and other forms of restraint in nursing homes.

S3W-828 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what training it recommends for staff responsible for dispensing medication in nursing homes.

S3W-829 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what staff-to-resident ratio it recommends for nursing homes with patients who have dementia.

S3W-830 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive whether particular procedures are in place in respect of foreign workers who require enhanced disclosure certificates and, if so, what these procedures are.

S3W-831 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will ensure that all pregnant women receive a consistent level of antenatal care, particularly in relation to the number of ultrasound scans they are offered.

S3W-832 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive which NHS boards offer pregnant women the recommended number of scans as part of their antenatal screening and care.

S3W-833 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take to address a national shortage of sonographers.

S3W-834 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will ensure that sufficient GPs are in place, in light of the number who are nearing retirement.

S3W-835 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to increase the number of training places for GPs.

S3W-836 Peter Peacock: To ask the Scottish Executive why Orkney Islands Council receives a lower rate of revenue support than Western Isles and Shetland councils; whether the gap has been narrowing over recent years, and what further steps the Executive plans to take to eliminate the gap in funding and in what timescales.

S3W-837 Peter Peacock: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to direct Historic Scotland to approve the plans of Anta Estates Ltd relating to the restoration of Castle Tioram and, if so, when.

S3W-838 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive when section 62 of the Legal Profession and Legal Aid (Scotland) Act 2007 will come into force.

S3W-839 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what discretion it has to vary the application of section 62 of the Legal Profession and Legal Aid (Scotland) Act 2007 in the case of solicitors who have retired from practice but continue to act as notaries public.

S3W-840 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what discretion it has to vary the application of section 62 of the Legal Profession and Legal Aid (Scotland) Act 2007 in the case of notaries public who make no charge for their service.

S3W-841 Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made by the Scottish Law Commission on its work on rape and other sexual offences.

S3W-842 Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to introduce a sexual offences Bill following the work of the Scottish Law Commission on rape and other sexual offences and, if so, when it expects the Bill to be introduced.

S3W-843 Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it anticipates that any forthcoming sexual offences Bill will propose the removal of the gender-specific element of rape offences.

S3W-844 James Tolson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it intends to make a decision on a new Forth crossing.

S3W-845 James Tolson: To ask the Scottish Executive what options are being considered in respect of a new Forth crossing and what the timescale is for such consideration.

S3W-846 James Tolson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the most recent estimate is of the cost of a new Forth crossing.

S3W-847 James Tolson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has made to secure the necessary capital funding for a new Forth crossing.

S3W-848 Charlie Gordon: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2O-10863 by Ross Finnie on 26 October 2006, when it will announce the funding of the upgrading of waste management facilities in the city of Glasgow.

S3W-849 Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive why the NHS does not contract private dentists to carry out free dental checks, given that it contracts private optometrists to carry out free eye checks.

S3W-850 Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive when residents of the John O’ Groats area will have universal access to broadband.

S3W-851 Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive when residents of the Kylesku area of north-west Sutherland will have universal access to broadband.

S3W-852 Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will maintain and boost centres providing for older persons in need of care and support, such as the Laurandy Centre in Wick.

S3W-853 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to amend the Community Regeneration Fund’s focus on delivering service for the 15% most deprived areas as identified by the Scottish Index of Multiple Deprivation.
