Business Bulletin No. 142/2002

Thursday 3 October 2002

Section D : Oral Questions selected for answer on 10 October 2002

  

Question Time

1. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps will be taken to improve the process of selecting sites for new health care facilities. (S1O-5736)

2. Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking in the field of flood prevention. (S1O-5753)

3. Elaine Thomson: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making in developing an integrated approach to the treatment and care of drug users. (S1O-5754)

4. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is in regular contact with British embassies. (S1O-5763)

5. Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive when Scottish Water will report on its review of tariff structures and whether the review will take account of the impact of recent increases in fixed and volumetric charges on the viability of small businesses. (S1O-5761)

6. Dorothy-Grace Elder: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to meet Glasgow Housing Association and Glasgow City Council with regard to the timetable for the proposed transfer of the council’s housing stock. (S1O-5762)

7. Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in ensuring that local authorities are able to introduce strategies that ensure that children with disabilities have equal access to education. (S1O-5751)

8. Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what provision it has made to ensure that fire services will be maintained should strike action be taken by firefighters. (S1O-5735)

9. Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the causes are of the fall in population. (S1O-5726)

10. Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any aspect of its policies has been amended as a result of representations made by any member of the Royal Family since May 1999. (S1O-5715)

11. John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to reduce binge drinking in the Highlands. (S1O-5721)

12. Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to reduce misuse of fireworks. (S1O-5718)

13. Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive, in the light of the decision of the Inland Revenue not to give a tax concession that would allow ex-gratia payments in respect of the Scottish Transport Group pension schemes to be made as tax-free lump sum payments, what action it will now take in the interests of the scheme members, given that the Inland Revenue has allowed tax-free lump sum payments to members of the National Bus Company pension scheme. (S1O-5716)

14. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it is providing to voluntary organisations working with survivors of heart disease and strokes. (S1O-5742)

15. Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has received about when the Regional Air Services Co-ordination Study will be published. (S1O-5747)

16. Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what the take-up rate has been for the new concessionary bus fares scheme. (S1O-5750)

17. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Cabinet Sub-committee on Sustainable Scotland last met and what matters were discussed. (S1O-5748)

18. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to attract, support and retain quality, high-skill call centre enterprises. (S1O-5727)

19. David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive how the NHS will cope with long-term population change. (S1O-5724)

20. Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to ensure that there are adequate transport links to the new accident and emergency department proposed under the acute services review in Glasgow. (S1O-5749)

21. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the McCrone settlement has benefited schools in rural areas. (S1O-5719)

22. Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making in developing services for women suffering from alcohol dependency. (S1O-5758)

23. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is supporting schemes that reduce the risk of re-offending and anti-social behaviour in children. (S1O-5746)

24. Mr Tom McCabe: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making in encouraging sporting excellence. (S1O-5760)

25. Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made of the impact of PPP and PFI schemes on the level of appropriate community access to school buildings. (S1O-5732)

26. John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to increase the number of junior doctors joining the NHS. (S1O-5720)

27. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what concerns it has regarding delay in the transfer of Scottish Borders Council housing stock. (S1O-5717)

28. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to promote British Sign Language and to encourage its use, particularly within the social work services. (S1O-5730)

29. Ms Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will issue updated planning advice on open space. (S1O-5737)

30. Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to introduce a decent homes standard. (S1O-5739)
