Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an “R”.

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http: //www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http: //www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

* S2M-2673 Dr Jean Turner: Europe’s Obesity Epidemic—That the Parliament welcomes the research conducted by the International Obesity Task Force which has prompted the European Commission to pledge joint action through the European Platform for Action on Diet and Physical Activity with the food industry, health experts and consumer groups to tackle Europe’s “obesity epidemic” and notes that more than 200 million adults across Europe may be overweight or obese, with a rapid rise amongst children, that obesity can cause many serious illnesses including respiratory disease, arthritis, heart disease, stroke, type 2 diabetes and certain types of cancer, that it is estimated that obesity accounts for between 2 and 8% of healthcare costs; that obesity is rising at the rate of 1% per annum in the United Kingdom and that a small reduction in weight loss of 10lbs can reduce risk of type 2 Diabetes by up to 30%.

* S2M-2672 Dr Jean Turner: Save Stobhill Hospital and Scottish Inpatient Beds—That the Parliament urges NHS Greater Glasgow to retain inpatient beds at Stobhill and to stop bulldozers moving in to Ward 4A after this condemned ward was reopened twice in the last fortnight on humanitarian grounds to move patients from trolleys into beds; considers that this clearly demonstrates that inpatient beds in Stobhill need to be retained in addition to the much desired Ambulatory Care and Diagnostic Unit being built; as a result urges the Scottish Executive not to cut further inpatient beds within the city of Glasgow; considers that to do so is not the way forward for the NHS in Scotland and that an assessment of the bed requirement for the 15% of patients who need inpatient treatment be conducted so that NHS Greater Glasgow and the NHS in Scotland have ample beds to meet patients needs.

* S2M-2671 Mr Duncan McNeil: Baby and Infant Mortality in Inverclyde—That the Parliament notes the publication of the Registrar General’s 2004 Preliminary Return; expresses concern that the figures reveal that Inverclyde has the highest rate of stillbirths and perinatal deaths per 1000 in Scotland, that neonatal deaths stand at over two and a half times the national rate and that infant deaths are more than double the national average; further notes that these figures follow the re-organisation of maternity and paediatric services by NHS Argyll and Clyde; acknowledges that these figures are unacceptable, and calls on the Scottish Executive and NHS Argyll and Clyde to investigate and address the reasons behind these mortality rates.

* S2M-2670# Mr Mark Ruskell: Millennium Ecosystem Assessment—That the Parliament notes with grave concern the Millennium Ecosystem Assessment, the most comprehensive scientific survey ever into the state of the planet, which concluded that human activities threaten the earth’s ability to sustain future generations; recognises the main findings of the report that humans have changed ecosystems more rapidly and extensively over the last 50 years than in any comparable period of time in history, that the changes made have contributed to substantial gains in well-being for some people but are unsustainable and have left many in poverty, that the degradation of ecosystems could grow significantly worse over the next 50 years and is a barrier to achieving the Millennium Development Goals and that the degradation of ecosystems may be reversed and increasing demands for ecosystem services managed but only if significant changes are made to policies, institutions and practices, and believes that the Scottish Executive should examine the Millennium Ecosystem Assessment, particularly the interventions suggested, and respond to it.

* S2M-2669 Michael Matheson: Crisis in Sudan—That the Parliament congratulates Alfred Taban on winning the 2005 House of Commons Speaker Abbot Award for exposing the scale of the killing in Darfur; recognises that Mr Taban has been arrested many times, harassed on a daily basis and his newspaper, the Khartoum Monitor, has been closed down on numerous occasions in fighting for media freedom and democracy in Sudan; recognises the scale of the crisis in Sudan with the death toll possibly reaching 300,000 and 2 million people having to abandon their homes; notes the recent House of Commons committee report which described the international communities response to the crisis as “scandalously ineffective”; believes that the international community must fulfil its responsibility to protect the people of Darfur, and supports the call for the situation in Darfur to be referred to the International Criminal Court.

* S2M-2668 Nora Radcliffe: Meeting Scotland’s Future Energy Needs—That the Parliament welcomes the House of Commons Scottish Affairs Committee’s report, “Meeting Scotland’s Future Energy Needs”; notes that that it was undertaken following discussions on a routine visit to the United Kingdom Atomic Energy Authority site at Dounreay because of the committee’s concerns about the effects on the current workforce and on the area’s economy of decommissioning Dounreay, about the lack of a long-term strategy for the management of nuclear waste and about meeting the shortfall in energy output once nuclear power no longer provides part of Scotland’s energy needs; notes that none of the committee’s conclusions and recommendations advocates commissioning new nuclear power stations, and endorses particularly those recommendations urging the Government to revisit the British Electricity Trading and Transmission Arrangements ( BETTA) as a matter of urgency and to invest in “clean coal” technology and research and development of emerging renewable technologies and recognising that conserving energy must be a major contributor to balancing energy supply and demand in Scotland in the future.

* S2M-2667 Mr Andy Kerr: Smoking, Health and Social Care (Scotland) Bill—That the Parliament agrees to the general principles of the Smoking, Health and Social Care (Scotland) Bill.
