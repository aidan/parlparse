Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-4613 Bill Butler: Students Stamping Out Sectarianism—That the Parliament welcomes the launch of NUS Scotland’s Stamp out Sectarianism campaign, supported by the Scottish Executive, which is aimed at combating sectarianism in Scottish colleges and universities and the communities in which students live; notes that the campaign incorporates posters, postcards, leaflets and the setting-up of a new website aimed at creating a wider awareness of the subject and educating students about the issue, and urges students throughout Scotland to sign the online pledge to challenge sectarian behaviour and attitudes.

Supported by: Mark Ballard*, Rob Gibson*, Robin Harper*

*S2M-4612 Patricia Ferguson: Tourist Boards (Scotland) Bill—That the Parliament agrees to the general principles of the Tourist Boards (Scotland) Bill.

Supported by: Ms Margaret Curran*

*S2M-4611 Cathy Jamieson: The Draft Fire (Scotland) Act 2005 (Consequential Modifications and Savings) Order 2006—That the Justice 2 Committee recommends that the draft Fire (Scotland) Act 2005 (Consequential Modifications and Savings) Order 2006 be approved.

Supported by: Hugh Henry*

*S2M-4610 Nicol Stephen: The Draft Fundable Bodies (Scotland) Order 2006—That the Enterprise and Culture Committee recommends that the draft Fundable Bodies (Scotland) Order 2006 be approved.

Supported by: Allan Wilson*

*S2M-4609 Cathy Peattie: Antonine Wall and Kinneil Museum—That the Parliament welcomes the support pledged to the World Heritage Site bid for the Antonine Wall by East Dunbartonshire, Falkirk, Glasgow, North Lanarkshire and West Dunbartonshire; notes that this is part of a multinational bid incorporating Germany, Austria, Poland, Slovakia and Hungary, linking sites that mark the boundaries of the Roman empire; believes that the Antonine Wall, running from Bo’ness in the east to Old Kilpatrick in the west, is an outstanding archaeological treasure, not just nationally but internationally, and should be promoted as such; is therefore concerned that Kinneil Museum, at the eastern end of the wall near to the remains of a roman fortlet, is threatened with downgrading or closure; notes that about 2,000 people have signed a petition backing the campaign to save the museum, and calls on Falkirk Council to heed local and international voices and recognise that Kinneil Museum is a unique resource that should be retained and enhanced.
