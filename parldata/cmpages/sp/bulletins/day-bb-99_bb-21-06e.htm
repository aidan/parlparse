BUSINESS BULLETIN 21/1999

Monday 21 June 1999

Section E –  Written questions lodged on 18 June 1999
 

S1W-139 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive, in the light of the recent report prepared for Glasgow City Council’s Best Value review, what plans it has to review the funding of Scotland’s museums and art galleries to ensure that council tax payers are not subsidising national resources.

 

S1W-140 Winnie Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to give an assurance that the Westminster Parliament will not legislate on devolved matters.

 

S1W-141 Winnie Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to give an official welcome to the Crofters Commission’s continuing investigation into absentee crofting.

 

S1W-142 Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive, in the light of the proposed convention that the Westminster Parliament should not legislate on devolved matters, what consultation has taken place with the Scottish Executive regarding the proposed amendments to the Housing (Scotland) Act 1987, the Social Work (Scotland) Act 1968, the Mental Health (Scotland) Act 1984 and the Children (Scotland) Act 1995 currently under consideration by Parliament as part of the Immigration and Asylum Bill.

 

S1W-143 Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive what rights of consultation and consent it will have under the terms of the Immigration and Asylum Bill currently being considered by Parliament, specifically as they relate to clauses 71 and 87 of the Bill.

 

S1W-144 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to make available details of any agreement reached during its recent meeting with the Scottish Salmon Growers Association and the Shetland Salmon Farmers Association to discuss the Infectious Salmon Anaemia crisis currently affecting the salmon farming industry.

 

S1W-145 Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to implement the Financial Issues Advisory Group recommendations in full and, if not, to state precisely which of the recommendations it does not intend to implement in full.

 

S1W-146 Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive a) to list the staff of the Executive Policy Unit, and the job titles, role and payscales of each member of staff and b) to specify the line manager, contract length and office location of each member of staff.

 

S1W-147 Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive (a) how many staff are employed in ministerial or junior ministerial private offices as policy advisers and related direct support staff and (b) what are the payscales in which each of these staff are placed.

 

S1W-148 Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to provide hospital care facilities in the Cumbernauld & Kilsyth constituency.

 

S1W-149 Andrew Wilson : To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to assess the impact of quarrying at Croy Hill on the historic Roman sites in the area and whether these are different from any existing plans.

 

S1W-150 Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive to state what plans it has to upgrade the A80 road and what representations it has received from home-owners affected by previous proposals.

 

S1W-151 Andrew Wilson: To ask the Presiding Officer what is the allocation of rooms on the fifth floor of Parliament Headquarters and specifically what is the name, function and job title of each person occupying each room.

 

S1W-152 Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer by Nicol Stephen on 17 June 1999, what plans it has to consult the Parliament on the number and range of new economic indicators required; how it intends to keep the Parliament informed of the progress of the review of additional needs and of any planned changes to official economics statistics, and what wider consultation it plans in relation to these changes.

 

S1W-153 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to make available information relating to the number of fixed penalties issued in respect of speeding offences in each of the last three years and to detail the local authority areas in which they were issued, the revenue received and the administrative costs incurred.

 

S1W-154 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what initiatives or legislative proposals it intends to bring forward to support or promote cycling proficiency in schools, and whether it intends to provide additional resources to do so either directly to schools or through local authorities.

 

S1W-155 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has had, or plans to have, any meetings with companies or organisations involved in the manufacture or supply of genetically modified food products.
