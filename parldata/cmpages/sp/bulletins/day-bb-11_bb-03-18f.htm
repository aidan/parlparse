Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally printed the day after they are lodged. If an amendment is lodged to a motion then the original motion will appear alongside the amendment, along with any support lodged that day, in this section.

Each Monday, this section also contains all motions and amendments lodged the previous week that are still live and any motions and amendments that have been lodged for debate in the coming week. A motion or amendment is live if it has not been debated, withdrawn or otherwise deleted from the list.

Where a motion or amendment has been withdrawn, it is indicated in this section the day after it has been withdrawn. Support for motions and amendments received after they are lodged is shown at the end of this section the day after such support is received.

Motions and amendments can be published with various symbols:

  • Asterisks before the motion or amendment number indicate a motion or amendment published for the first time;

  • Asterisks also identify alterations to the text of a motion or amendment made since it was first published;

  • A hash symbol identifies motions eligible for debate at Members’ Business;

  • A diamond symbol identifies motions lodged for Members’ Business that have not yet attracted the required cross-party support;

  • An "R", identifies motions or amendments in which the Member who lodged it has a registrable interest.

The Parliamentary Bureau periodically deletes motions or amendments not scheduled for debate and which are over six weeks old.

A full list of current motions is available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/motions.htm.

A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/index.htm.

Any questions in relation to this section should be directed to the Chamber Desk, for which contact details are provided at the end of the Bulletin.

*S3M-8195 Marilyn Livingstone: 40 Years of Victoria Radio Network—That the Parliament congratulates Kirkcaldy’s hospital radio, Victoria Radio Network (VRN), for entertaining patients, visitors and staff at Victoria Hospital for 40 years; understands that VRN was formed in February 1971 by a small group of radio enthusiasts who believed that there should be a radio station in Kirkcaldy that would act as an alternative therapy for those who were in hospital; believes that the success of VRN is a result of the determination of the volunteers at VRN, who have been nominated for three awards already in 2011, and wishes the team at VRN every success with entertaining patients, visitors and staff in the future.

*S3M-8194 Jack McConnell: Live Below the Line—That the Parliament welcomes the Live Below the Line campaign launched by the Global Poverty Project in March 2011; notes that supporters of the campaign will live on less than £1 a day between 2 and 6 May; believes that the campaign is an excellent way to highlight the plight of 1.4 billion people who live in extreme poverty, and looks forward to as many people as possible supporting the campaign and the Global Poverty Project’s aim of invigorating a global movement to take effective, concerted action to end extreme poverty in an a generation.

Supported by: Kenneth Gibson*, Jackie Baillie*, Patricia Ferguson*, Bill Kidd*, Michael McMahon*, Helen Eadie*, Ken Macintosh*, Elaine Murray*, Dr Richard Simpson*, Irene Oldfather*, Trish Godman*, Elaine Smith*, Patrick Harvie*, Mary Scanlon*, Ms Wendy Alexander*, Bill Butler*, Robin Harper*

*S3M-8193 Rob Gibson: Pilgrimage Route Development—That the Parliament welcomes plans to create a King James IV pilgrimage route from Edinburgh to Tain, with a possible extension south west from the capital to Whithorn; notes that the king made pilgrimages to Tain and Whithorn on several occasions; further notes that preparations are being made to launch the route in 2013, the 500th anniversary of the death of James IV; recognises the appetite for long-distance routes that can be accomplished by people of all abilities and using various means of transport; understands, in particular, that Whithorn was revered in the Middle Ages as the birthplace of Christianity in Scotland and that St Duthus Church in Tain had a strong association with the Bruce/Stewart dynasty; calls on public agencies such as Historic Scotland, Scottish Natural Heritage and VisitScotland to play a full part in this initiative in order that Scotland’s renaissance prince, James IV, may be remembered by many today who can celebrate his colourful life and achievements, and considers that the development of this pilgrimage route from north east to south west Scotland deserves the creation of local and national events along the way to celebrate the renaissance spirit of Scotland.

Supported by: Robin Harper*, Gil Paterson*, Stewart Maxwell*, Jamie Stone*, Brian Adam*, Bill Kidd*

*S3M-8192 Elaine Smith: Women’s Health and Hygiene—That the Parliament recognises the importance of supporting women’s health and wellbeing; is concerned that the purchase of sanitary products is having an increasing financial strain on women earning low incomes and young girls with no personal income; considers that these products are a necessity that are fundamental to the sustainment of good health and hygiene; commends the UNISON Women’s Conference 2010, which saw 200 votes in favour of providing free feminine hygiene products for women on low incomes; recognises that a lack of access to suitable sanitary provision can hinder young girls’ enthusiasm, concentration and general wellbeing in school; considers that schools should ensure that a sanitary product vending machine is readily available in school toilets; considers that access to sanitary products is a developmental issue that has a direct link to a young girl’s health, education and general welfare both in Scotland and across the world, and consequently believes that these items should not be considered to be a consumer luxury but should be available at no charge in healthcare facilities.

Supported by: Patricia Ferguson*, Marlyn Glen*, Bill Butler*, Trish Godman*
