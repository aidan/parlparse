Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-4822<> Ms Sandra White: Graduate Debt—That the Parliament notes the report published today by the Reform think tank which finds that UK-wide graduate debt has risen by 318% since 2000 and that graduates across the United Kingdom face a bleak financial future; further notes recent Royal Bank of Scotland research which found that, of all UK cities, Glasgow has, at 60%, the second-highest proportion of its students having to work part-time, and considers that the Graduate Endowment fee should be abolished and student loans replaced by grants as the best way to remove the debt burden and allow the full range of young people to participate in higher education.

*S2M-4821 Frances Curran: Scotland’s Long Walk for Peace Arrives at the Parliament—That the Parliament welcomes Scotland’s Long Walk for Peace which arrived at the Parliament on the afternoon of 19 September 2006 and congratulates all the walkers who have walked 85 miles from the Trident nuclear submarine base at Faslane to Edinburgh over the last six days; thanks the 36 MSPs who have signed the on-line petition supporting the walk for peace, and hopes that those MSPs who came out to welcome the marchers will have the courage of their convictions and vote in the Scottish Parliament at the next opportunity to rid Scotland of Trident’s nuclear weapons, noting that ordinary voters are disappointed by politicians who patronise them by doing one thing for the cameras of the assembled media but another at voting time.

*S2M-4820 Ms Maureen Watt: Ellon’s Fairtrade Status—That the Parliament congratulates the town of Ellon in Aberdeenshire on achieving Fairtrade status; notes that Ellon is the first Fairtrade town in Aberdeenshire; commends all members of the Ellon Fairtrade Town Steering Group who campaigned so successfully for Fairtrade status and the people of Ellon who have supported their efforts, and hopes that other Aberdeenshire towns will follow suit in order to help make the north east a Fairtrade region.

Supported by: Christine Grahame*, Ms Sandra White*, Richard Lochhead*

Supported by: George Lyon*

*S2M-4813 Ms Maureen Watt: Congratulations to Aberdeen—That the Parliament congratulates Aberdeen for winning the Beautiful Scotland in Bloom city award for the 39th year in a row and commends the city council workforce and the citizens of Aberdeen for their horticultural efforts.

*S2M-4812# Ms Sandra White: Pensioners’ Call for Link of Pensions to Earnings—That the Parliament congratulates the West of Scotland Seniors Forum (WSSF) and the Scottish Pensioners’ Forum (SPF) on their co-ordinated campaign across the country to oppose the Labour Government in stating that it cannot afford to raise the state pension or link it to earnings before 2012; agrees with the WSSF and SPF’s contention that this provides no immediate benefit for pensioners and that one million Scottish pensioners say the government is wrong, and believes that a citizens’ pension, linked to growth in earnings and funded by scrapping the present government refund to the highest-rated taxpayers on their private pension funds, should be introduced.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie*, Rob Gibson*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Richard Lochhead*, Alex Neil*, John Swinburne*

*S2M-4811 Karen Gillon: Clydesdale Parks—That the Parliament welcomes the decision by the Heritage Lottery Fund to award over £1.3 million to help renovate Castlebank Park and Delves Park in Lanark; congratulates South Lanarkshire Council on its ambitious proposals to significantly improve access, interpretation and learning opportunities at the two historic parks; believes that restoration of the 18th century walled garden, the reinstatement of the glasshouses and the development of a multi-sports play area will attract many new visitors and increase community involvement in local history, gardening and other activities, and looks forward to the parks playing an important role in celebrations such as Lanimer Day, the medieval fayre and other special events in the future.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie*

*S2M-4810 Miss Annabel Goldie: A Greener, Fairer Scotland—That the Parliament notes that, along with global poverty and terrorism, climate change is one of the three great challenges facing mankind; recognises that there needs to be a greater shared responsibility among government, business, individuals and families to meet this challenge; believes that combating the threat of climate change will require fresh ideas and radical thinking and, therefore, that the concept of decentralised energy should be seriously pursued; recognises that if we are going to achieve a sustainable Scotland we need to address culture and attitudes to raise awareness and encourage the public to be proactive and to assume personal responsibility for contributing to a sustainable Scotland, and calls, therefore, on the Scottish Executive to expand the Scottish Community and Householder Renewables Initiative to incentivise households, communities and small businesses to install modern energy creating and saving technologies as an important first step.
