Business Bulletin No. 111/2002

Friday 19 July 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-3280 Brian Adam: Northern Isles-Aberdeen-Rosyth-Zeebrugge Ferry Link-That the Parliament welcomes the successful introduction of the Rosyth-Zeebrugge ferry; notes that it hopes to serve all of Scotland as a maritime link to Europe, and calls on the Scottish Executive to investigate the extension of the northern isles ferry routes from the islands to Aberdeen and then to Rosyth to enhance access for the north and north east of Scotland to important business and tourism markets in Europe.

*S1M-3279 Ben Wallace: Sovereignty of Gibraltar-That the Parliament condemns the agreement reached by Her Majesty's Government with the Spanish Government on the future of Gibraltar; notes that, throughout talks leading to the agreement, Gibraltar and her people were denied a separate representative role; urges Spain to lift all current obstacles on the people of Gibraltar that restrict their enjoyment of free and full rights in accordance with membership of the European Union; endorses the position of the vast majority of Gibraltarians that their vote in any referendum should be the last word, and further notes the dire consequences to the strategic value of the United Kingdom of any move towards shared sovereignty.

Supported by: Phil Gallie*
