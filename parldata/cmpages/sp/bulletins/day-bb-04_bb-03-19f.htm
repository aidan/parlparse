Business Bulletin No. 51 / 2004

Friday 19th March 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-1073 Mike Pringle: Free Postage to Gulf Soldiers-That the Parliament supports all troops serving in the Gulf; notes that many friends and family in Scotland have boosted the morale of the troops by sending parcels and letters, and calls on the Scottish Executive to make representations to Her Majesty's Government to allow small packages and letters to be sent free through the British Forces Post Office to soldiers in the Gulf and not to levy charges on a vital service at this time of continued uncertainty in Iraq.

*S2M-1072# Sarah Boyack: World Water Day 2004-That the Parliament recognises the 11th World Water Day on 22 March 2004 as a time to remind everyone of the importance of water for our environment; acknowledges the need to focus attention on the vital need to protect and conserve water resources and supplies of drinking water; believes that in Scotland, the UK and worldwide everyone should have access to water that is safe and affordable; commends continued public ownership of water services in Scotland; notes with concern the situation in Indonesia and Argentina where foreign private ownership of water services has led to unreasonable and excessive water costs beyond the reach of poor households, and further notes with grave concern the suggestion that water may be included in the General Agreement in Trade Services negotiations and that this could lead to the privatisation of water services in Scotland.

*S2M-1071# Jackie Baillie: Loch Lomond Rescue Boat-That the Parliament warmly acknowledges the work of the Loch Lomond Rescue Boat; values the service it provides to local communities and visitors enjoying the delights of Loch Lomond; pays tribute to the dedication of the volunteers, who all give freely of their own time to run the service; recognises the importance of their work in promoting and teaching all aspects of safety in and around Loch Lomond, particularly for water sports; notes that a substantial portion of the annual funding is raised by the efforts of volunteers, and therefore considers that the Scottish Executive should investigate the provision of stable funding, similar to that provided for mountain rescue teams, to support this essential, life-saving service.

Supported by: Paul Martin*

*S2M-1070# Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: Proposed Closure of Five Primary Schools in Midlothian-That the Parliament believes that the proposed closure of five primary schools in a countryside setting in Midlothian is unjustifiable on educational and social grounds and that these schools are centres of educational excellence and of great importance to the local communities.

Supported by: David McLetchie*, Bill Aitken*, John Scott*

*S2M-1069# Ms Sandra White: Life Expectancy in Glasgow-That the Parliament notes with concern the recent life expectancy figures released by NHS Health Scotland; believes that these figures highlight a massive divide between the east and west and the more and less affluent areas of Scotland; notes that life expectancy in Glasgow Shettleston constituency is the lowest in Scotland at 63.9 years and that this is almost 15 years less than some parts of the country and on a par with many developing countries; believes that there should be a national debate on poverty and the links between ill-health and low life expectancy, and further believes that the Scottish Executive should make a ministerial statement about how it will address this problem.

*S2M-1068 Mike Rumbles: Prostate Cancer Awareness Week, 22 to 28 March 2004-That the Parliament notes that 22 to 28 March 2004 is Prostate Cancer Awareness Week; notes with concern that prostate cancer is now the most common cancer in men causing around 10,000 deaths annually in the UK, and recognises the important work carried out by prostate cancer support groups around the country in increasing awareness of this condition.

*S2M-1067 Mrs Nanette Milne: Congratulations to the University of Aberdeen on $1.85 million Gift-That the Parliament congratulates the University of Aberdeen on securing what is believed to be the single largest gift committed by an American fund to the study of humanities within a Scottish institution; notes that the $1.85 million gift will fund what is to be known as the Gluckman Chair of Irish and Scottish Studies; recognises that the university's Research Institute of Irish and Scottish Studies is the first of its kind in the world for graduate study and research on the history, language, literature and culture of Ireland and Scotland and one of the largest concentrations of Scottish or Irish expertise in any European university, and notes that the first holder of the new chair will be Professor Tom Devine, University Research Professor in Scottish history and Director of the Arts and Humanities Research Board Centre for Irish and Scottish Studies at the University of Aberdeen. *S2M-1066 Murdo Fraser: Strip Stamps on Scotch Whisky-That the Parliament deplores the announcement by the Chancellor of the Exchequer that Her Majesty's Government will introduce tax stamps on sprits throughout the UK; recognises that tax stamps will impact directly on the Scottish whisky industry; supports the statement by the Scotch Whisky Association that the introduction of tax stamps on whisky will impose financial pain on legitimate businesses; believes that a tax stamp system will be an ineffective means of combating fraud and illicit trade while imposing substantial costs and practical problems on the industry, particularly in relation to labelling and storage, and calls on the Scottish Executive to make immediate representations to Her Majesty's Government against the introduction of tax stamps.

Supported by: Phil Gallie*, Mr Ted Brocklebank*, Alex Fergusson*, Bill Aitken*, Margaret Mitchell*, David McLetchie*, Murray Tosh*, Mr David Davidson*, Miss Annabel Goldie*, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton*

*S2M-1065# Rob Gibson: Strategic Assessment of Far North Railway-That the Parliament welcomes the Scottish Executive's claim to record investment in public transport; notes its backing for reopening rail routes such as Stirling, Alloa to Kincardine and part of the Waverley line; further notes the recent Highlands and Islands Enterprise report showing that demand for the rail network in the Highlands is growing and that demand could be increased further by more investment in infrastructure and timetables, and considers that the Executive should initiate a strategic assessment of the rail service between Inverness, Thurso and Wick with particular attention to the feasibility of a Dornoch Firth rail crossing to shorten significantly the rail journey times on the route, taking into account specific provision for the Lairg loop stations.

Supported by: Ms Sandra White*, Mr Stewart Maxwell*, Tommy Sheridan*, Jim Mather*, Stewart Stevenson*, Mrs Margaret Ewing*, Richard Lochhead*, Eleanor Scott*

*S2M-1064 Mrs Nanette Milne: Blood Donations in the Parliament-That the Parliament encourages the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body to make facilities available to the Scottish National Blood Transfusion Service (SNBTS) to hold regular donor sessions for, specifically, MSPs and parliamentary staff; notes that the new policy of not accepting blood from donors who have received a blood transfusion since 1 January 1980 is likely to result in a loss of around four per cent of current donations; is concerned that, with secondary effects, this figure could increase to 10 per cent; welcomes the new initiative by SNBTS to recruit new donors and launch a new high profile media campaign later this year, and believes that the Parliament is in a unique position to be able to take the lead on the issue at a crucial time for securing national blood supplies.

*S2M-1063 Robin Harper: Measuring Quality of Life-That the Parliament acknowledges and agrees with the position of the New Economics Foundation report, Chasing Progress - beyond measuring economic growth, that the growth and consumption represented by gross domestic product does not give a true picture of quality of life and fails to deliver the Scottish Executive's own sustainable development targets; recognises that economic growth is not a good indicator of human progress and well-being; further recognises that present rates of economic growth are leading to unacceptable environmental risks, failing to guarantee social progress and do not make the population any happier, and calls on the Executive to introduce new social and environmental indicators such as environmental footprinting and the new measure of domestic progress as baselines to measure the overall performance of all its policies.

Supported by: Mark Ballard*, Shiona Baird*, Eleanor Scott*, Patrick Harvie*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Tommy Sheridan*, Richard Lochhead*, Mr Mark Ruskell*

*S2M-1062 Donald Gorrie: Support for Sport and Youth Activities-That the Parliament is concerned about the problems faced by many sports clubs in attracting young people, providing good quality coaching and giving them enough opportunities to play their sport, because of the lack or cost of facilities, the inadequate or non-existent funding of clubs from local or national sources and the lack of sport or physical activity in the lives of many young people*, and* calls on the Scottish Executive, local authorities and bodies such as sportscotland to provide an effective, extended curriculum in schools, including enough student and staff time to provide good opportunities for sports, music, drama and other activities and enough staff time to *develop fully* liaison between secondary schools and sports clubs and a full programme of sports and other activities both in schools after school time and at clubs, and *to recognise* the contribution made by school staff and club volunteers to the development of after-school sport through modest honoraria or other gestures and active measures to attract more voluntary helpers for sport and youth activities.

Supported by: Ms Sandra White*, Margo MacDonald*
