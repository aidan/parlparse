Section A – Today’s Business

Meetings of Committees

All meetings take place in the Scottish Parliament, unless otherwise specified. Contact details for Committee Clerks are provided at the end of the Bulletin.

Equal Opportunities Committee
20th Meeting, 2006

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in Committee Room 1

1. Budget process 2007-08 (in private): The Committee will consider a draft report.

Finance Committee
28th Meeting, 2006

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in Committee Room 2

1. Budget Process 2007-08: The Committee will take evidence from—

John Scott MSP and Nora Radcliffe MSP, Members of the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body; Ian Leitch, Director of Resources and Governance; and Derek Croll, Head of Financial Resources, Scottish Parliament.

2. Budget Process 2007-08: The Committee will consider the Scottish Commission for Public Audit’s report on Audit Scotland’s expenditure proposals for 2007-08.

3. Budget Process 2007-08: The Committee will consider issues to be included in its draft report.

4. Custodial Sentences and Weapons (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will take evidence on the Financial Memorandum from—

Willie Pretswell, Finance and Business Services Director, Scottish Prison Service; Rachel Gwyon, Director of Corporate Services, SPS; and Eric Murch, Director of Partnerships and Commissioning, SPS.

and then from—

Jane Richardson, Head of Parole and Life Sentence Review Division, Scottish Executive; Brian Cole, Community Justice Services Branch, Scottish Executive; and Gery McLaughlin, Head of Knife Crime Branch, Scottish Executive.

Procedures Committee
17th Meeting, 2006

The Committee will meet at 10.15 am in Committee Room 6

1. Review of Parliamentary Time: The Committee will consider the implications of an earlier deadline for lodging Stage 3 amendments; data on the use made so far of Rule 9.8.5A; the implications for SPICe of preparing briefings on all Bills amended at Stage 2; and the implications for topicality of an earlier deadline for lodging motions for debate.

2. Rule 10.3.2 (the "20-day rule"): The Committee will consider, on the basis of correspondence from committee conveners and from the Subordinate Legislation Committee, how to address the SLC request for a change to Rule 10.3.2.

3. Scottish Commission for Public Audit: The Committee will consider issues arising from the SCPA request for its meetings to be advertised in the Business Bulletin and reported in the Official Report.

4. Decisions on taking business in private: The Committee will decide whether to consider in private at future meetings draft reports on Rule 10.3.2 (the "20-day rule") and on the Scottish Commission for Public Audit.

5. Consolidation Bills (in private): The Committee will consider a revised draft report and draft standing order changes.

6. Review of Parliamentary Time (in private): The Committee will consider a draft report.

Subordinate Legislation Committee
31st Meeting, 2006

The Committee will meet at 10.30 am in Committee Room 5

1. Decisions on taking business in private: The Committee will consider whether to take agenda item 6 and all future discussions on its draft report in private.

2. Delegated powers scrutiny: The Committee will consider a response from the Executive to points raised on the following bill—

Planning etc. (Scotland) Bill at Stage 2.

3. Draft instruments subject to approval: The Committee will consider the following—

the Bus User Complaints Tribunal Regulations Revocation Regulations 2006, (SSI 2006/draft);

the Public Appointments and Public Bodies etc. (Scotland) Act 2003 (Treatment of Office or Body as Specified Authority) (Scottish Legal Complaints Commission) Order 2006, (SSI 2006/draft); and

the Registration Services (Consequential Provisions) (Scotland) Order 2006, (SSI 2006/draft).

4. Instruments subject to annulment: The Committee will consider the following—

the Building (Scotland) Amendment Regulations 2006, (SSI 2006/534);

Act of Sederunt (Fees of Sheriff Officers) 2006, (SSI 2006/539);

the Waste Management Licensing Amendment (Scotland) Regulations 2006, (SSI 2006/541); and

the Rice Products (Restriction on First Placing on the Market) (Scotland) Regulations 2006, (SSI 2006/542).

5. Instruments not laid before the Parliament: The Committee will consider the following—

the North Lanarkshire (Electoral Arrangements) Order 2006, (SSI 2006/532);

the Scottish Borders (Electoral Arrangements) Order 2006, (SSI 2006/533);

the West Lothian (Electoral Arrangements) Order 2006, (SSI 2006/535);

the City of Edinburgh (Electoral Arrangements) Order 2006, (SSI 2006/537); and

Act of Sederunt (Fees of Messengers-at-Arms) 2006, (SSI 2006/540).

6. Inquiry into the regulatory framework in Scotland: The Committee will consider evidence received to date in relation to its draft report and agree its programme for oral evidence.
