Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally printed the day after they are lodged. If an amendment is lodged to a motion then the original motion will appear alongside the amendment, along with any support lodged that day, in this section.

Each Monday, this section also contains all motions and amendments lodged the previous week that are still live and any motions and amendments that have been lodged for debate in the coming week. A motion or amendment is live if it has not been debated, withdrawn or otherwise deleted from the list.

Where a motion or amendment has been withdrawn, it is indicated in this section the day after it has been withdrawn. Support for motions and amendments received after they are lodged is shown at the end of this section the day after such support is received.

Motions and amendments can be published with various symbols:

  • Asterisks before the motion or amendment number indicate a motion or amendment published for the first time

  • Asterisks also identify alterations to the text of a motion or amendment made since it was first published;

  • A hash symbol identifies motions eligible for debate at Members’ Business;

  • A diamond symbol identifies motions lodged for Members’ Business that have not yet attracted the required cross-party support;

  • An "R", identifies motions or amendments in which the Member who lodged it has a registrable interest.

The Parliamentary Bureau periodically deletes motions or amendments not scheduled for debate and which are over six weeks old.

A full list of current motions is available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/motions.htm.

A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/index.htm.

Any questions in relation to this section should be directed to the Chamber Desk, for which contact details are provided at the end of the Bulletin.

New and altered motions and amendments

*S3M-6922 Johann Lamont: Congratulations to the Bullwood Project—That the Parliament congratulates the Bullwood Project on the official opening of its workshop in the Glasgow Pollok constituency; notes that the Bullwood Project is a woodturning and woodworking charity open to everyone; considers that it offers important support through training, skills development and the supervising of community service placements; in particular applauds its role in offering a place of comfort and welcome to those who may be vulnerable and isolated; celebrates the creativity, imagination, tenacity and determination of all those who have brought the project to this stage; considers that the project has an important environmental role in recycling trees from Glasgow’s parks and beyond, and believes that this project is an inspiring example of the potential of voluntary sector organisations to meet needs in a sustainable, practical way.

*S3M-6921 Ian McKee: Sexual Health Week 2010—That the Parliament welcomes the Family Planning Association’s (FPA) Sexual Health Week, to be held this year between 13 and 19 September; notes that the 2010 campaign will focus on raising awareness about sexually transmitted infections (STIs) in people over 50; further notes that the campaign was prompted by rising levels of STIs among over 50s considered in part to be a result of an increasing number of people starting new relationships later in life, and fully supports the FPA campaign to promote safer sex regardless of age.

Supported by: Robin Harper*, Sandra White*, Jackie Baillie*, Jackson Carlaw*, Kenneth Gibson*, Aileen Campbell*

*S3M-6920♦ David Stewart: The Importance of RAF Kinloss and RAF Lossiemouth—That the Parliament considers that RAF Kinloss and RAF Lossiemouth make a substantial contribution to the Moray economy; notes that the two bases collectively support 5,711 full-time equivalent jobs in the local economy, which equates to 16% of all full-time equivalent employment in Moray; further notes that direct on-site employment has been identified as 4,037 full-time equivalents and gross income as £158.3 million; considers that the wider impact on population is significant, with the families of RAF personnel accounting for 1,457 spouses and 1,919 children up to 16 years of age, which is 7% of the total population of Moray and 8% of its working-age population; believes that the economy and population of Moray are heavily dependent on the RAF and that the two bases make a highly significant contribution to the population and economic prosperity of the area, and considers that any decisions about the future of RAF Kinloss and RAF Lossiemouth should take into consideration the social and economic role played by both bases in the Moray area.

*S3M-6919 Pauline McNeill: Call for an Edwin Morgan Memorial—That the Parliament supports the proposal to erect a statue of Edwin Morgan on Byres Road in Glasgow’s west end; notes his success as the author of over 50 published books; believes that his reputation is world renowned and recognises his achievements as Glasgow’s first poet laureate and as Scotland’s first Makar; further notes that Morgan studied and taught at the University of Glasgow, and considers that it would be fitting to have a memorial in this area.

Supported by: Cathy Jamieson*

*S3M-6918 Cathy Jamieson: Knockshinnoch Castle Colliery Disaster—That the Parliament commemorates the 60th anniversary of the Knockshinnoch Castle Colliery disaster near New Cumnock, which saw 129 men trapped underground; remembers the 13 who lost their lives in the disaster; recognises the life-saving work carried out by all volunteers and rescuers, who helped bring 116 men back to safety, and, at this time, expresses solidarity with the miners and their families in Chile as work continues to rescue the men currently trapped underground in the San Jose copper and gold mine.

Supported by: Robin Harper*, David Stewart*, Bill Wilson*, Bill Kidd*, Mr Frank McAveety*, Margaret Smith*, Rhona Brankin*, Jackie Baillie*, Des McNulty*, Patricia Ferguson*, David Whitton*, Kenneth Gibson*, Aileen Campbell*

*S3M-6917 Sandra White: Job Boost for Glasgow’s Financial Services—That the Parliament welcomes the announcement by Barclays Bank to establish a global shared services hub in Glasgow’s financial district with the creation of an additional 600 jobs to complement its ongoing investment and wealth management divisions; supports the work of the Scottish Government in ensuring that Scotland continues to attract highly skilled jobs in what is seen as an increasingly competitive global market; understands that the terms of Regional Selective Assistance funding, which will support Barclays’ expansion plans, are conditional on Barclays meeting specific multi-year job creation targets, and believes that such conditions will lead to lasting benefits for Glasgow and Scotland’s financial services industry.

Supported by: Aileen Campbell*, Rob Gibson*, Alasdair Allan*, Bill Kidd*, Kenneth Gibson*

*S3M-6916 Margaret Smith: Congratulations to the Cramond Association on its 50th Anniversary—That the Parliament congratulates the Cramond Association on its 50th anniversary; notes that the association will run a series of events in the week beginning 6 September 2010 to celebrate, including genealogy workshops and guided walks around Cramond’s Roman fort, which was built around 142 AD; further notes the continued hard work of the association in promoting the amenity of the community of Cramond, Barnton and Cammo; commends its work to safeguard the area’s heritage, and wishes the association another prosperous 50 years.

*S3M-6915 Jackie Baillie: Loch Lomond Food and Drink Festival—That the Parliament welcomes the fifth annual Loch Lomond Food and Drink Festival, which takes place on Saturday 18 and Sunday 19 September 2010; notes that the festival is a celebration and demonstration of great Scottish cooking and excellent produce from both within Scotland’s first national park and throughout Scotland; understands that the festival will include cookery events, food stalls, whisky tasting and a continental market that will be sharing specialities from across Europe; congratulates the organisers on their hard work once again, and encourages everyone to visit Loch Lomond on 18 and 19 September to celebrate the best in Scottish food and drink.

*S3M-6914 Jackie Baillie: Entrepreneurial Success—That the Parliament congratulates Fraser Gibson and Asif Ali on their recent success in the Prince’s Scottish Youth Business Trust (PSYBT) Young Entrepreneur of the Year Awards; understands that Fraser and Asif, both of Dumbarton, finished first and second respectively in the awards; notes that Fraser Gibson’s company, United Sports USA, is an international sports scholarship consultancy that helps young talented athletes from across Scotland gain sports scholarships to American universities and colleges; further notes that Asif Ali’s company, Palladium Executive Hire, specialises in offering luxury cars for weddings and corporate and private parties; commends both men for being what it considers to be excellent role models for budding young entrepreneurs, and wishes both businesses the greatest of success for the future.

*S3M-6913 Stewart Maxwell: Israeli and Palestinian Peace Talks—That the Parliament welcomes the announcement that the Israelis and Palestinians are to resume peace talks; congratulates Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu and Palestinian National Authority President Mahmoud Abbas on what it considers a courageous move, and wishes the peace talks every success.

Supported by: Kenneth Gibson*, Mike Pringle*, Brian Adam*, Robin Harper*, Rob Gibson*, Bill Butler*, Bill Kidd*, Jackson Carlaw*

*S3M-6912 Elaine Smith: Well Done Ricky—That the Parliament congratulates Coatbridge boxer Ricky Burns on winning the WBO World super-featherweight championship; notes that he is currently Scotland’s only world boxing champion, and wishes him a long and successful reign.

*S3M-6911 Kenneth Gibson: Largs Has Got Talent—That the Parliament congratulates nine-year-old Becky Deveney on winning the Largs Has Got Talent grand final at a packed and atmospheric Barrfields Pavilion Theatre on Friday 3 September 2010 with her powerful rendition of Valerie; is aware that more than 60 acts auditioned for the contest with 13 reaching the grand final, and acknowledges what it considers to be the high standard of competition and the hard work of Largs Youth Theatre on producing the show in such a professional way and in providing weekly drama workshops for nearly 200 young people.

Supported by: John Lamont*, Aileen Campbell*, Stuart McMillan*, Sandra White*, Stewart Maxwell*, Bill Kidd*

S3M-6879 Bill Kidd: The UK June 2010 Budget and Equality Assessments—That the Parliament expresses concern, following remarks by the Equality and Human Rights Commission that the UK Government must consider the effect of budget cuts on vulnerable groups and that "Under equality legislation, the Treasury, like all public bodies, has a legal duty to pay "due regard" to equality and consider any disproportionate impact on vulnerable groups when making decisions, including decisions about the budget", at the apparent failure by the UK Government to carry out assessments of the effects of the June 2010 Budget on vulnerable groups and considers in particular that the severe cut-backs on disability living allowance, expressly to save money rather than based on the principle of fairness, would appear to fall disproportionably heavily on disabled people as a vulnerable group.

Supported by: Bill Wilson*

*S3M-6879.1 Johann Lamont: The UK June 2010 Budget and Equality Assessments—As an amendment to motion (S3M-6879) in the name of Bill Kidd, insert at end "; further believes that the Scottish budget must be assessed with due regard to equality; regrets that the Scottish Government has not ensured that single outcome agreements (SOA) submitted by local authorities have been subject to equality impact assessments, and believes that Scottish ministers should not accept SOAs without evidence of equality impact assessments having been carried out."

Supported by: Trish Godman*, Mary Mulligan*, Malcolm Chisholm*, Jackie Baillie*, Des McNulty*, David Whitton*, Mr Frank McAveety*, Patrick Harvie*, Claire Baker*, Alex Johnstone*
