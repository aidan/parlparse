Business Bulletin No. 137/2002

Thursday 26 September 2002

Section D : Oral Questions selected for answer on 3 October 2002
  

Question Time

1. Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is taking to protect vulnerable and at risk children. (S1O-5709)

2. Mr Keith Harding: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Minister for Social Justice last met Fife Council to discuss homelessness. (S1O-5686)

3. Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to reduce the risk of meningitis. (S1O-5668)

4. Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will ensure that the principles of the National Waste Strategy are adhered to by local authorities. (S1O-5688)

5. Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent action it has taken to encourage closing the pay gap between men and women. (S1O-5662)

6. Elaine Thomson: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is ensuring that all parts of Scotland benefit from the Spending Review 2002. (S1O-5697)

7. Mr John McAllion: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to protect the rights of children and adolescents under the Mental Health (Scotland) Bill. (S1O-5700)

8. Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the proposed Glasgow housing stock transfer will take place on 28 November 2002. (S1O-5701)

9. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making with the expansion of new community schools. (S1O-5714)

10. Ms Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what stage the discussions with the lead agency for the redevelopment of the Waverley Railway Station have reached. (S1O-5703)

11. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to increase the number of new community schools. (S1O-5669)

12. Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it gives to rural post offices to expand the public services that they offer. (S1O-5676)

13. Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will ensure that young people with special educational needs receive an education that helps them reach their fullest potential. (S1O-5694)

14. Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it intends to take to improve the disposable income of those households in Glasgow currently living in poverty. (S1O-5699)

15. Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures have been taken to improve the quality of life in the Highlands. (S1O-5696)

16. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is promoting walking and cycling. (S1O-5707)

17. Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will reduce the amount it spends on advertising. (S1O-5698)

18. Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to promote the new concessionary travel scheme. (S1O-5681)

19. Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to improve directional signing to the A92 coastal tourist route to Aberdeen. (S1O-5702)

20. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it now has any plans to measure the impact on the economy of Her Majesty's Government's 10% tax on oil and gas profits. (S1O-5666)

21. Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to promote enterprise in Fife. (S1O-5692)

22. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is implementing the Proceeds of Crime Act 2002 relating to the seizure of drug dealers' assets. (S1O-5708)

23. Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to improve services for disabled people. (S1O-5670)

24. Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive what effect the spending review will have on the A75 Trans-European Network Route. (S1O-5705)

25. Dorothy-Grace Elder: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to meet Glasgow Housing Association and Glasgow City Council concerning the timetable for the proposed Glasgow housing stock transfer. (S1O-5680)

26. Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to address any shortfall of new business start-ups in the greater Glasgow area. (S1O-5690)

27. Bristow Muldoon: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent progress it has made in tackling drug abuse. (S1O-5712)

28. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether its policy of free personal care for elderly people is being implemented nationally in line with its expectations. (S1O-5661)

29. Mr Murray Tosh: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the decision of Her Majesty's Government and the Strategic Rail Authority to reduce the South Central rail franchise from 20 to seven years will affect the award of the Scottish passenger rail franchise and what its current policy is on the duration of the next franchise. (S1O-5674)

30. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive what advice it has received on whether fluoridation of drinking water supplies, as considered in Towards Better Oral Health in Children, would be in contravention of any European Union law. (S1O-5704)
