Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (♦).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S3M-4609 Hugh O’Donnell: NHS Lanarkshire Youth Counselling Service—That the Parliament congratulates the NHS Lanarkshire Youth Counselling Service for its work with young people in North Lanarkshire; recognises the important contribution made by the service in supporting young people with varying emotional and psychological issues by providing non-judgemental, objective and independent counselling, and hopes that this success is continued as the service is introduced into South Lanarkshire schools.

Supported by: Bill Kidd*, Jim Tolson*, Jamie Hepburn*

*S3M-4608 Mr Frank McAveety: Reach for the Stars—That the Parliament congratulates St Anne’s Primary School in Calton, Glasgow, for achieving an outstanding report from Her Majesty’s Inspectorate of Education; recognises that, in the last two years, the school has turned itself around from a poor-performing school to one that has been highly commended for teamwork, boosting ambition and improving educational attainment; draws attention to the hard work of head teacher Louise Jarvie and all staff at the school who have worked together to overcome challenges facing the pupils, including poverty and poor health, and hopes that St Anne’s will give hope to schools across the east end whose pupils face similar challenges and encourage them that they, too, can reach for the stars, which has quite appropriately become the school’s motto.

S3M-4607 Anne McLaughlin: Castlemilk Community Champions—That the Parliament congratulates Castlemilk Pensioners’ Action Centre on winning the Community Champion Team Award in recognition of its work serving 500 pensioners from the local area; notes that the centre has been part of the community for over 20 years and serves up to 100 meals each day as well as providing activities and day trips to users, and wishes continued success and recognition for its excellent work in the future.

Supported by: Bob Doris*, Stuart McMillan*, Jamie McGrigor*, Rob Gibson*, Alasdair Allan*, Bill Kidd*, Aileen Campbell*

S3M-4606 Maureen Watt: Aberdeen City Young Single Persons Housing Project—That the Parliament congratulates Aberdeen City Council on the results of the recent Care Commission report on the council’s Young Single Persons Housing Project, which scored well out of six inspection areas, with two good outcomes and four very good outcomes, and recognises the efforts of the council’s staff in helping and supporting young, vulnerable, homeless people in their time of great need.

S3M-4605 Bill Kidd: Public Opposition to UK Government Plans to Renew Trident—That the Parliament notes the recent findings of an ICM poll, carried out on behalf of The Guardian newspaper, that show that a majority of the UK population is now opposed to the £76 billion Trident replacement programme, with 54% of voters indicating that they would prefer the UK Government to abandon nuclear weapons completely; believes that the obstinacy of the UK Government with regard to the renewal of Trident poses a major hurdle on the path towards international nuclear disarmament; considers that nuclear weapons provide little or no defence against the most immediate contemporary global security threats, namely climate change and terrorism, and, as such, calls on the UK Government to abandon immediately its misguided plans to lavish billions of pounds of scarce public resources on the renewal of this antiquated weapons system.

Supported by: Christine Grahame, Mike Pringle, Bill Wilson, Rob Gibson, Aileen Campbell, Alasdair Allan, Shirley-Anne Somerville, Stuart McMillan, Patrick Harvie, Bob Doris, Gil Paterson*, Anne McLaughlin*, Brian Adam*

S3M-4604 Jamie Hepburn: 50th Anniversary of Sacred Heart Parish, Cumbernauld—That the Parliament congratulates the parish of Sacred Heart, Cumbernauld, on celebrating its 50th anniversary in 2009; further congratulates the parish priest, Monsignor Pat Osborne, on the golden jubilee of his ordination to the priesthood, and recognises the important role that the parish and other churches in Cumbernauld have played in developing the community spirit of the new town in the years since its foundation.

Supported by: Jamie McGrigor, Aileen Campbell, Christina McKelvie, Bob Doris, Alasdair Allan, Gil Paterson, Bill Kidd, Maureen Watt, Sandra White, Stuart McMillan

S3M-4603 Mr Frank McAveety: Congratulations to St Paul the Apostle Church, Shettleston—That the Parliament congratulates St Paul the Apostle Church, in Glasgow Shettleston and the entire parish family involved in the organisation of the church’s golden jubilee celebrations, which took place on Sunday 12 July 2009 in the presence of His Grace, Archbishop Mario Conti; commends the continuing accomplishment of Catholic and Christian education, providing opportunity and progress for many young people in the east end; recognises that St Paul the Apostle Church was established 150 years ago and entrusts the present clergy, such as Father John Campbell, to continue the excellent work of the church in serving the local community, and praises the parish for the work that all parishioners do for the benefit of the east end of Glasgow.

Supported by: Margaret Curran, Alasdair Allan, Jackie Baillie, James Kelly, Bob Doris, Anne McLaughlin*, Jamie McGrigor*

S3M-4602 Liam McArthur: Scottish Education Awards 2009—That the Parliament congratulates the winners of the Scottish Education Awards 2009; celebrates the achievements of each of the schools, pupils and teachers nominated for awards this year and the high standards attained by all; applauds, in particular, the efforts of Shapinsay Primary School, whose commitment to becoming carbon neutral and self-sufficient in energy generation impressed the judges in the Greener Schools Award category; agrees that this achievement is all the more impressive given that the school has only 20 primary and six nursery pupils; hopes that the pupils, staff and parents will soon be rewarded with Green Flag status, and believes that the example set by Shapinsay Primary School and the other nominees for this year’s awards will help inspire schools across Scotland.

S3M-4601 John Wilson: £7.1 Million Investment in New Materials Recycling Facility at Bargeddie, Lanarkshire—That the Parliament welcomes the shift in focus from waste to resource reuse; recognises the importance of recycling in diverting 95% of household resources from landfill by 2020; notes the necessity of investment in the green jigsaw of recycling infrastructure, such as the £7.1 million investment at Viridor’s Materials Recycling Facility in Bargeddie, Lanarkshire; further notes the benefits of green jobs for communities and the creation of 19 new positions at the new recycling facility, and calls on the Scottish Government to continue its support for ongoing investment in zero waste technologies.

Supported by: Christina McKelvie, Mike Pringle, Gil Paterson, Bill Kidd, Sandra White

S3M-4600 Helen Eadie: Scottish Ambulance Service Response Following the Death of Kirsty Fotheringham—That the Parliament finds it reprehensible that, more than a year after the death of Kirsty Fotheringham following an epileptic fit, the Scottish Ambulance Service has not responded to representations made on behalf of her mother, Mrs Fotheringham; notes that letters to the Chief Executive of the Scottish Ambulance Service have not been acknowledged, and calls on the Cabinet Secretary for Health and Wellbeing to investigate as a matter both of urgency and importance to identify why prevarication and procrastination continue, with chief officials failing Kirsty’s mother and leaving all other epilepsy patients in West Fife at risk.

S3M-4599 Brian Adam: Aberdeen 3Rs Project—That the Parliament congratulates Aberdeen City Council on finalising the 3Rs project deal, which will refurbishment of one school and the building of nine others and a refurbished sports centre in the city; recognises that this is the biggest school-building programme in the city’s history, and wishes the council and its partners all the best in delivering the rest of the state-of-the-art school buildings, which will soon join the newly opened Heathryburn School.

Supported by: Alasdair Allan, Bill Kidd, Sandra White, Stuart McMillan, Nigel Don

S3M-4598 Mike Pringle: I Don’t Like Cricket (Oh No), I Love It!—That the Parliament acknowledges the importance of cricket as one of Scotland’s fastest-growing team sports; further notes cricket’s proud history in Scotland, which dates back as far as the 1780s; further notes the rising profile of the Scottish international cricket team, which played its first match in 1865, beating Surrey by 172 runs, and became an associate member of the International Cricket Council in 1994; acknowledges the importance of encouraging young people to get involved in sport including cricket, both in terms of promoting healthy living and ensuring Scotland’s continued sporting success; further acknowledges the vital role that the media has to play in promoting sport and the importance of television coverage of elite-level team sport to that function; welcomes the continued success and growth of cricket in Scotland, and encourages young people to get involved in all forms of team sport.

Supported by: John Lamont, Stuart McMillan, Ross Finnie, Ken Macintosh, Jim Hume, Patricia Ferguson, Liam McArthur, Nigel Don, Jamie McGrigor, Iain Smith, Shirley-Anne Somerville*

S3M-4597 Joe FitzPatrick: 10th Annual Dare to be Digital Competition—That the Parliament welcomes the launch of the 10th annual Dare to be Digital competition held by the University of Abertay, Dundee; notes that 70 students from nine different countries around the world will compete over the next 10 weeks to create a finished video game, which will then be judged on the basis of its creativity, market potential and use of new technology; notes that Dundee is a global player in the development of computer games and recognises the importance of the Scottish digital media industry, which employs 30,000 people and contributes £1 billion to GDP, and further welcomes the commitment of the Scottish Government to the digital media sector, which is demonstrated by the creation of a £3 million centre of excellence for computer games education at the University of Abertay, Dundee, and by its provision of funding to enable teams from India and China to compete in the Dare to be Digital competition.

Supported by: Nigel Don, Shirley-Anne Somerville, Alasdair Allan, Stuart McMillan, Rob Gibson, Bill Kidd, Gil Paterson, Aileen Campbell

S3M-4596 John Wilson: Children’s Asthma Practice Support (CAPS)—That the Parliament welcomes the NHS Lanarkshire project, Children’s Asthma Practice Support (CAPS), currently being piloted throughout Motherwell and Wishaw and which aims to provide improved asthma services in local communities; notes that 22 nurses have completed specialist training to ensure that every health centre in Motherwell and Wishaw has a practice trained nurse who is up-to-date with the most recent asthma management model; recognises the work of nurses, doctors and local parents who have worked with the paediatric respiratory services team to design this new model of asthma care for children, and understands that treating stable asthma in children within the local area will increase confidence in managing children’s asthma and allow hospital-based clinicians to allocate more time to those with more severe cases of the illness.

Supported by: Stuart McMillan, Mary Scanlon, Joe FitzPatrick, Bill Kidd, Christina McKelvie, Pauline McNeill, Gil Paterson

S3M-4595 John Wilson: An End to Concessionary Fares Scheme Abuses—That the Parliament notes with concern the abuses of the Scottish Government’s National Concessionary Travel Scheme, which have resulted in Transport Scotland pursuing approximately £100,000 in suspicious claims; further recognises that, in 2008, Transport Scotland claimed back £2 million following investigation into suspicious claims by bus operators; welcomes the zero tolerance approach adopted by Transport Scotland in 2009, which has resulted in several cases being referred to the police for further investigation and possible criminal convictions; worries that the National Concessionary Travel Scheme, which since 2006 has offered free travel to older and disabled people, will be undermined if suspect practices by rogue bus operators continue, but recognises the valuable work of the majority of transport operators who continue to abide by the regulations outlined in the scheme and who have assisted in highlighting incidents of bad practice.

Supported by: Stuart McMillan, Elaine Smith, Bill Kidd, Rob Gibson, Mike Pringle, Christina McKelvie, Bob Doris, Gil Paterson

S3M-4594 Sarah Boyack: Thank You to Ewan Williamson and Lothian and Borders Fire and Rescue Service—That the Parliament records its thanks to firefighter Ewan Williamson and the Lothian and Borders Fire and Rescue Service; salutes the bravery of firefighter Williamson and his colleagues from Lothian and Borders Fire Service who saved more than 20 people from a blaze above the Balmoral Bar in Dalry Road in the early morning of Sunday 12 July 2009; expresses its condolences to firefighter Williamson’s family, and thanks firefighters across the country for the exceptional work they do day in, day out protecting our local communities.

S3M-4593 Kenneth Gibson: Ayrshire Ladies’ County Golf Association—That the Parliament congratulates the Ayrshire Ladies’ County Golf Association on the celebration of its centenary; notes its important work over the last 100 years in furthering the interests of Scottish ladies’, junior and girls’ amateur golf, and wishes the association well in its junior competition in August and its senior team’s participation in the inter-county competition to be held later this year.

S3M-4592 Jackie Baillie: Aitkenbar Primary, Fabulous at 50—That the Parliament congratulates Aitkenbar Primary School on its 50th anniversary this year; notes that the school first opened its doors to pupils in 1959 and has been a vital part of the community ever since; understands that the school has a wide variety of activities planned to mark this momentous occasion, including a quilt made of old school jumpers from throughout the years, concert performances and an open week encouraging ex-pupils to come back and reminisce about their time at the school, and wishes all the pupils, teachers and parents well in their celebrations and for another successful 50 years.

Supported by: Stuart McMillan, David Stewart, Jamie McGrigor, Mr Frank McAveety, Gil Paterson, Marilyn Livingstone

S3M-4591 Sandra White: It’s Just Not Cricket—That the Parliament is concerned at the almost wall-to-wall coverage of the Ashes Test Series by the media; compares this to the limited coverage of other sports in Scotland such as curling and, in particular, Scotland international football matches, and considers this to be further evidence that Scotland needs its own broadcasting channel.

Supported by: Rob Gibson, Gil Paterson, Bill Kidd
