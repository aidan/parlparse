Tuesday 5 October 2004

Section A : Daily Business List
  

Meetings of Committees

All meetings take place in the Scottish Parliament, Edinburgh unless otherwise indicated.

Environment and Rural Development Committee

23rd Meeting, 2004

The Committee will meet at 9.30 am in Committee Room 2

1. Declaration of interests: The new member of the Committee will be invited to declare any relevant interests.

2. Subordinate legislation: Ross Finnie MSP, Minister for Environment and Rural Development, to move—

motion S2M-1715 in the name of Ross Finnie MSP—That the Environment and Rural Development Committee recommends that the Fishing Vessels (Satellite-tracking Devices) (Scotland) Scheme 2004 (SSI 2004/379) be approved;

motion S2M-1716 in the name of Ross Finnie MSP—That the Environment and Rural Development Committee recommends that the draft Ethical Standards in Public Life etc. (Scotland) Act 2000 (Modification of National Parks (Scotland) Act 2000) Order 2004 be approved; and

motion S2M-1717 in the name of Ross Finnie MSP—That the Environment and Rural Development Committee recommends that the draft Scotland Act 1998 (Functions Exercisable in or as Regards Scotland) Order 2004 be approved.

3. Petitions: The Committee will consider the following petitions—

PE517 and PE645 on control of odours from waste water treatment works.

4. Executive consultations on water services: The Committee will take evidence from Ross Finnie MSP, Minister for Environment and Rural Development, on the following Executive consultations—

Investing in Water Services 2006-2014: the Quality and Standards III Project;

Paying for Water Services 2006-2010: a consultation on the principles of charging for water services.

5. Water Services etc. (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will take evidence at Stage 1 from Ross Finnie MSP, Minister for Environment and Rural Development.

6. Subordinate legislation: The Committee will consider the following negative instrument—

the Pesticides (Maximum Residue Levels in Crops, Food and Feeding Stuffs) (Scotland) Amendment (No. 3) Regulations 2004, (SSI 2004/399).

Public Petitions Committee

15th Meeting, 2004

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in Committee Room 5

1. New Petitions: The Committee will consider the following new petitions—

PE772 Ensuring that any proposed clinical strategy emerging from NHS Boards, such as NHS Argyll and Clyde, must clearly demonstrate cross boundary working in the interests of patient care. Jackie Baillie MSP will make a brief statement in support of her petition accompanied by Jackie Pollock and John Corcoran.

PE771 Guidance for local authorities to safeguard the provision of playing fields and recreation open space. David Ferguson will make a brief statement in support of the petition accompanied by Olena Stewart and Kenneth Wilson.

PE774 Retaining the provision of acute 24 hour a day, all year round consultant-led services across Scotland, including rural communities. John Hutchison will make a brief statement in support of the petition accompanied by Stuart Maclean and Patricia Jordan.

PE754 Accelerating the review of local government finance and in the meantime consider a means of reducing the impact of this year’s increases. Christine Grahame MSP will make a brief statement in support of her petition.

PE769 Unifying the planning permission process for mobile phone and TETRA installations and halting all TETRA installations until the health and other effects are clarified.

PE770 Investigating apparent widespread undue influence of children by any family member as a result of parental separation and establishing Family Law and Children’s Law Centres.

2. Current Petitions: The Committee will further consider the following current petitions—

PE504 Preventing convicted murderers or members of their families profiting from their crimes by selling accounts of their crimes for publication. The Committee will consider a response from the Minister for Justice.

PE658 Reducing the voting age for all Scottish Parliament and Local Government elections from 18 to 16 years. The Committee will consider responses from the Scottish Executive and Electoral Commission.

PE691 Investigation into implementation of the Forestry Commission guidance. The Committee will consider a response from the petitioner.

PE703 Ensuring Historic Scotland is accountable for its decisions and responsive to the views of communities. The Committee will consider a response from the Scottish Executive.

PE714 Reviewing the validity of Field Impairment Tests (FIT) and issuing relevant guidance. The Committee will consider responses from the Scottish Executive, Association of Chief Police Officers in Scotland and the petitioner.

PE716 Replacing the Grampian Regional Council (Harbour) Order Confirmation Act 1987 with equitable legislation. The Committee will consider a further response from the Scottish Executive.

PE717 Providing adequate funding to allow organisations such as Independent Special Education Advice to continue its work across Scotland. The Committee will consider a response from the Minister for Education and Young People.

PE735 NHS Argyll and Clyde and NHS Greater Glasgow agreement on transferring responsibility for the design and provision of health services in the North Clyde area. The Committee will consider responses from the Scottish Executive, NHS Greater Glasgow and NHS Argyll and Clyde.
