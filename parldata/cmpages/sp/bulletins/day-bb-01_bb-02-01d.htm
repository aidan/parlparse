BUSINESS BULLETIN 20 / 2001

Thursday 01 February 2001

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 8 February 2001

 

 

Question Time

1. Mr John McAllion: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to promote a regional and strategic approach to housing provision which transcends local authority boundaries. (S1O-2926)

2. Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made regarding the appointment of the new Chief Executive of visitscotland. (S1O-2949)

3. Bristow Muldoon: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are being taken to expand the rail network. (S1O-2951)

4. Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive what the current total level is of debt accumulated by all NHS Trusts. (S1O-2945)

5. Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met representatives of the Convention of Scottish Local Authorities and what issues were discussed. (S1O-2946)

6. Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what it is doing to ensure that information and communications technology reaches disadvantaged communities. (S1O-2904)

7. Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive what process it is undertaking to seek public views on the appointment of a new Chair of the Scottish Arts Council. (S1O-2921)

8. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1O-2781 by Susan Deacon on 18 January 2001, whether it will provide further resources to assist regional NHSiS facilities such as Glasgow Royal Infirmary. (S1O-2940)

9. Mr Frank McAveety: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will ensure that the views of tenants are considered in stock transfer proposals. (S1O-2918)

10. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive whether elderly people are safe from abuse in institutions. (S1O-2924)

11. Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive what arrangements it is making to hold more NHS statistics centrally and which subjects are currently under consideration in this connection. (S1O-2905)

12. Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will instruct the Scottish Higher Education Funding Council to reconsider its proposals to amend its distribution of funding in view of the impact on newer universities. (S1O-2909)

13. Miss Annabel Goldie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the current procedure for certifying the cause of death prior to the disposal of mortal remains by cremation is adequate. (S1O-2903)

14. Tricia Marwick: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last made representations to the Department of Social Security in connection with compensation for ex-miners in Scotland. (S1O-2943)

15. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive what response it will make to the report of the McLean review group on the removal of organs from dead patients. (S1O-2927)

16. Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to increase awareness and treatment of conditions such as chronic fatigue syndrome and myalgic encephalomyelitis (ME), particularly among young people. (S1O-2935)

17. Janis Hughes: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Health Technology Board for Scotland will report on its findings regarding the clinical efficacy of Beta Interferon in the treatment of multiple sclerosis. (S1O-2911)

18. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made with respect to the implementation of the report Our National Health: A plan for action, a plan for change and what specific guidance has been given to Ayrshire and Arran Primary Healthcare Trust regarding this. (S1O-2928)

19. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in finding an alternative to warrant sales. (S1O-2920)

20. Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take to deal with any bullying and harassment experienced by health board staff. (S1O-2914)

21. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive how it intends to help health boards meet the costs of implementing European working time regulations with particular reference to junior doctors. (S1O-2941)

22. Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has identified since 1 July 1999 any areas where the environmental management of its departments and buildings could be improved. (S1O-2930)

23. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make it its policy to minimise the number and scale of areas designated in structure plans for open cast coal mining. (S1O-2913)

24. David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to impose statutory duties relating to flood defence and flood warning. (S1O-2925)

25. Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will ensure that the whole of Scotland has access to GP services of the highest quality. (S1O-2919)

26. Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures the Scottish Executive will take to help with the erection of a suitable and lasting national memorial to the pipers of the Highland regiments who fought in the First World War. (S1O-2952)

27. Ms Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive how much responsibility its Health Department and the Minister for Health and Community Care have in relation to NHS Trusts’ control of their budgets. (S1O-2950)

28. Mr Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive when the BCG vaccine was last administered to schoolchildren. (S1O-2932)

29. Cathie Craigie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will promote Scotland’s distinctive attractions to encourage tourism. (S1O-2917)

30. Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will fund extended opening of community sports facilities. (S1O-2907)
