Section E – Written questions lodged on 22 September 2005

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-19428 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answers to questions S1W-15477, S2W-16906 and S2W-19097 by Mr Jim Wallace, Mr Tom McCabe and Cathy Jamieson on 14 May 2001, 2 June and 21 September 2005 respectively, why information on the cost of fuel for police vehicles was available for 1999-2000 and 2000-01 but not for 2001-02, 2002-03, 2003-04 or 2004-05 and whether it will review its answer to question S2W-19097.

S2W-19429 Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many households with children and in fuel poverty, as defined in the Scottish House Condition Survey 2002, live in buildings rated (a) poor, (b) moderate and (c) good.

S2W-19430 Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many households with children and in fuel poverty, as defined in the Scottish House Condition Survey 2002, live in buildings with no central heating systems.

S2W-19431 Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many households with children and in fuel poverty, as defined in the Scottish House Condition Survey 2002, are in receipt of a disability benefit related to a child.

S2W-19432 Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many children, as distinct from households with children, live in fuel poverty as defined in the Scottish House Condition Survey 2002.

S2W-19433Δ Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list the voluntary organisations to which it has allocated (a) direct grants, indicating the amount awarded in each case and (b) indirect grants, indicating the amount offered in each case, in 2005-06.

S2W-19434 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive how many treatment courses of the antiviral drug, Tamiflu, it has purchased.

S2W-19435 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what procedures are in place to monitor the development by NHS boards of multi-agency contingency plans for an outbreak of pandemic influenza.

S2W-19436 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what additional resources will be made available to NHS boards in the event of an outbreak of pandemic influenza.

S2W-19437 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what the target rate for the uptake of immunisation against infection in the event of an influenza pandemic will be, assuming that an effective vaccine has been obtained.

S2W-19438 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive whether arrangements in Scotland meet the UK Department of Health’s timetable for (a) rolling out an influenza pandemic plan and (b) meeting the target for vaccine procurement.

S2W-19439 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects the documents known as “sleeping scripts”, referred to in UK Health Departments’ UK Influenza Pandemic Contingency Plan, to be published by NHS 24 to provide advice to patients in the event of an influenza pandemic and how frequently such scripts will be reviewed.

S2W-19440 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what evidence it has on the effectiveness of stockpiling Oseltamivir to reduce the impact of an influenza pandemic and how many doses of Oseltamivir it has obtained.

S2W-19441 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to publicise the steps that the public should take to minimise the risk of transmission and infection in the event of an outbreak of an influenza pandemic.

S2W-19442 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to hasten the development of a vaccine in the event of an outbreak of an influenza pandemic.

S2W-19443 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive when the phases specified in the UK Influenza Pandemic Plan published in March 1997 became inconsistent with the phases which trigger public health action, as defined by the World Health Organisation, and when it anticipates that the World Health Organisation will next amend the phases.

S2W-19444 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it has made in deciding whether to purchase protection for care workers, health workers and groups at risk in the event of a pandemic.

S2W-19445 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive how many NHS Scotland publications there have been in each of the last six years.

S2W-19446 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive how much funding has been provided for NHS Scotland publications in each of the last six years.

S2W-19447 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive how many NHS board publications there have been in each of the last six years, broken down by NHS board.

S2W-19448 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive how much funding has been allocated to each NHS board for NHS publications in each of the last six years.

S2W-19449 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive how many NHS staff are employed in the production of NHS publications.

S2W-19450 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive how many NHS staff have been employed in the production of NHS board publications in each of the last six years, broken down by NHS board.

S2W-19451 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive how many publicly funded health publications there have been in each of the last six years.

S2W-19452 Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive how much has been spent on publicly funded health publications in each of the last six years.

S2W-19453 Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many cases were processed in each sheriff court in each of the last five years.

S2W-19454 Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many cases were subject to a continuance in each sheriff court in each of the last five years and what the average duration of a continuance was.

S2W-19455 Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average time to process a case in each sheriff court was in each of the last five years.

S2W-19456 Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many cases were processed by each sheriff in each of the last five years.

S2W-19457 Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many cases were subject to a continuance by each sheriff in each of the last five years and what the average duration of a continuance was.

S2W-19458 Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average time to process a case by each sheriff was in each of the last five years.

S2W-19459 Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many forensic science centres there are in Scotland.

S2W-19460 Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many forensic scientists there were in Scotland in each of the last five years.

S2W-19461 Margaret Mitchell: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average time for forensic evidence to be processed was in each of the last five years.

S2W-19462 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how the £0.75 million funding will be spent to support older people and tackle age discrimination, as set out on page 115 of the Draft Budget 2006-07.

S2W-19463 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what action will be taken to enhance sustainable development by “dealing with vacant and derelict land”, as outlined on page 115 of the Draft Budget 2006-07.

S2W-19464 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-18690 by Peter Peacock on 20 September 2005, how much funding is available for the development of educational resources for the teaching of Scottish literature and the works of Robert Burns at both primary and secondary school levels.

S2W-19465 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has received any request for information from NHS Highland regarding the cost of services within the NHS Argyll and Clyde area transferring to it and, in particular, the (a) resource allocation that this area will attract, (b) share of any in-year deficit that remains outstanding and (c) contribution which NHS Highland will be required to make in respect of NHS Argyll and Clyde’s recovery proposals and, if so, whether it has provided, or will provide, the information.

S2W-19466 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether full access to all accounting and other relevant information will be given to NHS Highland in respect of the proposed re-drawing of boundaries between NHS Argyll and Clyde, NHS Highland and NHS Greater Glasgow.

S2W-19467 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it can take to mitigate the effects of high fuel prices on disabled people.

S2W-19468 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it released information regarding the tendering of the Gourock to Dunoon ferry services to any MSP before the Minister of Transport and Telecommunications issued the notice of his intentions in this respect.
