Business Bulletin No. 2/2011: Tuesday 11 January 2011

Section A – Today’s Business

Meetings of Committees

All meetings take place in the Scottish Parliament, unless otherwise specified. Contact details for Committee Clerks are provided at the end of the Bulletin.

Justice Committee
1st Meeting, 2011

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in Committee Room 6

1. Domestic Abuse (Scotland) Bill (in private): The Committee will consider a revised draft Stage 1 report.

2. Subordinate legislation (in private): The Committee will consider a draft report on four affirmative instruments considered at its meeting on 21 December 2010.

Finance Committee
1st Meeting, 2011

The Committee will meet at 1.30 pm in Committee Room 1

1. Decision on taking business in private: The Committee will decide whether its consideration of a draft report on Scotland’s Spending Plans and Draft Budget 2011-12 should be taken in private at this meeting and future meetings.

2. Scottish Variable Rate inquiry The Committee will take evidence by video conference from—

Sarah Walker, Director, PAYE, Self Assessment and NI Contributions, and Janet Clayton, Assistant Director, PAYE, Self Assessment and NI Contributions, HM Revenue and Customs.

3. Scotland’s Spending Plans and Draft Budget 2011-12: The Committee will consider a draft report on the Scottish Government’s Spending Plans and Draft Budget 2011-12.

Public Petitions Committee
1st Meeting, 2011

The Committee will meet at 2.00 pm in Committee Room 2

1. Consideration of a current petition: The Committee will consider the following current petition—

PE1319 Improving youth football. The Committee will take evidence from—
Shona Robison MSP, Minister for Public Health and Sport, Scottish Government;
Stewart Regan, Chief Executive, Scottish Football Association
Neil Doncaster, Chief Executive, Scottish Premier League;
Tam Baillie, Scotland’s Commissioner for Children and Young People;
Martin Bain, Chief Executive, and Jim Sinclair, Director of Youth Development, Rangers Football Club; and
Chris McCart, Head of Academy and Youth, Celtic Football Club

2. Consideration of new petitions: The Committee will consider the following new petitions—

PE1379 Green alternatives to incineration in Scotland. The Committee will take evidence from Michael Gallagher, Professor Paul Connett, StLawrence University, USA and Shlomo Dowen, United Kingdom Without Incineration Network (UKWIN)
PE1380
Prohibiting the resale of football tickets

3. Consideration of current petitions: The Committee will consider the following current petitions—

PE1056 DVT
PE1115 National and regional transport strategies
PE1196 Tail docking
PE1230 Tail docking
PE1295 The planning system and the protection of national scenic areas
PE1310 Amendment of Mental Health (Care and Treatment) Act
PE1312 Investigation into the 1949 Yangtze campaign medal awards system
PE1358 Use of sodium fluoride and calcium fluoride
PE1360 Lack of modern language training in initial teacher training programmes for primary school teachers
PE1361 Involvement of the Scottish judiciary in the legislative process
PE1362 Post legislative scrutiny of the Family Law (Scotland) Act 2006
PE1364 Clarify guidelines on Gypsy/Traveller encampments
PE1365 Needs of kinship care children.
PE1366 Abolition of church appointments to the General Teaching Council for Scotland
PE1367 Ban Mosquito devices now.
PE1368 Political education for all.
PE1369 Young people’s views on provision of leisure and cultural facilities.

Transport, Infrastructure and Climate Change Committee
1st Meeting, 2011

The Committee will meet at 2.00 pm in Committee Room 4

1. Mobility and Access Committee for Scotland: The Committee will take evidence from—

Anne MacLean, Convener, Andrew Holmes, Member, and Grahame Lawson, Member, Mobility and Access Committee for Scotland.

2. Sustainable development: progress by the Scottish Government: The Committee will take evidence from—

Maf Smith, Director, and Professor Jan Bebbington, Vice-Chair, Sustainable Development Commission Scotland.

3. Road safety - witness expenses: The Committee will be invited to delegate to the Convener responsiblity for arranging for the SPCB to pay, under Rule 12.4.3, any expenses of witnesses incurred during its work on road safety issues.

Scotland Bill Committee
1st Meeting, 2011

The Committee will meet at 2.15 pm in Committee Room 6

1. Decision on taking business in private: The Committee will decide whether to take item 3 in private.

2. Scotland Bill (UK Parliament legislation): The Committee will take evidence from—

Professor Iain McLean, Official Fellow in Politics, University of Oxford;

and then from—

Professor Drew Scott, Professor of European Union Studies, University of Edinburgh;
Professor Andrew Hughes Hallett, Professor of Economics and Public Policy, George Mason University.

3. Work programme: The Committee will review its work programme.
