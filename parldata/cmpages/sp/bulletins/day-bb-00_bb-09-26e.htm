BUSINESS BULLETIN 144 / 2000

Tuesday 26 September 2000

Section E - Written questions lodged on 25 September 2000

 

 

 

 

S1W-10064 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what input it has had into the report of the Dog Identification Working Group set up by the Department of the Environment, Transport and the Regions.

S1W-10065 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has received a copy of the report of the Dog Identification Working Group and, if so, what action it plans to take in the light of the report and what consultations it intends to hold prior to taking any such action.

S1W-10066 Mr Duncan Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-4691 by Sarah Boyack on 27 March 2000, what its share was, as an amount and as a percentage, of the cost of the UK Biodiversity Action Plan and whether it will give a breakdown of this cost.

S1W-10067 John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what the anticipated timescale is for the construction of the Hansel Village footbridge over the A77.

S1W-10068 Kay Ullrich: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are being taken to raise the number of infection control nurses in Scotland to the internationally recommended figure of one per 250 beds in the light of the increase in cases of methicillin resistant Staphylococcus aureas in recent years.

S1W-10069 Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has for a scheme to support the construction and development of village halls in rural Scotland.

S1W-10070 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive on how many occasions the powers of direction granted by (a) section 11 of the Natural Heritage (Scotland) Act 1991 and (b) section 9 of the Further and Higher Education (Scotland) Act 1996 have been exercised.

S1W-10071 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive on how many occasions a direction has been issued to a non-departmental public body and the direction has not been complied with.

S1W-10072 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is open to non-departmental public bodies to raise actions of judicial review against it and, if so, on what grounds and whether it will provide details of any such actions ever raised.
