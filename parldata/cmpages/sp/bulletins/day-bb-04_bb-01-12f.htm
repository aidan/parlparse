Business Bulletin No. 6 / 2004

Monday 12th January 2004

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Current Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S2M-774# John Swinburne: Recognition of Kinship Care-That the Parliament recognises that many grandparents have become the new foster care parents of children whose mothers and fathers have become disconnected from mainstream society; acknowledges that such care arrangements are usually more stable and result in a positive impact on the young person, as a consequence of grandparents' strong sense of commitment to provide care, but shares concerns about the financial consequences experienced by grandparents in providing this care; agrees that, if the needs of the child are the same, then the cost is the same, and therefore considers that the Scottish Executive should recognise this form of kinship care by instructing local authorities to ensure that related carers should have the same financial assistance and be subject to the same scrutiny as recognised foster carers.

*S2M-773 Alasdair Morgan: Parthenon Marbles-That the Parliament welcomes the visit to Edinburgh in January 2004 of the exhibition "Marbles Reunited: A Cultural Imperative", organised by the British Committee for the Restitution of the Parthenon Marbles, which digitally combines pieces of sculpture originally from the Parthenon and now separated between Athens and London to reveal what could be achieved if the various pieces of sculpture were to be reunited; notes the recent constructive proposals by the Greek Government for the return on loan of the Parthenon marbles to a new purpose-built museum close to the Parthenon, which involve putting aside the issue of ownership and instead entering into a partnership to ensure that the marbles are displayed and conserved in the best possible way; further notes that the Greek Government is willing to enter into a rolling loan agreement so that other artefacts of great significance which have never before been seen in the UK can be displayed in the British Museum; welcomes indications that Her Majesty's Government may be prepared to discuss these proposals, and hopes that Her Majesty's Government will respond positively so as to enable the marbles to be reunited in Athens in what would be a cultural gesture of world significance.

*S2M-772# Sarah Boyack: Access at Haymarket Railway Station-That the Parliament notes with concern that Haymarket Station in Edinburgh does not have full access to platforms or other facilities; further notes that the provisions of the Disability Discrimination Act 1995 which refers to access to services will come into force in October 2004; recognises that a vast number of stations across Scotland have limited, poor or no access for people with disabilities; understands that Haymarket Station is Scotland's busiest railway station; notes that during the weekend of 17 to 19 January 2004 Waverley Station will be closed for the replacement of signalling and that Haymarket Station will be used as the main station for the capital; commends the joint discussions of the City of Edinburgh Council, Network Rail, Scotrail and Transport Initiatives Edinburgh in order to work together proactively to find a solution to this problem, and believes that the Scottish Executive and the Strategic Rail Authority must prioritise transport investment to ensure that all rail passengers have equal access to services.

*S2M-771 Richard Lochhead on behalf of the European and External Relations Committee: The European Commission's Work Programme for 2004-That the Parliament notes the publication of the European Commission's work programme for 2004, which is dominated by the enlargement of the European Union, and agrees that it contains a number of legislative and non-legislative plans of importance to Scotland.

*S2M-770 Ms Rosemary Byrne: Sewage Dumping-That the Parliament accepts that the dumping of untreated or semi-treated sewage on land is a revolting concept to the public with potentially devastating health and environmental effects; notes with strong disapproval the current practice of the dumping of semi-treated waste in various sites in the south of Scotland by a subsidiary of Thames Water plc, and calls on the Scottish Executive to ban this practice completely by ensuring safe scientific methods of disposal under both the precautionary and proximity principles.

Supported by: Mr Stewart Maxwell*

*S2M-769 Sarah Boyack on behalf of the Environment and Rural Development Committee: Report on Inquiry into the National Waste Plan-That the Parliament notes the recommendations contained in the Environment and Rural Development Committee's 4th Report 2003 (Session 2): Inquiry into the National Waste Plan (SP Paper 47).

S2M-768# Robin Harper: Farmed Salmon and Human Health-That the Parliament notes the publication in the journal Science of an article exposing high levels of organic contaminants in Scottish farmed salmon; recognises that Scotland's reputation as a producer of high quality food is compromised by such contamination, and believes that the Scottish Executive must act without delay to investigate the source of toxic contaminants in feed and elsewhere in order to protect public health and in the interests of securing a sustainable future for the Scottish aquaculture industry.

Supported by: Patrick Harvie*, Mr Mark Ruskell*, Eleanor Scott*, Mark Ballard*, Shiona Baird*, Chris Ballance*

S2M-767 Michael McMahon: Fair Trade Status for Hamilton-That the Parliament supports the bid by Hamilton to gain fair trade status; commends the growing awareness of people in Scotland of the issues surrounding fair trade and the desire to be involved in such a positive way to tackle the injustice of poverty, and recognises the ongoing importance of fair trade as an effective way of empowering disadvantaged producers through receiving a fairer deal.

Supported by: Mr Stewart Maxwell*

S2M-766# Helen Eadie: Workers' Educational Association-That the Parliament notes that the Workers' Educational Association (WEA) is celebrating its centenary year; admires the significant contribution that the WEA has made across the UK in its history in helping to shape the lives of so many individuals, many of whom have progressed to make a real difference to their community and wider society; notes the confidence that has been built as a consequence in socially and economically deprived communities where family and social problems are acute, and, in recognition of the significant achievements that have been made and continue to be made by the WEA, encourages all public agencies across Scotland to help and support the WEA in the coming century in every way possible.

Supported by: Tricia Marwick*, Elaine Smith*, Michael McMahon*, Ms Sandra White*, Tommy Sheridan*, Mr Mark Ruskell*, Robin Harper*, Robert Brown*, Mike Watson*, Carolyn Leckie*, Mr Kenny MacAskill*, Brian Adam*, Dr Sylvia Jackson*, John Swinburne*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Marlyn Glen*, Kate Maclean*, Sarah Boyack*, Christine May*, Dr Elaine Murray*, Mr Stewart Maxwell*

S2M-765# Tommy Sheridan: Organ Donation Opt-out System-That the Parliament believes that it is time to introduce an organ donation opt-out system for Scotland to try to address the ongoing shortage of organs donated within our country, a shortage that leads to over 50 preventable deaths per year and undermines the quality of life for hundreds of others as they undergo medical treatment in the absence of available and healthy organs for transplants, and believes that such an organ donation opt-out system should be introduced with maximum safeguards to ensure maximum choice and information to all in Scotland who may wish, for whatever reason, to opt out of organ donation but at the same time believe that it is important to encourage organ donation as a life-saving and enhancing act of humanity.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson, Dennis Canavan*, Mr Stewart Maxwell*

S2M-764# Dennis Canavan: Business Rate Relief for Community Amateur Sports Clubs-That the Parliament welcomes the recent announcement by the Chancellor of the Exchequer regarding a minimum 80% relief from business rates for registered community amateur sports clubs and believes that the Scottish Executive should ensure that such relief is extended to registered community amateur sports clubs in Scotland.

Supported by: Brian Adam, Frances Curran, Carolyn Leckie, Elaine Smith, Dr Elaine Murray, Richard Lochhead, Michael Matheson, Stewart Stevenson, Chris Ballance, Mike Pringle, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Ms Sandra White, Linda Fabiani, John Swinburne, Eleanor Scott, Alex Neil, Christine May, Robin Harper*, Michael McMahon*, Mr Alasdair Morrison*, Gordon Jackson*, Bill Butler*, Mr John Home Robertson*, Janis Hughes*, Dr Jean Turner*, Shona Robison*, Christine Grahame*, Kate Maclean*, Marlyn Glen*, Margo MacDonald*, Mr Stewart Maxwell*

S2M-763 Bruce Crawford: Health of Dockyard Workers-That the Parliament calls on the Scottish Executive to enter into discussions with the Ministry of Defence and Fife NHS Board on the possibility of conducting a study into the potential ill health effects of exposure to radiation suffered by people while employed at Rosyth Dockyard in order to establish if there is any link between their exposure to radiation while employed at the dockyard and any ill health they have suffered and, if deceased, any link between their employment and cause of death.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson, Brian Adam, Rob Gibson, Eleanor Scott, Mr Adam Ingram, Michael Matheson, Shiona Baird, Roseanna Cunningham, Ms Sandra White, Jim Mather, Carolyn Leckie, Alex Neil, Mr Stewart Maxwell, Campbell Martin, Chris Ballance, Robin Harper*, Frances Curran*

S2M-758# Cathie Craigie: Achievements of Deaf Pupils in Scotland Project-That the Parliament congratulates the Scottish Executive for funding the Achievements of Deaf Pupils in Scotland project since November 2000; is concerned to note the gap in achievements between deaf and hearing children identified in its initial findings, and considers that the Executive should continue to fund the project so that groundbreaking year-on-year evidence can be collected to enable identification of factors which can be addressed by education services for deaf pupils in Scotland.

S2M-756# Maureen Macmillan: Belford Hospital, Fort William, Centre of Excellence-That the Parliament supports the proposal for the Belford Hospital at Fort William to become a centre of excellence for trauma training as part of a medical facility within the University of the Highlands and Islands; acknowledges that the Belford Hospital is already recognised nationally as one of the UK's leading hospitals in trauma treatment because of its experience in dealing with mountain casualties, and further recognises that this proposal would go a long way to help resolve the current recruitment difficulties being experienced in some specialisms of the medical profession and would give young people within the Highlands and Islands the opportunity to train and live at home rather than relocate to the central belt.

Supported by: Carolyn Leckie, Des McNulty, Marilyn Livingstone, Ms Sandra White, Tommy Sheridan, Mike Pringle, John Swinburne, Trish Godman, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Christine May

S2M-755 Mr Stewart Maxwell: Pregnant and Productive: An Investigation into Pregnancy Discrimination-That the Parliament welcomes the first ever investigation into pregnancy discrimination which has been launched by the Equal Opportunities Commission (EOC); notes with concern that the EOC helpline receives more calls from women facing problems at work because of pregnancy than on any other subject; further expresses alarm that women have been demoted, disciplined or sacked simply for having a baby; strongly believes that the Scottish economy needs the skills and experience of pregnant women in the workplace and women returning from maternity leave, and calls on the Scottish Executive to act upon the findings of the investigation to put a stop to pregnancy discrimination.

Supported by: Carolyn Leckie, Frances Curran, Richard Lochhead, Ms Sandra White, Alex Neil, Jackie Baillie, Elaine Smith, Mike Pringle, Stewart Stevenson, Shona Robison, Shiona Baird, Rob Gibson, Jim Mather, Christine Grahame, Mr Bruce McFee, Kate Maclean, Michael Matheson, Chris Ballance, Eleanor Scott, Tommy Sheridan, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Robin Harper*

S2M-754# Donald Gorrie: National Celebrations on St Andrew's Day-That the Parliament believes that many aspects of Scottish life would benefit from the adoption of St Andrew's Day as a holiday and day of national celebration; recognises the value that similar national days, such as Thanksgiving Day in the United States of America, bring to other countries, and considers that the Parliament and the Scottish Executive should work with all relevant bodies to make St Andrew's Day a forum for educational, cultural and sporting activity, family reunions, community events, the promotion of tourism and Scottish products abroad and for developing existing celebrations of Scottish life held abroad.

S2M-629# George Lyon: Medical Services in the West Highlands-That the Parliament recognises the deep concern of the communities in Fort William and Oban regarding the threat to future provision of acute medical services in the west Highlands; notes that over 3,500 people, so far, have turned out at public meetings to express their opposition to Argyll and Clyde and Highland NHS boards' plans to downgrade Oban and Fort William hospitals to community status; recognises that, if the proposals go ahead, patients from the West Highlands would be forced to travel hundreds of miles to Glasgow or Inverness to access treatment, and therefore believes that the Scottish Executive should reject these plans and give a commitment that the current service provision will continue to be delivered locally to communities in the West Highlands.

Supported by: Ms Sandra White, Maureen Macmillan, Donald Gorrie, John Farquhar Munro
