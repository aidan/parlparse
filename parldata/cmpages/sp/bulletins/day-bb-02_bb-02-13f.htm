*S1M-2740 Roseanna Cunningham: Review of Older People's Services in Perth and Kinross-That the Parliament notes with concern the recent publication by the Scottish Health Advisory Service (SHAS) of their Review of Older People's Services in Perth and Kinross which found that "there is effectively no planned NHS continuing care for frail older people in Perth and Kinross which is inequitable with the rest of Scotland and should be addressed as a matter of urgency"; further notes the report's recognition of the problems being caused by "bed-blocking" at Perth Royal Infirmary and that a number of the recommendations made following SHAS's last visit in 1998 have not been acted on; expresses the view that the closure of local authority-owned residential homes would exacerbate the problem of care provision and add to the existing anxieties of older people in Perth and Kinross, and calls on the Minister for Health and Community Care, NHS Tayside and Perth and Kinross Council to work together to provide the elderly of Perth and Kinross with the service they deserve.

Supported by: Mr John Swinney*

*S1M-2739# Pauline McNeill: Glasgow City Council and Free Breakfasts-That the Parliament congratulates Glasgow City Council for rolling out its free daily breakfasts to every pupil at primary schools in the city; notes that the pilot project starting in 20 Glasgow primary schools in February 2002 will provide children with a choice of toast and cereals with milk, fruit juice or water and that yoghurt, raisins and fresh fruit will also be piloted; further notes that the free breakfast plan complements the Council's other initiatives of free fruit three days a week to all 60,000 primary and nursery children and free swimming at Council pools for all children under the age of 18, and believes that these initiatives will go a long way to improving the diet and health of children in Glasgow as well as providing improved chances for better learning.

*S1M-2738 Pauline McNeill: Glasgow Underground-That the Parliament welcomes the decision by Strathclyde Passenger Transport Authority (SPTA) not to increase the fares on the Glasgow Underground from 90p to £1.00; notes that this fare freeze will ensure that the Glasgow Underground remains an accessible, environmentally friendly and affordable alternative to cars for many Glaswegians and visitors to the city; further notes that 95% of passengers report their satisfaction with the Underground service, confirming that it remains the quickest and most reliable way of getting around Glasgow, and calls on the Scottish Executive to work with SPTA to find alternative means of securing the extra £220,000 to cover the costs of essential repairs and to upgrade the system.

*S1M-2731# Mary Scanlon: Macmillan Cancer Relief - Cancer Talk-That the Parliament congratulates Macmillan Cancer Relief on its important and innovative educational programme, Cancer Talk; notes that Cancer Talk is key to realising the charity's vision of tackling unnecessary levels of fear about cancer by raising awareness and dispelling myths in the classroom; further notes that over two hundred Scottish schools have signed up to Cancer Talk, with several keen to publicise it within their local media and community, especially during the week beginning 4 March 2002; urges members to contact their local schools in order to encourage them to get involved in integrating Cancer Talk within their existing curriculum both during that week and afterwards; recognises the need for the Scottish Executive to consider the incorporation of Cancer Talk formally within the schools' curriculum, and believes that such incorporation would be a major source of help and support to the many schoolchildren, parents and teachers whose lives are touched by cancer each year.

Supported by: Mr David Davidson*, Bill Aitken*, David Mundell*, Miss Annabel Goldie*, Mr Brian Monteith*, Ben Wallace*, Alex Johnstone*, Mr Jamie McGrigor*

*S1M-2730 Ms Sandra White: Spearmint Rhino-That the Parliament notes the intentions of the US based firm Spearmint Rhino, which runs the world's largest table-dancing network, to expand its operations into Scotland by opening five "sex multiplexes"; further notes recent reports linking Spearmint Rhino with pornographic websites featuring the drugging, abduction and rape of women, and therefore calls upon the Scottish Executive to investigate this matter urgently and take whatever action is necessary to prevent Spearmint Rhino operating their proposed "sex multiplexes" in Scotland.
