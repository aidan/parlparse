Business Bulletin No. 19/2002

Thursday 31 January 2002

Section D : Oral Questions selected for answer on 7 February 2002
  

Question Time

1. Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to encourage healthy eating amongst pregnant mothers. (S1O-4640)

2. Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to regenerate Glasgow. (S1O-4591)

3. Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made of the impact on the forestry industry of its and Her Majesty's Government's policies. (S1O-4607)

4. Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to ensure that the Children's Hearings System complies with the European Convention on Human Rights. (S1O-4642)

5. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to improve housing standards in the Dumbiedykes area of Edinburgh. (S1O-4597)

6. Elaine Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to improve the provision of out-of-school care. (S1O-4643)

7. Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met representatives of Lothian and Borders Fire Board in order to discuss funding of pensions. (S1O-4625)

8. Mr Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive how many of the 26 Independent Assessors appointed to oversee Ministerial appointments to non-departmental public bodies have political affiliations. (S1O-4634)

9. Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to review the operation of immunology clinics. (S1O-4621)

10. Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive when a priority dispatch system for the Scottish Ambulance Service will be implemented. (S1O-4628)

11. Mr Keith Raffan: To ask the Scottish Executive what additional financial resources it will make available for the implementation of action points identified in its Plan for Action on Alcohol Problems and how such resources will be distributed. (S1O-4633)

12. Kate Maclean: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met Her Majesty's Government in order to discuss issues relating to asylum and immigration. (S1O-4646)

13. Elaine Thomson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to improve educational provision for school pupils with disabilities. (S1O-4636)

14. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive what meetings have taken place with local authorities other than the five cities to discuss the Cities Review. (S1O-4641)

15. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive how many discharge consents were issued by the Scottish Environment Protection Agency in 2001 for chemicals used in the treatment of sea lice at salmon farms and whether levels of discharge of such chemicals increased in 2001 compared with 1999 and 2000. (S1O-4592)

16. Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will review the structure of, and system of election for, the General Teaching Council for Scotland. (S1O-4601)

17. Brian Fitzpatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to improve public awareness of organ donation. (S1O-4648)

18. Mrs Margaret Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding is currently allocated to the upgrading of the A96 and what measures and further funding are planned to take this matter forward. (S1O-4620)

19. Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made of any impact on the economy in rural areas of the introduction of broadband services in the South of Scotland and the Highlands and Islands. (S1O-4594)

20. Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it has taken to ensure that there is an adequate public consultation process in respect of the proposed Glasgow Harbour development. (S1O-4613)

21. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take in order to reduce the circulation of illegal guns, following the report in the Sunday Mail on 27 January 2002 that there was a marked increase in the number of gun-related crimes and deaths in 2001. (S1O-4602)

22. Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how much money has been allocated for improvements to trunk roads during this parliamentary session. (S1O-4596)

23. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken in response to the placing of Aberdeen-based textiles group Richards in receivership. (S1O-4600)

24. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what role it sees for community pharmacies in achieving its objectives for the NHS. (S1O-4638)

25. Mr Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking in respect of road accident rates in rural areas. (S1O-4610)

26. Mr Duncan Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it complied with the timetable for implementation of the EU directive on electronic commerce. (S1O-4608)

27. Mr Frank McAveety: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to promote the arts and culture in schools. (S1O-4652)

28. Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what financial support it plans to make available to NHS Greater Glasgow for the modernisation of hospitals. (S1O-4615)

29. Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to revise the Farm Woodland Premium Scheme. (S1O-4606)

30. Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what the cost to local authorities of self-financing local authority pay awards was in 2001-02. (S1O-4631)
