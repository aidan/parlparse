Business Bulletin No. 155/2004

Monday 1 November 2004

Section E : Written questions lodged on Friday 29 October 2004

  

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-11830 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what Scottish literature is promoted and used within the school curriculum for (a) English and (b) other studies; what mandatory study of Scottish literature there is in the school curriculum, and what minimum knowledge of Scottish literature is required for children completing their education in Scottish schools.

S2W-11844 Mr John Swinney: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2O-3056 by Nicol Stephen on 8 September 2004 (Official Report, c 10055-10056), what steps have been taken to commence work on the upgrading of the A9 junction at Ballinluig.

S2W-11845 Mr John Swinney: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to establish a new station at Ballinluig on the Perth to Inverness rail line.

S2W-11846 Mr John Swinney: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to instruct the Scottish Solicitors’ Discipline Tribunal (SSDT) to make available the transcribed notes of the proceedings of a hearing by the SSDT if requested to by a party with an interest in the case.

S2W-11847 Mr John Swinney: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it would take if an individual who holds a judicial appointment as a sheriff was found, as a result of an investigation by the Scottish Legal Services Ombudsman, to have breached section 8 of the Code of Conduct for Solicitors.

S2W-11848 Mr John Swinney: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied that the membership of its Working Group for Research into the Legal Services markets in Scotland includes sufficient representation of lay opinion.

S2W-11849 Mr John Swinney: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied that the membership of its Working Group for Research into the Legal Services markets in Scotland includes sufficient consumer interest representation.

S2W-11850 Mr John Swinney: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied that the Master Policy for Professional Indemnity insurance of the Law Society of Scotland protects the interests of consumers.

S2W-11851 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will monitor the single survey scheme and how it will improve the scheme and ensure sufficient uptake of single surveys.

S2W-11852 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what analysis it has undertaken of the efficacy of its debtline pilot in Fife.

S2W-11853 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has from census records, or other sources, on the number of people working in Scotland who are fluent in a European language, broken down by (a) language and (b) job category.

S2W-11854 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to appoint an inspectorate for the judiciary; if not, how it intends to monitor the performance of judges and sheriffs, and what the reasons are for its position on the matter.

S2W-11855 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to address the system of house buying and selling; whether it is aware of any problems with the system and, if so, what these problems are.

S2W-11856 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-10861 by Cathy Jamieson on 8 October 2004, how many of the safeguarders referred to have been instructed in the last (a) year and (b) two years and whether safeguarders are withdrawn from Sheriff Clerks’ lists if they are no longer carrying out their responsibilities.

S2W-11857 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive when enhanced drug transition services will be introduced in all prisons.

S2W-11858 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the allocation of funding for enhanced drug transition services will be equitable across public and private sector prisons.

S2W-11859 Margaret Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the outcomes will be of enhanced drug transition services and how these will be measured and audited.

S2W-11860 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many reports it has received from the Scottish Law Commission in each year since 1999; of these, how many have been implemented, and what action it proposes to take regarding those reports which have not been implemented.

S2W-11861 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive when it received the Scottish Law Commission report, Title to Sue for Non-Patrimonial Loss; what action it has taken in relation to the report; what proposals it has to implement it, and what the reasons are for its position on the matter.

S2W-11862 Frances Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken to ensure that Scotland will make an equitable contribution to addressing climate change and what further steps it will take prior to the G8 summit at Gleneagles.

S2W-11863 Frances Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive why targets on climate change no longer appear in its Annual Expenditure Report.

S2W-11864 Frances Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of the answer by the team leader of the Finance, Environment and Rural Affairs section of its Finance and Central Services Department to the Environment and Rural Development Committee on 21 April 2004 that "we spend relatively little programme money on the issue of climate change" (Official Report c 975), what level of programme money is spent on climate change; what criteria are used to determine funding levels; which department or individual is responsible for the spending decisions, and how this spending level is compatible with the Executive’s commitment to address climate change.

S2W-11865 Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has the power to change the way in which single farm payments are calculated in Scotland.

S2W-11866 Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what the total agricultural subsidy payments in Scotland will be in 2004, broken down into Pillar 1 and Pillar 2.

S2W-11867 Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what advice its Environment and Rural Affairs department is giving to the Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs in respect of the UK negotiating position on the European Council’s draft rural development regulation.

S2W-11868 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with Her Majesty’s Government regarding the proposed new gambling legislation; what representations it has made in respect of Scotland; what the procedures will be for implementing any UK legislation, and what steps it will take to ensure that Scotland’s distinct position is acknowledged and that the Parliament is involved in the process.

S2W-11869 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to capitalise and build upon the world city of literature status awarded to Edinburgh and what funds have been (a) earmarked for this purpose and (b) allocated to relevant organisations.

S2W-11870 Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will ensure that new less favoured areas regulations will provide sufficient support to enable agriculture to remain viable in the most deprived areas of Scotland.

S2W-11871 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what the pension rights will be of new prison officers at any new build prison on the Low Moss site.

S2W-11872 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what land acreage was available to the Scottish Prison Service (SPS) on the site of HM Prison Low Moss; what the acreage is of the existing site development at Low Moss; what consideration has been given to a new build prison on SPS-owned land adjacent to the site, and what the reasons are for its position on the matter.

S2W-11873 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what the total annual value is of work carried out for it and its agencies by The Stationery Office (TSO), broken down by contract, and whether it has any powers of direction over TSO and, if so, what these are.
