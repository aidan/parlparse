Business Bulletin No. 108/2010: Thursday 24 June 2010
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 23 June 2010

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-34749 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive how much Scottish Water has paid Capgemini for consultancy services in the last 24 months, broken down by (a) amount, (b) date of payment and (c) service provided.

S3W-34750 Bill Kidd: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average cost is of building a primary school with pupil numbers between 400 and 450.

S3W-34751 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-33213 by Shona Robison on 28 April 2010, what plans it has to implement electronic prescribing for (a) information prescriptions, (b) minor ailment prescriptions, (c) long-term condition prescribing by pharmacists and (d) non-medical prescriptions.

S3W-34752 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of the UK Government’s decision to require the NHS in England to report rates of MRSA bacteraemia and Clostridium difficile infections weekly, what steps it is taking to provide the same level of information in Scotland.

S3W-34753 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken in terms of (a) pre-employment checks, (b) induction and supervision and (c) other performance management processes to ensure that any potential risks associated with the employment of locum doctors are minimised.

S3W-34754 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will require the NHS Scotland Central Legal Office to record whether doctors involved in incidents are locums.

S3W-34755 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure that the integrity of NHS IT systems is maintained by ensuring that each locum doctor uses an individual password.

S3W-34756 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason some NHS boards did not follow the advice issued in 2006 to employ locums through the contract agencies only.

S3W-34757 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason the employment of locum doctors other than through the contract agencies increased from 31% to 33% between 2006-07 and 2008-09.

S3W-34758 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason it allowed the national locum agency contract to lapse in 2009 and what the financial consequences were.

S3W-34759 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason the number of locum agency requests increased from 3,700 in 2006-07 to 8,200 in 2008-09 in seven NHS boards, as noted in the Audit Scotland report, Using locum doctors in hospitals.

S3W-34760 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure that NHS boards maintain data on locum doctor employment; that they do so using the most effective electronic data set already in use and that where locums are employed, junior doctors do not endanger patient safety by contracting on a regular or frequent basis beyond the 48 hours stipulated in the European working time directive.

S3W-34761 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken to review the potential for equalisation of business rates for off-trade and on-trade licensees as referred to in evidence by the Scottish Licensed Trade Association to the Health and Sport Committee on 10 March 2010, (Official Report c. 2897).

S3W-34762 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answers to questions S3W-33959 and S3W-33960 by Richard Lochhead on 18 June 2010, when it instructed the provision of the IT reports to provide a regional breakdown of Rural Development Contracts - Rural Priorities scheme payments and contracts; when the information on the regional breakdown will be made available to it, and when the information will be made available to the public.

S3W-34763 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how much it estimates that the increase in VAT from 17.5% to 20% will cost NHSScotland in one year.

S3W-34764 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive how much it estimates that the increase in VAT from 17.5% to 20% will cost each geographical and special health board in one year.

S3W-34765 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact it expects that the increase in VAT from 17.5% to 20% will have on NHS dentistry services in one year.

S3W-34766 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what areas in South Ayrshire are in the worst 5% of the Scottish Index of Multiple Deprivation.

S3W-34767 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to improve life expectancy in South Ayrshire.

S3W-34768 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussion it has had with South Ayrshire Council regarding life expectancy in South Ayrshire.

S3W-34769 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive when Scottish ministers last met (a) South Ayrshire Council and (b) NHS Ayrshire and Arran to discuss life expectancy and multiple deprivation in South Ayrshire.

S3W-34770 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive whether an assessment has been made of the impact that proposed NHS job losses will have on health services in East Renfrewshire.

S3W-34771 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive how many of the proposed NHS job losses are in East Renfrewshire.

S3W-34772 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive how many of the proposed NHS job losses will affect patients from East Renfrewshire.

S3W-34773 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive how many nursing and midwifery posts in East Renfrewshire will be lost as a result of the proposed NHS job losses.

S3W-34774 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) physiotherapist, (b) speech and language therapist and (c) other posts in professions allied to medicine and based in East Renfrewshire will be lost as a result of the proposed NHS job losses.

S3W-34775 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will publish details of the posts to be lost as part of the proposed NHS job losses in the Greater Glasgow area.

S3W-34776 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what information is collected on the incidence of anti-Semitism in Scotland.

S3W-34777 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there is evidence to suggest that recent anti-Semitic graffiti at Glenduffhill cemetery can be considered an isolated incident.

S3W-34778 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is participating in the Inter-Departmental Working Group on Antisemitism.

S3W-34779 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will implement the recommendations of the UK Parliament’s All-Party Inquiry into Antisemitism

S3W-34780 Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) primary and (b) secondary pupils were sent home from school because they were drunk in the last year, broken down by local authority.

S3W-34781 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what services it has requested for the First Minister during his trip to Delhi for the Commonwealth Games.

S3W-34782 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive who will host the First Minister during his trip to Delhi for the Commonwealth Games.

S3W-34783 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will provide a substantive answer to question S3W-33436.

S3W-34784 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive where the First Minister expects to stay during his trip to Delhi for the Commonwealth Games and how much this will cost the taxpayer.

S3W-34785 John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive how much it would cost to upgrade the (a) Abbey St Bathans, (b) Cappercleugh, (c) Longformacus and (d) Whitsome telephone exchanges to full platform ADSL exchanges.

S3W-34786 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what cost implications there are for the NHS as a consequence of the increase in VAT to 20%.

S3W-34787 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether changes have been made to the redundancy package available to the Permanent Secretary of the Scottish Executive since May 2007.

S3W-34788 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration has been given to legislating to provide for an elected element of the membership of college and university governing bodies.

S3W-34789 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of the income of each (a) college and (b) university came, directly or indirectly, from the Scottish Government in 2009-10.

S3W-34790 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive to what appointments to senior posts in colleges and universities the Nolan principles apply.

S3W-34791 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what mechanisms exist for providing accountability for the expenditure of public money in (a) colleges and (b) universities.

S3W-34792 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what the procedures are for appointments to the Scottish Funding Council and what aspects of these relate to accountability for the expenditure of public money.

S3W-34793 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what areas of its expenditure are subject to VAT.

S3W-34794 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to bring forward any major capital or revenue expenditure in order to avoid paying the increased cost resulting from the rise in VAT.

S3W-34795 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will issue guidance to local authorities and its agencies regarding the rise in VAT.

S3W-34796 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what the estimated impact on its expenditure is of the increase in VAT to 20%, broken down by directorate.

S3W-34797 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive which of its staff have the use of Scottish Government corporate credit cards and for what purposes.

S3W-34798 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive how many car parking fines have been issued by NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde in the last year.

S3W-34799 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive how many car parking fines have been issued by NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde to staff in the last year.

S3W-34800 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive how many car parking fines have been issued by NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde to patients in the last year.

S3W-34801 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to assess the impact of hospital car park charges on NHS staff.

S3W-34802 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there is evidence of a significant disadvantage to NHS staff arising from car park charges.

S3W-34803 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there is evidence of NHS hospital car park charges having a disproportionate affect on lower-paid staff.
