Business Bulletin No. 152/2001

Friday 26 October 2001

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions.

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-2370# Donald Gorrie: Battlefields—That the Parliament considers that the Scottish Executive should undertake archaeological research to establish the correct site of the Battle of Bannockburn and that sites of this and other important Scottish battlefields should be safeguarded from further development.

Supported by: Robert Brown*, Tavish Scott*, Nora Radcliffe*, Ian Jenkins*, Margaret Smith*, Mr Jamie Stone*, Mr Mike Rumbles*, John Farquhar Munro*

*S1M-2369# Donald Gorrie: Public Access to the Parliament—That the Parliament considers that the layout of the Holyrood Parliament buildings should allow the easiest possible public access to the Parliament and simple arrangements for members of the public to meet MSPs and that it should be possible for any additional security measures introduced at times of crisis to be easily dismantled, and asks the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body to report to the Parliament on this aspect of their work.

Supported by: Nora Radcliffe*, Mr Mike Rumbles*, John Farquhar Munro*, George Lyon*, Stewart Stevenson*, Alex Neil*, Ms Margo MacDonald*

*S1M-2368 Dr Richard Simpson: People Management in Scottish Universities—That the Parliament notes the statements made by the Minister for Enterprise and Lifelong Learning in her letter of 23 November 2000 to the Chairman of the Scottish Higher Education Funding Council (SHEFC) that "staff are the key resource of universities and colleges" and that the Scottish Executive "must continually strive to improve people management, staff development and succession planning"; agrees with the minister’s comment in the same letter that "a particular area where improvements are required is in human resource policies and management"; is concerned that pay levels for female academic staff are almost 20% lower than those for their male counterparts; recognises the importance of research staff in Scotland’s knowledge economy; further recognises that over 90% of such staff are employed on insecure fixed-term contracts; believes that greater job security in the higher education sector can better enable the use of our science and social science research base for the benefit of Scotland’s economy and society; welcomes the Executive’s commitment, outlined in its publication, A Science Strategy for Scotland, to "encourage funders and employers to ensure good career and staff development for those engaged in scientific research"; calls upon the minister to press for improvements in people management by more detailed guidance to SHEFC and to encourage SHEFC to promote and monitor such improvements through specific conditions of grant and by ensuring that contracted members of research staff are considered a key part of implementation of the science strategy, and recommends to the minister, to SHEFC and to universities the advantages of an approach in partnership with recognised unions to tackle these key issues. R

*S1M-2367 Bristow Muldoon: Outdoor Access for All—That the Parliament notes the calls of 14,550 individuals and organisations from both Scotland and around the world, including the Scottish Human Rights Centre, who have supported the petition to the Parliament on outdoor access by the Scottish Environment LINK Access Network, Scottish Sports Association and Scottish Countryside Access Network and believes that the Land Reform (Scotland) Bill should establish a right to responsible access for all and improve and confirm, rather than threaten and restrict, access opportunities for all those who enjoy Scotland's countryside and for those communities that depend upon the business which derives from that access.

*S1M-2366# Helen Eadie: Consultation and Advocacy Promotion Service—That the Parliament congratulates the Consultation and Advocacy Promotion Service on its 10th anniversary and commends the work of the service in promoting and supporting the collective involvement of mental health service users in Edinburgh, Midlothian and East Lothian; further congratulates mindscape.org.uk on the launch of its website on 18 June 2001, the aim of which is to provide a forum for debate on mental health issues, supply information for service users, their friends and families and to challenge stigma and discrimination, and believes that the Scottish Executive should continue to work towards ensuring that mental health service users have access to appropriate services and levels of support.

*S1M-2365 John Young: Control and Storage of Fireworks—That the Parliament notes with concern the injury, alarm and distress caused to the people of Scotland by the irresponsible use of fireworks and calls upon the Scottish Executive in liaison with Her Majesty’s Government to review the legislation dealing with the control and storage of fireworks.

Supported by: Mr Murray Tosh*, David Mundell*, Dennis Canavan*

*S1M-2364 John Farquhar Munro: Cast Ewe Compensation Scheme—That the Parliament condemns the decision by the EU Standing Veterinary Committee to refuse to allow Scottish lamb to be exported to EU markets; notes that the cull lamb scheme established by the Scottish Executive Environment and Rural Affairs Department in the wake of the foot-and-mouth disease outbreak has been substantially undersubscribed, and therefore strongly urges the Scottish Executive to consider allocating the surplus funding from the scheme to a disposal scheme for cast ewes and to address the animal welfare problems and environmental damage that the onset of winter is bringing.

Supported by: Tavish Scott*, Mr Jamie Stone*, George Lyon*, Mr Mike Rumbles*

*S1M-2363# Stewart Stevenson: HM Prison Peterhead and Beacon Site Status—That the Parliament congratulates HM Prison Peterhead on becoming the first prison in Scotland to be awarded Beacon Site Status as part of the Modernising Government initiative and looks forward to the prison fulfilling its role under the scheme whereby it will share the secrets of its success with groups of visitors from across the United Kingdom and Scottish governments.

Supported by: Nora Radcliffe*, Brian Adam*, Mr Kenny MacAskill*, Linda Fabiani*, Mr Adam Ingram*, Fiona Hyslop*, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton*, Christine Grahame*, Dennis Canavan*, Donald Gorrie*, Bruce Crawford*, Alex Neil*, Dorothy-Grace Elder*, Ms Margo MacDonald*, Kay Ullrich*, Mr Gil Paterson*, Shona Robison*, Ms Sandra White*, Richard Lochhead*, Michael Matheson*, Mrs Margaret Ewing*

*S1M-2362 Miss Annabel Goldie: Job Losses in the West of Scotland—That the Parliament notes with concern the escalating job losses in the West of Scotland culminating in the recent announcements from Rolls Royce in Hillington and Robinson Nugent and Compaq in Inchinnan that further losses may be expected and calls upon the Scottish Executive, the affected companies and all local and other relevant agencies to unite in seeking to maintain economic activity in Renfrewshire and to offer every assistance to those who lose jobs to find new jobs.

Supported by: Mr Keith Harding*

*S1M-2361 Mike Watson: Cosmic Rough Riders—That the Parliament congratulates everyone at C Sharp Community recording studios in Castlemilk for their part in the success of local band, the Cosmic Rough Riders, whose debut album Enjoy the Melodic Sunshine has reached sales of 60,000 in the UK and, as such, qualifies for a British Phonographic Industry Silver Disc award; notes that this year the songs on recordings made at C Sharp have been heard by the band’s audiences during their tours of the UK, the USA, Europe and Japan, and commends the work of community-based studios like this which is evidence that the grass-roots music industry is thriving across Scotland.

Supported by: Mr Kenneth Macintosh*
