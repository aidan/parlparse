Business Bulletin No. 187/2004

Thursday 16 December 2004

Section D : Oral questions selected for answer on 23 December 2004
  

Question Time

Education and Young People, Tourism, Culture and Sport

1. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will assess what impact the removal of charitable status would have on independent schools. (S2O-4732)

2. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether attracting the 2014 Commonwealth Games can provide economic and social benefits to Glasgow. (S2O-4767)

3. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what provision is made available for additional school building other than through the PPP route. (S2O-4770)

4. Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is improving Scotland’s profile as a tourist destination. (S2O-4762)

5. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to improve the marketing of Scotland as a tourist destination. (S2O-4765)

6. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to raise awareness of Scotland among the Scottish diaspora in North America. (S2O-4707)

7. Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to promote disability sport. (S2O-4751)

8. Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied with truancy levels in schools. (S2O-4768)

9. Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive what evaluation has been made of the programme, Hungry for Success: A Whole School Approach to School Meals in Scotland. (S2O-4760)

10. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what benefits will be brought to tourism by the Core Path Network, in addition to those that it will bring to public access. (S2O-4784)

11. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive how many schools are broadband-enabled. (S2O-4735)

12. Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to reduce racist and sectarian abuse in sports grounds and what discussions it has had on the issue with relevant sporting bodies. (S2O-4769)

Finance and Public Services and Communities

1. Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether its strategy aimed at reducing incidents of violence against women is meeting its targets. (S2O-4776)

2. Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to ensure that older people receiving pension credit are not living in fuel poverty. (S2O-4803)

3. Ms Rosemary Byrne: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in bringing civil service jobs to North Ayrshire. (S2O-4702)

4. Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in the provision of its central heating programme for pensioners. (S2O-4724)

5. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the recently-announced local government financial settlement will guarantee that all local authorities will be able to meet free personal care commitments. (S2O-4696)

6. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive what productivity is in the public sector in Scotland compared to productivity in England, Wales and Northern Ireland. (S2O-4752)

7. Susan Deacon: To ask the Scottish Executive what role community planning partnerships play in community regeneration. (S2O-4772)

8. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has received about the impact of changes to the Supporting People fund. (S2O-4737)

9. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action local communities or individuals can take when faced with the erection of a communication mast by Network Rail close to their homes. (S2O-4727)

10. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive how its pathfinder urban regeneration programme will benefit the poorest communities. (S2O-4774)

11. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many registered community amateur sports clubs are receiving relief from business rates. (S2O-4692)

12. Christine May: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to encourage the development of IT services in government service delivery. (S2O-4773)

General

1. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it is supporting in order to conserve wild Atlantic salmon. (S2O-4797)

2. Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the centralisation of health services is a policy objective. (S2O-4799)

3. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether, in light of the answers to questions S2W-12435 to S2W-12439 by Mr Jim Wallace on 6 December 2004, it will now carry out additional research into renewable energy and review its targets accordingly. (S2O-4697)

4. George Lyon: To ask the Scottish Executive what requirement it has placed on NHS boards to consult local communities before the closure of NHS services. (S2O-4722)

5. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there is a duty on doctors to report incidences under the doctrine of double effect. (S2O-4753)

6. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is making any representations about the new East Coast main line franchise regarding services in Scotland. (S2O-4706)

7. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made with the provision of support for the development of renewable technologies. (S2O-4709)

8. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the Home Office regarding a trespass law applying to Holyrood House and Balmoral Castle estates. (S2O-4728)

9. Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many passengers travelling via Glasgow Prestwick airport then travel on to Edinburgh city centre. (S2O-4777)

10. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will encourage graduates to consider teaching as their first career option. (S2O-4807)

11. Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made by the Energy Intermediate Technology Institute. (S2O-4747)

12. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to introduce a postal citation system for witnesses due to attend court. (S2O-4795)

13. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take to safeguard the health of people living and working in areas with significant air pollution. (S2O-4794)

14. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the UK Government over the proposed introduction of non-jury trials in England and Wales. (S2O-4740)

15. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will introduce its proposed animal welfare legislation. (S2O-4718)
