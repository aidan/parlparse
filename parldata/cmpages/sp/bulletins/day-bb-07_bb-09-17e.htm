Business Bulletin No. 119/2007: Monday 17 September 2007
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 14 September 2007

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-4324 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive how many of its officials are carrying out work in relation to the Trident missile system and what the estimated cost is of any such work, broken down by department.

S3W-4325 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what consultative committees, groups, councils and other bodies have been set up by ministers since 16 May 2007 and what the estimated cost is of running them.

S3W-4326 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what appointments have been made to public bodies since 16 May 2007 and whether any such appointees have declared activity in any political party and, if so, which party in each case.

S3W-4327 David McLetchie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has estimated the cost of extending the National Bus Travel Concession Scheme for Older and Disabled Persons to the disabled people on the lower rate of the care component of disability living allowance and, if so, how many people would benefit from such an extension of the scheme.

S3W-4328 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-3672 by Stewart Stevenson on 13 September 2007, whether its policy is that it does not consult other parties before implementing policies outlined in the 2007 SNP manifesto.

S3W-4329 Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact assessment has been made of the effects on local media of the creation of the two new public websites by the Scottish Improvement Service.

S3W-4330 Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will fund screening for people with a family history of heart disease.

S3W-4331 Tom McCabe: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to maintain current staffing levels to deliver smaller government.

S3W-4332 Tom McCabe: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has sufficient staff to carry out its functions.

S3W-4333 Tom McCabe: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any of its departments has requested more staff.

S3W-4334 Tom McCabe: To ask the Scottish Executive whether each of its departments has a full complement of staff.

S3W-4335 Tom McCabe: To ask the Scottish Executive how many staff it employs (a) directly and (b) indirectly.

S3W-4336 Tom McCabe: To ask the Scottish Executive how many staff are employed in the Office of the First Minister.

S3W-4337 Tom McCabe: To ask the Scottish Executive how many staff positions will be cut from the Office of the First Minister as a result of voluntary measures to achieve its aims for smaller government.

S3W-4338 Tom McCabe: To ask the Scottish Executive how many staff are employed in its economy department.

S3W-4339 Tom McCabe: To ask the Scottish Executive how many staff positions will be cut from its economy department as a result of voluntary measures to achieve its aim for smaller government.

S3W-4340 Tom McCabe: To ask the Scottish Executive how many staff are employed in its education department.

S3W-4341 Tom McCabe: To ask the Scottish Executive how many staff positions will be cut from its education department as a result of voluntary measures to achieve its aim for smaller government.

S3W-4342 Tom McCabe: To ask the Scottish Executive how many staff are employed in its environment department.

S3W-4343 Tom McCabe: To ask the Scottish Executive how many staff positions will be cut from its environment department as a result of voluntary measures to achieve its aim for smaller government.

S3W-4344 Tom McCabe: To ask the Scottish Executive how many staff are employed in its health department.

S3W-4345 Tom McCabe: To ask the Scottish Executive how many staff positions will be cut from its health department as a result of voluntary measures to achieve its aim for smaller government.

S3W-4346 Tom McCabe: To ask the Scottish Executive how many staff are employed in its justice department.

S3W-4347 Tom McCabe: To ask the Scottish Executive how many staff positions will be cut from its justice department as a result of voluntary measures to achieve its aim for smaller government.

S3W-4348 Tom McCabe: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-3994 by John Swinney on 12 September 2007, whether staff whose roles and duties change will have their salaries maintained at current levels.

S3W-4349 Tom McCabe: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the number of staff it employs has reduced as a result of the reduction in the number of its departments.

S3W-4350 Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive whether plans produced by inshore fisheries groups are subject to strategic environmental assessment.

S3W-4351 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what the latest estimate is of the revenue which would accrue if the income tax level in Scotland was raised by one penny in the pound, in accordance with the revenue raising powers of the Parliament.

S3W-4352 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list the occupation of each person convicted of poisoning birds since May 1999.

S3W-4353 Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-3373 by Michael Russell on 10 September 2007, whether there was an active grouse moor or pheasant shoot within a five mile radius of each incident.

S3W-4354 Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive who pays for autopsies carried out in cases where wildlife crime is suspected.
