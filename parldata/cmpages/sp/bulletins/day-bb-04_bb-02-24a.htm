Business Bulletin No. 33 / 2004

Tuesday 24th February 2004

Section A : Daily Business List
  

Meetings of Committees

All meetings take place in Committee Chambers, Edinburgh unless otherwise indicated.

Equal Opportunities Committee

4th Meeting, 2004

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in the Chamber, Assembly Hall,

The Mound, Edinburgh

1. Items in private: The Committee will consider whether to take items 3 and 4 in private.

2. European Year of Disabled People: The Committee will hear evidence from-

Ms Margaret Curran MSP, Minister for Communities.

3. Local Governance (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will consider its draft report.

4. Work programme: The Committee will consider a draft paper on its work programme.

Finance Committee

6th Meeting, 2004

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in Committee Room 1

1. Inquiry into the Relocation of Public Sector Jobs: The Committee will take evidence from-

Eddie Reilly, Scottish Secretary, Public and Commercial Services Union;

Anne Douglas, National Officer, Prospect;

Michael Byers, Information Development Officer, UNISON;

Rozanne Foyer, Assistant Secretary, Scottish Trades Union Congress.

2. Fire Sprinklers in Residential Premises (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will take evidence as part of its consideration of the Bill's Financial Memorandum from-

Michael Matheson MSP, Bill Proposer; David Cullum, Clerk and Zoé Dean, Assistant Clerk, Non-Executive Bills Unit, Scottish Parliament.

3. Scottish Water: The Committee will be updated on the investigation into Scottish Water by its Reporters.

Subordinate Legislation Committee

7th Meeting, 2004

The Committee will meet at 10.30 am in Committee Room 3

1. Delegated powers scrutiny: The Committee will consider the response from the Scottish Executive to points raised on the following Bill-

the Antisocial Behaviour etc. (Scotland) Bill at Stage 1.

2. Delegated powers scrutiny: The Committee will consider the delegated powers provisions in the following Bill-

the Tenements (Scotland) Bill at Stage 1.

3. Executive responses: The Committee will consider responses from the Scottish Executive to points raised on the following-

the Renewables Obligation (Scotland) Order 2004, (draft);

the Registration of Establishments Keeping Laying Hens (Scotland) Amendment Regulations 2004, (SSI 2004/27);

the Local Government Capital Expenditure Limits (Scotland) Regulations 2004, (SSI 2004/29);

the National Health Service (Tribunal) (Scotland) Regulations 2004, (SSI 2004/38);

the Sea Fishing (Restriction on Days at Sea) (Scotland) Order 2004, (SSI 2004/44); and

the Local Government in Scotland Act 2003 (Commencement No.2) Order 2004, (SSI 2004/28).

4. Draft instruments subject to approval: The Committee will consider the following-

the Advice and Assistance (Financial Conditions) (Scotland) Regulations 2004, (draft);

the Civil Legal Aid (Financial Conditions) (Scotland) Regulations 2004, (draft);

the Housing Support Grant (Scotland) Order 2004, (draft); and

the Renewables Obligation (Scotland) Order 2004, (draft).

5. Instruments subject to annulment: The Committee will consider the following-

the Advice and Assistance (Scotland) Amendment Regulations 2004, (SSI 2004/49);

the Civil Legal Aid (Scotland) Amendment Regulations 2004, (SSI 2004/50);

the Criminal Legal Aid (Fixed Payments) (Scotland) Amendment Regulations 2004, (SSI 2004/51);

the Motorways Traffic (Scotland) Amendment Regulations 2004, (SSI 2004/53);

the A1 (East of Haddington to Dunbar) Special Road Regulations 2004, (SSI 2004/54);

the Sea Fishing (Prohibited Methods of Fishing) (Firth of Clyde) Order 2004, (SSI 2004/55);

the Food (Chilli and Chilli Products) (Emergency Control) (Scotland) Regulations 2004, (SSI 2004/56);

the Non-Domestic Rate (Scotland) Order 2004, (SSI 2004/59); and

the Housing Revenue Account General Fund Contribution Limits (Scotland) Order 2004, (SSI 2004/60).

6. Instruments not subject to Parliamentary procedure: The Committee will consider the following-

the Food Protection (Emergency Prohibitions) (Radioactivity in Sheep) Partial Revocation (Scotland) Order 2004, (SSI 2004/48); and

the Food Protection (Emergency Prohibitions) (Amnesic Shellfish Poisoning) (West Coast) (No.5) (Scotland) Order 2003 Partial Revocation Order 2004, (SSI 2004/61).

7. Instruments not laid before the Parliament: The Committee will consider the following-

Act of Sederunt (Rules of the Court of Session Amendment) (Miscellaneous) 2004, (SSI 2004/52);

the Private Hire Vehicles (Carriage of Guide Dogs etc.) Act 2002 (Commencement No.1) (Scotland) Order 2004, (SSI 2004/57); and

the Primary Medical Services (Scotland) Act 2004 (Commencement) Order 2004, (SSI 2004/58).

Communities Committee

7th Meeting, 2004

The Committee will meet at 2.00 pm in Committee Room 4

1. Antisocial Behaviour etc. (Scotland) Bill (in private): The Committee will consider a draft Stage 1 report.

European and External Relations Committee

4th Meeting, 2004

The Committee will meet at 2.00 pm in Committee Room 2

1. The Irish Presidency of the European Union and the priorities of the Scottish Executive: The Committee will hear from-

Mr Andy Kerr MSP, Minister for Finance and Public Services; and

Alastair Wilson and Tim Simons, External Relations Division, The Scottish Executive.

2. The Scottish Executive's European Strategy: The Committee will hear from-

Mr Andy Kerr MSP, Minister for Finance and Public Services; and

Alastair Wilson and Tim Simons, External Relations Division, The Scottish Executive.

3. Convener's Report: The Convener will update the Committee on the-

Briefing paper on the Decision of the European Commission in the case of Ryanair and Charleroi Airport; and

Monthly report on the external relations activities in the Scottish Parliament.

4. Pre- and post-EU Council scrutiny: The Committee will discuss the agendas and information received from the Scottish Executive on the following meetings of the Council of the EU-

Forthcoming Councils

Education, Youth and Culture Council, 26 February;

Environment Council, 2 March;

Employment, Social Policy, Health and Consumer Affairs Council, 4 - 5 March;

Transport, Telecommunications and Energy Council, 8 - 9 March;

ECOFIN Council, 9 March;

Competitiveness Council, 11 March; and

Previous Councils

General Affairs and External Relations Council, 26 - 27 January;

ECOFIN Council, 20 January.

5. Implementation of EC legislation: The Committee will consider a proposal from the Scottish Executive to implement EC legislation using section 57 (1) of the Scotland Act, specifically the Council Regulation (EC) 1383/2003 concerning customs action against goods suspected of infringing certain intellectual property rights.

Justice 2 Committee

6th Meeting, 2004

The Committee will meet at 2.00 pm in Committee Room 1

1. Item in private: The Committee will consider whether to take item 4 in private.

2. Tenements (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will take evidence at Stage 1 from-

Joyce Lugton, Bill Team Leader, Edythe Murie, Solicitor and Norman Macleod, Solicitor, the Scottish Executive.

4. Antisocial Behaviour etc. (Scotland) Bill - leak inquiry: The Committee will consider a report on the alleged unauthorised disclosure of information about its draft Stage 1 report on the Antisocial Behaviour etc. (Scotland) Bill.
