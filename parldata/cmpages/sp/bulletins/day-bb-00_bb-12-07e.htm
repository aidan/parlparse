BUSINESS BULLETIN 189 / 2000

Thursday 7 December 2000

Section E - Written questions lodged on 6 December 2000

 

 

S1W-11697 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive who is responsible for the distribution of Ethnic Minority Grant and which organisations or bodies are consulted as part of the decision-making process.

S1W-11777 Dr Sylvia Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the timetable is for its consultation exercise on the regulation of the skin piercing industry.

S1W-11778 Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to measure the quality of drinking water.

S1W-11779 Ian Jenkins: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people enrolled on teacher training courses at educational establishments in each year since 1979.

S1W-11780 Euan Robson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many new recruits entered the police force in each year since 1980 and what the projected numbers of new recruits are for those future years for which figures are available, broken down by police force.

S1W-11781 Euan Robson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many police officers have left the service in each year since 1990.

S1W-11782 Euan Robson: To ask the Scottish Executive when public satisfaction with police services was last surveyed, what the results were and what plans it has to conduct any future surveys.

S1W-11783 Euan Robson: To ask the Scottish Executive how much was spent by (a) central government and (b) local government on crime prevention in each year since 1979.

S1W-11784 Mr Keith Raffan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many drug-related prosecutions were undertaken in the courts in each year since 1990.

S1W-11785 Mr Keith Raffan: To ask the Scottish Executive how much money was spent on the treatment and rehabilitation of drug users in each year since 1990 and how much has been allocated for those future years for which figures are available.

S1W-11786 Mr Duncan Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to help businesses prepare for possible entry into the Euro.

S1W-11787 Dorothy-Grace Elder: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will hold talks with Glasgow Women's Aid and Easterhouse Women's Aid over the intended provision of three extra places in Glasgow refuges.

S1W-11788 Dorothy-Grace Elder: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will hold talks with Glasgow City Council on the funding situation of Women's Aid refuges in the city.

S1W-11789 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the absence of central heating in a pensioner household is the only criterion used to estimate that 70,000 pensioner households will benefit from its proposed central heating scheme or whether other criteria are used to arrive at this figure and, if so, what the criteria are.

S1W-11790 Dorothy-Grace Elder: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to review the funding arrangements for Women's Aid projects and to remove any uncertainty over future funding arrangements.

S1W-11791 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has made to the Gaelic Broadcasting Committee to ensure that a daily news output remains part of the Gaelic television schedule. R

S1W-11792 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it or Comataidh Craolaidh Gaidhlig has received with regard to the proposed ending of a daily television news output in Gaelic. R

S1W-11793 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has received and what actions it has taken regarding the redundancy of 14 staff presently working on the Gaelic news programme Telefios. R

S1W-11794 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made of the employment created in Gaelic television since the creation of the Gaelic Television Committee, now the Gaelic Television Broadcasting Committee. R

S1W-11795 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has made to the Natural Heritage Lottery Fund to ensure that Tobar an Dualchais receives sufficient lottery funding to activate match funding for its project to conserve and disseminate the Gaelic and other material in its archives.

S1W-11796 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking or plans to take to ensure effective and systematic implementation of the UN Convention on the Rights of the Child.

S1W-11797 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to reduce general practitioner list sizes in areas of deprivation and greatest health need.

S1W-11798 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to extend the standard general practitioner training to four years with a minimum of two years in general practice by 2005.

S1W-11799 Mr John Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive what further progress has been made in implementing the Land Reform Action Plan published in August 1999.

S1W-11801 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many modern apprenticeships for 18 to 24 year olds each local enterprise company has funded in each year since 1997.

S1W-11802 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how much each local enterprise company has spent on modern apprenticeships for 18 to 24 year olds in each year since 1997.

S1W-11803 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how many staff are currently employed in the Energy Division of its Enterprise and Lifelong Learning Department, specifying the number of staff at each grade, what the equivalent figures were in each year since 1997 and, of these staff, how many in each year had or have duties relating (a) solely and (b) partially to the offshore oil and gas industry.

S1W-11804 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people died prematurely from illnesses which resulted from living in cold homes over the last three years and how many are expected to die over the next three years in similar circumstances.
