BUSINESS BULLETIN 68 / 2000

Tuesday 25 April 2000

Section E - Written questions lodged on 20 April 2000

 

 

S1W-6257 Ms Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it proposes to take in the light of the recent research published in Human Psychopharmacology on the organic nature of chronic fatigue syndrome.

 

S1W-6258 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria it used in deciding on the location of the headquarters of the Scottish University of Industry.

 

S1W-6259 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive what locations it actively considered as a location for the headquarters of the Scottish University for Industry.

 

S1W-6260 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive what consultation took place with other parties on the location of the headquarters of the Scottish University for Industry.

 

S1W-6261 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive what consultations it undertakes with local authorities on the dates for school holidays.

 

S1W-6262 Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has for the future of freshwater fishing.

 

S1W-6263 Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive what effect proposals to remove access to water in the draft Land Reform Bill will have on the Scottish tourist industry, in particular in relation to water sports and activities.

 

S1W-6264 Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive, in the light of concerns expressed by Argyll and Clyde Health Board about high levels of heroin abuse in the Greenock and Inverclyde area, what future provision is being made for increased rehabilitation services for drug users.

 

S1W-6265 Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive how many school children have been prescribed Ritanol in (a) Scotland and (b) the Greenock and Inverclyde area.

 

S1W-6266 Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive what procedures are followed by schools to monitor the numbers of children taking Ritanol.

 

S1W-6267 Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive what arrangements exist for the support of children taking Ritanol.

 

S1W-6268 Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-4481 by Mr Sam Galbraith on 3 April 2000, which 16 local authorities have revised bylaws in place on child work permits; which 10 local authorities have submitted bylaws on child work permits for confirmation, and which 6 local authorities are engaged in local consultation.

 

S1W-6269 Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-4483 by Mr Sam Galbraith on 3 April 2000, when figures will be published on employees who have been prosecuted for employing children in industrial undertakings in the last 12 months.

 

S1W-6270 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of curriculum time in schools is spent using information technology. R

S1W-6271 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has set targets for the amount of curriculum time to be spent using information technology in schools. R

S1W-6272 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive why a Scottish monthly e-commerce update was not included in the progress report by the UK e-Envoy dated 31 March. R

S1W-6273 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-5627 by Susan Deacon on 11 April 2000, when the fact finding exercise into specific aspects of the issue of hepatitis C and other infections contracted from contaminated blood products in Scotland commenced; when it is expected to be concluded; whether it will set up an independent inquiry into this issue and whether all documentation relating to the fact finding exercise will be placed in the public domain or made available to interested parties.

 

S1W-6274 Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive which of its agencies or departments and which public bodies currently operate systems of strategic environmental assessment in determining their policies and programmes.

 

S1W-6275 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how many applications for inshore fisheries regulatory orders are currently pending; when were they lodged and when decisions on them are expected.

 

S1W-6276 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how many incidents of escaped farm salmon have been notified to it in each of the last three years and to detail each incident.

 

S1W-6277 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list all applications it has received in each of the last three years for authorisation for the sale or production of genetically modified salmon or other fish and by whom each was made.

 

S1W-6278 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what its policy is on the sale or production of genetically modified salmon or other fish.

 

S1W-6279 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how many defibrillators are currently operated by the NHS and how many are available in public places.

 

S1W-6280 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to extend the availability of defibrillators throughout Scotland, particularly in public places.
