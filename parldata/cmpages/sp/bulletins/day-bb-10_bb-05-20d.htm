Business Bulletin No. 85/2010: Thursday 20 May 2010
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 27 May 2010

Question Time

General

1. Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people had their driving licences suspended and how many licences were revoked in the last year as a result of drug misuse. (S3O-10701)

2. Stuart McMillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what assistance it can provide to local authorities in collecting council tax payments. (S3O-10725)

3. Aileen Campbell: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to encourage road safety behaviour among young people. (S3O-10707)

4. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in working with the UK Borders Agency to develop alternatives to the detention of asylum seekers. (S3O-10689)

5. Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive what additional resources it provides to Glasgow City Council to recognise the pressures placed on it by the number of children and young people with English as an additional language enrolled in Glasgow educational establishments. (S3O-10645)

6. Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to protect library services across Scotland. (S3O-10661)

7. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what is being done to alleviate road traffic congestion in the Inverness area. (S3O-10678)

8. Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the announcement that 64,000 passenger journeys have been made from the reopened Laurencekirk Station, a 78% increase on the original estimate of 36,000, when it will bring forward the necessary funding to reopen Kintore Station. (S3O-10699)

9. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to ensure that good quality bus services are provided according to community need. (S3O-10675)

10. Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is aware of any local authorities that have suspended the recruitment of teachers on permanent employment contracts and, if so, what representations have been made on this matter. (S3O-10647)

Health and Wellbeing

1. Christopher Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made over the last year in the research, treatment and support of young people with Duchenne muscular dystrophy. (S3O-10716)

2. David McLetchie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action has been taken to reduce the failure rate of NHS medical equipment. (S3O-10685)

3. Angela Constance: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is supporting people with Huntington’s disease. (S3O-10709)

4. Ian McKee: To ask the Scottish Executive, in view of the hospital consultant bonus scheme now costing over a quarter of a billion pounds of UK NHS expenditure per year and millions in pension consequentials, whether it will consider a further approach to the Secretary of State for Health with the aim of securing co-ordinated action to curb this expenditure. (S3O-10723)

5. David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will publish the Scottish Diabetes Action Plan. (S3O-10674)

6. Not Lodged

7. Bob Doris: To ask the Scottish Executive what assurances it can give to workers in the NHS in the wake of projected reductions to the Scottish budget. (S3O-10711)

8. Jim Tolson: To ask the Scottish Executive what advice has been given to NHS boards regarding potential staffing problems caused by the European working time directive and the shortages of locum cover. (S3O-10704)

9. Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it is having with medical professionals and patient groups to improve early detection rates of oesophageal cancer. (S3O-10640)

10. Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met senior management of NHS Lothian and what issues were discussed. (S3O-10639)

11. Shirley-Anne Somerville: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will review policies and procedures to ensure that people with coeliac disease have appropriate and consistent access to gluten-free products on prescription. (S3O-10727)

12. Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to support young adult disabled people in care homes. (S3O-10691)

13. Bill Kidd: To ask the Scottish Executive what further progress has been made in response to the campaign championed by Ryan McLaughlin with regard to the importance of taking vitamin D supplements to combat multiple sclerosis and potentially other illnesses. (S3O-10718)

14. Derek Brownlee: To ask the Scottish Executive what work it is undertaking to reduce the rate of inflation in the NHS. (S3O-10680)

15. Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will outline how it will ensure a continuing robust NHS partnership with staff, patients and carers. (S3O-10648)

16. Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met the chairman of NHS Forth Valley. (S3O-10720)

17. Joe FitzPatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive what the total number of NHS Tayside staff will be in 2011 compared with 2007. (S3O-10713)

18. Cathie Craigie: To ask the Scottish Executive, in view of latest figures showing that the number of central heating installations under the Energy Assistance Package, including legacy installations, amounted to around half of those carried out in the final year of the Central Heating Programme, whether it considers that the Energy Assistance Package has matched its objectives and expectations. (S3O-10642)

19. Malcolm Chisholm: To ask the Scottish Executive when regulations concerning inspections as required by section 58 of the Public Services Reform (Scotland) Act 2010 will be laid. (S3O-10641)

20. Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking in conjunction with NHS Ayrshire and Arran to reduce drug addiction in North Ayrshire. (S3O-10714)
