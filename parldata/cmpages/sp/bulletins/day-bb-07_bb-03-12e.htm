Business Bulletin No. 46/2007: Monday 12 March 2007
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 9 March 2007

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S2W-32286 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people were convicted of (a) allowing disorderly conduct on licensed premises and (b) selling alcohol to people who are drunk in each of the last 10 years.

S2W-32316 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether Transport Scotland has taken account of the general increase in rail usage, alongside existing demand projections for the Airdrie to Bathgate rail link project, in the procurement of rolling stock.

S2W-32317 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the current patronage projections are for the Airdrie to Bathgate rail link project.

S2W-32318 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether Transport Scotland is taking account of the 3% per annum passenger growth identified in Network Rail’s Network Utilisation Strategy in the procurement of rolling stock for the Airdrie to Bathgate rail link project.

S2W-32319 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the patronage projections are for the Helensburgh to Waverley railway line following the completion of the Airdrie to Bathgate rail link.

S2W-32320 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the original patronage projections were for the Larkhall to Milngavie railway line and what the current patronage level is, expressed in passenger journey numbers and showing the percentage difference.

S2W-32321 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what patronage assumptions were made by Transport Scotland in respect of the procurement of rolling stock for the Airdrie to Bathgate rail link project.

S2W-32322 Mrs Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive how much each NHS board has spent on wheelchair provision in each year since 1999.

S2W-32323 Mrs Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive what actions it intends to take in response to Moving Forward: Review of NHS Wheelchair and Seating Services in Scotland.

S2W-32324 Mrs Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to ensure a more integrated service for those who need to use wheelchairs and for their families and carers.

S2W-32325 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-31895 by Cathy Jamieson on 2 March 2007, whether it will list the 10 local authority areas with the (a) biggest and (b) smallest (i) reductions and (ii) increases in the number of (A) serious assaults, (B) robberies, (C) crimes of indecency, (d) rapes and attempted rapes, (e) indecent assaults and (f) cases of lewd and indecent behaviour since 1996-97.

S2W-32326 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S2W-31895 by Cathy Jamieson on 2 March 2007, whether it will list the 10 local authority areas with the (a) biggest and (b) smallest (i) reductions and (ii) increases in the number of (A) crimes against public justice, (B) crimes of handling an offensive weapon and (C) drug-related crimes since 1996-97.

S2W-32327 Mr Bruce McFee: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration it has given to including primary biliary cirrhosis on the list of conditions exempt from NHS prescription charges.

S2W-32328 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether individuals entitled to object to a permanent Traffic Regulation Order (TRO) in respect of the Edinburgh trams scheme are entitled to object to the proposed temporary TRO and, if so, whether a public inquiry would be held to consider such objections, how long the Reporter to the inquiry could take to reach a conclusion, whether an inquiry would delay the construction work for the scheme and what impact any consequent delay would have on the costs of the scheme.

S2W-32329Δ Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to promote better energy efficiency and encourage a greater uptake of microgeneration.

S2W-32330 Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on whether the draft EU postal services directive will impact on the number of sub-post offices and post offices in Scotland, in particular those providing services in sparsely populated areas and on the islands.

S2W-32331 Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the guidelines on the use of biometric systems in schools, currently being developed by the British Educational Communications and Technology Agency, will apply in Scotland.

S2W-32332 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive what average life expectancy for (a) men and (b) women was in each parliamentary constituency in each year since 1997.

S2W-32333 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many nurses it expects to receive a pay increase of 1.9% in the next 12 months.

S2W-32334 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many (a) prosecutions were brought and (b) convictions were obtained for offences committed under the Computer Misuse Act 1990 in the last five years.

S2W-32335 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the percentage change in the prison population has been in each year since 1997.

S2W-32336 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many crimes were committed in each hospital in each of the last five years for which figures are available, broken down by type of crime.

S2W-32337 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has for future pensions provision for fire and rescue services’ staff.
