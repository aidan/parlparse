Business Bulletin No. 24/2010: Friday 5 February 2010
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 4 February 2010

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-31486Δ Shirley-Anne Somerville: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will provide further permitted development rights for domestic micro-wind turbines and air source heat pumps.

S3W-31487 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde being able to pay the contractor for the Southern General Hospital project 99% of the build costs before the new sites are completed.

S3W-31488 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason it has agreed to a contract permitting 99% of the cost of the new Southern General Hospital to be paid in advance of the completion of the site.

S3W-31489 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the planned payments to the building contractor for the new Southern General Hospital provided by NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde comply with best practice.

S3W-31490 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive what the planned profile is of capital expenditure for the building of the new Southern General Hospital.

S3W-31491 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason the Edinburgh tram project did not appear in the written evidence provided to the Public Audit Committee by the Permanent Secretary of the Scottish Executive in January 2010 regarding progress on major capital projects.

S3W-31492 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has been approached by Strathclyde Partnership for Transport for financial support to assist in maintaining the Renfrew Ferry.

S3W-31493 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has been approached with any requests to assist in maintaining the Renfrew Ferry.

S3W-31494 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive what the annual financial subsidy is to the Renfrew Ferry.

S3W-31495 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason it has not yet answered question S3W-27290, which was due for answer on 29 September 2009.

S3W-31496 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive which of the recommendations in Audit Scotland’s Review of major capital projects in Scotland in June 2008 regarding reporting publicly on the progress of major capital projects (a) have been implemented and (b) remain outstanding.

S3W-31497 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive whether its plans for enhanced reporting of government spending include providing a planned capital expenditure schedule for each major capital project supported by the Scottish Government and, if so, with what regularity it will be updated.

S3W-31498 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many adults in Fife reported ever using drugs in crime and justice surveys in the last three years, broken down by type of drug.

S3W-31499 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many adults in Fife reported using drugs in the last month in crime and justice surveys in the last three years, broken down by type of drug.

S3W-31500 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many adults in Fife reported using drugs in the last year in crime and justice surveys in the last three years, broken down by type of drug.

S3W-31501 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many students entered Scottish universities in each year from 2000-01, broken down by (a) university and (b) domicile of student.

S3W-31502 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many students were given places on Scottish university courses in each year from 2000-01, broken down by (a) university and (c) domicile of student.

S3W-31503 Claire Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many applications for places at Scottish universities were received in each year from 2000-01, including for 2010-11, broken down by (a) university and (c) domicile of student.

S3W-31504 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people or organisations in Scotland have been prosecuted for failure to comply with provisions of EU legislation in each of the last 10 years.

S3W-31505 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether NHS Education for Scotland is supporting or commissioning training in counselling as an evidence-based therapy to meet the needs of patients.

S3W-31506 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Scottish Government Health Directorate Psychological Interventions Team will look at counselling in relation to patient pathways and referral criteria for access to services.

S3W-31507 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the two pilots to help improve access to self-help therapies for people with mild to moderate depression or anxiety will include the use of counselling.

S3W-31508 David Whitton: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to address the legislative recommendations made in Professor Sir Patrick Bateson’s report, Independent Inquiry into Dog Breeding.

S3W-31509 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-29901 by Nicola Sturgeon on 18 December 2009 and in light of the threat from healthcare associated infection, whether it will revise the guidance to ensure early replacement of swan neck taps.

S3W-31510 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with stakeholders regarding the possible relocation of the clipper ship, City of Adelaide or HMS Carrick, from the Scottish Maritime Museum in Irvine.

S3W-31511 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made regarding the possible relocation of the clipper ship, City of Adelaide or HMS Carrick, from the Scottish Maritime Museum in Irvine.

S3W-31512 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to ensure that the clipper ship, City of Adelaide or HMS Carrick, is preserved as part of Scotland’s and Australia’s joint social, cultural and maritime heritage.

S3W-31513 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will ensure that the new Upper Forth Crossing name of Clackmannanshire Bridge is inserted on all signage in a 10-mile radius of the bridge as well as at the bridge itself.

S3W-31514 Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on the proposed review of the NHS (Pharmaceutical Services) (Scotland) Regulations 2009 referred to in the letter dated 21 May 2009 from the Primary and Community Care Directorate to the Public Petitions Committee.

S3W-31515 Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive when it intends to publish a formal consultation on the NHS (Pharmaceutical Services) (Scotland) Regulations 2009.

S3W-31516 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it or any of its agencies have been contacted regarding safety and security measures that will be needed if the proposed relocation of the UK continental shelf core and records collections to Keyworth goes ahead.

S3W-31517 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the British Geological Survey regarding the proposed relocation of the UK continental shelf core and records collections to Keyworth.

S3W-31518 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what effect the proposed relocation of the UK continental shelf core and records collections to Keyworth will have on the oil and gas industry in Scotland.

S3W-31519 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what effect the proposed relocation of the UK continental shelf core and records collections to Keyworth will have on Scottish universities’ research capabilities.

S3W-31520 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what effect the proposed relocation of the UK continental shelf core and records collections to Keyworth will have on students at Scottish universities.

S3W-31521 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive how many job losses will result from the proposed relocation of the UK continental shelf core and records collections to Keyworth.

S3W-31522 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it was consulted on the proposed relocation of the UK continental shelf core and records collections to Keyworth.

S3W-31523 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the Department of Energy and Climate Change regarding the proposed relocation of the UK continental shelf core and records collections to Keyworth.

S3W-31524 Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what (a) financial and (b) operational effect the proposed relocation of the UK continental shelf core and records collections to Keyworth will have on Scottish businesses.

S3W-31527 Ted Brocklebank: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with Fife Council regarding the flooding of the Kinness Burn in St Andrews since 2005.

S3W-31528 Ted Brocklebank: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the Scottish Environment Protection Agency regarding the flooding of the Kinness Burn in St Andrews since 2005.

S3W-31529 Ted Brocklebank: To ask the Scottish Executive whether Fife Council has applied to the Scottish Environment Protection Agency for a licence under the Water Environment (Controlled Activities) (Scotland) Regulations 2005 to dredge the Kinness Burn in St Andrews.

S3W-31530 Ted Brocklebank: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the Scottish Environment Protection Agency regarding dredging and removing silt berms in the Kinness Burn in St Andrews.

S3W-31531 Ted Brocklebank: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to prevent further flooding of the Kinness Burn in St Andrews.

S3W-31532 Ted Brocklebank: To ask the Scottish Executive what resources are available to assist local authorities to carry out work to prevent flooding of burns, rivers and streams.
