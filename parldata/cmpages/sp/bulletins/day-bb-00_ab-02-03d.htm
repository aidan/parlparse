BUSINESS BULLETIN 20 / 2000

Thursday 3 February 2000

Section D –  Oral questions selected for answer on 10 February 2000

 

 

Question Time

1. Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive what reports it has received on the recent STUC Conference of Trades Unions and Trades Union Councils in the Highlands and Islands. (S1O-1115)

 

2. Shona Robison: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met representatives of the Home Office and what matters were discussed. (S1O-1095)

 

3. Tricia Marwick: To ask the Scottish Executive what recognition and support is being given to the voluntary sector to ensure that its work is able to continue and be developed over the coming year. (S1O-1080)

 

4. Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will issue guidelines preventing the amputation of healthy limbs. (S1O-1089)

 

5. Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it expects the number of police officers employed by Dumfries and Galloway Constabulary to increase or decrease during the next two years. (S1O-1113)

 

6. Mr Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Minister for Rural Affairs last met the Secretary of State for Agriculture, Fisheries and Food and what issues they discussed. (S1O-1104)

 

7. Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what is the total number of beds currently in use at Craig Dunain and the total number of beds that will be available at the new Craig Phadraig. (S1O-1114)

 

8. Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what arrangements will be made for liaison between the new Drug Enforcement Agency and Grampian Police in order to tackle the problems of drug abuse and street crime in Aberdeen. (S1O-1120)

 

9. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what statistical information it has for population change in the Highlands and Islands over the past five years. (S1O-1119)

 

10. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will report on the summit for mental health interests recently held in Edinburgh. (S1O-1130)

 

11. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to improve care of the elderly. (S1O-1109)

 

12. Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to encourage and facilitate air freight into Scotland. (S1O-1082)

 

13. Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Scottish Prison Service has had any discussions with potential buyers of the former prison at Dungavel House. (S1O-1128)

 

14. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are being taken to provide detailed and comprehensive assistance to families who are victims of crime. (S1O-1131)

 

15. Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress the Scottish Law Commission has made in its review of the law of the foreshore and seabed and when it is expected to submit its report to the Scottish Executive. (S1O-1094)

 

16. Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive when Ministers last met representatives of the Scottish Prison Service Trade Union Side and what matters they discussed. (S1O-1106)

 

17. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to improve rail infrastructure and services in Lanarkshire. (S1O-1090)

 

18. Mr John Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-3200 by Ross Finnie on 24 December 1999, what plans it has developed, in the light of the responses received during the consultation exercise which ended on 14 December 1999, to ensure that all meat products are clearly labelled with their country of origin. (S1O-1129)

 

19. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-2109 by Susan Deacon on 10 November 1999, whether it has now received the advice from the National Services Advisory Group on the provision of cochlear implants, whether it will place a copy of this advice in the Scottish Parliament Information Centre and what action it plans to take in the light of this advice. (S1O-1111)

 

20. Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make a statement on the use of Scottish timber in the construction industry. (S1O-1121)

 

21. Mr Keith Raffan: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to hear the outcome of Her Majesty’s Government’s spending review as it affects Scotland. (S1O-1083)

 

22. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what response there has been from further education colleges to its proposals in the framework document Working Together for Wider Access to Further and Higher Education and a Fair Deal for Students. (S1O-1122)

 

23. Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive what its strategy is for tackling the issue of food poverty in Scotland. (S1O-1086)

 

24. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to privatise the water industry. (S1O-1097)

 

25. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to encourage the take-up of career opportunities in childcare. (S1O-1081)

 

26. Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in developing indicators of rural deprivation, whether these will be used in the determination of financial settlements for public bodies including local authorities and, if so, when. (S1O-1102)

 

27. Mr Duncan Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers the services offered by Caledonian MacBrayne to be the best possible deal for rural and island communities in Scotland. (S1O-1116)

 

28. Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to review legislation to ensure more effective enforcement in preventing sales of tobacco to underage children. (S1O-1092)

 

29. Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will take steps to ensure that the newly merged Scottish Opera and Scottish Ballet will have staff directors appointed to the new board. (S1O-1079)

 

30. Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to address the financial problems facing the farming industry. (S1O-1125)
