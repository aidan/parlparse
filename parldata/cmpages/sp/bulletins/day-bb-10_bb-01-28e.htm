Business Bulletin No. 18/2010: Thursday 28 January 2010
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 27 January 2010

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-31091 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what consultation took place between ministers and Scottish Enterprise regarding the decision to withdraw the Graduates for Business scheme.

S3W-31092 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive how many employees of Scottish Enterprise will be affected by the decision to withdraw the Graduates for Business scheme.

S3W-31093 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive how many student places will be lost as a result of the decision to withdraw the Graduates for Business scheme.

S3W-31094 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans ministers have to replace the Graduates for Business scheme.

S3W-31095 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to prevent the closure of Castle Toward as a residential facility for outdoor pursuits and courses in the expressive arts.

S3W-31096 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what (a) correspondence and (b) discussions ministers have had with Argyll and Bute Council regarding the proposed closure of Castle Toward.

S3W-31097 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it has taken to support outdoor education and, in particular, to sustain facilities such as Castle Toward.

S3W-31098 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive how many and what percentage of its employees earn less than £18,000 per annum.

S3W-31099 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prosecutions for using a mobile phone while driving have been undertaken in each police board area and whether guidance is being considered for police forces on dealing with this issue.

S3W-31100 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what buildings occupied by it and its agencies have been fitted with (a) solar panels, (b) wind generators and (c) other renewable power sources since May 2007.

S3W-31101 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive which ministerial journeys were made by public transport in 2009 that were made by car in 2008 and what plans there are for similar carbon saving transfers in 2010.

S3W-31102 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what ministers of foreign governments, both national and regional, the First Minister had formal meetings with in 2009.

S3W-31103 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what meetings took place in 2009 between ministers and representatives of the (a) Royal Bank of Scotland, (b) Lloyds Banking Group and (c) Clydesdale Bank regarding their operations in Scotland.

S3W-31104 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions the (a) First Minister, (b) Minister for Culture and External Affairs and (c) Minister for Public Health and Sport have had with the 2014 Commonwealth Games organisers since 1 January 2009; what topics were discussed, and what the arrangements are for coordinating plans across local and national government in the run up to 2014.

S3W-31105 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions officials and ministers have had with councillors or officials of the City of Edinburgh Council regarding the tram project in the last three months; what subjects were discussed, and whether any alterations to the scheme are envisaged as a result.

S3W-31106 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what alterations to the Edinburgh tram project would require (a) ministerial and (b) parliamentary approval.

S3W-31107 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions ministers or officials have had with (a) ScotRail, (b) East Coast and (c) CrossCountry about the rail service between Glasgow and Edinburgh and onward to England and what action it will take as a consequence.

S3W-31108 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what the senior management structure of Transport Scotland is; how it compares with that before the retirement of Dr Malcolm Reed as chief executive, and what the comparative costs are.

S3W-31109 Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it will take to promote groundskeeping through continued professional development, apprenticeships and careers services for young people.

S3W-31110 Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration it has given to the financial and environmental implications of local authorities installing artificial instead of grass playing surfaces.

S3W-31111 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answers to questions S3W-30829, S3W-30830, S3W-30831 and S3W-30832 by Adam Ingram on 26 January 2010, what additional support needs advocacy service will be available to parents between July 2010 and the start of the autumn term.

S3W-31112 Ken Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answers to questions S3W-30829, S3W-30830, S3W-30831 and S3W-30832 by Adam Ingram on 26 January 2010, whether ministers are aware of the support that organisations such as Independent Special Education Advice give parents and children with additional support needs over the summer months.

S3W-31113 Jackson Carlaw: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the First Minister’s statement that "Nicola Sturgeon has extended the opening hours of GP surgeries in Scotland" (Official Report c. 23006), whether it will provide details of these extended opening hours and where they are in operation.

S3W-31114 Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken to improve the understanding and awareness of flood risk in communities across Scotland.

S3W-31115 Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken to ensure that the planning system prevents inappropriate development in flood-risk areas.

S3W-31116 Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken to ensure that new developments in medium to high-risk areas are flood resistant.

S3W-31117 Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken to provide information about how to obtain flood insurance and promote access to such insurance for low-income households.

S3W-31118 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive which local authorities place an age restriction on vehicles used for school transport.

S3W-31119 Cathy Jamieson: To ask the Scottish Executive which local authorities insist on seatbelts being fitted to all vehicles used for school transport.

S3W-31120 Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Joint Warrior military exercises held off Scotland’s west coast in May and October 2009 operated in any environmentally sensitive conservation zones containing (a) protected marine habitats, (b) seabird breeding grounds and (c) protected fauna and flora and what precautions were taken to ensure that the exercises did not damage the environment or harm wildlife.

S3W-31121 Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive what precautions were taken to ensure that any sonar devices deployed or operated during the Joint Warrior military exercises held off Scotland’s west coast in May to October 2009 did not harm resident or migrating cetaceans and what monitoring was undertaken to record potential or actual impacts.

S3W-31122 Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive what precautions were taken to ensure that any weapons deployed or operated during the Joint Warrior military exercise held off Scotland’s west coast in May to October 2009 did not negatively impact on either marine or terrestrial wildlife in the area and what monitoring was undertaken to record potential or actual impacts.

S3W-31123 Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the environmental impacts of the biannual Joint Warrior military exercises off Scotland’s west coast are measured and, if so, what these were in (a) 2008 and (b) 2009 and what they are anticipated to be in 2010.

S3W-31124 Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the carbon footprints of the biannual Joint Warrior military exercises off Scotland’s west coast are measured and, if so, what these were in (a) 2008 and (b) 2009 and what they are anticipated to be in 2010.

S3W-31125 Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will announce the capital allocation for NHS Lanarkshire for 2010-11.

S3W-31126 Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what the capital allocation for NHS Lanarkshire will be for 2010-11.

S3W-31127 Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive when the demolition work will begin at the site of the new Airdrie health centre.

S3W-31128 Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive when building work on the new Airdrie health centre will commence.

S3W-31129 Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive when the new Airdrie health centre will be opened to the public.

S3W-31130 Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive when work will begin on the new mental health units in Lanarkshire.

S3W-31131 Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive when work will begin on the refurbishment of Monklands Hospital.

S3W-31132 Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will support universities offering degrees in architecture, built environment and planning following the proposal from the Scottish Funding Council to reduce the funding for these degrees by 22%.

S3W-31133 Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take given concerns regarding the quality of evidence on which the Scottish Funding Council has based its proposals to reduce funding for architecture, built environment and planning in universities by 22%.

S3W-31134 Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will support relatively specialist degrees such as construction management that could be at risk if the Scottish Funding Council implements its proposals to reduce funding for architecture, built environment and planning in universities by 22%.

S3W-31135 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive on how many occasions the Cabinet Secretary for Justice has contacted the Libyan authorities regarding the condition of Mr Abdelbaset Ali Mohmed Al Megrahi.

S3W-31136 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions ministers or officials have had with East Renfrewshire Council regarding the condition of Mr Abdelbaset Ali Mohmed Al Megrahi.

S3W-31137 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make public all medical reports submitted to the Cabinet Secretary for Justice on Mr Abdelbaset Ali Mohmed Al Megrahi prior to the decision to release him.

S3W-31138 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what powers it has to institute inquiries in areas other than planning and what consideration has been given to setting up an inquiry into the circumstances surrounding the conviction of Mr Abdelbaset Ali Mohmed Al Megrahi and subsequent events relating to the Lockerbie bombing.

S3W-31139 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to meet relatives of the people killed in the Lockerbie bombing.

S3W-31140 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list all advice that it has received from the Lord Advocate that has been put in the public domain.

S3W-31141 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria are used in deciding which countries the First Minister should visit; what consultation is undertaken with the Foreign and Commonwealth Office or other UK Government departments regarding such visits, and who is responsible for authorising the expenditure.

S3W-31142 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what visits the First Minister has made to (a) schools, (b) hospitals and (c) prisons since 1 October 2009.

S3W-31143 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what meetings have taken place involving ministers regarding learning from the experience of the recent severe weather period and what action is proposed as a result.

S3W-31144 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what meetings took place between Scottish and UK ministers in 2009; what topics were discussed, and who initiated the meetings.

S3W-31145 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration it has given to proposing that the Parliament make use of the power to raise income tax by up to 3p in the pound.

S3W-31146 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what the latest estimate is of what annual revenue would be raised if the power to raise income tax by 3p in the pound were used.

S3W-31147 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what arrangements will be made to institute a period of purdah during the forthcoming UK general election.

S3W-31148 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what efficiency savings were made in each of its directorates in 2008-09; by what means, and in what areas other than procurement.

S3W-31149 Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive which local authorities have contracted out cleaning and janitorial services to Mitie PFI Ltd.

S3W-31150 Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many staff have transferred from each local authority to Mitie PFI Ltd.

S3W-31151 Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether, under staff and service transfer terms, local authorities are expected to formally meet regularly with Mitie PFI Ltd to discuss service provision and, if so, how often such meetings have taken place.

S3W-31152 Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many staff who have transferred to Mitie PFI Ltd under respective school contracts have experienced an incorrect pension deduction from salary and how much the average shortfall is.

S3W-31153 Peter Peacock: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will confirm that all marine renewables and their associated works are legitimate uses of the sea, as referred to in the Marine (Scotland) Bill.

S3W-31154 Peter Peacock: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will confirm that each non-urgent Marine Protected Area designation will be subject to a 12-week consultation, as referred to in the Marine (Scotland) Bill.

S3W-31155 Alasdair Allan: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it will make to the UK Government to ensure that UK legislation does not have the effect of running counter to the aims of the Scottish Government in relation to the issue of croft and croft house tenancy valuation.

S3W-31156 Peter Peacock: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there will be a preference for existing users over new users in relation to legitimate uses of the sea, as referred to in the Marine (Scotland) Bill.
