Business Bulletin No. 91 / 2004

Thursday 27th May 2004

Section D : Oral questions selected for answer on Thursday 3rd June 2004
  

Question Time

Environment and Rural Development

1. Mike Watson: To ask the Scottish Executive what further measures it is taking to improve the efficiency of Scottish Water. (S2O-2628)

2. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it is giving to recycling in Edinburgh. (S2O-2568)

3. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it has made on meeting its environmental targets. (S2O-2622)

4. Carolyn Leckie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Environment and Rural Affairs Department made any representations on environmental issues in respect of the planning permission sought for the development of an enclosed, floodlit, synthetic football pitch at Dunbeth Park, Coatbridge. (S2O-2585)

5. Christine May: To ask the Scottish Executive whether Scotland will meet its targets for the reduction of landfill. (S2O-2631)

6. Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met Scottish Natural Heritage and what issues were discussed. (S2O-2579)

7. Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to encourage diversification in the use of forests and timber. (S2O-2533)

8. Frances Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive what levels of radiation there are within a 20-mile radius of Faslane Nuclear Base. (S2O-2587)

9. Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what proposals it has to assist crofters in the development of the quality of their sheep. (S2O-2528)

10. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on the Organic Action Plan. (S2O-2565)

11. Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive how much it has allocated to assist communities in the buy-out of land since 1999. (S2O-2547)

12. Mark Ballard: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to protect the public against toxic chemicals in the environment. (S2O-2605)

Health and Community Care

1. Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the guaranteed waiting times in the Partnership Agreement will be met. (S2O-2590)

2. John Swinburne: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to address the issue of elder abuse in residential care homes and domestic settings. (S2O-2542)

3. Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive what its response is to the House of Commons Health Committee's report on obesity published on 27 May 2004 and what plans it has to combat obesity within Scotland. (S2O-2588)

4. Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to combat the health causes of long-term absence of pupils from school. (S2O-2562)

5. Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the recent report by Asthma UK which indicates that 35% of people with asthma expect improvement in how the NHS manages their asthma over the next five years. (S2O-2636)

6. Janis Hughes: To ask the Scottish Executive what improvements to the quality of service to patients will flow from the new GP contracts. (S2O-2592)

7. Colin Fox: To ask the Scottish Executive what powers it has to intervene if it is dissatisfied with food hygiene standards at the new Royal Infirmary of Edinburgh. (S2O-2556)

8. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has with regard to the impact of sleep disorders on accidents at work and on roads. (S2O-2516)

9. Tricia Marwick: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to retain dentists offering NHS treatment. (S2O-2564)

10. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether targets set in The Same as you? for the closure of residential units that provide residential care for those with profound learning difficulties remain in place and, where such targets have not been met, whether they will be withdrawn. (S2O-2509)

11. Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is supporting young carers. (S2O-2540)

12. John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in ensuring that the standard of medical cover is not affected by the new opt-out arrangement. (S2O-2525)

General

1. Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive what proportion of the electricity currently generated at Hunterston B, Cockenzie and Longannet will be replaced with electricity generated using renewable technologies by 2020, given the planned closures of these power stations by 2020. (S2O-2609)

2. Bristow Muldoon: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to support enhancement of passenger rail services on the Edinburgh to Glasgow via Shotts line. (S2O-2552)

3. Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive how funds recovered under the Proceeds of Crime Act 2002 will be allocated in Scotland. (S2O-2603)

4. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will monitor the effectiveness of the education maintenance allowance. (S2O-2570)

5. Chris Ballance: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it supports the proposed re-opening of small stations between Lockerbie and Carstairs on the west coast main line. (S2O-2611)

6. Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to make representations to Her Majesty's Government on the impact of rising petrol prices in rural Scotland. (S2O-2563)

7. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take to secure the delivery of textile courses at Heriot-Watt University's Borders Campus. (S2O-2517)

8. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will adopt a policy of universal broadband coverage for the south of Scotland. (S2O-2573)

9. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what rate of take-up there has been for the Scottish Community and Householder Renewables Initiative to date. (S2O-2513)

10. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive why it is seeking a new logo for Scotland and whether it will instigate a public debate to seek suggestions on the issue. (S2O-2550)

11. Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans any future realignment of the A90 at the Bridge of Muchalls in Aberdeenshire. (S2O-2523)

12. Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met Aberdeenshire Council and what issues were discussed. (S2O-2580)

13. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether drug and alcohol detoxification and rehabilitation facilities are available to all prisoners in Scottish Prison Service prisons. (S2O-2560)

14. Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with Fife Council about the nursery nurses' dispute. (S2O-2545)

15. Christine May: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making on partnership working with the Scottish Trades Union Congress and trade unions. (S2O-2601)
