Business Bulletin No. 124/2002

Monday 9 September 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. Each Monday the full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin together with motions previously lodged that are due to be debated that week.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-3366 Mr Brian Monteith: The Success of Scottish Hockey-That the Parliament recognises the outstanding success of Scottish hockey and congratulates the Scottish Under-18 Men's and Women's teams on their promotions to Division A of the European Youth Trophy, the Scotland Under-21 Men's and Women's teams for winning their respective Junior Celtic Cup trophies and the Scotland's Women's team for winning the Women's Celtic Cup; notes that both male and female senior teams have qualified for the European Nations Cup finals in Spain in 2003, and wishes them every success.

*S1M-3365# Mr John McAllion: Scottish Gulf War Veterans-That the Parliament recognises the deep concern over the health conditions of Scottish Gulf War veterans and believes that the Scottish Executive should recognise their illness and ensure that they receive the appropriate treatment.

Supported by: Dennis Canavan*, Dorothy-Grace Elder*, John Farquhar Munro*, Elaine Smith*, Tommy Sheridan*, Alex Neil*, Ms Sandra White*, Tricia Marwick*, Mr Lloyd Quinan*, Tavish Scott*, Ms Margo MacDonald*, Dr Winnie Ewing*

*S1M-3364 Nora Radcliffe: Iraq-That the Parliament notes with concern the current situation in Iraq; acknowledges that there is, as yet, no firm evidence that military intervention in Iraq at this stage would have the support of the British public; believes that any military action in Iraq should not take place without a debate in the House of Commons followed by a substantive vote by MPs; further believes that the first priority of Her Majesty's Government must be to ensure the return of the United Nations (UN) weapons inspectors under UN auspices; considers that any further steps in relation to Iraq must be consistent with the rule of international law, and urges the Prime Minister to recall the UK Parliament as soon as he returns from the Camp David summit with President Bush, so that the international situation can be discussed in detail.

*S1M-3363# Ms Sandra White: Industrial Relations at Central College of Commerce, Glasgow-That the Parliament notes with concern the poor industrial relations situation at the Central College of Commerce, Glasgow, and, in particular, the sacking of Mr Jim O'Donovan, a lecturer at the college, for alleged gross misconduct; further notes the deep concerns relating to Mr O'Donovan's dismissal with many believing that the charges brought against him were incompetent, and believes that Mr O' Donovan should be reinstated immediately and that the Scottish Executive should undertake an investigation into the situation at the college.

*S1M-3362# Maureen Macmillan: World Alzheimer's Day - 20 September 2002 -That the Parliament notes the on-going campaign by Alzheimer Scotland - Action on Dementia, to raise awareness about the early symptoms of dementia and, in particular, World Alzheimer's Day, which will provide a focal point of activity throughout the world; further notes that Alzheimer's disease is the most common cause of dementia; recognises the dedicated and valuable work of carers and organisations in seeking to support people with dementia, and believes that the Scottish Executive and MSPs should (a) ensure that the needs of the people with dementia are addressed by government departments and committees of the Parliament and promoted among the wider Scottish society and (b) monitor the progress of legislation in order to ensure that local authorities and the NHS are working together to recognise, assess and support people with dementia and their carers.

S1M-3361 - Withdrawn

S1M-3360 Mr Brian Monteith: Doors Open Day 2002-That the Parliament welcomes the Doors Open Days 2002 initiative taking place in September 2002, co-ordinated by the Scottish Civic Trust, which gives people the opportunity to visit and explore for a day, free of charge, various buildings across Scotland such as castles, stately homes and law courts, many of which are not normally open to the public, and believes the initiative gives people the opportunity to improve their understanding of their local architectural environment and the country's rich built heritage.

Supported by: Mr Keith Harding, Murdo Fraser

S1M-3359 Mr John McAllion: Safe Child Scheme-That the Parliament welcomes the Safe Child Scheme launched in Milton of Craigie Retail Park, Dundee; notes that the use of contact cards allows a lost child to be reunited with his or her parents quickly, and further notes that this is part of a wider scheme aimed at providing a safe environment for the community in the east end of Dundee.

S1M-3357# Mary Scanlon: Importance of Primary Care-That the Parliament recognises the importance of primary care in managing 90% of patient contact with the NHS in Scotland; appreciates the vital role GPs play in providing primary care services as part of the wider primary care team; supports the statement that "if it can be done in primary care, it should be done in primary care", and acknowledges the urgent need for reform and support to invigorate staffing levels, facilities, IT provision and morale for this to become a reality.

S1M-3356 Ms Margo MacDonald: Drug Testing Regime of the International Olympic Committee-That the Parliament urges the International Olympic Committee to adopt a more rigorous regime that will conclusively and unambiguously identify methamphetamine in tests for banned substances carried out on athletes to ensure that no athlete is automatically banned from competing with the subsequent loss of reputation, earnings and, in the case of Alain Baxter, an Olympic medal which was won fairly and without the use of a performance-enhancing drug, as objective scientific opinion now verifies.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie, Mr Adam Ingram*, Mr Gil Paterson*, Ms Sandra White*, Mr Lloyd Quinan*, Brian Fitzpatrick*, Nora Radcliffe*

S1M-3355 Trish Godman: Erskine Hospital-That the Parliament congratulates the management and staff at Erskine Hospital for the creation of the excellent Erskine Bursary Scheme which, in co-operation with other care providers, will ensure that former servicemen and women will receive first-class care in care homes near to their families, friends and communities and believes that Erskine Hospital stands alongside the very best of care providers for service veterans and sets a benchmark for such services in relation to those who have served in the armed forces and who in retirement deserve no less.

S1M-3354# Christine Grahame: Crisis in Borders Public Sewerage System-That the Parliament notes with concern that there is no further capacity in water waste treatment works in some towns and villages in the Scottish Borders such as Duns, Earlston, Lauder, Newtown St Boswells, Stow, Peebles and West Linton; further notes that, as a consequence, developments are being delayed or restricted and that Scottish Water's current investment programme will be unable to resolve some of these issues until, for instance, 2004-05 in Peebles and 2005-06 in Earlston, and considers that the Scottish Executive should, as a matter of urgency, convene a meeting with representatives of Scottish Borders Council, the Chief Executive of Scottish Water, the Water Commissioner for Scotland and relevant MSPs.

Supported by: Ms Sandra White, Michael Matheson, Shona Robison, Fiona Hyslop, Alex Neil, Mr Kenneth Gibson

S1M-3353 Mr Andy Kerr: The Scottish Public Services Ombudsman Act 2002 (Amendment) Order 2002-That the Transport and the Environment Committee recommends that the Scottish Public Services Ombudsman Act 2002 (Amendment) Order 2002 be approved.

Supported by: Peter Peacock

S1M-3352# Tavish Scott: Support for Crofting in the Highlands and Islands-That the Parliament notes the importance of crofting to the Highlands and Islands; welcomes the publication of the White Paper on crofting reform and encourages crofters to participate in the consultation now underway; further notes the level of agricultural returns to crofting businesses and supports the agri-environmental focus that the Environmentally Sensitive Area scheme has brought to many crofting counties, and encourages continuing support to crofting through such measures as the Crofting Counties Agricultural Grants scheme.

Supported by: Mr Jamie Stone, John Farquhar Munro, Donald Gorrie

S1M-3349 Mr Andy Kerr: Public Appointments and Public Bodies etc. (Scotland) Bill: Financial Resolution-That the Parliament, for the purposes of any Act of the Scottish Parliament resulting from the Public Appointments and Public Bodies etc. (Scotland) Bill, agrees to any increase in the sums payable out of the Scottish Consolidated Fund in consequence of the Act.

Supported by: Peter Peacock

S1M-3348# Dennis Canavan: Opposition to Invasion of Iraq-That the Parliament is opposed to the invasion of Iraq by USA forces and believes that the Prime Minister should try to dissuade President Bush from such action, which would do incalculable damage to the cause of peace in the Middle East and would almost certainly destroy the lives of many innocent Iraqi people who are victims of the oppressive regime of Saddam Hussein.

Supported by: Ms Margo MacDonald, Elaine Smith, Robin Harper, Mr John McAllion, Fiona McLeod, Donald Gorrie, Mr Adam Ingram

S1M-3347# Bill Butler: Iraq-That the Parliament believes that a war against Iraq would result in an enormous loss of innocent lives and could provoke a wider war in the Middle East; is of the view that public opinion, both at home and abroad, is overwhelmingly against such a war, and believes that the Scottish Executive should make representations to the Prime Minister to urge him to dissociate himself and Her Majesty's Government from military action against Iraq, which is not sanctioned by the United Nations Security Council.

Supported by: Fiona Hyslop, Elaine Smith, Donald Gorrie

S1M-3343 Elaine Smith: Abolition of the Record of Needs System-That the Parliament notes the genuine fears of parents and organisations over the proposed abolition of the Record of Needs system for children with special educational needs; expresses concern that the alternative proposal of a "Co-ordinated Support Plan" has not been clearly defined; believes that the "Co-ordinated Support Plan" will result in a reduction of legal rights for children and their parents; further expresses concern that local authorities are not required to carry out a fully comprehensive series of assessments and that "Co-ordinated Support Plans" can only be reviewed every three years, and proposes that consideration be given instead to retaining and improving the current Record of Needs System.

Supported by: Mr Adam Ingram, Mr Lloyd Quinan, Mr John Swinney, Mr John McAllion, Donald Gorrie, Alex Neil, Phil Gallie*

S1M-3340# Sarah Boyack: Unauthorised Transfer of Supplier by Energy Companies-That the Parliament notes with concern the number of complaints from energy consumers in Scotland who are erroneously transferred from their chosen energy supplier without their consent; recognises the distress and financial detriment this can cause; welcomes energywatch's Stop Now campaign, aimed at introducing solutions to this problem; further welcomes the action already taken by the Scottish Executive and Her Majesty's Government on this matter, and believes that it would be beneficial to consumers if a system is introduced which protects consumers from being transferred without their prior knowledge and explicit consent and that individuals affected by erroneous transfers should receive compensation from the energy supplier.

Supported by: Brian Fitzpatrick, Rhoda Grant, Mr John McAllion, Dr Sylvia Jackson, Bristow Muldoon, Cathie Craigie, Kate Maclean, Karen Gillon, Jackie Baillie, Bill Butler, Margaret Jamieson, Des McNulty, Scott Barrie, Elaine Smith, Elaine Thomson, Paul Martin, George Lyon, Trish Godman, Donald Gorrie, Helen Eadie, Mr Kenneth Macintosh*, Ian Jenkins*

S1M-3339 Dorothy-Grace Elder: Thanks to Billy Connolly-That the Parliament joins with the people of Glasgow's East End in expressing profound thanks to Mr Billy Connolly for his magnificent effort in raising funds for the East End flood victims; is mindful of the time he gave up for free to play the SECC on three successive nights to packed audiences and how his gesture raised not only funds but also the hearts of people in Glasgow left with no possessions after devastating flooding, and salutes this international star as a true son of Glasgow, who returned to his roots at the moment intervention was most needed.

Supported by: Mr David Davidson, Paul Martin, Mr John McAllion, Brian Fitzpatrick

S1M-3337 Malcolm Chisholm: The Food Protection (Emergency Prohibitions) (Amnesic Shellfish Poisoning) (West Coast) (No.10) (Scotland) Order 2002-That the Health and Community Care Committee, in consideration of The Food Protection (Emergency Prohibitions) (Amnesic Shellfish Poisoning) (West Coast) (No.10) (Scotland) Order 2002, recommends that the order be approved.

Supported by: Mr Frank McAveety, Mrs Mary Mulligan

S1M-3336 Malcolm Chisholm: The Food Protection (Emergency Prohibitions) (Amnesic Shellfish Poisoning) (West Coast) (No.11) (Scotland) Order 2002-That the Health and Community Care Committee, in consideration of The Food Protection (Emergency Prohibitions) (Amnesic) Shellfish Poisoning) (West Coast) (No.11) (Scotland) Order 2002, recommends that the order be approved.

Supported by: Mr Frank McAveety, Mrs Mary Mulligan

S1M-3335 Malcolm Chisholm: The Food Protection (Emergency Prohibitions) (Amnesic Shellfish Poisoning) (Orkney) (No.2) (Scotland) Order 2002-That the Health and Community Care Committee, in consideration of The Food Protection (Emergency Prohibitions) (Amnesic) Shellfish Poisoning) (Orkney) (No.2) (Scotland) Order 2002, recommends that the order be approved.

Supported by: Mr Frank McAveety, Mrs Mary Mulligan

S1M-3325# Mary Scanlon: Ferry Services to the Northern Isles-That the Parliament notes with concern the on-going situation surrounding the transfer of the contract for ferry links to the northern isles; recognises that these services provide a crucial lifeline for island communities and businesses, particularly the agriculture and fishing industries, through a substantial volume of passenger and freight transport, and considers that the Scottish Executive should ensure that ferry links between the mainland, Orkney and Shetland are maintained into the future.

S1M-3210# Cathie Craigie: Causing Death by Dangerous Driving-That the Parliament notes the publication of the report by the Transport Research Laboratory, Road Safety Research Report No.26, Dangerous Driving and the Law, which examines the use and application of the law on careless and dangerous driving throughout the UK; supports the report where it states that in Scotland a "clearer message regarding the seriousness of these offences would be sent to both defendant and society if all causing death by dangerous driving cases were tried in the High Court" rather than sheriff courts, and considers that the Scottish Executive should (a) commission specific Scottish research in this area to take on board the report's conclusions and recommendations and (b) take the necessary steps to ensure that dangerous driving offences are heard in the appropriate court and not downgraded.

Supported by: Sarah Boyack, Donald Gorrie, Nora Radcliffe, Tommy Sheridan, Mr Tom McCabe, Trish Godman, Karen Gillon, Mr Kenneth Macintosh, Jackie Baillie, Margaret Jamieson, Kate Maclean, Janis Hughes, Mr Michael McMahon, Dr Sylvia Jackson, Mr John McAllion, Johann Lamont, Helen Eadie, Phil Gallie, Irene Oldfather, Scott Barrie, Bill Butler, Linda Fabiani, Andrew Wilson, Mr Keith Raffan, Maureen Macmillan, Nicola Sturgeon, Mr Kenny MacAskill, Elaine Smith, Alex Fergusson, Elaine Thomson*
