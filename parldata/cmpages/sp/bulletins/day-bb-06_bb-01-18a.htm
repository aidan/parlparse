Section A – Today’s Business

Meetings of Committees

All meetings take place in the Scottish Parliament, unless otherwise specified. Contact details for Committee Clerks are provided at the end of the Bulletin.

Communities Committee
2nd Meeting, 2006

The Committee will meet at 9.30 am in Committee Room 6

1. Item in private: The Committee will consider whether to take item 6 in private.

2. Draft Scottish Planning Policy (SPP) 21: Green Belts: The Committee will take evidence on the Executive’s draft planning guidance on green belts from—

Johann Lamont MSP, Deputy Minister for Communities.

3. Planning etc. (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will take evidence at Stage 1 from—

Professor Alan Prior, School of the Built Environment, Heriot Watt University; and
Professor Greg Lloyd, School of Town and Regional Planning, University of Dundee.

4. New petitions: The Committee will consider the following petitions—

Petition PE813 by Ronnie McNicol, on behalf of Laighdykes Residents group; and
Petition PE771 by Olena Stewart and Petition PE821 by Sheena Stark on planning issues relating to change of usage of recreational and open spaces.

5. Draft Scottish Planning Policy (SPP) 21: Green Belts: The Committee will consider the evidence heard at agenda item 2.

6. Draft Scottish Planning Policy (SPP) 21: Green Belts - Witness expenses: The Committee will consider a claim under the witness expenses scheme.

3rd Meeting, 2006

Environment and Rural Development Committee

Public Petitions Committee
1st Meeting, 2006

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in Committee Room 1

1. New petitions: The Committee will consider the following new petitions—

PE910 Review of the study of Scottish history, literature and languages at primary, secondary and tertiary levels. Dr Donald Smith, Chair, Literature Forum for Scotland, will make a brief statement in support of his petition accompanied by Dr Alan McGillivray and Valerie Gillies.

PE911 Scottish Executive support for market-led urban regeneration projects and the operation of privatised urban regeneration companies. Paul Nolan, Chair, Craigmillar Community Council will make a brief statement in support of his petition accompanied by David Walker and Patsy King.

PE916 To seek to secure real rights for all in the planning system and the forthcoming Planning Bill. Anne McCall will make a brief statement in support of the petition accompanied by Douglas Murray and Stephen Hawkings.

PE917 To consider and debate the final report of the Independent Review of NHS 24.

PE918 To review the protection afforded to amenity woodland within the current planning system.

PE919 To consider the creation of a mechanism for an Inter-Parliamentary Petitions process between the Scottish Parliament and the UK Parliament.

2. Current petitions: The Committee will further consider the following current petitions—

PE504 To stop convicted murderers or members of their families from profiting from their crimes by selling accounts of their crimes for publication. The Committee will consider a response from the Home Office.

PE808 A specialist inpatient mental health unit for deaf and deafblind people. The Committee will consider a response from the Deputy Minister for Health.

PE764 To request the Post Office to consider sympathetically the needs of disabled and elderly persons in urban areas in Scotland. The Committee will consider responses from the Scottish Executive and the Post Office.

PE842 To review the use of chloramines disinfectant in the treatment of drinking water. The Committee will consider responses from the Scottish Executive; Scottish Water; the Drinking Water Quality Regulator for Scotland; the Scottish Centre for Infection and Environmental Health; Friends of the Earth and the Scottish Environment Protection Agency.

PE860 To develop a coherent national policy in relation to the teaching of singing. The Committee will consider responses from the Scottish Arts Council; VOCAL (Voice of Chief officers for Cultural, Community and Leisure Services); HM Inspectorate of Education; COSLA; the Scottish Executive; East Ayrshire Council; North Ayrshire Council and South Ayrshire Council.

PE798 The provision of wheelchairs and specialist seating services within the NHS. The Committee will consider responses from the petitioner and the Disability Rights Commission.

PE859 To upgrade the Southern Section of the A77 between Ayr and Stranraer including providing passing places every six miles and developing a bypass at Maybole. The Committee will consider responses from the Scottish Executive; the A77 Safety Group; the RAC; the AA; and the Royal Society for the Prevention of Accidents.

PE783 To support the establishment of the golden eagle as the national bird of Scotland. The Committee will consider a response from the Minister for Finance and Public Service Reform.

S2M-3826 Mr Tom McCabe: International Strategy—That the Parliament welcomes the Scottish Executive’s ambitions and activities to build international relationships that benefit Scotland and Scotland’s interests throughout the world; supports its promotion of Scotland as an ideal place to live, work, study and do business; commends the Executive’s encouragement and support for Scottish contributions to international development and other challenges facing the international community; acknowledges the importance of working with the UK Government to achieve these aims, and underlines the importance of responding to changing international circumstances to ensure the greatest possible gains for Scotland in all sectors.

The Presiding Officer has selected the following amendments

S2M-3826.1 Mr John Swinney: International Strategy—As an amendment to motion (S2M-3826) in the name of Mr Tom McCabe, leave out from “acknowledges” to end and insert “but regrets that on major issues of international policy, such as the war in Iraq or CIA rendition flights, the Executive is unable effectively to represent the views of the people of Scotland, and recognises that this will be possible only when Scotland plays a constructive role as an independent member of the international community.”

S2M-3826.2 Murdo Fraser: International Strategy—As an amendment to motion (S2M-3826) in the name of Mr Tom McCabe, leave out from “welcomes” to end and insert “notes the Scottish Executive’s efforts to promote Scotland’s international image and to encourage Scottish contributions to international development; welcomes the work of Scottish Development International in building on the success of Locate in Scotland; is disappointed to note that no minister has participated in any Scottish Council for Development and Industry trade mission since 1999; believes that a more competitive economy and better public services are crucial to making Scotland a more attractive place in which to live and invest, and acknowledges that the Executive must work closely with Her Majesty’s Government in order to improve the image of Scotland overseas.”

Debate on the subject of—           

S2M-3744 Christine May: Safer Communities and Neighbourhoods—That the Parliament recognises the many opportunities to improve community safety provided under the Antisocial Behaviour etc. (Scotland) Act 2004 and other initiatives, such as Safer Routes to School; congratulates community safety partnerships, such as that in Fife, for the innovative ways in which they have developed local schemes; notes that initiatives, such as Fife’s Time2Act Safer Neighbourhoods campaign, cover a wide range of community safety matters such as safety of older people, women’s personal safety, reduction in domestic violence, drink driving and drunk pedestrians, safety of children and young people, safer town centres and neighbourhoods and support for victims of crime and anti-social behaviour; acknowledges the work done by public sector staff, uniformed services and many members of local communities to develop and support such schemes; believes that there is further scope for developments in this area, and considers that all concerned should continue to work together to make Scotland’s communities safer.
