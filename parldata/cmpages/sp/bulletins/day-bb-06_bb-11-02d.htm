Business Bulletin No. 154/2006: Thursday 2 November 2006

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 9 November 2006

Question Time

General

1. Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to reduce noise pollution in town centres. (S2O-10971)

2. Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will reconsider its decision not to hold an inquiry into the Shirley McKie fingerprint case. (S2O-11038)

3. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in reducing the waiting times in NHS Lothian for a first eye appointment after referral. (S2O-10983)

4. Janis Hughes: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making in developing electronic health records for NHS patients in Scotland. (S2O-11018)

5. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether drugs for the treatment of Alzheimer’s disease will be available to people in the early and mild stages of the disease. (S2O-10981)

6. Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what importance it places on ensuring that medication is appropriately administered in care homes. (S2O-11035)

7. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive what action can be taken to improve employment opportunities for recently graduated physiotherapists, in light of concerns raised by the Chartered Society of Physiotherapy. (S2O-11023)

8. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to extend community warden schemes. (S2O-11037)

9. Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is aware of a shortage of lymphoedema practitioners in the Lothian area. (S2O-10972)

10. Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many individuals have been recalled to prison since 1999 following breaches of their licences. (S2O-10996)

Education and Young People, Tourism, Culture and Sport

1. David McLetchie: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to celebrate the 300th anniversary of the Act of Union in 2007. (S2O-10979)

2. Not Lodged

3. Not Lodged

4. Susan Deacon: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is working with local authorities to develop high quality, modern school buildings that meet the educational and physical activity needs of young people. (S2O-11012)

5. Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what financial assistance it is giving to voluntary organisations that encourage positive lifestyles for young people through sport. (S2O-10995)

6. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made following the summit on the music industry on 4 October 2006. (S2O-11029)

7. Cathie Craigie: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to expand the Schools of Ambition programme. (S2O-11011)

8. Mr Mark Ruskell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many schools built or planned under PPP will have biomass heating installed. (S2O-11043)

9. John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will co-operate with the Battle of Prestonpans (1745) Heritage Trust to seek to safeguard the site of the battlefield and to promote awareness of its local and national significance. (S2O-11017)

10. Gordon Jackson: To ask the Scottish Executive what evaluation has taken place of the Hungry for Success initiative since its introduction. (S2O-11019)

Finance and Public Services and Communities

1. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive what specific financial support is provided to Scotland’s small towns. (S2O-10984)

2. Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will review its policy in respect of housing stock transfer. (S2O-10978)

3. Mark Ballard: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it supports the Public and Commercial Services Union in its call for a moratorium on the relocation of public service jobs. (S2O-11040)

4. Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive when it now intends to publish the results of the review of local government finance. (S2O-10987)

5. Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what initiatives are being taken to improve cost-effectiveness and promote best value in public authorities. (S2O-11030)

6. Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to encourage local authorities to provide voluntary sector organisations with three-year funding settlements. (S2O-11008)

7. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will support the work of voluntary organisations over the next 12 months. (S2O-11007)

8. Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive what reports on or relevant to the 2007 spending review it has received from (a) internal sources, (b) Her Majesty’s Government and (c) external sources and when such reports will be published. (S2O-10973)

9. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what statistics it has on the number of migrant workers sleeping rough in Inverness. (S2O-11022)

10. Mr Charlie Gordon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many full-time staff are employed, and at what financial cost, in administering community planning partnerships in Scotland. (S2O-11016)
