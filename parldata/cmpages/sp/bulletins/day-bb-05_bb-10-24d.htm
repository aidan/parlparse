Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 27 October 2005

Question Time

General

1. Mr Stewart Maxwell: To ask the Scottish Executive what resources are in place to reduce the number of road deaths and injuries. (S2O-7816)

2. Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many anti-social behaviour orders have been granted in the last six months. (S2O-7886)

3. Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make representations to the UK Government for legislation requiring seatbelts to be provided on all school transport. (S2O-7854)

4. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made by the Scottish Local Authorities Remuneration Committee. (S2O-7871)

5. Not Lodged

6. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to monitor progress towards reaching its 2007 targets for maximum class sizes in primary and secondary schools. (S2O-7813)

7. Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it will provide to enable youth football coaches to obtain first aid training. (S2O-7889)

8. Roseanna Cunningham: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are being taken to support and encourage small rural businesses. (S2O-7845)

9. Jim Mather: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to maximise the economic and job opportunities that will flow from oil and gas exploration and exploitation on the west coast of Scotland in the next 10 years. (S2O-7835)

10. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made with the provision of access for disabled people at Haymarket Station in Edinburgh. (S2O-7858)

Health and Community Care

1. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what arrangements are in place to check the quality of administration in the NHS, such as contact between hospitals and GP practices in respect of individual patients. (S2O-7824)

2. Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made with its consultation on redrawing NHS boundaries in Argyll and Clyde. (S2O-7856)

3. Euan Robson: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in reducing the number of women who smoke while pregnant. (S2O-7828)

4. Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to increase the availability of dental services in Dumfries and Galloway. (S2O-7884)

5. Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it supports the continuance of acute care provision 24 hours a day, seven days a week, at the Belford Hospital, Fort William and whether it will provide the necessary support to the hospital, its staff and NHS Highland in enabling that objective to be achieved in the long term. (S2O-7848)

6. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it has made on the new contract for NHS dentistry. (S2O-7853)

7. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in delivering NHS dentistry in Scotland. (S2O-7873)

8. Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive what advice and guidance it has given to NHS Ayrshire and Arran with regard to its proposal to centralise emergency and unscheduled care services at Crosshouse Hospital. (S2O-7819)

9. Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive how many vacancies exist for junior physiotherapy posts. (S2O-7844)

10. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied with the progress of the Calder Advisory Group. (S2O-7879)

Environment and Rural Development

1. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will reopen the sprat fishery on the Firth of Forth. (S2O-7825)

2. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the latest position is in respect of sewage dumping at Auchlin and Upper Beoch in Ayrshire. (S2O-7808)

3. Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are being taken to ensure that Scottish Water makes significant improvements in its efficiency and performance, following recent reports that Scotland still lags behind England and Wales in respect of customer performance. (S2O-7859)

4. Bristow Muldoon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many jobs it anticipates will be created by a new salmon processing facility in Livingston. (S2O-7882)

5. Bill Aitken: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent assessment it has made of the threat to the UK poultry industry from avian flu. (S2O-7814)

6. Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive what advice its Environment and Rural Affairs Department gives to local authorities and communities concerning the environmental aspects of developing brown field sites. (S2O-7864)

7. Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to tackle any nuisance caused to neighbours by high hedges. (S2O-7876)

8. Helen Eadie: To ask the Scottish Executive what proposals it has for the disposal of sewage sludge and other municipal waste, in particular whether it has plans to offer further support to energy projects offering disposal solutions. (S2O-7861)

9. Chris Ballance: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied that SEPA has sufficient powers and resources to ensure the safe and legal disposal of sewage sludge to land. (S2O-7839)

10. Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive how plans are progressing for the disposal of sewage after December 2005. (S2O-7863)
