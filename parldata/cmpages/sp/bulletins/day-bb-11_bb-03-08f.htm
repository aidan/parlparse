Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally printed the day after they are lodged. If an amendment is lodged to a motion then the original motion will appear alongside the amendment, along with any support lodged that day, in this section.

Each Monday, this section also contains all motions and amendments lodged the previous week that are still live and any motions and amendments that have been lodged for debate in the coming week. A motion or amendment is live if it has not been debated, withdrawn or otherwise deleted from the list.

Where a motion or amendment has been withdrawn, it is indicated in this section the day after it has been withdrawn. Support for motions and amendments received after they are lodged is shown at the end of this section the day after such support is received.

Motions and amendments can be published with various symbols:

  • Asterisks before the motion or amendment number indicate a motion or amendment published for the first time;
  • Asterisks also identify alterations to the text of a motion or amendment made since it was first published;
  • A hash symbol identifies motions eligible for debate at Members’ Business;
  • A diamond symbol identifies motions lodged for Members’ Business that have not yet attracted the required cross-party support;
  • An "R", identifies motions or amendments in which the Member who lodged it has a registrable interest.

The Parliamentary Bureau periodically deletes motions or amendments not scheduled for debate and which are over six weeks old.

A full list of current motions is available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/motions.htm.

A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/index.htm.

Any questions in relation to this section should be directed to the Chamber Desk, for which contact details are provided at the end of the Bulletin.

New and altered motions and amendments

*S3M-8090 Mr Frank McAveety: Sporting Success for St Mungo’s—That the Parliament congratulates St Mungo’s Academy on being named Glasgow Secondary Sports School of the Year at the Evening Times Sports Person of the Year Awards; considers that the award is recognition of the important role that sport plays in the development of Scotland’s youngsters, and welcomes what it believes is the deserved recognition that this award bestows on the pupils and staff at St Mungo’s.

*S3M-8089 Bill Butler: Scottish Premier League Trust—That the Parliament commends the work of the Scottish Premier League (SPL) Trust, a standalone charity responsible for promoting sport, physical activity and positive lifestyle messages to as wide an audience as possible; welcomes the publication of the SPL Community Report 2011; congratulates the trust and SPL clubs on the invaluable community work that it considers that they undertake across Scottish society; understands that every week SPL clubs dedicate 6,600 hours to enable 23,600 individuals to take part in club projects such as the Futures Jobs Fund Programme and Football Fans in Training, and supports the trust’s statement that "Football has the power to bring communities together and change people’s lives for the better".

*S3M-8088 Gil Paterson on behalf of the Standards, Procedures and Public Appointments Committee: Changes to Standing Orders—That the Parliament agrees that, in giving effect to the standing order rule changes set out in Annexe A of the Standards, Procedures and Public Appointments Committee’s 10th Report 2010 (Session 3), Minor changes to Standing Orders (SP Paper 552), and 2nd Report 2011 (Session 3), Scrutiny of SPCB supported bodies (SP Paper 566), the words "the 2010 Act" be deleted where they first appear in chapter 3A and any other occurrences of the words "the 2010 Act" in that chapter be replaced with "the Scottish Parliamentary Commissions and Commissioners etc. Act 2010."

*S3M-8087 Pauline McNeill: Glasgow, a City of Culture—That the Parliament congratulates Glasgow on being named in a report as the biggest cultural powerhouse in the UK outside of London; believes that Glasgow’s creative industry has gone from strength to strength since being named European Capital of Culture in 1990; further acknowledges that the city’s cultural sector now employs more than 5,000 people, and looks forward to the ongoing development of what it considers this vital sector in the city.

*S3M-8086 Sarah Boyack: Edinburgh Fair Trade Awards—That the Parliament congratulates the winners of the 2011 Edinburgh Lord Provost Fair Trade Awards, presented to local cafes and eateries for commitment to ethical and fair trade products; recognises Yum Cafe, Fair Trade Cafe of Edinburgh University Chaplaincy, Wardie Parish Church, and Equal Exchange for what it considers to be their well-deserved awards, and commends all such initiatives that encourage fair and responsible trade in Scotland and abroad.

Supported by: Patricia Ferguson*, Bill Butler*, Mike Pringle*, James Kelly*, Robin Harper*, Trish Godman*, Marlyn Glen*, John Scott*, David Whitton*, Dr Richard Simpson*, Malcolm Chisholm*

*S3M-8085 James Kelly: The Criminal Legal Aid (Fixed Payments) (Scotland) Amendment Regulations 2011—That the Justice Committee recommends that nothing further be done under the Criminal Legal Aid (Fixed Payments) (Scotland) Amendment Regulations 2011 (SSI2011/162).

*S3M-8084 Malcolm Chisholm: Congratulations to Finola Education Trust—That the Parliament recognises Leith teenager, Finola Forman, who has cerebral palsy, has chosen a specialist residential further education course requiring her to travel to Nottingham; congratulates the Finola Education Trust on raising £30,000 towards the second year of the three year course; notes that this brings the trust’s contribution for the first two years to £66,300, in addition to contributions by the City of Edinburgh Council; congratulates Finola on raising £3,500 by selling her own artwork; notes that, as part of Contact a Family Scotland’s Brill-Art exhibition, Finola’s painting of Edinburgh Castle will be on display in the garden lobby on 8, 9 and 10 March 2011; acknowledges the support of many others in promoting the campaign, and regrets that teenagers, such as Finola, have to rely on extensive fundraising to fulfil such choices.

*S3M-8083 Stewart Maxwell: The Jewish Way of Life—That the Parliament welcomes the launch ofthe Jewish Way of Life educational resource about Judaism; considers this an excellent resource and congratulates the PearsFoundation for funding it; further congratulates Learning and Teaching Scotland for working with the Scottish Council of Jewish Communitiestomake this resource available inScotland, and urges all schools to investigate this new teaching aidin the recognitionthat education is key to fostering goodcommunity relations.

Supported by: Ken Macintosh*, Bill Wilson*, Mike Pringle*, Bill Kidd*, Jackson Carlaw*

*S3M-8082 Maureen Watt: Scotland’s Finest Woods Award—That the Parliament welcomes the launch of what is considered the prestigious Scotland’s Finest Woods Award 2011 including the new award sponsored by the Woodland Trust Scotland for new native woodland creation projects that demonstrate exemplary use of management techniques; encourages woodland owners and managers across Scotland to enter their best projects in the competition before the closing date of 21 April, and looks forward to the award day on 24 June at the Royal Highland Show.
