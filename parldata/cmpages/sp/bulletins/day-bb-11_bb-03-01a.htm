Business Bulletin No. 33/2011: Tuesday 1 March 2011
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section A – Today’s Business

Meetings of Committees

All meetings take place in the Scottish Parliament, unless otherwise specified. Contact details for Committee Clerks are provided at the end of the Bulletin.

Equal Opportunities Committee
4th Meeting, 2011

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in Committee Room 6

1. Forced Marriage etc. (Protection and Jurisdiction) (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will consider the Bill at Stage 2.

2. Subordinate legislation: The Committee will take evidence on the following draft affirmative instruments—

the Equality Act 2010 (Statutory Duties) (Scotland) Regulations 2011;

the Equality Act 2010 (Specification of Public Authorities) (Scotland) Order 2011;

from—

Alex Neil MSP, Minister for Housing and Communities, Yvonne Strachan, Head of Equality Unit, Graeme Bryce, Policy Co-ordinator, Equality Unit, and Allison Coull, Deputy Director (Legal), Constitutional and Civil Law Division, Scottish Government.

3. Subordinate legislation: Alex Neil MSP (Minister for Housing and Communities) to move the following motions—

S3M-07858 - That the Equal Opportunities Committee recommends that the Equality Act 2010 (Statutory Duties) (Scotland) Regulations 2011 be approved;

S3M-07857 - That the Equal Opportunities Committee recommends that the Equality Act 2010 (Specification of Public Authorities) (Scotland) Order 2011 be approved.

Justice Committee
7th Meeting, 2011

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in Committee Room 1

1. Declaration of interests: John Lamont MSP will be invited to declare any relevant interests.

2. Choice of Convener: The Committee will choose a new Convener.

3. Decisions on taking business in private: The Committee will decide whether to take item 10 in private. The Committee will also decide whether its consideration of a draft legacy paper and draft reports on the instruments being considered under items 4 to 7 should be taken in private at future meetings.

4. Subordinate legislation: The Committee will take evidence on the following affirmative instruments—

the Sexual Offences Act 2003 (Remedial) (Scotland) Order 2011 (SSI2011/45);

the draft Proceeds of Crime Act 2002 Amendment (Scotland) Order 2011;

the draft Legal Profession and Legal Aid (Scotland) Act 2007 (Modification and Consequential Provisions) Order 2011;

the draft Scottish Charitable Incorporated Organisations (Removal from Register and Dissolution) Regulations 2011;

the draft Cross-Border Mediation (Scotland) Regulations 2011,

from—

Kenny MacAskill MSP, Cabinet Secretary for Justice;

Ian Fleming and Kerry Morgan, Police Division, John Nicholson, Organised Crime Unit, Colin McKay, James How and Lorna Gibbon, Legal System Division, Richard Dennis, Civil Law Division, Andrew Campbell, Carolyn Magill, Kevin Gibson, Fraser Gough, John St Clair and Graham Fisher, Scottish Government Legal Directorate, Scottish Government.

5. Subordinate legislation: Kenny MacAskill MSP (Cabinet Secretary for Justice) to move the following motions—

S3M-7832—That the Justice Committee recommends that the Sexual Offences Act 2003 (Remedial) (Scotland) Order 2011 (SSI2011/45) be approved;

S3M-7830—That the Justice Committee recommends that the Proceeds of Crime Act 2002 Amendment (Scotland) Order 2011 be approved;

S3M-7828—That the Justice Committee recommends that the Legal Profession and Legal Aid (Scotland) Act 2007 (Modification and Consequential Provisions) Order 2011 be approved;

S3M-7833—That the Justice Committee recommends that the Scottish Charitable Incorporated Organisations (Removal from Register and Dissolution) Regulations 2011 be approved; and

S3M-7887—That the Justice Committee recommends that the Cross-Border Mediation (Scotland) Regulations 2011 be approved.

6. Subordinate legislation: The Committee will take evidence on the Legal Aid and Advice and Assistance (Solicitors’ Travel Fees) (Scotland) Regulations 2011 (SSI2011/41) from—

Kenny MacAskill MSP, Cabinet Secretary for Justice;

Colin McKay and James How, Legal System Division, and Fraser Gough, Scottish Government Legal Directorate, Scottish Government.

7. Subordinate legislation: James Kelly MSP to move S3M-8012—

That the Justice Committee recommends that nothing further be done under the Legal Aid and Advice and Assistance (Solicitors’ Travel Fees) (Scotland) Regulations 2011 (SSI2011/41).

8. Subordinate legislation: The Committee will consider the following negative instruments—

the Scottish Charitable Incorporated Organisations Regulations 2011 (SSI2011/44);

Act of Sederunt (Fees of Sheriff Officers) 2011 (SSI2011/47);

the Scottish Crime and Drug Enforcement Agency (Scotland) Regulations 2011 (SSI2011/61); and

the Police Grant (Scotland) Order 2011 (SSI2011/62).

9. Double Jeopardy (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will consider the Bill at Stage 2.

10. Legacy paper: The Committee will consider its approach to a legacy paper.

11. Long Leases (Scotland) Bill (in private): The Committee will consider a revised draft Stage 1 report.

Finance Committee
8th Meeting, 2011

The Committee will meet at 2.00 pm in Committee Room 2

1. Subordinate legislation: The Committee will take evidence on the draft Budget (Scotland) Act 2010 Amendment Order 2011 from—

John Swinney MSP, Cabinet Secretary for Finance and Sustainable Growth, Stuart Dickson, Finance Co-ordination, and John Williams, Head of Finance Co-ordination, Scottish Government.

2. Subordinate legislation: John Swinney MSP (Cabinet Secretary for Finance and Sustainable Growth) to move S3M-07799—

That the Finance Committee recommends that the draft Budget (Scotland) Act 2010 Amendment Order 2011 be approved.

3. Public Bodies Bill (UK Parliament legislation): The Committee will take evidence on legislative consent memorandum LCM(S3) 32.1 from—

John Swinney MSP, Cabinet Secretary for Finance and Sustainable Growth, Keith Connal, Deputy Director, Public Bodies Policy Division, and Colin Miller, Public Bodies Policy Division, Scottish Government.

4. Decision on taking business in private: The Committee will decide whether its consideration of its legacy paper and its annual report should be taken in private at future meetings.
