Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-4982 Murdo Fraser: T in the Park Success at UK Festival Awards 2006—That the Parliament congratulates the organisers of T in the Park on winning "Best Major Festival" at the UK Festival Awards for the second year running; further congratulates the organisers of T in the Park for putting on the country’s largest and most successful music festival, putting on a spectacular festival for over 75,000 festival goers each day; applauds the festival organisers for making it the first festival to be "carbon neutral" and offset its carbon dioxide emissions by planting thousands of trees in three forestry projects in Scotland, Mexico and Uganda, making T in the Park the biggest carbon neutral festival in the world; welcomes the opportunity that T in the Park gives to new Scottish talent on the T Break Stage and Futures Stage; notes that T in the Park makes a massive contribution to the Scottish economy, bringing £7.5 million to the local economy in Perth and Kinross and another £10.5 million to Scotland’s tourism industry through worldwide media coverage, and wishes the organisers continued success with future T in the Park festivals.

Supported by: Mr Kenneth Macintosh*, Dave Petrie*

*S2M-4981 Bill Butler: Show Racism the Red Card - Fortnight of Action—That the Parliament welcomes Show Racism the Red Card’s (SRTRC) 3rd annual Fortnight of Action which takes place from 17 to 30 October 2006; notes its formal launch at an event held in the Parliament on Tuesday 17 October; applauds the campaign’s ambition to build on last year’s success when over 100 events involving minority ethnic groups, community organisations, all 42 professional clubs, football fans, schools and young people were organised nationwide, and commends the Scottish Executive, UNISON and the City of Edinburgh Council, in conjunction with SRTRC, for providing Action Grants which promote community involvement and partnership as part of the campaign to tackle racism and exclusion through football.

Supported by: Mrs Mary Mulligan*, Mr Kenneth Macintosh*, Mr Alasdair Morrison*, Michael McMahon*, Mr Charlie Gordon*, Janis Hughes*, Bruce Crawford*, Marlyn Glen*, Dr Elaine Murray*, Mr Kenny MacAskill*

*S2M-4980 Scott Barrie: ChildLine Scotland—That the Parliament congratulates ChildLine on its 20th birthday nationally and its 16 years in Scotland; supports the valuable counselling and outreach work which is offered so that children have a place to turn for advice and support; recognises that over the course of its 16 years ChildLine Scotland has counselled 370,700 children and advised around 15,000 adults; acknowledges that the service would not happen without the expertise and time commitment of more than 1,300 volunteers, and calls for people to support ChildLine in Scotland as it goes from strength to strength.

*S2M-4979 Margaret Smith: Edinburgh Park Station—That the Parliament notes that Edinburgh Park rail station, which serves Scotland’s largest and most successful business park and the fourth largest economic area outside the centres of Edinburgh, Glasgow and Aberdeen, has been consistently denied a stop on the Edinburgh to Glasgow mainline; considers that this deficiency severely and unnecessarily restricts employees and visitors from across Scotland travelling by train to Edinburgh Park; notes that research has shown a clear desire for the provision of this service among the 15,000 financial sector employees currently working in and around Edinburgh Park and that providing a mainline stop at Edinburgh Park could help reduce congestion by removing significant numbers of cars from the road, strengthen the links between the Edinburgh and Glasgow business communities and help safeguard west Edinburgh’s long-term economic growth, and calls for the Scottish Executive and Transport Scotland to push for the rescheduling of mainline trains from Edinburgh to Glasgow to stop at Edinburgh Park at least twice per hour at peak times.

*S2M-4978 Brian Adam: Plight of Farepak Customers—That the Parliament notes and condemns the actions of the Farepak Christmas hamper firm in continuing to accept money from customers, despite its precarious financial position; expresses concern for those customers who will not be able to retrieve all of their lost savings; urges agents and customers of Farepak to lodge their claims for compensation with the firm’s administrators, and calls on the Scottish Executive to make representations to the UK Government to tighten up consumer protection measures so that this cannot happen again.

*S2M-4977 Ms Rosemary Byrne: Support for the Lindsay Armstrong Support Group—That the Parliament congratulates the Lindsay Armstrong Support Group on its work; notes that the group offers free and confidential support to victims of rape and sexual assault and their families, regardless of age or gender, including practical and emotional support through the criminal justice process and anonymous 24-hour telephone and web-based support networks; is concerned that this group is currently suffering financial problems, and therefore calls on the Scottish Executive to ensure that adequate funding is made available to allow the group to carry out its much-needed work nationwide.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan*, Campbell Martin*

*S2M-4976 Ms Rosemary Byrne: Aberfan Anniversary—That the Parliament notes the 40th anniversary of the disaster in Aberfan on 21 October 1966 when 144 people were killed, 116 of them children; further notes that the tragedy happened at 9.15 am as the children of Pantglas Junior School were about to start their lessons when a coal slag heap started to shift and engulfed some terraced cottages, a farm and the school; recognises that a generation of children was wiped out, and expresses its sympathy to the people of Aberfan who have borne this loss.

Supported by: Mr Kenneth Macintosh*, Murray Tosh*, Dr Elaine Murray*, Campbell Martin*, Tommy Sheridan*, John Swinburne*, Phil Gallie*

*S2M-4975 Ms Rosemary Byrne: Congratulations to LGBT Youth Scotland—That the Parliament congratulates LGBT Youth Scotland on its work in tackling homophobic bullying in schools; notes that this campaign raises awareness of inequality and injustice for LGBT youngsters in schools and promotes acceptance and tolerance amongst children and young people, not just for the LGBT community but for all of Scotland’s children and young people, and calls on all organisations working with young people, including the established churches, to recognise and support the work of LGBT Youth Scotland.

Supported by: Campbell Martin*, Tommy Sheridan*

*S2M-4974 Mr Brian Monteith: Barnardo’s Youthbuild Service—That the Parliament commends the Barnado’s Youthbuild service for addressing issues at the very foundation of social exclusion as it helps young people to overcome obstacles of homelessness and the impact of poverty and to gain employment through extensive personal support and appropriate industry training, and welcomes the invitation to a young woman from Youthbuild to the Parliament to lead Time for Reflection on Wednesday 25 October 2006 at 9.00 am to tell how she has left behind her label of "not in employment, education or training" and whose story of taking control of her life is a positive example of what can be achieved.

*S2M-4973 Mr Brian Monteith: Free Maps for 11-Year-Olds—That the Parliament commends Ordnance Survey (OS) and its "Free Maps for 11-year-olds" programme which offers a free OS Explorer map to every Scottish pupil in Primary 7 through their school, simply by registering online, and notes that this raises awareness of local geography and encourages further inquiry and learning, while introducing many aspects of citizenship, and also builds on the programme’s past success in issuing over 60,000 maps to eligible schools in 2005.

*S2M-4972 Tommy Sheridan: Glasgow - Health Deprived City—That the Parliament expresses grave concern over the recent publication of Scottish Index of Multiple Deprivation 2006: General Report which states that Glasgow City contains over one third, 35%, of the 15% most health-deprived data zones in Scotland; notes also that, within local authority areas, almost half of Glasgow’s data zones are in the 15% most health-deprived data zones; further notes with great concern that the NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde area, which is the largest NHS board area, contains almost 50% of the 15% most health-deprived data zones, and urges the Scottish Executive to address the situation as a matter of urgency and acknowledge that these figures are an indicator that Glasgow citizens are still suffering from multiple deprivation.

Supported by: Ms Rosemary Byrne*

*S2M-4971 Tommy Sheridan: Glasgow - Education Deprived City—That the Parliament expresses grave concern over the recent publication of Scottish Index of Multiple Deprivation 2006: General Report which states that Glasgow City contains almost one third of the 15% most education-deprived data zones in Scotland and that the most education-deprived data zone in Scotland is Parkhead in Glasgow; notes with concern that Glasgow has four of the five most education-deprived data zones in Scotland, and calls on the Scottish Executive to act with urgency to improve education for the pupils of Glasgow City, to invest in teacher training and to reduce class sizes to no more than 20 pupils.

Supported by: Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Colin Fox*

*S2M-4970 Tommy Sheridan: Glasgow - Deprivation City—That the Parliament expresses grave concern over the recent publication of Scottish Index of Multiple Deprivation 2006: General Report which states that Glasgow City has one in four income-deprived people which totals 142,915 people; notes that Glasgow has the greatest concentration of income deprivation with the highest proportion, 48%, and number, 331, of Scotland’s 15% most income-deprived data zones; notes that over 70% of income-deprived people in Glasgow live in the 15% most income-deprived areas in Scotland, and considers that this is a sad indictment of the city which was once the industrial capital of Scotland and that Glasgow is becoming a tale of two cities with a huge underclass of the poor, unemployed and underemployed.

*S2M-4969 Donald Gorrie: Targeting of Alcohol Treatment Services—That the Parliament agrees to press for more and better-focused information to be provided about people currently engaging with alcohol treatment agencies and about these agencies and their services in order to enable future services to be targeted to deal with the unmet demand for services relating to alcohol.

*S2M-4968 Donald Gorrie: Treatment for Alzheimer’s Patients—That the Parliament would welcome the opportunity to take part in a full and widespread discussion of problems relating to Alzheimer’s disease and similar conditions, together with the Scottish Executive, medical professionals, health and social work managers and sufferers from Alzheimer’s and those caring for them, in order to seek out a consistent and rational approach to treating such conditions.

*S2M-4967 Mrs Nanette Milne: Congratulations to United Auctions Livestock Mart, Huntly—That the Parliament congratulates United Auctions livestock mart at Huntly on the recent extension and development of improved customer and staff facilities at the mart, including a 100-seat cafe; notes that the mart attracts buyers and sellers from across the north east of Scotland, as well as a growing number from the Black Isle and Caithness, and further notes that this investment is a welcome step forward with the potential to further develop the farming industry in the north east and provide farmers with a local choice for buying and selling livestock. 

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Dave Petrie*, Murdo Fraser*, Mr Jamie McGrigor*, Brian Adam*, Mr Ted Brocklebank*, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton*, Ms Maureen Watt*, Alex Fergusson*, Bill Aitken*, Shiona Baird*, Mr Brian Monteith*

*S2M-4966 Ms Rosemary Byrne: Clean Coal Technology—That the Parliament notes the announcement by British Energy of the imminent shut down of the nuclear power station at Hunterston in Ayrshire; welcomes such a move and hopes that this will be followed by an announcement of the closure of all nuclear facilities in Scotland; calls on the Scottish Executive to ensure that British Energy undertakes a full site-decontamination process, and further calls on the Executive to take this opportunity to initiate a programme of power production from clean coal technology as part of an energy mix that creates and maintains jobs in both the opencast and farming sectors while meeting Scotland’s commitment under the Kyoto Protocol.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan*

*S2M-4965 Ms Rosemary Byrne: Help for Farepak Customers—That the Parliament notes with grave concern that Farepak, the Christmas hamper firm, has ceased trading, with no warning to customers and that Farepak states that customers may receive some money back, but not all; further notes that many of Farepak’s customers are on low incomes and have been making regular payments to the company throughout 2006, up to and including September 2006, and that the closure leaves many of them in financial difficulties, and calls on the Scottish Executive to undertake a full inquiry into the situation and to urge Farepak’s liquidators, as a priority, to repay all customers in full.

Supported by: Richard Lochhead*, Tommy Sheridan*, Campbell Martin*, Colin Fox*

*S2M-4964 Mike Pringle: Anna Politkovskaya: Journalist and Author—That the Parliament notes with deep sorrow and concern the assassination of the internationally recognised journalist and author, Anna Politkovskaya, in Moscow on 7 October 2006 and sends condolences to her family; notes the significant and long-lasting contribution Anna Politkovskaya made to the development of democracy and the advancement of human rights in Russia through her great courage, often matched with considerable personal sacrifice, risk-taking and dogged insistence in never compromising the fundamental ideals of strengthening free speech and a free press; recognises that Anna Politkovskaya touched the hearts of many when she spoke up on behalf of those without a voice, including in 2005 when she was welcomed to Edinburgh as a speaker at the Edinburgh International Book Festival; notes that her personal integrity and commitment to the truth was recognised in 2004 through the award of the Olof Palme Prize, and sends a message of support and encouragement to the colleagues and friends of Anna Politkovskaya that they should continue their brave work to advance the development of democracy in Russia.

*S2M-4963 Ms Sandra White: Lebanon Visit—That the Parliament extends its support to those, including MSPs from many different parties, who are currently in Lebanon in an attempt to better understand the situation in the country through meeting the government and touring the worst-hit areas of the country; wishes them all the success possible, and recognises that these types of venture serve to boost Scotland’s standing on the world stage by demonstrating the values of collaboration and understanding that we wish to promote.

Supported by: Christine Grahame*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Jim Mather*, Chris Ballance*, Shiona Baird*

*S2M-4962 Dr Jean Turner: BackCare Awareness Week, 16 to 21 October 2006—That the Parliament commends all organisations involved in supporting people who develop and live with back pain, including BackCare which organises the annual health awareness campaign to ensure that the nation has healthy backs; recognises that two out of five adults will develop back pain in the next twelve months, costing businesses money in terms of days lost off work and costing the NHS a large portion of its budget for prescription painkillers and often for anti-depressants; further recognises that some sufferers may require to be reassessed by chronic pain clinicians; encourages employers to complete display screen equipment (DSE) checks on computer equipment and chairs for users who can frequently develop back complaints, and encourages everyone to develop and maintain good posture at all times to alleviate potential future back problems.

Supported by: Christine Grahame*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Tommy Sheridan*, Mr Stewart Maxwell*, Stewart Stevenson*, John Swinburne*, Ms Maureen Watt*, Mr Jamie Stone*, Rob Gibson*, Chris Ballance*, Shiona Baird*

*S2M-4961 Scott Barrie: Munros for All—That the Parliament notes the 150th anniversary of the birth of Sir Hugh Thomas Munro who was the first person to chart Scotland’s hills and mountains over 3,000 feet; further notes that the Land Reform (Scotland) Act 2003 makes it easier than ever to enjoy Scotland’s natural heritage; congratulates the roughly 3,500 people who have "bagged" every Munro, and encourages Scots and non-Scots alike to enjoy the Munro experience.

*S2M-4960 Ms Rosemary Byrne: Protection for Rural and Special Schools—That the Parliament notes with concern that the Scottish Executive has closed 64 rural and 23 special schools since 1999; further notes that rural and special schools foster an ethos of inclusion and sense of community which is advantageous to the intellectual and social development of children; believes that children learn better in smaller class sizes; calls therefore for smaller class sizes of no more than 20 children, and further calls on the Executive to initiate a presumption against closure of rural and special schools until an analysis has been undertaken of the needs and future needs of the local communities, including any present and future housebuilding and a projection of numbers of children likely to access local schools.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan*, Chris Ballance*

*S2M-4959 Nora Radcliffe: Grampian Police Force—That the Parliament congratulates Grampian Police for the high level of praise afforded it by HM Inspectorate of Constabulary; notes the commendation in the inspectorate’s report of its good working practice with local authorities and community groups in areas such as addressing alcohol-related violence, and asks the Scottish Executive to examine the recommendation of using this area of good practice as a template for all police boards and local authorities when dealing with alcohol-related disorders.

*S2M-4958 Tommy Sheridan: Local Meat for Glasgow—That the Parliament notes that Glasgow City Council’s meat contract, worth an estimated £508,000 annually, is currently out to tender; recognises that food miles now account for nearly 30% of goods transported on our roads and that this travel adds substantially to the carbon dioxide emissions that are contributing to climate change; notes that a recent report by the Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs states that food miles rose by 15% between 1992 and 2002; accordingly calls on Glasgow City Council to ensure that meat purchased has been sourced locally and organically reared, and further calls on all local authorities to source locally produced organic meat to reduce environmental costs.

Supported by: Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Chris Ballance*

*S2M-4957 Ms Rosemary Byrne: Local Foods for Local Kids—That the Parliament notes the success of the local food procurement scheme in East Ayrshire, whereby local primary schools were able to serve healthy, nutritious meals prepared using locally produced foods; recognises that food miles now account for nearly 30% of goods transported on our roads and that this travel adds substantially to the carbon dioxide emissions that are contributing to climate change; notes that a recent report by the Department for the Environment, Food and Rural Affairs states that food miles rose by 15% between 1992 and 2002; further notes that the use of locally produced foods adds a further market for local farmers, while educating children about farming and environmental issues; notes that under EU tendering regulations procurement officers are not permitted to state that they want only local produce, and accordingly calls on the Scottish Executive to seek a derogation from these regulations to ensure that local authorities can purchase locally produced foods.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan*, Chris Ballance*

*S2M-4956 Colin Fox: Need for Investment in Football Facilities—That the Parliament congratulates the national football team for its excellent start to the Euro 2008 qualifiers, notwithstanding the defeat in Kiev, and wishes the squad all the best in the remaining matches; believes that youth development provides the lifeblood for future success at club and international level but observes that much of our sporting facilities at local level across Scotland are in an awful state of disrepair, as most recently highlighted in Edinburgh; recognises the vital contribution which volunteers, who give up their time to run thousands of clubs across Scotland, make to the success of football in Scotland but notes the dispiriting environment in which they often find themselves working in pavilions, changing rooms and other facilities which are not up to scratch, and believes that the Scottish Executive must recognise the vital role that youth football plays and, for the future of the game, must invest in new or renovated facilities to sustain football at all stages from soccer sevens through to professional level.

*S2M-4955 Mr Ted Brocklebank: Fife Constabulary—That the Parliament commends the efforts of the officers of Fife Constabulary in providing security for the vitally important multi-party talks on the future government of Northern Ireland which finished in St Andrews on 13 October 2006; also commends Fife police for their successful stewarding of the Dunhill Cup; believes that, following their work on providing security for the G8 summit, policing the Open golf championship and Prince William’s graduation last year, Fife police are building on their reputation for the successful policing of large events, and further believes that the people of Fife are very fortunate to be served by such a professional and dedicated force.

S2M-4876*#* Mr John Swinney: Threat to the Rural Post Office Network in Scotland—That the Parliament notes the public concern over the future of the rural post office network in Perthshire, Angus and other parts of rural Scotland; notes that the UK Department of Trade and Industry (DTI) provides a subsidy to the rural post office network in Scotland that is scheduled to be removed in 18 months’ time; notes that, while the DTI provides this subsidy, other UK government departments such as the Department for Work and Pensions, the Department for Transport and the Department for Culture, Media and Sport are taking decisions that reduce the volume and value of transactions that can be undertaken at post offices, thereby damaging the profitability of these post offices; recognises that if the rural post office network is not supported there will be severe economic loss and loss of amenity in countless communities in Perthshire, Angus and rural Scotland, and considers that the Scottish Executive should make representations to the UK Government to provide a stable level of support that guarantees the viability of the rural post office network.
