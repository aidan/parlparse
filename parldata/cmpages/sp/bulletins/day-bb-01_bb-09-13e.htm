Business Bulletin No. 129/2001

Thursday 13 September 2001

Section E : Written questions lodged on 12 September 2001
  

S1W-18082 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive when its policy of zonal aggregated public sector procurement for telecommunications, as set out in Connecting Scotland: our broadband future, will first allow access for small- and medium-sized enterprise in rural areas to broadband facilities at prices comparable to that of Asymmetric Digital Subscriber Line in cities.

S1W-18083 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive what minimum technical service standards, in respect of (a) upstream bandwidth, (b) downstream bandwidth, (c) network latency, (d) internet protocol standard, (e) target availability per annum, (f) mean time between failure and (g) mean time to repair, its policy of zonal aggregated public sector procurement for telecommunications, as set out in Connecting Scotland: our broadband future, will deliver.

S1W-18084 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive when its policy of zonal aggregated public sector procurement for telecommunications, as set out in Connecting Scotland: our broadband future, will come into operation in rural areas outwith South of Scotland and Highlands and Islands.

S1W-18085 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive when its policy of zonal aggregated public sector procurement for telecommunications, as set out in Connecting Scotland: our broadband future, will deliver access to broadband facilities in the Banff & Buchan parliamentary constituency.

S1W-18086 Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive when its policy of zonal aggregated public sector procurement for telecommunications, as set out in Connecting Scotland: our broadband future, will deliver for private businesses bandwidths of at least eight megabits per second downstream and at least 512 kilobits per second upstream in under half a second for internet protocol serviced connections to the internet.

S1W-18087 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make representations to Her Majesty's Government that infectious salmon anaemia and viral haemorhagic septicaemia should be added to the list of diseases contained in Article 3 of EC Decisions 90/424/EEC.

S1W-18088 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what new steps it has taken or intends to take in response to the findings of the Enterprise and Lifelong Learning Committee’s Report on the Inquiry into Fuel Prices in Remote Rural Areas.

S1W-18089 Andrew Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive why lighting was active on Kincardine Bridge in daylight on the afternoon of 10 September 2001; on what dates and for what reason any similar situation has arisen since Amey Highways Ltd took over the maintenance contract from April 2001, and why the bridge’s "magic eye" system was not operational.

S1W-18090 Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive when the appeal by West of Scotland Water in June 1996 against the consents for discharges into the River Esk lodged by Solway River Purification Board on 28 March 1996 will be determined.

S1W-18091 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive what the roles and responsibilities of it and Her Majesty's Government are in deciding what is a sport or recreation.

S1W-18092 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive what process is followed designating an activity as a sport or a recreation.

S1W-18093 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list all officially recognised sports and recreations.

S1W-18094 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-16576 by Allan Wilson on 24 August 2001, what consultation sportscotland had with outside organisations before deciding not to make representations on recognising pigeon racing as a sport.

S1W-18095 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-16576 by Allan Wilson on 24 August 2001, what criteria were used to determine that pigeon racing is not a sport.

S1W-18096 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive what the role of sportscotland is in designating an activity as a sport.

S1W-18097 Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive what the official definition of a sport is.

S1W-18098 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how local loop unbundling will benefit the economy.

S1W-18099 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what the timetable is for local loop unbundling throughout Scotland.

S1W-18100 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will monitor local loop unbundling throughout Scotland.

S1W-18101 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make representations to Her Majesty’s Government in support of the establishment of a Scottish committee of the Office of Communications (OFCOM) and how it will monitor the work of OFCOM as it relates to Scotland.

S1W-18102 Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will ensure that Scotland’s interests are represented in the work of the Office of Communications.

S1W-18103 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive what action the Scottish Criminal Records Office intends to take with regard to the four fingerprint officers presently under suspension as a result of the Shirley McKie case.

S1W-18104 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will ask to make a ministerial statement to the Parliament on the continuing efforts by Shirley McKie to obtain compensation from Strathclyde Police following her acquittal on a charge of perjury at the High Court in Glasgow in May 1999.

S1W-18105 Brian Fitzpatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made on its commitment to address the rate of suicide amongst young men.

S1W-18106 Mr David Davidson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will give a substantive answer to question S1W-15829 lodged on 18 May 2001.

S1W-18107 Elaine Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to establish a Scottish Forestry Forum.
