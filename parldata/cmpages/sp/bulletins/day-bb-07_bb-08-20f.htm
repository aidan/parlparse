Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (♦).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S3M-370 Cathie Craigie: HMIE Inspection of Cumbernauld College—That the Parliament congratulates Cumbernauld College on its excellent HM Inspectorate of Education report published on 17 August 2007; notes that the report commends Cumbernauld College for its good learning and teaching processes, with its learners progressing and achieving more; praises the Serious About Learning initiative which has led to improvements across several key areas of college activity; welcomes the major transformation to Cumbernauld town centre that the main campus has achieved, and thanks the principal, senior staff, board of management and students for creating excellent learning environments in Cumbernauld town centre and at the East Dunbartonshire Campus of Further and Higher Education in Kirkintilloch.

*S3M-369 Bill Kidd: Single Equality Bill—That the Parliament expresses concern that the proposals by the Westminster Government to harmonise discrimination law, in order to produce a simpler framework, will not have the desired effect due to the reality that each group facing discrimination has different needs and that these differing needs will not be recognised by streamlined legislation; expresses further concern in relation to Inclusion Scotland’s fear that the proposal for the Single Equality Bill, which will allow public bodies to select aspects of equality to work on over a period of three years and also permit them to take cost into account, will result in potentially not addressing the needs of disabled people for several years; recognises that this could be a concern of all such organisations interested in equality for specific groups, and, while still encouraging a review of discrimination legislation, calls on Westminster to reverse its promotion of a Single Equality Bill in respect of anti-discrimination laws.

*S3M-368 Robin Harper: Wildlife Crime—That the Parliament, in light of the recent appalling wildlife crime events in Scotland, particularly the poisoning of a golden eagle, calls on the Scottish Executive to review urgently the provision of resources to combat wildlife crime, including education, surveillance, policing and scale of punishments, and to report back to the Parliament on progress.

*S3M-367 Rhona Brankin: Poisoning of Raptors—That the Parliament condemns the illegal poisoning of one half of the only breeding pair of golden eagles in the Borders, Scotland having seen too many incidents involving the illegal poisoning of birds of prey in the last few years; calls on the Scottish Executive to publish the times and exact locations of instances of illegal poisoning of wildlife, and further calls on the Executive to encourage owners to stand up and be counted and to condemn any instances of illegal wildlife poisoning that takes place on their land.

*S3M-366 David Whitton: East Dunbartonshire Social Work Service—That the Parliament congratulates the Intensive Support and Monitoring Service team of East Dunbartonshire Council Social Work Services on being awarded the 2007 Care Accolade from the Scottish Social Service Council for the initiatives that promote integrated working which improve services and notes that the services it provides on a daily basis are much valued by the people of Strathkelvin and Bearsden and should be given the highest praise.

*S3M-365 Robert Brown: High Speed Rail Links—That the Parliament notes the current protest in London against the UK Government’s proposal to develop a third runway at Heathrow Airport; believes that the development of high-speed rail links which will slash rail journey times between London, the north of England and Scotland in particular provides an attractive and sustainable alternative to many short-haul air flights within the United Kingdom, which may obviate the necessity for a third runway at Heathrow, and urges the government to develop and support proposals for such a high-speed rail link from London to Scotland as a matter of urgency.

Supported by: Liam McArthur*, Iain Smith*

*S3M-364 Alasdair Allan: Gaelic Films—That the Parliament welcomes the premiere of the first ever Gaelic feature film, Seachd: The Inaccessible Pinnacle, on Thursday 16 August 2007; notes that this is a great step forward for the Gaelic arts industry and Gaelic cinema; wishes the producers of the film success in their possible nomination for a foreign-language Oscar; believes that initiatives like this are needed to aid the long-term survival prospects of Gaelic as a spoken community language, and looks forward to many more similar Gaelic productions and initiatives in the future.

*S3M-363 Pauline McNeill: Dumbarton Road Corridor Environmental Trust—That the Parliament congratulates Dumbarton Road Corridor Environmental Trust Ltd on its successful application to the Big Lottery Fund for a Community Growing Assets grant and notes the sustained hard work done by many local individuals and organisations in the Scotstoun area of Glasgow to secure for the community a new facility which will act as both a community space for Scotstoun and a centre for the development of local peoples’ skills in conjunction with Anniesland and Clydebank colleges.

Supported by: Ken Macintosh*, Kenneth Gibson*, Trish Godman*, Des McNulty*, Jackie Baillie*, Hugh Henry*, Bob Doris*, Christopher Harvie*, Sandra White*

*S3M-362 Margaret Mitchell: World Rowing Under 23 Championships—That the Parliament congratulates the organisers on the success of the 2007 World Rowing Under 23 Championships held at Strathclyde Country Park; notes that the international rowing event, which was supported by North Lanarkshire Council and others such as Event Scotland and Glasgow 2014, attracted competitors from 50 countries across the globe and showcased the fantastic facilities at the park, and further notes that the park’s rowing course, a potential training location for the London 2012 Olympic Games, is built and maintained to FISA Grade A requirements and has been upgraded to Britain’s first Olympic-standard 8-lane 2,000 metre course.

*S3M-361 Cathy Jamieson: Gardening Leave—That the Parliament recognises the valuable work of the Gardening Leave initiative which provides horticultural therapy for clients of Combat Stress; welcomes this innovative pilot project, established at the Scottish Agricultural College, Auchincruive, which will help with the care and support of veterans by allowing them the opportunity to create, tend and maintain a garden at the site; commends the efforts of all those who have organised and developed Gardening Leave, and supports the excellent work undertaken by Combat Stress through the wide range of services and support.

*S3M-360 Bashir Ahmad: India and Pakistan, 60th Anniversary of Independence—That the Parliament congratulates India and Pakistan on the 60th anniversary of their independence from the British Empire; applauds both countries for their many successes over the last 60 years; notes the contribution of Indians and Pakistanis living in Scotland, and hopes that both countries continue to develop and strengthen their relationship with each other.

*S3M-359 Karen Gillon: Clydesdale Health Facilities—That the Parliament believes that there must be no delay in the building of the new community casualty unit and community hospital in Lanark; recognises that people in Clydesdale travel significant distances to access health services and that these new developments would bring these services much closer to local people, in line with the recommendations of the Kerr report, and calls on the Scottish Executive to ensure that any review of NHS Lanarkshire’s document, A Picture of Health, retains the previous commitment to build these facilities in Lanark.

Supported by: Sarah Boyack*, Pauline McNeill*, Iain Gray*, Ken Macintosh*, David Whitton*, Trish Godman*, Mr Frank McAveety*, Andy Kerr*, Jackie Baillie*, Marilyn Livingstone*, Hugh Henry*, Hugh O’Donnell*, Des McNulty*, Michael McMahon*

*S3M-358 Alasdair Allan: Scots Language Database—That the Parliament welcomes the creation of the Scottish Corpus of Texts and Speech website (www.scottishcorpus.ac.uk) by researchers at the University of Glasgow; notes that this website has more than four million words in Scots and Scottish English, includes texts from 1945 up to the present day and, as well as describing meaning and usage, the project also has audio links which allow people to hear the words being spoken, and recognises that this could be a vital resource for those interested in the Scots language the world over and that the Scots language is a source of interest across the world as one aspect of Scotland’s long and thriving cultural heritage.

The member has provided the following Scots translation:

*S3M-357 Robert Brown: Insight Radio—That the Parliament welcomes the work of Insight Radio (formerly VIP on Air), Europe’s first radio service for blind and partially sighted people; congratulates the consortium of funding organisations led by RNIB Scotland and including Glasgow City Council, the British Wireless for the Blind Fund, East Renfrewshire Council and South Lanarkshire Council on their support; notes that a high percentage of staff at the radio station are themselves visually impaired; welcomes the recent addition of a service on 101FM as well as digital, and looks forward to the station continuing to provide a high-class service for its listeners and acting as an exemplar of the potential contribution that can be made by blind and visually impaired employees.

Supported by: Bashir Ahmad*, Jim Hume*, Charlie Gordon*, Mike Pringle*, Pauline McNeill*, Stuart McMillan*, Ken Macintosh*, Robin Harper*, Bill Aitken*, Alasdair Allan*, Jim Tolson*, Bill Kidd*, Jackie Baillie*, Bob Doris*, Sandra White*, Bill Butler*, Ross Finnie*

*S3M-356 Sandra White: Proposed Regional Minimum Wage—That the Parliament, while acknowledging that one of New Labour’s flagship policies, the national minimum wage, as praised by Douglas Alexander MP in his maiden speech to the House of Commons saying "when people look back at our time, let the national minimum wage be the monument by which they remember our commitment to dignity and decency in the workplace", has helped around one million low-paid workers each year and has brought huge benefits to the economy, expresses serious concern over proposals supported by the new Prime Minister to vary the minimum wage and unemployment benefit region by region across Britain and believes that such a move would lead to further inequality and the reversal of the benefit that millions of people have experienced through the introduction of the minimum wage.

*S3M-355 Kenneth Gibson: Audio Trail of Largs—That the Parliament congratulates St Columba’s Parish Church in Largs on the launch of its Audio Trail of Largs on 11 August 2007, which allows one to experience and enjoy the rich history of the town, soaking up its atmosphere while hearing amusing tales and interesting facts; recognises the hard work of parishioners and friends of St Columba’s in making this a reality on a site which has been a place of Christian worship since at least 1253; acknowledges the innovative ventures planned by the kirk, which will include entertainment, an open air market, restaurant and a host of other activities while continuing to provide traditional services including weddings, christenings and funerals, and wishes St Columba’s every success with its Audio Visual Trail of Largs and its future plans.
