Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

*S2M-5157 Christine Grahame: Vexatious Exemptions Under Freedom of Information Legislation—That the Parliament notes the increasing pattern of failure of public bodies in Scotland to respond timeously to requests for information under the Freedom of Information (Scotland) Act 2002 and increasingly to use vexatious and inappropriate exemptions in response to requests; further notes that appeals from individuals must be dealt with more swiftly, and therefore urges the Scottish government to lead by example so that all public bodies uphold the spirit of the Act and respond positively to its provisions in order that they are truly open, accountable for their actions and transparent.

Supported by: George Lyon*

*S2M-5153 Ms Rosemary Byrne: Investigation of Sri Lankan Murders—That the Parliament condemns the murder on 10 November 2006 in Colombo, Sri Lanka, of the prominent Tamil MP, Nadarajah Raviraj and of one of his bodyguards; notes that the day before his assassination Mr Raviraj had been on a protest against an attack on a refugee camp on 8 November in which 45 Tamil civilians were killed and 60 were badly injured by the Sri Lankan forces; recognises that Nadarajah was a leading member of the United Socialist Party (CWI, Sri Lanka) whose Secretary, Siritunga Jayasuriya, has himself received frequent death threats; understands that, since the recent Geneva peace talks broke up without agreement and the three-year-old ceasefire broke down, the civil war in Sri Lanka continues, having so far cost more than 60,000 lives, and is draining the country’s economy and that a block on the supply route from the south means that the Tamil population in the north is starving; notes this ongoing humanitarian disaster and the failure of the Sri Lankan government to take action to prevent further suffering among its people and its own involvement in killings, abductions and disappearances, and calls for a totally independent international investigation into these actions to be undertaken immediately.

*S2M-5152# Cathy Peattie: Central Scotland, World Rescue Champions—That the Parliament congratulates Central Scotland Fire and Rescue Service whose team has won the World Rescue Organisation championship in Cape Town; notes that the aims of the competition are the development of skills and techniques that are used to save lives at real road accidents and the promotion of road safety; further notes that the team competed against 26 others from across the globe to take the title, in a competition that involved three simulated rescues of a casualty from an accident, in 20 minutes with full equipment, in 20 minutes with just hand tools and, finally, in just 10 minutes; also notes that the team train in its spare time and cut up over 100 cars in preparation for the competition, and wishes its members success in their endeavour to become the first team to defend the title successfully in Barcelona next year.

Supported by: Alex Neil*, Fiona Hyslop*, Ms Sandra White*, Bruce Crawford*, Tommy Sheridan*

*S2M-5149 Karen Gillon: Congratulations to the Carluke and Lanark Gazette—That the Parliament congratulates the Carluke and Lanark Gazette on its 100th birthday; notes that the paper is still based at its traditional home at 3 High Street, Carluke, where it was established by Andrew Beveridge in 1906; recognises the contribution made by the paper, now part of Johnston Press plc, to the local communities of Clydesdale, and thanks the staff team and, in particular, the local correspondents who ensure the Gazette’s ability to reach into the heart of every community within its circulation area.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie*, Derek Brownlee*, Eleanor Scott*, Colin Fox*

*S2M-5148 Carolyn Leckie: Scottish Power’s Profits—That the Parliament notes that fuel poverty has been identified as Scotland’s biggest winter killer of old age pensioners (OAPs) and that an extra 30,000 households fall into fuel poverty with each 5% increase in fuel prices; further notes that Scottish Power’s pre-tax profits for the first six months of 2006 show a 77% increase to £483 million; believes that this massive increase has been achieved through profiteering on increased energy prices, and believes that those who will really pay for Scottish Power’s profiteering are the thousands of OAPs who will die because they cannot afford to heat their homes and the tens of thousands of Scots families that will spend part of this winter shivering in unheated homes because they will suffer self-disconnection when they cannot afford to feed their meters.

*S2M-5147<> Mr Andrew Arbuckle: Community Energy Initiatives—That the Parliament supports community energy initiatives such those on Gigha and at Cathkin Braes; believes that not only do these initiatives involve and enthuse local people but that they also provide a template for other communities throughout Scotland, and further believes that the example of many continental towns where community heating projects exist also provides a possible answer to rising fuel bills and that pilot projects involving communities in Scotland should be encouraged.

Supported by: Margaret Smith*, Donald Gorrie*

*S2M-5146# Jeremy Purvis: Pioneering Co-location of Borders College and Heriot Watt University Borders Campus—That the Parliament welcomes the signing of the ground-breaking co-location agreement, on Friday 17 November 2006, between Borders College and Heriot Watt University to bring about a combined further and higher education campus in the Borders at Netherdale in Galashiels; recalls the concerns about the proposed relocation of the School of Textiles and Design by the university from the Borders campus to Edinburgh in 2004 but congratulates the group established to work with the university in successfully retaining the internationally renowned school at the Borders campus, and welcomes the extensive funding package from the Scottish Executive and EU structural funds, totalling £27.7 million for the redevelopment of the campus and a further £3.7 million being granted to fund a new community college in Hawick, to provide state-of-the-art learning facilities for the Borders and establish a "university college" model and creative, world-class learning facilities.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie*
