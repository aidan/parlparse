Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-4417 Ms Rosemary Byrne: Humanitarian Disaster in Africa—That the Parliament notes the urgent appeal by UNICEF in respect of the unfolding humanitarian disaster in the Horn of Africa; recognises that more than 8,780,000 people, including 4,455,000 children, are in urgent need of humanitarian assistance; notes that there has been a failure of crops and loss of livestock which has increased morbidity and mortality, as well as malnutrition, in children; notes that in all five countries in the Horn of Africa UNICEF is providing cluster co-ordination leadership in nutrition and water and sanitation whilst contributing significantly to World Health Organisation-led co-ordination in health and supporting education and child protection; therefore pledges to assist the UNICEF appeal on behalf of the people of Scotland, and calls on the Scottish Executive to contact UNICEF on this matter with a view to providing meaningful assistance to UNICEF in whatever way it can.

*S2M-4416 Mark Ballard: Carbon Trading - Emission Impossible—That the Parliament welcomes the establishment of ARREST, an EU-25 wide campaign calling for "A Real Review of EmissionS Trading"; believes that the European Union’s Emissions Trading Scheme (EU ETS), though well-meaning, is fundamentally flawed and unlikely to be effective in tackling climate change in an efficient, effective and equitable manner; strongly feels that ownership of greenhouse gas emission permits should be allocated to all adults on an equal per capita basis, not to existing large polluters free of charge under a grandfathering arrangement as is presently the case with the EU ETS; notes that a chronic oversupply of permits in the European market led to a collapse in the value of carbon from 29 euros per tonne in late April 2006 to a value in mid-May of less than nine euros per tonne; considers that such an oversupply is symptomatic of the total inability of the scheme to effectively address European carbon emissions, and calls on the Scottish Executive to make representations to the European Commission calling for a thorough review and a dramatic overhaul of EU ETS.

*S2M-4415 Mr Andrew Welsh: Literacy in Angus—That the Parliament congratulates Angus Council’s Education Department on the upcoming literacy conference, "Language for Learning - Literacy in a Curriculum for Excellence: A Conference to Launch the Angus Literacy Strategy", to be held in Carnoustie on 23 May 2006; is aware of the fact that in Angus there has been an increase in English language attainment in the last five years while the Scottish average for attainment in writing has declined in the same period, and hopes that the lessons learned in Angus from the many initiatives taken by Angus Council with regard to education will be useful in tackling the literacy problems throughout Scotland based on the Scottish tradition that education, and the right to education, is the key to progress.

*S2M-4414 Dr Sylvia Jackson: Recognising Scotland’s Volunteers Providing Overseas Aid—That the Parliament recognises the significant work carried out by the people of Scotland in providing help and support to poorer countries and, in particular, congratulates all those individuals, local groups, churches and charities from the Stirling constituency, encouraged by the Scottish Executive, for providing assistance to countries such as Malawi.

*S2M-4413 Dr Sylvia Jackson: Broadband Access Across Scotland—That the Parliament congratulates the Scottish Executive on the roll-out of broadband across Scotland but urges the Executive to accelerate the investment in technical solutions to connect the small percentage of communities that still await the provision of a broadband service.

*S2M-4412 Michael Matheson: Broadcast of Scottish Women’s Football Cup Final—That the Parliament congratulates Glasgow City LFC and Aberdeen LFC on reaching the Scottish Women’s Football Cup final; notes that this final will not be televised, and hopes that the broadcasters will reverse their decision for next year’s final and show one of Scotland’s fastest-growing sports.

*S2M-4411# Maureen Macmillan: Migrant Workers in the Highlands—That the Parliament notes the increase in population in the Highlands over the last five years; welcomes the contribution made to these figures by migrant workers who are taking up job vacancies in Inverness, Badenoch and Strathspey, Lochaber, Easter Ross and other parts of the Highlands; commends the initiatives of agencies in the Highlands to support the migrant workers, in particular noting the forthcoming "Information Day" in Inverness on 22 May 2006; expresses concern regarding the capacity available in the Highlands to support increasing numbers of migrant workers, and notes that more needs to be done to give language and other support to the children of migrant workers in our schools.

S2M-4410*#* Michael McMahon: Supporting the Telecommunications Industry in Lanarkshire and the Rest of Scotland—That the Parliament notes recent merger announcements by companies operating in the telecommunications industry; notes the impact of these mergers in relation to actual and potential reductions in the Lanarkshire workforce especially; *considers that* the Scottish Executive *should* work with the industry to develop a strategy for the retention and expansion of telecommunications, telecommunication services and telecommunications customer services jobs in Scotland and to make outsourcing an unattractive option, and *believes that* the Executive, Scottish Enterprise and the other support agencies *should* establish contact with telecommunications operators with operations throughout Lanarkshire and the rest of Scotland, including Cable and Wireless, NTL:Telewest, Orange, T-Mobile and O2, to ascertain what support can be offered to encourage the retention and expansion of telecommunications, telecommunication services and telecommunications customer services jobs in both Lanarkshire and the rest of Scotland.

Supported by: Alex Neil*
