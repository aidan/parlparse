Business Bulletin No. 50/2009: Thursday 19 March 2009
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 26 March 2009

Question Time

General

1. Stuart McMillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what assistance it is providing to people looking for employment in Scotland in light of recent figures showing that an average of 10 people apply for each job centre vacancy. (S3O-6434)

2. Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will instigate national testing in reading, writing and maths for 10-year-olds to ensure that underachieving pupils have the opportunity to catch up before starting secondary school. (S3O-6367)

3. David Whitton: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will announce a strategy on creating green jobs. (S3O-6414)

4. Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive what it is doing to protect children who live with a parent with a drug addiction. (S3O-6402)

5. Nicol Stephen: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made on refinancing the Aberdeen 3Rs school project to allow building work on the affected schools to restart. (S3O-6454)

6. Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive, in developing the details of the Town Centre Regeneration Fund, whether it will include plans to inform local communities about how they can apply for funding. (S3O-6423)

7. Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what meetings are planned between ministers and leaders of local authorities to discuss education. (S3O-6379)

8. Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive which road upgrades it has prioritised for the remainder of the parliamentary session. (S3O-6364)

9. Alison McInnes: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made of the impact on staffing of the reduction to the Regional Transport Partnership Travel Plan Grant for 2009-10. (S3O-6446)

10. Christina McKelvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on any progress on implementing the alternatives to detention pilot for asylum-seeking families announced by the Secretary of State for Scotland in 2008. (S3O-6433)

Rural Affairs and the Environment

1. John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of the recently published December 2008 Survey of Agriculture, what action it will take to halt the decline of agricultural production. (S3O-6366)

2. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive what support is available to individuals and community groups to undertake targeted environmental improvement projects in their local areas. (S3O-6449)

3. Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it intends to give to the Keep Scotland Beautiful National Spring Clean campaign. (S3O-6436)

4. John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive what it is doing to support farming in the Scottish Borders. (S3O-6361)

5. Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent meetings ministers have had with representatives of NFU Scotland. (S3O-6405)

6. Not Lodged

7. Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of the 2008 woodfuel demand and usage data published by Forestry Commission Scotland, whether it considers that the biomass energy industry is sustainable. (S3O-6365)

8. Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how many agricultural regulations it has removed since 2007. (S3O-6372)

9. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive how the Scottish Environment Protection Agency is contributing to the economic recovery programme. (S3O-6415)

10. Malcolm Chisholm: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the rising cost of the Water of Leith Flood Prevention Scheme will be factored into the financial support provided to the City of Edinburgh Council. (S3O-6381)

Justice and Law Officers

1. Shirley-Anne Somerville: To ask the Scottish Executive how many incidents of assault in the last year involved unlicensed taxis or private hire cars illegally picking up passengers. (S3O-6437)

2. Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent meetings it has had with the Chief Constable of Strathclyde Police. (S3O-6385)

3. Jim Tolson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it plans to take to overcome the long-term problem of overcrowding in young offenders institutions. (S3O-6456)

4. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to ensure that offenders begin community service orders within seven days. (S3O-6450)

5. Peter Peacock: To ask the Scottish Executive what responsibility its justice directorates have for ensuring that Scottish Government proposals comply with EU law. (S3O-6409)

6. Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive what legal or other measures it will put in place to prevent and reduce alcohol misuse. (S3O-6444)

7. Joe FitzPatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to reform criminal law during the parliamentary session. (S3O-6422)

8. Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it agrees with Her Majesty’s Chief Inspector of Prisons for Scotland that the conditions for young women at Cornton Vale are unacceptable. (S3O-6443)

9. Aileen Campbell: To ask the Scottish Executive what consideration it is giving to the impact of sentencing on the children of offenders while developing the Scottish Sentencing Council. (S3O-6417)

10. Dave Thompson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that Scottish criminal law is sufficient to deal with the activities of loan sharks. (S3O-6438)
