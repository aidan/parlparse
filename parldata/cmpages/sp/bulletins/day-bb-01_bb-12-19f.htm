*S1M-2557# Johann Lamont: Citizens Advice Bureau, Greater Pollok-That the Parliament welcomes the opening of a Citizens Advice Bureau in Greater Pollok; notes that it has received a remarkable number of enquiries already, 370 in its first month of being open; further notes that local people have come forward to volunteer and currently form 90% of the workforce in the bureau; congratulates Greater Pollok Social Inclusion Partnership and Citizens Advice Scotland on their partnership approach in working together to provide the necessary funding, and believes that the bureau will complement the excellent work of the seven other bureaux in Glasgow in tackling social exclusion through the provision of independent advice, assistance and representation services.

*S1M-2556# Dr Sylvia Jackson: Sheep Farming around Loch Katrine-That the Parliament notes West of Scotland Water's decision to cease its sheep farming operations around Loch Katrine; recognises the potential effects this could have on the Trossachs community; further notes the discussions that West of Scotland Water is now having with local community groups, and urges West of Scotland Water to work with all organisations, including the Scottish Executive, to fully investigate the environmental, social and economic impact this decision will have on the area and to use the integrated catchment management plan as a basis to find solutions to sustain the local community.

*S1M-2555 Patricia Ferguson: Membership of the Committee of the Regions-That the Parliament endorses the Executive's proposal to nominate Irene Oldfather MSP as a representative of the Parliament to replace Hugh Henry MSP as a full member on the UK delegation to the Committee of the Regions for the forthcoming session from 2002 to 2006.

Supported by: Mr Jim Wallace*
