BUSINESS BULLETIN 9 / 2000 

Wednesday 19 January 2000

Section E -   Written questions lodged on 18 January 2000

 

 

S1W-3776 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive what is the most recent assessment it has made as to whether the A709 and A701 are suitable as strategic road links between Dumfries and the M74.

 

S1W-3799 Tricia Marwick: To ask the Scottish Executive how many evictions from Scottish local authority housing have been carried out in the last five years for (a) rent arrears and (b) anti-social behaviour.

 

S1W-3800 Tricia Marwick: To ask the Scottish Executive how many Scottish local authority tenants have exercised their right to buy after proceedings to evict them for anti-social behaviour have been initiated in each of the last five years.

 

S1W-3801 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any guidelines are provided to police officers as to the approach to be taken to a civil property dispute.

 

S1W-3802 Mr Murray Tosh: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the M74 Northern Extension sufficiently meets its strategic roads assessment criteria to be allowed to progress in its current form.

 

S1W-3803 Christine Grahame: To ask the Presiding Officer what efforts the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body has made to publicise to the general public the new regulations on the operation of cross-party groups.

 

S1W-3804 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive when Glasgow City Council tenants whose homes are to be demolished following the proposed stock transfer are to be informed that their home is excluded from the list of those to be modernised.

 

S1W-3805 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive how much was spent in financial years 1997-98 and 1998-99 on stock transfer feasibility studies, how much will be spent this financial year and how much will be spent in each of the next two financial years.

 

S1W-3807 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it will take to ensure that health boards encourage NHS staff to get vaccinated against influenza next winter and in subsequent years.

 

S1W-3808 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average waiting time is for nursing home placements in each local authority for which figures are available and what plans it has for reducing waiting times.

 

S1W-3809 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to retain the services of Mr Brian Souter as one of its "Champions of Change".

 

S1W-3811 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will confirm its commitment to the Larkhall rail link in the light of the threat to DAKs Simpson jobs in Larkhall and the need to improve the transport infrastructure of the town, and when the link can be expected to open.

 

S1W-3812 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive when the next Scottish Abstract of Statistics will be published.

 

S1W-3813 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will give a commitment to return to annual publication of the Scottish Abstract of Statistics.

 

S1W-3814 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive why the Scottish Abstract of Statistics due to be published in December 1999 has not yet appeared.

 

S1W-3815 Fiona McLeod: To ask the Scottish Executive how local people can apply to become members of their local Health Board or local Health Trust Board and what measures it is taking to monitor how accessible the application process is to local people.

 

S1W-3816 Mr Duncan Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will investigate the Food Safety Authority of Ireland’s decision to allow the continued sale of scallops which have been "shucked" from areas affected by amnesic shellfish poisoning with a view to introducing this policy in Scotland.

 

S1W-3819 Dorothy-Grace Elder: To ask the Scottish Executive what its policy is in relation to the provision of council housing for single people convicted of non-violent crimes and who were council tenants prior to serving a prison sentence but are homeless on their release, and what impact this policy has on the likelihood of such individuals reoffending.

 

S1W-3820 Dorothy-Grace Elder: To ask the Scottish Executive to provide updated information on the workings of the "Twenty is Plenty" experimental speed limit scheme set up in a number of areas from 1998.

 

S1W-3821 Dorothy-Grace Elder: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider the results of the "Twenty is Plenty" limit in the Gorbals area and investigate the possibility of extending the 20 mph speed limit throughout the Gorbals.

 

S1W-3822 Mike Watson: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of Scottish Executive funding for the voluntary sector is allocated to black and ethnic minority organisations.

 

S1W-3823 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive what would be the estimated cost to Scottish public authorities of classifying dyslexia as a disability.

 

S1W-3824 Mr Keith Harding: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-2224 by Mr Frank McAveety on 25 November 1999, whether it has received the full account of Stirling Council’s practice in relation to the co-option of individuals to council committees which it requested and whether it is satisfied that Stirling Council’s practice in this regard complies with all of the relevant statutory requirements.

 

S1W-3825 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to develop a Scottish national allergy centre.

 

S1W-3826 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider funding a Scottish allergen imuno-therapy unit.

 

S1W-3827 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive what arrangements are in place for liaison between the Digital Scotland Taskforce and the Knowledge Economy Taskforce.

 

S1W-3828 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there is an overlap between the respective remits of the Knowledge Economy Taskforce and the Digital Scotland Taskforce and how that overlap is to be managed.

 

S1W-3829 David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many of its staff are engaged in the task of promoting and supporting e-commerce.
