Business Bulletin No. 26/2002

Monday 11 February 2002

Section E : Written questions lodged on 8 February 2002
  

S1W-22867 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many more debtors will receive advice as a result of the £3 million additional funding for new debt advice services; how much earlier than at present debtors will now be able to access free and independent debt advice and what support will be available for front line advisors.

S1W-22868 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of the £3 million additional funding for new debt advice services will be used to employ new and additional debt advice workers.

S1W-22869 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will detail those groups and/or individuals who have been consulted on the allocation of the £3 million additional funding for new debt advice services.

S1W-22870 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any new monies allocated for debt advice services will be used to expand the breadth and quality of existing free advice services rather than establishing new organisations.

S1W-22871 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive when the next phase of its diligence review will be published.

S1W-22872 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive how much new money for debt advice services will be available as a result of the report of the Working Group on a Replacement for Poinding and Warrant Sales, Striking the Balance: A New Approach to Debt Management; how it will ensure that this is additional and not recycled money; how the money will be allocated, and who will decide the allocation and in consultation with whom.

S1W-22873 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive, with regard to its news release SE5173/2002 of 16 January 2002, whether the £10,000 threshold on annual income for the charitable relief scheme under the Water Industry (Scotland) Bill will apply to the income of the organisation as a whole or to income derived from the premises for which relief is sought.

S1W-22874 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will obtain a valuation of the land and buildings occupied by Scottish Natural Heritage (SNH) at Hope Terrace, Edinburgh; whether, should the SNH head office be relocated from Edinburgh, the capital receipt from the sale of the premises at Hope Terrace would be available (a) in whole or (b) in part for relocation costs, and how any partial contribution to relocation costs arising from any such capital receipt would be calculated.

S1W-22875 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any deadline has been fixed for the submission by Scottish Natural Heritage (SNH) of a report costing the options for relocating its headquarters from Edinburgh; which locations are being considered for such a relocation; whether SNH staff will have access to the full report prior to its final submission, and whether any parts of the report will not be disclosed and what the reasons are for the position on this matter.

S1W-22876 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list any headquarters functions of Scottish Natural Heritage that are carried out (a) at Hope Terrace, Edinburgh, (b) at Bonnington Bond, Leith, (c) in Inverness, (d) in Aberdeen, (e) in Battleby and (f) in Clydebank.

S1W-22877 Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to have the current ban on fishmeal in ruminant feedstuff lifted. R

S1W-22878 Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it accepts the scientific findings by Professor Emeritus John Robinson of the Scottish Agricultural College, Aberdeen, of 29 November 1991 that in typical weather conditions 10% of the national sheepflock could die over the lambing season as a result of the current ban on fishmeal in ruminant feedstuff. R

S1W-22881 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it will take to ensure that derelict and vacant land reclamation in sites near or adjacent to the M74 northern extension is carried out simultaneously with the construction of the road.

S1W-22882 Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what it estimates to be the cost to (a) local authorities and (b) the NHS of recent severe weather damage.

S1W-22883 Ben Wallace: To ask the Scottish Executive what 24 hour acute psychiatric provision is currently available.

S1W-22884 Ben Wallace: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list the publication dates for all up-dated data previously available in Chapter M, Hospital and Clinical Activity, of Scottish Health Statistics 2000.

S1W-22885 Ben Wallace: To ask the Scottish Executive what current figures are available on the incidence of suicide and attempted suicide in the community.

S1W-22886 Ben Wallace: To ask the Scottish Executive what specialist assistance is available for mental health services to ethnic minorities.

S1W-22887 Ben Wallace: To ask the Scottish Executive how many patients received an angiography within 12 weeks of their initial referral, expressed also as a percentage of the total number of patients waiting to receive an angiography, in the most recent 12-month period for which figures are available.

S1W-22888 Ben Wallace: To ask the Scottish Executive how many patients received an angioplasty operation within 24 weeks of having an angiography, also expressed as a percentage of the total number of patients waiting to receive an angioplasty operation, in the most recent 12-month period for which figures are available.

S1W-22889 Ben Wallace: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of patients were admitted to hospital from the waiting list for treatment within nine months of their initial referral, in the most recent 12-month period for which figures are available.

S1W-22890 Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive in respect of how many PFI/PPP schemes or proposed schemes Arthur Andersen or any of its subsidiaries and associated companies have been awarded (a) contracts and (b) sub-contracts and (c) have undertaken consultancy work since May 1997.

S1W-22891 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether clear guidance will be issued in regard to the concept of notional capital in relation to assessment of those eligible for the payment of care in the community and residential care allowances.

S1W-22892 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will ensure that, once an individual’s care needs have been assessed, those needs are then met regardless of the resources available to either the individual concerned or the relevant local authority.

S1W-22893 Mr Keith Harding: To ask the Scottish Executive how many copies of Communities Scotland’s Quality News are issued each year and what the total annual cost is of printing, publication and distribution.

S1W-22894 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Minister for Enterprise, Transport and Lifelong Learning will visit Aviemore and travel along the A95 in order to observe the condition of the road and what assessment it has made of the importance of the road to the whisky industry and the local economy as a whole.

S1W-22895 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to enhance the provision of greenspace in East and West Dunbartonshire.

S1W-22896 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-18229 by Susan Deacon on 15 October 2001, what initiatives will be undertaken in 2002 to strengthen the relationship between the NHS and the hospice movement.

S1W-22897 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list the projects being undertaken by (a) North of Scotland Water Authority, (b) East of Scotland Water Authority and (c) West of Scotland Water Authority to comply with the provisions of the EU urban wastewater treatment directive (91/272/EEC subsequently amended by Commission Directive 98/15/EC) including costings.

S1W-22898 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive how successful it has been in assisting with the establishment of credit unions and what is being done to ensure that existing credit unions such as those in Dalmuir, Dunbartonshire and East Clydebank, continue to trade successfully.

S1W-22899 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what assistance is being given to West Dunbartonshire Council to improve its council tax collection rates.

S1W-22900 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what assistance is being given to West Dunbartonshire Council to improve its rate of collection of rent from council-owned housing.

S1W-22901 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people have visited the National Museum of Scotland since April 2001.

S1W-22902 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any additional element of surplus in the Scottish Transport Group pension funds which accrues in the current financial year will be made over in full to the members of the funds or whether any such sum will be paid to other persons or bodies.

S1W-22903 Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will reply to my letter of 27 December 2001, to the Minister for Enterprise, Transport and Lifelong Learning in respect of the Scottish Transport Group.

S1W-22904 Miss Annabel Goldie: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made by local economic forums in determining what they do in Year 2.

S1W-22905 Miss Annabel Goldie: To ask the Scottish Executive how research funding will change in the light of the Research Assessment Exercise in 2001.

S1W-22906 Miss Annabel Goldie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether traffic congestion is damaging to business.

S1W-22907 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will detail and cost the alternatives to the construction of a new water treatment plant at Milngavie as part of the Katrine Water Project submitted by (a) Trossach Water, (b) Balfour Beatty et.al., (c) Craigmaddie Water and (d) Alfred McAlpine et.al.

S1W-22908 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what the highest point above ground will be of the proposed new water treatment plant at Milngavie.

S1W-22909 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive on what basis West of Scotland Water considers the construction of a new water treatment plant at Milngavie to be the best value solution for the Katrine Water Project.

S1W-22910 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what consultation was undertaken by West of Scotland Water before it determined its preferred option for the location of a new water treatment plant as part of the Katrine Water Project.

S1W-22911 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will detail the reasons for the decision by West of Scotland Water Authority to engage Stirling Water as contractor for the Katrine Water Project.

S1W-22912 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive when the construction of the works associated with the Katrine Water Project will commence and be completed.

S1W-22913 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact any delays in the development approvals process would have on the costs of the Katrine Water Project.

S1W-22914 Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what will happen to the eight members of West of Scotland Water’s tourist office at Loch Katrine and the SS Walter Scott screw steamer following the commissioning of a new water treatment plant at Milngavie.

S1W-22915 Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions the Minister for Health and Community Care and/or either of his deputies have had with the Chief Medical Officer regarding the postponement of tonsillectomy operations in the Argyll and Clyde NHS Board area.

S1W-22916 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations it has made to the Director of Education at South Ayrshire Council regarding any proposed Public/Private Partnership initiative for schools in the area.

S1W-22917 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will detail the current guidance to local authorities on Public/Private Partnerships and Private Finance Initiatives and what plans it has to revise this guidance.

S1W-22918 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive what criteria is applied in the consideration of a school closure and what bearing does a report by Her Majesty’s Inspectorate of Education have.

S1W-22919 Mr Adam Ingram: To ask the Scottish Executive what role rural schools play in the local community.

S1W-22920 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-22235 by Malcolm Chisholm on 6 February 2002, whether the Freedom of Information (Scotland) Bill will have an effect in allowing access to the correspondence between it and Her Majesty’s Treasury, the Department of Work and Pensions and any other individuals or departments, in connection with the loss of Attendance Allowance to Scottish pensioners resulting from the implementation of free personal and nursing care for pensioners.

S1W-22921 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-22235 by Malcolm Chisholm on 6 February 2002, whether the correspondence between it and Her Majesty’s Government concerning the loss of Attendance Allowance to Scottish pensioners resulting from the implementation of free personal and nursing care for pensioners was automatically covered by Part II of its Code of Practice on Access to Scottish Executive Information and what consideration it gave or will now give to releasing this correspondence under Part I, 3(v).
