Business Bulletin No. 182/2004

Thursday 9 December 2004

Section D : Oral questions selected for answer on 16 December 2004
  

Question Time

Enterprise, Lifelong Learning and Transport

1. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of staff and students in further education colleges it estimates will require to be vetted by Disclosure Scotland in the next academic year. (S2O-4624)

2. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive whether its target of reopening the Airdrie to Bathgate railway line by the end of 2008 will be met. (S2O-4691)

3. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in improving travel across the Forth Estuary. (S2O-4638)

4. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the Strategic Rail Authority on the franchise arrangements for the East Coast Main Line. (S2O-4589)

5. Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive how it will ensure that Scottish Enterprise delivers environmentally-sustainable development. (S2O-4685)

6. Trish Godman: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to make public transport less vulnerable to antisocial behaviour. (S2O-4664)

7. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in making ex-gratia payments to former members of the Scottish Transport Group pension fund. (S2O-4557)

8. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in making public transport fully accessible to disabled people. (S2O-4656)

9. Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive how much funding will be allocated for the rail functions to be devolved to the Scottish Parliament. (S2O-4586)

10. Shiona Baird: To ask the Scottish Executive what role the Minister for Enterprise and Lifelong Learning will play in ensuring that Scottish Enterprise delivers environmentally-sustainable development. (S2O-4681)

11. Susan Deacon: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to promote and support employers in the development of flexible working practices. (S2O-4553)

12. Michael McMahon: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to reduce the number of fatalities on roads. (S2O-4650)

Justice and Law Officers

1. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made of the level of fear of crime. (S2O-4604)

2. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to implement the Partnership Agreement commitment to expand the role of restorative justice as a means of improving the range and quality of sentences available to courts. (S2O-4678)

3. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to improve facilities at Aberdeen’s Craiginches Prison. (S2O-4593)

4. Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will introduce community repatriation orders. (S2O-4634)

5. Mark Ballard: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will expand the role of restorative justice in sentencing. (S2O-4677)

6. Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance it has given to procurators fiscal about how crimes of assault involving a weapon or implement should be dealt with. (S2O-4569)

7. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to help prisoners enter the job market on release. (S2O-4612)

8. Christine May: To ask the Scottish Executive how its Debt Arrangement Scheme will address "loan sharks" and illegal money lending. (S2O-4670)

9. Mr Mark Ruskell: To ask the Scottish Executive when the TETRA system will be fully operational and ready for use by police. (S2O-4679)

10. Murdo Fraser: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that householders should be able to defend their property from intruders. (S2O-4574)

11. Phil Gallie: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to replace the loss of secure places for serious young offenders following the closure of Kerelaw School. (S2O-4564)

12. Mr Kenny MacAskill: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it plans to take on firearms offences. (S2O-4588)

General

1. Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what role it envisages for the royal colleges in NHS workforce planning. (S2O-4669)

2. Mrs Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive how the Minister for Health and Community Care’s statement that the NHS should be as local as possible but as specialised as necessary is being applied in the Linlithgow constituency. (S2O-4663)

3. Iain Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with NHS Fife on the proposed hospital and health centre for St Andrews. (S2O-4590)

4. Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made on agreeing rules for co-existence between GM and non-GM crops. (S2O-4619)

5. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will include measures in the school curriculum to encourage more school leavers to work in the construction industry. (S2O-4673)

6. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive how it can improve the number of young people choosing to study a foreign language. (S2O-4665)

7. Elaine Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to increase the availability of social rented housing and affordable housing to buy. (S2O-4659)

8. Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers Scottish Water’s consultation procedures to be satisfactory. (S2O-4641)

9. Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consult the UK Government on the implications of the removal of the 50% council tax discount on second homes for the eligibility of such homeowners to vote also in the local elections in which their second home is located. (S2O-4640)

10. Chris Ballance: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is concerned about levels of radioactivity in the environment of Dumfries and Galloway. (S2O-4686)

11. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with the UK Government regarding events to be held in Scotland as part of the UK Presidency of the Council of Ministers. (S2O-4606)

12. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it can address public concern over local NHS reforms. (S2O-4639)

13. Mr Mark Ruskell: To ask the Scottish Executive whether its sitefinder website gives sufficient access to information on the location of TETRA communication masts. (S2O-4689)

14. Mark Ballard: To ask the Scottish Executive who owns the carbon rights in trees that are planted under the Scottish Forestry Grants Scheme. (S2O-4687)

15. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive how it assesses the outcome of additional financial support to public bodies where additional weighting is given to deprivation factors in funding formulae. (S2O-4625)
