Business Bulletin No. 169/2010: Thursday 18 November 2010
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D – Oral questions selected for answer on 25 November 2010

Question Time

General

1. Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what efforts are being made to bring the Nigg yard in Easter Ross into full use. (S3O-12065)

2. Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what importance it places on good animal welfare. (S3O-12136)

3. Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made with the Scottish Investment Bank. (S3O-12057)

4. Hugh O’Donnell: To ask the Scottish Executive, in light of its policy on renewable energy, what its best estimate is, at current prices, of the amount and value of the opencast coal reserves remaining in the land transferred to Scottish Coal at privatisation. (S3O-12102)

5. Derek Brownlee: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact its policies have had on the new business start-up rate. (S3O-12063)

6. Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what advice it has given to local authorities on the allocation of housing. (S3O-12060)

7. Jim Tolson: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to encourage bodies such as local authorities and NHS boards to improve their levels of local food sourcing. (S3O-12062)

8. John Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent contact it has had with equine welfare organisations. (S3O-12064)

9. Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many affordable homes for rent will be built in 2011-12 through the Scottish Futures Trust. (S3O-12077)

10. Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive when it received the report on the local public inquiry into the M8 Bailleston to Newhouse upgrade. (S3O-12068)

Education and Lifelong Learning

1. Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made of the impact on schools in Orkney of its class size commitment for P1 to P3. (S3O-12099)

2. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive how much funding and what resources the Cabinet Secretary for Education and Lifelong Learning has been allocated following the recent budget statement. (S3O-12142)

3. Bill Wilson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is aware of research and anecdotal evidence indicating that outdoor education can improve the behaviour of disruptive pupils. (S3O-12095)

4. Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it has offered to aid in the development and training of skills in the textiles industry. (S3O-12098)

5. Rob Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made toward meeting demand for Gaelic medium education across Scotland. (S3O-12078)

6. Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive what priority it gives to early years education. (S3O-12117)

7. Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what educational and economic benefits the new City of Glasgow College will bring for students and Glasgow. (S3O-12093)

8. Charlie Gordon: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress local authorities have made toward delivering the Scottish Government’s commitment on free school meals. (S3O-12119)

9. Elizabeth Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans the Cabinet Secretary for Education and Lifelong Learning has to give more control of schools to head teachers. (S3O-12059)

10. Gil Paterson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many apprenticeship places it is supporting in 2010-11. (S3O-12089)

Europe, External Affairs and Culture

1. Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive what events are planned to celebrate St Andrew’s Day. (S3O-12075)

2. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with local authorities regarding the termination of the cultural coordinator contracts. (S3O-12052)

3. Maureen Watt: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to support the use of the Scots language. (S3O-12091)

4. Tricia Marwick: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to promote public art. (S3O-12082)

5. Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has received the report that it commissioned on museum funding. (S3O-12113)

6. John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment the Minister for Culture and External Affairs has made of the Scottish Government’s recent visit to China. (S3O-12101)

7. Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to promote cultural activity in the Linlithgow parliamentary constituency. (S3O-12134)

8. David McLetchie: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent discussions it has had with ministers of the Government of the Republic of Ireland on the benefits of membership of the Euro. (S3O-12066)

9. James Kelly: To ask the Scottish Executive what procedures are in place to monitor the progress of Creative Scotland. (S3O-12122)

10. Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether projects working with prisoners in Malawi can receive support from the Scottish Government’s International Aid Fund. (S3O-12083)
