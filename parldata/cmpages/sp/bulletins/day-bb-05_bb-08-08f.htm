Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-3144 Christine Grahame: Sir William Wallace - Guardian of Scotland—That the Parliament recognises the seminal role of William Wallace in securing the status of Scotland as a nation which endures to this day; celebrates his historic victory at Stirling Bridge in 1297 against military odds through his imaginative and inspiring patriotism and devotion to restoring independence to Scotland; recognises that at Selkirk, in the "forest kyrk", the Kirk of the Forest, supported by nobles and clergy, he was declared Guardian of the Kingdom of Scotland, and further recognises that, though betrayed and brutally executed as a commoner on 23 August 700 years ago, his patriotic spirit inspires many Scots seven centuries on, when his legacy is still "Guardian of Scotland".

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson*, Fergus Ewing*, Brian Adam*

*S2M-3143 Chris Ballance: 60th Anniversary of Hiroshima—That the Parliament observes the 60th anniversary of the first nuclear attack on Japan at Hiroshima on 6 August 1945; is gravely concerned about the global proliferation of nuclear weapons, including the current development of uranium enrichment in Iran; believes that nuclear proliferation will not be prevented unless all countries disarm, and therefore calls on the UK Government to abide by the terms of the Nuclear Non-Proliferation Treaty and to set an example by announcing that it intends to abandon its plan to replace the United Kingdom’s Trident nuclear weapons.

*S2M-3142 Patrick Harvie: Identity Cards Oversold by the Home Office—That the Parliament welcomes the acknowledgement by UK Minister Tony McNulty that the Home Office has exaggerated and oversold the advantages of an identity card and national identity register; believes that the Identity Cards Bill offers an ineffective response to problems of security and fraud and poses an unacceptable threat to civil liberties; recognises concern that ID cards could worsen harassment of minority groups by providing another pretext for stop-and-search, often directed at ethnic minorities; notes the falling public support for this impractical, unworkable and costly scheme, which has dropped from 78% to 45% in two years; further notes that any ceiling placed upon the cost of an individual card will not lessen the overall cost of the scheme to the public purse, and repeats its call for the UK Government to drop its proposals for an identity card scheme.

*S2M-3141 Shiona Baird: Office of Fair Trading Failure on Supermarket Code—That the Parliament expresses its concern and dismay at the conclusion of the Office of Fair Trading investigation into supermarket trading practices that no further regulation is needed; recognises the significant implications that these conclusions have for the many independent businesses and farms already struggling to compete against the stranglehold of the four main supermarket chains; calls on the Scottish Executive to use its influence to ensure that the Supermarket Code of Practice provides effective protection for those in Scotland and abroad who produce our food, and urges the Executive to take action, as other countries have done, in maintaining diversity and real choice in the high streets by supporting a fair and transparent planning system able to encompass wider considerations of the fair trade benefits for the whole community.

*S2M-3140# Mrs Margaret Ewing: Prospective School Closures in Moray—That the Parliament believes that rural schools are a vital part of local communities and that there should be no presumption of closure; recognises that rural schools and their high standards of education often act as magnets to attract and retain young families; believes that the Moray Council’s option to close 21 such local schools is driven by financial rather than educational considerations, that tighter rules and guidelines must be implemented when it comes to the process of any prospective school closures and mergers in the future and that councillors should be free to express their views as democratically elected representatives of their communities prior to officials bringing forward recommendations, and re-iterates the principle that elected members not officials decide the priorities of their communities.

*S2M-3139 Patrick Harvie: Civil Partnership Ceremonies—That the Parliament notes with concern indications from some other Scottish local authorities that they intend to deny couples registering their civil partnership a ceremony to mark the occasion; notes that under the Civil Partnership Act 2004 local authorities have a duty to provide a registration service but no such duty to offer a ceremony; notes that ceremonies are also discretionary for civil marriage but that no local authority in Scotland has refused to offer them; considers that a partnership or marriage ceremony is an important and meaningful act for many couples; believes that local authorities which allow such ceremonies for civil marriage but refuse them for civil partnership may be in breach of their duty under the Local Government in Scotland Act 2003 to discharge their functions in a manner which encourages equal opportunities, and calls on the Scottish Executive to amend the Family Law (Scotland) Bill to place a clear requirement on local authorities to offer a ceremony both for civil marriages and for civil partnerships, based on the General Register Office for Scotland’s recommended form of words, unless the couple wish to agree a different form of words with the local registrar.

Supported by: Nora Radcliffe*, Rosie Kane*, Chris Ballance*, Margaret Smith*, Mark Ballard*

*S2M-3138 Patrick Harvie: Campaign for a Fairer Planning System—That the Parliament congratulates Scottish Environment LINK and the Association of Scottish Community Councils (ASCC) for their work in campaigning for a fairer planning system; recognises that public support for community rights in the planning system has been clearly demonstrated, for example in the Executive’s consultation on rights of appeal in planning; acknowledges that the issues raised by LINK and the ASCC in their e-petition on the forthcoming Planning (Scotland) Bill are serious and valid concerns, and urges the Executive to consider the importance of community rights and public scrutiny in the planning system.

Supported by: Rosie Kane*, Mark Ballard*, Shiona Baird*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, John Swinburne*, Chris Ballance*, Mike Pringle*, Mike Rumbles*, Ms Sandra White*

*S2M-3137 Shiona Baird: Fair Milk Prices for Farmers—That the Parliament shares the anger of NFU Scotland over continuing exploitation of dairy farmers by the big four supermarkets, Tesco, Asda, Morrisons and Sainsbury’s, and milk processors; notes new research by the Milk Development Council which reveals that, despite supermarkets increasing the price of a litre of milk by 3.5 pence, virtually none of that increase has been seen by the farmers; remains concerned that farmers are paid less than the costs of producing milk, and calls again for an independent supermarket regulator and a stronger code of conduct to control the trading practices of the big four supermarket chains.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson*, Campbell Martin*, Patrick Harvie*, Roseanna Cunningham*, Chris Ballance*, Mr Stewart Maxwell*, Ms Sandra White*, Rosie Kane*, Brian Adam*, Bruce Crawford*, Rob Gibson*

*S2M-3136 Alex Neil: Judicial Appointments—That the Parliament considers that, in light of its existence and its responsibility for justice matters, it is absurd that the Prime Minister is still responsible for the appointment of the Lord Justice General; further considers that it is insulting that the First Minister is relegated to the role of an adviser in respect of this appointment, and calls for the urgent introduction of an Order in Council to transfer responsibility for all judicial appointments from Westminster to the Scottish Parliament.
