Business Bulletin No. 84/2009: Tuesday 19 May 2009
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 18 May 2009

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-24057Δ Alasdair Allan: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to publish its consultation paper on a draft crofting reform Bill.

S3W-24058 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-22908 by Nicola Sturgeon on 11 May 2009, when it will publish the responses to the consultation on the establishment of the Care Environment Inspectorate on the NHS Quality Improvement Scotland website.

S3W-24059 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what the 10 most common offences were for which (a) men and (b) women in Edinburgh were sent to prison in each of the last five years, broken down by age group and number of offences.

S3W-24060 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what proportion of the housing stock in the Lothians region was affordable housing in each year since 1999, broken down by local authority area.

S3W-24061 Gavin Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what the (a) longest and (b) shortest sentence has been for anyone convicted of (i) drug dealing, (ii) possession of drugs, (iii) drug trafficking, (iv) employing others to deal in drugs and (v) living off immoral earnings in the Lothians region in each of the last five years.

S3W-24062 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how much it spent running the Safer Scotland knife amnesty, which ran from 24 May to 30 June 2006, broken down by expense.

S3W-24063 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many fine defaulters prosecuted for handling an offensive weapon subsequently served prison sentences in 2007-08 and what proportion that figure constitutes of the total number of (a) people served with fines and (b) fine defaulters.

S3W-24064 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people were prosecuted for handling an offensive weapon in 2007-08, broken down by (a) main penalty and (b) police force area.

S3W-24065 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people prosecuted for handling an offensive weapon in 2007-08 had a criminal record at the time of prosecution, broken down by most recent offence.

S3W-24066 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people prosecuted for handling an offensive weapon in 2007-08 had previously been prosecuted for such an offence, broken down by main penalty issued.

S3W-24067 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people prosecuted for handling an offensive weapon in 2007-08 were on bail at the time of their offence, broken down by police force area.

S3W-24068 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive how many allied health professionals there were in NHS Tayside in (a) arts therapy, (b) dietetics, (c) occupational therapy, (d) orthoptics, (e) orthotics, (f) podiatry, (g) prosthetics, (h) speech and language therapy and (i) radiography in each of the last two years for which information is available, expressed as headcount and as full-time equivalent.

S3W-24069 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what projects have been allocated funding from the action plan for youth football and how much each project has been awarded.

S3W-24070 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has received an application from Aberdeen City Council for funding from sportscotland’s Building for Sport programme.

S3W-24071 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what arrangements it has put in place to provide funding to local authorities for community sports arenas.

S3W-24072 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what commitment it has given to the proposed 50-metre swimming pool in Aberdeen.

S3W-24073 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met officials from Aberdeen City Council to discuss the proposals for a 50-metre swimming pool.

S3W-24074 Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has given a funding commitment to Aberdeen City Council in respect of the proposed 50-metre swimming pool and, if so, whether it is subject to any conditions.

S3W-24075 Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many individuals aged (a) 18 and under, (b) 19 to 24, (c) 25 to 34, (d) 35 to 44 and (e) 45 and over underwent plastic surgery procedures following an exceptional referral in each of the last five years, broken down by type of procedure.

S3W-24076 Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive how many plastic surgery procedures have been carried out following an exceptional referral in each of the last five years, broken down by type of procedure and NHS board.

S3W-24077 Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive what the median waiting time was for patients undergoing plastic surgery procedures in each of the last five years, broken down by NHS board.

S3W-24078 John Park: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is aware of any plans to remove the A977 from the primary road network.

S3W-24079 John Park: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will make additional funds available to Perth and Kinross Council to introduce traffic-calming measures to meet the needs of increased road usage caused by traffic diverted from the Forth Road Bridge.

S3W-24080 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has discussed with the UK Department of Health the development of the Family Nurse Partnership programme in England since 2007 and, if so, on how many occasions.

S3W-24081 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive how many of its staff are employed in the piloting and evaluation of the Family Nurse Partnership model; when these posts were advertised, and when they commence.

S3W-24082 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive whether its staff posts related to the piloting of the Family Nurse Partnership involve secondment to the Family Nurse Partnership programme in England and, if so, for what period of time.

S3W-24083 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Lothian pilot of the Family Nurse Partnership will be completed and when it will be evaluated.

S3W-24084 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding is being made available for the Lothian pilot of the Family Nurse Partnership.

S3W-24085 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive what grades of nurses are participating in the Lothian pilot of the Family Nurse Partnership.

S3W-24086 David McLetchie: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-23444 by Alex Neil on 12 May 2009, what the maximum monetary discount under the modernised right to buy would be if it were indexed by reference to the rise in house prices, not the retail price index.

S3W-24087 David McLetchie: To ask the Scottish Executive what index or indices it uses to analyse movements in house prices.

S3W-24088 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-23453 by Keith Brown on 15 May 2009, how much of the £23 billion is to be spent on anti-sectarianism projects in schools in the period 2008 to 2010, broken down by local authority.

S3W-24089 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-23453 by Keith Brown on 15 May 2009, whether it will reinstate funding for anti-sectarianism projects in schools in the 2009-10 academic year.

S3W-24090 Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to encourage twinning of denominational and non-denominational schools in the 2009-10 academic year.

S3W-24091 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what the (a) design capacity, (b) assessed operational limit and (c) average number of inmates held at each (i) prison and (ii) young offenders institution was in each month in 2008.

S3W-24092 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive what the (a) design capacity, (b) assessed operational limit and (c) average number of inmates held at each (i) prison and (ii) young offenders institution was in each month of 2009 for which information is available.

S3W-24093 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive what adaptations would be required to the Family Nurse Partnership programme in England to allow it to be provided in Scotland.

S3W-24094 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Family Nurse Partnership pilot in Lothian will operate under the same type of licence agreement as that in England.

S3W-24095 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive whether ministers have visited locations in the United States of America where the Family Nurse Partnership scheme is in operation and, if so, which ministers; on how many occasions, and on what dates.

S3W-24096 Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider funding climate change adaptation projects in developing countries from its environment budget.

S3W-24097 Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive how many local authorities are operating projects as part of the Youth Music Initiative.

S3W-24098 Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive which hospitals operate a cook-chill process for inpatient food.
