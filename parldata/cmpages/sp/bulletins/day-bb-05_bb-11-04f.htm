Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-3518 Carolyn Leckie: Haemophilia Blood Product Disaster—That the Parliament recognises the continuing flow of new information pointing to complacency, neglect and culpability at the root of the haemophilia blood product disaster, including the recent film by Kelly Duda, Factor 8: The Arkansas Prison Blood Scandal; believes that this film is essential viewing for the mainstream media in Scotland, not least for the information it provides to haemophiliacs, especially since the Government has admitted that it has destroyed significant and relevant documents, and further believes that nothing less than a full independent public inquiry is necessary to get to the truth of the circumstances that have caused horrendous levels of infection from blood products in the haemophiliac population and that final settlement in relation to compensation should only be expected to occur when the full, truthful information is in the public domain.

*S2M-3517 Michael Matheson: Major Award for "Big in Falkirk"—That the Parliament congratulates Falkirk Council and partners for winning a 2005 Scottish Thistle Award for "Big in Falkirk"; acknowledges the high standard of its competition, Edinburgh’s Hogmanay and International Pairs, Fife; praises the hard work and dedication of all involved in organising and participating in the event which is now recognised as Scotland’s number one street arts festival, and is confident that the winning of this award will mean that "Big in Falkirk" will continue to evolve and grow into a major international arts festival.Supported by: Alex Neil*, Linda Fabiani*

*S2M-3516 Mr Frank McAveety: Kerb Crawling—That the Parliament commends the Scottish Executive’s recent announcement of more objective standards for prosecuting kerb crawling, noting the exceptional work of Glasgow City Council and agencies in combating street prostitution for many years in both the Calton and Glasgow Green areas, and hopes that the problems created by street prostitution continue to be tackled and that all the relevant major agencies work together to reduce the level of street prostitution and to ensure that our local communities are safer places to live and work in.

*S2M-3515 Lewis Macdonald: The Food Protection (Emergency Prohibitions) (Amnesic Shellfish Poisoning) (West Coast) (No. 14) (Scotland) Order 2005—That the Health Committee recommends that the Food Protection (Emergency Prohibitions) (Amnesic Shellfish Poisoning) (West Coast) (No. 14) (Scotland) Order 2005 (SSI 2005/529) be approved.Supported by: Mr Andy Kerr*

*S2M-3514 Sarah Boyack: Further Congratulations to Edinburgh Junior Chamber of Commerce—That the Parliament congratulates Edinburgh Junior Chamber of Commerce on the recent award of "Most Outstanding Chamber in the World" for the third successive year, announced at the Junior Chamber International (JCI) World Congress in Vienna; believes that this achievement further enhances Scotland’s global reputation for entrepreneurship combined with social responsibility, and commends the JCI network on its work in encouraging young business people to be active citizens through community enterprises, training projects and international initiatives.Supported by: Margaret Smith*

*S2M-3513 Shiona Baird: Scottish Parliament Visitor and Outreach Services—That the Parliament congratulates members of the Visitor and Outreach Services team on their work in taking the Scottish Parliament out to the people of Scotland; believes that, by facilitating greater participation by individuals, organisations and communities, the outreach team has already made significant progress towards achieving its aim of encouraging greater political awareness and engagement by the public across Scotland; notes the overwhelmingly positive and enthusiastic response by members of the public who attended recent outreach events in Arbroath, Inverurie and Peterhead in the north-east region; recognises that community-based parliamentary events have an important role to play in helping MSPs reach out to constituents and in educating the general public about the work of the Parliament; considers that outreach events have already helped increase public confidence in, and accessibility to, the Parliament and may ultimately help boost electoral turnout, and believes that continuing support for outreach services should remain a key priority for the Parliament if it is to succeed in becoming a truly inclusive institution.

*S2M-3512 Mr Mark Ruskell: Climate Change - Prime Minister’s U-turn—That the Parliament notes the comments on climate change made by Tony Blair to a summit of G8 energy and environment ministers in London earlier this week; condemns Mr Blair’s craven submission to those misguided nations who wrongly believe that binding targets for the emissions of greenhouse gases will somehow undermine their economies; strongly believes that a sustainable environment need not be incompatible with a strong and sustainable economy, and acknowledges that, while reducing such emissions is a challenge, it is one that must be faced if we are to avoid the far greater challenge of a rapidly changing climate.
