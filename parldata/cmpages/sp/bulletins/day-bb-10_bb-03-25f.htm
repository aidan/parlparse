Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (♦).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S3M-6060 Liam McArthur: Amnesty on Sheep EID Penalties—That the Parliament welcomes the call made by the European Parliament’s Agricultural Committee for an amnesty of three years on cross-compliance penalties relating to electronic identification (EID) of sheep and goats; notes that the committee made this call because this is a new and complex technology that will require some time for farmers to become accustomed to it and for the systems to be road tested; believes that such an amnesty would remove the current threat of high fines that Scottish farmers and crofters face as they struggle to cope with the technology, which many believe has been shown to be far from perfect by the current pilot, and calls on the Scottish Government to seek the agreement of the European Commission for the early introduction of such an amnesty in Scotland.

Supported by: Tavish Scott*, Jamie Stone*, John Farquhar Munro*

*S3M-6059 Jamie Stone: Best of Luck to Ross County FC—That the Parliament congratulates Ross County Football Club on its victory over Hibernian Football Club in the quarter-final of the Active Nation Scottish Cup; notes that this historic win comes after a recent derby success over local rivals Inverness Caledonian Thistle Football Club, and wishes the club success in its forthcoming semi-final against Celtic Football Club.

*S3M-6058 Dr Richard Simpson: Purple Day, Raising Awareness of Epilepsy—That the Parliament welcomes Purple Day on Friday 26 March 2010, which aims to raise awareness of epilepsy; notes that around 40,000 Scots are affected by epilepsy; hopes that MSPs and staff will show their support by wearing something purple on that day in order to support the national charity, Epilepsy Scotland, and wishes its chief executive the best of luck with her trek up the Great Glen to raise funds for the charity.

*S3M-6057 Michael Matheson: Walk Around the Block Protest—That the Parliament recognises the need for the regeneration of Denny town centre; notes with concern the growing frustration in the Denny and Dunipace area at the lack of progress in taking this regeneration forward, which resulted in the "walk around the block" protest where more than 1,000 people signed a petition calling on the administration of Falkirk Council to bring forward an alternative plan for the regeneration of the town, and considers that further years of inaction on this matter will not be tolerated by the local community.

*S3M-6056 John Swinney: Housing (Scotland) Bill: Financial Resolution—That the Parliament, for the purposes of any Act of the Scottish Parliament resulting from the Housing (Scotland) Bill, agrees to any expenditure, charge or payment of a kind referred to in Rules 9.12.3(b)(ii) and 9.12.4 of the Parliament’s Standing Orders arising in consequence of the Act.

*S3M-6055 Dave Thompson: Young Consumers of the Year Scotland 2010—That the Parliament congratulates Boroughmuir High School in Edinburgh on its victory in the Scottish final of the Young Consumers of the Year 2010; notes that the school’s team of Douglas Boubert, Imogen Ross, Elspie Horne and Alice MacKenzie beat three other finalists to win the prestigious competition for the third consecutive year; congratulates the other three teams from Dingwall Academy, Ayr Academy and Linlithgow Academy for beating off tough regional competition to reach the Scottish final; further considers that the competition, which is run by the Trading Standards Institute and Scottish local authorities and sponsored by Consumer Focus Scotland, is a great opportunity for young people to learn more about their consumer rights and responsibilities, and wishes the Boroughmuir High School team every success when it goes on to represent Scotland at the UK final at the Trading Standards Institute annual conference in Edinburgh in June 2010.

Supported by: Rob Gibson*, Shirley-Anne Somerville*, Kenneth Gibson*, Michael Matheson*, Sandra White*, John Wilson*, Brian Adam*, Mike Pringle*, Jamie Stone*

*S3M-6054 Rob Gibson: BBC Alba Freeview Decision Delay—That the Parliament notes with disappointment the decision by the BBC Trust to delay announcing a decision on whether BBC Alba will be made available on Freeview; notes the success of the station so far and considers that the vast majority of people in Scotland responding to the trust’s consultation on the issue were overwhelmingly supportive; further considers that there is cross-party political support for such a move as well as support from BBC management, and therefore urges the trust to make a speedy decision to make the station available on Freeview.

*S3M-6053 Rob Gibson: Historic Progress at Ross County FC—That the Parliament congratulates the players, coaches and everyone connected with Ross County Football Club on its stunning victory of two goals to one against Hibernian Football Club in the quarter-finals of the Active Nation Scottish Cup; further applauds the sell-out crowd for making an extra special atmosphere on an extra special night in Dingwall; considers that this victory is a monumental result for football in the Highlands and Islands, and wishes all those connected with the club the best of luck against Celtic Football Club in the semi-final at Hampden and for the rest of the season.

Supported by: Dave Thompson*

*S3M-6052 Anne McLaughlin: Fair Fares for Glaswegians—That the Parliament notes that First in Glasgow is yet again increasing its bus fares for passengers in Glasgow; is concerned that fares on some routes have increased by 71% from 2003 to 2010, at a time when the cost of diesel has risen by 42%, and calls on First in Glasgow to reverse its decision to raise fares and to give the people of Glasgow a fair fare.

Supported by: Bob Doris*, Christina McKelvie*, Bill Kidd*, Aileen Campbell*, Stuart McMillan*

S3M-6038 Sarah Boyack: Number Shop Cut Doesn’t Add Up—That the Parliament expresses its deep concern at the City of Edinburgh Council’s proposals to close the Number Shop, a project that has helped thousands of adults across Edinburgh improve their numeracy and literacy skills over its 25-year history, as part of a programme of cuts to save £120,000; notes the concerns of the Friends of the Number Shop campaign group that the centre is irreplaceable because of its specialist teaching equipment, ease of disabled access and supportive atmosphere; recognises that the Number Shop’s central location makes it a facility that can be accessed to the benefit of people across the city; further notes the campaign’s assertion that plans to provide the service through other facilities will not result in any savings on staff costs, resulting in only minor savings and thus making the closure pointless; further notes that the Number Shop is one of six community centres and countless other community facilities faced with closure following the passing of the City of Edinburgh Council’s budget, and considers that these closures could have been avoided through Labour amendments that would have seen cuts made to external consultants, unnecessary schemes, non-essential back-office functions and pay freezes for chief officers and those earning more than £40,000 a year.

Supported by: Hugh Henry*, George Foulkes*, Jackie Baillie*, Elaine Smith*, Dr Richard Simpson*, Mary Mulligan*, Pauline McNeill*, Marilyn Livingstone*, Rhona Brankin*

*S3M-6038.1 Shirley-Anne Somerville: Number Shop Cut Doesn’t Add Up—As an amendment to motion (S3M-6038) in the name of Sarah Boyack, leave out after "to the benefit of people across the city" to end and insert "; notes that this is only a proposal and that no final decision has been made by the council, and further believes that such proposals would have been avoided had Labour, Liberal Democrat, Conservative and Green party members not voted in both the Scottish Parliament and the City Chambers in favour of the plan for Tram Line 1a based on an entirely flawed business case, the consequence of which is that the city is having to pay the cost in next year’s budget and for many years to come."

S3M-6011 Bill Wilson: Renfrew Ferry Saved—That the Parliament notes the awarding of a new Renfrew ferry service contract to Silvers Marine; congratulates Silvers Marine on winning this contract, and also congratulates Renfrewshire Council and all those who campaigned to overturn the original decision of the Labour-dominated Strathclyde Partnership for Transport to close the 500-year-old Renfrew ferry.

Supported by: Gil Paterson*, Aileen Campbell*

*S3M-6011.1 Ms Wendy Alexander: Renfrew Ferry Saved—As an amendment to motion (S3M-6011) in the name of Bill Wilson, leave out after "service contract" to end and insert "by Strathclyde Partnership for Transport (SPT) to Argyll-based Silvers Marine; congratulates Silvers Marine on winning this contract; also congratulates all those who campaigned to keep a ferry crossing, particularly Renfrew local resident Liz Grant, and commends the hard work of officials at SPT to ensure that a service is retained on the Clyde, with no interruption in service to passengers, while also saving nearly £500,000 of taxpayers money."
