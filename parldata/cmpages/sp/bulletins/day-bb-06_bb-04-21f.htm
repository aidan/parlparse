Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-4275 Linda Fabiani: The Panchen Lama—That the Parliament notes that 25 April 2006 is the 17th birthday of Gedhun Choekyi Nyima, Tibet’s Panchen Lama; notes that in May 1995, at the age of six, a few weeks after being named by HH The Dalai Lama as the new reincarnation of the Panchen Lama, the second most important leader in Tibet, Gedhun Choekyi Nyima was abducted from his home in Tibet by the Chinese authorities and that no-one there has seen him or heard from him since; sends all good wishes to the Panchen Lama, and urges the international community to continue its efforts to secure his safety.

*S2M-4274# Margaret Jamieson: Future Funding of Borderline Theatre Company—That the Parliament recognises the contribution of Borderline Theatre Company during its 32 years of work from its base in Ayrshire, delivering quality, professional entertainment to almost a million people across Scotland as well as 1,100 workshops to 18,168 participants in its extensive lifelong learning programme; condemns the decision of the Scottish Arts Council (SAC) to cease to provide revenue funding for Borderline Theatre Company; deplores the actions of the SAC in changing the funding criteria during the application process, and believes that an examination should take place of the processes and outcomes of SAC funding decisions.

Supported by: Ms Rosemary Byrne*

*S2M-4273 Dr Jean Turner: Scottish Monument in Flanders—That the Parliament commends the people of Zonnebeke in Flanders for their efforts in raising funds to build a Scottish monument, a Celtic cross made from Corrennie Pink granite from Scotland, which will be dedicated to all the Scottish soldiers who fought in the Great War, wherever they came from; notes that it is not aimed at glorifying war but that its sole purpose is to remember all Scottish involvement, from the Canadian Scottish to the 1st South African Brigade which was part of the 9th (Scottish) Division; further notes that there is no monument at present in any part of the Ypres Salient dedicated to any Scottish regiments and that this monument will be a tribute to every Scottish soldier, whatever his regiment; recognises the efforts of the local authorities of Passchendaele, Wytschaete and Messines for their planning of year-long events in 2007 to commemorate the 90th anniversary of the Third Battle of Ypres, otherwise known as "Passchendaele" in which there were over 500,000 British and German casualties, and further recognises that the Celtic cross will be an important, permanent reminder in keeping alive the memory of the Scottish soldiers from the Great War and will also serve as a wonderful testament to the people of Flanders in showing their thanks for the sacrifice of the Scottish soldiers and also to those Scots whose final resting place is far away from home in "Flanders Fields".

*S2M-4267 Ross Finnie: Animal Health and Welfare (Scotland) Bill—That the Parliament agrees that the Animal Health and Welfare (Scotland) Bill be passed.

*S2M-4266 Ms Wendy Alexander: Congratulations to St Mirren Football Club—That the Parliament recognises the fantastic achievement of St Mirren Football Club in winning the First Division Championship and, with it, promotion to the Scottish Premier League; pays tribute to Gus MacPherson and his team for a successful season and capturing two trophies, and wishes the team well in the Scottish Premier League next year.

Supported by: Colin Fox*, Michael McMahon*, Mrs Mary Mulligan*, Jackie Baillie*, Mr Bruce McFee*, Brian Adam*, Mr Frank McAveety*, Mr Jamie Stone*, Dr Elaine Murray*, Bill Butler*, Jim Mather*, Murray Tosh*, Christine May*, Karen Gillon*, Trish Godman*

*S2M-4265 Brian Adam: No to Closure of the Raeden Centre, Aberdeen—That the Parliament welcomes the proposals to abandon the closure of the Raeden Centre in Aberdeen and the promise that there will be no withdrawal of services for children currently attending the Raeden Centre and commends the stakeholders, particularly the parents, for their successful campaign.

*S2M-4264 Rosie Kane: Hazizi Family Released—That the Parliament is delighted that the Hazizi family have been released from Yarlswood Detention Centre in Bedfordshire; deeply regrets that they were subjected to a brutal dawn raid on 18 April 2006; agrees that this release once again shows that there was never any need to smash this family’s door in, drag them from their beds, keep them from school and university, drive them from Glasgow to Bedfordshire in a Home Office van and place them into detention; hopes that the Hazizi family recovers soon from its ordeal, and demands that the Scottish Executive recognises that many asylum seekers are taken into detention and removed, despite the fact that their case has not reached an end and that they cannot therefore be labelled as "failed asylum seekers", which is a term that allows politicians to wash their hands of the plight of innocent, vulnerable and frightened individuals and families.
