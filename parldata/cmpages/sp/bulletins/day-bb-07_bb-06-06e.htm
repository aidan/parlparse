Business Bulletin No. 82/2007: Wednesday 6 June 2007
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 5 June 2007

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S3W-511 John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take to improve doctors’ out-of-hours services in the Scottish Borders.

S3W-512 John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will support the reopening and development of Jedburgh and Coldstream hospitals in the Scottish Borders, in light of its decision to support the retention of accident and emergency services at Ayr and Monklands hospitals.

S3W-513 John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will support local communities that object to the building of new wind farm developments in the Scottish Borders.

S3W-514 Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive when it intends to publish the report on the evaluation of the Rural Voices programme which funded action research projects in rural communities and whether it will consider reviving Rural Voices as part of the new Scottish Rural Development Programme.

S3W-515 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many convictions for possession of an offensive weapon there were in the Grampian Police area in each of the last five years for which information is available.

S3W-516 Richard Baker: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people aged under 18 were convicted of possession of an offensive weapon in the Grampian Police area in each of the last five years for which information is available.

S3W-517 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to widen access to higher education for young people.

S3W-518 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to widen access to higher education for disabled people.

S3W-519 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to accredit informal learning as a means of accessing further and higher education.

S3W-520 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive how many students (a) across Scotland and (b) in the Lothians are currently in receipt of young persons’ bursaries.

S3W-521 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive how many students (a) across Scotland and (b) in the Lothians are currently in receipt of full young students’ bursaries.

S3W-522 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive when it intends to publish its plans for part-time student support.

S3W-523 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive when it intends to publish its plans for postgraduate student support.

S3W-524 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive how many students are currently exempt from the graduate endowment, broken down by exemption category.

S3W-525 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what the (a) annual and (b) total cost will be of abolishing the graduate endowment.

S3W-526 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether plans to scrap the graduate endowment would have retrospective application.

S3W-527 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to fully implement the findings of the Review of Scotland’s Colleges and when it intends to publish its plans on the matter.

S3W-528 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to publish plans to abolish the student loans system and, if so, when.

S3W-529 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what the projected cost is of abolishing the student loans system.

S3W-530 Jim Hume: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will ensure that maternity services that do not currently meet the NHS Quality Improvement Scotland standards in respect of routine second ultrasound scans are able to do so in the near future.

S3W-531 David Whitton: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made towards awarding the contract to build the new Low Moss prison at Bishopbriggs.

S3W-532 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive what the number and percentage of jobs in the public sector have been in each of the last five years in (a) Dundee City, (b) Angus and (c) Aberdeenshire local authority areas.

S3W-533 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average annual number of long-term unemployed people has been in Dundee and what percentage of the total number of unemployed they accounted for in each of the last five years.

S3W-534 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average annual number of long-term unemployed people has been in Angus and what percentage of the total number of unemployed they accounted for in each of the last five years.

S3W-535 Marlyn Glen: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average annual number of long-term unemployed people has been in Aberdeenshire and what percentage of the total number of unemployed they accounted for in each of the last five years.

S3W-536 Peter Peacock: To ask the Scottish Executive whether a local authority councillor, who declares a principled opposition to particular types of development which may become the subject of planning applications, can thereafter take part in any consideration of any planning application concerning that type of development.

S3W-537 Peter Peacock: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to announce continuation of the Violence Against Women Fund; whether it is proposing any steps to assist local authorities to match any funds that it makes available, and whether any delay in announcing funding has had an impact on any projects run by voluntary organisations across Scotland.

S3W-538 Peter Peacock: To ask the Scottish Executive what importance it attaches to the funding to support parenting orders which has been provided to sustain family support work in a variety of contexts and which is designed to enable families to operate effectively in family units.

S3W-539 John Park: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to consult local people adjacent to the Forth Road Bridge on its intention to remove tolls from the crossing.

S3W-540 John Park: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures will be put in place to ensure appropriate filtering onto the Forth Road Bridge if tolls are removed.

S3W-541 John Park: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to alleviate traffic in the Rosyth area if the Forth Road Bridge is closed to heavy goods vehicles in 2013-14.

S3W-542 John Park: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to redeploy Forth Estuary Transport Authority employees who find their posts redundant following the removal of tolls from the Forth Road Bridge.

S3W-543 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what the total costs were to constabularies across Scotland of policing official visits by the Prime Minister in each year since 1999.

S3W-544 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what the total costs were to constabularies across Scotland of policing official visits by the Home Secretary in each year since 1999.

S3W-545 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what the total costs were to constabularies across Scotland of policing official visits by the Chancellor of the Exchequer in each year since 1999.

S3W-546 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many officers were deployed by constabularies across Scotland to police official visits by the Prime Minister in each year since 1999.

S3W-547 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many officers were deployed by constabularies across Scotland to police official visits by the Home Secretary in each year since 1999.

S3W-548 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many officers were deployed by constabularies across Scotland to police official visits by the Chancellor of the Exchequer in each year since 1999.

S3W-549 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many official visits by the Chancellor of the Exchequer have required dedicated Scottish police resources in each year since 1999.

S3W-550 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many official visits by the Prime Minister have required dedicated Scottish police resources in each year since 1999.

S3W-551 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many official visits by the Home Secretary have required dedicated Scottish police resources in each year since 1999.

S3W-552 Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive to what extent it takes Highland Council’s strategy for renewable energy into consideration when granting planning permission for renewable energy projects in the Highlands.
