Business Bulletin No. 67 / 2004

Thursday 22nd April 2004

Section D : Oral questions selected for answer on Thursday 29th April 2004
  

Question Time

Environment and Rural Development

1. Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive how Scottish perspectives will be integrated into the review of the UK Sustainable Development Strategy. (S2O-2197)

2. Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will direct the Deer Commission for Scotland to suspend any further mass cull of deer and from whom it has sought advice in the issues involved, other than its own advisers and the Deer Commission. (S2O-2148)

3. Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what proportion of climate change emissions is from damaged peatlands and what action it is taking to restore damaged bogs. (S2O-2183)

4. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making in the pursuit of environmental justice. (S2O-2195)

5. Mr Mark Ruskell: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans and resources it has in place to deal with the impacts of climate change. (S2O-2181)

6. Alex Johnstone: To ask the Scottish Executive how many copies it plans to publish of the finalised Outdoor Access Code. (S2O-2095)

7. Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to aid the development of rural Scotland, given the impact of a lack of affordable housing in rural areas. (S2O-2092)

8. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive what recent consideration it has given to the application of the Nature Conservation (Scotland) Bill to land owned by the Queen in her private capacity. (S2O-2134)

9. Patrick Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on Her Majesty's Government's chief scientist's comment that climate change is a bigger threat than terrorism. (S2O-2179)

10. Alex Fergusson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is committed to a national envelope for the specialist beef sector within common agricultural policy reform. (S2O-2153)

11. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it has in place to reduce the impact of plastic waste on the marine environment. (S2O-2174)

12. Mark Ballard: To ask the Scottish Executive what opportunities are afforded by climate change. (S2O-2180)

Health and Community Care

1. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it is giving to establish accessible community-based health facilities. (S2O-2173)

2. Dr Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to reduce GP waiting times. (S2O-2129)

3. Karen Gillon: To ask the Scottish Executive what NHS services are being decentralised to smaller hospitals, GPs and primary care teams. (S2O-2201)

4. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to improve diabetes care. (S2O-2211)

5. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive what the percentage increase has been in respect of ambulance call-out rates as a result of the introduction of NHS 24. (S2O-2165)

6. Mr Jamie McGrigor: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it intends to downgrade maternity services at the Lorne and Islands District General Hospital, Oban. (S2O-2167)

7. Elaine Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what the impact would be on health care of the use of a voucher or passport system allowing the purchase of private health care. (S2O-2202)

8. Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has taken any recent steps to address concerns of families who have an adult member with learning difficulties living in the family home and what support it intends to give such families. (S2O-2221)

9. Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on whether there should be no reduction of services at the Belford Hospital, Fort William. (S2O-2149)

10. Mrs Nanette Milne: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to establish the competency of professionals who have gained their medical qualifications and training in the EU accession states and who seek to work in the health services in Scotland. (S2O-2090)

11. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive what the timetable is for consideration of NHS Greater Glasgow's recommendation to close the Queen Mother's Hospital in Glasgow. (S2O-2084)

12. Mr Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to promote physical activity as a method of preventing heart disease. (S2O-2117)

General

1. Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made towards improving international air links to Scotland. (S2O-02157)

2. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many local authorities have agreed to participate in the voluntary arrangement whereby community amateur sports clubs will receive at least 80% rates relief. (S2O-02136)

3. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive whether there will, as a matter of urgency, be a presumption against school closures. (S2O-02170)

4. Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will investigate the possibility of using NHS surplus land for the development of affordable housing. (S2O-02222)

5. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in raising the limits for small claims in the courts. (S2O-02154)

6. Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to encourage sport for all. (S2O-02155)

7. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making on its target to eradicate homelessness. (S2O-02171)

8. Fergus Ewing: To ask the Scottish Executive how much it estimates it will spend, in total, on public relations in this and the next two financial years and whether it will take any steps to reduce these figures. (S2O-02150)

9. Mike Rumbles: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met representatives of Aberdeenshire Council and what issues were discussed. (S2O-02089)

10. Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made with regard to the relocation of civil service jobs. (S2O-02086)

11. Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider raising fines against landlords for non-compliance with a multiple occupancy licence as a deterrent to unscrupulous landlords. (S2O-02125)

12. Eleanor Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to encourage the use of Green Tourism Business Scheme accredited accommodation. (S2O-02190)

13. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps have been taken to support the introduction of direct air links to the new EU accession states. (S2O-02145)

14. Bruce Crawford: To ask the Scottish Executive what specific funds are available to help cities and towns to attract tourists. (S2O-02139)

15. Carolyn Leckie: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to develop waste land for the benefit of young people. (S2O-02141)
