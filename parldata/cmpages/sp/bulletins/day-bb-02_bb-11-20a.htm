Business Bulletin No. 168/2002

Wednesday 20 November 2002

Section A : Daily Business List
  

Meetings of Committees

All meetings take place in Committee Chambers, Edinburgh unless otherwise indicated.

Transport and the Environment Committee

32nd Meeting, 2002

The Committee will meet at 9.30 am in Committee Room 1

1. Items in private: The Committee will consider whether to take agenda item 5 in private.

2. Water Environment and Water Services (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will consider the Bill at Stage 2 (Day 2).

3. Subordinate legislation: The Committee will consider the following negative instrument-

Water Customer Consultation Panels (Scotland) Order 2002, (SSI, 2002/473).

4. Building (Scotland) Bill (private): The Committee will consider a draft report on Stage 1 of the Bill.

5. Work programme: The Committee will consider its work programme.

Justice 2 Committee

43rd Meeting, 2002

The Committee will meet at 9.45 am in the Chamber, Assembly Hall, The Mound, Edinburgh

1. Crown Office and Procurator Fiscal Service Inquiry: The Committee will consider the reporter's remit.

2. Subordinate legislation: The Committee will consider the following negative instrument-

The Discontinuance of Legalised Police Cells (Ayr) Rules 2002 (SSI 2002/472).

3. Criminal Justice (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will consider the Bill at Stage 2 (Day 3).

Health and Community Care Committee

30th Meeting, 2002

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in the Hub, Castlehill, Edinburgh

1. Items in private: The Committee will consider whether to take items 3 and 4 in private.

2. Inquiry into GM crops: The Committee will take evidence from-

Dr Charles Saunders, British Medical Association

Professor Allan Gray, Advisory Committee on Releases to the Environment (ACRE)

Professor Janet Bainbridge, Advisory Committee on Releases to the Environment (ACRE)

Dr Steven Hill, Advisory Committee on Releases to the Environment (ACRE)

Professor Tony Trewaves, Royal Society of Edinburgh

Professor Chris Lamb, Royal Society of Edinburgh

Ross Finnie MSP, Minister for Environment and Rural Development

Derek Bearhop, Scottish Executive.

3. Witness expenses: The Committee will consider a claim under the witness expenses scheme.

4. Mental Health (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will consider a draft Stage 1 Report.

Social Justice Committee

21st Meeting, 2002

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in Committee Room 2

1. Homelessness etc. (Scotland) Bill: The Committee will take evidence on the general principles of the Bill at Stage 1 from-

Hugh Henry, MSP, Deputy Minister for Social Justice.

2. Petition PE396: The Committee will consider its response to petition PE396 by Mr Nick Fletcher calling for the Scottish Parliament to take the necessary steps to ensure that the citizens of Scotland continue to have access to free and independent advice services.

Standards Committee

16th Meeting, 2002

The Committee will meet at 10.00 am in Committee Room 3

1. Item in private: The Committee will consider whether to take item 4 and 5 in private.

2. Cross-Party Groups: The Committee will consider responses to its consultation paper on Cross-Party Groups.

3. Lobbying: The Committee will consider a report from the Deputy Convener concerning a conference on lobbying in Budapest in October 2002.

4. Scottish Parliamentary Business Exchange: The Committee will consider a draft report.

5. Complaint against Christine Grahame MSP: The Committee will consider a draft report.

debate on the subject of-

S1M-3486 Mr Duncan McNeil: Mis-selling of Utilities-That the Parliament notes the proposal of energy regulator, Ofgem, to penalise London Electricity for failing to prevent its sales staff from mis-selling products to customers; expresses concern over the high-pressure selling tactics employed by representatives of certain utility companies; believes that vulnerable members of the public are entitled to protection from such practices; seeks clarification over what safeguards are currently in place and how these are enforced, and considers that the industry, the Scottish Executive and all interested parties should undertake a concerted effort to put an end to underhand sales practices and restore public confidence in the utilities market.
