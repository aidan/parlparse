Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-4534 Shona Robison: Fifth National Men’s Health Week—That the Parliament welcomes the fifth National Men’s Health Week, which takes place between 12 and 18 June 2006; notes that this year’s theme is men and mental well-being; remains concerned that suicide is the most common cause of death in young men under 35, that studies suggest depression occurs as often in men as in women but that men are less likely to be diagnosed and that one unemployed man in seven will develop depression within six months of losing his job; congratulates National Men’s Health Week for seeking to raise awareness about these and other issues and for encouraging the adoption of positive and proactive methods to deal with mental health problems; further notes the importance of Wellman clinics in Scotland which specifically target men to encourage them to use the clinics and which are designed to make them feel comfortable as a way to get men to think about the importance of their health, and calls upon MSPs to help promote this campaign in their constituencies.

*S2M-4533# Cathie Craigie: Assurances Regarding Glasgow to Edinburgh High Speed Rail Link—That the Parliament notes recent press reports regarding various options for a high-speed Glasgow to Edinburgh rail link; believes that the services between Croy and other stations along the main Glasgow to Edinburgh line provide a vital service, particularly for people living in the Cumbernauld and Kilsyth area, and help to boost the local economy; further believes that any consideration of proposals for a Glasgow to Edinburgh bullet train or high-speed link should look very carefully at the needs of people living across the whole of the central belt, and considers that assurances should be given that service levels through Croy station will not face cuts because of any future changes to the rail connection between Glasgow and Edinburgh.

*S2M-4532 Jackie Baillie: Solar Powered Boat on Loch Lomond—That the Parliament welcomes Scotland’s first solar powered boat based in Loch Lomond and the Trossachs National Park; notes that it will be used as a floating classroom, providing passengers with a knowledge of the loch and its islands, crannogs, plants, animals, geology and history; acknowledges that the boat is practically silent, generates no emissions and can accommodate up to 12 passengers, including wheelchair users; considers, in light of the recent good weather, that a solar powered boat is an imaginative and environmentally friendly addition to the national park, and encourages all to visit Loch Lomond and enjoy its natural beauty.

*S2M-4531 Campbell Martin: Journalists in Iraq—That the Parliament recognises the bravery of journalists currently working in Iraq; further recognises the invaluable role played by the journalists in ensuring that what is actually happening in Iraq is relayed to the rest of world; acknowledges that since the invasion of Iraq, which many consider to have been illegal, over 40 journalists and media assistants have been kidnapped and that, in total, more journalists have been killed than UK soldiers, and fully supports the recent decision of the National Union of Journalists at its annual conference that there should be a complete, immediate and unconditional withdrawal of coalition forces from Iraq.
