Business Bulletin No. 001/2002

Monday 7 January 2002

Section D : Oral Questions
  

Question Time

1. David Mundell: To ask the Scottish Executive what role mediation can play in the civil courts. (S1O-4390)

2. Rhona Brankin: To ask the Scottish Executive what importance it attaches to protecting communities in Midlothian from the effects of heavy traffic. (S1O-4387)

3. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made in implementing the Discipline Task Group report Better Behaviour - Better Learning and ensuring that all schools have a discipline policy closely linked to policies for learning and teaching. (S1O-4417)

4. Scott Barrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to support families and improve parenting skills. (S1O-4405)

5. Colin Campbell: To ask the Scottish Executive how many Social Inclusion Partnership boards are under investigation for misapplication of funds. (S1O-4418)

6. Donald Gorrie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to reduce the number of tenants being evicted from their homes. (S1O-4413)

7. Maureen Macmillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken following its For Scotland's children report to address the exclusion of vulnerable children from services through de-registration from general practitioners, school exclusion and eviction. (S1O-4402)

8. Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what its main priorities will be in 2002. (S1O-4400)

9. Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what comparative data concerning the performance of local authorities in meeting the requirements of the Home Energy Conservation Act 1995 is currently available. (S1O-4391)

10. Mr John Home Robertson: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is seeking to promote small businesses across Scotland. (S1O-4389)

11. Rhoda Grant: To ask the Scottish Executive what role partnership with Her Majesty's Government will play in delivering its priorities for the year ahead. (S1O-4401)

12. Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met representatives of the relevant community councils and local authorities to discuss the proposed Loch Lomond and Trossachs National Park. (S1O-4388)

13. Tavish Scott: To ask the Scottish Executive what assessment it has made of the likely economic impact on the fish catching and fish processing industries of the 2002 quotas for whitefish and for pelagic fish landings and what action it plans to take to mitigate any detrimental effects. (S1O-4412)

14. Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress it is making in mainstreaming sustainable development across all areas of policy. (S1O-4403)

15. Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last contacted Scottish Borders Council and what issues were discussed. (S1O-4420)

16. Cathy Peattie: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to support the recruitment of children's panel members. (S1O-4406)

17. Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive why no-one in north east Scotland has been nominated to sit on the board of Scottish Water. (S1O-4392)

18. Stewart Stevenson: To ask the Scottish Executive what support it has given to the voluntary sector for undertaking work with those suffering from drug addiction. (S1O-4386)

19. Dennis Canavan: To ask the Scottish Executive when it expects to wind up the Scottish Transport Group pension funds. (S1O-4410)

20. Lord James Douglas-Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the funding being made available by Railtrack for the Forth Rail Bridge will be honoured by Railtrack or any successor body and what plans it has to ensure the safe upkeep of the bridge. (S1O-4384)

21. Irene McGugan: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been achieved by any Cabinet sub-committee dealing with children's services. (S1O-4395)

22. George Lyon: To ask the Scottish Executive how its proposals for land reform will benefit rural communities. (S1O-4407)

23. Mr Duncan McNeil: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to the first supplementary question to question S1O-4290 by Lewis Macdonald on 13 December 2001, what progress is being made in discussions with Railtrack regarding its decision of 5 December 2001 not to support four Scottish Executive-funded rail enhancement projects. (S1O-4394)

24. Ian Jenkins: To ask the Scottish Executive how it intends to maximise the participation of school pupils in the forthcoming debate on the future of school education. (S1O-4408)

25. Bill Aitken: To ask the Scottish Executive how many current warrants in respect of failure to appear were outstanding at Glasgow Sheriff Court at 31 October 2001 and what the corresponding figure was for 31 October 2000. (S1O-4416)

26. Mr Kenneth Macintosh: To ask the Scottish Executive how many people have been charged with litter offences over the last three years. (S1O-4393)

27. Nora Radcliffe: To ask the Scottish Executive when it intends to publish the transport delivery plan for Scotland. (S1O-4414)

28. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to improve child protection. (S1O-4404)

29. Mr Kenneth Gibson: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with Glasgow City Council regarding planned teacher redundancies. (S1O-4419)

30. Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive when the findings of the GM crop trials will be published. (S1O-4411)
