Business Bulletin No. 181/2002

Tuesday 10 December 2002

Section E : Written questions lodged on 9 December 2002
  

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S1W-32436 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average number of half days of unauthorised absence was per (a) primary and (b) secondary school pupil due to (i) truancy and (ii) temporary exclusion in (1) 1998-99, (2) 1999-2000, (3) 2000-01 and (4) 2001-02, broken down by local authority area.

S1W-32437 Tommy Sheridan: To ask the Scottish Executive what funding for scientific cancer research projects it has made available in each of the last three years and how much it intends to make available for such projects in future years.

S1W-32438 Susan Deacon: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance exists on the procedures for filling permanent vacancies for teaching posts in schools in the light of the McCrone agreement.

S1W-32439 Susan Deacon: To ask the Scottish Executive what monitoring has been carried out into the impact of the appointment of first year probationer teachers under the McCrone agreement on the employment of teachers that had previously been on temporary contracts.

S1W-32440 Susan Deacon: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are in place to ensure that the new arrangements for the appointment of first year probationer teachers under the McCrone agreement are consistently applied across all local authorities.

S1W-32441 Susan Deacon: To ask the Scottish Executive what guidance will be given to local authorities regarding the advertising of teacher vacancies for the 2003-04 session to ensure that teachers currently on temporary or supply contracts have the same opportunities to apply as those that have just completed their probationer year.

S1W-32442 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to assist local authorities in contributing to meet its climate change targets.

S1W-32443 Sarah Boyack: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has estimated the contribution that local authority action will make to meeting its climate change targets.

S1W-32444 Susan Deacon: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in finalising the arrangements for a successor scheme to replace the individual learning accounts scheme.

S1W-32445 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether fire boards need the approval of Scottish ministers when seeking to sell property or land and whether boards are entitled to retain all the receipts of any such sales.

S1W-32446 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive what legislation will inform its consideration of policy on first-time connections to the sewerage system.

S1W-32447 Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it plans to provide any financial assistance for disadvantaged households for first-time connections to the sewerage system.

S1W-32448 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive how many NHS hospital wards remained mixed sex on 31 October 2002, broken down by NHS board area.

S1W-32449 Linda Fabiani: To ask the Scottish Executive whether any consideration is being given to the reform of fire safety legislation.

S1W-32450 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will detail any changes to the curriculum since 1999 that have had an effect on classroom, furniture, fittings and equipment requirement in schools.

S1W-32451 Mr Brian Monteith: To ask the Scottish Executive what the average waiting time for oral surgery has been in each NHS board area in each of the last three years.
