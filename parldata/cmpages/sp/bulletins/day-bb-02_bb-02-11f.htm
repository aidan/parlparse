Monday 11 February 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-2725 Mr Murray Tosh: Ayr United Football Club and a New Stadium —That the Parliament congratulates Ayr United Football Club on its achievement in reaching the final of the CIS Insurance Cup; welcomes the congratulations being offered from all quarters and, in particular, from residents and natives of Kilmarnock and from supporters of Kilmarnock Football Club and also expressed in motion S1M-2713 in the name of Adam Ingram MSP and notes the generous spirit which inspired it; is aware that Ayr United have appeared twice in the final of the B&Q Cup; further notes that planning permission exists for the construction of a new stadium in Heathfield, Ayr and further welcomes the Scottish Executive's abandonment of its defence against the appeal by Heritage Scotland Ltd. against the Scottish Ministers' determination of the planning application; calls for the removal of the onerous conditions attached by the Scottish Ministers to the consent granted for the stadium and looks forward to Ayr United playing Premier League football in a new stadium.

Supported by: John Scott*

S1M-2724 Mr Keith Raffan: Scottish Zimbabwe Group—That the Parliament offers its wholehearted support to the Scotland Zimbabwe Group, recently formed to highlight the appalling abuse of human rights in Zimbabwe; strongly endorses the principles of the group, which seeks a free and fair presidential election in Zimbabwe and freedom of speech for its people; encourages MSPs and members of the public to attend the group’s vigil to be held on the Mound in Edinburgh on Saturday 9 February, so demonstrating our solidarity with the people of Zimbabwe, and demands that the President and Government of Zimbabwe cease their abuse of human rights forthwith and show respect for both the rule of law and democracy.

Supported by: Mr Jamie Stone*

S1M-2723 Mr Brian Monteith: Burdens on the Scotch Whisky Industry—That the Parliament notes with concern Her Majesty’s Government’s decision to apply strip stamps to Scotch Whisky products, but not to wine or beer, in an attempt to curb fraud; believes that the case for such strip stamps is not yet proven as they are costly and relatively easy to forge, as found in the USA and Greece where they have been abolished, and believes that the Scottish Executive should work with the Scotch whisky industry to avoid such additional burdens being placed on our whisky industry, for example by taking advantage of the exemption from the EU’s Water Framework Directive.

Supported by: Mr Jamie McGrigor, Stewart Stevenson*

S1M-2722 John Scott: Ayr United Football Club—That the Parliament congratulates all the players, staff and supporters of Ayr United Football Club on reaching the CIS Insurance Cup Final, its first major cup final in the club’s 92 year history; recognises the vital role played by the club in the local community, and wishes the team every success for the remainder of the season and beyond.

Supported by: Mr Murray Tosh*

S1M-2721# Maureen Macmillan: National Outdoor Training Centre, Aviemore—That the Parliament notes, in this International Year of Mountains, the excellent training and adventure courses provided by sportscotland at the National Outdoor Training Centre at Glenmore Lodge, Aviemore and recognises the importance of outdoor pursuits to the development of young people’s health, fitness and self-reliance, the worth of careers as instructors for young people in rural areas and the importance of outdoor pursuits such as skiing and mountaineering to the rural economy.

Supported by: Mr Jamie Stone*

S1M-2720 Kay Ullrich: Ayr United Football Club—That the Parliament congratulates Ayr United Football Club on the magnificent achievement of reaching the CIS Insurance Cup Final and wishes the "Honest Men" continued success in the tournament.

Supported by: John Scott*, Mr Murray Tosh*, Ms Sandra White*

S1M-2719# Scott Barrie: World Wide Clubhouse Movement—That the Parliament notes the establishment in New York of the worldwide clubhouse movement for psychiatric rehabilitation in 1948; further notes the siting of the first clubhouse in Scotland under the management of the Scottish Association for Mental Health in Dunfermline in 1992, which moved to new, expanded premises in the summer of 2001 and is now one of seven situated throughout Scotland; congratulates it on being the first in Scotland to be accredited by the International Centre for Clubhouses; welcomes the success to date of this innovative community care model for adults with a mental illness; is aware that the members of the Dunfermline Clubhouse are currently organising a major international conference at Heriot Watt University from 3 to 6 July 2002 involving representatives from similar projects in Europe, the Americas, Asia, Africa, and Australasia, and believes that the Scottish Executive should encourage and expand this model of community care.

Supported by: Mr Jamie Stone*, Tommy Sheridan*

S1M-2718# Scott Barrie: NHS Fife—That the Parliament congratulates NHS Fife on its decision to bring all domestic service, portering and catering contracts which were previously outsourced back into the direct management of the NHS; notes that due to an increase in the cleaning contract specification there will be a welcome improvement in the general cleanliness of Fife’s health facilities which will benefit patients and several hundred staff, and believes that this action recreates the "NHS team" and that this policy should be extended throughout Scotland.

Supported by: Mr Jamie Stone*, Stewart Stevenson*, Tommy Sheridan*

S1M-2717# Dr Sylvia Jackson: Treatment of Multiple Sclerosis—That the Parliament congratulates the Scottish Executive on its recent announcement which will ensure that disease modifying drugs for multiple sclerosis sufferers (MS) are made available on the NHS; recognises the role of MS campaigners over many years on this issue; notes that up to 1,600 people with MS could benefit from this decision; welcomes the innovative 'risk-taking' scheme, and believes that a partnership approach with patients, the voluntary sector and others must be an important part of the future development of services for people with MS.

Supported by: Mr Murray Tosh*, Karen Gillon*, Mr Jamie Stone*, Tricia Marwick*, Johann Lamont*, Tommy Sheridan*

S1M-2716 Richard Lochhead: Scottish Executive Policy on Advertising—That the Parliament notes the current recruitment advertising policy of the Scottish Executive that has seen over £1 million spent in the Scotsman and Herald newspapers from 1998 to 2001 compared to barely £100,000 in the Courier and Press and Journal; is concerned that the dissemination of information on job availability with the Scottish Executive may not reach many able candidates outwith the central belt, particularly since the Press and Journal and Courier readerships are on a par with the Scotsman and Herald; further notes that more has been spent advertising vacancies in London based newspapers than in newspapers in the north east of Scotland and that posts where the specialism exists in this area, such as the Chief Executive of the Fisheries Research Service, have not been advertised in north east newspapers, and calls on the Scottish Executive to review its current policy to ensure that people from across all of Scotland learn about available posts.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson*, Ms Sandra White*

S1M-2715 Roseanna Cunningham: The Scottish Arts Council and VisitScotland Traditional Music and Tourism Initiative 2002—That the Parliament welcomes the publication of the joint Scottish Arts Council and VisitScotland document A Soundtrack for Scottish Tourism which describes a variety of pilot projects operating in a number of different areas throughout Scotland from 1999 to 2002; notes the widespread local, national and international interest shown in each project; further notes that the effect on all local areas involved included clear business and tourism benefits in both the short and longer term; recognises the uniqueness of Scotland’s traditional culture and the enormous contribution it can make to revitalising the tourist sector and, in particular, the rural economy, and calls on all local authorities to investigate ways in which they can further encourage traditional culture in their respective areas.

Supported by: Brian Adam, Richard Lochhead, Mr John McAllion, Michael Matheson, Alex Fergusson, Fergus Ewing, Mr Kenny MacAskill, Ms Sandra White, Tavish Scott*, Mr Adam Ingram*, Fiona McLeod*

S1M-2714# Mr Lloyd Quinan: British Trade Ban on Israel—That the Parliament notes the continued incursions by the Israeli defence forces into areas controlled by the Palestinian Authority and believes that Her Majesty’s Government should cease all trade with Israel until the Israeli Government complies with United Nations Resolution 242.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan*

S1M-2711# Des McNulty: UN 0.7% GNP Target for Development Assistance—That the Parliament notes that the economic consequences of the tragic events of 11 September 2001 will have severe and adverse impacts on developing countries, particularly sub-Sahara Africa; recognises the commitment by international institutions and Her Majesty's Government to the goal of halving the proportion of people living in extreme poverty by 2015; shares the concerns raised by the World Bank and others that without significantly increased development assistance many countries will fail to reach this goal; praises the actions by the Netherlands, Sweden, Norway and Denmark for consistently achieving the UN 0.7% GNP target for development assistance over many years, and congratulates Luxembourg for achieving the target in 2000 and Portugal and Ireland for setting dates to achieve the target, in 2006 and 2007 respectively; recognises the principle that successful poverty reduction is an essential foundation of stability in an increasingly globalised world; believes that the adoption of an intermediate target of 0.4% for overseas development assistance by 2006 with the goal of achieving the UN 0.7% GNP target by 2012 would strengthen the United Kingdom’s advocacy role; welcomes the leadership shown by the Chancellor of the Exchequer, Gordon Brown, and the Secretary of State for International Development, Claire Short, in establishing a Commonwealth Education Fund to support disadvantaged children in Commonwealth countries, and commends the work of those charities and agencies in Scotland who not only take forward development work in many of the poorer countries but also play a vital role in informing the Scottish people about the importance of development assistance.

Supported by: Brian Fitzpatrick, Mr Jamie Stone*

*S1M-2711.1 Tommy Sheridan: UN 0.7% GNP Target for Development Assistance—As an amendment to motion (S1M-2711) in the name of Des McNulty, leave out from "believes" to end and insert "and criticises Her Majesty’s Government for not achieving the UN 0.7% GNP target."

S1M-2710# Colin Campbell: Establishment of Task Force for the Regeneration of Inverclyde—That the Parliament notes with concern the Registrar General’s prediction of decline in the population of Inverclyde between 2001 and 2016, the highest predicted depopulation of Scotland’s 32 council areas; recognises that a main cause of this depopulation is lack of job opportunities; believes that this decline places an intolerable burden on the remaining population in terms of council tax and service delivery, and considers that the Scottish Executive should establish a task force with the resources to address the problems facing Inverclyde, with a view to reversing decline through regeneration and restoring prosperity.

Supported by: Mr Kenneth Gibson, Richard Lochhead, Mr Kenny MacAskill, Mr Andrew Welsh, Christine Grahame, Andrew Wilson, Ms Sandra White, Nicola Sturgeon, Mrs Margaret Ewing, Fiona McLeod, Kay Ullrich, Mr Lloyd Quinan, Mr Gil Paterson, Mr Adam Ingram, Shona Robison, Mr Duncan Hamilton, Bruce Crawford, Michael Russell, Fergus Ewing

S1M-2709 Ms Margo MacDonald: Cancer Research UK—That the Parliament welcomes the establishment of Cancer Research UK as a result of the merger between the Imperial Cancer Research Fund and the Cancer Research Campaign; recognises the unique and essential contribution to cancer research and therapies made by both charities, and, following the creation of Cancer Research UK, welcomes the expansion of specialist trials centres in NHS hospitals.

Supported by: Michael Matheson, Tommy Sheridan*

S1M-2704 Murdo Fraser: Heritage Lottery Fund—That the Parliament congratulates the Heritage Lottery Fund on the publication of its report Sustaining our Living Heritage - Skills and training for the heritage sector and for the support provided to the teaching of traditional skills, helping to maintain these aspects of our national heritage which might otherwise be lost to future generations.

Supported by: Richard Lochhead, Mr Jamie McGrigor

S1M-2701 Mr Tom McCabe: Merger of AEEU and MSF—That the Parliament welcomes the merger of the Amalgamated Engineering and Electrical Union with MSF to form Amicus, the second largest union in Britain, bringing the benefits of union membership to over one million people, and believes that Amicus will continue to support and promote training and skills development, pension rights, health and safety issues, environmental concerns and involvement and consultation in decision-making by and on behalf of its members.

Supported by: Elaine Thomson, Scott Barrie, Brian Fitzpatrick*R, Karen Whitefield*, Sarah Boyack*, Cathie Craigie*, Mr Jamie Stone*, Pauline McNeill*, Mr Duncan McNeil*, Johann Lamont*

S1M-2700 Bruce Crawford: GM Crop Trials—That the Parliament believes that the GM crop trials, proposed for three farms in Aberdeenshire and Fife and announced by the Scottish Executive on 31 January 2002, should not proceed in the light of (a) the report by the Royal Society on 4 February 2002, (b) the study by English Nature revealing that genes from separate GM varieties can accumulate in plants that grow from seed spilled at harvest through a process known as "gene-stacking" and (c) the recent admission by the Executive that horizontal gene transfer from GM foods has taken place under laboratory conditions.

Supported by: Fiona McLeod, Mr Andrew Welsh, Christine Grahame, Richard Lochhead, Tricia Marwick, Irene McGugan, Michael Russell, Stewart Stevenson, Ms Margo MacDonald, Alex Neil, Dorothy-Grace Elder, Fiona Hyslop*, Ms Sandra White*

S1M-2699 Irene McGugan: Iain Anderson on BBC Scotland—That the Parliament, while respecting the right of BBC Radio Scotland to set its own schedules, notes the station's intention to end this Spring the afternoon programme presented by Iain Anderson; regrets the damage done to the programme over the last year by constant managerial interference in a highly successful formula which mixed classical and Celtic music, now allowing only the blandest of contemporary music to be broadcast in the key afternoon slot; observes in particular the damage that this did to audience numbers and to the total air time given to Scottish music by the main, publicly-funded Scottish broadcaster; is saddened that such a wrong-headed policy was persisted with, despite much opinion to the contrary expressed to the BBC by loyal listeners, and hopes that Iain Anderson’s new programme, to be broadcast on weekday evenings, will be left free of such meddling and allowed to develop the style and content that has made Iain Anderson deservedly popular with a very wide range of listeners.

Supported by: Stewart Stevenson, Richard Lochhead, Michael Matheson, Fiona Hyslop*, Tommy Sheridan*, Ms Sandra White*

S1M-2698# Mr Gil Paterson: Child Witness Reform—That the Parliament welcomes the report Justice for Children – The Welfare of Children in the Justice System; commends Children 1st, Childline, NCH Scotland and the other participants for producing this report, and considers that the Scottish Executive should look closely at the findings and recommendations of the report and act accordingly.

Supported by: Mr Adam Ingram, Fergus Ewing, Stewart Stevenson, Ms Sandra White, Mr Kenneth Gibson, Richard Lochhead, Mr John McAllion, Nora Radcliffe, Scott Barrie, Michael Matheson, Mrs Margaret Ewing, Tommy Sheridan*

S1M-2695# Pauline McNeill: Election of Mr Afif Safieh as Honorary President of the Glasgow University Dialectic Society—That the Parliament congratulates Mr Afif Safieh, Palestinian General Delegate to the United Kingdom, on his successful election as Honorary President of the Glasgow University Dialectic Society, on the basis of his presentation to the Society during the 2000-01 academic year, and welcomes the action of the Dialectic Society as progressing the understanding in this country of the plight of the Palestinian people, particularly at this time of great instability and threat to the Palestinian Authority by the actions of the Israeli Government.

S1M-2694# Tricia Marwick: Fife Council and Third Age Group—That the Parliament notes that it is a matter of public record that Fife Council paid a total of £40,178 from 1998 to 1999 to the Third Age Centre in Glenrothes at a time when it was no longer a properly constituted organisation; further notes that the first employee of the Third Age Group was a serving Fife Labour councillor, the second employee was a Labour activist and that the Council's link worker with the Group was the election agent to the MP, Henry McLeish; is concerned that all the papers relating to the grant applications were shredded by the Council; considers that the report of 4 December 2001 by Douglas Sinclair, the Council's Chief Executive, which contained the following extracts—

"The Third Age Centre was a properly constituted voluntary organisation…and merited financial support from the Council"…; "…it would appear that all normal procedures were followed when processing the Group's applications for grant"…; "…it was necessary to destroy the files as insufficient storage space was available"…; "…that the Council's actions with regard to the Third Age Group are beyond reproach"… and "No further action requires to be taken."

calls into question the ability of the Council to investigate this matter further in a manner which will satisfy the people of Fife, and therefore considers that Audit Scotland should carry out an investigation as a matter of urgency which should consider the Council's conduct in relation to the Third Age Centre during the period 1995 to 2000 when grant assistance was being paid and conduct a thorough review of the practices currently employed by the Council.

Supported by: Kay Ullrich, Richard Lochhead, Fiona Hyslop*, Ms Sandra White*
