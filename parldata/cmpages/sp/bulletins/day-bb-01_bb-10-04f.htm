Business Bulletin No. 144/2001

Thursday 4 October 2001

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

The full text of all outstanding motions and amendments will appear in the Business Bulletin every Monday.

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-2297 Mr Tom McCabe on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Committee Membership—That the Parliament agrees that Brian Fitzpatrick be appointed to replace Des McNulty on the Enterprise and Lifelong Learning Committee.

Supported by: Euan Robson*

*S1M-2296# Ms Sandra White: Glasgow Housing Stock Transfer—That the Parliament notes the continuing uncertainty over the proposals for the wholesale transfer of Glasgow City Council’s housing stock; further notes the continuing uncertainty over the date of the ballot of tenants on the housing stock transfer proposals, and believes that in order to remove the uncertainty and to assist the tenants of Glasgow City Council to make a balanced decision based on the merits of the housing stock transfer proposal Her Majesty's Government should immediately write off Glasgow’s housing debt and the Scottish Executive should make available resources to improve Glasgow’s housing stock.

Supported by: Mr Kenneth Gibson*

*S1M-2295 Michael Russell: Kidney Donation and Provision of Renal Units—That the Parliament records its concern at the current shortage of kidneys for transplant to patients suffering renal failure and that many such patients in Scotland die for want of a transplant; notes that Scotland has fewer renal units per head of population than most other European countries and is near the bottom of the European league for transplantation rates, and calls upon the Scottish Executive to bring forward proposals to address these problems, which may include a "presumed consent" system for organ donation of the kind which operates in several other European countries and an improved provision of renal units, particularly in rural Scotland, where patients often have to travel huge distances several times a week for dialysis.

*S1M-2294# Trish Godman: Welfare Rights Service—That the Parliament notes with appreciation recent research findings of the Fraser of Allander Institute which show that last year the benefits generated by the Glasgow City Council Welfare Rights Service led to the creation of 264 jobs in Scotland with a value in wages of £4,491,003; congratulates all of those involved in this remarkable example of job creation, and commends the valuable work undertaken by welfare rights advisers throughout the country.

*S1M-2293 Ian Jenkins: Closure of Glencorse Barracks—That the Parliament notes with regret the decision of the Ministry of Defence to downgrade its only Scottish training centre to a selection centre resulting in the loss of around 100 jobs and calls on the Scottish Executive to consider what assistance it can offer Midlothian Council and local agencies in tackling the loss of a significant local employer in Penicuik and Midlothian.

*S1M-2291# Patricia Ferguson: Conference of Women Parliamentarians held in the Chamber, 28 and 29 September 2001—That the Parliament notes the conference of women parliamentarians held in the chamber on 28 and 29 September 2001; congratulates the National Democratic Institute and the Vital Voices Global Partnership for its initiative in bringing together women parliamentarians in this way; further notes that this was the first time women parliamentarians from Oireachtas Eireann, Westminster, the Northern Ireland Assembly, the National Assembly for Wales and the Scottish Parliament have had the opportunity to come together to discuss issues of interest to them, and agrees to the importance of building on the good progress made at the conference in addressing the issue of gender inequality.

*S1M-2290 Mr Adam Ingram: National Air Traffic Control Centre, Prestwick—That the Parliament believes that the building of a new National Air Traffic Control Centre at Prestwick must proceed without further delay in the interests of public safety and the economic well-being of Scotland and calls upon the Scottish Executive to make the necessary representations to ensure that public investment delivers a fully operational centre no later than 2007 as currently planned.

Supported by: Andrew Wilson*, Alex Neil*

*S1M-2289# Mr Brian Monteith: Closure of Scottish Executive Environment and Rural Affairs Department Offices in Stirling, Dundee and Forfar—That the Parliament regrets the decision of the Scottish Executive to close its Environment and Rural Affairs Department offices in Stirling, Dundee and Forfar; notes that this decision has been taken for financial reasons to reduce annual running costs at a time when the department has admitted to a £66 million underspend in its budget and that it will mean the transfer of staff to a centralised regional office in Perth, and deplores the failure of ministers to consult with the local farming communities on the closure of these offices.

Supported by: Alex Fergusson*, Mr Keith Harding*, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton*

*S1M-2288 Fergus Ewing: Proposed Liberal Democrat Policy on "Tourism Tax"—That the Parliament notes with concern the proposals to be put to a Liberal Democrats conference that a new tax on tourists should be imposed in Scotland; believes that this proposal would cause further damage to an industry which is already facing severe difficulties, and calls upon all who support a vibrant tourist industry to reject this proposed Liberal Democrat policy.

*S1M-2287 Michael Russell: Consuls and Consuls General in Scotland—That the Parliament notes the effective contribution made to Scottish public life by the Consuls and Consuls General in Scotland; encourages all nations to appoint such representatives pending the opportunities that will exist after independence to welcome Ambassadors and their staffs to Scotland, and, in particular, expresses its thanks to Dan Mulhall, Consul General of Ireland and Kare Hauge, Consul General of Norway, for their work over the crucial period of the establishment of the Parliament and wishes them well as they leave their Edinburgh postings.

*S1M-2286 Tricia Marwick: Fife Rail Services—That the Parliament recalls motion S1M-379 on rail services in Fife which it debated on 15 December 1999; is concerned that the rail services provided to the people of Fife and beyond are still appalling, that carriages continue to be overcrowded and that trains are still cancelled without notice; believes that the level of delays is still unacceptable and that at peak times insufficient carriages are being provided, and demands that ScotRail and Railtrack take immediate action and provide a service which is commensurate with the high levels of train fares extorted from the long-suffering passengers using these services.

*S1M-2285.1 Irene McGugan: Sports Promotion in Scotland's Schools—As an amendment to motion (S1M-2285) in the name of Allan Wilson, insert at end "; notes that other European countries prioritise sport in the curriculum but that Scottish schools do not even offer the recommended minimum of two hours of physical education per week and that children at one in seven secondary schools no longer have access to a grass playing field; further notes that almost 40% of schools do not have a football pitch and that the already unacceptably high obesity rates among young Scots are still rising, and therefore calls upon the Scottish Executive to cease lodging self-congratulatory motions, to take meaningful action to prioritise sport for children and young people and to ensure that locally available and appropriate facilities are accessible to all."

Supported by: Alex Neil*

*S1M-2285.2 Mr Brian Monteith: Sports Promotion in Scotland's Schools—As an amendment to motion (S1M-2285) in the name of Allan Wilson, leave out from "and others" to end and insert "head teachers, teachers, school boards and other sports and educational bodies to embrace and participate fully in these healthy initiatives."
