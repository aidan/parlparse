Business Bulletin No. 171/2002

Monday 25 November 2002

Section E : Written questions lodged on 22 November 2002
  

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

S1W-31878 Fiona Hyslop: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to its letter to me of 11 November 2002, whether it is its intention to consult local authorities on the abolition of capital receipts set-aside rules as announced by the Minister for Social Justice in her ministerial statement on housing on 7 November 2002 or to abolish these rules as indicated by the Deputy Minister for Finance and Public Services during the debate on quality of life later that day.

S1W-31879 Mr Duncan Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive what rationale is used to decide the length of time between tests for amnesic shellfish poisoning levels in any given designated area.

S1W-31880 Mr Duncan Hamilton: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the level of toxins in scallops can fluctuate on a daily and weekly basis.

S1W-31881 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-31271 by Malcolm Chisholm on 18 November 2002, what advice it is taking in formulating guidance to users of anticoagulation monitoring strips and when it expects to be able to make such guidance available.

S1W-31882 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive what action is being taken to ensure that people in the Renfrewshire Council area that are eligible to receive free personal and nursing care receive that care.

S1W-31883 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive how the £4 billion additional spending on public services up to 2005-06 as a result of the UK spending review has been, or will be, allocated and how much has been, or will be, allocated to the Renfrewshire Council area.

S1W-31884 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive how much of the £25 million for free local off-peak bus travel for elderly and disabled concessionary card holders has been allocated to the Renfrewshire Council area.

S1W-31885 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive how much of additional £3.2 billion to be spent on health by 2007-08 as a result of the UK Budget 2002 will be allocated to the Argyll and Clyde NHS Board and what instructions will be issued about the use of these funds.

S1W-31886 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive how many of the 350 one-stop clinics are located in the Renfrewshire Council area; where such clinics are located, and what services they offer.

S1W-31887 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive how much of the £48 million to build and renovate health centres has been, or will be, allocated to Argyll and Clyde NHS Board and what projects the funds will be used for.

S1W-31888 Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-30942 by Malcolm Chisholm on 6 November 2002, what procedure is followed in deciding whether to dispatch the Rapid Response Unit, the Cardiac Response Unit or an accident and emergency ambulance with thrombolytic capability in response to an emergency call and who makes the decision on which vehicle is dispatched.

S1W-31889 Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-29906 by Malcolm Chisholm on 8 October 2002, what the on-call arrangements for the Rapid Response Unit are for patients living south of Forfar.

S1W-31890 Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what tonnage of sand eels has been caught in the North Sea in each of the last five years.

S1W-31891 Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive where in the North Sea sand eel fishing takes place.

S1W-31892 Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what monitoring takes place of sand eel fishing in the North Sea.

S1W-31893 Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures are being taken to reduce sand eel fishing in the North Sea.

S1W-31894 Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact sand eel fishing has on white fish supplies in the North Sea.

S1W-31895 Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what impact sand eel fishing has on conservation methods introduced to protect supplies of white fish in the North Sea.

S1W-31896 Mr Andrew Welsh: To ask the Scottish Executive what representations have been made to the European Commission regarding sand eel fishing in the North Sea.

S1W-31897 Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-30910 by Cathy Jamieson on 19 November 2002, who the members of the Special Educational Needs Forum are; what qualifications and expertise each member has, and what criteria were used to decide on the forum’s membership.

S1W-31898 Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-30910 by Cathy Jamieson on 19 November 2002, how the Special Educational Needs Forum will gather its evidence.

S1W-31899 Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-30910 by Cathy Jamieson on 19 November 2002, how many meetings of the Special Educational Needs Forum have taken place to date, what issues were discussed, and whether the minutes of the meetings are publicly available.

S1W-31900 Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the further stage of its review of initial teacher education is now complete and, if so, when the report of the review will be published.

S1W-31901 Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether its review of initial teacher education will include appropriate training provision to ensure that the education of pupils suffering from autistic spectrum disorders reflects its mainstreaming policy.

S1W-31902 Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has developed its new framework for teachers’ continuous professional development and whether the framework includes specific reference to provision for ensuring that the education of pupils suffering from autistic spectrum disorders reflects its mainstreaming policy.

S1W-31903 Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-29088 by Mr Frank McAveety on 20 September 2002, how often the Autistic Spectrum Disorders Needs Assessment Report implementation group will meet and whether the agenda and minutes of the meetings will be made publicly available.

S1W-31904 Mr Lloyd Quinan: To ask the Scottish Executive how many applications it received for the post of Policy Officer in the special educational needs Bill team and whether the post has now been filled.

S1W-31905 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive how much, in total, the Scottish National Gallery of Modern Art has spent on purchasing new acquisitions in each of the last five years; what proportion of these sums has been spent on works purchased from, or originating from, artists resident in Scotland, and what criteria are used in deciding on such purchases, particularly with regard to the representation of the work of modern artists resident in Scotland.

S1W-31906 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive what purchasing policy the Scottish National Gallery of Modern Art is intending to pursue over the next three years with regard to developing its collection so that it can present a wide and deep view of the work of modern artists resident in Scotland.

S1W-31907 Michael Russell: To ask the Scottish Executive what influence the Scottish National Gallery of Modern Art should have on the economy of the arts sector.

S1W-31908 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-31271 by Malcolm Chisholm on 18 November 2002, how the appropriate guidance for users of CoaguChek testing strips is likely to differ from that already provided by the British Society of Haematology to NHS prescribers and dispensing contractors in England, Wales and Northern Ireland where strips are already available on prescription, and when it expects to finalise such guidance.

S1W-31909 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-31271 by Malcolm Chisholm on 18 November 2002, how many patients it estimates would undertake anticoagulation self-monitoring if monitoring strips were to be made available under the Scottish Drug Tariff.

S1W-31910 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S1W-31271 by Malcolm Chisholm on 18 November 2002, what the estimated savings are to the NHS of prescribing self-monitoring anticoagulation strips given that such strips cost £2.44 and the estimated cost of a laboratory test for an identical blood sample is £80.

S1W-31911 Richard Lochhead: To ask the Scottish Executive how many sudden deaths from alcohol poisoning have occurred in each NHS board area in each of the last five years showing the percentage change (a) year on year and (b) over the whole five-year period.

S1W-31912 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive, with regard to the debate on its response to the foot-and-mouth disease inquiries on 21 November 2002 (Official Report c 15673), how many sniffer dogs are now actively engaged in the detection of illegal meat imports.

S1W-31913 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive whether x-ray equipment has been introduced to scan containers and personal luggage to detect the presence of illegal food imports; if so, where the equipment is located and how many items of equipment there are.

S1W-31914 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive whether amnesty bins to encourage the surrender of unintended illegal personal imports of meat have been introduced; if so, where they are located and how many there are.

S1W-31915 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what research is being carried out, or has been instructed, into technologies to assist in the detection of illegal meat imports.

S1W-31916 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has had any discussions on any measures towards establishing a single agency approach to deal with any future animal disease epidemic, as referred to in the House of Commons Environment, Food and Rural Affairs Committee Seventh Report of Session 2001-02, Illegal Meat Imports, and whether such an approach would establish such an agency in Scotland separate from any other such agency in the United Kingdom.

S1W-31917 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Minister for the Environment and Rural Development has had any role or input, in relation to the Executive’s responsibilities, into proposals contained in the House of Commons Environment, Food and Rural Affairs Committee Seventh Report of Session 2001-02, Illegal Meat Imports, that relate to matters reserved to Her Majesty’s Government.

S1W-31918 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive how many additional staff have been recruited to police controls as referred to in the debate on its response to the foot-and-mouth disease inquiries on 21 November 2002 (Official Report c 15674).

S1W-31919 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what the enhanced checks and controls are that have been put in place as referred to in the debate on its response to the foot-and-mouth disease inquiries on 21 November 2002 (Official Report c 15674).

S1W-31920 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Veterinary Laboratory Agency is expected to report on its detailed risk assessment as referred to in the debate on its response to the foot-and-mouth disease inquiries on 21 November 2002 (Official Report c 15674).

S1W-31921 Christine Grahame: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it has on when the Machinery of Government Secretariat is expected to report on the review of the system of import controls and the role of the responsible agencies.

S1W-31922 John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Scottish Environment Protection Agency is currently undertaking any work with regard to the environmental impact of salmon farms and, if so, what the remit is of any such work and when any results will be published.

S1W-31923 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive how many of the 4,000 jobs planned to be created or safeguarded through Regional Selective Assistance have been, or will be, located in the Renfrewshire Council area.

S1W-31924 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive how many of the (a) 20,000 new jobs, (b) 5,000 new classroom assistants, (c) 5,000 child care workers and (d) 10,000 construction jobs anticipated to be created by the Scottish Employment Partnership will be located in the Renfrewshire Council area.

S1W-31925 Ms Wendy Alexander: To ask the Scottish Executive how much of the (a) £100 million to tackle drug misuse in communities, (b) funding for 74 new CCTV projects and (c) £45.5 million to tackle youth crime will be spent in the Renfrewshire Council area and how these funds will be used.

S1W-31926 Dorothy-Grace Elder: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to encourage shorthand and other forms of speed writing in schools and colleges.

S1W-31927 Dorothy-Grace Elder: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will invite the Health Technology Board for Scotland to consider the availability of MSPs to attend its seminars and briefings on Health Technology Assessments and Comments when the board is considering the most suitable time for such seminars.

S1W-31928 Mr Tom McCabe: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to improve access to taxi cab services for people with disabilities and whether it will consider grant aid schemes to increase the number of taxis operating that are able to accommodate disabled users.
