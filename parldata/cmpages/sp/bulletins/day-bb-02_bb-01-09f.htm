*S1M-2586 Brian Adam: Winter Maintenance of North East Roads-That the Parliament calls on the Minister for Enterprise, Transport and Lifelong Learning to investigate the performance of BEAR Scotland Ltd and councils in the north east in maintaining roads and pavements in a safe condition during the recent spell of well forecasted adverse weather.

*S1M-2585# Angus MacKay: The Colin O'Riordan Trust -That the Parliament recognises the outstanding contribution made by the late Dr Colin O'Riordan to the provision of access to music for all children in Edinburgh, and particularly Sciennes Primary School, and throughout Scotland, in founding the City of Edinburgh Music School and in the many youth orchestra activities with which he was associated, and congratulates his family on the foundation of the Colin O'Riordan Trust, which aims to assist aspiring young musicians by promoting musical excellence and improving access to musical instruments for those children who might otherwise not have the opportunity to pursue music.

*S1M-2584 Tommy Sheridan: Scottish Power Industrial Dispute-That the Parliament supports the strike action of Scottish Power workers in pursuit of their agreement of 31 May 2000 with Scottish Power management which stated that no job losses and no fundamental changes to conditions would result from the trade unions' agreement to work in partnership alongside the company; regrets that Scottish Power management is now reneging on this agreement and is intent on fundamentally changing working conditions, which includes transferring staff against their will to a new joint venture company; notes that this has resulted in a democratic ballot of union members within Scottish Power; further notes union members' decision to take part in a series of two-day strike actions, and therefore calls on the management of Scottish Power to end its confrontational stance and honour the agreement it signed in good faith with the unions in order to avoid any further inconvenience to Scottish citizens, particularly in relation to power cuts and consequential safety issues.

*S1M-2582 Miss Annabel Goldie: Scottish Economy-That the Parliament notes with grave concern that in excess of 20,000 jobs were lost throughout the Scottish economy in 2001 and that the Scottish economy has failed to perform as well as the UK economy; further notes that the higher business rate poundage in Scotland relative to England is having a damaging effect on the competitiveness of Scotland's economy, and calls upon the Scottish Executive to restore as a matter of urgency a business rate poundage uniform with the rest of the UK as part of a sustained effort to reduce burdens of tax and regulation, which are hindering the creation of jobs and prosperity in Scotland, and demonstrate to the business community a commitment to restoring growth and competitiveness to the Scottish economy.

*S1M-2581 Roseanna Cunningham: Perth Royal Infirmary Laboratory Services-That the Parliament notes the discussion document produced by Tayside University Hospitals NHS Trust on the future of laboratory services at Perth Royal Infirmary (PRI); recognises that upheaval and uncertainty have been the hallmark of health service provision in Perthshire in recent years and that patients, staff and the general public require a period of stability; welcomes the recognition within the document that, as an Acute Hospital with the full range of District General Hospital Services, PRI will require on-site laboratory services for biochemical medicine, haematology and blood transfusion "at the very least"; acknowledges the strong arguments put forward for relocating laboratory facilities closer to the wards, and rejects suggestions that pathology and microbiology services be relocated to Ninewells Hospital.

Supported by: Mr John Swinney*, Bruce Crawford*

*S1M-2580 Mr Andy Kerr: Education (Disability Strategies and Pupils' Records) (Scotland) Bill: Financial Resolution-That the Parliament for the purposes of any Act of the Scottish Parliament resulting from the Education (Disability Strategies and Pupils' Records) (Scotland) Bill, agrees to any increase in expenditure payable out of the Scottish Consolidated Fund in consequence of the Act.

Supported by: Cathy Jamieson*, Nicol Stephen*, Peter Peacock*

*S1M-2579# Mr Jamie Stone: Non-Trunk Roads-That the Parliament notes that the first serious winter weather has resulted in non-trunk roads in the Highlands being in an extremely dangerous condition; recognises that this has come about as a result of the Highland Council downsizing its winter roads maintenance service as a result of the trunk roads maintenance contract being awarded to BEAR Scotland Ltd, and accordingly considers that the Scottish Executive should undertake an urgent review of all roads maintenance in the Highland Council area in order to avoid any future repetition of the recent dangerous problems.

Supported by: Tommy Sheridan*

*S1M-2578.1 David McLetchie: Scottish Executive's Priorities-As an amendment to motion (S1M-2578) in the name of Mr Jack McConnell, insert at end "but, whilst accepting that these are worthy objectives, does not believe that the approach adopted by the Executive will fulfil them."

*S1M-2578.2 Mr John Swinney: Scottish Executive's Priorities-As an amendment to motion (S1M-2578) in the name of Mr Jack McConnell, leave out from "and that" to end and insert "notes the failure of the Executive since its inception to deliver both those first class public services and to create a country in which all children can reach their full potential; recognises that the Parliament has undertaken impressive work but regrets the loss of trust in government caused by the actions of the Executive; calls on the Executive to dramatically improve its performance in delivering public services on health, education, transport and jobs, crime and on the environment, and agrees that only by equipping this Parliament with the powers of a normal independent Parliament will Scotland be able to reach its full potential."

Supported by: Roseanna Cunningham*

*S1M-2578.3 Tommy Sheridan: Scottish Executive's Priorities-As an amendment to motion (S1M-2578) in the name of Mr Jack McConnell, insert at end "furthermore, accepts that the delivery of first class public services by well-paid and motivated public service workers is incompatible with the continued use of PFI/PPP methods of public service financing; resolves therefore to end the havoc of service and staff reductions caused by PFI/PPP and instead to ensure adequate funding of public service expansion and improvement through public investment dedicated to the needs of all Scotland's citizens, instead of through PFI/PPP which is dedicated to the delivery of profit to private corporation shareholders, and resolves that 2002 should become the Public Investment in Public Services year, delivering tangible improvements in public services throughout Scotland and improving significantly the wages and working conditions of the army of currently underpaid and undervalued public service workers."

Supported by: Robin Harper*, Dennis Canavan*

*S1M-2577.1 Ross Finnie: Foot-and-Mouth Disease - Public Inquiry-As an amendment to motion (S1M-2577) in the name of Alex Fergusson, leave out from "calls on" to end and insert ", recognising the need for independent scrutiny of the handling of the foot-and-mouth disease outbreak, supports the participation of the Scottish Executive in the Royal Society Inquiry, the Anderson "Lessons Learnt" Inquiry and the Royal Society of Edinburgh Inquiry and believes that these inquiries provide ample opportunities for all interested parties to contribute to the process."

Supported by: Allan Wilson*

*S1M-2577.2 Fergus Ewing: Foot-and-Mouth Disease - Public Inquiry- As an amendment to motion (S1M-2577) in the name of Alex Fergusson,

leave out from "calls on" to end and insert "welcomes the cross-party approach towards the policy adopted in Scotland to eradicate the foot-and-mouth disease epidemic, but believes that it is essential that all lessons are learned by means of a full, open and independently convened public inquiry in Scotland and that it is important to monitor closely the impact of the crisis upon the whole Scottish economy and, in particular, upon those regions of Scotland most affected by the outbreak."
