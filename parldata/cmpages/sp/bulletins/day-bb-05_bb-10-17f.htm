Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday.  That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (u).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an “R”.

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm).  A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at: http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-3417 Cathie Craigie: Muscular Dystrophy “My Life” Campaign—That the Parliament supports the 3,000 people in Scotland with inherited neuromuscular disorders and muscular dystrophy; supports the work of the Muscular Dystrophy Campaign in pioneering the search for treatments and cures for over 40 years, whilst also providing practical, professional and emotional support for people affected by neuromuscular conditions; supports the Muscular Dystrophy “My Life” campaign and its work in Scotland to encourage a co-ordinated approach by local authorities and NHS boards when assessing individuals’ needs and requirements, and considers the findings of the My Life expert group to be a model for future delivery of services to people affected by neuromuscular conditions.

*S2M-3416 Patrick Harvie: Protect the Human—That the Parliament welcomes the launch of Amnesty International’s (AI) new initiative, Protect the Human Week, from 16 to 22 October 2005 which will see schools across Scotland lobby the Home Office to implement key recommendations from AI’s report, Seeking Asylum is not a Crime; notes AI’s concerns regarding the detrimental effects of detention, especially on those who are particularly vulnerable such as elderly people, mentally ill people and torture survivors; notes the growing momentum in the Parliament and Scottish civic society to ensure that the children of asylum-seeking families are treated humanely; calls on the Scottish Executive urgently to conclude negotiations with the Home Office regarding a protocol on the removal of asylum seekers and to ensure that it includes an endorsement of children’s rights as defined in the UN Convention on the Rights Of The Child, and congratulates children and schools across Scotland who participate in the AI campaign in defence of human rights.

*S2M-3415u Roseanna Cunningham: TV Licence and Digital Reception—That the Parliament recognises that there are still many areas in Scotland, including parts of Perthshire, where digital television reception is not possible; notes with concern the desire by the BBC to increase the television licence fee by 2.3% above inflation, and believes that, until such time as the BBC’s entire broadcast output is available to all licence payers, a differential should be introduced into the licence fee to ensure that people who are not receiving a full service do not pay the same licence fee as those who do.

*S2M-3414 Des McNulty: Trade Justice—That the Parliament recognises that global trade rules are weighted against poor countries, their people and the environment; believes that governments in poor countries should have the flexibility to choose those policies that will help them to end poverty and protect the environment; notes the Trade Justice Movement’s mass lobby of the UK Parliament on 2 November 2005, and recognises that more than 600,000 people across the United Kingdom have already voted for trade justice, an end to export dumping and regulations that stop big business from profiting at the expense of people and the environment.

*S2M-3413 Ms Sandra White: European Maritime Safety Agency - Glasgow Bid—That the Parliament notes the success of the Freedom of Information (Scotland) Act 2002 which has ensured the release of information about Glasgow’s bid to be the new base of operations for the European Maritime Safety Agency (EMSA); notes that the bid for the new EMSA headquarters has since been awarded to Lisbon; notes that, at a meeting of heads of government, Tony Blair did not table the Glasgow bid; notes that assurances were repeatedly given to Scottish MPs and MSPs that the Glasgow bid was going ahead; notes that Glasgow was even referred to as a part of the “strong competition” by Nicol Stephen in January 2004, despite the fact that a civil servant said “It is our understanding that securing the EMSA for the UK is not the main objective. The preferred scenario seems to be to attract the police training college to a location in England. Any bid for the EMSA would not therefore proceed in the hope that it could be won, but rather so that it could be tactically withdrawn later to boost the chances of getting the police college”; further notes that the Glasgow bid was never mentioned on the EMSA website, which did mention Genoa, Nantes, Athens and Gothenburg as competitors to Lisbon, and believes that it is clear from this information that the best interests of Glasgow and Scotland are not being served by the UK Government or the Scottish Executive.

*S2M-3412 Mr Andrew Arbuckle: Burma—That the Parliament condemns the illegal government of Burma in its widespread abuse of human rights; calls on the relevant institutions, including the UN Security Council, to act to stop the terror of the Burmese Government; further condemns the overthrow of the previous democratically elected government of Aung San Suu Kyi, and recognises the brutality inflicted on ethnic groups, the use of forced labour and child soldiers and the huge numbers of refugees created as a result of the actions of this regime.

Supported by: Frances Curran*, Mark Ballard*, Shiona Baird*, Stewart Stevenson*

*S2M-3411 Mr Duncan McNeil: Report on Prioritisation of the Non-Executive Bills Unit’s Workload—That the Parliament endorses the prioritisation criteria set out in paragraphs 7 to 9 of the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body’s 2nd Report, 2005 (Session 2): Report on the Prioritisation of the Non-Executive Bills Unit’s Workload (SP Paper 434) and therefore agrees the recommendations in paragraph 19 of the report.

Supported by: Nora Radcliffe*, John Scott*

*S2M-3410 Margaret Jamieson: Guardian Public Services Awards Finalists 2005—That the Parliament congratulates Robin Gourlay, Head of Onsite Services at East Ayrshire Council, and his school meals staff in being the only Scottish finalists in this year’s Guardian Public Services Awards in two categories: Service Delivery – Children’s Services and Innovation and Progress - Customer Service and recognises the dedication of the school meals staff in contributing to the successful revolution in school meals in East Ayrshire with the roll-out of the organic and local produce pilot.

*S2M-3409 Margaret Jamieson: Music Award for East Ayrshire Council—That the Parliament congratulates East Ayrshire Council on being awarded a national diploma by the National Music Council and recognises the commitment and hard work of the pupils and staff of East Ayrshire in gaining this award and being the only Scottish recipient.

*S2M-3408 John Farquhar Munro: Condemnation of the Scottish Executive Inquiry Reporters Unit’s Decision on Annat Bay—That the Parliament condemns the decision of the Scottish Executive Inquiry Reporters Unit recommending that the Crown Estate Commission approves the application for a new salmon farm in Annat Bay, near Ullapool, overturning the democratic decision taken by Highland Council after extensive consultation with the local community; believes that it has made a mockery of the local democratic process by ignoring the views of the local community and its right to manage the local environment in its own way; notes that it is nearly three years since the Parliament voted for planning controls for marine fish farms to be transferred to local authorities; regrets the lack of urgency by the Scottish Executive in bringing into force the relevant section in the Water Environment and Water Services (Scotland) Act 2003, and calls on it to end the interim measures as soon as possible and ensure that any decisions taken under interim arrangements can be reviewed by the democratically elected local authorities.

*S2M-3407 Des McNulty: Earthquake in Kashmir—That the Parliament expresses its deepest sympathy for the victims of the earthquake affecting Kashmir and Pakistan’s North-West Frontier Province which took place on Saturday 8 October 2005; recognises that many families in Scotland will have lost relatives and friends as a result of the tragedy or are awaiting information about those who are missing; congratulates Islamic Relief and other charitable organisations on their swift response, and welcomes the steps taken by the UK Government to provide urgent assistance to the affected areas.
