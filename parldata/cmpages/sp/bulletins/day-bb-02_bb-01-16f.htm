Business Bulletin No. 8/2002

Wednesday 16 January 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-2612 Mr Lloyd Quinan: Congratulations to Pat Cox MEP-That the Parliament congratulates Pat Cox MEP on his election as President of the European Parliament; further congratulates the members of the European Parliament on electing a President from Ireland, and looks forward to a positive relationship between the Scottish Parliament and the European Parliament.

*S1M-2611# Mr Lloyd Quinan: Appointment of Autism and Asperger's Syndrome Co-ordinators-That the Parliament recognises the on-going problems faced by adults diagnosed with autism and Asperger's syndrome; notes the initiative taken by Lothian Health Board to create an adults with autism co-ordinator, and acknowledges the requirement for similar initiatives to be set up nationally to ensure that this section of society can access services and resources and receive support from experienced and qualified professionals.

*S1M-2610 Mr Lloyd Quinan: Sickness Record at West Dunbartonshire Council-That the Parliament acknowledges the recent report by West Dunbartonshire Council regarding the absence and sickness record of staff there which reached 30,000 days lost in the first two quarters of 2000-01 and recommends a full investigation into low staff morale and the council's management.

S1M-2606 Scotland's Railways (lodged on 14 January 2002) Tricia Marwick*

*S1M-2606.1 David Mundell: Scotland's Railways-As an amendment to motion (S1M-2606) in the name of Mr Kenny MacAskill, leave out from "that the Executive's" to end and insert "with regret the acquiescence of the Scottish Executive in Her Majesty's Government's woeful handling of our railways which has led to the uncertainty and chaos that now exists throughout the UK rail industry and, in particular, the undermining of the confidence that is needed to secure the necessary private investment in our railways in order to meet the needs of the travelling public and business users, and calls upon the Executive to set out its strategic objectives for the rail network and services in Scotland, how and when these are to be achieved and how they will be funded."

*S1M-2606.2 Ms Wendy Alexander: Scotland's Railways-As an amendment to motion (S1M-2606) in the name of Mr Kenny MacAskill, leave out from first "notes" to end and insert "welcomes the publication of the Strategic Rail Authority's Strategic Plan and the vision it contains as a first, significant step towards a bigger, better and safer railway for Scotland."
