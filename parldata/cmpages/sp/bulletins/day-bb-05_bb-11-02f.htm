Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week. Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section. Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (u ).Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

*S2M-3503 Dennis Canavan: Breast Cancer Care’s "Same Difference" Campaign—That the Parliament supports Breast Cancer Care’s "Same Difference" campaign to improve breast awareness among people from diverse backgrounds; is concerned at the campaign’s findings that people from black and minority ethnic groups, older people and socially disadvantaged groups have poorer access to breast awareness and breast cancer information; supports the recommendation that the Scottish Executive, primary care, the NHS screening programme, breast cancer charities, community groups and the media need to work in partnership on strategies to increase breast awareness among diverse groups, and calls on the Executive to make funding available to enable breast cancer charities to produce culturally sensitive breast awareness information in a variety of formats and languages.

*S2M-3501 Ms Margaret Curran on behalf of the Parliamentary Bureau: Business Motion—That the Parliament agrees that consideration of the Scottish Commissioner for Human Rights Bill at Stage 1 be completed by 3 March 2006.Supported by: George Lyon*

*S2M-3500 Karen Whitefield: National Youth Work Week—That the Parliament recognises the importance of national Youth Work Week from 1 to 7 November 2005; congratulates YouthLink Scotland on organising a national event and local activities to raise awareness about, and to celebrate, Youth Work Week; recognises the significant contribution which projects such as Route 98 and YouthBank have made to developing new work practices in relation to training and youth empowerment; further recognises the positive contribution that the youth work sector makes to the lives of thousands of young people across Scotland, and welcomes the Scottish Executive’s commitment in A Partnership for a Better Scotland to developing a national strategy for youth work.

*S2M-3499 Alex Neil: Enterprise Week—That the Parliament celebrates Enterprise Week, taking place from 14 to 20 November 2005, and the opportunity to raise the profile of enterprise and entrepreneurship in Scotland; congratulates the winners of the UK Science Enterprise Centres National Business Plan competition on their success against high-quality applicants from 19 other universities across the United Kingdom; wishes them well as they begin to negotiate investment to grow their companies, starting with competition sponsors, the National Endowment for Science, Technology and the Arts (NESTA), and recognises the role played by the Scottish Institute for Enterprise in developing enterprise and business skills in Scottish higher education institutions.Supported by: Christine Grahame*

*S2M-3498 Chris Ballance: Ken Saro-Wiwa—That the Parliament notes that 10 November 2005 marks the 10th anniversary of the judicial murder of Nigerian writer, Ken Saro-Wiwa, and eight of his comrades by the then Nigerian military government; applauds the decision of the members of Scottish PEN to mark the occasion with a reading from Dance the Guns to Silence, a new international anthology of poetry commissioned in his memory, and joins PEN in condemning all acts of violence and repression of free speech against writers and their families.

*S2M-3497 Murdo Fraser: The Decline of Scottish Manufacturing—That the Parliament notes with concern that the value of the manufacturing sector fell every year between 1997 and 2002, having previously risen year-on-year between 1992 and 1997, and that nearly 100,000 manufacturing jobs have been lost since 1997; further notes with concern the recent findings of the Scottish Council for Development and Industry that exports have fallen below £15 billion in value for the first time in a decade; believes that Scotland can benefit from a solid manufacturing base as part of a diverse and modern economy, and therefore calls on the Scottish Executive to take the action required to increase the competitiveness of the sector by taking measures such as reducing red tape and the size of the public sector, privatising Scottish Water, encouraging greater participation in vocational training at further education colleges and bringing forward the promised business rates cut to April 2006.

*S2M-3496 Shiona Baird: Tayside Hospital Broadcasting Group—That the Parliament congratulates the Tayside Hospital Broadcasting Group on its 50 years of service to patients in and around Dundee, firstly as TOC H, then Radio Tayside, Hospital Radio Tayside, Radio Liff and most recently BRIDGEfm; acknowledges the important therapeutic role that hospital radio plays in aiding the recovery of patients, as well as its ability to bring the community to patients who might otherwise feel isolated from everyday life; commends BRIDGEfm on the quality and diversity of the programmes it broadcasts to hospitals in Tayside, and commends the invaluable work undertaken by hospital radio volunteers past and present, whose commitment of time, energy and enthusiasm has ensured the success of this popular local broadcasting service.Supported by: Mrs Nanette Milne*, Chris Ballance*, Murdo Fraser*, Shona Robison*, Ms Rosemary Byrne*, Mr Andrew Arbuckle*, Ms Sandra White*, Eleanor Scott*

*S2M-3495 Chris Ballance: Trinity Mirror—That the Parliament notes that Trinity Mirror is Britain’s biggest newspaper group with over 20 Scottish national and regional titles, including the Daily Record and the Sunday Mail; notes the recently announced 20% rise in operational profits and 5.8% increase in revenue; further notes Trinity Mirror’s plans to make extensive staff cuts across the United Kingdom which will put further pressure on newspaper titles that are already understaffed and under-resourced; believes that a wide range of vibrant newspapers with a plurality of opinion is vital to democracy; expresses sympathy with the concern of the employees of Trinity Mirror, and calls on the company to reconsider the proposed cuts.

*S2M-3490.1 Ms Rosemary Byrne: Children with Special Needs—As an amendment to motion (S2M-3490) in the name of Lord James Douglas-Hamilton, leave out from "however" to end and insert "that, in order to provide for the additional support needs of all children, class sizes require to be reduced to no more than 20, and to no more than 15 for composite classes, in all state primary and secondary schools; calls for the provision of additional teachers and assistants who are appropriately trained and qualified to meet the needs of all pupils with additional support needs; further calls for senior management teams to be appropriately trained and qualified to meet the needs of all pupils with additional support needs; acknowledges that these needs can most effectively be met in well-resourced integrated community schools, and therefore calls on the Scottish Executive to put in place a moratorium on the closure of special schools and an immediate review of inclusion in mainstream schools."

*S2M-3471.1 Colin Fox: Tribute to Rosa Parks, American Civil Rights Campaigner—As an amendment to motion (S2M-3471) in the name of Trish Godman, after "acknowledges that" insert "Rosa Parks was a member of the American Communist Party and that".
