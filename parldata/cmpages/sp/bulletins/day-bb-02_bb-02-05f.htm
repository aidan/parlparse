Business Bulletin No. 22/2002

Tuesday 5 February 2002

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-2696 Robin Harper: Cost of Private Finance Initiative Projects—That the Parliament notes with concern that the cost to the taxpayer of Private Finance Initiative projects may increase several fold between the selection of the "preferred bidder" and the signing of the final contract and requests that Her Majesty’s Government withdraw its opposition to the European Union draft directive on consolidated procurement which would ban the selection of preferred bidders before a contract has been developed and prevent inflation of project costs.

*S1M-2695# Pauline McNeill: Election of Mr Afif Safieh as Honorary President of the Glasgow University Dialectic Society—That the Parliament congratulates Mr Afif Safieh, Palestinian General Delegate to the United Kingdom, on his successful election as Honorary President of the Glasgow University Dialectic Society, on the basis of his presentation to the Society during the 2000-01 academic year, and welcomes the action of the Dialectic Society as progressing the understanding in this country of the plight of the Palestinian people, particularly at this time of great instability and threat to the Palestinian Authority by the actions of the Israeli Government.

*S1M-2694# Tricia Marwick: Fife Council and Third Age Group—That the Parliament notes that it is a matter of public record that Fife Council paid a total of £40,178 from 1998 to 1999 to the Third Age Centre in Glenrothes at a time when it was no longer a properly constituted organisation; further notes that the first employee of the Third Age Group was a serving Fife Labour councillor, the second employee was a Labour activist and that the Council's link worker with the Group was the election agent to the MP, Henry McLeish; is concerned that all the papers relating to the grant applications were shredded by the Council; considers that the report of 4 December 2001 by Douglas Sinclair, the Council's Chief Executive, which contained the following extracts—

"The Third Age Centre was a properly constituted voluntary organisation...and merited financial support from the Council";

"…it would appear that all normal procedures were followed when processing the Group's applications for grant";

"…it was necessary to destroy the files as insufficient storage space was available";

"…that the Council's actions with regard to the Third Age Group are beyond reproach" ; and

"No further action requires to be taken."

calls into question the ability of the Council to investigate this matter further in a manner which will satisfy the people of Fife, and therefore considers that Audit Scotland should carry out an investigation as a matter of urgency which should consider the Council's conduct in relation to the Third Age Centre during the period 1995 to 2000 when grant assistance was being paid and conduct a thorough review of the practices currently employed by the Council.

*S1M-2693 Mr Brian Monteith: British Winter Olympic Team—That the Parliament extends its best wishes to the 51 members of the Great Britain team participating in the 2002 Olympic Winter Games in Salt Lake City, particularly to the 19 Scottish members who are involved in events such as skiing, curling, snowboarding and the biathlon.

Supported by: David Mundell*, Mr David Davidson*

*S1M-2692 Phil Gallie: Ayr United Football Stadium—That the Parliament welcomes the Scottish Executive's abandonment of its appeal against Heritage Scotland Ltd’s planning consent for Ayr United's new football stadium with related development, as granted by South Ayrshire Council and supported by the findings of the Scottish Executive initiated public inquiry, and urges the Scottish Executive to now record its approval for this consent, thereby allowing the building of the new stadium to be commenced at the earliest possible date.

Supported by: Mr Gil Paterson*

*S1M-2691 Trish Godman: Scottish Public Service Employees—That the Parliament has every confidence in Scottish public service employees and acknowledges their integrity and commitment to the efficient and effective provision of a vast range of public services; notes their readiness to adapt to changing circumstances and willingness to improve services especially when such changes are based upon genuine consultation, and believes that many of the individual and communal needs and requirements in both urban and rural communities are best met by services, utilities and resources which are retained in the public sector and managed and staffed by those who have a sympathetic understanding of, and engagement with, a public service ethos.
