Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally printed the day after they are lodged. If an amendment is lodged to a motion then the original motion will appear alongside the amendment, along with any support lodged that day, in this section.

Each Monday, this section also contains all motions and amendments lodged the previous week that are still live and any motions and amendments that have been lodged for debate in the coming week. A motion or amendment is live if it has not been debated, withdrawn or otherwise deleted from the list.

Where a motion or amendment has been withdrawn, it is indicated in this section the day after it has been withdrawn. Support for motions and amendments received after they are lodged is shown at the end of this section the day after such support is received.

Motions and amendments can be published with various symbols:

  • Asterisks before the motion or amendment number indicate a motion or amendment published for the first time;
  • Asterisks also identify alterations to the text of a motion or amendment made since it was first published;
  • A hash symbol identifies motions eligible for debate at Members’ Business;
  • A diamond symbol identifies motions lodged for Members’ Business that have not yet attracted the required cross-party support;
  • An "R", identifies motions or amendments in which the Member who lodged it has a registrable interest.

The Parliamentary Bureau periodically deletes motions or amendments not scheduled for debate and which are over six weeks old.

A full list of current motions is available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/motions.htm.

A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/index.htm.

Any questions in relation to this section should be directed to the Chamber Desk, for which contact details are provided at the end of the Bulletin.

New and altered motions and amendments

*S3M-7659 Ken Macintosh: The Town and Country Planning (Tree Preservation Order and Trees in Conservation Areas) (Scotland) Regulations 2010—That the Local Government and Communities Committee recommends that nothing further be done under the Town and Country Planning (Tree Preservation Order and Trees in Conservation Areas) (Scotland) Regulations 2010.

*S3M-7658 Murdo Fraser: Submarine Dismantling Project—That the Parliament notes that the Ministry of Defence has named Rosyth Royal Dockyard as one of the two potential sites for the Submarine Dismantling Project, along with Devonport Royal Dockyard; notes that the project has been established to dismantle 27 defueled nuclear submarines after they have left service with the Royal Navy; believes that, should Rosyth be awarded the contract, it would bring a massive economic boost to Rosyth, Fife and Scotland; further believes that Rosyth is home to one of the most highly skilled workforces suited for this type of work and that there is a proud history and connection in Fife to the Royal Navy, and hopes that Rosyth is successful in winning the Submarine Dismantling Project.

*S3M-7657 Robert Brown: Closure of Noranside Open Prison—That the Parliament is concerned by the proposal by the Scottish Prison Service to close HMP Noranside, part of the open estate; notes that the open estate population has decreased by 47% since 2007; is concerned that only 36% of long-term prisoners released in 2008-09 spent time in the open estate before full release and that this percentage has decreased in each year since 2006-07; agrees with the comment in Scotland’s Choice: Report of the Scottish Prisons Commission that Scotland "needs a well-run open estate because it is not in the public interest to release long-term prisoners from closed institutions without preparing them for release and training them for freedom"; believes that open prison plays an important function by providing a supported progression back into the community and that it is an integral part of the rehabilitation process, key for reducing reoffending and making Scotland’s communities safer; further believes that open prison is necessary for the re-integration of serious offenders, as stated in the report, Balancing Risk and Need; further agrees with the former Chief Inspector of Prisons, Dr Andrew R C McLellan, when he said "It will require courage to maintain open prisons which are not full in a time of unprecedented overcrowding: but it is courage which will serve the public good"; welcomes the support of John Swinney MSP who has stated that he intends "to put forward a vigorous case to retain the prison as part of the open estate"; is disappointed that the Cabinet Secretary for Justice did not bring this important decision to the Parliament, and calls on the Scottish Government to come before the Parliament and explain why it is proposing to close what is considered an integral part of Scotland’s prison system.

Supported by: Jim Hume*, Mike Rumbles*, Hugh O’Donnell*

*S3M-7656 Bob Doris: Criminal Cash Better Spent in Scotland—That the Parliament welcomes the significant increase in criminal asset recovery under the proceeds of crime legislation and what it considers to be the tremendous success of the Scottish Government’s Cashback for Communities initiative, which allocates seized criminal assets to community projects in some of Scotland’s most deprived areas; considers that, while the annual limit of £30 million that Scotland is permitted to retain under the current rules has never before been reached, this figure is expected to be exceeded by the end of the 2010-11 financial year; believes that any such additional money would be better spent helping Scotland’s crime-affected communities than going to HM Treasury, and calls on the UK Government to recognise the success of the scheme and immediately remove the £30 million threshold.

Supported by: Mary Scanlon*, Bill Kidd*, Anne McLaughlin*, Bill Wilson*, Nigel Don*, Stewart Maxwell*, Joe FitzPatrick*, Kenneth Gibson*, Sandra White*, Maureen Watt*, John Wilson*, Brian Adam*

*S3M-7655 Hugh O’Donnell: Commercial Pressure on Israel—That the Parliament considers that there is a growing number of calls from the Scottish public and from businesses for a boycott of goods on sale in Scotland from Israel, owing to the continued illegal occupation and ongoing land grab in East Jerusalem, the West Bank and the Gaza Strip; is deeply concerned at the successive failure of UK and US governments to persuade Israel to implement a complete settlement freeze, and calls on the Israeli authorities to recognise their obligations under international law to end the occupation and what it sees as the colonisation of East Jerusalem and the West Bank to the detriment of the local population.

*S3M-7654 Richard Lochhead: The Prohibited Procedures on Protected Animals (Exemptions) (Scotland) Amendment Regulations 2011—That the Rural Affairs and Environment Committee recommends that the Prohibited Procedures on Protected Animals (Exemptions) (Scotland) Amendment Regulations 2011 be approved.

Supported by: Roseanna Cunningham*

*S3M-7653 Jackie Baillie: Bangladesh Bonus for Aggreko—That the Parliament congratulates Dumbarton-based Aggreko on securing a £114 million contract to supply power to Bangladesh over a three year period; understands that Aggreko is the world leader in temporary power solutions and was selected to provide 150 megawatts of emergency gas-power for the Bangladesh Power Development Board; notes that Aggreko employs more than 4,000 people worldwide, including around 120 in Dumbarton; further notes that a further 110 jobs are likely to be created when Aggreko relocates to its new £20 million purpose-built base at Lomondgate in 2011, and commends Aggreko for adding yet another project to a list that includes the Beijing Olympics, the 2010 World Cup and the inauguration of US President Barack Obama.

*S3M-7652 Jackie Baillie: Cameron House Hotel Wins Top Accolade—That the Parliament congratulates Cameron House on being awarded Hotel of the Year 2010 at the prestigious Catey Awards in London; understands that the Loch Lomond hotel beat off competition from all over Scotland, England, Ireland and Wales; notes that the hotel, operated by De Vere Hotels and Resorts, was refurbished at a cost of £50 million last year; further notes that the Carrick Spa at Cameron House recently won the title of Scottish Spa of the Year, adding to a raft of awards for the hotel, and congratulates all the staff for their hard work in securing these accolades, which it believes demonstrates that Loch Lomond continues to be a premier holiday destination.

*S3M-7651 Jackie Baillie: John Corcoran, Chief Executive of Dunbartonshire Chamber of Commerce—That the Parliament acknowledges the upcoming retirement of John Corcoran, Chief Executive Officer of Dunbartonshire Chamber of Commerce; understands that John has announced that he will be standing down from his post on 31 December 2010; notes that John was the co-founder of Dunbartonshire Chamber of Commerce in 2003 and believes that he has been successful in his mission to provide businesses in Dunbartonshire with a bigger and more effective voice, and congratulates John for all of his hard work and dedication in turning Dunbartonshire Chamber of Commerce into the vital resource that it is considered to be today.

*S3M-7650 Jackie Baillie: Aggreko to Power London 2012—That the Parliament congratulates Aggreko on winning a £37 million contract to supply power to the London 2012 Olympic and Paralympics Games; notes that the Dumbarton-based company will provide about 220 megawatts of power for both events; understands that Aggreko has also signed up as a sponsor after winning the contract; further understands that this latest success for the company comes on the back of news that the first half yearly revenue has increased by £29 million as a result of the firm powering the 2010 World Cup, and commends all the hard work and dedication of the Aggreko staff in making the company a world leader in temporary power solutions.
