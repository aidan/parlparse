Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally only printed the day after the day on which they are lodged and then with the complete list of motions and amendments lodged the previous week which is published in this section of the Bulletin every Monday. That list also contains any motions lodged for debate in the forthcoming week.

Where the text of a motion or amendment is altered, the motion or amendment is re-printed with the changes marked by asterisks in the text.

Where a motion or amendment attracts additional support after it is first published, that additional support is shown separately at the end of this section.

Motions eligible for consideration for debate as members’ business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#).

Motions submitted for members’ business in the Parliament but which have not yet received the requisite cross-party support are marked with a diamond symbol (<>).

Motions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

An indication is also provided where motions and amendments have been withdrawn.

A full list of current motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site at Current Motions

(http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/motions.htm). A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at:

http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsAndAmendments/index.htm

New and altered motions and amendments

*S2M-4277 John Home Robertson: Tribute to Andrew Fletcher of Saltoun 1653-1716, Patriot and Unionist—That the Parliament recognises the courageous role of Andrew Fletcher of Saltoun, Member for East Lothian, in the debate on the Treaty of Union in 1706; endorses his wise statement that "it is certainly in the interest of all good men to promote a nearer union with our neighbours in England"; believes that 67 members of the old Scottish Parliament, including Fletcher and Patrick Home, Member for Berwickshire, were right to vote against the abolition of Scotland’s Parliament; notes Fletcher’s warning that "the Scots deserve no pity, if they voluntarily surrender their united and separate interests to the mercy of an united Parliament" and regrets the fact that an incorporating union was delivered by 110 votes, later described as the "parcel of rogues"; welcomes the fact that the Scotland Act 1998 has achieved a fair and final resolution to the constitutional status of Scotland within the United Kingdom, and rejoices in the fact that Scotland now has the benefits of both union and autonomy, as advocated by Andrew Fletcher in 1707, as we approach the 300th anniversary of the Act of Union and the immensely successful historic partnership of the United Kingdom.

Supported by: Mr Alasdair Morrison*, Mr Jamie Stone*

*S2M-4276 Pauline McNeill: Rock the Kelvin! Restore the Kelvingrove Park Bandstand and Amphitheatre—That the Parliament congratulates the Friends of Kelvingrove Park for their excellent campaign to have Glasgow’s last remaining bandstand restored to its former and deserved glory; notes the proposals to have Edinburgh’s Ross Bandstand upgraded as a major new cultural facility; further notes that Historic Scotland has declared that the Kelvingrove Park Bandstand has cultural and social significance; welcomes the support for the campaign from contemporary Scottish music acts Belle and Sebastian, Franz Ferdinand and Teenage Fanclub; further notes that the Heritage Lottery Fund, which is currently considering a funding application, has previously provided support to some 50-plus bandstand restoration projects across the United Kingdom, and calls on Glasgow City Council to lead the way in ensuring that a viable and sustainable future can be secured for this proud piece of the city’s heritage and so create a unique open air venue in the heart of Glasgow’s West End.

Supported by: Patrick Harvie*

S2M-4275 Linda Fabiani: The Panchen Lama—That the Parliament notes that 25 April 2006 is the 17th birthday of Gedhun Choekyi Nyima, Tibet’s Panchen Lama; notes that in May 1995, at the age of six, a few weeks after being named by HH The Dalai Lama as the new reincarnation of the Panchen Lama, the second most important leader in Tibet, Gedhun Choekyi Nyima was abducted from his home in Tibet by the Chinese authorities and that no-one there has seen him or heard from him since; sends all good wishes to the Panchen Lama, and urges the international community to continue its efforts to secure his safety.

S2M-4274# Margaret Jamieson: Future Funding of Borderline Theatre Company—That the Parliament recognises the contribution of Borderline Theatre Company during its 32 years of work from its base in Ayrshire, delivering quality, professional entertainment to almost a million people across Scotland as well as 1,100 workshops to 18,168 participants in its extensive lifelong learning programme; condemns the decision of the Scottish Arts Council (SAC) to cease to provide revenue funding for Borderline Theatre Company; deplores the actions of the SAC in changing the funding criteria during the application process, and believes that an examination should take place of the processes and outcomes of SAC funding decisions.

Supported by: Ms Rosemary Byrne, Mr Jamie Stone*, Bill Butler*

S2M-4273 Dr Jean Turner: Scottish Monument in Flanders—That the Parliament commends the people of Zonnebeke in Flanders for their efforts in raising funds to build a Scottish monument, a Celtic cross made from Corrennie Pink granite from Scotland, which will be dedicated to all the Scottish soldiers who fought in the Great War, wherever they came from; notes that it is not aimed at glorifying war but that its sole purpose is to remember all Scottish involvement, from the Canadian Scottish to the 1st South African Brigade which was part of the 9th (Scottish) Division; further notes that there is no monument at present in any part of the Ypres Salient dedicated to any Scottish regiments and that this monument will be a tribute to every Scottish soldier, whatever his regiment; recognises the efforts of the local authorities of Passchendaele, Wytschaete and Messines for their planning of year-long events in 2007 to commemorate the 90th anniversary of the Third Battle of Ypres, otherwise known as "Passchendaele" in which there were over 500,000 British and German casualties, and further recognises that the Celtic cross will be an important, permanent reminder in keeping alive the memory of the Scottish soldiers from the Great War and will also serve as a wonderful testament to the people of Flanders in showing their thanks for the sacrifice of the Scottish soldiers and also to those Scots whose final resting place is far away from home in "Flanders Fields".

Supported by: Mr Brian Monteith*, Mr Kenneth Macintosh*, Campbell Martin*, Murray Tosh*, Fergus Ewing*, Robin Harper*, Mr Mark Ruskell*, Brian Adam*

S2M-4257 Michael Matheson: Privatisation of Search and Rescue Services—That the Parliament recognises the invaluable service provided by Scotland’s mountain rescue teams who, in partnership with the RAF, Royal Navy and Coastguard helicopters, have saved thousands of lives over the decades; notes with concern plans by Her Majesty’s Government to privately outsource search and rescue services; believes that replacing a public professional service with private commercial companies would not meet the high standards of the military air crews and recognises that military air crews would lose essential training if no longer involved in civilian rescues; believes that the highly professional and well-established practice of civilian and military search and rescue services working in partnership has served Scotland well, and calls on the Scottish Executive to make representations to Her Majesty’s Government that the existing search and rescue service should be preserved. S2M-4247 Rosie Kane: Removal of the Hazizi Family Flies in the Face of the Fresh Talent Initiative—That the Parliament condemns the continued treatment, considered by many to be barbaric, of asylum seekers by the Home Office; is concerned for the well-being and safety of the Hazizi family who were "dawn raided" this morning at their home at the Red Road flats in Glasgow; is concerned for David Hazizi, aged 14, who had hoped to return to school today after the Easter break but is now extremely traumatised and is destined for detention and removal along with his sister Marita, aged 20, who has been studying mathematics and science at the University of Strathclyde but who has now been grabbed away from the final year of her degree and her hopes of becoming a maths teacher here in Scotland, and their brother Gani, aged 19, who has been taken from his partner Lisa and from his unborn child who is due in six weeks time; regrets that the Hazizi family, who have lived in the UK since May 2001, now find themselves in danger and fear, and demands that the First Minister, along with Paul Martin, MSP for the Springburn constituency, and Malcolm Chisholm, Minister for Communities, intervene immediately in the interest of attracting and holding "Fresh Talent" here in Scotland, keeping Gani with his partner and their child and allowing Marita to complete her studies and work here in Scotland.

S2M-4246 Rosie Kane: Stress on Asylum Seekers—That the Parliament is concerned at the continued stress that asylum seekers find themselves under due to their treatment at the hands of the Home Office; commends all who work with asylum seekers in an effort to alleviate their pain and distress; is concerned that an asylum seeker attempted to take his own life at the Home Office building in Brand Street, Glasgow on 18 April 2006 by cutting his throat with a shard of glass, and invites all members of the Scottish Parliament to show compassion towards Scotland’s new citizens for the good of those who come here and for the economic and demographic security of Scotland.

S2M-4245 Christine Grahame: Lorry Drivers, Politicians and Sleep Apnoea—That the Parliament welcomes the exhibition in the Garden Lobby from 18 to 20 April 2006 on sleep disorders; notes that in such conditions the upper airway collapses intermittently and repeatedly during sleep, preventing breathing; notes that there are estimated to be 50,000 adults in Scotland who have this condition, affecting around 1 to 2% of middle-aged men, which is a similar prevalence to Type 1 diabetes; notes that being overweight and in a sedentary occupation increases the likelihood of suffering from this disorder which includes occupations such as lorry and train drivers and, indeed, politicians; further notes that only some 5,000 people receive treatment and that the person suffering from Obstructive Sleep Apnoea is often the last to be aware of the problem; notes that this condition can be successfully managed with a Continuous Positive Airway Pressure (CPAP) machine which is recommended as the first line of treatment by the Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network; notes that research at the Edinburgh Sleep Centre concluded that treating 500 patients with CPAP machines for five years would cost £400,000 whereas the estimated cost of accidents caused by 500 untreated sleep apnoea patients over the same period could be £5.3 million; notes that the waiting time for treatment at the Edinburgh Sleep Centre has, however, now risen to 23 months, and therefore urges the Scottish Executive to fund sleep centres adequately and to promote awareness of Obstructive Sleep Apnoea.

Supported by: Eleanor Scott, Murray Tosh, Frances Curran, Ms Rosemary Byrne, Tricia Marwick, Mr David Davidson, Brian Adam

S2M-4244 Shona Robison: National Depression Week—That the Parliament recognises that 17 to 23 April 2006 is National Depression Week and supports the work by the Depression Alliance Scotland in raising awareness about depression; realises that depression is a common illness affecting one in five people in Scotland; notes that estimates suggest that as many as 75% of people with depression do not seek help from their doctor because they see it as a weakness and not a treatable illness, and hopes that National Depression Week will help to overcome the stigma that those who suffer from depression in Scotland still face.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie

S2M-4241 Christine Grahame: Just Pull Yourself Together?—That the Parliament recognises the work of Depression Alliance Scotland in providing information and support for anyone affected by depression and anyone caring for someone affected by depression; supports its campaign in raising awareness and understanding of depression, an illness which affected around 300,000 people in Scotland in the last recorded year, half of whom attempt to struggle on without seeking help; recognises that it is an illness and is not simply a case of "pulling oneself together" but that in the majority of cases is treatable, and fully supports its "Mother Father Son Daughter Brother Sister Lover Friend You" campaign raising awareness that depression affects us all either through personal experience or by knowing someone who suffers from depression and that depression is not something to be afraid to admit to or be ashamed of.

Supported by: Donald Gorrie

S2M-4057# Des McNulty: 20th Anniversary of Nuclear Accident at Chernobyl—That the Parliament notes that 26 April 2006 is the 20th anniversary of the nuclear accident at Chernobyl and that the cross-party International Development Group is holding a meeting on that date to observe the anniversary of the accident, learn lessons from the past and look to the future; reflects upon the terrible social and environmental legacy that this horrific accident left, and continues to leave, in Ukraine, Belarus, Russia and beyond; pays respect to the many people whose lives were lost following the explosion; acknowledges with sadness the implications for all those who continue to suffer ill-health caused by radiation poisoning today and the severe difficulties still faced by the communities worst affected; further notes that pollution from the accident affected countries as far away as Scotland, where it continues to leave an environmental legacy; strongly commends Scottish charities and civic organisations for their outstanding work in supporting individuals and affected communities, and recognises the need to take national and international action to avoid any possibility of future environmental catastrophes such as the Chernobyl nuclear accident ever being repeated.
