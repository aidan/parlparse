Section F – Motions and Amendments

Motions and amendments are normally printed the day after they are lodged. If an amendment is lodged to a motion then the original motion will appear alongside the amendment, along with any support lodged that day, in this section.

Each Monday, this section also contains all motions and amendments lodged the previous week that are still live and any motions and amendments that have been lodged for debate in the coming week. A motion or amendment is live if it has not been debated, withdrawn or otherwise deleted from the list.

Where a motion or amendment has been withdrawn, it is indicated in this section the day after it has been withdrawn. Support for motions and amendments received after they are lodged is shown at the end of this section the day after such support is received.

Motions and amendments can be published with various symbols:

  • Asterisks before the motion or amendment number indicate a motion or amendment published for the first time;

  • Asterisks also identify alterations to the text of a motion or amendment made since it was first published;

  • A hash symbol identifies motions eligible for debate at Members’ Business;

  • A diamond symbol identifies motions lodged for Members’ Business that have not yet attracted the required cross-party support;

  • An "R", identifies motions or amendments in which the Member who lodged it has a registrable interest.

The Parliamentary Bureau periodically deletes motions or amendments not scheduled for debate and which are over six weeks old.

A full list of current motions is available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/motions.htm.

A search facility is also available on the Scottish Parliament web site at http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/business/motionsandamendments/index.htm.

Any questions in relation to this section should be directed to the Chamber Desk, for which contact details are provided at the end of the Bulletin.

*S4M-00432 Margaret Mitchell: Dalziel High School’s Choral Shield Show 2011—That the Parliament congratulates the upper school pupils of Dalziel High School, Motherwell on their successful Choral Shield Show 2011, performed at the SECC in Glasgow; notes that the annual competition between the three school houses of Barclay, Colville and Greig saw each house perform its own show in front of an audience on two nights; commends the hard work of the pupils and dedication of the staff in producing entertaining and high quality performances developed by the pupils themselves; acknowledges that the competition was sponsored by local businesses Reid Printers Ltd., GM Training, Graham and Sibbald, and Hamilton Road Dental Practice; further congratulates Colville House on winning the shield; considers that the competition is an excellent opportunity not only for the pupils to work together as a team but for the school to show its talents to the local community, and further commends the pupils of Dalziel High School for using this annual event to raise funds for St. Andrew’s Hospice, Airdrie.

*S4M-00431 Bob Doris: Young Player Awards—That the Parliament congratulates the winners of the North Glasgow Football Development Group’s (NGFDG) 2011 Young Player Awards, Robbie McPhee, Francis Gallagher, Kyle Sloss, Kieran Marshall, Calvin O’Brien, Jay Findlay, Reegan Shearer, Louis Fisher, Dylan Shannon and Aiden Laurie, and recognises what it considers the enormous contribution of NGFDG volunteers over the past year in encouraging these youngsters to take pride in their sporting endeavours.

*S4M-00430 James Dornan: Celebrating the 10th Anniversary of the Scottish Football Museum—That the Parliament celebrates the 10th anniversary of the Scottish Football Museum at Hampden Park; notes that the Scottish Football Museum exists to promote the unique football heritage of Scotland, to build and maintain a national football collection and to educate and inspire future generations; considers the museum to be a valuable national asset in the ambition to challenge sectarianism across Scotland; congratulates all the staff at the museum over the years who have contributed to its success, and encourages as many people as possible to visit the museum on 9 or 10 July 2011 and to take up its special anniversary offer of a 5p entry.

*S4M-00429 Patricia Ferguson: Maryhill Locks Transformational Regeneration Area Site Start—That the Parliament notes the site start for the Maryhill Locks Transformational Regeneration Area (TRA); recognises that this project will create over one hundred new houses for social rent and shared equity and provide employment and apprenticeship opportunities for local people, and congratulates the Maryhill TRA Local Delivery Group and its partners on this landmark event.

*S4M-00428 Liam McArthur: Mackerel Fishing Dispute Threatens Stocks Sustainability—That the Parliament commends Scottish mackerel fishermen on having achieved Marine Stewardship Certification in January 2009; expresses concern at the threats to this certification and to the sustainability of mackerel stocks due to the setting of autonomous quotas by Iceland and Faroes; considers that the decline of the mackerel stock would be an environmental disaster with serious socioeconomic implications; acknowledges the deep concern of fishermen, scientists and NGOs such as RSPB Scotland for the future of the stock, and supports calls for a swift and fair resolution based on the principles of sound science and sustainable fishing.

*S4M-00427 James Dornan: Congratulations to Deanpark Nursery—That the Parliament congratulates the staff, children and parents of Deanpark Nursery School on achieving what it considers a fabulous HMIE report containing four very good and one excellent rating; further congratulates them on achieving, on the same day, their first green flag for their environmental work; commends what it considers the excellent work of the headteacher, Sharon Downey, and her staff at the nursery in taking the nursery forward, and wishes the nursery every success for the future.

*S4M-00426 Jim Eadie: Mobilise for DLA—That the Parliament notes the recent Leonard Cheshire Disability campaign, Mobilise for DLA, expressing concern about the UK Government’s Welfare Reform Bill; is concerned at the proposal in the bill to remove the mobility component of the Personal Independence Payment, the benefit that is replacing the Disability Living Allowance for people in state-funded residential care; further notes the concerns expressed by disability charities and disabled people across the country that the bill could have a devastating impact on 80,000 disabled people across the United Kingdom; applauds those who made the long journey to London to express their fears in person at the Hardest Hit march in May 2011, and calls on the UK Government and the House of Lords, which is currently considering the bill, to think again and save the independence of disabled people.

*S4M-00425 Jenny Marra: Derecognition of UCU and UNITE the Union at Robert Gordon University—That the Parliament condemns the derecognition of the University and College Union (UCU) and UNITE the Union by Robert Gordon University (RGU), Aberdeen, without any prior warning, discussion or consultation; questions the university’s adherence to due conduct without properly consulting with the Board of Governors on this decision; calls for the new Principal of RGU to reconsider this decision, which was taken just prior to his arrival, and supports the union members in their right to recognition in the workplace.

*S4M-00424 Colin Keir: Edinburgh Airport Voted Best in Europe—That the Parliament congratulates Edinburgh International Airport on being voted the best European airport of its size at the Airport Council International Europe Best Airport Awards in Lisbon; notes that the awards cover four categories of airport and recognise outstanding performance in areas including customer service, retail, security and environmental awareness; further notes that the airport was praised by judges for the dedication of its management and staff and excelling in all major areas of operations in the 5 to 10 million passengers category, and considers the award to be a further testimony of Edinburgh Airport’s success and international reputation characterised by its ability to attract new routes and airlines to Scotland and its ability to grow into a competitive and leading European airport.

*S4M-00423 Jackie Baillie: Top Marks for Mary McKeirnan—That the Parliament congratulates Mrs Mary McKeirnan, Principal Teacher of Modern Studies at Hermitage Academy in Helensburgh, on her retirement; notes that Mrs McKeirnan is retiring on 30 June 2011; further notes that she started teaching at Hermitage Academy in 1985 and has been Principal Teacher of Modern Studies for over 12 years; acknowledges the contribution that she has made to the modern studies department at Hermitage Academy, including her organisation of visits to the Scottish Parliament; understands that pupils and colleagues both past and present appreciate her hard work and dedication to Hermitage Academy over the last 26 years, and wishes Mrs McKeirnan a long and happy retirement.

*S4M-00422 Lewis Macdonald: Aberdeen to Frankfurt Air Link—That the Parliament congratulates Lufthansa and Aberdeen Airport on the forthcoming launch of a three-times-a-day service between Aberdeen and Frankfurt later in 2011; notes that Aberdeen now enjoys direct routes with flag carriers to London Heathrow, Dublin, Charles de Gaulle, Schiphol and Frankfurt, hub airports which provide a wide range of connections around the world; believes that direct routes between airports in Scotland and overseas destinations are good for exports, tourism, inward investment and the wider economy, and looks forward to further new initiatives to improve Scotland’s connectivity, which, it considers, should include support from the Scottish Government for the promotion and development of new direct routes.

*S4M-00421 Jamie Hepburn: 1st July, International Tartan Day—That the Parliament observes that 1 July 2011 marks the Australasian International Tartan Day; notes that this will fall on the 229th anniversary of the repeal of the Act of Proscription 1746, which made wearing tartan or a kilt illegal in Scotland and punishable by imprisonment or seven years’ transportation; considers that tartan days provide an opportunity for the communities of the Scottish diaspora to reconnect and celebrate their common ancestry; wishes those celebrating international tartan day in Australia and New Zealand every success, and calls on members to commemorate the day by wearing tartan on 1 July at the official opening of the fourth session of the Parliament.

*S4M-00420 Sandra White: Budget for Bursaries, Scottish Charity Awards Campaign of the Year—That the Parliament congratulates NUS Scotland and its member students’ associations on winning the Campaign of the Year award at the recent SCVO Scottish Charity Awards for their Budget for Bursaries campaign, which highlighted the importance of college bursaries in providing support for students who otherwise would be unable to study; welcomes the support of what it considers the overwhelming majority of MSPs in support of this campaign alongside the Scottish Government’s announcement to provide £15 million investment in support for the poorest college students; understands that much still needs to be done to ensure that everyone continues to be given the opportunity to access further education, and hopes that by continuing to work constructively with NUS Scotland and others this can be achieved.

*S4M-00418 Bob Doris: School of Rugby for St Roch’s Secondary—That the Parliament congratulates Saint Roch’s Secondary School S1/S2 rugby team on its successes over the past year; notes that the squad played at the Glasgow schools sevens tournament, where the team won the plate competition, the Emerging Schools cup, the Glasgow youth game and the Allan Glen’s tournament; further notes that, as a result of the team’s high standard of performance, it finished the season playing in the top division of Glasgow schools, and wishes the team well in its application to the Scottish Rugby Union to join its School of Rugby programme for next year.

Supported by: Stuart McMillan*, Humza Yousaf*

*S4M-00417 Liam McArthur: Orkney Housing Association Ltd to Complete its 800th house—That the Parliament notes that the Orkney Housing Association Ltd expects to complete its 800th house in July 2011; further notes that, since its formation in 1985, the housing association has built homes on 44 sites spread across the islands and mainland of Orkney, including a safe refuge for women and children fleeing domestic abuse and a 2-unit project on a remote isle to bring new families into the community, so saving the local school; recognises the challenge of building affordable, warm homes in the islands where construction costs are particularly high and the climate is challenging, and where the high cost of living puts particular pressure on affordable rent levels; is concerned that, despite these achievements, the demand for affordable housing in Orkney still outstrips supply so that the decimation of the association’s new build programme caused by what is considered to be the unrealistic level of subsidy now available to Registered Social Landlords, urgently needs to be addressed, and congratulates the housing association on its achievements to date and wishes it well for the future.

*S4M-00412 Jackie Baillie: Action on Standards for Children with Asthma—That the Parliament notes the survey by Health Improvement Scotland on part of the mandatory standards of care for children and young people with asthma published on 27 June 2011; understands that there are 72,000 children with asthma in Scotland, one of the highest rates in the world, and that the standards were developed to ensure that children and young people with asthma receive a high level of care no matter where they live in Scotland; notes that the survey shows that some NHS boards have no register of local children and young people who have asthma, fail to record the number of children admitted to hospital with asthma symptoms and have not developed a care protocol for such children and young people; believes that the Scottish Government must make asthma a priority given that emergency admissions for children have remained at a high level for the last 10 years without any sign of being reduced; further believes that Health Improvement Scotland should examine NHS boards against all of the standards of care for children, and calls on the Scottish Government to ensure that NHS boards end what is considered the complacency that exists towards children with asthma.

*S4M-00403 Jim Eadie: ChildLine Edinburgh—That the Parliament expresses its deep concern at the proposal by the National Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Children (NSPCC) to close the Edinburgh office of ChildLine; notes that, if carried out, this will result in the loss of 12 staff and 100 volunteer posts; considers that, since opening three years ago, ChildLine Edinburgh has provided an outstanding level of service; pays tribute to the work of the highly skilled and trained managers, staff and volunteers from across east central Scotland and Fife, who, it believes, have helped ChildLine make a difference to the lives of so many children and young people across the country; recognises the concerns of staff and volunteers regarding these proposals; considers that they have been both poorly informed and poorly consulted; believes that the loss of what it sees as this talented pool of highly motivated staff and volunteers to the ChildLine service would be a serious blow to the voluntary sector in Edinburgh, and urges the NSPCC to extend its period of consultation in order for alternatives to closure to be fully explored and to allow its proposal to be reconsidered.
