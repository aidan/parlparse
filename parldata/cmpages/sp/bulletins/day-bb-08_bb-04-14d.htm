Business Bulletin No. 57/2008: Monday 14 April 2008
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 17 April 2008

Question Time

General

1. Hugh Henry: To ask the Scottish Executive how many of the teachers who finished their probationary year in June 2007 have failed to find a job in teaching during the current academic year. (S3O-2933)

2. Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what issues will be discussed at the next Joint Ministerial Committee. (S3O-2883)

3. Brian Adam: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has any plans to monitor the provision of alternative and augmentative communication aids by individual health authorities. (S3O-2859)

4. Patricia Ferguson: To ask the Scottish Executive what savings have been accrued from the merger of the seven previously available funds into the Fairer Scotland Fund. (S3O-2926)

5. Alasdair Morgan: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with Her Majesty’s Government regarding ITV’s proposals for news broadcasting in the Border TV area. (S3O-2858)

6. Alex Neil: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied with the performance of Historic Scotland. (S3O-2878)

7. Pauline McNeill: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider compiling annual statistics on the incidence of offences committed against workers in the cash and valuables in transit industry. (S3O-2945)

8. David Whitton: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Minister for Communities and Sport will produce his strategy for housing. (S3O-2957)

9. Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive whether guidance has been issued to local authorities regarding the payment of kinship care allowances. (S3O-2948)

10. David Stewart: To ask the Scottish Executive what incentives and financial support it is considering to encourage people to train in rural medicine and commit to working in remote and rural areas. (S3O-2955)

Finance and Sustainable Growth

1. Jackie Baillie: To ask the Scottish Executive why, in advance of a study by Audit Scotland to establish whether the First Scotrail franchise is giving value for money, it announced the extension of that franchise for a further period of three years without prior reference to the Parliament. (S3O-2916)

2. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive what support is to be provided to businesses to expand work placement opportunities. (S3O-2894)

3. Iain Gray: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to support Scotland’s tourism industry. (S3O-2932)

4. Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is satisfied with the way in which Scottish Water provides compensation for damage it causes to farm land. (S3O-2874)

5. Des McNulty: To ask the Scottish Executive what response it has made to representations from the bus industry regarding the Bus Service Operators Grant. (S3O-2946)

6. Johann Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive how the Small Business Bonus Scheme will aid economic growth. (S3O-2937)

7. Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in relation to its plans for an airport station at Gogar since the decision on 27 September 2007 regarding the Edinburgh Airport Rail Link. (S3O-2898)

8. Ian McKee: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is considering making further budgetary provision to support artists at the outset of their careers, in light of the new funding provided for the arts via its budget. (S3O-2875)

9. Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive how it intends to support Business Improvement Districts. (S3O-2947)

10. Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it proposes to increase the level of public expenditure in Scotland. (S3O-2887)

11. Malcolm Chisholm: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will reconsider the distance criteria that are proposed as a precondition of permitted development rights for wind turbines. (S3O-2922)

12. Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider providing additional financial assistance to Highland Council to help in delivering education services. (S3O-2899)

13. Lewis Macdonald: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it can take to help Aberdeen City Council achieve long-term financial stability and avoid cuts to essential services. (S3O-2941)

14. Christina McKelvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what its current position is on the use of ID cards to access services for which it has responsibility. (S3O-2876)

15. Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with Her Majesty’s Government regarding maximising European funding opportunities for Scotland. (S3O-2949)

16. Bill Aitken: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with Strathclyde Partnership for Transport about the development of the Glasgow Subway. (S3O-2903)

17. Stuart McMillan: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with COSLA regarding the recent Audit Scotland report, Improving the School Estate, and in particular the annual charges from PFI contracts. (S3O-2877)

18. Alison McInnes: To ask the Scottish Executive why it chose to extend the First Scotrail franchise three years before the end of the term. (S3O-2890)

19. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will publish details of how and from which budget it will fund the building and running of Bishopbriggs prison. (S3O-2938)

20. John Park: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had regarding the development of the port of Rosyth ferry services. (S3O-2950)
