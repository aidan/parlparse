Business Bulletin No. 90/2010: Thursday 27 May 2010
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section D - Oral questions selected for answer on 3 June 2010

Question Time

General

1. Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it will take to support nurseries in the independent sector in Fife that have seen the rates on their buildings increase by between just under 30% and 200% in the absence of transitional rates relief. (S3O-10754)

2. Ross Finnie: To ask the Scottish Executive how it plans to engage with the development of the UK Government legislation to transfer further powers to Scotland. (S3O-10817)

3. Robin Harper: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress has been made in revising or withdrawing section five of the Autism Toolbox, Overview of Interventions, following its acceptance of the findings of the review in November 2009. (S3O-10776)

4. Not Lodged

5. Margaret Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive whether NHS patients referred to private facilities for treatment have recourse to the NHS’s complaints process and, if not, for what reason and whether patients are informed of this before treatment. (S3O-10826)

6. Charlie Gordon: To ask the Scottish Executive when it will report to the Parliament on the outcome of its review of ferry services. (S3O-10748)

7. Alasdair Allan: To ask the Scottish Executive what it will do to work with local authorities willing to consider making Gaelic the default medium of education in the early years of mainstream primary education. (S3O-10789)

8. Bill Butler: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde in respect of the community health and care partnership framework in Glasgow. (S3O-10740)

9. Jeremy Purvis: To ask the Scottish Executive when post offices will be able to access the £1 million Post Office Diversification Fund included in the 2010-11 budget. (S3O-10823)

10. John Park: To ask the Scottish Executive when adult mental health will come under the current waiting time guarantee. (S3O-10767)

Justice and Law Officers

1. Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive when the Common Performance Management Platform will be running in all eight Scottish police forces and the Scottish Crime and Drug Enforcement Agency. (S3O-10764)

2. Elizabeth Smith: To ask the Scottish Executive how many prison officers have been prosecuted for crimes involved in supplying drugs to prisoners since 1999. (S3O-10784)

3. Ted Brocklebank: To ask the Scottish Executive what percentage of those prosecuted for carrying a knife in the last year has received a sentence of six months or less. (S3O-10781)

4. Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what its responsibilities are regarding policing. (S3O-10816)

5. Anne McLaughlin: To ask the Scottish Executive what action will be taken following the report of the short-life working group on naloxone. (S3O-10805)

6. Joe FitzPatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive what progress is being made to ensure swift justice following a conviction. (S3O-10796)

7. David McLetchie: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider reviewing the discounts given on sentences to accused persons who plead guilty at different stages of the trial process. (S3O-10783)

8. Karen Whitefield: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to tackle antisocial behaviour. (S3O-10773)

9. John Lamont: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met representatives of Lothian and Borders Police. (S3O-10780)

10. Shirley-Anne Somerville: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps are being taken to tackle homophobic and other hate crimes. (S3O-10810)

Rural Affairs and the Environment

1. Sandra White: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it has taken to improve the legal protection of wildlife corridors in the urban environment. (S3O-10814)

2. Christopher Harvie: To ask the Scottish Executive what contribution rural specialist food production has made to the Scottish GDP in the last year and whether information is available on the balance between home and export sales of such rural products. (S3O-10799)

3. Nicol Stephen: To ask the Scottish Executive what work is underway to increase proportionality in the penalty system for farmers breaching EU rules. (S3O-10827)

4. Jamie Stone: To ask the Scottish Executive how it is encouraging new entrants into crofting. (S3O-10828)

5. Nigel Don: To ask the Scottish Executive what steps it is taking to promote food and drink from the north-east. (S3O-10793)

6. Paul Martin: To ask the Scottish Executive what it is doing to promote the development of waste management infrastructure. (S3O-10755)

7. Cathie Craigie: To ask the Scottish Executive what encouragement it gives to local authorities and community organisations to protect natural environments. (S3O-10742)

8. Mike Pringle: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to protect endangered plant and animal species as part of the United Nations International Year of Biodiversity. (S3O-10822)

9. Alison McInnes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has reconsidered the interpretation of the Rural Priorities scheme guidance to permit anaerobic digestion plants to receive funding. (S3O-10820)

10. Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive when it last met the chief executive of Scottish Natural Heritage. (S3O-10803)
