Business Bulletin No. 191/2010: Tuesday 21 December 2010
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 20 December 2010

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-38450 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive how many beavers have been captured in Tayside as a result of efforts by Scottish Natural Heritage and Tayside Police and where they have been relocated to.

S3W-38451 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive what the reasons are for the recent efforts by Scottish Natural Heritage and Tayside Police to capture and relocate beavers found in Tayside.

S3W-38452 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has identified the species of the beaver populations found in Tayside.

S3W-38453 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the recent efforts by Scottish Natural Heritage and Tayside Police to capture and relocate beavers found in Tayside resulted from concerns regarding invasive non-native species and, if so, what assessment has been made of the animals’ potential impact and how the results of the assessment compare with those of any similar assessments in relation to the animals involved in the Scottish Beaver Trial.

S3W-38454 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive in what way its experiences of beaver populations in Tayside and in the Scottish Beaver Trial have influenced the proposals in the Wildlife and Natural Environment (Scotland) Bill concerning invasive non-native species and natural range.

S3W-38455 Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive in what way the additional houses to be built under the National Housing Trust will benefit lower income households as stated by the Minister for Housing and Communities to The Herald on 14 December 2010.

S3W-38456 Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive what housing rents were charged by each (a) housing association and (b) local authority in 2009-10.

S3W-38457 Mary Mulligan: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the reported comment by the chief executive of the Scottish Federation of Housing Associations that "when the value of forward commitments is deducted from the draft housing budget, the amount of money left could be as little as £150 million".

S3W-38458 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-38043 by Shona Robison on 16 December 2010, what the annual cost was of NHS Tayside’s Quit 4 U smoking cessation pilot project.

S3W-38459 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to answer to question S3W-38043 by Shona Robison on 16 December 2010, whether NHS Tayside’s Quit 4 U smoking cessation pilot project was evaluated and, if so, whether it was regarded as a success and, if so, for what reason funding was not continued.

S3W-38460 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-37439 by Shona Robison on 10 November 2010, what evaluation has made of the effectiveness of the expenditure on smoking cessation in (a) 2008-09 and (b) 2009-10.

S3W-38461 Dr Richard Simpson: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-37464 by Shona Robison on 11 November 2010, how the £2,500,000 allocated in 2010-11 to smoke-free laws is being spent.

S3W-38462 Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive how many hospitals have accident and emergency units.

S3W-38463 Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive how many hospital accident and emergency units have been forced to close in the last year due to staff shortages.

S3W-38464 Marilyn Livingstone: To ask the Scottish Executive how many closures of hospital accident and emergency units due to staff shortages in the last year have been attributed to the impact of the European working time directive.

S3W-38465 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason it has not considered the report of the short-life working group on school meals and clothing grants in the 21 months since it was submitted.

S3W-38466 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will consider the report of the short-life working group on school meals and clothing grants before the end of the current parliamentary session.

S3W-38467 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive for what reason it has not published the report of the short-life working group on school meals and clothing grants and when it will be published.

S3W-38468 Michael Matheson: To ask the Scottish Executive what its response is to the report, A Community and Landowner Renewable Energy Loan Fund, by the Scottish Agricultural College.

S3W-38469 Elaine Murray: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures it can take to influence the rules governing the repayment of student loans to the Student Loans Company.

S3W-38470 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how much of the (a) £70 million Change Fund and (b) £5 million Carer Information Strategies funding will be available to support local carers’ organisations.

S3W-38471 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive how the £70 million Change Fund is to be distributed.

S3W-38472 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive where the Saltire flags procured for the purpose of celebrating St Andrew’s Day 2010 were manufactured.

S3W-38473 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list the (a) agencies, (b) organisations and (c) public bodies and services that it has provided with Saltire flags for the purpose of celebrating St Andrew’s Day 2010 and what the total cost was.

S3W-38474 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what information it provided to schools regarding St Andrew’s Day 2010 and at what cost.

S3W-38475 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list the Saltire-branded products that it commissioned or produced for the purpose of celebrating St Andrew’s Day 2010.

S3W-38476 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive how much of its mail was despatched by (a) the Royal Mail and (b) other carriers in the last year and at what cost per carrier.

S3W-38477 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive what its estimate is of the impact of the rise in the cost of a (a) first class stamp to 46p and (b) second class stamp to 36p on the budget of (i) the Scottish Government, (ii) each Scottish Government directorate and (iii) the NHS in 2010-11.

S3W-38478 Nicol Stephen: To ask the Scottish Executive how many newly qualified nursing graduates have been employed in each of the last six intakes, broken down by NHS board.

S3W-38479 Nicol Stephen: To ask the Scottish Executive how many newly qualified midwifery graduates have been employed in each of the last six intakes, broken down by NHS board.

S3W-38480 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it will list all (a) activities and (b) events that each minister attended in connection with St Andrew’s day 2010, providing the (i) date, (ii) name, (iii) description and (iv) purpose of each.

S3W-38481 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has given direction to local authorities regarding the compilation and maintenance of a comprehensive record of common good assets.

S3W-38482 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it is considering imposing a legal duty on local authorities to compile and maintain a record of common good assets

S3W-38483 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it considers that an amendment to the Public Records (Scotland) Bill would be an appropriate vehicle for imposing a requirement on local authorities to compile and maintain a comprehensive record of common good assets.

S3W-38484 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to reduce procedural errors in appeals against planning decisions.

S3W-38485 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it takes to ensure that local authorities notify owners of land significantly affected by planning applications.

S3W-38486 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive which neighbouring owners of land require to be notified about planning applications.

S3W-38487 Robert Brown: To ask the Scottish Executive what its position is on the adequacy of the 20-metre limit for notifying neighbours of a planning application.
