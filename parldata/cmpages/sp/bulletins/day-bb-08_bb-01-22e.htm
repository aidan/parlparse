Business Bulletin No. 12/2008: Tuesday 22 January 2008
Contents An A B C D E F G H I J

Section E – Written questions lodged on 21 January 2008

Questions marked with a triangle (Δ) are initiated by the Scottish Executive in order to facilitate the provision of information to the Parliament.

Questions in which a member has indicated a declarable interest are marked with an "R".

S3W-8693 Alasdair Allan: To ask the Scottish Executive whether Orkney and Shetland sheep stockholders are allowed to include adjoining seaweed grazing areas below the level of the mean high water spring tide level as part of their eligible forage areas.

S3W-8694 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many landlords have been refused registration under the National Registration Scheme for Private Landlords, broken down by local authority area.

S3W-8695 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how it can be assured of the validity and accuracy of electronic data in applications relating to the National Registration Scheme for Private Landlords.

S3W-8696 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many landlords have registered under the National Registration Scheme for Private Landlords, broken down by local authority.

S3W-8697 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive how many landlords have been prosecuted for providing false information in applications for registration under the National Registration Scheme for Private Landlords.

S3W-8698 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether all applications for registration under the National Registration Scheme for Private Landlords require to be made on a signed application form.

S3W-8699 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the absence of a signed application form for registration under the National Registration Scheme for Private Landlords removes the prospect for any form of prosecution in respect of the scheme.

S3W-8700 Mary Scanlon: To ask the Scottish Executive what advice, assistance and complaints investigation local authorities offer under the National Registration Scheme for Private Landlords.

S3W-8701 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it has had discussions with (a) Ofgem and (b) Her Majesty’s Government regarding proposals from Ofgem for a possible windfall tax on energy companies benefiting from the free allocation of tradable emissions permits; if so, what the outcome has been and, if not, whether it will consider initiating such discussions as a matter of urgency.

S3W-8702 Liam McArthur: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it agrees with the Ofgem proposal to use the money raised through a windfall tax on energy companies to introduce measures targeted at addressing the problems of fuel poverty; if so, how it would envisage any such measures being taken forward in Scotland and, if not, what alternative proposals it has for the use in Scotland of the funds raised.

S3W-8703Δ Nigel Don: To ask the Scottish Executive whether the Inquiries (Scotland) Rules 2007 which came into force on 19 January 2008 apply to the public inquiry into the explosion on 11 May 2004 at the factory in Glasgow occupied by the ICL group of companies.

S3W-8704 Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body when it will be able to determine the nature and extent of the water penetration in the foundations and structures of the building in the area adjacent to the garden level restaurant.

S3W-8705 Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body whether the cost of repairs to the foundations and structures of the building in the area adjacent to the garden level restaurant will be borne by it or the relevant contractors.

S3W-8706 Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body what retentions in respect of the Holyrood complex are still being held.

S3W-8707 Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body what investigations it has initiated into the cause of the ingress of water around the windows of the MSP block.

S3W-8708 Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body by what process it decided to close the Parliament building to members of the public, other than those who are accompanied by pass holders, on Sundays.

S3W-8709 Margo MacDonald: To ask the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body what discussions it has had with personnel at the Palace of Holyroodhouse or Our Dynamic Earth regarding the attraction of greater numbers of visitors on Sundays.

S3W-8710 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with transport partners regarding improving transport links between Cunninghame South, Glasgow and the rest of Scotland.

S3W-8711 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with transport partners regarding the upgrading of the A737.

S3W-8712 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with transport partners regarding improving the rail link between Ayr and Glasgow.

S3W-8713 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive what discussions it has had with transport partners regarding reducing journey times between Ayr and Glasgow.

S3W-8714 Irene Oldfather: To ask the Scottish Executive how much money will be allocated to improving transport links between Cunninghame South and Glasgow in (a) 2008, (b) 2009 and (c) 2010.

S3W-8715 John Farquhar Munro: To ask the Scottish Executive how many accidents were reported on the A87 between 25 December 2007 and 21 January 2008 and how this figure compares with previous years.

S3W-8716 Bashir Ahmad: To ask the Scottish Executive what the incidence is of (a) type 1 and (b) type 2 diabetes in each NHS board area, broken down by ethnic group.

S3W-8717 Margaret Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive what measures of diabetes-related hospital admissions rates and bed days will be used in order to assess its HEAT target.

S3W-8718 Margaret Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive whether it can guarantee that the homebase of the National Theatre of Scotland will be in Easterhouse and that all the implications of the previous administration’s commitment to base the theatre in Easterhouse will be honoured.

S3W-8719 Margaret Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive what plans it has to increase the number of speech and language therapists.

S3W-8720 Margaret Curran: To ask the Scottish Executive what consultation was undertaken in relation to the extension of the Emergency Workers (Scotland) Act 2005.

S3W-8721 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-8271 by John Swinney on 18 January 2008, how many staff are employed by the Scottish Government’s Video Production Unit.

S3W-8722 George Foulkes: To ask the Scottish Executive, further to the answer to question S3W-8271 by John Swinney on 18 January 2008, how the output of the Scottish Government’s Video Production Unit is distributed; where it can be accessed, and how its effectiveness is monitored.

S3W-8723 Joe FitzPatrick: To ask the Scottish Executive what action it is taking to support local authorities in providing additional support for learning needs.
