Business Bulletin No. 187/2001

Monday 17 December 2001

Section F : Motions and Amendments
  

A full list of outstanding motions is available to view each Monday in paper copy at the Chamber Desk or alternatively on the Scottish Parliament web site as Outstanding Motions. The full text of all motions lodged the previous week will appear in the Business Bulletin the following Monday.

 

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are new or have been altered. Asterisks in the text show the extent of alterations made.

Motions which members wish to be considered for debate as members' business in the Parliament are marked with a hash symbol (#)

*S1M-2551 Robert Brown: Ofgem Removal of Price Controls-That the Parliament notes with concern Ofgem's recent proposals to lift the remaining price controls on energy use in Scotland and, in particular, to lift the price caps on pre-payment tariffs which will have a detrimental effect on the less well off in paying fuel bills; believes that the energy market is not sufficiently developed to provide protection from uncompetitive pricing practices; further notes that customers with remote teleswitching meters are unable to change suppliers and therefore have no access to competition; is concerned that these proposals will compromise the Scottish Executive's ability to deliver an end to fuel poverty through measures including the Housing (Scotland) Act 2001, and urges Ofgem to withdraw their proposals insofar as they relate to electricity prices and pre-payment meter tariffs.

S1M-2550 Brian Adam: Donald Whyte, Genealogist-That the Parliament applauds the significant role played by Donald Whyte in encouraging the development of family history and genealogy in Scotland and calls on the Scottish Parliamentary Corporate Body to recognise in the new Parliament building the importance of researching family history in preserving Scotland's culture and heritage.

Supported by: Margaret Smith, Stewart Stevenson, Ms Sandra White, Michael Russell, Richard Lochhead, Mr Kenny MacAskill, Mr Murray Tosh, Fergus Ewing, Colin Campbell, Fiona McLeod*, Robin Harper*

S1M-2549# Marilyn Livingstone: Fifth Anniversary of Rehab Scotland's Fife Brain Injury Vocational Service-That the Parliament commends Rehab Scotland's Fife Brain Injury Vocational Service, based in Kirkcaldy, on completing five years this month of providing essential assistance to sufferers and increasing local awareness of the effects of brain injuries; welcomes the significant role which the service has played in empowering those affected by brain injuries throughout Fife in rebuilding their lives and increasing their independence; notes concern that many of the service's clients are young men under the age of 30, and calls upon the Scottish Executive to work with the voluntary sector to increase awareness of brain injuries, particularly among young people.

S1M-2547 Mr Brian Monteith: Physical Education in Primary Schools-That the Parliament welcomes the HM Inspectorate of Education report on improving standards of physical education in primary schools in Scotland; notes that the report states that schools with very good programmes often used flexible curriculum time to ensure a minimum of two physical education (PE) sessions of around 40 minutes weekly for pupils, provide broad and balanced programmes which include games, sports, gymnastics, dance and swimming and that the programmes in these schools often extend beyond the school day; compares this with provision at independent schools where PE is often given more time, regular input from specialist PE teachers and additional extracurricular opportunities are provided, and believes that the provision of more specialist PE teachers providing education in individual and team sports, dance and games would increase the number of schools achieving HM Inspectorate of Education's 'very good' status and lead to improvements in the health and well-being of Scotland's children.

Supported by: Alex Johnstone, Phil Gallie, Mr Keith Harding, Murdo Fraser, Tommy Sheridan, Lord James Douglas-Hamilton

S1M-2540 Trish Godman: Career Opportunities in the Scottish Maritime Industry-That the Parliament warmly commends the crew of the Caledonian MacBrayne ferry "Caledonian Isles", their shorebased colleagues and members of the Glasgow and West of Scotland Maritime Careers Group for their active participation in recent promotional events designed to raise schoolchildren's awareness of career opportunities in the Scottish maritime industry and believes that such recruitment initiatives will be of considerable importance to the industry and to Scottish school leavers who are keen to engage in satisfying and worthwhile careers both afloat and ashore.

Supported by: Marilyn Livingstone, Michael Russell, Tommy Sheridan

S1M-2539# Mr Duncan McNeil: Research into Cancer Levels in the Semiconductor Industry-That the Parliament notes the publication Cancer Among Current and Previous Workers at National Semiconductor (UK) Ltd, Greenock: Results of an investigation by the Health and Safety Executive; further notes that the study gives cause for concern which can only be addressed through further research across the semiconductor industry; believes that steps should be taken to ensure that GPs and other health professionals are well briefed and prepared for any enquiries from concerned current or former members of the workforce, and further believes that Ministers and officials of the Scottish Executive should work in partnership with all those concerned to ensure that issues arising from this research are addressed as a matter of priority.

Supported by: Marilyn Livingstone, Trish Godman, Tommy Sheridan, Alex Neil

S1M-2537 Mr Gil Paterson: Civil Legal Aid-That the Parliament supports the Cross-Party Group on Men's Violence Against Women and Children in its campaign to make it easier for abused women to access civil legal aid; calls on the Executive to implement the recommendations of the Justice 1 Committee that the eligibility criteria for civil legal aid be widened by removing the inconsistencies in the treatment of benefits and by increasing the lower capital limit; urges the Executive to increase as a matter of urgency the current qualifying income levels which ultimately determine whether or not an application can be considered eligible to receive legal aid, and further urges the Executive to review the scale of contributions payable by applicants to minimise their financial burden in order to recognise that many women who leave their abuser have additional demands on their income which further disadvantage them.

Supported by: Michael Matheson, Mr Kenneth Gibson, Mr Adam Ingram, Mr Kenny MacAskill, Stewart Stevenson, Fergus Ewing, Brian Adam, Ms Margo MacDonald, Michael Russell, Ms Sandra White, Shona Robison, Mrs Lyndsay McIntosh*, Robin Harper*

S1M-2536 Nora Radcliffe: European Notes and Coins-That the Parliament notes that the introduction of the new notes and coins for the single European currency on 1 January 2002 will begin the phasing out of the existing currencies of the eurozone; further notes that a recent survey found that the average British household presently has £35 worth of EU coins squirreled away, which suggests that up to £80 million's worth of left-over holiday money, which will become worthless after the changeover, may be presently lying about in Scotland, and urges all Scots to take advantage of the schemes run by banks to facilitate the donation of these monies to charities in the run-up to Christmas.

Supported by: John Farquhar Munro, Dennis Canavan, Mr Keith Raffan, Mr Mike Rumbles

S1M-2535 Mr Kenneth Gibson: Condemnation of Female Circumcision-That the Parliament expresses concern about violence against women and children especially in the form of female genital mutilation (FGM); commends the research on FGM being carried out by the International Centre for Reproductive Health; declares FGM to be a violation of the human rights of girls and women as it represents an attempt to control women's sexuality and subordinate their status in society; notes the existing legislation on this subject, and supports the continued promotion of women's rights in an effort to stop FGM.

Supported by: Mr Adam Ingram, Mr Gil Paterson, Tommy Sheridan, Robert Brown, Fergus Ewing, Michael Matheson, Ms Sandra White, Mrs Lyndsay McIntosh, Mr Kenneth Macintosh, Fiona McLeod, Roseanna Cunningham, Tricia Marwick, Dr Winnie Ewing, Robin Harper, Colin Campbell, Nora Radcliffe
