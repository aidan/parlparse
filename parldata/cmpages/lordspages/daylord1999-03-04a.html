The Answer is as follows:

    "The EU and US have been engaged in a long-running dispute in the World Trade Organisation over the EU's banana regime. Following two adverse WTO rulings in 1997, an amended regime was agreed by the Agriculture Council in June 1998.

    "The US and other complainants maintain that this regime still does not comply with WTO procedures. In November 1998, the US announced its intention to retaliate against the EU by imposing 100 per cent. import duties on selected products. The US sought WTO authorisation for that in January; at the request of the EU, the level of damage the US was seeking in retaliation was referred to arbitration due to be decided on 2nd March.

    "On Tuesday this week, the arbitrator, in an initial ruling, said that he needed more information to make a decision, and asked the parties to answer a series of questions by 15 March. He would then return to the issue and come to a final ruling 'soon' afterwards.

    "However, the US yesterday announced that, with immediate effect, the US Customs Service would begin 'withholding liquidation' on imports of various EU products, including those of cashmere knitwear. This prejudges the panel ruling and we believe is contrary to the WTO dispute settlement rules. The US argues that they will not apply the duties until after the arbitrator has ruled, but the practical effect of their measures is the same as if they applied now as exporters have to put up a bond to cover possible duties in the future.

    "I deplore the action which the United States has taken. It is completely unauthorised by any WTO procedures and wholly ignores the arbitrator's appeal for discussions to continue. Even now, it is not too late for the US to reverse this decision. It should do so not only in the interests of EU-US relationships but to safeguard the whole framework of dispute settlement within the WTO.

    "The US action is irrational and unacceptable. I have summoned the US Ambassador and will make these points to him later this afternoon. We shall also support an urgent convening of the WTO General Council.
