Future Business Part A: Business for the period ending on Thursday 16 September 2010

Includes the business announced by the Leader of the House (which may be changed up to the rising of the House on the day before it is to be taken and is therefore provisional.)

For Backbench business see Part B of this paper. For Remaining Orders and Notices see Part C of this paper. For Business to be taken in Westminster Hall see Part D of this paper. For Other future business see Part E of this paper. For Business to be taken in European Committees see Part F of this paper. For Notices of Public Petitions see Part G of this paper. For Notices of written ministerial statements see Part H of this paper.

Notes:
* Amended
†† On this day Private Members' Bills do not have precedence and the Bill cannot make progress.

A
Business for the period ending on Thursday 16 September 2010
   Includes the business announced by the Leader of the House (which may be changed up to the rising of the House on the day before it is to be taken and is therefore provisional).
Tuesday 27 July
Questions to the Deputy Prime Minister, including Topical Questions, to the Attorney General, to the Church Commissioners, to the Public Accounts Commission and to the Speaker’s Committee on the Electoral Commission.
Ten minute Rule Motion: Andrew Rosindell: Dog Control and Welfare: That leave be given to bring in a Bill to repeal the Dangerous Dogs Act 1991; to require the introduction of compulsory microchipping of dogs; to make provision relating to the welfare of dogs and public safety around dogs; and for connected purposes.
General Debate: Matters to be considered before the forthcoming adjournment.
   The Backbench Business Committee has chosen the usual format for business in which a Member can raise any issue.
The House will not adjourn until the Speaker has signified Royal Assent.
At the end of the sitting: Adjournment: Jim Fitzpatrick: Trigeminal neuralgia.
Monday 6 September
Questions to the Secretary of State for the Home Department, including Topical Questions.
Second Reading of the Parliamentary Voting System and Constituencies Bill.
At the end of the sitting: Adjournment: Subject to be announced.
Tuesday 7 September
Questions to the Secretary of State for Health, including Topical Questions.
Ten minute Rule Motion: Tom Brake: Freedom of Information (Amendment): That leave be given to bring in a Bill to amend the Freedom of Information Act 2000 to remove provisions permitting Ministers to overrule decisions of the Information Commissioner and Information Tribunal; to limit the time allowed for public authorities to respond to requests involving consideration of the public interest; to amend the definition of public authorities; and for connected purposes.
   Stephen Gilbert has withdrawn his notice of motion that leave be given to bring in a Bill to amend the Statutory Instruments Act 1946.
Second Reading of the Superannuation Bill.
At the end of the sitting: Adjournment: Subject to be announced.
Wednesday 8 September
Questions to the Secretary of State for Wales and to the Prime Minister.
Ten minute Rule Motion: Chris Bryant: Sex and relationships education: That leave be given to bring in a Bill to require schools to provide sex and relationships education to registered pupils; and for connected purposes.
Opposition Day (4th allotted day). There will be a debate on an Opposition Motion. Subject to be announced.
At the end of the sitting: Adjournment: Subject to be announced.
Thursday 9 September
Questions to the Secretary of State for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs, including Topical Questions.
Backbench Business Committee debate on UK armed forces in Afghanistan.
At the end of the sitting: Adjournment: Subject to be announced.
Monday 13 September
Questions to the Secretary of State for Defence, including Topical Questions.
Second Reading of the Fixed-Term Parliaments Bill.
At the end of the sitting: Adjournment: Subject to be announced.
Tuesday 14 September
Questions to the Secretary of State for Foreign and Commonwealth Affairs, including Topical Questions.
Ten minute Rule Motion: Catherine McKinnell: Statutory Instruments Act 1946 (Amendment): That leave be given to bring in a Bill to amend the Statutory Instruments Act 1946.
Second Reading of the Equitable Life (Payments) Bill.
At the end of the sitting: Adjournment: Subject to be announced.
Wednesday 15 September
Questions to the Secretary of State for Northern Ireland and to the Prime Minister.
Ten minute Rule Motion: Mr Douglas Carswell: Parliamentary Elections (Primaries): That leave be given to bring in a Bill to enable local people to petition their Returning Officer to hold a primary ballot for the selection of a Westminster Parliamentary candidate on the date of a local or European Parliamentary election; and for connected purposes.
Motion to approve Ways and Means Resolutions on which a Finance Bill will be introduced.
Remaining stages of the Identity Documents Bill.
At the end of the sitting: Adjournment: Subject to be announced.
Thursday 16 September
Questions to the Secretary of State for Energy and Climate Change, including Topical Questions.
Backbench Business Committee debate on the future of the UK’s armed forces.
At the end of the sitting: Adjournment: Subject to be announced.
FORTHCOMING END OF DAY ADJOURNMENT DEBATES
MONDAY 6 SEPTEMBER TO MONDAY 13 SEPTEMBER
        Applications should be made in writing to Mr Speaker to reach his office by 7 p.m. or rise of the House, whichever is the earlier, on Tuesday 27 July. The Ballot will take place on Wednesday 28 July.
