Future Business Part E: Business to be taken in European Committees

Business to be taken in European Committees .

For Business for the period ending on Monday 1 March 2010 see Part A of this Paper. For Business to be taken at Sittings in Westminster Hall see Part B of this Paper. For Remaining Orders and Notices see Part C of this Paper. For Other Future Business see Part D of this Paper.

 

 
E
Business to be taken in European Committees
   The following list consists of European Union Documents to be considered in European Committees. The dates appointed for consideration are provisional.
 
European Committee A
No outstanding debates.
European Committee B
1 FINANCIAL SERVICES
 
   European Union Document No. 15049/09 and Addenda 1 to 3 were referred to European Committee B on 9 December 2009 by the European Scrutiny Committee [3rd Report of Session 2009-10, HC 5-iii, Chapter 3].
To be considered at 4.30 pm on 2 March 2010.
2 FUTURE RELATIONS BETWEEN THE EU AND THE OVERSEAS COUNTRIES AND TERRITORIES
Chris Bryant
 
   That the Committee takes note of European Union Document No. 15647/09, a Commission Communication on Elements for a new partnership between the EU and the Overseas Countries and Territories (OCTs); recalls that such Communications are consultation documents and are not legally binding; and welcomes the Commission Communication as a continuation of the process of the renegotiation of the Overseas Association Decision [3rd Report of Session 2009-10, HC 5-iii, Chapter 1].
To be considered at 4.30 pm on 9 February 2010.
3 EUROPEAN DEFENCE AGENCY THREE YEAR FINANCIAL FRAMEWORK POSTPONEMENT AND 2010 BUDGET ADOPTION
 
   European Union Documents Nos. 16275/09 and 16462/09 and an Unnumbered Explanatory Memorandum dated 3 December 2009, submitted by the Ministry of Defence, were referred to European Committee B on 15 December 2009 by the European Scrutiny Committee [4th Report of Session 2009-10, HC 5-iv, Chapter 3].
To be considered at 4.00 pm on 1 March 2010.
4 OBTAINING EVIDENCE IN CRIMINAL MATTERS
Meg Hillier
 
   That the Committee takes note of European Union Document No. 17691/09, Commission Green Paper on obtaining evidence in criminal matters from one Member State to another and securing its admissibility; and supports the Government’s objective of only supporting a new EU Mutual Legal Assistance instrument that will bring coherence and add real value to the operation of Mutual Legal Assistance amongst EU Member States. [5th Report of Session 2009-10, HC 5-v, Chapter 2].
To be considered at 4.00 pm on 8 February 2010.
5 ALTERNATIVE INVESTMENT FUND MANAGERS
Sarah McCarthy-Fry
 
   That the Committee takes note of European Union Documents No. 9494/09 and Addenda 1 and 2, Draft Directive on Alternative Investment Fund Managers and amending Directives 2004/39/EC and 2009/.../EC, and No. 15162/09, Opinion of the European Central Bank of 16 October 2009 on a Draft Directive on Alternative Investment Fund Managers and Amending Directives 2004/39/EC and 2009/.../EC; and endorses the Government’s approach in seeking agreement to the Directive.
[7th Report of Session 2009-10, HC 5-vi, Chapter 2].
To be considered at 4.30 pm on 23 February 2010.
6 COUNCIL DECISION: SOMALI SECURITY FORCES
 
   An Unnumbered Explanatory Memorandum dated 19 January 2010 submitted by the Foreign and Commonwealth Office relating to a Council Decision on a European Union military mission to contribute to the training of Somali Security Forces was referred to European Committee B on 20 January 2010 by the European Scrutiny Committee [8th Report of Session 2009-10, HC 5-vii, Chapter 2].
Date and time of consideration to be arranged.
 
European Committee C
No outstanding debates.
