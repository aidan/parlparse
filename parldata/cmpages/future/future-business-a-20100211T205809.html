Future Business Part A: Business for the period ending on Monday 1 March 2010

Includes the business announced by the Leader of the House (which may be changed up to the rising of the House on the day before it is to be taken and is therefore provisional.)

For Business to be taken at Sittings in Westminster Hall see Part B of this Paper. For Remaining Orders and Notices see Part C of this Paper. For Other Future Business see Part D of this Paper. For Notices of Business to be taken in European Committees see Part E of this Paper.

Notes:
* Amended
†† On this day Private Members' Bills do not have precedence and the Bill cannot make progress.
 

 
A
Business for the period ending on Monday 1 March 2010
   Includes the business announced by the Leader of the House (which may be changed up to the rising of the House on the day before it is to be taken and is therefore provisional).
Tuesday 23 February
Questions to the Secretary of State for Health, including Topical Questions.
Ten minute Rule Motion: Tony Lloyd: Rental Accommodation (Thermal Insulation Standards): That leave be given to bring in a Bill to require providers of rental accommodation to ensure that the accommodation complies with certain minimum standards of thermal insulation; and for connected purposes.
Remaining stages of the Children, Schools and Families Bill.
At the end of the sitting: Adjournment: Subject to be announced.
Wednesday 24 February
Questions to the Secretary of State for Scotland and to the Prime Minister.
Ten minute Rule Motion: Mr David Drew: Public Bodies (Procurement of Seafood): That leave be given to bring in a Bill to require public bodies to purchase only those seafood species and stocks that are demonstrably sustainable; and for connected purposes.
Remaining stages of the Energy Bill.
At the end of the sitting: Adjournment: Subject to be announced.
Thursday 25 February
Questions to the Secretary of State for Energy and Climate Change, including Topical Questions.
General debate on Welsh Affairs.
At the end of the sitting: Adjournment: Subject to be announced.

Monday 1 March
Questions to the Secretary of State for Culture, Media and Sport, including Topical Questions, and to the Minister for the Olympics.
Opposition Day [half day] (4th allotted day). There will be a debate on an Opposition Motion. Subject to be announced.
Motion relating to the draft Prevention of Terrorism Act 2005 (Continuance in Force of sections 1 to 9) Order 2010.
Motion relating to the draft Social Security Benefits Up-rating Order 2010.
At the end of the sitting: Adjournment: Subject to be announced.
