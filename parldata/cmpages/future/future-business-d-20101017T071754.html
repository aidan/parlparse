Future Business Part D: Business to be taken in Westminster Hall

Includes the items of business to be taken at sittings of the House in Westminster Hall.

Sittings of the House take place in Westminster Hall on Tuesdays and Wednesdays from 9.30 a.m. to 11.30 a.m. and 2.00 p.m. to 4.30 p.m. and on Thursdays from 2.30 p.m. to 5.30 p.m. (in the latter case with extra time if there have been suspensions owing to divisions in the House or a Committee of the whole House).

For Business for the period ending on Thursday 28 October 2010 see Part A of this paper. For Backbench business see Part B of this paper. For Remaining Orders and Notices see Part C of this paper. For Other future business see Part E of this paper. For Business to be taken in European Committees see Part F of this paper. For Notices of Public Petitions see Part G of this paper. For Notices of written ministerial statements see Part H of this paper.

FORTHCOMING WESTMINSTER HALL ADJOURNMENT DEBATES
TUESDAY 26 OCTOBER AND WEDNESDAY 27 OCTOBER
        Applications for General or Short Debates should be made in writing to Mr Speaker to reach his office by 10 pm or the rise of the House, whichever is the earlier, on Tuesday 19 October. The Ballot will take place on Wednesday 20 October.
FOLLOWING DEPARTMENTS TO ANSWER
WESTMINSTER HALL DEBATES
        From the weeks beginning 25 October to 1 November 2010, Departments will answer adjournment debates in Westminster Hall according to the following rota:
        Week beginning 25 October:
        Cabinet Office; Communities and Local Government; Energy and Climate Change; Environment, Food and Rural Affairs; Health; Home Office; Justice; Northern Ireland; Work and Pensions; Wales.
        Week beginning 1 November:
        Attorney General; Business, Innovation and Skills; Culture, Olympics, Media and Sport; Defence; Deputy Prime Minister; Education; Foreign and Commonwealth Affairs; International Development; Leader of the House; Minister for Women and Equalities; Scotland; Transport; Treasury.
