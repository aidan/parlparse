Future Business Part A: Business for the period ending on Friday 5 February 2010

Includes the business announced by the Leader of the House (which may be changed up to the rising of the House on the day before it is to be taken and is therefore provisional.)

For Business to be taken at Sittings in Westminster Hall see Part B of this Paper. For Remaining Orders and Notices see Part C of this Paper. For Other Future Business see Part D of this Paper. For Notices of Business to be taken in European Committees see Part E of this Paper.

Notes:
* Amended
†† On this day Private Members' Bills do not have precedence and the Bill cannot make progress.
 

 
A
Business for the period ending on Friday 5 February 2010
   Includes the business announced by the Leader of the House (which may be changed up to the rising of the House on the day before it is to be taken and is therefore provisional).
Wednesday 27 January
Questions to the Minister for the Cabinet Office and to the Prime Minister.
Ten minute Rule Motion: Helen Southworth: Young People Leaving Care (Accommodation): That leave be given to bring in a Bill to make provision for minimum standards in respect of accommodation for young people leaving care; to impose a duty on local authorities to ensure that such standards are met; and for connected purposes.
Opposition Day (3rd allotted day). There will be a debate on Dementia Services and Care of the Elderly followed by a debate on Out of Hours Care. Both debates will arise on an Opposition Motion.
At the end of the sitting: Adjournment: Richard Younger-Ross: Funding for primary and secondary education in Devon.
Thursday 28 January
Questions to the Minister of State, Department for Transport, including Topical Questions and to the Minister for Women and Equality.
Topical debate on Holocaust Memorial Day.
The Chairman of Ways and Means will name opposed private business for consideration.
At the end of the sitting: Adjournment: Mr David Winnick: Arrest warrants in the UK in respect of alleged war crimes by individuals resident abroad.
Friday 29 January
Private Members’ Bills
1
MORTGAGE REPOSSESSIONS (PROTECTION OF TENANTS ETC.) BILL: Second Reading.
Member in charge: Mr Brian Iddon
2
SUNBEDS (REGULATION) BILL: Second Reading.
Member in charge: Julie Morgan
3
TOWN AND COUNTRY PLANNING ACT 1990 (AMENDMENT) BILL: Second Reading.
Member in charge: Simon Hughes
4
DEVELOPMENT ON FLOOD PLAINS (ENVIRONMENT AGENCY POWERS) BILL: Second Reading.
Member in charge: Bob Spink
5
CO-OPERATIVE AND COMMUNITY BENEFIT SOCIETIES AND CREDIT UNIONS BILL [LORDS]: Second Reading.
Member in charge: Malcolm Wicks
At the end of the sitting: Adjournment: John Mann: Use of anti-social behaviour orders by local authorities.
Monday 1 February
Questions to the Secretary of State for Work and Pensions, including Topical Questions.
Remaining Stages of the Flood and Water Management Bill.
At the end of the sitting: Adjournment: Ann Coffey: Planning applications for children’s homes.
Tuesday 2 February
Questions to Mr Chancellor of the Exchequer, including Topical Questions.
Ten minute Rule Motion: Jeremy Wright: Amendment of Compensation Act (2006): That leave be given to bring in a Bill to amend the Compensation Act 2006 to ensure that courts considering a claim of negligence or breach of statutory duty apply a presumption that defendants undertaking a desirable activity have satisfied the relevant standard of care; and for connected purposes.
Consideration in Committee of the Constitutional Reform and Governance Bill (Day 5).
At the end of the sitting: Adjournment: Subject to be announced.
Wednesday 3 February
Questions to the Secretary of State for Northern Ireland and to the Prime Minister.
Ten minute Rule Motion: Mr David Burrowes: Desecration of War Memorials: That leave be given to bring in a Bill to amend the law to make provision about damage to war memorials; and for connected purposes.
Motions relating to the Police Grant and Local Government Finance Reports.
At the end of the sitting: Adjournment: Subject to be announced.
Thursday 4 February
Questions to the Secretary of State for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs, including Topical Questions.
Remaining Stages of the Corporation Tax Bill.
Remaining Stages of the Taxation (International and Other Provisions) Bill.
At the end of the sitting: Adjournment: Subject to be announced.
Friday 5 February
Private Members’ Bills

Member in charge: Mr David Chaytor
2
ANTI-SLAVERY DAY BILL: Second Reading.

3
DAMAGES (ASBESTOS-RELATED CONDITIONS) (No. 2) BILL: Second Reading.
Member in charge: Mr Andrew Dismore
4
EMPLOYERS’ LIABILITY INSURANCE BUREAU BILL: Second Reading.
Member in charge: Mr Andrew Dismore
5
VETERANS’ WELFARE BILL: Second Reading.
Member in charge: Mr Michael Moore
6
CONTAMINATED BLOOD (SUPPORT FOR INFECTED AND BEREAVED PERSONS BILL [LORDS]: Second Reading.
Member in charge: Mr Edward O’Hara
At the end of the sitting: Adjournment: Subject to be announced.
FORTHCOMING END OF DAY ADJOURNMENT DEBATES
TUESDAY 2 FEBRUARY TO MONDAY 8 FEBRUARY
        Applications should be made in writing to Mr Speaker to reach his office by 7 p.m. or rise of the House, whichever is the earlier, on Wednesday 27 January. The Ballot will take place on Thursday 28 January.
