Summary Agendas and Orders of Business

Future Business Part A: Business for the period ending on Thursday 24 June 2010

Includes the business announced by the Leader of the House (which may be changed up to the rising of the House on the day before it is to be taken and is therefore provisional.)

For Business to be taken in Westminster Hall see Part B of this paper. For Remaining Orders and Notices see Part C of this paper. For Other future business see Part D of this paper. For Business to be taken in European Committees see Part E of this paper. For Notices of Public Petitions see Part F of this paper. For Notices of written ministerial statements see Part G of this paper.

Notes:
* Amended
†† On this day Private Members' Bills do not have precedence and the Bill cannot make progress.

A
Business for the period ending on Thursday 24 June 2010
   Includes the business announced by the Leader of the House (which may be changed up to the rising of the House on the day before it is to be taken and is therefore provisional).
Tuesday 15 June
Questions to the Secretary of State for Justice, including Topical Questions.
The House will be asked to approve Motions relating to the establishment of a Back Bench Business Committee, September sittings, Private Members Bills, Deferred Divisions, Select Committee sizes and sittings of the House.
At the end of the sitting: Adjournment: Ms Rosie Winterton: Activities of private wheel clamping companies in Doncaster Central constituency.
 
Wednesday 16 June
Questions to the Secretary of State for Scotland and to the Prime Minister.
Opposition day (1st allotted day). There will be a debate on Government support for industry. This debate will arise on an Opposition Motion.
At the end of the sitting: Adjournment: Tony Baldry: Future of Horton General Hospital.
Thursday 17 June
Questions to the Secretary of State for Transport, including Topical Questions and to the Minister for Women and Equalities.
General debate on Building a high skilled economy.
At the end of the sitting: Adjournment: Lilian Greenwood: Transport infrastructure for Nottingham.
Friday 18 June
The House will not be sitting.
Monday 21 June
Questions to the Secretary of State for Culture, Olympics, Media and Sport, including Topical Questions and to the Leader of the House and House of Commons Commission.
General debate on Strategic defence and security review.
At the end of the sitting: Adjournment: Mr Robert Buckland: Housing development in Swindon.
Tuesday 22 June
Questions to the Deputy Prime Minister, Attorney General, Church Commissioners, Public Accounts Commission and Speaker’s Committee on the Electoral Commission.
Mr Chancellor of the Exchequer will open the Budget Statement.
At the end of the sitting: Adjournment: Subject to be announced.
Wednesday 23 June
Questions to the Secretary of State for Wales and to the Prime Minister.
Continuation of the Budget Debate.
At the end of the sitting: Adjournment: Subject to be announced.
Thursday 24 June
Questions to the Secretary of State for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs, including Topical Questions.
Continuation of the Budget Debate.
At the end of the sitting: Adjournment: Subject to be announced.
FORTHCOMING END OF DAY ADJOURNMENT DEBATES
TUESDAY 22 JUNE TO MONDAY 28 JUNE
        Applications should be made in writing to Mr Speaker to reach his office by 10 p.m. or rise of the House, whichever is the earlier, on Wednesday 16 June. The Ballot will take place on Thursday 17 June.
