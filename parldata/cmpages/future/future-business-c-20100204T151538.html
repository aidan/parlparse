Future Business Part C: Remaining Orders and Notices

Consists of items of business set down formally for today but not expected to be taken today. (These may include items included in the business statement and therefore listed in Part A above.) Also includes items on which divisions have been deferred.

For Business for the period ending on Tuesday 9 February 2010 see Part A of this Paper. For Business to be taken at Sittings in Westminster Hall see Part B of this Paper. For Other Future Business see Part D of this Paper. For Notices of Business to be taken in European Committees see Part E of this Paper.

Notes:
** On these Fridays Private Members' Bills have precedence. Proceedings will commence at 9.35 a.m. Only the first Bill is certain to be debated. If the debate ends before 2.30 p.m., the second Bill and, possibly, subsequent Bills may be debated in the time remaining. After 2.30 p.m., only those Bills which are unopposed may make further progress.

†† On these day Private Members' Bills do not have precedence and the Bill cannot make progress.

NON-GOVERNMENT BUSINESS
Mr Graham Allen
(1)   
That this House considers that non-government business should be managed by hon. Members and not the executive, and accordingly endorses recommendations 17 and 18 of the First Report of the House of Commons Reform Committee, HC 1117 of Session 2008-09.
(2)   
That the following Standing Orders shall be made, to come into force at the start of the next Parliament.
“A. Backbench business
 
   All business of the House shall be treated as backbench business except—
(a)   
Ministerial primary and secondary legislation and associated motions and orders
(b)   
Substantive Ministerial motions required in support of their policies
(c)   
Ministerial statements
(d)   
Opposition business
(e)   
Private Members’ bills
(f)   
Estimates Days
(g)   
Motions for an Address
(h)   
Private Business
(i)   
Debates currently allocated by the Speaker’s ballot or following a decision of the Liaison Committee.
B. Backbench Business Committee
(1)   
There shall be a committee known as the Backbench Business Committee to decide on the backbench business to be taken in the following sitting week, and to make provisional determination of such business to be taken in the subsequent sitting week.
(2)   
The chair of the Committee shall be elected by a secret ballot under the same arrangements as are made for the election of chairs in Standing Order (Election of Chairs of Select Committees) and subject to the same power of the Speaker to make detailed arrangements. Any Member of the House may stand for the office, subject to their nomination papers being signed by at least 25 members of the House, with at least 10 members of each of the two largest parties.
(3)   
The Committee shall have between seven and nine members, of which four shall be the quorum. The Chair shall count in the quorum. The members of the Committee shall be elected by secret ballot under rules to be determined by the Speaker. The election must be held within seven sitting days of the election of the Chair under paragraph (2) above.
(4)   
The Committee shall have power to invite the attendance of Government officials at all or part of its meetings.
(5)   
The Committee shall report its decisions under paragraph (1) above to the Leader of the House, and in the case of Westminster Hall business which falls within its remit also to the Chairman of Ways and Means, at least 24 hours before the weekly Business Statement.”

ELECTION OF CHAIRS OF SELECT COMMITTEES
Mr Graham Allen
(1)   
That this House considers that select committee chairs should be elected by a ballot of the whole House: and accordingly endorses recommendations 5 and 7, and paragraphs 85 to 86, of the First Report from the House of Commons Reform Committee, HC1117 of session 2008-09.
(2)   
That the following Standing Order shall be made, to come into effect from the start of the new Parliament.
 
    “Election of chairs of Select Committees
(1)   
The chairs of the select committees listed in paragraph (2) shall be elected by the House using the alternative vote method. The elections will be conducted under arrangements to be determined and published by the Speaker, reflecting the terms of paragraphs 84 and 85 of the First Report from the House of Commons Reform Committee, HC1117 of session 2008-09.
(2)   
The relevant committees for the purposes of paragraph (1) above are those appointed under Standing Orders Nos 146 (Select Committee on Public Administration), 148 (Committee of Public Accounts), 152 (Select committees related to government departments) and 152A (Environmental Audit Committee).
(3)   
No later than two days after his election, the Speaker shall tell the leaders of parties in the House the proportions of electable chairs of committees listed in paragraph (2) above to which each party is entitled, drawing on information available to him as to the likely number of such committees to be established in the new Parliament.
(4)   
No later than a week after the Queen’s Speech, the parties shall table a motion showing to which party each electable chair has been allocated: the Question on any such motion shall be put forthwith and may be decided at any hour, though opposed.
(5)   
Candidates for a chair must submit their candidature within two weeks of the House’s decision on the motion referred to in paragraph (4) above, subject to the arrangements made under paragraph (1) above
(6)   
A Chair elected under this Order shall hold office until the end of the Parliament unless (a) he ceases to be a Member of the House, or (b) he resigns by giving notice to the Clerk of the House, or (c) the Committee resolves unanimously or by a majority comprising at least two-thirds of the members of the Committee and of more than one party represented on it that the Chair has lost the confidence of the committee, A resolution moved under (c) requires notice and once decided may not be moved again for a period of 12 months.”

SELECT COMMITTEE MEMBERS
Mr Graham Allen
(1)   
That this House considers that parties should elect their own members of select committees in a secret ballot, and accordingly endorses recommendation 6 and paragraphs 87 to 92 of the First Report of the House of Commons Reform Committee, HC 1117 of session 2008-09.
(2)   
That the following Standing Order shall be made, with effect from the start of the next Parliament—
 
    “Members of select Committees
(1)(a)   
The leader of each party represented in the House with more than 30 members shall inform the Speaker within seven days of a new Parliament of the method of election which the party intends to use for electing members of select committees for that Parliament, if it is different from the system previously approved, or if there is no approved system in place for that party.
(b)   
The Speaker shall examine the proposed means of election to ensure that it is transparent and democratic.
(c)   
If the Speaker approves the system as transparent and democratic, it will be published.
(d)   
If the Speaker does not approve it, the party leader concerned must propose a system which can be so approved.
(2)   
The Speaker shall, no later than two days after his election, convey to the leaders of all parties represented in the House of Commons the number of members to which each such party shall be entitled on a range of possible committee sizes.
(3)(a)   
Each party entitled to at least one place on a select committee shall hold elections to select committees in accordance with the system certified by the Speaker under paragraph (2) above.
(b)   
Where there are insufficient candidates for places, the party leader may nominate members of that or another party or of no party.
(c)   
The leaders of each party shall send the names of those members thus elected or nominated to the Chair of the Committee of Selection.
(d)   
The Speaker may send to the Committee of Selection for nomination to a committee the name of no more than one additional member to any of the committees subject to direct election of a Chair under Standing Order (Election of Chairs of Select Committees), providing that the additional member is a member of a party not otherwise represented on the committee or a member of no party.
(e)   
In making such nominations, the Speaker shall have regard to the proportion of such members in the membership of the House.
(f)   
In the case of the Northern Ireland Affairs, Scottish Affairs and Welsh Affairs Committees, the Speaker may forward more than one such name to the Committee of Selection.
(g)   
The Committee of Selection shall assemble the lists of members elected or nominated under this paragraph and give notice of the motion for nomination in the House in accordance with Standing Order No. 121.”
