2    Message from the Lords,-A Message was brought from the Lords, as follows:

    The Lords have agreed to the Consolidated Fund Bill, without Amendment.

3    European Parliamentary and Local Elections (Pilots) Bill,-The House, according to Order, proceeded to consider the European Parliamentary and Local Elections (Pilots) Bill, not amended in the Standing Committee.

    A Clause (NC2) (Additional provisions in respect of postal voting)-(Mr David Heath)-brought up and read the first time.

    Question put, That the Clause be read a second time.

    The House divided.

      Tellers for the Ayes, Richard Younger-Ross, Mr Mark Field: 183.

      Tellers for the Noes, Gillian Merron, Mr Jim Murphy: 291.

    Another Clause (NC3) (Additional provisions in respect of electronic voting)-(Mr David Heath)-brought up and read the first time.

    Question proposed, That the Clause be read a second time:-Clause, by leave, withdrawn.

    Another Clause (NC4) (Conduct of elections under a pilot order)-(Mr David Heath)-brought up and read the first time.

    Question put, That the Clause be read a second time.

    The House divided.

      Tellers for the Ayes, Angela Watkinson, Richard Younger-Ross: 185.

      Tellers for the Noes, Gillian Merron, Mr Jim Murphy: 296.

    Amendment (No. 8) proposed to the Bill, in page 1, line 4, after the word 'must', to insert the words ', if a three-quarters majority of the local authorities in the area covered by the pilot agree,'.-(Mr Nick Hawkins.)

    Question put, That the Amendment be made.

    The House divided.

      Tellers for the Ayes, Angela Watkinson, Mr Mark Field: 135.

      Tellers for the Noes, Mr Fraser Kemp, Gillian Merron: 357.

    Another Amendment (No. 6) proposed to the Bill, in page 1, line 18, at the end to insert the words-

      '(4A) The Secretary of State must not make an order under this section specifying a region which the Electoral Commission has identified as-

      (a)not suitable for a pilot scheme; or

      (b)a region for which the Commission does not feel able to make a positive recommendation as to its suitability.'.-(Mr David Heath.)

    Question put, That the Amendment be made.

    The House divided.

      Tellers for the Ayes, Richard Younger-Ross, Mr Mark Field: 181.

      Tellers for the Noes, Mr Fraser Kemp, Charlotte Atkins: 304.

    It being after Six o'clock, the Deputy Speaker put the Question on the remaining Government Amendments, pursuant to Orders [21st October and 6th November].

    Other Amendments (Nos. 50, 4, 48 and 49) made to the Bill.

    A Motion was made, and the Question being put, That the Bill be now read the third time;

    The House divided.

      Tellers for the Ayes, Joan Ryan, Mr Vernon Coaker: 304.

      Tellers for the Noes, Angela Watkinson, Richard Younger-Ross: 166.

    So the Question was agreed to.

    The Bill was accordingly read the third time, and passed.
