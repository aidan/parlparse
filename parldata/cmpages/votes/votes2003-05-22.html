The House met at half-past Eleven o'clock.

PRAYERS.

1    Anti-social Behaviour Bill,-Mr James Cran reported from Standing Committee G, That it had gone through the Anti-social Behaviour Bill, and made Amendments thereunto.

    Bill, as amended in the Standing Committee, to be considered on Tuesday 3rd June; and to be printed [Bill 116].

    Minutes of Proceedings of the Committee to be printed [No. 725].

2    Fourth Standing Committee on Delegated Legislation,-Mr Eric Illsley reported from the Fourth Standing Committee on Delegated Legislation the draft Release of Short-Term Prisoners on Licence (Amendment of Requisite Period) Order 2003.

    Minutes of Proceedings of the Committee to lie upon the Table.

3    Clergy Discipline Measure,-Resolved, That the Clergy Discipline Measure, passed by the General Synod of the Church of England, be presented to Her Majesty for Her Royal Assent in the form in which the said Measure was laid before Parliament.-(Mr Stuart Bell.)

4    Municipal Waste Recycling Bill [Money],-Her Majesty's Recommendation having been signified to the proposed Motion relating to Municipal Waste Recycling Bill [Money];

    Resolved, That, for the purposes of any Act resulting from the Municipal Waste Recycling Bill, it is expedient to authorise the payment out of money provided by Parliament of any increase attributable to the Act in the sums payable out of money so provided under any other enactment.-(Mr Michael Meacher.)

5    Adjournment (Matters to be considered before the forthcoming adjournment),-A Motion was made, and the Question being proposed, That this House do now adjourn-(Joan Ryan);

    The Motion was, with leave of the House, withdrawn.

6    Public Petitions,-Public Petitions from-

    (1) residents of South Swindon against the unrestricted opening of pharmacies able to dispense NHS prescriptions;

    (2) Michael Morrell against the unrestricted opening of pharmacies able to dispense NHS prescriptions;

    (3) Mr Richard Else against the unrestricted opening of pharmacies able to dispense NHS prescriptions;

    (4) Anthony Milward against the unrestricted opening of pharmacies able to dispense NHS prescriptions;

    (5) Judith Hagan against the unrestricted opening of pharmacies able to dispense NHS prescriptions;

    (6) Hollowood Chemists against the unrestricted opening of pharmacies able to dispense NHS prescriptions;

    (7) Derek Blackford for restrictions on the period during which fireworks may be sold to the public; and

    (8) Mr Stephen Jones for restrictions on the period during which fireworks may be sold to the public

    were presented and read; and ordered to lie upon the Table and to be printed.

7    Public Petitions,-A Public Petition from Liz Hill and others against restrictions on the availability of natural health products was presented; and ordered to lie upon the Table and to be printed.

8    Adjournment,-Resolved, That this House do now adjourn.-(Mr Phil Woolas.)

    And accordingly the House, having continued to sit till two minutes past Six o'clock, adjourned till Tuesday 3rd June, pursuant to Resolution [13th May].

[Adjourned at 6.02 p.m.

Michael J. Martin
Speaker
