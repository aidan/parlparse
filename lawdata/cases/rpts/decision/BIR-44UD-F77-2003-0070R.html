RENT ASSESSMENT COMMITTEE – EXTENDED REASONS FOR DECISION

 

RENT ACT 1977

 

 

Address of Premises:                                          The Committee Members were

10 Plomer Close                                  Mr A Bell MA LLB

Rugby                                                   Mr W Andrews FRICS

Warwickshire CV22 7JZ                                      Mrs M McKenzie JP

 

 

 

The above property is a semi-detached steel framed house with brick cladding and a tiled roof built in approximately 1948. It comprises on the ground floor a hall, two living rooms, a kitchen and utlity room with an external WC and storeroom and on the first floor a landing, three bedrooms and a bathroom and WC. The property has full gas fired central heating with 8 radiators and a gas fire and UPVC double glazed windows throughout.

 

 

The property is situate about a mile and a half from the centre of Rugby with local amenities within a short distance. The property provides above average sized accommodation and is in a fair condition. There is an open plan garden to the front which is maintained by the landlord, in respect of which a service charge is paid, and an enclosed garden to the rear. The only service that is provided by the landlord is the maintenance of the open spaces on  the estate and the flats at Montgomery Drive and Queensferry Close. The figures produced by the landlord for the period from the 1 October 2001 to 27 September 2002 showed an apportioned figure in respect of each property of £2.28 per week in respect of the grass cutting and maintenance of the open spaces. The Committee considered that the amount payable by the tenants for this service provided by the landlord, in accordance with section 19(2) of the Landlord & Tenant Act 1985, was reasonable.

 

 

While both parties made representations to the Committee no hearing was requested by either party and no comparables were put forward by either party in their written representation. Consequently the Committee had to rely on its own knowledge and experience of market rents in the area of Rugby and, in doing so, took into account the age, location and state of repair of the property in accordance with section 70 (1) of the Rent Act 1977. The one significant draw-back to the property is the nature of its construction since it is  steel framed. Had it not been for the steel framed construction the Committee were of the view that the market rent of the property would be £65.00 per week having particular regard on the extent of the accommodation and locality of the property. However, having regard to the construction of the property, the Committee made a deduction of £3.00 per week reducing the hypothetical rent of £65.00 per week to £62.00 per week. There were no tenants’ improvements which required to be disregarded.

 

 

The Committee did consider there was substantial scarcity in this case and, as required by section 70(2) of the Rent Act 1977, made a deduction of 10% to reflect this element. This left an occupational  element of  the fair rent of £55.80 per week exclusive of services. With the service element of £2.28 per week added this resulted in a fair rent  of  £58.08 which the Committee rounded down to the nearest 50p, when making its determination of a fair rent,  to  £58.00 per week inclusive of services of £2.28 per week.
