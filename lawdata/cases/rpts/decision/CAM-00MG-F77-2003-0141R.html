The Property:                                    

The property is an end of terrace two-storey house of stone under a slate roof, built circa 1800. The accommodation comprises an entrance hall, two living rooms, a kitchen, utility room and bathroom on the ground floor and two double bedrooms and one single bedroom on the first floor. Outside there is a yard with access to the road and a further paved garden area. A gas fired central heating system installed by the Tenant provides space and water heating. Originally space heating was by open fires in the living rooms. The property has mains gas, water, drainage and electricity. The property is situated on a busy road (A509) close to the centre of Olney.

 

Condition:

The Committee inspected the property in the presence of the Tenant. The exterior of the property was in fair condition. It had recently been repainted and new double glazed windows had been fitted to two of the bedrooms and the kitchen and rear living room. The guttering had also been repaired and a new cover fitted to the drain inspection chamber. The roof appeared to be in need of overhauling and there were signs of water penetration internally in the single bedroom. The front and back doors fitted poorly. Internally the kitchen and bathroom, which had been installed by the Tenant when she moved into the property in 1980, were now dated. The plaster ceilings were cracked and bowed in places. No carpets, curtains or white goods were provided. The property was let unfurnished.

 

Hearing:

The Committee noted that on taking the tenancy the Tenant had created a kitchen, utility room and bathroom out of one room and formed a third single bedroom from the landing area. She had also rewired the house and installed water and space heating in order to make the property habitable. In return the Landlord had given a concessionary rent for five years. Evidence was given in relation to the extent and effect of the works carried out on the windows by the Landlord since the last rent registration. It was noted that the new windows had made a substantial difference to the noise and warmth of the property.

 

Law:

Attached to this Statement of Reasons is a resumé of the law applied by the Committee.

 

Open Market Rent

The Landlord submitted opinions of two local agents as to the rent the subject property was likely to achieve with and without the work carried out since the last rent registration which were noted by the Committee and added to this the knowledge and experience of its members.  The Committee was of the opinion that the subject property, taking in to account its age and location in good condition with central heating, modern kitchen and bathroom, double-glazing and carpets, curtains and white goods let on an Assured Shorthold Tenancy would be £135.00 per week. The Committee considered that the creation of the kitchen, utility room, bathroom and additional bedroom and the rewiring and installation of a basic hot water system had been improvements made as a condition of the tenancy to make the property habitable and had in effect been paid for by the Landlord in the low rent of the first five years. Therefore these were not disregarded. However even taking account of these, the property as let would not have had full central heating and the kitchen and bathroom are now dated. There are also repairs needed to the roof and miscellaneous items such as the ill-fitting external doors. The property was also let without carpets, curtains or white goods. Therefore the Committee made a global deduction of £40.00 per week. It should be noted that this figure cannot be a simple arithmetical calculation and is not based specifically upon capital cost but is the Committee’s estimate of the amount by which the rent would have to be reduced to attract a tenant.

 

Scarcity

It was decided that there is substantial scarcity of  “... similar dwelling-houses in the locality...” in this case Milton Keynes and adjoining parts of Northamptonshire and  Bedfordshire, that are available for letting and a deduction would be made to reflect this of 15%

 

Committee’s Calculations:

Open Market Rent:                                                       £135.00 per week

Less global deduction                                                   £ 40.00 per week

                                                                        £  95.00 per week

Less Scarcity 15%                                                        £ 14.25 per week

                                                                                    £  80.75 per week

Uncapped rent say £80.00 per week

 

The Landlord submitted that the uncapped rent should be registered, as the exemption in Paragraph 2(7) of the Rent Acts (Maximum Fair Rent) Order 1999 should apply. The provisions of the Rent Acts (Maximum Fair Rent) Order 1999 require that the registered rent is either the capped Fair Rent or the Fair Rent decided by the Committee whichever is the lower. The capped Fair Rent is calculated in accordance with a statutory formula using the existing rent as a base. However Paragraph 2(7) of the Order provides that capping does not apply if repairs and improvements by the landlord undertaken since the last registration of rent have led to an increase in the rent by at least 15% of the previous registered rent and the increase by this amount is wholly attributable to the repairs or improvements by the landlord.

The Committee found that the Landlord had made repairs and improvements since the last registration by the fitting of new windows with double glazed units and external re-decoration and repairs. In this case the previous registered rent was £41.00. 15% of this rent would be £6.15 per week and therefore the matter for the Committee is whether at least £6.15 per week of the increase in the fair rent determined could be attributed to the Landlord’s repairs or improvements. The Committee considered that if the repairs and improvements had not been carried out, the global deduction would have been increased to £48.00 giving a rent prior to a deduction for scarcity of £87.00. The amount that could therefore be attributed to the improvements is £8.00 which is greater that £6.15 (15% of the previous registered rent). The rent is therefore exempt from the ‘capping’ provisions of the Rent Acts (Maximum Fair Rent) Order 1999 and the uncapped rent falls to be registered.

 

FAIR RENT = £80.00 per week
