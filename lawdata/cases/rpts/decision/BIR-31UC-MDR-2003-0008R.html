EXTENDED REASONS FOR THE DETERMINATION BY THE RENT ASSESSMENT COMMITTEE OF THE OPEN MARKET RENTAL VALUE OF NO. 96 DERBY ROAD, LOUGHBOROUGH, LEICESTERSHIRE

 

 

1.                  BACKGROUND

 

By application dated 17th October 2003 but received on 24th November 2003 the Tenants of the subject property applied to the Committee for a determination of the open market rental value of the subject premises under Section 22 of the Housing Act 1988.

 

The tenancy commenced on 1st July 2003 for a term of 12 months.

 

The Tenancy Agreement for furnished premises is dated 15th January 2003 and the rent payable thereunder is £4,212.00 per quarter payable quarterly in advance on the dates referred to in the Agreement.  The Tenants agreed to pay a bond in the sum of £1,500.00 for dilapidations, rent arrears and any charges arising under the Agreement.  It is not intended here to recite the other terms of the Tenancy Agreement except to say that in other respects it conformed with a standard shorthold tenancy agreement.

 

2.                  PREMISES

 

The subject property is a six bedroomed shared student terraced house with a shared lounge, kitchen and bathroom.  At the rear is a small garden and at the side a narrow yard useful for the storage of bicycles.  There is no off-road car parking. 

 

3.                  INSPECTION

 

The Committee inspected the property on 21st January 2004 and found it to be in fair condition. 

 

4.                  EVIDENCE

 

The Committee received written representations from the Landlord and from the Tenant, which included details of comparable properties.

 

Neither party requested a hearing at which oral representations could be made.

 

5.                  THE LAW

 

In accordance with the terms of Section 22(3) of The Housing Act 1988, the Committee may make a determination of rent at which it considers that the subject property might reasonably be expected to be let under the assured shorthold tenancy.  However the Committee may not make such a determination unless it considers:

 

(a) that there is a sufficient number of similar dwellinghouses in the locality let on a assured tenancies (whether shorthold or not); and

(b) that the rent payable under the assured shorthold tenancy in question is significantly higher than the rent which the Landlord might reasonably be expected to be able to obtain under the tenancy having regards the level of rent payable under the tenancies referred to in paragraph (a) above

 

6.         COMPARABLE EVIDENCE

 

The comparable evidence available to the Committee included the following “shared student house” accommodation at the following rates per person per week, all available June/July 2004:

 

13 Forest Road, Loughborough - £37.50

110 Old Ashby Road, Loughborough - £43.90

145 Hermitage Road, Loughborough - £43.90

3 Wordsworth Road, Loughborough - £47.00

54 Gracedieu Road, Loughborough - £47.00

57 Gracedieu Road, Loughborough - £47.00

55 Storer Road, Loughborough - £49.00

62 Oxford Street, Loughborough - £49.00

111 Derby Road, Loughborough - £50.00

94 Derby Road, Loughborough - £51.00

the five first referred to properties all have off-road car parking

 

 

7.         DETERMINATION

 

The Committee concluded that the requirements of Section 22(3) are satisfied.  In coming to its decision the Committee had regard to the evidence supplied by the parties and their own general knowledge of market rents in the area of Loughborough and determined that the rent at which it considers the subject property might reasonably be expected to be let under the assured shorthold tenancy is £3,900.00 per quarter.

 

The rent is to take effect from 24th November 2003, being a date not earlier than the date of the application
