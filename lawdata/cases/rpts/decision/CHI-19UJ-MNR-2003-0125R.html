1.      Background

 

On 15 August 2003 the Tenant of the above property referred to the Committee a notice of increase of rent served by the Landlord under section 13 of the Housing Act 1988.

The Landlord's notice, which proposed a rent of £325 per month with effect from 1st October 2003 is dated 1st August 2003   

 

The tenancy commenced in or about 1930 and the Tenant now remains in occupation as a statutory periodic tenant. The passing rent is £281.66 per calendar month

 

 

2.      Inspection

 

A duly appointed Committee inspected the property on 26th September 2003 and found it to be in good condition both internally and externally. The property is a two storey terraced house fronting immediately on to Guernsey Street. It comprises two living rooms and kitchen on the ground floor and two bedrooms and bathroom on the first floor. From the kitchen at the rear, access is gained by external steps to a courtyard from which access is obtained to an outhouse building, part of which is used for washing facilities (there is a cold water supply only) and the other part a WC. There is rear access to a footpath. There is no off-street parking.

 

Improvements had been made to the property. It was not disputed that the tenant had provided:

·        All kitchen fittings

·        Hot water to the kitchen and bathroom

·        Space heating

 

The Tenant submitted that she had also converted a room into a bathroom and provided all of its fittings, while the Landlord asserted that they may have been provided by a previous tenant and therefore had now become the property of the Landlord.

 

The present Landlord or a predecessor had installed double glazing to the rear windows and the electrical wiring which had been carried out about 10 years ago.

 

No services are provided for the Tenant.

 

3.      Evidence

 

The Committee had the benefit of written representations received from the Landlord and Tenant and copied to the parties.

 

Neither party requested a hearing at which oral representations could be made.

 

4. The decision

 

The Committee found that, in relation to provision of the bathroom, this had been carried out also by the Tenant who had lived in the house for 73 years and been tenant of the property for very many years.

 

In the present case the Committee had regard to the evidence supplied by the parties and their own knowledge of market rent levels in the area of Weymouth and Portland and concluded that the rent at which the property might reasonably be expected to be let on the open market in its present condition (including improvements, carpets and curtains) would be £100 per week.

 

The Committee noted the Landlord’s evidence as to appropriate deductions to be made in respect of the improvements concerning the bathroom and heating, but considered an appropriate deduction for the bathroom was £8. To take account of the tenant’s provision of carpets and curtains and the other tenant’s improvements also, the following deductions were made:

 

Item

Deduction (£ per week)

Bathroom

8

Kitchen

10

Heating

10

Carpets and curtains

5

Total

£33

 

Accordingly the Committee calculated the new rent would be approximately £67 per week. The rent is, however, payable monthly and was determined by the Committee at £290 per calendar month.

 

This rent will take effect from 1st October 2003 being the date specified by the Landlord in the notice of increase.
