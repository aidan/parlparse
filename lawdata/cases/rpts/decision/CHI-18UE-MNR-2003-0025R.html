1. Background

On February 18th 2003, the tenant of the above property, Mr D N Cox, referred to the Committee a notice of increase of rent served by the landlord, Fairholme Partners, Ltd, under Section 13 of the Housing Act 1988.

The landlord's notice, proposing a rent of £485 per calendar month (pcm) with effect from February 28th 2003 is dated January 30th 2003..

The tenancy, which is a periodic tenancy, commenced on April 12th 1995. The current rent is £385 pcm.

 

2. Inspection

The Committee inspected the property on April 16th 2003 and found it to be in fair condition best described below:

A three storey terraced house built prior to 1900 with three bedrooms, a dining room, living room, kitchen and bathroom with bath, basin & wc. Hot water and overall heating is supplied by way of a gas Combi boiler with radiators resident throughout the house..

The landlord had carried out considerable improvements throughout the property including the introduction of double glazed windows.

To the rear is situated a yard housing a large outhouse currently in poor condition. There is no rear access.

The house is situated some fifteen minutes walking distance from the centre of Barnstaple. There is no garage or parking area relevant to the subject property itself. There is restricted vehicle parking in Victoria Street to the front of the building.

The Committee found no evidence of qualifying tenant's improvements and no services are provided for the tenant.

3. Evidence

The Committee received written representation from the landlord and tenant and these were copied to

the parties.

Neither party requested a hearing at which oral representation could be made.

 

4. The Law

In accordance with the terms of section 14 Housing Act 1988, the Committee proceeded to determine

the rent at which it considered that the subject property might reasonably be expected to be let on the open market by a willing landlord under an assured tenancy.

In so doing, the Committee, as required by section 14( 1), ignored the effect on the rental value of the property of any relevant tenant's improvements as defined in section 14(2) of that Act.

In coming to its' decision, the Committee had regard to the evidence supplied by the parties and the members' own general of market rents in the area of Barnstaple and concluded that an appropriate market rent for the property would be £475 pcm.

 

5. The decision.

The Committee therefore concluded that the rent at which the property might reasonably be expected to be let on the open market would be £475 per calendar month.

The rent will take effect from February 28th 2003, being the date specified by the landlord in the notice of increase.
