5.         THE LAW

 

5.1               In general terms, the task of the Committee under the provisions of section 14 of the Housing Act 1988 is to determine the rent at which the dwelling-house concerned might reasonably be expected to be let in the open market by a willing landlord. It follows that the personal circumstances of the parties are irrelevant and the Committee cannot take them into account.

 

5.2               The Committee must disregard any effect on the rent attributable to the granting of a tenancy to a sitting tenant, any increase in value attributable to “relevant” tenants’ improvements and any reduction in value attributable to a failure by the tenant to comply with any terms of the tenancy. Full Reasons (if requested) will set out the relevant legal principles in detail.

 

 

6.         THE DECISION

 

6.1        Summary of findings and conclusions:-

 

6.1.1          The parties offer no evidence of rental value. Although it is small and has a rather austere exterior, the property provides good modern accommodation for a small family and is well located in relation to local facilities. The immediate locality is not very attractive; but proximity to the City centre and other facilities makes the property attractive to prospective tenants. Off-street parking is a bonus. There is a shortage of this type of accommodation in central Cambridge.

 

6.1.2          The Committee's own knowledge and experience suggest an open market rent, if the property were let on assured shorthold terms, with curtains and fitted carpets, but without the tenant’s improvements, of £625 p.c.m. Deducting £30 p.c.m. because the property was let without curtains or carpets, the Committee concludes that a reasonable open market rent for the property would be £595 p.c.m. This equates to £137.31 per week. In addition, the variable service charge of £1.38 per week is payable by the tenant.

 

 

 

6.1.3          In practice, of course, the Society sets its rents, not by reference to the open market, but by reference to Government guidelines; thus the rent actually payable by the tenant will, as the Committee understands, be £68.61 + £1.38, a total of £69.99 per week.

 

 

6.2.1          Tenant's improvements (to be disregarded) have been stripped out; so no separate deduction is necessary.

 

6.2.2          Service charges (not to be included)         £1.38 per week

Rates/water rates (to be deducted)           None

Reasonable open market rent                   £137.31 per week
