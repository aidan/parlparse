1. The Application

 

On 1 August 2003 District Judge Ackner sitting at Aldershot and Farnham County Court ordered that

  1. Upon being satisfied that the statutory criteria under Chapter 1 of the Leasehold Reform, Housing and Urban Development Act 1993 have been complied with, it is ordered that there be a vesting order pursuant to Section 26 of that Act in respect of freehold premises at 168-176 Lower Farnham Road, Aldershot together with the parking spaces, access roads and grounds in favour of Scorecharge Limited for an amount and upon terms to be determined by the Leasehold Valuation Tribunal.
  2. Matter to be transferred to the Leasehold Valuation Tribunal.
  3. Costs reserved.

 

The applicants then applied to the Tribunal under Section 27 of the Act to determine the amount to be paid for and the terms of the transfer of the freehold.

 

 

2. The Property

 

The building is a purpose-built block, which was constructed in the early 1970’s.  It comprises five flats: two at ground floor level; two at first floor level; one at second floor level.  Each flat is understood to consist of the following accommodation: -

  • Hall
  • Sitting room
  • Kitchen/dining room
  • Two bedrooms
  • Bathroom

 

There are main services and gas-fired central heating

 

In addition there are five parking spaces, which are located within the building at ground floor level.  There are roadways, footpaths and other grounds, which are included within the property.

Scorecharge Limited is a private company limited by shares, which was incorporated on 24 September 2002.  The Articles of Association of the Company were adopted on 28 October 2002. The company registration number is 4543722.

In his letter dated 9 April 2002, Mr. Davis provided a valuation report for Mrs. Helstrip, who was the owner of no. 174 at that time.  His assessment of the ‘enfranchisement price’ was £18,200, subject to a number of qualifications and reservations.

 

In his letters dated 31 July 2002 and 14 November 2003 to Charles Russell, Mr. Davis provided a valuation in the sum of £18,500 in respect of the enfranchisement of four out of the five leases, there being one ‘non-participating leaseholder’.

 

 

6. The Draft Transfer

 

With their letter of 2 February 2004, Charles Russell enclosed a draft Transfer (Form TR1), as advised by HM Land Registry, together with an abstract of title of Mr. N C Matthews dated 1972.

7. Inspection of the Property

 

The Tribunal inspected the property before the Hearing. It is situated on the north west side of Lower Farnham Road, Aldershot.  This is the B3208, which runs between the A325 and the A323.

 

No representative of the applicants was present to accompany the Tribunal.

 

The property comprises a three-storey block with access roads on each side leading to an area of garden to the rear.  This is mainly grass and appeared to be in communal use, having no divisions, although it was noted on the Land Registry plans that a specific area is allocated to each flat.

 

On the ground floor, there is a flat to front (no. 168) and rear (no. 170) with a central covered area for parking cars.  There is a concrete staircase to the upper floors and a rubbish chute.

On the first floor, there is a flat to front (no.172) and rear (no. 174).

On the second floor there is a single flat (no. 176), which faces to both front and rear.

 

Internal inspection was only possible to no. 168.  The accommodation and layout of this flat were found to be in accordance with the floor plan provided.  It was noted that the kitchen was fitted with modern units and that the bathroom appeared to contain the original 1970’s sanitary ware.

 

The building appeared to be structurally sound and in a fair state of repair externally.  The original timber-framed windows to flats 170, 174 and 176 had been replaced with UPVC framed windows.  Elsewhere the timber windows showed signs of being in need of repair and painting.

 

It was noted that a vehicular access was in use to the adjoining property, no. 166, over the access road on the north east side of the building.  This appeared to have been established some time ago, in that there was nothing to indicate that any works had been carried out recently.

 

 

8. The Hearing

 

Miss E Gibbons (Counsel) appeared for the applicants.  She called Mr. Anthony Davis of Churchod & Co as expert witness on valuation matters.  Mr. Davis is a Chartered Surveyor with a degree in Estate Management.  He is a consultant to Churchod and Co, a firm of Chartered Surveyors.

 

There was no respondent present.

 

In opening the hearing the Chairman indicated that the matters to be covered were circumscribed by the Order of the Court, being the amount of the valuation and the terms of the transfer and that, in addition, the date of the valuation had to be determined.

 

Miss Gibbons submitted that this should be the date of the hearing.

 

Mr. Davis presented his evidence on valuation.  His valuation parameters and schedule are attached in Schedule 1 to this decision.  His valuation was in the sum of £19,100 as at 1 August 2003, being the date of the Court Order.

 

Mr. Davis had only had access to no.174, this being in 2002.  He could not report on the internal condition of the other flats.  From the plans submitted it was accepted that the layout of each flat was similar.  While the actual floor area for no.176 was larger that that for the other flats, this had to be balanced by the reduced effective floor area resulting from the sloping ceilings to parts of the rooms.  For valuation purposes, he did not distinguish between the flats.

 

He made the following comments on and amplifications to his valuation, in response to the Tribunal’s questions: -

 

·        Yield.  His figure of 8% was based on comparable settlements in the area, of 8% to 10%, and compared with Central London figures of 6% to 8%.

·        Tenants’ Improvements.  He had made an allowance of up to £5,000 for each flat, as he understood that kitchen and bathroom fittings had been upgraded.  He had taken account of this in has valuation.

·        Comparable Sales.  The sale of no.172 in March 2001 for only £67,500 had, according to the selling agents, reflected the absence of a freeholder and the consequent inability of prospective buyers to obtain a mortgage.  He felt that it might also have been due in part to the condition of the property at that time.  In contrast the sale of no.174 in October 2002 for £124,000 was at a time when enfranchisement proceedings were already in hand.

·        Current Capital Values.  To arrive at these (for September 2003) Mr. Davis submitted details from Land Registry statistics, showing a 46.23% increase in prices for flats and maisonettes in the Rushmoor area between March 2001 and September 2003.  From December 2002 to September 2003 there was a fall of 1.5%.

·        Relativity.  He considered his figure of 7.5% to be middle of the road.  He quoted a reported decision on no. 19 Hale Road, Farnham for which the figure was 6% for a lease with 76 years remaining.

·        Valuation up-date.  Mr. Davis stated that he would not wish to alter his August 2003 figure, although the remaining term had been reduced by 6 months.  In his opinion the local market had been unchanged and relatively inactive during that period.  In some cases it had been in reverse and the increase in Base Rate could be a factor.

 

The Tribunal then turned to the matter of the terms of the Transfer.  The Chairman drew attention firstly, to the apparent burden of the right-of-way in favour of no. 166 and secondly, to the need for a nomination for the transferor, which had not been given in the Court Order.  Miss Gibbons was not in a position to make representations on these points.  To comply with the provisions of Section 34 and Schedule 7 of the 1993 Act, further information would be sought from Charles Russell.

 

 

 

 

9. The Decision

 

Valuation Date.

The Tribunal decided that in accordance with Clause 1, Part 1 of Schedule 6 to the 1993 Act, the date was the date of the determination, namely 12 February 2004.

 

Valuation.

The Tribunal decided that the price for enfranchisement in accordance with the Court Order should be £20,300.  The analysis of this is shown in Schedule 2 to this decision.  The Tribunal took into account the following: -

 

·        Yield: Mr. Davis’ figure of 8% was accepted.

·        Tenants’ improvements: Inspection of flat no. 168 indicated that not all bathrooms had been modernised, though it was assumed that all kitchens had been improved.

·        Comparables: In reviewing the Land Registry Title No. HP365805 in respect of flat no. 4 (no. 174) the Tribunal noted that the price paid on 29 November 2002 was £119,500.  The difference between this and the price stated by Mr. Davis, namely £4,500, was assumed to relate to tenant’s fixtures and fittings, which were included in the purchase of the flat.  They also noted that in Land Registry Title No.HP604634 in respect of flat no.3 (no.172) the price paid on 28 March 2001 was £95,000. This contrasted with Mr. Davis’ figure of £67,500.  There did not appear to be any explanation for this and the Tribunal accepted the Land Registry figure. 

·        The Tribunal did not regard the Land Registry statistics as a reliable basis for valuation, but found them useful as corroboration of values determined otherwise.

·        Current Capital Value: The Tribunal were of the opinion that this was £108,000 per flat and that the virtual freehold value was £116,000 per flat, with relativity of 7.5%.

·        Hope Value: This related to the non-participating flat, for which the Tribunal decided, taking account of the risk and the potential benefit, that the value would be fairly assessed at 25%, as opposed to Mr. Davis’ 10%.  They calculated Marriage Value accordingly.

 

Transfer.

 

The Tribunal referred the draft form of transfer back to Charles Russell for further research and information, in particular on the matter of the apparent right-of-way over part of the property enjoyed by the owner of No. 166 Lower Farnham Road.  Charles Russell contacted Mr. Shepherd at that address, but it appears that neither side has any documentary evidence of a right-of-way.  Paragraph 12.4 of Form TR 1 has been worded to provide for this.  The new form of transfer submitted by Charles Russell has been approved by the Tribunal for execution on behalf of the freeholder by an officer of the Court and is attached to this decision.
