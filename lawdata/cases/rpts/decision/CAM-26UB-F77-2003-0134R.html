The Property:                                    

The property is a two storey mid-terrace late Victorian house constructed of brick under a slate roof. There is a flat roof single storey extension containing the bathroom. The house comprises a front living room, back living room with door to stairs rising to the first floor, kitchen and bathroom on the ground floor. There are two bedrooms on the first floor. In the rear bedroom there is a door to stairs rising to an attic bedroom. Access to the front living room is directly from the pavement with no front garden or hallway. Water heating is by gas multipoint; space heating was originally by open fires but is now by Tenant’s gas fires. There is a garden at the back of the house with pedestrian access at the rear. The property has main gas, electricity, water and drainage. The property is situated in a residential area. The property is let unfurnished.

 

Condition:

The Committee inspected the property in the presence of the Tenant. The exterior of the property is in need of decoration. The kitchen and bathroom windows have exposed wood. The guttering at the rear of the property has become disconnected. There is a fitted kitchen and basic modern bathroom. There is no central heating. Carpets, curtains and white goods are not provided.

 

Law:

Attached to this Statement of Reasons is a resumé of the law applied by the Committee.

 

 

 

Open Market Rent

Neither party submitted comparables. The Committee using the experience of its members determined that a market rent for a property similar to the subject property in good condition with central heating, modern kitchen and bathroom, and let with carpets, curtains and white goods on an Assured Shorthold Tenancy would be £150.00 per week. However the Committee considered that a global deduction of £38.00 per week should be made in respect of the subject property for the lack of central heating, the poor exterior decoration and detached guttering and the lack of any carpets, curtains or white goods. It should be noted that this figure cannot be a simple arithmetical calculation and is not based specifically upon capital cost but is the Committee’s estimate of the amount by which the rent would have to be reduced to attract a tenant.

 

Scarcity

It was decided that there is substantial scarcity of  “... similar dwelling-houses in the locality...” in this case South East Hertfordshire, Broxbourne and adjoining parts of South West Essex and outer London areas that are available for letting and a deduction would be made to reflect this of 10 %

 

Committee’s Calculations:

Open Market Rent:                                                                               £150.00 per week

Less global deduction                                                                           £  38.00 per week

                                                                                                            £112.00 per week

Less Scarcity 10%                                                                                £  11.20 per week

                                                                                                            £100.80 per week

‘Uncapped’ fair rent say £100.00 per week

 

The provisions of the Rent Acts (Maximum Fair Rent) Order 1999 require that the registered rent is either the capped Fair Rent or the Fair Rent decided by the Committee which ever is the lower. The capped Fair Rent is calculated in accordance with a statutory formula using the existing rent as a base. The capped rent in this case is £88.50 per week, which is lower than the Fair Rent assessed by the Committee and therefore the capped rent is to be registered.

 

FAIR RENT = £88.50 per week
