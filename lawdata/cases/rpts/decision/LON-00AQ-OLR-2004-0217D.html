THE TRIBUNAL’S DECISION

 

This was an application by Ryan D’Austin, the tenant of 1 Malcolm Court, Marsh Lane Stanmore, Middlesex proposing a premium of £12,000 for an extended lease.  The respondent landlords, Ian Gerrard, Sandra Gerrard  and Merilyn Gerrard rejected that proposal and made a counter offer of £24,300.

 

At a hearing on 26 January 2004 Mr D Cooper BA, BSc, MRICS attended on behalf of the tenant and Jennifer Branscombe FRICS attended on behalf of the landlords.  Valuations were then produced on behalf of the tenant suggesting a revised premium of £14,900 and on behalf of the landlords a revised premium of £20,202.

 

The following matters had all been agreed prior to the hearing:

 

1.                  Subject property

2.                  Lessees improvements

3.                  Value of existing lease

4.                  Valuation date

5.                  Capitalisation rate

 

Therefore the only matters in issue were:

 

1.                  Extended Lease Value

2.                  Terms of lease

3.                  Lessors reasonable legal and survey costs.

 

1.         Extended Lease Value

 

Mr Cooper in his valuation referred to 17 Malcolm Court for which a lease extension had been agreed at £10,750 in August 2000.  Using that figure as a basis he arrived at a premium of £14,900 (see Appendix A).

 

In evidence he referred to comparables at 17 Hill Close, 15 Malcolm Court and 17 Springfield Close to obtain long lease values with particular mention of 17 Hill Close which had been sold at £168,000 only 17 days before the valuation date of the subject property.  After adjustments for central heating, double glazing and fitted units and for the larger size of the subject that he arrived at an estimated value for the subject property with a long lease at £170,000.

 

Ms Branscombe gave evidence of previous settlements which the Tribunal noted.  She also gave evidence of various comparables including 17 Springfield Close and 25 Malcolm Court but agreed that 17 Hill Close was the best evidence of the long lease value.  However her calculations from that sale arrived at a value of £180,500 using each of the comparable properties as a base figure.  She arrived at a proposed lease extension price of £20,202 (See Appendix B).

 

2.         Terms of the Lease

 

The only issue relating to the terms of the lease was in Clause 4(14) which contained a provision for the lessee to pay a registration fee of one guinea in respect of each assignment, assent, transfer, underlease or devolution.

 

It was argued that the sum of one guinea was no longer realistic in modern times and that the lease should be adjusted under the terms of Section 57(6)(b) of the Leasehold Reform Act 1993.

 

Mr Cooper suggested that the payment should be £50 plus value added tax doubling every 25 years.

 

Ms Branscombe had suggested an amendment from “one guinea” to “0.5% of the purchase price”.

 

3.         Inspection

 

The Tribunal inspected the subject maisonette which they found to be in a pleasant close of similar properties off Marsh Lane, Stanmore. The inside of the property was well planned but somewhat compact, having three rooms, kitchen, bathroom and separate wc.

 

They also inspected the exteriors of the other properties mentioned in evidence and considered that 17 Hill Close was the best comparable, although slightly more attractive.  Springfield Close was significantly further from the centre of Stanmore.

 

4.         Decision

 

1.         Extended lease value

 

Having considered all the evidence, the Tribunal decided that the long lease value of 1 Malcolm Court was £174,000.  This provided an amount payable for the extended lease of £16,920 as per valuation in Appendix C.

 

2.         Term of Lease

 

The Tribunal accepted that a fee of one guinea was no longer reasonable in current circumstances and should be amended.  They felt that a figure of £50 plus value added tax was appropriate but that it should be doubled every 30 years.  Therefore clause 4(14) of the lease should read as follows:-

 

(14)          Within one month after every assignment assent transfer or underlease (otherwise than by way of mortgage) of the demised premises to give notice thereof in writing with particulars thereof to the Lessors and to produce such assignment assent transfer or underlease to the Lessors.  In the case of a devolution of the interest of the Lessee not perfected by an assent within twelve months after the happening thereof to produce to the Lessors the probate of the will or letters of administration under which devolution arises.  To pay to the Lessors a registration fee of £50 plus value added tax doubling every 30 years from the date of the lease in respect of each such assignment assent transfer underlease or devolution.

 

3.         Costs

 

No arguments were presented in respect of costs but the parties were given liberty to return to the Tribunal in the event of a failure to agree.
