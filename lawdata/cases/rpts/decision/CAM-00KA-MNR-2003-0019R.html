The Property:                                    

The subject property is a semi-detached house of brick under a tile roof probably constructed in the 1950s. The property comprises a hall with stairs to the first floor, a living room, bathroom and kitchen on the ground floor and three bedrooms on the first floor. Outside there is a detached prefabricated double garage and gardens to front and rear. The property has main gas, water, electricity and drainage and space and hot water was provided by a gas central heating system. It is situated in a residential area on the outskirts of Luton.

 

Condition:

Externally the property was in fair condition. The windows had recently been replaced with upvc double glazed units. Internally there was a new gas fire in the living room with back boiler for the central heating. The kitchen was dated as was the bathroom suite and w.c. 

 

Law:

Sections 13 and 14 of the Housing Act 1988 apply

 

Open Market Rent:

In written representations the Tenant referred to the inadequacy of the windows, gas heater in the living room, cooker and the lack of cleanliness of the property when it is let. At the inspection it was noted that these defects had been remedied.  Neither party put forward rents of comparables and therefore the Committee used the experience of its members in assessing a rent. The Committee decided that the open market value of the subject property taking into account its present condition is £530.00 per calendar month.

 

Market Rent for subject property                            £530.00 per calendar month
