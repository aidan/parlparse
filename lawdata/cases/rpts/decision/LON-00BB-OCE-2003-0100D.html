1.   Introduction

      1.1   The Respondents have previously given notice to the Applicant, pursuant  to section 13 of the Act claiming to exercise the right to a collective enfranchisement to acquire the freehold interest in the Premises, and comprise the nominee purchaser. The Applicant is freeholder and thereby the reversioner for the purposes of the Act.

     1.2    We were told that the claim has been accepted.

     1.3    We were told that the terms of the acquisition have been either agreed between the parties or determined by an earlier Tribunal.

     1.4    The application  presently before us is made pursuant to section 33 of the Act, and it relates to costs. The papers before us show that the parties have already agreed that the Respondents shall pay the following costs:

 

Valuation fees       £587.50

Transfer costs        £248.00

 

In addition the Applicant seeks the costs and expenses set out in the schedule attached marked “A”. We shall therefore concentrate on these costs.

    1.5     Evidently at all material times the Applicant has been represented by Mr C Lipman.

     1.6     The Applicant was neither present nor represented at the hearing, but the tribunal considered the representations made by Mr Lipman in his letter dated 10 February 2004 and the enclosure thereto. The Respondents were represented by Ms M Franco of Bowling & Co, which firm has represented the Respondents throughout.

     1.7   Prior to the commencement of the hearing Mr Tobin disclosed that he was from time to time appointed by Bowling & Co to carry out expert witness work on behalf of their clients. Mr Tobin stated that he had not been concerned with 72 Odessa Road in any way, and neither had he been instructed by or on behalf of the Respondents.                        

 

 

 

2.   The Law

    2.1      Section 33(1) of the Act provides that :-

“ Where a section 13 notice is given the nominee purchaser is liable, to the extent that they have been incurred in pursuance of the notice by the reversioner, for the reasonable costs of and incidental to any of the following matters:-

                                                                                                                                            (a)   any investigation reasonably undertaken

       (i)   of the question whether any interest in the specified premises or other

          property is liable to acquisition in pursuance of the initial notice, or

      (ii)   of any other question arising out of that notice;

(b)   deducing, evidencing and verifying the title to any such interest;

(c)   making out and furnishing such abstracts and copies as the nominee purchaser

       may require;

(d)    any valuation of any interest in the specified premises or other property;

(e)    any conveyance of any such interest.”

 

   2.2       Section 33(2) provides that:-

“ For the purposes of subsection (1) any costs incurred by the reversioner or any other relevant landlord in respect of professional services rendered by any person shall only be regarded as reasonable if and to the extent that costs in respect of such services might reasonably be expected to have been incurred by him if the circumstances had been such that he was personally liable for all such costs.”                                          

 

 

3.   The Costs Claimed

 

   3.1   It will be noted from Appendix A that the costs claimed fall into 2 discrete classes:

            1. The costs of Mr Lipman. These costs are said to relate to time spent by him travelling, typing, on the telephone, deliberating, in discussion and ancillary thereto. The claim is £85 per hour for matters requiring knowledge and £16 per hour for non-complicated matters. The costs are broken down into 2 segments:-

             2003   February  to April      £ 90

                        August to November £650

             2.   The costs of  the Applicant. These costs are said to relate to time spent by him commensurate with his earning power, but limited to £120 per hour. Again the costs are broken down into 2 segments:-

              2003   February to April      £ 40

                      August to November £220

 

3.2    Ms Franco submitted to the Tribunal that no evidence had been provided by the Applicant of any agreement to pay Mr Lipman fees, or of any hourly rates agreed to be paid, or of the detail of work allegedly carried out. Ms Franco told us that Mr Lipman had been asked for details, but nothing had been forthcoming. Ms Franco told us that this matter had been going on for 5/6 years. Mr Lipman’s involvement and relationship with the Applicant was unclear. Ms Franco said that Mr Lipman did not appear to be acting in any professional capacity; he claimed to be the Applicant’s agent. No agency agreement has been provided. No  receipted fee notes have been provided. Ms Franco  said that no evidence had been provided to explain the basis on which the costs were incurred and no evidence provided to show that they had been incurred or paid.

3.3   Further Ms Franco submitted that even if the costs claimed had been incurred, they were not reasonably incurred. The valuation fee had been agreed; it was not reasonable for the Applicant to incur further fees discussing the report with Mr Lipman. No evidence was provided by the Applicant to explain Mr Lipman’s involvement, and that such involvement was reasonably required.

3.4   Ms Franco also submitted that the claim for costs representing the Applicant’s time dealing with the entitlement to enfranchise were costs incurred by him and were not within the scope of section 33(1) of the Act.

3.5   Ms Franco invited the Tribunal not to allow any of the costs claimed in Appendix A.

 

4.   Decision

 

4.1    We note the absence of evidence from the Applicant of his relationship with Mr Lipman, the work Mr Lipman undertook for him, the basis on which that work was to be remunerated, and that it was in fact remunerated in accordance with the agreed arrangement.

4.2   We also note that no clear explanation has been given of the role of Mr Lipman and of the work carried out by him on behalf of the Applicant.

4.3   In the circumstances, the Tribunal could not be satisfied that the costs of Mr Lipman had been incurred by the Applicant; still less was the Tribunal satisfied that such costs as may have been incurred were reasonably incurred, or reasonably incurred in respect of any matters falling within section 33(1) of the Act. Accordingly, the Tribunal does not allow any of the costs claimed in respect of services said to have been provided by Mr Lipman.

4.4   Turning to the second limb of the claim, no evidence was provided to the Tribunal to explain what the Applicant did during the 2 and a half hours claimed. No explanation was given to justify the claim at £120 per hour. No evidence was provided to show that any loss had been suffered by the Applicant when spending the time claimed. No evidence was provided to show that the costs had been incurred by the Applicant; still less reasonably incurred on matters within section 33(1) of the Act.

4.5   It seemed to the Tribunal that the claim was, in effect, a claim to compensation for time said to have been spent by the Applicant in dealing with the Respondent’s admitted right to enfranchise. In the absence of any evidence that the time so spent resulted in a cost to the Respondent, the Tribunal determined that it did not have jurisdiction to award such compensation.

4.6  In the circumstances, the Tribunal could not be satisfied that the costs claimed as representing the Applicant’s time were costs incurred by the Applicant; still less was the Tribunal satisfied that such costs as may have been incurred were reasonably incurred, or reasonably incurred on matters falling within section 33(1) of the Act. Accordingly, the Tribunal does not allow any of the costs claimed by the Applicant in respect of his time dealing with the Respondents’ right to enfranchise.
