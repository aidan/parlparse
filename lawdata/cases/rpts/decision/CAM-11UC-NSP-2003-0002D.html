The Application

 

1.         On the 22nd July 2003 the Applicant applied to this Tribunal:

 

a)      Under section 19(2A) of the Landlord and Tenant Act 1985 as amended by the Housing Act 1996 for a determination of reasonableness of service charge where costs already incurred in the financial years 1999, 2000, 2001 & 2002

 

b)      Under section 19(2B) Landlord and Tenant Act 1985 as amended by the Housing Act 1996 for a determination of reasonableness of service charge where costs are proposed or to be incurred in the financial year 2003

 

2.         Together with the aforementioned applications a further application was made, pursuant to section 20C of the Landlord and Tenant Act 1985 as amended by the Housing Act 1996, for an order that the Landlord’s costs in connection with these proceedings should not be regarded as relevant costs to be taken into account in determining the amount of any service charge payable by the tenants of the property.

 

3.         A copy of a letter dated 9th May 2001 from the Respondent’s Managing Agents stated that the Applicant had not paid the ground rent or service charge. The Applicant confirmed that he had paid the ground rent but that the service charge continued to be outstanding. The Tribunal therefore had jurisdiction to hear the Application.

 

The Law

 

4.         A tenant or a landlord may make application to the leasehold valuation tribunal under s 19(2A) of the Landlord and Tenant Act 1985 as amended by the Housing Act 1996 to determine in relation to a service charge

a)   whether costs incurred for services, repairs, maintenance, insurance or management were reasonably incurred,

b)      whether services or works for which costs were incurred are of a reasonable standard, or

c)      whether an amount payable before costs are incurred is reasonable

 

5.         A tenant or a landlord may make application to the leasehold valuation tribunal under s 19(2A) of the Landlord and Tenant Act 1985 as amended by the Housing Act 1996 to determine in relation to a service charge

a)   whether if costs were incurred for services, repairs, maintenance, insurance or management of any specified description would be reasonable,

b)   whether services or works carried out to a particular specification would be of a reasonable standard, or

c)      whether an amount payable before costs are incurred would be reasonable

 

6.         A tenant may make application to the leasehold valuation tribunal under section 20C of the Landlord and Tenant Act 1985 as amended by the housing Act 1996 that all or any of the costs incurred in connection with the proceedings are not to be regarded as relevant costs in determining the amount of any service charge payable by the tenant and the tribunal may make such order as it considers just and equitable in the circumstances.                                                                                                                                                     

 

Description of the Building and Property

 

7.         The Applicant’s property is a two-bedroom first floor flat (“The Property”) in a purpose built block four flats constructed in the 1980s. It is attached to another block with a separate entrance, which was constructed sometime earlier originally as two semi-detached houses and was later converted to two flats. The whole Building is defined in the Lease produced (which was for 4 Harding Road) as 2 and 4 Harding Road including any additions, which would take account of the flats 5, 6, 7,and 8 together with the parking area. The Building is constructed of brick under a tile roof.  There is a car park to the side and rear of the Building edged with conifers. At the front of the Building there is a grass verge and communal gardens to the rear.

In the absence of contrary evidence the Tribunal accepted that the Lessor had complied with this term of the Lease and therefore the Lease produced was in the same form as the actual lease granted.

Hearing

The Applicant’s Case

 

13.       The Application made the following points in relation to the application under section 19(2A) of the Landlord and Tenant Act 1985 as amended by the Housing Act 1996.

 

14.       Works in relation to the Section 20 Notice

 

a) The Applicant stated that the decoration of the windows was in such a poor condition in 2000 that he had had the work carried out at his own expense. He therefore considered that he should be exempt from the cost in relation to the service charge. He stated that he considered that the Respondents had set a precedent for such an arrangement by exempting the tenant of flat 4 from the cost of the redecoration of the flat windows because the replacement upvc windows had been fitted at that tenant’s sole expense and not through the service charge.

 

The Applicant stated that the Respondents had not re-decorated within the times set out in the lease. He said that he had informed them when the section 20 Notice was served that the windows had already been painted and produced a copy of letter to the Respondents dated 25th October 2000. He also affixed a notice to the windows to inform the painter that he did not want the windows painted as the work had already been done.

 

Nevertheless the Applicant said that a coat of paint had been applied and produced a copy of a letter dated 9th May 2001 from the Respondents’ Management Company confirming this. The painting had not been carried out in a satisfactory manner and the jamb of the window had not been painted.

 

The Applicant stated that he would be prepared to pay his contribution to the redecoration of the common areas as had been calculated for the tenant of flat 4 subject the work being of a reasonable standard.

 

b) In addition the Applicant stated that the works to which the Section 20 Notice referred were of a poor quality. Other tenants had expressed dissatisfaction to him with the standard of the work. He said that paint was peeling and in particular parts of the soffits and fascias were rotten and therefore the paintwork had rapidly deteriorated in those areas. He therefore considered the work in relation to the common areas and the windows of the flats was not of a satisfactory standard.

 

15.       Cleaning of the common parts

 

The Applicant stated that the cleaner had only been 4 times over a period of 9 months. The inside of the windows were dirty and the area around the windows is covered in dust and cobwebs. There are also cobwebs hanging from the ceiling. The tenants carried out any cleaning such as vacuuming the carpets.

 

 

 

 

16.       Electricity

Determination of Application under Section 19(2A) of the Landlord and Tenant Act 1985 as amended by the Housing Act 1996

 

24.       Works in relation to the Section 20 Notice

 

a) The Tribunal were of the opinion that no exemptions should be made from the service charge if a tenant undertakes work on his own account. The Tribunal referred to the case of Broomleigh Housing Association Ltd v Hughes [1999] EGCS 134. In that case a tenant who had paid for her own windows to be replaced objected to paying by way of service charge for the replacement of windows in other flats in a block. It was held that a tenant of a flat was often obliged to contribute towards work done on other flats. On this principle it would be expected that according to Clause 4 of the Lease provided both the cost of re-decoration and that of replacing the windows in flat 4 would be borne by all the tenants equally. The Section 20 Notice refers to two quotations for replacement windows and declares that the financial responsibility lies with the tenant of flat 4. The tenant of flat 4 is not a party to this Application and it is not known why the cost is to be borne by the tenant of flat 4 only. The Tribunal is restricted to making a decision as to the reasonableness of the service charge. As the cost of replacing the windows of flat 4 are not included in the service charge they were not a matter upon which the Tribunal was able to make a decision.

 

However in relation to the cost of the works referred to in the Section 20 Notice that were included in the service charge the Tribunal considered that one sixth of the total cost should be paid by each tenant in accordance with clause 4 of the Lease provided.

 

With regard to the times set within the Lease provided for re-decoration clause 3 (6) of the Lease provided states that external painting should be carried out by the Lessor every 4 years or as the Lessor’s Managing Agent may in their absolute discretion advise.

 

b) The Tribunal at the inspection noted the extent of the work that had been carried out together with the standard of work. The Tribunal was of the opinion that the charge of £2,500 for the works carried out under the section 20 Notice was excessive and therefore unreasonable. The Tribunal determined a charge of £1,750 for the work carried out and an administration fee @ 10% of £175 (VAT being added as appropriate) as being reasonable. As stated above each tenant in accordance with clause 4 of the Lease provided should pay one sixth of the total cost.

 

25.       Cleaning of the common parts

 

As mentioned in paragraph 10 relating to the Inspection the extent of the cleaning of the common parts had been very limited over the period to which the Application relates and corresponded to the evidence given by the Applicant. The charges were therefore considered excessive and unreasonable. The Tribunal decided that a sum of £120 per annum was reasonable.

 

26.       Electricity

 

            The Tribunal accepted that prices per unit and standing charges varied between suppliers. However it would be unduly onerous for a landlord to be obliged to obtain the cheapest supply at any one time. The Tribunal therefore accepted the service charge cost stated as reasonable.

 

27.       Gardening

 

            The Tribunal accepted the Applicant’s evidence that the charges for gardening were unreasonable. The Tribunal determined that a charge of £150 for the cutting down of a tree in 1999 and a charge of £450 for mowing the lawns in the summer of 2001 only should be made as reasonable.

 

28.       Insurance

 

There was insufficient evidence to support the Applicant’s contention that the insurance premium was excessive. The Respondent had failed to produce the insurance certificate as required by the pre-hearing direction. It appeared to the Tribunal that the issue raised by the Applicant would need to answered by an application under paragraph 8 of the Schedule to the Landlord and Tenant Act 1985 to consider whether an alternative insurer should be nominated.

 

29.       Repairs and Maintenance

 

            Apart from the work carried out under the section 20 Notice there was no evidence of any repair or maintenance having been undertaken.

 

30.       Window Cleaning

           

            The Tribunal accepted the Applicant’s evidence that the charges for window cleaning were unreasonable and determined that a charge of £100 per annum should be substituted for the costs referred to in the service charge accounts.

 

31.       Management Fee

 

            The tribunal considered that a management fee of 10% of the total expenditure (as amended by the tribunal) was reasonable and this item in the service charge accounts should be recalculated accordingly.

Determination of Application under Section 19(2A) of the Landlord and Tenant Act 1985 as amended by the Housing Act 1996

 

33.       As no evidence could be adduced on this application no determination was made.

 

Determination of Application under Section 20(c) of the Landlord and Tenant Act 1985

 

34.       The Respondents made no reply to either the Application or the Pre-hearing Directions. In the absence of any evidence to the contrary the Tribunal accepted the Applicant’s case in respect of many of the amounts that he disputed in the service charge accounts. The matters that were not accepted such as the electricity and insurance costs might have been settled without the need for an application to the Tribunal if the Respondent had produced evidence to the Applicant to explain the expenditure. The Tribunal therefore determine that these proceedings should not be regarded as relevant costs to be taken into account in determining the amount of any service charge payable by the Applicant.
