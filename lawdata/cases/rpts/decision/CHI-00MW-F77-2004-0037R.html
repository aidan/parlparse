REASONS FOR THE DECISION OF THE COMMITTEE

_______________________________________________________________

 

 

 

1.         The Applications

 

1.1        The landlord had sought the registration of new fair rents in respect of the several Rent Act tenancies set out above.  The landlord wishes to refer the Rent Officer s decision in each case to the Rent Assessment Committee.

 

2.         The Properties

 

2.1        The description of the properties and their condition is set out in the Rent Officer s Valuation and Decision Notes dated December 2003.  They were all similar in design although they varied in the number of units contained in each property and whilst they were all very pleasant, some were better than others, the newer units tending to be superior to the older units.  The Committee felt that the landlord was to be complimented on the standard of maintenance and cleanliness of the properties and on the high level of service provision in every case.

 

3.         The Law

 

            When determining a fair rent the Committee, in accordance with the Rent Act 1977, section 70, had regard to all the circumstances including the age, location and state of repair of the property.  It also disregarded the effect of (a) any relevant tenant s improvements and (b) the effect of any disrepair or other defect attributable to the tenant or any predecessor in title under the regulated tenancy, on the rental value of the property.

 

            In Spath Holme Ltd v Chairman of the Greater Manchester etc. Committee [1999] QB 92 the Court of Appeal emphasised:

 

            (a)        that ordinarily a fair rent is the market rent for the property discounted for  scarcity (ie that element, if any, of the market rent, that is attributable to there being a significant shortage of similar properties in the wider locality available for letting on similar terms  other than as to rent  to that of t he regulated tenancy); and

 

            (b)        that for the purposes of determining the market rent, assured tenancy (market) rents are usually appropriate comparables.  (These rents may have to be adjusted where necessary to reflect any relevant differences between those comparables and the subject property.)

 

4.         Inspection

 

4.1        The Committee inspected the properties on 27th April 2004.  It was unable to gain access to Flat 8 Byrnhill Grove or Flat 15 Buckler s View but the Committee assumed that they were similar to other units in the building.

 

 

 

 

5.         The Hearing

 

5.1        There was no attendance at the hearing held at The Quay Arts Centre, Newport, Isle of Wight, on 28th April 2004.  Written representations had been made by the landlord and by some of the tenants.  The landlord s representation in each case was two-fold.  First, it said that the Rent Officer s determination was not in line and was lower than government controlled target rents for assured tenancies.  Secondly, the Rent Officer had included the  Supporting People element in the Fair Rent calculation.  The landlord said that this was not a housing cost and was means-tested and therefore it should not be included in the calculation.

 

5.2        Where a tenant had made written representations, it was to the effect that the increase in rent was larger than they thought reasonable.

 

5.3        The landlord had not provided the Committee with any information as to what the  Government controlled target for these particular rents was, nor had it given a full explanation of what the  Supporting People element was, how it was charged and how it would be collected if not included in the service charge.

                                      

5.4        Prior to the hearing the Committee asked to be provided with copies of the tenancy agreements but was provided only with a blank pro-forma tenancy agreement with no detail, particularly no service charge detail, supplied.

 

5.5        The Committee felt that the landlord had supplied insufficient detail of its objections to the Rent Officer s determination to deal with them adequately.  The Committee Chairman tried on several occasions to speak on the telephone with those responsible for the management of the Association s business, without success.

 

5.6        The Committee decided therefore that it had no alternative but to proceed with the determination on the following basis:-

 

            (a)        that it would simply apply the provisions of Section 70 of the Rent Act 1977, as it is required by statute to do, without any regard to Government targets with which it had been provided with no information, in any event;

 

            (b)        that the  Supporting People element would be treated as part and parcel of the service charge element in the fair rent calculation because:-

 

(i)                   the landlord had included this in its application to the Rent Officer and had included it in its Service Charge Schedule;

 

(ii)                 it would appear that the Fair Rent previously registered had included this element;

 

(iii)                the Committee felt that it had been provided with insufficient information to exclude it from the service charge.

 

5.7        The Committee determined the rents to be registered in each case in accordance with the schedule attached hereto.  In each case, deductions from Open Market Rent were as follows:-

 

                        Lack of white goods                                           £3      per week

                        Internal decoration liability                                   £3      per week

                        Carpets and curtains                                          £4      per week

                        Lack of double-glazing (where appropriate)           £2      per week

 

5.8               The Committee found that there were no relevant tenants improvements.

 

5.9               The Committee found that there was no scarcity of similar properties to let on similar tenancies in the area of the Isle of Wight.
