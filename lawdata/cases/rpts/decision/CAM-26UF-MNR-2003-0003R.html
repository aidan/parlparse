1.          THE PREMISES

 

1.1               The type of property:      3 bedroom mid-terrace house dating from 1950’s or early 1960’s

Location:                       Rural location east of Luton close to Luton Airport

 

Inspection: 

Two very small living rooms; a small kitchen, with minimal fittings by the landlord and additional wall cupboards and worktop by the tenants; a dated bathroom with high level WC, badly worn bath and cracked white tiling; three modest bedrooms, of which two are doubles, the third being used as a study; shed and coal store; good-sized gardens for a house of this type. Door fitted by tenant to side passageway (DIY but serviceable) provides security and prevents draughts.

 

Red brick and tile construction. The exterior is generally in fairly good condition, with UPVC double-glazed windows, though gutters and downpipes and the brickwork on the chimney are poor.

 

There is some damp in the study and front bedroom, adjacent to the chimney breast. The only space heating supplied by the landlord is one open fire (with back boiler). Immersion heater. White goods and some limited furniture, all cheap and of little rental value, are provided by the landlord. All flooring is by the tenant. Internal decorations are generally clean and of good standard.

5.         THE LAW

 

5.1        In general terms, the task of the Committee under the provisions of section 14 of the Housing Act 1988 is to determine the rent at which the dwelling-house concerned might reasonably be expected to be let in the open market by a willing landlord. The Committee must disregard any effect on the rent attributable to the granting of a tenancy to a sitting tenant, any increase in value attributable to “relevant” tenants’ improvements and any reduction in value attributable to a failure by the tenant to comply with any terms of the tenancy. Full Reasons (if requested) will set out the relevant legal principles in detail.

 

 

6.         THE DECISION

 

6.1               Summary of findings and conclusions:-

 

The parties offer no evidence of rental value. The house is very small and not pretty. The area is popular but the location is not particularly attractive and is too near the airport for comfort.

 

The Committee's knowledge and experience suggests open market rent for property, if fully modernised and in good condition throughout, with central heating, curtains carpets and white goods, would be £625 p.c.m.

 

The Committee deducts 20% for minor disrepair and for lack of modernisation, central heating, carpets, curtains. In conducting this exercise, the Committee has stripped out the tenants’ improvements.

 

 

6.2               Tenant's improvements (to be disregarded) – flooring and some units in kitchen – already taken into account under 6.1.above.

 

6.3               Service charges (not to be included)         Nil

Rates/water rates (to be deducted)           Nil

Reasonable open market rent                   £500 per calendar month

Undue hardship to tenant:                        No – not argued
