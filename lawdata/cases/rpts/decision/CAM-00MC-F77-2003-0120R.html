1.1       On 3 July 2003 the landlord of the premises applied to the rent officer for the registration of a fair rent of £8905 per year (£742 per month) for the premises, pursuant to section 67 of the Act.

 

1.2              The rent was previously registered on 5 September 2001 with effect from that date at a rent of £645 per month.

 

1.3              On 4 August 2003 the rent officer registered a fair rent of £702.50 per month    with effect from 5 September 2003.

 

1.4       By letter dated  13 August 2003 the tenant objected to the rent determined by the rent officer, and the matter was referred to the Rent Assessment Committee.

 

2.                  Inspection

The Committee therefore carried out an external inspection and was able to look into part of the downstairs of the premises through the ground floor windows. The Committee noted a sketch layout of a similar property (mirror image) provided by the rent officer. The Committee found the premises to be in fair condition but largely unimproved since original construction in the 1970s.

4.                  The Law

 

4.1              When determining a fair rent the Committee, in accordance with section 70 of the Act, had regard to all of the circumstances including the age, location and state of repair of the premises. It also disregarded the effect of:-

a)                  any relevant tenant's improvements, and

b)                  the effect of  any disrepair or other defect attributable to the tenant or any predecessor in title under the regulated tenancy

on the rental value of the premises.

 

4.2              In  Spathe Holme Limited v Chairman of the Greater Manchester etc Committee (1995) 28 HLR 107 and Curtis v London Rent Assessment Committee (1999) QB 92, the Court of Appeal emphasised:-

a)                  that ordinarily a fair rent is the market rent for the premises discounted for "scarcity" (i.e. that element, if any, of the market rent, that is attributable to there being a significant shortage of similar premises in the wider locality available for letting on similar terms - other than as to rent to that of the regulated tenancy in question, and

b)                  that for the purpose of determining the market rent, assured tenancy (market) rents are usually appropriate comparables. These rents may have to be adjusted where necessary to reflect any relevant differences between those comparables and the subject premises.

 

 

5.                  Valuation

 

5.1              In the first instance the Committee determined what rent the landlord could reasonably be expected to achieve for the premises in the open market if the premises were let today in the condition that is considered usual for such an open market letting.

 

5.2       It did this by having regard to the information submitted by the parties. The landlord's representative submitted details of rents achieved from AST lettings on other properties on the Field View Estate. Some of these properties were flats and some were of houses smaller than the subject premises. The comparable properties had not been identifies specifically and therefore the Committee was unable to inspect them. Such properties were not overly helpful as comparable. However, 2 of the comparables were of 4 bedroom houses on the Field View Estate. Although these 2 properties were not specifically identified and therefore incapable of inspection by the Committee, the Committee found them to be helpful comparables and ones which supported the Committee's own general knowledge of market rent levels in the area of Caversham.

 

5.3       The landlord's representative also submitted as comparables letting details of 4 properties on the market in the Caversham area. The Committee did not consider these details to be overly helpful as comparables. One was a 1 bedroom flat, another was a 2 bedroom terraced house. The other 2 were 3 bedroom houses in locations and with characteristics quite different to the subject premises.

 

5.4       Having considered all relevant information and having regard to its own knowledge and experience, the Committee concluded that such a likely market rent for the subject premises would be £975 per month.

 

5.5       However, the premises appeared not to be in the condition considered usual for a modern market letting at a market rent.  The Committee had regard to the disrepair and to the deficiencies in the condition of fittings  and furnishings that could be seen from the exterior, supported by the tenant's description of various defects which have not been contradicted by the landlord's agents. Therefore, it was necessary to adjust the hypothetical rent of £975 per month to allow for the differences between the condition considered usual for such a letting and the condition of the subject premises, as observed by the Committee, (but disregarding the effect of any disrepair or other defect attributable to the tenant or any predecessor in title). The Committee considered that this required a deduction of £ 150 per month.

 

5.6       The Committee determined that the market rent for the premises, so adjusted was £825 per month, inclusive of services, valued at £74.73 per month.

 

 

5.7       The Committee found that was substantial scarcity in the locality of  Berkshire and that there was strong demand for 4 bedroom properties. Therefore it made a further deduction of £ 125 (approx. 15%) from the market rent to reflect this element.

 

5.8        Calculations:

 

Open market rent                                             £975 pcm

Deduction for lack

of modern amenities and disrepair                     £150

Open market rent for the premises                    £825

Deduction for scarcity                                       £125

Fair rent to be registered                                   £700

 

6        The Decision

 

 

6.1       The uncapped fair rent determined by the Committee, pursuant to section 70 of the Act, was accordingly £700 per calendar month, inclusive of services.

 

6.2       The Committee considered the Rent Acts (Maximum Fair Rent) Order 1999 and the question whether the fair rent was to be capped in accordance with its provisions. The Committee decided that the fair rent was to be capped and made the required calculation. This showed a maximum capped rent of £703.50. As this was lower than the fair rent determined by the Committee, the rent was not to be capped at this figure.

 

6.3       Accordingly, the sum of £700 per calendar month, inclusive of services will be registered as the fair rent of the premises with effect from 7 October 2003 being the date of the Committee's decision.
