THE LAW APPLIED

 

In short, the task of the Committee is to determine a 'fair rent' within the meaning of section 70(1) of the Rent Act 1977, taking into account all relevant circumstances (other than personal circumstances). A fair rent is a market rent adjusted for scarcity and disregards under section 70(3) – in particular, tenants’ improvements. A discount will be appropriate under section 70(2) where there is a significant scarcity of similar dwellings available for letting in the locality. Rent capping may apply (see below). The rent will be exempt from capping if the landlord carries out repairs or improvements to the property as a result of which the uncapped fair rent determined for the property is at least 15% more than the existing registered rent. Full Reasons (if requested) will set out the relevant legal principles in detail.
