1.      Background

 

On 28 April 2003 the tenant of the above property referred to the Committee a notice of increase of rent served by the landlord under section 13 of the Housing Act 1988.

 

The landlord's notice, which proposed a rent of £160 per week with effect from 18 May 2003 is dated 15 April 2003.

 

The tenancy commenced on 6 June 1993 for a term of one year. The tenant remains in occupation as a statutory periodic tenant. The current rent is £80 per week.

 

The tenancy is a periodic tenancy which commenced on 6 June 1993. The current rent is £80 per week.

 

2.         Inspection

 

The Committee inspected the property on 10 June 2003 and found it to be in fair to poor condition.

 

The external walls were in fair condition and some windows/doors had been renewed, but several of the older windows were in poor condition, and guttering in places was badly dilapidated and tied up with string.

 

The following qualifying tenant's improvements had been made to the property:-

            Not applicable

 

3.      Evidence

 

The committee received written representations from the landlord and tenant and these were copied to the parties.

 

A hearing was held at Okehampton on 10 June 2003 in the White Hart Hotel at which oral representations were made by/on behalf of the landlord and/tenant.

 

4.      The law

 

In accordance with the terms of section 14 Housing Act 1988 the Committee proceeded to determine the rent at which it considered that the subject property might reasonably be expected to be let on the open market by a willing landlord under an assured tenancy.

 

In so doing the Committee, as required by section 14(1), ignored the effect on the rental value of the property of any relevant tenant's improvements as defined in section 14(2) of that Act.

 

In coming to its decision the Committee had regard to the evidence supplied by the parties/ and the members' own general knowledge of market rent levels in the area of Devon and concluded that an appropriate market rent for the property in good condition would be £500-550 per month.  The Committee considered that both comparable bungalows “The Bryhers” and “Brookfield” put forward by the landlords were better than the subject property.

 

5.         The decision

 

The Committee therefore concluded that the rent at which the property might reasonably be expected to be let on the open market would be £480 per month or £110 per week having made allowance for the external dilapidations, inefficient heating and hot water system, and general disrepair.  This rent will take effect from 18 May 2003 being the date specified by the landlord in the notice of increase.
