The Property:                                    

The subject property is a semi detached period house rendered to front and rear elevations with stone side elevation under a slate roof. The property comprises an entrance hall off which is the bathroom and living room of which is the kitchen and stairs rising to the first floor on the ground floor. On the first floor are two bedrooms and a ladder style access to two attic rooms. The property has mains water, electricity and drainage. Space heating is by an open fire in the living room and Tenant's appliances. An immersion heater provides hot water. There is a garden to the rear with an outhouse that has been refurbished by the Tenant. There is off road parking to the side of the property. The property is situated in a village with limited bus service.

 

Condition:

Externally the property is in fair condition although the windows need repair or replacement. Internally the property has no central heating, and the kitchen is un-modernised. There are few power sockets throughout the property. The bathroom is dated. There are no carpets, curtains or white goods provided. 

 

Law:

Attached to this Statement of Reasons is a resumé of the law applied by the Committee.

 

Open Market Rent

The Committee considered that the rent for the subject property in good condition with central heating, modern kitchen and bathroom, double-glazing and carpets, curtains and white goods let on an Assured Shorthold Tenancy would be £475.00 per calendar month. The Committee considered that a global deduction of £134.00 per calendar month should be made for the poor condition of the windows, lack of central heating, the un-modernised kitchen, dated bathroom and the lack of any carpets, curtains or white goods. It should be noted that this figure cannot be a simple arithmetical calculation and is not based specifically upon capital cost but is the Committee’s estimate of the amount by which the rent would have to be reduced to attract a tenant.

 

Scarcity

It was decided that there is substantial scarcity of  “... similar dwelling-houses in the locality...” in this case Northamptonshire, that are available for letting and a deduction would be made to reflect this of 10 %

 

Committee’s Calculations:

Open Market Rent:                                                                   £475.00 per calendar month

Less global deduction                                                               £134.00 per calendar month

                                                                                                £340.00 per calendar month

Less Scarcity 10%                                                                    £ 34.00 per calendar month

                                                                                                £ 306.00 per calendar month

£306.00 per calendar month equates to £70.60 per week

 

As this figure was is so close to that arrived at by the Rent Officer the Committee decided the rent should be assessed at £73.00 per week.

 

‘Uncapped’ fair rent £73.00 per week

 

The provisions of the Rent Acts (Maximum Fair Rent) Order 1999 require that the registered rent is either the capped Fair Rent or the Fair Rent decided by the Committee which ever is the lower. The capped Fair Rent is calculated in accordance with a statutory formula using the existing rent as a base. The capped rent in this case is £77.00 per week, which is higher than the Fair Rent assessed by the Committee and therefore the rent assessed by the Committee is to be registered.

 

FAIR RENT = £73.00 per week
