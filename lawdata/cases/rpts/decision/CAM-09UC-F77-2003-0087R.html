The Property:                                    

The property is a detached two-storey house constructed in 1795 rendered to all elevations under a tile roof. The house comprises two living rooms connected by an archway, a kitchen and a bath/shower room and w.c. on the ground floor. There are three bedrooms on the first floor however when let the property only had two bedrooms and the third was partitioned off from the landing area. Access to the second bedroom is via the first. The property has mains electricity, water and drainage. Space and water heating is by a combination oil-fired central heating system, the radiators were paid for by the Tenant. There are gardens to front and rear and hard standing for parking. The property is situated in a rural village about 3 miles from Biggleswade.

 

Condition:

The Committee inspected the property in the presence of the Tenant. Externally the property is in fair condition. Internally there is a modern kitchen and bathroom although the Tenant fitted these. Originally the kitchen only had a deep white sink and the bathroom was dated. The property is in good decorative order internally. No carpets, curtains and white goods are provided and the property is let unfurnished.

 

Law:

Attached to this Statement of Reasons is a resumé of the law applied by the Committee.

 

Open Market Rent

The Landlord’s Agent submitted a number of comparables of which 21 School Lane and 31 High Street, Southill both of which are 2 bedroom semi-detached properties let on an Assured Shorthold Tenancy for £650 and £625 per calendar month respectively appeared to be the most comparable. Neither property was let furnished nor were floor coverings or white good provided. Unfortunately few details were given about the properties and therefore the rents can only be accepted as a broad guide. The Committee valued the property as a two-bedroom house with access to the one bedroom being through the other. The Committee assessed the market rent for the subject property in good condition, with central heating, modern kitchen and bathroom but without floor coverings or white goods let on an Assured Shorthold Tenancy would be £140.00 per week. The Committee considered that a global deduction of £50.00 per week should be made in respect of the subject property for the lack of modernisation and condition and the Tenant's contribution to the installation of the heating system. It should be noted that this figure cannot be a simple arithmetical calculation and is not based specifically upon capital cost but is the Committee’s estimate of the amount by which the rent would have to be reduced to attract a tenant.

 

Scarcity

It was decided that there is substantial scarcity of  “... similar dwelling-houses in the locality...” in this case the districts of Bedford, mid-Bedfordshire, North Hertfordshire and adjacent parts of Huntingdonshire and South Cambridgeshire that are available for letting and a deduction would be made to reflect this of 15 %.

 

Committee’s Calculations:

Open Market Rent:                                                                               £140.00 per week

Less global deduction                                                                           £ 50.00 per week

                                                                                                            £ 90.00 per week

Less Scarcity 15%                                                                                £ 13.50 per week

                                                                                                            £76.50 per week

‘Uncapped’ fair rent say £76.50 per week

 

The provisions of the Rent Acts (Maximum Fair Rent) Order 1999 require that the registered rent is either the capped Fair Rent or the Fair Rent decided by the Committee which ever is the lower. The capped Fair Rent is calculated in accordance with a statutory formula using the existing rent as a base. The capped rent in this case is £61.00 per week, which is lower than the Fair Rent assessed by the Committee and therefore the capped rent is to be registered.

 

FAIR RENT = £61.00 per week
