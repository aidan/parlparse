1. Background

By an application dated 8th February 2003, the tenants of the above property referred 10 the Committee the rent of £675.00 per calendar month under an assured Shorthold tenancy agreement dated 11th September 2002.

2. Inspection

On 19th March 2003 the Committee inspected the property in the presence of the

Landlords, Mr Guy Broughton and Mrs Linda Spelman both from the letting agents, the tenant and Mr Martin Diamond from Carrick District Council.

The property previously formed 2 dwellings and is L shaped. One half fronts on to a steep one-way street giving access to the property, and the other onto a road giving access to the parking area and leading to a public car park.

The property consists of 3 reception rooms kitchen and WC on the ground floor, and 4 bedrooms and 2 bathrooms on the first floor with an attic access through a bathroom. The Committee was unable to carry out a detailed inspection of some of the rooms that were being used for storage purposes. However from our limited inspection we  noted damp penetration through some of the exterior walls, rising damp, dry rot and damp ingress in some places. The internal decoration was in an acceptable state.

 

 

 

 

However the exterior required decoration. The roof was in good order. There was partial central heating.

The Tenant pointed out some aspects of the electricity supply which were dangerous and also minor problems with the water supply. These are mentioned in his letter dated 12th March 2003 together with various other matters.

Outside was a small cobbled area together with a separate car parking area.

3. Evidence

The committee received written representations from the landlord and tenant and these were sent to the parties.

A hearing was held at the Coleman Room, Royal Duchy Hotel, Cliff Road, Falmouth on 19th March 2003 at which oral representations were made by and on behalf of the landlord. The tenant was not present or represented.

4. The law

In accordance with the terms of section 14 Housing Act 1988 the Committee proceeded to determine the rent at which it considered that the subject property might reasonably be expected to be let on the open market by a willing landlord under an assured tenancy.

In so doing the Committee, as required by section 14(1), ignored the effect on the rental value of the property of any relevant tenant's improvements as defined in section 14(2) of that Act.

In coming to its decision the Committee had regard to the evidence supplied on behalf of the Landlords and the members' own general knowledge of market rent levels in the area of Penryn and Falmouth and concluded that an appropriate market rent for the property if it was in a letting condition would be £770.00 per calendar month. However, the property lacked full central heating and repairs were needed to rectify the dry rot, rising damp, damp penetration, damp ingress and electrical defects. The

 

 

 

Committee estimated the cost of dealing with these matters would be in the region of £14,000.00. This sum equates to £117.00 per calendar month.

Taking this sum away from the market rent of £770.00 we arrive at a rent of £653.00 which we rounded down to £650.00 per calendar month.

5. The decision

The Committee therefore concluded that the rent at which the property might reasonably be expected to be let on the open market would be £650.00 per calendar month.

This rent will take effect from 19th March 2003 being the date of the Committees decision.
