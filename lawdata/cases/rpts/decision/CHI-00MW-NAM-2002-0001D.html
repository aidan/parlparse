Introduction

 

1.         This matter came before the Tribunal following an application made by Mr Smith (“the Applicant”) for the appointment of a Manager of Cliff House, Bonchurch Shute, Ventnor, Isle of Wight.  (“the Premises”).  The application was dated 6 February 2003 and was made pursuant to Section 24 of the Landlord and Tenant Act 19876 February  (“the Act”)  as amended by Section 85 of the Housing Act 1996.  In accordance with Section 22 of the Act a preliminary notice was served by the Applicant on the Respondent on 17 September 2002.  A first hearing took place on 25 March 2003, but as not all lessees had been notified of the proceedings, the matter was adjourned to 1 may 2003 to enable all interested parties to be notified of the application and the adjourned hearing date.

 

The law

 

2.         Section 24 (2) of the Act provides that the Tribunal may only make an Order in respect of such an application in the following circumstances namely:-

a)  where it is satisfied:-

            (i)         that the Landlord either is in breach of any obligation owed by him to the tenant under his tenancy and relating to the management of the premises in question or any part of them or (in the case of an obligation dependent on notice) would be in breach of any such obligation but for the fact that it has not been reasonably practicable for the tenant to give him the appropriate notice, and

            (ii)        that it is just and convenient for the order to be made in all the circumstances of the case

ab)       where the Tribunal is satisfied:-

            (i)         that unreasonable service charges have been made or are proposed or likely to be made and

(ii)        that it is just and convenient to make the order in all the circumstances of the case;

ac)       where the Tribunal is satisfied:-

            (i)         that the Landlord has failed to comply with any relevant provision of a code of practice approved by the Secretary of State under Section 87 of the Leasehold Reform Housing and Urban Development Act 1993 (Codes of Management Practice) and

            (ii)        that it is just and convenient to make the order in all the circumstances of the case; or

b)         where the Tribunal is satisfied that other circumstances exist which make it just and convenient for the order to be made.

 

Inspection

 

3.                  The Tribunal inspected the Premises on 25 March 2003 in the presence of the

Mr Baker (Flat 1), Mr and Mrs Moss (Flat 3), Mr Moss (Flat 2). Mr Smith (Flat 5), Mrs Murch (Flat4), Mr Calloway (Applicant’s solicitor) and Mr Chick  (BSC Management

 

Description and condition

 

4.         The Premises are a three storey detached building constructed approximately sixty years ago.  There are nine flats, three in each storey.  It is accessed by a drive way shared with a block of neighbouring flats.  Each flat has a designated car parking space.  The western flank wall has been recently rebuilt.  The outside paint work was in need of attention.  There is a vertical cliff very close to and behind the Premises.  There was evidence of instability in this cliff.

 

5.         The freehold of the Premises is owned by the Respondent who had carried out functions of a manager.  All nine flats are the subject of long term leases.  Flat 5 is occupied by the Applicant and the Respondent occupies Flat 6.

 

The Leases

 

6.         A copy of the lease relating to Flat 5 was produced to the Tribunal.  All the leases are in similar form.  It demised the flat for a term of 99 years from 25 December 1979 at a rent of £30 per annum, doubling every 33 years.  The Lease contains covenants, extracts of the relevant parts are-

 

By the Landlord:

 

“Clause 5(5) Subject to and conditional upon payment being made by the Lessee of the Interim Charge and the Service Charge……and at the time and in the manner hereinbefore provided:-

(a)             To maintain renew and keep in good and substantial repair and condition:

 

(i)         the main structure of the Building….and the foundations and the roof

(ii)        gas and water mains and pipes…. used in common with other lessees

                        (iii)       the common parts

                        (v)        boundary walls and fences

           

(b)        as and when the lessor shall deem necessary:

                        (i)         to paint the whole of the outside

 

(c)        To insure and keep insured the building….against loss or damage by fire…in the full value thereof.

 

By the Lessee

 

(1)        To pay the yearly rent and the Interim Charge and the Service Charge

 

Hearing

 

7.         Mr H Calloway solicitor of Messrs Glanvilles appeared on behalf of the Applicant.  He called as witnesses the Applicant and Mr C Chick, the proposed manager.  Mr Acutt a director of the Respondent company appeared at the adjourned hearing.  All the lessees appeared at the adjourned hearing, with the exception of Mrs Wilkinson of Flat 9 who was ill, but had informed the Applicant that she agreed with the appointment of Mr Chick as manager.  Mr and Mrs F Chown of Flat 3 appeared originally as objectors to the application.

 

The Applicant’s Case

 

8.         The landlord had not complied with its obligations under the terms of the lease as follows-

(a)        Accounts from the Landlord contained many inaccuracies,

(b)        Work was undertaken to the western wall of the Premises, but no accounts were provided in respect of this work

(c)        There was a failure to insure and to provide buildings insurance,

(d)        There was a failure to put into effect fire safety alarms despite a requirement to do so by the Isle of Wight Council.

 

9.         The Applicant proposed Mr Cameron Chick of BSC Management Services as manager of the Premises

 

The case of the Respondent.

 

10.       By a letter dated 16th September 2002, the Respondent notified the Residents’ Association that all further new and direct management functions by it would cease.  Also by a letter dated 22 April 2003, the Respondent confirmed that it endorses the appointment of BSC Management Services to manage the Premises to include:-

 

Estimates of annual running costs.

Invoicing lessees for service charges and ground rent

Setting up and/or administering a service charge trust account

Administering the terms and covenants of the lease

Ensuring the Landlord and Tenant Acts are complied with

Dealing with complaints and enquiries from the lessees

Regular inspection of the Premises

Organising maintenance and repairs

Settling of regular accounts for electricity, gardener, cleaner etc

Employing builders and contractors as required

Paying contractors, service and insurance

Preparing interim service charge accounts

Preparing the annual certificate for audit and an annual summary of costs

Liasing with the Chartered Accountant on production of annual accounts

Handling insurance matters and vet all possible claims

Dealing with solicitors’ enquiries and assignments of leases

Keeping proper files and records

Collecting arrears of service charges

Offering advice and assistance

Acting firmly and o impartially when dealing with defaulting lessees

Generally assisting to ensure the smooth running of the Premises for the benefit of the lessees and the Freeholder.

 

Conclusion

 

11.       All the lessees and the Landlord have agreed to the appointment of Mr Chick as manager.

 

12.       Applying the provisions of Section 24(2) of the Act the Tribunal is satisfied:

by virtue of subsection 2(a)

(i)                  That there have been breaches of obligations owed by the Lessor to the Lessees under their tenancies and relating to the management of the Premises as alleged by the Applicant.

(ii)        That it is just and convenient for the order to be made in all the circumstances of the case

and by virtue of subsection 2(ab)

(i)                  That the Lessor has failed to comply with the provisions of a code of practice approved by the Secretary of State under Section 87 of the Leasehold Reform Housing and Urban Development Act 1993 (Codes of Management Practice) namely the Service Charge Residential Management Code issued by the Royal Institution of Chartered Surveyors in that the following provisions have not been complied with

a)                  Part 11  (Accounting for Service Charges)

b)                  Part 14  (Repairs)

 

(ii)        That it is just and convenient  for the order to be made in all the circumstances of the case

 

13.       The Tribunal next considered the suitability of Mr Cameron Chick of BSC Management for the appointment as receiver and manager.  He was a property manager connected with a company that had the relevant experience of leasehold flat management in the Isle of Wight.

 

14.       The Tribunal concluded that Mr Chick would be a suitable manager and receiver for the Premises and made an Order as attached.

Upon hearing the Applicant and his representative and a director of the Respondent:

 

IT IS ORDERED pursuant to Section 24 of the Landlord and TenantAct 1987, that:

 

1.           Mr. Cameron Chick (“the Manager”) of BSC Management Services Limited of 4 The Courtyard Calbourne Isle of Wight PO30 4HU is hereby appointed manager and receiver of Cliff House, Bonchurch Shute, Ventnor, Isle of Wight  (“the Property”) with effect from 1st May 2003 (“the Relevant Date”), for an initial period of two years from that date.

 

2.                   The manager is hereby authorised to carry out the following functions:

(a)        Prepare an interim budget for the period from the Relevant Date to 24 December 2003 a copy of which shall be delivered to the Respondent and the Lessees of the Property and to receive and recover all rents and other payments due by the Lessees for the time being in respect of the Property in accordance with the terms of the Leases entered into by the Lessees of the Property (“the Leases”), which without prejudice to the generality of the foregoing, shall include the right to receive such monies as shall be required to comply with the Service Charge provisions of the Leases.

(b)               To recover any arrears of any such sums as aforesaid.

(c)                On or before 30th April 2004 and on 30th April and in each subsequent year during which he remains appointed to account to the landlord of the Property for the time being for any rents and other payments received, any interest accrued thereon and any other monies lawfully due to the said landlord in respect of the Property;

(d)        To calculate and recover any service charges payable under the Leases and to administer any reserve account and other bank account required or agreed by the Respondent and the lessees in connection with the management of the Property and to create and maintain a sole mandate in respect of the operation of all such bank accounts;

(e)        To carry out the obligations of the landlord contained in the Leases and in particular and without prejudice to the generality of the foregoing those relating:-

(i)    To the provision of services to the Property,

(ii)    To the repair of the Property, and

(iii) To the insurance of the Property

(f)         To consider, grant refuse or otherwise deal with all applications for consents in relation to dealings, alterations or any other matters requiring the consent of the landlord under the Leases to which the Property is subject, provided that he shall not consent to or register the assignment of any lease under which any rent or service charge properly payable in respect of any period to the relevant date remains unpaid.

 

3.         The said manager shall within twenty-one days of any request by the landlord provide the landlord with such information relating to the management of the Property as the landlord shall have reasonably requested.

 

4.         The Respondent shall prepare or arrange that its agents shall prepare by 1st June 2003 an account of any monies received by it on behalf of the lessees of the Property.  The account shall show all expenditure since the date of the last annual account and the amount (if any) remaining unexpended remaining in its hand.  It shall deliver copies of the account, or arrange for them to be delivered to each of the lessees of the Property and to the Manager by that date.  It shall also arrange for a cheque for any unexpended amount in its hands or in the hands of its agents as shown by that account to be paid to the Manager by 1st June 2003.  The Manager shall then place such sum in a separate designated account to be applied for the purposes of the management of the Property.

 

5.         The Respondent shall arrange for any documents relating to the continuing management of the Property to be delivered to the Manager by 1st June 2003.

 

6.         Within twenty-eight days of the relevant date the manager will enter into a written contract of employment with the lessees incorporating the following terms.

(i)         The manager shall be paid a fee of £125.00 (plus Value Added Tax) per flat per annum.  Included within that fee will be the following services:-

(a)   Collecting ground rents and service charges from Lessees.

(b)   Preparation and submission of rent statements at half yearly intervals.

(c)   Instructing and assisting Solicitors to recover unpaid charges

(d)   The payment for general maintenance out of funds provided

(e)   The production of annual spending estimates to calculate service charges

                        (f)     To administer funds and provide information to auditors.

(g)    To produce and circulate service charge accounts and provide information to the Lessees and any residents associations

(h)   To administer building and other insurances

(i)    To engage and supervise staff as necessary

(j)    To arrange periodic Health and Safety checks

(k)   To inspect the property on a regular basis to deal with repairs to the Property and its plant fixtures and fittings

(l)         To deal with enquiries from Lessees

(m)      To keep records of tenancies

(n)   To keep the  lessees and the Respondent informed of any changes in legal requirements

(o)   To advise on a day to day management policy

(ii)        In addition to the above the manager shall be paid further remuneration at a rate not exceeding 10% of the cost of any major building and/or maintenance work (including redecoration works) which exceed £1,000.00. excluding VAT.

(iii)               Value Added Tax shall be payable in addition to the fees and remuneration mentioned in the preceding paragraphs. 

(iv)              For the avoidance of doubt all the Manager’s fees and remuneration (and VAT) shall be recoverable as part of the service charges provided for in the Leases.

(v)        Subject to the foregoing the management of the Property shall be undertaken in accordance with the Service Charge Residential Management Code.

 

7.         Any of the Applicant the Respondent and the Manager shall each be at liberty to apply to the Leasehold Valuation Tribunal for further directions.
