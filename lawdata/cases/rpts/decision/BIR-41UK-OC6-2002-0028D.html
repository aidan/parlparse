DECISION OF THE MIDLAND LEASEHOLD VALUATION TRIBUNAL

 

APPLICATION

 

This is  a reference to determine the price to be paid by the Tenants for the freehold interest in the property known as 4 Claremont Road Coton Green Tamworth Staffordshire in accordance with the provisions of the Leasehold Reform Act 1967 as amended.  The Tenants hold the property under a Lease dated 12th  March 1964 for the unexpired residue of the term of 99 years from 24th  June 1963 at a yearly ground rent of £17.50.  The Tenants Notice of Claim to acquire the freehold interest was dated 17th May 2002 when some 60 years of the term remained unexpired.  The Parties accept that the qualifying conditions for enfranchisement under the Act have been met.  There is also an application regarding costs.

 

INSPECTION

 

Prior to the Hearing the Tribunal called to see the property but in the absence of anyone to admit them had to confine their viewing to an external inspection.  The house is constructed of brick and tile and is sited on a road of similar style properties on the northern outskirts of Tamworth but not far from the Town Centre.  We are given to understand that the accommodation comprises an almost open plan ground floor living area and kitchen with three bedrooms and a combined bathroom and WC on the upper floor.  The property is semi detached with a garage/utility room at the side and front and rear gardens.  One member of the Committee had inspected other similar properties in the vicinity on previous occasions within the past year.  The site of the subject property has a road frontage of some 8.08 metres and an area of approximately 373 square metres.

 

THE HEARING

 

This was attended by Mr J. Moore of Midland Valuation Limited on behalf of the Tenants.  The Landlord was not represented and had filed no representations.  Mr Moore in presenting his case (a copy of which he tabled) submitted his valuation as follows :

 

Term

Annual Ground Rent                             £17.50

YP 60 years @7%                                 14.039                       £245.68          

 

Reversion

Entirety Value                                       £102,500

Site Value @ 33%                                £ 33,825

Section 15 Rent @7%                          £2367.75

YP deferred 60 years @7%                         0.246       £582.47

 

                                                                                    £828.15

 

                                                            say                   £828.00

 

He adopted a 7% yield since this appeared to be the rate consistently adopted by this Tribunal and the lands Tribunal.  He cited in support of his entirety value recent previous decisions of the tribunal concerning similar houses in the vicinity  No. 49 Claremont Road (£105,000) and No. 5 Clifton Avenue (£100,000) made only three months or so prior to the valuation date now under consideration.  He stressed the proximity of the subject property to the road junction and the local primary school but accepted that prices had hardened during the year.  No. 25 Claremont Road was at present on offer at £112,950 but remained unsold for two months suggesting over pricing.

 

He had adopted a 33% site apportionment to standing house value in reliance on a number of previous Tribunal decisions concerning nearby properties.

 

 

FINDING

 

In the absence of any evidence to the contrary the Committee sees no reason to disagree with Mr Moores valuation.

 

COSTS

 

The Tenants had also applied to the Tribunal to determine the amount of the Landlords costs for which they would be responsible.  Mr Moore proposed a figure of £225 for legal costs and no valuation fee (in the absence of any evidence that a valuation had been made prior to the application).

 

FINDINGS

 

It is the view of the Tribunal that legal costs should at the present time be set in cases such as this at £250.  No valuation fee is believed to be payable.

 

DECISION

 

Accordingly, we determine the price to be paid by the Tenants for the freehold interest in the subject property at £828 plus the Landlords reasonable costs calculated in accordance with Section 9(4) Leasehold Reform Act 1967 and Schedule 22 Rule 1 (5) Housing Act 1980 Of £250 (plus VAT if applicable) and any Land registry fee for office copies.
