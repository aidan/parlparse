1.      Background

 

On 30 June 2003 the tenant of the above property referred to the Committee a notice of increase of rent served by the landlord under section 13 of the Housing Act 1988.

 

The landlord's notice, which proposed a rent of £650.00 per month with effect from 15 July 2003 is dated 16 May 2003.

 

The tenancy commenced on 12 September 2000. The tenant remains in occupation as a statutory periodic tenant. The current rent is £490.00 per month.

 

2.      Inspection

 

The Committee inspected the property on 20.08.03. and found it to be in fair condition.

There were signs of damp penetration on the ceiling of the landing and bathroom on the first floor.  The exterior decorations need attention as did some of the aluminium windows.  The landlord had installed a new gas boiler for the central heating.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

The following qualifying tenant's improvements had been made to the property.

The tenant had installed a new kitchen and had supplied his own carpets, curtains and white goods at his own expense.

 

 

 

3.      Evidence

 

The committee received written representation from the landlord/tenant and these were copied to the parties.

A hearing was held at Crawley on 20.08.03. in the Leisure Centre at which oral representations were made by/on behalf of  the landlord and tenant.

 

4.  The law

 

In accordance with the terms of section 14 Housing Act 1988 the Committee proceeded to determine the rent at which it considered that the subject property might reasonably be expected to be let on the open market by a willing landlord under an assured tenancy.

 

In so doing the Committee, as required by section 14(1), ignored the effect on the rental value of the property of any relevant tenant's improvements as defined in section 14(2) of that Act.

 

In coming to its decision the Committee had regard to the evidence supplied by the parties and the members' own general knowledge of market rent levels in the area of West Sussex and concluded that an appropriate market rent for the property in a good standard would be £650.00 per month.

In particular the committee had regard to the evidence supplied by the landlord of a  recent letting of a similar property in the same area at a rent of £650.00 per month.  The tenant had carried out some improvements at his own expense and some repairs were needed to bring the property up to a good standard for full open market letting.

 

5.         The decision

 

The Committee therefore concluded that the rent at which the property might reasonably be expected to be let on the open market would be £530.00 per month.

 

This rent will take effect from 20 August 2003 the committee being satisfied that undue hardship would otherwise be caused to the tenant.
