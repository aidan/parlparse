A.  THE PROPERTY                                   :           Semi detached house with garden

                                                                                    built around 1925 comprising five rooms, kitchen, bathroom and W.C.

 

B.  THE CONDITION                                  :           Fair condition save that new UPVC

                                                                                    Windows and patio door to the rear had been installed.  The front door and surrounding window had not been replaced.  The kitchen door to the garden had been blocked off.  Evidence of movement. The wiring is dated and there is only one socket on the first floor and none in the kitchen.

 

 

C.  TENANT’S IMPROVEMENTS                        :           None  

 

D.  LOCATION                                             :           East Hertfordshire,

Hertford/Welwyn Garden City,

                                                                                    Broxbourne and Western Essex

 

E.  SCARCITY                                              :           10%

 

F.  THE LAW APPLIED                              :           Attached

 

G.  OPEN MARKET RENT                        :           £675.00

 

H.  COMMITTEE’S  CALCULATIONS     :           Open Market Rent £675.00 less

                                                                                    deduction for lack of heating, white goods, curtains and carpets and dated/unmodernised kitchen/bathroom, dated wiring and cracking to walls and ceilings @ 37.5% say £253.00 = £422.00

                                                                                    less scarcity @ 10% = £379.80 … say £380.00

 

I.  THE RENT ACTS (MAXIMUM FAIR

                        RENT ORDER) 1999          :           Not Applicable

 

 

J.  DECISION                                               :           Our finding is that the installation of

                                                                                    Double glazed windows resulting in the loss of the door from the kitchen to the garden does not result in an increase in rental value attributable to these improvements of 15% or more.  Indeed it appears that the Landlords representative accepts that position for the increase they sought from the previously requested rent of £114.23 per week on 20 December 2002 (£495.00 per month) to £537.80 per month is not an increase of 15% or more.  Their outdated submission which contains no reference to the Rent Acts (Maximum Fair Rent Order) 1999 and lack of comparable evidence was unhelpful to us in reaching our determination. 

                                                                                    We find that the fair rent for the subject premises is £380.00 per month effective from 14 October 2003.
