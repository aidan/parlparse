1.      Background

On 26th November 2003 the tenant of the above property referred to the Committee a notice of increase of rent served by the Landlord under Section 13 of the Housing Act 1988.

The Landlords notice, which proposed a rent of £563.33 per calendar month with effect from 24th November 2003 is dated 29th September 2003.

The joint tenancy began on 24th November 1993.

 

 

2.      Inspection

In the presence of the tenants, the Committee inspected the property on 26th November 2003 and found it to be in good condition. The property is in a quiet residential area close to the local junior school and surrounded on three sides by a larger than average garden. The property has 4 bedrooms, a bathroom, kitchen and a lounge/dining room. There is a garage attached to the house. The tenant has built a greenhouse outside and internally re-fitted the fire mantle and surround, a new kitchen (9 years ago) a new kitchen floor (3 years ago). The tenants painted the exterior of the windows in a deal with the landlords. Some of this painting needs re-doing. Two  double glazed units need replacing.

 

3.      Evidence

The committee received written representations  from both parties and these were copied to the other party.

A  hearing had been requested. It was attended by the tenants and their representative Ms Marsh. Ms Marsh produced evidence of properties let by the Guinness Trust in the same locality.  However, she indicated that they were not in the exact same locality nor were they comparables. She drew the attention of the Committee to the fact that the tenants have an assured tenancy which she pointed out has greater security than the usual AST.

 

4.         The Law

In accordance with the terms of Section 14 of the Housing Act 1988 the Committee proceeded to determine the rent at which it considered that the subject property might reasonably be expected to be let on the open market by a willing landlord under an assured tenancy.

In so doing the Committee as required by Section 14(1), ignored the effect on the rental value of any relevant tenants improvements as defined by section 14 (2) of that Act.

In coming to its decisions the Committee had regard to the evidence supplied by the parties and the members own general knowledge of market rent levels in the area of Plymouth and Devon and concluded that an appropriate market rent for the property would be £563.33 per month.

 

5.      Decision

The Committee decided that the appropriate market rent for the property was £563.33 per month.

This rent will take effect from 24th November 2003 being the date specified by the Landlord in the notice of increase.
