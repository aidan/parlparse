4.                  The Law

 

4.1              When determining a fair rent the Committee, in accordance with section 70 of the Act, had regard to all of the circumstances including the age, location and state of repair of the premises. It also disregarded the effect of:-

a)                  any relevant tenant's improvements, and

b)                  the effect of any disrepair or other defect attributable to the tenant or any predecessor in title under the regulated tenancy

on the rental value of the premises.

 

4.2              In  Spathe Holme Limited v Chairman of the Greater Manchester etc Committee (1995) 28 HLR 107 and Curtis v London Rent Assessment Committee (1999) QB 92, the Court of Appeal emphasised:-

a)                  that ordinarily a fair rent is the market rent for the premises discounted for "scarcity" (i.e. that element, if any, of the market rent, that is attributable to there being a significant shortage of similar premises in the wider locality available for letting on similar terms - other than as to rent to that of the regulated tenancy in question, and

b)                  that for the purpose of determining the market rent, assured tenancy (market) rents are usually appropriate comparables. These rents may have to be adjusted where necessary to reflect any relevant differences between those comparables and the subject premises.

 

 

5.                  Valuation

 

5.1              In the first instance the Committee determined what rent the landlord could reasonably be expected to achieve for the premises in the open market if the premises were let today in the condition that is considered usual for such an open market letting.

 

It did this by having regard to the information submitted by the parties.

Neither party submitted details of any comparables. The Committee relied on its own general knowledge of market rent levels in the area of Slough.

 

Having considered all relevant information and having regard to its own knowledge and experience, the Committee concluded that such a likely market rent for the subject premises would be £675 per calendar month.

 

5.2              However, it is assumed that the premises are not in the condition considered usual for a modern market letting at a market rent.  The Committee assumed that the property was let without carpets and curtains, white goods and that the property does not have a central heating system. Therefore it was necessary to adjust the hypothetical rent of £675 per calendar month to allow for the differences between the condition considered usual for such a letting and the condition of the subject premises as assumed by the Committee, (but disregarding the effect of any disrepair or other defect attributable to the tenant or any predecessor in title). The Committee considered that this required a deduction of £169 per calendar month.

 

5.3              The Committee determined that the market rent for the premises, so adjusted was £506 per calendar month.

 

5.4              The Committee found that was substantial scarcity in the locality of Berkshire and that there was strong demand for two and three bedroom houses.  Therefore it made a further deduction of £76 (approx 15%) from the market rent to reflect this element.

 

5.5              In this matter we were obliged to calculate a 50% share of the market rent of the property to reflect the shared ownership element.

The Decision

 

 

6.1               The uncapped fair rent determined by the Committee, pursuant to section 70 of the Act, was accordingly £430 per calendar month, which equates to £5,160 per annum. A 50% share of this rent is £2,680 per annum, to which the annual sum of £67.56 for service charges should be added, giving a gross fair rent of £2,647.56 per annum.

 

 

6.2              However, the fair rent of £2,647.56 per annum is capped by the Rents Acts (Maximum Fair Rent) Order 1999 and the amount to be registered as the fair rent is limited to £2,094.06 per annum. Details are provided with this decision form. This rent will be registered with effect from 16th December 2003 being the date of the Committee's decision.
