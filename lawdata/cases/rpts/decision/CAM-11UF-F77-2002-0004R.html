1.   This was an objection by the Landlord to a determination by the Rent Officer of £540 per month, registered on and effective from 2 October 2002. Both parties sent written representations which were considered at a meeting.

 

2.  The Committee inspected the property, a self contained, purpose built,  first floor flat, built in the 1960s, on a large estate, near some local shops.  The external decorations needed attention.  There was access at the front, and also at the rear by a service road and shared open steps.  There was partial heating by ducted warm air to the living room and kitchen.  The living room was L-shaped, and had secondary glazing.  The two bedrooms were double size;  the bathroom was dated.  Likewise the kitchen was dated, and the white goods belonged to the Tenant.  A garage was included in the letting, and a small area of garden is shared.

 

3.  The Tenant holds over under a letting agreement dated 20 February 1986, which expired on 21 October 1987, and he is accordingly a regulated tenant. The tenancy is subject to the provisions of Section 11 of the Landlord & Tenant Act 1985.

 

4.  The Landlord stated that the property was centrally heated.  He said the Tenant was under an obligation to keep in repair so any disrepair should be disregarded.  He said an identical ground floor flat nearby was available to rent at £775 per month.  He had obtained this from a Web site  .  He considered that a first floor flat would let for more.  There was no trace of the original inventory for this letting, but at the time of the letting it had been fully furnished.

 

5.  The Tenant said the heating was by an antiquated hot air blow vent system and did not extend to the bedrooms or the bathroom.  He said he had replaced some items of furniture and the white goods with the Landlord’s consent, and had redecorated the main living areas.  His own enquiries from a local estate agent indicated that a fully modernised flat would let for £600 per month, not £775.  He did not believe any errors had been made by the Rent Officer.  The kitchen and bathroom were suffering from wear and tear.

 

6. The Committee considered the evidence very carefully.  Section 70 of the Rent Act 1977 states that in order to determine a Fair Rent, regard shall be had to all the circumstances (other than personal circumstances ) and in particular to the age, character, locality and state of repair of the dwelling.  We are aware that we are to make the market rent for such dwelling our starting point, from which we must make an adjustment for any substantial scarcity in accordance with Section 70(2) of the Rent Act.

 

7.  In the absence of any actual market evidence from the parties, the Committee used its extensive knowledge and experience of rentals in the area. The furniture was minimal, but in the Committee’s view, the presence of furniture does not have an significant bearing on the rental value of the property.  This flat, suitably improved to a modern Assured Shorthold standard, could in our view if upgraded in this way command a market rental of £700 per month.  However we deducted from this figure a total of 25% to reflect the lack of proper central heating, the dated  kitchen and bathroom, for carpets, curtains and white goods which would normally be included in a modern market letting, and the difference in terms  between an Assured Shorthold letting and the regulated tenancy which we have here:  arriving at an adjusted market rent of £525 per month.

 

8.  Neither of the parties presented any evidence as to scarcity.  However, relying on our own knowledge and experience we are of the opinion that there is  substantial scarcity of similar properties in Buckinghamshire and have determined this at approximately 15%.  Accordingly the figure arrived at for the Fair Rent was £450 per month.

 

9. In view of the  Rent Acts (Maximum Fair Rent) Order (SI 1999 No 6), Fair Rents have normally to be capped in accordance with the formula laid down.  However this does not apply in this case, being a first application since the Order came into force.

 

10.  The Fair Rent is therefore £450 per month, exclusive of rates and Council Tax.
