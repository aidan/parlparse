DECISION

A       BACKGROUND:

1.       This application is made to us under s60 of the Leasehold Reform Housing and Urban Development Act 1993 (“the Act”).  The application is dated the 1st December 2003 and follows on from an earlier application made by Mr David Charles Fernley (“the Applicant”) for a lease extension under s42 of the Act.  An earlier Tribunal had dealt with the leasehold extension application when a figure of £3,450.00 was determined in respect of the extended lease. 

 

2.       Thereafter the Respondents, Daejan Properties Limited through their solicitors Messrs Wallace and Partners, sought to recover costs, as they are entitled to do, pursuant to s60 of the Act.  The Respondents claimed costs of £1,075.00 plus VAT for the legal fees and a figure of £500.00 plus VAT for the Surveyors fees.  The Applicant contends that the reasonable legal costs for the freeholder are £400.00 plus VAT and the reasonable costs on behalf of the surveyor would be £350.00 plus VAT.

 

B       THE LAW:

3.       The relevant section of the Act reads as follows:

(1)     Where a notice is given under section 42, then (subject to the provisions of this section) the tenant by whom it is given shall be liable, to the extent that they have been incurred by any relevant person in pursuance of the notice, for the reasonable costs of and incidental to any of the following matters, namely

(a)      any investigation reasonably undertaken of the tenant’s right to a new lease;

(b)     any valuation of the tenant’s flat obtained for the purpose of fixing the premium or any other amount payable by virtue of Schedule 13 in connection with the grant of a new lease under section 56;

(c)      the grant of a new lease under that section

          but this subsection shall not apply to any costs if on a sale made voluntarily a stipulation that they were to be borne by the purchaser would be void.

(2)     For the purposes of subsection (1) any costs incurred by a relevant person in respect of professional services rendered by any person shall only be regarded as reasonable if and to the extent that costs in respect of such services might reasonably be expected to have been incurred by him if the circumstances had been such that he was personally liable for all such costs.

 

(5)     A tenant shall not be liable under this section for any costs which a party to any proceedings under this Chapter before a leasehold valuation tribunal incurs in connection with the proceedings.

 

C       EVIDENCE:

4.       At the hearing on the 23 February 2004 the Applicant attended in person and Miss S Bone, the fee earner having the conduct of the matter from Messrs Wallace and Partners represented the Respondent.

 

5.       The relevant documents were to be found at a Costs Schedule which had been prepared by the Respondents solicitors, a document headed “Particular Comments on Freeholders Claim for Costs “ which had been prepared by the Applicant and a document in Schedule form entitled Replies to Tenant’s Point of Dispute which took up the numbering that had been adopted by the Applicant in his comment document.  For ease of reference we have attached to this Decision as a form of Schedule the replies to the Tenant’s points of dispute and we adopt the numbering thereon for dealing with the evidential matters and in the final Decision that we reach.  We should however point out that one element from the replies to the Tenant’s point of dispute has been omitted which was the consideration of the Counter Notice conducted by a partner in Wallace and Partners for which a fee of £60.00 had been claimed.

 

6.       Mr Fernley relied on his particulars with regard to the individual items and expanded upon same in submissions to us.  His general point was that he felt as he had not accepted the original offer from the Respondents of £15,000.00 for the Lease extension he was being asked to pay extra legal fees.  He pointed out that the Counter Notice that had been served by the Respondents put forward a figure of £10,250.00 against his initial offer of £4,250.00.  In fact the Tribunal assessed the appropriate price for the Lease extension at £3,450.00.  He submitted that the case was straight forward, of little value and that the costs should be proportionate.  He referred to two other Leasehold Valuation Tribunal Decisions where the costs relating to the preparation and service of a Counter Notice in those cases had been disallowed.  So far as the preparation of the draft Lease was concerned his submission was that Wallace and Partners acted for Daejan on a regular basis, that the Lease was basic and would have been on the word processor and that the costs of preparing same would therefore be reduced.  He also asked us to give a final figure for costs reflecting future work to be done on finalising the terms of the Lease.  Apparently he had not yet instructed solicitors to approve those terms although he himself felt those terms were satisfactory.

 

7.       On the question of the Valuers fees he told us that originally Mr Shapiro, who he understood was to be the Valuer had quoted an hourly rate of £300.00.  In fact it was not Mr Shapiro but his associate who carried out the inspection and he also reminded us that the valuation figures had fluctuated considerably.  His view was that a figure of £350.00 plus VAT was reasonable and in support of that he relied upon a letter he had received from a local firm of Chartered Surveyors, Buntings, where a figure of £350.00 plus VAT had been quoted.

 

8.       On behalf of the Respondents Miss Bone confirmed that the charging rates uses were those payable by the Respondents and that they were reasonable.  Detailed responses had been made by the Respondents in the replies to the Tenant’s points of dispute and she adopted those.  So far as future costs are concerned she submitted that those were not a matter that the Tribunal could deal with as the section provided for consideration of costs “to the extent that they have been incurred”  accordingly the future costs for finalising the Lease were not something we could deal with at this stage.  She did however indicate that little more she thought needed to be done.

 

9.       Her submission with regard to the costs of the Counter Notice were that these were allowable and formed part of the provision of s60(1)(a) namely the investigation into the Tenant’s right to a new Lease.  She also submitted that a £250.00 per hour charge for the assistant Valuer was appropriate.  Moss Kaye Pemberton apparently do a lot of work for Daejan and the Freshwater Group and those level of fees are regularly paid and as such therefore were reasonably incurred and were of a reasonable level.

 

10.     She told us that she does 90% Leasehold enfranchisement work  and qualified in 1998.  She believed the charging rates were reasonable and that in fact they were not challenged by Mr Fernely.

 

D       DECISION:

11.     For ease of understanding, we will go through the replies to the Tenants points of dispute and by reference to those will indicate those matters, which we are prepared to allow, and those that we do not.  Having heard submissions from both parties, we allow in full the items claimed at numbers 1,4,5,7,8,17 &18 and insofar as item 6 is concerned, £18.00 is allowed.  The costs for considering the Counter Notice by the Partner, although not referred to in Mr Fernley’s particular comments or in the Tenants points of dispute, for reasons that we will refer to later, are not allowed.

 

12.     We find that it is reasonable for an hour to be spent in considering the original Notice.  This is an important document and time must be taken.  The correspondence to the Managing Agent, the Valuer and to Mr Fernley following on from the service of the Notice all seemed to us to deal with the question of determining his right and should be allowed.  Items 7 & 8, the consideration of the Office Copies and a further letter to Mr Fernley were conceded by him as being due and owing as was the final letter at number 18.  So far as the preparation of the Lease is concerned we find that an hour is a very reasonable time spent on considering the existing Lease and preparing the draft Lease arising from the Lease extension. 

 

13.     We would also say that although no challenge was made by Mr Fernley to the hourly rates of Messrs Wallace and Partners we find them reasonable in any event having regard to the expertise available and the time generally that was taken to deal with a matter of this nature.  Accordingly a rate of £180.00 per hour for Miss Bone and £275.00 for the partner seem to us to be reasonable and allowable.

 

14.     Insofar as the Valuer’s fee of £500.00 plus VAT is concerned, we think this is reasonable.  If the hourly rates of £250.00 for the assistant at Moss Kaye Pembertons was reasonable then two hours spent on the matter seems to us to be wholly proportionate.  In fact the handwritten addition to the fee note from Moss Kaye Pembertons which originally claimed £881.75 shows that some three hours was spent which means the hourly rate is less.  We find that the fee of £500.00 plus VAT is in the circumstances reasonable.

 

15.     We now turn to those items which we find are not payable by the Applicant, Mr Fernley, in the matter.  Utilising the numbering as we have before those are items 2,3,9,10,11,12,13,14,15, & 16.  In addition the consideration of the Counter Notice by a Partner for which a fee of £60.00 had originally been claimed.

 

16.     Our reasoning behind the findings are as follows. 

(a)      We do not believe that the ordering of Office Copies is a matter that should be dealt with by a fee earner.  It merely requires a telephone call from a secretary to the Land Registry to order the required document.  Miss Bone said this was a matter that should be dealt with by a fee earner to ensure the correct items were obtained.  That seems to fly in the face of the claim made for ordering further Office Copy Entries, which we were told, was because there had been an original error on the request made previously.

(b)     We do not think the letter to the client dealing with receipt of instructions is a matter that has anything to do with the investigation into the Applicant’s right to enfranchise.

(c)      The letter to the Valuer in which the copy of a Lease and a Questionnaire was sent should have been dealt with in the initial letter of instruction which we have allowed.  We therefore think this second letter is not covered by s60 of the Act.

(d)     The ordering of the additional Office Copies were conceded as not being recoverable. 

(c)      We do not think it is part of s60 (1a) or(b) for the legal representatives to be considering the valuation prepared by a well known and experienced Valuer for what is essentially a relatively straightforward enfranchisement.  Accordingly we disallow the charge made for considering the valuation.

(f)      Likewise we do not find that there is any provision in s60 to cover the cost of a letter to the client, sending a copy of the valuation and seeking instructions on the Counter Notice.

 

17.     We now turn to the work that has been claimed in respect of the Counter Notice.  We find that the provision of the Counter Notice is not covered by s60 of the Act.  It does not relate to the investigation of the Tenant’s right to a new Lease; it does not deal with the valuation of the flat nor has it anything to do with the grant of the new Lease.  It is merely the response by the Landlord to the Notice and is not, in our finding, recoverable under this section.  For that reason therefore both the preparing and the issuing of the Counter Notice, letters serving it and correspondence arising there from are disallowed.

 

18.     We calculate therefore that the allowable sum in respect of solicitors costs is £583.00 plus VAT.  It appears that no claim has been made in respect of the provision for Office Copies for which ordinarily a fee of £8.00 would be charged by the Land Registry.  If such an expense has been incurred it seems to us that it should be added to the costs claimed by the solicitors in this matter.  The Valuers fee is £500.00 plus VAT which make a total claim of £1,083.00 plus VAT and the Office Copy disbursement of £8.00, if incurred, and we make an award accordingly under s60 of the Act.
