6.         THE LAW

 

6.1       In short, the task of the Committee is to determine a 'fair rent' within the meaning of section 70(1) of the Rent Act 1977, taking into account all relevant circumstances (other than personal circumstances). A fair rent is a market rent adjusted for scarcity and disregards under section 70(3) – in particular, tenants’ improvements. A discount will be appropriate under section 70(2) where there is a significant scarcity of similar dwellings available for letting in the locality. Rent capping may apply (see below). The rent will be exempt from capping if the landlord carries out repairs or improvements to the property as a result of which the uncapped fair rent determined for the property is at least 15% more than the existing registered rent. Full Reasons (if requested) will set out the relevant legal principles in detail.

7.         THE DECISION

 

7.1       Summary of findings and conclusions:-

 

7.1.1        The parties offer no evidence of rental value.

7.1.2        The Committee's own knowledge and experience suggests that this type of property in a reasonable location in the Ely area, if let on assured shorthold terms, fully modernised, centrally heated, in good condition throughout, with curtains, carpets and white goods, would attract an open market rent of £600 per calendar month. This is very close to the figure adopted by the Rent Officer and is broadly consistent with the Rent Officer Service Market Evidence Report with our papers.

7.1.3        By their improvements the tenants have made this property fairly attractive. If the tenants’ improvements were disregarded, this would be a very unattractive property in a poor location. Substantial deductions are called for. The Committee deducts £50 for minimal kitchen and dated bathroom; £35 for lack of central heating; £55 for lack of carpets, curtains and white goods; £125 for disrepair (principally damp) and £75 for location. Total deductions amount to £340 p.c.m. Thus the adjusted open market rent is £260 p.c.m.

 

7.2       Tenant's improvements (to be disregarded) have been stripped out and no separate deduction is appropriate.

 

7.3              The locality considered for the assessment of scarcity is Fenland and East Cambridgeshire.

 

7.4              Scarcity: Substantial – deduct 15%. Uncapped “fair rent” is £221 p.c.m. or £51.00 per week.

 

7.5              Capping applies. See capping calculation attached.

Exempt:            No

Maximum fair rent is £25.00 per week.
