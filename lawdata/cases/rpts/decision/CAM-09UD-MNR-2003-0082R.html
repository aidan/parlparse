The Property:

The Property is a two-bedroom ground floor flat in a substantial three storey late Victorian building converted to 8 flats and constructed of brick under a tile roof. There is a door entry system to the building, which gives access to a common hallway off which is the Property. The Property comprises a hall with storage cupboard and a living room off, which is a kitchen. Off the hall are a bathroom and two bedrooms. Outside there is a driveway and parking. The property has mains electricity, gas, water and drainage. Space and water heating are by a gas central heating system. The property is situated in a residential area of Bedford on the outskirts of the town centre.

 

The Condition

The Committee inspected the property in the presence of Mrs Sharp, one of the Landlords, and the Tenant. Externally the property was in fair condition although the decoration of the common hallway was tired. The property is maintained through a service charge paid by the Landlords. The interior was well maintained. There were a few signs of damp, which appeared to be due to condensation.  Both the kitchen and bathroom were rather dated. Carpets, curtains and white goods, except the cooker, were not provided.

 

Law:

Sections 13 and 14 of the Housing Act 1988 apply

 

Open Market Rent:

The Committee using the experience of its members determined that the open market value of the subject property in good condition, with carpets curtains and white goods to be £525.00 per calendar month. However the Committee made a deduction of £50.00 per calendar month to take account of the dated kitchen and bathroom and the lack of carpets, curtains or white goods (other than a cooker). It should be noted that this figure cannot be a simple arithmetical calculation and is not based specifically upon capital cost but is the Committees estimate of the amount by which the rent would have to be reduced to attract a tenant.

Market Rent for subject property                            £475.00 per calendar month                                                                         

The Committee determined that in accordance with section 14 (1)(a) and (b) Housing Act 1977 the rent should commence from the beginning of the period of the Tenancy and having taken account of section 14 (7) Housing Act 1988, determined that the increased rent should take effect from the 1st July 2003.
