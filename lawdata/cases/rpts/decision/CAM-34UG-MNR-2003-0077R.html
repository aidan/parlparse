1.                  Background

 

1.1       On 9 June 2003 the tenant of the above premises referred to the Committee a notice of increase in rent served by the landlord pursuant to section 13(2) of the Act.

 

1.2       The landlord's notice dated 29 May 2003 proposed a rent of £450                   per month payable from 1 July 2003.                    .

 

1.3              The tenancy commenced in 1994. The tenant remains in occupation as a statutory periodic tenant. The current rent is £100 per month.

 

2.                  Inspection

 

2.1       The Committee inspected the premises on Friday 15 August 2003                     and found them to be in poor condition.

 

2.2        The following tenants improvements had been made to the premises:

Installation of a fire in the living room; fitted units in the kitchen.

 

2.3        The following services are provided for the tenant:

None

           

            2.4       At the inspection Mr & Mrs Oakley were handed a copy of a letter dated 14 August 2003 from the landlord's valuer comprising further submissions and details of alleged comparables. Mr & Mrs Oakley said they were unable to attend the hearing. They were requested to write to the Committee within 14 days if they wished to make any comments on the landlord's further representations handed to them.

 

3.                  Evidence

 

3.1              The committee received written representations from the landlord and from the tenant. These were copied to the other party.

 

3.2              A hearing was held at    The Town Hall, Brackley on 15 August 2003. The tenant was not present and was not represented. The landlord was not present but she was represented by Mr R W Moore MRICS of Anker & Partners, as an advocate. A colleague, as an observer accompanied him.

 

Oral evidence was given and representations were made on behalf of the landlord. Mr Moore referred to his letter dated 14 August 2003 and amplified some details of some of his comparables.

 

 

4.                  The Law

 

4.1              In accordance with the terms of section 14 of the Act, the Committee proceeded to determine the rent at which it considered the premises might reasonably be expected to be let on the open market by a willing landlord under an assured tenancy.

 

4.2              In doing so, the Committee, as required by section 14(1) of the Act, ignored the effect on rental value of the premises of any relevant tenant's improvements as defined in section 14(2) of the Act.

 

4.3       In coming to its decision the Committee had regard to the evidence supplied by the parties, and the members own general knowledge of market rental levels in the area of Helmdon and Brackley and the surrounding district. The Committee concluded that an appropriate market rent for the premises in their present condition would be £400 per month.

 

5.                  The Decision

 

5.1       The Committee decided that the rent, at which the premises might reasonably be expected to be let on the open market, would be £400 per month inclusive of any services.

 

5.2       This rent takes effect from 1 July 2003 being the date specified in the landlord's notice.
