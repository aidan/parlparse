The Property:

The property is a two storey mid-terraced house with rendered elevations to front and side with brick at the rear under a tile roof probably built in the 1920s. The property comprises an entrance hall from which rises stairs to the first floor, a living room, a dining room with small single storey flat roof extension and kitchen on the ground floor with three bedrooms an a bathroom on the first floor. Outside there is a garden to the rear and hard standing for off road parking to the front. There is a shared pedestrian access to the rear of the property. The property has mains electricity, gas, water and drainage. Space heating is by a gas central heating system and water heating is by a gas multi point heater. The property is situated in a relatively quiet road residential area about half a mile from the centre of London Colney.

 

The Condition

The Committee inspected the property in the presence of the Tenant. Externally the property had aluminium double glazed window units set in wooden frames. The frames showed signs of rot and the double glazed unit seals appeared to have perished. The exterior was in need of re-decoration. The roof and pointing on the chimneys was in poor condition. Internally the kitchen and bathroom were a little dated and the cistern overflow in the bathroom was unsatisfactory as it was connected to the sink outflow. The third bedroom was very small and there were also signs of damp in the rear bedroom. No carpets or curtains were provided. The kitchen was fitted with a cooker however the Tenant had replaced the fridge freezer provided by the Landlord. The property was let unfurnished except for two double beds and two wardrobes.

 

Law:

Sections 13 and 14 of the Housing Act 1988 apply

 

 

Open Market Rent:

The Tenant provided comparables of rent from London Colney and St Albans and the Landlord submitted comparables from St Albans. None of the comparables were direct and were asking rents as opposed to achieved rents. Therefore although the Committee noted the quoted comparables nevertheless it predominantly used the experience and knowledge of its members in assessing a rent. The Committee are of the opinion that the open market value of the subject property in good repair is £800.00 per calendar month. However the Committee made a deduction of £80.00 per calendar month to take account of the external disrepair and the lack of carpets and curtains. It should be noted that this figure cannot be a simple arithmetical calculation and is not based specifically upon capital cost but is the Committee’s estimate of the amount by which the rent would have to be reduced to attract a tenant. The Committee was of the opinion that the removal of the shelves and patio doors did not effect the rental value.

 

Market Rent for subject property                            £720.00 per calendar month                                                                         

The Committee determined that in accordance with section 14 (1)(a) and (b) Housing Act 1977 the rent should commence from the beginning of the period of the Tenancy and having taken account of section 14 (7) Housing Act 1988, determined that the increased rent should take effect from the 4th November 2003.
