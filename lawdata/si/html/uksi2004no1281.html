      STATUTORY INSTRUMENTS

      2004 No. 1281

      LOCAL GOVERNMENT, ENGLAND AND WALES

      The Recreation Grounds (Revocation of Parish Council Byelaws) (No. 2) Order 2004

        Made 6th May 2004 
        Laid before Parliament 13th May 2004 
        Coming into force 4th June 2004 

      Whereas it appears to the Secretary of State that the byelaw specified in article 2 of this Order has been substantially superseded by an instrument which applies to the area to which the byelaw applies:

           Now, therefore, the Secretary of State, in exercise of the powers conferred upon him by section 262(8)(d) of the Local Government Act 1972[1], hereby makes the following Order;

      Citation and commencement
           1. This Order may be cited as the Recreation Grounds (Revocation of Parish Council Byelaws) (No. 2) Order 2004 and shall come into force on 4th June 2004.

      Revocation
                 2. Byelaw No. 4 in a series of byelaws made by the Parish Council of Milnthorpe on 13th of October 1930[2] and allowed by the Minister of Health on 2nd December 1930 with respect to Milnthorpe Village Green is hereby revoked.



      Signed by authority of the First Secretary of State


      Phil Hope
      Parliamentary Under Secretary of State, Office of the Deputy Prime Minister

      6th May 2004


      EXPLANATORY NOTE

      (This note is not part of the Order)


      This Order revokes a byelaw which was made in exercise of the powers conferred on parish councils by section 8(1)(d) of the Local Government Act 1894 (56 and 57 Vict. c.73). The relevant part of that section was repealed by section 272(1) of, and Schedule 30 to, the Local Government Act 1972. The byelaw continued in force notwithstanding the repeal, by virtue of section 262(1) of the 1972 Act. The byelaw is revoked as being substantially superseded by an order making provisions as to the use of parking places, which was made by South Lakeland District Council under section 35 of the Road Traffic Regulation Act 1984.

      A full regulatory impact assessment has not been produced for this instrument, as it has no impact on the costs of businesses, charities or voluntary bodies.

       [1] 1972 c. 70back

      [2] Byelaw No. 4 was amended by a byelaw made by the Parish Council of Milnthorpe on 11th September 1961 and confirmed by the Secretary of State on 1st July 1962.back
