STATUTORY INSTRUMENTS

1999 No. 3208 (C. 84)

NORTHERN IRELAND

CONSTITUTIONAL LAW

The Northern Ireland Act 1998 (Appointed Day) Order 1999

  Made 1st December 1999 
  Coming into force 2nd December 1999 

At the Court at Buckingham Palace, the 1st day of December 1999

Present,

The Queen's Most Excellent Majesty in Council

Whereas, it having appeared to the Secretary of State that sufficient progress had been made in implementing the agreement reached at multi-party talks on Northern Ireland set out in Command Paper 3883 ("the Belfast Agreement"), he laid before Parliament the draft of this Order:

     And whereas the draft Order has been approved by a resolution of each House of Parliament:

     And whereas the Secretary of State has submitted the draft Order to Her Majesty in Council:

     Now, therefore, Her Majesty, in exercise of the powers conferred by section 3(2) of the Northern Ireland Act 1998[1] is pleased, by and with the advice of Her Privy Council, to order, and it is hereby ordered, as follows:-

     1. This Order may be cited as the Northern Ireland Act 1998 (Appointed Day) Order 1999 and shall come into force on 2nd December 1999.

     2. The appointed day for the commencement of Parts II and III of the Northern Ireland Act 1998 is 2nd December 1999.


A. K. Galloway
Clerk of the Privy Council

EXPLANATORY NOTE

(This note is not part of the Order)


This Order appoints 2nd December 1999 as the day for the commencement of Parts II and III of the Northern Ireland Act 1998. Part II, which deals with the legislative powers of the Northern Ireland Assembly, and Part III, which deals with the Executive Authorities, implement, in those regards, the Belfast Agreement.

The Northern Ireland Act 1998 (Commencement No. 5) Order 1999 (S.I. 1999/3209 (C. 85)) brings into force on the day appointed under this Order the following provisions of the 1998 Act:
